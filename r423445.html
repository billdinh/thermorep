Plain Yoghurt in Varoma

Ingredients

  • 120 g plain yoghurt
  • 800 g whole milk (see tip)
  • 40 g sugar (see tip) (optional)
  • 250 g water
  • 30 g lemon juice (1 lemon, see tip)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
400 kJ / 96 kcal
Protein
4 g
Carbohydrates
10 g
Fat
4 g

Preparation

  1. Place yoghurt, milk and sugar (if using) in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Start Warm Up /37°C.
  2. Pour yoghurt mixture into glass yoghurt jars (120 g each, see tip) and arrange in centre of Varoma dish. Close jars with their lids or cover with a loose sheet of heat-resistant cling film.
  3. Place water and lemon juice in mixing bowl. Place covered Varoma into position and start Fermentation /10h/70°C. Remove Varoma and place jars in fridge for at least 2 hours before serving or store in fridge for up to 8 days.

Useful Items

glass yoghurt jars (120 g), heat-resistant cling film, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Plain, unflavoured yoghurt is delicious on its own, or combine with other ingredients for an endless variety of flavours. Serve with fruit and cereal, or use in smoothies or ice creams. Yoghurt also works well in savoury dishes, providing a good base for dips, sauces or salad dressings.
  • Use either cold or room temperature milk.
  • If not using sugar, mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  • To protect against rust, lemon juice or ascorbic acid must be added to the water in step 3.
  • To prepare jars, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jars and lids upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jars and lids in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heat in the oven at 100˚C until required.
