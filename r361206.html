Florida Cuban Rolls

Ingredients

  • vegetable oil, to grease
  • 20 oz all-purpose flour
  • 4 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, softened and cubed
  • 12 oz water, plus extra to brush or spray

Preparation

  1. Line two baking sheets with parchment and lightly grease a large bowl with oil.
  2. Place flour, sugar, salt, yeast, butter and water into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5, then knead 3 min/.
  3. Shape dough into a ball, transfer to prepared bowl, and turn to coat entire surface with oil. Cover with cling wrap and let rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hr.).
  4. Punch down risen dough and divide into six pieces. Roll each piece into a rough log shape (approx. 6 in.) and gently flatten. Fold dough into thirds and continue to roll into a log applying gentle pressure from the middle of the roll outward, shaping each piece into a round log (approx. 8 in., tapered at ends), taking care to preserve the air bubbles trapped within. Pinch seam of roll with fingers to seal.
  5. Place shaped dough seam side down onto prepared baking sheet (approx. 2 in. apart). Cover with a damp tea towel and let rise for 20-30 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 375°F. Brush or spray rolls with water. With a sharp knife, slash one long lengthwise slit down the middle of each roll (approx. ¼ in. deep).
  7. Bake for 20-22 minutes (375°F) or until golden brown. Allow to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, pastry brush, wire rack, cling wrap, baking sheets, tea towel, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Rolls may be made one day in advance: wrap tightly in cling wrap and store at room temperature or freeze for several weeks.
  • Use rolls to make Cuban sandwiches: slice rolls in half lengthwise. Spread 2 tbsp butter on half of a Cuban Roll and a thin layer of mustard on the other. Add 1-2 layers of ham, pork, cheese, and sliced pickles on the buttered bread and top with the mustard-spread half. Cook on a panini maker or grill for 2-3 minutes until browned and cheese is melted.
