Angel Berry Trifle

Ingredients

White Chocolate Pudding

  • 5 ½ oz white chocolate, in pieces
  • 18 oz milk
  • 3 ½ oz sugar
  • 1 oz corn starch
  • 2 large eggs

Trifle

  • 6 oz cream cheese, room temperature
  • 8 oz plain Greek yogurt
  • 4 oz whipped cream, store-bought

Assembly

  • 26 oz angel food cake, store-bought, cubed (¾ in.), (approx. 2 x 13 oz ea.), divided
  • 30 - 40 oz fresh mixed berries (see Tip), plus extra to garnish, divided
  • 12 oz whipped cream, store-bought, divided

Preparation

White Chocolate Pudding

  1. Place white chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add milk, sugar, corn starch and eggs and cook 12 min/195°F/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl, set aside and allow to cool before making the Trifle.

Trifle

  1. Place cream cheese, yogurt, whipped cream and reserved White Chocolate Pudding into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4.

Assembly

  1. Place ½ of the cake cubes into the base of a trifle dish. Layer ½ the trifle mixture over the cake followed by ⅓ of the berries, and then ⅓ of the whipped cream. Repeat layers, finishing with remaining ⅔ whipped cream and mixed berries. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Useful Items

trifle bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Make angel food cake and whipped cream before starting Trifle or buy pre-made.
    1. Make the angel food cake from recipe in Taste of Home Thermomix® recipe collection. Store in an airtight container until needed. Angel food cake can be made and frozen then defrosted to room temperature before using.
    2. Make whipped cream from recipe in Basic Cookbook. Store whipped cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator until needed.
  • Examples of mixed berries: strawberries (hulled and sliced), raspberries, blueberries, blackberries.
  • Mixed berries can be replaced with canned or fresh peaches or pears.
