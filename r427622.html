Spiced Carrot Fritters with Yoghurt Dip

Ingredients

Yoghurt Dip

  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 150 g fat-free plain yoghurt

Fritters

  • 120 g wholemeal bread (approx. 3-4 slices), torn in pieces
  • 500 g water
  • 500 g carrots, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch ground turmeric
  • 45 g spring onions (approx. 4), thinly sliced (5 mm)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes (optional)
  • 2 tsp olive oil, for frying

Preparation

Yoghurt Dip

  1. Place coriander in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl, place bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in yoghurt. Stir to combine, cover with cling film and place in fridge until needed.

Fritters

  1. Place bread in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in 250 g carrots then steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard steaming water.
  3. Place remaining 250 g carrots, steamed carrots, cumin, coriander, salt, pepper and turmeric in mixing bowl then mix 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add spring onions, egg, reserved breadcrumbs and chilli flakes (if using) then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Roll tablespoons of mixture into small flat discs (approx. 25 g each, 1 cm thick) and place on a plate until all mixture has been used (approx. 26 patties). Place in fridge for 30 minutes.
  5. After this time, heat oil in a large frying pan over a medium-high heat and fry fritters in batches for approx. 4-5 minutes each side or until golden brown and cooked.
  6. Serve fritters warm with reserved yoghurt dip.

Useful Items

large frying pan, cling film, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • The additional fibre in wholemeal bread compared to white bread means that food is broken down more slowly, resulting in a steadier increase in blood sugar. This simple substitute is an easy way to help lower the GI of a meal. This and the use of low-GI carrots make these fritters a great, low-GI snack or starter.
