Crunchy Granola

Ingredients

  • 600 g oat flakes
  • 60 g dried grated coconut
  • 100 g wheat germs
  • 60 g sunflower seeds, shelled
  • 70 g sesame seeds
  • 30 g pumpkin seeds, shelled
  • 200 g mixed nuts (e.g. hazelnuts, pecans, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, almonds, peanuts) (see tip)
  • 70 g linseeds
  • 250 g raisins
  • 180 g spelt flakes
  • 200 g dried apricots
  • 100 g honey
  • 210 g coconut oil
  • 60 g milk
  • 100 g barley malt syrup (see tip)
  • 1 tsp fine salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C and line two large baking pans (approx. 45 x 30 x 5 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in: oats, coconut, wheat germ, sunflower seeds, sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds, mixed nuts, flax seeds, raisins and spelt flakes and set bowl aside.
  3. Place apricots into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer to the bowl of dry ingredients.
  4. Place honey, oil, milk, malt syrup and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/37°C/speed 3.5. Pour over dry ingredients in large bowl and stir well with spatula until completely covered.
  5. Divide uncooked granola into four batches by weighing uncooked granola back into mixing bowl, (approx. 600 g per batch). Spread out two batches of granola on prepared baking pans. Bake two baking pans (batches) at the same time, for 20-21 minutes (160°C) in total. Set the oven timer for 7 minute intervals, stirring carefully and gently each time. Set aside and allow to cool completely. Repeat process with remaining two batches. Once granola is fully cooled transfer to air-tight jars or containers and store until needed.

Useful Items

baking paper, large bowl, air-tight jars or containers, baking trays, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Halve ingredients to make a smaller quantity.
  • Perfect served on its own or with milk, yogurt or fresh fruit.
  • Sprinkle over vanilla ice cream for a quick dessert.
  • Chop mixed nuts into bite sized pieces before starting recipe.
  • Barley malt syrup can be found in health food stores. It may be substituted with molasses or honey.
  • This recipe is very flexible, so feel free to adapt it depending on what ingredients you have on hand.
