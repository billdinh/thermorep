Chocolate and Sesame Ganache

Ingredients

Chocolate and Sesame Ganache

  • 125 g double cream
  • ½ tsp cocoa powder
  • 50 g liquid glucose
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • 200 g dark chocolate (64%), small pieces or callets
  • 200 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 3 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 50 g sesame oil
  • 25 g vegetable oil
  • 250 g double cream, semi whipped (soft peak stage)

Ginger Ice Cream

  • 750 g whole milk
  • 250 g double cream
  • 75 g liquid glucose
  • 40 g trimoline
  • 30 g milk powder
  • 50 g fresh root ginger, grated
  • 200 g egg yolks
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 20 g stem ginger in syrup, syrup only
  • 50 g stem ginger, chopped in small pieces

Pear Purée

  • 3 pears, medium, peeled, cored (approx. 300 g)
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • ½ lemon, juice only

Garnish

  • 16 halved walnuts, for grating over plates
  • 4 pears, medium, peeled, sliced (1 mm) then cut in 2 cm discs
  • coriander cress, for garnish

Preparation

Chocolate and Sesame Ganache

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 125 g double cream, cocoa powder, glucose and salt in mixing bowl then mix 2 min/50°C/speed 2.
  2. Without measuring cup, set speed 1 and slowly add dark chocolate and milk chocolate through hole in mixing bowl lid. Melt 5 min/40°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks in mixing bowl then whisk 3 min/speed 4, until pale and creamy.
  4. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid then weigh in sesame oil and vegetable oil. Without measuring cup, set speed 3. Slowly drizzle in oils. Fold into chocolate mixture. Then fold in semi-whipped cream.
  5. Using cling film, form mixture into 4 x 32 cm x Ø 3 cm logs. Place in fridge to set for approx. 6 hours, preferably overnight. Clean mixing bowl.

Ginger Ice Cream

  1. Place milk, double cream, glucose, trimoline, milk powder and ginger in mixing bowl then boil 5 min/100°C/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl, leave to cool, then allow to infuse overnight in the fridge. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. The next day, strain infused liquid and place in mixing bowl.
  3. Add egg yolks, sugar and syrup then cook 6 min/85°C/speed 5. Strain through a fine sieve then churn in an ice cream machine (see tip for alternative method). Add ginger near end of churning. Store in freezer until required. Clean mixing bowl.

Pear Purée

  1. Place pears in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add butter and sugar then cook 5 min/100°C/speed . Blend 2 min/speed 6. Remove mixing bowl and leave to cool. Add lemon juice. Pass through a fine sieve then cover and place in fridge to cool. Remove 30 minutes before required.

Garnish

  1. Remove chocolate ganache logs from fridge and trim either end through cling film, using a sharp knife dipped in hot water. Cut 3 even cylinders from each log then remove cling film.
  2. Place a ganache cylinder on a plate. Place some grated walnuts next to it then add a scoop of ginger ice cream on top of walnuts. Dot pear purée around the plate. Arrange pear discs and remaining grated walnuts over the plate. Dress with a little coriander cress before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, fine mesh sieve, long sharp knife, jug, ice cream machine, fridge, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Slice the pears on a mandolin into 1 mm slices then cut out discs using a 2 cm pastry cutter.
  • To make ice cream without an ice cream machine - pour mixture into ice cube trays and freeze until solid. Place ice cream cubes in mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 6. Add stem ginger pieces then, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid, churn 10 sec/speed 4.
