Sago plum pudding

Ingredients

  • 80 g sago
  • 520 g full cream milk, for soaking
  • 40 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 180 g dark brown sugar
  • 70 g breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 60 g pitted dates
  • 60 g dried figs
  • 30 g dried apricots
  • 30 g dried apples
  • 30 g dried currants
  • 30 g glacé cherries
  • 50 g sultanas
  • 1 pinch ground allspice
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 egg
  • 1700 g water

Preparation

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh sago and milk into it. Place bowl in refrigerator and leave sago to soak for a minimum 8 hours or overnight.
  2. Grease and line a pudding basin with lid (13 cm) and set aside.
  3. Place lemon zest and dark brown sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add breadcrumbs, bicarbonate of soda and 40 g unsalted butter and combine 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place pitted dates, dried figs, dried apricots and dried apple into mixing bowl and chop 5-8 sec/speed 8, or until an evenly chopped consistency is achieved.
  6. Add dried currants, glacé cherries, sultanas, ground allspice, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, egg, reserved sago and milk and reserved brown sugar-breadcrumb mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  7. Transfer into prepared pudding basin, cover with a sheet of baking paper and secure lid. Place pudding basin into Varoma dish. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and steam 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  9. Check water level in mixing bowl and top up if necessary. Place Varoma back into position and steam for a further 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  10. Check water level in mixing bowl again and top up if necessary. Place Varoma back into position and steam for a further 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  11. Check water level in mixing bowl again and top up if necessary. Place Varoma back into position and steam for a final 60 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until pudding is cooked through and consistent in colour. Your pudding is cooked through when a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the pudding comes out clean. Serve warm.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, baking paper, pudding basin with lid (13 cm), wooden skewer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

Cloth-wrapped pudding: increase the breadcrumbs to 370 g, adding 270 g into the mixing bowl in step 4 and setting aside 100 g of the breadcrumbs for wrapping the pudding later. In step 7, transfer pudding mixture into a sealable container and store overnight in the refrigerator.

Cut 2 pieces of calico (45 x 45 cm). Place 1 piece of calico into a bowl of boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then carefully remove calico wearing thick rubber gloves and using a pair of tongs. Squeeze calico to remove excess water, then lay calico flat onto a silicone bread mat or work surface. Place approx. 50 g of the reserved breadcrumbs into the centre of the calico and spread out to cover an area approx. 15 cm in diameter. Remove pudding mixture from refrigerator and place exactly half of the mixture into the middle of the the breadcrumbs (return remaining mixture to the refrigerator until required).

Roll mixture in calico into a ball, covering it completely with the breadcrumbs as you do. Gather the cloth around the pudding, avoiding deep pleats. Pat into a round shape and tie calico as tightly and as close to the pudding as possible with cooking string. Pull the ends of the calico up through the string to ensure pudding is as round and firm as possible.

Place pudding string side down into the simmering basket. Place simmering basket with pudding into the Varoma dish then steam pudding as per steps 8-11, ensuring that the Varoma lid is secure and properly in position at all times. Repeat the same process with remaining pudding mixture when required. 

To store: remove calico while pudding is still warm to prevent skin from sticking. Leave pudding to stand for at least 20 minutes, or until the pudding skin thickens and becomes firm. Leave pudding to cool completely, then wrap in plastic wrap and place into a sealable storage container. Place pudding into refrigerator to store for up to 1 week.

In Australia, we generally prefer to remove the calico from the pudding before we store it as mould can form in warmer weather.

  • Serve pudding warm with Brandy cream sauce or Brandy cashew cream, garnished with fresh cherries.
  • If using a different size pudding basin or baking tin please be aware cooking times will be lengthened or shortened accordingly. Continue checking water level and topping up as necessary.
