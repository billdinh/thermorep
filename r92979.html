Arbroath Smokie Soup

Ingredients

  • 800 g fresh tomatoes, halved
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 30 g butter
  • 50 g shallots
  • 50 g leeks, white part, cut in pieces
  • 50 g unsmoked bacon, cut in pieces (optional)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g Arbroath smokie, bones and skin removed, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 20 g tomato purée
  • 500 g fish stock
  • 25 g lemon juice
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 140 g double cream

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Place tomatoes in a roasting dish, then drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with sugar and thyme leaves. Roast for 20 minutes (170°C). Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place butter, shallots, leeks, bacon (if using) and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add Arbroath Smokie or smoked haddock, roasted tomatoes together with juices, tomato purée, stock, lemon juice, balsamic vinegar, paprika, salt and pepper then cook 12 min/100°C/speed 2. Blend 2 min/speed 10.
  4. Add cream and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Taste and add extra seasoning if needed before serving hot.

Useful Items

roasting dish, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This delicately flavoured soup would be equally at home on the table of a fine restaurant, as a starter at a dinner party, or as a lunch served with crusty bread. You can substitute smoked mackerel or kippers for the Arbroath Smokie if you wish - all are oily fish which are rich in vitamins A and D and omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Serves 6 for lunch or 8 as a starter.
