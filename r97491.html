Cauliflower and ricotta pizza bases with blue cheese, pear and prosciutto

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Pizza bases

  • 500 g cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 egg

Pizza topping

  • 1 tbsp olive oil, for brushing
  • ½ pear, thinly sliced
  • 100 g blue cheese, crumbled
  • 150 g prosciutto, thinly sliced
  • 40 g rocket leaves

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pizza bases

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a large baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place cauliflower into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer onto lined baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (200°C), stirring every 5 minutes to ensure cauliflower doesn't burn. Set aside and leave to cool slightly. Rinse and dry mixing bowl. Reduce oven temperature to 180°C.
  3. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add cooled cauliflower, egg and 80g of the reserved ricotta and mix 6 sec/speed 6.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 6 sec/speed 6.
  6. Spread cauliflower mixture onto lined baking tray, forming 1 large or 2 smaller pizza bases. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C), until lightly golden. Allow to cool slightly.

Pizza topping

  1. Brush pizza bases with oil. Top pizzas with sliced pear, blue cheese and prosciutto, then bake for 5-10 minutes (180°C), until cheese has melted. Top with rocket and serve hot.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, bowl, baking paper, pastry brush, large baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use any combination of toppings you like on your pizza.
  • Save the extra ricotta to use in pasta bakes, as a filling for baked mushrooms or in calzones.
