Salted Caramel and Chocolate Brownie Cake

Ingredients

Chocolate Sponge Cakes

  • 60 g butter, unsalted, cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 8 eggs (53-63 g each)
  • 170 g self-raising flour
  • 80 g cocoa powder, unsweetened
  • 2 pinches salt

Cream and Salted Caramel Filling

  • 100 g liquid caramel
  • 100 g butter, cut in pieces
  • 1 tsp sea salt, adjust to taste
  • 680 g cream, min. 30% fat

Preparation

Chocolate Sponge Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease 2 springform tins (Ø 20 cm) and line the bases with baking paper, then set aside.
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 4 min/75°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place sugar and eggs in mixing bowl then whisk 9 min/37°C/speed 4.
  4. Whisk for a further 3 min/speed 4 while very slowly pouring reserved melted butter on mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades.
  5. Add flour, cocoa powder and salt around butterfly whisk then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully complete stirring with spatula. Transfer mixture to prepared springform tins (approx. 450 g per tin, see tip) and bake immediately for 20-25 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile clean and dry mixing bowl. Allow cakes to cool approx. 10 minutes before removing from springform tins and transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Cream and Salted Caramel Filling

  1. Place liquid caramel and butter in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/70°C/speed 1.
  2. Add sea salt and 80 g cream and mix 10 sec/speed 4, then reduce without measuring cup 9 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer salted caramel to a bowl, allow to cool and clean mixing bowl immediately.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 600 g cream in mixing bowl and whip without measuring cup speed 3, without setting a time, until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid overwhipping. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. To assemble the cakes, cut each one in half horizontally using a long knife. Place one sponge bottom half on a serving plate, spread 2 tbsp reserved salted caramel and a quarter of reserved cream on top. Put cream on the next sponge bottom and repeat this step two more times, then place the last layer of cake on top. Spread the remaining cream over the top of the cake, cover the surface of the cream with small drops of salted caramel and then drag a wet fork through the caramel and cream to create waves. Refrigerate until ready to serve. 

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, cooling rack, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Liquid caramel is a useful flavouring to have in the kitchen. It is available online or at any good delicatessen or supermarket.


  • Use the built-in Thermomix scales to measure out the exact quantity of cake batter for each cake tin. At the end of the recipe, turn scales on, lift out the mixing bowl and pour some cake batter into the prepared tin. You need to pour 450 g into each tin. When you return the mixing bowl to the base, it will show a minus figure indicating the weight removed (- 450 g).
  • Cutting the cake horizontally is easy - you can either use a long knife, or this method: Score a line around the outside of the cake as a guide, keeping the line 1 cm from the table all the way around. Slide a string into the cut and pull through the cake to make two layers.
