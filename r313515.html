Steamed pork buns (baozi)

Ingredients

Dough

  • 250 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 230 g baker's flour
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 50 g white sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

Filling

  • 5 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 500 g pork mince
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • ¼ tsp Chinese five spice powder (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine

Assembly

  • oil, for greasing
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 700 g water

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add yeast and baking powder and mix 30 sec/speed 2.
  3. Add flours, sugar and salt and knead 5 min/. Transfer dough into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside to rest for approx. 1 hour or until dough triples in volume. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Prepare filling while dough rests.

Filling

  1. Place spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add mince, pepper, sugar, five spice powder, oyster sauce, soy sauce, sesame oil and cooking wine and mix 30 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Oil both Varoma dish and Varoma tray and set aside. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured work surface, shape into a log and cut into 24 portions. Using a rolling pin, flatten each portion into a circle (approx. 10 cm). Place 1 tablespoon of filling into the centre of each circle, then wrap sides of dough over filling, similar to a flattened money-bag shape, and pinch edges together to seal. Place 6 buns each into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray, seam side up. Set aside to rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  3. Place remaining buns into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve Pork buns warm with sauce of choice (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, rolling pin, bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own Chinese five-spice powder, please refer to the recipe in the "Hong Kong-style roast pork belly" recipe in this collection.
  • You can serve these buns on their own, or with any dipping sauce of choice, such as soy sauce.
