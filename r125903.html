Peach and plum upside-down cake

Ingredients

  • 100 g extra virgin coconut oil, plus extra for greasing
  • ¼ tsp grated nutmeg (optional)
  • 80 g brown sugar, plus extra 2 tsp for sprinkling (see Tips)
  • 2 fresh blood plums (approx. 100 g each), stones removed and cut into 6 or 8 wedges
  • 2 peaches (approx. 100 g each), stones removed and cut into 6 or 8 wedges
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only (approx. 2-3 tsp)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 100 g milk (see Tips)
  • 200 g wholemeal spelt flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • Greek natural yoghurt, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Grease and line a round cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Sprinkle nutmeg (optional) and 2 teaspoons of the brown sugar over base of prepared tin. Arrange plum and peach wedges in a circular pattern, alternating colours.
  3. Place oil, rosemary and remaining 80 g sugar into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add eggs, vanilla and milk and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add flour, baking powder and bicarbonate of soda and mix 10 sec/speed 4, until combined.
  6. Spoon mixture over fruit and smooth surface. Bake for 35-40 minutes (150°C), or until golden and a skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5-10 minutes, then turn out onto a serving plate. Serve with yoghurt (optional).

Useful Items

round cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, serving plate, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make this recipe dairy free, use almond milk instead of cow’s milk and skip the yoghurt when serving.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this recipe - either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
