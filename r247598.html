Creamy Truffled Mushroom Tagliatelle - Tagliatelle con crema di funghi tartufata

Ingredients

  • 180 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 40 g truffle oil, plus extra for serving
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 300 g mixed fresh mushrooms, sliced if large
  • water, for cooking pasta
  • 2 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g white wine
  • 250 g double cream
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra for serving
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 320 g dried tagliatelle

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions, garlic, oil and butter in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, without measuring cup, sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add mushrooms and, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/90°C//speed . Meanwhile, bring a large saucepan of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil.
  4. Add wine, cream, 100 g reserved grated Parmesan, thyme, remaining 1½ tsp salt and pepper to mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, cook 8 min/90°C//speed . Meanwhile, cook tagliatelle in saucepan according to packet instructions, or until al dente.
  5. Drain tagliatelle using a colander and transfer to a serving bowl. Pour mushroom sauce over pasta and toss thoroughly to combine.
  6. Serve hot, sprinkled with remaining 80 g grated Parmesan and thyme leaves, then drizzled with truffle oil, if desired.

Useful Items

serving bowl, large saucepan, colander, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Tagliatelle comes from the Italian 'tagliare', meaning ‘to cut’. It originated in the Emilia-Romagna region in Northern Italy and dates back to the 15th century. Wider, thicker pasta shapes like tagliatelle work well when paired with thick sauces. To showcase this, we’ve created a luxurious pasta dish that features earthy mushrooms stirred through a rich Parmesan cream sauce, finished with the unmistakably heady aroma of white truffle oil.
  • The dried tagliatelle in this recipe can be replaced with homemade or fresh shop-bought pasta, if preferred.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
  • Substitute the tagliatelle with any other variety of long, dried pasta such as pappardelle or spaghetti.
