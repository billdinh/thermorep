Ice cream cookie bowls

Ingredients

Ice cream

  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 150 g condensed milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 2 tbsp brewed espresso coffee, cooled
  • 2 tbsp Kahlua®

Cookie bowls

  • 100 g butter, cut into pieces
  • 150 g light brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 180 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch salt

Coconut crumble

  • 30 g coconut oil
  • 60 g pecan nuts
  • 50 g hazelnuts, skins removed
  • 45 g honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 50 g shredded coconut

Chocolate sauce

  • 100 g dark chocolate, cut into pieces
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

Ice cream

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream, condensed milk and vanilla bean paste into mixing bowl and whisk 30-40 sec/speed 4, until stiff peaks form, taking care not to overwhip cream.
  2. Add coffee and Kahlua® and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer mixture into a freezable container and place into freezer for a minimum 6 hours or overnight. Remove butterfly whisk. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cookie bowls

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with muffin wrappers (see Tips) and set aside.
  2. Place butter, brown sugar, egg and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 2 min/speed 3.
  3. Add flour, bicarbonate of soda and salt and mix 40 sec/speed 3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface. Shape dough into a ball, then wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Divide dough into 10 equal portions (approx. 40 g each portion). On a silicone bread mat or floured work surface, gently flatten each dough portion into a disc shape large enough to cover the base and sides of each muffin hole. Transfer disc into prepared muffin holes, gently pressing dough into base and sides, to form a bowl shape. Line each cookie bowl with baking paper and fill with dried beans or uncooked rice.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes (180°C), or until just starting to brown. Carefully remove beans or rice and baking paper, then return to oven for a further 5-8  minutes or until crisp and brown. Cool for 5 minutes in muffin pan, then carefully remove and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Coconut crumble

  1. Reduce oven temperature to 160°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place coconut oil into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add pecans, hazelnuts, honey and vanilla and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/2 times.
  4. Add shredded coconut and mix 5 sec//speed 1.
  5. Spread mixture onto prepared baking tray and bake for 8-10 minutes (160°C) or until brown. Cool completely, then transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate sauce

  1. Place dark chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cream and heat 2 min/50°C/speed 3.
  3. To assemble, place a scoop of Ice cream into Cookie bowl, then drizzle with Chocolate sauce and sprinkle with reserved crumble. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

freezable storage container, 12-hole muffin tray, muffin wrap papers, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, baking paper, pie weights, rice or dried beans, baking paper, wire rack, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), bowl, ice cream scoop, freezer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We suggest preparing the ice cream, cookie bowls and coconut crumble several hours in advance (or the day before). Make the chocolate sauce about 10 minutes prior to serving, as it will be easier to drizzle while it's still warm.
  • Strong black instant coffee (brewed and cooled) can be used in place of brewed espresso coffee.
  • Muffin wrappers can be found in the baking aisle of supermarkets. Do not use any other kind of paper liners (i.e. cupcake liners) as the pleats will get stuck in the cookie dough.

 Vanilla ice cream:  Remove Kahlua® and espresso coffee in step 2 and proceed as per recipe.
