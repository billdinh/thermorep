Prosciutto and Mushroom Pizza

Ingredients

Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 400 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for flouring
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus 1 pinch
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 200 g button mushrooms, sliced (3 mm)
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 200 g mozzarella, drained and sliced (4 mm)
  • 100 g prosciutto, sliced

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil.
  2. Add remaining 170 g water, flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film then set aside to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours). Meanwhile, make topping.

Topping

  1. Place tomatoes, 10 g oil, and salt in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place remaining 10 g oil and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add mushrooms, thyme, 1 pinch salt and pepper then cook 5 min/120°C//speed 1. Drain any excess liquid through a sieve then set aside.
  4. Towards end of rising time, preheat oven to 230°C and line 3 baking trays with baking paper.
  5. Divide dough into 3 balls then, on a floured work surface, roll out 3 circles (approx. Ø 26 cm). Cover each circle with cling film while rolling out remaining circles to prevent drying out.
  6. Transfer pizza bases to prepared baking trays and spread reserved tomato sauce, sliced mozzarella, reserved cooked mushrooms and prosciutto equally between pizzas then bake for 15-20 minutes (230°C) until edges are golden.
  7. Serve hot, cut in slices.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, medium mesh sieve, 3 baking trays and baking paper, rolling pin, oven
