Char Siu Pork with Rice

Ingredients

  • 4 ½ oz hoisin sauce
  • 4 oz ketchup
  • 3 oz dark brown sugar
  • 3 oz dry sherry
  • 2 ½ oz soy sauce, light
  • ¾ tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 2 pork tenderloins (approx. 2-2½ lb total)
  • 8 oz jasmine rice
  • 30 oz water
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • green onions, chopped, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place hoisin, ketchup, brown sugar, sherry, soy sauce and five spice into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/160°F/speed 3.
  2. Reserve approximately half the sauce for basting and set aside. Transfer remaining sauce into a large zip lock bag and add pork tenderloins. Fully coat the pork and refrigerate overnight.
  3. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in rice. Remove simmering basket with spatula, rinse rice under cold running water until water runs clear.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket with rinsed rice. Set Varoma dish into postition. Cut two pieces of parchment (approx. 12 in. x 12 in.). Discard marinating liquid, place marinated pork onto parchment and fold to form two parcels. Place parcels into Varoma dish, secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and stir rice with spatula. Set Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and set aside. Insert measuring cup and steam rice a further 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Season rice with salt and pepper and set aside to keep warm.
  5. Preheat oven broiler. Line a baking pan with foil and place pork on prepared pan, baste steamed pork tenderloins with reserved sauce and broil for 3-5 minutes. Slice Char Siu Pork, garnish with green onions and serve with steamed rice and remaining sauce.

Useful Items

large zip lock freezer bag, basting brush, parchment paper, baking pan, aluminum foil, refrigerator, oven broiler
