Lemon and Poppy Seed Battenberg Cake

Ingredients

Lemon Curd

  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin and 35 g juice
  • 75 g granulated sugar
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs

Sponge

  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin and 1 tsp juice
  • 175 g caster sugar
  • 175 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 175 g self-raising flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • pink food colouring paste
  • 2 Tbsp poppy seeds

Assembly and Decoration

  • 50 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 320 g marzipan, homemade
  • 1 - 2 tsp lemon juice
  • cake decorations

Preparation

Lemon Curd

  1. Place lemon peel and 30 g sugar in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining 45 g sugar, butter, lemon juice and egg yolks then mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then heat 5 min/80°C/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make sponges.

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and line 2 large loaf tins (900 g/2 lb) with baking paper.
  2. Place lemon peel and 50 g caster sugar in mixing bowl then grind 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add remaining 125 g sugar and butter then cream 20 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add flour, eggs, baking powder and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.

Flavourings

  1. Set scale to zero and transfer half of sponge mixture (approx. 340 g) to a bowl (scales will show -340 g). Stir in food colouring and lemon juice then transfer to one of the prepared tins.
  2. Add poppy seeds to remaining mixture in mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Finish mixing by hand with spatula then transfer to second prepared tin.
  3. Bake for 35-40 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Allow to cool in tins for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. When sponges have cooled, trim tops of cakes if risen, so the surfaces are flat. Place one sponge on top of the other then trim edges so they are both the same size. Cut sponges in half lengthways to make 4 long rectangles.
  2. Brush one long side of a pink sponge with reserved lemon curd and sandwich together with a poppy seed sponge. Repeat with the remaining sponges. Sandwich the sponge pairs together with lemon curd, like a chequerboard.
  3. On a work surface lightly dusted with icing sugar, roll out marzipan into a rectangle as long as sponges, about 4 mm thick and large enough to wrap around cake completely, with ends exposed.
  4. Place cake on top of marzipan. Spread lemon curd on long edges of sponge then, leaving ends exposed, wrap marzipan around it. Make sure marzipan is smooth and sealed tightly with the joins slightly offset from sponge joins. Trim excess marzipan then transfer to a serving plate.

Decoration

  1. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in icing sugar. Remove and add 1 tsp lemon juice. Mix together, adding up to 1 tsp more lemon juice if necessary, until a thick consistency. Drizzle lemon glacé icing over marzipan and decorate with cake decorations and dots of lemon curd.
  2. Serve in slices.

Useful Items

2 x 900 g loaf tins (2 lb), baking paper, large bowl, wire rack, skewer, bread knife, pastry brush, rolling pin, serving plate, long sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The sponge trimmings can be made into cake pops or simply enjoyed with some lemon curd.
  • A Battenberg tin can also be used instead of the loaf tins if you have one.

This slight spin on the classic, using lemon curd and poppy seeds, makes a really pretty centrepiece. Although the name refers to the town of Battenberg in Germany, the cake’s origins lie in England where it is thought to have been named in honour of the marriage of Princess Victoria to Prince Louis of Battenberg in 1884.
