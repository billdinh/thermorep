Herbed mushrooms with pearl barley and roasted hazelnuts

Ingredients

Herbed butter

  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 30 g fresh chives, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 120 g unsalted butter, softened

Steamed mushrooms

  • 200 g pearl barley, soaked overnight
  • 20 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 400 g boiling water
  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 150 g), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 80 g fresh bella mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 sprig fresh basil, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ¼ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1000 g water
  • 4 fresh Portabello mushrooms, gills and stems removed, if preferred
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 30 g hazelnuts, roasted and skins removed

Preparation

Herbed butter

  1. Place parsley, chives and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat chopping 3 sec/speed 7, until a fine consistency is achieved.
  2. Add butter and blend 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Herbs and butter should be well combined. Repeat blending if necessary. Transfer into a bowl, cover and place in refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Steamed mushrooms

  1. Drain soaking barley through simmering basket and set aside.
  2. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh porcini mushrooms and boiling water into it.
  3. Set mushrooms aside to soak for 30 minutes. Drain porcini stock through a fine mesh sieve into a jug and set mushrooms and stock aside separately.
  4. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place onion, garlic and bella mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add oil, basil, thyme, salt and pepper and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Add reserved drained porcini mushrooms and sauté 5 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until nearly all liquid has evaporated, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and set aside to keep warm.
  8. Place water and reserved porcini stock into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and place drained barley into it. Cook 12 min/100°C/speed 1.
  9. Place Portabello mushrooms onto Varoma tray, gill side up, sprinkle with salt and pepper and spoon 1-2 tsp of the reserved herb butter into each.
  10. Place Varoma into position and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until mushrooms and barley are cooked to your preference. Retain porcini stock in mixing bowl.
  11. Set Varoma with mushrooms aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, drain residual liquid from barley and transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl with reserved porcini mixture to keep warm.
  12. Add reserved Parmesan, spinach, roasted hazelnuts and 60 g of the reserved porcini stock to thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and stir to combine.
  13. Place a portabello mushroom on each plate, top with barley mixture and a little extra herbed butter, if desired and serve hot.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, fine mesh sieve, large jug, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Herb butter can be stored in the refrigerator until ready to use. Add herb butter to your favourite savoury stews, casseroles, risottos or soups for extra depth of flavour. Adjust the quantity and variety of herbs to your preference.
  • Mix and match the mushrooms used in this dish to your preference and what is readily available and in season.
