Vegan walnut and black bean burger

Ingredients

Burger buns

  • 1 tsp oil, for greasing and brushing
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 220 g water
  • 340 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp salt

Patties

  • 1200 g water, plus an extra 3 tablespoons
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 100 g white quinoa
  • 1 tbsp flaxseeds (linseeds)
  • 30 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 30 g eschalots
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 slice bread, torn into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 400 g canned black beans, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 35 g walnuts
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp sweet paprika
  • 2 - 3 tsp poppy seeds

Assembly

  • 70 g tomato sauce (see Tip)
  • 4 - 8 green lettuce leaves
  • 8 - 12 slices fresh tomato
  • 1 avocado, flesh only, cut into slices (optional)
  • 1 red onion, cut into thin rings (optional)

Preparation

Burger buns

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil and set aside.
  2. Place sugar, yeast and water into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2 .
  3. Add flour and salt and knead 4 min/. Transfer dough into prepared bowl, cover with plastic wrap and leave in a warm place to prove until doubled in size (approx. 2-3 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Patties

  1. Place 1200 g of the water and ½ teaspoon of the salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh quinoa into it, then cook 20 min/100°C/speed 1 . Meanwhile, place flaxseeds (linseeds) and remaining 3 tablespoons water into a small bowl and stir to combine. Set aside.
  2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer cooked quinoa into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place oil, eschalots, garlic, bread and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add black beans, walnuts, tomato paste, cumin, paprika, reserved flaxseed mixture with water, cooled quinoa and remaining ½ teaspoon salt and combine 20 sec//speed 4 . Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, then place into refrigerator to firm for approx. 30 minutes while shaping burger buns.
  6. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Set aside a plate for patties.
  7. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and gently shape into a ball. Divide dough into 6 equal portions (approx. 100 g each) and shape into burger buns. Arrange buns onto prepared baking tray, cover with a kitchen towel and allow to prove for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, shape bean mixture into 6 patties, place onto prepared plate. Transfer patties into refrigerator to firm while buns rise.
  8. Preheat oven to 180°C. Brush tops of risen rolls with oil and sprinkle with poppy seeds. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C), until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer rolls onto a wire rack to cool.
  9. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add 1 tablespoon oil. Fry patties on both sides until browned and cooked through (approx. 3 minutes per side). Transfer onto a plate and cover to keep warm.

Assembly

  1. Slice buns into halves horizontally. Place a patty onto bottom half of bun, then top with tomato sauce, lettuce, tomato, avocado (optional) and red onion (optional) to serve.
