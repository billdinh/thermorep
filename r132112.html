Lamb dhansak

Ingredients

Lamb and spice paste

  • 500 g boneless lamb shoulder, cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • 3 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste, plus 1 tsp extra (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 100 g toor dal (see Tips)
  • 50 g mung dal (see Tips)
  • 50 g chana dal (see Tips)
  • hot water, for soaking
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 1 tbsp black peppercorns
  • 4 cardamom pods (green)
  • 2 cardamom pods (black)
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 4 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves, plus extra sliced to serve

Masala

  • 1 tbsp ghee (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 brown onions (approx. 360 g), cut into quarters
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 300 g fresh tomatoes (approx. 3 tomatoes), cut into quarters
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 200 g water
  • 100 g pumpkin flesh, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • fresh mint leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Lamb and spice paste

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh lamb into it. Add 1 teaspoon of the garlic paste, 1 teaspoon of the ginger paste and 1 teaspoon of the salt to bowl and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate overnight.
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh toor dal, mung dal and chana dal into it. Cover with hot water, allow to stand for a few minutes, then cover with plastic wrap and set aside to soak overnight.
  3. Place cumin seeds, coriander seeds, cinnamon, peppercorns, cardamom pods (both black and green), bay leaves and fenugreek seeds into mixing bowl and roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 1 minute, then mill 1 min/speed 10.
  4. Add fresh chilli, remaining 1 tsp salt, remaining 2 teaspoons garlic paste and remaining 1 tablespoon ginger paste and blend 10 sec/speed 7.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Masala

  1. Place ghee into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add onions and chop 3 sec/speed 5, then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add turmeric and all of the reserved spice paste mixture and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes and Tamarind paste and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1,  without measuring cup, until mixture is thick and glossy.
  5. Drain and rinse dal well. Add soaked dal, salt and water to mixing bowl and stir with aid of spatula, then cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add marinated lamb and cook 30 min/90°C//speed 1.
  7. Add pumpkin and stir with aid of the spatula, then cook 30 min/90°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until lamb is tender. Garnish with mint and serve with fresh chillies and side of choice.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee and Tamarind paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Toor dal (split yellow lentils), mung dal (dried split mung beans) and chana dal (dried brown chick peas) can all be found at Indian food stores or online.
  • You can serve this with rice, bread, chutney or your favourite side. We served ours with Garlic and coriander naan (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
