Tuna and Spring Onion Quiche

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 300 g plain flour
  • 150 g unsalted butter, chilled, diced
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 50 g water, ice cold

Filling

  • 75 g spring onions, sliced (2 cm)
  • 200 g Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 230 g tinned tuna, in water, drained
  • 250 g crème fraîche
  • 250 g milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, butter, salt, thyme and egg yolk in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add cold water and pulse Turbo/1 sec/0 times until just clinging together; if pastry is too dry add 1 tsp more water at a time with a Turbo pulse after each addition. Tip out and form into a flat disc approx. 1 cm thick.
  3. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Roll out pastry and use to line a loose-bottomed, fluted tart tin (Ø 28 cm). Prick pastry base with a fork then chill in fridge for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  5. Line pastry in tart tin with baking paper and fill with baking beans or rice then bake blind for 15 minutes (180°C) until golden. Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place spring onions and Cheddar in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add tuna, crème fraîche, milk, eggs, mustard, salt and pepper then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Pour into pastry case and bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) until golden brown and just set. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

cling film, fluted tart tin (Ø 28 cm), rolling pin, baking beans, baking paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This light, flavourful quiche makes a tasty lunch, that can be enjoyed warm or cold. Try other quiches from Cookidoo® such as Pumpkin, Leek and Lancashire Cheese, or the classic Quiche Lorraine.
  • Serve with a fresh green salad.
  • Any leftover filling can be poured into greased muffin cases and baked at 180°C for 25 minutes.
  • Makes 1 large quiche or 2 smaller ones. Can also be made as mini tartlets for canapés.
