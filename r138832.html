Gravity cake

Ingredients

Cake

  • 200 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 600 g plain flour
  • 80 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 540 g sugar
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs

Chocolate buttercream icing

  • 500 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 240 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 40 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 1 tbsp boiling water
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Assembly

  • 300 - 400 g chocolate finger biscuits
  • lollies of choice, for decorating

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line 2 round cake tins (23 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place 100 g of the butter, 300 g of the flour, 40 g of the cocoa powder, 270 g of the sugar, 1 tablespoon of the baking powder, 1 pinch of the salt, 250 g of the milk and 1 teaspoon of the vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add 2 of the eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5. Pour batter into 1 of the prepared cake tins and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place remaining 100 g butter, 300 g flour, 40 g cocoa powder, 270 g sugar, 1 tablespoon baking powder, 1 pinch salt, 250 g milk and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  6. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  7. Add remaining 2 eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5. Pour batter into remaining prepared cake tin. Tap cakes 2-3 times on work surface to get an even surface and prevent air bubbles. Bake cakes for 1 hour (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in tins for 10 minutes, then transfer onto wire racks to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar, butter and cocoa powder into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer 100 g of the icing into a piping bag fitted with a small plain nozzle and set aside. Transfer remaining icing into a bowl and set aside (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Slide a long, thick wooden skewer into the long end of a flexible straw. Trim skewer to 10 cm longer than base of straw.
  2. Bend flexible straw and slide another wooden skewer into the flexible end. Trim skewer so it is 5-10 cm longer than end of straw (length dependent on container used for decorating). Secure the extended part of the skewer and flexible end of the straw into the inside bottom of the container (side that is closest to cake) using tape or a stapler, then set aside.
  3. Trim tops of cakes to create a level surface. Place cakes on top of each other. Place a chocolate finger biscuit vertically against the cake to measure - ensure biscuit is 1 cm taller than cakes. Trim cakes accordingly, if needed.
  4. Place 1 cake onto a serving board and spread with 5-6 tablespoons of the reserved icing. Top with remaining cake. Place 2 tablespoons of the icing into a piping bag to secure lollies onto straw and set aside.
  5. Spread remaining icing over top and sides of cake to cover completely. Arrange finger biscuits around cake, side by side, leaving a 10 cm gap. Position longer skewer into the cake and slip over the straw with container attached - push straw at least 2 cm into the cake so it is secure.
  6. Pipe icing up the length of the straw to the lip of the container. Attach lollies of choice onto straw (you may need to put cake into the refrigerator and work in stages, to firm up icing as you go) to cover straw completely. Decorate cake surface with remaining lollies to create the effect of the lollies being poured from the container onto the cake and through the 10 cm gap onto cake board.
  7. Place cake into refrigerator, and remove 10 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

2 round cake tins (23 cm), baking paper, wire rack, wooden skewers, bowl, 2 piping bags with a plain nozzle, flexible straw, cake board or serving plate, palette knife, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a soft, sifted icing sugar for the Buttercream icing recipe.
  • If you are not using icing immediately, place into refrigerator until ready to use. To use, bring icing back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread.
