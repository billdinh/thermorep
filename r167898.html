Crêpes with herbs

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 10 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, washed and dried
  • 10 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only, washed and dried
  • 10 g spring onions, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 4 eggs
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 500 g milk
  • 200 g flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 - 10 g sunflower oil for frying

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place mint leaves, parsley leaves and spring onions into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add ground Parmesan, eggs, olive oil, milk, flour, salt and black pepper and mix 20 sec/speed 5. The batter should have the consistency of liquid cream. If it seems too thick, add a little milk and combine using spatula. Refrigerate and allow batter to rest for at least 20 minutes before using.
  4. Lightly grease a non-stick frying pan with sunflower oil and heat on medium heat. Pour in a ladle of batter, twirling the pan as you pour to cover base of pan completely. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until edges of crêpe are light golden. Turn crêpe over and cook for a further 1 minute. Transfer to a plate and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter. Serve warm.

Useful Items

bowl, ladle, plate, non-stick frying pan, tea towel, refrigerator, stove

Hints & Tricks

  • Crêpes can be served with various fillings (e.g. butter, yogurt and a sprinkle of sumac or sour cream and salmon).
  • Crêpes can be frozen for later use. Place a piece of baking paper or cling film between each crêpe before freezing so they can be easily defrosted. After defrosting, wrap crêpes in aluminium foil and warm in a preheated oven (150ºC) for 5-10 minutes.
