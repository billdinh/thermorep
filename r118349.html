Hearty vegetarian lasagne

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 150 g lupin flour (see Tips)
  • 70 g gluten free cornflour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 60 g water

Vegetable sauce

  • 2 celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 2 - 3 carrots (approx. 190 g), cut into pieces
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 200 g button mushrooms, cut into slices
  • 2 tsp dried mixed herbs
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 120 g dry red wine
  • 400 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp raw sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp gluten free cornflour
  • 3 tbsp water

Cheese sauce

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g mozzarella cheese, cut into pieces
  • 100 g unsalted butter
  • 80 g gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 600 g full cream milk
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp Dijon mustard (gluten free)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Assembly

  • butter, for greasing
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts

Preparation

Pasta

  1. Place lupin flour, cornflour, xanthan gum, nutmeg and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add eggs and water and knead 1 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside for a minimum of 10 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into 4 equal portions and use a pasta machine to roll out (see Tips). Use extra cornflour to dust dough if it becomes sticky. Cut pasta into 10 pieces (approx. 10 x 18 cm each). Cover with a kitchen towel and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Vegetable sauce

  1. Place celery and carrot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, onion and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add mushrooms, mixed herbs, nutmeg and reserved chopped vegetables and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add stock paste, wine, tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar and salt and cook 30 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Place cornflour and water into a small bowl and stir until smooth. Add cornflour mixture into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, or until sauce thickens. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cheese sauce

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place mozzarella into mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place all remaining cheese sauce ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 8-10 min/90°C/speed 4, until cooked and thickened.
  4. Add half of reserved Parmesan cheese and mix 5 sec/speed 2. Set aside.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a baking dish (32 x 25 x 4.5 cm).
  2. Cover base of dish with one-third of the vegetable sauce. Place a layer of pasta sheets (2-3 sheets) over sauce. Spoon another one-third of the vegetable sauce over pasta, cover with one-third of the cheese sauce and sprinkle with one-third of the mozzarella cheese. Top with a layer of pasta (2-3 sheets) and the remaining one-third of the vegetable sauce. Cover with one-third of the cheese sauce and one-third of the mozzarella. Top with a final layer of pasta (2-3 sheets), then top with remaining cheese sauce and remaining mozzarella.  Sprinkle with pine nuts and remaining half of the Parmesan cheese.
  3. Bake for 35-45 minutes (170°C), until golden brown. Allow lasagne to cool for 10 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, pasta machine, kitchen towel, 3 bowls, small bowl , deep-sided baking dish (32 x 25 x 4.5 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can buy lupin flour at health food stores and online.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Please refer to the Gluten free flour mix recipe on this recipe chip.
  • If you do not have a pasta machine, the pasta can be rolled out thinly and cut by hand. If using a pasta machine, gluten free pasta should not be rolled any thinner than the NO. 4 setting on your pasta machine.
  • If you have leftover pasta, place a layer of baking paper between each sheet, then wrap in plastic wrap and store in freezer for up to 1 month. Bring back to room temperature before using. We suggest cutting into pappardelle for a quick meal for 1 or 2 people.
