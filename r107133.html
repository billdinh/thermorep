Raspberry Dacquoise

Ingredients

Dacquoise Layer

  • 170 g caster sugar
  • 20 g plain flour
  • 90 g ground almonds
  • 100 g egg whites, from approx. 3 large eggs

Crème pâtissière

  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only
  • 3 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 250 g milk
  • 30 g cornflour
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 250 g double cream, cold
  • 500 g fresh raspberries, small

Preparation

Dacquoise Layer

  1. Place 90 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add flour and ground almonds then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a swiss roll tin with baking paper.
  4. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in remaining 80 g sugar.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and whisk 3 min/speed 3.5, whilst adding reserved sugar, 1 tsp at a time, through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Add reserved flour-almond mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 3. Pour into prepared tin, spreading out evenly until 2 mm thick then bake for 13 minutes (180°C) until slightly golden. Remove from oven and immediately cut into 16 equal-sized rectangles (approx. 7.5 cm x 4.5 cm). Set aside to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make crème pâtissière.

Crème pâtissière

  1. Place vanilla seeds, egg yolks, milk, cornflour and sugar in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl, cover surface with damp greaseproof paper and leave until completely cold (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whisk without setting a time/speed 3, watching carefully until softly whipped.  Remove butterfly whisk then transfer cream to a bowl, cover and set aside in fridge.
  3. Place cooled crème pâtissière in mixing bowl and mix 40 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add whipped cream and mix 20 sec/speed 3 then finish stirring in by hand with spatula.
  5. Transfer mixture to a piping bag with a star-shaped nozzle, then pipe small dollops of cream on the surface of the dacquoise pieces. Neatly arrange raspberries over the cream on each layer. Place one dacquoise layer on top of another, repeating until all pieces are used. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

swiss roll tin (30 cm x 20 cm x 1 cm), baking paper, long sharp knife, greaseproof paper, cling film, piping bag and nozzle, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • A large lipped baking tray can be used if a swiss roll tin is not available.
  • Another impressive-looking showstopper of a dessert that has all the hard work taken out of it with Thermomix®. The almond meringue makes lovely light, crunchy layers.
