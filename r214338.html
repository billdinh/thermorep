Moroccan lamb meatballs with batbout flatbreads

Ingredients

Batbout

  • 320 g water
  • 30 g sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 80 g wholemeal flour
  • 240 g semolina
  • 1 ½ tsp salt

Lamb meatballs

  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing and frying
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 500 g lamb mince
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 ½ tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 800 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp sugar

Preparation

Batbout

  1. Place water, sugar, yeast and oil into mixing bowl and warm 2 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add plain flour, wholemeal flour, semolina and salt and knead 5 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly oiled work surface. Divide dough into 8 equal portions, and shape each into a ball. Cover dough balls with a damp kitchen towel and allow to rest for 1 hour. Continue with recipe after the first 30 minutes of resting time.

Lamb meatballs

  1. Grease Varoma dish and tray with oil and set aside.
  2. Place 2 of the garlic cloves and mint into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add mince, black pepper, 1 tsp of the paprika, 1 tsp of the ground cumin, 1 tsp of the salt and ½ tsp of the ground cayenne and mix 15 sec//speed 4 or until completely combined. Transfer mixture into a bowl. With oiled hands, shape into small meatballs (approx. 30 g each) and place into prepared Varoma dish and tray. Set Varoma aside.
  4. Place parsley and remaining 2 garlic cloves into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add tomatoes, oil, sugar, remaining 1½ tsp paprika, remaining 1 tsp ground cumin, remaining ½ tsp cayenne pepper and remaining ½ tsp salt. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 25 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Meanwhile, place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil for frying. Press each dough ball out into a flat circle (approx. 20 cm). Working in batches, fry batbout, turning several times, until golden brown on both sides and cooked through (approx. 10 minutes). Transfer flatbreads onto a wire rack to drain and cool.
  7. Transfer meatballs into a serving dish and pour over sauce. Serve warm with flatbreads.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, kitchen towel, bowl, wire rack, serving dish, large frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Batbout is a soft, chewy Moroccan bread very similar to pita.
