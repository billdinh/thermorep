Spiced Garlic Prawns and Squid

Ingredients

  • 3 squid hoods (approx. 300 g)
  • 20 prawns, raw, large, peeled, (approx. 200 g)
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 pieces lemon zest, (approx. 5 x 2 cm), no white pith
  • 1 fresh red chilli, deseeded if preferred and cut into halves
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, plus extra finely chopped for garnishing
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, plus extra finely chopped for garnishing
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 30 g dry white wine
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 - 2 lemons, cut into wedges, to serve

Preparation

  1. Cut squid hoods open by slicing in half lengthways, across the body. Cut into 1 cm strips along length of each piece. Transfer strips into a ceramic or glass bowl, add prawns and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, lemon zest, chilli, parsley and coriander in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop 3 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl again with spatula.
  3. Add oil, wine, paprika and salt then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add ⅓ reserved squid and prawns then cook 1-2 min/Varoma//speed , until cooked to your preference. Using tongs, carefully remove cooked squid and prawns from mixing bowl and transfer into a warm serving bowl or warm ceramic dish and cover to keep warm. Leave the oil and herb mixture in mixing bowl.
  5. Add another ⅓ reserved squid and prawns then cook 1-2 min/Varoma//speed , until cooked to your preference. Carefully remove cooked squid and prawns then transfer to bowl with previous batch. Leave oil and herb mixture in mixing bowl.
  6. Add remaining ⅓ reserved squid and prawns then cook 1-2 min/Varoma//speed , until cooked to your preference. Carefully remove cooked squid and prawns then transfer to bowl with reserved seafood. Transfer oil and herb mixture into a serving jug.
  7. Squeeze 1-2 lemon wedges over seafood. Garnish with remaining wedges and extra chopped parsley and coriander. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, long sharp knife, serving bowl with lid, tongs, jug

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe is best when served immediately, so we recommend pre-preparing all of your ingredients, including garnishes, before you commence cooking your seafood in steps 4-7.
