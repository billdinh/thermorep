Lighter Burgers with Sweet Potato Wedges

Ingredients

Burgers

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 400 g beef mince, 5% fat, broken in pieces
  • 1 Tbsp tomato purée
  • ½ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce

Sweet Potato Wedges

  • cooking spray, any oil
  • 3 sweet potatoes (approx. 180 g each), cut lengthways into 8 wedges
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Assembly

  • 4 burger buns, seeded or wholemeal
  • 4 green lettuce leaves (e.g. iceberg, baby cos)
  • 2 beef tomatoes, sliced (1 cm)
  • 4 cheese slices, reduced fat
  • 1 red onion, sliced (5 mm)
  • tomato ketchup, to taste, for topping

Preparation

Burgers

  1. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place garlic and onions in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add beef mince, tomato purée, chilli flakes, salt, pepper and Worcestershire sauce then mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  4. Divide mixture into 4 balls then, using wet hands, flatten to form large patties (Ø 12-13 cm). Transfer to prepared tray, cover with cling film and chill in fridge for 30 minutes. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 190°C.

Sweet Potato Wedges

  1. Spray a large baking tray with oil. Arrange sweet potato wedges on prepared tray, sprinkle with garlic powder, paprika, salt and pepper then toss to coat. Bake for 40 minutes (190°C). With 15 minutes remaining, place chilled patties in oven too and bake for 12-15 minutes (190°C) until cooked, flipping halfway through.

Assembly

  1. Divide lettuce and tomatoes between buns then place a cooked beef patty on each. Top with cheese, onion and ketchup. Serve with sweet potato wedges.

Useful Items

cling film, large baking tray, baking tray, baking paper, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Think you can't eat burgers and chips on a diet? Think again! These clever beef burgers are made with low-fat mince, and we've baked them instead of frying them. Topped with a slice of reduced fat cheese and served with sweet potato wedges, all that's needed is a squirt of tomato ketchup to finish!
  • To reduce the calories even more, try serving these burgers with baked carrot or butternut squash chips instead of sweet potato wedges.
