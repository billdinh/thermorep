Tri-coloured snow cones

Ingredients

Pineapple snow cones

  • 250 g pineapple juice
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • 20 g caster sugar

Watermelon snow cones

  • 250 g watermelon, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 20 g caster sugar

Berry snow cones

  • 200 g frozen mixed berries
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • 120 g water
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Assembly

  • 1000 g ice cubes

Preparation

Pineapple snow cones

  1. Place pineapple juice, lemon juice and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 2,  without measuring cup or until syrupy. Transfer into a bowl and set aside for 10 minutes to cool, then transfer into refrigerator to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Watermelon snow cones

  1. Place watermelon, lemon juice and sugar into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 7, then cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2, without measuring cup or until syrupy. Transfer into a bowl and set aside for 10 minutes to cool, then transfer into refrigerator to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Berry snow cones

  1. Place mixed berries, lemon juice, water, sugar and vanilla into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until syrupy,  placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Strain mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl and set aside for 10 minutes to cool, then transfer into refrigerator to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. When ready to serve, place 500 g of the ice cubes into mixing bowl and crush 30 sec/speed 9, with aid of spatula.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 9. Use an ice cream scoop to place scoops of crushed ice into paper cones or cups and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 500 g ice cubes into mixing bowl and crush 30 sec/speed 9, with aid of spatula.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 9. Use an ice cream scoop to place scoops of cruched ice into paper cones or cups.
  5. Drizzle 2 tablespoons of desired syrup flavour over each cone to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, fine mesh sieve, ice cream scoop, paper cones or cups, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Syrups can be made a day in advance and stored in the refrigerator.
