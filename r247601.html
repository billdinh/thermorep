Venetian Duck Ragù - Ragù veneziano con anatra

Ingredients

Duck Ragù

  • 4 duck breasts, skinless, approx. 650-700 g, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 50 g celery, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 50 g carrots, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 20 g olive oil, plus 2 Tbsp for browning meat
  • 30 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 250 g red wine
  • 800 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 25 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary, needles only
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Pasta

  • water, for cooking pasta
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, for cooking pasta
  • 500 g dried paccheri pasta

Preparation

Duck Ragù

  1. Heat 2 Tbsp olive oil in a frying pan over a medium-high heat. Brown duck pieces, in batches, then transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions, celery, carrots, oil, butter and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed 0.5.
  4. Add wine and, without measuring cup, cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add browned duck pieces, tomatoes, flour, cinnamon, stock cube, rosemary, bay leaves, sugar, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 60 min/100°C//speed .
  6. Check tenderness of meat and if desired, continue cooking, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, 5-10 min/100°C//speed . Meanwhile, cook pasta.

Pasta

  1. Bring a large saucepan of water with salt added to the boil. Cook paccheri according to packet instructions, or until al dente. Drain using a colander then transfer to a serving bowl.
  2. Ladle ragù over cooked pasta, sprinkle with reserved Parmesan and serve immediately.

Useful Items

frying pan, saucepan, serving bowl, colander, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • This rich, comforting ragù is from the Venetian islands and is traditionally eaten during winter months – one of the few meat dishes in a region famed for its fresh seafood. A perfect main course option for a winter dinner party when you really want to impress, the duck simmers slowly in the Thermomix® with the blades gently rotating, resulting in a thick, flavourful sauce with meat that simply melts in the mouth.
