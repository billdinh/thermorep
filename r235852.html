Spicy Seafood Soup (Tom Yum)

Ingredients

  • 100 g shallots, halved
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g tomatoes, quartered
  • 50 g fresh lemon grass, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 15 g galangal (Thai ginger), fresh root, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 100 g Thai chilli sauce, hot
  • 1000 g water
  • 8 fresh kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 fish stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 250 g fish fillets (e.g. red snapper, sea bream, monkfish), cut in pieces
  • 120 g fresh oyster mushrooms, halved
  • 20 g palm sugar
  • 100 g fresh clams
  • 250 g medium raw prawns, peeled
  • 20 g fish sauce
  • 20 g lime juice
  • 3 spring onions, thinly sliced (1 cm)
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Place shallots and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add tomatoes, lemon grass, galangal, Thai chilli sauce, water, kaffir lime leaves and fish stock cube. Place fish fillets in Varoma dish and on Varoma tray, then place covered Varoma into position and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside.
  3. Add mushrooms and palm sugar then cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1.
  4. Add clams, prawns, fish sauce and lime juice then cook 2 min/90°C//speed 1.
  5. Divide steamed fish fillets between 4 soup bowls, pour soup over then sprinkle with spring onions and coriander. Serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • Probably one of the most ancient and famous soups in Thailand, the spicy Thai seafood soup, also called Tom Yum, is a Thai food icon, known for its distinct sour and hot flavours with the generous use of fragrant herbs. This soup consists of a basic broth flavoured with fresh ingredients such as lemon grass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, lime juice, fish sauce and chilli peppers.
  • If preferred, use brown sugar instead of palm sugar.
