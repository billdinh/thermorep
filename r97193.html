Poppy seed strudel

Ingredients

Dough

  • 150 g butter, cut into cubes (3-4 cm)
  • 180 g full cream milk
  • 50 g fresh yeast
  • 120 g white sugar
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 6 egg yolks (reserve whites for filling)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 ½ tbsp rum

Poppy seed filling

  • 420 g poppy seeds
  • 1000 g boiling water, for soaking
  • 50 g almonds
  • 50 g walnuts
  • 150 g white sugar
  • 50 g honey
  • 100 g sultanas
  • 30 g butter
  • 150 g full cream milk
  • 40 g mixed peel
  • 1 ½ tsp natural almond extract
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ orange, zest only, no white pith, finely grated
  • 20 g orange juice
  • 2 tbsp rum (optional)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 6 egg whites

Glaze

  • 180 g icing sugar
  • 20 - 40 g orange juice

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/70°C/speed 2, or until completely melted. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place milk, yeast, 60 g of the sugar and 70 g of the flour into mixing bowl and warm 3 min/37°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add all remaining dough ingredients, including remaining 430 g of flour, 60 g of sugar and reserved melted butter and knead 2 min/.
  4. Transfer dough into a large bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Poppy seed filling

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh poppy seeds and boiling water into it. Leave seeds to soak for 1 hour, then strain through a fine mesh sieve and set aside.
  2. Place almonds and walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place 250 g of the reserved poppy seeds into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat milling 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a separate bowl to the nuts and set aside.
  4. Place remaining reserved poppy seeds into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat milling 10 sec/speed 10.
  5. Add sugar, honey, sultanas, butter, milk and reserved milled poppy seeds and cook 10 min/90°C/speed 3.
  6. Add mixed peel, almond extract, cinnamon, orange zest, orange juice, rum (optional), salt and reserved milled nuts and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer poppy seed mixture into a large bowl and set aside to cool. Throughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a baking tray (approx. 30 x 40 cm) and 3 sheets of baking paper (approx. 35 x 20 cm).
  8. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 3 min/speed 3, without measuring cup, until stiff peaks form. Transfer whipped egg whites into the cooled poppy seed mixture and stir gently to combine.
  9. Divide the proven dough into 3 equal portions. Dust a silicone bread mat or work surface with flour, then roll each portion of dough into a large rectangle (31 x 35 cm, approx. 5-7 mm thick).
  10. Turn the rectangles of dough so that one of the longer sides is closest to you (landscape). Spread reserved poppy seed mixture over each rectangle of dough, leaving approx. 4 cm edge along the top. Starting at the bottom, roll each rectangle up to enclose the filling and create a strudel shape, using the 4 cm edge along the top to seal strudel closed.
  11. Place each of the 3 strudels onto 1 of the prepared sheets of baking paper, ensuring the seal of the strudel is on the underside. Wrap strudels up loosely in the baking paper and place onto baking tray. Bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown. Allow to cool completely.

Glaze

  1. Place icing sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add orange juice and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 30 sec/speed 3. Pour sugar glaze over the top of cooled strudels, then slice and serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, kitchen towel, ceramic or glass bowl, fine mesh sieve, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), silicone bread mat, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a popular recipe to make at Christmas time. Wrap up one or more of the strudels to give to friends and family as a traditional Polish treat.
