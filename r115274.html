Chilorio shredded pork with lime-pickled onions

Ingredients

Lime-pickled onions

  • 2 red onions, cut into thin rings
  • 1 tbsp salt flakes
  • 2 limes, juice only
  • 1 fresh green chilli, deseeded and cut into thin slices

Chilorio

  • 1000 g pork shoulder (fatty), cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1300 g water
  • 40 g dried ancho chillies, stems removed
  • 2 tsp fine salt
  • 60 g apple cider vinegar
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 piece orange, zest (1 x 2 cm), no white pith
  • 1 orange, juiced
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges, to serve
  • tortillas, to serve

Preparation

Lime-pickled onions

  1. Place onions into a bowl and using hands, squeeze onions to soften slightly.
  2. Add salt, lime juice and chilli to bowl and stir to combine. Cover and set aside to macerate for a minimum of 2 hours.

Chilorio

  1. Place pork and 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl and cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1.5, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Insert measuring cup. Cook for a further 60 min/100°C//speed 1.5 until tender. If required, cook for a further 10 min/100°C//speed 1.5 until tender.
  2. With aid of spatula, scrape sides of mixing bowl and discard any residue. Using simmering basket, strain liquid into a jug. Set liquid aside (see Tips). Transfer pork into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place chillies and remaining 300 g water into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add salt, vinegar, garlic, cumin, thyme, oregano and orange zest and juice and mix 30 sec/speed 9.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Add reserved pork to mixing bowl and, with aid of spatula, break up pork then cook 2 min/90°C//speed 1.5, or until pork is warmed through.
  7. Serve pork with reserved Lime-pickled onions, lime wedges and tortillas (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, jug

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with salad, vegetables and sour cream.
  • To make your own tortillas, refer to the recipe on this recipe chip
  • Chilli sauce can be prepared in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  • Pork can be cooked in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • The pork cooking liquid will cool to a jelly with a layer of lard on top. This lard can be used to fry the pork (see Variation) or added to tortilla dough (refer to Flour tortilla recipe on this recipe chip).
  • If cooking ahead of time, you can re-fry pork in its own lard (which has set on top of the cooking liquid). Place 60 g lard into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Add chilli sauce and pork and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.5.
