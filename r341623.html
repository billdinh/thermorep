Spinach Fatayers

Ingredients

Bread Dough

  • 50 g milk
  • 130 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 vanilla pod
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 15 g olive oil

Baharat Spice Mix

  • 100 g white peppercorns
  • 1 Tbsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp cloves
  • 15 cardamom pods
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon

Spinach Filling

  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp sumac (see tip)
  • 1 tsp harissa paste
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g water
  • 600 g fresh spinach leaves
  • 30 g pine nuts

Preparation

Bread Dough

  1. Place milk, water and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, dust a bowl with flour.
  2. Split vanilla pod lengthwise and scrape out seeds (see tip). Add vanilla seeds, flour, baking powder, egg, sugar and 1 tsp salt then knead 8 min/. Tip out onto a floured surface and shape into a ball. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with a tea towel and leave to rise for approx. 30 minutes. Meanwhile, grease a baking tray with oil and clean mixing bowl.
  3. Divide dough into 8 small balls (Ø 5 cm) and arrange on prepared baking tray, then re-cover with tea towel. Keep cool and continue with recipe.

Baharat Spice Mix

  1. Place peppercorns, nutmeg, cloves, cardamom and cinnamon in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to an airtight container and set aside (see tip).

Spinach Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and grease a baking tray.
  2. Place lemon juice, onions, oil, sumac, harissa, ½ tsp reserved baharat spice mix and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water, 300 g spinach and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add remaining 300 g spinach and cook 5 min/100°C/speed . Drain spinach through simmering basket and transfer to bowl with onions.
  5. Place bowl with cooked spinach on mixing bowl lid and weigh in pine nuts. Mix with a spoon and leave to cool for approx. 15 minutes.
  6. Roll each dough ball into a disc (Ø 8 cm, approx. 5 mm thick). Place 1 Tbsp spinach filling in centre of each disc, then fold in 3 sides to make a triangular turnover. Repeat until all ingredients have been used. Place turnovers back on prepared baking tray and bake for 30 minutes (200°C). Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

baking tray, rolling pin, tea towel, airtight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The empty vanilla pod can still be used, as it has lots of flavour. For example, you can make your own vanilla sugar, or soak the pod in milk for desserts.
  • Store baharat spice mix in an airtight container for future use.
  • Sumac is a widely used spice mix, with a slight lemony flavour. It is available in most supermarkets or online.
  • Sumac has a tendency to deteriorate when cooked. It is therefore preferable not to overcook it. It can also make food very salty. Therefore take care not to over-salt the mixture.
