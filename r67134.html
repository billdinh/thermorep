Tagliatelle with porcini mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 30 - 50 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 30 g dried porcini mushrooms, soaked in warm water 20 minutes, drained
  • 1 ¼ tsp salt
  • 1200 g water
  • 300 g tagliatelle pasta
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place flat-leaf parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer 1 tbsp chopped parsley into a bowl and set aside. Leave remainder in mixing bowl.
  3. Add garlic clove and 30 g extra virgin olive oil and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add porcini mushrooms and ¼ tsp salt and cook 12 min/100°C//speed . Transfer into a ThermoServer or bowl and keep warm. Remove garlic clove if desired.
  5. Place water into mixing bowl and bring to the boil 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add tagliatelle and remaining 1 tsp salt through hole in mixing bowl lid and cook time indicated on package/100°C//speed 1 without measuring cup, until al dente. Drain pasta through Varoma dish, then transfer into ThermoServer or bowl with reserved porcini mushrooms. Add remaining 1½ tbsp extra virgin olive oil (20 g), grated Parmesan cheese, reserved chopped parsley and black pepper and mix well with spatula. Serve hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • If using tagliatelle nests, place pasta straight into mixing bowl in step 5. If using long dried tagliatelle pasta, insert through hole in mixing bowl lid and allow pasta to drop into the mixing bowl as it softens.
  • Pasta that is cooked al dente is tender but still firm to the bite.
  • The quantity of salt can be increased to taste depending on the quality of the mushrooms.
  • You can also make this dish with homemade pasta. Cook pasta in a pot while sautéing porcini mushrooms in the Thermomix.
