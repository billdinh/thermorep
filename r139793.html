Sausage dogs

Ingredients

Filling

  • 1 brown onion (approx. 180 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 carrot, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp dried basil
  • 100 g rolled oats
  • 3 eggs
  • 100 g cottage cheese
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 50 g breadcrumbs
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Dough

  • 60 g tasty cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 500 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 300 g plain yoghurt
  • 50 g water
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 40 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp baking powder

Assembly

  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed, for eyes and collars
  • 1 egg, beaten for glazing
  • 4 currants, flattened (for noses)

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place onion, carrot and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add paprika, basil and oats and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add eggs, cottage cheese, soy sauce, breadcrumbs, salt and pepper and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until firm (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Place flour, yoghurt, water, oil, butter, salt and baking powder into mixing bowl and knead 1 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or a lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Set aside.

Assembly

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Cut white part of the spring onion/shallot into 8 small slices (for eyes). Cut green part of the spring onion/shallot lengthways into 4 long strips (for collars). Set aside.
  3. Divide dough into 5 equal portions (approx. 200 g each). Divide 1 piece of the dough into 16 equal pieces. Roll 8 of the pieces into long sausages (approx. 8 cm) for legs and set aside. Form remaining 8 pieces of dough into teardrop shapes (for ears). Set aside.
  4. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  5. Working in batches, roll out 1 piece of the remaining dough into a rectangle (approx. 12 x 28 cm). Place 5 heaped tablespoons of reserved filling down the centre of pastry, leaving a 4 cm border at each short end. Sprinkle one-quarter of the reserved cheese over filling, fold pastry over filling and seal to form a sausage shape (makes 4 sausage dogs in total).
  6. With the seam side up, place 2 of the rolled dough legs over body (at least 4 cm from ends of roll) and gently press into place. Transfer onto prepared baking tray, seam side down. Form a long tail from the 4 cm excess at one end of the pastry. Shape 4 cm excess at other end of pastry into a teardrop shape for a head and nose. Gently tie a strip of reserved spring onion/shallot behind head to form a neck and collar and trim off any excess.
  7. Brush bodies, ears and legs with egg wash. Place an ear on each side of the head and press gently to attach. Attach spring onion/shallot slices for eyes and a currant to the tip of the nose. Bake for 15-20 minutes (190°C) or until golden brown. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, small bowl, silicone bread mat, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven
