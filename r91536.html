Scottish Tablet

Ingredients

  • 600 g golden caster sugar
  • 130 g double cream
  • 40 g unsalted butter
  • 120 g condensed milk
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
320 kJ / 76 kcal
Protein
0.3 g
Carbohydrates
14 g
Fat
2.1 g

Preparation

  1. Place all ingredients in mixing bowl then heat 20 min/80°C/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then replace measuring cup with simmering basket on mixing bowl lid and cook 50 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, line a square tin (22 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl if any sugary crystals remain then cook again, with measuring cup in place,  10 min/Varoma/speed 2. When a small amount is taken out, it should set firm but not solid in a few minutes (soft ball stage); the temperature of the mixture will be 113°C on a temperature probe - prolong cooking time by 10 minutes if necessary.
  3. When soft ball stage is reached, replace measuring cup with simmering basket on mixing bowl lid, then beat 3 min/speed 5.5.
  4. Immediately pour into prepared tin, spreading out evenly. Leave to cool for 10 minutes, then cut into squares (approx. 3 cm). Leave in tin to cool completely at room temperature before removing and separating the pieces to serve.

Useful Items

temperature probe, long sharp knife, baking paper, square cake tin (22 cm)

Hints & Tricks

  • Placing the simmering basket on the mixing bowl lid allows steam to escape during cooking and for any spits to be safely caught.
  • For other varieties of tablet, add different flavourings as desired at the end of step 3 (e.g. vanilla extract, raisins or rum and raisins). If adding liquids, beat the mixture again 2 min/speed 2 to evenly distribute the flavour.
  • This sugary confection is Scotland’s answer to fudge. Slightly harder and more crumbly than its English cousin, tablet is a lovely sweet treat that dates back to the eighteenth century.
