Green Vegetable Frittata - Frittata verde

Ingredients

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 10 fresh basil leaves
  • 60 g shallots, halved
  • 250 g courgettes, cut in pieces
  • 20 g olive oil, plus 1 Tbsp for frying
  • 6 large eggs
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 80 g asparagus, fine stems

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 12 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place basil in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place shallots, courgettes and oil in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down again then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed .
  5. Add eggs, salt, pepper, reserved chopped basil and all but 1 Tbsp reserved grated Parmesan then mix 10 sec//speed 3.
  6. Heat oil in an oven-proof frying pan over a medium heat. Pour egg mixture into pan then scatter asparagus over. Cook over a medium-low heat on hob until eggs start to set on the bottom (approx. 5 minutes). Transfer to oven and allow to cook for 10 minutes (190°C) or until fully set.
  7. Sprinkle with reserved grated Parmesan and serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

oven-proof frying pan, hob, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The frittata can also be steamed in the Varoma. Place 800 g water in mixing bowl. Line Varoma tray with a sheet of wet, wrung out baking paper keeping some holes uncovered for steam to circulate then pour egg mixture onto it. Cover with Varoma lid then steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 3. Carefully remove Varoma then transfer frittata to a serving plate. Cut in wedges and serve hot or cold.
  • The word frittata derives from the Italian verb ‘friggere’, meaning ‘to fry’. It is the Italian equivalent of our omelette made with a variety of different ingredients and flavour combinations, depending on what is on hand. Frittatas can be eaten warm, accompanied by a fresh mixed salad, or sliced and eaten cold as picnic or party food. This simple recipe is brimming with the flavours of fresh spring vegetables and is a great way to use up eggs leftover from other recipes.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
  • Serves 8 as part of an antipasti board or 2-3 for lunch with a side salad.
