Freckle cake

Ingredients

Cake

  • coconut oil spray, for greasing
  • 750 g butternut pumpkin, peeled and cut into cubes (2-3 cm)
  • 400 g honey
  • 2 tbsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp gluten free baking powder
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 4 eggs
  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 70 g liquid coconut oil
  • 120 g coconut flour
  • 80 g raw cacao powder

Chocolate 'buttercream' frosting

  • 150 g coconut cream, plus extra for blending
  • 400 g dairy free milk chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 50 g liquid coconut oil
  • 80 g icing sugar
  • warm water, for softening

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Liberally spray 2 springform cake tins (20 cm) with coconut oil and set aside.
  2. Place half of the pumpkin into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining pumpkin into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add all remaining ingredients, including reserved pumpkin, and mix 20 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 20 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 10 sec/speed 5.
  7. Divide cake mixture evenly between prepared cake tins. Bake for 55-60 minutes (160°C), or until edges start to pull away from the sides of tin and a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cakes comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in tins (approx. 30 minutes), then cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator overnight to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate 'buttercream' frosting

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 80 g of the coconut cream into it. Set jug aside.
  2. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and melt 10 min/80°C/speed 3.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min/speed 3 , without measuring cup.
  5. Mix speed 3, pouring the reserved 80 g coconut cream through hole in mixing bowl lid, stopping when you hear the mixture thicken (approx. 3-5 seconds).
  6. Add coconut oil and mix 1 min/speed 5 (it may appear that the chocolate and coconut oil have split, but this is normal).
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 30 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Add remaining 70 g coconut cream and blend 30 sec/speed 7.
  9. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula, then allow mixture to cool in mixing bowl for 1 hour.
  10. Insert butterfly whisk. Add 40 g of the icing sugar and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  11. Add remaining 40 g icing sugar and mix 30 sec/speed 4 . Remove butterfly whisk.
  12. Line a bowl with plastic wrap and transfer icing into bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill (approx. 5 hours or overnight) or until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  13. Place the bowl of icing into a bowl of warm water, allowing icing to soften enough to remove it from the bowl. Turn out icing onto a chopping board, remove plastic wrap and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm).
  14. Place half of the frosting pieces and 1 tablespoon of the extra coconut cream into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 5.
  15. Add remaining frosting pieces and another 1 tablespoon extra coconut cream and mix 40 sec/speed 5, with aid of spatula.
  16. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and add more coconut cream if needed, then blend 1 min/speed 5.
  17. Spread a thin layer of frosting on top of one cake. Place second cake on top of frosting to create a double layer. Spread frosting all over top and sides of cake until completely covered. Decorate with chocolate freckles (or as desired) (see Tips) and serve or place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Useful Items

springform cake tin (20 cm), bowl, wooden skewer, plastic wrap, jug, large bowl, wooden chopping board, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Some coconut cream products can be made using equipment that has come into contact with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern for you, be sure to check the label of your coconut cream.
  • Cake and frosting can be made 2-3 days in advance and stored in the refrigerator. Decorate on the day you plan to serve it.
  • Decorate cake as desired, we have used chocolate freckles, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can completely frost and decorate cake, then wrap in plastic wrap and store in the freezer for up to 1 month. Serve cold or bring to room temperature before serving.
