Sultana, apple and custard pastries

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 tbsp caster sugar
  • 170 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast (see Tips)
  • 40 g butter, softened
  • 350 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Custard

  • 30 g cornflour
  • 50 g raw sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 160 g full cream milk

Assembly

  • baker's flour, for dusting
  • 90 g sultanas
  • 90 g apple (approx. 1 medium apple), peeled and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • full cream milk, for brushing

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place sugar, milk and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add all remaining dough ingredients and mix 5 sec/speed 5, then knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat and wrap up in mat or transfer into a lightly floured ceramic or glass bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Leave to prove in a warm place for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Custard

  1. Place cornflour and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 4 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add egg and milk and cook 6-8 min/90°C/speed 3 or until a thick consistency is achieved. Custard has reached desired level of thickness when it coats the back of a spoon. Set custard aside to cool for 5 minutes.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface and roll out into a square (30 cm). Spread cooled custard over dough, then sprinkle with sultanas, apple and cinnamon.
  3. Tightly roll up dough along the longer edge into a log, then slice into 16 pieces (approx. 2 cm thick) to form scrolls.
  4. Lay scrolls flat on prepared tray and cover with a kitchen towel. Leave scrolls to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Brush each scroll with a little milk, then bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Set aside to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes) before serving.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, ceramic or glass bowl, plastic wrap, baking paper, rolling pin, kitchen towel, pastry brush, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), spoon, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the dried yeast with 20 g fresh yeast, if preferred.
  • Drizzle buns with icing for a sweeter variation.
