Chia and quinoa tortillas with a trio of dips

Ingredients

Chia and quinoa tortilla chips

  • 50 g quinoa
  • 50 g chia seeds, white or black
  • 50 g cornmeal, coarse
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 pinches fine salt, to taste
  • 2 tsp lime juice (approx. ½ lime)
  • 60 g water

Sun-dried tomato, chilli and cashew dip

  • 1 red chilli, deseeded if preferred
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 120 g raw cashew nuts
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and cut into quarters
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 100 g sun-dried tomatoes, drained
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil

Pumpkin dip

  • 500 g pumpkin, flesh only, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 100 g pine nuts, toasted
  • 2 tbsp goat's cheese
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 pinch fine salt
  • 1 pinch black pepper, freshly ground

Artichoke dip

  • extra virgin olive oil, for greasing
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • ¼ onion, cut into halves
  • 250 g marinated artichoke hearts, drained
  • 135 g mayonnaise

Preparation

Chia and quinoa tortilla chips

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place quinoa and chia into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add cornmeal, sesame seeds, salt, lime juice and water and mix 6 sec/speed 6, or until a dough forms.
  4. Divide the dough into 4 pieces. Place 1 piece of the dough between 2 pieces of baking paper and roll out into a rectangle (1-2 mm thick). Using a sharp, wet knife, cut rectangle of dough into triangles, then place triangles onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining 3 portions of dough.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes (200°C), or until just golden. Set aside and allow tortilla chips to cool before removing from baking tray.

Sun-dried tomato, chilli and cashew dip

  1. Place chilli, garlic and Parmesan into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add coriander, cashew nuts, red pepper, lemon juice, sun-dried tomatoes and olive oil and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/2-3 times. Transfer into a serving bowl, cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pumpkin dip

  1. Preheat oven to 250°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper, then place pumpkin onto it and drizzle with olive oil.
  2. Bake for 10 minutes (250°C), then reduce oven temperature to 180°C and bake for a further 10-15 minutes (180°C). Set pumpkin aside to cool.
  3. Place pine nuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add goat's cheese, basil, cumin, salt, pepper and cooled pumpkin and blend 6 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a serving bowl, cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Artichoke dip

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a heat-resistant bowl (approx. 450 ml) and set aside.
  2. Place half of the Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add remaining Parmesan and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add artichoke and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/1-3 times.
  6. Add mayonnaise and reserved Parmesan and mix 3 sec/speed 4. Transfer into prepared bowl and bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Serve hot.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, serving bowl, cling film, heat-resistant bowl, oven, refrigerator
