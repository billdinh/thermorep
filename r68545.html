Beef rendang

Ingredients

  • 2 candlenuts
  • 40 g shredded coconut, toasted deep golden brown
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • ¼ tsp cardamom seeds
  • 1 star anise
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 - 2 fresh bird's eye chillies, to taste (optional)
  • 70 g long red chillies, deseeded if preferred, adjust to taste
  • 100 g eschalots, peeled
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 3 - 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 3 - 4 cm piece fresh galangal, peeled
  • 2 stalks fresh lemongrass, white part only, cut into pieces
  • 4 fresh Kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tbsp peanut oil
  • 1000 g gravy beef, cut into pieces (5 - 6 cm)
  • 270 g canned coconut milk
  • 1 pinch salt, to taste
  • 20 - 30 g palm sugar, to taste

Preparation

  1. If using macadamia nuts, skip to grating coconut. Place candlenuts (optional) into a bowl, cover with boiling water and leave to soak for 30 minutes. Drain candlenuts and discard soaking water. Set drained candlenuts aside.
  2. Place toasted coconut into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cinnamon quill, cardamom seeds, star anise and whole cloves into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Allow to cool slightly.
  4. Add drained candlenuts or macadamia nuts and mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  5. Add bird's eye chillies (optional), long red chillies, eschalots, garlic cloves, fresh ginger, fresh galangal and fresh lemongrass into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat process if necessary until a paste consistency is achieved.
  6. Add Kaffir lime leaves, peanut oil and reserved coconut and sauté 4 min/120°C/speed 2.
  7. Add beef and cook 10 min/120°C//speed 1.
  8. Add coconut milk and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  9. Add salt and palm sugar to taste and  cook 30-40 min/90°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  10. Allow to rest 5 minutes before serving.
