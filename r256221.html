Pear and burghul salad

Ingredients

  • 200 g fine burghul (see Tips)
  • 80 g sultanas
  • 250 g boiling water, plus extra for soaking
  • 200 g red wine vinegar
  • 150 g caster sugar, plus an extra 1 teaspoon for dressing
  • 2 pears (approx. 150 g - see Tips)
  • 5 black peppercorns
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • 30 g grapeseed oil
  • ½ tsp french mustard
  • ground black pepper, to season
  • 100 g radicchio, leaves separated
  • 100 g pecans, roasted and roughly chopped (see Tips)
  • 200 g Gorgonzola cheese, cut into pieces (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh burghul and sultanas into it. Cover with boiling water and set aside to soak. Meanwhile, proceed with recipe.
  2. Place vinegar, boiling water and 150 g of the sugar into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/120°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, peel, core and slice each pear into 8 wedges.
  3. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and place pears, peppercorns, bay leaf, mustard seeds and star anise into it. Drain soaked burghul and sultanas using Varoma dish. Add salt to burghul mixture and stir to combine. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 4 or until pears and burghul are just tender. Remove Varoma and set burghul aside to cool. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set pears aside to cool. Discard pickling spices. Transfer pickling liquid into a jug (see Tips).
  4. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 40 g of the reserved pickling liquid and oil into it. Add mustard and remaining 1 teaspoon sugar, then season with salt and pepper and whisk to combine.
  5. Arrange radicchio onto a serving platter. Top with cooled burghul and pears. Top with pecans and Gorgonzola, then drizzle with dressing to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, serving platter, jug, small bowl, whisk

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find burghul in most supermarkets and health food stores. Be sure to purchase fine burghul, as it will cook faster.
  • Use a firm variety of pear, such as Bosc, for this recipe. Pear should be ripe, but firm.
  • You can replace the pecans and Gorgonzola with any nuts and cheese of choice.
  • You can store any leftover pickling liquid in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Use in salad dressings or to pickle vegetables of choice.
