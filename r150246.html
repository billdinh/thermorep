Chocolate energy bars

Ingredients

Trail mix

  • 460 g raw almonds
  • 130 g sunflower seeds
  • 130 g pumpkin seeds
  • 140 g sultanas
  • 50 g coconut flakes
  • 50 g dried goji berries
  • 140 g macadamia nuts
  • 90 g dried apricots, cut into halves
  • 90 g dried figs, cut into halves

Chocolate bars

  • 200 g raw cacao butter, cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 50 - 60 g raw cacao powder, to taste
  • 60 g honey
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • &frac18; tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

Trail mix

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 5. Transfer onto prepared baking tray.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sunflower and pumpkin seeds into it. Transfer seeds onto baking tray with almonds and bake for 15-20 minutes (150°C) or until golden. Allow mixture to cool on tray. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  4. Place a large sealable container onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sultanas, coconut flakes and goji berries into it. Set container aside.
  5. Place macadamias into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 4. Transfer into sealable container with fruit mixture.
  6. Place apricots and figs into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer into sealable container with fruit and nut mixture.
  7. Add cooled almonds and seeds to the container and stir to combine (see Tips).

Chocolate bars

  1. Line a rectangular slice tin (20 x 30 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place cacao butter into mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 8, then melt 5 min/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and melt 6 min/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Add cacao powder, honey, vanilla and salt and blend 1 min/speed 8.
  5. Add 800 g of reserved trail mix and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Transfer into prepared tin and spread evenly. Press mixture down firmly. Place into freezer to set for 30 minutes or until firm, making sure tin is level so bar sets evenly.
  6. Remove from freezer and cut into lengths (2 cm wide), then cut each bar into thirds (makes approx. 18 bars in total). Serve immediately or wrap bars in baking paper, place into a sealable container and store in freezer until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, large sealable container, slice tin (20 x 30 cm), oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the apricots and figs with sour cherries and Inca berries, respectively.
  • You can replace the trail mix with your own fruit, nut and seed mix of choice. 
  • Store leftover trail mix in a sealable container in a cool, dark place for up to 1 month. Serve it for breakfast, as a snack or sprinkled over desserts. 
  • You can store the energy bars in the freezer for up to 1 year. These can be eaten straight from the freezer or kept in the refrigerator for at least 1 month.
