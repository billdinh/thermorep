Minestrone, Pork Roll with Vegetables, Steamed Raspberry Puddings, Berry Muffins

Ingredients

Steamed Raspberry Puddings and Berry Muffins

  • 4 tsp raspberry jam (40 g)
  • 2 eggs
  • 200 g sugar
  • 60 g butter, cut in pieces
  • 200 g milk
  • ½ tsp real vanilla extract
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 200 g fresh raspberries
  • 150 g fresh blueberries

Pork Roll

  • 500 - 600 g pork shoulder, boneless, in one piece, butterflied to approx. 1.5 cm thick
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 4 - 5 slices bacon, any type
  • 20 g sun-dried tomatoes
  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 1 egg

Minestrone, Mediterranean Vegetables

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 300 g onions, of which 100 g cut in slices, the rest in pieces
  • 130 g carrots, sliced (5 mm)
  • 70 - 80 g mixed vegetables (e.g. red or green peppers, celery), cut in pieces
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 50 g white wine (optional)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • 1000 g water
  • 250 g potatoes, sliced (5 mm)
  • 160 g courgettes, sliced (1 cm)
  • 150 g ripe tomatoes, sliced (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • 60 g short pasta, dried (e.g. elbow macaroni), for soup

Preparation

Steamed Raspberry Puddings and Berry Muffins

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a muffin tray with paper muffin cases and set aside. Distribute jam between 4 individual silicone or aluminium muffin moulds (Ø 7 cm, height 4 cm, see tip) and set aside.
  2. Place eggs, sugar, butter, milk, vanilla extract, flour and baking powder in mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Using spatula, scrape down and stir in any remaining flour on sides of mixing bowl. Pour enough batter over jam in the 4 individual moulds to come up to 5 mm below the rims and set aside on Varoma tray.
  3. Distribute remaining batter between prepared paper muffin cases, filling to 1 cm from the top as many as the batter yields (7-9 muffins), and arrange raspberries and blueberries over the muffins. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C) (see tip). While baking, continue with step 4.

Pork Roll

  1. Place butterflied meat on a sheet of heat-resistant cling film or baking paper (30 cm x 50 cm), sprinkle with salt and pepper and cover with bacon slices.
  2. Place sun-dried tomatoes, Parmesan cheese and parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add egg and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Spread mixture evenly over bacon slices and roll up the meat, wrapping it tightly with the heat-resistant cling film or baking paper. Place roll in Varoma dish and set aside.

Minestrone, Mediterranean Vegetables

  1. Place garlic, 200 g onion quarters, 50 g carrots, mixed vegetables and olive oil in mixing bowl, chop 2 sec/speed 5, then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add white wine (if using) and, without measuring cup, reduce 1 min/Varoma/speed 1 to help alcohol evaporate.
  3. Add 1 tsp salt, vegetable stock paste and water. Insert simmering basket and weigh in potatoes, remaining 80 g carrots, 100 g onion slices, courgettes and tomatoes. Season with dried oregano and ½ tsp salt, then set Varoma dish in position. Insert Varoma tray with puddings, close Varoma and steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep puddings and pork roll warm and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Transfer vegetables from simmering basket to a serving dish, cover to keep warm and set aside.
  4. Add pasta to mixing bowl and cook time indicated on packet/100°C//speed 1. Transfer soup to a serving bowl.

To Serve

  1. Serve minestrone as a starter. After serving soup, remove cling film from meat roll, slice, arrange on serving dish with vegetables and serve as the main course. Turn out steamed puddings onto plates and serve for dessert.

Useful Items

muffin tray, paper muffin cases, silicone or aluminium muffin moulds (Ø 7 cm, height 4 cm), heat-resistant cling film or baking paper, serving dish, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If cooking an all-in-one three-course meal isn’t enough of a culinary challenge, why not make some delicious muffins for breakfast while you’re at it? Tasty minestrone soup, a pork roll filled with flavourful bacon and sun-dried tomato stuffing, served with Mediterranean vegetables. Follow that with steamed raspberry puddings, the batter of which, when baked instead of steamed, produces completely different but equally wonderful berry muffins.
  • For the muffins, use an 8-hole or 12-hole muffin tray, leaving some holes unfilled depending on how much batter you have left. For the puddings, use any heatproof individual muffin or pudding moulds that fit 4 on Varoma tray.
    - After baking, allow muffins to cool for 5 minutes in the tin, then transfer to a wire rack. Once at room temperature, sprinkle with icing sugar, store in an airtight container and serve within 24 hours.
    - Serve a spoonful of whipped cream or crème fraîche with the steamed puddings.
    - If you have room in your fridge, you can prepare this recipe the night before. Prepare up until step 9, stopping just before steaming. Place prepared Varoma, mixing bowl and simmering basket in the fridge. When ready to finish, continue recipe from steaming in step 9.
  • Use different berries or small pieces of mango or banana for the muffins.
    - Use other jams or marmalades in the steamed puddings instead of raspberry jam.
