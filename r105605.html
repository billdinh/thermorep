Corn chips with salsa roja

Ingredients

Corn chips

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • ½ tsp mustard powder
  • ½ tsp ground paprika
  • 190 g polenta
  • 110 g baker's flour
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 120 g milk
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp tamari

Salsa roja

  • 30 g brown onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ - 1 tsp dried chilli flakes, to taste
  • 300 g fresh tomatoes, blanched, skins removed, deseeded and cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 15 g olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt

Preparation

Corn chips

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan, mustard powder and paprika into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place polenta, flour, salt, baking powder, milk, oil and tamari into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 6, then knead 2 min/.
  4. Divide mixture into four portions. Place one portion between two sheets of baking paper and roll out thinly into a square (approx. 25 x 25 cm). Place onto prepared baking tray and sprinkle with ¼ of the reserved Parmesan mixture. Score with a pizza cutter or knife into triangles. Bake for 12-15 minutes (190°C) or until golden. Repeat rolling, scoring and baking process with remaining mixture. Break corn chips apart along scored lines and leave to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salsa roja

  1. Place all ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Serve with Corn chips or transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, sealable container, pizza cutter, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), small bowl, rolling pin, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the tamari with soy sauce or Worcestershire sauce in this recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Remove tomato skins by using a sharp knife to cut a small cross in base of each tomato and place into bowl. Carefully pour boiling water into bowl to cover tomatoes. Leave until skins split (approx. 1 minute). Remove from water and once cool enough to touch, peel away skins, then cut flesh into quarters.
