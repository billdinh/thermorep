Vegie crackers

Ingredients

  • 340 g vegetable pulp (see Tips)
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 30 g flaxmeal (see Tips)
  • 30 g chia seeds
  • 20 g tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt
  • 1 - 2 tsp beetroot powder (optional)
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground turmeric (optional)
  • 30 g sesame seeds
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
667.8 kJ / 159 kcal

Preparation

  1. Preheat dehydrator to 41°C or oven to 100°C. If using the oven, line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place vegetable pulp, spring onions/shallots, flaxmeal, chia seeds, tamari, pepper, salt, beetroot powder (optional) and ground turmeric (optional) into mixing bowl and pulse Turbo/2 sec/2-3 times.
  3. Add sesame seeds and mix 30 sec/speed 5.
  4. If using dehydrator, thinly spread mixture onto dehydrator sheets (approx. 3-5 mm thickness) and dehydrate for approx. 12 hours (41°C). Allow to cool completely, then break into pieces to serve (see Tips).
  5. If using oven, thinly spread mixture over lined baking tray (approx. 3-5 mm thickness) and bake for 3 hours (100°C). Remove from oven, carefully peel biscuits off baking paper, turn over and bake for a further 30 minutes (100°C). Turn off oven and allow crackers to dry out in the oven overnight. Allow to cool completely, then break into pieces to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

dehydrator, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), dehydrator sheets, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own vegetable pulp, place 450 g vegetables of choice and 300 g water into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 7. Alternatively, use the leftover pulp from making vegetable juices.
  • You can purchase flaxmeal at most major supermarket, but to make your own, simply place 200 g of flaxseeds into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable container and store in the fridge or freezer for up to 3 months.
  • If you have leftover flaxmeal, you can use it as an egg substitute in baking. Combine 1 tablespoon flaxmeal with 30 g boiling water and set aside until it becomes jelly-like (equates to approx. 1 egg).
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
  • If using the dehydrator method, these crackers are suitable for those following a raw food diet.
  • You can store these crackers in a sealable container in the pantry for 2-3 days.
