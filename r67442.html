Potato cakes

Ingredients

  • 1000 g potatoes, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (approx. 1/2 lemon)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 20 g cornflour (starch)
  • 45 g plain flour, plus extra if needed
  • 20 g rolled oats, plus extra if needed
  • 1 egg
  • oil, for frying

Preparation

  1. Place potatoes, brown onion, lemon juice, salt, pepper, cornflour, plain flour, rolled oats and egg into mixing bowl and chop 12 sec/speed 5 with aid of spatula. Check consistency of mixture. If mixture is too wet, add extra flour and rolled oats and combine 2-3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Pour oil into a frying pan (enough to cover) and place over a medium-high heat. Place 2 heaped tbsp of mixture per potato cake into the hot pan and using the spoon, flatten each to form a pancake shape (approx. 10 cm). Fry potato cakes on both sides (approx. 2 minutes) until golden brown and crisp. Place into a ThermoServer or onto a plate lined with paper towel to drain oil. Repeat process with remaining mixture. Serve hot.

Useful Items

paper towel, frying pan, ThermoServer, kitchen towel, turner / fish slice, stove top, stove

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with apple sauce, or for a savoury alternative with sour cream and chives.
  • Depending on the kind of potatoes used and how long the batter is left to rest before frying, you may need to add more cornflour, plain flour or rolled oats or remove excess moisture from the batter with a spoon.
  • Nadine, Desiree or Royal Blue potatoes will work well in this recipe.
  • Use fresh potatoes. If you clean the potatoes well, there is no need to peel them.
  • Vegetable potato cakes: replace ⅓ of the potatoes with vegetables (e.g. carrots, kohlrabi, cauliflower, zucchini etc.). Double the amount of cornflour and rolled oats. Season with ground black pepper and serve with a sour cream and herb dip.
  • As the batter can be prepared in seconds, it is easy to repeat this recipe quickly to increase portions for a party.

A traditional German dish, served with sweet or savoury side dishes depending on the region.
