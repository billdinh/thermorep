Lemon and caper aioli

Ingredients

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 pieces lemon zest (5 x 2 cm), no white pith
  • 12 pickled capers, drained
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 - 2 tsp lemon juice, to taste
  • 250 g grapeseed oil
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place garlic and zest into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat chopping 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add capers, egg yolks, Dijon mustard and lemon juice and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula
  4. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Remove jug.
  5. Mix 2-3 min/speed 4, slowly pouring oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it trickle into mixing bowl to emulsify.
  6. Season with salt to taste then mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  7. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Useful Items

2L jug, serving jug, sealable storage jar or container

Hints & Tricks

  • Aioli is a slightly stronger flavoured emulsion, very similar to mayonnaise. Serve with hand cut chips, or enjoy in a more traditional fashion with steamed fish or vegetables.
  • Try serving your aioli with Steamed salmon with broccoli pesto or Lemon chicken and crispy potatoes.
  • Use up leftover aioli throughout the week on sandwiches, rolls, salads and wraps. Homemade aioli complements many different flavours.
  • If your aioli has not thickened after step 4, mix for a further 2 min/speed 4 or until a mayonnaise consistency is achieved.
  • Garlic aioli: omit the lemon zest and capers. Add 1- 2 extra garlic cloves to taste in step 1 and proceed as per recipe.
  • Lemon aioli: omit the capers. Add an extra 1 - 2 pieces of lemon zest (5 x 2 cm) to taste in step 1 and proceed as per recipe.
  • Citrus aioli: omit the capers and use a combination of orange, lemon and lime zest pieces (5 x 2 cm) in stp 1 to create your flavour of choice. Proceed as per recipe.
