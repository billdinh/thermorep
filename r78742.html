Apple and cinnamon muffins

Ingredients

  • 90 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g Granny Smith apples, peeled and cut into quarters, plus 2 extra apples, unpeeled, for garnishing
  • 1 egg
  • 180 g full cream milk
  • 250 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 100 g brown sugar

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a 12-hole muffin tray with cupcake liners and set aside.
  2. Place apple into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place 90 g butter into mixing bowl and melt 1-2 min 30 sec/60°C/speed 1, until butter has melted.
  4. Add egg, milk, flour, honey, cinnamon and brown sugar and combine 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved apple and combine 5 sec//speed 4. Spoon mixture into prepared muffin tray. Cut the top and bottom off the 2 extra unpeeled apples, then cut each apple into 6 rounds (approx. 5 mm thick). Gently place 1 slice on top of each muffin.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of a muffin comes out clean. Transfer muffins onto a wire rack to cool (approx. 20 minutes) then serve.

Useful Items

12-hole muffin tray, cupcake liners, wire rack, wooden skewer, bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These quick and easy muffins are so simple you can make them every week. Omit the apple slice garnishes to make these muffins even faster and simpler if desired.
