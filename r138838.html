Fairy doll cake

Ingredients

Vanilla cake

  • 200 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 680 g plain flour
  • 540 g sugar
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs

Buttercream icing

  • 250 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 120 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 2 tsp boiling water
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract

Fondant

  • 40 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g glucose syrup
  • 15 g glycerine (see Tips)
  • 620 g icing sugar, plus extra sifted for dusting and kneading (see Tips)
  • natural food colouring of choice (see Tips)

Preparation

Vanilla cake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line 1 round cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Grease and line a Dolly Varden cake tin (19 cm at widest part) with baking paper cut into strips (2 x 30 x 5 cm strips) and set aside.
  2. Place 340 g of the flour, 270 g of the sugar, 1 tablespoon of the baking powder, 1 pinch of the salt, 100 g of the butter, 250 g of the milk and 1 teaspoon of the vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 .
  3. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add 2 of the eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5 . Transfer batter into prepared round cake tin. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place remaining 340 g flour, 270 g sugar, 1 tablespoon baking powder, 1 pinch salt, 100 g butter, 250 g milk and 1 teaspoon vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 .
  6. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 .
  7. Add remaining 2 eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5 .
  8. Transfer batter into Dolly Varden tin. Tap both tins gently on work surface 2-3 times to even out batter and prevent any air bubbles. Place both cakes into oven and bake for 1 hour 30 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in tins for 15 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6 . Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk . Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Fondant

  1. Place water and gelatine into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  2. Add glucose and glycerine and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3 .
  3. Add 600 g of the icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 6, then knead 15 sec/ . Dust a silicone bread mat or work surface well with sifted icing sugar. Transfer fondant onto a dusted silicone bread mat or work surface. Fondant will be heavy and sticky.
  4. To remove any remaining fondant in mixing bowl, place remaining 20 g icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Add to fondant on silicone bread mat. Dust hands with icing sugar and knead into a ball, using extra sifted icing sugar if needed, until fondant is smooth and less sticky.
  5. On a dusted silicone bread mat or work surface, add a small amount of food colouring to your fondant. Knead until there are no streaks. If the fondant feels sticky, add some sifted icing sugar (see Tips). Transfer into a sealable container and set aside until ready to use (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Trim base of Dolly Varden cake and trim top of the round cake to create flat surfaces. Place Dolly Varden cake on top of the round cake and trim edges of round cake to meet edge of Dolly Varden cake. Set Dolly Varden cake aside.
  2. Place round cake onto a cake board or serving platter and cut into halves horizontally. Spread 2-3 tablespoons icing over bottom half of cake, then replace top of cake. Spread 2-3 tablespoons icing on top of round cake.
  3. Cut Dolly Varden cake into halves horizontally. Spread 2-3 tablespoons icing over bottom half of cake, then replace top of cake. Place Dolly Varden cake on top of round cake (there will be 3 layers of icing within cake).
  4. If using a Dolly Varden doll (see Tips), cut a small slit in the top of cake and place the skewer into cake. If using a standard doll, make a hole in top of cake using an apple corer. Insert doll into hole up to her waist.
  5. Spread remaining icing in a thin, even layer (crumb coat) over entire cake. Set cake aside.
  6. Roll fondant out to 3 mm thickness. Using a cookie cutter (we used a 4 cm leaf-shaped cutter), cut out as many shapes as possible. Working from the base of cake upwards, attach shapes to cake, with edges overlapping, all the way around cake. Add other decorations as desired, then set aside until ready to serve.

Useful Items

round cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, Dolly Varden cake tin (19 cm at widest part), wooden skewer, wire rack, bowl, silicone bread mat, sealable container, cake board or serving plate, palette knife, cookie cutter, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a soft, sifted icing sugar for this recipe.
  • If you are not using icing immediately, place into refrigerator until ready to use (can be stored for up to 1 week). Before using, bring the icing back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread.
  • Glycerine is an edible product and can be purchased from cake decorating shops or the health and beauty aisle in most supermarkets.
  • Liquid food colouring may affect the texture of your fondant; add sifted icing sugar as needed. Once your fondant is coloured, try to avoid excess use of icing sugar when rolling as this will leave white marks. We recommend natural food colouring gel when possible.
  • If your fondant feels dry and cracked, feels very firm or crumbly, knead in a few drops of glycerine or a few drops of cooled boiled water to soften and make it more elastic. This will help avoid cracks in the finished product. You can also rub some vegetable shortening (Copha®) onto your palms and work surface and continue to knead.
  • A Dolly Varden doll has the upper half of a doll's body and the lower half is a skewer.
  • Get creative with this cake! You can use any colour in your fondant (or divide the fondant into portions and use a different colour for each portion), any size or shape of cookie cutter, or simply create a smooth gown (without cutting out shapes) by draping fondant over the cake.
