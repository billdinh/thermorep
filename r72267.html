Falafel with beetroot hommus

Ingredients

Beetroot hommus

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 50 g lemon juice (approx. 1-2 lemons)
  • 1 tbsp tahini
  • Â½ - 1 raw beetroot, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin (optional)
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground paprika, for sprinkling

Falafel

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp spelt flour
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 2 pinches sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • olive oil, for frying

Preparation

Beetroot hommus

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining hommus ingredients, except paprika, and chop 10-15 sec/speed 5, or until desired consistency is achieved. Transfer into a serving bowl, sprinkle with paprika and set aside until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Falafel

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining falafel ingredients, except oil, and chop 15 sec/speed 4, or until a slightly chunky consistency is achieved.
  3. Form falafel mixture into walnut-sized balls (approx. 20). Place a frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Cook falafel balls in batches for 3-4 minutes, turning occasionally to brown on all sides. Transfer falafels onto a plate lined with paper towel.
  4. Serve falafels warm or cold with Beetroot hommus.
