Pork and coriander steamed dumplings (shumai)

Ingredients

Sui mai wrappers

  • 50 g water
  • 180 g plain flour
  • 1 egg

Filling

  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 6 pieces dried shiitake mushrooms, cut into slices
  • 300 g pork mince
  • 200 g shrimp (small), peeled (see Tips)
  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, stalks and roots only, roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 2 tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 ½ tsp sesame oil
  • 500 g water
  • fish roe, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

Sui mai wrappers

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/80°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and egg and knead 1 min/. Transfer into a large bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place ginger and mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add mince, shrimp, coriander roots, salt, sugar, cornflour, pepper and sesame oil and mix 30 sec/speed 10.
  3. Line Varoma dish and tray with baking paper and poke a few small holes in the paper to allow steam to circulate.
  4. Divide reserved dough into 20 small portions (approx. 10 g each). Roll each portion into a flat disc (8 cm). Place 2 teaspoons of the pork filling into the centre of each disc. Pull up sides of wrapper around filling, forming a cylinder shape, keeping the filling at the top of the dumpling uncovered. Tap the bottom of the dumplings lightly on a flat work surface to ensure they stand up straight. Place 10 dumplings into Varoma dish and 10 dumplings on Varoma tray.
  5. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve dumplings warm, garnished with fish roe (optional).

Useful Items

baking paper, large bowl, rolling pin, plastic wrap, wooden skewer

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, you can use pre-made wonton wrappers in this recipe.
  • You can replace the small shrimp with peeled prawns, cut into small pieces, if preferred.
  • These dumplings are usually served with a dipping sauce; often a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil and black vinegar.
  • Coloured sui mai: To make yellow sui mai wrappers, add 10 g ground turmeric to the dough when adding flour. To make green sui mai wrappers, replace the water with 50 g spinach juice.
