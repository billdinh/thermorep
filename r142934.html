Vegetable spring rolls

Ingredients

  • 50 g mung bean vermicelli
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 100 g cabbage, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 20 g vegetable oil, plus extra for deep frying
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 40 g oyster sauce
  • 150 g fresh bean shoots, cut into halves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 12 spring roll pastry sheets
  • water, for brushing
  • sweet chilli sauce, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh noodles into it. Cover noodles with boiling water and set aside to soak for 10 minutes. Drain well and cut noodles into pieces (3 cm), then transfer back into bowl and set aside.
  2. Place carrot and cabbage into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into bowl with noodles and set aside.
  3. Place garlic and 20 g of the oil into mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 10.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add sugar, fish sauce, soy sauce and oyster sauce and mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  6. Add bean shoots, salt and reserved carrot and noodle mixture, then cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer into simmering basket and set over a bowl to drain and cool (approx. 30 minutes).
  7. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  8. Place 1 spring roll wrapper, floured side facing up, onto a clean work surface, with one corner facing down (wrapper will look like a diamond). Cover remaining wrappers with a clean, damp kitchen towel. Form 2 tablespoons of the mixture into a sausage shape, then place horizontally onto wrapper towards bottom edge of diamond. Brush sides of wrapper with water. Fold in bottom point of diamond to enclose filling, then fold in both sides to overlap filling. Gently roll wrapper up, making sure edges are tucked inside and filling is completely sealed. Place seam side down onto prepared tray. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  9. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside.
  10. Place a large saucepan over medium heat. Add oil (approx. 8 cm high) and bring to 175°C (see Tip). Keep your oil at this temperature while frying. Working in batches, carefully place 5-6 spring rolls into oil and fry for 5 minutes or until golden in colour, keeping rolls apart during first minute of frying to prevent sticking. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove and transfer onto prepared plate to drain. Serve spring rolls with sweet chilli sauce on the side. 

Useful Items

pastry docker, bowl, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), kitchen towel, 1 plate, paper towel, kitchen thermometer, slotted spoon, large saucepan, pastry brush, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To check your oil is at 175°C, use a kitchen thermometer or drop a small cube of bread in the oil; it should turn golden in 15-20 seconds.
