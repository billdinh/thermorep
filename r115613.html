Chicken and leek pies

Ingredients

Pie filling

  • 800 g water
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 500 g leeks, green part removed, cut into pieces; white part cut into rounds (1 cm)
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • ½ tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 700 g skinless chicken thigh fillets
  • 60 g salted butter
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 30 g gluten free cornflour

Pastry

  • 50 g cheddar cheese (mature)
  • 400 g gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 200 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • 40 g wholegrain mustard (gluten free)
  • 3 tbsp water
  • arrowroot flour, for dusting
  • 1 egg yolk

Preparation

Pie filling

  1. Place water, stock paste and green portion of leeks into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Insert simmering basket. Place carrot, parsley, peppercorns, bay leaf and chicken to simmering basket and cook 15-20 min/100°C/speed 2, mixing with aid of spatula as needed to ensure even cooking, until chicken is cooked. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Transfer chicken into a bowl and strain stock over it to keep moist. Discard all other contents. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place white portion of leeks, butter, thyme, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  4. Remove chicken from stock and cut into large pieces (approx. 3-4 cm). Retain poaching liquid (see Tips).
  5. Add reserved chicken, cream and 150 g of the poaching liquid into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/90°C//speed .
  6. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh cornflour and 100 g of the poaching liquid into it and stir until smooth. Add cornflour mixture into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/90°C//speed . Transfer into a large bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes) before placing into refrigerator to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pastry

  1. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add flour mix, butter, xanthan gum and salt and mix 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add egg, mustard and 2 tablespoons of the water and mix 25 sec/speed 6.
  4. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Divide into 2 equal portions, wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 1 hour.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Set a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) aside. Set aside 6 individual pie dishes (14 cm).
  6. Roll out 1 pastry portion between two sheets of baking paper (5 mm thick). Cut into 6 discs (12 cm). Use discs to line pie dishes. Roll out remaining pastry portion between 2 sheets of baking paper (5 mm thick). Cut into 6 discs (13 cm). Set aside.
  7. Place egg yolk and remaining water into a small bowl and stir to combine. Set aside.
  8. Divide cooled chicken filling evenly between pie dishes. Brush pastry edges with egg yolk mixture. Place 6 remaining discs over filling. Pinch pastry together at the edges and crimp to seal using a fork or your fingertips. Cut a slit in the top of each pie to let steam escape.
  9. Brush pastry with egg yolk mixture and place into refrigerator for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 170°C.
  10. Glaze pies with egg yolk mixture and place onto baking tray. Bake for 45 minutes (170°C), or until golden. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, small bowl , large bowl, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), 6 individual pie dishes (14 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Please refer to the Gluten free flour mix recipe on the recipe chip.
  • Use leftover poaching liquid to use in future recipes (such as making a gravy).
  • To cut out pastry discs, use the base of your pie dishes or small dinner plates as your guide.
  • One large pie can be made by lining a 22 cm metal pie tin. Make a cross in the middle of the pie to let out the steam. Preheat oven to 200°C.  Reduce oven to 170°C and bake pie for 45-50 minutes, or until golden and a metal skewer inserted into the centre comes out hot.
