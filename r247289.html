Calzones with Marinara Sauce - Calzoni con salsa marinara

Ingredients

Dough

  • 450 g milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g honey
  • 700 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • olive oil, for greasing

Marinara Sauce

  • 40 g olive oil
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 800 g tinned whole peeled tomatoes (2 x 400 g tins)
  • 100 g white wine
  • 2 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp fresh parsley leaves, chopped

Fillings

  • 70 g Scamorza cheese (smoked mozzarella), cut in pieces
  • 180 g vintage Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces
  • 75 g pepperoni, sliced
  • 60 g pitted black olives, halved
  • 2 pinch fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 200 g cheese (3 types e.g. mozzarella, Cheddar, Gorgonzola)
  • water, for sealing
  • 75 g ricotta cheese, drained
  • 70 g fresh baby spinach

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place milk, yeast and honey in mixing bowl then warm 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and sprinkle with flour.
  3. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make marinara sauce.

Marinara Sauce

  1. Place oil, onions and garlic in mixing bowl, chop 3 sec/speed 5 then sauté 7 min/95°C/speed .
  2. Add tomatoes, wine, tomato purée, oregano, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 25 min/100°C/speed .
  3. Add parsley and blend 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and keep warm. Clean mixing bowl.

Fillings

  1. Towards the end of rising time, preheat oven to 230°C and line 2 large baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Divide risen dough into 4 equal pieces. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each piece into a circle (Ø 20-25 cm) then place on prepared baking trays.
  3. Place smoked mozzarella and Cheddar in mixing bowl then grate 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Divide grated cheese between 2 pizza bases, placing over one half of each base. Sprinkle over pepperoni and olives. Season to taste then wet edges and fold over other halves of dough, pressing to form a tight seal. Bake for 10 minutes (230°C). Meanwhile, prepare remaining 2 calzones.
  5. Place mixed cheese (except ricotta) in mixing bowl then grate 5 sec/speed 5. Scatter over one half of remaining 2 bases. Place spoonfuls of ricotta over cheese then scatter spinach leaves on top. Season then wet edges and fold over other halves of dough to seal as before. Bake for 10 minutes (230°C).
  6. Serve calzones hot with small bowls of warm marinara sauce.

Useful Items

baking trays and baking paper, large bowl, rolling pin, cling film, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Like pizza, calzone originated in Naples, where they are believed to have been created as a more portable form of pizza that could be eaten without utensils. They are essentially a circle of pizza dough stuffed with fillings then folded in half before being baked in a pizza oven. Fillings vary from meats, cheeses and vegetables, and traditionally do not contain tomato sauce. Instead, a small dish of tomato sauce is served on the side. Our recipe makes calzones with two different fillings. The ingredients can be doubled for either filling to make four calzones the same.
