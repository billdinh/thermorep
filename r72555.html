Bacon and spring onion potato salad

Ingredients

Salad

  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds, plus extra thinly sliced for garnishing
  • 150 g diced bacon
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 500 g water
  • 1000 g potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 6 eggs

Dressing

  • 200 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 20 g wholegrain mustard
  • 2 tsp balsamic vinegar

Assembly

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, shaved, plus extra for garnishing
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

Salad

  1. Place spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add bacon and oil and sauté 3 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer into a large serving bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, then weigh potatoes into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Remove Varoma dish and set aside. Insert simmering basket, then place eggs into it. Place Varoma dish back into position and stir potatoes gently with spatula to ensure even cooking. Secure Varoma lid and cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until potatoes are soft (see Tips).
  5. Remove Varoma dish and set potatoes aside to cool (approx. 15 minutes), placing Varoma lid under the Varoma dish to catch any drips.
  6. Fill a bowl with cold water, then remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer eggs into cold water to cool (approx. 15 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Once cooled, peel and quarter eggs, then set aside.

Dressing

  1. Place all dressing ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Add cooled potatoes and peeled eggs to large serving bowl, then place serving bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh Parmesan into it. Pour dressing over salad, season with ground black pepper to taste and gently mix with spatula until combined.
  2. Refrigerate until chilled (approx. 30 minutes), then garnish with extra spring onions/shallots and Parmesan cheese before serving.

Useful Items

large serving bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Potatoes should be cooked through, but still firm so that a knife passes easily through them.
  • Red-skinned potatoes, such as Ruby Lou, work best in this recipe.
  • To make your own mayonnaise for the dressing, see The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the recipe.
