Mini Everything Bagels

Ingredients

  • 480 g water, plus extra for boiling
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 900 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 10 g vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 Tbsp barley malt extract
  • 3 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 Tbsp poppy seeds
  • 1 Tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 Tbsp fried onions, ready-made
  • 1 Tbsp garlic flakes
  • 1 large egg, beaten

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add yeast, flour, oil, malt extract and 2 tsp salt, mix 20 sec/speed 3 then knead 3 min 30 sec/.
  3. Tip dough out onto a lightly-floured surface and divide into 20 equal-sized pieces (approx. 70 g each). Shape into balls then cover loosely with cling film and leave to rest for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, grease and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  4. Flatten rested rolls slightly, push the end of a greased wooden spoon or your thumb through the centre of each then stretch into bagel shapes. Place on prepared trays and cover tightly with greased cling film. Leave at room temperature for 20 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  6. Bring a large saucepan, about ⅔ full with water and 1 Tbsp bicarbonate of soda added, to a gentle simmer.
  7. Place bagels in water, three or four at a time, cooking for 30 seconds then flipping over with a slotted spoon and cooking for a further 30 seconds. Transfer bagels to paper towels to drain then continue until all bagels have been boiled.
  8. Return bagels to prepared trays. In a small bowl, mix together poppy seeds, sesame seeds, fried onions, garlic flakes and 1 tsp salt. Brush each bagel with a little beaten egg then sprinkle with seed mixture.
  9. Bake for 12-15 minutes (220°C) until golden brown. Leave to cool slightly on baking trays then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, baking trays, baking paper, wooden spoon, large saucepan, slotted spoon, pastry brush, paper towels, wire rack, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Bagels are a great alternative to sandwiches and these mini ones are more manageable for little hands. Fun to make with their characteristic holes, this is a great recipe to prepare at the weekend then freeze, ready to defrost and toast throughout the week.
