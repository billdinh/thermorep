Prawn spring rolls with sweet and sour sauce

Ingredients

Sweet and sour sauce

  • 60 g white sugar
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato sauce
  • 100 g pineapple juice
  • 1 tsp cornflour

Spring rolls

  • 200 g cabbage, cut in pieces
  • 3 - 5 sprigs fresh coriander, to taste (optional)
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g dried shiitake mushroom, cut into slices
  • 150 g spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut in pieces
  • 12 canned water chestnuts, cut into halves
  • 150 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 50 g sesame oil
  • 100 g pork mince
  • 100 g peeled raw prawns, deveined and roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper
  • 2 tsp cornflour
  • 20 spring roll pastry sheets (see Tips)
  • water, for sealing
  • oil, for frying
  • 1 egg, beaten

Preparation

Sweet and sour sauce

  1. Place sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, tomato sauce, pineapple juice and cornflour into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Spring rolls

  1. Place cabbage and coriander (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place ginger and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add mushrooms, spring onions/shallots, water chestnuts and carrots and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into bowl with cabbage and stir well to combine.
  4. Place sesame oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add mince, prawns, soy sauce, salt, pepper and cornflour. Stir with aid of spatula, then sauté 2 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer into bowl with chopped vegetables and stir to combine.
  6. Place 1 spring roll pastry sheet on a clean work surface with 1 corner facing you (in a diamond shape). Place 2 tablespoons of the filling in top corner of pastry sheet. Roll pastry over until it covers the filling; fold side corners in to create a roll (approx. 12-14 cm wide), then continue rolling to the bottom corner and seal. If needed, brush a little water on the edge to help seal the ends. Transfer onto a large plate and repeat with remaining pastry sheets and filling.
  7. Place a deep saucepan over high heat and fill three-quarters full with oil. Working in batches, brush spring rolls with egg wash and carefully place into hot oil. Fry for 3-5 minutes or until golden all over. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove from oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with reserved Sweet and sour sauce.

Useful Items

paper towel, pastry brush, deep medium-sized saucepan, large bowl, bowl, large plate, slotted spoon, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find spring roll pastry sheets in the frozen food aisle of most major supermarkets or from Asian food stores. Ensure that you purchase spring roll pastry sheets; rice vermicelli sheets will not work in this recipe.
