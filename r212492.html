Southern Chicken and Cornbread Dressing

Ingredients

  • 600 g cornbread, broken in large pieces (see tip)
  • 4 slices sliced white bread, toasted
  • 375 g cooked chicken meat, cut in pieces
  • 110 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing and 25 g thinly sliced for topping
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 celery stalk, cut in 3 pieces
  • 1 Tbsp Cajun spice mix
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 1 tsp piri piri seasoning
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 300 - 400 g chicken stock, chilled
  • 3 medium eggs

Preparation

  1. Place cornbread in mixing bowl and grate Turbo/1 sec/3 times. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place toast in mixing bowl and grate Turbo/1 sec/2-3 times until grated. Transfer to bowl with cornbread, mix then set aside.
  3. Place cooked chicken in mixing bowl and shred 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Place 110 g butter in mixing bowl and melt 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add onion and celery, chop 3 sec/speed 5 then sauté 5 min/100°C//speed 0.5. Transfer to bowl with cornbread.
  6. Add Cajun spice mix, sage, piri piri seasoning, salt and pepper to cornbread then stir with spatula. Add 300 g cold stock, stir well then leave to stand for 10 minutes (see tip). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C and generously grease a large baking dish with butter.
  7. Place eggs in mixing bowl and beat 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to bowl with cornbread mixture and stir gently with spatula.
  8. Spread half cornbread mixture over base of prepared dish. Top with reserved shredded chicken. Spread remaining cornbread mixture over chicken and level surface. Dot with slices of remaining 25 g butter then bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or longer for a crunchy topping. Leave to rest for 5-10 minutes before serving with chicken gravy (see tip).

Useful Items

large bowl, large baking dish, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own cornbread, simply search for the recipe on Cookidoo®.
  • If mixture still seems a little dry in step 6, add up to 100 g more chicken stock until moistened.
  • While the main dish is baking, you can make a chicken gravy in your Thermomix®. Melt 100 g diced, unsalted butter 2 min/100°C/speed 1. Add 75 g plain flour and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Without measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1 while adding 450 g chicken stock, or until desired consistency is reached.
  • Alternatively, for an instant gravy, dilute a can of condensed cream of chicken soup with 150 g hot chicken stock and stir to a smooth consistency.
  • Dressing is the name given to this classic Southern baked chicken and cornbread dish. This recipe is pure comfort food at any time of year.
