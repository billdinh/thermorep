Vada

Ingredients

  • 270 g urad dal (see Tips)
  • cold water, for soaking
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 dried red chillies
  • 1 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 50 g brown onion
  • 12 fresh curry leaves
  • 2 - 5 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 9 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves, stalks and roots (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh urad dal into it. Cover with cold water and set aside to soak for 2 hours. Using simmering basket, strain dal. Discard soaking liquid.
  2. Place fennel seeds and peppercorns into mixing bowl and roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add dried chillies, ginger paste, garlic paste, onion and soaked dal and blend 30 sec/speed 10.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat blending 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place curry leaves, green chillies, coriander, sugar, salt and baking powder and gently stir with aid of spatula, then chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  6. Gently stir mixture with aid of spatula and repeat chopping 3 sec/speed 7.
  7. Add reserved dal mixture and combine 20 sec//speed 3.
  8. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside. Set aside a separate plate. Place a large saucepan over high heat and add oil. Heat oil to 160°C (see Tips).
  9. Using wet hands, shape approx. 1 tablespoon of the batter into a round shape. Using your thumb, make a hole in the centre of the circle (see Tips). Transfer onto a plate and continue with remaining batter (makes approx. 25 in total).
  10. Working in batches, carefully place vada into saucepan and fry for 4-5 minutes or until golden brown all over. Carefully remove using a slotted spoon and transfer onto prepared plate to drain. Serve with your favourite chutney (see Tips).

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, bowl, large saucepan, large plate, paper towel, 1 plate, slotted spoon, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Urad dal is a small black lentil, with a creamy white interior. Be sure to use dehusked, split urad dal.
  • To make your own ginger paste and garlic paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • To check your oil is at 160°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into oil; bread will turn golden brown in 25 - 30 seconds at 160°C.
  • The hole made with the thumb in the vada reduces frying time and allows for even cooking.
  • To ensure your recipe is gluten free, always check the labels of your products to make sure they don't contain traces of wheat or gluten.
  • We served our vadas with Green chutney from the Chicken 65 and green chutney recipe (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip), but you can pair with any chutney you choose.
