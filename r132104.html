Amritsari dal

Ingredients

Everyday Indian curry paste

  • 30 g cumin seeds
  • 2 - 3 dried bay leaves
  • 30 g coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp whole cloves
  • 12 cardamom pods (black)
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 5 brown onions (approx. 700 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 40 g canola oil
  • 30 fresh curry leaves
  • 800 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt

Dal

  • 100 g urad dal (see Tips)
  • 80 g toor dal (see Tips)
  • 250 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 15 g fresh coriander, leaves, stalks and roots, cut into thirds, plus extra leaves for garnishing (see Tips)
  • 10 g fresh mint, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 30 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 brown onions (approx. 200 g), cut into quarters
  • 5 fresh long green chillies, trimmed, cut into halves and deseeded, if preferred
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt

Preparation

Everyday Indian curry paste

  1. Place cumin seeds, bay leaves, coriander seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon quill and peppercorns into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, then mill 1 min/speed 9 to a powder.
  2. Add chilli powder, ground ginger, turmeric, onions and fresh chillies (optional) and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee, oil and curry leaves then sauté 6 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes, sugar, stock paste and salt and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 20 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7. Set aside to cool before transferring into freezable containers or snaplock bags. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dal

  1. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh urad dal and toor dal into it. Rinse and drain. Transfer into a large bowl and cover with water. Set aside to soak overnight. Rinse, drain and set aside.
  2. Place coriander and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place ghee, onion and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add garlic paste, ginger paste and tomatoes and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved dal and 150 g of the water and cook 40 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add chilli powder, garam masala, salt, 110 g of the reserved curry paste and remaining 100 g water and cook 40 min/90°C//speed 2.
  7. Add reserved chopped herbs and combine 20 sec//speed 2. Serve garnished with extra coriander and mint leaves.

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Urad dal (a small black lentil, with a creamy white interior - be sure to use dehusked, split urad dal) and toor dal (a split, yellow lentil, also known as split pigeon peas) are both available at Indian food stores or online.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • When adding mint leaves to mixing bowl, be sure to add it in big handfuls, as the weight of individual leaves may not register on the scales.
  • To save time, rinse, drain and soak dal first. Then make curry paste and store in the refrigerator until ready to use the next day when cooking remainder of recipe.
  • You can store this paste in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 1 month.
  • You can serve this with your favourite side. We served ours with Garlic and coriander naan. Please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
