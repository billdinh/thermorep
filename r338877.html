Ras el Hanout

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ Tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 ½ Tbsp black peppercorns
  • 1 ½ Tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 Tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 Tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 Tbsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 Tbsp whole allspice berries
  • ½ Tbsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ Tbsp whole aniseed
  • ½ Tbsp cloves
  • ½ Tbsp cardamom pods
  • ½ Tbsp ground turmeric

Preparation

  1. Place all ingredients in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, toast 7 min/120°C/speed .
  2. With measuring cup in place, grind 30 sec/speed 10.
  3. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula then grind again 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer ras el hanout to a sterilised glass jar (see tip). Store in a dark place and use as needed.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Ras el hanout is a spice blend that originated in the Maghreb and combines many different ingredients. The composition differs regionally and from shop to shop. Usually around 25 different spices are used.
  • The ratio of ingredients can be varied slightly according to taste.
  • Star anise, galangal, black cumin, bay or fennel are often added to the spice mixture.
  • A good ras el hanout is made with dried rose petals or buds.
  • To sterilise glass jar, preheat oven to 120°C. Thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot soapy water. Rinse by pouring boiling water over them, then place on a baking tray and heat for approx. 20 minutes (120°C) until required.
  • In a clean glass jar, stored in a dry place, the spice mixture will keep for an almost unlimited time. However, since long storage affects the aroma, it should be used within 1 year.
