Spaghetti Squash with Chilies, Lime and Dukkah

Ingredients

  • 4 oz unsalted pistachios, shelled, peeled
  • 4 oz whole raw almonds, skin on
  • 1 oz white sesame seeds
  • 2 tsp whole cumin seeds
  • 1 ½ tsp coriander seeds
  • ½ tsp salt, divided
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 10 oz red onions, quartered
  • 1 serrano chili, stem removed, plus extra to garnish
  • 4 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 1 oz sugar
  • 11 oz water
  • 1 spaghetti squash (approx. 24-32 oz), cut in half and seeds removed
  • ½ oz fresh cilantro, chopped (approx. 16 leaves and tender stems)
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice

Preparation

  1. Place pistachios, almonds, sesame seeds, cumin and coriander into mixing bowl and without the measuring cup, toast 8 min/250°F//speed 1. Allow to cool for 10 minutes or until contents reach 140°F.
  2. Add ¼ tsp salt and peppercorns and pulse Turbo/2 sec/3-4 times. Remove Dukkah from mixing bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onion and chili into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Add apple cider vinegar, sugar and water.
  4. Place Varoma dish into position and place in spaghetti squash, flesh side down. Secure Varoma lid and steam 40 min/Varoma//speed 2 or until squash is cooked to your liking. Remove Varoma and Varoma lid and set spaghetti squash aside to cool. Drag a fork across flesh of spaghetti squash, pulling up the strands. Transfer spaghetti squash and onions into a large serving bowl.
  5. Add cilantro, lime juice and ¼ tsp salt to spaghetti squash and stir through with spatula to combine. Sprinkle with reserved Dukkah, to taste. Can be served either hot or cold.

Hints & Tricks

  • Cut spaghetti squash into quarters if the Varoma lid does not fit flush with the Varoma dish. Check to make sure squash is done: for a softer texture steam for an additional 5 minutes.
  • For Dukkah with more texture, only pulse 1 sec/turbo. Leftover Dukkah can be refrigerated in an airtight container for 2-4 months. Dukkah can also be stored in the freezer for 6 months where it stays crisp and does not absorb moisture.
  • Roast squash seeds and add to a trail mix; toss in salads or make a hearty granola with these nutritious seeds. Line a baking pan with parchment paper and set aside. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and add pumpkin seeds, 32 oz boiling water and 4 oz sea salt. Stir through until salt has dissolved and set aside to soak for 3 hours or overnight. Drain and pat dry with paper towels. Preheat oven to 350°F. Place 1 oz butter into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/160°F/speed 2. Add pumpkin seeds and mix through butter 30 sec//. Spread the seeds in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 45 minutes or until golden brown. Stir 2 times (approx. every 15 min.).
  • Replace apple cider vinegar with balsamic vinegar. Replace serrano pepper with Shishito or jalapeño.
