Pajeon (Savoury Seafood Pancake)

Ingredients

Dipping Sauce

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 Tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp water
  • ½ Tbsp brown rice vinegar
  • ½ Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 spring onion, thinly sliced (5 mm)
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes

Pajeon

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 90 g plain flour
  • 2 Tbsp cornflour
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 180 g water
  • vegetable oil, for frying
  • 10 spring onions, halved lengthways
  • 250 g large, raw prawns
  • 1 fresh red chilli, thinly sliced
  • 2 large eggs, beaten

Preparation

Dipping Sauce

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, water, vinegar, oil, spring onion, sugar, sesame seeds and chilli flakes then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Pajeon

  1. Place garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add flour, cornflour and salt then mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add water and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Heat oil in a frying pan (Ø 23 cm) over a medium heat. Place 5 spring onions, 125 g prawns and ½ chilli in the pan. Drizzle over half pancake mixture, filling any gaps, then pour over 1 beaten egg.
  5. When pancake looks almost cooked (3-4 minutes), flip over and cook for a further 3-4 minutes until edges are crispy and browned. Transfer to a chopping board, covering to keep warm, then repeat with remaining 5 spring onions, 125 g prawns, ½ chilli, remaining pancake mixture and 1 beaten egg to make a second pancake.
  6. Using a sharp knife, slice pancakes into squares then serve immediately with reserved dipping sauce.
