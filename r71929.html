Shepherd's pie with butter bean mash

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 600 g water
  • 250 g lamb meat, cut into cubes
  • 4 tsp dried oregano
  • 80 g olive oil
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 carrots, cut into pieces
  • 2 celery stalks, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 60 g tomato purée
  • 20 g Worcestershire sauce
  • 800 g canned butter beans, (2 cans), rinsed and drained

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position. Weigh lamb into Varoma dish then rub lamb with 2 teaspoons of the dried oregano and 20 g of the olive oil. Season lamb to preference with salt and pepper, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place cooked lamb into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/1-2 times or until a mince consistency is achieved. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  5. Place garlic, carrot, celery, spring onions/shallots, remaining oregano and 30 g of the olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/100°C//speed 1.
  7. Add reserved lamb mince, stock paste, tomato purée and Worcestershire sauce and cook 10 min/100°C//speed . Transfer into an ovenproof dish (approx. 15 x 22 cm) and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Place butter beans and remaining 30 g olive oil into mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 5.
  9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, season to taste with salt and pepper and repeat blending until a smooth consistency is achieved.
  10. Spread butter bean mash over the top of the lamb mixture and sprinkle with reserved Parmesan. Bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C) or until top is golden brown and bubbling. Serve hot.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, ovenproof dish (15 x 22 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • If cooking for one, transfer any leftovers into a freezable container and place into the freezer to enjoy later in the week.
  • Butter beans, also known as lima beans, have a rich buttery texture - making them the perfect substitute for mashed potato in this traditional dish. High in fibre and protein, butter beans also help to stabilise blood sugar levels after eating.
