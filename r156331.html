Five-spice duck with mushrooms, Asian vegetables and rice

Ingredients

  • 15 g fresh ginger, peeled, cut into thin slices (2-3 mm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ - 1 fresh red chilli, deseeded if preferred (optional)
  • 700 g duck breast fillets, skin on
  • 1 ½ - 2 tbsp Chinese five spice powder (see Tips), to taste
  • 2 pinches salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1200 g water
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (refer to the recipe in Basic Cookbook)
  • 80 g soy sauce
  • 200 g long-grain white rice
  • 200 g pak choi, cut into pieces
  • 100 g broccoli florets, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 250 g fresh mushrooms
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil (10 g)
  • 2 ½ - 3 tbsp honey (approx. 80 g)

Preparation

  1. Place ginger, garlic cloves and chilli (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Using a sharp knife, score duck skin in a criss-cross pattern. Rub duck all over with Chinese five-spice powder and salt, then place skin side down onto Varoma tray and set aside.
  3. Place water, stock paste and 40 g of the soy sauce into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, weigh rice into it and mix well using spatula to wet rice. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh pak choi and broccoli florets into it. Insert Varoma tray with duck breast fillets, secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, slice mushrooms and set aside.
  4. Set Varoma aside and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Reserve 100 g of the cooking liquid and discard the rest. Drain rice well and transfer into a serving bowl. Separate the rice grains with a fork, then cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm.
  5. Place sesame oil and reserved garlic-ginger mixture into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup. Meanwhile, pat the duck dry with a paper towel.
  6. Add reserved sliced mushrooms into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed 0.5, without measuring cup. Meanwhile, heat a small frying pan over high heat without oil, place duck breasts skin side down into hot pan and press down with a fish slice for 20 seconds to flatten. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 2-4 minutes or until skin is very crisp and most of the fat is rendered. Turn duck over and cook for a further 1-2 minutes. Transfer duck breasts, skin side up, into serving bowl with rice and cover to keep warm.
  7. Drain ¾ of the fat from the frying pan, leaving a little in the pan. Return frying pan to high heat and de-glaze by adding reserved steaming liquid, remaining 40 g soy sauce (2-3 tablespoons) and 2.5-3 tbsp honey. Allow to reduce for a few minutes, watching carefully to avoid burning, until it becomes a sticky glaze, then set aside.
  8. Add reserved pak choi and broccoli florets into mixing bowl and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Transfer into a serving dish. Transfer duck breasts onto a cutting board, slice diagonally and transfer to a serving dish containing vegetables. Drizzle duck with glaze and serve hot with rice.

Useful Items

kitchen towel, paper towel, frying pan, serving bowl, jug, small bowl, serving dish, fish slice, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make homemade five-spice powder place 2 teaspoons Sichuan peppercorns, 3 star anise, 2 teaspoons dried fennel seeds, 2 cinnamon sticks and 6 cloves into a clean and dry mixing bowl and dry-roast 5 min/Varoma//speed 1. Remove mixing bowl lid, set mixing bowl aside and allow to cool for 10 minutes, then mill 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mill a further 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable glass jar and store until ready to use.
  • Five-spice tofu with mushrooms, Asian vegetables and rice: for a vegetarian variation replace duck breast fillets with 500 g firm tofu, cut in slices (2 cm), and add a little oil when frying tofu in the pan.
