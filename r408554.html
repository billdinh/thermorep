Spiced beef casserole purée

Ingredients

  • boiling water
  • 200 g ripe tomatoes
  • 350 g gravy beef cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 20 g spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 70 g celery, cut into pieces
  • 120 g carrot, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 200 g sweet potato, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • &frac18; tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 15 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (see Tips)
  • filtered water, as needed

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh tomatoes into it. Remove tomato skins by using a sharp knife to cut a small cross in the base of each tomato. Carefully pour boiling water into bowl to cover tomatoes. Leave until skins split (approx. 1 minute). Remove from water and once cool enough to touch, peel away skins then cut flesh into quarters. Set tomatoes aside.
  2. Place beef into mixing bowl and Turbo/2 sec/0 times. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place spring onions/shallots, celery, carrot and sweet potato into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved beef, reserved tomatoes and garam masala, then cook 20 min/95°C/speed 1 or until beef and vegetables are cooked through.
  6. Add parsley and blend 45 sec/speed 1-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 9 or until smooth.
  7. Set aside 1 serving, then freeze the remainder in individual portions for future use. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, bowl, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Adding spice and herbs such as garam masala and fresh parsley to your baby's purées is a source of goodness and it also helps your baby become accustomed to new flavours. Exposure to a variety of tastes is particularly crucial in the first year of life when babies are typically more accepting of unfamiliar tastes.
  • Be sure to add the parsley leaves in handfuls, as individual leaves may not register on the scale. You can also replace the parsley with fresh coriander for variety.
