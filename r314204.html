Semolina pancakes (Beghrir)

Ingredients

Pancakes

  • 420 g milk
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 200 g fine semolina
  • 50 g plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • ½ tsp salt
  • sunflower oil, for frying

Honey and butter syrup

  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut in pieces
  • 100 g honey
  • 1 tsp floral water (rose water or orange blossom water)

Preparation

Pancakes

  1. Place milk, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and mix 2 min/37°C/speed 3.
  2. Add semolina, flour, egg and salt and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Set mixing bowl aside, with lid closed, and stand for 30 minutes.
  3. Place mixing bowl back into position and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer mixture into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add a little oil. Working in batches, pour in a ladle (approx. ¼ cup) of batter and cook until bubbles start bursting on pancake surface (approx. 1 minute), then flip and cook for just a few seconds. The pancakes will be smooth and golden on one side while porous and white on the other (18-20 cm). Transfer pancakes onto a piece of baking paper and cover to keep warm (don’t stack them while they cool). Makes approx. 6 pancakes in total.

Honey and butter syrup

  1. Place butter and honey into mixing bowl and melt 5 min/50°C/speed 3.
  2. Add floral water and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a serving jug and drizzle over pancakes to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, serving jug, non-stick frying pan, ladle, egg flip, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • These pancakes are sold on the streets of Morocco as a morning snack or made at home for breakfast. They are commonly known as "thousand holes pancakes".
  • These pancakes reheat well, so you can prepare them the day before. They can also be frozen; to reheat from frozen, place them into a hot oven to defrost and heat.
  • If no holes start appearing when baking the pancakes, it is most likely that the batter is too thick and/or the batter has not rested long enough. In this case, add 2 tablespoons warm water to mixture and stand for an extra 15 minutes before cooking.
