Tex-Mex BBQ Brisket

Ingredients

Dry Rub

  • 3 dried ancho chilies, de-seeded and de-stemmed
  • 2 dried chipotle chilies
  • 1 oz black peppercorns
  • ½ oz brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp mustard powder
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground cayenne pepper

Brisket

  • 64 - 80 oz beef brisket, untrimmed
  • 16 oz smoked bacon, sliced

BBQ Sauce

  • 6 oz fresh Roma tomatoes, quartered
  • 4 oz white onions, quartered
  • 3 ½ oz brown sugar
  • 3 canned chipotle chilies, in adobo sauce, to taste
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 1 oz Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 oz molasses
  • 1 heaping tbsp Dijon mustard
  • ¾ tsp salt, to taste

Preparation

Dry Rub

  1. Place dried ancho and chipotle chilies, peppercorns, brown sugar, onion powder, mustard powder, oregano, garlic powder, salt and cayenne into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Brisket

  1. Preheat oven to 275°F. Use approximately half the reserved dry rub and coat the brisket on all sides. Reserve remaining half for another use.
  2. Line the bottom of a baking pan (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.) with half the bacon (approx. 8 oz). Place the rubbed brisket, fattier side facing down, on top of the bacon, and layer the remaining bacon over the brisket. Cover with foil and bake for 4 hours (275°F).
  3. Remove brisket from oven and carefully flip brisket over. Cover with foil, return to the oven and bake a further 1 hour (275°F) until fork slides into meat with little resistance.
  4. Turn off oven. Remove the brisket and carefully remove and reserve the cooked bacon and drain off the fat into a bowl and set aside. Cover brisket with foil and place in oven to rest. Meanwhile make the sauce.

BBQ Sauce

  1. Place tomatoes, onion, brown sugar, chipotles, garlic, 4 oz reserved bacon and 2 oz reserved brisket/bacon drippings into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, molasses, mustard and salt and mix 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Insert measuring cup into mixing bowl lid and blend 15 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Lightly brush top of brisket with reserved BBQ sauce and place under broiler until the top is slightly crispy (approx. 5-7 min.) watching carefully to prevent any burning.
  4. Transfer brisket to a cutting board, brush with more BBQ sauce, and thinly slice against the grain. Serve immediately with more BBQ sauce.

Useful Items

aluminum foil, basting brush, cutting board, metal baking pan (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Smoked bacon provides the smoky flavor of traditionally smoked Texas brisket while saving you an additional 7-10 hours of smoking time. Bacon can be omitted if desired.
