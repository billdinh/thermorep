Turmeric fried chicken

Ingredients

  • 1000 - 1200 g chicken winglets (see Tips)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 165 g coconut milk
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 ½ tbsp ground turmeric
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh curry leaves (optional)
  • 80 g mirin
  • 3 tbsp Sriracha sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 1500 g vegetable oil, for frying

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chicken into it. Set aside. Place garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add coconut milk, soy sauce, coriander, chilli flakes, 1 teaspoon of the salt, 2 tablespoons of the turmeric and curry leaves (optional) and combine 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer into bowl with chicken and stir to combine. Cover and place into refrigerator overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh mirin into it. Add Sriracha sauce and brown sugar, then stir to combine and set aside.
  4. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a shallow bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour into it. Add remaining 1 teaspoon salt and remaining 2 teaspoons turmeric into bowl and stir to combine. Set aside.
  5. Place a large saucepan over medium heat and add enough oil to cover chicken. Bring oil to 165°C. To check oil temperature, drop a small cube of bread into oil; it should turn golden brown in 15-20 seconds. Keep your oil at this temperature while frying.
  6. Remove chicken winglets from marinade and coat in flour mixture. Working in batches, fry chicken for 15-20 minutes, turning until cooked, crispy and golden. Transfer onto prepared plate to drain. Serve warm with reserved Sriracha mixture.

Useful Items

bowl, large plate, paper towel, large saucepan, shallow bowl, plastic wrap, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Chicken winglets are smaller than chicken wings. At some stores, they are sold as chicken wing nibbles.
  • The amount of oil needed for frying will depend on the size of your saucepan. The oil should cover the chicken completely.
  • Chicken is great served by itself as a snack or starter. Serve it with a garden salad or steamed rice as part of a larger meal.
