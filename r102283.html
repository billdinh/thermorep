Milk tart

Ingredients

Base

  • 120 g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3-4 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 1 egg
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 180 g self-raising flour

Filling

  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 800 g full cream milk
  • 4 eggs
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 40 g cornflour
  • 200 g white sugar
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 2 ½ tsp cinnamon sugar (see Tips)

Preparation

Base

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a rectangular fluted tart tin (34 x 13 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place all base ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 7, until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Transfer mixture into prepared dish and press into base and sides. Prick base with a fork or pastry docker, then line pastry base with baking paper and fill with pie weights, rice or dried beans (this will prevent pastry from rising during baking). Place tart tin onto a baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (180°C). Remove baking paper and pie weights, rice or dried beans. Bake for a further 5-7 minutes (180°C), or until base is golden. Set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place cinnamon quill and 400 g of the milk into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/90°C//speed 1. Set aside to infuse for 10 minutes, then remove and discard quill.
  2. Add eggs, flours and sugar and blend 7 sec/speed 7
  3. Add vanilla, butter and remaining 400 g milk and cook 16 min/90°C/speed 4, until thick and glossy.
  4. Transfer mixture into cooled tart base. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar over tart.
  5. Cool slightly, then place into refrigerator for a minimum of 2 hours to set before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

rectangular fluted tart tin (34 x 13 cm), large baking tray, pie weights, rice or dried beans, baking paper, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own cinnamon sugar, combine 2 tsp white sugar and ½ tsp ground cinnamon in a small bowl.
  • This tart is traditionally made in a round fluted tart tin (25 cm).
