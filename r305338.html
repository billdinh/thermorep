Hong Kong-style roast pork belly

Ingredients

Five-spice powder

  • 2 tsp szechuan peppercorns
  • 3 star anise
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 cinnamon quills
  • 6 whole cloves

Roast pork belly

  • 1 tbsp caster sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1000 - 1500 g boneless pork belly, rind on
  • 2 tbsp sea salt flakes

Preparation

Five-spice powder

  1. Place peppercorns, star anise, fennel seeds, cinnamon quills and cloves into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/Varoma//speed 1. Set mixing bowl aside and allow to cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Place mixing bowl back into position and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Mill for a further 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable container and use as needed.

Roast pork belly

  1. Place sugar, salt and 2 tablespoons of the reserved five-spice powder into a bowl and stir well to combine.
  2. Rub spice mixture evenly over pork belly meat only (not the rind). Wrap pork belly (meat only) in aluminium foil, and transfer into a baking dish, with meat side down. Using a sharp knife, poke small, well-spaced holes in the rind. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 8 hours to marinate.
  3. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  4. Remove plastic wrap and sprinkle sea salt flakes evenly over rind, then roast for 30 minutes (220°C), keeping meat covered with aluminium foil. Remove from oven, scrape all salt off rind, then carefully poke more holes in the rind with a sharp knife.
  5. Return pork belly to oven and roast for 15-25 minutes (220°C) or until skin is crispy. Remove from oven and allow to cool to room temperature. Remove aluminium foil, then cut into pieces to serve.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, sharp knife, plastic wrap, bowl, baking dish, sealable storage container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can add other spices, such as black pepper, chilli powder, nutmeg or turmeric, to the five-spice powder as desired.
  • Sea salt absorbs the fat in the pork rind, to make it crispy when roasted.
