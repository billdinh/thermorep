Monkfish tagine with preserved lemon

Ingredients

  • 100 g onions, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10 g fresh ginger, thinly sliced
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded
  • 30 g fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 30 g fresh coriander, roughly cut, plus extra to garnish
  • 60 g olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juice only
  • 1 tsp salt, plus one pinch extra
  • 600 g monkfish fillet, skinless, boneless, cut into 4 pieces
  • 30 g fresh mint, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • 180 g carrots, cut into pieces (3 x 3 cm)
  • 100 g turnips, cut into pieces (3 x 3 cm)
  • 150 g potatoes, cut into pieces (3 x 3 cm)
  • 1 preserved lemon, cut into quarters (see tip)
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
  • 550 g water

Preparation

  1. Place onions, garlic, ginger, red chilli, parsley and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 40 g olive oil, lemon juice and salt and mix10 sec/speed 2. Transfer to a large bowl and add monkfish, mix with spatula to coat and allow to marinate for 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Place mint into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add carrots, turnip, potatoes, preserved lemon, salt and pepper, cumin, turmeric, nutmeg, cinnamon and olive oil to the mixing bowl and sauté 10 min/120°C//speed .
  5. Transfer contents of mixing bowl into simmering basket, reserving cooking liquid. Place water, marinade (without pieces of monkfish) and reserved cooking liquid into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with vegetables and cook 25 min/Varoma//speed .
  6. Place pieces of monkfish on Varoma tray. Place Varoma into position and continue steaming 8 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma and simmering basket, set aside and keep warm.
  7. Insert measuring cup and blend 30 sec/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually. Strain sauce using a sieve and adjust seasoning, if necessary. Transfer monkfish tagine to a serving dish, sprinkle with mint and fresh coriander leaves and serve with the sauce.

Useful Items

large bowl, serving dish, fine-mesh sieve, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For a more generous accompaniment, add some wheat semolina which you can cook in the Varoma dish.
  • Preserved lemon is a common condiment in North African cuisine and a key ingredient in many Moroccan dishes such as tagines. Whole lemons are pickled in a brine of water, lemon juice and salt to obtain a slightly tart but very lemony flavour. If preserved lemons cannot be found, a small lemon and ½ teaspoon salt may be used instead. The flavour will not be the same, though.
