Gruyère-Stuffed Salmon Patties with Salad

Ingredients

Gruyère-Stuffed Salmon Patties

  • 80 g white bread, cut in pieces
  • 15 g olive oil
  • 50 g butter, cut in pieces
  • 350 g floury potatoes, peeled, cut in cubes (1 cm)
  • 50 g water
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 30 g spring onions, cut in pieces
  • 350 g fresh salmon fillets, skinless, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 80 g Gruyère cheese, cut in 8 pieces

Salad

  • 120 g baby leaf salad, mixed
  • 100 g cherry tomatoes, cut in halves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 pinch freshly ground pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tbsp balsamic vinegar

Preparation

Gruyère-stuffed salmon patties

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place white bread in mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. Place olive oil, butter, potatoes, water, salt and black pepper in mixing bowl then cook 15 min/100°C/speed 1, and then mash 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place spring onions and salmon fillets in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 6.
  5. Add reserved mashed potatoes and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Spread the salmon mixture evenly on a plate, lightly score with a knife to divide into 8 and freeze for 10 minutes.
  6. Take ⅛ of the salmon mixture (approx. 60 g), push a piece of Gruyère cheese into it and shape into patties (2 cm thick). Coat with breadcrumbs and arrange on prepared baking tray. Repeat for remaining 7 patties. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile prepare the salad.

Salad

  1. Place a serving bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh baby leaf salad and cherry tomatoes into it and set aside. Season salad with salt, ground pepper, olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Serve the patties hot with the salad.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Make the patties ahead of time and store in the refrigerator for up to 4 hours, until ready to cook.
  • Try this recipe with a different cheese, such as cheddar, brie or dolcelatte.
