Meringue Ghosts

Ingredients

  • 270 g caster sugar
  • 100 g egg whites, at room temperature
  • &frac18; tsp fine sea salt
  • chocolate chips, to make ghost eyes
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
305 kJ / 73 kcal
Protein
1 g
Carbohydrates
17 g
Fat
0 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 135°C. Line 2 baking trays with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 7.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add egg whites and salt then, without measuring cup, whisk 10 min/37°C/speed 2 until thick and glossy.
  4. Gently transfer mixture to a large icing bag with the end cut (approx. 1 cm when lying flat). Pipe meringue into ghost shapes directly onto prepared baking trays, starting with a circle (approx. Ø 4 cm) and spiraling upwards to a point for the top of the ghost's head. Leave a 5 cm space between ghosts to allow for spreading. Carefully press two chocolate chip eyes into each meringue ghost.
  5. Bake for 40 minutes (135°C). Turn oven off immediately after 40 minutes and allow meringues to completely cool down inside closed oven (approx. 3 hours or overnight). Slide spatula under meringues to release and serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking tray (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm), piping bag, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can also garnish these with sprinkles.
  • Once cooled, dip bottoms of meringue ghosts in melted chocolate, gently scrape off excess chocolate and allow to set. Do not refrigerate as they will absorb moisture from the fridge and lose their crispness.
  • For best results, make sure mixing bowl, lid and butterfly whisk are thoroughly clean and dry before starting meringues. Any residual grease or moisture will prevent eggs from properly aerating. Eggs at room temperature provide better results.
  • Store Meringue Ghosts in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.
