Peanut butter gelato (Gelato Messina)

Ingredients

Peanut butter

  • 150 g roasted salted peanuts
  • 30 g peanut oil, to taste

Peanut butter gelato

  • 650 g full cream milk
  • 140 g caster sugar
  • 50 g skim milk powder

Peanut bark

  • 50 g roasted salted peanuts
  • 150 g dark chocolate chips

Preparation

Peanut butter

  1. Place peanuts into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add oil and mix 1 min/speed 5, until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Peanut butter gelato

  1. Without washing mixing bowl, place milk into mixing bowl and warm 5 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add sugar, milk powder and reserved peanut butter and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 2, then blend 10 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Pour mixture, up to 4 cm high, into shallow freezable containers or ice cube trays and cover with plastic wrap. Allow mixture to cool to room temperature before placing into freezer overnight.
  4. Transfer frozen gelato mixture into refrigerator for 15 minutes to soften slightly, then cut into pieces (4 x 4 cm). Place half of the gelato pieces into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4 with aid of spatula. Transfer into a freezable storage container.
  5. Place remaining half of the gelato pieces into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4 with aid of spatula. Transfer into freezable container with gelato and return to freezer for a minimum of 2 hours. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Peanut bark

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place peanuts into mixing bowl and crush 8 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Melt 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  5. Spread melted chocolate out thinly onto prepared baking tray. Sprinkle with 2 tablespoons of the reserved crushed peanuts. Place into refrigerator to set (approx. 10 minutes).
  6. Break bark into shards. Serve gelato with bark and sprinkle with remaining crushed peanuts.

Hints & Tricks

Thermomix® has been an integral part of the Gelato Messina kitchen for over six years. The multi-purpose technology enables chef Donato Toce and his team to experiment and create new pastries, fillings and gelati in a third of the time compared to conventional methods.

Messina's peanut butter gelato is by far one of their most popular flavours and also one of the easier ones to make. "Using peanut butter in gelato creates a velvety smooth, creamy mouthfeel and a rich nutty taste which melts in your mouth... there's nothing else quite like it!", says Toce.

This gelato recipe was adapted from Gelato Messina - The Recipes cookbook. You can find recipes for their cakes, mono-portions and soft serves in their latest book Gelato Messina: The Creative Department.
