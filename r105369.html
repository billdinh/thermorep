Sundried tomato hommus with crackers

Ingredients

Quinoa and spelt crackers

  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 100 g white quinoa
  • 100 g spelt flour
  • 100 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g water
  • 50 g macadamia oil
  • 25 g linseeds
  • 45 g sesame seeds
  • ½ tsp salt

Hommus

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (weight is pre-draining)
  • 50 - 60 g sun-dried tomatoes
  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for serving
  • 20 g lemon juice, (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 50 g tahini
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only, plus extra for serving
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch paprika, to serve

Preparation

Quinoa and spelt crackers

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place thyme, quinoa, spelt flour, plain flour, water and macadamia oil into mixing bowl and mix 6 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add seeds and salt and knead 2 min/.
  4. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured work surface and divide into 4 equal portions. Place 1 portion of dough onto a piece of baking paper and roll out to form a square (approx. 20 cm x 25 cm, 3 mm thick). Transfer baking paper with dough onto baking tray and score surface with pizza cutter to form squares (approx. 5 x 5 cm). Bake for 12-14 minutes (180°C), or until browned. Repeat rolling, scoring and baking with remaining 3 portions of dough. Cool crackers completely, then carefully break crackers apart along scored edges. Set aside in a sealable storage container until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Hommus

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining hommus ingredients, except paprika, and mix 20 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 6, or until desired consistency is achieved. Transfer into a serving bowl and garnish with extra basil leaves and paprika, then drizzle with extra olive oil. Serve with reserved Quinoa and spelt crackers. 

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), sealable storage container, rolling pin, baking paper, pizza cutter, serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Crackers can be stored in a sealable container in a cool, dark place.
  • Hommus can be stored in a sealable container in the refrigerator.
