Stuffed Brioche with Pork, Swiss Chard, Fennel and Cheese

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 ½ tsp caster sugar
  • 2 Tbsp milk
  • 15 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 1 Tbsp water, warm
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 3 medium eggs
  • 200 g unsalted butter, at room temperature, diced (1 cm)

Filling

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 50 g mozzarella cheese
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200 g pork belly, boneless, skinless, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 100 g fennel, thinly sliced
  • 200 g Swiss chard, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g water
  • 1 Tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped (optional)
  • ½ chicken stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Assembly

  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 80 g soft goat's cheese, crumbled
  • 1 egg
  • 1 Tbsp milk
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp sunflower seeds

Preparation

Dough

  1. Mix salt, sugar and milk in a small bowl until dissolved.
  2. Place yeast and water in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Add 50 g flour and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Add remaining flour and allow to sit for 15 minutes.
  4. Add salt, sugar and milk mixture then knead 4 min/. After 1 minute, add 2 eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid and continue kneading for the duration.
  5. Add a further egg then knead 7 min/. The dough should be soft and not break when stretched.
  6. Set 2 min/, then add butter gradually but quickly through hole in mixing bowl lid within the first minute. Transfer to a large bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1½ - 2½ hours). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place mozzarella in mixing bowl and shred 8 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add pork and oil then sauté 7 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Add fennel, Swiss chard, water, dill (if using), chicken stock cube, salt and pepper then, without measuring cup, cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Strain using simmering basket and allow to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. Knock back dough and, on a well-floured surface, roll a circle approx. 2 cm thick.
  2. Leaving a 5 cm border, sprinkle half the reserved Parmesan, top with crumbled goat's cheese, shredded mozzarella and reserved filling then top with remaining Parmesan.
  3. Bring edges together and seal. Turn upside down and place on a baking tray lined with baking paper then leave to prove for 2 hours. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven 180°C.
  4. Whisk egg and milk together in a small bowl then brush over top of bread. Scatter with sunflower seeds then bake for 35-40 minutes (180°C) until cooked and golden. Serve warm with salad.

Useful Items

small bowl, large bowl, cling film, rolling pin, baking tray and baking paper, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The juices from the strained pork and vegetable mixture make a good base for a delicious soup or stock.
  • When handling the brioche dough, generously flour your hands and work surface, working as quickly as possible.
  • The filled dough may be left in the fridge overnight. Remove from the fridge and prove for an hour before baking.
  • The pork belly can be left out for a meat-free version and ricotta can be used in place of goat's cheese for a milder flavour.
