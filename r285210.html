Lamb Korma

Ingredients

  • 160 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1000 g lamb shoulder meat, boneless, cubed (3-4 cm)
  • 30 g ghee
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 60 g tomato purée
  • 150 g plain yoghurt, plus extra for serving
  • fresh coriander leaves, for serving
  • cooked rice, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place 80 g onions, ginger, garlic, coriander, cumin, cardamom, chilli flakes and salt in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer marinade to a large bowl.
  2. Place bowl with marinade on mixing bowl lid and weigh in lamb. Stir well then cover with cling film and place in fridge to marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. After this time, place remaining 80 g onions and ghee in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add marinated lamb and, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 7 min/120°C//speed 0.5.
  5. Add garam masala, tomato purée and yoghurt then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 99 min/90°C//speed .
  6. Adjust seasoning to taste then transfer to serving bowls. Top with extra yoghurt and coriander leaves before serving with cooked rice.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • There are so many wonderful flavours associated with Indian cooking, and this mild korma is a great choice when cooking for a family. Slowly simmering the lamb in a creamy and slightly sweet sauce results in beautifully tender meat. Serve with rice, naan breads and your favourite Indian accompaniments.
