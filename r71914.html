Potted scrambled eggs with asparagus

Ingredients

  • oil, for greasing
  • 4 green asparagus
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 10 g butter
  • ½ tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 500 g water, plus 2 tbsp extra
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 2 eggs
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1122 kJ / 267 kcal
Protein
17 g
Carbohydrates
2 g
Fat
21 g

Preparation

  1. Grease a ramekin (10 - 15 cm) with oil and set aside.
  2. Trim asparagus ends, then remove spears from stalks and set aside for later use. Cut stalks into pieces. Place asparagus stalks and spring onion/shallot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.  Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and apple cider vinegar and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of water, white pepper, sea salt and eggs and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  5. Transfer egg mixture into prepared ramekin and gently arrange reserved asparagus spears on top. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place water into mixing bowl. Place ramekin into Varoma dish, then place Varoma into position and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve immediately in ramekin.

Useful Items

1 ramekin (10 - 15 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This also makes a great lunch served either hot or cold with a side salad.
  • Steam some whole eggs and vegetables in the Varoma dish, while you cook the potted scrambled eggs, to use in sandwich fillings or salads the next day.
  • Use the discarded ends of your asparagus to make your own vegetable stock paste. See The Basic Cookbook for the recipe.
  • Recipe for two: to serve this as a breakfast for two, cut the potted scrambled eggs in half and serve alongside smoked salmon or bacon, grilled tomatoes, mushrooms and a piece of your favourite bread lightly toasted.
