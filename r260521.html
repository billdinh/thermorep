Pork and gravy with red wine cabbage

Ingredients

  • 700 - 800 g pork scotch fillets (approx. 6 steaks)
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 ½ tsp salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 40 g pitted prunes, plus 3 extra prunes
  • 500 g red cabbage, cut into pieces
  • 50 g raisins
  • 250 g red wine
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 20 g red wine vinegar
  • 200 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 6 dried shiitake mushrooms or dried porcini mushrooms (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g water
  • 2 - 3 potatoes (approx. 300-350 g), peeled and cut into 3-4 cm pieces

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pork into it. Remove bowl and sprinkle both sides of the fillets with pepper, paprika and ½ teaspoon of the salt, then drizzle with oil. Cover and place into refrigerator to marinate while proceeding with recipe.
  2. Place 40 g of the prunes into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add cabbage and chop 10 sec/speed 4, mixing with aid of spatula.
  4. Add raisins, wine, butter, sugar, cloves and remaining 1 teaspoon salt and cook 25 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add vinegar and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1. Season to taste. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Rinse mixing bowl.
  6. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  7. Add tomato paste, mushrooms, remaining 3 prunes, stock paste and water. Place Varoma into position and divide marinated meat and potatoes between Varoma dish and Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and cook 35-40 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until pork and potatoes are cooked to your liking. Remove Varoma and set aside to keep warm.
  8. Blend sauce 30 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9. Serve pork with gravy, potatoes and cabbage.

Useful Items

large bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, plastic wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Dried shiitake mushrooms can be found in the international food aisle of most major supermarkets. Dried porcini mushrooms can be found at gourmet or specialty food stores; they are often more expensive than the dried shiitake variety, but they add an extra depth of flavour.
