Seafood Quesadillas

Ingredients

Quesadilla Filling

  • 8 oz mozzarella cheese, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 10 ½ oz fresh raw shrimp, peeled, deveined, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 10 ½ oz cooked octopus, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 10 ½ oz red snapper fillet, boneless, skinless, cut into pieces (1 in.)

Adobo

  • 1 ancho chili, de-stemmed, de-seeded
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • ¾ tsp chipotle chili powder
  • 1 whole clove
  • ¾ tsp ground cumin
  • ¾ tsp coriander seeds
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • 2 oz white onion, cut into pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 5 ½ oz fresh pineapple, cut into pieces
  • ½ oz apple cider vinegar
  • 1 oz orange juice
  • ½ oz vegetable oil
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Quesadillas

  • 12 flour tortillas, divided

Preparation

Quesadilla Filling

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place mozzarella cheese into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in raw shrimp, cooked octopus and red snapper. Set bowl with seafood aside.

Adobo

  1. Place ancho chili, paprika, cayenne pepper, chipotle powder, whole clove, cumin, coriander seeds and oregano into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onion, garlic, pineapple, apple cider vinegar, orange juice, oil, salt and pepper and blend 30 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with reserved seafood, cover with cling wrap, refrigerate and allow to marinate at least one hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F.

Quesadillas

  1. Place marinated seafood onto prepared baking sheet and bake for 25 minutes (350°F).
  2. Remove seafood from oven, transfer to a bowl and mix gently. Remove parchment from baking sheet.
  3. Place six tortillas onto baking sheet, spread reserved grated cheese evenly over tortillas, top with cooked seafood and cover with remaining tortillas.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes (350°F) or until golden brown.
  5. Cut Seafood Quesadillas into wedges and serve.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, cling wrap, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cook quesadillas over a medium-hot skillet or comal.
  • For a more developed flavor, marinate seafood for at least six hours.
  • Serve with Grilled Pineapple Salsa.
