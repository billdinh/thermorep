Lamb fillets with pink peppercorn sauce

Ingredients

  • 40 g butter, cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 2 eschalots (approx. 30 g), peeled and cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 220 g water, plus 1 tbsp extra to de-glaze frying pan
  • ½ tsp Meat stock paste, to taste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp pink peppercorns
  • 300 g small potatoes, unpeeled, washed and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 400 - 500 g boneless lamb loins (approx. 3-4 fillets)
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 1 bunch green asparagus (130-150 g), trimmed and cut into halves
  • 1 carrot, cut into matchsticks
  • 1 tbsp whisky
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 60 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

  1. Place butter, eschalots and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add water, stock paste and 1 tsp of the peppercorns to mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, then weigh potatoes into it and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, place a large frying pan over high heat and season lamb with salt and pepper. Once the pan is hot, sear lamb on both sides (approx. 1-2 minutes each side) until lightly browned.
  3. Transfer seared lamb into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray, then set Varoma aside. Place 1 tbsp water into hot frying pan and stir over heat to de-glaze the pan. Turn off heat and allow pan to cool on stove top.
  4. Arrange asparagus and carrot around seared lamb in Varoma dish and on Varoma tray, then place Varoma into position and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Lamb will be cooked medium-rare. If desired, continue cooking until lamb, asparagus, carrots and potatoes are cooked to your preference.
  5. Transfer lamb, asparagus, carrots and potatoes into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to rest and keep warm. The lamb will continue to cook as it rests. Retain stock in mixing bowl.
  6. In a ceramic or glass bowl, combine whisky and cornflour together, then set aside.
  7. Add remaining 1 tsp peppercorns, cream, reserved pan juices and reserved whisky and cornflour mixture to stock in mixing bowl and reduce 10-12 min/90°C//speed , without measuring cup.
  8. Slice lamb and serve with potatoes, carrots and asparagus, drizzled with pink peppercorn sauce.

Useful Items

frying pan, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, ceramic or glass bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Meat stock paste recipe.
  • Pink peppercorns can be sourced at specialty grocery stores.
