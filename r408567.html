Almond milk chocolate biscuits

Ingredients

Almond milk

  • 250 g raw almonds
  • 1000 g filtered water, plus extra for soaking
  • 5 pitted Medjool dates
  • ½ tsp salt

Chocolate biscuits

  • 80 g rolled oats
  • 100 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces
  • 90 g honey (see Tips)
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 tbsp raw cacao powder
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 2 - 3 tbsp dark chocolate chips (optional)

Preparation

Almond milk

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh almonds into it. Cover almonds with water and set aside to soak overnight. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse nuts.
  2. Place soaked almonds and dates into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add 500 g of the water and blend 25 sec/speed 8.
  4. Add salt and remaining 500 g water, then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 10 minutes to infuse.
  5. Using a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth, strain mixture into a large jug (squeezing firmly to extract all liquid). Transfer almond milk into a sterilised sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips). Transfer almond pulp into a bowl and set aside.

Chocolate biscuits

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place oats, butter and 350 g of the reserved almond pulp into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec//speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 15 sec//speed 3, until roughly combined.
  4. Add honey, eggs, 60 g of the reserved almond milk, raw cacao powder, cinnamon, cardamom and salt, then mix 30 sec//speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 10 sec//speed 5.
  6. Place tablespoons of biscuit mixture onto prepared baking tray (makes approx. 25 biscuits - see Tips). Lightly shape each biscuit and flatten into a round. Top each biscuit with 1-2 chocolate chips (optional).
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes (160°C) or until lightly golden but still a little soft. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, large jug, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, wire rack, bowl, sealable container, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Almonds and oats are thought to be galactagogues (foods that can help increase breastmilk supply), although the research is still inconclusive and individual results vary. But, if you're breastfeeding, you may find that these biscuits help boost your milk supply.
  • Honey is not suitable for children under 1 year of age.
  • These biscuits are a great way to use leftover almond milk pulp.
  • You can store the almond milk in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • In hot weather, transfer the biscuit mixture into the refrigerator for 15-30 minutes to chill and firm; it will make shaping the mixture much easier.
  • Dairy-free almond milk chocolate biscuits: to make the biscuits completely dairy free, replace the butter with virgin coconut oil. Proceed as per recipe.
