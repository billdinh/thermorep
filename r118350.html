Fruit mince pies

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 60 g blanched almonds
  • 60 g coconut flour (see Tips)
  • 80 g arrowroot flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 140 g buckwheat flour (see Tips)
  • 160 g unsalted butter, chilled
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 50 g water
  • 2 egg yolks

Filling

  • 1 Granny Smith apple, peeled, cored and cut into quarters
  • 40 g soft dark brown sugar
  • 100 g dried cranberries
  • 100 g raisins
  • 50 g currants
  • 30 g mixed peel
  • 40 g dried blueberries
  • ½ lemon, zest (no white pith) and juice
  • ½ orange, zest (no white pith) and juice
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 60 g brandy
  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • arrowroot flour, for dusting
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten, for brushing
  • pure icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add all remaining pastry ingredients, except water and egg yolks, and mix 6 sec/speed 6, or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Add water and egg yolks and knead 30 sec/ to form a dough.
  4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to rest for a minimum of 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place apple into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place all remaining filling ingredients, except butter, arrowroot flour, egg and icing sugar, into mixing bowl and Turbo/1 sec/5 times.
  3. Add reserved apple and combine 10 sec//speed 2.
  4. Transfer into a sealable jar or bottle and place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).
  5. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease 2 x 12-hole patty pan tins and set aside.
  6. Remove pastry from refrigerator and cut into 2 equal portions. Place 1 portion of dough between 2 sheets of baking paper and roll out thinly (3 mm thickness), dusting with arrowroot flour as needed to prevent sticking. Using a round scone or cookie cutter (7 cm), cut pastry into 24 discs, and use to line prepared trays.
  7. Place remaining dough between 2 sheets of baking paper and roll out thinly (3 mm thickness), dusting with arrowroot flour as needed to prevent sticking. Using a scone or cookie cutter (6 cm) for lids, cut out an additional 24 rounds.
  8. Spoon 2 heaping teaspoons of mince filling into each lined patty pan mould. Brush underneath of each lid with beaten egg and place over mince filling. Push down gently to seal edges. Transfer into refrigerator for 10 minutes. Brush lids with a little beaten egg and cut a small slit for the steam to escape. Bake for 15 minutes (180°C), or until golden.
  9. Leave tarts in tin to cool for 5 minutes. Dust with icing sugar and serve warm or cool completely and transfer into a sealable container until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, bowl, sealable jar or bottle, 2 x 12 hole patty pan tins, baking paper, rolling pin, round cookie cutter (7 cm), round cookie cutter (6 cm), pastry brush, sealable container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find coconut flour at health food stores, specialty stores and bulk food stores.
  • To make your own buckwheat flour, place 250 g buckwheat into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10.
  • You can make fruit mince up to 6 months in advance and store in a sealable jar or bottle in the refrigerator.
  • You can store pies in a sealable container for up to 3 days in a cool, dry place.
