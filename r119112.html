Steamed snapper, black beans and rice

Ingredients

  • 80 g fermented black beans (see Tips)
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for soaking beans
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 5 eschalots
  • 70 g piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into thin slices
  • 40 g peanut oil
  • 20 g light soy sauce
  • 40 g Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp ground white pepper
  • 350 g jasmine rice
  • 8 snapper fillets with skin on (approx. 120 g each)
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, white and light green part only, cut into batons (6 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh black beans into it. Cover with cold water and set aside to soak for 10 minutes. Drain and rinse beans, discarding soaking water. Pat beans dry with a paper towel.
  2. Place beans into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place garlic, eschalots and 20 g of the ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and cook 14 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved black beans, soy sauce, wine and pepper and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other small bowl and cover to keep warm.
  6. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear. Re-insert simmering basket with rice and steam 12 min/100°C/speed 4.
  7. Line Varoma dish with baking paper and spread remaining 50 g ginger across baking paper. Place snapper fillets on top of ginger, skin side down (if fish doesn't fit comfortably in Varoma dish, line Varoma tray with baking paper and some of the ginger slices. Place several of the fish fillets onto Varoma tray). Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 8 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  8. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  9. Place spring onions/shallots on top of fish fillets, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 2-4 min/Varoma/speed 2, until fish is cooked through.
  10. Divide rice between 8 bowls. Top with fish, spring onions/shallots, ginger and 1 tablespoon of the reserved black beans before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, paper towel, small bowl , thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, baking paper, 8 serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Fermented black beans are available in Chinese grocers; they are sometimes called 'douchi'.
  • Snapper can be replaced with any other firm white fish or prawn cutlets.
  • Peanut oil can be replaced with vegetable or coconut oil.
