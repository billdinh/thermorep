Cheese and Walnut Patties

Ingredients

Breadcrumbs

  • 130 g stale bread, torn in pieces
  • 15 g fresh coriander
  • 1 garlic clove

Dough

  • 250 g plain flour
  • 250 g water
  • 30 g butter
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing

Filling

  • 200 g shelled walnuts
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200 g curd cheese (quark)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • water, for brushing
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • oil, for frying (e.g. sunflower or groundnut)

Preparation

Breadcrumbs

  1. Place all breadcrumb ingredients in mixing bowl then grate 15 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Dough

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in flour then set aside.
  2. Place water, butter, salt and oil in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease a bowl.
  3. Add reserved flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to prepared bowl and set aside to cool. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place nuts in mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil, onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add curd cheese, reserved nuts, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Tip cooled dough out onto a work surface and roll out to 1-2 mm thick. Using a cutter (Ø 10 cm), cut out 40 circles of dough, re-rolling any trimmings as necessary.
  5. Place 1 tsp filling in centre of each circle then brush rim of each circle with water. Fold over patty, encasing filling and removing air. Brush patties with a beaten egg then coat in reserved breadcrumbs and set aside.
  6. Heat some oil in a large frying pan over a medium-high heat. Shallow fry patties for 2-3 minutes until cooked through and golden brown, adding oil as necessary. Drain on paper towel to remove excess oil. Serve hot or cold.

Useful Items

rolling pin, pastry brush, frying pan, tongs, paper towel, round pastry cutter (Ø 10 cm), hob
