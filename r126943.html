Fish fingers with tomato sauce

Ingredients

  • 200 g beer
  • 120 g flour
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp dried yeast
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • 500 g cod fillets, skinless and boneless, cut into sticks (2 x 6 cm) (see tip)
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 400 g tomato passata
  • ½ tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1 pinch baking soda
  • 300 g oil for frying

Preparation

  1. Place beer, flour, garlic, parsley, yeast and ½ teaspoon of salt into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer batter to a bowl and add cod fillets. Cover well with batter and let rest for 10 minutes. Clean the mixing bowl.
  2. Place olive oil, tomato, salt, sugar and bicarbonate of soda into mixing bowl. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid to facilitate evaporation and cook 15 min/120°C/speed . Meanwhile, heat oil in a non-stick pan and fry the fish fingers on each side until golden. Drain fish fingers on kitchen paper. Serve immediately with the tomato sauce.

Useful Items

large bowl, paper towels, non-stick frying pan

Hints & Tricks

  • The batter can also be used to make fried onion rings: cut an onion into rings (5 mm), cover well in the mixture and fry in hot oil until golden.
  • Instead of cod, any other type of white fish can be used to prepare fish fingers.
