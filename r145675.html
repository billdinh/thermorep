Dairy free coconut caramel ice cream with crêpes

Ingredients

Ice cream

  • 500 g oat milk
  • 200 g coconut cream
  • 100 g coconut sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp arrowroot flour
  • 1 pinch salt

Crêpes

  • 250 g plain flour
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 60 g vegetable oil
  • 500 g water (see Tips)
  • pure maple syrup, to serve

Preparation

Ice cream

  1. Place all ice cream ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 7 min/80°C/speed 4. Transfer into a freezable container and set aside to cool (approx. 1 hour). Transfer into freezer for approx. 6 hours or until frozen. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Crêpes

  1. Place all crêpe ingredients, except maple syrup, into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4 or until smooth (see Tips).
  2. Place a non-stick frying pan (19 cm) over medium heat. Working in batches, ladle ⅓ cup of the batter into frying pan and swirl to thinly cover base of pan. Cook until edges start to lift away from pan (approx 2-3 minutes), then flip and cook other side until golden (approx. 2-3 minutes). Transfer onto a plate and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm (makes approx. 10 pancakes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Remove ice cream from freezer and cut into pieces (4 x 4 cm). Place frozen cubes into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 9.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 20 sec/speed 8, with aid of spatula.
  3. Serve ice cream with crêpes and drizzle with maple syrup.

Useful Items

freezable storage container, large plate, non-stick frying pan (19 cm), ladle, kitchen towel, freezer, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store batter in the refrigerator for up to 1 day until ready to use.
  • You can replace the water in the pancakes with any milk of choice (oat, rice, almond, etc).
