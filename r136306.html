Sabji (vegetable) curry

Ingredients

Everyday Indian curry paste

  • 30 g cumin seeds
  • 2 - 3 dried bay leaves
  • 30 g coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp whole cloves
  • 12 cardamom pods (black)
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 5 brown onions, cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 40 g canola oil
  • 30 fresh curry leaves
  • 800 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt

Sabji

  • 170 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 30 g cashews
  • 2 fresh tomatoes, cut into quarters and deseeded (approx. 100 g)
  • 200 g water
  • 200 g potato, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 200 g sweet potato, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g coconut cream
  • 2 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • fresh long red chillies, cut into thin slices, for garnishing

Preparation

Everyday Indian curry paste

  1. Place cumin seeds, bay leaves, coriander seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon quill and peppercorns into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, then mill 1 min/speed 9, until a powder.
  2. Add chilli powder, ground ginger, turmeric, onion and fresh chillies (optional) and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee, oil and curry leaves and sauté 6 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes, sugar, stock paste and salt and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 20 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7 .
  5. Set aside to cool then use as needed, or transfer into sealable containers or snaplock bags and store until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sabji

  1. Place spinach into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place cashews into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add tomatoes and 200 g of the reserved curry paste and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add water, potato, carrot and sweet potato and carefully stir with aid of spatula then cook 10 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  5. Add coconut cream, garam masala, salt, pepper and reserved chopped spinach and stir with aid of spatula, then cook 5 min/100°C//speed . Transfer into a serving dish and garnish with chillies and serve with rice and condiment of choice.

Useful Items

snaplock bag, bowl, sealable container, freezeable containers, small bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can store the Everyday Indian curry paste in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 6 weeks.
  • We served ours with pilau rice and cucumber raita, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip, but you can serve with any side and condiment of your choice.
