Steak Provençal Pie

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 12 shallots (round)
  • 700 g braising steak, diced (3 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 piece orange zest (10 cm)
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 Tbsp plain flour
  • 250 g red wine
  • 200 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 100 g fresh portobello mushrooms, diced
  • 1 red pepper, diced (2 cm)
  • 25 g pitted black olives, halved
  • 400 g puff pastry, ready-made (see tip)
  • 1 medium egg, beaten

Preparation

  1. Place garlic and oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add shallots and sauté 5 min/100°C/speed 0.5. Meanwhile, heat a little oil in a frying pan over a high heat and brown beef, in batches if necessary.
  3. Add browned meat and juices, thyme, bay leaf, orange zest, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec//speed 0.5.
  4. Add flour and stir 2 min/100°C//speed 0.5.
  5. Add wine and, without measuring cup, simmer 2 min/100°C//speed 0.5.
  6. Add tomatoes and mushrooms then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 30 min/98°C//speed .
  7. Add red pepper and olives then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 30 min/98°C//speed . Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  8. Roll out pastry to form a thick lid, large enough to cover a large pie dish. Transfer pie filling to pie dish.
  9. Wet rim of pie dish then lay over pastry 'lid' and seal around the edges. Brush with beaten egg and cut 2 slits in the centre for steam to escape. Bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C) until golden.
  10. Leave to rest for 5 minutes before serving warm.

Useful Items

frying pan, pie dish, pastry brush, rolling pin, hob, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A Provençal twist on a British classic, this hearty and comforting dish makes a great alternative to a Sunday roast and will be loved by all. Flaky pastry sits atop a rich meat pie, bursting with the Mediterranean flavours of black olives, red peppers, and a hint of orange.
  • To make your own puff pastry, follow the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
  • Serve with mashed potatoes and steamed green vegetables for a complete meal.
