Turkey Burgers with Caramelised Onion and Pepper

Ingredients

Turkey burgers

  • 60 g wholemeal bread (approx. 2 slices), roughly torn
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (approx. 10 cm long), leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh sage leaves
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 90 g brown onion, cut in halves
  • 500 g turkey mince
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Caramelised onion and pepper

  • 500 g brown onion (approx. 3-4), cut in eighths, layers separated
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and cut in thin strips (1 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 40 g butter, cut in pieces (1-2 cm)
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • olive oil, for frying

Assembly

  • 6 bread rolls
  • 150 g Swiss cheese, thinly sliced
  • 50 g rocket leaves

Preparation

Turkey burgers

  1. Place bread and herbs in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onion in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add reserved bread-herb mixture, turkey mince, egg, sea salt and pepper and mix 5 sec/speed 5. With damp hands, shape mixture into 6 patties, then transfer to a plate, cover and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Caramelised onion and pepper

  1. Place all caramelised onion ingredients, except olive oil, in mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed .
  2. Cook for a further 10 min/Varoma//speed , without measuring cup.
  3. Place a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over medium-heat and add a little olive oil. Cook patties until golden brown on either side (approx. 3-4 minutes each side) or until cooked to your preference.

Assembly

  1. Cut bread rolls in half and toast, if desired. Top each roll with a cooked burger patty, a slice of cheese, some caramelised onion and pepper and finish with rocket to serve.

Useful Items

bread knife, cling film, non-stick frying pan, turner / fish slice

Hints & Tricks

  • To make this an even faster meal, fry your turkey burgers on the hob while the caramelised onion and pepper finishes cooking in step 5.
  • To mince your own meat: Cut partially frozen meat in pieces (2-3 cm), place in mixing bowl and mince 8-15 sec/speed 6-7, or until desired consistency is achieved. Use minced meat immediately or place in fridge until ready to use. For a chunkier consistency, chop Turbo/1.0 sec/3-5 times.
  • This process can be used with any kind of red meat, seafood or poultry. Chopping time will vary according to the type and weight of meat used.
  • Chicken patties: replace turkey mince with chicken mince.
