Ricotta and banana pancakes with honeycomb butter

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Honeycomb butter

  • 100 g honeycomb, (e.g. Crunchie® or Violet Crumble®), broken into pieces
  • 600 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 500 g chilled water
  • 2 tbsp honey

Ricotta and banana pancakes

  • 75 g unsalted butter, plus extra for frying
  • 40 g caster sugar
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 500 - 570 g full cream milk
  • 450 g ripe bananas, thinly sliced

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Honeycomb butter

  1. Place honeycomb chocolate into mixing bowl and crush 7 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip 1-3 min/speed 4, or until solids and liquids have separated. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Place simmering basket over large bowl and strain liquid from solids, reserving buttermilk (liquid portion) to use in other recipes if desired (see Tips).
  4. Place water and butter solids into mixing bowl and mix 5-10 sec/speed 4. Liquid and solids should again separate.
  5. Place simmering basket over bowl and strain liquid from solids a second time, so that only butter remains. Squeeze as much liquid out of the butter as possible.
  6. Place butter into mixing bowl. Add honey and reserved crushed honeycomb and combine 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ricotta and banana pancakes

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1.
  2. Add sugar, baking powder, eggs, flour, milk, 160 g of the bananas and 200 g of the reserved ricotta and combine 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat. Place a little of the extra butter into frying pan and allow to melt. Pour 2 tbsp pancake batter into pan and cook for approx. 3 minutes, or until bubbles form on top of pancake. Turn pancake and cook for a further 1-2 minutes, or until browned. Transfer into a Thermoserver or other plate and cover to keep warm.
  5. Repeat with remaining batter to make approx. 24 pancakes, mixing the batter occasionally with spatula between batches. Serve pancakes warm, topped with honeycomb butter and remaining banana slices.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, bowl, sealable container, medium-sized non-stick frying pan, egg flip, ThermoServer or large bowl, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe will produce approx. 250 g buttermilk after draining in step 2. Try using it in scones, breads or even soups. Buttermilk can also be frozen for use in future recipes.
  • Use the extra ricotta in blintzes or smoked salmon fritters.
