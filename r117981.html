Ingredients

Tzatziki dip

  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, unpeeled, sliced lengthways and deseeded
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 300 g Greek yoghurt (see Tips)
  • lemon juice, to taste
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Pita bread

  • 200 g rice flour (see Tips)
  • 150 g brown rice flour (see Tips)
  • 100 g potato starch
  • 60 g arrowroot flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 500 g warm water
  • 2 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 tsp pure maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 2 tbsp psyllium husk (see Tips)

Lamb kleftiko

  • 750 g boneless lamb shoulder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ lemon, juice only (approx. 20 g)
  • 2 tbsp white wine
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp sumac (optional)
  • 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled and crushed
  • 2500 g water

Preparation

Tzatziki dip

  1. Place cucumber and salt into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into simmering basket and set aside to drain (approx. 10 minutes). Squeeze excess liquid from cucumber and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place garlic and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add all remaining dip ingredients, including reserved cucumber and combine 15 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pita bread

  1. Place rice flour, brown rice flour, potato starch, arrowroot, xanthan gum and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water, yeast and syrup into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 2.
  3. Add oil and psyllium husk and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add reserved flour mixture and mix 30 sec/speed 4 until combined. Dough should be slightly sticky. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat and wrap in mat or transfer into a ceramic or glass bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Set aside to prove for 40 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  6. On a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, divide dough into 8 equal balls (approx. 120 g each). Shaping 1 ball at a time, gently push the dough out from the middle to form a circle (approx. 15-17 cm, 3mm thickness). Transfer onto prepared baking trays, drizzle with oil and bake for 7 minutes (200°C). Remove baking tray and turn over pitas, return to oven and bake a further 5 minutes (200°C) or until just browned. Repeat process until all pitas are baked. Allow to cool, then cut into halves and carefully slice horizontally to form a pocket. Cover and set aside.

Lamb kleftiko

  1. Place a double sheet of aluminium foil (big enough to cover lamb leg) into Varoma dish, then layer with a sheet of baking paper. Place lamb onto baking paper and drizzle with olive oil, lemon juice, wine, salt, pepper, oregano and sumac (optional). Add garlic cloves, then seal baking paper and aluminium foil to form a parcel.
  2. Place 1500 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish with lamb into position, then secure Varoma lid and steam 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Remove Varoma and add another 500 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam for a further 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Remove Varoma and add remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam for a further 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, reheat pitas and transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a plate and cover to keep warm.
  5. Remove Varoma. Carefully open parcel, remove lamb leg and shred meat into pieces using forks.
  6. Fill warm pita pockets with lamb and Tzatziki dip (see Tips).

Useful Items

kitchen towel, ceramic or glass bowl, sealable container, silicone bread mat, bowl, plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, aluminium foil, thermal serving bowl or plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Strain yoghurt overnight through a fine-mesh sieve lined with paper towel or a nut milk bag to get a thick and creamy tzatziki dip.
  • To make your own rice flour, place 250 g rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10. Use as needed.
  • You can buy psyllium husks from most supermarkets and health food stores.
  • To save time, you can make the pita bread 1 day in advance. Store in a sealable container in a cool, dark place. Reheat before serving, if desired.
  • Serve with a fresh Greek salad.
