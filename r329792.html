'Egg and sausage' breakfast sandwich

Ingredients

  • 500 g firm tofu, pressed and cut into 4 slices horizontally (approx. 0.5-1 cm thick - see Tips)

"Sausage" patties

  • 75 g rolled oats
  • ½ red onion (approx. 80 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 apple (approx. 100 g), cored and cut into halves
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp dried marjoram
  • 120 g fresh Swiss brown mushrooms, cut into quarters
  • 40 g puffed brown rice
  • 1 - 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for frying

Yolk

  • 10 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 150 g water
  • ¼ tsp mustard (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • 10 g tapioca starch
  • 1 pinch kala namak (black salt) (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground turmeric

Assembly

  • 4 English wholemeal muffins, toasted (see Tips)
  • 4 slices vegan cheese
  • kala namak (black salt), to season
  • ground black pepper, to season

Preparation

  1. Place tofu onto a chopping board. Using a round cookie cutter (9 cm), cut a circle into each slice. Using a smaller cookie cutter (4 cm), cut out a small circle in the centre of each larger circle, to make a hole for the "yolk". Set the large tofu circles aside and the off-cuts can be reserved for other recipes (see Tips).

"Sausage" patties

  1. Place oats into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add onion, garlic, apple, tomato paste, tamari, nutritional yeast, salt, pepper, marjoram and mushrooms, then chop 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add puffed rice and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Using damp hands, divide mixture into 4 patties (mixture will be quite wet, but will thicken during cooking). Transfer onto a plate and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add 1-2 tablespoons of the oil. Working in batches, fry patties for 5 minutes on each side. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a plate and cover to keep warm.
  5. If the pan appears dry, add a little more oil, then fry reserved large tofu rounds for 2-4 minutes on each side or until cooked to your liking. Transfer into the thermal serving bowl or onto the plate with patties and cover to keep warm.

Yolk

  1. Place all yolk ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/90°C//speed 4.

Assembly

  1. Place bases of English muffins onto serving plates. Top each base with 1 slice of vegan cheese, 1 "sausage" patty and 1 slice of fried tofu. Spoon 1 tablespoon of the yolk mixture into the hole in each tofu slice, seasoning the tofu with kala namak and pepper. Top with muffin lids and serve with sauce of choice.

Useful Items

large non-stick frying pan, thermal serving bowl or plate, plate, chopping board, serving plates, round cookie cutter (9 cm), round cookie cutter (4 cm), stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Tofu is often packaged in water and it is best to press it before cooking to achieve a better result. To press tofu, use a tofu press or line a plate with paper towels. Place tofu block on the paper towels and cover with another layer of paper towel. Place a plate or a cutting board on top of the paper towels, then place heavy weights (books or cans) on top. Set aside for several hours until liquid has been pressed out.
  • We recommend using organic tofu whenever possible.
  • Use the tofu off-cuts in our Leek quiche or Blue cheese tofu dressing (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip).
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
  • We used Australian mustard in this recipe but any mild yellow mustard can be used.
  • Kala namak, also known as Himalayan black salt, is a pungent smelling salt typically used in South Asian cuisine (and by vegans as it mimics the flavour and aroma of eggs). It can be purchased at health food stores, Indian food stores or online.
  • To make your own English muffins, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
