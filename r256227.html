Sweet tamarind fish with mixed vegetables

Ingredients

  • 180 g basmati rice
  • 1150 g water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 carrot, cut into thin slices (approx. 5mm)
  • ½ red capsicum, cut into strips
  • 1 broccoli (approx. 200 g), cut into florets
  • 4 firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces (6 cm - see Tips)
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 eschalots
  • 1 - 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves, to taste
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, stalks and roots, plus extra leaves for garnishing
  • 40 g tamarind paste
  • 30 g fish sauce
  • 45 g palm sugar
  • 2 tsp cornflour

Preparation

  1. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Rinse rice until water runs clear. Place 1000 g of the water and salt into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket with rice. Place Varoma dish into positon and place carrot, capsicum and broccoli into it. Insert Varoma tray and place fish onto it. Secure Varoma lid, then steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 4. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, then transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  2. Place Varoma with fish and vegetables back into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 4-5 min/Varoma/speed 4, until vegetables and fish are cooked to your liking. Transfer into a separate thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place garlic, eschalots, chillies and coriander stalks and roots (see Tips) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, cornflour and remaining 150 g water and cook 4 min/120°C/speed 1 or until slightly thickened. Drain any excess liquid from reserved fish and vegetables, then cover with sauce.
  5. Divide reserved rice between serving bowls and top with fish and vegetables. Garnish with coriander leaves before serving.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • For the best results, use fresh fish fillets. Cooking time may vary according to type and thickness of fillets.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside before adding to the mixing bowl.
