Sicilian ricotta cheesecake with botrytis soaked figs

Ingredients

Botrytis soaked figs

  • 150 g botrytis semillon
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, cut into half lengthways, seeds scraped
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 150 g dried figs

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Shortcrust pastry

  • 100 g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 230 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 50 g icing sugar
  • 3 egg yolks

Cheesecake filling

  • 80 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), broken into pieces
  • 40 g raw hazelnuts, toasted
  • 150 g cream cheese
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 100 g sour cream
  • 2 eggs
  • 40 g mixed peel
  • 40 g brandy

Preparation

Botrytis soaked figs

  1. Place all ingredients (including vanilla bean pod) into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a sealable container and set aside overnight to infuse figs. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into lined simmering basket and allow to drain for 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Shortcrust pastry

  1. Grease base and sides of a fluted tart tin (23 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place butter, flour, salt and icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6, or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Knead 30 sec/, adding egg yolks one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 300 g of the pastry into it. Shape pastry into a tight ball. Wrap pastry in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum 15 minutes.
  5. Shape remaining pastry (approx. 140 g) into a tight ball, wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator or freezer to use in a future recipe.
  6. Place rested pastry (300 g) onto a silicone bread mat or between 2 pieces of baking paper and roll out into a circle (minimum 24 cm). Line base and sides of prepared tart tin with pastry, removing any excess pastry from edges with a sharp knife. Place into refrigerator to rest for 15 minutes.

Cheesecake filling

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Prick pastry base with a fork to prevent air bubbles. Line pastry base with baking paper and fill with pie weights, rice or dried beans. Place tart tin onto a baking tray and bake for 10 minutes (180°C).
  3. Remove pie weights, rice or dried beans and baking paper. Bake for a further 5-10 minutes (180°C) or until pastry is light golden. Set aside to cool. Reduce oven temperature to 160°C.
  4. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add all remaining ingredients, including 250 g of the reserved ricotta and mix 5 sec//speed 5.
  6. Transfer ricotta mixture into pastry case and smooth surface with the back of a spoon. Bake for 30-40 minutes (160°C), or until filling is just set. Turn off oven and leave cheesecake to cool in oven with door ajar for 40 minutes. Remove from oven and leave to cool completely. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum 2 hours or until ready to serve.
  7. Remove star anise, cinnamon quill and vanilla bean pod from syrup and discard. Place cheesecake onto serving platter and drizzle with soaked figs and syrup before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • Botrytis semillon is a type of sweet dessert wine. If you are unable to find botrytis semillon, use 150 g brandy in step 1.
  • Store extra ricotta in the refrigerator. Stir through pasta dishes, use it to fill crepes or add to the batter for savoury fritters.
  • Use the extra pastry to make individual apple pies or mini tart shells for sweet canapes.
