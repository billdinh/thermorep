Prawn Tempura Rolls

Ingredients

  • 20 large raw prawns, peeled
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 250 g sushi rice, rinsed well
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar, plus 2 Tbsp for brushing
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 tsp sriracha sauce
  • 3 Tbsp mayonnaise (kewpie)
  • sunflower oil, for frying
  • 140 g plain flour
  • 1 Tbsp cornflour
  • 260 g sparkling mineral water
  • 4 sheets nori seaweed
  • 2 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 fresh jalapeño chillies, cut in matchsticks
  • ¼ cucumber, cut in matchsticks, deseeded
  • ½ avocado, thinly sliced (2 mm)
  • soy sauce, for dipping

Preparation

  1. Slice tiny slits along the inside of each prawn then stretch straight and freeze for at least 1 hour.
  2. Insert simmering basket. Place water in mixing bowl. Weigh rice into simmering basket then steam 16 min/Varoma/speed 4. Meanwhile, place vinegar, sugar and salt in a small bowl and stir until grains are dissolved then set aside. Place mayonnaise and sriracha sauce in a small bowl, mix together and set aside.
  3. Leave simmering basket in position for a couple of minutes to drain, remove with aid of spatula and transfer steamed rice to a large non-metal shallow dish, then sprinkle vinegar mixture all over. Using a soft spatula, toss gently to distribute vinegar mixture then leave to cool. Discard steaming water then heat oil at 170°C in a deep fat fryer or saucepan.
  4. When oil has nearly reached temperature, place flour, cornflour, sparkling water and ½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer batter to a bowl then once oil is hot, in batches, dip frozen prawns in batter then fry in hot oil for 1 minute. Remove from oil then place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  5. Wrap a bamboo sushi mat in cling film. Place a sheet of nori, shiny side down on bamboo sushi mat then gently spread ¼ prepared sushi rice over nori sheet with a wet, soft spatula (see tip), leaving ⅓ clear at the far end. Sprinkle ½ Tbsp toasted sesame seeds over rice then, very carefully, flip nori sheet over so that it is rice side down on mat and the clear end is nearest to you.
  6. At the end of nori sheet closest to you, spread 2 tsp chilli mayonnaise in a line 1 cm from the edge. Lay 5 tempura prawns along chilli mayonnaise, then lay a line of jalapeño chilli, cucumber and avocado beside prawns. Using bamboo sushi mat, begin rolling tightly to form a roll. Before reaching the other end brush edge with vinegar-water (see tip) to help seal, then continue rolling tightly. Set finished roll aside then continue assembling remaining rolls.
  7. Slice each roll into 1.5 cm pieces using a wet, sharp knife then serve with soy sauce and remaining chilli mayonnaise, for dipping.

Useful Items

shallow bowl or dish, deep saucepan or deep fat fryer, paper towels, cling film, pastry brush, sharp knife, bamboo sushi mat, silicone spatula, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Many people think sushi means raw fish, but it is actually a reference to vinegared rice combined with various ingredients.
  • Place 200 g water and 2 Tbsp rice vinegar in a bowl to use for brushing and keeping hands and utensils wet so they don't stick to rice.
  • Traditionally sushi rice should be body temperature rather than chilled, therefore for optimum quality, sushi is best served immediately or within a few hours of making. Wrap rolls in cling film and leave in a cool dry place then slice just before serving.
  • Sushi can be wrapped in cling film and refrigerated. Consume within 24 hours but be mindful that the quality decreases quickly due to the rice losing moisture.
