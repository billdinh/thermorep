Yule Log Cake

Ingredients

Cake

  • 4 egg whites, from large eggs, (approx. 5 oz), room temperature
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp cream of tartar
  • 4 tbsp confectioners sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 5 ½ oz ultrafine sugar
  • 2 oz unsweetened cocoa powder (approx. 6 heaping tbsp)
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • &frac18; tsp salt

Assembly

  • 1 tbsp confectioners sugar
  • non-stick cooking spray

Frosting

  • 16 oz confectioners sugar
  • 6 oz unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 2 oz unsweetened cocoa powder (approx. 6 heaping tbsp)
  • 4 oz whole milk
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • confectioners sugar (approx. 2 tbsp)

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and line a baking pan with parchment, also grease top of parchment, leaving an overhang at ends and sides.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 4 egg whites, cornstarch and cream of tartar into mixing bowl and without measuring cup whip 5 min/100°F/speed 3. Mix 1 min/120°F/speed 3 gradually adding 4 tbsp confectioners sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk and transfer batter to a bowl that has been warmed, cover with cling wrap and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 2 eggs and 4 egg yolks into mixing bowl. Insert measuring cup and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Add sugar and mix a further 30 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add cocoa, vanilla and salt and increasing speed gradually mix 30 sec/speed 1-3. Scrape down side of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 30 sec/speed 3. Add 6 heaping tbsp (approx. 2 oz) of reserved whipped egg whites and mix 5 sec/speed 2. Gently fold in remaining reserved whipped egg whites by using spatula to gently rotate the butterfly whisk until thoroughly combined. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer batter to prepared baking pan, evenly distributing to all corners of the pan. Bake for 22-25 minutes (350°F). Meanwhile, wash and dry butterfly whisk.

Assembly

  1. Once removed from oven sprinkle cake with 1 tbsp confectioners sugar using a sieve. Lay a clean, linen kitchen towel onto cake then place a cooling rack upside down on the towel and gently invert the baking pan to remove cake. Allow cake to cool for 5 minutes then carefully remove parchment and spray cake with non-stick cooking spray. Carefully draw kitchen towel with cake off the cooling rack and place on a solid surface. Starting at short edge carefully roll the cake up with the kitchen towel. Transfer rolled cake back on the cooling rack seam side down and allow cake to cool (approx. 30-40 min.) before unwrapping and adding frosting. Make frosting while cake cools.

Frosting

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place confectioners sugar, butter, cocoa, milk and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer frosting to an airtight container and set aside until cake has cooled.
  2. Carefully unroll cake and spread with half the reserved frosting. Reroll cake (without kitchen towel) and place cake seam side down on a serving plate. Cut ends off sponge roll (approx. ½ in.) and place one section on top of the log at one end to form a knot, cut the other round in half and place both halves on top of each other at side of log to form a stub or cut-off branch. Cover entire log with remaining frosting with an icing spatula. Mark lines in the frosting with tines of a fork, to resemble wood grain. Cover with cling wrap and refrigerate. Dust with confectioners sugar just before serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, icing spatula, serving plate, baking pan (12 in. x 17 in. x 1.5 in.), wire rack, cling wrap, stainless-steel bowl, linen kitchen towel, fine mesh sieve, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To replace sugar frosting with a cream frosting: Insert butterfly whisk. Place 16 oz heavy whipping cream, 4 oz sugar, 4 oz unsweetened cocoa and 1 tsp vanilla extract into mixing bowl and without setting a time whip speed 3, until desired stiffness is achieved, while watching carefully to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk and store cream frosting in an airtight container in the refrigerator until needed.
  • Halve sugar frosting and cream frosting recipe if using cream frosting inside cake and sugar frosting on the outside. Alternatively halve cream or sugar frosting and use to fill inside of cake and cover outside of log with confectioners sugar, sprinkling with a sieve.
  • Cake is done when it springs back when lightly touched.
  • Bring cake to room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.
  • Make 2 cakes and join together to make it look like a real log, and decorate.
  • Do not get any egg yolk in whites that are being whipped.
  • The mixing bowl, blade and butterfly whisk should be free from any grease or fat, wash and dry thoroughly before use.
