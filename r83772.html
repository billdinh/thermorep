Dundee Cake

Ingredients

  • 220 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 120 g blanched almonds
  • 100 g currants
  • 100 g raisins
  • 100 g mixed candied peel
  • 75 g glacé cherries, halved
  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • 270 g plain flour
  • 220 g light brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 Tbsp whisky

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Grease a cake tin (Ø 20 cm) and line base and sides with a double layer of baking paper.
  2. Place 60 g blanched almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in currants, raisins, mixed peel, cherries and 20 g flour. Stir together with spatula until evenly coated then set aside.
  4. Place lemon peel in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  5. Add butter and sugar then cream 20 sec/speed 6.
  6. Add eggs, reserved ground almonds, mixed spice, baking powder, whisky and remaining 250 g flour then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 15 sec/speed 6.
  7. Add reserved floured fruit and mix 20 sec//speed 4. Transfer to prepared tin and indent the centre slightly to allow for rising. Arrange remaining 60 g blanched almonds in concentric circles on the top. Bake for 45 minutes (150°C) then cover with foil to prevent top from browning too much. Bake for a further 1¼-1½ hours (150°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean.
  8. Leave to cool in tin for 20 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve or wrap in greaseproof paper and foil and allow to mature for 1-2 weeks.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, large bowl, aluminium foil, skewer, wire rack, greaseproof paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This famously Scottish, traditional fruit cake is a great recipe to make ahead, as leaving the finished cake for a few days or weeks helps the flavour to mature. Not as rich as the fruit cake we associate with Christmas, this cake has a lighter, more crumbly sponge and is best enjoyed with a cup of tea.
