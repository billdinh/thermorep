Smoky chicken thighs

Ingredients

  • 400 g green tomatoes (unripe), cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 3 chipotle chillies (see Tips)
  • 3 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 300 g hot water
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 50 g sesame seeds
  • 100 g raw peanuts
  • 50 g pitted prunes
  • 2 tbsp dried Mexican oregano or Mediterranean oregano
  • 1 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste
  • 500 g water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, for frying
  • 8 chicken thighs, bone in and skin on
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp salt flakes
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a roasting or baking dish (30 cm).
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh tomatoes into it.
  3. Place a large, heavy-based frying pan (26 cm) over medium high heat and add chillies, garlic cloves and tomatoes and cook until slightly blackened all over, turning over as required (approx. 5 minutes).
  4. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh hot water into it and set aside.
  5. Remove chillies from frying pan, discard stems and place chillies into hot water. Set tomatoes and garlic aside. Peel skin from garlic once cool.
  6. Place cumin, fennel seeds, peppercorns, sesame seeds and peanuts into mixing bowl and toast 10 min/100°C/speed 1, then mill 20 sec/speed 9.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 25 sec/speed 9.
  8. Add prunes and oregano and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2.
  9. Add cocoa, stock paste, water, reserved tomatoes, peeled garlic and chillies (with soaking water) and blend 5 sec/speed 5, then cook 15 min/100°C/speed 2. Blend 30 sec/speed 1 -10 , gradually increasing speed from speed 1 to speed 10.
  10. Place a large, heavy-based frying pan (26 cm) over medium-high heat and add oil. Add chicken thighs and brown on both sides to a deep golden colour (approx. 2-3 minutes each side). Transfer chicken into prepared baking dish in one layer, skin side up. Sprinkle with ground coriander and salt. Pour reserved chilli sauce into dish, leaving top of chicken exposed. Cover with aluminium foil and bake for 30 minutes (180°C). Remove aluminium foil, increase oven temperature to 190°C and bake for a further 15 minutes (190°C).
  11. Scatter with coriander leaves before serving.

Useful Items

baking dish (30 x 23 x 5 cm), bowl, heavy-based frying pan (26 cm), ceramic or glass bowl, aluminium foil, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Tomatillos (fresh or canned) would traditionally be used in place of green (unripe) tomatoes, but can be difficult to source. If neither tomatillos or green tomatoes are available, use red tomatoes and add the juice of half a lemon with the chicken stock in the recipe.
  • Chipotle chillies are smoked, dried jalapeños. If they cannot be found, replace with 2 teaspoons smoked paprika and add with cocoa.
