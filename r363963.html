Feijoa jelly

Ingredients

  • 1000 g feijoas, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, cut into thin slices
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 400 g water
  • 500 - 700 g jam setting sugar, according to amount of syrup

Preparation

  1. Place feijoas, ginger, orange zest, lemon zest and water into mixing bowl and cook 15-18 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until feijoas are completely soft.
  2. Line simmering basket with nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths and place over a bowl. Transfer feijoa mixture into lined simmering basket and set aside to drain for a minimum of 2 hours (do not squeeze). Rinse mixing bowl well.
  3. Weigh strained syrup into mixing bowl. For every 250 g of syrup, add 200 g of jam setting sugar (if your feijoas are not as ripe, you may need more sugar). Add lemon juice, then cook 60 min/100°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Skim top of jelly and discard any skin. Test setting point of jelly (see Tips).
  4. If necessary, cook for a further 2 min/100°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Allow jelly to cool slightly, then transfer into sterilised sealable jars and seal tightly. Set aside to cool to room temperature, then place into refrigerator overnight to set. Serve as desired or store in the refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).

Useful Items

nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, sterilised sealable jars, plate, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If using frozen feijoas, defrost completely and drain off any excess moisture before using.
  • Setting point test: place a small plate into the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 teaspoon jelly onto the chilled plate. When jelly is cool, push it with your finger. If the jelly wrinkles, it is ready. If not, cook for a further 2 minutes, then test again.
  • This recipe makes a large portion of jelly (945 g), so you will need several sealable jars for storing.
  • Serve with roast pork, with crackers as part of a cheese board, on scones or toast.
  • You can store jelly in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
  • Save any leftover pulp after straining to add to smoothies or porridge, or to serve alongside yoghurt.
