Polenta Squares with Gorgonzola and Porcini - Quadrotti di polenta con Gorgonzola e porcini

Ingredients

  • 40 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • water, boiling, for soaking
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1100 g water
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 1 Tbsp fine sea salt
  • 500 g polenta
  • 200 g Gorgonzola, diced (1 cm)
  • 15 g unsalted butter, diced

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line 2 square cake tins (20 cm) with baking paper. Soak porcini mushrooms in a bowl of boiling water for 15 minutes.
  2. Place Parmesan and garlic in mixing bowl then grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place 1000 g water, stock cube and salt in mixing bowl then bring to the boil 7 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Set to speed 2 then slowly weigh in polenta through hole in mixing bowl lid. Replace measuring cup and cook time indicated on packet/100°C/speed 4.
  5. Add remaining 100 g water to mixing bowl, stir with spatula then cook again 2 min/100°C/speed 4.
  6. Add reserved grated Parmesan and garlic, 100 g Gorgonzola and butter then, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid, mix 2 min/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl lid, stir with spatula then mix again 30 sec/speed 4.
  7. Immediately transfer to prepared tins, level surface (see tip) then bake for 25 minutes (200°C).
  8. Remove from oven, top with remaining 100 g Gorgonzola and drained porcini mushrooms then bake for a further 5-10 minutes (200°C) until cheese is melted. Leave for 5 minutes before removing from tins and cutting into squares (2 cm). Serve warm.

Useful Items

square cake tins (20 cm), baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Originating in northern Italy, polenta was a staple food of the poor providing a simple yet satisfying dish for the working classes. Historically it was made from grains such as farro and millet, while the classic yellow polenta we know today came about with the introduction of corn in the 16th century. In this recipe, the flavoured, cooked polenta is topped with earthy porcini mushrooms and Gorgonzola cheese, then cut into bite-sized pieces for canapés or antipasti.
  • Dip a soft spatula in hot water and use to flatten and smooth polenta out in the tins.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese and Gorgonzola for a vegetarian blue cheese.
