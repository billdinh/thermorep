Apple and Maple Syrup Bacon Scones

Ingredients

  • 180 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 160 g plain flour
  • 160 g wholemeal flour
  • 1 Tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • &frac18; tsp ground nutmeg
  • &frac18; tsp ground cloves
  • 120 g apples, peeled, cored, diced (5 mm)
  • 50 g maple syrup
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 slices cooked bacon, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 50 g milk
  • Demerara sugar, for sprinkling (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place 65 g butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/70°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place plain flour, wholemeal flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg and cloves in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  3. Add 115 g butter and pulse Turbo/1 sec/3-4 times until fine breadcrumbs form.
  4. Add apples and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add maple syrup, eggs and reserved melted butter then stir 10-15 sec//speed 3 until mixed well.
  6. Add bacon and stir in with spatula.
  7. Line a baking tray with baking paper then tip dough out onto it. Shape into a circle (Ø 12-15 cm), cut circle into 8 wedges and pull apart slightly. Freeze for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 220°C.
  8. Remove tray from freezer. Brush tops of dough with milk and sprinkle with sugar (if using). Bake for 15 minutes (220°C) or until golden.
  9. Leave on tray to cool for 5 minutes before serving warm.

Useful Items

pastry brush, baking tray, baking paper, freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Inspired by the classic American pairing of bacon and maple syrup, these sweet and savoury scones will soon become a family favourite. Serve warm from the oven topped with a little butter.
