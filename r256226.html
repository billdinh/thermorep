Spinach and rocket salad with cream cheese dressing

Ingredients

  • 500 g water
  • 4 whole eggs (see Tips)
  • 125 g fresh baby corn, cut into slices on the diagonal (2 cm)
  • 1 avocado, flesh only
  • cold water
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 5 sprigs fresh chives, cut into pieces (3 cm), plus extra for garnishing
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 - 3 tsp white wine vinegar, to taste
  • 100 g soft cream cheese (see Tips)
  • 2 pinches nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 60 g rocket leaves
  • 250 g mini Roma tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts, toasted

Preparation

  1. Place 400 g of the water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and place eggs into it. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh corn into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 4 or until eggs are cooked to your liking. Meanwhile, cut avocado into bite-size pieces. Place into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer eggs into a bowl with cold water. Rinse mixing bowl with cold water and dry.
  3. Place garlic and chives into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil, vinegar, cream cheese, nutmeg, salt, pepper and remaining 100 g water and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. When eggs are cool enough to handle, peel and cut into wedges. Place a serving platter onto mixing bowl lid and weigh spinach and rocket onto it. Top with eggs, steamed baby corn, tomatoes, avocado and pine nuts. Top with cream cheese dressing and garnish with extra chives to serve.

Useful Items

serving platter, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • We used X-Large eggs (59 g); using room temperature eggs or smaller eggs will reduce the total cooking time. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for timings for both soft and hard boiled eggs. If you prefer soft boiled eggs, take care when peeling soft boiled eggs.
  • Be sure to use a soft cream cheese for the dressing. Using a firm cream cheese will make dressing too thick and dressing may not combine properly. If needed, add more water for a thinner consistency if desired.
