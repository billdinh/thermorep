Rice crackers

Ingredients

Furikake seasoning

  • 70 g sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 nori sheets, each torn into approx. 10 pieces
  • 2 tbsp Bonito flakes

Crackers

  • 130 g white rice
  • 40 g cooked rice, cooled (see Tips)
  • 40 g vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp tamari
  • 70 g water

Glaze

  • 2 tsp tamari
  • 2 tsp mirin

Preparation

Furikake seasoning

  1. Place all Furikake ingredients into mixing bowl and toast 12 min/Varoma/speed 1 , without measuring cup, then chop 15 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Crackers

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place uncooked rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10.
  3. Add cooked rice, vegetable oil and tamari and mix 30 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 30 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it. Set bowl aside.
  6. Add 2 tablespoons of the reserved furikake seasoning and knead 1 min/, gradually adding water through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  7. Using your fingertips, press teaspoon-sized amounts of mixture into thin rounds (approx. 2 mm thickness). Place onto prepared baking tray and bake for 12 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, prepare glaze.

Glaze

  1. Place tamari and mirin into a small bowl and stir to combine.
  2. Allow crackers to cool on tray for 2 minutes, then brush with glaze. Serve once cooled.

Useful Items

small bowl , baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, pastry brush, sealable container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe works best using leftover cooked rice. Cooked rice can be stored in the freezer and defrosted before adding to cracker mixture.
  • Store cooled crackers in a sealable storage container.
  • Store remaining Furikake seasoning in a sealable jar or container and use as a seasoning on rice, soups, salads, grills, eggs...just about anything.
