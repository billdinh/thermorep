Caramelised fennel and ricotta tarts with Parmesan crisps

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1500 g full cream milk
  • 40 g white vinegar
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith, grated

Shortcrust pastry

  • 200 g plain flour
  • 100 g butter, cut into pieces and chilled
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • 50 - 60 g chilled water

Filling

  • 20 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g fennel, trimmed, core removed and cut into quarters, reserve fronds for garnishing
  • 1 tbsp raw sugar
  • 30 g apple cider vinegar
  • 30 g fresh silverbeet, leaves only, stalks removed, leaves thinly sliced
  • 12 pitted green olives, cut into slices (optional)
  • 2 eggs
  • 30 g full cream milk
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • ½ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Parmesan crisps

  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 2 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 1 minute. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with two layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for at least 1 hour. Set drained ricotta (approx. 170 g) aside in a bowl and stir through lemon zest.

Shortcrust pastry

  1. Place flour, butter and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6, or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Add water and knead 10-20 sec/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a tight ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to rest for at least 15 minutes.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease 8 fluted tart tins (8-10 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place fennel into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter, sugar and vinegar and cook 12 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh silverbeet into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook 2 min 30 sec/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma, transfer silverbeet into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Roll out pastry to line the base and sides of prepared tart tins and prick bases with a fork or pastry docker to prevent any air bubbles from rising. Line bases with baking paper and fill with rice, dried beans or pie weights (this will stop the pastry base from rising during baking).
  6. Place tart tins onto a baking tray and blind bake for 15 minutes (180°C). Remove pastry cases from oven, then remove baking paper with rice, dried beans or pie weights.
  7. Evenly distribute fennel mixture, reserved silverbeet, reserved ricotta and olives (optional) between pastry cases.
  8. Place all remaining filling ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Carefully spoon mixture evenly over each tart, then bake tarts for 15 - 20 minutes (180°C) or until just set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Parmesan crisps

  1. While tarts are baking, line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place fennel seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 2 min/120°C/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl (approx. 10 minutes).
  3. Add Parmesan and grate 15 sec/speed 9.
  4. Spread mixture thinly over prepared baking tray, then bake for 7 - 10 minutes (180°C) until golden and bubbling. Allow to cool for 5 minutes before roughly breaking into crisps.
  5. Serve tarts warm, garnished with reserved fennel fronds and Parmesan crisps.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, rolling pin, baking paper, pie weights, rice or dried beans, layers muslin cloth, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), tartlet tins (Ø 10 cm), bowl, silicone bread mat, refrigerator, oven
