Silverbeet quiche with oat and Parmesan crust

Ingredients

Oat and Parmesan crust

  • butter, for greasing
  • 70 g raw almonds
  • 40 g sesame seeds
  • 150 g rolled oats
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • 80 g water

Sliverbeet filling

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 80 g red onion, cut into wedges (1 cm), segments separated
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 90 - 100 g fresh portobello mushrooms (approx. 1 large), cut into halves and then cut into slices (0.5-1 cm)
  • 120 g fresh silverbeet, leaves only, cut into thin strips
  • 4 eggs
  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 3 pinches sea salt
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper
  • 70 g goat's cheese

Preparation

Oat and Parmesan crust

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a round fluted tart tin (24 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place almonds, sesame seeds, oats, Parmesan and salt into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add water and combine 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and allow to sit for approx. 5-10 minutes. Mixture will be sticky at first, but should dry out slightly after 5-10 minutes. Once pastry is dry enough to work with, press into base and sides of tin to form a crust. Bake for 7 minutes (180°C) then remove crust from oven and set aside. Leave oven temperature at 180°C.

Sliverbeet filling

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onion and olive oil and cook 4 min/100°C//speed .
  3. Add thyme and mushrooms and cook 4 min/100°C//speed .
  4. Add silverbeet and cook 2 min/100°C//speed , or until silverbeet has wilted.
  5. Transfer into simmering basket to drain excess liquid from filling then set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place eggs, cream, nutmeg, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 3.
  7. Evenly spread reserved silverbeet mixture over crust and pour egg mixture over. Crumble goat's cheese over egg mixture and bake for 25 minutes (180°C) or until golden and firm on top.
  8. Allow to cool slightly, then slice and serve.

Useful Items

tart tin (24 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve quiche with a fresh green garden salad.
