Fillet of Beef, Mushroom and Stilton Rarebit Tarts with Celeriac Purée

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 70 g salted butter, cold, diced
  • 140 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 40 g water, chilled

Mushroom Duxelle

  • 1 shallot, halved
  • 10 g butter
  • 200 g chestnut mushrooms, halved
  • 3 - 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 5 g truffle oil

Stilton Rarebit

  • 40 g beer
  • 75 g Stilton, cut in pieces
  • 100 g Cheddar cheese, extra mature, cut in pieces
  • 30 g breadcrumbs
  • 10 g plain flour
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 2 tsp English mustard
  • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce

Celeriac Purée

  • 600 - 700 g celeriac (1 whole celeriac), peeled , cut in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 380 g whipping cream
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

  • 6 pieces beef fillet (175-200 g each)
  • olive oil, for brushing
  • fine sea salt, for seasoning steak
  • ground black pepper, for seasoning steak
  • 2 Tbsp fresh parsley, leaves only, chopped, for garnish

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place butter and flour in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add water then pulse Turbo/1 sec/2-4 times until just clinging together. Tip out, form into a ball then wrap in cling film and chill for 30 minutes.
  3. Roll out pastry on a lightly floured surface and use to line 6 tartlet tins (Ø 8-9 cm). Prick bases with a fork, then return to fridge for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C. Line pastry shells with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C) until baking paper can be removed easily. Remove beans and paper then bake for a further 10 minutes (180°C) until golden.

Mushroom Duxelle

  1. Place shallot and butter in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add mushrooms then sauté 7 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add parsley, thyme, salt and pepper then blend 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and drizzle with truffle oil. Clean mixing bowl.

Stilton Rarebit

  1. Place beer, Stilton and Cheddar in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/90°C/speed 4.
  2. Add breadcrumbs, flour, egg, egg yolk, mustard and Worcestershire sauce then cook 5 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl to cool. Clean mixing bowl.

Celeriac Purée

  1. Place celeriac, cream, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then cook 20 min/90°C/speed 4. Meanwhile, divide reserved mushroom duxelle between pastry cases then spread a heaped tablespoon of reserved rarebit on top of each. Keep purée warm after cooking.
  2. Bake tartlets for 10-15 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Meanwhile, place steaks on a baking tray. Brush all over with a little olive oil then season with 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch black pepper per side. Roast (see tip) for 4-6 minutes (220°C), turning once, until cooked to taste, then cover with foil and rest for a minimum of 5 minutes. To serve, carve each fillet and serve on a plate with a tartlet and some celeriac purée scattered with chopped parsley.

Useful Items

cling film, small tartlet tins (Ø 9 cm), baking beans, baking paper, rolling pin, aluminium foil, baking tray, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have a double oven, preheat one to 180°C and one to 220°C, otherwise you can cook the steaks at the top, and tartlets in the lower part of the same oven.
  • Steaks may be pan-fried if preferred.
  • For a smooth celeriac purée, blend 1 min/speed 9 after step 9.
