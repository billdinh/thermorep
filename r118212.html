Berry and red wine trifle

Ingredients

Jelly

  • 270 g water
  • 5 tsp gelatine powder
  • 450 g red wine
  • 270 g apple juice
  • 120 g caster sugar

Sponge

  • butter, for greasing
  • 220 g raw sugar
  • 6 eggs, room temperature
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 ½ tsp cream of tartar
  • 130 g gluten free cornflour

Custard

  • 100 g raw sugar
  • 40 g gluten free cornflour
  • 3 eggs
  • 800 g full cream milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Assembly

  • 150 g blackberry jam
  • 50 g flaked almonds, toasted
  • 400 g fresh mixed berries
  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

Jelly

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it. Add gelatine and set aside to soak for 5 minutes or until spongy.
  2. Place wine, apple juice and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/100°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Add reserved gelatine mixture and mix 30 sec/50°C/speed 2, or until dissolved. Transfer mixture into a bowl and set aside to cool completely to room temperature (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a cake tin (20 x 30 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add eggs and vanilla and whisk 15 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Add bicarbonate of soda, cream of tartar and cornflour and combine 50 sec//speed 1. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and bake for 30 minutes (180°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the sponge comes out clean. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  5. Leave sponge to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely. Once cake has cooled, cut into 30 fingers (2 x 10 cm) and set aside.

Custard

  1. Place  sugar and cornflour into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add eggs, milk and vanilla and cook 10 min/90°C/speed 4, or until thickened. Transfer into a bowl. Cover surface with baking paper and set aside to cool for 10 minutes, then place into refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Line a glass trifle bowl (20 cm) with one-third of the sponge fingers and spoon over one-third of the jelly (approx. 400 g). Set aside to soak for 5 minutes.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh half of the blackberry jam (75 g) into it. Spoon jam over sponge. Sprinkle with one-third of the almonds and one-quarter of the mixed berries.
  3. Place cold custard into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5, or until smooth. Spread half of the custard over berries.
  4. Layer with another one-third of the sponge, one-third (400 g) of the jelly, remaining 75 g jam, one-third of the almonds, one-quarter of the berries and remaining half of the custard. Top with remaining sponge, remaining jelly and one-quarter of the berries. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3.5, watching cream carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid over-whipping. Spread cream over trifle and decorate with remaining berries and almonds. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill (minimum of 30 minutes or overnight). Serve chilled.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, bowl, cake tin (20 x 30 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, trifle dish, plastic wrap, oven, refrigerator
