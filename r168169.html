Wholemeal bread with green olives and za'atar

Ingredients

  • 150 g water
  • 90 g olive oil
  • 50 g honey
  • 3 tsp dried yeast
  • 270 g wholemeal flour
  • 250 g white flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 3 tsp za'atar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 60 g green olives, pitted, coarsley chopped (approx. 20 olives)
  • 1 tbsp water

Preparation

  1. Place water, oil, honey and yeast into bowl and mix 1 min/37°C/speed 3.
  2. Add wholemeal flour, white flour, 2 eggs and salt and knead 2 min/.
  3. Add za’atar and olives and knead 1 min/. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and divide into 3 pieces and roll each piece into a rope (approx. 40 cm x 4 cm). Join the 3 ropes at one end, plait keeping the braid tight, and join again at the other end. Place plait onto prepared baking tray, tuck pinched ends at both sides of the bread under the loaf to keep them secured, cover with cling film or a damp tea towel and allow to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 h). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  5. Brush plait with an egg whisked with 1 tbsp water, sprinkle with za’atar and bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or until brown and bread sounds hollow when tapped on the underside. Allow to cool on a cooling rack before slicing and serve.

Useful Items

cooling rack, baking tray and paper, cling film or tea towels, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Make two smaller loaves by dividing the dough into two and then follow the directions in step 5. For individual bread rolls divide the dough into 10-12 pieces. Bake the two smaller breads for 30-35 minutes and bake the rolls for 15-20 minutes.
