Leftover Roast Bao Buns with Hoisin Sauce

Ingredients

Hoisin Sauce

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g soy sauce
  • 150 g smooth peanut butter
  • 80 g dark brown sugar
  • 40 g brown rice vinegar
  • 50 g sesame oil
  • 2 tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • ¼ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Bao Buns

  • 250 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch caster sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp milk
  • 125 g water
  • groundnut oil, for greasing

Assembly

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 spring onions (approx. 30 g), cut in 3 pieces
  • 20 g soy sauce
  • 30 g sesame oil
  • 300 - 400 g leftover roast lamb, chicken, pork or beef, cut in pieces
  • 3 spring onions, sliced in thin batons (3 cm)
  • ½ cucumber, sliced in thin batons (3 cm)
  • coriander leaves, for garnishing
  • 1 fresh red chilli, sliced (5 mm)
  • sesame seeds, for garnishing

Preparation

Hoisin Sauce

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add remaining hoisin sauce ingredients and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and place in fridge until needed. Clean mixing bowl.

Bao Buns

  1. Place flour, yeast, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then blend 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add milk and 125 g water then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil. Tip out dough, shape into a ball, then place in prepared bowl. Cover with cling film then leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 40 minutes).
  3. Tip risen dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide into 12 balls, approx. 30 g each. Place on 2 greased baking trays with enough space between to double in size. Cover lightly with oiled cling film and leave until doubled in size (approx. 20-30 minutes). Meanwhile, cut baking paper into 12 squares (approx. 8 cm).
  4. Roll each ball of dough into an oval 10 cm long. Place a square of baking paper onto each oval and fold buns over to create semi-circles. Place buns back on trays, lightly cover with oiled cling film and leave to prove for another 10-15 minutes until risen.
  5. Place 1500 g water in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease Varoma dish and tray.
  6. Place 3 buns in prepared Varoma dish and 3 in Varoma tray, leaving space for them to puff up. Place covered Varoma into position then steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Steam remaining buns in batches, adding more water as necessary. Keep buns warm after steaming.

Assembly

  1. Place garlic, spring onions, soy sauce and sesame oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add meat and ½ reserved hoisin sauce then heat 10 min/90°C//speed 1.
  3. Serve bao buns filled with roasted meat mixture, remaining hoisin sauce, and garnishes of spring onions, cucumber, coriander, chilli, and sesame seeds.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, baking trays, rolling pin, baking paper, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Any leftover roasted or barbecued meat will work well in this recipe.
  • These bao buns are steamed in the Varoma, resulting in soft, fluffy buns that you can fill with leftover meat from a roast dinner or barbecue.
  • For a vegetarian version, replace the roasted meat with mushrooms. Simply heat 1 Tbsp vegetable or frying oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat, then sauté 300-400 g thinly sliced mushrooms until cooked. Omit the meat in Step 9 and add mushrooms to mixing bowl, then continue with recipe.
