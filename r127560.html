Curried sausages and lentils

Ingredients

  • ½ tsp dried fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 piece fresh ginger (2 cm), peeled
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 - 2 long red chillies, deseeded if preferred
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 2 celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 100 g red pepper, deseeded and cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (see tip)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 900 g water
  • 300 g dried French green lentils (see tip)
  • 500 g raw sausage (e.g. beef or pork)
  • 270 g coconut cream
  • 60 g peanut butter
  • 150 g broccoli, broken into florets (3 cm)
  • 150 g cauliflower, broken into florets (3 cm)
  • 100 g fresh green beans, trimmed and cut into thirds

Preparation

  1. Place fennel, cumin and coriander seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1, then mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add ground turmeric, ginger, garlic, chillies and coriander and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add olive oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add carrot, celery and capsicum and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add soy sauce, tamarind paste, oyster sauce, brown sugar, water and lentils and cook 20 min/Varoma//speed .
  6. Place Varoma into position, insert Varoma tray, then weigh sausages onto it. Close Varoma lid and cook 15-20 min/Varoma//speed , or until sausages are cooked through. Remove Varoma tray and set sausages aside to cool.
  7. Add coconut cream and peanut butter to mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, then weigh broccoli, cauliflower and green beans into it. Close Varoma lid and steam 10-12 min/Varoma//speed , or until vegetables are cooked.
  8. Remove Varoma and set aside. Transfer curried lentils and steamed vegetables into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Peel casings from reserved cool sausages and cut into slices (1-2 cm). Stir sausage slices through lentil and vegetable mixture then serve.

Hints & Tricks

  • Tamarind paste: The tropical tamarind fruit grows in the shape of long, leathery pods that resemble beans. The brown, sticky pulp inside the pods is processed to a concentrated paste or purée and sold in jars. The tangy fruit adds an acidic accent to foods and sauces. If you can't find tamarind paste, use the same quantity of lime or lemon juice instead.
  • French green lentils or Puy lentils remain firm after cooking and have a rich flavor. They cook a bit slower than other lentils. Puy lentis can be replaced by beluga lentils or brown lentils (these won't hold their shape as well as French green lentils).
  • Coconut cream is similar to coconut milk but has a thicker, paste-like consistency. If you can't find coconut cream, use coconut milk instead. The lentil curry will then be more liquid.
  • Vegetarian option: omit the sauages and increase vegetables by 500 g. Steam additional vegetables on the Varoma tray in step 7 and adjust cooking time accordingly. You may want to rotate your vegetables in the Varoma dish and tray half-way through cooking time to ensure even cooking.
