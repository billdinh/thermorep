Sardines with tomato couscous

Ingredients

  • 8 fresh whole sardines, cleaned inside and out and deboned (see Tips)
  • 1 lemon, cut into 8 slices and each slice cut into halves
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • 200 g vine-ripened cherry tomatoes
  • 180 g couscous, rinsed and drained
  • 180 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 ½ tbsp oil
  • 330 g boiling water
  • 1 tsp raw sugar
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 100 g pitted black olives, cut into halves
  • 30 g caperberries
  • 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 130 g water
  • mixed leaf salad, to serve

Preparation

  1. Rinse sardines and pat dry with a paper towel. Cut 4 pieces of baking paper (30 x 40 cm). Place 2 sardines onto each piece of baking paper. Place 2 lemon slices into each fish. Season each pair of fish with 1 pinch of salt. Fold up baking paper into parcels to enclose fish. Place 2 parcels, seam side up, into Varoma dish and 2 parcels, seam side up, onto Varoma tray. Place tomatoes on the vine around parcels in Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Place a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh couscous into it. Set aside.
  3. Place onion, garlic and 1 tablespoon of the oil into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 15 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Place a large jug onto mixing bowl lid and carefully weigh boiling water into it. Pour water over couscous, add salt and ½ teaspoon of the oil and mix with aid of spatula. Spread couscous out into an even layer, cover and allow water to fully absorb (approx. 15 minutes).
  6. Add sugar, pepper, chilli flakes, olives, caperberries, canned tomatoes and water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until sardines are cooked. Remove Varoma and set aside. Pour sauce over couscous and fluff with a fork. Serve couscous with sardines, vine-ripened cherry tomatoes and salad.

Useful Items

paper towel, baking paper, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, jug

Hints & Tricks

  • Ask your fish monger to clean and debone (and fillet, if desired) the fresh sardines for you.
  • If you are unable to find fresh sardines, use tinnned sardines instead (tinned sardines do not require any cooking - simply steam vine-ripened tomatoes on their own in the Varoma dish). Serve tinned sardines with couscous, tomatoes and salad at the end of the recipe.
