Chocolate Cheesecake with Marshmallow Topping

Ingredients

  • 160 g granulated sugar

Chocolate Cheesecake

  • 100 g butter, diced
  • 150 g rich tea biscuits
  • 150 g shortbread biscuits
  • 200 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 350 g cream cheese
  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 1 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp real vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs

Marshmallow Topping

  • 3 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C and line base of a spring form tin (Ø 26 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place 160 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside for marshmallow topping.

Chocolate Cheesecake

  1. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add rich tea and shortbread biscuits then grind 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer to prepared tin and press down with back of a spoon to form a base with a 1 cm rim up the sides. Set aside in fridge to set.
  3. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3 min/45°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt again 2 min/45°C/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place cream cheese, 150 g sugar, cocoa powder, vanilla extract and eggs in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved melted chocolate then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Pour into tin over biscuit base and bake for 45 minutes (150°C). Meanwhile, thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Remove cheesecake from oven and set aside. Increase oven temperature to 200°C.

Marshmallow Topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk into a very clean mixing bowl. Place egg whites, cornflour, lemon juice, salt and 150 g reserved icing sugar in mixing bowl, then whisk 6 min/80°C/speed 3. Transfer to a large piping bag. Snip off the bottom of bag then pipe peaks over cheesecake, leaving a 1 cm border around the edge. Return to oven and bake for 8-10 minutes (200°C) until marshmallow topping is golden in colour.
  2. Remove from oven, run a knife around edge to loosen then leave in tin to cool completely.
  3. Dust with remaining 10 g icing sugar before removing from tin and cutting in slices to serve.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 26 cm), baking paper, piping bag, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • A large nozzle can also be used in the piping bag, rather than just snipping the end off the bag, if desired.
  • Do a self wash after making marshmallow topping to help with easy cleaning.
