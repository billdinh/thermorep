Gougère puffs with salmon mousse

Ingredients

Salmon mousse

  • 150 g fresh boneless skinless salmon fillet, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 20 g butter
  • 150 g hot smoked salmon, broken into pieces
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 sprig fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1 tbsp pickled capers, drained
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp gelatine powder
  • 1 - 2 tbsp boiling water

Gougère puffs

  • 80 g Gruyère cheese, rind removed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 150 g water
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 pinch chilli powder
  • 80 g butter
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 3 eggs
  • fresh chives, finely chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

Salmon mousse

  1. Place fresh salmon and butter into mixing bowl and cook 5-7 min/100°C/speed 1, or until salmon is cooked.
  2. Add smoked salmon, cream, dill, capers, lemon juice and pepper and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Place gelatine and boiling water into a small bowl and combine until gelatine has dissolved, then mix 1 min/speed 3, adding gelatine mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Transfer salmon mixture into a bowl, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until set (approx. 2 hours or overnight).

Gougère puffs

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place Gruyère or Swiss cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water, salt, chilli powder and butter into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4, or until mixture begins coming away from sides of mixing bowl. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then set mixture aside in mixing bowl and allow to cool (approx. 10 minutes).
  5. Crack eggs into a small bowl, then mix 40 sec/speed 4, adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add reserved grated cheese and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  7. Transfer choux pastry mixture into a piping bag. Pipe small walnut-sized balls onto prepared baking tray (approx. 18-20 balls) to form puffs, leaving 5 cm space between each.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C), or until golden. Turn oven off, then using a wooden skewer, pierce each puff to release steam.
  9. Return puffs to oven and open oven door slightly, leaving it ajar for 10 minutes to allow pastry puffs to dry out. Transfer pastry puffs onto a wire rack and allow puffs to cool completely.
  10. Once cooled, cut puffs in half and pipe reserved salmon mousse into the middle. Top mousse with chopped chives or dill and serve immediately.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm), wooden skewer, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Try filling your Gourgère puffs with some chilled Baked artichoke dip if you prefer.
