Ukrainian borscht

Ingredients

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 250 - 300 g baby beetroot, peeled, reserving 7 leaves and stalks, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 60 g carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 50 g celery stalk, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 150 g potato, peeled and cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 60 g cabbage, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 60 g parsnip, peeled and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 750 g water
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 allspice berries
  • 1 fresh bay leaf
  • 400 g canned cannellini beans, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 60 g tomato paste
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • 80 g sour cream
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, cut into thin slices

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, onion and beetroot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place carrot, celery, potato, cabbage and parsnip into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add water, stock paste, pepper, allspice berries, bay leaf, reserved beetroot mixture and reserved beetroot leaves and stalks and cook 20 min/90°C//speed 1.
  4. Add cannellini beans, tomato paste and lemon juice and mix 1 min 30 sec//speed 1. Divide between serving bowls, top each with 1 tablespoon of sour cream and garnish with sliced spring onions/shallots before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • You can replace the cabbage with sauerkraut, if preferred. To make your own, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Borscht is typically a thinner soup (similar to a broth) and often served with dumplings in it. Ukranian borscht is much heartier and chunkier, containing a wide variety of vegetables.
