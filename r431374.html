Raw Quinoa Bars

Ingredients

  • 7 ½ oz tri-color quinoa
  • 16 oz water
  • 5 oz dried dates, pitted
  • 3 oz almonds
  • 3 oz sunflower seeds
  • 3 oz dried apricots, pitted
  • 1 pinch salt (optional)
  • 3 oz dried cherries
  • 2 oz old fashioned rolled oats
  • 3 oz almond butter
  • 3 oz pumpkin seeds
  • 2 oz coconut oil
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
1155 kJ / 276 kcal
Protein
8 g
Carbohydrates
30 g
Fat
16 g

Preparation

  1. Line a square baking pan (9 in. x 9 in.) with parchment, leaving an overhang on two sides (excess parchment will serve in compacting mixture and easy removal from pan).
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in quinoa and water. Stir with spatula to combine and let soak overnight.
  3. Drain soaked quinoa through a fine mesh sieve, press firmly with spatula to remove as much water as possible and set aside.
  4. Place dates, almonds, sunflower seeds, dried apricots and salt into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  5. Add cherries and oats and chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  6. Add almond butter, pumpkin seeds, coconut oil and reserved drained quinoa and mix 30 sec//speed 3 with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 30 sec//speed 3 with aid of spatula. Transfer to prepared pan and press down firmly to an even thickness.
  7. Freeze for one hour. Use parchment to lift out quinoa mixture and with a serrated knife cut into 16 equal-sized bars. Transfer to a freezer bag and keep frozen until ready to serve. Allow to thaw before serving.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, parchment paper, large freezer bag, large bowl, serrated knife, square baking pan (9 in. x 9 in.), freezer
