Pizza with red pepper sauce, spinach and eggs

Ingredients

Pizza dough

  • 30 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 400 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp fine salt

Topping and assembly

  • 240 g medium-hard cheese (e.g. Gouda, Cheddar etc.)
  • 120 g onions, cut into quarters
  • 200 g red peppers, cut into pieces
  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g tomato passata
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika powder
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • plain flour for dusting and rolling
  • 300 g frozen chopped spinach, defrosted and well-drained
  • 4 eggs

Preparation

Pizza dough

  1. Grease a large bowl and set aside. Place water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl and cover with cling film. Leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl then continue with recipe.

Topping and assembly

  1. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 7 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions and red peppers in mixing bowl then chop 8 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add olive oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add passata, tomato purée, salt, pepper, paprika, cayenne pepper and sugar, then place simmering basket instead of measuring cup on lid of mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile preheat oven to 200°C and grease 2 large baking trays then set aside.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, divide dough into two and roll each piece out into a rectangle then place one on each baking tray. Spread sauce on dough then sprinkle spinach followed by reserved chopped cheese over the top. Bake for 15 minutes (200°C).
  6. Crack 2 eggs over each pizza then bake for a further 5 minutes (200°C). Serve hot, cut into slices.

Useful Items

large bowl, baking trays, cling film, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute spinach and eggs with almost anything you have in the fridge (e.g. ham, salami, mushrooms, mixed peppers, courgette, olives).
