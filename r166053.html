Beef Jungle Curry

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp long-grain white rice
  • 2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 5 - 15 dried chilies, stems removed, to taste
  • 6 cardamom pods
  • 1 oz fresh galangal (Thai ginger), thinly sliced
  • 1 fresh lemongrass, thinly sliced (see Tip)
  • 3 ½ oz shallots, cut into pieces (approx. 2 large shallots)
  • 1 oz shrimp paste
  • 5 fresh kaffir lime leaves, midrib removed
  • 36 oz water, divided
  • 16 oz sirloin steak, thinly sliced (approx. 2 in. x ¼ in.)
  • 9 oz Thai eggplants, stems removed, quartered (approx. 7 eggplants)
  • 6 oz fresh mixed mushrooms, e.g. straw, cremini, white button, cut into pieces if too large
  • 2 oz fish sauce
  • ¼ tsp palm sugar
  • 2 fresh red Thai chilies, thinly sliced
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh holy basil

Preparation

  1. Place rice, peppercorns, chilies and cardamom into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add galangal, lemongrass, shallots, shrimp paste, kaffir lime leaves and 6 oz water and blend 40 sec/speed 10 to a smooth paste. Remove half the paste and transfer into a sterilized jar and refrigerate for another day.
  3. Add 30 oz water and beef and with simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid, cook 25 min/200°F//speed 1.
  4. Add eggplants, mushrooms, fish sauce and sugar and simmer for 15 min/212°F//speed 1.
  5. Transfer into bowls and garnish with fresh Thai chilies and holy basil to serve.

Useful Items

sterilized jar

Hints & Tricks

  • When using lemongrass: trim off the root and the woody stems and only use the soft white part of the grass.
  • Depending on the type of mushrooms you use, you may need to remove the stems if they are tough.
  • To make slicing the beef easier, freeze beef up to one hour prior to using.
