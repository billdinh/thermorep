Bagels

Ingredients

  • 8 ½ oz water
  • ¾ tsp active dry yeast
  • 16 oz bread flour
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 ½ tsp vegetable oil, plus extra to grease
  • 1 ½ tsp barley malt syrup
  • water, to boil
  • 1 tbsp baking soda
  • poppy seeds, to sprinkle
  • sesame seeds, to sprinkle
  • pumpkin seeds, to sprinkle

Preparation

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/100°F/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, salt, oil and barley malt and mix 20 sec/speed 3. Then knead 3 min/. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and divide into six equal-sized pieces. Roll into balls and cover with oiled cling wrap and let rest for 20 minutes.
  3. Line a baking sheet (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.) with parchment.
  4. Push the end of a wooden spoon or your thumb through the center of each dough ball and stretch into bagels. Place on prepared baking sheet and cover with oiled cling wrap. Leave at room temperature for 20 minutes until they just begin to rise.
  5. Preheat oven to 500°F.
  6. Fill a large deep pot about ⅔ with water, add baking soda and bring to a gentle boil.
  7. Place bagels into gently boiling water, two or three at a time. Boil for 1 minute, then flip over with a slotted spoon and boil for another minute. Drain on a rack and sprinkle with seeds as desired, while boiling remaining bagels.
  8. Return boiled bagels to prepared baking sheet and bake for 8 minutes (500°F). Reduce oven temperature to 450°F then bake a further 6-8 minutes until lightly browned. Allow to cool on wire rack and serve.

Useful Items

cling wrap, pot, slotted spoon, wire rack, baking sheet (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.), parchment paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Adding baking soda to the water alkalizes it, which helps to produce a better crust.
  • If you do not want to cook the bagels immediately, after they've risen: tightly wrap shaped bagels with cling wrap and refrigerate for up to 48 hours then proceed as directed by recipe.
  • Baked bagels can be frozen for up to a month.
  • Bagels are unusual in that they are boiled before baking and they also must have a very stiff dough. This produces the typical chewy texture with a good crust. The advantage of this method is that the bagels can be prepared in advance and cooked as required.
