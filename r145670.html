Chocolate cupcakes with vanilla coconut icing

Ingredients

Vanilla coconut icing

  • 175 g blanched almonds
  • 700 g water
  • 200 g coconut cream
  • 50 g pure maple syrup
  • 20 g honey
  • 1 ½ tsp agar agar
  • 1 ½ tbsp cornflour
  • 30 g raw cacao butter
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla bean paste

Cupcakes

  • 150 g raw sugar
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 40 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 4 eggs
  • 140 g grapeseed oil
  • 2 tsp vanilla bean paste

Preparation

Vanilla coconut icing

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh almonds and water into it. Set bowl aside to soak overnight.
  2. Place almonds and water into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 8 or until smooth.
  3. Line a jug with a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and pour almond milk into it. Squeeze firmly to extract as much milk as possible. Set milk and pulp aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place coconut cream, syrup, honey, agar agar and 310 g of the reserved almond milk into mixing bowl and  whisk 4 min/100°C/speed 3, then simmer 6 min/90°C/speed 3.
  5. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and place cornflour into it. Weigh 60 g of the reserved almond milk into bowl and stir to combine. Add almond milk mixture to mixing bowl and whisk 3 min/90°C/speed 3 or until boiling.
  6. Add cacao butter and vanilla bean paste and mix 10 sec/speed 3, until well combined.  Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Cover with a piece of baking paper and place into refrigerator for 1 hour or until set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Place cooled mixture into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 5 or until silky smooth. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator to set (approx. 2-3 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line 2 x 12-hole muffin moulds with 18 cupcake liners (9 cupcake liners into each tray).
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10.
  3. Add remaining cupcake ingredients, 200 g of the reserved almond pulp and 120 g of the reserved almond milk and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 20 sec/speed 3 or until combined. Place 2 tablespoons of batter into each cupcake liner and bake for 15-20 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cupcake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  5. Transfer icing into a piping bag and decorate cupcakes as desired before serving.

Useful Items

wire rack, bowl, large bowl, jug, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, muffin wrap papers, baking paper, sealable container, 2 x 12-hole muffin trays, wooden skewer, piping bag with a plain, round nozzle (1.5 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • During the cooler months, icing is best left at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before piping.
  • Icing will last in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. You can store cupcakes in a sealable container for up to 3 days.
