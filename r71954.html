Miso chicken and rice

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 2 tbsp tamari
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp Japanese pickled ginger, plus extra to serve
  • 1 skinless chicken breast fillet (approx. 200-250 g)

Stir-fry

  • 120 g sushi rice
  • 550 g water
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked in boiling water for 30 minutes, stalks removed and thinly sliced
  • 4 snow peas, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 80 - 100 g baby corn, cut into halves
  • ¼ red capsicum, deseeded and cut into cubes (1-2 cm)

Sauce

  • 1 tsp rice wine
  • 1 - 2 spring onions/shallots, ends trimmed and green fronds finely sliced to garnish

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Combine tamari, rice wine vinegar and pickled ginger into a ceramic or glass bowl. Add chicken breast to bowl and coat well. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for 30 minutes or overnight..

Stir-fry

  1. Insert simmering basket. Weigh rice into simmering basket and rinse until the water runs clear. Place water and miso paste into mixing bowl. Re-insert simmering basket.
  2. Place Varoma into position, place marinated chicken and marinade into Varoma dish and steam 17-20 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until chicken and rice are just cooked. Place chicken and rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Transfer steaming liquid into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place sesame oil into mixing bowl and heat 30 sec/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add shiitake mushrooms, snow peas, baby corn and capsicum and cook 4 min/Varoma//speed . Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl with rice and chicken.

Sauce

  1. Place reserved steaming stock and rice wine into mixing bowl and reduce 4 min/100°C/speed 2, without measuring cup.
  2. Combine the rice and vegetables then place into serving bowls. Slice chicken diagonally across the grain and place on top. Pour over sauce and garnish with finely sliced spring onions and extra pickled ginger to serve.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass baking dish, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, ceramic or glass bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: reduce the rice to 60 g and proceed as per recipe.
  • Miso paste is a fermented blend of soy beans, salt, either rice or barley and a fungus called kōjikin. Light miso is mild in flavour and sweeter on the palate; whilst the darker is stronger in flavour and lends itself to more robust meals. We recommend darker miso in this recipe.
  • Miso will keep for a long time in a sealable container.  Try using any leftover in other sauces, marinades, stocks, stews, braises or soups for added flavour and nutrients.
  • Marinate your chicken overnight for a quick and tasty Japanese-inspired meal.
