Vegetarian Lasagna

Ingredients

  • unsalted butter, to grease
  • 16 oz kabocha squash, cut into 1 in. pieces (approx. 1 med. squash)
  • 6 oz red bell pepper, cut into strips (approx. 1 lg. pepper)
  • 16 oz cauliflower, cut into 1 in. slices (approx. 1 med. cauliflower)
  • 2 oz olive oil
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed (approx. 1 in.)

Tomato Sauce & Squash Mash

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (approx. 14.5 oz)
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano, leaves only (approx. 5 stalks)
  • 2 oz unsalted butter

Béchamel Sauce

  • 18 oz whole milk
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 oz all-purpose flour
  • &frac18; tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 oz ricotta cheese

Lasagna Assembly

  • 13 sheets dried lasagna noodles (4 in. x 8 in. sheets)
  • 10 oz zucchini, cut into ribbons (approx. 1 lg. zucchini)
  • 6 oz carrot, cut into ribbons (approx. 2 lg. carrots)
  • 12 oz portobello mushrooms, cut into ⅛ in. slices (approx. 4 lg. mushrooms)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 320°F. Lightly grease lasagna pan and set aside.
  2. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment. Place squash, red pepper and cauliflower onto baking sheets, brush vegetables with oil and toss through to coat, sprinkle with salt. Bake 40 minutes (320°F).
  3. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.

Tomato Sauce & Squash Mash

  1. Place garlic and 1 oz oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add tomatoes and oregano and sauté 4 min/212°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside
  3. Place roasted squash and 2 oz butter into mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, set aside and clean mixing bowl.

Béchamel Sauce

  1. Place milk, 2 oz butter, flour, nutmeg, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/195°F/speed 3.
  2. Add ricotta cheese and mix 10 sec/speed 5.

Lasagna Assembly

  1. Turn oven to 350°F. Cover base of casserole dish with half the reserved tomato sauce. Place a layer of dried lasagna noodles over the sauce, cover with half the zucchini and carrot ribbons then half the mushrooms, red pepper, squash mash and finally the cauliflower. Repeat layers and top with a final layer of dried lasagna noodles.
  2. Finish lasagna with a layer of béchamel sauce, cover with foil and bake for 60 minutes (350°F). Remove foil and sprinkle with reserved Parmesan cheese and bake a further 10 minutes until melted. Allow lasagna to rest for 15-20 minutes before cutting into squares and serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, pastry brush, aluminum foil, roasting/lasagna pan (approx. 13 in. x 16 in. x 3 in.), baking sheets, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a good dish for entertaining guests as it can be prepared ahead of time and baked when guests arrive.
  • Dried lasagna pasta doesn't usually require boiling before baking (check package instructions). If you prefer cooking the pasta in the traditional method, pre-cook lasagna pasta in a large saucepan of salted boiling water (approx. 1 tsp salt per 35 oz of water) for approximately 5 minutes. Drain in Varoma dish and blot excess moisture by laying pasta in a single layer on a kitchen towel. Prepare the pasta while the béchamel sauce is cooking.
  • Leftovers keep well in the freezer.
