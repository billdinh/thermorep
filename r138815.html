Pinwheel biscuits

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces lemon zest (1 x 4 cm), no white pith
  • 130 g sugar
  • 220 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 350 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • natural food colouring of choice (see Tips)
  • 60 g 100s and 1000s sprinkles

Preparation

  1. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 8 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add vanilla, egg and mix 1 min/speed 4.
  6. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a floured work surface and shape into a ball. Divide dough into 2 equal portions.
  8. Place 1 piece of dough back into mixing bowl, add pink food colouring, then mix 20 sec/speed 4 or until evenly coloured. Transfer dough onto a piece of plastic wrap and shape into a rectangle, then wrap dough and place into refrigerator for 2 hours or until firm enough to roll. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  9. Place remaining dough into mixing bowl, add green food colouring and mix 20 sec/speed 4 or until evenly coloured. Transfer dough onto a piece of plastic wrap and shape into a rectangle, then wrap dough and place into refrigerator for 2 hours or until firm enough to roll.
  10. Working with one dough portion at a time, roll dough out between 2 sheets of floured baking paper into a rectangle (approx. 28 x 22 cm, 5 mm thickness).
  11. Place green dough on top of pink dough. Keeping edges even, use a rolling pin to gently press layers together.
  12. With the long edge facing you, tightly roll pastry into a log, ensuring there are no air pockets or gaps.
  13. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sprinkles into it. Place a large sheet of plactic wrap onto a work suface and evenly spread sprinkles onto it. Roll dough log in sprinkles to coat, then wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator overnight (see Tips).
  14. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  15. Cut log into slices (5 mm, approx. 40 biscuits total) and place onto baking trays. Bake for 10-12 minutes (170°C) or until crisp. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool, then serve or transfer into a sealable container until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, rolling pin, baking paper, bowl, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Rolled biscuit log can be frozen for up to 2 months. Transfer into the refrigerator to defrost overnight before slicing and baking.
  • The colder the dough, the easier it is to roll.
  • You can store biscuits for 2-3 days in a sealable storage container.
  • We used pink and green food colouring in this recipe, but you can use any colours you like.
