Pineapple Vanilla Ice Lollies

Ingredients

  • 80 g honey
  • 60 g whole milk
  • 420 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 1 tsp cornflour, mixed with 1 tsp water
  • 480 g pineapple, frozen in bite-sized pieces
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
463 kJ / 110 kcal
Protein
4.7 g
Carbohydrates
15.3 g
Fat
3.2 g

Preparation

  1. Place honey in mixing bowl and heat 2 min/50°C/speed 1.
  2. Add milk, yoghurt and vanilla then mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add cornflour mixture and combine 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place frozen pineapple and 100 g reserved vanilla yoghurt mixture in mixing bowl then blend 20 sec/speed 8, stopping as necessary to scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Blend again 30 sec/speed 5.
  5. Divide pineapple purée between 10 ice lolly moulds until half full, then top with remaining vanilla yoghurt mixture. Insert wooden ice lolly sticks halfway into mixture then freeze until solid.
  6. Remove from moulds and serve from frozen.

Useful Items

wooden sticks for ice lollies or ice cream, ice lolly moulds, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • There’s nothing like an ice lolly to cool you down on a hot summer’s day. These good-for-you lollies are bursting with natural sweetness and fresh fruit. This is definitely one to try as the fresh and tasty pineapple pairs beautifully with the creamy vanilla flavours.
  • Vanilla seeds can easily be removed from the vanilla pod; hold the pod at one end, slit with a knife lengthwise, then scrape out the seeds with the tip of the knife.
  • The empty pod can still be used, as it has plenty of flavour. For example, you can make your own vanilla sugar, or cook the pod in milk for desserts.
  • Substitute pineapple with frozen strawberry or mango.
