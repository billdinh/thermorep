Meatloaf filled with Spinach and Cheese

Ingredients

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 1 garlic clove, or to taste
  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 40 g breadcrumbs
  • 400 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • 2 medium eggs
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 50 g Gruyère cheese, sliced (2-3 mm)
  • 25 g fresh baby spinach
  • ½ - 1 garlic clove, to taste
  • 450 g yellow peppers, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 4 - 5 fresh basil leaves
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 200 g water
  • 1 pinch sugar (optional)

Preparation

  1. Grease Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese, onions, 1 garlic clove and parsley in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add breadcrumbs, beef mince, eggs, black pepper and 1 tsp salt and knead 1 min/. Transfer mixture to a sheet of baking paper (40 cm x 40 cm) and spread out to create a rectangular shape (approx. 25 cm x 20 cm, 1 cm thick). Arrange Gruyère cheese and baby spinach leaves evenly over the meat.
  4. Roll up meat with aid of baking paper to create a loaf shape. Remove baking paper and place meatloaf in greased Varoma dish.
  5. Place extra virgin olive oil, ½-1 garlic clove, peppers and basil in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, water, 1 tsp salt and sugar, set Varoma in position and cook 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Set Varoma aside and allow meatloaf to cool for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, insert measuring cup and blend sauce 40 sec/speed 8. Carefully remove meatloaf from Varoma, cut in slices and serve with sauce.

Hints & Tricks

  • This is not your ordinary meatloaf. Carefully rolled around a Gruyère-spinach filling, gently steamed to preserve a tender and moist interior and served with a fruity tomato and yellow pepper sauce, it’s excellent both hot and cold. Great for a lunchbox or a picnic, or can also be served at a dinner party or family supper.
  • Gruyère cheese can be replaced with other cheeses (e.g. smoked mozzarella, Gouda).
    - For an easy side dish, in step 6 steam up to 400 g vegetables (e.g. carrots, potatoes, green beans, courgettes, broccoli), cut in bite-size pieces, in simmering basket. Remove simmering basket when the vegetables are cooked to your liking.
