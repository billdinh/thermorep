Cottage Loaf

Ingredients

  • 220 g water
  • 100 g milk
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 30 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp sugar

Preparation

  1. Place water, milk and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.5.
  2. Add flour, butter, salt and sugar then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Knead 3 min 30 sec/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and baking tray then set aside. Transfer dough to greased bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 2 hours).
  3. Tip dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knock back to deflate. Cut off a third then shape both pieces into tight balls, tucking edges underneath. Place smaller ball on top of larger ball and place on baking tray then dust the handle of a wooden spoon with flour, before pushing it right through the centre of both balls of dough. Remove spoon.
  4. Make vertical cuts with a very sharp knife from the top to the bottom of loaf, then cover with greased cling film and leave to rise for a further 45 minutes. Towards the end of resting time preheat oven to 210°C.
  5. Turn oven down to 190°C and bake loaf for 25-30 minutes (190°C) until it sounds hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer to a wire rack to cool before slicing.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, wooden spoon, baking tray, long sharp knife, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A traditional English bread, characterised by its distinctive shape: a small, round loaf placed on top of a larger one. It is thought the shape may have come about as a way of saving space in old-fashioned bread ovens. A wonderful, soft everyday bread – use for sandwiches or with soup. It is also good toasted.
