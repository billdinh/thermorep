Gingerbread house

Ingredients

Gingerbread house

  • gluten free cornflour, for dusting
  • 380 g gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground allspice
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 150 g golden syrup
  • 150 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces, softened
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • ½ tsp guar gum

Royal icing

  • 240 g pure icing sugar
  • 1 egg white
  • ½ tsp lemon juice

Preparation

Gingerbread house

  1. Dust silicone gingerbread house moulds lightly with cornflour and set aside.
  2. Place all gingerbread ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 12 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and knead 50 sec/.
  4. Transfer dough into a large bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Place dough into refrigerator to chill (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Preheat oven to 170°C.
  6. Press dough into prepared moulds to fill half way. Bake for 23-25 minutes (170°C), until firm. Cooking time will vary slightly depending on the size and shape of your moulds.
  7. Allow to cool for 2 hours in moulds, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool and firm, before decorating.

Royal icing

  1. Place sugar, egg white and lemon juice into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6 (see Tips).
  2. If icing is too thick, add a few more drops of lemon juice or water and mix 3 sec/speed 6 or until desired consistency is achieved
  3. Transfer icing into a piping bag and use to assemble and decorate house. You will need a serving plate or cake board to place your house onto.
  4. Decorate house as desired.

Useful Items

silicone gingerbread house moulds, large bowl, plastic wrap, wire rack, piping bag (thin nozzle), cake board or serving plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to the Gluten free flour mix recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Depending on how you decorate your house, you may need more than 1 batch of icing.
  • If royal icing is too thick, add a few more drops of lemon juice or water.
  • To make coloured icing, distribute icing between several different bowls, then add a few drops of food colouring to each bowl.
  • This gingerbread house needs to be assembled within 12 hours of removing from the moulds as it can become soft. The humidity can also play a big part in the gingerbread softening. You may need to return your gingerbread to the oven and bake for a few minutes to dry out.
  • You may need to make 2 batches of gingerbread depending on the size of your moulds.
