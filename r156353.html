Dim sum 'Shao mai'

Ingredients

  • 10 g carrot

Shao mai dough

  • 50 g water
  • 180 g plain flour
  • 1 eggs

Filling

  • 300 g pork belly, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 15 - 20 g fresh mushrooms (e.g. shiitake, button)
  • 5 - 10 g piece fresh ginger, peeled, cut into thin slices (optional)
  • 270 g raw prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil (30 g, see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • oil, for greasing
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 500 g water

Preparation

  1. Place carrot into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.

Shao mai dough

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/80°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour then egg and knead 40 sec/speed 3-5, increasing speed gradually from speed 3 to speed 5. Shape dough into a ball, transfer into a bowl, cover and allow to rest for 15 minutes.

Filling

  1. Place pork belly, mushrooms, ginger (optional) and prawns into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add sugar, sesame oil, salt, white pepper and cornflour and mix 1 min/speed 2.5. Meanwhile, oil Varoma dish and Varoma tray and set aside.
  3. Divide dough into 40 equal pieces and shape into small ball shapes. Roll each ball of dough on a floured surface into a circle, as thin as possible (approx. 7-10 cm diameter). Place 1½ teaspoons of the meat mixture onto shao mai skin. Pinch thumb and forefinger of left hand together to form a circle. Place a shao mai skin with filling on the circle and let it fall down slowly but not completely through the circle of your finger and thumb, while at the same time using the other hand to press meat slightly and to pinch sides of shao mai into many points, folding skin clockwise around the filling. The result should resemble a little purse with a flat bottom (open on top). Garnish each shao mai with a pinch of reserved chopped carrot. Arrange shao mai with a little space between them into prepared Varoma dish and onto tray. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 17 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

rolling pin, small bowl, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • If only toasted sesame oil is available (dark), decrease quantity to 1 tablespoon and add 1 tablespoon sunflower oil.
    - These shao mai are usually served without a sauce; however you can mix 2 tablespoons soy sauce with 3-5 drops vinegar and serve as a dip if desired.
    - To save time, use ready-made wonton wrappers, available in Asian grocers and some supermarkets. You can cut the corners off to make them round, or just use them as they are.
  • Add 15 g spring onion/shallots, cut into pieces, with pork belly.
    - Vegetarian shao mai: Place 400 g mixed fresh mushrooms, 5 g fresh ginger, cut into thin slices, 50 g carrots and 30 g oil into mixing bowl, chop 3 sec/speed 5, and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed . Add 120 g rice, ½ teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon sugar, 2 teaspoons soy sauce and cook 8 min/100°C//speed 0.5. Transfer the filling into a bowl and proceed and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 1.
    - Fish shao mai: Add 500 g white fish fillets (cut into pieces), 5 g fresh ginger, cut into thin slices, 8 sprigs coriander (leaves only), 1 teaspoon sugar, 2 teaspoons sesame oil, 2 teaspoons soy sauce, 1 teaspoon cornflour, 1 teaspoon salt and ¼ teaspoon ground white pepper and mix 7 sec/ speed 5. Transfer the filling into a bowl and proceed as per recipe. Before steaming, garnish each shao mai with a small coriander leaf.
