Pesto Baked Eggs

Ingredients

Puff Pastry and Pesto

  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 150 g butter, unsalted, diced (1 cm), then frozen
  • 60 g water, chilled
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 15 g toasted pine nuts
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 40 g fresh basil, leaves only
  • 70 g extra virgin olive oil

Assembly

  • 6 medium eggs
  • fine sea salt, for seasoning
  • ground black pepper, for seasoning

Preparation

Puff Pastry and Pesto

  1. Place flour, butter, water and ½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Form into a ball and flatten into a rough square, then wrap in cling film and place in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make pesto.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add pine nuts, basil, garlic, oil and salt then blend 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. Unwrap dough and place on a lightly floured work surface. Using a rolling pin, roll out dough to a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 40 cm x 15 cm). Divide dough into thirds, fold bottom third up and top third down over this. Give dough a quarter turn so that fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice, then wrap in cling film and place in freezer for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.

Assembly

  1. Roll dough out to a rectangle (36 cm x 24 cm) then cut into 6 squares (12 cm). Transfer squares to prepared baking tray then use a knife to score a border 2 cm inside outer edge, pressing in firmly but not cutting through. Prick centres firmly with a fork then place in fridge for 10 minutes.
  2. Bake for 8 minutes (200°C) until edges have risen. Press middle of pastry down using the back of a spoon if needed. Add 1 Tbsp pesto to each (see tip) and spread to edges of inner square, then carefully crack an egg in the middle. Season eggs then bake for a further 10-12 minutes (200°C) until egg whites are fully cooked.
  3. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking tray and baking paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Children can assist with this recipe, weighing ingredients in and setting the controls. Never leave them unattended with Thermomix®, while it is running or between steps.
  • Children can also help with the rolling, shaping and topping of the pastry.
  • Save any leftover pesto and stir through cooked pasta for a quick dinner.
  • For a speedier recipe, use shop-bought puff pastry.
  • These pastries are also delicious without the pesto.
