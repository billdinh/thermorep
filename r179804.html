Three cup chicken

Ingredients

  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 - 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed, deseeded and cut into pieces
  • 10 g sesame oil
  • 1000 g chicken winglets (see Tips)
  • 40 g Chinese rice wine (Shaoxing)
  • 30 g dark soy sauce (see Tips)
  • 25 g sugar (rock sugar - see Tips)
  • 60 g light soy sauce
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, ginger, chillies and sesame oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add chicken and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed .
  4. Stir with aid of spatula to ensure even cooking. Add Chinese rice wine, dark soy sauce, sugar and light soy sauce and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed .
  5. Stir with aid of spatula to ensure even cooking, then cook for a further 10 min/Varoma/speed  or until chicken is cooked.
  6. Stir with aid of spatula. Add basil leaves and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed . Transfer into a serving bowl and serve with sides of choice (see Tips).

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use winglets or drumettes for this recipe.
  • Rock sugar is crystallised, refined sugar available at most Asian food stores. Alternatively, you can use white sugar.
  • Dark soy sauce is richer, aged longer and saltier than light soy sauce and sweeter due to added sugars. Light soy sauce is thinner, saltier and more commonly used as a light seasoning or dipping sauce. Both are available at most major supermarkets.
  • We served our dish with cooked white rice.
  • This dish's name derives from the three cups of sauces that were typically used to make the dish.
