Baked Pappardelle - Pappardelle al forno

Ingredients

Dough

  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 15 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 medium eggs

Ragù

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 70 g carrots, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 30 g onion, halved
  • 50 g celery, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 150 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • 150 g pork mince, broken in pieces
  • 180 g water, plus extra for cooking pasta
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus 1 tsp for cooking pasta
  • 200 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 100 g tomato purée
  • 5 - 6 fresh basil leaves
  • 1 fresh bay leaf
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Assembly

  • 300 g ricotta cheese

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, oil and eggs in mixing bowl then knead 2 min/. Tip out into a large bowl and bring together into a ball. Cover with cling film then leave to rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Divide rested dough into 4 equal-sized pieces. Take 1 piece, keeping remaining dough covered with cling film in bowl and, using a rolling pin or pasta machine, roll into a long rectangular sheet of pasta (approx. 2-3 mm thick), then dust with flour.
  3. Fold rectangle in half so short ends meet, then fold in half again. With a sharp knife, cut into strips approx. 3-4 cm wide, then unfold strips and place on a baking tray dusted with plenty of flour. Cover with cling film to prevent drying out then repeat with remaining dough.

Ragù

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil, carrots, onion, celery and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add beef, pork, 150 g water and salt, mix 5 sec//speed 3 then cook 7 min/100°C//speed 1.
  4. Add tinned tomatoes, tomato purée, remaining 30 g water, basil, bay leaf and nutmeg then cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 180°C and bring a large saucepan of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil. Cook pappardelle for 2-3 minutes until al dente then drain through a colander.

Assembly

  1. Transfer cooked pappardelle to a baking dish. Cover with ragù and top with spoonfuls of ricotta, then finish with reserved grated Parmesan.
  2. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) until golden. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

colander, large saucepan, baking dish, cling film, rolling pin or pasta machine, sharp knife, baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If preferred, use 320 g dried pappardelle instead of fresh pasta, and cook according to packet instructions.
  • ‘Al forno’ is the term given to a dish that has been baked in an oven, which traditionally was wood fired or open flamed. This modern day oven baked pasta dish is perfect for an informal meal with friends as it can be made ahead and then baked when needed. In addition, with this recipe you make your own pasta, an easy task in the Thermomix® as all the mixing and kneading is done for you, making an even more impressive meal to serve.
