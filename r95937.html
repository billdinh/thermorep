Raised Game Pie

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 500 g mixed game cut in pieces (3 cm) (e.g. pheasant, partridge, duck)
  • 500 g chicken, pheasant, partridge or pigeon breasts (or mixture)
  • 120 g dried sour cherries
  • 200 g port
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 4 juniper berries, crushed

Hot Water Shortcrust Pastry

  • 140 g lard, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 90 g water
  • 80 g milk
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 500 g plain flour

Filling

  • 250 g pork belly, boned, rind removed, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 250 g smoked bacon, rind removed, cut in pieces
  • ½ tsp ground mace
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 egg, beaten, for glazing

Jelly Filling

  • 250 g beef stock
  • 3 gelatine leaves (or sufficient to set 250 g liquid), soaked in cold water for 5 minutes

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in mixed game, breast meat, dried cherries and port then add thyme leaves and juniper berries.  Cover with cling film then leave in fridge to marinate for at least 1 hour or preferably overnight.

Hot Water Shortcrust Pastry

  1. The next day, grease a deep spring form tin (Ø 20 cm) then set aside.
  2. Place lard, water, milk and salt in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Working quickly while pastry is still hot, take ⅔ of pastry and line tin using fingers to press pastry around the bottom and up sides forming a wall at least 6 cm high. Roll out remaining pastry to a circle slightly bigger than tin (approx. 5 mm thick). Set tin and pastry circle aside while preparing the filling.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Place pork belly and smoked bacon in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop again 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Drain marinated meat, set breast meat aside then add mixed game to large bowl with pork.
  3. Add mace, salt and pepper then mix together with hands. Add half the game-pork mix to prepared tin. Lay breast meat neatly over game to form a single layer. Top with remaining game-pork mix, forming a dome, leaving a small gap around the edge to tuck the lid into.
  4. Brush rim of pastry in tin with beaten egg then place pastry lid on top, pinching wall and lid together to seal. Remove any overlapping pastry. Brush lid with beaten egg then make a hole in centre (2-3 cm) to allow steam to escape during baking.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes (200°C) then lower oven temperature to 160°C. Cover pie with foil then bake for 1½ hours (160°C). Allow pie to cool completely in tin. Meanwhile, make jelly filling

Jelly Filling

  1. Place beef stock in clean mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Squeeze excess water from soaked gelatine and add to hot stock. Mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool to room temperature.
  3. Once pie and jelly are cold, use a jam funnel to carefully pour jelly into steam hole (see tip). Place in fridge to set for at least 5 hours or overnight. Before serving, remove from tin and cut in wedges.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, deep spring form tin (Ø 20 cm), rolling pin, medium mesh sieve, pastry brush, aluminium foil, jam funnel, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditionally used for pork and game pies, hot water crust pastry must be shaped while it is still warm.
  • If there is no space for the jelly in the centre, it is possible to make small slits in the pastry nearer the edge and pour in different places.
  • Best made at least 48 hours before serving to allow time for flavours to develop.
  • The UK game season typically runs from autumn to the end of winter, so this is a perfect dish to make during these later months. For the rest of the year when game may be hard to source, substitute other poultry. This beautifully decadent pie goes wonderfully with homemade piccalilli.
