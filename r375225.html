Turmeric cauliflower soup

Ingredients

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus 1-2 tablespoons extra for frying
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 leek (approx. 160 g), white part only, cut into slices
  • 2 potatoes (approx. 200 g), peeled and cut into pieces (3-5 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp ground chilli (optional)
  • 500 g cauliflower, broken into small florets
  • 800 g liquid chicken stock (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste (see Tips)
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 brown onion, cut into thin slices, for garnishing
  • 40 g cashews, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place oil, leek and potato into mixing bowl and sauté 10 min/120°C/speed 1 or until leek just becomes translucent.
  2. Add turmeric, coriander, cumin and ground chilli (optional) and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add cauliflower and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add liquid stock, salt and pepper and cook 20 min/90°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, place a frying pan over medium-high heat. Add 1-2 tablespoons of oil, then add onions and cashews and fry until golden (approx. 5-8 minutes). Remove from pan and set aside.
  5. Allow soup to cool slightly, then blend 1 min/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9 or until smooth. Divide between serving bowls and garnish with fried onions and cashews to serve.

Useful Items

non-stick frying pan, serving bowls, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have fresh turmeric on hand, you can use it instead of the ground turmeric. Simply use a 3 cm piece of peeled fresh turmeric and add it with the cauliflower in step 3.
  • You can replace liquid Chicken stock with chicken stock paste and water in this recipe. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe. In step 4 replace liquid stock with 1 tablespoon Chicken stock paste and 800 g water.
  • This soup is also perfect for the whole family. Just be sure to omit the salt and chop up the cashews and onion garnish if serving to younger children.
