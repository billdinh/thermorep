Gozleme

Ingredients

Dough

  • 450 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 10 g dried instant yeast
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 50 g plain yoghurt
  • 260 g water, warm
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Filling

  • 200 g Swiss chard
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 15 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 120 g feta cheese

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, yeast, oil, yoghurt, water and salt in mixing bowl then knead 3 min/.
  2. Tip dough out onto lightly-floured work surface and knead lightly. Divide into 6 pieces then shape each piece into a ball. Place on a baking tray, cover with cling film and leave to rest until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place Swiss chard in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 2 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions and oil in mixing bowl, chop Turbo/2 sec/2-3 times, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  3. Add reserved Swiss chard and paprika then mix 30 sec//speed 2. Crumble in feta and stir with spatula.

Assembly

  1. Lightly flour a work surface and rolling pin, then roll out each piece of dough to a thin circle approx. Ø 35 cm.
  2. Fold upper and lower edges in towards the centre by approx. 7 cm. The edges should not meet in the middle; there should be a gap of approx. 3 cm between them. Total width of the dough should now be approx. 18 cm.
  3. Divide filling into 6 portions. Spoon one portion into the centre of the dough, spreading to form a square, leaving a 1 cm border of dough along the folded edge.
  4. Fold the left and right edges in to cover the filling forming a square (18 cm) gozleme. Press edges together well. Repeat until all dough and filling have been used.
  5. Heat a heavy-based frying pan over a medium heat. Brush one side of the gozleme with olive oil and fry oil-side down in the hot pan until well browned. Brush the top side with oil then flip and fry until browned.
  6. Transfer gozleme to a chopping board and leave to cool for a few minutes. Meanwhile, repeat with remaining gozleme. Cut each gozleme into 4 and serve.

Useful Items

baking tray, cling film, rolling pin, large frying pan, pastry brush, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Gozleme is a traditional savoury Turkish flatbread, stuffed with various fillings, before being cooked over a griddle.
  • If the Gozleme are a little puffy after folding, press with spatula to expel any air.
  • Alternative fillings could include roasted sweet potatoes with baby spinach and ricotta, spicy lamb mince or roasted pumpkin with prosciutto and rocket.
