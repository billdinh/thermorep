Focaccia with Pear and Gorgonzola - Focaccia pere e Gorgonzola

Ingredients

  • 40 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing and topping
  • 350 g Williams pears, peeled, cored, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp dried instant yeast
  • 300 g strong white bread flour
  • 200 g rye flour
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 150 - 200 g Gorgonzola, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • oil, for greasing

Preparation

  1. Place 20 g butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Add pears and cook 5 min/100°C//speed . Drain pears using simmering basket, then transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec//speed 2.
  4. Add bread flour, rye flour, 20 g butter and salt then knead 3 min/. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add cooked pears and knead again 2 min/. Transfer to prepared bowl, cover with cling film (see tip) and leave until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, line base and sides of a round cake tin (Ø 28 cm) with baking paper.
  6. Once dough has risen, transfer to prepared tin and spread out evenly. With the tips of your fingers, create small dimples. Place Gorgonzola in the dimples, pushing lightly into the dough, then dot with small knobs of butter. Cover with greased cling film and leave to stand for a further 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C.
  7. Bake for 20-30 minutes (200°C). Leave in tin to cool for 10 minutes then remove to a chopping board. Slice focaccia and serve warm.

Useful Items

cling film, round cake tin (Ø 28 cm), baking paper, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Focaccia is a yeasted bread that originated in Liguria. Early versions were cooked over a hot fire or on a heated tile, similar to early flatbreads. Unlike flatbread, however, bakers ‘dimple’ the top of focaccia with their hands or the handle of a utensil, before drizzling with olive oil to preserve moisture. Typically eaten plain or with ingredients incorporated into the dough, focaccia is a classic Italian bread that is enjoyed all over the world. This version combines the classic combination of pear and Gorgonzola, making this simple bread even more delicious.
  • Lightly grease cling film to prevent it from sticking to the dough during the resting and leavening stages.
