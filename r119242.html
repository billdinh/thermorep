Champagne poached chicken terrine

Ingredients

Terrine

  • 500 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 230 g skinless chicken breast fillet, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 150 g white sparkling wine
  • 350 g liquid chicken stock
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1500 g water
  • 150 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g eschalots
  • 2 sprigs fresh tarragon, leaves only (optional)
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 50 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (2 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g shelled and unsalted pistachios
  • 70 g pine nuts
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • salt, to taste
  • pepper, to taste
  • 10 slices prosciutto

Spiced pears

  • 800 g pears, peeled, cored and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g raw sugar
  • 200 g apple cider vinegar
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 5 black peppercorns

Preparation

Terrine

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place chicken thigh, chicken breast, wine, stock and 1 sprig of the thyme into mixing bowl and cook 12 min/90°C/speed 1.  Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Transfer chicken and liquid into a bowl and set aside to cool.
  3. Place 500 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, weigh spinach into it, secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Rinse spinach under cold water and squeeze to remove liquid. Roughly cut into pieces and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  4. Place garlic, eschalots, remaining thyme (leaves only), tarragon (optional) and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add butter and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed .
  6. Drain cooled chicken and discard liquid. Add chicken, pistachios, pine nuts, cream, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  7. Grease and line a terrine or loaf tin (approx. 22 x 10 x 7 cm - ensure tin will fit into Varoma dish and lid closes comfortably) with plastic wrap, ensuring plastic wrap hangs 15 cm over sides of tin. Line again with a sheet of baking paper large enough to enclose terrine. Line bottom of the loaf tin with prosciutto, ensuring long ends of prosciutto hang over edges of tin.
  8. Place half of the chicken mixture onto prosciutto and smooth down into an even layer. Top with spinach, leaving a 1 cm perimeter along the sides. Add remaining chicken mixture. Fold prosciutto over chicken mixture to encase and press down.
  9. Using plastic wrap, remove terrine from tin. Wrap firmly in baking paper, then in overhanging plastic wrap. Firmly wrap terrine in another layer of plastic wrap and return to tin.
  10. Place remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, place tin into it, secure Varoma lid and steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove tin from Varoma and allow terrine to cool completely in tin. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Spiced pears

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place all spiced pear ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/100°C/speed . Transfer into a bowl to cool. Once cooled, strain pears over a large jug.
  2. Cut terrine into thick slices and serve with spiced pears.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, baking paper, loaf tin (22 x 10 x 7 cm), jug
