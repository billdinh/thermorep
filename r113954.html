Crunchy Granola

Ingredients

  • 21 oz old fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 oz desiccated coconut, unsweetened
  • 3 ½ oz wheat germ
  • 2 oz sunflower seeds, shelled
  • 2 ½ oz sesame seeds
  • 1 oz pumpkin seeds, shelled
  • 7 oz mixed nuts (e.g. hazelnuts, pecans, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, almonds, peanuts) (see Tip)
  • 2 ½ oz flaxseeds
  • 9 oz raisins
  • 6 ½ oz spelt flakes
  • 7 oz dried apricots, pitted
  • 3 ½ oz honey
  • 7 ½ oz coconut oil
  • 2 oz whole milk
  • 3 ½ oz barley malt
  • 1 tsp salt
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
697 kJ / 167 kcal
Protein
4.1 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F and line two large baking pans (approx. 18 x 12 x 2 in.) with parchment.
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in: oats, coconut, wheat germ, sunflower seeds, sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds, mixed nuts, flax seeds, raisins and spelt flakes and set bowl aside.
  3. Place apricots into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer to the bowl of dry ingredients.
  4. Place honey, oil, milk, malt syrup and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/100°F/speed 3.5. Pour over dry ingredients in large bowl and stir well with spatula until completely covered.
  5. Divide uncooked granola into four batches by weighing uncooked granola back into mixing bowl, (approx. 21 oz per batch). Spread out two batches of granola on prepared baking pans. Bake two baking pans (batches) at the same time, for 20-21 minutes (325°F). Set the oven timer for 7 minute intervals, stirring carefully and gently each time. Repeat this three times until the 21 minutes is up. Set aside and allow to cool completely. Repeat process with remaining two batches. Once granola is fully cooled transfer to airtight jars or containers and store until needed (see Tip).

Useful Items

parchment paper, large bowl, airtight jars or containers, large baking pans (approx. 18 in. x 12 in. x 2 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Store granola in airtight jars in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. When stored properly, it will keep for up two weeks.
  • Perfect served on its own or with milk, yogurt or fresh fruit.
  • Sprinkle over vanilla ice cream for a quick dessert.
  • Chop mixed nuts into bite sized pieces before starting recipe.
  • Barley malt syrup can found in health food stores.
  • This recipe is very flexible, so feel free to adapt it depending on what ingredients you have on hand.
  • Halve ingredients to make a smaller quantity.
