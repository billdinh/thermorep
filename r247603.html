Ricotta Gnudi with Sage Butter - Gnudi di ricotta con burro con salvia

Ingredients

Gnudi

  • 1000 g ricotta cheese
  • 150 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg, freshly ground (see tip)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • fine semolina, for dusting

Sage Butter

  • 150 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 20 fresh sage leaves
  • 50 g toasted pine nuts
  • ½ lemon, finely grated zest only
  • water, for cooking gnudi
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • fine semolina, for dusting

Preparation

Gnudi

  1. Line a sieve with muslin or a nut milk bag and set over a large bowl. Place ricotta cheese in muslin and squeeze tightly until liquid stops coming out of cheese.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 12 sec/speed 10. Set scales on and remove 50 g to a bowl (scales will read -50 g) then set aside.
  3. Add drained ricotta, nutmeg (see tip), salt and pepper then stir gently with spatula until combined. Transfer to a bowl then chill in fridge for at least 1 hour. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  4. Dust a large baking tray with semolina. Remove ricotta mixture from fridge and shape into roughly 40 balls (approx. Ø 3 cm), then roll in semolina until completely coated. Place balls on a large plate and leave uncovered in fridge for 8 hours or overnight - the semolina will dehydrate the ricotta, giving the balls a firm coating.

Sage Butter

  1. The next day, place butter in mixing bowl and melt 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add sage, toasted pine nuts and lemon zest then cook 8 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Bring a large saucepan half-full of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil.
  4. Turn heat down on hob so water is simmering gently. Remove gnudi from fridge and give them a final dusting of semolina so that they are completely coated. Place 10-12 gnudi in saucepan and boil for 2-3 minutes, taking care not to damage them. Once cooked, remove with a slotted spoon, transfer to a large bowl and keep warm. Repeat until all gnudi are cooked.
  5. Divide gnudi between 6 warmed serving plates, pour over sage butter, sprinkle with reserved grated Parmesan and serve immediately.

Useful Items

large bowl, large plate, slotted spoon, large saucepan, muslin or nut milk bag, sieve, large baking tray, serving plates, fridge, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Literally translated as ‘naked’ in Tuscan, gnudi are dumplings of ravioli filling served without the pasta. Traditionally made using ricotta, these dumplings are light, delicate, and probably unlike anything you’ve had before. This recipe isn’t a quick one but don’t be put off by the timings; the gnudi need to sit in a coating of semolina for at least 8 hours, during which time the semolina forms a thin shell around the ricotta that helps keep them intact during cooking. The result? Soft pillows of ricotta gently flavoured with Parmesan that simply melt in the mouth.
  • Use Thermomix® ground nutmeg for the best flavour. Place 3-10 whole nutmegs in mixing bowl then grind 30 sec/speed 10 or until fine.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
