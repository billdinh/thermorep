Quinoa crumbed fish with slaw

Ingredients

Yoghurt tartare

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 sprig fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 30 g cornichons
  • 1 tsp lemon juice (approx. ¼ lemon)
  • 140 g Greek-style natural yoghurt (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp salted capers, rinsed

Slaw

  • 100 g white cabbage, cut into pieces
  • 100 g red cabbage, cut into pieces
  • 1 carrot (approx. 80 g), cut into pieces
  • 5 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • 100 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt, to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches freshly ground pepper, to taste

Quinoa crumbed fish

  • ½ lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches freshly ground pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 120 g quinoa flakes
  • 600 - 800 g skinless snapper fillets, cut into strips

Tortillas

  • 170 g brown rice flour (see Tips)
  • 60 g arrowroot flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp gluten free baking powder (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp xanthan gum
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 120 g water, plus extra if needed
  • ½ tsp olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 1000 g vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges, to serve

Preparation

Yoghurt tartare

  1. Place garlic and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining tartare ingredients and mix 3 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a small bowl or sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Slaw

  1. Place all slaw ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 3 sec/speed 4 until desired consistency is achieved. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Quinoa crumbed fish

  1. Place lemon zest, parsley, garlic, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add eggs and whisk 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh quinoa flakes into it. Set bowl aside. Set a large plate aside.
  4. Dip snapper pieces into bowl with egg mixture, then transfer into bowl with quinoa flakes, gently patting to coat all sides, then place onto large plate. Repeat dipping and coating with remaining snapper pieces. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tortillas

  1. Place rice flour, arrowroot, baking powder, xanthan gum and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add butter and mix Turbo/1 sec/1 time.
  3. Add water and oil and mix 10 sec/speed 3, or until mixture forms a soft dough, adding more water if needed. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and work into a ball. Divide into 10 equal portions (approx. 25 g each) and cover with plastic wrap.
  4. Lightly dust a large piece of baking paper with arrowroot flour. Roll out each portion of dough between 2 pieces of baking paper into a circle (approx. 15 cm). Transfer onto a plate.
  5. Place a non-stick frying pan (19 cm) over medium high heat. Lightly drizzle tortillas with oil on 1 side. Working 1 tortilla at a time, cook for approx. 3 minutes or until golden then turn over and cook for 1 minute, taking care not to overcook. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a large plate and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat until all tortillas are cooked.
  6. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside.
  7. Place a large saucepan over medium heat, add enough oil to cover fish and bring to 175°C (see Tips). To check your oil is at 175°C, drop a small cube of bread into the oil; it should turn golden brown in 15-20 seconds. Keep your oil at this temperature while frying. Working in batches, fry fish for 4-6 minutes, turning, until cooked, crispy and golden all over. Transfer onto prepared plate.
  8. Serve fish with tortillas, slaw, tartare sauce and lemon wedges.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Strain yoghurt through a nut milk bag or fine-mesh sieve lined with paper towel to get a thick and creamy tartare sauce.
  • To make your own mayonnaise, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook.
  • To make your own rice flour, place 250 g rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10. Use as needed.
  • To make your own Gluten free baking powder, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • If you have a kitchen thermometer to check the temperature of your oil, you can omit the bread test in step 13.
  • Tortillas can be made a day in advance, stored in a sealable container overnight and reheated just before serving.
