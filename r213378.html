Red, White and Blueberry Ice Cream Cake

Ingredients

Strawberry Layer

  • 5 ½ oz sugar
  • 7 oz frozen strawberries
  • 1 oz frozen raspberries
  • 2 ½ oz blackberries
  • 8 oz whole milk
  • 18 oz organic heavy whipping cream
  • 3 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch salt

Vanilla Layer

  • 5 ½ oz sugar
  • 8 ½ oz whole milk
  • 16 ½ oz organic heavy whipping cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 1 pinch salt

Blueberry Layer

  • 5 ½ oz sugar
  • 11 oz frozen blueberries
  • 8 oz milk
  • 18 oz organic heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • &frac18; tsp salt

Serving

  • mixed berries, to garnish

Preparation

Strawberry Layer

  1. Place sugar, strawberries, raspberries and blackberries into mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and grind a further 5 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add milk, whipping cream, egg yolks, vanilla extract and salt and cook 8 min/175°F/speed 4. Drain through a sieve and discard seeds.
  3. Transfer to a large freezable container with lid (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.) and set aside to cool then freeze for at least 6 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make vanilla layer.

Vanilla Layer

  1. Place sugar, milk, whipping cream, eggs, vanilla bean paste and salt into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/175°F/speed 4.
  2. Transfer to a large freezable container with lid and set aside to cool then freeze for at least 6 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make blueberry layer.

Blueberry Layer

  1. Place sugar and blueberries into mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and grind a further 5 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add milk, whipping cream, vanilla extract, egg yolks and salt and cook 8 min/175°F/speed 4.
  3. Transfer to a large freezable container with lid and set aside to cool then freeze for at least 6 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and line sides and base of a springform pan with parchment (see Tip).

Churning and Assembly

  1. After 6 hours or once completely frozen, remove strawberry ice cream from freezer and allow to soften (approx. 30-60 min.). Cut into 1 inch squares. Place half the strawberry squares into mixing bowl and churn 5 sec/speed 4. Add the remaining strawberry squares and churn 5 sec/speed 4. Insert butterfly whisk and mix 1 min/speed 4. Transfer to prepared springform pan and level the top and freeze for at least 4 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. After 4 hours, remove vanilla ice cream from freezer and allow to soften (approx. 30-60 min.). Cut into 1 inch squares and place half the vanilla squares into mixing bowl and churn 5 sec/speed 4. Add the remaining vanilla squares and churn a further 5 sec/speed 4. Insert butterfly whisk and mix 1 min/speed 4. Transfer to prepared springform pan over frozen strawberry layer and level the top. Freeze for at least 4 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  3. After 4 hours, remove blueberry ice cream from freezer and allow to soften (approx. 30-60 min.). Cut into 1 inch squares and place half the blueberry squares into mixing bowl and churn 5 sec/speed 4. Add the remaining blueberry squares and churn a further 5 sec/speed 4. Insert butterfly whisk and mix 1 min/speed 4. Transfer to prepared springform pan over top of frozen vanilla layer and level the top. Return to freezer for at least 8 hours or overnight.

Serving

  1. When ready to serve, unmold cake from pan and invert on a plate, revealing strawberry ice cream as the top layer. Garnish with mixed berries and serve immediately or store in freezer until serving.

Useful Items

large freezable containers with lids (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.), springform pan (11 in.), fine mesh sieve, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Place serving plate in freezer for 30 minutes before serving.
  • Leftover egg whites can be used for meringues.
  • Using a larger springform pan will give thinner layers. Make sure if using a smaller pan that you extend the height of pan by adding 2 inches of parchment to the inner edges for all three layers to fit.
  • Parchment paper: make sure to line sides of pan first then add a layer of parchment to the base of pan, covering up about ½ inch of the sides. Press parchment smooth before adding ice cream.
  • This recipe is best made three days in advance to allow ice cream to fully freeze before churning.
