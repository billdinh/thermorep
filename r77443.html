Victoria Sponge

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 170 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 170 g caster sugar
  • 3 medium eggs
  • 170 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp real vanilla extract (optional)

Filling

  • 100 g jam (e.g. strawberry and raspberry)
  • 200 g double cream
  • 150 g raspberries
  • 1 Tbsp icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line base of two cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add eggs, flour, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda and vanilla extract (if using) then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 20 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Divide mixture evenly between prepared cake tins and bake immediately for 25-35 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  5. Allow sponges to cool for 5 minutes in tins before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

Filling

  1. When sponges have cooled, remove baking paper and spread one sponge with jam.
  2. Place cream in mixing bowl and insert clean butterfly whisk. Whip speed 3, without setting a time, until softly whipped, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Spread whipped cream over jam layer then place second sponge on top. Decorate with raspberries and a dusting of icing sugar before serving.

Useful Items

2 round cake tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, skewer, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Chocolate Sponge Cake: use only 120 g self-raising flour and add 50 g cocoa powder. Fill the middle and ice the top with chocolate buttercream.

  • It doesn’t get much more British than a cake named in honour of Queen Victoria, does it? Particularly when filled with homemade jam (try the strawberry and raspberry jam), this simple classic is perfect for sharing with friends at tea time.
