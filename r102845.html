Spring Rolls

Ingredients

  • 50 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 50 g peanuts, roasted, unsalted
  • 25 g sunflower oil
  • 10 sprigs fresh chives, snipped (2 mm)
  • 1500 g water, plus warm water for softening
  • 50 g Chinese vermicelli rice noodles
  • 120 g bean sprouts, tinned, drained
  • 8 spring roll wrappers, rice variety
  • 50 g iceberg lettuce leaves, chopped in small pieces
  • 16 fresh basil leaves, roughly chopped
  • 16 fresh mint leaves, roughly chopped
  • 80 - 100 g prawns, cooked, peeled
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp fish sauce

Preparation

  1. Place carrots and peanuts in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sunflower oil and chives in mixing bowl then heat 1 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside to steep.
  3. Place water in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Add vermicelli noodles then cook 5 min//speed 2. Drain through simmering basket then run under cold running water to stop cooking. Add to bowl with oil and chives then mix with spatula.
  5. Add beansprouts and half reserved carrot-peanut mixture to noodles then mix with spatula.
  6. Dip rice wrapper (Ø 16 cm) in warm water for 3-4 seconds then transfer to a chopping board.
  7. In centre of rice wrapper, lay ⅛ of chopped iceburg lettuce, ⅛ noodle mixture and sprinkle with chopped herbs. Roll bottom of wrapper over filling and fold in sides. Lay 3-4 prawns on rice wrapper just before finishing rolling, so that it is visible through the spring roll. Repeat with remaining 7 wrappers.
  8. Add fish sauce to remaining half of carrot-peanut mixture. Serve dipping sauce with the spring rolls.

Useful Items

large bowl, chopping board

Hints & Tricks

  • If tinned bean sprouts cannot be found, use fresh but ensure they are cooked and drained according to instructions before using.
  • If rice paper is difficult to get hold of, use 3 sheets of filo pastry. Repeat layers as described in method down one long side of pastry (about 2 cm from pastry edge) roll, folding in sides and seal with water. Cut into pieces as desired. Brush lightly with oil before baking. Bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C).
  • Omit prawns for a vegetarian alternative, and replace with peanut halves, if desired, when folding so nuts are visible through rice paper.
