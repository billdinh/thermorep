Russian Beetroot Salad

Ingredients

  • 150 g pickled gherkins, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 150 g carrots, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 350 g raw beetroots, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 500 g water
  • 250 g potatoes, cut in small pieces (approx. 1 cm)
  • 100 g frozen peas
  • 100 g tinned sauerkraut (optional)
  • 50 g spring onions, finely sliced
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh dill, plus extra to garnish
  • 60 g vegetable oil
  • 20 g white wine vinegar (optional)
  • 1 tsp salt

Preparation

  1. Place gherkins in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a large salad bowl and set aside.
  2. Place carrots in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4.5 (pieces should be no larger than 1 cm). Transfer to Varoma tray and set aside.
  3. Place beetroots in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4.5. Transfer to simmering basket.
  4. Place water in mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Set Varoma dish in position and add potatoes and frozen peas to it. Insert Varoma tray, cover, then steam 27 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Remove Varoma and set Varoma dish and tray aside separately to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside on a deep plate to drain and cool (see tip). Meanwhile, discard cooking water, place salad bowl with gherkins on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sauerkraut (if using) and sliced spring onions, then set aside.
  6. Add vegetables from Varoma and simmering basket to salad bowl and set serving bowl aside.
  7. Place dill, oil, vinegar and salt in mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 6. Transfer to salad bowl, stir to coat all vegetables with dressing, adjust seasoning to taste and serve garnished with extra chopped fresh dill, or chill to serve later.

Useful Items

large salad bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Made with beets and vinaigrette, this recipe is a slight departure from the traditional ‘Russian salad’ (see tips and variations). Cooking several ingredients at the same time using the different levels of the Thermomix® is a great time-saver and makes this salad a dish that can be enjoyed any time.
  • In step 5, if you are in a hurry to cool the vegetables, run cold water over each vegetable separately while they are still in the Varoma dish, Varoma tray and simmering basket.
    - For a traditional Russian flavour, omit the vinegar.
  • For a creamy pink version of the salad, instead of making the vinaigrette in step 7, dress the cooled vegetables with sour cream or mayonnaise (quantity to taste), stir gently to combine, season to taste and garnish with chopped dill.
    - Replace frozen green peas with tinned green peas, well drained. Omit steaming in step 4 and add peas to the salad in step 5.
