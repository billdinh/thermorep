Tortillas

Ingredients

  • 170 g brown rice flour (see Tips)
  • 60 g arrowroot flour
  • ½ tsp gluten free baking powder (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp xanthan gum
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 120 g water, plus extra if needed
  • gluten free cornflour, for dusting
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
628 kJ / 149 kcal

Preparation

  1. Place rice flour, arrowroot, baking powder, xanthan gum and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add butter and oil and Turbo/1 sec/1 time.
  3. Add water and mix 6-10 sec/speed 3, or until mixture forms a soft dough, adding more water if needed. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Divide into 8 equal portions (approx. 40 g each).
  4. Roll out each portion of dough between 2 pieces of baking paper into a circle (approx. 15-17 cm diameter), dusting with cornflour as needed. Transfer tortillas between sheets of baking paper until ready to fry. Set aside.
  5. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat. Fry tortillas until slightly browned (approx. 3 minutes), then turn over and fry for a further 1 minute, taking care not to overcook. Transfer onto a plate and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

large non-stick frying pan, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, baking paper, kitchen towel, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own rice flour, place 250 g rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10. Use as needed.
  • To make your own Gluten free baking powder, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Tortillas can be stored in a sealable storage container overnight and reheated in a warm frying pan. Do not overcook as tortillas will become hard.
  • Serve tortillas with the Quinoa crusted fish, slaw and tartare sauce recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Tortillas can be rolled and kept between sheets of baking paper in the refrigerator and fried the next day. Remove the tortillas from the refrigerator and bring them to room temperature before frying.
