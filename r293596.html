Spaetzle

Ingredients

  • 2060 g water, plus extra water for wetting
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 150 g milk
  • 1 pinch nutmeg
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • fresh chives, roughly chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place 2000 g water and 1 teaspoon of the salt into a deep saucepan and bring to a simmer. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place flour, eggs, milk, nutmeg, remaining 60 g water and remaining 1 teaspoon salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Wet a chopping board and bowl scraper with water (see Tips). Spread one-quarter of the batter onto the wet chopping board in a smooth, even layer (approx. 3 mm in thickness) and the width of your bowl scraper. Carefully position board slightly above the simmering water and in one quick motion using scraper, cut off thin strips (approx. 1 cm) of the batter into the simmering water, dipping bowl scraper carefully into the water to release mixture (to make it easier, spread and move mixture down to the end of the chopping board as you go). Spaetzle will rise to the surface when cooked. Remove with a slotted spoon and place into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  4. Continue with remaining batter until all spaetzle is cooked.
  5. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add butter. Once melted, working in batches, fry spaetzle for 2-3 minutes, until slightly coloured. Transfer onto a serving plate and garnish with chives to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

deep saucepan, slotted spoon, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, large frying pan, bowl, serving plate, chopping board, bowl scraper, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • For ease, use a lightweight chopping board, preferably with a handle.
  • Spaetzle is a traditional German egg noodle. Serve it with stews, in soups or with a savoury sauce of choice (such as a cheese or mushroom sauce).
