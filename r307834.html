Floral Cordial

Ingredients

  • 30 oz water
  • 1 oz dried rose hips, plus extra to garnish
  • ½ oz dried hibiscus flowers
  • 4 oz sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 bottle Prosecco, chilled
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
452 kJ / 108 kcal
Protein
0 g
Carbohydrates
19 g
Fat
0 g

Preparation

  1. Place water, rose hips, hibiscus, sugar, cinnamon stick and star anise into mixing bowl, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 20 min/212°F/speed 1.
  2. Strain through a fine mesh sieve and transfer to a bottle. Refrigerate one hour or until ready to serve.
  3. To serve, pour 4 oz floral mixture into each flute, top with 2 oz Prosecco. Garnish with rose hips and serve immediately.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, champagne flutes, airtight jar or bottle, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Not all flowers are edible, some are poisonous. Flowers should be purchased from a reliable source.
  • Pregnant or nursing women should consult with their physicians before consuming hibiscus.
  • Look for dried, edible hibiscus flowers (often called “flor de jamaica” in specialty shops and Mexican food markets).
  • Store any unused rose-hibiscus mixture in refrigerator for up to three days or make ahead and store up to three days.
  • For a non-alcoholic drink, replace Prosecco with ginger ale or sparkling mineral water.
