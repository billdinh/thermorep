Parmesan baskets with goat's cheese mousse

Ingredients

Goat's cheese mousse

  • 350 g goat's cheese , softened
  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Parmesan baskets

  • 280 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Assembly

  • ½ Granny Smith apple, cut into matchsticks (see Tips)
  • ½ raw beetroot, cut into small cubes (5 mm)
  • 40 g walnuts, cut into pieces
  • micro herbs, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

Goat's cheese mousse

  1. Place all mousse ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat blending 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Parmesan baskets

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Set chosen moulds aside (see Tips).
  2. Place Parmesan and pepper into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Spoon 4 level tablespoon-size mounds, 5 cm apart, onto prepared baking tray. With your fingers, gently spread mounds into flat circles and an even thickness. Bake for 4-6 minutes (180°C), or until bubbly and lightly golden. Allow Parmesan crisps to cool slightly (approx. 40 seconds). While still warm and pliable, gently remove with a palette knife and press and form each crisp over moulds, until hardened. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Repeat with remaining mixture to make approx. 24 baskets. Set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Transfer mousse into a piping bag fitted with a 1 cm fluted nozzle. Pipe a rosette of mousse into each Parmesan basket. Top with a little of the apple, beetroot, walnuts and micro herbs (optional). Serve immediately. 

Useful Items

sealable container, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, palette knife, wire rack, piping bag with fluted nozzle (1 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A mandolin can be used to easily cut your apple into matchsticks.
  • You can use shot glasses, wine corks, miniature muffin tins or small, handleless espresso cups to mould your baskets.
  • This dish is best served on the day it's made.
