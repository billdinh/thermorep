Rava idli

Ingredients

  • 170 g semolina
  • 50 g cooked rice
  • 220 g coconut milk
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 170 g plain flour
  • 80 g sugar (optional)
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 700 g water
  • oil spray, for greasing
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
665 kJ / 158 kcal

3.5 g

Preparation

  1. Place semolina, rice and 120 g of the coconut milk into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to soak for 15 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place yeast and 60 g of the coconut milk into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour, sugar, baking powder, cardamom, remaining 40 g coconut milk and reserved soaked semolina mixture and knead 2 min/. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside to prove for 2 hours (or until doubled in size). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Spray 6 idli moulds, silicone egg poaching moulds or silicone muffin cases with oil. Transfer 1 tablespoon of mixture (approx. 60 g) into each of the prepared moulds. Place moulds into Varoma dish, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  5. Re-spray moulds with oil and distribute remaining idli mixture between moulds. Place moulds into Varoma dish, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve warm.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, silicone egg poaching moulds or idli moulds, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditional idlis are made using fermented lentils and rice and then steamed. The fermention process (which usually takes 12-24 hours) breaks down the starches, making them easier to digest. Our version uses semolina, making the recipe quicker and easier to make.
  • Idlis are meant to be eaten with a chutney or other flavoursome sauces or spreads, or used to accompany curries. In the southern part of India, idli is often part of a traditional breakfast.
  • You can serve these warm with butter. We served ours with Sambhar (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip) and rice.
