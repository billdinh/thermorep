Beef Rendang

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp peanut oil
  • 1000 g stewing steak, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 5 g coriander seeds
  • 5 g cumin seeds
  • 5 g dried fennel seeds
  • 10 g fine sea salt
  • 5 g black peppercorns
  • 240 g onions, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 fresh lemon grass, white part only, cut in pieces
  • 4 fresh red chillies, halved, deseeded
  • 15 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 15 g fresh galangal, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 50 g water
  • 800 g coconut cream
  • 1 Tbsp tamarind paste
  • ½ bunch fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Heat oil in a heavy-based ovenproof dish and brown beef on a medium-high heat.
  2. Place coriander seeds, cumin, fennel, salt and peppercorns in mixing bowl then grind 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions, garlic, lemongrass, chillies, ginger, galangal and water in mixing bowl, chop 10 sec/speed 6 then cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Transfer onion mixture to dish with beef, add coconut cream and reserved ground spices then stir well with spatula.
  5. Bring to the boil, reduce heat to a simmer and stir in tamarind.
  6. Cover with foil or lid and cook in oven for 2-2½ hours (160°C) until beef is tender.
  7. Sprinkle with coriander leaves and serve with steamed rice.

Useful Items

casserole dish, aluminium foil, ovenproof dish, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Vegetable or coconut oil may be substituted for peanut oil.
  • If there is liquid remaining after the beef is tender, transfer beef to a bowl, add liquid to mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, cook 3 min/120°C/speed 1, or until reduced by half.
  • This dish lasts very well and can be refrigerated when cooled.
