Birthday present cake

Ingredients

Pink food colouring

  • 75 g raw beetroots
  • 70 g water

Sponge cake

  • 310 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 280 g white sugar
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 6 eggs
  • 225 g plain flour
  • 80 g self-raising flour
  • 2 tbsp full cream milk

White fondant icing

  • 40 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g glucose syrup
  • 15 g glycerine (see Tips)
  • 620 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting

Pink fondant icing

  • 25 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g glucose syrup
  • 15 g glycerine
  • 620 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting

Buttercream icing

  • 250 g icing sugar
  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 tsp boiling water
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste

Assembly

  • 2 tbsp jam, (flavour of choice)
  • icing sugar, for dusting
  • water, for brushing

Preparation

Pink food colouring

  1. Place beetroot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 30 sec/speed 4-10, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 10. Strain beetroot mixture using a fine-mesh sieve into a large jug. Discard solids and set jug aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sponge cake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line a square cake tin (20 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and orange zest into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Mix 2 min/speed 3.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 1 min/speed 3.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 2 min/speed 3, slowly adding eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  8. Add flours and mix 50 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk.
  9. Add milk and mix 20 sec/speed 1. Transfer batter into prepared tin and smooth surface with spatula. Bake for 75 minutes (160°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

White fondant icing

  1. Place water and gelatine into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  2. Add glucose and glycerine and cook 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  3. Add 600 g of the icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 6, then knead 15 sec/. Lightly dust a silicone bread mat or work surface with icing sugar. Transfer fondant onto silicone bread mat or work surface. It will be heavy and sticky.
  4. To remove any remaining fondant in mixing bowl, place remaining 20 g icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Add to fondant on silicone bread mat. Dust hands with icing sugar and knead into a ball until fondant is smooth and less sticky. Transfer into a sealable container and set aside. Do not wash mixing bowl.

Pink fondant icing

  1. Place water, gelatine and 20 g of the reserved pink food colouring into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  2. Add glucose and glycerine and cook 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  3. Add 600 g of the icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 6, then knead 15 sec/. Lightly dust a silicone bread mat or work surface with icing sugar. Transfer fondant onto silicone bread mat or work surface. It will be heavy and sticky.
  4. To remove any remaining fondant in mixing bowl, place remaining 20 g icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Add to fondant on silicone bread mat. Dust hands with icing sugar and knead into a ball until fondant is smooth and less sticky. Transfer into a sealable container and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips).

Buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar and butter into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla bean paste and mix 3 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Trim top of cake to make it level (if required). Slice cake horizontally into 2 layers. Place 1 teaspoon of the buttercream icing onto centre of a cake board or plate. Secure base of sponge to board. Spread 2 tablespoons of jam onto cake. Spread the cut-side of the remaining half of cake with 2 tablespoons of the buttercream icing and place on top of jam to sandwich together.
  2. Using a palette knife, spread remaining buttercream over top and sides of cake. Place into refrigerator for 30 minutes or until buttercream icing is firm.
  3. Place white fondant onto a work surface lightly dusted with icing sugar. Gently knead by hand to soften fondant. Dust rolling pin with icing sugar and roll out fondant large enough to cover top and sides of cake (approx. 25-30 cm square, 5 mm thickness). Roll icing loosely over rolling pin and lower onto cake.
  4. Dust hands with icing sugar. Using the palms of your hands, gently press and smooth fondant over cake. Smooth and trim any excess.
  5. Dust rolling pin with icing sugar and roll out any remaining white fondant onto a work surface lightly dusted with icing sugar. Using round cookie cutters (3 cm and 5 cm), cut out circles (as many as desired for decorating) and set aside.
  6. Place pink fondant onto a work surface lightly dusted with icing sugar. Gently knead by hand to soften fondant, then divide into 2 portions. Place 1 portion onto a work surface lightly dusted with icing sugar. Using round cookie cutters (3 cm and 5 cm), cut out circles (as many as desired for decorating) and set aside.
  7. Brush backs of smaller white circles with water and place onto larger pink circles. Brush backs of smaller pink circles with water and place onto larger white circles. Brush backs of larger circles with water and place onto cake to decorate (the water will secure them to the cake).
  8. Knead remaining portion of pink fondant by hand to soften. Dust rolling pin with icing sugar and roll out to approx. 35 cm (5 mm thickness). Cut into 2 strips (5 x 35 cm). Set aside excess fondant. Brush back of each strip with water. Place 1 strip over cake horizontally. Place remaining strip over cake vertically, with strips overlapping in the centre of the cake.
  9. Gently knead excess pink fondant and roll out. Cut into 5 strips (5 x 8 cm).
  10. Fold a strip into a dome shape, placing a ball of crumpled plastic wrap underneath dome for support. Repeat folding with 1 more strip of fondant. Brush bottom of domes with water and gently secure to cake to resemble a bow. Wrap remaining strip over bow joins. Cut a V-shape from 1 end of remaining 2 strips of fondant. Brush backs with water and attach at base of bow to resemble ribbon. Remove and discard plastic wrap when fondant has set. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, square cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, silicone bread mat, sealable container, bowl, palette knife, rolling pin, round cookie cutter (3 cm), round cookie cutter (5 cm), pastry brush, plastic wrap, glass bowl or large jug, cake board or serving plate, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Glycerine - is an edible product and can be purchased from cake decorating shops or the health and beauty aisle in supermarkets.
  • Fondant will become firmer in steps 15 and 19 - this will make it easier to work with when rolling and decorating.
  • To help clean your mixing bowl after making fondant, place 400 g hot water into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 8. This will melt and loosen any remaining fondant, making it easier to clean.
  • Add extra loops to your bow to make your cake look more striking.
  • Try lines, flowers or another shape to decorate your present rather than circles.
