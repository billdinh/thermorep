Steamed Bao Buns, Pork Belly and Wasabi Mayo

Ingredients

Pork Belly

  • 2 tsp Chinese five spice
  • 4 Tbsp soy sauce, dark
  • 2 Tbsp runny honey
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 10 g butter
  • 40 g onion, halved
  • 1000 g pork belly meat, boned, without rind
  • 200 g water, cold

Bao Buns

  • 250 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp fresh yeast
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch caster sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp milk
  • 1625 g water, tepid
  • groundnut oil, for greasing

Wasabi Mayonnaise

  • 500 g groundnut oil
  • 4 - 5 tsp wasabi paste, to taste
  • 1 Tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp lime juice
  • 1 pinch caster sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs

To Serve

  • 1 little gem lettuce, washed and separated

Preparation

Pork Belly

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Line a deep roasting tin with baking paper.
  2. Place Chinese five spice, soy sauce, honey, garlic, butter and onion in mixing bowl then blend 40 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 40 sec/speed 10.
  3. Place pork belly in prepared roasting tin, pour marinade all over then rub into meat using your hands. Add 200 g cold water to the tray and cover with foil. Roast for 2 hours (180°C). Remove foil then roast for a further 20-30 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare bao buns.

Bao Buns

  1. Place flour, yeast, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then blend 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add milk and water then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil. Tip out dough, form into a ball, then place in greased bowl. Cover with cling film then leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 40 minutes). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare mayonnaise.

Wasabi Mayonnaise

  1. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Remove jug.
  2. Place wasabi paste, vinegar, lime juice, sugar, salt, pepper and egg yolks in mixing bowl then blend 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Mix speed 3 while slowly pouring reserved oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades. If mayonnaise is too thick, add 1 Tbsp hot water then blend 20 sec/speed 4. Adjust seasoning as necessary, then transfer to a bowl and chill until needed.

Buns

  1. Tip risen dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide into 30 g balls (approx. 12 balls). Place on 2 greased baking trays with enough space between to double in size. Cover lightly with oiled cling film and leave until doubled in size (approx. 20-30 minutes).
  2. Grease a chopstick or thin wooden spoon handle with oil. Roll each ball of dough into an oval 10 cm long. Place chopstick in middle and fold bun over to create a half moon shape. Slowly pull chopstick out and use to shape rest of buns. Place buns back on trays, lightly cover with oiled cling film and leave to prove for another 10-15 minutes until risen. Meanwhile, rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place 1500 g water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and bring to a boil 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease Varoma dish and tray.
  4. Place 3 buns in prepared Varoma dish and 3 in Varoma tray, leaving space for them to puff up. Place covered Varoma into position then steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Steam remaining buns in batches adding more water as necessary. Keep buns warm after steaming.

To Serve

  1. Slice pork belly thinly. Split steamed buns and spread a dollop of wasabi mayonnaise on each side. Place a couple of slices of pork and some gem lettuce in each bun, close buns and serve.

Useful Items

roasting tin, baking paper, aluminium foil, cling film, large bowl, jug, baking trays, chopsticks, rolling pin, long sharp knife, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • These bao buns are great fun to make. I like to prepare them when I have friends around, serve the steamed buns either pre-assembled in the centre of the table or place all the components in dishes and each person can assemble their own. Alternatively, they are fantastic for parties. The wasabi mayonnaise makes a bit more than needed; keep the remaining wasabi mayo in a container in the fridge for up to three days and use in sandwiches or as a dip with prawn crackers and crudités.
