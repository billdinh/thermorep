Asian-style rice with eggs and vegetables

Ingredients

  • 20 g light sesame oil
  • 100 g onions, cut in halves
  • 150 g carrots, cut in large pieces (4 cm)
  • 150 g bacon cubes
  • 30 g soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 heaped tsp stock paste, homemade (optional)
  • 350 g short grain rice (18-20 minute cooking time)
  • 1200 g water
  • 400 g mixed vegetables (e.g. broccoli in small florets, frozen peas, cauliflower in small florets, green beans cut in pieces, courgettes cut in pieces)
  • 4 eggs

Preparation

  1. Place light sesame oil and onions in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add carrots and chop roughly 2 sec/speed 4.5.
  3. Add bacon cubes, soy sauce and sugar and sauté 7 min/120°C/speed  without measuring cup. Transfer to a large serving bowl, cover and set aside.
  4. Place stock paste in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh rice into it. Add water over rice in mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, weigh vegetables into it and close Varoma lid. Steam 14 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, line Varoma tray with damp baking paper. Whisk eggs in a small bowl.
  5. Carefully open Varoma, stir vegetables to ensure even cooking, insert prepared Varoma tray and pour whisked eggs onto it. Steam 6-7 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Remove Varoma, remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice and vegetables to serving bowl with reserved bacon mixture. Using the spatula, cut eggs into squares (2-3 cm) in the Varoma tray and transfer to serving bowl. Using the spatula, combine all ingredients in serving bowl and serve hot.

Useful Items

baking paper, large serving bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • This dish can be varied endlessly according to the vegetables you have on hand. Place the vegetables that require the longest cooking time first in the Varoma dish, and the others a few minutes later.
  • If one ingredient isn’t fully cooked, transfer the others to the serving bowl and continue to steam remaining ingredient for a few minutes/Varoma/speed 2 before combining with ingredients in serving bowl.
  • If you only have dark sesame oil (from toasted sesame seeds), use groundnut oil or another mild cooking oil in step 1 and add 1 tsp dark sesame oil for flavour. Or omit sesame oil entirely.
  • Replace the soy sauce and sugar with either 60 g soy paste or 60 g hoisin sauce, or omit entirely.
  • Use any type of rice, making sure to adapt cooking time according to packet instructions. For instance for basmati rice, reduce steaming time in step 5 to 12 minutes.
  • You can mix in chopped herbs, spring onions or nuts before serving.
