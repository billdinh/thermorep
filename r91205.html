Potato Pancakes

Ingredients

  • 36 oz potatoes, cut into pieces
  • 6 oz yellow onions, halved
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 oz corn starch
  • 1 oz oat flakes
  • 1 large egg
  • oil, to fry
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1972 kJ / 472 kcal
Protein
8 g
Carbohydrates
52 g
Fat
24 g

Preparation

  1. Place potatoes, onions, lemon juice, salt, black pepper, corn starch, oat flakes and egg into mixing bowl and with aid of spatula chop 12 sec/speed 5.
  2. Pour oil into a hot non-stick frying pan, covering the bottom of the pan with a thin layer, and heat. Ladle 2 heaping tablespoons of mixture per potato pancake into the hot oil and using the spoon, flatten each heap to form pancake shapes (approx. 3¾ in.). Fry on both sides until golden brown and crisp. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot.

Useful Items

paper towel, non-stick frying pan

Hints & Tricks

  • A traditional German dish, served with sweet or savory side dishes depending on the region.
  • As the batter can be prepared in seconds, it is easy to repeat this recipe quickly to increase portions for a party.
  • Serve with apple sauce or for a savory alternative, with sour cream, smoked salmon or bacon.
  • Use fresh potatoes. If you potatoes are well scrubbed, there is no need to peel them.
  • Depending on the kind of potatoes used and how long the batter is left to rest before frying, it may be necessary to add more corn starch or oat flakes or to remove excess moisture from the batter with a spoon or drain in simmering basket placed in another serving bowl.
  • Vegetable potato pancakes: replace ⅓ of the potatoes with vegetables (e.g. carrots, kohlrabi, cauliflower, zucchini etc.) Double the amount of corn starch and oat flakes. Season with ground pepper and serve with a sour cream herb dip.
