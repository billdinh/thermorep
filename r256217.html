Beef 'Burgundy'

Ingredients

  • 750 g beef fillet, trimmed
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 20 g salted butter
  • 4 pieces streaky bacon (rindless), cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 8 pickling onions
  • 200 g button mushrooms, cut into halves
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • 100 g red Burgundy wine (see Tips)
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 2 carrots, peeled
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 300 g water
  • 200 g potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 3 tbsp plain flour (see Tips)
  • salt, to season
  • crusty bread, to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh whole beef fillet into it. Set aside.
  2. Place parsley and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil, butter, bacon, onions, mushrooms, bay leaves and pepper and cook 3 min/Varoma//speed . Meanwhile, cut beef fillet into 3 cm pieces.
  4. Add red wine and tomato paste and cook 2 min/120°C//speed , without measuring cup. Meanwhile, cut carrots into 1.5 cm slices.
  5. Add stock paste, water, potatoes and carrot slices and cook 15 min/120°C//speed 2 or until potatoes are cooked. Meanwhile, place flour into a snaplock bag. Add reserved beef, season with extra salt and pepper and toss to coat.
  6. Add beef and cook 3 min/120°C//speed 1 or until beef is cooked to your liking. Garnish with extra parsley and serve warm with crusty bread (see Tips).

Useful Items

snaplock bag, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • A well-known, traditional French recipe, Beef Burgundy (or boeuf bourguignon) originates from the Burgundy region of France and is made with the red wine produced in the region. Slowly simmering the beef in wine over an extended period of time helps tenderise the meat, but this "cheat's version" produces a tasty dish in less than an hour.
  • You can replace the Burgundy wine with any good-quality red wine in this recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can replace plain flour with gluten free flour, if desired
  • Serve with mashed potato instead of bread, if desired.
