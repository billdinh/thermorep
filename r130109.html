Quinoa, Banana, Cranberry and Chia Granola Bars

Ingredients

  • 90 g quinoa, puffed
  • 80 g rolled oats, gluten free if desired
  • 20 g chia seeds
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 35 g blanched almonds
  • 35 g pecan nuts
  • 50 g dried cranberries
  • 30 g sultanas
  • 2 ripe bananas, cut in pieces (approx. 170 g)
  • 50 g almond butter
  • 40 g agave syrup
  • 50 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and line a square cake tin (22 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place quinoa, oats, chia seeds, cinnamon and salt in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place almonds and pecans in mixing bowl then chop 2 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add cranberries and sultanas then transfer to bowl with quinoa and oats.
  5. Place bananas, almond butter and agave syrup in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 5 sec/speed 5.
  6. Return dry ingredients to mixing bowl and combine 20 sec//speed 3.5. Transfer to prepared tin and press down firmly with back of a spoon to form an even layer. Bake for 25 minutes (170°C) until golden brown at the edges. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  7. Allow bake to cool in tin before removing. Meanwhile, place chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3 min/40°C/speed 0.5.
  8. Drizzle melted chocolate over cooled granola bake and allow to set before cutting into 10 bars. Serve at room temperature or store in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

square cake tin (22 cm), baking paper, airtight container, long sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Store any leftover bars in the freezer, wrapped up well. Defrost fully before serving.
  • Use any dried fruit or nuts as desired, creating many different flavour combinations. Cut any larger pieces of dried fruit, such as dried apricots, into pieces (1 cm) before adding to mixing bowl in step 4.
