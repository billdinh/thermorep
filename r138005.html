Bot chien with pickled vegetables

Ingredients

Rice flour cake

  • 1 tsp vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1120 g water
  • 210 g rice flour
  • 1 tbsp arrowroot flour
  • ¼ tsp salt

Pickled vegetables

  • 100 g white wine vinegar
  • 70 g sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 50 g carrot, peeled into ribbons
  • 50 g daikon, peeled into ribbons

Dipping sauce

  • 1 fresh long red chilli, trimmed and deseeded if preferred
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp black (Chinese) vinegar
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar

Assembly

  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices

Preparation

Rice flour cake

  1. Grease a slice tin (14 x 22 cm) (ensure tin fits comfortably into the Varoma dish) and set aside. Carefully grease mixing bowl.
  2. Place 320 g of the water, rice flour, arrowroot flour, oil and salt into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 3. Transfer paste into prepared tin and, with oiled hands, flatten surface. To facilitate cleaning, soak your mixing bowl immediately in hot soapy water after removing flour paste. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place remaining 800 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and place slice tin into Varoma dish. Secure Varoma lid and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Carefully remove tin and set aside to cool for 20 minutes. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pickled vegetables

  1. Place vinegar, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl. Place bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh carrots and daikon into it. Stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dipping sauce

  1. Place chilli into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, vinegar and sugar and mix 20 sec/speed 3 or until sugar dissolves. Transfer into a serving bowl and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Remove rice cake from tin and cut into small rectangles (5 x 2 cm). Transfer into a bowl. Add soy sauce and toss to combine.
  2. Place a heavy non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add 2 teaspoons of the oil. Add ¼ of the rice flour cakes and fry on both sides (approx. 5 minutes each side) until crispy and brown. Group cakes together in the pan to minimize spacing between them.
  3. Crack 1 of the eggs on top of the rice flour cake, then working quickly break the yolk to bind the cakes together. Sprinkle with ¼ of the spring onions and cook for 1-2 minutes, until egg is slightly set. Gently turn the cake over and cook for 1 minute. Transfer onto a serving plate and cover to keep warm. Repeat with remaining ingredients to form another 3 cakes.
  4. Using the simmering basket drain and discard pickling liquid from vegetables. Serve Bot chien with pickled vegetables and dipping sauce.

Useful Items

slice tin (14 x 22 cm), plastic wrap, bowl, serving bowl, large non-stick frying pan, kitchen towel, serving plate, refrigerator, stove top
