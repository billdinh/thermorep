Cheesy Animal Rolls

Ingredients

  • 150 g Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g milk
  • 100 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 Tbsp caster sugar
  • 500 g strong white bread flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • oil, for greasing
  • 16 raisins, for eyes

Preparation

  1. Place Cheddar in mixing bowl and grate 2 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place milk, water, yeast and sugar in mixing bowl then warm 1 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Add flour and salt, mix 30 sec/speed 4 then knead 3 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  4. Add reserved grated cheese and knead 2 min/. Tip out into prepared bowl, cover with greased cling film then leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1½ hours).
  5. After this time, preheat oven to 180°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper. Divide risen dough into 8 equal-sized pieces. Shape each piece of dough into an animal (see tips). Ask an adult to cut details with scissors and make eyes using raisins, pushing in firmly. Place on prepared trays.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes (180°C) until rolls are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer to a wire rack to cool before serving with sandwich fillings of choice or butter and jam.

Useful Items

cling film, scissors, wire rack, baking trays, baking paper, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Next time you’re looking for an activity to do with the children, try this fun and creative bread recipe. The simple dough results in deliciously light and cheesy bread rolls that your children can shape into whatever animals take their fancy - let their imagination run wild!
  • For a hedgehog roll: squeeze and pull out one end of a dough ball to make a snout, forming a shape similar to a pine cone. Ask an adult to carefully snip all over the back of the hedgehog using scissors to create the spikes. Firmly push in raisins to make eyes.
  • For a mouse roll: take ⅓ of a dough ball then roll out between your hands to form the tail. Roll remaining ⅔ dough into a ball, then squeeze and pull out one end to make a nose, forming a shape similar to a pine cone. Push the tail firmly into the back of the body. Ask an adult to cut two deep slits in the front using scissors to form the ears then firmly push in raisins to make eyes.
  • For a turtle roll: divide a dough ball in half then divide one piece into 6, with one piece slightly larger than the others to form the head. Roll all the dough pieces into balls then attach the head piece to one end of the largest ball. Arrange 4 balls around the large one to form the flippers then slightly flatten remaining ball and attach at the end for the tail. Ask an adult to carefully use a sharp knife to make markings on the body to form the shell, then firmly push in raisins to make eyes.
