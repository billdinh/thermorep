Florentine-style Spelt Pizza - Pizza fiorentina con farina di farro

Ingredients

Dough

  • 270 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 heaped tsp dried instant yeast
  • 60 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing and drizzling
  • 500 g spelt flour
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 100 g mozzarella, cut in pieces
  • 100 g feta cheese
  • 150 g kale, shredded, large stalks removed
  • 4 medium eggs

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add remaining 220 g water, oil, flour and salt then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film (see tip) and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 45 minutes).

Topping

  1. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 220°C and line 2 large baking trays with baking paper(see tip).
  2. Place mozzarella and feta in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to simmering basket and leave to drain for 10 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into 2 equal pieces, then place one on each tray and push out into an oval shape, using the tips of your fingers. Drizzle oil over each base then divide kale between them (leaving a space in the centre of each half of the bases). Scatter grated cheese evenly over the top then bake for 4-6 minutes (220°C) until starting to brown.
  4. Remove pizzas from oven and carefully crack 2 eggs into spaces on each. Return to oven and bake for 3-5 minutes (220°C) until edges of pizzas are brown and eggs are set.
  5. Drizzle pizzas with extra oil then serve hot, cut in slices.

Useful Items

baking trays, large bowl, cling film, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This unusual pizza is a move away from more traditional options in terms of both its base and toppings. Spelt flour is growing in popularity as a healthier alternative to refined white flour. It’s lower in gluten and gives a crunchy texture to a pizza crust. The toppings on this pizza are also lighter, with shredded kale acting as the base combined with salty feta cheese and mozzarella. The finishing touch? Two eggs are cracked into each half of the pizzas and baked for a few minutes, leaving the runny yolks free to ooze into every bite once the pizzas are cut.
  • Lightly grease cling film to prevent it from sticking to the dough during the resting and leavening stages.
  • There is no need to line non-stick baking trays.
