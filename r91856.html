Cauliflower Stuffed Parathas

Ingredients

Dough

  • 140 g water
  • 300 g chapati flour
  • 30 g ghee
  • 15 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Cauliflower filling

  • 1 fresh coriander leaves
  • 200 g cauliflower florets
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 5 g fresh root ginger, sliced in rounds (2 mm)
  • 1 - 2 fresh green chillies, seeds removed if desired
  • 20 g ghee, plus extra for frying
  • ¼ tsp cumin seeds
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 1 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, ghee, oil and salt then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 4 min/. Tip dough out and bring together into a ball. Place in a large bowl, cover with cling film and set aside while continuing with recipe.

Cauliflower filling

  1. Wash and dry mixing bowl. Place coriander leaves in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Place cauliflower in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place garlic, ginger and chillies in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 10.
  4. Add ghee, cumin seeds and cayenne pepper then cook 3 min/80°C/speed .
  5. Add reserved cauliflower and cook 4 min/80°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook again 1 min/80°C/speed 1. Transfer to a fine sieve and strain off any excess liquid then set mixture aside to cool.
  6. Add reserved coriander to cooled cauliflower mixture.
  7. Divide rested dough into six balls and roll out into circles (Ø 10 cm). Place a circle in the palm of your hand then place 2 Tbsp cauliflower mixture in the centre of each circle. Pinch edges to the middle, encasing the filling and press together to form a ball. Carefully and gently, use your fingertips to flatten each circle (approx. Ø 20 cm).
  8. Heat a frying pan over a medium heat. Fry parathas in a little ghee until golden brown, approx. 3-4 minutes on each side.
  9. Keep breads warm while cooking remaining parathas, adding extra ghee each time. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, fine sieve, rolling pin, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • If you can't get chapatti flour, use bread flour (150 g white and 150 g wholemeal).
  • Brush excess flour off after rolling out.
  • Best made just before serving.
  • Can be frozen - reheat wrapped in foil in a moderate oven.
