Fish tacos with Kewpie mayonnaise

Ingredients

Kewpie mayonnaise

  • 250 g grapeseed oil
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp rice wine vinegar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp wasabi paste

Fish tacos

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into halves
  • 5 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes
  • 500 - 600 g firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces 7-8 cm (see Tips)
  • 60 g rice wine vinegar
  • 40 g white sugar
  • 500 g water, plus extra 1 tablespoon
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into julienne
  • 2 - 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 4 radishes, cut into julienne
  • 8 tortillas (small)
  • fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

Kewpie mayonnaise

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Set jug aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks, vinegars, salt and wasabi paste into mixing bowl and whisk stop when ready/speed 4, gradually pouring reserved oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it trickle into mixing bowl to emulsify. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a sealable storage container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Fish tacos

  1. Place garlic, ginger and dill into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add fish sauce, turmeric and chilli flakes and blend 3 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a large bowl.
  3. Place large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh fish into it, stir to coat. Set aside to marinate for 30 minutes.
  4. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh vinegar, sugar and 1 tablespoon of the water into it and stir to combine. Add carrots, spring onions/shallots and radishes and stir to combine. Set aside for 15 minutes.
  5. Place 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and place marinated fish onto Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 1, until fish is cooked.
  6. Spread 2 teaspoons of the reserved mayonnaise over each tortilla. Top with steamed fish and pickled vegetables, then garnish with coriander to serve.

Useful Items

jug, sealable container, large bowl, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend a firm white fish for this recipe such as ling or snapper.
  • For best results, Kewpie mayonnaise ingredients should be at room temperature. You can store leftover mayonnaise in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • You can also use the Miso coleslaw in these tacos (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip). Just replace the pickled vegetables with the coleslaw.
