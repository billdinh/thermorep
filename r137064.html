Sambhar

Ingredients

  • 180 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 80 g eggplant, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 80 g fresh green beans, cut into halves
  • 80 g carrot, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 60 g pumpkin, peeled and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 tsp canola oil
  • ½ tsp black mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ fresh long red chilli, trimmed and cut into halves
  • ¼ tsp asafoetida (see Tips)
  • 10 fresh curry leaves
  • 50 g fresh tomatoes
  • 25 g ground coriander
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 250 g toor dal (see Tips)
  • 1000 g water
  • 40 g tamarind paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp sea salt, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place eggplant, green beans, carrot and pumpkin into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place oil, mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, cumin seeds, chilli, asafoetida and curry leaves into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1
  4. Add tomatoes, coriander, chilli powder, turmeric and reserved onion and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add dal and water and cook 45 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add reserved vegetables and cook 20 min/100°C//speed .
  7. Add tamarind paste and salt and stir with aid of spatula until combined. Transfer into a serving dish and serve warm. 

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • This lentil-based vegetable stew is often served with rice, Rava idli or Roti (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip).
  • To make your own Tamarind paste, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Asafoetida comes from the root of a perennial Indian herb. It has a strong odour, but the aroma softens as it cooks, and it tastes similar to onion and garlic, enhancing the savoury flavour of the dish. It helps reduce flatulence and constipation, and reduce the growth of microflora in the gut. A little goes a long way, so you only need to use a small amount. You can find asafoetida at Indian grocers or online.
  • Toor dal (also known as split pigeon peas) are a split, yellow lentil and can be found in some supermarkets and most Indian food stores.
