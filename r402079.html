Kalguksu (Chicken Noodle Soup)

Ingredients

Dough

  • 230 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 30 g cornflour
  • 130 g water
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Chicken

  • 80 g onions, sliced (1 cm)
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for cooking noodles
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 350 g chicken breasts, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 8 - 10 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 2 ½ Tbsp sesame oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Soup

  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced (5 mm), for garnishing
  • Korean chilli paste (gochujang), for serving (optional)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, cornflour, water, vegetable oil and salt in mixing bowl then knead 5 min/. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Chicken

  1. Place onions, water and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, weigh in chicken and add garlic. Steam 20 min/100°C/speed .
  2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer chicken to a large bowl, reserving garlic cloves separately. Strain stock through simmering basket into a large jug. Discard contents of simmering basket.
  3. Place reserved steamed garlic, sesame oil, pepper and ½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add reserved cooked chicken and shred 4 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.

Noodles

  1. On a well-floured surface, halve dough and set one half aside. Roll out to a large rectangle (36 cm x 26 cm x 2 mm). Sprinkle over plenty of flour then fold bottom third up to cover middle third and top third down over this. Cut vertically into thin noodles (approx. 3 mm). Sprinkle over more flour then separate with fingers. Repeat with remaining half of dough then set aside.

Soup

  1. Place reserved chicken stock in mixing bowl and heat 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, in a large saucepan, bring to the boil plenty of water with salt added. Once boiling, cook noodles over a medium heat for 2-3 minutes then drain in Varoma dish. Divide noodles between 4 serving bowls.
  2. Pour broth over noodles and top each with 2-3 Tbsp reserved shredded chicken. Drizzle with sesame oil, garnish with spring onions and Korean chilli paste (if using) then serve hot.
