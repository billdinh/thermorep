Salmon Fishcakes

Ingredients

  • 150 g white bread, torn in pieces
  • 800 g water
  • 650 g floury potatoes, peeled, diced (1 cm)
  • 450 g fresh salmon fillets, skin removed
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 Tbsp sunflower oil
  • 1 lemon, cut in wedges, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place bread in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl then insert simmering basket. Add potatoes then steam 22 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Place salmon fillets in Varoma dish, cover then place into position. Steam potatoes and salmon 8 min/Varoma/speed 2. Set Varoma aside and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Discard steaming water then wash and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place cooked potato, 100 g reserved breadcrumbs, lemon juice, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. Flake steamed salmon into big pieces then stir into potato mix with spatula. Transfer to a bowl, allow to cool, then cover with cling film and set aside in fridge for at least 1 hour.
  6. Once chilled, use wet hands to shape mixture into twelve patties then coat patties in remaining breadcrumbs.
  7. Heat a non-stick frying pan with oil over a medium heat then fry fishcakes for 4-5 minutes on each side. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

Useful Items

cling film, non-stick frying pan, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Homemade fishcakes taste so much better than the shop-bought ones – and of course you know exactly what’s going in them, unlike many packaged ones where the fish content is very low. These make a lovely lunch or light supper with a crisp green salad and a wedge of lemon.
  • The mixture for these fishcakes can be made up to 24 hours in advance. Prepare to the end of step 5, then shape and cook before serving.
