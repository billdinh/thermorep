Harissa lamb with green couscous

Ingredients

  • 200 g couscous
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 850 g hot water
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 4 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 50 g shelled unsalted pistachio nuts
  • 150 g red onion, cut into 12 wedges, segments separated
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground paprika
  • 500 - 600 g lamb rump steak, trimmed and cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 - 2 tbsp harissa paste, to taste (see Tip)
  • 8 - 10 Dutch carrots, trimmed and cut into halves lengthways
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices (5 mm)
  • honey, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh couscous, 20 g of the oil and 350 g of the water into it. Add 1 teaspoon of the salt and set aside.
  2. Place mint, coriander and pistachios into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 20 g oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Add onions, cumin, paprika and remaining ½ teaspoon salt and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 2.
  5. Add lamb and harissa and cook 4-5 min/120°C//speed 2, without measuring cup, until lamb is cooked to your liking. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  6. Place remaining 500 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place carrots into Varoma dish. Line Varoma tray with a piece of wet, well-wrung baking paper. Fluff up reserved couscous with a fork to separate the grains. Add spring onions/shallots to couscous, then spread evenly over Varoma tray with spatula. Place Varoma dish and tray into positon, secure Varoma lid and steam 8 min/Varoma//speed 4 or until carrots are cooked to your liking.
  7. Transfer couscous onto a serving plate. Add reserved herb and nut mixture and stir to combine. Drizzle carrots with honey. Serve green couscous topped with lamb and carrots.

Useful Items

bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, fork, serving plate, baking paper

Hints & Tricks

  • Harissa is a spicy chilli paste from North Africa, and can be found in most supermarkets (in the international or sauces aisle) and gourmet food stores. To make your own harissa, please refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
