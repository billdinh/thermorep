Biscuits with date, pistachio and cardamom filling

Ingredients

Dough

  • 120 g water
  • 110 g butter, cut in pieces
  • 250 g flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt

Filling

  • 30 g unsalted pistachio nuts
  • 10 g butter
  • 300 g pitted dates
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom

Assembling and baking

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 20 g sesame seeds

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid, weigh water into it and set aside.
  2. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour, baking powder, salt and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 4 sec/speed 4. The mixture should look like bread crumbs.
  5. Mix speed 3 while slowly pouring reserved water onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades to obtain a soft buttery dough. Transfer dough onto a piece of cling film and refrigerate for at least 15 minutes. Clean mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place pistachio nuts into clean and dry mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add butter, dates, cardamom and mix 8 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a work surface, divide filling into 4 equal portions and shape each portion into a thin string (approx. 20 cm long) and set aside.

Assembling and baking

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Divide dough into 4 equal pieces and using a rolling pin, roll out each piece on a lightly floured surface or silicon mat, shaping dough into a rectangle (20 cm x 7 cm and ½ cm thick). Place date paste string in the middle and roll the dough around it to enclose. Transfer the rolls to prepared baking tray keeping the seam at the bottom, brush with an egg beaten with water and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for 10-15 minutes (200°C) or until lightly golden.
  3. Allow date rolls to cool down and then cut gently diagonally with a bread knife into pieces (approx. 1.5 cm). Serve as desired.

Useful Items

jug, cling film, rolling pin, bread knife, baking tray and paper, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with coffee or tea.
  • The filling can also be used to fill maamoul.
