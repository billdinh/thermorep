Honey poached pears with chocolate sauce and nut crumble

Ingredients

Nut crumble

  • 20 g pecans
  • 10 g walnuts
  • 20 g shredded coconut
  • 20 g rolled oats
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp honey

Honey poached pears

  • 4 pears (approx. 200 g each), peeled
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 1 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 vanilla bean, seeds scraped, seeds and pod reserved
  • 50 g honey
  • ¼ tsp salt

Chocolate sauce

  • 100 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 50 g full cream milk
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • fresh mint leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Nut crumble

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place pecans and walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add all remaining crumble ingredients and combine 10 sec//speed 6. Transfer onto prepared baking tray and mix well using spatula to ensure crumble is evenly spread over baking tray. Bake for 10-12 minutes (160°C), or until toasted. Set aside to cool. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

Honey poached pears

  1. Slice off the bottom of pears, so they sit flat. Place pears upright into simmering basket (pears should sit comfortably in basket and not obstruct hole in mixing bowl lid. If pears are crowded, place 2 of the pears into Varoma dish). Set aside.
  2. Place water, cinnamon, ginger, vanilla bean seeds and pod, honey and salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket (place Varoma into position, if required) and cook 20-25 min/Varoma//speed 1, until pears are softened but still holding their shape.  Check to see if pears are cooked; if still firm cook for a further 5-10 min/Varoma//speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer pears into a large bowl.
  3. To thicken syrup, cook 20 min/Varoma//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Strain syrup through a fine mesh sieve over pears, then allow pears and syrup to cool completely, turning pears regularly in the syrup as they cool (approx. 30 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate sauce

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining sauce ingredients and heat 1 min 30 sec - 2 min/50°C/speed 3, or until melted. Transfer into a small bowl.
  3. Carefully dip cooled pears in chocolate sauce and place into refrigerator to set (approx. 5 minutes). Serve pears with nut crumble and garnish with mint leaves.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, large bowl, fine mesh sieve, small bowl , oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important that the pears do not obstruct the hole in the mixing bowl lid. If the pears are crowded, it will take longer for them to cook.
  • To save time, start on step 4 while the nut crumble is in the oven. You can also drizzle the pears with chocolate just before serving.
