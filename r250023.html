Lamb cutlets with spinach and pea purée

Ingredients

  • 1 lamb rack, trimmed (see Tip)
  • ¼ tsp salt, plus extra for seasoning
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra for seasoning
  • 40 g olive oil, plus 1 tablespoon for frying
  • 120 g red wine
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 10 g pickled capers, drained
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 sprig fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh mint, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh basil, leaves only
  • 500 g water, plus extra for cooking
  • 450 g frozen peas
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 120 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp unsalted butter

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Season lamb with salt and pepper. Place a frying pan over high heat, add 1 tablespoon of the oil and sear lamb until browned. Transfer lamb onto prepared baking tray and set aside.
  3. Drain and discard any excess fat from the frying pan. Return frying pan to the heat and add wine. Cook wine for 2-3 minutes, until slightly reduced. Add stock paste and ½ cup water. Stir and simmer gently until reduced to a light jus (approx. 5 minutes). Set frying pan with jus aside.
  4. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place remaining 40 g oil, capers, garlic, parsley, mint and basil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  6. Scrape down sides of bowl with spatula and chop for a further 5 sec/speed 7. Coat the top of the reserved lamb rack with herb paste. Place lamb into oven and roast for 15-20 minutes (200°C) or until cooked to your liking. Cover with aluminium foil and set aside to rest while making spinach and pea purée.
  7. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, weigh peas into it and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  8. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh spinach into it. Secure Varoma lid and and cook 8 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until peas are just cooked and spinach wilts. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Discard cooking liquid.
  9. Place peas, spinach, lemon zest, cream, nutmeg, reserved Parmesan and remaining salt and pepper into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 7.
  10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and heat purée 2 min/100°C/speed 2.
  11. Place frying pan with reserved jus over medium-high heat. Add butter and whisk until melted and jus is smooth and glossy.
  12. Carve reserved lamb rack into individual cutlets. Divide purée between serving plates and top with lamb cutlets. Drizzle with reserved jus just before serving.

Useful Items

frying pan, bowl, serving plates, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), aluminium foil, whisk, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Ask your butcher to trim your lamb rack for you - it's not hard to do yourself, but it can be time consuming.
  • To make your own Vegetable stock paste, please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook.
