Gratin dauphinois

Ingredients

  • butter, for greasing
  • 30 g Swiss cheese (optional)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1200 g potatoes, peeled and cut into round slices (2-3 mm)
  • 250 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Grease a gratin dish (approx 20 x 25 cm) (see tips), set aside.
  2. Place Swiss cheese (optional) into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer cheese into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add all remaining ingredients , except cheese, and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Pour mixture into prepared dish and sprinkle with reserved grated cheese (optional). Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown on top. Serve hot.

Useful Items

gratin dish (approx. 25 x 20 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A gratin dish is the traditional French casserole dish, however any rectangular casserole dish can also be used.
  • This dish is the perfect accompaniment to roasted or grilled meats.
  • Any hard cheese can be used in place of the Swiss cheese.
  • Originally from Dauphiné (French Alps) the authentic Gratin dauphinois is made with cream but without the cheese.
  • Substitute the cream with a mixture of cream and full cream milk.
