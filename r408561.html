Orange, raspberry and coconut jellies

Ingredients

  • virgin coconut oil, for greasing

Kefir coconut layer

  • 270 g coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp gelatine powder (see Tips)
  • 60 g raw sugar (see Tips)
  • 250 g kefir milk
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract

Orange raspberry layer

  • 250 g filtered water
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • 3 tbsp gelatine powder (see Tips)
  • 300 g orange, flesh only (no white pith), cut into pieces
  • 125 g fresh raspberries

Preparation

  1. Grease a square glass dish (22 cm) with coconut oil and set aside.

Kefir coconut layer

  1. Place coconut milk into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Add gelatine and sugar and mix 45 sec/speed 4, until gelatine dissolves.
  3. Add kefir milk and vanilla and mix 15 sec/speed 3, until combined. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain mixture into prepared dish. Place into refrigerator until set (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Orange raspberry layer

  1. Place water and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Add gelatine and mix 45 sec/speed 4, until gelatine dissolves.
  3. Add orange pieces and raspberries, then blend 15 sec/speed 8. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain mixture into a jug. Remove Kefir coconut layer from refrigerator, ensure it is set enough to support orange mixture, then pour mixture evenly over layer. Return to refrigerator until set (approx. 1 hour). Cut into small squares to serve.

Useful Items

square glass dish (22 cm), fine-mesh sieve, jug, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because these jellies include some added sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before serving them.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small.
  • Gelatine is of current research interest for its possible role in improving gut health - just make sure you buy a quality, grass-fed (preferably organic) gelatine if you can find one.
  • You can replace the sugar with honey in this recipe. However, honey is not suitable for children under 12 months.
  • The base is like a fruit frappé jelly! As it cools, the orange and raspberry layer will separate into 2 distinct layers.
