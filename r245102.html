Chocolate and Hazelnut Gelato - Gelato al cioccolato e nocciola

Ingredients

  • 170 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 90 g toasted hazelnuts
  • 390 g double cream
  • 210 g whole milk
  • 130 g granulated sugar
  • 3 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • 5 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
Nutrition

Calories
2502 kJ / 603 kcal

Preparation

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add cream, milk, sugar, reserved chocolate, cocoa powder, egg yolks and salt then cook 6 min/80°C/speed 4. Pour through a fine sieve into a shallow freezer-proof container then stir in vanilla extract. Chill in fridge for 8 hours or overnight for flavours to develop then freeze until solid.
  4. Remove frozen mixture from freezer, turn out onto a chopping board then cut into 4 cm squares. Place ⅓ frozen squares in mixing bowl and crush 15 sec/speed 6 then churn 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl.
  5. Place another ⅓ frozen squares in mixing bowl and crush 15 sec/speed 6 then churn 10 sec/speed 4. Add to bowl with churned gelato.
  6. Place remaining ⅓ frozen squares in mixing bowl and crush 15 sec/speed 6 then churn 10 sec/speed 4. Add to bowl with churned gelato.
  7. Serve immediately in scoops or return to freezer until required.

Useful Items

fine sieve, freezer-proof container, long sharp knife, fridge, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Frozen desserts have featured in Italian cuisine for many centuries, however, it wasn’t until the 1920s, in the city of Varese that gelato, as we know it today, was born. Nowadays gelato forms a huge part of Italian culture, with more than 5,000 ice cream parlours across the country! Similar to ice cream, gelato is made with a base of milk, cream and sugar, then flavoured with fruit and nut purées as well as other flavourings. A subtle difference between ice cream and gelato is that the latter is typically made using less cream, if any. Gelato is also generally richer and more dense because it is churned at a slower speed.
