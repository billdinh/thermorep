Swedish meatballs with gravy

Ingredients

Meatballs

  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 slices bread (approx. 60 g), torn into pieces
  • 60 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • ¼ tsp ground allspice
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • 500 g lean beef mince

Gravy

  • 50 g salted butter, cut into pieces
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 400 g water
  • 2 - 3 tsp Meat stock paste, to taste (see Tips)
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • fresh dill, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

Meatballs

  1. Place dill, garlic, bread and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add allspice, nutmeg, pepper, salt, egg and mince and combine 30 sec//speed 5, mixing with aid of spatula. Roll mixture into approx. 30 balls (2 teaspoons per ball) and transfer onto a plate. Cover and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Gravy

  1. Place butter, flour, water, stock paste, cream, Worcestershire sauce and mustard into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/90°C/speed 4.
  2. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Add meatballs and cook for approx. 6-8 minutes, turning, until browned all over. Garnish meatballs with dill and serve with gravy and sides of choice (see Tips).

Useful Items

plate, large frying pan, plastic wrap, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Meat stock paste recipe.
  • The Swedes traditionally serve their meatballs with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam, but you can serve them with any kind of starch (such as hot chips, pasta or rice). You can find lingonberry jam at specialty food stores or replace it with cranberry sauce or chutney.
  • You can replace half of the beef mince with 250 g pork mince, if desired.
  • To keep meatballs warm while you make your side dishes, transfer meatballs and gravy into a thermal serving bowl and cover to keep warm.
