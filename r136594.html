Iced Ring Biscuits

Ingredients

Dough

  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 25 g cornflour

Icing

  • 160 g icing sugar
  • 3 Tbsp water, hot
  • food colouring pastes, as desired

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place caster sugar, butter, egg yolk and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add flour and cornflour then pulse Turbo/2 sec/4 times. Tip out, form into a flattened ball and wrap in cling film then set aside in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  3. Towards the end of resting time, preheat oven to 180°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  4. Roll out chilled dough on a well floured surface to 4 mm thick. Using a large round pastry cutter (Ø 7 cm), cut out circles, then make into rings by cutting out centres using a smaller round cutter (Ø 3 cm), re-rolling any trimmings.
  5. Transfer rings to prepared baking trays and bake for 8-10 minutes (180°C) until slightly firm to the touch and still pale. Allow to cool on trays.

Icing

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 80 g icing sugar then remove bowl. Place another bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in remaining 80 g icing sugar then remove from lid. Add 1½ Tbsp hot water to each bowl and mix until a spreadable consistency (adding extra water if neccessary). Colour each bowl of icing with your choice of food colouring.
  2. Transfer 2 Tbsp of one colour into a small piping bag fitted with a 1-2 mm plain nozzle.
  3. Dip top surface of a cooled biscuit in the other colour to that in the piping bag, then immediately pipe straight lines across biscuit in second colour of icing. Feather the icing by drawing a cocktail stick across the biscuit perpendicular to the lines of icing. Repeat this process until half of the biscuits are iced then ice remaining biscuits the opposite way around.
  4. Leave to set before serving, or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

cocktail sticks, 2 baking trays and baking paper, palette knife, cling film, round pastry cutter (Ø 7 cm), round pastry cutter (Ø 3 cm), rolling pin, 2 bowls, piping bag and nozzle, airtight container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

Another party classic you probably hadn’t thought of making at home, but these are much easier than you’d think – and great fun to decorate.
