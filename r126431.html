Plum and raspberry cobbler

Ingredients

  • 700 g plums, stones removed and flesh cut into 6 wedges
  • 300 g raspberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 ½ tbsp brown sugar (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp orange juice
  • 110 g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 80 g wholemeal spelt flour (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 90 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 eggs
  • milk, for brushing
  • pouring (whipping) cream, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C.
  2. Place an ovenproof baking dish (approx. 22 cm) onto mixing bowl lid and weigh plums and raspberries into it. Sprinkle 2 teaspoons of the brown sugar over fruit and toss lightly to coat.
  3. Drizzle orange juice over fruit, cover with aluminium foil and bake for 15 minutes (160°C). Remove foil and bake for a further 10-15 minutes (160°C), or until fruit is almost tender.
  4. Place flours, baking powder, butter, cinnamon and remaining 1 tablespoon sugar into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add eggs and knead 10-12 sec/, until just combined.
  6. Transfer mixture onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Gently shape into a ball, then press into a flat disc (approx. 2 cm thickness). Using a round cookie cutter (5 cm), cut out rounds from dough. Re-roll pastry off cuts and cut out remaining rounds (approx. 11 rounds total).
  7. Arrange rounds on top of fruit, pressing down gently, and brush with a little milk. Bake for 30-35 minutes (160°C), or until golden and cooked through. Drizzle with cream (optional) and serve warm.

Useful Items

ovenproof baking dish, aluminium foil, silicone bread mat, round cookie cutter (5 cm), pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this recipe – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
  • If you don’t have wholemeal spelt flour on hand, you can use wholemeal plain flour instead.
