Slow cooked Mexican beef

Ingredients

Mexican spice mix

  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 12 small dried chillies
  • 2 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 4 tbsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp sweet paprika
  • 4 dried bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp granulated garlic
  • 2 tbsp ground cinnamon

Slow cooked beef

  • 1 onion, cut into quarters
  • 20 g oil
  • 350 g chuck steak, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 tsp meat stock paste (see Tips)
  • 110 g water
  • 4 pitted dates
  • 1 tsp peanut butter

Green chilli salsa

  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 30 g pepitas
  • 20 g pecan nuts
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, white and green part, cut into pieces
  • ½ - 1 fresh green chilli, deseeded if preferred and cut into halves
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, roots and stems included, chopped
  • ½ lime, zest only, no white pith
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 green capsicum, deseeded and cut into pieces (approx. 250 g)
  • 1 jalapeño chilli, cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 20 g lime juice (approx. 1 lime)
  • 60 g Cheddar cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 4 corn tortillas, warmed
  • sour cream , to serve

Preparation

Mexican spice mix

  1. Place all Mexican spice ingredients into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool slightly then  insert measuring cup and mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Allow to cool completely then transfer into a sealable container to store until ready to use.

Slow cooked beef

  1. Place onion and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add steak, stock paste, water, pitted dates and 2 teaspoons reserved Mexican spice mix and cook 60 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add peanut butter and blend 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer beef into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Green chilli salsa

  1. Place sesame seeds, pepitas, pecans and cumin seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool slightly then mill 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, spring onions/shallots, green chilli, coriander, lime zest and olive oil  into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add green capsicum and jalapeño chilli and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add pepper, lime juice and reserved nut and seed mixture and combine 10 sec//speed 1. Transfer into serving bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place Cheddar cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7.
  7. To serve, place beef inside warm tortillas then top with cheese, green chilli salsa and sour cream.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Meat stock paste recipe.
  • You can substitute 1 jalapeño chilli for 20 g marinated jalapeño chillies.
  • The smoky, fragrant spices combined with the heat from the jalapeño chilli will awaken your senses. This recipe uses a lot of different ingredients but the fresh and fragrant flavours are well worth it!
  • The sour cream in this recipe can be replaced with Yoghurt and jalapeño dressing.
  • If cooking for one, use any leftover beef in a sandwich or wrap the next day.  Leftover salsa can be served as a dip or tossed through a rice or pasta salad.
  • Store leftover Mexican spice mix in an airtight container to use in other dishes.
  • Corn tortillas are not always gluten free, as some use a combination of wheat and corn to create a softer end result.  Select certified gluten free corn tortillas to make this a gluten free recipe.
  • You can make your own tortillas in the Thermomix.  Visit the online Recipe Community (recipecommunity.com.au) for free tortilla recipes.
