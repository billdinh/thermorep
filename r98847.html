Golden baklava

Ingredients

Baklava

  • 200 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • 1 vanilla bean
  • 100 g raw sugar
  • 150 g shelled and unsalted pistachios
  • 150 g walnuts
  • 375 g filo pastry

Syrup

  • 220 g raw sugar
  • ¼ lemon, zest and juice
  • 80 g honey
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • 70 g water
  • 4 drops rose water, to taste (optional)
  • 4 drops orange blossom water, to taste (optional)

Preparation

Baklava

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 5 min/50°C/speed 1. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place cinnamon quill, vanilla bean and 30 g of sugar into mixing bowl  and mill 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add pistachios, walnuts and remaining 70 g of sugar and chop 5 sec/speed 5.  Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Grease a rectangular baking tin (31 x 20 x 4 cm) and set aside.  Cut filo into three rectangular portions, to fit prepared baking tin. Keep filo covered with a damp kitchen towel to prevent pastry from drying out.
  5. Layer one filo portion into prepared tin, brushing liberally with reserved melted butter. Spread half of reserved nut mixture over pastry. Repeat process with second portion of filo, brushing with reserved melted butter and then topping with remaining half of nut mixture. Top with final portion of filo, brushing liberally with melted butter. Place into refrigerator to firm for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  7. Cut cold baklava in a diamond pattern, avoiding cutting through bottom layer of pastry (this will prevent syrup from collecting on base of tray). Bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or until golden.

Syrup

  1. Meanwhile, to make syrup, place sugar and lemon zest into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add honey, cinnamon quill and water and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Add lemon juice and rose water (optional) and orange blossom water (optional) and leave syrup to infuse and cool in mixing bowl (approx. 20-30 minutes). Strain syrup through a fine mesh sieve into a small jug.
  4. Leave baklava to cool slightly, then drizzle syrup evenly over top of pastry. Set aside, uncovered, overnight before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

small bowl , bowl, kitchen towel, rectangular baking tin (31 x 20 x 4 cm), pastry brush, small jug, fine mesh sieve, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important to allow baklava to rest uncovered so it remains crispy. If it is covered, the top layers of the pastry may become soggy.
