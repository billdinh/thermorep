Lamb Kibbeh

Ingredients

Kibbeh

  • 100 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 600 g lamb mince
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 - 2 tsp salt flakes, to taste
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes (optional)
  • 160 g fine burghul
  • 80 g water

Filling

  • 70 g cheddar cheese, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 3 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 30 g brown onion
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 50 g walnuts, cut into pieces

Green tahini

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 lemon, juice and zest, peeled into strips (1 x 4 cm), no white pith
  • 8 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only (approx. 20 g)
  • 120 g tahini
  • salt flakes, to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 80 g water

Assembly

  • vegetable oil, for frying

Preparation

Kibbeh

  1. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lamb, spices, salt, pepper, chilli flakes (optional) and burghul and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add water and mix 1 min/speed 3. Transfer into a large bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place cheese and mint into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and cook 2 min/100°C/speed  .
  4. Add walnuts and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed . Transfer into bowl with cheese and mint and gently stir to combine. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Green tahini

  1. Place garlic, lemon zest and mint into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lemon juice, tahini, salt, pepper and water and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Adjust seasoning as desired. Transfer into a small serving bowl and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Using wet hands, shape 1 heaped tablespoonful of meat mixture and press into a flat circle in the palm of your hand. Place 1 teaspoon of the cheese filling in the centre of the kibbeh. Close your hand to enclose filling and form a rugby ball shape. Place kibbeh onto a large plate.
  2. Place a small saucepan over medium-high heat. Add oil (to 8 cm depth) and heat to 170°C (see Tips). Line a plate with paper towel.
  3. Working in small batches, fry kibbeh for approx. 3-4 minutes. Transfer onto lined plate and allow to drain. Serve kibbeh warm with Green tahini dipping sauce.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, bowl, small bowl , large plate, saucepan, paper towel, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace walnuts with chopped almonds or pine nuts.
  • To mince your own lamb, refer to the guidelines in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook. 
  • To check your oil is at 170°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into the oil; the bread will turn golden brown in 20 seconds.

  • Baked kibbeh: Preheat oven to 190°C. Place kibbeh on a lined baking tray (30 x 40 cm) and bake for 20 minutes (190°C), or until browned and sizzling.
