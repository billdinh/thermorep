Jungli Laal Maas

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 600 g venison, diced (2-3 cm)
  • 200 g plain yoghurt
  • 3 Tbsp chilli paste, red
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 15 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)

Curry

  • 5 dried Kashmiri chillies
  • 1 ½ Tbsp coriander seeds
  • ¾ tsp cumin seeds
  • 200 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g ghee
  • 1 cinnamon stick, small
  • 5 green cardamom pods
  • 3 black cardamom pods
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 10 garlic cloves
  • 250 g water

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in venison and yoghurt. Remove bowl, add red chilli paste and salt then set bowl aside.
  2. Place 4 garlic cloves and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Add to bowl with meat and stir well with spatula. Cover and set aside in fridge to marinate for at least 3 hours or overnight. Clean mixing bowl.

Curry

  1. Place dried Kashmiri chillies, coriander seeds and cumin seeds in mixing bowl then grind 2 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place ghee, cinnamon stick, green cardamom and black cardamom pods in mixing bowl then fry 5 min/Varoma//speed .
  4. Add bay leaves and reserved chopped onions then fry 6 min/120°C//speed .
  5. Add 10 garlic cloves and reserved ground spices then fry 3 min/120°C//speed .
  6. Add reserved marinated venison and fry 4 min/120°C//speed 0.5.
  7. Add water and stir well with spatula. Cook 75-90 min/98°C//speed  until meat is tender. Adjust seasoning if necessary and serve.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The venison can be left to marinate overnight for even greater depth of flavour.
  • This fiery curry originated in Rajasthan. ‘Jungli’ means ‘wild’ and traditionally this dish was prepared with game meat such as wild boar. The long cooking time means it is a dish that also suits tougher meats such as mutton. ‘Laal’ means ‘red’ and here it refers to the red meat as well as the red chillies.
