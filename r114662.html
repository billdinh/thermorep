Pappadums with mint and coriander chutney

Ingredients

Pappadums

  • 180 g water
  • 400 g urad dal , whole or split, husks removed (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil, plus 300-500 g extra for rolling and frying

Mint and coriander chutney

  • 12 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 10 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 long green chilli, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 tsp salt flakes
  • 1 lime, juice only (approx. 20 g)
  • 150 g natural yoghurt

Preparation

Pappadums

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it. Set jug aside.
  2. Place urad dal into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10.
  3. Add salt and chilli and knead 2 min/, slowly pouring water onto mixing bowl lid and letting it trickle into mixing bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of the oil and knead 1 min/, until a hard, crumbly dough forms.
  5. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Divide dough into 15 pieces and roll into balls. Press each ball into a small disc and brush each side with oil. Using a rolling pin, place 1 dough portion between 2 sheets of baking paper and roll out thinly. Turn baking paper and continue rolling to form a paper-thin circle (see Tips). Set aside (uncovered) while rolling remaining pappadums.
  7. Place a medium saucepan over high heat and place oil (4 cm high) into it. Bring oil to 180°C (use a kitchen thermometer, or drop a small cube of bread into oil; it should turn golden brown in 15 seconds). Using tongs, place 1 pappadum into hot oil; it will puff up immediately. Carefully turn over using tongs and fry for 2-3 more seconds until golden, then use tongs to remove to paper towels to drain. Repeat with remaining pappadums. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable container and store at room temperature.

Mint and coriander chutney

  1. Place all chutney ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 8.
  2. Transfer into a sealable storage container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve with pappadums.

Useful Items

jug, silicone bread mat, pastry brush, baking paper, rolling pin, paper towel, saucepan, sealable container, stove top, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Urad dal is available from Indian grocers. It is a black-skinned lentil, but the lentil itself is a creamy colour. Ask for dehusked urad dal.
  • Roll pappadums as thinly as you can, until you can see your fingers through the dough.

  • Pappadums can be cooked in a dry frying pan, but they won't puff up as much. Place a large, heavy-based frypan over medium-high heat and toast pappadums until they bubble (approx. 1 minute). Carefully turn using tongs and fry for another 1 minute, or until light golden brown and crisp.
