Crunchy turtles

Ingredients

Dough

  • 150 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 60 g water
  • 130 g full cream milk
  • 300 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp matcha powder (see Tip - optional)
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • oil, for greasing
  • 2 currants

Crunchy shell

  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 40 g water
  • 2 tsp raw sugar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1 tsp grapeseed oil
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • 30 g rice flour
  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/60°C/speed 2. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water, 60 g of the milk and 40 g of the flour into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and heat 2 min/80°C/speed 3.
  5. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  6. Place sugar, yeast, matcha powder (optional), remaining 260 g flour and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  7. Add vanilla, egg, remaining 70 g milk and reserved paste and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Add reserved butter and knead 5 min/ (dough will be sticky).
  9. Lightly oil a ceramic or glass bowl. Transfer dough into oiled bowl and cover with a kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place for 1½ hours. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  10. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  11. Transfer dough onto a well floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Cut off 2 pieces of dough, a 100 g piece and a 50 g piece. Divide 100 g piece of dough into 4 portions, roll each portion into a ball and set aside. Roll 50 g piece of dough into a ball and set aside.
  12. Press out or roll remaining dough into a flat disc (approx. 20 cm). Place reserved chocolate into the centre and bring edges together, folding and pleating edges to cover and seal in chocolate. Flip the turtle over onto prepared baking tray (sealed side down). Place the larger ball at one end of the turtle (for the head) and position 2 smaller balls on either side of the turtle (for the legs). Pinch legs to body to attach. Press currants onto the head (for the eyes).
  13. Cover turtle with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place for approx. 30 minutes to rise.

Crunchy shell

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place yeast, water, sugar, oil, salt, flour and cocoa powder into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  4. Using a pastry brush, evenly coat top of the turtle shell with cocoa mixture. Sprinkle with extra sugar and bake for 30 minutes (180°C) or until it sounds hollow when tapped underneath.
  5. Transfer to a wire rack and allow to cool for 10 minutes. Serve warm or store in a sealable container and gently reheat before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, ceramic or glass bowl, kitchen towel, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, silicone bread mat, pastry brush, rolling pin, wire rack, sealable container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Mini turtles (makes 10): Divide dough into 11 equal portions (approx. 50 g each). Cover with a kitchen towel.
  • Divided 1 portion into 10 equal portions for heads. Roll each portion into a ball, then set aside.
  • Roll or flatten the remaining 10 dough portions into circles (8 cm). Place 1 tablespoon chocolate into the centre of each circle and fold the dough around the chocolate, pressing together to ensure it is sealed. Flip the dough (sealed side down) and place onto baking trays lined with baking paper. Press one small ball (for the head) onto each turtle. Pinch dough on both sides of turtle to create 2 legs on each side. Repeat with remaining turtles.
  • Using a toothpick, place 2 linseeds onto each head to create eyes. Cover turtles with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place for approx. 30 minutes to rise.
  • Using a pastry brush, evenly coat turtles with cocoa mixture (re-pinch legs into shape, if needed). Sprinkle with extra sugar and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until cooked through. Allow to cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before serving.

  • The matcha powder will give the turtles a green color.
