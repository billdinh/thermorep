Kimchi

Ingredients

  • 1 Chinese cabbage (approx. 700 g), halved lengthways then cut in pieces (5 cm)
  • 4 Tbsp fine sea salt
  • water, for soaking cabbage
  • 200 g daikon (Japanese radish), cut in matchsticks (4 cm x 3 mm)
  • 6 spring onions (approx. 100 g), cut in matchsticks (4 cm x 3 mm)
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp caster sugar
  • 20 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 2 Tbsp shrimp paste
  • 3 - 4 Tbsp Korean chilli paste (gochujang), to taste

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in cabbage then remove bowl from lid. Add salt then stir well. Cover with water and set aside for 2 hours.
  2. After this time, drain cabbage in Varoma dish, rinse thoroughly under cold water then leave to drain for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in daikon and spring onions then set aside.
  4. Place garlic, sugar and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 5 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add shrimp paste and Korean chilli paste then blend 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 5 sec/speed 5.
  6. Squeeze out any excess water from cabbage then add to bowl with daikon. Transfer chilli paste mixture to bowl with cabbage then, wearing disposable gloves, mix together thoroughly, massaging until vegetables are limp and brine is released (8-10 minutes).
  7. Transfer to a large sterilised jar or 2 smaller jars and press down so juices rise enough to cover surface of vegetables. Make sure there is a gap at the top then close jar and seal lid (see tip).
  8. Leave to ferment at room temperature for 2-5 days, checking every day, letting out some gas and pressing vegetables down into brine ensuring the surface is covered.
  9. Once kimchi is flavoured to taste (see tip), store jar in fridge for up to 3 months and use as needed.

Useful Items

large bowl, disposable gloves, sterilised jars with airtight lids, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Depending on the size of the jar, if it is quite full, place jar on a plate to catch any brine that may bubble over.
  • The longer kimchi is left to ferment, the stronger the flavour will be. For a milder taste, ferment for the minimum time - even this shorter fermentation time will produce flavoursome results.
