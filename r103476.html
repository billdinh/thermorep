Pork pies

Ingredients

Pastry

  • oil, for greasing
  • 250 g water
  • 150 g lard
  • 600 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp salt

Filling

  • 500 g water
  • 3 eggs
  • 80 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 100 g rindless bacon rashers, cut into pieces
  • 20 g lard
  • 300 g pork mince
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground pepper
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp dried sage
  • 20 g fresh baby spinach leaves (approx. 12 leaves)
  • 70 g blue cheese, sliced (optional)
  • English mustard, to serve
  • cornichons , to serve

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Grease a 12-hole muffin tray well and set aside.
  2. Place water and lard into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Allow mixture to cool to 50°C (approx. 20 minutes, see Tips).
  3. Add flour and salt and knead 2 min/. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and divide pastry into 12 x 50 g portions and 12 x 30 g portions.
  4. Roll out each 50 g portion into a 12-13 cm circle and line holes of muffin tray with pastry. Place tray into refrigerator until ready to use.
  5. Roll out each 30 g portion into a 8-10 cm circle. Transfer pastry rounds onto a plate and place into refrigerator until ready to use.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket, place eggs into it and cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, then place eggs under running water to cool. Once cool enough to handle, peel eggs. Cut off ends of eggs and slice each egg into 4 thick, even slices. Discard water in mixing bowl.
  3. Place onion, bacon and lard into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add pork mince, nutmeg, salt, pepper, thyme and sage and combine 5 sec/speed 5.
  6. Place 2 teaspoons mince mixture into bottom of each pastry case and press down to flatten. Top each pie with an egg slice, a thin layer of spinach (1-2 leaves) and a thin layer of blue cheese (optional). Top each pie with remaining mince filling (approx. 1 teaspoon) and press down to flatten.
  7. Place a pastry round on top of each pie, and seal edges together, using your fingertips to crimp edges. Using a sharp knife, cut a small hole or slit in the top of each pie, then bake for 30-40 minutes (180°C), or until golden brown.
  8. Allow pies to cool for 30 minutes before serving with mustard and cornichons.

Useful Items

12-hole muffin tray, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, 1 plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It's important to let the lard mixture cool in step 2, as this allows the pastry to knead more easily.
