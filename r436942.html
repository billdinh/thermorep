Oysters Rockefeller

Ingredients

  • rock salt
  • 6 fresh oysters, cleaned and shucked (see Tip)
  • 1 oz fresh baby spinach
  • 1 green onion, in pieces
  • 2 tsp bread crumbs
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp anise-flavored liqueur, e.g. Pernod
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 - 2 tsp hot sauce
  • ½ oz unsalted butter
  • 1 lemon, in wedges, to serve
  • Tabasco sauce, to serve
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
301 kJ / 72 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
5 g
Fat
3 g

Preparation

  1. Position oven rack in top third of oven and preheat broiler. Line a large cast iron skillet with a layer of rock salt (approx. 1 in. deep), moisten the salt very slightly with water (approx. 4-6 tbsp) and set oysters in half shells in the rock salt, ensuring they are level.
  2. Place spinach, green onion, bread crumbs, parsley and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop a further 3 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add anise liqueur, lemon juice, hot sauce and butter and mix 3 sec/speed 8.
  4. Top each oyster half with spinach mixture and broil 5-8 minutes, until topping is slightly browned. Top with any remaining spinach mixture and serve with lemon wedges and Tabasco sauce.

Useful Items

large cast iron skillet, oven, oven broiler

Hints & Tricks

  • Choose fresh oysters: pick oysters with closed shells, a fresh sea smell and a heavy feel. Scrub oysters clean and rinse under cool water.
  • Always be careful when shucking oysters, keep hands protected. Use a doubled tea towel or wear tough rubber or canvas gloves and hold the oyster firmly in one hand, curved side down. Using an oyster-shucking knife in the other hand, place the tip of the knife at the base of the hinge (pointy part of oyster), twist the knife using pressure, then without pressure, lever the knife upwards, or twist it to pry the hinge open. Slide the knife under the top shell to release the oyster and remove the shell.
