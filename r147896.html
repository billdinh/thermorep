Sea Bass with Raisins and Pine Nuts

Ingredients

  • 100 g sultanas
  • 50 g pine nuts
  • 50 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 6 fresh sea bass fillets, boneless (approx. 100 g each)
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 500 g fresh spinach leaves
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1799 kJ / 430 kcal

23.8 g

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sultanas, cover with water and set aside to swell.
  2. Place pine nuts in a dry frying pan and brown over a medium-high heat for a few minutes, stirring continuously. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Add a little oil to frying pan and lightly brown sea bass fillets (skin facing upwards) then transfer to paper towels.
  3. Place onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Drain soaked sultanas then place in mixing bowl. Add 50 g oil then sauté 10 min/120°C/speed .
  5. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  6. Add 250 g spinach and, without measuring cup, cook 2 min/120°C//speed , while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  7. Add remaining 250 g spinach and, without measuring cup, cook 3 min/120°C//speed , while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Transfer to a large baking dish, place browned sea bass on top and then scatter toasted pine nuts over.
  8. Bake for 10-15 minutes (220°C). Remove from oven and serve immediately.

Useful Items

frying pan, paper towel, large baking dish (approx. 26 cm x 24 cm x 5 cm), hob, oven
