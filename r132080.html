Parathas

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 - 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 500 g wholemeal flour (see Tips), plus extra for rolling
  • 50 g ghee (see Tips), plus extra for brushing
  • 15 g olive oil, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 220 g hot water

Preparation

  1. Place cardamom, garlic and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add flour, ghee, oil, salt and water and mix 10 sec/speed 5, then knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Transfer into a bowl, cover with a damp tea towel and allow to rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Transfer dough onto silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Divide dough into 12 equal portions (approx. 70 g each) and cover with a damp tea towel. Using a rolling pin, roll each ball into a circle (approx. 18 cm), then cover with a damp tea towel.
  5. Place a large non-stick frying pan over high heat. Brush both sides of the parathas with oil. Working 1 at a time, fry parathas for 1 minute on each side until evenly browned. Lift paratha and drizzle a few drops of oil into frying pan. Continue frying for a few seconds on each side until they start to look a little flaky. Transfer onto a plate and brush with ghee (optional). Cover to keep warm until ready to serve.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, tea towel, bowl, rolling pin, 1 plate, large non-stick frying pan, pastry brush, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • For a more authentic paratha, you can replace wholemeal flour with atta flour (available at Indian grocers or online). Alternatively, you can replace the wholemeal flour with plain flour.
  • Parathas are an unleavened bread (similar to naan) and often served with curries or potato-based dishes.
