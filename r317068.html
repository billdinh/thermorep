Honey and Soy Cod with Rice and Vegetables

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 30 g sesame oil
  • 200 g honey
  • 140 g light soy sauce
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes (optional)
  • 4 cod fillets (approx. 125 g each)
  • 100 g tinned baby corn, drained, halved lengthways
  • 100 g sugar snap peas
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 250 g basmati rice

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, ginger and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Chop again 3 sec/speed 6 then sauté 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add honey, soy sauce and chilli flakes (if using) then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Blend 30 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually.
  3. Place each cod fillet on a sheet of baking paper (approx. 35 cm x 30 cm). Divide baby corn and sugar snap peas between fillets. Divide half the sauce between fish, spooning over each fillet. Cover remaining sauce to keep warm and set aside. Fold up baking paper lengthways, then twist the ends to form 4 parcels. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Place water and salt in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in rice. Rinse 10 sec/speed 5 then bring to the boil 8 min/Varoma/speed 3.
  5. Place fish parcels seam side up, 2 in Varoma dish and 2 on Varoma tray, then insert Varoma tray. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 3 until fish and rice are fully cooked.
  6. Set covered Varoma aside for parcels to rest for 2 minutes then carefully transfer contents to serving plates. Serve fish with rice and remaining reserved sauce poured over.

Useful Items

baking paper, aluminium foil

Hints & Tricks

  • Getting kids to enjoy fish dishes can sometimes be tricky, but we think they’ll love the sweet honey and soy marinade on these cod fillets. Using the Varoma to steam the cod and vegetables together in paper parcels, while cooking the rice below, keeps the fish tender. Serve the parcels unopened - children will love unwrapping them to see what’s inside!
  • Substitute cod with any white fish (e.g. pollock, haddock or hake).
