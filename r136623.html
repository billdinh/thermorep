Mediterranean salmon and couscous parcels

Ingredients

  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • ½ lemon, zest and juice, no white pith, plus extra wedges to serve
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 70 g spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 9 sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper, plus extra to season
  • 220 g couscous
  • 4 fresh boneless, skinless salmon fillets (approx. 150 g each)
  • 500 g warm water
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place rosemary, thyme, parsley and lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic, spring onion/shallots, 2 teaspoons of the lemon juice, sun-dried tomatoes, salt and pepper and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer herb-tomato mixture into a large bowl, transfer 4 tablespoons of the mixture in a small bowl and set both bowls aside.
  4. Place the large bowl with the herb-tomato mixture onto mixing bowl lid and weigh couscous into it. Set bowl aside.
  5. Cut 4 squares of baking paper (25 x 25 cm). Place a salmon fillet onto each square. Season each salmon fillet with salt, pepper and 1 tablespoon of the reserved herb-tomato mixture. Fold baking paper to seal parcel, tucking the folded ends of paper underneath the parcels, and place 2 of the parcels seam side up into Varoma dish (see Tips) and 2 parcels onto Varoma tray. Insert Varoma tray into Varoma dish and set aside.
  6. Place water, oil and stock paste into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside to keep salmon warm. Pour cooking water from mixing bowl over reserved couscous mixture, stir with a fork to combine, cover and set aside for approx. 5 minutes. Fluff couscous with a fork, then serve with salmon parcels and lemon wedges.

Useful Items

small bowl , large bowl, baking paper, tea towel

Hints & Tricks

  • Instead of fresh herbs, you can use 2-3 teaspoons dried mixed herbs in step 1.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • To make sure a few holes in the Varoma dish remain unobstructed for steam to circulate, you can place the butterfly whisk in the centre of the Varoma dish underneath the parcels to raise it off the bottom of the Varoma dish.
