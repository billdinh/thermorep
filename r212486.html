Chicken Étouffée

Ingredients

Roux

  • 30 g vegetable oil
  • 2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 300 g water
  • 1 chicken stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled

Étouffée

  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 1 onion, quartered (approx. 100 g)
  • 1 green pepper, quartered (approx. 120 g)
  • 1 red pepper, quartered (approx. 120 g)
  • 1 stick celery, quartered (approx. 70 g)
  • 1 red chilli, halved, deseeded
  • ½ tsp Cajun spice mix
  • 1 tsp fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 400 g chicken breasts, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 4 spring onions, sliced (1 cm)

Preparation

Roux

  1. Place vegetable oil in mixing bowl and heat 3 min/120°C/speed .
  2. Add flour and stir 5 min/120°C/speed 0.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then stir again 2 min/120°C/speed 0.5 until lightly browned.
  3. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in water. Remove jug.
  4. Add chicken stock to mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, mix 5 min/100°C/speed 2 while slowly adding water from jug during the first minute. Transfer to a jug.

Étouffée

  1. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 1 min/100°C/speed .
  2. Add onion, green pepper, red pepper, celery and chilli, chop 3 sec/speed 5 then sauté 5 min/100°C//speed .
  3. Add Cajun spice mix, basil and salt then cook 2 min/98°C//speed .
  4. Add reserved sauce and, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, simmer 15-20 min/100°C//speed  until reduced and thickened.
  5. Add chicken and spring onions then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/100°C//speed  until chicken is thoroughly cooked
  6. Serve with rice or couscous.

Hints & Tricks

  • Étouffée means smothered and is a popular way of presenting food in Cajun cuisine. The key to this dish is the roux, which imparts a nutty flavour to the sauce.
