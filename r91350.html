Biscuits

Ingredients

  • 9 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • 4 tsp baking powder
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra to serve
  • 8 oz buttermilk, chilled
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
539 kJ / 128 kcal

3 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F. Grease and lightly flour a rectangular baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.).
  2. Place flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder and butter into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add buttermilk and mix 5 sec/speed 5, then knead 20 sec/. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface, dust top of dough with flour and gently press into a circle 1¼ inch thick.
  4. Dip a cookie cutter (2 in.) in flour to prevent dough sticking. Cut out biscuits by pushing cutter into dough starting around outside edges and working towards center. Gently press leftover dough together and repeat process. Place dough pieces onto prepared baking sheet leaving approximately ½ inch between each biscuit and bake 15-20 minutes (450°F) or until golden brown. Wrap biscuits in a kitchen towel and place in a bread basket to keep warm while serving. Serve with butter.

Useful Items

cookie cutter (2 in.), kitchen towel, baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For English scones: add 2 tbsp sugar, extra 5 oz buttermilk and 6 oz all-purpose flour to this recipe, serve with fresh cream and strawberry jelly.
  • This is a wet dough.
  • Be careful not to knead the dough unnecessarily with your hands, as this will make the biscuits tough.
  • Pushing cookie cutter into dough starting at the outside edge and working towards the center or from one side to the other helps to keep dough fluffy.
  • If you do not have a cookie cutter use an upturned glass to cut out rounds.
  • Baking biscuits close to one another helps them rise upwards.
