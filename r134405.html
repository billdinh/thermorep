Banana Ice Cream with Peanut Butter and Chocolate

Ingredients

  • 400 g coconut milk (1 x 400 ml tin), chilled in fridge for approx. 8 hours
  • 200 g peanuts, roasted, unsalted
  • 600 g bananas, ripe (see tip), sliced, then frozen
  • 130 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 20 g coconut oil

Preparation

  1. Drain coconut milk from solid coconut cream, collecting both in separate bowls (see tip).
  2. Place 50 g peanuts in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 150 g peanuts in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then grind again 20 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add reserved chopped peanuts and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a freezer-proof container and freeze for 1 hour.
  5. After this time, place bananas and reserved solid coconut cream in mixing bowl then, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid, blend 20 sec/speed 5.
  6. Add frozen peanut mixture, cut in pieces and, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid, blend 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer to serving bowls and set aside while making chocolate sauce. Clean mixing bowl.
  7. Place chocolate in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Add coconut oil then melt 3 min/70Â°C/speed 1. Serve ice cream drizzled with the melted chocolate.

Useful Items

freezer-proof container, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Place the tin of coconut milk in the fridge the evening before making the recipe.
  • The reserved coconut milk can be added to smoothies or juices for extra flavour.
  • Using ripe bananas will result in a sweeter ice cream.
