Plain Yogurt

Ingredients

  • 35 oz whole milk
  • 4 ½ oz plain yogurt, min. 1.8% fat
  • 2 oz powdered milk (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 jar
Calories
370 kJ / 89 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
7 g
Fat
5 g

Preparation

  1. Place milk, yogurt and powdered milk into mixing bowl and warm 6 min/120°F/speed 3. Transfer yogurt mixture into 8 airtight jars or small containers and wrap immediately in a blanket to keep warm. Leave jars well wrapped for 11-13 hours in a warm place (see Tip), without moving them during this incubation period. Chill in refrigerator for at least 3 hours before serving.

Useful Items

airtight jars or containers, blanket, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Keeping the yogurt warm and undisturbed during incubation is essential to achieving the right texture. To increase insulation you can place wrapped jars in a picnic cooler. A hot water bottle can be used to pre warm the cooler before adding the wrapped yogurt.
  • The cooler the room temperature, the longer the yogurt should be left to incubate. In very warm environments, 8-10 hours may be sufficient.
  • The longer the incubation time, the more acidic the yogurt.
  • For best results, make sure all utensils are very clean before making yogurt.
  • Avoid using low-fat milk or low-fat yogurt for this recipe.
  • Powdered milk helps the yogurt to set.
  • If using raw milk, heat milk 10 min/195°F/speed 2, and allow the milk to cool to room temperature before starting the recipe.
  • The heating and cooling process described for raw milk can also be used with pasteurized milk to produce a thicker consistency
  • Remember to reserve some plain yogurt as a starting culture for the next batch.
  • For sweetened yogurt, add 3 tbsp sugar along with the other ingredients.
  • For creamy yogurt, add 3.5 oz cream along with the other ingredients.
