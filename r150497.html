Prawn salad with avocado dressing

Ingredients

Avocado dressing

  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, stalks and leaves, cut into thirds
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 avocado, flesh only
  • 2 tbsp Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • 3 tsp avocado oil
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • ¼ - ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp paprika
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Prawn salad

  • 500 g water
  • 150 - 200 g frozen edamame beans
  • 500 g raw prawns, peeled and deveined
  • salt, to season
  • ground black pepper, to season
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp Tabasco® sauce, to taste (optional)
  • 2 baby cos lettuce, cut into quarters lengthways
  • 2 Lebanese cucumbers, peeled into ribbons

Preparation

Avocado dressing

  1. Place coriander and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining dressing ingredients and blend 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 8 or until smooth. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tip). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Prawn salad

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh edamame beans into it, then cook 6-8 min/100°C/speed 2, until beans are cooked. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside to cool.
  2. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh prawns into it. Season prawns with salt and pepper, then secure Varoma lid and steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until prawns are just pink. Meanwhile, shell edamame beans and place beans into a bowl. Discard pods.
  3. Add cooked prawns to bowl, with lemon juice and Tabasco® (optional), and toss to combine.
  4. Divide lettuce between serving plates. Divide prawn mixture between plates and garnish with cucumber ribbons. Serve with Avocado dressing.

Useful Items

sealable container, bowl, serving plates, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Leftover Avocado dressing can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
