Sun-dried Tomato and Rosemary Breadsticks - Bastoncini di pane con pomodori secchi e rosmarino

Ingredients

Dough

  • 200 g water
  • 350 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing

Paste

  • 1 tsp dried fennel seeds
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, needles only, to taste
  • 80 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, drained

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 1 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, yeast, sugar, salt and oil, mix 10 sec/speed 4 then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Tip dough out onto a well floured work surface and bring together into a ball. Place in prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place to prove until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Paste

  1. Place fennel seeds and rosemary in mixing bowl then grind 40 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add sun-dried tomatoes and chop 4 sec/speed 5.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line 2 large baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Tip risen dough out onto a lightly floured surface then roll out into a rectangle (approx. 50 cm x 30 cm). Spread reserved tomato paste over dough.
  3. Fold dough in half along vertical axis to form a 25 cm x 30 cm rectangle, enclosing the paste. Cut folded dough horizontally into 1 cm thick strips. Stretch each strip to approx. 35 cm in length, then twist several times.
  4. Place twists on prepared baking trays and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) or until golden, then transfer to a wire rack. Depending on size of your baking trays, you may need to bake breadsticks in batches.
  5. Turn off oven when all breadsticks have been baked, transfer them back onto baking trays and return to oven to dry out and cool completely for a few hours or overnight.
  6. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, large baking trays, baking paper, rolling pin, long sharp knife, wire rack, airtight container, soft spatula, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These long, thin, crispy Italian-style breadsticks are hard for anyone to resist and make a great snack or accompaniment to any antipasti – try with the caponata. Making your own dough allows you to add any number of flavourings or fillings. This version filled with delicious sun-dried tomato paste is made quickly and easily in your Thermomix®. Also works well with a homemade basil pesto or simply scattered with flaky sea salt.
