Nut paste

Ingredients

  • 55 g macadamia oil
  • 400 g raw almonds
  • 100 g pumpkin seeds
  • 100 g sunflower seeds
  • 60 g sesame seeds
  • 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Set aside.
  2. Place 250 g of the almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, sesame seeds, sea salt, caster sugar (optional) and remaining 150 g almonds into mixing bowl and mix 40 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Mix 1 min/speed 4, slowly pouring oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it trickle into mixing bowl until a paste consistency is achieved. Mix for a further 1 min/speed 8 with aid of spatula.
  5. Transfer into a sealable jar or container until ready to use.

Useful Items

jug, sealable storage jar or container

Hints & Tricks

  • This nut paste must be stored in the refrigerator. It will last for up to two months.
  • Use this paste as a replacement for butter in both sweet and savoury recipes.
  • Nuts are a rich source of protein, vitamins, minerals and complex carbohydrates. Nut butters are an especially rich source of good quality fats, Vitamin E, essential fatty acids, calcium and protein.
  • This recipe makes 700 g total.
