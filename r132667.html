Ginger, Clementine and Honey Steamed Puddings

Ingredients

  • 90 g butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 10 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 90 g sugar
  • 2 clementines
  • 2 Tbsp runny honey
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 90 g self-raising flour
  • 30 g milk
  • ¾ tsp baking powder
  • 1000 g water
  • cream, for serving (optional)

Preparation

  1. Generously grease 4 individual pudding moulds (Ø 7 cm) and 4 circles (Ø 7 cm) of baking paper.
  2. Place ginger in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3. Meanwhile, juice 1 clementine and set juice aside. Peel remaining 1 clementine then slice through middle into 4 thick rounds. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Mix again 1 min/speed 3. Meanwhile, place 1 prepared clementine round into each pudding basin and spoon ½ Tbsp honey on top of each slice, then set aside. Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Without measuring cup, beat 30 sec/speed 3, while adding eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add flour, milk, baking powder, 1 tsp reserved chopped ginger and reserved clementine juice then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Mix well with spatula.
  7. Divide batter between pudding basins, filling them ¾ full. Cover desserts with prepared baking paper circles, buttered side down and push mixture down a little. Cover each pudding basin with foil or baking paper, secure with string or an elastic band and place into Varoma dish. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Place water in mixing bowl then place Varoma into position. Steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve warm with cream (if using).

Useful Items

individual pudding basins, baking paper, aluminium foil, butcher's string or elastic band

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with homemade custard (see recipe in The Basic Cookbook) instead of cream if preferred.
