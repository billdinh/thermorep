Parmesan-crusted lamb cutlets, creamed spinach and smashed spuds

Ingredients

Parmesan crust

  • 60 g day old sourdough bread
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 60 g Brazil nuts
  • 1 egg
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

Lamb

  • 6 lamb cutlets, French trimmed (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 600 g water
  • 300 g kipfler potatoes, cut into halves
  • 200 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • salt, to season
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season
  • 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, finely chopped
  • 20 g unsalted butter
  • 50 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg

Preparation

Parmesan crust

  1. Place bread, garlic, Parmesan cheese, parsley and Brazil nuts into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add egg, butter, mustard and pepper and blend 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lamb

  1. Using a pastry brush, brush surface of lamb with honey and place onto Varoma tray.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh potatoes into it. Place Varoma tray with lamb into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma tray, transfer lamb onto a plate and cover to keep warm. Secure Varoma lid and steam potatoes 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Place Varoma tray back into position, weigh spinach onto it, secure Varoma lid and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Remove Varoma and set aside. Place a large non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add 1 tbsp of the oil. Add cooked potatoes, season with salt and pepper. Smash potatoes down with the back of a spoon and fry for 10-12 minutes, or until golden. Transfer into a serving bowl and sprinkle with parsley. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place butter, reserved spinach, cream, salt, pepper and nutmeg into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/90°C/speed 2, then blend 10 sec/speed 6.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 7. Set aside in mixing bowl and keep warm.
  7. Press reserved Parmesan crust onto lamb cutlets. Place non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Fry cutlets on both sides (approx. 1-2 minutes each side) until golden, carefully turning once (ensure crust stays on lamb). Serve lamb with spinach and potatoes.

Useful Items

bowl, pastry brush, aluminium foil, 1 plate, serving bowl, large non-stick frying pan, non-stick frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • French trimmed lamb cutlets mean that the protruding bone has been stripped of meat and sinew for a neater presentation.You can trim the cutlets yourself or ask your butcher to do it for you.
  • Use oregano or mint instead of parsley.
  • Any potato can be used instead of kipfler potatoes; simply cut into pieces (3 cm).
  • The lamb is steamed to medium-rare in this recipe; if you prefer your lamb cooked a bit longer, steam 14 min/Varoma/speed 1.
