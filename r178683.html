Squid Crackers, Oyster Emulsion, Frozen Cucumber

Ingredients

Oyster Emulsion

  • 400 g fresh oysters (weight including shells)
  • 110 g breadcrumbs
  • 50 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 600 g rapeseed oil
  • fine sea salt, to taste
  • lemon juice, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Garnish

  • ½ cucumber, peeled, deseeded
  • fine sea salt, for sprinkling

Squid Crackers

  • 325 g long grain rice
  • 650 g fish stock, hot
  • 5 g squid ink, liquid
  • lemon juice, to taste
  • 5 g fish sauce
  • rapeseed oil, for frying
  • fine sea salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • borage leaves, small, cut in half lengthways, for garnish
  • vinegar powder, for garnish

Preparation

Oyster Emulsion

  1. Shuck oysters, reserving any juices and place in mixing bowl. Pass juice through a muslin cloth then set aside in a separate bowl.
  2. Add breadcrumbs and parsley to mixing bowl then mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid then weigh in 300 g rapeseed oil. Remove jug. Set to speed 4 and drizzle rapeseed oil onto mixing bowl lid so that it drips slowly onto blades.
  4. Add filtered oyster juice.
  5. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid then weigh in remaining 300 g rapeseed oil. Remove jug. Set to speed 4 and drizzle remaining rapeseed oil onto mixing bowl lid so that it drips slowly onto blades.
  6. Add salt, pepper and lemon juice to taste. Stir then pass mixture through a fine sieve then transfer to a squeezy bottle and keep in fridge until required.

Garnish

  1. Cut cucumber in 3 mm dice. Sprinkle with a little salt then place in a sieve over a bowl to draw a little water out.
  2. Rinse under cold water then drain well. Scatter on a paper towel to dry.
  3. When dry, place dice on an oiled tray in freezer for 3-4 hours until needed.

Squid Crackers

  1. Preheat oven to 65°C. Line 2 large baking trays with silicone mats.
  2. Place rice and stock in mixing bowl then cook 35-45 min/95°C/speed  until rice is very soft.
  3. Add squid ink and cook 3 min/70°C/speed .
  4. Allow bowl to cool to 60°C then pulse Turbo/1 sec/2-3 times to break rice down.
  5. Add lemon juice and fish sauce then pulse Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Tip onto prepared trays and spread out to a depth of 2 mm. Dry in oven for 2 hours (65°C). Turn mixture over then dry for a further 1 hour (65°C). Remove from oven and break into pieces (3 cm x 4 cm).
  6. Heat oil in a large saucepan to 190°C.
  7. Drop squid wafers into hot oil (in batches) and cook for 20-30 seconds until puffed. Drain on paper towels. Lightly season then store in an airtight container until needed.
  8. To serve, dot wafers with oyster emulsion, arrange a few pieces of frozen cucumber on each, along with borage leaves. Sprinkle with vinegar powder then serve.
