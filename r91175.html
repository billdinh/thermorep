Quick Puff Pastry

Ingredients

  • 7 oz unsalted butter, diced (½ -1 in.), frozen
  • 7 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 3 oz water, chilled
  • ½ tsp salt

Preparation

  1. Place butter, flour, chilled water and salt into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Remove dough from mixing bowl. Dough will appear crumbly. Form into a ball and flatten into a rough square. Wrap pastry in cling wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  2. Unwrap dough and place onto a lightly floured work surface. Roll out dough with a rolling pin into a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 24 in. x 8 in). Fold dough into thirds and give folded dough a quarter turn so that the fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice, then return to refrigerator for 20 minutes or place in freezer for 5 minutes before using.
  3. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.) with parchment and set aside.
  4. Roll dough into a rectangle (¼ in. thick) and cut into required shape or shapes right before baking. Bake for 20 minutes (400°F) for one large piece of pastry, 12-15 minutes (400°F) for smaller pastries, or as directed by other recipes. Allow to cool on a wire rack before serving or storing in an airtight container.

Useful Items

cling wrap, rolling pin, baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), parchment paper, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This pastry serves as a base for cakes, pastries, cream puffs and fruit pies.
  • The success of this recipe depends on keeping the pastry dough chilled so the layers of butter do not melt. A cold work surface (marble or stone) and a cool room temperature are helpful. If at any time during the process the dough feels warm, place into the freezer for 5 minutes before rolling out again. To prevent the dough from sticking to the work surface, whenever necessary use a light dusting of flour, or roll dough between two sheets of parchment.
  • Puff pastry dough freezes very well before baking. Defrost in the refrigerator before using. Partially rolling out the dough before freezing allows for faster defrosting.
  • Once baked, pastry keeps well for up to 15 days stored in a sealed container in a cool dry place.
  • Replace water with very cold white wine.
