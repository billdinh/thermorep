Chicken banh mi

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 skinless chicken thigh fillets
  • 60 g white sugar
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 eschalots
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 stalk fresh lemongrass, crushed and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • vegetable oil, for frying

Pickled carrot

  • 100 g white wine vinegar
  • 70 g sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 carrot, peeled into ribbons

Chicken liver pâté

  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ brown onion (approx. 60 g)
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 250 g chicken livers, washed, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp brandy
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream

Assembly

  • 1 baguette loaf (see Tips)
  • 50 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 10 pieces pickled jalapeño chillies, cut into pieces
  • ¼ cucumber, cut into thin slices
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only (optional)

Preparation

Chicken

  1. Place chicken into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sugar, fish sauce, pepper, eschalots, garlic, sesame oil and soy sauce into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into bowl with chicken. Add lemongrass to bowl with chicken and stir to coat completely. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 3 hours or overnight (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Drizzle chicken with a little of the vegetable oil and fry on both sides until browned and cooked through (approx. 5-10 minutes on each side). Cut into thin slices (approx. 5 mm). Cover and set aside to cool.

Pickled carrot

  1. Place vinegar, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl. Add carrot to bowl and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes.

Chicken liver pâté

  1. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add all remaining pâté ingredients and cook 5-7 min/100°C/speed 1, until livers are cooked but still pink, then blend 30 sec/speed 8.
  4. Place a fine-mesh sieve over a bowl and transfer pâté mixture into it. Using spatula, press pâté through sieve to get a smooth consistency. Discard contents of sieve. Transfer pâté into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Drain and discard pickling liquid from carrots. Cut baguette into 4 portions, then slice each portion into halves lengthways. Spread each portion with mayonnaise and reserved pâté, then layer with cooled chicken, pickled chillies, cucumber, pickled carrot and coriander (optional) to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, 1 plate, kitchen towel, large frying pan, sealable container, fine mesh sieve, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • If you are short on time, you can marinate the chicken for only 1 hour, however the flavours won't be quite as strong.
  • You can cover pâté with extra melted butter to seal, if desired. You can store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • To make your own baguette, please refer to The Basic Cookbook.
  • To make your own mayonnaise, please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook.
