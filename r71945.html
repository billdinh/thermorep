Five spice duck with mushrooms and Asian greens

Ingredients

Five spice powder

  • 2 tsp szechuan peppercorns
  • 3 star anise
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 cinnamon quills
  • 6 cloves

Duck

  • 1 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ - 1 long red chilli, deseeded if preferred (optional)
  • 2 duck breasts, skin on
  • 2 tsp Five spice powder
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 500 g water
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 100 g Asian greens, cut into pieces
  • 50 g broccolini, cut into thirds
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 4 fresh mushrooms, cut into slices

Preparation

Five spice powder

  1. Place all five spice powder ingredients into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool then mill 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and store until ready to use.

Duck

  1. Place ginger, garlic and chilli (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Using a sharp knife, score duck skin in a criss-cross pattern. Rub duck with Five spice powder, salt and pepper then place into the Varoma dish skin side down. Place water, stock and 1 tablespoon of the soy sauce into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and steam 9 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Insert Varoma tray. Weigh Asian greens and broccolini onto Varoma tray and steam 6 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer Asian greens and broccolini into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Transfer steaming liquid into a bowl and set aside. Pat duck dry with paper towel.
  4. Heat a small frying pan over a high heat without oil. Place duck breast skin side down into hot pan and press to flatten. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 2-4 minutes or until skin is very crispy and most of the fat is cooked out. Turn duck over and cook for a further 1-2 minutes. Transfer duck, skin side up, into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl to rest.
  5. Drain ¾ of the fat from the frying pan, leaving ¼ in the pan to create a reduction. Return frying pan to high heat and de-glaze by adding honey, 80 g reserved steaming liquid and remaining 1 tablespoon soy sauce. Leave to reduce for a few minutes then set aside.
  6. Place sesame oil and reserved garlic and ginger mixture into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  7. Add mushrooms and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  8. Add reserved Asian greens and broccolini and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Transfer into simmering basket and allow to drain.
  9. Thinly slice duck breast diagonally across the grain and serve immediately with drained vegetables and a drizzle of the reserved glaze.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, paper towel, turner / fish slice, frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: cook both duck breasts and use the leftovers in soup, noodles or salad.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Substitute soy sauce with tamari for a gluten free option.
  • Place any uncooked duck breast into the refrigerator and use as a substitute to the pork  in Pork San Choy Bau.
