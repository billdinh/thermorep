Bagels

Ingredients

  • 240 g water, plus extra for cooking
  • 450 g strong white bread flour (see tip), plus extra for dusting
  • ¾ tsp dried yeast
  • 1 ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 ½ tsp vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 ½ tsp barley malt extract
  • 1 Tbsp bicarbonate of soda
  • poppy seeds, for sprinkling
  • sesame seeds, for sprinkling
  • pumpkin seeds, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 1 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, yeast, salt, oil, and barley malt then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Knead 3 min 30 sec/. Tip out onto a lightly floured surface and divide into 6 equal-sized pieces. Roll into balls then cover with cling film and leave to rest for 20 minutes.
  3. Line and grease a large baking tray (approx. 38 cm x 32 cm).
  4. Flatten rested rolls slightly and push the end of a wooden spoon or your thumb through the centre then stretch into bagels. Place on prepared baking tray, and cover securely with oiled cling film. Leave at room temperature for 20 minutes until they just begin to rise.
  5. Preheat oven to 260°C.
  6. Bring a large deep pan, about ⅔ full with water and 1 Tbsp bicarbonate of soda to a gentle boil.
  7. Place bagels in the boiling water - two or three at a time. Boil for 1 minute, then, using a slotted spoon, flip them over and boil for another minute. Drain on a rack and sprinkle with seeds as desired, while boiling remaining bagels.
  8. Return bagels to baking tray and bake for 8 minutes (260°C). Reduce oven temperature to 230°C then bake for a further 6-8 minutes until nicely browned. Allow to cool on a wire rack.
  9. Serve with spreads, cream cheese or other fillings of choice.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray and paper, large saucepan, slotted spoon, wire rack, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Canadian hard wheat flour is best for making bagels.
  • Adding bicarbonate of soda to the water alkalises it, which helps to produce a better crust.
  • If you do not want to cook the bagels immediately, refrigerate bagels after step 4 for up to 48 hours.
  • Cooked bagels can be frozen for up to a month.
  • Ingredients can be doubled to make 12 bagels.
  • Bagels are unusual in that they are boiled before baking and they also must have a very stiff dough - much stiffer than any other bread. This produces the typical chewy character with a good crust. The advantage of this method is that the bagels can be prepared ahead and cooked as required.
