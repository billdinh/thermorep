Veggie Wellington

Ingredients

Filling

  • 200 g sweet potatoes, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme
  • 5 Tbsp olive oil
  • 100 g sourdough bread, toasted, torn in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 150 g red onions, quartered
  • 1 ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¾ tsp ground black pepper
  • 150 g kale, sliced in strips (1 cm)
  • 200 g fresh baby spinach
  • 100 g cooked chestnuts, peeled, crumbled
  • 50 g pine nuts
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin and juice
  • 10 g butter
  • 250 g button mushrooms
  • 1 egg, for glazing
  • 1 Tbsp milk, for glazing

Puff Pastry

  • 200 g butter (diced 2 cm and previously frozen for 1-2 hours)
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g water, cold
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Place sweet potatoes on a large piece of foil on a baking tray. Place rosemary and thyme sprigs on top then drizzle with 1 Tbsp olive oil. Seal parcel and roast for 35-45 minutes (190°C) until potatoes are soft, then remove, open parcel and allow to cool. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place toasted sourdough pieces, in a small bowl and drizzle over 2 Tbsp olive oil then set aside.

Puff Pastry

  1. Place frozen butter, flour, water and salt in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Tip out, form into a ball then wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place onions, ¼ tsp salt, ¼ tsp pepper and 2 Tbsp olive oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add kale. Place Varoma into position and weigh in spinach then steam 5 min/Varoma/speed 0.5.
  3. Remove Varoma and add chestnuts to mixing bowl. Replace Varoma and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed . Transfer everything to a large sieve to drain then set aside in a large bowl to cool.
  4. Roll out rested dough on a lightly floured surface to a long rectangle (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Fold bottom third up and top third down then, give dough a quarter turn so fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice more, then return pastry to fridge for 20 minutes before using. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  5. Stir soaked toast, roasted sweet potato, pine nuts, 1 tsp salt and ½ tsp pepper into onion, kale and spinach mix then set aside.
  6. Place lemon peel and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Chop again 3 sec/speed 9 then scrape down.
  7. Add butter and mushrooms then chop 2 sec/speed 7. Cook 5 min/100°C/speed  then tip out into a sieve and allow to drain before transferring to a small bowl.
  8. Stir 10 g lemon juice into drained mushrooms then set aside.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Roll out pastry on a sheet of baking paper to approx. 40 cm x 30 cm and place on a large baking tray. Spread reserved mushrooms over pastry, leaving a 4 cm border.
  2. Arrange vegetable mixture in a thick line down centre of pastry leaving a 3 cm border at each end. Hold one side of the baking paper and fold towards the middle so filling is half-covered with pastry. Peel back paper then repeat with the other side so pastry overlaps in the middle.
  3. Beat egg and milk together then brush pastry to seal. Fold ends in and seal, then carefully roll over so the join is underneath. Brush all over with remaining egg wash. Bake for 40-50 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Serve hot, cut in slices.

Useful Items

baking tray, aluminium foil, cling film, large bowl, medium mesh sieve, rolling pin, baking tray and paper, pastry brush, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with mash and vegetarian gravy.
  • A vegetarian variation on the classic beef Wellington. Making your own Wellington may look complicated, but the results are worth it. This makes an excellent dinner party, Christmas or Sunday dinner choice for your vegetarians…and we defy them, or you, not to finish it off!
