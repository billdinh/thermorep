Taiwanese Steamed Buns

Ingredients

  • 35 g sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 395 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 5 g dried instant yeast
  • 190 g water, warm
  • 75 g toasted sesame oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1000 g water

Preparation

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add salt, flour, yeast and water then knead 1 min/.
  3. Transfer dough to a lightly-oiled bowl and cover with cling film. Leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours).
  4. Preheat oven to 50°C or lowest setting (see tip).
  5. Tip dough out and divide into 16 even pieces. Roll each ball out into an oval shape, brush with sesame oil and fold in half. Arrange buns on a lightly-floured baking tray then prove in oven until buns have doubled in size (approx. 45 minutes).
  6. After this time, place remaining 1000 g water in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.5. Meanwhile, place 4-5 buns on Varoma tray.
  7. Insert Varoma tray, place covered Varoma into position then steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  8. Transfer cooked buns to a plate and repeat with remaining buns.
  9. Split cooked buns in half and stuff generously with desired filling.

Useful Items

baking tray, cling film, rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If your oven does not have a 50°C setting, preheat to the lowest setting then switch off before placing the buns in to prove.
  • When rolling out each ball, make the ends curved instead of pointed so that when the dough is folded in half it forms a ‘D’ shape.
  • Popular fillings for Taiwanese buns include braised pork belly, Asian barbecue duck, sautéed Asian mushrooms or spicy prawns with fresh coriander leaves.
