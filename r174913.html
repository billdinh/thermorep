Breakfast Burger

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp dried sage
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp paprika
  • ¼ tsp ground coriander
  • &frac18; tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 20 oz boneless pork shoulder, sinew free, cubed (1 in.), partially frozen, divided
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 4 eggs, fried
  • 4 English muffins, toasted
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories

Preparation

  1. In a small bowl place sage, thyme, maple syrup, salt, pepper, paprika, coriander and cayenne. Stir to combine and set aside.
  2. Place 10 oz pork and half the reserved spice mixture into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 10 oz pork and remaining spice mixture into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with reserved pork mixture.
  4. Divide and shape pork mixture into four equal-sized patties and create a small dimple in the center by pressing down with your fingers to prevent them from rising while cooking. Cook patties on grill or stovetop over medium high heat to taste (see Tip). Flip patties and top with a slice of cheese and a fried egg. Place on a paper towel to drain.
  5. Serve patties immediately between toasted English muffins.

Useful Items

paper towels, thermometer, grill

Hints & Tricks

  • Partially freeze pork for up to 1 hour before grinding to ensure an even grind and to optimize texture.
  • Suggested internal cooking temperature for ground pork is 160°F.
  • Serve your breakfast burgers with ketchup, salsa or the condiment of your choice and a side of hash browns.
