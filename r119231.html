Orange slushy cups

Ingredients

  • 10 oranges, flesh only and cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp dark coconut nectar syrup, plus extra to serve (optional)
  • 250 g cold coconut water
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
161 kJ / 38 kcal

0.9 g

Preparation

  1. Place flesh from 5 of the oranges into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add coconut nectar and remaining orange flesh and blend 1 min/speed 7.
  3. Strain juice mixture through a fine mesh sieve or a nut milk bag into a jug.
  4. Transfer juice mixture into ice cube trays (approx. 4) and place into freezer to set overnight.
  5. Place 2 trays of frozen orange mixture into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Place remaining 2 trays of frozen orange mixture into mixing bowl and repeat mixing 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer into the thermal serving bowl or other large bowl.
  6. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh coconut water into it. Transfer coconut water into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Gently stir to combine mixture and allow to melt slightly.
  7. Once mixture melts to a slushy texture, spoon mixture into serving cups, then drizzle with extra coconut nectar (optional) and serve with a spoon or straw.

Useful Items

nut bag or fine mesh sieve, jug, ice cube tray, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, bowl, teaspoons, cups, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to the Strawberry jelly boats recipe on this recipe chip, for instructions on how to easily it is to separate the orange flesh from the rind.
  • This mixture can be served as a juice and not frozen into a slushy, if you prefer. Simply add coconut nectar and place into refrigerator to chill in step 2 until ready to serve.
  • Some coconut water products are produced on equipment that comes into contact with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern, check the label of your coconut water.
