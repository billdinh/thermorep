Mint choc chip ice cream

Ingredients

  • 200 g pistachio nuts
  • 320 g coconut cream, chilled (see Tips)
  • 12 fresh mint leaves
  • 140 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 drops food-grade peppermint oil (see Tips)
  • 4 tsp cacao nibs

Chocolate coating

  • 200 g coconut oil
  • 100 g pure maple syrup
  • 120 g raw cacao powder
  • 1 pinch salt

Preparation

  1. Place pistachios, coconut cream, mint, syrup, vanilla and peppermint oil into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10 or until smooth.
  2. Add cacao nibs and mix 5 sec/speed 2. Divide mixture between popsicle moulds (see Tips), then insert a wooden popsicle stick into each mould. Place into freezer to set (approx. 4 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate coating

  1. Place coconut oil into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/37°C/speed 2 or until melted.
  2. Add syrup, cacao powder and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a jug and set aside.
  3. Remove popsicles from freezer and moulds. Dip into chocolate coating or drizzle over popsicles - chocolate should set immediately. Serve immediately or place into a sealable container and store in the freezer until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

popsicle moulds, popsicle sticks, jug, sealable container, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • If desired, you can replace the pistachios with soaked almonds or cashews.
  • Place a tin of coconut cream into the refrigerator for several hours to chill. Cream and water may separate into layers. Simply stir to combine before using. Store any leftover coconut cream in the refrigerator in a sealable container.
  • To make your own vanilla bean paste, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • Be sure to use only food-grade peppermint oil in this recipe. You can find it at most baking supply stores or online.
  • We used silicone ice cream moulds (available at TheMix Shop - www.thermomix.com.au/shop or www.thermomix.co.nz/shop) to create 6 popsicles. However, the number of popsicles you make will depend on the size of your moulds.
  • Store any leftover chocolate in a sealable container in the refrigerator. Warm (to a liquid state) before serving.
  • If you don't want to make popsicles, you can scoop ice cream into bowls. Simply remove from freezer 5-10 minutes before serving to soften slightly.
