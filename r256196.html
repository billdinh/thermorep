Apricot Danish slice

Ingredients

  • 60 g blanched almonds
  • 30 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 40 g caster sugar
  • ½ tsp natural almond extract
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 sheets puff pastry (25 cm square sheets - see Tips)
  • 400 g canned apricot halves, drained (approx. 260 g after draining - see Tips)
  • 20 g flaked almonds
  • 50 g smooth apricot jam, lightly beaten

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Place a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) into oven to warm.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add butter, sugar, almond extract and 1 of the eggs and mix 40 sec/speed 3.
  4. Place 1 sheet of pastry onto a large sheet of baking paper. Spread with almond mixture, leaving a 2 cm border around all edges. Distribute 9 of the drained apricot halves evenly on top of the filling.
  5. Crack remaining egg into a small bowl and beat lightly, then brush over pastry border. Using a small sharp knife, cut slits (1 cm apart) into the second sheet of pastry, leaving an uncut 2 cm border around the edge. Place cut pastry sheet carefully over apricot filling, making sure the edges match up. Press edges together to seal, brush with beaten egg, then sprinkle with flaked almonds. Carefully remove heated baking tray from oven and transfer baking paper with pastry onto it. Bake for 20 minutes (200°C), until golden brown.
  6. Brush pastry immediately with lightly beaten apricot jam, then slice and serve warm (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, small bowl, whisk, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own puff pastry, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook. Please note, this will increase the total time of this recipe.
  • You can replace the apricots with drained tinned fruit of choice, if preferred.
  • You can serve this slice with teaspoonfuls of mascarpone cheese, if desired.
  • Use any leftover apricots in smoothies, muffins or cakes, or simply serve with yoghurt.
  • When in season, you can use fresh apricots. However, you will need to steam them gently to remove the skins and also remove the pits. This will increase the total time of the recipe.
