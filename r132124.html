Rajma (kidney bean curry)

Ingredients

Everyday Indian curry paste

  • 30 g cumin seeds
  • 2 - 3 dried bay leaves
  • 30 g coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp whole cloves
  • 12 cardamom pods (black)
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 5 brown onions (approx. 700 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 40 g canola oil
  • 30 fresh curry leaves
  • 800 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp sea salt

Rajma (kidney bean curry)

  • 30 g canola oil
  • 3 brown onions (approx. 450 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 125 g Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • 800 g canned kidney beans, rinsed and drained (approx. 500 g after draining)

Preparation

Everyday Indian curry paste

  1. Place cumin seeds, bay leaves, coriander seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon quill and peppercorns into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, then mill 1 min/speed 9 to a powder.
  2. Add chilli powder, ground ginger, turmeric, onions and fresh chillies (optional) and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee, oil and curry leaves and sauté 6 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes, sugar, stock paste and salt and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 20 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7. Set aside to cool before transferring into freezable containers or snaplock bags. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Rajma (kidney bean curry)

  1. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Add onions and ginger paste and chop 3 sec/speed 5, then cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add salt, pepper, garam masala and 140 g of the reserved curry paste and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add yoghurt and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add kidney beans and cook 2 min/90°C//speed 1. Transfer into a serving bowl and serve warm.

Useful Items

freezable containers (e.g. plastic, silicone), snaplock bag, serving bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • To make you own garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • The Everyday Indian curry paste can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 1 month.
  • You can serve this with rice, bread, chutney or your favourite side. We served ours with rice.

You can use dried beans, instead of canned beans, in this recipe, but you will need to soak and cook them before using.

Weigh 200 g dried kidney beans into a bowl and cover with warm, filtered water. Allow to soak overnight, or up to 48 hours, at room temperature. Drain, rinse and re-cover with warm, filtered water at least 2-3 times in 24-48 hour period. Drain and rinse beans and weigh 200 g of the soaked beans into simmering basket. Place 900 g water into mixing bowl and cook 60 min/90°C/speed 3. If beans are not cooked after 60 minutes, top up with water to 500 g and cook for a further 30 min/90°C/speed 3.
