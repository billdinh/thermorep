Hot and sour prawn soup

Ingredients

  • 40 g dried shiitake mushrooms
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 2 stalks fresh lemongrass, tough outer layer removed, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 bunches fresh coriander (bunches cut into thirds), plus extra leaves for garnishing
  • 3 - 6 fresh bird's eye chillies, trimmed, deseeded if preferred and cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 3 fresh Kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 fresh tomato (approx. 150 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 cm piece galangal, peeled
  • 70 g eschalots, cut into halves
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 700 g water
  • 2 tsp tom yum paste
  • 40 g fish sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 200 g raw peeled prawns
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed, cut into halves and shredded lengthways, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh shiitake mushrooms into it. Cover with boiling water and set aside to soak for 20 minutes. Drain mushrooms and set aside.
  2. Place lemongrass, coriander, chillies, Kaffir lime leaves, tomato, ginger, garlic, galangal and eschalots into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 10
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 4 sec/speed 10.
  4. Add stock paste, water, tom yum paste, fish sauce, sugar and drained mushrooms and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add prawns and cook 3 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add lime juice and stir using spatula, then divide between 4 serving bowls. Garnish with shredded spring onion/shallot and extra coriander leaves just before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, 4 serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • This is a very spicy Thai soup. For a truly authentic soup, use 6 chillies, but prepare for the heat!
