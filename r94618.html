Cornish Fairings

Ingredients

  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 100 g golden caster sugar
  • 60 g golden syrup
  • 175 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground mixed spice
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
Nutrition
per 1 pieces
Calories
461 kJ / 110 kcal
Protein
0.9 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line two baking trays with baking paper then set aside.
  2. Place butter, sugar and golden syrup in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/80°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and around blade with spatula.
  3. Add flour, ginger, mixed spice, cinnamon, bicarbonate of soda and salt then mix 2 min/speed 2.
  4. Spoon out 1 Tbsp-sized pieces of dough and roll into balls then place on prepared baking trays at least 3 cm apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes (180°C) until golden brown (see tip).
  5. Leave biscuits to cool on trays for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

wire rack, 2 baking trays and baking paper, air-tight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The biscuits should have a cracked appearance that occurs during cooking. If biscuits still look smooth after 5 minutes, remove from oven and carefully bang the baking trays on the counter top to make the biscuits crack and spread, before returning to oven to finish baking.

  • These traditional ginger biscuits are commonly found in Cornwall. The term ‘fairing’ originally applied to any edible treat sold at a fair, although it is now synonymous with these crunchy, cracked ginger biscuits.
