Beef stew with buttermilk dumplings

Ingredients

Dumplings

  • butter, for greasing
  • 60 g butter, chilled
  • 230 g self-raising flour
  • 110 g buttermilk
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Stew

  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (optional)
  • 2 brown onions, cut into halves
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 800 g chuck beef, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 500 g water
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable stock paste or Meat stock paste (see Tips)
  • 350 g carrots, cut into rounds (1 cm)
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 100 g tomato paste
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 2 pinches sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to taste

Preparation

Dumplings

  1. Lightly grease Varoma dish and tray then set aside.
  2. Place all dumpling ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5, or until dough forms into a ball
  3. Roll dough into 18 walnut-sized balls and place into Varoma dish and tray. Set Varoma aside.

Stew

  1. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place parsley (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onion, garlic and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add beef and cook 2 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Add all remaining stew ingredients, except reserved chopped parsley, and cook 45 min/80°C//speed .
  6. Place Varoma with dumplings into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 35 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma and set aside.
  7. Carefully remove bay leaves. Transfer stew into serving bowls and garnish with reserved chopped parsley (optional). Season stew to taste and top with dumplings. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste or Meat stock paste recipe.
  • Don't be tempted to use a more expensive cut of beef. Cheaper meats cook better over a long period of time, making this the perfect recipe to use chuck steak or casserole beef.
  • This stew will freeze well in a freezable container. The dumplings do not freeze as well, so if you are planning to only eat half of the stew after cooking, halve the dumpling ingredient quantities and proceed as per recipe. Then, while thawing and re-heating the reserved stew, make a fresh batch of dumplings (placing 700 g water into mixing bowl for steaming dumplings).
