Prawns with Tomato, Saffron and Anchovy Sauce

Ingredients

  • 100 g red peppers, cut in pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 100 g dry white wine
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 2 tinned anchovy fillets
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 24 large raw prawns, peeled
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch sugar

Serving

  • 2 cucumbers, deseeded and cubed
  • 4 Tbsp fresh chives, finely sliced
  • 1 lemon, cut into 6 wedges

Preparation

  1. Place red peppers and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add oil then, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add wine then, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add saffron, anchovies and tomatoes then mix 3 sec/speed 3. With simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed  (see tips). Meanwhile, arrange prawns on Varoma tray and insert Varoma tray in Varoma dish.
  5. Remove the simmering basket and place Varoma into position. Cook 5 min/100°C/speed . Prawns are cooked when firm to the touch. Depending on the size of the prawns, they may need more cooking.
  6. Remove Varoma and cover prawns with aluminium foil to keep warm.
  7. Season sauce to taste with salt, pepper and sugar then blend 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend again 10 sec/speed 8.

Serving

  1. Toss cucumbers with 2 Tbsp chives in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Spread a layer of sauce on each plate, allowing approx. 50 g per portion. Spoon a pile of diced cucumber and chives on the sauce.
  3. Divide cooked prawns between the plates. Scatter with remaining 2 Tbsp chives and serve with a wedge of lemon.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, serving plates

Hints & Tricks

  • Moving the prawns around in the Varoma while cooking ensures even cooking of all the prawns.
  • At the end of step 7 the sauce mixture should be quite thick and will be reduced by approx. one third; if not, cook for a further 5 minutes.
  • Use the back of a spoon to spread sauce on each dinner plate to cover the inner two-thirds of the plate.
  • Instead of serving sauce with prawns, toss through hot pasta.
