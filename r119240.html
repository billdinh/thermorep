Strawberry amaze-balls

Ingredients

  • 40 g freeze dried strawberries (2 packets - see Tips)
  • 70 g puffed brown rice (see Tips)
  • 180 g sunflower seeds
  • 20 g coconut oil
  • 240 g rice malt syrup
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
89 kJ / 21 kcal
Protein
0.6 g
Carbohydrates
2.7 g
Fat
0.8 g

Preparation

  1. Place strawberries into mixing bowl and mill 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place puffed brown rice into mixing bowl and blend Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Transfer into bowl with strawberries and stir to combine. Set bowl aside.
  3. Place sunflower seeds into mixing bowl and grind 1 min/70°C/speed 6.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and grind 40 sec/70°C/speed 6 .
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add coconut oil and grind 40 sec/70°C/speed 6 , until mixture resembles damp breadcrumbs.
  6. Add rice malt syrup and cook 4 min/80°C/speed 3 , without measuring cup , adding vanilla through hole in mixing bowl lid during last 20 seconds of cooking time.
  7. Transfer into bowl with strawberry mixture and fold together using spatula. Allow to cool for 1-2 minutes.
  8. Using damp hands, roll 2 teaspoons of mixture into a ball and transfer into a sealable container. Repeat with remaining mixture (makes approx. 30 balls). Serve immediately or store in the refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

large bowl, sealable container

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any flavour of freeze-dried fruit you wish (e.g. apple, pineapple, mango). You can buy freeze-dried fruit at most supermarkets in the tinned fruit aisle, at specialty food stores or online. We recommend using pure freeze-dried fruit (not combined with other ingredients).
  • You can buy puffed brown rice in the health food aisle at most major supermarkets.
  • You can serve these on a platter, as "pops" on wooden skewers or in mini-cupcake liners.
  • These will keep for 1 week in the refrigerator and up to 3 weeks in the freezer.
