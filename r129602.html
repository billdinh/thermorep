The Great Aussie 'bite'

Ingredients

Cheesecake

  • 250 g cream cheese, room temperature
  • 40 g caster sugar
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 1 egg
  • 1000 g water

Biscuits

  • 180 g plain flour
  • 50 g cocoa powder
  • 80 g icing sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 120 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 egg

Vegemite® caramel

  • 130 g brown sugar
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 2 tsp Vegemite®

Raspberry coulis

  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 200 g raspberries

Assembly

  • 350 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), broken into pieces
  • 30 g vegetable oil
  • 150 g raspberries

Preparation

Cheesecake

  1. Line base and sides of a small slice tin (14 x 22 cm, ensure it fits comfortably in Varoma dish) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place cream cheese, sugar, cream and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  3. Mix speed 4, adding egg through hole in mixing bowl lid until mixture is smooth. Pour cream mixture into prepared baking tray and cover with aluminium foil.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and place cheesecake into Varoma dish. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20-22 min/Varoma/speed 1, until centre is set. Set aside to cool for 30 minutes, then place into refrigerator for 6 hours or overnight (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Biscuits

  1. Place flour, cocoa, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add butter and mix 5 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add egg and combine 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a flat disc. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 2 hours (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.

Vegemite® caramel

  1. Place all ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes), then transfer into refrigerator to thicken (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Biscuits (cont'd)

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of baking paper into a large rectangle (approx. 3 mm thickness). Cut rectangle into at least 28 biscuits (3 x 7 cm). Transfer biscuits with bottom sheet of baking paper onto baking tray and bake for 10-12 minutes (180°C), until firm. Allow to cool completely on tray, then transfer onto a wire rack.

Raspberry coulis

  1. Place sugar, lemon juice and 200 g of the raspberries into mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Strain coulis into a bowl using a fine-mesh sieve. Set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes).

Assembly

  1. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Cut cheesecake into 14 even slices (3 x 7 cm). Transfer caramel into a piping bag fitted with a thin nozzle (5 mm) and pipe a little caramel onto each biscuit. Place 1 cheesecake portion onto 14 of the biscuits. Top with remaining biscuits (caramel side down), pressing gently to form a sandwich. Smooth sides of cheesecake for an even edge. Place onto prepared tray and place into refrigerator.
  3. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then melt 3 min/50°C/speed 3 or until chocolate is smooth. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with aid of spatula.
  4. Add oil and mix 10 sec/speed 4 or until combined. Transfer into a large bowl to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Carefully dip each cheesecake biscuit into chocolate, draining off any excess. Place onto prepared baking tray and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 40 minutes to set (see Tips).
  5. Decorate biscuits with reserved coulis and remaining raspberries to serve.

Useful Items

slice tin, baking paper, aluminium foil, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, bowl, rolling pin, wire rack, fine mesh sieve, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), piping bag with nozzle (5mm), large bowl, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Biscuit dough can be made up to 1 week in advance and stored in the refrigerator.
  • Biscuits can be baked in advance and stored in a sealable container for 2 days. Leftover biscuits can be crumbled over ice cream or sandwiched together with filling of choice.
  • Cheescake can be made up to 2 days in advance and stored in the refrigerator.
  • Caramel can be stored in a sealable jar for up to 1 week. Bring to room temperature before using. Leftover caramel can be used as a topping for ice cream or as filling for a cake.
  • Assembled biscuits can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • Use leftover chocolate on ice cream or use to sandwich leftover biscuits.
