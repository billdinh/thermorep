Spiced Labne with Squash and Rocket Salad

Ingredients

Spiced Labne

  • 500 g natural yoghurt
  • 100 g dukkah

Mint Vinaigrette

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar beet molasses
  • 30 - 40 g lemon juice, to taste
  • 60 g extra virgin olive oil

Salad

  • 500 g water
  • 450 g butternut squash, unpeeled, cut in wedges (1 cm)
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 100 g red onions, cut in wedges (1 cm)
  • Â½ pomegranate, seeds only
  • 200 g rocket leaves, washed and patted dry
  • 1 - 2 Tbsp pumpkin seeds, to taste (optional)

Preparation

Spiced Labne

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in yoghurt.
  2. Line simmering basket with two layers of muslin cloth and set over a large jug or bowl. Pour yoghurt into simmering basket and place in fridge to drain for 12 hours - the labne will be soft and dense when ready.
  3. After this time, place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in dukkah.
  4. Using a teaspoon, form 12 bite-sized balls of labne and roll each in dukkah to coat. Transfer to a plate, cover with cling film and place in fridge until ready to serve.

Mint Vinaigrette

  1. Place all vinaigrette ingredients in mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 20 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a jug and refrigerate until ready to use.

Salad

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in butternut squash. Rub with oil and sprinkle with oregano then steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Insert Varoma tray. Weigh red onion onto Varoma tray and steam a further 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set squash and onions aside to cool for 10 minutes before refrigerating to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes).
  3. On a serving platter, arrange squash, onions, pomegranate seeds, rocket and pumpkin seeds (if using). Dress with vinaigrette and lightly toss through, then top with reserved spiced labne balls and serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, muslin cloth, small bowl, cling film, jug, serving platter, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To store extra labne, place in an airtight container, cover with oil and place in fridge.
  • All parts of this recipe can be made ahead of time and plated just before serving.
