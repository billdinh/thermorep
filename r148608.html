Broccoli soup with sage oil

Ingredients

Sage oil

  • 30 fresh sage leaves
  • 80 g olive oil

Tahini sauce

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 40 g pine nuts
  • 60 g tahini
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 3 tsp shiro (white) miso paste
  • 100 g water
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt

Broccoli soup

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 500 g broccoli, cut into florets (approx. 3 cm)
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 550 g water
  • salt, to taste
  • dukkah, to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

Sage oil

  1. Place sage leaves into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer into a jug and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Tahini sauce

  1. Place garlic, pine nuts and tahini into mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 8.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add remaining sauce ingredients and whisk 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Broccoli soup

  1. Place garlic, onion and oil into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add 250 g of the broccoli and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add remaining 250 g broccoli and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add stock paste, water and salt and cook 20 min/100Â°C/speed 2.
  6. Blend 1 min/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9. Divide soup between serving bowls. Top each bowl with 1 tablespoon of the Tahini sauce, 1 teaspoon of the Sage oil and a pinch of dukkah to serve.

Useful Items

jug, sealable container, serving bowls, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • To make your own dukkah, try our Hazelnut dukkah with fennel (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • For a spicier flavour, replace the Sage oil with the Garlic chilli oil from the Yoghurt soup with chickpeas and garlic chilli oil (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • You can store leftover Tahini sauce in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Leftover Sage oil can be stored in a sealable container in the pantry for up to 5 days.
