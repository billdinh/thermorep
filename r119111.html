Master stock chicken

Ingredients

Master stock

  • 1000 g water
  • 70 g light soy sauce
  • 20 g dark soy sauce
  • 20 g Shaoxing wine
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 piece orange peel (4 x 3 cm), no white pith

Chicken

  • 800 g chicken winglets, skin on
  • 150 g rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp molasses
  • 1 tbsp Chinese five spice

Salad

  • 200 g carrots, cut into thirds
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, white parts only, cut into thirds
  • 120 g radishes
  • 200 g daikon, peeled and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 1 fresh green chilli, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 fresh red chilli, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 40 g rice wine vinegar
  • 20 g light soy sauce
  • ½ lemon, juice only
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 50 g roasted unsalted peanuts
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds

Preparation

Master stock

  1. Place all master stock ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/90°C/speed .

Chicken

  1. Add chicken to mixing bowl and cook 20 min/90°C//speed .
  2. Place simmering basket or colander over a large bowl. Use tongs to carefully transfer chicken pieces into simmering basket or colander, skin side up. With master stock in mixing bowl, cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1. Place a large sieve over a sealable, sterilised jar or container and strain masterstock into it (see Tips). Discard any solids. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Position oven shelf to highest position. Preheat oven to 250°C. Line a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Place vinegar and molasses into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Pour hot vinegar mixture over chicken, catching excess liquid in bowl. Place simmering basket or colander over another bowl and pour hot vinegar mixture from bowl over chicken again. Place simmering basket or colander over first bowl and pour hot vinegar mixture over chicken for a third time. Discard vinegar mixture.
  5. Transfer chicken onto lined baking tray, skin side up. Place onto top shelf in oven and bake for 15 minutes (250°C), or until dark golden. Remove from oven and dust with Chinese five spice. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salad

  1. Place carrots, spring onions/shallots, radishes, daikon and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Chop for a further 2 sec/speed 5 for a finer texture.
  3. Add coriander, vinegar, soy sauce, lemon juice, sesame oil and pepper and combine 5 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a serving bowl, then scatter with peanuts and sesame seeds. Serve chicken with salad.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, sealable container, large bowl, baking paper, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), bowl, serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Chicken winglets are the chicken wing with the tip and drumette removed. They are sometimes called wingettes or silkies. Drumettes, with skin on, can also be used in this dish (adjust baking time as needed).
  • Boil and strain masterstock, then store in the refrigerator in a sealable, sterilised jar or container in the refrigerator. It will set to a jelly and can be reused many times, developing in flavour through further use. Boil before each use, then boil again and strain before storing again in the refrigerator (up to 1 week) or in the freezer (up to 3 months). Use it to braise meats or even tofu.
