Rolled Shoulder of Lamb

Ingredients

  • 15 g tinned anchovies (approx. 6 fillets)
  • 25 g fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 1 Tbsp capers, drained
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10 g lemon juice
  • 10 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1500 - 2000 g shoulder of lamb, boned
  • 150 g dry white wine
  • 150 g water
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1924 kJ / 460 kcal
Protein
33.8 g
Carbohydrates
0.5 g
Fat
35.9 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Place anchovies, parsley, capers and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add lemon juice, oil, mustard and pepper then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Unroll lamb joint and spread herb mixture over meat. Roll up lamb joint and tie securely with butcher's string.
  3. Place in roasting tin then roast for 30 minutes (220°C). Meanwhile, place wine and water in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  4. After the initial 30 minutes roasting time, pour wine and water over lamb then return to oven. Reduce temperature to 160°C and cook for 1 ½-2 hours depending on weight of joint and desired result (see tip).
  5. Remove lamb from oven, cover with foil to keep warm and allow to rest for 20 minutes. Carve and serve with gravy made from the cooking juices (see tip).

Useful Items

butcher's string, roasting tin, aluminium foil, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Select cooking time according to weight of lamb: 20 minutes per 500 g, plus 20 minutes.
  • To make gravy in your Thermomix®, place pan juices from roast plus stock to make 1 litre, 50 g plain flour, salt and pepper to taste in mixing bowl then cook 12-15 minutes/Varoma/speed 2.

  • Few things say roast dinner more than lamb. This dish uses surprisingly few ingredients for such a flavoursome result. This is a great dish for get-togethers – it’s beautifully tender and looks really impressive in the middle of the table, ready for carving. Best enjoyed in the spring when British lamb is available.
