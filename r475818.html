Korean Beef Sauté

Ingredients

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • ½ oz fresh ginger, peeled, sliced
  • 3 oz Bosc pear, peeled, in pieces
  • 2 oz soy sauce
  • 1 ½ oz brown sugar
  • ½ oz sesame oil
  • ½ oz Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 oz yellow onion, halved, thinly sliced
  • 16 oz prime ribeye steak, partially frozen (see Tip)
  • 1 oz vegetable oil, divided
  • sesame seeds, to garnish
  • fresh cilantro leaves, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, ginger, pear, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, gochujang, cornstarch and pepper into mixing bowl and purée 30 sec/speed 8. Transfer marinade to a large zip lock bag and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in onions. Slice partially frozen meat ⅛ inch thick. Place onion and meat into bag with reserved marinade and refrigerate 1 hour.
  3. Drain meat and onions, discard marinade and set meat and onions aside. Place 0.5 oz vegetable oil into mixing bowl and without measuring cup, heat 3 min/250°F/speed .
  4. Add half the drained meat and onions and without measuring cup, cook 5 min/250°F//speed . Transfer to a bowl and keep warm. Place 0.5 oz vegetable oil and remaining meat and onions into mixing bowl and without measuring cup cook 5 min/250°F//speed . Transfer to another bowl.
  5. Garnish each bowl with sesame seeds and fresh cilantro and serve hot.

Useful Items

large zip lock bag, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks


  • Place meat into freezer and freeze up to 1 hour.
