Chicken rolls with apples, sweet potatoes and curry sauce

Ingredients

  • 60 g Gouda cheese, cut in pieces,
  • 60 g bacon cubes
  • 800 - 850 g chicken breast fillets, skinless, boneless, each sliced open into a single flat steak
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp beef extract (e.g. Bovril, Maggi)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 280 - 300 g onions, cut in quarters
  • 2 - 3 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 400 g sweet potatoes, peeled, cut in thick slices (4 cm)
  • 500 g apples (e.g. Granny Smith, Golden Delicious), cored, cut in quarters
  • 180 g cooking apples (e.g. Bramley, Reinette), peeled, cored, cut in quarters
  • 1 tsp vinegar, preferably cider or rice vinegar (see tip)
  • 1 heaped tsp curry powder, hot or mild, adjust to taste
  • 200 g water

Preparation

  1. Place Gouda cheese in mixing bowl and grate 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place bacon cubes in mixing bowl and sauté 6 min/120°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, slice each chicken breast open with a sharp knife, cutting parallel to the work surface and opening each fillet like a book. Pound lightly with a meat mallet or a rolling pin wrapped with cling film to make them thinner.
  3. In a small bowl, using a pastry brush, stir together soy sauce, beef extract and 1 tbsp (10 g) olive oil. Place each chicken fillet, cut side up, on a piece of baking paper (30 cm x 40 cm) and brush centre with soy sauce mixture. Set remaining soy sauce mixture aside.
  4. Transfer bacon to a bowl and set aside to cool. Place onions, garlic cloves, salt and 30 g olive oil in mixing bowl. Chop 3 sec/speed 5, then sauté 6 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved grated cheese to cooled bacon cubes, stir using spatula to combine and distribute along the middle of each chicken fillet. Roll fillets, wrapping them tightly with the baking paper. To make sure a few holes in the Varoma dish remain unobstructed, place butterfly whisk flat in Varoma dish and arrange parcels on top. Add sweet potatoes around parcels, insert Varoma tray and add Granny Smith apple wedges on it. Set Varoma aside.
  6. Add cooking apples, vinegar, curry powder, water and remaining soy sauce mixture to mixing bowl. Set Varoma in position and cook 35 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Remove Varoma and set Varoma tray aside. Using a toothpick, prick chicken rolls to check whether juices run clear, indicating they are cooked through. If juices are pink, add 50 g water to mixing bowl, set Varoma in position and cook for a further 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  8. Remove Varoma and transfer apple wedges and sweet potatoes to a serving dish. Unwrap chicken rolls, cut in 2 cm slices and transfer to serving dish. Transfer curry sauce from mixing bowl to a sauce boat. Serve chicken, sweet potatoes and apple wedges hot with the curry sauce.

Useful Items

meat mallet or rolling pin, cling film, pastry brush, baking paper, toothpick, sauceboat

Hints & Tricks

  • The sweet and spicy flavour combinations in this main course dish make it a treat for a weeknight or a special occasion. Steamed apples and sweet potatoes accompany the cheese and bacon-filled chicken roll, and the apple flavour is echoed in the curry sauce. Beef extract gives colour and umami flavour to the juicy, tender meat, and if you wish, you can add even more colour by browning the rolls briefly in a hot pan before serving.
  • If the cooking apple is not very tart, add the optional vinegar.
  • The apples for steaming can be left unpeeled if desired.
  • In step 7, instead of further steaming, pan-fry the chicken rolls to complete cooking if they appear underdone, or just to add browning.
