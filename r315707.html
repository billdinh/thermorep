Sweet Potato, Pecan and Blue Cheese Risotto

Ingredients

  • 800 g sweet potatoes, skin on, diced (2 cm)
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 15 g fresh sage leaves, plus 1-2 sprigs leaves only for garnishing
  • 30 g olive oil, plus 1-2 Tbsp for drizzling
  • 320 g risotto rice (e.g. carnaroli, Arborio)
  • 60 g dry white wine
  • 820 g water, boiling
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 20 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 100 g blue cheese, crumbled
  • 80 g pecan nuts, roughly chopped

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place sweet potatoes on baking tray, drizzle with 1-2 Tbsp olive oil and season with salt and pepper, to taste. Roast for 25-30 minutes (200°C) until browned and soft. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place garlic, onions, 15 g sage leaves and 30 g oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add rice and wine then, without measuring cup, sauté 2 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add water, vegetable stock, ½ tsp salt and 2 pinches pepper then scrape base of mixing bowl well with spatula to loosen rice. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid and cook 12-13 min/100°C//speed 1 until rice is cooked.
  7. Add butter, blue cheese, pecans, reserved grated Parmesan and half roasted sweet potatoes. Stir gently with spatula to combine. Leave risotto to rest in mixing bowl for 2 minutes then divide between serving plates and serve immediately, topped with remaining roasted sweet potatoes and remaining 1-2 sprigs sage leaves.

Useful Items

baking tray and baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any crumbly blue cheese in this risotto; Stilton works particularly well.
