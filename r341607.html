Steamed Sea Bass with Watercress Orange Couscous

Ingredients

  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 30 g red wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper
  • 200 g fresh watercress
  • 4 sea bass fillets, skin on (approx. 90 g each)
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 500 g water
  • 180 g couscous
  • 2 ½ oranges, 2 peeled, pith removed, cut in pieces (2 cm), 30 g juice and finely grated zest of ½
  • 20 g toasted pine nuts (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place 100 g oil, 30 g orange juice, vinegar, mustard, ¼ tsp salt and 2 pinches pepper in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 100 g watercress in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 100 g watercress in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer to bowl with chopped watercress and set aside.
  4. Drizzle sea bass with 1 tsp oil then season with ½ tsp salt and 1 pinch pepper. Arrange, skin side down, 2 in Varoma dish and 2 on Varoma tray, leaving some holes uncovered for steam to circulate, then insert Varoma tray.
  5. Place water in mixing bowl, place covered Varoma into position then steam 15-18 min/Varoma/speed 1 until fish is cooked. Set covered Varoma aside to keep warm, reserving cooking liquid in a jug.
  6. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in couscous and 180 g reserved cooking liquid. Remove bowl, cover with cling film and set aside for 5 minutes.
  7. Gently fluff couscous with a fork to separate grains and transfer to bowl with watercress. Add orange pieces and reserved dressing to bowl then mix well with spatula. Divide between serving plates, sprinkle with pine nuts (if using), top each with a sea bass fillet and garnish with grated orange zest. Serve immediately.
