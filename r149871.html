Mini Egg Cupcakes

Ingredients

Icing

  • 150 g double cream
  • 175 g cream cheese
  • 90 g icing sugar
  • 2 lemons, finely grated zest and 15 g juice only
  • ¼ tsp vanilla sugar

Cupcakes

  • 100 g blanched almonds
  • 100 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 130 g butter, soft
  • 130 g brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 2 lemons, finely grated zest only
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 36 mini chocolate eggs

Preparation

Icing

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and without measuring cup, whip until soft peaks form/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk and transfer to a bowl.
  2. Place cream cheese, icing sugar, finely grated lemon zest, lemon juice and vanilla sugar in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 3. Carefully fold into whipped cream with a soft spatula then set aside in fridge. Clean mixixng bowl.

Cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a 12 hole muffin tin with paper muffin cases.
  2. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place carrots in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with ground almonds.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place butter, sugar, vanilla sugar, salt, grated lemon zest and eggs in mixing bowl then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3.5.
  5. Add flour and baking powder then mix 15 sec/speed 2.5. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Add reserved almond and carrot mixture then gently stir in with spatula. Divide between paper cases and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Leave to cool in tin for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and allow to cool completely.
  7. Transfer reserved icing to a piping bag with a star-shaped nozzle and pipe in a swirling motion onto cupcakes. Place 3 mini chocolate eggs carefully on each cupcake and serve.

Useful Items

soft spatula, 12 hole muffin tin, paper muffin cases, wire rack, piping bag with star nozzle, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you want to serve a little later or the next day, keep cupcakes and frosting separately and pipe icing just before serving.
  • To ensure that the cakes are cooked, pierce with a cocktail stick. If stick comes out clean, cupcakes are ready.
