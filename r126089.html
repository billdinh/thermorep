Gnocchi with Taleggio Cheese

Ingredients

  • 35 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for cooking gnocchi
  • 370 g floury potatoes, peeled, sliced (0.5 cm)
  • 225 g pumpkin flesh, cubed (2-3 cm)
  • 660 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 - 2 pieces lemon peel
  • 1 medium egg
  • 3 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 25 g butter
  • 300 g milk
  • 150 g Taleggio, in pieces
  • 2 - 3 fresh chives, chopped

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in potatoes. Insert Varoma tray and weigh in pumpkin. Cover then steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place 50 g flour and lemon peel in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl.
  4. Add reserved cooked potatoes and reserved cooked pumpkin then blend 20 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add 580 g flour, egg, reserved Parmesan cheese and 1½ tsp salt then mix 40 sec/speed 4.
  6. Tip out dough onto a well-floured surface and form into a ball. Cover loosely with cling film and allow dough to cool for 30 minutes.
  7. Divide dough into 4 pieces and, with lightly floured hands, roll each piece into a sausage shape (Ø 2 cm). Cut sausages into pieces 1 cm long to form dumplings. Press lightly on gnocchi with a floured fork, removing gnocchi from work surface gently with a spatula if necessary. Brush off excess flour from gnocchi then transfer to a baking tray, cover loosely with cling film and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Place remaining 30 g flour, butter, milk, Taleggio and remaining 1 tsp salt  in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/90°C/speed 4. Meanwhile, bring a large pan of water, with 1 tsp salt added,  to a boil and cook gnocchi, in batches, until they rise to the surface. Remove and drain gnocchi with a slotted spoon then transfer to a serving bowl.
  9. Pour Taleggio cheese sauce over gnocchi and sprinkle with chives to serve.

Useful Items

pastry brush, baking tray, soft spatula, cling film, serving bowl, fork , slotted spoon, large saucepan, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Freeze any leftover, uncooked gnocchi (without sauce). Arrange pieces on a tray and put them in the freezer for 30 minutes until firm, then transfer to a freezer-proof bag. They can be cooked straight from frozen, with a few extra minutes added to the cooking time.
  • To taste, sprinkle with grated Parmigiano Reggiano.
