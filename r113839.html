Greek Walnut Cake 'Karidopita'

Ingredients

Cake

  • 2 ½ oz unsalted butter, plus extra to grease
  • 6 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 oz stale bread, broken into pieces
  • 9 oz walnut pieces
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 3 large eggs
  • 4 ½ oz sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp brandy
  • 4 ½ oz milk

Syrup

  • 18 oz sugar, divided
  • ½ organic lemon, thin peel only, no pith
  • 12 ½ oz water
  • 1 vanilla bean pod

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and flour a springform pan (10 in.) and set aside.
  2. Place bread into mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add walnuts and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place flour and cloves into mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer into bowl with bread-walnut mixture.
  5. Place butter, eggs and sugar into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 4.
  6. Add reserved bread-walnut-flour mixture, cinnamon, baking powder, brandy and milk and mix 25 sec/speed 4. Transfer mixture to prepared springform pan and bake for 50-60 minutes (350°F). Allow cake to cool in pan. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl

Syrup

  1. Place 3 oz sugar and lemon peel into mixing bowl and zest 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add remaining 15 oz sugar and water. Split vanilla pod lengthwise, scrape out seeds and add seeds and vanilla pod into mixing bowl and cook 9 min/212°F/speed 1.
  3. Remove vanilla pod. Pour syrup over cooled walnut cake and wait until all the syrup is absorbed. Slice cake and serve.

Useful Items

springform pan (10 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Greek walnut cake “Karidopita”: one of the most popular cakes in Greece made with walnuts and bread, giving a soft but crunchy texture in every bite. Its texture, thanks to soaking in sugar syrup, is surprisingly sponge-like.
  • Brandy can be replaced with prune juice, apple cider or simply water.
  • To optimize the absorption of syrup use a wooden skewer to evenly poke holes in the cake, allowing syrup to be more uniformly absorbed. Top cake with finely chopped walnuts to cover skewer holes.
