Vegan parmesan shards

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 60 g sunflower seeds (see Tips)
  • 60 g blanched almonds (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking

Shards

  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp salt
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
816.4 kJ / 194.4 kcal

9.2 g

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sunflower seeds and almonds into it. Add salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse nuts and seeds.

Shards

  1. Preheat oven to 120°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place drained nuts and seeds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add remaining ingredients to mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend 10 sec/speed 8 or until paste is almost smooth.
  5. Place mixture between two sheets of baking paper and roll out until 2-3 mm thick. Transfer mixture with baking paper onto baking tray and peel off top layer of baking paper. Bake for 40-45 minutes (120°C), until dry and firm but not browned. Set aside to cool completely, then snap into large shards to serve.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), bowl, baking paper, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your nuts and seeds, soak them in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
  • These shards pair well with soups, salads and dips. Try them with the Creamy tomato and basil soup or the Kale, sprouts and red cabbage salad (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip).
  • You can store these shards in a sealable container in the pantry for up to 3 days.
