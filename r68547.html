Meatloaf with red sauce

Ingredients

Meatloaf

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 500 g topside beef, cut into cubes (3 cm), slightly frozen
  • 2 eggs
  • 50 g fresh breadcrumbs
  • 1 pinch salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 8 - 10 slices prosciutto
  • 2 slices double smoked ham, cut into halves
  • 90 g mozzarella, cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • 500 - 800 g mixed vegetables (e.g. celery, potato, carrot), cut into slices as needed, for serving

Red sauce

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 180 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into pieces
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 150 g white wine
  • 800 g canned whole peeled tomatoes
  • 600 g sweet potato, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 170 g carrot, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 170 g potato, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 pinch salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

Meatloaf

  1. Place Parmesan cheese and fresh flat-leaf parsley into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic clove and fresh rosemary into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add beef and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/8 times.
  4. Add eggs, fresh breadcrumbs, salt, pepper and reserved Parmesan and parsley and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/4 times, then mix 20 sec/speed 5 to incorporate.
  5. Lay out ThermoMat or a long sheet of aluminium foil on to a work surface and lay prosciutto lengthways onto it. Place mince mixture on top of prosciutto and shape mince mixture into a large rectangle (approx. 3 cm thick). Place ham and cheese cubes along the longest edge and roll mince into a log to enclose. Gently remove the ThermoMat or foil from underneath and ensure prosciutto is wrapped securely around the outside of the meatloaf.
  6. Lightly oil Varoma tray and place meatloaf into the centre. Distribute mixed vegetables of choice loosely around meatloaf and in Varoma dish, ensuring there is no overcrowding. Set Varoma aside.

Red sauce

  1. Place garlic clove, brown onion, fresh flat-leaf parsley and celery into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add olive oil and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add white wine, canned tomatoes, sweet potato, carrot, potato, salt and pepper and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Place Varoma with meatloaf and vegetables into position and cook  35-40 min/Varoma/speed 1. Test to ensure meat is cooked through by slicing open and ensuring the colour is uniform, or continue cook to your preference.
  5. Once cooked, place meatloaf onto a large platter with steamed vegetables and pour sauce over the top or serve on the side.

Useful Items

ThermoMat, aluminium foil, serving platter

Hints & Tricks

  • Substitute the beef in this recipe with lamb, pork or chicken for a variation in flavour.
  • Picnic meatloaf: Use traditional sandwich fillings such as boiled eggs and sun-dried tomatoes to substitute the ham and cheese filling. Hard-boil your eggs at step 1, then proceed as per recipe above. You may want to refrigerate your meatloaf to a terrine-type texture and then slice just before serving at your picnic.
  • If you prefer a smoother consistency to your red sauce, blend 3 sec/speed 5 after cooking in step 10.
  • This is a fantastic family meal that is sure to change your perception of 'meatloaf'. Incorporate fillings of choice into this versatile meatloaf recipe.
