Five-spice Duck with Mushrooms, Asian Vegetables and Rice

Ingredients

  • 15 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2-3 mm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ - 1 fresh red chilli, deseeded (optional)
  • 700 g duck breasts, skin on
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp Chinese five spice powder, shop-bought or homemade (see tip), to taste
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1200 g water
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • 80 g soy sauce
  • 200 g white long-grain rice
  • 200 g pak choi, cut in pieces
  • 100 g broccoli florets, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 250 g button mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 - 4 Tbsp honey (approx. 80 g)

Preparation

  1. Place ginger, garlic and chilli (if using) in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Using a sharp knife, score duck skin in a criss-cross pattern. Rub duck all over with Chinese five-spice powder and salt, then place skin-side down on Varoma tray and set aside.
  3. Place water, vegetable stock paste and 40 g soy sauce in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, add long-grain rice to it and mix well using spatula to wet rice. Set Varoma dish in position and add pak choi and broccoli florets to it. Insert Varoma tray with duck breasts, close Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Set Varoma aside and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Reserve 100 g (1 measuring cup) of the cooking liquid and discard the rest. Drain rice well and transfer to a serving bowl. Separate rice grains with a fork, then cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm.
  5. Place sesame oil and reserved garlic-ginger mixture in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, heat 1 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, pat duck dry with a paper towel.
  6. Add mushrooms to mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, cook 10 min/Varoma//speed 0.5. Meanwhile, heat a small frying pan over high heat without oil, place duck breasts skin side down in hot pan and press down with a spatula for 20 seconds to flatten. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 2-4 minutes or until skin is very crisp and most of the fat is rendered out. Turn duck over and cook for a further 1-2 minutes. Transfer duck breasts, skin-side up, to serving bowl with rice and cover.
  7. Drain ¾ of the fat from the frying pan, leaving a little in the pan. Return frying pan to high heat and deglaze by adding reserved steaming liquid, remaining 40 g soy sauce (2-3 Tbsp) and 3-4 Tbsp honey. Allow to reduce for a few minutes, watching carefully to avoid burning, until it becomes a sticky glaze, then set aside.
  8. Add reserved pak choi and broccoli florets to mixing bowl and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Transfer to a serving dish. Place duck breasts on a cutting board, slice diagonally and transfer to serving dish containing vegetables. Drizzle duck with glaze and serve hot with rice.

Useful Items

kitchen towel, paper towel, large serving bowl, metal spatula, non-stick frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • The duck breasts in this recipe are gently steamed and then pan-seared, resulting in tender meat that is beautifully infused with spices. The sweet and sour glaze, together with the vegetables and rice, make this a complete main dish. And do try the vegetarian version with tofu – an excellent alternative to duck.
  • To make homemade five-spice powder place 2 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, 3 star anise, 2 tsp dried fennel seeds, 2 cinnamon sticks and 6 cloves in a clean and dry mixing bowl and toast 5 min/Varoma//speed 1. Remove mixing bowl lid, set mixing bowl aside to cool for 10 minutes, then grind 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and grind a further 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer to an airtight container and store until ready to use.
  • Five-spice Tofu with Mushrooms, Asian Vegetables and Rice: for a vegetarian variation, replace duck breast fillets with 500 g firm tofu, cut in slices (2 cm), and add a little oil when frying tofu in the pan.
