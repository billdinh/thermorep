Cranberry, Lemon and Almond Cake (egg, dairy and gluten free)

Ingredients

  • 120 g water
  • 3 Tbsp chia seeds
  • 400 g blanched almonds
  • 80 g rapeseed oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 120 g maple syrup
  • 1 ½ lemons, finely grated zest and juice (approx. 85 g)
  • 2 Tbsp poppy seeds
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp gluten free baking powder
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 100 g dried cranberries
  • 1 batch vegan vanilla frosting

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in water. Set aside.
  2. Place chia seeds in mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to bowl with water then mix together well with a fork. Set aside to soak for 15 minutes.
  3. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease sides and line base of a cake tin (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  5. Place oil and maple syrup in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/60°C/speed 1.
  6. Add grated lemon zest and juice, reserved ground almonds, poppy seeds, salt, baking powder and bicarbonate of soda then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  7. Add cranberries and reserved chia mixture then mix 15 sec//speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 15 sec//speed 5.
  8. Transfer to prepared tin. Bake for 50-60 minutes (180°C), covering with foil for the last 15 minutes if browning too much, until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tin before removing.
  9. Once sponge is cool, use a bread knife to slice in half horizontally to create 2 even layers. Arrange a sponge on a cake stand then ice with vegan frosting (if using, see page XX) then top with other sponge layer.
  10. Serve in slices.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, skewer, aluminium foil, serving plate or cake stand, bread knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Store in an airtight container - will keep for 2-3 days.
  • For a truly allergy free cake, always check that all your ingredients come from a source free of allergens.

The almonds give this flour-free cake a richness that is beautifully offset by the tartness of the cranberries and lemon. Replacing eggs with a chia seed mixture and traditional flour with almonds makes this cake perfect for those with multiple allergies. Can be enjoyed with or without the vegan vanilla icing.
