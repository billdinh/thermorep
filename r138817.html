Whoopie pies

Ingredients

Lemon butter

  • 40 g lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 90 g caster sugar
  • 80 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 eggs

Whoopie pies

  • 250 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 120 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 1 egg
  • 100 g full cream milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Lemon butter

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place lemon juice, caster sugar, butter and eggs into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/80°C/speed 3.
  2. Insert a spoon into mixture to test if it has reached setting point; it should coat the back of the spoon in a thick layer. If butter is not set, cook until desired consistency/80°C/speed 3 . Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a sealable storage jar or container and allow to cool (approx. 5 minutes). Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator (minimum of 30 minutes - see Tips) until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Whoopie pies

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper. Using a pencil, draw 20 circles (5 cm diameter) onto each sheet of baking paper, leaving approx. 3 cm between each circle. Set trays and paper aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour into it. Add baking powder and salt and stir to combine. Set bowl aside.
  3. Place sugar and lemon zest into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9 .
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add butter and mix 10 sec/speed 5 .
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 10 sec/speed 4 or until pale and fluffy.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add egg and mix 5 sec/speed 6 .
  7. Add half of the reserved flour mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add milk and vanilla and mix 5 sec/speed 5 .
  9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add remaining flour mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 5, until well combined. Transfer mixture into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (1 cm).
  10. Pipe 40 circles (approx. 1 teaspoon of mixture) onto prepared baking trays (see Tips). Bake for 10 minutes (170°C) until risen and just firm to touch. Allow to cool for 5 minutes on trays, then use a palette knife to transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. Spread or pipe 1 teaspoon of reserved lemon butter onto flat side of 20 cake halves and top with remaining cake halves. Dust with icing sugar, just before serving.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, storage jars with sealable lids, spoon, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm), palette knife, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Lemon butter will thicken as it cools. Ensure it is cold when piping onto pies as it will hold its shape and will be easier to work with.
  • To save time you can prepare the lemon butter 2-3 days in advance and store in the refrigerator. You can make the whoopie pies a day in advance and store in the refrigerator, then assemble on the day of the party.
  • You can replace the Lemon butter with jam and cream or Marshmallow icing (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • A Whoopie pie tin can be used in place of baking trays; recipe will yield 6 large pies.
