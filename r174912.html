Mediterranean Lamb Burger

Ingredients

Lamb Burgers

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 ½ oz yellow onions
  • 2 sprigs parsley, leaves only
  • 16 oz lamb shoulder, partially frozen, cut into 1 in. pieces, divided
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ tsp prepared horseradish
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp mustard
  • 2 oz bread crumbs

Yogurt Mint Dressing

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 mint leaves, fresh
  • 4 oz plain yogurt
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp salt
  • &frac18; heaping tsp freshly ground black pepper

Cooking and Assembly

  • 4 oz hummus
  • 4 oz feta cheese, drained and thinly sliced
  • 4 oz tomatoes, sliced
  • ½ oz salad greens

Preparation

Lamb Burgers

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add onions and parsley and chop 3 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add 8 oz lamb, egg, horseradish, cumin, coriander, mustard and bread crumbs and grind 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place remaining 8 oz of lamb into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add reserved ground lamb mixture and mix Turbo/0.5 sec/1 time.
  5. Divide and shape lamb mixture into four equal-sized patties and create a dimple in the center by pressing down with your fingers to prevent them from rising while cooking. Transfer to a plate, cover with cling wrap and place in the refrigerator. Clean mixing bowl.

Yogurt Mint Dressing

  1. Place garlic and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add yogurt, lemon juice, cumin, salt and pepper and mix 10 sec/speed 3.

Cooking and Assembly

  1. Cook patties over medium high heat on a grill or stovetop to taste (see Tip).
  2. Spread hummus on base of bun, add patty and top with Yogurt Mint Dressing, feta, tomatoes and salad greens. Spread Yogurt Mint Dressing on inside of top bun and serve immediately.

Useful Items

grill, thermometer, cling wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace lamb shoulder with ground lamb and omit meat grinding step. Place ground lamb with egg and spices into mixing bowl and Turbo/0.5 sec/2 times.
  • Partially freeze lamb for up to 1 hour before grinding to ensure an even grind and to optimize texture.
  • Lamb cooking guidelines: medium rare is approximately145°F, medium is approximately 160°F, and well-done is approximately 170°F.
  • Do not flatten burger when cooking.
  • Make your own hummus using recipe from The Basic Cookbook.
