Pancakes with Spinach, Bacon and Ricotta

Ingredients

Buckwheat Pancakes

  • 75 g buckwheat flour
  • 75 g plain flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 250 g milk
  • 100 g water
  • 20 g sunflower oil

Filling

  • 120 g bacon, cured and smoked, sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 300 g fresh spinach leaves, roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 250 g ricotta cheese
  • 30 g single cream
  • 50 g pine nuts
  • 10 - 20 g butter, for frying

Cheese Sauce

  • 200 g single cream

Preparation

Buckwheat Pancakes

  1. Place buckwheat flour, plain flour, eggs, milk, water and oil in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to rest for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Filling

  1. Place bacon in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, onions and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Cook 10 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add reserved chopped bacon and cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add spinach, salt and pepper then cook 15 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Drain cooked spinach mixture through simmering basket, squeezing out well and discarding liquid, then transfer to a bowl.
  6. Place bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in ricotta, cream and pine nuts. Mix well then set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  7. Once batter has rested, heat a knob of butter in a large pancake or frying pan over a medium heat. Pour a small ladle of batter into the centre then spread out thinly. Cook for 2-3 minutes, then flip over and cook for a further minute, then transfer to a plate, cover and keep warm. Repeat, adding more butter when necessary, until all batter is used, then preheat oven to 180°C.
  8. Drain cooked spinach mixture through simmering basket, squeezing out well and discarding the liquid.
  9. Arrange pancakes on a work surface and distribute filling evenly between each. Roll pancakes up and place in an oven-proof dish.

Cheese Sauce

  1. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 7 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add cream and heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1 to thicken sauce. Pour sauce over pancakes.
  3. Bake for 10 minutes (180°C) then serve immediately while still hot.

Useful Items

pancake pan or frying pan, small ladle, cling film, oven-proof dish for serving, hob, oven
