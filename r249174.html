Ginger chicken and crunchy noodle salad

Ingredients

Ginger soy dressing

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 30 g caster sugar
  • 60 g light olive oil
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 40 g white wine vinegar

Poached chicken and salad

  • 10 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1400 g water
  • 2 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 500 g chicken tenderloins, cut into halves
  • 200 - 300 g mixed salad greens (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 1 carrot, cut into thin slices (see Tips)
  • 4 - 6 radishes, cut into thin slices (see Tips)

Fried noodles

  • oil, for frying
  • 20 - 30 g fresh chow mein noodles (see Tips)

Preparation

Ginger soy dressing

  1. Place all dressing ingredients into mixing bowl and combine 30 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Poached chicken and salad

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water, stock paste and chicken and poach 10-12 min/100°C//speed 1 or until chicken is cooked. Drain chicken and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.
  3. Arrange salad greens, spring onions/shallots, carrot and radishes onto a serving platter. Place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Fried noodles

  1. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large saucepan over high heat and add 2-3 cm oil. Place a plate onto mixing bowl lid and weigh noodles onto it. Carefully drop 1 noodle into oil; if it starts to bubble, then oil is ready for frying. Carefully add remaining noodles to pan and fry until golden and crispy (approx. 5 minutes). Using tongs or a slotted spoon, transfer onto lined plate and allow to drain and cool.
  2. Roughly chop poached chicken and arrange on top of reserved salad. Drizzle with reserved dressing, then top with fried noodles to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, serving platter, plate, paper towel, large saucepan, tongs, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • We used a mandoline to easily cut the vegetables into thin slices.
  • You can replace the mixed salad greens with shredded silverbeet or iceberg lettuce.
  • Add any leftover noodles to stir-fries or soups.
