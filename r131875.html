Fried Cheese and Tomato Pizzas

Ingredients

Dough

  • 350 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • oil, for greasing
  • 300 g semolina
  • 300 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for flouring
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 100 g pecorino cheese, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • oil, for frying

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 100 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil.
  2. Add semolina, flour, salt and remaining 250 g water then knead 5 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film then set aside to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours). Towards end of rising time, prepare topping.

Topping

  1. Place pecorino cheese in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer half to a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Add garlic and oil then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then heat 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add tomatoes, salt, basil and oregano then cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. On a lightly floured work surface, knead dough gently. Divide into 25 golf ball sized pieces then roll into circles (approx. Ø 10 cm). Cover with cling film and allow to rest for 15 minutes.
  5. Heat a little oil in a frying pan then fry dough circles, 3-4 pieces at a time, over a medium-high heat, until cooked through (approx. 2-3 minutes) then drain on paper towels. Blot excess oil with paper towels then spread tomato sauce onto fried bases and sprinkle with reserved grated pecorino.
  6. Serve immediately while still hot.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, rolling pin, frying pan, paper towel, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Add other toppings to the pizzas (e.g. anchovies, capers, olives, salami...)
  • Keep dough balls covered with cling film until frying to prevent dough from drying out.
