Pear Frangipane

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 120 g unsalted butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 180 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 40 g icing sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 3 tsp water

Pears

  • 500 g water
  • 3 pears (e.g. Conference or Comice) peeled, halved, cored (approx. 320 g)

Frangipane Filling

  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • 120 g caster sugar
  • 120 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 120 g ground almonds
  • 15 g plain flour

Glaze

  • 1 Tbsp apricot jam, melted

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place butter, flour, icing sugar, salt, egg yolk and water in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Tip out and bring together into a flattened ball, then wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, cook pears.

Pears

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Weigh in pears then steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set pears aside to cool (see tip). Discard water then rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Preheat oven to 170°C.
  3. Roll out chilled pastry between two pieces of cling film, or on a lightly floured surface, and use to line a loose bottom, fluted tart tin (Ø 22 cm). Set aside in fridge for 45 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Frangipane Filling

  1. Place lemon peel and 20 g sugar in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and remaining 100 g sugar then beat 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add eggs and mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add almonds and flour then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to pastry shell in tart tin.
  5. Slice cooled pears lengthways, starting 1 cm from the top. Fan slices out and place cut side down on tart filling. Bake for 35-45 minutes (170°C) until risen and golden.
  6. Glaze with melted apricot jam while still warm, then leave in tin for 15 minutes before removing. Serve warm or at room temperature – delicious with a dollop of crème fraîche.

Useful Items

cling film, fluted tart tin (Ø 22 cm), rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Grind your own almonds if desired. Place 120 g blanched almonds in mixing bowl before starting recipe then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside until needed.
  • The pears will be just cooked after step 2 (a knife should just pierce them easily) – and they will continue to cook in the oven.
  • To prepare the pears, cut them in half lengthways then, using a teaspoon, scoop out and discard the core from the centre.
  • Scatter 1 Tbsp toasted flaked almonds over the surface for garnish, if desired.
  • Tinned pear halves can also be used. Omit step 2 and continue with recipe.

This delicate tart would make a lovely almond-flavoured autumnal dessert. You could always make it ahead and serve cold or reheat if preferred.
