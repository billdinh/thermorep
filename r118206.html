Sunny gazpacho

Ingredients

  • 100 g sourdough bread, cut into slices
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika, plus extra for garnishing
  • 120 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for garnishing
  • 50 g sherry vinegar, plus extra to taste
  • 600 g heirloom yellow tomatoes
  • 1 yellow capsicum, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 20 g white onion
  • 100 g water
  • 100 g ice cubes, plus extra to serve
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to taste
  • ½ tsp white pepper, plus extra to taste

Assembly

  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, peeled, deseeded and cut into thin slices, for garnishing
  • garlic flowers, for garnishing (optional)
  • fresh chives, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place a heavy grill pan or frying pan over high heat and grill bread on both sides. Remove once slightly blackened. Cut 1 of the garlic cloves into halves and while bread is hot, rub generously on both sides with garlic.
  2. Place 50 g of the bread and smoked paprika into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it.
  4. Tear remaining 50 g bread into pieces. Place bread, sherry vinegar, tomatoes, capsicum, cucumber, onion and remaining garlic into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10, slowly pouring oil through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add water, ice, salt and pepper and blend 2 min/speed 10.
  6. Season to taste and add extra vinegar if desired, then blend 1 min/speed 10.

Assembly

  1. Place 2-3 ice cubes into serving glasses or small bowls and divide gazpacho between glasses. Garnish with cucumber, reserved bread crumb mixture, garlic flowers (optional), chives, extra smoked paprika and a drizzle of oil. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

large non-stick high-sided griddle or frying pan, bowl, jug, 8 serving glasses, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Yellow tomatoes are sometimes found in small 'cherry' size, which are fine to use. If yellow tomatoes can not be found, replace with orange heirloom tomatoes. Orange and yellow heirloom tomatoes are sweeter and fruitier than red tomatoes and have a lower acid content. If yellow or orange tomatoes can not be sourced, use the sweetest, ripest red tomatoes available.
  • This soup keeps well in the refrigerator for a couple of days. If it separates, emulsify by placing soup and 5 ice cubes into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10.
  • You can replace white onion with eschalots or red onion, if preferred.
  • Turn this appetiser into a larger entrée by serving it with barbecued prawn cutlets and a chopped salad of yellow tomatoes, capsicum, cucumber and onion.
