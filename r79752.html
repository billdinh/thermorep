Balinese prawn curry

Ingredients

Spice paste

  • 230 g eschalots
  • 130 g garlic cloves
  • 50 g fresh galangal, peeled
  • 60 g fresh ginger, peeled
  • 100 g candlenuts
  • 120 g fresh turmeric, peeled
  • 25 - 50 g fresh bird's eye chillies, deseeded (see Tips)
  • 2 stalks fresh lemongrass, white part only, cut into pieces
  • 90 g coconut oil
  • 50 g palm sugar
  • 150 g water
  • 3 tsp sea salt

Curry

  • 2 tsp coconut oil
  • 1 stalk fresh lemongrass, white part only, bruised
  • 500 g raw tiger prawns, shelled and deveined
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 100 g water
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g coconut cream
  • rice, cooked, to serve
  • fried shallots, for garnishing
  • fresh coriander, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

Spice paste

  1. Place eschalots, garlic, galangal, ginger, candlenuts, turmeric, chillies and lemongrass into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat chopping 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat chopping until a fine consistency is achieved.
  3. Add coconut oil and cook 25 min/90°C//speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add palm sugar, insert measuring cup and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 2.
  5. Add water and salt and purée 15 sec/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 10.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then purée for a further 15 sec/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat blending until a smooth paste consistency is achieved.
  7. Cook 20 min/100°C/speed 2.5, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer spice paste into a sealable storage jar.

Curry

  1. Place 160 g of the Spice paste into mixing bowl, then set the remaining paste aside to cool to room temperature before placing into the refrigerator to store. Add coconut oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add lemongrass, prawns, pepper, water and stock paste and cook 4 min/100°C//speed 1, or until prawns are just cooked, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Remove prawns using a slotted spoon and set aside in a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  3. Add coconut cream to sauce in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 3 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Then allow sauce to simmer 10 min/90°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Pour sauce over prawns in thermal serving bowl or large bowl and remove lemongrass.
  5. Serve curry on a bed of cooked rice, garnished with fried shallots and fresh coriander leaves.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar, slotted spoon, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To ensure your bird's eye chillies weigh accurately, we recommend placing larger quantities into the bowl at once rather than weighing each individual chilli.
  • This curry is quite spicy; adjust chillies to taste.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Once Spice paste has cooled, freeze any leftover paste in an ice cube tray.
