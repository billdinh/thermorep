Banh cam (sesame balls)

Ingredients

Mung bean filling

  • 90 g dried split mung beans (yellow)
  • 1210 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 30 g sugar
  • 50 g desiccated coconut

Dough

  • 250 g glutinous rice
  • 100 g white rice
  • 200 g water, plus extra for rolling
  • 100 g white sugar
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 30 g potato flakes
  • 100 g sesame seeds, for coating
  • vegetable oil (approx. 750 g), for deep frying

Preparation

Mung bean filling

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh mung beans into it. Cover with water and set aside to soak overnight.
  2. Place 1200 g of the water into mixing bowl. Using simmering basket, strain soaked mung beans. Insert simmering basket (with soaked mung beans) into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/90°C/speed 4 ,  without measuring cup.
  3. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside for 10 minutes to drain and cool. Discard water then rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place sugar and remaining 10 g water into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add coconut and reserved cooled beans and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a plate and spread out into a thin layer to cool and dry out. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Once cooled, press and form mixture into 20-25 bite-size balls (approx. 1½ teaspoons each). Transfer onto a plate and set aside.

Dough

  1. Place glutinous rice and white rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min 30 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place water and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add baking powder, potato flakes and reserved rice flour and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and allow to cool (approx. 10 minutes).
  4. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sesame seeds into it. Set aside and place a spoon into the bowl. Fill another bowl with water (for wetting your hands) and set aside. Set aside a baking tray for rolled balls. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside.
  5. With wet hands, place 1 tablespoon of the dough into the palm of your hand and press out into a disc. Place one of the mung bean balls into the middle of the dough and fold edges together to form a ball, rolling it around in the palm of your hands (you may need extra water on your hands as you roll). With aid of the spoon, roll ball in sesame seeds to coat, then transfer onto baking tray. Repeat with remaining dough and mung bean balls to form 20-25 balls.
  6. Place a large saucepan over medium heat and add oil. Heat oil to 175°C (to check your oil is at 175°C, use a kitchen thermometer or drop a small cube of bread in the oil; it should turn golden brown in 15-20 seconds).
  7. Working in batches, carefully drop 5 balls into oil and fry for 5-7 minutes, turning until golden brown all over. Remove balls using a slotted spoon and transfer onto lined plate. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

bowl, 1 plate, large bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), paper towel, kitchen thermometer, large saucepan, slotted spoon, stove top
