Mango Kulfi

Ingredients

  • 40 g granulated sugar
  • 20 g pistachio nuts, unsalted, shelled, plus extra for decoration
  • 20 g blanched almonds
  • 600 g ripe mango, peeled, cut in pieces
  • 250 g Greek yoghurt
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
577 kJ / 138 kcal
Protein
3.3 g
Carbohydrates
17.5 g
Fat
6.1 g

Preparation

  1. Place sugar, pistachios and almonds in mixing bowl then grind Turbo/0.5 sec/2-5 times until finely ground. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place mango and Greek yoghurt in mixing bowl then blend 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add reserved sugar and nut mixture then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer mixture to ice cube trays, cover with cling film and place in freezer for at least 4 hours.
  4. Once frozen, place half frozen mango mixture in mixing bowl and churn 10 sec/speed 6 with aid of spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Transfer to a bowl then place remaining frozen mango mixture in mixing bowl and churn 10 sec/speed 6 with aid of spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Add to bowl. Serve immediately in small bowls with a sprinkling of unsalted, shelled pistachio nuts.

Useful Items

ice cube tray, cling film, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Use double cream instead of yoghurt. Once the mango has been blended twice, add the double cream and blend again at speed 8 until the mixture thickens.
  • Kulfi is a typical Indian frozen dessert. It is similar to ice cream although denser and creamier.
  • As an alternative to freezing the kulfi, spoon into individual glass dishes and serve chilled with a crisp biscuit on the side.
