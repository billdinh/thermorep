Chicken broth with bread dumplings

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 250 g slightly stale bread rolls, cut in pieces (2-3 cm),
  • 80 g onions, cut in halves
  • 6 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves and stalks
  • 25 g butter
  • 200 g milk
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 pinches freshly ground pepper
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 1000 g water
  • 4 chicken wings (approx. 600 g)
  • 200 g mixed vegetables (e.g. carrots, onions, celery), cut in pieces (2-3 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place slightly stale bread rolls in mixing bowl and chop with aid of spatula 6 sec/speed 5 into large rough breadcrumbs. Transfer to another bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions and parsley leaves in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Set parsley stalks aside. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  4. Add butter and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add milk and warm 2 min/60°C/speed 1.
  6. Add reserved breadcrumbs, eggs, 1 tsp salt, pepper and nutmeg and mix 15 sec//speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula and mix a further 15 sec//speed 3. Transfer mixture to a bowl. With wet hands form 30 balls (Ø 4 cm) and arrange them in Varoma dish and tray. Clean mixing bowl.
  7. Place water, chicken wings, mixed vegetables, reserved parsley stalks and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl. Set Varoma in position and cook 30 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma and transfer bread dumplings to individual soup bowls. Strain broth through a fine mesh strainer over dumplings. Serve hot with reserved grated Parmesan cheese on the side.

Hints & Tricks

  • The quintessential comfort food, chicken broth with dumplings is a perfect way to ward off winter chills or to enjoy as a light spring or summer lunch. The dumplings can be served with other soups too, or on their own as a side dish – they’re perfect for soaking up sauces.
  • Dry rolls or white bread should be at least one day old but not completely hard.
  • After straining the broth in step 7, allow chicken wings to cool for a few minutes, then pick the meat off the bones with the aid of a fork, discarding the skin, and add chicken meat to soup bowls or save it for another use.
  • The broth has a delicate chicken flavour. For a stronger broth, feel free to add extra vegetables or meat in step 7 (e.g. chicken, beef, veal).
  • For an even easier version, replace chicken wings with 2 heaped tsp homemade chicken stock paste or 2 chicken stock cubes (each for 0.5 l).
  • Add 120 g bacon cubes or diced ham to dumplings in step 4.
