Date, orange and olive oil cake with salted white chocolate ice cream

Ingredients

Salted white chocolate ice cream

  • 175 g sugar
  • 250 g water
  • 500 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 pinch salt

Date, orange and olive oil cake

  • 200 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 50 g panko breadcrumbs
  • 125 g almonds
  • 125 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp ras el hanout (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 4 eggs
  • 40 g freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 80 g freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 55 g white sugar
  • 6 pitted Medjool dates
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 8 - 10 cardamom pods
  • 2 star anise

Preparation

Salted white chocolate ice cream

  1. Place sugar and water into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/100°C/speed 2 or until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add chocolate and mix 30 sec/speed 2 or until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks into mixing bowl and whisk 5 min/speed 4 or until thick and pale. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into bowl with chocolate and stir to combine. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  4. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whisk without setting time/speed 3, until soft peaks form, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk.
  5. Add salt and reserved chocolate mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer mixture into a freezable container and place into freezer until firm (approx. 6-8 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Date, orange and olive oil cake

  1. Grease and line a loaf tin (21 x 11 cm), then set aside.
  2. Place orange and lemon zests into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add panko breadcrumbs, almonds, sugar, ras el hanout and baking powder and mill 7 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add eggs and oil and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into prepared tin and place into a cold oven. Turn oven to 170°C and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until golden and a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Pierce cake all over with a wooden skewer and leave in tin to cool slightly. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place lemon juice, orange juice, sugar, dates, cinnamon quill, cardamom pods and star anise into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/98°C/speed 1. Drizzle syrup over cake and set aside for a few minutes to soak up the syrup. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool. Serve cake with Salted white chocolate ice cream.

Useful Items

wooden skewer, bowl, deep-sided loaf tin (21 x 11 x 7 cm), wire rack, baking paper, freezable container, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Ras el hanout is a North African spice mix containing a wide variety of spices, including cardamom, cumin, coriander seeds and turmeric. It's available at specialty Middle Eastern grocers, spice shops and some supermarkets.
  • If you don't have a loaf tin, you can use a cake tin, however you will need to adjust the cooking time accordingly.
