Corn, pea and turmeric purée

Ingredients

  • 600 g water, plus extra if needed
  • 2 sweet corn cobs (approx. 600 g total), husks removed
  • 150 g peas, fresh or frozen
  • 2 cm piece fresh turmeric, peeled and cut into slices (see Tip)

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and place corn cobs into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 25-30 min/Varoma/speed 2, until corn is tender.
  2. Carefully remove Varoma lid, place Varoma tray into position and weigh peas onto it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 5 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until corn and peas are tender. Remove Varoma and set aside to cool slightly. Transfer cooking liquid into a jug and set aside.
  3. Once corn cobs are cool enough to handle, remove kernels and place into mixing bowl. Discard cobs. Add peas and turmeric and blend 15 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 8.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add 100 g of the reserved cooking liquid and blend 20 sec/speed 5, to desired consistency.
  6. Set aside 1 serving, then freeze the remainder in individual portions for future use. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, jug, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Turmeric has generated a lot of research interest as a functional food. It's an antioxidant and has been found to have anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects. It's of particular interest to scientists for its potential role in warding off neurological disease and for its possible anticancer and antidiabetic potential. I like to include it in baby purées for some flavour training. If your child has tasted turmeric in their early years they are far more likely to accept the flavour as they get older.
