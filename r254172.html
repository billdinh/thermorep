Veal involtini

Ingredients

  • 8 pieces veal scallopini (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 100 g fresh mozzarella, cut into 8 sticks (approx. 1 x 5 cm)
  • 8 slices pancetta
  • ½ tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 50 g sun-dried tomato pesto (see Tips)
  • 500 g tomato passata
  • 2 bunches asparagus, trimmed

Preparation

  1. Place scallopini onto a clean work surface. Place 3-4 basil leaves onto each scallopini. Roll each stick of mozzarella in a piece of pancetta tucking in edges to seal in cheese. Place a cheese parcel onto bottom edge of each scallopini and roll up tightly to enclose filling. Secure with a toothpick and place seam-side down into Varoma dish and tray. Season with salt and pepper and set aside.
  2. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and sauté 2 min/120°C/speed 2.
  4. Add pesto, passata, salt and pepper. Place Varoma with involtini into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Add asparagus into Varoma around involtini. Secure Varoma lid and cook 5-7 min/Varoma/speed 2, until involtini are still sightly pink inside and the asparagus are tender. Carefully remove toothpicks from involtini. Serve involtini with tomato sauce and asparagus.

Hints & Tricks

  • Ask your butcher to prepare your scallopini for you. Make sure your scallopini is thin (approx. 3 mm in thickness), otherwise cooking time may vary. Girello, a cut of beef, can replace veal in this recipe.
  • To make your own pesto, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
  • For a heartier meal, add other quick-cooking vegetables to the Varoma when adding asparagus (such as green peas, snow peas or thinly sliced potatoes).
