Star Burgers

Ingredients

  • 90 g linseeds
  • 140 g blanched almonds, soaked overnight in water, then drained
  • 150 g tomatoes, quartered
  • 55 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, drained
  • 125 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 40 g pitted dates, medjool
  • 65 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp herb salt
  • 1 Tbsp ground cumin

Preparation

  1. Place linseeds in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add almonds and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add tomatoes, dried tomatoes, carrots, dates, olive oil, herb salt and cumin then mix 30 sec/speed 5, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Take handfuls of the mixture and shape into patties about 1.5 cm thick; you can use cookie cutters for better shapes or just use your hands. Place in a dehydrator for 10 hours. Serve at room temperature.

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have a dehydrator, and don't want the burgers to be raw, then they can be fried lightly in coconut oil or grilled lightly until sealed on both sides. They can also be baked in an oven on its lowest setting (50°C) for 4 hours.
  • Will keep for up to 2 weeks, in an airtight container in the fridge.

  • Many thanks for this recipe to Kate Magic, globally renowned raw food expert and Creative Director of RawLiving.eu.

  • Wrap a romaine lettuce leaf around them before serving.
