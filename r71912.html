Breakfast bars

Ingredients

  • 30 g dried apricots
  • 30 g dried figs, cut into halves
  • 30 g pitted dates
  • 40 g walnuts
  • 40 g raw cashew nuts
  • 40 g macadamia nuts
  • 250 g almonds
  • 3 eggs
  • 60 g honey
  • 1 tbsp apple juice
  • 70 g desiccated coconut
  • 40 g dried cranberries
  • 40 g sunflower seeds
  • 40 g sesame seeds
  • 40 g pepitas

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a slice tin (approx. 18 x 30 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place apricots, figs, dates, walnuts, cashews and macadamia nuts into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/3 times. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add eggs, honey and apple juice and mix 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add all remaining ingredients, including reserved fruit and nut mixture and combine 20 sec//speed 3.  Mix well with spatula until fully combined.
  6. Spread into prepared slice tin then, using wet hands, flatten mix out evenly. Bake for 25 - 30 minutes (160°C) or until golden.
  7. While still warm, cut into 12 rectangular bars then transfer onto a wire rack to cool before serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, slice tin (20 x 30 cm), wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Keep these natural breakfast bars on hand for an on-the-go snack or breakfast, perfect for busy mornings. These bars provide a great energy boost with their essential fatty acids, complex carbohydrates, protein, vitamins, minerals and fibre.
  • Breakfast bars can be stored up to a week in a sealable container.
  • For a 'green breakfast bar' with added nutritional value, add ½ tsp spirulina powder or 4 - 6 spirulina tablets to the mix in step 3 and proceed as per recipe (your bars will turn green in colour).
  • You can substitute honey with maple syrup.
  • Chocolate breakfast bars: add 50 - 80 g dark chocolate chips to the mix in step 5 and proceed as per recipe for a delicious afternoon tea or snack treat.
