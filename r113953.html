Kedgeree

Ingredients

  • ½ oz fresh parsley
  • 35 oz water
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 7 oz long-grain rice
  • 1 tsp salt, divided
  • 4 large eggs
  • 12 ½ oz smoked haddock
  • 1 oz salted butter, cut into pieces
  • 3 oz organic whipping cream
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 1 tsp curry powder

Preparation

  1. Place parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and oil into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl, weigh in rice and add ½ tsp salt on top. Place eggs into Varoma dish and place Varoma into position. Insert Varoma tray and weigh in haddock. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 3.
  3. Set Varoma aside. Transfer eggs into a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes to cool; transfer haddock to a plate and set aside. Meanwhile, line Varoma dish with wet parchment paper, leaving some holes in the Varoma uncovered so steam can circulate.
  4. Remove simmering basket with spatula, transfer cooked rice to prepared Varoma dish and dot with butter. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in cream and add half of the reserved chopped parsley. Add ½ tsp salt, pepper and curry powder. Stir cream mixture together, pour over rice and mix together gently.
  5. Flake haddock and scatter over rice. Peel eggs, cut into quarters, and arrange on rice. Place Varoma into position and steam 3 min/Varoma/speed 2 to warm through. Adjust seasoning to taste, sprinkle with remaining chopped parsley and serve immediately.

Useful Items

parchment paper, large bowl, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Kedgeree is a dish of curried rice, fish and hard-boiled eggs. Originally developed for the British in India, made there with a mixture of rice cooked with butter and dahl, spices and shredded onions. British colonials brought the dish back to the UK during Victorian times and it is still enjoyed today, typically for breakfast, although it makes a tasty lunch or dinner too!
  • Smoked haddock can be replaced with smoked salmon or trout.
