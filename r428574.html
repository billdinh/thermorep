Christmas Tree Spinach Puff

Ingredients

  • 2 sheets puff pastry, ready-made, thawed
  • 5 oz cheddar cheese, cubed
  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried minced onions
  • ½ tsp dried rosemary
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp dried red pepper flakes, to taste
  • 10 oz frozen chopped spinach, thawed, squeezed
  • 8 oz cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp water
  • sweety drop miniature peppers, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Lay 2 sheets of parchment on a work surface. Roll out two sheets of puff pastry (9 in. x 12 in.) on prepared parchments and set aside.
  3. Place cheddar and Parmesan cheeses into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place garlic, parsley, basil, oregano, dried onion, rosemary, salt and red pepper flakes into mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add spinach, cream cheese and reserved grated cheese and mix 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 10 sec/speed 10.
  6. Evenly spread spinach mixture over one sheet reserved rolled out puff pastry. Cover with second sheet of pastry and gently roll over dough with a rolling pin to seal the two pieces together. Cut out a large triangle, or tree shape from pastry (see Tip). Transfer prepared tree with parchment to a baking sheet.
  7. To make tree branches, place a ruler down the center of the tree. With a sharp knife, evenly cut strips from the edge of tree, towards the ruler, leaving the center intact. Twist each strip to form branches.
  8. In a small bowl, whisk egg and water until combined. Brush the assembled tree with egg wash.
  9. Bake for 20-25 minutes (400°F) until puffed and golden brown. Garnish with sweety drops and serve hot.

Useful Items

parchment paper, pastry brush, rolling pin, baking sheet, ruler, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Garnish with mini teardrop tomatoes.
  • Use excess pastry to make twisty straws, cut out with cookie cutters or form into a second spinach tree.
