Gooseberry Curd

Ingredients

  • 400 g fresh gooseberries, rinsed and drained
  • 80 g lemon juice
  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 360 g caster sugar
  • 5 large eggs

Preparation

  1. Place gooseberries and lemon juice in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Blend 20 sec/speed 8 then strain through a fine sieve into a bowl to remove pips and skins. Set purée aside and leave to cool completely before continuing with recipe. Meanwhile, prepare glass jars with airtight lids (see tip) and clean mixing bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place butter, sugar, eggs and cooled gooseberry purée in mixing bowl then cook 10-12 min/80°C/speed 3 until thickened - it will continue to thicken as it cools. While still warm, using a jam funnel, transfer to glass jars with airtight lids (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm. Make sure that the rims of the jars are clean and close immediately. Leave to cool completely then store in fridge and use within 1 month.

Useful Items

fine sieve, glass jars with airtight lids, jam funnel, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To prepare jars, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jars and lids upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heat in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • The slightly tart gooseberry has a relatively short season in the UK, from June-July, but you can always freeze them if you want to use them at a different time. Use this curd in the gooseberry layer cake, also delicious made into a gooseberry fool, or spread on toast or buns.
