Fig and Apricot Jam

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dried figs, stems removed and soaked in water to rehydrate (see Tip)
  • 8 oz soft dried apricots soaked in water to rehydrate (see Tip)
  • 8 oz sugar
  • 10 ½ oz water, use water that you soaked dried fruit in
  • 1 oz lemon juice, freshly squeezed

Preparation

  1. Place figs and apricots into mixing bowl and roughly chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add sugar, water and lemon juice and cook 35 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Test setting point of jam (see Tip), pour into sterilized jars. Turn hot jars upside down for 10 minutes before turning right side up and allowing to cool. Serve or store in a cool, dry place.

Useful Items

sterilized jars

Hints & Tricks

  • Setting point test: place a saucer in the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 tsp jam onto the saucer. When cool, push jam with your finger. If it wrinkles, it is ready. If not, cook jam for a further 2 min/230°F/speed 3, then test again.
  • To rehydrate dried fruit: place fruit in a bowl and pour enough boiling water over it to cover it by about 1 inch. Cover the bowl, and let the fruit soak until it plumps - the exact amount of time depends on the fruit's dryness. Then strain the water and reserve for use in recipe.
  • You can add 2 oz chopped walnuts to give this jam a twist. First chop walnuts 5 sec/speed 4 and reserve, then add them in with the sugar.
