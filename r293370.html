Country-style chicken liver fritters

Ingredients

  • 150 g day old bread (approx. 4 slices), crusts removed (and torn into pieces - see Tips)
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 500 g chicken livers, trimmed
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • oil (approx. 2 tablespoons), for frying

per 1 portion

Preparation

  1. Place bread into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place parsley and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add livers, eggs, salt, pepper and reserved breadcrumbs and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside for 10 minutes to thicken.
  4. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Working in batches, place 6 x ¼ cupfuls of mixture into frying pan and fry for approx. 3 minutes each side, until slightly pink inside or cooked to your liking (makes 12 fritters in total). Transfer to a plate, then serve as desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, large frying pan (26 cm), plate, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace bread with a gluten free bread to make breadcrumbs. Choose a bread with a light texture. A dense bread will not work for this recipe.
  • We served our fritters with Rye loaf and Quick beetroot relish (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip) and pickles as part of a ploughman's platter. But you can also serve them with mashed potatoes or as burger patties (burgers are a great way to introduce children to liver and boost their iron intake!).
