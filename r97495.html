Rhubarb and ricotta bread and butter pudding

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Stewed rhubarb and raspberries

  • 300 g rhubarb, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 100 g frozen raspberries
  • 140 g caster sugar
  • 1 orange, juice only
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 1 vanilla bean, cut into halves lengthways and seeds removed

Bread and butter pudding

  • butter, for greasing
  • 12 slices fruit bread, day-old (approx. 750 g) (see Tips)
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 30 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g rhubarb, thinly sliced (½ cm)
  • 4 eggs
  • 160 g full cream milk
  • 220 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 50 g brandy
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • raw sugar, for sprinkling

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for at least 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Stewed rhubarb and raspberries

  1. Place rhubarb, raspberries, sugar, orange juice, lemon juice and vanilla bean seeds into mixing bowl and cook 10-12 min/100°C//speed , until softened but still retaining some shape. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 30 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Bread and butter pudding

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a baking dish (30 x 23 x 5 cm) and set aside.
  2. Line a large baking tray with baking paper. Place fruit bread slices onto baking tray and bake for 7-10 minutes (160°C), or until lightly toasted.
  3. Place orange zest into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Chop 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add icing sugar and 200 g of the reserved ricotta and blend 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Mix 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Spread 6 toasted bread slices with the ricotta mixture and 300 g of the reserved stewed rhubarb and raspberries. Top with remaining 6 toasted bread slices, then cut each sandwich in half diagonally.
  8. Spread remaining reserved stewed rhubarb mixture over base of prepared dish. Arrange sandwich halves over rhubarb mixture, cut side down, so they are standing up slightly. Scatter sliced rhubarb between sandwiches.
  9. Place eggs, milk, cream, brandy and caster sugar into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Pour over sandwiches in baking dish and stand for 10 minutes (for bread to absorb liquid).
  10. Dot with 50 g of the remaining reserved ricotta, sprinkle with raw sugar and bake for 40 minutes (160°C), or until custard sets and pudding is golden. Leave to cool for 5 minutes, then dust with icing sugar and serve hot.

Useful Items

bowl, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, baking dish (30 x 23 x 5 cm), baking paper, baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a good quality fruit bread or a good quality sourdough fruit bread from your local bakery. Commercial fruit breads from supermarkets may be too soft and absorb too much liquid.
  • Use any extra ricotta in omelettes, crepes or simply spread on sourdough toast and top with tomato and avocado slices.
