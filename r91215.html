Rustic Bread

Ingredients

  • 21 oz wheat grains, divided
  • 14 oz water
  • 1 oz fresh yeast
  • 4 oz buttermilk
  • 5 ½ oz bread flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp malt powder (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
314 kJ / 75 kcal
Protein
3 g
Carbohydrates
14 g
Fat
0 g

Preparation

  1. Place 7 oz wheat grains into mixing bowl and grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a large bowl.
  2. Again, place 7 oz wheat grains into mixing bowl and grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to the large bowl.
  3. Again, place remaining 7 oz wheat grains into mixing bowl and grind 1 min/speed 10, transfer into the large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place water, yeast and buttermilk into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/100°F/speed 2.
  5. Add reserved ground grains, flour, salt, vinegar and malt powder. Knead 3 min/ then let rise in mixing bowl with lid on until dough rises to top of bowl (approx. 30 min.).
  6. Knead a further 1 min/.
  7. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.) with parchment and set aside.
  8. Turn dough out onto a floured surface. Using floured hands, shape dough into a loaf, place onto prepared baking sheet and let rise again while oven is heating (approx. 15 min.). Using a sharp knife, slash top of loaf in a criss-cross pattern and bake for 15 minutes. (425°F).
  9. Reduce temperature to 375°F and bake a further 20-25 minutes or until loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the underside. Allow to cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If some of the dough sticks to the blades when transferring it to the floured surface, set mixing bowl back into position and mix 2 sec/speed 10. The remaining dough should now be easy to remove using the spatula.
  • You can let the loaf rise in a rising basket and then tip it onto the baking sheet just before baking. This will help the loaf retain its shape while rising.
  • If living at high altitude let dough rise longer than recipe suggests. A mesh pop up food cover can be placed over bread dough for the second rise. Place hot, wet tea towels laid over top and sides of mesh food cover to stop dough from drying out while rising. Alternatively use proof function in oven.
  • Replace buttermilk with yogurt or with milk to which 1 tsp of vinegar has been added, or with sour cream.
  • Replace wheat grains with spelt grains or replace 3.5 oz of the wheat grains with 3.5 oz rye grains.
  • To bake the bread in a loaf pan: after baking, remove loaf from pan and allow to cool on a wire rack.
