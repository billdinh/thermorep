Almond Briouats with Honey and Sesame Seeds

Ingredients

Almond paste filling

  • 250 g blanched almonds
  • 80 g sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 30 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 Tbsp orange flower water

Folding and frying briouats

  • 250 g filo pastry, cut in strips (36 cm x 6 cm)
  • 50 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly whisked
  • oil, for frying
  • 300 g honey
  • ½ Tbsp orange flower water
  • 2 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds

Preparation

Almond paste filling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Spread 125 g blanched almonds in a single layer on prepared baking tray and toast for 5-10 minutes (200°C) or until lightly browned. Allow to cool.
  3. Place reserved toasted almonds, remaining 125 g blanched almonds and sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add ground cinnamon, salt, butter and orange flower water and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and shape almond paste into cherry-sized balls (approx. 40) and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Folding and frying briouats

  1. Brush bottom ⅔ of a strip of filo pastry with melted butter. Place one almond paste ball near the bottom. Wrap up the bottom of the pastry to enclose filling, and shape the triangle by folding the filling, up to the right and then left, until reaching the end of the pastry. Trim excess pastry to make a neat flap, dab a little egg yolk on the flap to help seal and tuck into the fold. Repeat for remaining almond paste balls.
  2. Place a large frying pan (26 cm) over high heat and fill with 3-4 cm of vegetable oil. Fry briouats in batches (approx. 1-2 minutes), turning occasionally, until golden all over. Remove with slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  3. Place honey and orange flower water in mixing bowl and heat 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, place Varoma tray on Varoma lid and set aside. Transfer hot honey to a bowl. Using two forks, dip each briouat in hot honey and place on the Varoma tray to drain. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and allow to cool before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray, large frying pan (Ø 26 cm), paper towel, baking paper, pastry brush, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Instead of dipping the briouats in hot honey you can serve them dusted with icing sugar.
  • The briouats can be kept in a tightly sealed container at room temperature for two weeks, or in the freezer for 2 months. If storing in the freezer, place a sheet of cling film between layers to make it easy to remove only as many briouats as you need.
  • Filo pastry dries out very quickly, so handle it carefully. Unroll the sheets of filo pastry, and cover them with a piece of cling film. Remove one at a time, replacing the cling film after each one.
