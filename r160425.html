Pulled Pork

Ingredients

  • ½ oz garlic cloves (approx. 6 cloves)
  • 5 oz yellow onion, quartered (approx. 1 md. onion)
  • 1 oz oil
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp mustard powder
  • 5 tsp sweet paprika
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • &frac18; tsp cayenne pepper
  • 14 ½ oz canned diced tomatoes, drained (approx. 1 can)
  • 1 tsp chicken stock paste
  • 2 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 oz dark brown sugar
  • 4 oz barbeque sauce (see Tip)
  • 105 oz water, divided
  • 56 oz pork shoulder, trimmed of excess fat

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, onion, oil, chili powder, onion powder, mustard powder, paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, thyme and cayenne into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add tomatoes, stock paste, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, sugar and barbeque sauce, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 10 min/250°F/speed 1. Transfer sauce to a heatproof bowl and set aside to cool. Rinse excess sauce from mixing bowl.
  3. Place 55 oz water into mixing bowl.
  4. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in pork.
  5. Baste pork with approximately ¼ of the reserved sauce. Secure Varoma lid and steam cook 90 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Add 25 oz water, baste pork again with approximately ¼ of the reserved sauce, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 90 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  7. Add an additional 25 oz water and baste pork again with approximately another ¼ of the reserved sauce, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 60 min/Varoma/speed 2 until pork is soft enough to shred (see Tip).
  8. Remove Varoma dish. Shred pork with a fork and add remaining sauce to shredded pork until reaching desired consistency. Transfer to a serving bowl and keep warm until ready to serve.

Useful Items

serving bowl, basting brush, heatproof bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Make your own sauce following the BBQ Sauce recipe in the US Basic Cookbook. Increase spices to taste. Flavor will vary with store-bought barbeque sauces.
  • Meat may need further cooking before shredding due to fat content and cut of meat. Cook meat in increments of 10 min/Varoma/speed 2 until tender enough to break up with a fork when tested. The USDA Guidelines and National Pork Board recommend cooking to an internal temperature of at least 145°F, followed by a 3 minute rest.
  • Alternative shredding method: after steam cooking, break up pork into large pieces and place into mixing bowl and insert measuring cup. Shred pork 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer pork to a large serving bowl and keep warm until ready to serve.
