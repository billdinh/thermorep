Almond biscotti

Ingredients

  • butter, for greasing
  • 3 egg whites, room temperature
  • 170 g caster sugar
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 130 g raw almonds
  • 40 g dark chocolate chips (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
151 kJ / 36 kcal
Protein
1 g
Carbohydrates
5 g
Fat
1.3 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease 2 small loaf tins (18 x 8 cm), then line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 5 min/37°C/speed 3.5, without measuring cup, until soft peaks form.
  3. With butterfly whisk still in place, whip 3 min/37°C/speed 3.5, slowly adding sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Add flour, almonds and chocolate chips (optional) and combine 10 sec//speed 1.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then combine for a further 20 sec//speed 1.5, or until completely combined.
  5. Divide mixture evenly between the 2 prepared loaf tins and smooth over the tops using a dampened pastry brush. Bake for 25-35 minutes (180°C) or until lightly golden and a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the loaves comes out clean.
  6. Remove biscotti from oven and cut while still hot into thin slices (3mm) using a serrated bread knife. Place slices onto prepared baking tray, then bake slices for a further 5 minutes (180°C) or until just crispy.
  7. Set biscotti aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes), then serve or place into a gift bag to give to friends and family.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, 2 small loaf tins (18 x 8 cm), wooden skewer, pastry brush, serrated bread knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve biscotti with your cup of coffee, tea or hot chocolate as a special treat.
  • If you are giving biscotti as a gift, include a bag of freshly ground coffee beans to complete the gift and create a mini-hamper any coffee drinker would love. Place 150 g coffee beans into mixing bowl and grind 10-30 sec/speed 9. For a coarser grind (ideal for plunger coffee) grind for 10 seconds or for a finer grind (ideal for espresso/coffee machines), grind for 30 seconds.
