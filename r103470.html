Pork gyoza with soy dipping sauce

Ingredients

Dipping Sauce

  • 1 cm fresh ginger, peeled
  • 50 g rice wine vinegar
  • 50 g soy sauce
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • ¼ tsp dried chilli flakes

Filling

  • 800 g water, plus extra for wrapping and steaming
  • 100 g cabbage, cut into pieces (5-6 cm)
  • 3 cm fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 sprigs fresh chives, cut into quarters
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 300 g pork mince
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp potato flour
  • 1 package gyoza wrappers (approx. 35 wrappers)
  • 2 tbsp oil, plus extra for greasing

Preparation

Dipping Sauce

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining dipping sauce ingredients and mix 10 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a serving bowl and set aside to infuse.

Filling

  1. Line a baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Place 300 g of the water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and weigh cabbage into it. Cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set cabbage aside to drain. Discard water, then rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place ginger, garlic, chives, spring onions/shallots and Reserved cabbage into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat chopping 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add all remaining filling ingredients, except wrappers and oil, and combine 8 sec//speed 4 . Transfer into a bowl.
  5. Place 1 gyoza wrapper onto a clean work surface. Place 1-1½ teaspoons of filling into centre of wrapper. Using a wet fingertip, dampen edges of wrapper and fold over to enclose filling. Pinch edges together to seal, making little pleats along edge. Place onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling to make a total of 32 gyozas.
  6. Place remaining 500 g of the water into mixing bowl. Lightly grease Varoma dish and tray with oil and place into position. Fill Varoma tray and dish with half of the gyozas, ensuring they do not touch. Secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Repeat steaming with remaining gyoza (check water levels and top up if required).
  7. Place a large non-stick frying pan over high heat and add 1-2 tablespoons oil. Place 4-5 steamed gyoza into pan and fry until golden brown (approx. 1-2 minutes on each side). Transfer onto a serving platter and set aside. Repeat frying with remaining gyozas, then serve warm with reserved dipping sauce. 

Useful Items

serving bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, serving platter, large non-stick frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Gyozas can be frozen. Place gyozas onto a baking tray at the end of step 7 and place into freezer for 2-3 hours, then transfer semi-frozen gyoza into a freezable container. Steam the first batch of frozen gyoza in step 8 for 15-17 minutes, or until cooked through. Top up water levels before steaming second batch of frozen gyozas.
  • Gow Gee pastry wrappers or wonton wrappers can be substituted for goyza wrapppers, however steaming times may vary.
