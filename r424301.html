Breakfast Burrito

Ingredients

Salsa

  • 40 g onions, quartered
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, cut in pieces
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • 1 fresh jalapeño chilli, halved, deseeded (optional)
  • 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved, seeds removed
  • 20 g lime juice
  • 1 avocado (approx. 140 g), quartered
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Scrambled Eggs and Assembly

  • 40 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g olive oil, plus 1 Tbsp for frying
  • 100 g red pepper (approx. 1 pepper), quartered
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp dried oregano
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • ¼ tsp paprika
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes (optional)
  • 2 pork sausages, halved lengthways (see tip)
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 15 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 50 g semi-skimmed milk
  • 4 flour tortillas, wholewheat
  • 2 Tbsp fresh coriander leaves, roughly chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

Salsa

  1. Place onions, coriander, garlic (if using) and jalapeño (if using) in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add tomatoes, lime juice, avocado, salt and pepper then chop 1-3 sec/speed 5 until roughly chopped. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Scrambled Eggs and Assembly

  1. Place onions, 10 g oil, red pepper, thyme, oregano, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, paprika and chilli flakes (if using) in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, heat 1 Tbsp oil in a frying pan over a medium heat and fry sausages until cooked (approx. 5 minutes).
  2. Add eggs, salt, pepper, butter and milk to mixing bowl then cook 5 min/98°C//speed 2. Meanwhile, warm tortillas according to packet instructions.
  3. Divide scrambled eggs and salsa between warmed tortillas. Place half a cooked sausage on each, garnish with coriander then roll up into burritos and serve warm.

Useful Items

frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Wholewheat tortillas have more fibre than white tortillas and will keep you fuller for longer. In addition, onions and red peppers are low GI vegetables.
  • If preferred, sausages can be cut in bite-sized pieces. These will cook quicker than halved sausages. Adjust frying time accordingly.
