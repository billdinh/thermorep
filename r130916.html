Strawberry Bavarois

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 60 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 60 g self-raising flour
  • 1 medium egg

Strawberry Mousse

  • 500 g double cream
  • 500 g strawberries, halved if large, plus extra for garnish
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 8 gelatine leaves
  • 100 g plain yoghurt

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease sides and line base of a spring form cake tin (Ø 23 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place butter, sugar, flour and egg in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer to prepared tin, level surface, then bake for 15 minutes (170°C). Set aside on a wire rack and allow to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare mousse.

Strawberry Mousse

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whip until just softly whipped/speed 3, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Transfer to a bowl and set aside in fridge. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place strawberries, sugar and lemon juice in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 4. Cook 8 min 30 sec/100°C/speed 3 then allow to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. After 10 minutes, when temperature of mixing bowl is less than 80°C (see tip), soak gelatine leaves in cold water for 5 minutes until softened then squeeze out excess water. Add squeezed gelatine to strawberry mixture and mix 30 sec/speed 1. Set aside to cool to room temperature.
  4. Once strawberry mixture is cool, transfer 8 Tbsp to a small bowl to decorate the bavarois later.
  5. Add yoghurt and reserved whipped cream to remaining strawberry mixture then mix 10 sec/speed 5.

Assembly

  1. Place base of a loose bottom cake tin (Ø 20 cm) over sponge and, using a sharp knife, cut around it.
  2. Line base of this cake tin (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper then lay sponge circle inside. Pour strawberry mousse over sponge then set aside in fridge.
  3. After 2 hours, when partially set, gently re-heat reserved strawberry mixture on hob until liquid, then spread evenly over strawberry mousse layer. Return to fridge and allow to set completely for at least 3 hours or overnight.
  4. Serve chilled, decorated with halved fresh strawberries.

Useful Items

baking paper, spring form tin (Ø 23 cm), wire rack, round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), oven, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

This beautifully light summer dessert would prove extremely popular at a dinner party. The very thin sponge layer supports the lightest strawberry mousse. Your guests will finish this off in seconds!

  • To check the temperature, place mixing bowl in position and the temperature will be displayed on the screen.
