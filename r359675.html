Spicy Sausage Macaroni and Cheese

Ingredients

  • unsalted butter, to grease
  • 16 oz andouille sausage, pre-cooked, sliced (½ in.)
  • 4 oz Asiago cheese, cubed
  • 6 oz sharp white cheddar cheese, cubed
  • 6 oz Monterey Jack cheese, cubed
  • 45 oz water
  • 11 oz macaroni pasta
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, sliced
  • 36 oz whole milk
  • 3 oz all-purpose flour
  • salt, to taste
  • white pepper, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper (optional)

Preparation

  1. Lightly grease a baking dish (9 in. x 13 in.). Distribute sliced sausages over the bottom of prepared baking dish.
  2. Place Asiago and cheddar cheese into mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place Monterey Jack cheese into mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 7. Transfer to bowl with grated cheeses and set aside.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/212°F/speed 1. Add pasta through hole in mixing bowl lid and cook 7 min/212°F//speed 1.
  5. Drain pasta through Varoma dish. Drizzle pasta with olive oil, transfer to baking dish over reserved sausages and refrigerate while making sauce.
  6. Place whipping cream and jalapeño into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/195°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  7. Place milk, flour, salt, pepper and nutmeg into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 8, then cook 15 min/195°F/speed 2.
  8. Strain reserved cream over mixing bowl into sauce and discard jalapeño slices. Add 10 oz reserved grated cheese and mix 10 sec/speed 7. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, to taste.
  9. Preheat oven to 375°F. Pour sauce over pasta and sliced sausage. Gently mix with spatula to coat all pasta and ensure sauce coats bottom of baking dish. Sprinkle with remaining grated cheese and cayenne.
  10. Place baking dish onto a baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes (375°F) or until top is brown and crunchy.
  11. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

baking dish (9 in. x 13 in.), baking sheet, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a spicier flavor use hot Andouille sausages.
