Honey ginger tofu with greens

Ingredients

Wakame gomasio

  • 50 g unhulled sesame seeds
  • 50 g black sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp wakame flakes (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp Celtic sea salt (see Tips)

Honey ginger tofu

  • 700 - 750 g firm tofu, cut into thick slices (1.5 cm)
  • 5 cm cube fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g rice vinegar
  • 50 g tamari sauce
  • 50 g honey
  • 40 g mirin
  • 1 - 2 tsp Sriracha® sauce, to taste
  • 2 tsp sesame oil, plus extra for frying

Greens

  • 500 g water
  • 1 bunch broccolini (approx. 200 g), trimmed and cut into pieces (10 cm)
  • 1 bunch bok choy (approx. 300 g), cut into pieces (10 cm)
  • 50 g red onion, cut into slices (5 mm)
  • 100 g sugar snap peas, cut into halves
  • 1 fresh red chilli, cut into thin slices (optional)
  • fried shallots, for garnishing
  • sesame seeds, for garnishing

Preparation

Wakame gomasio

  1. Place all wakame gomasio ingredients into mixing bowl and toast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Allow to cool slightly in mixing bowl (approx. 10 minutes), then mill Turbo/0.5 sec/2 times, or until a coarse powder. Transfer into a sealable storage container. Allow to cool completely, then seal and store until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Honey ginger tofu

  1. Place tofu into a square or rectangular casserole dish (see Tips) and set aside.
  2. Place ginger and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add remaining tofu ingredients and warm 3 min/50°C/speed 3. Transfer marinade into dish with tofu and ensure tofu is coated in marinade. Cover and  place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Place a frying pan over medium heat and add a little of the extra sesame oil. Drain tofu, reserving marinade, and add to frying pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side, until warmed through and golden brown. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.

Greens

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and line Varoma dish with a piece of wet, well-wrung baking paper. Place broccolini stems and bok choy stems onto Varoma dish. Weigh red onion into dish, secure Varoma lid and cook 7 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add broccolini florets, bok choy leaves and sugar snap peas to Varoma dish. Drizzle vegetables with 3-4 tablespoons of the reserved marinade and sprinkle with 1-2 teaspoons of the wakame gomasio. Secure Varoma lid and steam 5-7 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until vegetables are cooked to your liking.
  3. Divide vegetables and tofu between serving plates. Garnish with sliced chilli (optional), fried shallots, sesame seeds and wakame gomasio to taste. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, casserole dish, plastic wrap, frying pan, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, baking paper, serving plates, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

Wakame gomasio (or gomashio) is a Japanese condiment that is popular in macrobiotic diets. It is a healthier alternative to salt and contains calcium, iron, magnesium, iodine and B vitamins.

  • Unhulled sesame seeds have a high nutritional value, but may have a slightly bitter taste. You can use standard hulled seeds in this recipe.
  • Wakame is a type of seaweed with a subtle, sweet flavour. You can find it in health food stores or Asian food stores.
  • Celtic sea salt may contain a number of minerals and trace elements. You can find it in health food stores and some gourmet food stores. You could also use standard sea salt in this recipe.
  • We recommend marinating tofu in a rectangular or square baking dish so that the tofu can lay flat and better absorb the marinade.
  • Store wakame gomasio in a sealable container in a cool, dark place. Use it to season steamed rice and vegetables, as a dukkah or to flavour popcorn.
  • For a heartier meal, serve with steamed rice or udon noodles.
  • This recipe contains a number of nutrients, including calcium, zinc, vitamin C and protein.
