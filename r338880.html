Stuffed Moroccan Breads

Ingredients

Moroccan Bread

  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 350 g plain flour
  • 150 g durum wheat semolina
  • 300 g water
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric (optional)
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Spicy Beef Filling

  • 100 g onions, halved
  • 1 bunch fresh mint, leaves only (15 g)
  • 1 bunch fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (15 g)
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only (15 g)
  • 300 g beef mince, broken up
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ - ½ tsp ground cumin, to taste
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper

Preparation

Moroccan Bread

  1. Place yeast, flour, semolina, water, turmeric (if using) and salt in mixing bowl then knead 8 min/. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film and set aside to rise (approx. 1 hour). Towards the end of this time, prepare filling.

Spicy Beef Filling

  1. Place onions, mint, parsley and coriander in mixing bowl then chop 6 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add beef, turmeric, cumin, salt and pepper then mix 30 sec//speed 2.5.
  3. Divide risen dough into 8 pieces and shape each into a ball. Press out flat (approx. 1 cm thick) into a rectangular shape. Place some filling in the middle of each piece of dough and flatten slightly. Tuck sides in then fold dough over filling to make a mini square loaf. Turn so closed side is facing down, press down lightly then repeat with remaining pieces of dough and filling. Cover with a tea towel and set aside for 1 hour.
  4. Line a heavy-based frying pan with a sheet of baking paper then heat over a low heat. Cook breads gradually closed side down for 2 minutes then flatten with a fish slice. Cook for 5 minutes on each side.
  5. Serve loaves warm or cold.

Useful Items

tea towel, frying pan, baking paper, fish slice, cling film, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Instead of lining the pan with baking paper, use clarified butter or vegetable oil to get a nice even colour on the breads.
  • Make plain bread by simply forming round flat breads and cooking in the pan as directed by recipe. These breads go very well with salads and dips.
  • The breads can also be filled with a mixture of onions, herbs, spinach and feta cheese.
  • Substitute spices in filling with 1½-2 tsp ras el hanout.
