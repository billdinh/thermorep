Curried Carrot And Ginger Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, halved
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 cm fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 60 g butter, diced
  • 1 - 2 tsp curry powder, to taste
  • 4 carrots, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 - 2 Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored and cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 500 g water
  • 1 Tbsp meat stock paste (see tips)
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 400 g tinned chickpeas, rinsed and well drained (weight is pre-draining)
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to garnish
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • ¼ tsp curry powder
  • 250 g whipping cream
Nutrition

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place onion, garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add curry powder and carrots then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add apples and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add water, stock paste, tomato paste and bay leaves then cook 30-40 min/100°C/speed 1.
  7. Place chickpeas, pepper, salt, curry powder and olive oil in a large bowl then toss to combine. Transfer to baking tray and bake for 20 minutes (200°C) or until chickpeas are crispy.
  8. Carefully remove bay leaves from soup. Add cream and purée 1 min/speed 4, increasing speed gradually to speed 9.
  9. Divide soup between bowls, then season with pepper and garnish with crispy chickpeas then serve.

Useful Items

baking tray (20 cm x 30 cm), baking paper, serving bowls, large bowl, oven
