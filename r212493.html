Mississippi Mud Pie

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 125 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 30 g caster sugar
  • 1 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • 4 Tbsp water, ice cold

Filling

  • 400 - 450 g natural frozen yoghurt
  • 400 - 450 g chocolate frozen yoghurt
  • 175 g caster sugar
  • 3 egg whites, from large eggs

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place flour and butter in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add sugar and cocoa then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add water and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Tip out onto a lightly-floured surface and roll out to line a loose-based fluted tart tin (Ø 24 cm). Trim excess pastry. Line with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Remove paper and beans then bake for a further 10-15 minutes (180°C) until crisp and cooked. Set aside to cool completely.

Filling

  1. When base is completely cool, remove frozen yoghurt from freezer to soften.
  2. Using an ice cream scoop, place balls of each frozen yoghurt flavour in cooled pastry case (spreading the different flavours around) then place case in freezer for 1 hour. At the same time, place a serving plate in the freezer.
  3. Towards the end of this time, place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sugar. Set bowl aside. Preheat oven to 210°C.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whisk speed 3 until stiff peaks form (approx. 1 min 30 sec). Without measuring cup, set speed 3 then slowly add reserved sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid until meringue is thick and glossy. Swirl or pipe over frozen ice cream in pastry case up to pastry edge to enclose completely. Bake immediately for 4-5 minutes (210°C) until meringue edges are tinged golden.
  5. Transfer to chilled serving plate and serve immediately (see tip), cut in wedges.

Useful Items

rolling pin, baking paper, baking beans, loose-based fluted tart tin (Ø 24 cm), serving plate, ice cream scoop, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Named after the Bayou areas of Louisiana, Mud Pie has many variations. This one consists of frozen yoghurt providing a sour tang to the sweet fluffy meringue on top and the crisp chocolate base beneath.
  • Once out of the oven, this pie will melt quite quickly. Have your chilled serving plate and a knife for cutting slices ready so that you can portion it up immediately!
