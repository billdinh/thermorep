Eccles Cakes

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 90 g ice cubes
  • 180 g butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp water

Filling

  • 25 g butter
  • 50 g dark brown sugar
  • 100 g currants
  • ½ tsp ground allspice
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 Tbsp caster sugar, for sprinkling

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place ice cubes, butter, flour and salt in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add water and knead 3 min/. Tip out and form into a flattened ball, then wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes until firm.
  3. On a lightly floured surface, roll out dough into a rectangle (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Fold bottom third of dough up then fold top third down and seal edges gently with rolling pin. Give folded dough a quarter turn so that fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice more, then return to fridge for 20 minutes (or place in freezer for 5 minutes). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C, clean and dry mixing bowl then make filling.

Filling

  1. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then melt 2 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add currants, allspice and nutmeg then mix 10 sec//speed 2.
  3. On a lightly floured surface, roll out pastry to 2 mm thick and use a pastry cutter (Ø 8 cm) to cut out rounds. Place 1 tsp filling in middle of half the pastry rounds.
  4. Brush egg white around the edges then lay the remaining pastry rounds on top of each mound of filling. Seal edges firmly then make three small slits in the tops for steam to escape during baking. Brush tops with egg white and sprinkle with caster sugar. Place on a baking tray and bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) until golden. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, round pastry cutter (Ø 8 cm), pastry brush, baking tray, refrigerator, freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Invented in the English town of Eccles in Lancashire, these delightfully sweet, currant-filled pastries are now popular throughout the world. Traditionally, they might be served with a piece of Lancashire cheese, though they’re lovely just with a cup of tea…especially warm out of the oven.
