Mexican shredded beef cheeks with quinoa chia tortillas

Ingredients

Tortillas

  • 50 g quinoa
  • 50 g chia seeds
  • 50 g polenta (cornmeal)
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 2 tsp lime juice, freshly squeezed (approx. ½ lime)
  • 60 g water

Corn and coriander salsa

  • 700 g water
  • 650 g sweet corn cobs, husks removed
  • ¼ red onion
  • 8 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 avocado, flesh only
  • 30 g lime juice, freshly squeezed, (approx. 2 limes)
  • sea salt, to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Shredded beef cheeks

  • 400 g boiling water
  • 1 ancho chilli, deseeded
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, including roots
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 fresh bird's eye chilli, deseeded if preferred
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 900 g beef cheeks, trimmed and cut into cubes (3 - 4 cm)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 30 g dark cooking chocolate (optional)
  • 120 g water
  • 1 tbsp meat stock paste
  • 3 tsp peanut butter
  • 3 tsp tomato paste
  • 30 g lime juice, freshly squeezed, (approx. 2 limes)

Preparation

Tortillas

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place quinoa and chia seeds into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add remaining ingredients and combine 6 sec/speed 6, until a dough forms.
  4. Divide dough into four pieces. Working one piece at a time, place dough between a silicone bread mat and a piece of baking paper or two sheets of baking paper and roll out to 2 mm thickness. Cut into triangles and place onto prepared baking tray. Bake for 9-11 minutes (200°C), until lightly golden.
  5. Leave to cool before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Corn and coriander salsa

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and place corn into Varoma dish. Place Varoma into position and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Set corn aside to cool. Once cooled, cut corn kernels off cob, place into a bowl and set aside. Discard cobs. Empty and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place red onion and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add avocado, lime juice, salt and pepper and mix 2 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved corn kernels and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer into serving bowl, cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Shredded beef cheeks

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh boiling water into it. Add ancho chilli and set aside to soak for 1 hour. Drain and set aside, reserving 50 g of the soaking liquid.
  2. Place coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place reserved soaked ancho chilli and 1 tbsp of oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Sauté 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved soaking liquid (50 g) and blend 10 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Blend 10 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7. Transfer ancho chilli mixture into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Place bird's eye chilli, garlic and remaining 1 tbsp of the oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Sauté 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  9. Add beef cheeks and sauté 7 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  10. Add paprika, cumin, cinnamon, cocoa powder, chocolate (optional), water and stock paste and reserved ancho chilli mixture and cook 60 min/100°C//speed 1.
  11. Add peanut butter and tomato paste and cook 40 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer into a serving bowl, then stir through lime juice and reserved chopped coriander. Serve immediately with tortillas and Corn and coriander salsa.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, wire rack, bowl, plastic wrap, serving bowl, small jug, ceramic or glass bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Ancho chillies can be found in specialty food stores or spice stores, or online. Ancho chilli can be substituted with 2 pitted prunes.
  • Clean the roots of your coriander before adding to the mixing bowl. Scrape down the root to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside.
