Chia and Quinoa Tortillas with a Trio of Dips

Ingredients

Chia and Quinoa Tortilla Chips

  • 50 g white quinoa
  • 50 g white chia seeds
  • 50 g polenta
  • 60 g water
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 2 tsp lime juice (approx. ½ lime)

Sun-dried Tomato, Chilli and Cashew Dip

  • 1 long red chilli , deseeded if preferred
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, cubed (3 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and quartered
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 120 g raw cashew nuts
  • 100 g sun-dried tomatoes, drained
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil

Pumpkin Dip

  • 500 g pumpkin, flesh only, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 100 g pine nuts, toasted
  • 2 tbsp goat's cheese
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Artichoke Dip

  • extra virgin olive oil, for greasing
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cubed (3 cm)
  • ¼ brown onion, halved
  • 250 g marinated artichoke hearts, drained
  • 135 g mayonnaise (see tip)

Preparation

Chia and Quinoa Tortilla Chips

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place quinoa and chia in mixing bowl then mill 15 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add all remaining tortilla chip ingredients and mix 6 sec/speed 6, or until a dough forms.
  4. Divide dough into 4 pieces. Place 1 piece of dough between 2 pieces of baking paper and roll out into a rectangle (1-2 mm thick). Using a sharp, wet knife, cut rectangle of dough into triangles, then place triangles on prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining 3 portions of dough.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes (200°C), or until just golden. Set aside and allow tortilla chips to cool before removing from baking tray.

Sun-dried Tomato, Chilli and Cashew Dip

  1. Place chilli, garlic and Parmesan in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add all remaining sun-dried tomato dip ingredients and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/2-3 times. Transfer to a serving bowl, cover with cling film and refrigerate until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pumpkin Dip

  1. Preheat oven to 250°C. Line a baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper, then place pumpkin on it and drizzle with olive oil.
  2. Bake for 10 minutes (250°C), then reduce oven temperature to 180°C and bake for a further 10-15 minutes (180°C). Set pumpkin aside to cool.
  3. Place pine nuts in mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add goat's cheese, basil, cumin, salt, pepper and cooled pumpkin then blend 6 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a serving bowl, cover with cling film and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Artichoke Dip

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease an ovenproof dish (450 ml) and set aside.
  2. Place half of the Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add remaining Parmesan and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onion in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add artichoke and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/1-3 times.
  6. Add mayonnaise and reserved Parmesan and mix 3 sec/speed 4. Transfer to prepared baking dish and bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Serve hot.

Useful Items

ovenproof dish (450 mL), baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, cling film, serving bowl, cling wrap, oven, refrigerator
