Dosa with spiced potato filling

Ingredients

Dosa

  • 250 g long grain rice
  • 100 g split black lentils (urad dal) (see Tips)
  • 600 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 30 g cooked rice (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt

Spiced potato filling

  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 500 g water
  • 750 g potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 long green chilli, deseeded if preferred
  • 1 tsp yellow mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 60 g ghee, plus extra for frying
  • 1 red onion (approx. 120 g), cut into thin slices
  • 10 fresh curry leaves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 120 g fresh baby spinach leaves

Preparation

Dosa

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh long grain rice into it. Set aside. Place another ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh lentils into it. Cover both with water and set aside to soak for a minimum of 3 hours or overnight.
  2. Skim and discard any black husks from lentils. Rinse lentils well and discard any remaining black husks. Drain and rinse rice.
  3. Place cooked rice, water, salt and soaked rice and lentils into mixing bowl and mix 4-5 min/speed 9, or until texture is smooth.
  4. Pour mixture into a deep bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside to ferment at room temperature for a minimum of 12 hours or until increased in size (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Spiced potato filling

  1. Place coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, then weigh potatoes into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 1, until tender. Remove Varoma and transfer potatoes into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Discard cooking water. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place garlic, ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric and ghee and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add onion, curry leaves and salt and cook 3-4 min/100°C//speed 1, until onions are soft.
  6. Add spinach and cook 2-3 min/100°C//speed , or until wilted.
  7. Add reserved coriander and combine 5 sec//speed . Transfer into thermal serving bowl with potatoes, stir to combine then cover and set aside to keep warm.

Frying dosa

  1. Place a non-stick frying pan (28 cm) over high heat and add a little of the ghee for frying. When pan is very hot, pour ½ cup dosa mixture into pan and spread out thinly to cover base (swirling batter or using the back of a ladle or wooden spoon) to form a thin, lacy-looking crêpe. Cook for 5-6 minutes until base is very crisp (cook on 1 side only). Slide dosa onto a plate and roll into a loose cylinder while still warm. Repeat with remaining ghee and batter (makes approx. 12 dosas).
  2. Serve dosas immediately with spiced potato filling.

Useful Items

2 ceramic or glass bowls, deep bowl, plastic wrap, bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, large plate, non-stick frying pan (28 cm), stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Dosa is similar to a crêpe, made from fermented batter. It's popular in many regions of India as well as other countries such as Sri Lanka and Nepal.
  • You can buy split black lentils (urad dal) at Indian food stores, some health food stores, some bulk food stores and online.
  • To make cooked rice, place 700 g water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and weigh rice into it and cook 14 min/100°C/speed 4. Or use leftover rice.
  • In hot weather, the batter mixture will rise quite quickly, but in the cooler months you may want to place the mixture in a warm area to assist with the fermenting process. The batter will increase in size, become frothy with little bubbles and may have a "yeast" aroma when fermented properly.
  • If your batter is too thick, add a little water until batter is the consistency of pouring (whipping) cream.
