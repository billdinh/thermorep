Spiralised vegetable soup

Ingredients

  • 100 g dried lima beans
  • 800 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 carrot, spiralised or cut into julienne, core reserved and cut into pieces (4 cm) (see Tips)
  • 400 g canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • ½ parsnip (approx. 100 g), spiralised or cut into julienne
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 zucchini (approx. 200 g), spiralised or cut into julienne
  • ½ lemon, juice only, to taste
  • 8 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 8 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh lima beans into it. Cover lima beans with water and soak overnight.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain lima beans and rinse until water runs clear.
  3. Place 500 g of the water into mixing bowl, then insert simmering basket with beans and cook 16 min/100°C/speed 4, or until tender. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside to drain. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place garlic, onion, celery and carrot core pieces into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5, then cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add tomatoes, stock paste and remaining 300 g water, then cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Place Varoma dish into position and place spiralised carrot and parsnip into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and set aside.
  7. Add lima beans, salt and pepper into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and add zucchini into it, then cook 8 min/100°C//speed . Transfer vegetables in Varoma into a large bowl and set aside.
  8. Add lemon juice, coriander and dill and heat 2 min/100°C//speed . Divide between 4 serving bowls and garnish with spiralised vegetables before serving.

Useful Items

4 serving bowls, large bowl, bowl, spiralizer

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Spiralizers are available to buy from most home and kitchen shops. These can be used to shred a range of vegetables into ribbons. This is a fun and appealing way to add vegetables to your dish. You can also use a julienne peeler.
