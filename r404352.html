Avocado and egg yolk purée

Ingredients

  • 1900 g cold water, plus extra for cooling
  • 4 whole eggs, room temperature (see Tips)
  • 180 g avocado, flesh only, cut into pieces
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • filtered water, if needed

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and place whole eggs into it. Cook 16 min/100°C/speed 2 or until yolks have started to thicken (see Tips). Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and submerge eggs into a bowl of cold water for 1-2 minutes. When cool enough to handle, peel away egg shells and egg whites. The egg whites can be saved for an adult's serving or given to an older child as a finger food. Discard cooking water.
  2. Place egg yolks, avocado and lemon juice into mixing bowl and blend 45 sec/speed 3.5.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend for a further 45 sec/speed 3.5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend for a further 45 sec/speed 3.5 or until smooth. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • Egg yolks are a wonderful iron-rich food for babies. They can be added to other purées as well, or served as is, blended with your baby's milk to the desired consistency.
  • Egg cooking times can vary depending on the size of your eggs and whether they have been brought to room temperature before cooking. For food safety, cook the eggs at least until the whites are cooked through and the yolks have just started to thicken (but they do not need to be hard boiled).
  • For parents, this dish is lovely on toast, with a little sea salt and pepper to taste. It can also be used as an iron-rich, nutrient-dense butter replacement or dip for any age group.
