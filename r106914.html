Salmon with Ginger Sauce and Spiced Cashews

Ingredients

  • ½ - 1 long red chili, de-seeded if preferred
  • 1 piece fresh ginger, peeled (approx. 1¼ in.)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 shallot, peeled, halved
  • 2 sprigs fresh cilantro, stems and leaves
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ¼ tsp Sichuan peppercorns
  • ½ tsp Chinese five spice
  • 2 oz raw cashews
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 18 oz water
  • 4 fresh salmon fillets, skinless (approx. 16 oz-24 oz total) (see Tip)
  • 5 ½ oz zucchini, cut into sticks (½ in. x 3 in.)
  • 1 bunch broccolini (approx. 5.5 oz), trimmed and halved
  • 5 ½ oz bok choy, cut into thirds

Preparation

  1. Place chili, ginger, garlic, shallot and cilantro into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lemon or lime juice, honey and soy sauce and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer ginger sauce into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place olive oil, Sichuan peppercorns, Chinese five spice, cashews and salt into mixing bowl and sauté 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer spiced cashews into a bowl, separate from the sauce, and set aside.
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and place 1 salmon fillet into the Varoma dish and 3 salmon fillets onto Varoma tray. Drizzle 1-2 tbsp of the reserved ginger sauce over salmon. Weigh zucchini into Varoma.
  5. Arrange zucchini and broccolini around salmon in Varoma, secure Varoma lid and cook 6 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Weigh bok choy into Varoma and arrange around salmon and other vegetables in the Varoma, secure Varoma lid and cook 5-7 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until salmon and vegetables are cooked to your liking.
  7. Drizzle remaining ginger sauce over salmon and vegetables, sprinkle with spiced cashews and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • For thick salmon fillets you may need to cook 3-4 minutes first before adding the zucchini and broccolini and proceeding as per recipe.
  • Cook fish to an internal temperature of 145°F or until flesh is opaque and separates easily with a fork.
  • Serve with jasmine rice, brown rice or vermicelli noodles.
  • Szechuan peppercorns, Chinese five spice and bok choi can be found in specialty grocery stores and Asian supermarkets.
  • Bok choy can be replaced with Pak choi.
