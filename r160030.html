Parker Rolls

Ingredients

  • 8 oz whole milk
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, diced (approx. ½ in.), plus extra to grease
  • 2 ½ oz sugar
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 15 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 large egg, beaten (optional)
  • milk, to taste (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place milk and butter into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/120°F/speed 1.
  2. Add sugar and yeast and mix 20 sec/speed 1. Let rest for 5 minutes.
  3. Add flour, eggs and salt and knead 3 min/.
  4. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and quickly shape into a ball. Place into a lightly greased bowl, cover with cling wrap and let rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  5. Place parchment paper on a baking sheet. Punch down the dough and reshape into a ball. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces. Roll into balls and place on prepared baking sheet, almost touching. Make an egg wash (optional) by combining beaten egg with a splash of milk or water. Brush tops of Parker Rolls with egg wash. Cover and refrigerate 30 minutes. Meanwhile preheat oven to 350°F.
  6. Bake Parker Rolls for 25-30 minutes (350°F) until tops are golden. Allow to cool for 5 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, cling wrap, baking sheet and parchment paper, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve Parker Rolls with whipped honey-butter.
  • For traditional Parker House fold over: once dough is divided into 12 equal pieces, flatten slightly and brush the center of each round with melted butter. Take a chopstick or any similar sized object and make a deep indentation in the center of the circle, without breaking through the dough. Fold over one third of each round and press down to seal. Arrange these folded rolls on a buttered baking sheet about 1/2 inch apart and continue with recipe.
