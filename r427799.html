Peanut Brittle

Ingredients

  • 150 g dry-roasted peanuts, unsalted
  • 250 g brown sugar
  • 30 g unsalted butter, cold, in one piece (60 mm x 40 mm x 10 mm)
  • 1070 g water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 50 g vinegar
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
213 kJ / 51 kcal
Protein
0 g
Carbohydrates
8 g
Fat
2 g

Preparation

  1. To guarantee the success of recipes using Sugar Stage modes, the ingredients used must match exactly the quantity and description given. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with the other ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Line a baking tin with baking paper, sprinkle with peanuts then set aside on a heat-resistant surface.
  3. Place sugar, butter, 70 g water and lemon juice in mixing bowl then start Sugar Stages . Immediately pour over nuts on prepared tray and, using spatula, immediately scrape off any mixture left on rim of mixing bowl so mixing bowl lid closes securely for Pre-clean mode
  4. Place remaining 1000 g water and vinegar in mixing bowl then start Clean .
  5. Leave brittle to cool completely to room temperature then break into pieces. Store in an airtight container at room temperature (see tip) and use as desired.

Useful Items

airtight container, baking paper, baking tin

Hints & Tricks

  • Make confectionery at home with all-natural ingredients as an alternative to shop-bought sweets. This simple, sweet treat can be made your own by adding sesame seeds, dried strawberries or flaked coconut.
  • Store brittle for up to 1 week in an airtight container in a cool place to prevent it from absorbing moisture.
  • Adding lemon juice prevents the sugar from burning or crystallising, without affecting the flavour.
  • Garnish the brittle as desired. Try toasted walnuts, seeds or coconut flakes.
