Soparnik

Ingredients

  • 300 g plain flour
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 150 g water
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 4 Tbsp fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 100 g rainbow chard, stems removed, leaves sliced
  • extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling

Preparation

  1. Place plain flour, 1 Tbsp oil, 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch black pepper in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  2. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in water. Without measuring cup, set speed 3 and gradually add water until dough comes together. Knead 3 min/. Transfer to an oiled bowl and cover with a tea towel. Leave to rest for 1 hour. Meanwhile, prepare filling.
  3. Place onion, garlic and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add chard, remaining 1 Tbsp oil, 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch pepper then mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  5. Preheat oven to 190°C and line a large baking tray (approx. 40 cm x 30 cm) with baking paper.
  6. When dough has rested, divide into two pieces. Roll one piece out as thinly as possible to fit prepared baking tray (approx. 40 cm x 30 cm). Place on baking tray. Spread filling over right up to edges of pastry.
  7. Roll out second piece of dough to same size then place over filling. Use a rolling pin to lightly press layers together (removing air) and seal edges tightly. Make a few small holes in the top with a fork. Bake for 20 minutes (190°C) until lightly browned.
  8. Cut in diamonds using a knife or pizza cutter and serve warm drizzled with extra virgin olive oil.

Useful Items

rolling pin, tea towel, jug, large baking tray, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Soparnik is a traditional Dalmatian dish. It was first considered for fasting or food for the poor, being made from a simple dough of plain flour, water, salt and a splash of olive oil then filled with chard. It is now declared as part of the cultural heritage of Croatia.
