Cap and headphones

Ingredients

Basic chocolate cake

  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 40 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 270 g sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 250 g full cream milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs, room temperature

Buttercream icing

  • 250 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 120 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 2 tsp boiling water
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract

Fondant

  • 40 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g glucose syrup
  • 15 g glycerine (see Tips)
  • 620 g icing sugar, plus extra sifted for dusting and kneading (see Tips)
  • natural food colouring of choice (see Tips)

Preparation

Basic chocolate cake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Place 2 cupcake liners into a 12-hole muffin tray and set aside. Set aside a silicone cake sphere (18 cm).
  2. Place butter, flour, cocoa, sugar, baking powder, salt, milk and vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 .
  3. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 .
  4. Add eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5 . Fill each cupcake liner three-quarters full with batter and bake for 18-20 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cupcake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool.
  5. Pour remaining batter into sphere mould and bake for 1 hour 30-40 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in mould for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Fondant

  1. Place water and gelatine into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3 .
  2. Add glucose and glycerine and cook 1 min/60°C/speed 3 .
  3. Add 600 g of the icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 6, then 15 sec/. Dust a silicone bread mat or work surface well with icing sugar. Transfer fondant onto dusted silicone bread mat or work surface. Fondant will be heavy and sticky.
  4. To remove any remaining fondant in mixing bowl, place remaining 20 g icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Add to fondant on silicone bread mat. Dust hands with sifted icing sugar and knead into a ball, using extra sifted icing sugar if needed, until fondant is smooth and less sticky.
  5. To colour fondant. Place a sealable container onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 200 g fondant into it, for headphones. Set aside. Place a separate sealable container onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 50 g fondant into it, for decorations. Set aside. Add a small amount of colouring of choice to your remaining fondant, for cap and lid. Knead in colour until there are no streaks. If the fondant feels sticky, add extra sifted icing sugar (see Tips). Transfer into a sealable container and set aside until ready to use (see Tips). Add a small amount of black colouring to the reserved 200 g fondant and knead in colour until there are no streaks. If the fondant feels sticky, add extra sifted icing sugar (see Tips). Transfer into a separate sealable container and set aside until ready to use (see Tips).

Cap assembly

  1. Trim base of the sphere cake, if needed, to create a flat surface, then cut into halves horizontally. Place bottom of the sphere cake onto a cake board or platter and spread with approx. 2 tablespoons icing. Place the top (dome) cake on top. Evenly spread a thin layer of icing (approx. 5 tablespoons) to crumb coat over entire cake. Place cake into refrigerator until firm to the touch (10-15 minutes).
  2. Roll out reserved coloured fondant to 5 mm thickness. Lay rolled fondant over cooled sphere cake and press out any creases to create a smooth surface. Trim off any excess fondant at base of cap. Set aside trimmed excess fondant for peak and decorations. To make stitching detail, see Tips.
  3. Roll out excess coloured fondant to 5 mm thickness. Use the peak of a baseball cap as a template for your peak. Draw and cut it out on a piece of paper and use as a guide to make peak. Place fondant peak up against base of cake.

Headphones and decoration assembly

  1. Trim dome off both cupcakes to create a flat surface. Evenly spread a thin layer of icing (3-4 tablespoons) over cupcakes to create a crumb coat with a smooth and even surface. Place into refrigerator until firm to the touch (10-15 minutes).
  2. Roll out black fondant to 5 mm thickness and cut out headphone strap (approx. 30 x 3 cm) and place over top of cake.
  3. Roll out remaining black fondant to 5 mm thickness. Using a cookie cutter (10-12 cm), cut out 2 circles. Place 1 fondant circle over base of each cupcake to cover completely. Pleat and mould fondant to shape headphones.
  4. Insert several toothpicks or pieces of spaghetti into the side of the cupcake with fondant seams. Attach 1 cupcake to each side of the cap at black strap. Decorate headphones and cap with any remaining fondant as desired. Set aside until ready to serve.

Useful Items

2 cupcake liners, 12-hole muffin tray, silicone cake sphere (18 cm), wooden skewer, wire rack, silicone cake sphere mould (18 cm), bowl, silicone bread mat, sealable container, cake board or serving plate, rolling pin, cookie cutter (10-12 cm), toothpick or wooden skewer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a soft, sifted icing sugar for this recipe.
  • If you are not using icing immediately, place into refrigerator until ready to use (can be stored for up to 1 week). Before using, bring the icing back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread or pipe.
  • Glycerine is an edible product and can be purchased from cake decorating shops or the health and beauty aisle in most supermarkets.
  • Liquid food colouring may affect the texture of your fondant; add sifted icing sugar as needed. Once your fondant or modelling paste is coloured, try to avoid excess use of icing sugar when rolling or modelling as this will leave white marks. We recommend natural food colouring gel when possible.
  • If your fondant feels dry and cracked, feels very firm or crumbly, knead in a few drops of glycerine or a few drops of cooled boiled water to soften and make it more elastic. This will help avoid cracks in the finished product. You can also rub some vegetable shortening (Copha®) onto your palms and work surface and continue to knead.
  • Fondant can be stored for up to 2 months at room temperature. Fondant can be frozen for up to 1 month; simply bring back to room temperature before using.
  • To make stitching detail on cap and peak, use a piece of string to create 8 sections in the cap (string will make indented lines). Then use a design/quilting tool (available at cake decorating stores or online) and make indents on either side of the string markings.
