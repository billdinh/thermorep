Ginger chicken wings

Ingredients

  • 50 g fresh ginger, peeled and cut into pieces (approx. 8 cm)
  • 20 g sesame oil
  • 30 g light soy sauce
  • 20 g dark soy sauce
  • 30 g oyster sauce
  • 40 g dark brown sugar
  • 1000 g chicken winglets
  • 250 g jasmine rice
  • 1050 g water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin strips (approx. 5 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place ginger, sesame oil, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and place bowl onto mixing bowl lid. Weigh chicken winglets into it, toss to coat and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Rinse rice until water runs clear. Place 1000 g of the water and salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with rice and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Place simmering basket with rice into Varoma dish. Discard cooking water and rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place reserved marinated chicken and remaining 50 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma with simmering basket and rice into position. Secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma and transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, then cover to keep warm.
  4. Stir chicken with aid of spatula and cook for a further 15 min/120°C/speed , without measuring cup or until chicken is cooked. Garnish with spring onions/shallots and serve with rice.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Dark soy sauce is richer and less salty than light soy sauce (and may also have added sugar). Light soy sauce is thinner and often used as a light seasoning or in dipping sauces in Chinese cooking. You can find both types of soy sauce at major supermarkets or Asian food stores.
  • Chicken winglets may also be sold as chicken wing nibbles in some supermarkets.
