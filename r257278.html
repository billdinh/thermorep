Everyone's favourite chicken curry

Ingredients

  • 250 g basmati rice
  • 500 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 300 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves, roots and stems, plus extra for garnishing (see Tips)
  • 50 g ghee
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 800 g boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 3 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 50 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 - 2 dried red chillies, to taste (optional)
  • 6 cardamom pods, crushed (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Cover with water and set aside.
  2. Place onion, ginger, garlic, fresh coriander stalks and roots into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add ghee, cumin, ground coriander, garam masala and turmeric and sauté 2 min/120°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, cut chicken into pieces (3 cm).
  4. Add tomatoes, stock paste and chicken pieces and cook 15 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add yoghurt and fresh coriander leaves and cook 1 min/100°C//speed . Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  6. Transfer reserved rice into simmering basket and rinse until water runs clear. Place water and salt into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Add chilli (optional) and cardamom pods (optional), stir to combine and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 4 or until rice is cooked to your liking. Carefully discard cardamom and chillies if used.
  7. Garnish curry with extra coriander leaves and serve with rice.

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own Garam masala, please refer to the recipes on Cookidoo (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Soaking the rice reduces the amount of time needed to cook it.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside before adding to the mixing bowl.
