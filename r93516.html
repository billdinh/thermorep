Shepherd's Pie

Ingredients

Lamb Mince

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 180 g onions, quartered
  • 120 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 100 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 500 g lamb mince, broken in pieces
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • 100 g vegetable stock
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only

Potato and Leek Topping

  • 800 g water, for steaming
  • 800 g potatoes, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 250 g leeks, halved then cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 25 g milk
  • 25 g butter

Preparation

Lamb Mince

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8 then scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil, onions, carrots and celery then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 4 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add lamb mince and sauté 5 min/120°C//speed .
  4. Add tomato purée, stock, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, bay leaf and thyme leaves then cook 15 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer to a large ovenproof dish (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm x 5 cm). Remove bay leaf then set dish aside while making the topping. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Potato and Leek Topping

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in potatoes then steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, place leeks in Varoma dish.
  2. Place Varoma into position and steam leeks above potatoes 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile preheat oven to 190°C.
  3. Remove Varoma and simmering basket with aid of spatula then discard steaming water. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cooked potatoes, salt, pepper, milk and butter in mixing bowl then mash 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add leeks and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Spoon mashed potato topping over meat layer then bake for 20-30 minutes (190°C) until golden. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

rectangular baking dish (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm x 5 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This dish can be made ahead then reheated for 35-40 minutes (190°C).
  • Another real British classic – and one most families have their own way of making! Here’s ours, which features leek in the potato topping. Traditionally a dish popular for using leftover roasted lamb, this winter warmer is a constant firm staple on British menus.
  • Works well served with steamed buttered cabbage.
