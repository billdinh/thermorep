Pomegranate Glazed Stuffed Beef Tenderloin

Ingredients

Pomegranate Glaze

  • 3 ½ oz shallots, in pieces
  • 10 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 6 oz port wine
  • 4 oz pomegranate molasses
  • 1 oz sugar
  • 1 oz Dijon mustard
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, in pieces

Beef Tenderloin

  • 64 oz beef tenderloin, trimmed, spiral cut to lay flat (approx. 4 lb)
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 8 - 10 oz homemade holiday stuffing
  • 8 oz pomegranate arils/seeds (approx. 1⅓ cup), divided
  • 30 oz red onions, sliced into half-moons (¼ in.)
  • 1 - 2 pomegranates, halved, to garnish
  • lemon leaves, to garnish

Preparation

Pomegranate Glaze

  1. Place shallots and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add port, pomegranate molasses and sugar, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add mustard and butter and blend 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer glaze to a bowl and set aside.

Beef Tenderloin

  1. Unroll beef tenderloin and lay flat on a baking sheet. Season both sides of tenderloin with salt and pepper. Evenly place 8-10 oz stuffing and 2 oz pomegranate seeds on tenderloin. Re-roll and use kitchen twine to secure tenderloin, spaced approximately every 2 inches.
  2. Preheat oven to 350F. Meanwhile, brush approximately ⅓ of the reserved pomegranate glaze all over tenderloin. Arrange red onions around and under tenderloin.
  3. Bake uncovered for 30 minutes (350°F).
  4. Increase oven temperature to 450°F. Baste tenderloin with ⅓ of the reserved pomegranate glaze and bake 10 minutes (450°F).
  5. Baste with remaining pomegranate glaze and bake a further 10 minutes (450°F) or until cooked to desired doneness. Cover with foil and let rest 10 minutes.
  6. Slice 1-1½ inch thick medallions. Garnish with 6 oz pomegranate seeds, halved pomegranates and lemon leaves. Serve hot with roasted red onions.

Useful Items

baking sheet, basting brush, aluminum foil, kitchen twine, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Internal temperatures for beef: 140°F for medium, 135°F for medium-rare, 145°F for medium-well or 155°F for well done.
  • Use Holiday Stuffing recipe from the Christmas Dinner collection.
  • Ask a butcher to slice the tenderloin in a spiral to save some time.
  • Replace stuffing with mushroom-artichoke dip from the Christmas Dinner collection.
