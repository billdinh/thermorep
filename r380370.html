Ingredients

  • 500 g filtered water, plus extra as needed
  • 350 g beetroot (red or orange), peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 blood orange, juice only (80-90 g - see Tips)
  • natural yoghurt, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and weigh beetroot into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until tender. Set Varoma aside. Discard cooking water.
  2. Place juice and steamed beetroot into mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. If needed, add 1-2 tablespoons water and blend 15 sec/speed 6 or to desired consistency (see Tips).
  4. Set aside 1 serving, then freeze the remainder in individual portions for future use. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving. Serve with natural yoghurt.

Hints & Tricks

  • Unlike other citrus fruits, blood oranges contain anthocyanins, the same antioxidant pigment found in purple carrots. They're also a source of vitamin C, fibre and folate. But if they aren't in season, you can replace the blood orange with a standard orange.
  • Citrus fruits, including orange, are high in salicylates - natural compounds that protect their fruit. While they usually cause no problem for us, some people have salicylate intolerance with symptoms such as gut discomfort and skin rashes. The research is still developing but it is worth being aware of such intolerances.
  • When starting solids, babies typically prefer a smooth consistency. But as your baby gets accustomed to solid food you can increase the texture in your purees.
