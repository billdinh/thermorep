Bramley Apple Shortcake

Ingredients

  • 250 g butter, cold, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g granulated sugar
  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1200 g Bramley cooking apples, peeled, cored, sliced (3 cm), in a bowl of water with 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 20 g milk, for glazing

Preparation

  1. Grease a shallow metal pie dish or tart tin (Ø 20 cm) then set aside.
  2. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer 3 Tbsp of the sugar to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Add 200 g butter to mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add flour, baking powder and egg then knead 1 min 30 sec/.
  5. Tip dough out onto a floured surface and divide into two portions, one slightly larger than the other. Press the larger portion into the prepared pie dish, using floured hands, then place in fridge for 20 minutes. Form remaining dough into a disc (2 cm thick), wrap in cling film then place in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, place a roasting tin in oven then preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Drain apple slices then place in a bowl lined with kitchen towels. Place 50 g butter in the heated roasting tin then add apple slices and sprinkle over 2 Tbsp of the reserved ground sugar. Bake for 10 minutes (180°C), stirring halfway through.
  7. Transfer apple slices to pie dish and pile up high. Roll out remaining dough on a floured surface then carefully lay it over the fruit. Press edges together to seal pie, and patch any holes if necessary. Brush gently with 20 g milk and sprinkle over ½ Tbsp reserved ground sugar.
  8. Place on a baking tray to catch any drips then bake for 30 minutes (180°C) until golden. Sprinkle with remaining ½ Tbsp reserved ground sugar before serving hot or setting aside to cool completely.

Useful Items

shallow metal pie dish or shallow tart tin (Ø 20 cm), cling film, roasting tin, pastry brush, rolling pin, baking tray, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Placing the apple slices in a large bowl of water with 2 tsp lemon juice helps prevent the peeled apples from discolouring.
  • Hearty and warming, this traditional British dessert makes use of those classic Bramley cooking apples. With large slices of apple encased in a rich and crumbly pastry, this is best enjoyed with custard, cream or ice cream.
