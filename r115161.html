Fish ambul thiyal (Sour fish curry)

Ingredients

  • 1060 g water
  • 1 lime, juice only (approx. 20 g)
  • 500 g firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 - 4 fresh green chillies, small, deseeded if preferred
  • 10 - 12 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 100 g coconut cream
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 - 3 pandan leaves (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 500 g of the water into it. Add lime juice and stir to combine. Add fish and gently wash in lime water, then transfer onto a plate and set aside. Discard lime water.
  2. Place peppercorns, chilli powder and cinnamon quill into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add ginger, garlic, green chillies and half of the curry leaves and chop 30 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then roast 1 min/Varoma/speed .
  5. Add tamarind paste and 60 g of the water and mix 30 sec/speed 8, or until a smooth paste forms.
  6. Add coconut cream and salt and mix 8 sec/speed 8.
  7. Line bottom of a small ceramic baking dish (12 cm) with pandan leaves. Place fish on top of pandan leaves and pour over curry liquid. Sprinkle remaining curry leaves over curry and set aside to marinate (approx. 30 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Place remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position and place ramekin with fish into Varoma. Secure Varoma lidand steam 12-14 min/Varoma/speed 1, until fish is cooked to your liking. Serve warm with steamed rice, pittu or coconut roti (see Tips).

Useful Items

large plate, ceramic or glass bowl, ceramic baking dish (12 cm)

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find pandan leaves in the freezer section of most Asian grocery stores. You can find fresh curry leaves in most supermarkets and Asian grocery stores.
  • To make pittu, please refer to the Pittu recipe in this collection.
  • To make coconut roti, please refer to the Kuruma iraichchi with coconut roti recipe in this collection.
