Tomato Basil Pasta

Ingredients

  • 1 - 2 oz garlic cloves (approx. 7-9 cloves), to taste
  • 3 oz extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • 35 oz ripe tomatoes, i.e. dry-farmed tomatoes (see Tip)
  • 2 anchovies, preserved in olive oil
  • 1 heaping tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 43 oz water
  • 16 oz pasta
  • grated pecorino cheese, to taste
  • 15 fresh basil leaves, torn into small pieces

Preparation

  1. Place garlic and 2 oz olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6 and sauté 5 min/250°F/speed .
  2. Add tomatoes, anchovies, chili and salt and chop 3 sec/speed 5 then cook 13 min/212°F/speed . Transfer to a bowl and keep warm
  3. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/212°F/speed 1. Add pasta through hole in mixing bowl lid and without measuring cup cook for time indicated on package/212°F//speed  or until al dente.
  4. Drain cooked pasta through simmering basket. Place pasta in a large serving bowl, drizzle with 1 oz olive oil and mix well with spatula. Pour tomato sauce over pasta, sprinkle with grated pecorino or Parmesan cheese and basil and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • If dry-farmed tomatoes are not available, make sure to use really ripe, flavorful tomatoes. Use the best extra virgin olive oil available.
  • When cooking pasta, follow the cooking time indicated on the package, or to your liking. Pasta that is cooked al dente is tender but still firm to the bite.
  • Tearing instead of cutting basil into pieces releases more flavor and prevents oxidation.
