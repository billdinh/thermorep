Lime Yoghurt Mousse with Strawberry Coulis

Ingredients

Lime Yoghurt Mousse

  • 3 gelatine leaves
  • water, for soaking
  • 70 g sugar
  • 200 g double cream
  • 30 g lime juice
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only (see tip)
  • 200 g yoghurt, 1.5% fat
  • 60 g crème fraîche

Strawberry Coulis

  • 150 g strawberries
  • 20 g lime juice
  • 1 sprig fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 lime, thinly sliced, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

Lime Yoghurt Mousse

  1. Soak gelatine leaves in a bowl of water for 5 minutes then squeeze out water. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whip without setting a time/speed 3 until desired stiffness, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place lime juice in mixing bowl and heat 2 min/60°C/speed 1.
  5. Add squeezed gelatine leaves and stir 5 sec/speed 2 until gelatine is completely dissolved.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Add vanilla seeds, yoghurt, crème fraîche and 50 g reserved icing sugar then mix 30 sec/speed 3.5.
  7. Add reserved whipped cream and stir 20 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Divide between dessert glasses (approx. 140 g each) and place in fridge for 2 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make coulis.

Strawberry Coulis

  1. Place strawberries, lime juice, remaining reserved icing sugar and basil in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 6. Serve with lime yoghurt mousse, garnished with lime slices (if using).

Useful Items

dessert glasses, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Vanilla seeds can easily be removed from the vanilla pod; hold the pod at one end, slit with a sharp knife lengthwise, then scrape out the seeds with the tip of the knife.
  • The empty vanilla pod can still be used, as it has lots of flavour. For example, you can make your own vanilla sugar, or cook the pod in milk for desserts.
