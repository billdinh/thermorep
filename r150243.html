Falafel crackers

Ingredients

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g chia seeds
  • 50 g sunflower seeds
  • 50 g pepitas
  • 50 g sesame seeds
  • 50 g flaxseeds (linseeds)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 35 g water
  • 25 g olive oil
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
247 kJ / 59 kcal
Protein
2.1 g
Carbohydrates
0.4 g
Fat
5.2 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Cut 2 pieces of baking paper (approx. 30 x 40 cm). Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add chia seeds, sunflower seeds, pepitas, sesame seeds, flaxseeds (linseeds), cumin, turmeric, oregano and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add water and oil and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or clean work surface and shape into a ball.
  5. Place dough onto 1 piece of baking paper and flatten slightly using hands. Place other piece of baking paper over dough and, using a rolling pin, evenly roll out dough into a rectangle (approx. 25 x 30 cm). Press any loose dough back together. Remove the top layer of baking paper and discard. Transfer baking paper with rolled dough onto baking tray. Using the tip of a sharp knife, score dough into squares (5 cm). Gently press dough back together if it tears.
  6. Bake for 25 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown at edges. Remove tray from oven and transfer to a wire rack to cool slightly. Gently break crackers apart along scored lines to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), silicone bread mat, non-serrated knife, rolling pin, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Store in a sealable container at room temperature for up to 1 week or store in the freezer for up to 4 months.
