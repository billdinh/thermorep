Vadouvan spiced mini rolls

Ingredients

Caramelised onions

  • 200 g red onion, cut into quarters
  • 30 g olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt flakes
  • 30 g red wine vinegar
  • 30 g raw sugar

Vadouvan spice mix

  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 20 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp brown mustard seeds
  • 2 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp salt flakes
  • ½ tsp black peppercorns

Bread rolls

  • 280 g water
  • 1 tsp dried yeast
  • ½ tsp salt flakes
  • 500 g baker's flour
  • 30 g olive oil, plus extra for brushing

Preparation

Caramelised onions

  1. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil, salt, vinegar and sugar and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Scrape base of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 7 min/Varoma/speed 1, without measuring cup. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Vadouvan spice mix

  1. Place all Vadouvan spice mix ingredients into mixing bowl and toast 6 min/Varoma/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  2. Insert measuring cup. Mill 1 min/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Bread rolls

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add salt, flour and oil and knead 2 min/. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or into an oiled bowl and cover with a kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

Assembly

  1. Divide dough into 20 equal portions (approx. 40 g each) and flatten slightly. Place 1 teaspoon of the reserved onion mixture into centre of each dough portion, then enclose to seal and shape into a smooth ball. Place onto a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) with edges of rolls just touching.
  2. Brush rolls lightly with oil, then sprinkle generously with Vadouvan spice mix.
  3. Place rolls into a cold oven and turn temperature to 190°C. When temperature reaches 190°C, cook for a further 10 minutes (190°C), or until rolls sound hollow when tapped. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, kitchen towel, silicone bread mat, large bowl, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Double or triple onion quantities to make enough for burger and hot dog toppings.
  • Remaining Vadouvan spice mix can be stored in a sealable jar in a cool, dry place. Use it as a curry spice base, or add to soups, butter, marinades and dressings.
  • Serve these rolls with the Yoghurt soup cups (refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • Make larger rolls for sit-down dinner parties.
