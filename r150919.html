Apricot, Hazelnut and Feta Couscous with Mixed Vegetables

Ingredients

  • 150 g raw hazelnuts
  • 150 g dried apricots
  • 300 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g garlic cloves
  • 60 g olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 200 g couscous
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • 600 g water
  • 200 g carrots, sliced (5 mm)
  • 200 g courgettes, sliced (7-10 mm)
  • 150 g red peppers, cut in bite-size pieces
  • 100 g frozen peas
  • 200 g feta cheese, diced (5 mm)
  • 2 tsp fresh parsley, chopped, to garnish (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place hazelnuts and dried apricots in mixing bowl and chop 5-10 sec/speed 5 or until roughly chopped (see tip). Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions, garlic, olive oil and salt in mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 4.5, then sauté 13 min/120°C/speed .
  3. Add couscous and vegetable stock paste and stir 10 sec//speed 2.
  4. Add 200 g water, stir using spatula, cover with mixing bowl lid and allow couscous to absorb liquid for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, place simmering basket on mixing bowl lid and add carrots, courgettes, red peppers and frozen peas and set aside.
  5. Transfer soaked couscous to large bowl containing chopped nuts and apricots. Add 150 g feta cubes and stir gently to mix well. Spread a quarter of the couscous mixture loosely on Varoma dish, leaving some holes unobstructed so steam can circulate, and remaining couscous on Varoma tray. Place 400 g water in mixing bowl and insert simmering basket with vegetables. Set Varoma in position and steam 17 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Transfer couscous to a large serving dish and gently fluff the couscous using a fork. Drizzle steamed vegetables with 1 Tbsp olive oil and stir gently to coat. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and arrange vegetables around the couscous. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley (if using) and reserved feta cheese.

Hints & Tricks

  • Dried apricots when soft take as little as 5 seconds to chop with the hazelnuts; however, if very dry and tough, they can take up to 15 seconds.
  • Inspired by a favourite couscous dish adapted for Thermomix®, this recipe now saves considerable effort, gives very tasty results, and reduces the washing up! You will find that steaming the couscous in addition to soaking gives a lovely light finish. The portions for six are very generous, and you can easily serve up to ten people if you add a salad or two to the menu.
  • Add pistachios and/or pomegranate seeds as extra garnishes.
    - Replace half or all the couscous with quinoa for a slightly nutty flavour and a higher protein content.
