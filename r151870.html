Crème caramel

Ingredients

  • 100 - 130 g caster sugar, to taste
  • 500 g whole milk
  • 4 eggs
  • 100 g liquid caramel (see tip)
  • 500 g water
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1048 kJ / 250 kcal

7 g

Preparation

  1. Choose 6-8 heat-resistant glass jars or ceramic cups (approx. 120 ml capacity) that fit in the Varoma dish with lid on, then set aside.
  2. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add whole milk and eggs and mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  4. Place 1-2 Tbsp liquid caramel in each jar. Fill jars with egg mixture and cover with aluminium foil or heat-resistant cling film and place in Varoma dish.
  5. Place water in mixing bowl, place covered Varoma into position and steam 50 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Set Varoma aside. Allow jars to cool uncovered for 1 hour before placing in fridge. Chill for a minimum of 1 hour. Tip each crème caramel upside down on a plate (see tip) and carefully remove jar. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, heatproof glass jars (Ø 6 cm, height 8 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Cooking in the Varoma at 100°C allows gentle heating, similar to baking in a bain-marie.
  • To make your own liquid caramel, melt 100 g sugar with 1 tbsp water and 1 tsp lemon juice in a medium-size, very clean saucepan over medium-high heat. Do not stir, but occasionally swirl the saucepan, until sugar becomes a dark amber. At this point, carefully pour in approx. 50 g hot water (it will bubble up and spatter). Stir over medium heat until all caramel is dissolved and syrup thickens slightly. Allow to cool before using.
  • Glass yoghurt containers of approx. 125 ml capacity make excellent moulds.
  • If using thicker jars, extend cooking time by 5-10 minutes.
  • To unmould, run a small sharp knife around the créme caramel, place a plate upside down on jar, flip jar and plate at the same time, hold them together tightly and give a sharp shake or two to loosen the créme caramel so that it falls on the plate.
  • Crème caramel, or flan, is a traditional dessert served in France and Spain.
  • To serve a larger crowd, double the quantities and bake in a preheated oven (140°C) in a bain marie for 1 hour 20 minutes. Bain marie: place filled jars in baking dish, place dish on middle shelf in oven and fill the dish with very hot water up to ¾ of the jars' height.
