Ice Cream Cone Cupcakes

Ingredients

Cakes

  • 16 ice cream cones, flat bottomed
  • 90 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 4 medium eggs, separated
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 Tbsp water, hot

Icing

  • 180 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 360 g icing sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 6 chocolate flake bars, each cut in 3 pieces

Preparation

Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place ice cream cones on a baking tray.
  2. Place flour, bicarbonate of soda and cream of tartar in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whisk until stiff peaks form/speed 3.
  5. Whisk again, without measuring cup, 2 min/speed 3 while slowly adding reserved ground sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Whisk again 1 min/speed 0.5 while adding egg yolks one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add vanilla extract, water and reserved flour mixture then mix 10 sec/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 5 sec/speed 1.  Remove butterfly whisk then finish mixing by hand with spatula.
  8. Divide evenly between ice cream cones, filling each just over halfway. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Transfer cones to a wire rack and allow to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make icing.

Icing

  1. Place butter, icing sugar and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then beat 45 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a fluted nozzle.
  2. Once cakes are cool, pipe icing in a swirling motion onto ice cream cones.
  3. Push a piece of flake into the icing of each cake. Serve or store in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

baking tray, skewer, wire rack, piping bag and nozzle, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If a fluted nozzle is not available, the icing can be piped using a disposable icing bag with the end snipped off.

Perfect for children’s parties, these are great fun to make. Unusually, the sponge is baked straight in the ice cream cones which looks impressive. You could experiment with different food colouring in the icing too, to make different flavoured ‘ice creams’.
