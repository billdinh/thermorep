Peri peri sweet potato fries with coriander yoghurt sauce

Ingredients

Peri peri spice mix

  • 1 tbsp sea salt
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • 15 cardamom pods
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tbsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp dried chilli flakes (optional - see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp dried oregano

Sweet potato fries

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 - 3 sweet potatoes (approx 700 g), cut into batons (1.5 cm)

Coriander yoghurt sauce

  • 40 g fresh coriander (approx. 2 bunches), leaves only
  • 10 g fresh mint (approx. 1 bunch), leaves only
  • 1 lime, zested, no white pith and juiced
  • 170 g Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • 2 tsp honey
  • ¼ tsp salt

Preparation

Peri peri spice mix

  1. Place all spice mix ingredients into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10 (see Tips). Transfer into a sealable container and store in a cool, dry place until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sweet potato fries

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place oil, sweet potatoes and 1-2 teaspoons of the reserved spice mix into a large bowl and stir until potatoes are coated. Transfer onto prepared tray and bake for 35-45 minutes (190°C), until potatoes are cooked through and golden.

Coriander yoghurt sauce

  1. Place coriander, mint and lime zest into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lime juice, yoghurt, honey and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a serving bowl and serve with warm Sweet potato fries.

Useful Items

sealable container, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), large bowl, serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend making the Peri peri spice mix in a well-ventilated area, as the finely chopped chillies may cause eye, nose or throat irritation. You can store spice mix in the pantry for up to 6 months.
  • Adjust the amount of chilli in your spice mix to taste.
