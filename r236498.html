Pork, apricot & coconut wraps

Ingredients

  • 90 g hot chilli sauce
  • 90 g soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine
  • 500 - 600 g pork loin steaks, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 10 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves and stalks
  • 4 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 40 g lime juice (1 lime)
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 80 g coconut chips (see Tips)
  • 50 g sunflower seeds
  • 100 g dried apricots, cut into halves
  • 50 g pepitas
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 - 2 baby cos lettuce (approx. 250 g), leaves separated and chopped as desired
  • 6 tortillas, wraps or flatbreads of choice

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chilli sauce and soy sauce into it. Add Chinese cooking wine and stir to combine. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 50 g of the chilli soy dressing into it and set aside.
  2. Place bowl with remaining dressing back onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pork into it. Stir to combine, cover and set aside to marinate for 30 minutes.
  3. Place coriander and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add lime juice, salt, pepper and coconut chips and combine 10 sec/speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Rinse mixing bowl.
  5. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sunflower seeds, apricots and pepitas into it; set aside.
  6. Place garlic and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  8. Add marinated pork, along with 50 g of the marinating juices, and cook 5-8 min/Varoma//speed 1 or until pork is cooked to your liking.
  9. Carefully drain off excess liquid, then shred 3-5 sec//speed 5. Allow to cool for 5 minutes.
  10. Transfer cooled pork, reserved seed mixture, reserved coconut mixture and reserved dressing into a serving bowl and toss to combine. Serve with lettuce and wraps (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, jug, plastic wrap, serving bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find coconut chips (also known as shaved coconut) at most major supermarkets.
  • This is also a great dish to serve as a salad. Simply omit the wraps and toss the lettuce with the pork mixture in the last step.
