Watermelon and raspberry ice cream sandwiches

Ingredients

Ice cream

  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 800 g watermelon flesh, cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 150 g frozen raspberries
  • 380 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 120 g full cream milk

Wafers

  • 100 g rice malt syrup
  • 50 g pure maple syrup
  • 50 g puffed amaranth grain (see Tips)
  • 20 g puffed millet (see Tips)
  • 20 g shredded coconut
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • oil, for greasing

Preparation

Ice cream

  1. Place vanilla bean paste, watermelon, sugar and raspberries into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 5 minutes.
  2. Insert measuring cup. Blend 10 sec/speed 2, or until smooth. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a shallow freezable container and place into freezer for 30 minutes, or until cold. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and milk into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 3.5.
  4. Add chilled watermelon purée and whip 10 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer mixture up to 2 cm high into shallow freezable containers (plastic or silicone) and cover with plastic wrap. Allow mixture to cool completely, then place into freezer for 3 hours or until firm and just frozen.
  5. Cut frozen mixture into pieces (approx. 4 x 4 cm) with a sharp knife. Place pieces into mixing bowl and crush 30 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a freezable container and place into freezer overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Wafers

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place syrups into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add all remaining wafer ingredients, except oil, and combine 1 min//speed 2, or until well combined.
  4. Transfer mixture onto a large sheet of baking paper. Cover with a second sheet of baking paper and roll out thinly (5 mm thickness). Place oil into a shallow bowl and dip round biscuit or scone cutter (7 cm) into it and use to cut out wafers. Using the baking paper, wafer mixture can be reformed and rolled out again to cut additional rounds (20 wafers total). Carefully transfer onto prepared baking trays. Bake for 6 minutes (170°C), or until just browned. Allow to cool on baking trays for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to harden.
  5. Remove ice cream from freezer and, working quickly, use a round biscuit or scone cutter (7 cm) to cut out ice cream portions (10 x 2 cm rounds). Sandwich each ice cream portion between 2 wafers. Wafers will stick to ice cream as it softens. Serve immediately or place into freezer until ready to serve.

Useful Items

freezable storage container, fine mesh sieve, plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, round cookie cutter (7 cm), rolling pin, bowl, wire rack, freezer, oven
