Cheese Scones

Ingredients

  • 450 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 level tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 120 g butter, cold, cut in pieces
  • 140 g Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 10 sprigs fresh chives, cut in lengths (3 cm)
  • 250 g milk, plus extra for glazing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C and line a baking tray with baking paper. Place flour, baking powder and salt in mixing bowl then mix 4 sec/speed 8. Add butter, cheese and chives then chop 4 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add milk and knead 20 sec/. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead again 3-10 sec/ until dough just pulls together.
  3. Turn mixing bowl upside down on a floured surface and release mixing knife to allow dough to drop out. Gently bring dough together into a round shape and flatten to about 1.5 cm thick. Using a pastry cutter (Ø 6 cm), cut into rounds (press the cutter straight down without twisting). Gently reshape remaining dough and cut out more circles until dough is used up. Arrange scones on prepared baking tray and brush the tops with milk.
  4. Bake 15 minutes (220°C) or until golden. Serve warm or cold as desired - works well with a bowl of soup or as a snack.

Useful Items

baking tray, baking paper, baking tray and paper, round pastry cutter (Ø 6 cm), pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We like to use strong or extra mature cheddar. For children, use medium or mild cheddar.
  • When using a biscuit cutter on scone dough, do not twist it or your scones will rise unevenly. Just press straight down and lift straight up, shaking cutter to loosen dough if necessary.
  • Best eaten same day. If you have any extras, they freeze well. Freeze on day of baking once completely cool.
  • Triangular scones are traditional in Scotland - cut the flattened dough into triangles with a knife instead of using a round pastry cutter.
  • Rough cheese scones - instead of patting dough flat and cutting out shapes, drop spoonfuls of dough onto baking tray straight from mixing bowl. The scones will bake with a rough look and a crispy outside with lots of flavour.
  • Scones are traditional throughout Britain and Ireland. They are quick to make anytime, and plain or fruited scones are popular for afternoon tea. These cheese scones are delicious with soups - make the scones and put them in the oven, and while they bake you can make a soup in your Thermomix®.
