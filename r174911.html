Salmon Burger

Ingredients

Tartar Sauce

  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp capers, drained
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ oz olive oil

Salmon Burger

  • 1 lemon, peel only, no pith
  • 2 oz bread cubes
  • 1 oz shallot
  • ½ oz parsley, leaves only
  • ½ oz fresh basil, leaves only
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground pepper
  • 16 oz salmon, skinless and boneless, in pieces (1 in.), chilled
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • oil, to fry
  • 4 hamburger buns, toasted
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced

Preparation

Tartar Sauce

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks, lemon juice, capers, mustard, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/140°F/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Mix 1 min/100°F/speed 4 while slowly pouring oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades to create an emulsion. Transfer to a bowl, cover and refrigerate until ready to use. Clean mixing bowl.

Salmon Burger

  1. Place lemon peel into mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl.
  2. Add bread, shallot, parsley, basil, salt and pepper and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add salmon and Worcestershire sauce and chop 5 sec//speed 5.
  4. Divide and shape salmon mixture into four equal-sized patties (1 in. thick). Create a dimple in the center of patties by pressing down with your fingers to prevent them from rising while cooking. Place a frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Fry patties for 3-4 minutes each side or until golden brown and cooked to desired temperature.
  5. Spread bun with Tartar Sauce, add salmon patty and red onion slices and serve warm.

Useful Items

large frying pan

Hints & Tricks

  • Wash lemon and herbs before use.
  • For a gluten-free salmon patty replace Worcestershire sauce with 1 tsp liquid aminos, (e.g. Bragg)
