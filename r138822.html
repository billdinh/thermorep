Creamy mango icy poles

Ingredients

  • 80 - 100 g honey, to taste
  • 40 g milk (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 250 g vanilla yoghurt
  • 1 tsp arrowroot flour
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 300 g frozen mango cheeks, cut into cubes (3 cm)
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
327 kJ / 78 kcal
Protein
1.5 g
Carbohydrates
15.5 g
Fat
1 g

Preparation

  1. Place honey into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/50°C/speed 1
  2. Add milk, vanilla paste and yoghurt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6 or until smooth.
  3. Place arrowroot and water into a small bowl and stir to combine. Add arrowroot mixture to mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a jug and set aside.
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place mango and 80 g of reserved yoghurt mixture into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 8.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 30 sec/speed 5 or until smooth.
  6. Divide mango purée (approx. 2 tablespoons) between 8-10 popsicle moulds (see Tips), then gently spoon milk mixture over fruit to fill moulds. Insert mould lid or wooden popsicle sticks (3-4 cm into mango mixture) and place into freezer to set (approx. 8 hours).
  7. Allow to stand at room temperature for a few minutes to soften before unmoulding and serving.

Useful Items

small bowl , jug, 8 popsicle moulds, popsicle sticks , freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any milk of choice in this recipe.
  • The number of icy poles made will depend on the size of the moulds you use. It is important that your moulds have a long enough popsicle stick to be inserted into the mango; if the stick is not long enough you may not be able to remove the icy pole from moulds.
  • Creamy strawberry icy poles: Replace mango with 300 g frozen strawberries and proceed as per recipe.
