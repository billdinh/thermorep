Fresh Fruit Tarts

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 120 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 175 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g icing sugar
  • 2 egg yolks, from medium eggs

Crème Pâtissière

  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 40 g cornflour
  • 500 g whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only (see tip)

Assembly

  • 160 g apricot jam
  • 30 g water
  • 450 g mixed fresh fruit, thinly sliced (e.g. blueberries, raspberries, kiwi fruit, strawberries)

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place butter, flour and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add egg yolks and pulse Turbo/2 sec/5 times. Tip out and bring together into a flattened ball, then wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 1 hour. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make crème pâtissière.

Crème Pâtissière

  1. Place all crème pâtissière ingredients in mixing bowl then heat 7 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer to a large bowl and cover with cling film, pressing cling film against surface. Leave to cool at room temperature for 1 hour then chill in fridge for 2 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. On a lightly-floured surface, roll out chilled dough to 5 mm thick. Cut out 6 circles using a pastry cutter (Ø 11 cm) and line 6 loose-based tartlet tins (Ø 10 cm). Prick bases with a fork then chill in fridge for a further 30 minutes. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Line chilled pastry cases with baking paper, fill with baking beans then blind bake for 8-10 minutes (180°C) until edges are lightly golden. Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C). Transfer to a wire rack and leave to cool completely (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Place jam and water in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Pass through a fine sieve into a small bowl.
  4. Divide cooled crème pâtissière between pastry cases until full and slightly raised in the centre. Carefully arrange fresh fruit over crème pâtissière, pressing in slightly to secure, then brush all over with apricot glaze. Place in fridge to chill for 1 hour before serving chilled.

Useful Items

cling film, wire rack, baking paper, baking beans, fine sieve, large bowl, rolling pin, pastry brush, pastry cutter, loose-based tartlet tins (Ø 10 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Vanilla seeds can easily be removed from the vanilla pod; hold the pod at one end, slit with a sharp knife lengthways, then scrape out the seeds with the tip of the knife.
  • The empty vanilla pod can still be used, as it has lots of flavour. For example, you can make your own vanilla sugar, or cook the pod in milk for desserts.
