Thai Peanut Chicken with Coconut Rice

Ingredients

Peanut Sauce

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 160 g peanut butter
  • 80 g light soy sauce
  • 80 g orange juice
  • 80 g dark brown sugar
  • 10 g sesame oil
  • 1 - 2 Tbsp sriracha sauce, to taste (see tip)

Chicken, Rice and Vegetables

  • 600 g water
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 350 g basmati rice
  • 20 g desiccated coconut
  • 600 g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, diced (1-2 cm)
  • 200 g fresh baby sweetcorn, halved lengthways
  • 200 g sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • unsalted peanuts, roughly chopped, for garnishing
  • fresh coriander leaves, roughly chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

Peanut Sauce

  1. Place garlic, ginger and olive oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 2 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add coconut milk, peanut butter, soy sauce, orange juice, sugar, sesame oil and sriracha then cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1. Transfer to a jug and cover to keep warm. Rinse mixing bowl.

Chicken, Rice and Vegetables

  1. Place water and coconut milk in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket then weigh in rice and desiccated coconut. Rinse 10 sec/speed 5.
  2. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in chicken, leaving some holes unobstructed for steam to circulate. Cover Varoma and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 4.
  3. Set covered Varoma aside and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Transfer cooked rice to a large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  4. Remove Varoma lid and stir chicken with spatula to ensure even cooking.
  5. Insert Varoma tray, place Varoma back into position then weigh in baby sweetcorn. Cover Varoma and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Set covered Varoma aside and empty mixing bowl.
  6. Place reserved peanut sauce, reserved steamed chicken, reserved cooked baby sweetcorn and sugar snap peas in mixing bowl then cook 10 min/110°C//speed .
  7. Divide coconut rice between serving bowls, ladle over peanut chicken then garnish with chopped peanuts and coriander. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

jug, aluminium foil, large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • After a long day at work when the takeaway menus are calling, give this quick and tasty Thai dish a go. Steamed chicken and veggies are served in a gorgeously creamy peanut sauce accompanied by fragrant coconut rice - the perfect family fakeaway!
  • Sriracha sauce is a Thai hot sauce made from a paste of chillies, vinegar, garlic, sugar and salt. It is often used as a dipping sauce, but can also be eaten on burgers or eggs. If sriracha sauce is not available, substitute with chilli sauce of choice.
