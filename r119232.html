Delish pesto nuggets

Ingredients

  • 100 g gluten free rice crumbs
  • 1 garlic clove (small)
  • 80 g pine nuts
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • ¼ - ½ tsp sea salt
  • 500 g chicken mince (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g coconut cream
  • 2 - 3 tbsp coconut oil, for frying
Nutrition

Preparation

  1. Place a plate onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice crumbs onto it. Set plate aside.
  2. Place garlic, pine nuts, spinach, oil and salt into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add chicken mince, stock paste, coconut cream and combine 10 sec//speed 3 .
  4. Place 2 teaspoons of the mince mixture onto plate with rice crumbs. Cover mixture with crumbs, then form into a nugget shape. Transfer onto a separate plate. Repeat until all mince mixture is coated and shaped into nuggets (approx. 30 nuggets).
  5. Place a frying pan over medium-low heat and add coconut oil. Working in batches, fry nuggets for approx. 10 minutes, turning several times to prevent sticking, until golden on both sides and just firm. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

large plate, 1 plate, large frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find gluten free rice crumbs in the health food aisle of most supermarkets.
  • This recipe works best using homemade mince. To make chicken mince, partially freeze 500 g chicken (breast or thigh meat), cut into 3 cm cubes. Place 250 g partially frozen chicken into mixing bowl and Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Add remaining 250 g chicken cut into cubes (3 cm) and Turbo/2 sec/1 time.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Some coconut cream products can be made using equipment that has come into contact with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern for you, be sure to check the label of your coconut cream.
  • Leftover coconut cream can be used to make Blueberry icing or Chocolate 'buttercream' frosting, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Not only are these a low-allergen party food for kids and adults, but they are great to have in the freezer for snacks, dinners and lunchboxes.
  • You can serve these warm or cold, prepared the day before and reheated.
  • Enjoy on their own or serve with a salsa, dipping sauce or homemade tomato sauce.
