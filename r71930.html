Traditional chicken broth with angel hair pasta

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 ½ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 1400 g water
  • 50 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 70 g celeriac, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 30 g leek, white part only, cut into pieces
  • 3 - 4 chicken wings
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 5 black peppercorns
  • 2 allspice berries
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 150 g angel hair pasta
  • fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, chopped, to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place apple cider vinegar, salt and 1200 g of the water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket. Weigh carrot, celeriac and leek into it. Add chicken wings, bay leaves, peppercorns and allspice berries to simmering basket and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  2. Add remaining 200 g water and ground black pepper and cook 40 min/90°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, cook angel hair pasta as per packet instructions then transfer into serving bowls and set aside.
  3. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard basket contents. Adjust seasoning to taste, pour broth over the pasta and garnish with parsley to serve.

Useful Items

stock pot (3-4 L), serving bowls, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute celeriac for celery stalks.
  • You can substitute fresh flat-leaf parsley with fresh dill, leaves only.
  • Nothing soothes like a warm bowl of chicken broth. If you feel a little under the weather, whip up this simple European broth and sip. You'll feel better in no time.
  • Leftover broth can be used in risotto recipes in place of stock paste and water.
  • The resulting broth can be used as a base for many other soups by simply adding minimal ingredients and blending.  Add fresh tomatoes and a little cream for tomato soup or fresh mushrooms and a little créme frâiche for mushroom soup.
  • For a gluten free option, use rice vermicelli noodles or buckwheat soba noodles.  Any leftover vermicelli noodles can be used in the Thai beef salad with noodles.
  • Any small cuts of chicken on the bone can be used in this recipe (e.g. drumsticks or chicken lovely legs).
