Seafood Ravioli - Ravioli ai frutti di mare

Ingredients

Filling

  • 2 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 150 g white fish fillets, skinless, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 30 g dry white wine
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 150 g potatoes, peeled and diced (1 cm)
  • water, for cooking potatoes
  • 1 medium egg

Pasta

  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g semolina
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Assembly

  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 50 g onions, halved
  • 100 g courgettes, cut in round slices (1 cm), then halved
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 400 g cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra for cooking pasta
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 100 g large raw prawns, peeled
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • water, for cooking pasta

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil and garlic in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 7 then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add fish and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add wine, salt and pepper then cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, cook potatoes in a saucepan of boiling salted water for 10-15 minutes or until completely cooked, then drain.
  5. Add cooked potatoes to mixing bowl and mash 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add egg and reserved chopped parsley then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Adjust seasoning to taste. Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool, then cover with cling film and place in fridge. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make pasta.

Pasta

  1. Place flour, semolina, eggs, oil and salt in mixing bowl then knead 2 min/. Tip out onto a work surface and bring together into a ball. Place in a bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rest for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Divide rested dough into 4 equal-sized pieces. Take 1 piece, keeping remaining dough covered with cling film in bowl and, using a rolling pin or pasta machine, roll into a long rectangular sheet of pasta (approx. 2-3 mm thick, 10-12 cm wide).
  3. Fold sheet in half lengthways and press gently to create a crease, then reopen. Place teaspoons of mixture along the middle of one half of the sheet, leaving approx. 2-3 cm between. Fold other half of dough over, taking care to press out any air bubbles before sealing completely. Using a ravioli cutter or pastry wheel, cut ravioli, transfer to prepared tray and sprinkle with flour. Repeat until all dough and filling have been used.

Assembly

  1. Place oil and onions in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 7 then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add courgettes and thyme then cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Add tomatoes, salt and pepper then cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  4. Add prawns and butter then stir 3-4 min//speed . Meanwhile, bring a large saucepan of salted water to the boil then cook ravioli for 3-4 minutes or until al dente. Remove ravioli with a slotted spoon, transfer to a serving dish and cover with sauce. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

saucepan, cling film, toothed wheel for ravioli, baking tray and baking paper, large bowl, rolling pin or pasta machine, large saucepan, slotted spoon, colander, fridge

Hints & Tricks

  • Ravioli are a type of dumpling containing a filling sealed between two thin layers of pasta dough. References to ravioli date back to 16th century Rome, and they remain a staple of Italian cuisine today. Ravioli are served either in a thin broth as a starter, or with pasta sauce as a main. Traditional Italian fillings include ricotta mixed with grated cheese and spinach, or mixed meat and vegetables. As with many recipes, location has a bearing on the dish, with seafood ravioli such as this one popular in the coastal regions.
