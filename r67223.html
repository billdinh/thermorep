Quiche Lorraine

Ingredients

  • 50 g Gruyère cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm) (optional)

Pastry

  • 75 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for flouring
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 50 g water

Filling

  • 200 g bacon cubes
  • 20 g olive oil (optional)
  • 4 eggs
  • 50 g milk
  • 100 g crème fraîche
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place Gruyère cheese or Swiss cheese (optional) into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and flour a quiche tin (26 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place butter, flour, salt and water into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer pastry onto a ThermoMat or floured surface and work into a ball.
  3. Using a rolling pin, roll out pastry. Line prepared tin with the pastry, prick with a fork to avoid air bubbles and set aside.Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place bacon cubes and olive oil (optional) into mixing bowl and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed . Drain bacon through simmering basket and discard fat. Transfer bacon into a bowl and aside.
  2. Place eggs, milk, crème fraîche or cream, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Distribute bacon cubes onto pastry and cover with egg mixture. Sprinkle reserved cheese over the top (optional) and bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

quiche tin (26 cm), ThermoMat, rolling pin, oven, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with a mixed salad as a main course.
  • Depending on the fattiness of your bacon you may need to add the olive oil in step 5 before sautéing.
  • For a crispier base, blind bake your pastry for 10 minutes at the end of step 4, then poceed as per recipe. Reduce baking time in step 7 to 20 - 25 minutes.
  • Vegetarian quiche: instead of bacon cubes, use sun-dried tomatoes in oil. Omit step 5, drain tomatoes and cut into small cubes before placing onto pastry in step 7. Proceed as per recipe.
