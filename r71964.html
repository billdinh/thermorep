Saffron and cardamom poached pears

Ingredients

  • 2 firm brown pears (see Tips)
  • 300 g white wine
  • 100 g water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 100 g sugar
  • 8 cardamom pods, slightly crushed
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • crème fraîche, to serve
  • sugar coated macadamia nuts, crushed, to serve

Preparation

  1. Peel pears then cut off the bottom so both pears sit flat. Place prepared pears into simmering basket and set aside.
  2. Place white wine, water, lemon juice, sugar, cardamom, saffron, cinnamon and salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with pears and cook 15-20 min/Varoma//speed 1. Check to see if pears are cooked. Pears should be softened but not breaking apart. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Place pears into a large bowl. Rinse and dry simmering basket.
  3. Continue to cook syrup 10 min/Varoma//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Strain syrup through simmering basket over the top of pears. Allow pears and syrup to cool completely, gently turning pears regularly in the syrup as they cool. Once cooled, serve pears with a sprinkle of crushed macadamia nuts, a drizzle of the syrup and a dollop of crème fraîche on the side.

Useful Items

vegetable peeler, large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: reduce all ingredients by half and proceed as per recipe.
  • You can substitute firm brown pears for red pears.
  • The flesh of the pears are infused with a sweet and aromatic syrup making this a delicate dessert, perfect with a dollop of créme frâiche.
  • Leftover syrup can be kept in the refrigerator for several days and drizzled over other desserts.
  • Traditionally brown and red pear varieties such as Beurre Bosc, Corella and Red Sensation are used when poaching however; the more commonly available Williams pear can also be used in this recipe.
