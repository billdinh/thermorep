Chicken noodle soup

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 1 whole chicken (1.2-1.4 kg, fitting in Varoma dish)
  • 1 lemon, cut into halves
  • 150 g onion, cut into quarters
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 piece fresh ginger (2 cm), peeled
  • 150 g spring onions, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 3 spring onions)
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander
  • 2 tbsp miso paste (see tip)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin (see tip)

Broth

  • 150 g onion (approx. 1 onion), cut into halves
  • 1 - 2 long red chillies, deseeded if preferred
  • 3 - 4 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and sliced
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp chicken stock paste, homemade
  • 1550 g water
  • 4 eggs (whole)
  • 400 g udon noodles, rinsed and separated (see tip)
  • 120 g carrot (approx. 1-2 carrots), cut into halves lengthways and cut into slices (1 cm)
  • 100 - 150 g canned baby sweetcorn, drained and cut into halves
  • 150 - 200 g broccoli, trimmed and cut into small florets
  • 1 - 2 tsp fine salt, to taste
  • 1 - 2 spring onions, trimmed and cut into thin slices, to garnish
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, torn, to garnish
  • 1 tbsp ready-made fried onions, to garnish (optional) (see tip)
  • 1 seaweed sheet (nori sheet), cut into thin strips, to garnish (optional) (see tip)

Preparation

Chicken

  1. Reverse Varoma lid and place Varoma dish onto it. Place chicken into Varoma dish and fill chicken cavity with lemon and onion.
  2. Place garlic, ginger, spring onions/shallots and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add miso paste, soy sauce and mirin and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Rub garlic ginger marinade all over chicken and set Varoma aside.

Broth

  1. Place onion and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into simmering basket and set aside.
  2. Place mushrooms, oil, stock paste and water into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket with onion and chilli. Place Varoma with chicken into position and steam 45 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add eggs to Varoma dish, arranging them around the chicken, then secure Varoma lid and steam 10-15 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until chicken and eggs are cooked. Set Varoma aside and allow chicken and eggs to cool. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard contents.
  4. Re-insert simmering basket, then weigh udon noodles into it and cook 1 min 30 sec/100°C/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, drain noodles, then divide between 4 serving bowls.
  5. Re-insert simmering basket, then weigh carrot, corn and broccolini into it and cook 5-8 min/100°C//speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, drain vegetables, then divide between the serving bowls placing over noodles. Season broth to taste with salt, then set aside to keep warm.
  6. Shred reserved cooled chicken meat and discard carcass. Divide shredded chicken evenly between the serving bowls.
  7. Peel reserved cooled eggs, cut each egg into halves and add 2 halves to each serving bowl. Pour broth into serving bowls, then garnish with spring onions, coriander leaves, fried onions and nori strips (optional). Serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • Miso paste is a staple of Japanese cuisine, made of fermented soybeans. It is a savory paste commonly used to give depth and flavor to soups and may also be used to make sauces and marinades. It can be found in Asian grocery stores or in the international section of general supermarkets. If you can't find miso paste, use 2 teaspoons chicken or vegetable stock paste instead.
  • Mirin is a type of Japanese rice wine similar to sake, but with a lower alcohol content and higher sugar content. If you can’t find mirin, use 1 tablespoon dry sherry plus ½ teaspoon sugar instead.
  • Udon noodles are a type of thick wheat flour noodles of Japanese cuisine, soft and chewy in texture. Udon can be found in Asian grocery stores or in the international section of general supermarkets. If you're cant find udon noodles, use rice or chinese noodles instead, adapting cooking time in step 7 according to the packet.
  • Fried onions (bawang goreng in Indonesian) are most often added as a garnish to soups, noodle dishes and congee in Southeast Asian cooking. They can be found in Asian grocery stores.
  • Seaweed sheets or nori sheets are paper-like crisp sheets of edible seaweed usually used to wrap sushi.You can find packages of it at any Asian grocery stores and regular supermarkets.
