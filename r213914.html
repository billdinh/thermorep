Chicken moghrabieh

Ingredients

  • 250 g dried chickpeas
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp caraway seeds
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 750 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 70 g unsalted butter
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 300 g pickling onions (small)
  • 250 g Lebanese pearl couscous (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh dried chickpeas into it. Cover with water and soak overnight.
  2. Place 1 tablespoon of the oil, 1 teaspoon of the caraway seeds and 1 teaspoon of the cinnamon into a large bowl and stir to combine.
  3. Place the large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chicken into it. Toss well to coat in spice mixture. Place coated chicken into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray and set aside.
  4. Place water, pepper, butter, 1 teapsoon of the salt, remaining 1 teaspoon caraway seeds, remaining 1 teaspoon cinnamon and remaining 1 tablespoon oil into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/100Â°C/speed 1.
  5. Drain chickpeas using simmering basket. Insert simmering basket with chickpeas into mixing bowl and weigh pickling onions into it. Place Varoma with chicken into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until chicken is cooked through. Transfer chicken into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  6. Weigh pearl couscous into Varoma dish, add remaining 1 teaspoon salt and stir to combine with spatula. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1.5. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer chickpeas and onions into thermal serving bowl with chicken and cover to keep warm.
  7. Transfer couscous from Varoma into remaining liquid in mixing bowl and set aside for 5 minutes. Transfer couscous and liquid into thermal serving bowl with chicken and chickpeas, stir well to combine, cover and set aside for 5 minutes to allow flavours to absorb before serving.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Moghrabieh is a pearl couscous; it is larger than traditional couscous and made with semolina flour rolled into pearl-sized balls. You can find pearl couscous in some major supermarkets and specialty food stores.
