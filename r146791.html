Lemon and White Chocolate Cream Tart

Ingredients

  • 250 g plain flour
  • 125 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 lemon, finely grated zest only
  • 1 large egg
  • 165 g caster sugar
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 80 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 1 gelatine leaf
  • 125 g lemon juice
  • 3 medium eggs
  • 10 g unsalted butter
  • 100 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets

Preparation

  1. Place flour, butter and lemon zest in mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6 until mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs.
  2. Add egg and sugar then mix 15 sec/speed 4 until mixture comes together. Tip out, wrap in cling film then shape into a thin round. Refrigerate for 1 hour. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Roll out pastry on a lightly floured surface and use to line 8 tart tins (Ø 10 cm 3 cm deep). Line pastry with baking paper then fill with baking beans and blind bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Remove beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Leave in tins to cool on a wire rack.
  4. When cool, place dark chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3 min/40°C/speed . Brush inside of cases with melted chocolate then set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  5. Place gelatine leaf in a small bowl of cold water to soak for 5 minutes.
  6. Place lemon juice, lemon zest, sugar and eggs in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/85°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add butter, white chocolate and squeezed-out gelatine then blend 5 sec/speed 8. Pour into a shallow dish, cover with cling film and refrigerate until solid. Once set, pipe or spoon into prebaked cases and decorate as desired. Serve at room temperature.

Useful Items

rolling pin, wire rack, cling film, piping bag and plain nozzle, loose-based tartlet tins (Ø 10 cm), pastry brush, baking paper, baking beans, shallow bowl or dish, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cream can either be piped in small bulbs and decorated with chocolate shavings, or leveled off, dusted with caster sugar and caramelized with a blow torch.
