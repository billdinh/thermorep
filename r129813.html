Duck confit with lentil & orange salad

Ingredients

Duck confit

  • 4 duck marylands (approx. 1 kg)
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 500 g duck fat
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2000 g water

Lentil salad

  • 4 raw baby beetroots, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • 1 pinch salt, plus extra to season
  • 200 g puy lentils
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 brown onion
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1000 g water
  • 30 g Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • ½ red onion, cut into small cubes (5 mm)
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into small cubes (5 mm)
  • 2 oranges, segmented and cut into pieces (5 mm)

Orange vinaigrette

  • 2 tsp honey
  • 40 g freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 100 g grapeseed oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 2 pinches freshly ground black pepper

Assembly

  • sea salt, to season
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season
  • 30 g frisee lettuce, to serve

Preparation

Duck confit

  1. Place duck, skin side down, onto a large plate. Season with pepper and 1 tsp of the salt. Place garlic, bay leaves and thyme sprigs on top of 2 of the marylaynds. Place remaining 2 marylands on top, flesh to flesh. Sprinkle with remaining 2 tsp salt, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 12 hours or overnight.
  2. Place duck fat into mixing bowl and melt 4-6 min/90°C/speed 3, until fat is completely melted. Set aside in mixing bowl.
  3. Remove garlic, bay leaves and thyme from duck and set aside. Rinse duck under cool water and pat dry with paper towel.
  4. Place peppercorns, reserved garlic, bay leaves and thyme into a ceramic or glass baking dish (18 x 6 cm). Place duck skin side down into dish.
  5. Pour melted fat over duck, ensuring they are completely covered. Cover with a sheet of baking paper and aluminium foil.
  6. Place 1000 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, lay 2 wooden skewers (18 - 19 cm in length) across the base of Varoma dish and carefully place baking dish on top, ensuring steam can circulate freely. Secure Varoma lid and steam 50 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma.
  7. Add remaining 1000 g of the water, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 50 min/Varoma/speed 1. Check if meat pulls away from the bone; if not, steam for a further 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove duck from fat and set aside on a plate to drain. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lentil salad

  1. Place beetroot, 2 of the garlic cloves, vinegar, oil, thyme, pepper and 1 pinch of the salt onto a sheet of baking paper (approx. 30 x 50 cm) and wrap to form a sealed parcel. Place into Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh lentils into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse lentils under cool water.
  3. Press cloves into onion. Place onion, bay leaf and remaining garlic into simmering basket with lentils.
  4. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket. Place Varoma with parcel into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 40-43 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until lentils and beetroot are just tender. Remove Varoma with beetroot and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard onion, herbs and garlic. Transfer lentils into a large bowl and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add red onion, celery and orange slices to lentils and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Orange vinaigrette

  1. Place all vinaigrette ingredients, except frisee, into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Add half of the vinaigrette to lentil salad and toss to combine. Adjust seasoning to taste. Transfer remaining vinagrette into a jug and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Place a non-stick frying pan over high heat. Season duck with salt and pepper and place into frying pan, skin side down. Fry for approx. 4 minutes each side, until skin is crispy. Remove from pan and set aside. Reduce heat to low and add reserved beetroot with any cooking liquid in the parcel and fry until glazed (approx. 3 minutes). Serve duck with beetroot, lentil salad, frisee and extra vinaigrette.

Useful Items

large plate, plastic wrap, paper towel, baking paper, aluminium foil, wooden skewers, plate, large bowl, jug, non-stick frying pan, serving plates, ceramic or glass baking dish (18 x 6 cm), refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Confit is a term used to cook and preserve duck or goose in its own fat.
  • Covering the confit duck with a layer of the strained duck fat, allows duck to sit in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
  • Strained duck fat can be stored in a sealable storage container in the refrigerator and used for roasting potatoes, cooking green beans and pan frying veal.
  • To make your own Chicken stock paste, please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook.
