Fish Tacos

Ingredients

  • 2 limes, zest only
  • 1 ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 15 g fresh parsley, leaves and tender stems
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 50 g lime juice
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 680 g white fish fillets (e.g. tilapia, halibut, cod)
  • 170 g red cabbage, cut in pieces
  • 30 g carrot, cut in half
  • 1 fresh jalapeño chilli, large, stem and seeds removed
  • 60 g mayonnaise
  • 60 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 30 g cider vinegar
  • 510 g water
  • 12 maize tortilla

Preparation

  1. Place lime zest, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add parsley, garlic, oil, lime juice, cumin and paprika then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 6. Transfer marinade to a large freezer bag and add fish fillets, making sure fish is fully coated. Seal bag and set aside to marinate (see tip). Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place cabbage, carrot and jalapeño into mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  4. Place mayonnaise, yoghurt, honey, vinegar, ¼ tsp salt and ¼ tsp pepper into mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  5. Evenly distribute marinated fish between Varoma dish and tray. Place water in mixing bowl, cover Varoma with Varoma lid, place Varoma into position and cook 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 1 until fish is cooked to your liking.
  6. Meanwhile, heat tortillas and keep warm. Divide fish among tortillas and top generously with shredded cabbage and a drizzle of honey sauce. Serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • The fish can marinate for about 20 minutes at room temperature. Any longer, and it would be better marinated in a fridge. Fish and shellfish should marinate for 30-60 minutes; any longer and the flesh may start to “cook” and go mushy.
  • Serve with rice, beans and plenty of lime wedges.
  • The sauce and shredded cabbage can be made ahead and stored in the fridge until needed. Store sauce in an airtight jar. For the cabbage, place in a bowl and cover with a wet paper towel to prevent from drying out.
