Lamb Cooked Two Ways with Carrot and Ginger Purée and Red Wine Jus

Ingredients

Slow Cooked Lamb Shoulder

  • 25 g vegetable oil
  • 750 g lamb shoulder meat, boneless, diced
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp cornflour
  • 1 meat stock cube (for 0.5 l) (lamb)
  • 500 g water
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 garlic clove

Red Wine Jus

  • 1 banana shallot, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 small handful mixed fresh herbs, leaves only (approx. 5 g)
  • 50 g salted butter
  • 100 g port
  • 150 g red wine
  • 20 g balsamic vinegar
  • 35 g dark brown sugar (optional)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste

Carrot and Ginger Purée

  • 30 g olive oil
  • 500 g carrots, cut in 4 pieces
  • 15 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in slices (2 mm)
  • 100 g water
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g butter, cut in pieces
  • ground black pepper, for seasoning

Roasted Lamb Loin

  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 6 lamb loin (100-150 g each)
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt, for seasoning
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, for seasoning
  • 10 g butter, for basting
  • fresh spinach, wilted, for serving (optional)

Preparation

Slow Cooked Lamb Shoulder

  1. Preheat oven to 120°C. Place a casserole dish on a high heat and add oil. Season diced lamb and add to casserole dish. Fry until browned then add cornflour, lamb stock cube and water and stir well. Add rosemary and garlic then cover and cook in oven for 4 hours (120°C).
  2. Remove lamb using a slotted spoon and set aside, reserving cooking juices separately and skimming any fat from them.

Red Wine Jus

  1. Place shallot, garlic and herbs in mixing bowl then chop 2 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and, without measuring cup, cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add port, red wine, balsamic vinegar, sugar and 100 g reserved cooking juices then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 3 until desired consistency. Pass through a fine sieve into a saucepan, add salt and pepper to taste then keep warm over a low heat.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  5. Place cooked lamb shoulder in mixing bowl and shred 10 sec//speed 4. Add 2-3 Tbsp of jus to moisten then divide between 6 chef rings (Ø 6 cm) on a baking tray, pressing down with the back of a spoon. Cover then chill until set. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make purée.

Carrot and Ginger Purée

  1. Place olive oil in mixing bowl and heat 1 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Add carrots, ginger, 100 g water and salt then cook 10 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add butter and blend 1 min/speed 10. Season to taste and leave in mixing bowl to keep warm.

Roasted Lamb Loin

  1. Heat an oven-proof frying pan over a high heat then add 1 Tbsp vegetable oil. Season loins with salt and pepper then cook until caramelised and brown all over. Add 10 g butter and baste loins.
  2. Transfer to oven and roast for 6-8 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, place lamb shoulder patties, still in the rings, in oven at the same time and heat for 10 minutes (180°C).
  3. Rest lamb loins for 5 minutes before carving. Smear some carrot and ginger purée across each plate, top with slices of lamb loin then place a warmed lamb patty (remove rings) to one side. Drizzle lamb loin with a little red wine jus. Serve with remaining jus, and wilted spinach if desired.

Useful Items

casserole dish, slotted spoon, fine sieve, saucepan, cling film, baking tray, 4 chef rings (Ø 6 cm), oven-proof frying pan, hob, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For a more substantial meal, serve with parmentier potatoes.
