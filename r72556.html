Poached egg pots with smoked salmon and avocado salsa

Ingredients

Smoked salmon and avocado salsa

  • 1 tbsp spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 80 g smoked salmon
  • 10 fresh baby spinach leaves (optional) (optional)
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 20 g lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon), to taste
  • ½ avocado, flesh only, cut into cubes (1 cm)

Poached egg pots

  • 700 g water
  • 150 g fresh baby spinach leaves, stalks removed
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs

Preparation

Smoked salmon and avocado salsa

  1. Place spring onion/shallot, smoked salmon and spinach (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 3, increasing speed gradually to speed 5. Transfer salsa into a bowl and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  2. Add lemon juice and avocado and gently combine with spatula. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside until ready to use.

Poached egg pots

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Place Varoma dish into position, then weigh spinach into it. Season spinach to taste with salt, secure Varoma lid and steam 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Transfer wilted spinach into simmering basket to drain and squeeze out any excess water by pressing down on spinach with spatula. Set Varoma aside and retain water in mixing bowl.
  4. Divide wilted spinach between 2 ramekins (8 cm). Divide cream and Parmesan cheese between ramekins. Crack 1 egg into each ramekin, then place ramekins into Varoma dish.
  5. Place Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until eggs are cooked to your liking.
  6. Carefully remove ramekins from Varoma and transfer to serving plates. Serve warm with reserved smoked salmon and avocado salsa.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, 2 ramekins (8 cm)

Hints & Tricks

  • For a complete meal, serve with toast or flat bread.
  • Adjust the cooking time in Varoma depending on how you like your eggs poached.
