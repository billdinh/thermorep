Tomatoes Stuffed with Mushrooms and Nuts

Ingredients

  • 50 g mixed nuts (e.g. almonds, walnuts)
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 1300 g ripe tomatoes (Ø 5-6 cm)
  • 100 g fresh mushrooms (e.g. button, oyster)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 10 - 12 fresh mint leaves
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • 75 g white long grain rice
  • 20 g freshly squeezed lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)

Preparation

  1. Place nuts in mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 20 g oil and onions in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5 then, without measuring cup, sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, cut a slice (approx. 2 cm thick) off the bottom (opposite the stem) of each tomato, making sure tomatoes fit under Varoma lid when on Varoma tray. Reserve tomato slices.
  3. Add 125 g reserved tomato slices, mushrooms, ½ tsp salt, pepper, 6-8 mint leaves and parsley to mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5, then cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, scoop out tomatoes with a melon baller or spoon, reserving seeds and pulp and setting them aside with remaining reserved tomato slices. Arrange hollowed tomatoes in Varoma dish and tray with openings face up.
  4. Add rice, reserved ground nuts and lemon juice to mixing bowl then mix 5 sec//speed 3. Fill tomatoes with rice mixture.
  5. Place reserved tomato pulp and any remaining slices (approx. 500 g), 10 g oil, 4 mint leaves and ½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then blend 5 sec/speed 5. Place Varoma into position and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside. Transfer sauce to a serving dish, arrange stuffed tomatoes on sauce and garnish with parsley. Serve warm or cold.

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe from Cyprus is bursting with the fresh flavours of tomato, lemon and mint. The rice mixed with nuts and mushrooms has a crunchy and almost meaty texture. Serve as a satisfying main course or as an impressive side dish.
  • Choose tomatoes that are large enough to fill, yet small enough to fit under the lid on the Varoma tray (Ø 5-6 cm). The number of tomatoes may vary between eight and twelve depending on their size and shape.
  • The same mixture can be used to stuff vine leaves. Vine leaves can be bought preserved or fresh. Soak leaves for 1 minute in boiling water, then drain and pat dry before filling. To fill vine leaves, you will need only 600-650 g tomatoes. Use 125 g chopped tomatoes instead of tomato slices in step 3, and remaining chopped tomatoes in step 5 instead of reserved tomato pulp and slices. Fill vine leaves in step 4 and proceed with the recipe.
    - Use the same mixture to stuff kale leaves, courgettes, bell peppers, onions, or other vegetables.
