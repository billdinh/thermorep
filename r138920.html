Bagels with Bacon Jam and Egg

Ingredients

Bagels

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 920 g water, plus 1 tbsp extra for egg wash
  • 250 g whole milk
  • 1 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 3 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 520 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 Tbsp white sesame seeds, for sprinkling (optional)

Bacon Jam

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 200 g red onion, cut into eighths
  • 500 g smoked bacon, cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 150 g brewed coffee
  • 60 g apple cider vinegar
  • 60 g orange juice
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp orange peel, zest only, finely grated
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • 1 star anise
  • 2 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 40 g whisky, bourbon

Eggs

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 500 g water
  • 4 eggs (depending on number of servings)

Preparation

Bagels

  1. Grease a bowl with olive oil and set aside. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place 120 g water, milk, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/50°C/speed 2.
  3. Add flour and salt then knead 4 min/.
  4. Transfer dough into greased bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2 hours).
  5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface. With lightly oiled hands, divide dough into 12 equal-sized portions, then shape each portion into a ball. Press your thumb through middle of each ball to form a hole and create a bagel shape. Cover bagels with a kitchen towel and leave to prove again in a warm place for 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 220°C. Place remaining 800 g water in mixing bowl and heat 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  7. Line Varoma tray with a piece of wet and well-wrung baking paper, then place 4 bagels onto Varoma tray. Place Varoma into position. Insert Varoma tray, secure Varoma lid and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer steamed bagels to prepared baking tray and repeat twice more with remaining bagels.
  8. Place egg yolk and 1 tbsp water into a small bowl and lightly beat to create an egg wash. Brush bagels with egg wash, then sprinkle with sesame or poppy seeds (optional) and bake for 8-10 minutes (220°C) or until golden.

Bacon Jam

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onion and bacon then sauté 10 min/Varoma//speed 1, without measuring cup.
  3. Add all remaining jam ingredients, except bourbon, and cook 40 min/100°C//speed 1, with simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add bourbon and cook for a further 80 min/100°C//speed 1, with simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a sealable container and allow to cool before placing into refrigerator until ready to use (jam will thicken as it cools).

Eggs

  1. Grease 4 egg rings with oil and set aside. Line Varoma tray with a piece of wet and well-wrung baking paper, ensuring paper goes all the way to the edges. Place water in mixing bowl, then place Varoma into position and insert Varoma tray.
  2. Place egg rings onto baking paper and crack 1 egg into each ring. Secure Varoma lid and steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until eggs are cooked to your liking.
  3. To serve, slice bagels in half horizontally, spread with bacon jam and top with an egg.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), tea towel, silicone pastry mat, pastry brush, sealable container, 4 egg rings, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe can serve up to 12 people at once. Simply repeat step 14 and cook as many eggs as you require. Serve any leftover bagels within 2 days.
  • Bacon jam and bagels can easily be prepared ahead of time for a quick and easy weekend breakfast.
  • Bacon jam is a perfect condiment on toasted cheese sandwiches, burgers, cheese platters or even pancakes.

Bacon jam is a 'magical' cross between tomato sauce and fruit chutney... but with bacon! Its rich flavour is complemented by hints of coffee, citrus, brown sugar and bourbon.
