Oat and Cashew Pasties

Ingredients

Filling

  • 150 g rolled oats
  • water, for soaking
  • 100 g cashew nuts
  • 15 g fresh parsley
  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 250 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 50 g red peppers, cut in pieces
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Dough

  • 800 g water
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 600 g potatoes, red skin, peeled, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 240 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 600 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 egg yolks, beaten, for brushing

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in oats. Remove then cover with water and set aside.
  2. Place cashews in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl.
  3. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Transfer to bowl with cashews and set aside.
  4. Place onions, tomatoes, peppers, oil, garlic and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, drain oats and discard water.
  5. Add well-drained oats and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add eggs and stir in with spatula then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  7. Add reserved cashews and parsley, remaining 1 tsp salt and oregano then mix together with spatula. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make dough.

Dough

  1. Place water and salt in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in potatoes then cook 25 min/100°C/speed 3. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Discard steaming water.
  2. Place cooked potatoes and butter in mixing bowl then mash 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour and whisk 1 min/speed 6. Tip out, wrap in cling film and set aside for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C and line 3 baking trays with baking paper.
  4. On a floured surface, roll out dough (see tip). Using a pastry cutter (approx. Ø 9 cm) cut out 80 circles, re-rolling any trimmings (see tip). Place 1 tsp cooled filling in one half of each circle, then fold over into a half-moon and seal by pinching pastry edges together.
  5. Place pasties on prepared trays, brush with beaten egg yolks and bake for 25 minutes (180°C). Serve hot or cold.

Useful Items

medium mesh sieve, cling film, baking trays and baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, round pastry cutter (Ø 9 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The cashew nuts can be replaced with walnuts, pine nuts or toasted hazelnuts.
  • Vary the filling as desired.
  • When cutting dough circles, divide dough into 8 pieces (keeping remaining dough covered under cling film) and roll out each piece to 1-2 mm thick. Cut 10 circles from each dough piece, re-rolling as necessary.
  • If oven space is limited or only 1-2 baking trays are available, cook in batches. Prepare pasties, place on baking paper, then slide onto a baking tray when one becomes available. Remove hot pasties to a wire rack once cooked.
