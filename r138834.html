Giant doughnuts

Ingredients

Doughnuts

  • oil spray, for greasing
  • 280 g raw sugar
  • 240 g grapeseed oil
  • 480 g plain flour
  • 30 g baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 480 g full cream milk
  • 4 eggs

Buttercream icing

  • 250 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 2 tsp boiling water
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract

Glacé icing

  • 400 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 55 - 60 g boiling water
  • natural food colouring of choice
  • lollies of choice, for decorating

Preparation

Doughnuts

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Liberally spray a doughnut tin (19 cm - see Tips) with oil and set aside.
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add oil, flour, baking powder, salt, nutmeg, milk and eggs and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 8 sec/speed 4.
  5. Transfer one-quarter (approx. 400 g) of the batter into prepared doughnut tin. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool. Clean and dry doughnut tin.
  6. Spray doughnut tin with oil again. Transfer another one-quarter (approx. 400 g) of the batter into prepared doughnut tin. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool. Repeat with remaining batter to make a total of 4 cakes, cleaning and re-greasing tin after each cake.
  7. When all cakes are cool, trim as needed to create a level surface. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Buttercream icing

  1. Place icing sugar and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Glacé icing

  1. Place icing sugar and boiling water into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Icing should be thick enough to drizzle, but still hold its shape (see Tips). Divide icing evenly between 2 bowls, add food colouring of choice and stir well to combine (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Place 1 doughnut cake, flat side up, onto a plate and spread half of the reserved buttercream icing onto top of cake. Place another cake, flat side down, onto icing to form a whole doughnut. Place another doughnut cake, flat side up, onto a plate and spread with remaining buttercream icing. Top with remaining cake, flat side down, onto icing.
  2. Pour half of the reserved glacé icing over each doughnut cake, allowing icing to drizzle down sides of cake. Decorate immediately with lollies as desired. Transfer into refrigerator for 30 minutes to set.
  3. Carefully arrange cakes onto a cake board or large plate just before serving.

Useful Items

large plate, 2 bowls, bowl, wooden skewer, doughnut tin (19 cm), wire rack, cake board or serving plate, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You need a soft, sifted icing sugar for this recipe.
  • You can find doughnut tins (19 cm) at cake decorating stores, kitchenware stores or online.
  • If you are not using Buttercream icing immediately, place into refrigerator until needed. To use, bring back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread.
  • If Glacé icing is too thick for decorating, carefully add a few drops of water and stir until desired consistancy is achieved.
  • When working with Glacé icing, keep covered to prevent it from drying out.
