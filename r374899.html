Almond shortbread biscuits

Ingredients

Shortbread

  • 100 g white sugar
  • ½ vanilla bean pod
  • 200 g unsalted butter, chilled, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1 egg

Almond mixture

  • 70 g white sugar
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 40 g apricot jam
  • 200 g flaked almonds

Preparation

Shortbread

  1. Split vanilla bean pod lengthways and scrape out seeds. Reserve pod and set seeds aside separately to use later. Place sugar and scraped vanilla bean pod into mixing bowl and mill 40 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add butter, flour, salt and egg and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Divide dough in half and roll each portion into a log (30 cm long). Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until firm enough to slice (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Almond mixture

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar, butter, jam and reserved vanilla bean seeds into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/60°C/speed 2.
  3. Add almonds and combine 8 sec//speed 2.
  4. Remove dough logs from refrigerator. Cut dough logs into slices (approx. 1 cm thickness). Place onto prepared baking trays and top each slice with 1 heaped teaspoon of the almond mixture. Bake (1 tray at a time) for 16-18 minutes (180°C), until golden brown. Transfer onto wire racks to cool.
  5. Place into a sealable container or wrap up to give as a gift (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, wire rack, silicone bread mat, sealable container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe works best with chilled butter, as the cold butter makes the dough pliable (but not too soft or sticky).
  • These biscuits can be stored in a sealable container for up to 2 weeks.
