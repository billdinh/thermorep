Mango truffles, strawberry spheres and white chocolate crumble

Ingredients

Mango truffles

  • 250 g mango flesh
  • 25 g glucose syrup
  • 80 g white sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp agar agar powder (see Tips)
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 120 g cocoa butter
  • ½ vanilla bean, seeds scraped

Sour meringue

  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1 tsp citric acid

Strawberry spheres

  • 350 g frozen strawberries (see Tips)
  • 2000 g water, plus extra cold water
  • ½ tsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 tsp calcium lactate gluconate (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp xanthan gum
  • 2 tsp sodium alginate (see Tips)

White chocolate powder

  • 120 g white chocolate
  • 30 g full cream milk
  • 30 g milk powder
  • 30 g maltodextrin powder (see Tips)

Assembly

  • 16 fresh basil leaves
  • 8 strawberries, cut into slices

Preparation

Mango truffles

  1. Place mango into mixing bowl and purée 15 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add glucose syrup, sugar and agar agar, then mix 15 sec/speed 10.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into a shallow bowl and leave to set at room temperature (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place mango mixture into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 10.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 15 sec/speed 3, until smooth.
  6. Add cream and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer mixture into 2 silicone ice cube trays or small silicone moulds (approx. 16 truffles) and place into freezer for a minimum of 4 hours or until firm (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Place cocoa butter into mixing bowl and melt 5 min/50°C/speed 2.
  8. Add vanilla seeds and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool slightly.
  9. Dip mango ice cubes into cocoa butter mixture. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sour meringue

  1. Preheat oven to 100°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sugar into it. Set bowl aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whisk 1 min/speed 4, or until firm. Mix for a further 1 min/speed 4, slowly adding sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid until combined.  Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Transfer mixture onto prepared baking tray and spread out to a thin layer. Dry in oven for 2 hours (100°C). Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  5. Break dried meringue into pieces. Place dried meringue and citric acid into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to serve.

Strawberry spheres

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid (ensure bowl fits comfortably into Varoma dish) and weigh frozen strawberries into a it. Place 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl and place bowl of strawberries into Varoma. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Carefully remove bowl with strawberries. Transfer strawberries and any liquid into a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and strain into a separate bowl (see Tips). This strawberry 'essence' will be used to make spheres. Place essence into refrigerator to cool for approx. 30 minutes (see Tips). Discard water and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Once cooled, place strawberry essence into mixing bowl. Add vinegar, lemon juice, salt and calcium lactate gluconate and blend 20 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add xanthan gum and mix 6 sec/speed 6. Transfer mixture into a shallow container and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes to allow large air bubbles to disperse (this may take longer than 30 minutes; small bubbles will remain). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl and mix 10 min/speed 3 , without measuring cup, adding sodium alginate through hole in mixing bowl lid. Sodium alginate will dissolve and thicken to a clear, thin jelly.
  6. If specks are still visible, scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer mixture (spherification bath) into a container and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 1 hour to allow air bubbles to disperse. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Place strawberry mixture, spherification bath, a bowl of cold water, 2 small spoons, a slotted spoon and paper towels onto kitchen bench.
  8. Dip spoon into strawberry mixture, then wipe base of the spoon clean with paper towel. Rest spoon on surface of spherification bath then smoothly flip it over, sliding strawberry mixture into bath. Using the other spoon, gently stir bath without touching the strawberry sphere, ensuring spheres are submerged but do not rest on the base of the bath until set. Leave for 2 minutes. Carefully remove sphere with a slotted spoon and gently place into bowl of cold water.
  9. Repeat with remaining strawberry mixture, adjusting timing as necessary to ensure spheres do not break when transferred.
  10. Store spheres in water until ready to serve (try to allow plenty of room between them so they don't stick together). Serve within 30 minutes.

White chocolate powder

  1. Place white chocolate, milk and milk powder into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until mixture is golden and caramelised. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Break white chocolate mixture into pieces, then place into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  6. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add maltodextrin, then mix 5 sec/speed 7, until a coarse powder has formed.

Assembly

  1. Place small mounds of white chocolate powder onto 8 dessert plates. Arrange 2 mango truffles onto white chocolate powder. Place a small mound of sour meringue onto each plate. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove a strawberry sphere from water and gently place onto meringue. Garnish with basil leaves and fresh strawberries to serve.

Useful Items

shallow freezable container, 2 silicone ice cube trays, bowl, sealable container, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, ceramic or glass bowl, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, 2 spoons, slotted spoon, paper towel, 8 serving plates, freezer, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, prepare the Sour meringue while the Mango truffles are freezing.
  • Frozen strawberries are used because the cell walls of the fruit burst during freezing and release more juice once steamed. Use the leftover strawberry pulp in smoothies, muffins or as muesli topping.
  • Calcium lactate gluconate is a crystalline salt, available from specialty cooking stores.
  • Sodium alginate is a gum extracted from brown seaweed, available from specialty cooking stores. At completion of recipe, you need to dispose in bin, not down the sink as it may block pipes.
  • Maltodextrin is a starch extracted from tapioca, available from specialty cooking stores.
