Chicken korma

Ingredients

  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 6 cardamom pods (green)
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 cinnamon quill, broken into halves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 40 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 3 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • ¾ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 140 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 200 g water
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1000 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into cubes (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • fried shallots, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon quill and 1 tablespoon of the oil into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1, until fragrant. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add garlic paste, ginger paste and onion and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, salt, chilli powder and remaining 1 tablespoon oil and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2.
  5. Add cream, water and tomato paste and blend 5 sec/speed 6.
  6. Add reserved spiced oil mixture and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2, then blend 1 min/speed 1 -10, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add chicken, stir well with aid of spatula, then cook 15-17 min/100°C//speed 1, until chicken is cooked through.
  8. Add garam masala and stir well with aid of spatula. Transfer into a serving bowl and garnish with fried shallots before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the almonds with raw unsalted cashews, if preferred. You can also replace the cream with natural yoghurt or coconut milk, if preferred.
  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Fried shallots can be found in the international aisle of supermarkets and at Asian and Indian grocers.
  • Serve with natural yoghurt, if desired
