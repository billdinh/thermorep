Beetroot salad with raita dressing

Ingredients

  • 2 raw beetroots, peeled and cut into thin slices (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes, plus extra 2 pinches for dressing
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 500 g boiling water
  • 6 cm piece fresh ginger
  • 150 g sweet potato
  • sunflower oil (approx. 250 g), for shallow frying
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, peeled, deseeded and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 200 g Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • alfalfa sprouts, to serve (optional)
  • black sesame seeds, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place beetroot into Varoma dish and toss with 1 teaspoon of the chilli flakes, vinegar, pepper and ½ teaspoon of the salt.
  2. Place boiling water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma, with beetroot, into positon, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 4. Meanwhile, peel ginger and cut into matchsticks, then peel sweet potato and slice into ribbons.
  3. Remove Varoma and Varoma lid and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Line a plate with paper towel.
  4. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Fry ginger and then sweet potato in batches (approx. 1-2 minutes), until just crisp (see Tips). Transfer onto prepared plate to drain.
  5. Place garlic, cucumber and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add yoghurt, coriander, cumin, remaining 2 pinches chilli flakes and remaining ½ teaspoon salt and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  7. Arrange beetroot slices in a single layer onto a serving platter. Top with fried sweet potato and ginger. Garnish with alfafa sprouts (optional) and black sesame seeds (optional). Serve with dressing on the side.

Useful Items

plate, paper towel, serving platter, large frying pan, bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • We used a mandoline to slice our beetroot.
  • Take care when frying the ginger and sweet potato as they can burn easily.
