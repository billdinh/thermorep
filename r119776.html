Prawns, wasabi panna cotta and yuzu sauce

Ingredients

Panna cotta

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 2 gold grade gelatine leaves
  • cold water, for soaking
  • 140 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 50 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp wasabi paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp yuzu juice (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp sea salt

Prawns

  • 6 cooked prawns (large) peeled and deveined, with tail intact
  • 10 sprigs fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 10 sprigs fresh chervil, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • sea salt, to season
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season, plus extra to serve

Yuzu sauce

  • 60 g yuzu juice (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp xanthan gum
  • 5 red grapes, cut into halves, to serve

Preparation

Panna cotta

  1. Grease 2 small glass tumblers or ramekins with butter and set aside.
  2. Place gelatine leaves into a small bowl and cover with cold water. Set aside to soak.
  3. Place cream, milk, wasabi and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/90°C/speed 3.
  4. Squeeze excess water from gelatine. Add gelatine into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 2, until dissolved.
  5. Add yuzu and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 3.
  6. Transfer mixture into prepared tumblers and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 3 hours (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Prawns

  1. Place prawns, three-quarters of the chives and chervil (optional), oil, salt and pepper into a large bowl and toss to combine. Set aside.

Yuzu sauce

  1. Place yuzu and xanthan gum into mixing bowl, ensuring xanthan gum is added to liquid and not onto mixing bowl blades. Mix 1 min/speed 3. If xanthan gum flecks remain, mix for a further 4 sec/speed 5 to dissolve.
  2. Turn out panna cottas onto small serving plates and arrange prawns around them. Drizzle with a little yuzu sauce and sprinkle with extra pepper, remaining herbs and grapes. Serve with extra yuzu sauce on the side.

Useful Items

2 ramekins, 2 small glass tumblers, small bowl , large bowl, 2 plates, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Add extra wasabi to panna cotta, if desired.
  • If buying raw prawns, before step 1, place 500 g water into mixing bowl, place prawns into Varoma and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until cooked to your liking.
  • Yuzu is a Japanese citrus and the juice is sold at Asian grocers. If unavailable, use lime juice as a substitute.
  • You can store prepared panna cottas in refrigerator (step 6) up to 3 days in advance.
