Gulab jamuns

Ingredients

Syrup

  • 450 g water
  • 400 g white sugar
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 3 cardamom pods (green), crushed
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Gulab jamuns

  • 80 g semolina
  • 2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 140 g full cream milk
  • 140 g milk powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • vegetable oil, for frying

Preparation

Syrup

  1. Place water and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/Varoma/speed 1 , without measuring cup, until boiling.
  2. Add saffron and cardamom pods and boil 12 min/Varoma/speed 1 , without measuring cup.
  3. Add lemon juice and stir to combine. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or another large dish and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Gulab jamuns

  1. Place semolina, cardamom, saffron and milk into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Set aside in mixing bowl for 15 minutes to allow the semolina to absorb.
  2. Add milk powder and baking powder to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Set aside to rest for 1 hour (dough should be soft and sticky).
  3. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside. Using wet hands, divide dough into 12 balls (approx. 25 g each).
  4. Place a large saucepan over high heat and add oil (8 cm deep). Heat oil to 160°C (see Tips). Working in batches, carefully lower dough into oil and fry for 2-3 minutes, until dark golden. Using a slotted spoon, carefully transfer onto prepared plate. Allow to cool for 10 minutes, then transfer into dish with warm syrup. Leave fried doughs to soak in syrup for approx. 30 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, kitchen towel, 1 plate, paper towel, large saucepan, slotted spoon, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To check your oil is at 160°C, use a kitchen thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into oil; the bread will turn golden brown in 25-30 seconds.
