Prawn and saffron risotto

Ingredients

  • 560 g water, lukewarm
  • ½ tsp saffron threads
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 180 g Arborio rice
  • 80 g dry sherry
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste, plus extra to serve
  • 200 g raw prawns, shelled and cleaned
  • ½ lemon, zest finely grated, no white pith
  • 2 - 3 tsp butter (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl on top of mixing bowl lid and weigh 400 g of the water into it. Add saffron threads to the bowl with the water and set aside to soak.
  2. Place spring onions/shallots, garlic and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add Arborio rice and dry sherry and cook 1 min/100°C//speed 1, without measuring cup.
  5. Add vegetable stock paste, remaining 160 g water, reserved saffron threads with soak water, salt and pepper and cook 13-14 min/100°C//speed 1, until cooked to desired consistency.
  6. Add prawns and lemon zest and combine 2 min//speed 1.
  7. Transfer risotto into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Add butter (optional) to risotto in thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, then stir to combine with spatula. Allow the risotto to rest for 5 minutes then serve with extra black pepper to taste.

Useful Items

bowl, ceramic or glass bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: reduce all ingredients by half and proceed as per recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Saffron is a common ingredient in rice dishes as you only need a touch of it to impart both beautiful colour and flavour.
  • Saffron is officially the most expensive spice in the world thanks to the very delicate and time consuming method required to harvest the fine threads. Thankfully, a little goes a long way.
  • Use any leftover prawns in the Prawn stir-fry.
  • Leftover risotto can be used to make Arancini balls. Search "Arancini" on the Recipe Community home page (recipecommunity.com.au).
