Mixed tomato and labne salad

Ingredients

Labne

  • 500 g natural yoghurt

Dressing

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 tsp dried mixed herbs
  • 50 g honey
  • 30 g wholegrain mustard
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 60 g apple cider vinegar
  • 120 g olive oil

Salad

  • 50 g shelled and unsalted pistachios
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tsp sunflower seeds
  • 1 tsp sea salt flakes, plus extra to taste
  • 500 g Roma tomatoes, cut into thick slices
  • 500 g heirloom tomatoes, cut into slices
  • 60 g pitted green Sicilian olives, cut into slices
  • ¼ red onion, cut into thin slices
  • 120 g fresh rocket
  • 30 g snow pea sprouts
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

Labne

  1. Line the simmering basket with a nut milk bag or two layers of muslin cloth and set over a large jug or bowl. Place the large jug or bowl with lined simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh yoghurt into simmering basket.
  2. Place simmering basket and large jug or bowl into refrigerator and allow yoghurt to drain for 24 hours to create labne. The labne will be soft, yet dense, when ready.
  3. Form labne into 20 bite-sized balls (1 teaspoon per ball) and transfer onto a plate. Cover with plastic wrap and place labne balls into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Dressing

  1. Place all dressing ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat blending 20 sec/speed 8. Transfer dressing into a serving jug and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salad

  1. Place pistachios into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add sesame seeds, sunflower seeds and salt and dry roast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool.
  3. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh Roma tomatoes, heirloom or cherry tomatoes and olives into it. Add the red onion and ½ of the reserved dressing and combine well with spatula.
  4. Arrange rocket or spinach, snow pea sprouts and basil onto a large serving platter, then arrange tomato and onion mixture on top.
  5. Drizzle salad with remaining dressing, then top with reserved labne balls and reserved pistachio and seed mixture. Season to taste with salt and pepper then serve.

Useful Items

large jug, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, plastic wrap, serving jug, serving platter, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The labne balls and dressing can easily be made well in advance and stored in the refrigerator. Dress and assemble salad, as per recipe, just before serving.
  • Feel free to use any combination of tomatoes you have available. Try using Mixed medley, golden grape tomatoes or Kumato tomatoes to add more colour and flavour variety.
