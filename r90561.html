Pork and chicken terrine

Ingredients

  • 300 g pork fillet, cut into cubes (3 cm), slightly frozen
  • 200 g chicken breast fillet, cut into cubes (3 cm), slightly frozen
  • 150 g brown onion (approx. 1), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 50 g white wine
  • 50 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 50 g shelled unsalted pistachios
  • 40 g dried cranberries
  • 1 egg
  • ½ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste
  • 12 slices prosciutto (approx. 300 g)
  • 1000 g water

Preparation

  1. Place pork and chicken into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/7 times. Transfer mince into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place onion, garlic, parsley and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and sauté 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add wine and sauté 2 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set mixing bowl aside and allow mixture to cool slightly (approx. 5 minutes).
  5. Add reserved mince, spinach leaves, pistachios, cranberries, egg, salt and pepper and combine 20 sec//speed 5.
  6. Place 2 long sheets of aluminium foil (approx. 50 x 40 cm) onto a work surface.
  7. Place 6 slices of prosciutto onto each piece of foil to form a rectangle. Distribute mince mixture evenly between the two pieces of foil, placing the mince down the center of the prosciutto.
  8. Fold the prosciutto ends over the mince to enclose, then wrap the aluminium foil around the prosciutto to form a sausage shape. Twist the ends of the foil to seal. Place both wrapped terrines into the centre of the Varoma tray.
  9. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position and steam 50-55 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until terrines are evenly cooked through.
  10. Once cooked, remove terrines from aluminium foil, place onto a plate and cover with plastic wrap. Place terrines into refrigerator to chill, then slice and serve.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, plastic wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To check if your terrines are cooked, cut a slice off the end of each terrine. The terrines should be consistent in colour and cooked all the way through. If further cooking is required, check the water level in mixing bowl and add more water if necessary.
