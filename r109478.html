Moroccan lamb with pilaf

Ingredients

Moroccan lamb

  • 2 red onions (approx. 200 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra for seasoning
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2 pinches saffron threads
  • 1 piece orange zest (1 x 4 cm strip), no white pith
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 700 - 800 g boneless lamb shoulder, trimmed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 800 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon

Pilaf

  • 2 tbsp barberries (see Tips) (optional)
  • 1150 g warm water, plus extra for soaking
  • 200 g basmati rice
  • 100 g white quinoa
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into quarters
  • 40 g grapeseed oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra for seasoning
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 30 g blanched almonds, for garnishing
  • ½ pomegranate, arils only
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, cut into pieces
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch saffron, steeped in 1 tbsp hot water

Preparation

Moroccan lamb

  1. Place onion, garlic and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add salt, pepper, cumin, ginger, paprika, saffron, orange zest and 20 g of the oil and combine 5 sec//speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl. Add cinnamon quill and lamb into bowl with spice mixture and stir well to coat. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for 3 hours or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place remaining 30 g oil into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add reserved marinated lamb and cook 5 min/Varoma//speed .
  5. Add tomatoes and tomato paste and cook 50 min/90°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and continue cooking 30 min/90°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  7. Add honey and ground cinnamon and cook 5 min/90°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pilaf

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and place barberries and 150 g of the water into it. Set aside to soak (approx. 20 minutes).
  2. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice and quinoa into it. Cover with water and set aside to soak for 20 minutes. Transfer mixture into a large fine-mesh sieve and rinse until water runs clear. Set aside to drain.
  3. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add cumin, cinnamon, cardamom and salt and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add drained rice and quinoa and cook 1 min/100°C//speed 1.
  7. Transfer rice mixture into simmering basket. Place stock paste, 20 g of the butter and remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with rice mixture in it and cook 13 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice mixture into a separate thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  8. Place a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add remaining 20 g butter. Place almonds into pan and fry until evenly browned, then drain on paper towel and season with salt. Transfer lamb into a serving bowl and scatter with almonds.
  9. Drain barberries and discard soaking liquid. Add barberries, pomegranate arils, parsley and mint to reserved rice mixture and stir to combine. Transfer onto a serving plate and scatter with saffron (with soaking water) before serving with lamb.

Useful Items

large bowl, bowl, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, large non-stick frying pan, paper towel, fine mesh sieve, serving bowl, serving plate, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Barberries are used in Afghan rice dishes. They have a tart, fruity taste which enhances the flavour of savoury and sweet dishes, such as fruit pies, preserves and home made syrups. You can buy barberries in spice stores or online. You can also replace the barberries with currants in this recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
