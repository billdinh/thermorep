Spinach and feta muffins

Ingredients

  • 40 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 130 g fresh English spinach
  • 40 g sun-dried tomatoes
  • 100 g natural yoghurt
  • 2 eggs
  • 30 g rice bran oil
  • 60 g water
  • 80 g brown rice flour (see Tips)
  • 30 g gluten free cornflour
  • 20 g buckwheat flour (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp gluten free baking powder (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 80 g Danish feta, cut into pieces (approx. 1 cm)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Lightly grease a 6-hole Texas muffin tray with butter or line with muffin wrap papers and set aside.
  2. Place spinach into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place tomatoes into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into bowl with spinach and set aside.
  4. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/100°C/speed 3.
  5. Add yoghurt, eggs, oil and water and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  6. Add flours, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, salt and pepper and mix 5 sec/speed 4, or until combined.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add feta and reserved spinach mixture and combine 20 sec//speed , or until incorporated. Spoon mixture evenly into prepared muffin tray.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes (170°C), until muffins are golden brown or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the muffin comes out clean. Serve warm.

Useful Items

6-hole Texas muffin tray, muffin wrap papers, bowl, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own rice and/or buckwheat flour, place 250 g rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10. Use as needed.
  • Danish feta has a smoother, creamier texture than Greek or Australian feta.
  • These muffins are best eaten on the day they are made. They are also an excellent lunchbox idea.
