Spinach, Ricotta and Tomato Calzone, Fruit Kebabs with Chocolate Fondue

Ingredients

Grating

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, diced (2 cm)

Dough

  • 250 g milk
  • 25 g fresh yeast
  • 10 g honey
  • 400 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Fondue and Filling

  • 200 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 200 g tomatoes, quartered
  • 50 g white wine
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 50 g fresh baby spinach
  • 200 g ricotta cheese
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 2 medium eggs
  • water, for brushing
  • fresh fruit selection, to your taste

Preparation

Grating

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Dough

  1. Place milk, yeast and honey in mixing bowl then heat 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour and salt then mix 2 min/. Transfer to a large greased bowl sprinkled with flour. Cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Fondue and Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 250°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Divide chocolate between 2 small jars and place in Varoma dish.
  3. Place onions, oil, tomatoes, white wine, oregano, salt and 1 pinch pepper in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Place covered Varoma into position and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and keep it closed.
  4. Divide risen dough in half. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each piece to a circle (approx. Ø 25 cm). Place on prepared tray then spread tomato sauce over half of each circle. Garnish each with spinach, ricotta cheese, 1 pinch of pepper, ¼ tsp garlic powder and reserved grated Parmesan. Break an egg carefully over each pile of filling, brush edges of dough with water then fold over other halves of dough and seal edges. Press with your fingers to ensure filling does not leak out. Bake for 10 minutes (250°C).
  5. Serve calzone as a main course followed by chocolate fondue with fresh fruit for dessert.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, baking tray and paper, small jars, pastry brush, baking tray, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Using fridge-cold eggs helps to prevent them running over the edges when broken onto the fillings.
