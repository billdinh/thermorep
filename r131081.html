Peanut Butter and Cookies Cake

Ingredients

Chocolate Cookies

  • 100 g butter, unsalted, softened, cut in pieces
  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 250 g brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp vanilla sugar
  • 200 g cream, 35-37 % fat
  • 2 eggs
  • 100 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), cut in pieces
  • 350 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt

Filling and Topping

  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 800 g cream cheese, cut in pieces
  • 450 g peanut butter
  • 100 g white chocolate, cut in pieces

Preparation

Chocolate Cookies

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Draw a circle (Ø 20 cm) on 6 pieces of baking paper to use as a guide. Line 2 baking trays with 2 prepared baking papers and set aside the remaining prepared baking papers.
  2. Place butter, sugar, brown sugar, vanilla sugar, cream and eggs in mixing bowl then mix 4 min/speed 3.5.
  3. Add dark chocolate, plain flour, baking powder and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  4. Mix again 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place 1/6 of the dough (approx. 180 g) in the middle of one prepared baking tray. Then add another 1/6 of the dough in the middle of the other prepared baking tray. Use a spoon to spread the cookie batter into the drawn circle. Bake for approx. 12 minutes or until edges are pale golden brown. Remove trays from oven. Use spatula to reshape cookie circle, gently pushing the edges back in until you can see the drawn circle again. Return cookies to oven, then bake for a further 8-10 minutes or until edges are golden brown. Transfer cookies on the paper to a wire rack and allow to cool. Meanwhile bake the next 4 cookie layers in the same way.

Filling and Topping

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add cream cheese and peanut butter then mix 3 min/speed 4.5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a reserved cookie layer on a serving plate. Spread 1/6 of the reserved filling over and set the next reserved cookie layer on top. Repeat the process with each layer, then finish with a layer of filling on the top.
  4. Place white chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  5. Melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1. Use a teaspoon to drizzle the melted white chocolate over the top of the cake. Serve immediately or refrigerate until needed.

Useful Items

2 baking trays, baking paper, cooling rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This cake can be served immediately, or refrigerated and served the next day. The texture improves after one day.
  • If transporting the cake, it is better to refrigerate it first. It will set to a firm consistency, making it easy to slice.
  • To make a cake with vanilla cookie layers, simply omit the dark chocolate in the cookie recipe. The texture will not change, and the dough will still work well.
