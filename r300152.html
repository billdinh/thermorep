Coronation Chicken Sandwich Filling

Ingredients

  • 1000 g water
  • 500 g chicken breasts, skinless (approx. 3), halved horizontally
  • 5 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 50 g raisins
  • 50 g unsalted peanuts
  • 250 g mayonnaise (see tip)
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ - 1 tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in chicken, leaving some holes unobstructed for steam to circulate. Cover Varoma and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Remove Varoma and transfer chicken to a plate. Leave to cool for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, discard steaming water.
  3. Place reserved cooked chicken in mixing bowl and shred 10 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place parsley, raisins and peanuts in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add mayonnaise, reserved shredded chicken, curry powder, turmeric, salt and pepper then mix 30 sec/speed 1. Serve immediately or cover with cling film and store in fridge for up to 3 days.

Hints & Tricks

  • Invented in 1953 for the Queen’s coronation, coronation chicken has become a buffet table staple. This version is made using freshly steamed chicken in the Thermomix®, and can be enjoyed as is, with a jacket potato or, as we suggest, as a sandwich filling. Flavoured with sweet raisins and delicate curry seasoning, we’re bringing this retro classic back!
  • To make your own mayonnaise, follow the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
  • Any leftovers can be stored in the freezer for up to 1 month.
