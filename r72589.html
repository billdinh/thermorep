Chilli and apple jelly

Ingredients

  • 600 g Granny Smith apples, unpeeled and cut into quarters
  • 700 g water
  • 300 g apple cider vinegar
  • 6 long red chillies, stalks removed and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 3 fresh bird's eye chillies, stalks removed
  • 800 g white sugar

per 1 jar

3652 kJ / 869 kcal

0.8 g

Preparation

  1. Place apples, water, vinegar, 5 of the long red chillies and 1 of the bird's eye chillies into mixing bowl and cook 45 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Strain apple mixture through a nut bag or muslin cloth into a large jug. Discard solids in nutmilk bag or cloth. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place remaining long red chilli and remaining 2 bird's eye chillies into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved apple liquid and sugar and cook 60 min/Varoma/speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Cook for a further 15 min/Varoma/speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Divide jelly between 4 sealable jars (250 mL) and leave to set at room temperature (approx. 2 hours). Once set, place into refrigerator until ready to use.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large jug, 4 sealable jars (250 mL), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

    • This versatile jelly is an ideal edible gift for friends and family. It's great as an addition to cheese platters or in sandwiches with cold meats, cheeses or roasted vegetables. You can also use it to add spice and texture to marinades or sauces.
    • The spiciness of the jelly depends on the strength of your chillies, which can vary significantly depending on where they are from and when they are picked. The chilli heat of the jelly will intensify as it cooks, so taste as you go and adjust to your preference.
    • If you prefer a less spicy version, deseed some or all of your chillies and proceed as per recipe.
    • If you prefer a less sweet version, reduce the sugar content to 600 g and proceed as per recipe.
    • The chilli and vinegar in this recipe create strong vapours while cooking. It is recommended that the cooking area is well-ventilated by opening a window or using an extraction fan.
    • This jelly does not have a firm set; if you prefer your jelly to have a firmer set, store in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours before serving.
