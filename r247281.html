Pizza Diavola

Ingredients

Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 400 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling

Topping

  • 200 g mozzarella, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 150 g spicy salami, sliced
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 250 g pomodorino tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add remaining 170 g water, flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Tip dough out onto a work surface, shape into a ball then transfer to prepared bowl. Cover with cling film (see tip) then leave until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Topping

  1. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 210°C and line 3 pizza trays (Ø 30 cm) with baking paper (see tip).
  2. Place mozzarella in mixing bowl and grate 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to simmering basket and leave to drain for 10 minutes.
  3. Place salami in mixing bowl and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/5-6 times. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place oil, tomatoes and salt in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. On a floured surface, divide dough into 3 equal pieces and, working one at a time, roll out using a rolling pin into a circle (Ø 30 cm) then transfer to prepared trays.
  6. Spread tomato sauce over pizza bases then top with grated mozzarella and chopped salami. Drizzle with oil then bake for 10-15 minutes (210°C) until cheese has melted and edges are brown.
  7. Serve pizza hot, cut in slices.

Useful Items

cling film, baking paper, rolling pin, pizza trays (Ø 30 cm), large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Pizza Diavola translates to ‘devil’s pizza’. Given the spiciness of this pizza, it’s a well-suited name! We’ve used spicy salami in our version, but for a really devilish pizza we recommend adding some 'nduja sausage. 'Nduja is an extremely spicy, pork salami, similar to the French andouille sausage, which was introduced to the Calabrian region of Italy in the 13th century. The spreadable salami gets its fiery taste from roasted hot red peppers and is traditionally eaten spread on bread or alongside cheese.
  • Lightly grease cling film to prevent it from sticking to the dough during the resting and leavening stages.
  • There is no need to line non-stick pizza trays.
  • Cooking time may be reduced on a thinner pizza tray or one with holes in.
  • Cook pizzas in batches or use normal baking trays if you don't own 3 pizza trays.
  • For a strong spicy taste, you can add some 'nduja crumbled over the surface of the pizza before baking.
