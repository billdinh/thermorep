Black-eyed bean stew with baby squid

Ingredients

  • 120 g brown onions, cut into halves
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to drizzle
  • 500 g ripe tomato, cut into pieces
  • 15 g tomato paste
  • 370 g mixed vegetables (e.g. carrots, leek, celery stalks), cut into slices (1 cm)
  • 1 tbsp ground sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra for the squid
  • 1 pinch freshly ground pepper, plus extra for the squid
  • 350 g dried black-eyed beans, soaked for at least 4 hours (see Tips)
  • 350 g water
  • 1 heaped tsp Vegetable stock paste (refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook)
  • 1000 g whole baby squid, cleaned, fresh or thawed
  • 20 g freshly squeezed lemon juice (approx. 1/2 lemon) (optional)
  • fresh coriander, cut into pieces (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place onions, garlic and oil into mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5, then sauté 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add tomatoes and tomato paste and blend 7 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add 100 g of the mixed vegetables, sweet paprika, salt, pepper, soaked black-eyed beans, water and stock paste. Arrange remaining 270 g mixed vegetables and squid into Varoma dish and tray, season with salt and pepper, drizzle with extra oil and combine using spatula. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 30 min/Varoma//speed . Set covered Varoma aside onto a plate to keep warm and check that beans are cooked through. If not, cook for a further 5-10 min/Varoma//speed .
  4. Add lemon juice (optional) to beans and allow to rest in mixing bowl for 10 minutes. Transfer stew into a deep serving plate, sprinkle with chopped fresh coriander and serve hot with squid and vegetables on the side.

Hints & Tricks

  • The combination of beans and seafood is common in many Mediterranean countries. In this recipe the small black-eyed beans serve as a bed for the pleasant firmness of the squid, giving this dish an interesting balance of flavour and texture. Packed with nutrients, this recipe feeds many and can be adapted to your ingredients and preferences.
  • Replace whole baby squid with other types of seafood (e.g. calamari, prawns, cuttlefish), adjusting steaming time accordingly.
  • Soak black-eyed beans in 900 g cold water. The net weight after soaking will be approx. 750-800 g. Black-eyed beans are sufficiently soaked when the skin can be peeled off easily (usually after 4 hours). If it’s more convenient, you can leave them to soak overnight.
