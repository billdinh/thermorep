Spicy Tuna Rolls

Ingredients

  • 1000 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 250 g sushi rice, rinsed well
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar, plus 2 Tbsp for brushing
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp wasabi paste
  • 2 Tbsp mayonnaise (kewpie)
  • 2 tsp sriracha sauce (chilli sauce)
  • 200 g fresh tuna, (sashimi grade) diced (1 cm)
  • 2 spring onions, sliced (2 mm)
  • 4 sheets nori seaweed
  • 2 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • spring onion, sliced (2 mm), for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Insert simmering basket. Place water in mixing bowl.
  2. Weigh rice into simmering basket then steam 16 min/Varoma/speed 4. Meanwhile, place vinegar, sugar and salt in a small bowl. Stir until grains are dissolved, then set aside. Place soy sauce and wasabi paste in a small dish, mix together then set aside. Place mayonnaise and chilli sauce in a bowl, mix together and set aside.
  3. Leave simmering basket in position for a couple of minutes to drain, remove with aid of spatula, and transfer steamed rice to a large non-metal shallow dish and sprinkle vinegar mixture all over. Using a soft spatula, toss gently to distribute vinegar mixture then leave to cool.
  4. Add tuna and spring onions to bowl with spicy mayonnaise mixture and stir until combined. Wrap a bamboo sushi mat in cling film. Place a sheet of nori, shiny side down onto bamboo sushi mat then gently spread ¼ prepared sushi rice over nori sheet with a wet, soft spatula (see tip), leaving ⅓ clear at the far end. Sprinkle ½ Tbsp toasted sesame seeds over rice then, very carefully, flip the nori sheet over so that it is rice side down on mat and the clear end is nearest to you.
  5. Arrange ¼ tuna mixture across nori sheet 1 cm from the edge. Using bamboo sushi mat, begin rolling tightly to form a roll. Before reaching the other end brush edge of nori with vinegar-water (see tip) to help seal, then continue rolling tightly. Set roll aside then continue assembling remaining rolls.
  6. Slice each roll into 1.5 cm pieces using a wet, sharp knife. Serve with reserved soy and wasabi dipping sauce garnished with spring onions.

Useful Items

shallow bowl or dish, sharp knife, cling film, pastry brush, silicone spatula, bamboo sushi mat

Hints & Tricks

  • Many people think sushi means raw fish, but it is actually a reference to vinegared rice combined with various ingredients.
  • Place 200 g water and 2 Tbsp rice vinegar in a bowl to use for brushing and keeping hands and utensils wet so they don't stick to the rice.
  • Traditionally sushi rice should be body temperature rather than chilled, therefore for optimum quality, sushi is best served immediately or within a few hours of making. Wrap rolls in cling film and leave in a cool dry place then slice just before serving.
  • Sushi can be wrapped in cling film and refrigerated. Consume within 24 hours but be mindful of the freshness of the fish.
  • When using raw fish make sure it is sashimi/sushi grade. This ensures the fish has been frozen at extremely low temperatures to get rid of parasites making it safe to eat raw.
  • Sriracha is a hot, chilli sauce made from a paste of chili peppers, vinegar, garlic, sugar and salt. It is typically used as a dipping sauce or to accompany seafood.
