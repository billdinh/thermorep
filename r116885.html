Cashew and Banana Muffins

Ingredients

  • 340 g cashew nuts (raw or activated - see tip)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¾ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 175 g bananas, peeled, sliced and frozen until hard
  • 50 g avocado oil
  • 20 g raw honey
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ tsp real vanilla extract
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
972 kJ / 232 kcal
Protein
6.4 g
Carbohydrates
9.9 g
Fat
18.6 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease, or line with paper muffin cases, a 12 hole muffin tin.
  2. Place cashews in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add cinnamon, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place frozen bananas, oil, honey, eggs and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 25 sec/speed 9.5
  5. Add cashew nut mixture then mix 15 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer to prepared muffin tray then bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until golden brown on top. Transfer to a wire rack to cool. Serve once cooled.

Useful Items

12 hole muffin tin, paper muffin cases, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Soaking nuts and seeds increases their nutrient value along with breaking down the problematic compounds that help enhance their digestibility. Activating nuts is actually a traditional practice that historically required the soaking of both nuts and seeds in salty water and letting them dry in the sun. Without the level of sunshine required to do this in many countries and with slightly more sanitary methods of drying we can activate nuts in our own kitchens. We can do this by a) using a dehydrator for around 12-24 hours or using an oven, at the lowest temperature possible, preferably no more than 65°C. Stir or turn them occasionally, for the required drying time:
  • Almonds : 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak for 12-14 hours then dry for 12-24 hours.
  • Cashews: 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 3-6 hours* then dry for 12-24 hours.
  • Macadamias: 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 7-12 hours then dry for 12-24 hours.
  • Walnuts: ½ tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 7-12 hours then dry for 12-24 hours.

* Cashews can and should be soaked for no more than 6 hours and should be dried rapidly (you can increase the heat to 120°C). That’s because they have already been treated (with heat) to get rid of a toxic oil called cardiol.
