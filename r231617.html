Sopapilla

Ingredients

  • 3 oz sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 7 oz whole milk
  • 11 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 tbsp vegetable shortening, plus extra to fry
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
301 kJ / 72 kcal
Protein
2 g
Carbohydrates
14 g
Fat
1 g

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in sugar and cinnamon. Stir to combine and set aside.
  2. Place milk, flour, shortening, baking powder, brown sugar and salt into mixing bowl and knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and shape into a ball, cover with cling wrap and refrigerate 30 minutes.
  4. Divide dough in quarters. Working with one piece at a time, roll dough into a circle (approx. 7 in.). With a pizza cutter, cut each quarter of dough into six equal wedges. Cover with a kitchen towel until ready to fry.
  5. Place enough oil in a medium sized pot to fry (approx. 3-4 in. deep) and heat oil to 360°F-370°F. When oil is hot enough, fry dough wedges in batches until puffed and golden (approx. 1½-2 min. per side).
  6. Drain briefly on paper towels and quickly toss in reserved cinnamon and sugar mixture. Serve hot.

Useful Items

cling wrap, kitchen towel, paper towels, pot, pizza cutter, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with honey or whipped honey-butter.
  • Use a candy thermometer to help maintain the right frying temperature. Additionally, fry in small batches as overcrowding the pan may cause temperature to drop.
