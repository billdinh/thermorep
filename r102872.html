Crusted Pâté (Spain)

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 400 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 200 g butter, diced
  • 100 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Pâté

  • 6 - 8 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 3 teaspoons pickled green peppercorns
  • 2 slices white bread (50 g)
  • 100 g button mushrooms
  • 900 g pork meat, lean, diced (2 cm)
  • 150 g chicken livers, trimmed, cut in pieces
  • 3 teaspoons fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 large eggs
  • 50 g milk
  • 20 g Oloroso sherry
  • 50 g foie gras

Assembly

  • 3 slices unsmoked bacon
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 3 gelatine leaves
  • 350 g water
  • 50 g sweet wine (sweet sherry or Madeira)

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place all pastry ingredients in mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 6. Tip out, form into a ball, wrap in cling film and place in fridge.

Pâté

  1. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add green peppercorns and chop 3 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add bread and mushrooms then chop 2 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place 450 g pork and 75 g chicken livers in mixing bowl then mince 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to large bowl.
  5. Place remaining 450 g pork and 75 g chicken livers in mixing bowl then mince 10 sec/speed 7.
  6. Add contents of large bowl, salt, pepper, eggs, milk, sherry and foie gras then mix 50 sec//speed 3. Check that mixture is well blended. If not, tip into large bowl and mix well with spatula.
  7. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a loaf tin (length 30 cm minimum) with baking paper.

Assembly

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out ¾ rested dough and use to line prepared tin, leaving an overhang of 1 cm. Check corners well and ensure there are no holes in the pastry. Place slices of bacon in bottom of pastry and pour over contents of mixing bowl, filling all spaces well. Roll out remaining ¼ dough and cover top of tin so it overlaps with pastry side overhang. Using lid and side pastry overhang, form a decorative bead around edge of tin, to seal.
  2. Brush surface with beaten egg and make 2 small holes to allow steam to escape then mark diagonal stripes with the point of a sharp knife without cutting the surface. Bake for 10 minutes (200°C) then lower temperature to 180°C and bake for a further 1 hour 5 minutes (180°C). Remove from oven and cool completely in tin on a wire rack.
  3. When pie is completely cool, soak gelatine leaves in cold water for 5 minutes.
  4. Place water and sweet wine in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add squeezed-out gelatine leaves and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a jug and cool to room temperature. Pour cooled liquid into open holes in pastry lid (see tip) and chill for at least 4 hours or overnight. Unmould pie carefully onto a plate or board and serve sliced.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, loaf tin (31 cm x 15 cm x 10 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, wire rack, long sharp knife, pastry brush, jug, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To pour the liquid jelly easily into the pastry, use a small funnel or make one from a small piece of greaseproof paper.
  • Serves 12 as a first or second course. It would serve over 20 people as part of a buffet menu.
  • If gelatine is left over, let it solidify in the fridge then cut into cubes as garnish.
