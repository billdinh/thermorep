Frozen strawberry mousse with cream cheese topping

Ingredients

White balsamic reduction

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 500 g white balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g dry sherry
  • 80 g raw sugar
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 8 whole white peppercorns
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into halves

Frozen strawberry mousse

  • 100 g frozen strawberries
  • 4 fresh mint leaves, plus extra to garnish
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 tbsp white balsamic reduction
  • 20 g plain yoghurt

Cream cheese frosting

  • 50 g plain yoghurt
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup, to taste
  • 100 g cream cheese
  • fresh strawberries, diced, to serve

Preparation

White balsamic reduction

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then repeat grating 10 sec/speed 8 until a fine consistency is achieved.
  3. Add all remaining balsamic reduction ingredients and cook 30 min/Varoma//speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a hot-rinsed sealable jar or bottle and seal. Allow to cool completely before placing into a cool, dark area to store until ready to use.

Frozen strawberry mousse

  1. Place frozen strawberries, mint and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then repeat chopping 5 sec/speed 6.
  3. Move mixture to the side of the mixing bowl and insert butterfly whisk. Add egg white, balsamic reduction and yoghurt and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 or until a smooth consistency is achieved. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Transfer mixture into 4 freezable serving dishes or glasses and place into freezer to set (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cream cheese frosting

  1. Place yoghurt, maple syrup and cream cheese into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat mixing 10 sec/speed 6, until a smooth consistency is achieved. Transfer frosting into a bowl and place into refrigerator until ready to use.
  3. Once mousse has set, remove from freezer. Spoon reserved cream cheese frosting over the top of mousse. Garnish with extra mint leaves and diced strawberries then serve immediately.

Useful Items

sealable jar or bottle, fine mesh sieve, 4 freezable serving dishes or glasses, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute white peppercorns for black peppercorns.
  • This frozen mousse is a fabulous play on sweet and sour flavours thanks to the addition of the White balsamic reduction.
  • Leftover cream cheese can be used in the Steamed pistachio cheesecakes with mango.
