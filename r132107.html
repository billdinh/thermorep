Pork vindaloo

Ingredients

Spice paste

  • 250 g boiling water
  • 1 - 3 dried whole red chillies (Kashmiri or long red chillies)
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 2 ½ cm piece cinnamon quill
  • 4 cardamom pods (green)
  • ¼ star anise
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 ½ tsp poppy seeds (white)
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 ½ tsp coriander seeds
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 65 g white wine vinegar
  • 30 g Tamarind paste (see Tips)

Pork

  • 800 g boneless pork leg roast, cut into cubes (2.5 cm - see Tips)
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 3 brown onions (approx. 380 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 - 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp dark brown sugar
  • 30 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 2 garlic cloves, cut into thin slices
  • 15 - 20 fresh curry leaves

Preparation

Spice paste

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh boiling water into it. Add dried chillies and set aside for 10 minutes. Using simmering basket, strain chillies over a bowl, reserving liquid. Set chillies and reserved liquid aside.
  2. Place chilli powder, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, peppercorns, poppy seeds, ground nutmeg, cumin seeds, coriander seeds and fenugreek seeds into mixing bowl and roast 1 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 1 minute, then mill 1 min/speed 9 to a fine powder. Scrape down side of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add drained chillies and blend 1 min/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add garlic paste, ginger paste, vinegar and Tamarind paste and mix 30 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 30 sec/speed 4 to a paste. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pork

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pork into it. Remove bowl, add half of the reserved Spice paste and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  2. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add onions and chillies (optional) and chop 5 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add remaining half of the Spice paste and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add pork with marinade and cook 4 min/100°C//speed .
  7. Add salt, sugar and 100 g of the reserved chilli liquid and cook 40-50 min/90°C//speed ,  without measuring cup or until pork is tender. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  8. Place ghee into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  9. Add garlic and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed  or until just starting to brown.
  10. Add curry leaves and cook 20 sec/Varoma/speed 1. Scatter curry leaf mixture over pork to garnish before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This vindaloo recipe is more authentic than the "British-style" of vindaloo which tends to be quite spicy. For a spicier version, please refer to the variation below.
  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste, Tamarind paste and ghee, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can replace pork with lamb or chicken if you prefer. If using chicken reduce the cooking time from 40-50 min/90°C//speed /speed , without measuring cup, until chicken is cooked through.

"British-style" vindaloo: When making the Spice paste, increase the Kashmiri chilli powder from 2 teaspoons to 1 tablespoon and add 1 tablespoon tomato paste when adding garlic and ginger paste. After cooking onions and chilllies, add 400 g potatoes (cut into 2 cm pieces) when adding the remaining half of the Spice paste to the mixing bowl. After transferring vindaloo to a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, stir 2 tablespoons natural yoghurt through sauce. Proceed as per recipe.
