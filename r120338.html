Mystery soup

Ingredients

  • 100 g bacon, cut into pieces (approx. 2 cm)
  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters (approx. 150 g)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 500 g pumpkin flesh, cut into pieces (approx. 2 cm)
  • 250 g water
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp sour cream

Preparation

  1. Place bacon into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1,  without measuring cup. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and paprika and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1,  without measuring cup.
  4. Add pumpkin and chop 10 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add water, stock paste, bay leaf, salt and pepper and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Carefully remove bay leaf and blend 30 sec/speed 5 -9, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 9.
  7. Transfer soup into serving bowls (see Tips) and garnish with reserved bacon and cream. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, 4 serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • To serve soup in a pumpkin tureen (as in image), place a large saucepan or stockpot over high heat, fill about halfway with water (enough to cover pumpkin) and bring to the boil. Cut a lid from the pumpkin and scoop out seeds. Immerse pumpkin into boiling water (top up with water as needed to cover) for 5-10 minutes, then carefully transfer pumpkin into a large container of cold water to stop cooking. Place the pumpkin upside down on a wire rack to drain. To serve, fill the pumpkin with soup, garnish with bacon and sour cream and serve immediately. The pumpkin flesh will not be cooked enough to eat and should only be used for decorative purposes.
