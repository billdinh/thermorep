Acorn Squash Gratin

Ingredients

  • non-stick spray oil
  • 64 oz acorn squash, halved, seeds removed (approx. 2-3)
  • 3 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed
  • 3 oz Gruyère cheese, cubed
  • 1 oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, cubed
  • 8 oz yellow onions, in pieces
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • &frac18; tsp ground nutmeg
  • 8 oz baby spinach, divided
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened and cubed
  • 3 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 1 red bell pepper, de-seeded, de-stemmed, sliced (¼ in.)
  • pumpkin seeds, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly grease a casserole dish (approx. 12 in. x 9½ in. x 2 in.) with non-stick spray and set aside.
  2. With a sharp knife, cut acorn squash into thin half-moon slices (½ in.) and set aside.
  3. Place Parmesan and Gruyère cheeses into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place flour and butter into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/250°F/speed 1.
  5. Add onions, garlic, salt, pepper and nutmeg and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 3 min/250°F//speed 0.5.
  6. Add 4 oz spinach and cook 2 min/212°F//speed 0.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add remaining 4 oz spinach and cook a further 2 min/212°F//speed 0.5.
  8. Add cream cheese, whipping cream and half the reserved grated cheese and mix 1 min//speed 2.
  9. Layer reserved pumpkin and red pepper slices in prepared casserole, cover with spinach mixture and top with remaining reserved grated cheese. Cover casserole with foil and bake 50 minutes (375°F). Remove foil and bake a further 30 minutes, until browned. Garnish with pumpkin seeds and serve hot.

Useful Items

aluminum foil, casserole dish (approx. 12 in. x 9½ in. x 2 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is not necessary to peel acorn squash; the skin is full of fiber and antioxidants. Clean squash thoroughly and leave the skin intact.
