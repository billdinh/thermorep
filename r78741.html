Blueberry breakfast buns

Ingredients

Dough

  • 110 g white sugar
  • 20 g fresh yeast or 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 eggs
  • 100 g full cream milk
  • 370 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Filling

  • 250 g fresh blueberries or frozen blueberries, thawed and dried with paper towel
  • 70 g white sugar
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten, for brushing

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place sugar, yeast, eggs and milk into mixing bowl and warm 3 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add all remaining dough ingredients and knead 3 min/. Transfer mixture into a large bowl then, cover with plastic wrap or a kitchen towel and allow to prove in a warm place until the dough increases in size by at least 50% (approx. 4 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Place dough onto a floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Roll out to a rectangle (approx. 30 x 45 cm and 5 mm thick), then remove dough from silicone mat (if using) and cut into 16 evenly-sized rectangles. Set rectangles of dough aside while you make the filling.

Filling

  1. Place blueberries, sugar and flour into mixing bowl and mix 2 sec//speed 2. Spoon an even amount of the blueberry filling into the centre of each dough rectangle. Working 1 rectangle at a time, pinch all 4 corners together into the middle so you encase the filling. Seal well, then lightly roll between your hands to shape into round buns. Place buns seam-side down onto prepared baking trays allowing space for each bun to rise during baking. Allow buns to prove for a further 1 hour.
  2. Preheat oven to 160°C. Brush egg evenly over each bun. Bake for 25 minutes (160°C) or until lightly golden. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

wire rack, large bowl, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, silicone bread mat, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These buns are best eaten the day they are made.
