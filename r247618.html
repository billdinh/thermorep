Chicken Florentine Involtini - Involtini di pollo alla fiorentina

Ingredients

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 80 g frozen spinach, defrosted and drained
  • 80 g ricotta cheese
  • 50 g toasted pine nuts
  • 4 chicken breast fillets, butterflied (see tip)
  • 8 slices prosciutto
  • 125 g mozzarella, sliced
  • 600 g water

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add spinach, ricotta, pine nuts and reserved Parmesan then mix 10 sec/speed 1.
  4. Lay 1 butterflied chicken breast (see tip) on a large piece of cling film then cover with another piece of cling film. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, pound chicken into a thin cutlet. Repeat with remaining 3 chicken breasts.
  5. Lay 2 slices of prosciutto vertically on a piece of cling film, so long sides are slightly overlapping. Place 1 flattened chicken breast on top of prosciutto across the middle. Spoon ¼ of filling mixture horizontally along chicken breast, leaving approx. 3 cm space at each end. Lay slices of mozzarella, approx. ¼ of the total amount, along the filling.
  6. Starting from the edge of the chicken closest to you, carefully roll prosciutto tightly around chicken and filling to form a log. Wrap cling film around log and twist ends tightly, removing any air. Repeat with remaining 3 chicken breasts.
  7. Place water in mixing bowl. Place chicken logs in Varoma dish then place covered Varoma into position and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. To check if chicken is cooked through, remove biggest log, unwrap it slightly, and use a serrated knife to cut through the middle. If it needs further cooking, re-wrap cling film and cook for a further 2-5 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until done.
  8. Once chicken is cooked through, remove from Varoma and remove cling film, taking care to drain any excess water. Slice each log into 3-4 pieces and serve immediately with polenta and vegetables of choice.

Useful Items

cling film, meat mallet or rolling pin

Hints & Tricks

  • ‘Involtini’ is used to describe wrapped bundles of food. Eaten across Italy, involtini could refer to meat, poultry or fish, with a filling of cheese, cured meats or vegetables. Florentine refers to a dish prepared in the cooking style of Florence meaning it features spinach. This recipe turns plain chicken breasts into a main course that wouldn’t look out of place at a dinner party.
  • To butterfly a chicken breast, place on a chopping board and with one hand flat on top, slice into the thicker side of the breast with a long sharp knife - take care not to cut all the way through. Then open out the breast so it is flat.
