Mediterranean capsicums

Ingredients

  • 130 g freekeh
  • 500 g filtered water, plus extra for soaking
  • 250 g cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 30 g pickled capers
  • 50 g pine nuts
  • 50 g walnuts
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 3 tsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 zucchini, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 3 capsicums (any colour), cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • salt, to season
  • ground black pepper, to season

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh freekeh into it. Cover with filtered water and set aside to soak for a minimum of 30 minutes (or overnight). Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh tomatoes and capers into it, then set aside
  2. Place pine nuts, walnuts, oil, nutritional yeast and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place zucchini, garlic, spring onions/shallots and basil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into bowl with tomatoes and set aside.
  4. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper. Brush each capsicum half inside and out with oil and place onto prepared baking tray.
  5. Using simmering basket, drain freekeh. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with freekeh and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer into bowl with zucchini mixture and stir to combine.
  6. Divide freekeh mixture evenly between capsicums, packing tightly as capsicums will soften during cooking. Season with salt and pepper. Top each filled capsicum with reserved nut mixture. Cover with aluminium foil and roast for 45 minutes (190°C). Remove aluminium foil and roast for a further 15 minutes (190°C) or until golden brown and tender. Serve warm.

Useful Items

bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, aluminium foil, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • For a nut free topping, replace pine nuts and walnuts with 50 g sunflower seeds and 50 g pepitas. Proceed as per recipe.
