Hot cross buns

Ingredients

Buns

  • ½ lemon, zest only, no white pith (optional)
  • 500 g baker's flour
  • 250 g full cream milk
  • 1 tbsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 70 g butter
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • 100 g sultanas
  • 70 g currants

Piping

  • 80 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 100 g water

Syrup

  • 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp sugar

Preparation

Buns

  1. If using zest, proceed with step - if not, skip to step 2. Place lemon zest or lime zest (optional) and 100 g baker's flour into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place full cream milk and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add grated zest and flour mix (optional), remaining baker's flour (or total 500 g if you skipped step 1), salt, butter, caster sugar, egg, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg and ground cloves and mix 6 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 2-3 min/.
  4. Add sultanas and currants and mix 10 sec//speed 5.
  5. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  6. Transfer dough onto ThermoMat or floured work surface and work into a ball. Wrap in ThermoMat, or place into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap or kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes).
  7. Knock down dough and divide into 12 equal-sized pieces.  Form into rolls and place close together onto prepared baking tray then set aside to prove again while you prepare the piping.

Piping

  1. Place plain flour, salt, olive oil and water into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  2. Pour into piping bag and pipe crosses on to the top of buns.  Bake for 10-15 minutes (220°C) or until golden brown.

Syrup

  1. Place water and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 4.
  2. Using a pastry brush, apply hot sugar syrup to buns while still warm.
  3. Allow to cool on tray 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Serve warm.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, ThermoMat, piping bag, pastry brush, wire rack, oven, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Hot cross buns are perfect at Easter. Serve fresh or toasted with butter.
  • If your hot cross buns are not served warm, allow to cool completely then transfer into an airtight container to store until use.
