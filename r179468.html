Pineapple buns

Ingredients

Dough

  • oil, for greasing
  • 140 g milk
  • 20 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 270 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 30 g unsalted butter, softened

Topping

  • 60 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp milk powder
  • 120 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place milk, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flours, egg and salt and knead 30 sec/.
  4. Add butter and knead 5 min/.
  5. Transfer dough into prepared bowl, brush dough with oil, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 8 hours or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Topping

  1. Place butter and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add milk powder, flour and half of the beaten egg and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer onto a floured silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap mixture in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes or until ready to use.

Assembly

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Remove dough from refrigerator and allow to come to room temperature (approx. 30 minutes). Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and divide into 8 portions (approx. 70 g each), roll into balls, place onto prepared baking tray and cover with a kitchen towel.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Remove reserved topping from refrigerator and divide into 8 portions. Roll out each portion into a disc (approx. 10 cm). Top each ball with a disc, tucking edges under ball. Score each topping lightly, taking care not to score to deeply, with a pineapple diamond pattern. Cover and set aside to prove until doubled in size (approx. 40 minutes).
  4. Brush topping with remaining beaten egg and bake for 20 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown. Transfer to a wire rack and allow to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, kitchen towel, rolling pin, pastry brush, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Pineapple buns - traditionally a Chinese treat, but popular in both Hong Kong and Taiwan - don't actually contain any pineapple, but refer to the pineapple-like texture of the topping.
