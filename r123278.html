Pepita almond Florentines

Ingredients

  • 50 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 50 g walnuts (see Tips)
  • 60 g dried cranberries (see Tips)
  • 40 g extra virgin coconut oil
  • 180 g rice malt syrup (see Tips)
  • 50 g rolled oats
  • 50 g pepitas
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 180 g dark chocolate, broken into pieces

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line 2 large baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place almonds, walnuts and cranberries into mixing bowl and chop 8-10 sec/speed 7 or until finely chopped. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place oil and syrup into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until warm and combined.
  4. Add oats, pepitas, mixed spice, flour and reserved nut mixture and combine 20 sec//speed 2, until well combined.
  5. Place 2 teaspoonfuls of mixture onto prepared baking trays. Using damp hands, flatten slightly and shape into a rough circle. Repeat with remaining mixture (makes approx. 25 Florentines), leaving room between each Florentine to allow for spreading. Bake for 12 minutes (160°C), or until light golden. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Remove from oven and neaten edges with spatula or a palette knife by gently pushing edges in if needed. Allow to completely cool on trays.
  7. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 8.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 2 min/80°C/speed 2 or until chocolate melts. Spread base of each cooled Florentine with a thin layer of melted chocolate. Allow chocolate to set for a few minutes before serving.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, palette knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use raw, roasted or activated nuts in this recipe.
  • Try to use unsweetened cranberries if available. If you prefer, you can use dried blueberries or dried cherries instead of the cranberries.
  • You might like to use honey instead of the rice malt syrup, however, you will get a sweeter result. You can also use half rice malt syrup and half maple syrup.
  • Store these Florentines in a sealable container in the refrigerator. Just be sure to bring them to room temperature before serving for the best texture and flavour.
