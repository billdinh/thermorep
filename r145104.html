Rosemary and bacon stuffed onions

Ingredients

Stuffed onions

  • 70 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1450 g water
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 6 brown onions (approx. 120 g each), peeled, leaving root end intact and trimmed to sit flat
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 80 g rindless bacon rashers, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 40 g pouring (whipping) cream

Crumb topping

  • 20 g crusty bread, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • olive oil, for drizzling

Preparation

Stuffed onions

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 7 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place a small ceramic or glass baking dish (approx. 16 x 5 cm) onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 250 g of the water into it. Place into Varoma dish (leaving enough room for steam to circulate freely and to ensure Varoma lid closes securely). Stir stock paste into water to dissolve. Place onions into stock, stem side down.
  3. Place remaining 1200 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 55 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until onions are tender (see Tips). Remove Varoma, carefully remove onions from stock, transfer onto a plate and set aside until cool enough to handle (approx. 15 minutes). Discard poaching and steaming liquids. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Taking care to keep the base and sides of onions intact, use the tip of a small, sharp knife to cut out the centre of each onion, leaving 2-3 outside layers of onion. If you find there is a hole in the bottom of your onion, place a small section of a removed onion layer in the bottom to seal. Place removed onion flesh into mixing bowl.
  5. Add garlic, rosemary, oil, salt and pepper to mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  6. Add bacon and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 2, without measuring cup.
  7. Add cream and reserved grated Parmesan and combine 10 sec//speed 2.
  8. Spoon stuffing mixture into onions and onto top of onions and place into an ovenproof dish (approx. 23 x 15 cm). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Crumb topping

  1. Place all topping ingredients, except olive oil, into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Place oven rack into the center of the oven and heat oven grill. Drizzle onions with olive oil. Place onions into oven and grill until stuffing is golden and bubbling (approx. 10 minutes - see Tips). Remove from oven and carefully press reserved topping onto top of the onions, drizzle with olive oil and grill until golden brown (approx. 5 minutes). Serve warm. 

Useful Items

bowl, ceramic or glass baking dish, 1 plate, oven-proof dish, oven grill

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Cooking times may vary depending on the size of your onions.
  • Stuffed onions are a great side dish to lamb, chicken and beef dishes.
