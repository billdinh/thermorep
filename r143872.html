Twice-Baked Fruit Toasts

Ingredients

  • butter, for greasing
  • 90 g hazelnuts, blanched
  • 120 g wholemeal flour
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 450 g buttermilk
  • 60 g dark brown sugar
  • 75 g honey
  • 65 g pumpkin seeds
  • 75 g medjool dates, pitted, cut in thin strips
  • 50 g raisins
  • 45 g dried sour cherries (optional)
  • 1 orange, finely grated zest only

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a large loaf tin (900 g/2 lb).
  2. Place hazelnuts in bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add wholemeal flour, plain flour, bicarbonate of soda and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add buttermilk, brown sugar and honey then stir 30 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add pumpkin seeds, dates, raisins, cherries (if using) and orange zest then mix 20 sec//speed 3. Finish mixing by hand then transfer to prepared tin. Bake for 50 minutes-1 hour (180°C), covering with foil after 30 minutes if the top begins to brown too much, until a skewer inserted in centre comes out almost clean (see tip). Leave to cool in tin for 10 minutes then remove to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. Once cool, wrap loaf tightly in cling film then freeze for 30 minutes before slicing. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 150°C and line 2 large baking trays with baking paper.
  7. Slice loaf thinly (2-3 mm) using a sharp knife and place slices on prepared baking trays. Bake for 10 minutes (150°C) then turn slices over and bake for a further 10 minutes (150°C) until golden. Transfer slices to a wire rack to cool and harden.
  8. Serve or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

skewer, 900 g loaf tin (2 lb), aluminium foil, wire rack, 2 baking trays and baking paper, cling film, long sharp knife, airtight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • When testing the baked loaf with a skewer, you may pierce a piece of fruit so it doesn't matter if not completely clean.
  • Bake in 2 smaller tins, for smaller slices.
  • Wrap resulting slices in cling film, for a softer, chewier result.
