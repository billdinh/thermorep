Simnel Cake

Ingredients

Fruit

  • 150 g sultanas
  • 150 g currants
  • 50 g chopped mixed peel
  • 50 g glacé cherries
  • 30 g brandy

Marzipan

  • 240 g granulated sugar
  • 220 g blanched almonds
  • 2 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 Tbsp orange flower water
  • ¼ tsp real vanilla extract
  • 1 - 2 tsp natural almond extract

Cake

  • 175 g butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 lemon, zest only
  • 175 g soft brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 15 g golden syrup
  • 175 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Glaze

  • 20 g apricot jam, strained
  • 1 egg white, beaten

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sultanas, currants, mixed peel, glacé cherries and brandy. Mix well with spatula, then cover with cling film and set aside for flavours to develop (1-24 hours).

Marzipan

  1. Place 100 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind to caster sugar 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place remaining 140 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind to icing sugar 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place blanched almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  4. Add reserved caster sugar and all but 2 Tbsp reserved icing sugar then mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add egg yolks, lemon juice, orange flower water, vanilla extract and almond extract then knead 1 min/ until a rough paste is formed (see Tip). Tip out, wrap in cling film and set aside while continuing with recipe.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line a cake tin (Ø 20 cm) then set aside.
  2. Place lemon zest and brown sugar in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and 3 eggs then cream 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add golden syrup, flour, mixed spice, cinnamon and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Transfer to bowl containing soaked fruit and brandy then mix well with spatula.
  5. On a surface sprinkled with the reserved icing sugar, roll out 200 g prepared marzipan to a circle (Ø 20 cm). Pour half the cake mixture into cake tin then place marzipan disc on top. Add remaining cake mixture, smoothing down the top. Bake 1 ¼ hours (170°C) or until cooked.
  6. Remove from oven and allow to cool in tin for 30 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Meanwhile roll out another 200 g marzipan to a circle (Ø 20 cm). Divide remaining marzipan into 11 pieces and roll each into a ball then set aside.
  7. Brush cooled cake with strained apricot jam then place second marzipan disc on top and brush with beaten egg white.  Place marzipan balls on top of the cake, brushing these with beaten egg white too. Colour under a preheated grill until marzipan is light golden brown (alternatively use a blow torch). Serve in slices.

Useful Items

grill, large bowl, cling film, bowl, round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, cooling rack, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The longer you can leave the fruit to soak in the brandy in Step 1, the stronger the flavours will be.
  • Add more almond extract at Step 6 for a stronger flavour, and knead again 30 sec / .
  • Simnel Cake is a dense fruit cake packed full of spices and marzipan that is traditionally made during Lent. The eleven marzipan balls on the top represent the eleven faithful disciples.
