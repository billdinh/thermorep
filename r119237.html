Chocolate zucchini cupcakes with blueberry icing

Ingredients

Chocolate zucchini cupcakes

  • coconut oil spray, for greasing
  • 100 g dairy free milk chocolate
  • 250 g zucchini, cut into large pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 80 g arrowroot flour
  • 20 g raw cacao powder
  • 50 g coconut flour
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 90 g dark coconut nectar syrup
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs

Blueberry icing

  • 50 g coconut cream
  • 1 tbsp gelatine powder
  • 125 g fresh blueberries (1 punnet)
  • 220 g icing sugar
  • 20 g liquid coconut oil

Preparation

Chocolate zucchini cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with cupcake liners and liberally spray liners with coconut oil. Set aside.
  2. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place zucchini into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add all remaining ingredients, including reserved chocolate and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer into prepared cupcake liners. Bake for 18-20 minutes (170°C), until cakes puff up and feel springy to touch or a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cupcakes comes out tacky but not wet. Cakes will continue to cook after removing from oven. Allow to stand for 5-10 minutes in tray, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Blueberry icing

  1. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 20 g of the coconut cream into it, then set aside. Place gelatine into a small bowl and set aside. Place a fine mesh sieve over a separate bowl and set aside.
  2. Place blueberries into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 2. Working quickly, transfer blueberries into fine mesh sieve pushing all juice out of the blueberries using the back of a spoon. Discard blueberry pulp. Rinse and dry mixing bowl and sieve.
  3. Add gelatine into bowl with warm blueberry juice and stir vigorously with a fork until combined. Add the reserved 20 g coconut cream, stirring with a fork until combined.
  4. Place fine mesh sieve over mixing bowl and transfer blueberry mixture into it, pushing all contents into mixing bowl using the back of a spoon. Discard any pulp.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk . Add icing sugar, coconut oil and remaining 30 g coconut cream into mixing bowl and mix 5 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk (see Tips).
  6. Leave icing in mixing bowl, remove lid and allow to cool for 20 minutes, then whip 20 sec/speed 5.
  7. Transfer into a piping bag with a decorative nozzle and decorate cupcakes as desired (see Tips). Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable container until ready to serve.

Useful Items

cupcake liners, 12-hole muffin tray, bowl, wire rack, wooden skewers, fine mesh sieve, small bowl , large spoon, fork, piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Some coconut products are manufactured on equipment shared with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern for you, be sure to check the label of your coconut cream.
  • The moisture in the cupcakes may give some paper cupcake liners a wet appearance. We recommend using cupcake liners with a shiny foil appearance.
  • The colour of your icing will depend on the ripeness of your blueberries and how much juice they yield. You can also use other fruits (e.g. strawberries, raspberries) to colour your icing.
  • After adding remaining coconut cream to icing and mixing, if not using icing immediately, transfer into a container and store in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. It will become very hard during this time. To make it pliable again, place into mixing bowl and whip 20-30 sec/speed 5, adding 2-3 tablespoons coconut cream through hole in mixing bowl lid. Continue whipping until icing is pliable and can easily be transferred into a piping bag.
  • When piping, the warmth from your hands helps make the icing pliable. If you find the icing becomes runny (too warm), set aside or place into the refrigerator for a few minutes to become firm again.
  • If icing cupcakes in warm weather, place cupcakes with icing into refrigerator immediately after piping until ready to serve.
  • Store cupcakes at room temperature until ready to serve.
