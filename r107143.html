Cake Pops

Ingredients

Cake

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 2 medium eggs
  • ½ tsp vanilla sugar
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 60 g self-raising flour
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Decoration

  • 150 g mascarpone
  • 250 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 2 Tbsp coloured sugar sprinkles
Nutrition

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a springform cake tin (Ø 22 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, vanilla sugar and sugar in mixing bowl, heat 6 min/37°C/speed 4 then whisk 6 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and salt then mix 4 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk, scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix gently with spatula. Transfer to prepared tin.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes (180°C) until golden and a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Remove from oven and leave in tin to cool for 5 minutes then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Decoration

  1. When cake has cooled, cut in pieces (approx. 4 cm). Place cake pieces in mixing bowl and grate 2 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add mascarpone and mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  3. Form 14 egg shapes using your hands (approx. 4 cm x 3 cm). Place on a tray and set aside in fridge to firm up for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place 50 g white chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 2 min/50°C/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl.
  5. Dip the end of a cake pop stick in melted chocolate then gently push halfway into egg. Repeat with remaining eggs then return tray to fridge for 30 minutes.
  6. Once cake pops have set on the sticks, place remaining 200 g white chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 4 min/50°C/speed . Transfer to a bowl.
  7. Dip each egg in the melted chocolate, turning so that it is completely coated. Hold above chocolate and keep turning until all excess chocolate has run back into bowl. While chocolate is still warm, decorate eggs with coloured sugar sprinkles or other edible decorations. Leave to stand upright while chocolate sets then place in fridge until ready to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, skewer, wire rack, baking tray, cake pop sticks, springform tin (Ø 22 cm), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Children can assist with this recipe, weighing ingredients in and setting the controls. Never leave them unattended with Thermomix®, while it is running or between steps.
  • Children can also help with shaping, coating and decorating the cake pops.
  • For a great finish on your pop, make sure the cake mixture is smooth before coating with chocolate.
  • Tweezers can be used to apply decorations for a really neat look or sprinkle while turning the cake pop for a continous line.
  • If you do not have a cake pop stand, a great alternative is to serve cake pops on a tray with the sticks in the air. If you choose to do this focus on decorating the underside area, around the stick.
  • A mug filled with sugar is a great help to keep your egg pops upright to dry. Once you've dipped your pop in chocolate and decorated it, place them in with enough space so they do not touch each other.
