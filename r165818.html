Two Crust Pie Pastry

Ingredients

  • 10 oz unsalted butter, chilled and cut into 1 in. pieces
  • 3 ½ oz heavy whipping cream
  • 2 oz lard, chilled and cut into 1 in. pieces
  • 18 oz pastry flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
1255 kJ / 300 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
25 g

Preparation

  1. Place butter, cream and lard into mixing bowl and whip 15 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add flour, sugar and salt and mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface, gently knead into a ball, divide in half, flatten into disks and wrap each in cling wrap. Refrigerate for 2 hours to overnight.
  4. Place one chilled dough disk on the counter for 20-30 minutes to slightly soften. Roll out dough between cling wrap to ¼ inch thick, with a rolling pin and fold in half. Roll out again ⅛ inch thick and 12 inch round working quickly and handling as little as possible to keep it cool and easy to work with. Peel off one sheet of cling wrap and line a pie pan, with dough side down. Gently press over cling wrap to mold dough into pie pan. Gently remove cling wrap and trim edges to ½ inch beyond lip of pan. Tuck overhanging edge under itself so folded edge is flush with edge of pie plate. Crimp using fingers. Cover with cling wrap and chill in freezer for 1 hour. Repeat procedure with second dough disk.
  5. Preheat oven to 425°F. Remove pie dough from freezer and line with foil covering all edges of dough. Fill with pie weights and bake for 25 minutes (425°F) until pastry looks dry and pale in color. Carefully remove foil and weights. Transfer to a wire rack to cool. Make pie fillings and cook as per recipe directions.

Useful Items

cling wrap, rolling pin, pie pans (approx. 9 in.), aluminum foil, wire rack, pie weights, refrigerator, freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Pie crusts can be made 2 days ahead. Store in an airtight container or covered in cling wrap and freeze until ready to bake.
  • Collect trimmed dough, place in cling wrap and refrigerate to firm up for 20 minutes. Once firm re-roll dough and cut leaf shapes with a small cookie cutter to decorate the edges or surface of pie.
  • For convenience, you can also use half of the pastry dough for a one-crust pie and freeze the other round of pastry—wrapped tightly in cling wrap—for another pie.
