Quatre quarts cake (four fourths cake)

Ingredients

  • 250 g unsalted butter, cut into cubes (4-5 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g raw sugar
  • 250 g self-raising flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a loaf tin (approx. 25 x 9 x 11 cm) well with butter and set aside.
  2. Place butter and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/37°C/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour, eggs, baking powder and vanilla and mix 10 sec/speed 7.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing with spatula. If necessary, mix for a further 10 sec/speed 7, to ensure ingredients are well combined.
  5. Pour batter into prepared loaf tin and bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Set aside to cool for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

wooden skewer, wire rack, loaf tin (25 x 9 x 11 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This "quarter cake" (or quatre quarts, meaning "four fourths") is used in France to teach maths to primary school children as it uses equal quarters of butter, flour, sugar and eggs. It means they learn to calculate and cook at the same time!
  • This cake is traditionally served plain or with fruit, but you can top it with just about anything! We decorated it with Royal icing, desiccated coconut and walnuts.
