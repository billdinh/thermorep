Butterfly cakes

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 140 g honey
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract
  • 190 g buttermilk
  • 120 g extra virgin coconut oil, softened
  • 250 g self-raising flour
  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream, to serve (see Tips)
  • jam of choice, to serve (see Tips)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1120 kJ / 266 kcal
Protein
4.4 g
Carbohydrates
27.1 g
Fat
15.9 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with paper cases and set aside.
  2. Place eggs, honey, vanilla, buttermilk and oil into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and lid with spatula.
  4. Add flour and mix 10 sec/speed 4, or until just combined.
  5. Spoon mixture into paper cases and bake for 20-25 minutes (150°C), or until lightly golden and a skewer inserted into the centre of the cupcakes comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping.  Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Cut a circle (1-2 cm deep) from the top of each cupcake, then cut circle into halves. Place a spoonful of whipped cream into hole and top with a small dollop of jam. Press the 2 half circles into each cake to make butterfly wings. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

12-hole muffin tray, 12 cupcake liners, wooden skewer, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Many commercial jams are at least half sugar. Cut down on the sweetness by making your own with minimal sugar such as Berry chia jam (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip) or try to find a brand with a higher fruit content. Some quality jams are available with 70 per cent fruit - check the label so you know what you‘re getting.
  • If you’re watching calories, you’re best to choose (freshly) whipped cream over double cream. It has almost one-third of the calories and one-third of the fat of the same volume of double cream.
  • These cakes are best eaten fresh on the day they’re made.
