Sweet shortcrust pastry

Ingredients

  • 100 g sugar
  • 200 g butter, chilled, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 370 g plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 ½ tsp homemade vanilla sugar

Preparation

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add butter, plain flour, egg, salt and vanilla sugar or vanilla extract and mix 25-30 sec/speed 5.
  3. Transfer pastry onto a ThermoMat or floured surface, work into a flattened ball and wrap in ThermoMat or plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 1 hour.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a tart tin (28 cm) or individual tart tins and set aside.
  5. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of baking paper or plastic wrap to a thickness of 5 mm.Line prepared tart tin with dough and prick pastry with fork to avoid air bubbles. Bake a tart case (28 cm) for 15 - 20 minutes (180°C), or individual tart cases for 10 - 15 minutes (180°C), or until golden. Allow to cool completely on a wire rack before storing in an airtight container or filling.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, ThermoMat, airtight container, individual tart tins, tart tin (28 cm), rolling pin, wire rack, baking paper or plastic wrap, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Be careful not to knead the dough unnecessarily with your hands, as this will make the tart cases tougher.
  • If the dough becomes warm and difficult to work with, place rolled out dough, still covered with sheets of baking paper or plastic wrap, into refrigerator until dough is firm enough to handle.
  • To make quick shortcrust biscuits, roll out dough in step 5 and cut into shapes as desired. Place onto a baking tray lined with baking paper. Bake for 10 - 12 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Allow to cool on a wire rack before decorating or dusting with icing sugar to serve.
  • Instead of rolling out dough with a rolling pin, you can shape it into a log. For biscuits, roll the log until it is round then slice into round slices (5 mm thick). For a tart case, make a flatter log, wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm enough to cut. Cut dough into long thin slices (5 mm thick). Place slices into tart tin so they are slightly overlapping and press down with fingers to fill any cracks. Bake as per recipe.
  • Sweet shortcrust pastry can be tightly wrapped in plastic wrap and frozen for use within 3 months. Thoroughly thaw pastry before use.
  • Decorated biscuits: decorate biscuit shapes with coloured icing, chocolate coating and decoration sweets.
  • Jam-filled biscuits: spread cooled biscuit with jam and place another on top. Dust with icing sugar.
