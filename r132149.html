Nankhatai (Cardamom biscuits)

Ingredients

  • 170 g raw sugar
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 180 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 2 eggs (separated)
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 60 g semolina
  • 50 g natural yoghurt
  • 350 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 35 blanched almonds

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar, nutmeg and cardamom into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add ghee and mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add egg yolks, bicarbonate of soda, baking powder, semolina and yoghurt and mix 30 sec/speed 4.  Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add flour and salt and mix 30 sec/speed 3, until consistency of biscuit dough. Form mixture into 35 walnut-size balls (approx. 20 g each). Place onto prepared baking trays, leaving a 3 cm space between balls.
  6. Lightly beat reserved egg whites with a fork. Dip each almond into beaten egg whites and press onto top of each biscuit. Bake for 12-18 minutes (180°C). Transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks
