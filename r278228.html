Rosemary and Parmesan Almond Pulp Crackers

Ingredients

  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 180 - 200 g almond pulp, leftover from homemade almond milk (see tip)
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, needles only, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp ground flaxseed
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp water

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place almond pulp, 20 g reserved grated Parmesan, rosemary, flaxseed, bicarbonate of soda, garlic powder and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Without measuring cup, mix 1 min/speed 1 while slowly adding olive oil through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, without measuring cup, mix 1 min/speed 1 while adding 2-3 Tbsp water, until mixture comes together into a moist dough.
  4. Transfer dough to a bowl, shape into a ball, cover with cling film and place in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 190°C.
  5. Transfer chilled dough to a sheet of baking paper, then cover with another sheet of baking paper. Roll dough out to a large rectangle (approx. 3 mm thick), then remove top sheet of paper and use a sharp knife to score dough into 25 - 30 rectangular crackers (approx. 5 cm x 4 cm).
  6. Lift up baking paper with crackers on and transfer to a baking tray. Sprinkle crackers with remaining grated Parmesan, then prick each cracker twice with a fork.
  7. Bake for 15 minutes (190°C). Using a spatula, carefully separate crackers and flip over, then bake for a further 10-15 minutes (190°C) until edges of crackers are golden brown.
  8. Leave crackers to cool on baking tray then transfer to an airtight container and store until needed.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray, baking paper, airtight container, rolling pin, sharp knife, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These crackers can be made with leftover pulp from other nuts, e.g. hazelnuts.
  • To make almond milk: Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 200 g almonds. Cover with cold water and set aside in fridge to soak overnight (or for at least 6 hours).

    Rinse soaked almonds under running water then drain and place in mixing bowl. Add 1000 g water and blend 3 min/speed 10.

    Line simmering basket with a muslin cloth, or use a nut milk bag, then slowly pour almond mixture through muslin, collecting milk in a bowl below. Reserve almond pulp in muslin then continue with recipe.
  • Transfer almond milk to a 1 litre bottle, then serve or refrigerate until needed. The almond milk can be drunk as is, or used as a basis for smoothies, or with porridge or cereal.
