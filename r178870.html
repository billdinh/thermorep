Vegetable Stock Paste

Ingredients

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm) (optional)
  • 200 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 250 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 100 g tomatoes, cut in pieces
  • 150 g courgettes, cut in pieces
  • 50 g fresh mushrooms
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 dried bay leaf (optional)
  • 6 sprigs fresh mixed herbs (e.g. basil, sage, rosemary), leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 120 g coarse salt (see tip)
  • 30 g dry white wine
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan (if using) in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place celery, carrots, onions, tomatoes, courgettes,mushrooms, garlic, bay leaf (if using), mixed herbs and parsley in mixing bowl then, with aid of spatula, chop 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add salt, wine and oil then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 25-30 min/Varoma/speed 2 (see tip). Mixture will become thick and fairly dry.
  4. Add reserved grated Parmesan (if using) and blend 1 min/speed 4-7, increasing speed gradually. Transfer to a glass jar with screw-top lid (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm. Make sure that the rim of the jar is clean and close immediately then leave to cool before using or storing in fridge (see tip).

Hints & Tricks

  • The salt in this recipe is used as the preserving agent. When mixed with water, the stock does not taste too salty. If you wish to decrease the salt in the recipe, you must store the stock paste in the freezer.
  • If vegetables are very fresh (and therefore have a high water content), cook for 30 minutes in step 3.
  • To prepare jar, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jar and lid upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heat in the oven at 100ËšC until required.
  • Store in the fridge for up to 3 months, or freeze in cubes for up to 6 months.
  • 1 heaped tsp stock paste is roughly equivalent to 1 shop-bought vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l).
  • To make vegetable stock, use 1 heaped tsp per 500 g water.
  • Adapt the stock paste recipe using the natural ingredients of your choice.
  • Add more mushrooms for a vegetable stock paste with a stronger mushroom flavour.
  • For a vegetarian stock paste, omit Parmesan in step 1.
