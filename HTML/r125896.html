Apricot apple pie

Ingredients

Apricot filling

  • 700 g water
  • 4 Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 800 g apricots, stone removed and flesh cut into thin wedges
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp mixed spice

Hazelnut pastry

  • 60 g hazelnuts, skins on
  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (see Tips)
  • 80 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 eggs, 1 lightly beaten
  • natural yoghurt or pouring (whipping) cream, to serve

Preparation

Apricot filling

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, then weigh apples into Varoma dish. Insert Varoma tray and weigh apricot slices onto it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until fruit is tender. Set aside for 30 minutes to drain and cool.
  2. Transfer cooked fruit into a pie dish (23 cm) and stir through sugar and mixed spice until well combined. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Hazelnut pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C.
  2. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add flour, baking powder, sugar and butter and mix 12 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add 1 of the eggs and knead 15 sec/, until mixture just comes together.
  5. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or large lightly floured sheet of baking paper. Gently shape into a ball, then flatten into a thick disc. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Using a rolling pin, roll pastry out onto silicone bread mat or lightly floured sheet of baking paper until large enough to cover pie dish (approx. 4 mm thickness). Carefully place pastry over fruit and press gently around edges to seal. Trim any excess pastry from edges. Crimp edges with your fingers.
  7. Brush pastry with beaten egg and place onto a baking tray (20 x 30 cm). Using a sharp knife, cut a small cross in the centre of the pie. Bake for 30-35 minutes (160°C), or until pastry is golden. Serve warm with yoghurt or cream.

Useful Items

pie dish (23 cm), silicone bread mat, baking paper, plastic wrap, rolling pin, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), pastry brush, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Apricots can be substituted with plums or whichever seasonal stone fruit you have on hand. If your fruit is ripe and tender, you may wish to steam it for less time in the Varoma.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this recipe – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
