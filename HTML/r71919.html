Chocolate mousse soufflés

Ingredients

Chocolate custard

  • 50 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), broken into pieces
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 egg
  • 200 g milk
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Chocolate soufflés

  • butter, for greasing
  • 3 tbsp caster sugar
  • 2 egg whites (reserved from custard)
  • 200 g chocolate custard
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Chocolate custard

  1. Place chocolate and cornflour into mixing bowl and mill 8 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add egg yolks, egg, milk and sea salt and cook 5 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate soufflés

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) into the oven to preheat. Generously grease 2 soufflé ramekins (8.5 cm, 180 ml) with butter. Place ½ tbsp caster sugar into each and rotate ramekin to coat bottom and sides evenly. Set ramekins aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/37°C/speed 4, without measuring cup, until egg whites are stiff and shiny.
  3. With butterfly whisk still in place, mix speed 4, slowly adding 2 tbsp caster sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid, ensuring the first tablespoon of sugar is fully incorporated before adding the next. Set aside half of the egg white mixture.
  4. Add chocolate custard to egg white mixture in mixing bowl and combine 4 sec//speed 2 until just combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved egg white mixture and combine 4 sec//speed 2 until just combined. Mixture should be light and fluffy. Divide mixture between prepared ramekins. Smooth over tops and wipe around ramekin rims with a clean kitchen towel.
  6. Place ramekins onto preheated baking tray. Bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C), or until soufflés have risen approx. 3 cm above the rim and are lightly golden on top. Remove from oven and dust with icing sugar. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), 2 soufflé ramekins (8.5 cm, 180 ml), kitchen towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This soufflé is slightly denser in consistency than a traditional soufflé, similar in texture to a baked custard. The rise while baking provides a lovely mousse like texture to the end result.
  • Custard will keep in a sealable container for a couple of days in the refrigerator.
