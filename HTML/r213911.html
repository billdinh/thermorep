Shish barak (Meat dumplings in yoghurt sauce)

Ingredients

Meat dumplings (preparation)

  • 150 g plain flour
  • 75 g full cream milk
  • 25 g vegetable oil
  • 4 pinches salt
  • 50 g brown onion, leaves only
  • 100 g beef mince
  • 100 2 - 3 pinches baharat, to taste (see Tips)
  • 2 2 - 3 pinches ground cinnamon, to taste (see Tips)
  • 2 pinches nutmeg
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts (optional)

Yoghurt sauce

  • 20 g olive oil
  • 20 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 3 garlic cloves, leaves only
  • 1500 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 pinch salt

Meat dumplings (assembly)

  • plain flour, for dusting

Preparation

Meat dumplings (preparation)

  1. Place flour, milk, oil and 2 pinches of the salt into mixing bowl and knead 2 min 30 sec/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Transfer dough into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Proceed with recipe while dough rests.
  2. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add beef mince, baharat, cinnamon, nutmeg, pepper, remaining 2 pinches salt and pine nuts (optional) and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Proceed with yoghurt sauce.

Yoghurt sauce

  1. Place oil, mint and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8, then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place yoghurt, cornflour and salt into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 5.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Cook 25 min/80°C/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.

Meat dumplings (assembly)

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Divide reserved dough into 4 equal portions. On a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, thinly roll out each piece of dough (1 mm thickness).
  3. Using a round cookie cutter (5-6 cm), cut dough into rounds (approx. 36). Place ½ teaspoon of the meat filling into the centre of each circle. Fold one end over filling into a crescent or half moon shape. Wrap the ends of the dough around your finger and join together to form tortellini-shaped dumplings. Transfer onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes (180°C) or until crispy and golden brown.
  5. Divide sauce and dumplings between serving bowls, then garnish with reserved mint and garlic mixture before serving.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, silicone bread mat, bowl, round cookie cutter (5 cm), thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, serving bowls, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baharat is a Middle Eastern spice mix, and is used on a wide variety of meat, fish and vegetable dishes. You can find baharat in specialty food stores or online, or you can make your own (please refer to the recipe in this collection).
