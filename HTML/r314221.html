Spiced lamb flatbread

Ingredients

Flatbread

  • 20 g olive oil
  • 150 g water
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 300 g baker's flour
  • ½ tsp salt

Meat topping

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 250 g brown onions, cut into quarters (approx. 4)
  • 500 g ripe tomatoes, cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 5 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • ½ tsp dried chilli flakes, to taste
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 500 g lamb mince
  • 30 g pine nuts

Assembly and baking

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • salt, for sprinkling
  • 2 lemons, cut into quarters

Preparation

Flatbread

  1. Place oil, water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and mix 2 min/37°C/speed 3.
  2. Add flour and salt and knead 3 min/. Transfer dough into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Proceed with recipe while dough rises.

Meat topping

  1. Place garlic, onion and tomatoes into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer mixture into simmering basket, sprinkle with salt and leave to stand for 5 minutes, then squeeze out as much liquid as possible (see Tips).
  2. Place parsley and mint into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add strained tomato mixture, cumin, cayenne pepper, cinnamon, paprika, allspice, chilli flakes, pomegranate molasses and lamb mince and knead 3 min/.
  4. Add pine nuts and stir well with aid of spatula. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Assembly and baking

  1. Grease 2 large baking trays (approx. 30 x 40 cm) well with oil and place into oven. Preheat oven to 250°C (see Tips).
  2. Turn out risen dough onto a floured surface. Using floured hands, divide dough into 6 equal portions (approx. 80 g each). Using a rolling pin, roll out each portion into a thin, flat circle (18-20 cm). Remove baking tray from oven and carefully place 1-2 dough circles onto each tray (see Tips).
  3. Divide half of the reserved meat topping between flatbreads on trays and gently spread thinly and evenly over each piece of dough. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and bake for 10-15 minutes (250°C) or until the edges of the flatbreads are golden and crispy. Transfer to a wire rack and cover to keep warm.
  4. Place remaining dough circle onto baking trays. Gently spread remaining meat topping thinly and evenly over each peice of dough. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and bake for 10-15 minutes (250°C) or until the edges of the flatbreads are golden and crispy. Serve with lemon wedges (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, rolling pin, baking trays (30 x 40 cm), bowl, large bowl, wire rack, kitchen towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To achieve a crispy base on the flatbreads, ensure your baking trays are hot and well-greased before placing flatbread onto it to bake. This prevents the breads from sticking. Grease and heat tray in between baking of flatbreads.
  • Freeze any leftover tomato juice to be added to stews or soups.
  • We suggest squeezing a little lemon juice over flatbreads just before serving.
  • Accompany with salad and garnish with chopped mint and parsley, if desired.
