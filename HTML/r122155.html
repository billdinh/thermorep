Banana cupcakes with peanut butter frosting

Ingredients

Banana cupcakes

  • 60 g raw almonds
  • 170 g ripe banana, broken into pieces
  • 2 eggs
  • 70 g freshly squeezed orange juice (see Tips)
  • 80 g pure maple syrup (see Tips)
  • 120 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 190 g wholemeal self-raising flour
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon

Peanut butter frosting

  • 250 g cream cheese, cut into pieces (3-4 cm) and softened
  • 90 g smooth peanut butter
  • 1 ½ tbsp pure maple syrup (see Tips)
  • banana chips, for decorating (optional)

Preparation

Banana cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with paper cases and set aside.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 9, or until finely ground. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add banana and mash 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add eggs, juice, syrup and oil and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add flour, baking powder and cinnamon and mix 8 sec/speed 4, or until combined.
  6. Divide mixture between paper cases and bake for 20-25 minutes (160°C), or until a skewer inserted into the centre of the cupcakes comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Peanut butter frosting

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream cheese and peanut butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add syrup and mix 10 sec/speed 3, or until well combined and smooth. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Transfer icing into a piping bag with a round nozzle (see Tips). Pipe icing onto cooled cupcakes and decorate with banana chips (optional) before serving.

Useful Items

12-hole muffin tray, 12 cupcake liners, wooden skewer, wire rack, piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have no oranges on hand, you can use milk or almond milk instead of orange juice.
  • If you prefer, you can replace the maple syrup with brown sugar, Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar. You can also replace the extra virgin olive oil with coconut oil.
  • For the icing, you may need a fraction more or less syrup depending on the sweetness of your peanut butter. It’s preferable to use a brand of peanut butter with no added salt or sugar (or, better still, make your own). If your peanut butter does have added sugar, you might like to cut the syrup quantity by half. To make a thinner frosting, add 1-2 tbsp of milk when adding syrup in frosting.
  • If you prefer, you can spread the icing over the cupcakes with a small spatula, instead of piping it.

Nut-free cupcakes: For a nut-free version, replace the ground almonds with a ground seed mix, such as a mix of sunflower seeds, pepitas and linseeds. Omit frosting.
