Clementine and Poppy Seed Buttermilk Scones

Ingredients

Scones

  • 35 g caster sugar
  • 2 clementines, thin peelings of skin only
  • 250 g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 60 g salted butter, cold, diced
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 120 g buttermilk
  • 2 Tbsp poppy seeds
  • 1 egg, beaten

Vanilla Butter

  • 150 g salted butter, soft
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place sugar and clementine peel in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour, butter and baking powder then mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add buttermilk and poppy seeds then mix 20 sec/. Tip out onto a lightly floured surface and bring together into a ball. Flatten to 2.5 cm thick then use a pastry cutter (Ø 6 cm) to cut out scones and place on prepared baking tray. Re-roll and cut any trimmings to make 8 scones.
  5. Brush scones with beaten egg then bake for 12-15 minutes (220°C) until golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare butter.

Vanilla Butter

  1. Place butter and vanilla bean paste in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a small serving bowl and set aside.
  2. Enjoy scones warm or at room temperature, accompanied by vanilla butter.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, round pastry cutter (Ø 6 cm), pastry brush, small serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The butter can be made ahead then rolled up in cling film into a log shape and placed in the fridge. Slices can then be served to accompany the scones.
  • Scones are best served the day they are made.

Scones have become synonymous with teatime, and the flavouring in these makes a lovely variation. They’re so delicious they don’t need very much with them, although the vanilla butter takes them to another level.
