Sticky Toffee Sponge Cake

Ingredients

Toffee Sauce

  • 80 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 120 g dark brown sugar
  • 200 g double cream

Sponge

  • 200 g margarine, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g light brown muscovado sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 200 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Buttercream Icing

  • 220 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 350 g icing sugar

Preparation

Toffee Sauce

  1. Place butter, dark brown sugar and cream in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/120°C/speed 2. Transfer to a heat-proof jug and set aside to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make sponge.

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line base of 2 loose-based cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place margarine and light brown sugar in mixing bowl then cream 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cream again 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Without measuring cup, set to speed 3 then add eggs, one at a time, through hole in mixing bowl lid, stopping once fully incorporated.
  4. Add flour, baking powder and vanilla extract then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Finish mixing by hand with spatula if necessary then divide evenly between prepared tins. Bake for 25-30 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tins for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make icing.

Buttercream Icing

  1. Place butter, icing sugar and 120 g cooled toffee sauce in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 30 sec/speed 4.
  2. Bring remaining toffee sauce to a rapid boil in a saucepan on hob then boil for 2 minutes until thickened to a drizzling consistency. Transfer to a heat-proof jug and leave to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. Cut each cooled sponge in half horizontally with a serrated knife. Layer sponges with buttercream then ice top and sides.
  2. Once thickened toffee sauce has cooled, spread over top and drizzle down the sides. Serve in slices.

Useful Items

skewer, wire rack, saucepan, baking paper, serrated knife, heat-proof jug, loose-based cake tins (Ø 20 cm), oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • With all the flavours of a classic sticky toffee pudding, this light and golden sponge stands tall and proud. Drizzled with the thickest, stickiest toffee sauce, this is a true showstopper of a cake that is sure to become a family favourite.
  • Any leftover sauce can be enjoyed drizzled over ice cream or yoghurt.
