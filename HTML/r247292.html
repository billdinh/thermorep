Tiramisu Cheesecake - Cheesecake al tiramisu

Ingredients

Base Layers

  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g digestive biscuits (dark chocolate topped)
  • 2 Tbsp coffee grounds (see tip)
  • 250 g espresso, brewed, cold
  • 60 g sweet Marsala wine
  • 18 - 20 sponge finger biscuits (depending on size)
  • 60 g brandy

Filling

  • 250 g double cream
  • 220 g caster sugar
  • 5 gelatine leaves
  • 750 g cream cheese
  • 2 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 500 g mascarpone
  • 30 g brandy

Topping

  • 385 g double cream
  • 175 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 55 g caster sugar
  • 200 g mascarpone
  • 2 Tbsp dark chocolate shavings, for garnish
  • 1 Tbsp cocoa powder, for garnish

Preparation

Base Layers

  1. Grease and line base and sides of a springform tin (Ø 24 cm) with enough cling film to form a 5 cm overhang all the way round.
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/70°C/speed 0.5.
  3. Add biscuits and coffee grounds then pulse Turbo/2 sec/2-3 times. Press into base of prepared tin using the back of a spoon.
  4. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in espresso coffee, Marsala and brandy. Remove from lid then stir. Dip sponge fingers briefly in mixture to soak, one at a time, and place over biscuit base (break if necessary to fit in a single layer). Chill until needed. Cover and chill remaining coffee mixture. Clean mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place cream and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/98°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, soak gelatine in a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add squeezed-out gelatine and mix 20 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool (stirring occasionally to prevent lumps forming). Clean mixing bowl.
  3. When mixture has cooled, insert butterfly whisk. Place cream cheese and vanilla bean paste in mixing bowl then whisk 20 sec/speed 4.
  4. Without measuring cup, mix 45 sec/speed 3, while adding cooled cream mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add mascarpone and brandy then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Spread mixture over sponge biscuit layer. Cover with cling film and chill overnight. Clean mixing bowl.

Topping

  1. The next day, place 185 g cream in mixing bowl and heat 3 min/95°C/speed 1.
  2. Without measuring cup, and without setting a time, mix speed 1, while adding dark chocolate slowly through hole in mixing bowl lid. Once added, insert measuring cup and mix 45 sec/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl to cool for 30 minutes. Once cool, spread over top of cake then return to fridge to chill for 2 hours. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Meanwhile, place reserved coffee mixture and sugar in a saucepan and reduce over a high heat until slightly thickened. Transfer to a small bowl and allow to cool.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place mascarpone and double cream in mixing bowl then whip 30 sec/speed 3. Spread over top of ganache, swirling with a fork to create peaks.
  5. Decorate with dark chocolate shavings and a dusting of cocoa powder, then drizzle cooled coffee syrup over before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, saucepan, springform tin (Ø 24 cm), refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Tiramisu is undoubtedly one of the most famous desserts to come out of Italy. Literally meaning ‘a pick me up’, it’s a rich dessert consisting of layers of sweet mascarpone cheese, ladyfinger biscuits soaked in coffee and Marsala wine, and grated chocolate. It’s a stalwart of Italian restaurant menus and eaten across the country, with many variations of the traditional recipe to be found. We’ve created our own by combining the familiar tiramisu flavours in a cheesecake. One for a special occasion, this makes for a real showstopper of a pudding!
  • Coffee grounds are ground coffee beans that have been used to make coffee.
  • Swap the brandy for dark rum.
