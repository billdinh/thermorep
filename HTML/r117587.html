Chickpea Pancakes

Ingredients

Houmous

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 240 g tinned chick peas, drained and rinsed (1 x 400 g tin)
  • 30 g tahini
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • ½ - ¾ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp paprika
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper

Pancakes

  • 35 g spring onions, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 130 g red peppers, cut in pieces
  • 120 g chickpea flour (gram flour)
  • 120 g water
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp olive oil, for frying

To Serve

  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • 10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley leaves only, roughly chopped

Preparation

Houmous

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chick peas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, paprika and cayenne pepper then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pancakes

  1. Place spring onions and red peppers in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk then place chickpea flour, water, baking powder, garlic powder, salt and pepper in mixing bowl. Whisk 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then whisk again 10 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Add reserved chopped vegetables and mix 20 sec//speed 2.
  4. Heat 1 tsp oil in a large pancake or frying pan over a medium heat. Transfer batter to frying pan and spread out with spatula. Cook for 5-6 minutes on one side then flip over and cook for a further 3-5 minutes until cooked through.

To Serve

  1. Top pancake with reserved houmous, sliced avocado and cherry tomatoes, then scatter with freshly chopped parsley and serve while pancake is still warm.

Useful Items

pancake pan or frying pan, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Any leftover houmous can be stored in the fridge for 2-3 days.
