Banana split

Ingredients

Coconut ice cream

  • 500 g coconut milk (see Tips)
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 60 g raw sugar

Salted caramel sauce

  • 120 g salted butter
  • 150 g double cream (see Tips)
  • 100 g coconut sugar (see Tips)
  • ¼ - ½ tsp salt

Banana split

  • butter, for frying
  • 4 bananas, halved lengthways
  • raw cacao nibs, to serve (optional)

Preparation

Coconut ice cream

  1. Place all ice cream ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 7 min/80°C/speed 3. Pour mixture into ice cube trays (see Tips) and place into freezer overnight or until frozen solid.
  2. Place frozen cubes into mixing bowl and blend 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 or until smooth and creamy. Transfer into a glass sealable container (see Tips) and return to freezer until ready to serve.

Salted caramel sauce

  1. Place butter, cream, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and cook 5-6 min/120°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.

Banana split

  1. Place a frying pan over medium heat and add butter. Working in batches, add bananas and cook, turning until golden on both sides (3-5 minutes). Place 2 banana halves into each serving bowl and top with 1 scoop of Coconut ice cream, a drizzle of Salted caramel sauce and a sprinkle of raw cacao nibs (optional) to serve.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, frying pan, serving bowls, sealable container, freezer, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Dairy-free banana split: cook bananas in virgin coconut oil and omit the Salted caramel sauce.
  • Baby's serve: because this banana split includes some added sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before serving. However, you can serve fried banana alone (omit cacao nibs), blended, mashed or as finger food, as appropriate for your baby.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small. You may wish to omit cacao nibs as they contain some caffeine.
  • You can replace the coconut sugar with Rapadura sugar in this recipe.
  • You can replace the double cream with pouring (whipping) cream in this recipe.
  • If you would prefer to avoid using plastic ice cube trays, you can instead pour the mixture into a glass or porcelain rectangular dish to freeze. Once frozen, use a sharp knife to cut mixture into cubes before placing back into the mixing bowl. Store any remaining coconut ice cream in the freezer for another occasion.
  • If you have only a 400 ml can of coconut milk on hand, you can use regular milk or pouring cream to make up the difference to 500 g (not suitable for dairy-free). You can also use coconut cream, which will give a slightly richer result.
