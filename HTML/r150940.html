Steamed Lemon Cake

Ingredients

  • 150 g unsalted butter, cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 Tbsp semolina, for dusting
  • 180 g sugar
  • 5 g lemon zest (approx. half lemon)
  • 3 eggs
  • 20 g milk
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 180 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1400 g water
  • 100 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), cut in pieces
  • 1 tsp neutral oil (e.g. sunflower, groundnut)
Nutrition
per 1 slice

1209 kJ / 289 kcal

Preparation

  1. Grease a metal baking tin that fits in the Varoma without obstructing all holes (e.g. Bundt cake tin, Ø 20 cm top and Ø 12 cm bottom, or loaf tin 21 cm x 10 cm x 6 cm), dust with semolina and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and 5 g lemon zest (from approx. half a lemon) in mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter, eggs, milk, 20 g lemon juice, flour, baking powder and salt and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer to prepared tin and cover tin with heat-resistant cling film. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place water in mixing bowl and set Varoma dish in position. Place baking tin in Varoma dish, close Varoma and steam 70 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, to prepare glaze, place chocolate and oil in a heat-resistant freezer bag, seal tightly and set aside.
  5. Remove Varoma and place bag containing chocolate mixture in Varoma dish, next to baking tin, making sure some holes remain unobstructed. Set Varoma back in position and steam a further 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Set Varoma aside, remove baking tin and bag with melted chocolate and set aside. After 5 minutes, remove cling film and allow cake to cool for 30 minutes in tin.
  7. Transfer cake from tin to wire rack. Mix chocolate glaze by kneading it carefully, snip a corner off the bag and pour glaze evenly over cake. Slice and serve warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

metal baking tin (e.g. Bundt cake tin, Ø 20 cm top and Ø 12 cm bottom, or loaf tin 21 cm x 10 cm x 6 cm), heat-resistant cling film, heat-resistant freezer bag, wire rack

Hints & Tricks

  • If you use a silicone baking mould, increase steaming time in step 4 to 80 minutes and increase amount of water for steaming to 1600 g.
    - If using a loaf tin, line with baking paper instead of greasing it.
    - If you are not sure if your freezer bags are heat-resistant, you can make your own: cut a large piece of heat-resistant cling film (approx. 30 cm x 30 cm), place chocolate and oil in its centre and gather the sides and corners to form a pouch, which you can tie with a clip or string.
  • Instead of chocolate glaze you can dust the cake with icing sugar, cover with icing or leave it plain. Refer to The Basic Cookbook for an icing recipe.
    - Lemon Icing: Grind 160 g sugar 15 sec/speed 10. Add 25 g lemon juice and stir 20 sec/speed 3.5.
    - Steamed Chocolate Cake: Omit lemon zest and lemon juice in step 2 and add 15 g (2 Tbsp) cocoa powder and 15 g more milk (total milk 45 g). Then continue with recipe.
    - Steamed Chocolate Cardamom Cake: Prepare chocolate cake as above and add 1-1½ tsp ground cardamom in step 2.
    - Steamed Cupcakes: Prepare ⅔ of lemon, chocolate or chocolate cardamom batter. Place 5-6 heat-resistant cups or glasses ( Ø 6-7 cm) and height approx. 6-7 cm) in Varoma dish. Distribute batter between them then cover each cup with heat-resistant cling film and steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  • No oven? No problem! Steaming produces surprisingly tender cakes, and the gentle heat allows you to melt chocolate for glazing at the same time as the cake steams. Different types of tins or bowls can be used for steaming cakes, as the temperature is lower than in an oven. Have a look at the variations to see the many ways this recipe can be adapted to your preferences.
