Roasted Cauliflower and Hazelnut Orecchiette - Orecchiette con cavolfiore arrosto e nocciola

Ingredients

Roasted Cauliflower

  • 1 cauliflower, approx. 650-700 g, cut in small florets
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, plus 1 tsp for cooking pasta
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 50 g blanched hazelnuts
  • 1200 g water, for cooking pasta
  • 300 g dried pasta, orecchiette shape

Brown Butter Sauce

  • 150 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 150 g double cream
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Preparation

Roasted Cauliflower

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place cauliflower florets on prepared tray. Drizzle cauliflower with oil then sprinkle with thyme leaves, nutmeg, 1 pinch salt and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes (180°C) until browned around the edges. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and toast 6 min/Varoma/speed . Transfer to a chopping board and leave to cool slightly. Once cooled, roughly chop then transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place water and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then bring to the boil 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add pasta and cook time indicated on packet/98°C//speed 1. Drain pasta through Varoma dish, reserving one ladle of cooking water, and transfer pasta to a serving bowl.

Brown Butter Sauce

  1. Place a small saucepan on mixing bowl lid and weigh in butter, then set aside. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in cream, then set aside. Add 1 tsp salt to butter in saucepan then cook over a medium-low heat, stirring often, until butter begins to brown (approx. 5 minutes). Stir in reserved cream, reserved grated Parmesan, garlic powder and pepper then remove from heat.

Assembly

  1. Add roasted cauliflower, most of the toasted hazelnuts and brown butter sauce to cooked pasta then stir to combine, adding a little reserved cooking water if needed (see tip). Serve immediately, garnished with remaining chopped hazelnuts.

Useful Items

baking tray and baking paper, serving bowl, saucepan, ladle, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • With their rounded shape and central indentation, it’s easy to see why this quirky pasta type is named after the Italian orecchia, or ‘ear’. While orecchiette are often eaten with a meat ragù or tomato-based sauce, we’ve moved away from tradition with a more unusual flavour combination. Delicate roasted cauliflower florets are paired with fragrant, toasted hazelnuts that add a subtle crunch. Finished with an indulgent browned butter sauce, this is a modern pasta dish that will impress even the most seasoned pasta eaters.
  • It's common for Italian cooks to add a little starchy pasta cooking water as it helps to bring the sauce together and make it thicker and silkier.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
