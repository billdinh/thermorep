Vietnamese Beef Pho

Ingredients

  • 450 g beef bones, cut in pieces (see tips)
  • 1400 g water
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 6 cm fresh root ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 1 stalk fresh lemon grass, white part only, halved
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp mixed peppercorns
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp dried fennel seeds
  • 80 g fish sauce
  • 50 g soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp lime juice (approx. 1 lime)
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper, to taste
  • 300 - 350 g flat rice noodles (approx. 6 mm wide)
  • 300 - 350 g beef fillet steak, partially frozen, thinly sliced (approx. 3 mm thick)
  • 2 - 3 spring onions, thinly sliced diagonally, to serve
  • 8 - 10 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, to serve
  • 6 - 8 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, to serve
  • 2 sprigs fresh Thai basil, leaves only, to serve
  • 100 g fresh bean sprouts, to serve
  • 1 long red chilli, deseeded if preferred and finely sliced, to serve
  • 1 - 2 limes, cut in wedges, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place beef bones, 1200 g water, onion, ginger, lemongrass, star anise, cinnamon, peppercorns, cloves, coriander seeds and fennel seeds in mixing bowl, then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 60 min/100°C/speed .
  2. Add remaining 200 g water, fish sauce, soy sauce, lime juice, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 30 min/100°C//speed .
  3. Place noodles in a glass bowl, cover with boiling water and leave to soak for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain beef stock in mixing bowl through simmering basket into a large jug. Discard contents in simmering basket.
  5. Drain rice noodles through simmering basket and divide evenly between 6 serving bowls.
  6. Place beef fillet slices on top of rice noodles, then pour over hot beef stock (the hot stock will cook the beef slightly).
  7. Garnish with spring onions, coriander, mint, basil, bean sprouts, chillies and limes. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, serving bowls, large jug

Hints & Tricks

  • If you cannot buy beef bones pre-cut into pieces, please ask your butcher to cut your beef bones into pieces for you. We do not recommend you do this yourself at home.
