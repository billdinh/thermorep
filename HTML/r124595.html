Raw carrot cake

Ingredients

  • 250 g cashews, for frosting (see Tips)
  • water, for soaking

Carrot cake

  • 40 g extra virgin coconut oil, melted, plus extra for greasing
  • 70 g walnuts
  • 150 g cashews (see Tips)
  • 50 g shredded coconut (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 orange, zested, no white pith and juiced (approx. 60 g)
  • 60 g rolled oats
  • 500 g carrot, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 100 g pitted fresh (Medjool) dates
  • 80 g sultanas

Cashew lemon frosting

  • 40 g lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 130 g coconut cream
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp raw honey

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews into it. Cover with water (approx. 400 g) and set aside to soak for 3-4 hours, or until soft. Meanwhile, continue with Carrot cake.

Carrot cake

  1. Grease and line base and sides of a round springform cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cashews, coconut, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, orange zest and oats into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer into bowl with walnuts and set aside.
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl place carrot into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add dates and coconut oil and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  6. Add sultanas and mix 3 sec/speed 6.
  7. Add reserved oat mixture and 60 g orange juice and combine 10 sec//speed 3, folding through any remaining uncombined mixture with spatula. Transfer mixture into prepared cake tin and spread with spatula. Tap cake tin on bench to release any air pockets, then cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 5 hours. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cashew lemon frosting

  1. Drain cashews well. Place cashews into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining frosting ingredients and blend 4 min/speed 1 -6, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 1 min/speed 1 -6, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 6, until smooth and creamy.
  4. Remove cake from refrigerator and smooth surface with the back of a spoon. Remove from tin and place onto a serving plate. Spread frosting evenly over cake and dust lightly with extra nutmeg before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, springform cake tin (20 cm), large bowl, plastic wrap, colander , serving plate, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use raw or activated nuts in both the filling and icing. (You can also use roasted nuts, however, these aren’t a raw ingredient).
  • Try to source organic shredded coconut if possible, since it's generally preservative-free.
  • This cake can be stored in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week (but no longer than the expiration date of your ingredients).
