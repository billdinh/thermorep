Sweet and sour pork

Ingredients

  • 200 g basmati rice
  • 1060 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 50 g plain flour
  • 70 g cornflour, plus an extra 1 tbsp
  • 700 g pork loin steaks, cut into bite-size pieces (approx. 2.5 cm)
  • 40 g soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 150 g tomato passata
  • 80 g rice wine vinegar
  • 80 g white sugar
  • 250 g fresh pineapple, peeled, cored and cut into pieces (2-3 cm - see Tips)
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g red capsicum, deseeded and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2000 g peanut oil, for frying

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Cover with water and set aside.
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour and 70 g of the cornflour into it. Set aside.
  3. Place a separate small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 60 g of the water into it. Add remaining tablespoon cornflour into it, mix to form a smooth paste and set aside.
  4. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pork and soy sauce into it. Add five spice, sesame oil, egg and mix to combine and set aside. Meanwhile continue with recipe.
  5. Place garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add tomato passata, vinegar, sugar, pineapple, spring onions/shallots and capsicum and cook 8 min/120°C//speed 2.
  7. Add reserved cornflour paste and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer sauce into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  8. Transfer reserved rice into simmering basket and rinse until water runs clear. Place remaining 1000 g water and salt into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket with rice and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 4. Meanwhile line a plate with paper towel and set aside.
  9. Place a large saucepan with oil over medium high-heat and bring to 175 °C (see Tips). Add reserved pork pieces, a few at a time, into the bowl with reserved flour mixture and toss to coat. Fry pork in batches, turning frequently until cooked and crispy (approx. 3 minutes).Transfer onto the prepared plate to drain.
  10. Transfer pork into thermal serving bowl with sauce and toss to combine before serving immediately with rice.

Useful Items

bowl, large saucepan, plate, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, small bowl, large bowl, paper towel, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, you can start frying the pork pieces while the rice is cooking.
  • To make this recipe gluten free, replace the plain flour, cornflour and soy sauce with gluten free versions.
  • If pineapple isn't in season, you can use tinned pineapple pieces (drain well before using).
  • To check your oil is at 175 °C, drop a small square of bread into the oil, it should turn golden brown in 30-35 seconds. The oil is now ready for frying. Keep your oil at this temperature while frying.
