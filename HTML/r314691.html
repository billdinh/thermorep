Cevapcici with Ajvar (Balkan sausage with Aubergine and Pepper Dip)

Ingredients

Ajvar

  • 4 red peppers, halved, deseeded
  • 2 aubergines, halved
  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 2 lemons, juice only (approx. 60 g)
  • 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper

Cevapcici

  • 3 - 5 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 75 g onions, quartered
  • 450 g beef mince, broken up
  • 250 g pork mince, broken up
  • 200 g lamb mince, broken up
  • 2 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp frying oil
  • 40 g sour cream, for serving (if desired)
  • 600 g peppers, pickled, , for serving, if desired

Preparation

Ajvar

  1. Preheat grill to 230°C. Place red pepper and aubergine halves cut-side down on a large baking tray then place under hot grill until blackened. Transfer vegetables to large freezer bags and seal then leave to cool.
  2. Peel skins from cooled peppers and aubergines. Place peeled vegetables, onions, lemon juice, parsley, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then blend 1 min/speed 8 until a smooth paste. Transfer to a serving bowl and chill until required. Clean mixing bowl.

Cevapcici

  1. Place garlic and onions in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add beef, pork, lamb, bicarbonate of soda, paprika, salt and pepper then mix 30 sec//speed 3. Mould mixture into approx. 12 finger-long, fat sausages. Cover and place in fridge for 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat oil in a griddle pan over a medium-high heat. Cook sausages until browned all over. Leave to rest before serving.
  4. To serve, place cevapcici in warm pitta bread or flatbread and top with ajvar, sour cream, and pickled peppers (if desired).

Useful Items

large baking tray, freezer bags, serving bowl, griddle pan, cling film, grill, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Ajvar is a Balkan roasted aubergine-sweet pepper mixture, sometimes referred to as vegetarian caviar! The smoky flavour is a great match for grilled or roasted meats, especially lamb.
