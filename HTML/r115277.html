Coconut crêpes with apricot sauce

Ingredients

Pastry cream

  • 30 g gluten free cornflour
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 430 g full cream milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 30 g orange liqueur

Apricot sauce

  • 30 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 125 g white sugar
  • 410 g tinned apricots, drained
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced (approx. 40 g)
  • 120 g orange juice
  • 2 tsp gluten free cornflour
  • 2 tsp water
  • 30 g orange liqueur

Coconut crêpes

  • 4 eggs
  • 270 g coconut milk
  • 30 g gluten free cornflour
  • 40 g arrowroot flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 sprig fresh mint, leaves only, to serve

Preparation

Pastry cream

  1. Place cornflour and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add milk and egg yolks and mix 5 sec/speed 4, then cook 10-12 min/90°C/speed 4, until custard thickens. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add orange liqueur and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and allow to cool completely to room temperature (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Apricot sauce

  1. Place butter, sugar, 300 g of the apricots, lemon zest, lemon juice and orange juice into mixing bowl and purée 15 sec/speed 7, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Place cornflour and water into a small bowl and stir until dissolved. Add cornflour mixture into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add orange liqueur and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coconut crêpes

  1. Place eggs, coconut milk, cornflour, arrowroot and salt into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a jug and set aside for 15 minutes.
  3. Place a non-stick frying pan (15 cm) over medium heat. Ladle 2 tablespoons of batter into frying pan and swirl to thinly cover base of pan. Cook until edges start to lift away from the pan (approx. 1 minute), then flip and cook the reverse side until golden (approx. 1 minute). Place crêpe onto a plate, then cover with a kitchen towel. Repeat with remaining batter to make a total of 16 crêpes. Set crêpes aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching cream carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Transfer cream into bowl with reserved cooled pastry cream and fold through gently.
  5. Place 2 tablespoons of the pastry cream onto each crêpe. Fold crêpe in half, then in half again to form triangles, and place onto a serving platter. Repeat with remaining pastry cream mixture and crêpes.
  6. Pour reserved warm apricot sauce over crêpes. Top with remaining apricots (cut into small wedges) and mint leaves, then serve immediately.
