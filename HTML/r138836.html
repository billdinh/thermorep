Robot cake

Ingredients

Basic chocolate cake

  • 200 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 600 g plain flour
  • 80 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 540 g sugar
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs, room temperature

Buttercream icing

  • 500 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 240 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 1 tbsp boiling water
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • natural food colouring of choice

Assembly

  • lollies of choice, for decorating

Preparation

Basic chocolate cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a lamington tin (18 x 27 cm) and a rectangular cake tin (23 x 32 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place 100 g of the butter, 300 g of the flour, 40 g of the Dutch cocoa powder, 270 g of the sugar, 1 tablespoon of the baking powder, 1 pinch of the salt, 250 g of the milk and 1 teaspoon of the vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add 2 of the eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5. Place three-quarters of the mixture into the lamington tin (18 x 27 cm) and spread out evenly. Place remaining mixture into rectangular cake tin (23 x 32 cm). Set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  5. Place remaining 100 g butter, 300 g flour, 40 g Dutch cocoa powder, 270 g sugar, 1 tablespoon baking powder, 1 pinch salt, 250 g milk and 1 teaspoon vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  6. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  7. Add remaining 2 eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5. Add mixture to rectangular cake tin (23 x 32 cm) and spread out evenly. Tap tins 2-3 times on work surface to level mixture and prevent air bubbles.
  8. Bake cakes for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in tins for 5 minutes, then transfer onto wire racks to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to use (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh approx. 300 g of the icing into it. Add food colouring of choice and stir to combine until desired shade is achieved (this is to cover the hands, feet, ears, neck, and antenna base). Add food colouring of choice (we suggest using a different colour) to remaining icing and stir to combine until desired shade is achieved (this is to cover the head and body).
  2. Trim top of both cakes to create a flat surface. From the 18 x 27 cm cake, cut out the head (16 x 16 cm). Cut remaining rectangle in half lengthways (16 x 4 cm) to make arms. Place next to body and cut arms to desired length, then cut top of arms so they sit flush with body.
  3. From the 23 x 32 cm cake, cut out the body (23 x 23 cm). From remaining rectangle, cut out the neck (3 x 7.5 cm) and 2 legs (5 x 7.5 cm each). From remaining cake, cut into 2 large triangles. One large triangle is used for antenna head, cut remaining triangle into 2 equal triangles for ears.
  4. Cover hands, feet, ears, neck and antenna base with a thin layer of icing (crumb coat) in colour of choice. Cover head and body with a thin layer of icing (crumb coat) in your alternate colour. Transfer all pieces into refrigerator until icing is firm to the touch (approx. 10 minutes). Place body onto a large cake board (approx. 80 x 45 cm) and spread with icing to create a smooth, even surface. Continue assembling robot, icing each body part as you go. Decorate as desired with lollies of choice.

Useful Items

baking paper, lamington tin (18 x 27 cm), rectangular baking tin (23 x 32 cm), wooden skewer, wire rack, bowl, palette knife, cake board or serving plate, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a soft, sifted icing sugar for this recipe.
  • If you are not using icing immediately, place into refrigerator until ready to use. To use, bring icing back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread.
  • We decorated our robot with licorice, coloured chocolate buttons, chocolate biscuits, lollipops and hard candies.
