Sage and buckwheat stuffing balls

Ingredients

  • 500 g filtered water
  • 30 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 1 tsp stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 180 g buckwheat groats
  • 2 brown onions, cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp dried sage
  • ½ tsp dried mixed herbs
  • 1 apple, cored and cut into quarters
  • 25 g slivered almonds
  • 10 g psyllium husks
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place water, mushrooms, stock paste, bay leaf, pepper, ¼ teaspoon of the salt and 10 g of the oil into mixing bowl. Set aside to soak for 10 minutes, then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  2. Add buckwheat and cook 15 min/100°C//speed 2, then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl (see Tips), cover to keep warm and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Place remaining 20 g oil, onion, garlic, sage and mixed herbs into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 2.
  6. Add apple, almonds, psyllium husks, pepper and remaining ½ tsp salt and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  7. Add reserved buckwheat mixture and mix 5 sec//speed 4. Roll tablespoonfuls of the stuffing mixture into balls (approx. 30 balls in total) and place onto prepared baking tray. Bake for 25 minutes (180°C). Serve as desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to Mushroom stock paste recipe on this recipe chip or The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • If you have a second mixing bowl, simply set it aside with the buckwheat to let it soak and proceed with recipe using your second mixing bowl.
  • To save time, you can transfer the stuffing mixture into an oiled baking dish (20 cm), then decorate with fresh sage leaves, spray with oil and bake as per recipe.
  • We recommend serving this stuffing alongside roasted vegetables, potatoes and gravy.
