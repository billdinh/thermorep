Mango and labne slice

Ingredients

Labne

  • 1000 g Greek-style natural yoghurt

Gingernut base

  • 125 g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g gingernut biscuits, broken into pieces
  • 80 g shredded coconut

Labne filling

  • 250 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp gelatine powder
  • 60 g icing sugar

Mango topping

  • 300 g frozen mango, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar
  • 125 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder

Preparation

Labne

  1. Line a simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large jug or bowl. Place large jug or bowl with lined simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt into it. Place into refrigerator to drain for 12 hours. The labne will be soft yet dense when ready. Reuse or discard whey (see Tips).

Gingernut base

  1. Grease a lamington tin (20 x 30 cm) and line with baking paper, leaving a 3 cm overhang on each side. Set aside.
  2. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until melted.
  3. Add biscuits and coconut and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer into prepared tin and press evenly into base of tin, then place into refrigerator to chill. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Labne filling

  1. Place cream and gelatine into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/70°C/speed 2 or until gelatine dissolves. Set aside to cool for approx. 10-15 minutes or until mixing bowl reaches 37°C (see Tips).
  2. Add reserved labne and icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 5 or until smooth and combined. Spread filling evenly over base and smooth with spatula. Place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mango topping

  1. Place mango, sugar, water and gelatine powder into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/60°C/speed 3, then blend 10 sec/speed 7. Set aside to cool to room temperature (approx. 30 minutes).
  2. Gently pour mango topping over labne filling. Place into refrigerator to set for approx. 3 hours or overnight. Cut into squares to serve.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, large jug or bowl, baking paper, lamington tin (20 x 30 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Use any leftover whey in sweet or savoury baking recipes, breads or smoothies.
  • You can replace the frozen mango with frozen raspberries.
  • To check the temperature of the contents in your mixing bowl, simply remove and replace the mixing bowl to display the current temperature.
