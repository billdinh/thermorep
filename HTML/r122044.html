German Bread

Ingredients

  • 500 g buttermilk
  • 160 g treacle
  • 40 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 250 g rye flour
  • 250 g wholemeal bread flour
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 80 g sunflower seeds
  • 1 Tbsp fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp coarse oat flakes
Nutrition

1028 kJ / 246 kcal

Preparation

  1. Line a large loaf tin (900 g/2 lb) with baking paper.
  2. Place buttermilk, treacle and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add rye flour, wholemeal bread flour, plain flour, sunflower seeds and salt then knead 3 min/. Transfer to prepared tin pressing down, sprinkle oats over the top then cover and leave to rest in a warm place for a minimum of 1 hour (or overnight in fridge).
  4. Towards end of resting time, preheat oven to 150°C. Bake for 2 hours (150°C) covering with foil towards end of baking time if top begins to brown too much.
  5. Remove bread from loaf tin and allow to cool completely on a wire rack. Cut in thin slices to serve.

Useful Items

900 g loaf tin (2 lb), baking paper, cling film, aluminium foil, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Try adding 80 g sunflower seeds, or 100 g hazelnuts or walnuts in step 2.
  • In Germany, beet sugar is used to make this bread. Treacle makes a good substitute for us here.
  • Whole grains can be used for this recipe instead of flour for an even more authentic German bread recipe. Place 250 g rye grains in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl. Place 250 g wheat grains in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Add to bowl with rye. Continue with recipe.
  • This bread freezes well, so to fully exploit the long cooking time, make up two batches of dough and bake the two loaves at the same time. Cut in slices before freezing, removing slices as and when needed.
