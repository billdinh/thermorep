Milk bread

Ingredients

  • 300 g full cream milk
  • 50 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 20 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 60 g sugar
  • 550 g baker's flour
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten, for brushing
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
391 kJ / 93 kcal
Protein
3 g
Carbohydrates
16 g

2 g

Preparation

  1. Place full cream milk, butter, yeast and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, lightly grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Add baker's flour and salt and knead 3 min/. The dough will be slightly sticky. If the dough is very wet, add 1 tbsp extra flour and knead a further 30 sec/, if necessary. Place dough into prepared bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside. Transfer dough onto a ThermoMat or lightly floured work surface. Divide dough into 3 pieces and roll each piece into a long thin 'rope' (approx. 45 cm). Join the 3 'ropes' at one end, plait, and join again at the other end. Place plaited dough onto prepared baking tray, cover with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel and leave to prove again in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  4. Once dough has proved, brush with beaten egg and bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C) or until bread sounds hollow when tapped on the underside. Allow to cool on a wire rack then slice and serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, ThermoMat, baking tray and paper, wire rack, pastry brush, oven, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Sprinkle with flaked almonds or pearl sugar after brushing with egg.
  • One minute before the end of step 2, add 80 g raisins.
  • Form dough into small men, using raisins for eyes and buttons.

Milk bread, or Zopf, is traditionally served in Switzerland on Sunday mornings for breakfast.
