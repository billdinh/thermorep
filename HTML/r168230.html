Ingredients

  • 300 g puff pastry, ready-made, in sheets
  • 50 g unsalted pistachio nuts
  • 100 g blanched almonds
  • 1000 g milk
  • 100 g cream
  • 70 - 120 g sugar, adjust to taste
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 20 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 70 g raisins
  • 70 g pine nuts

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 220˚C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Cut puff pastry sheets into long strips (1½ cm wide), distribute them on prepared tray and bake for 15 minutes (200˚C) or until golden and crispy. Allow to cool for 15 minutes.
  3. Place pistachio nuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place almonds into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place milk, cream, sugar, cinnamon and vanilla into mixing bowl and cook 7 min/90°C/speed 3.
  6. Preheat oven to 180ºC. Lightly grease a casserole dish (approx. 30 cm x 24 cm x 6 cm) and set aside.
  7. Cover base of preapred casserole dish with a layer of baked puff-pastry pieces, sprinkle with 35 g raisins, half of reserved chopped almonds, and 35 g pine nuts. Repeat with a second layer of baked puff-pastry, remaining 35 g raisins, second half of chopped almonds and remaining 35 g pine nuts ensuring even distribution of raisins and nuts between the pastry layers. Slowly pour milk mixture on top allowing pastry to soak and absorb the liquid. Allow to stand for 15 minutes.
  8. Sprinkle with reserved chopped pistachio nuts and small pieces of butter and bake for 25 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Useful Items

casserole dish (30 cm x 24 cm x 6 cm), baking tray and paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Substitute chopped pistachios with 30 g desiccated coconut for a different flavour.
  • Substitute puff pastry with milk bread or brioche slices for a quicker and easier alternative.
