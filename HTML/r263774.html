Black Sesame Glutinous Rice Dumplings

Ingredients

  • 25 g black sesame seeds
  • 25 g granulated sugar
  • 10 g unsalted butter
  • 120 g glutinous rice flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g water (see tips), plus extra for boiling

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sesame seeds. Transfer to a small frying pan and toast on the hob over a medium heat (approx. 2-3 mins).
  2. Place toasted sesame seeds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add sugar then grind 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and mix 15 sec/speed 6. Divide mixture into 10 pieces (approx. 5 g each) and roll into balls. Place sesame balls on a plate, cover with cling film and refrigerate. Clean mixing bowl.
  5. Place rice flour and water in mixing bowl then knead 30 sec/ÓÄÅ.
  6. Remove dough and divide into 10 pieces (approx. 20 g each). Roll each piece into a ball and press thumbs into the middle to form a bowl shape. Add a reserved black sesame filling ball, wrap dough around black sesame and seal well. Place on a plate dusted with rice flour and repeat until all dough has been used.
  7. Boil water in a saucepan, place dumplings in saucepan and boil until they float (approx. 5 min). Remove with a slotted spoon and transfer to serving bowls. Add 1-2 Tbsp of cooking water to each serving bowl to prevent sticking and serve immediately.

Useful Items

frying pan, saucepan, cling film, serving bowls, slotted spoon, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • These glutinous rice dumplings are traditionally eaten for the Lantern Festival which is celebrated on the 15th day of the new year. Their spherical shape is said to represent continuity and reunion and will bring good fortune to the family.
  • Different brands of glutinous rice flour will require different amounts of water. The dough should be a little powdery but it should be possible to form dough balls without sticking to hands.
  • If dough begins to crack when forming balls, use a little water to moisten the dry areas and bind the dough together.
  • Uncooked dumplings can be refrigerated for later use.
  • If the surface of the dumplings cracks after refrigeration, rub a little water on the cracks then dip in glutinous rice flour to prevent cracking during cooking.
