Noughts and crosses

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces lemon zest (1 x 4 cm), no white pith
  • 130 g sugar
  • 220 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 350 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder

Preparation

  1. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 8 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add vanilla and egg and mix 1 min/speed 4.
  6. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or well-floured work surface and divide into 2 equal portions. Shape 1 portion into a disc and wrap in plastic wrap. Set aside.
  7. Place remaining dough portion back into mixing bowl. Add cocoa powder and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto silicone bread mat or well-floured work surface and shape into a disc. Wrap in plastic wrap and place both dough portions into refrigerator for 1 hour or until firm enough to roll.
  8. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  9. Transfer chocolate dough onto a silicone bread mat or well-floured work surface and roll out (7 mm thickness). Using a X-shaped cookie cutter (approx. 5 cm), cut out 50 crosses and transfer onto prepared baking trays. Bake for 8-10 minutes (170°C). Allow to cool on trays for 10 minutes, then transfer onto wire racks to cool completely.
  10. Transfer vanilla dough onto a silicone bread mat or well-floured work surface and roll out (7 mm thickness). Using a 0-shaped cookie cutter (approx. 5 cm), cut out 50 noughts and transfer onto prepared baking trays. Bake for 8-10 minutes (170°C) or until lightly golden. Allow to cool on trays for 10 minutes before transferring onto wire racks to cool completely.
  11. Draw a noughts and crosses game board onto the front of a cloth or paper bag. Place 5 noughts and 5 crosses into each bag and serve as a party favour.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, alphabet cookie cutters, rolling pin, wire rack, 2 baking trays, cloth or paper bag, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To freeze biscuit dough, simply wrap in plastic wrap and place into freezer. Dough can be frozen for up to 1 month. Remove from freezer 1-2 hours before rolling and baking. 
  • Gluten free noughts and crosses: To make gluten free biscuits, replace plain flour with 350 g gluten free plain flour.
