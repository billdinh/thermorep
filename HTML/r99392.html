Mushroom croustade

Ingredients

Quick puff pastry

  • 200 g butter, cut into pieces (1-2 cm) and frozen
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g water, chilled
  • ½ tsp sea salt

Mushroom croustade

  • 20 g vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 300 g water
  • 750 g fresh mushrooms, cut into slices (0.5 cm)
  • 3 tsp dried mixed herbs, (e.g. marjoram, rosemary, thyme)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 eggs
  • 20 g plain flour
  • 100 g thickened cream
  • 10 g full cream milk, for brushing

Preparation

Quick puff pastry

  1. Place all pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 6.
  2. Transfer pastry dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured surface, then work into a ball and flatten into a rough square. Wrap pastry square in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to rest for 20 minutes.
  3. Unwrap dough and place onto silicone bread mat or floured surface. Using a rolling pin, roll out dough into a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 60 x 20 cm). Fold dough into thirds and give folded dough a quarter turn to the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice. Divide dough into 2 equal balls, then re-wrap in plastic wrap and set aside to chill in refrigerator until needed.

Mushroom croustade

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a pie dish (25 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed , without measuring cup. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, then weigh mushrooms into it. Sprinkle mushrooms with dried mixed herbs, then secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Stir mushrooms with spatula to ensure even cooking, then secure Varoma lid and continue cooking 4 min/Varoma/speed 2.  Remove Varoma and set mushrooms aside to drain. Discard water from mixing bowl.
  6. Transfer chilled dough balls onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface, then roll out each dough ball into a circle (27 cm in diametre and 5 mm thick).
  7. Line prepared pie dish with one of the dough circles, allowing the excess dough to hang over the sides. Score the second circle of dough with a sharp knife to create an open cross shape in the centre and set aside.
  8. Place salt, pepper, eggs, flour, cream and reserved sautéed garlic into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/80°C/speed 2
  9. Transfer reserved drained mushrooms into lined pie dish and pour over garlic-cream mixture in mixing bowl. Place the second dough circle over the top of pie dish to enclose.
  10. Using your fingertips, press the edges of the 2 pastry circles together to seal and brush with milk. Bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C), or until pastry on top is golden. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

pie dish (25 cm), silicone bread mat, rolling pin, plastic wrap, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Scoring a cross in the top circle of pastry allows steam to escape during baking and prevents the pie and pastry from becoming soggy.
