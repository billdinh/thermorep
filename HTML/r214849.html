Parker Rolls

Ingredients

  • 225 g whole milk
  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced (approx. 1 cm)
  • 70 g sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 425 g plain flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • plain flour, to dust
  • unsalted butter, to grease
  • 1 large egg, beaten (optional)
  • milk, to taste (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place milk and butter in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/50°C/speed 1.
  2. Add sugar and yeast then mix 20 sec/speed 1. Leave to rest for 5 minutes.
  3. Add flour, eggs and salt then knead 3 min/.
  4. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and quickly shape into a ball. Place in a lightly greased bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  5. Place baking paper on a baking tray. Punch down dough and reshape into a ball, then divide into 12 equal pieces. Roll into balls and place on prepared baking tray, so balls are almost touching. Make an egg wash (optional) by combining beaten egg with a splash of milk or water. Brush tops of rolls with egg wash. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Bake rolls for 25-30 minutes (180°C) until tops are golden. Allow to cool for 5 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray and paper, bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve rolls with
  • For traditional Parker House fold over rolls: once dough is divided into 12 equal pieces, flatten slightly and brush the centre of each round with melted butter. Take a chopstick or any similar sized object and make a deep indentation in the center of the circle, without breaking through the dough. Fold over one third of each round and press down to seal. Arrange these folded rolls on a buttered baking tray about 1 cm apart and continue with recipe.
