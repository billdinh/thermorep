Lobster Bisque

Ingredients

  • 32 oz water
  • 4 fresh lobster tails, medium (approx. 4 oz each)
  • 1 oz salted butter
  • 2 oz dry white wine (e.g. chardonnay)
  • 1 oz cognac
  • 4 oz yellow onions, quartered (approx. ½ large onion)
  • 2 oz carrots, cut into ¾ in. pieces (approx. 1 large carrot)
  • 2 oz celery, cut into ¾ in. pieces (approx. 2 stalks)
  • 1 leek, cut into ½ in. pieces, white part only
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 3 sprigs parsley
  • 24 oz seafood stock
  • 2 ½ oz long-grain white rice
  • 2 oz tomato paste
  • 1 ¼ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • &frac18; tsp cayenne pepper, to taste
  • 2 oz heavy whipping cream

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Place lobster tails (shell side down) into Varoma dish, secure Varoma lid. Place Varoma into position and steam 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and allow to cool slightly. Remove lobster meat from shells (see Tip). Cut lobster meat into 1 inch pieces and refrigerate. Reserve shells and discard all but 24 oz hot water, transfer water to a heatproof pitcher and set aside.
  3. With a sharp knife cut lobster shells into equal sized pieces (approx. 1 in.). Place shell pieces and butter into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5 and sauté 5 min/212°F/speed 0.5.
  4. Add wine and cognac and without measuring cup cook 5 min/212°F/speed 0.5.
  5. Add onions, carrots, celery, leeks, garlic, thyme and parsley and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add reserved water and seafood stock. Place simmering basket on top of mixing bowl lid and cook 30 min/212°F/speed 1. Insert simmering basket and holding in place with spatula, strain liquid into a pitcher then strain again through a fine sieve (or 4 layers of cheesecloth) into a bowl. Press remaining contents with spatula to extract as much liquid as possible. Discard solids, reserve broth and rinse mixing bowl.
  7. Place strained broth, rice, tomato paste, salt, pepper and cayenne into mixing bowl and cook with simmering basket on top of mixing bowl lid 20 min/212°F/speed 1. Blend 1 min/speed 8 increasing speed gradually.
  8. Add cream and reserved lobster meat into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 1. Divide bisque between 4 bowls and serve hot.

Useful Items

heatproof pitcher, sharp knife, pitcher, bowls, fine mesh sieve, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To remove lobster meat from shells: cut the cooked lobster tails lengthwise, loosen the meat with fingers and pull it out in one whole section.
  • Serve bisque with chopped fresh herbs (e.g. chives, parsley or tarragon) and sliced buttered sourdough or fresh baguette.
