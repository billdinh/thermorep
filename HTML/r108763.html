Strawberry champagne cake

Ingredients

Strawberry and Champagne jam

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 270 g strawberries (over ripe strawberries are preferred)
  • 100 g white sugar
  • 100 g Champagne

Roasted strawberries

  • 500 g strawberries, hulled and cut into halves
  • 75 g Champagne
  • 10 g caster sugar

Sponge

  • butter, for greasing
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • 80 g cornflour
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 130 g caster sugar
  • 4 eggs, seperated and at room temperature
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp hot water
  • 300 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

Strawberry and Champagne jam

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lemon juice and strawberries and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Add sugar and cook 20-25 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket intead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, or until jam has set. Test to see if your jam has set (see Tips). If jam isn't set, cook for a further 2-3 min/Varoma/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add Champagne and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer jam into a sterilised jar (see Tips) and allow to cool completely. Once cooled, place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Roasted strawberries

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a slice tin (18 x 26 cm) with baking paper, ensuring that there is a 4 cm overhang on 2 sides of the tray.
  2. Place strawberries onto tray. Place tray onto mixing bowl lid and weigh Champagne and sugar onto it. Stir gently to coat. Roast for 10 minutes (180°C), or until soft. Set strawberries and syrup aside. Keep oven heated to 180°C.

Sponge

  1. Grease 2 springform cake tins (20 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place bicarbonate of soda, cream of tartar, cornflour and plain flour into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place caster sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 2-4 min/speed 3, without measuring cup, until stiff peaks form.
  5. Mix 4-5 min/speed 3, adding reserved icing sugar, 1 teaspoon at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. It is important not to rush this process.
  6. Mix without setting a time/speed , adding egg yolks, 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid, until combined. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Add vanilla, hot water and reserved flour mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix for a further 4 sec/speed 3, or until fully combined.
  8. Divide mixture between prepared cake tins and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Transfer cakes onto wire racks and brush each sponge with 2 tablespoons of Champagne syrup. Allow to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  9. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and whip 25-40 sec/speed 4, or until soft peaks form, watching cream carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid over whipping.
  10. Place 1 cake onto a large serving plate and spread 2-3 tablespoons of the jam over sponge base. Spoon half of the whipped cream onto jam and spread evenly over cake. Place ¼ of the roasted strawberries in a single layer over cream. Place second cake on top of strawberries, then spread remaining cream over cake.
  11. Add 1 tablespoon of the jam to remaining roasted strawberries and stir to coat. Place strawberries on top of cream to decorate cake. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, baking tin (18 x 26 cm), baking paper, springform cake tins (20 cm), bowl, wooden skewer, wire rack, pastry brush, serving plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If fresh strawberries are not in season, you can use thawed frozen strawberries.
  • Setting point test: place a saucer into the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 tsp jam onto the chilled saucer. When jam on saucer is cool, push it with your finger. If the jam wrinkles, it is ready. If not, cook jam for a further 2-3 minutes, then test again.
  • Clean jam jars in hot soapy water, then dry in a low oven, or run them through the dishwasher. Pour jam into jars while jars are still warm.
