Traditional fruit jam

Ingredients

  • 500 g fruit, cut into pieces as needed
  • 30 g lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 500 g sugar

Preparation

  1. Place fruit, lemon juice and sugar into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 6. Cook 28 min/Varoma/speed 1 (90°C for strawberry jam, see variation), placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid to prevent splashing.
  2. Test setting point of jam (see tip),  pour into hot-rinsed airtight jars and seal immediately. Turn airtight jars upside down for 10 minutes before turning right side up and allowing to cool. Serve or store in a cool dry place.

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any fruit or fruit combination for this recipe.
  • With certain fruit such as strawberries, the jam may bubble up onto the mixing bowl lid. If this happens, reduce temperature to 100°C or even to 90°C as needed.
  • Strawberry jam: include 1 apple in the 500 g fruit. The pectin in the apple will help the jam reach setting point faster.
  • Only use ripe fruit in this recipe.
  • Setting point test: place a saucer into the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 tsp jam onto the chilled saucer. When jam on saucer is cool, push it with your finger. If the jam wrinkles, it is ready. If not, boil jam for a further 2 minutes, then test again.
  • To ensure jam preserves; clean jam jars thoroughly in hot soapy water, then dry in a low oven or run them through the dishwasher. Pour jam into jars while jars are still warm and proceed as per recipe.
  • This is a traditional jam recipe with sugar acting as the only conservation and thickening agent. Thanks to the high sugar content, this jam can be stored in the pantry for several months. Once opened, this jam will keep in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
