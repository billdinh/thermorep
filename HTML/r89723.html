Turkey breast roulade with apple and sage stuffing

Ingredients

Apple sage stuffing

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 150 g), cut into halves
  • 40 g butter
  • 2 Granny Smith apples, core removed and cut into quarters
  • 8 fresh sage leaves
  • 1 tbsp verjuice
  • 160 g day old bread (approx. 3-4 slices) torn into pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste

Turkey roulade

  • 4 turkey breast steaks
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 700 g water

Preparation

Apple sage stuffing

  1. Place garlic, onion and butter into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add apple, sage and verjuice or vinegar and blend 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add bread, salt and pepper and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer stuffing into a bowl and set aside to cool completely.

Turkey roulade

  1. Wrap turkey breast steaks in plastic wrap and place onto work surface.  Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, lightly pound turkey breast steaks until approx. 5 mm thick, then season with salt and pepper.
  2. Lay 4 sheets of aluminium foil down onto work surface, then line aluminium foil pieces with small pieces of baking paper.
  3. Remove plastic wrap from turkey breast steaks and lay 1 turkey steak into the center of each piece of baking paper. Spread reserved stuffing mixture evenly over each steak, then roll steak up.
  4. Wrap rolled turkey steaks tightly in the baking paper and aluminium foil, twisting the ends to form a firm sausage shape. Place 2 turkey roulades into Varoma dish and 2 turkey roulades onto Varoma tray.
  5. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Swap the turkey rolls in the Varoma dish and tray to ensure even cooking, then secure Varoma lid and steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 2, or until turkey is cooked.
  7. Remove turkey roulades from Varoma and set aside to rest for 5 minutes, then unwrap from aluminium foil and baking paper before placing into refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes). Once cooled, slice turkey roulades and place onto a platter to serve.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, meat mallet or rolling pin, baking paper, aluminium foil, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For added colour, you may like to brown your turkey roulade in a hot frying pan just before serving.
  • Try using the Prune and orange stuffing, Apricot stuffing or Cranberry bacon stuffing in this recipe instead.
  • Substitute the turkey breast steaks in this recipe with chicken breast fillets for a different flavour.
