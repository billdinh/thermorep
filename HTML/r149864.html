Coconut and Chocolate Easter Cake

Ingredients

Coconut Cake and Filling

  • 100 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 140 g self-raising flour
  • 50 g desiccated coconut
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 100 g sugar
  • 100 g coconut yoghurt
  • 150 g apricot jam

Chocolate Coating and Decoration

  • 50 g sunflower oil
  • 300 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), in small pieces or callets
  • 50 g desiccated coconut

Preparation

Coconut Cake and Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line base with baking paper and grease sides of a cake tin (Ø 20 cm).
  2. Place flour, desiccated coconut, baking powder and salt in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place eggs and sugar in mixing bowl then beat 20 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add yoghurt and oil then mix 10 sec/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved flour mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Finish mixing gently with spatula then pour into prepared tin.
  6. Bake for 50-55 minutes (160°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  7. Leave sponge to cool in tin for 5 minutes then remove to a wire rack to cool.
  8. Slice cake in half then spread jam on one half. Sandwich second half on top then return cake to wire rack. Place a large piece of baking paper under wire rack.

Chocolate Coating and Decoration

  1. Place oil in mixing bowl and warm 4 min/80°C/speed 1.
  2. Add chocolate and melt 2 min/37°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt again 2 min/37°C/speed 1. Pour chocolate coating over sponge cake.
  3. Sprinkle coconut on sides of cake then place in fridge for approx. 1 hour. Finish decorating as desired before serving.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), skewer, wire rack, bread knife, baking paper, palette knife, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Decorate the cake with Easter decorations of choice such as eggs and chocolate figures, coloured feathers or fondant figures.
