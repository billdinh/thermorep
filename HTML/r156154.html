Thai-style prawn broth with spicy apples

Ingredients

Spicy apples

  • 4 cooking apple
  • 2 tsp vanilla sugar (refer to recipe in The Basic Cookbook)
  • 4 star anise
  • 4 pinches ground ginger
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 4 pinches ground cinnamon

Thai broth

  • 150 g onions, cut into halves
  • 20 - 30 g fresh ginger, cut into thin slices
  • 1 fresh chillies, deseeded if preferred
  • 15 g olive oil
  • 800 g unpeeled raw prawns, with heads and shells removed and reserved
  • 1400 g water
  • 40 g rice vinegar
  • 30 g fish sauce
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 star anise
  • 3 pinches salt, adjust to taste
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 80 g dried Chinese egg noodles
  • 2 pinches wakame flakes (optional)
  • 100 g fresh bean sprouts
  • 15 fresh coriander leaves

Preparation

Spicy apples

  1. Hollow out the apples with an apple-corer, removing the cores and leaving the bases intact. Place each one onto a sheet of baking paper (20 cm x 20 cm). Fill each apple cavity with ½ tsp vanilla sugar, 1 star anise, 1 pinch ginger, 1 clove and 1 pinch cinnamon. Close each parcel tightly, place into Varoma dish and set aside.

Thai broth

  1. Place onions, ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add olive oil, prawn heads and prawn shells and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed , without measuring cup. Meanwhile, set peeled raw prawns aside in refrigerator.
  3. Add water, rice vinegar, fish sauce, cloves, star anise, salt and pepper to mixing bowl. Place Varoma with apples in position, secure Varoma lid and steam 35 min/Varoma/speed . Remove Varoma and set aside.
  4. Filter broth through a sieve over a large bowl, pressing solids using spatula to extract their juices. Discard solids, rinse mixing bowl well and transfer filtered broth back into mixing bowl. Add egg noodles to mixing bowl, place Varoma back in position, secure Varoma lid and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed . Remove Varoma.
  5. Add wakame (optional), reserved peeled prawns and bean sprouts to mixing bowl. Place Varoma back in position, secure Varoma lid and cook 3 min/Varoma//speed 0.5. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep apples warm. Transfer soup to a serving bowl, add coriander leaves, stir to combine and serve immediately. Serve the apples for dessert.

Useful Items

apple-corer, baking paper, large bowl, plate, serving bowl, fine mesh sieve, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Wakame is an edible seaweed that is often used in Japanese, Korean and Chinese cuisines.
  • If apples are large and prevent closing of Varoma lid, slice off the top portion of the apples.
  • Replace bean sprouts with thinly sliced fresh mushrooms.
