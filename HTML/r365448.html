Pea and Ginger Soup, Lemon Salmon with Broccoli and Potatoes

Ingredients

  • 80 g olive oil
  • 50 g soy sauce
  • ½ tsp brown sugar
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest of half and 25 g juice
  • 4 fresh salmon fillets, skinless (150-180 g each)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 5 - 10 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm), to taste (optional)
  • 100 g shallots, halved
  • 100 g leeks, white part only, cut in pieces
  • 100 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 350 g frozen green peas
  • 450 g water
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 250 g potatoes, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 400 g broccoli florets
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 50 - 100 g single cream, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place 30 g oil, soy sauce, sugar, lemon zest and lemon juice in mixing bowl then emulsify 20 sec/speed 5. Place salmon fillets in a deep dish and pour over marinade from mixing bowl. Refrigerate for 10 minutes and continue with recipe.
  2. Place garlic, ginger (if using), shallots, leeks and remaining 50 g oil in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 7 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, line Varoma tray with baking paper.
  3. Add celery and peas to mixing bowl then chop 8 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add water and 1 tsp salt. Insert simmering basket and weigh in potatoes. Place Varoma dish into position, weigh in broccoli and season with ½ tsp salt. Insert prepared Varoma tray, arrange salmon fillets on the tray, season with ½ tsp salt and pepper then drizzle with marinade. Cover Varoma and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep warm. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, set aside on a plate and cover with a tea towel to keep warm.
  5. Add cream to mixing bowl, insert measuring cup and blend 2 min/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually. Serve soup for starter (see tip), then salmon fillets, potatoes and broccoli for the main course.

Useful Items

tea towel, baking paper, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • A quick, healthy meal full of bright flavours and colours, starting with a vibrant green pea soup with zingy ginger, followed by lemon-marinated salmon with steamed potatoes and broccoli. This multi-level recipe can turn a weeknight dinner into a special treat.
  • If making a carrot soup instead of a pea soup, make sure carrots are cut in small pieces.
  • If the celery stalks have leaves, place a few leaves on top of salmon fillets before steaming to add extra flavour.
  • For a milder taste, omit drizzling salmon with marinade in step 4.
  • After serving the soup, set mixing bowl and covered Varoma back in position so the main course keeps warm while the soup is enjoyed.
