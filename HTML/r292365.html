Breakfast Bars

Ingredients

  • 70 g brown rice
  • 1 Tbsp linseeds
  • 1 Tbsp chia seeds
  • 140 g Pink Lady apple (approx. 1 apple), cored, quartered
  • 200 g dried mixed fruit (e.g. dates, apricots, cranberries, sultanas, goji berries)
  • 90 g rolled oats
  • 50 g puffed brown rice
  • 20 g desiccated coconut
  • 70 g sugar
  • 50 g honey
  • 50 g coconut oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 Tbsp pumpkin seeds
  • 2 medium eggs

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a brownie tin (32 cm x 18 cm) with baking paper, leaving a 2 cm overhang.
  2. Place brown rice, linseeds and chia seeds in mixing bowl then grind 1 min/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place apple in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add dried mixed fruit and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add oats, puffed brown rice, desiccated coconut, sugar, honey, coconut oil, pumpkin seeds, eggs and reserved rice and seed mixture then, with aid of spatula, mix 30 sec//speed 3.
  6. Transfer mixture to prepared tin, then using the back of a spoon gently smooth down to form an even surface. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until lightly browned on top. Leave to cool in tin for 20 minutes then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. Cut into 20 equal-sized bars then serve or store in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

baking paper, airtight container, wire rack, brownie tin (32 cm x 18 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Weekday mornings can be hectic trying to get everyone fed, the kids to school and make it to work on time. These bars, packed with energy-rich oats and healthy seeds, provide a great grab-and-go breakfast solution - just wrap in baking paper and off you go!
  • Sweeter Breakfast Bars: in step 4, reduce mixed dried fruit to 130 g and add 70 g dried pitted dates. Proceed as per recipe.
