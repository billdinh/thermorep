Ombre Cake

Ingredients

Ombre Sponge

  • 80 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 4 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 150 g milk
  • 320 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • pink food colouring paste

Ombre Icing

  • 180 g milk
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 200 g vegetable shortening
  • 1400 g icing sugar
  • 1 ½ Tbsp vanilla extract
  • pink food colouring paste

Preparation

Ombre Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C and grease 5 layer cake tins (Ø 15 cm).
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 20 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add egg whites and milk then mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add flour, baking powder, vanilla extract and salt then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. Remove lid, set scales to zero then remove 180 g at a time into 5 individual bowls, zeroing scales each time (scales will show -180 g). Colour each bowl with different strengths of pink food colouring paste, increasing from light coloured to dark coloured. Transfer mixture from each bowl to a prepared tin.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes (160°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tins for 5 minutes then remove to wire racks to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Ombre Icing

  1. Place milk and salt in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add shortening, icing sugar and vanilla extract then, without measuring cup, beat 30 sec/speed 6 while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and beat again 30 sec/speed 6 while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid.

Assembly

  1. Slice any peaked tops from each sponge if necessary so they are flat. Place darkest coloured sponge on a cake stand then spread with a layer of icing. Repeat with remaining sponges, from darkest to lightest.
  2. Ice sides and top of cake with a crumb layer (see tip) then set aside.
  3. Transfer remaining icing to 5 bowls with a little more in the first bowl. Leave first bowl white then colour remaining bowls with different strengths of pink food colouring paste, increasing from light coloured to dark coloured. Transfer each colour to a piping bag fitted with a straight nozzle then, starting with the darkest, pipe dots onto bottom sponge. Use the back of a spoon to spread the icing, creating a petal effect. Work all the way round the cake, doing 2 rows of each colour before proceeding with next colour. Finish with white rows and pipe this over top of cake as well.
  4. Serve in slices.

Useful Items

5 layer cake tins (15 cm), 5 small bowls, wire racks, skewer, serving plate or cake stand, bread knife, palette knife, piping bags and nozzles, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Using vegetable shortening enables you to create a true white icing which cannot be achieved with butter.
  • If the cake begins to lean when stacked, insert one or two wooden skewers, cut to size, through middle of cake to hold all the layers in place - just remember to remove them before serving!
  • A crumb layer is a thin base layer of icing to which an extra layer of icing is added later on once the first coat is set. It ensures a smooth finish as it seals in any stray crumbs.
  • You can bake and decorate the cake in an ombre style, using different shades of one colour, or you can use different colours for a rainbow sponge and icing.
  • For a rainbow icing; after applying crumb layer, divide remaining icing between five bowls then colour each bowl a different colour.

A cake with a definite wow factor! Make the sponges ahead of time, as the decorative icing will take most of the time…but the challenge is well worth it!
