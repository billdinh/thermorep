Arancini

Ingredients

Rice

  • 130 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 800 g water
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 250 g Arborio rice
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 1 Tbsp water, hot

Red Pesto

  • 150 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, drained
  • 20 g fresh basil leaves
  • 60 g toasted pine nuts
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 40 - 50 g extra virgin olive oil, to taste

Batter

  • 170 g plain flour
  • 160 g water
  • 1 medium egg

Assembly

  • frying oil (e.g. sunflower or vegetable)
  • 200 g breadcrumbs (see tip)

Preparation

Rice

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water, stock cube and salt in mixing bowl then bring to the boil 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add rice and cook 13 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, soak saffron in 1 Tbsp hot water.
  4. Add 100 g reserved grated Parmesan and saffron water then mix well using spatula. Transfer to a wide dish and allow to cool completely, placing in fridge after 1 hour. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Red Pesto

  1. Place sun-dried tomatoes, basil, pine nuts, garlic and pepper in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 8 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl.
  2. Add remaining reserved grated Parmesan then stir 5 sec/speed 3.
  3. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in oil. Set 1 min/speed 3 then slowly pour oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Batter

  1. Place flour, water and egg in mixing bowl then beat 20 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Place 2 Tbsp cooled rice in the palm of your wet hand and shape into a ball. Press a finger in the middle to form a hollow. Add ½-1 tsp pesto filling in the centre then seal and shape into a ball. Set aside and continue until all rice has been used.
  2. Heat frying oil in a deep saucepan or deep fat fryer. Meanwhile, dip rice balls in batter then coat in breadcrumbs.
  3. Once oil is hot, fry arancini, a few at a time, turning in the oil with a slotted spoon, until golden and crisp (approx. 5 minutes). Remove with slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve while still warm.

Useful Items

slotted spoon, paper towels, deep saucepan or deep fat fryer, jug, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own breadcrumbs, place 200 g bread in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10.
  • Any leftover pesto can be stored in an airtight jar in the fridge for up to 5 days. Enjoy with pasta, spread a layer on a salmon fillet or use to stuff a chicken breast.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
  • Part of traditional southern Italian cuisine for centuries, arancini are large rice balls that are stuffed with a savoury filling then coated in breadcrumbs and deep fried. Fillings vary from region to region but one of the most popular is a rich meat ragù with peas. Arancini that are formed with mozzarella in the centre are called arancini di riso al telefono due to the mozzarella forming a long string like a telephone line when broken apart.
  • Add 1 cm cubes of mozzarella in the middle of the arancini for extra flavour.
