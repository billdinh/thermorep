Risotto with Broccoli Rabe

Ingredients

  • 60 g vegetarian hard cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 jar sun-dried tomatoes in oil (total weight 280 g), with oil
  • 2 shallots (approx. 60 g), halved
  • 40 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 10 g olive oil
  • 30 g pine nuts
  • 320 g risotto rice (e.g. carnaroli, Arborio)
  • 60 g white wine, dry
  • 820 g water
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade (see tip)
  • 700 g broccoli rabe (Cime di Rapa, approx. 1000 g untrimmed), cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add tomatoes and their oil then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a large frying pan and set aside.
  4. Place shallots in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 20 g butter and olive oil then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, toast pine nuts in a dry frying pan until golden brown.
  6. Add risotto rice to mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, cook 3 min/120°C//speed 1.
  7. Add wine and, without measuring cup, reduce 1 min/100°C//speed 1.
  8. Add water, vegetable stock and salt then scrape base of mixing bowl with spatula to loosen rice. With simmering basket in place of measuring cup, simmer 12-13 min/100°C//speed 1 until rice is cooked. Meanwhile, heat pan with tomato mixture on hob, add broccoli rabe and cook for 5-10 minutes until cooked then season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  9. Leave risotto to rest in mixing bowl for 1 minute then add remaining 20 g butter, reserved grated cheese and ⅔ broccoli rabe mixture then stir with spatula. Transfer to serving dishes, then top with remaining broccoli rabe mixture. Sprinkle with reserved toasted pine nuts and serve immediately.

Useful Items

large frying pan, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Substitute broccoli rabe with white Swiss chard, cut in pieces (1 cm).
  • Depending on the rice variety used different amounts of liquid are absorbed. If the risotto looks too dry before the rice is cooked, add more liquid.
  • Risotto tastes best when served immediately.
  • For a vegetarian stock paste, omit the Parmesan from the Vegetable Stock Paste in The Basic Cookbook.
