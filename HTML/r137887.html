Plum and Blackberry Upside-Down Cake

Ingredients

  • 125 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g light muscovado sugar
  • 2 - 3 plums, halved, stone removed then cut in wedges (5 mm)
  • 175 g fresh blackberries
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 175 g milk
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease sides and line base of a cake tin (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place 50 g butter and muscovado sugar in mixing bowl then melt 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer to prepared tin.
  3. Arrange plum wedges over caramel around edge of prepared tin, overlapping slightly then pile blackberries in centre. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place remaining 75 g butter and caster sugar in mixing bowl then cream 30 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add egg and vanilla extract then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add flour, milk, baking powder and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Mix again 5 sec/speed 4. Pour batter carefully over fruit. Bake for 35-40 minutes (180°C) until a skewer inserted in sponge comes out clean.
  7. Rest cake for 5 minutes then invert tin onto a serving plate. Serve warm - perfect with cream or ice cream.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, skewer, serving plate, oven

Hints & Tricks

Upside-down cakes are great fun to make, and always look really striking once baked and turned out. Try different fruit variations according to what is in season if you wish.
