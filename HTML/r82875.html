Bakewell Tart

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
  • 35 g icing sugar
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg

Filling

  • 120 g almonds, blanched
  • 65 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 90 g caster sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ tsp natural almond extract
  • 120 g raspberry jam
  • 1 Tbsp flaked almonds

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, icing sugar, butter and egg yolk in mixing bowl then mix 25 sec/speed 4. Tip out and bring together into a flattened ball. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out chilled pastry (approx. 3 mm thick). Transfer pastry to a rectangular tart tin (36 cm x 12 cm), pressing well into corners then trim edges with a knife. Cover with cling film and refrigerate for a further 20 minutes. Meanwhile, make filling.

Filling

  1. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, sugar, eggs and almond extract then cream 1 min/speed 3. Set mixing bowl aside then preheat oven to 190°C.
  3. Once pastry shell has chilled, prick base with a fork then line with baking paper and fill with baking beans. Bake blind for 15 minutes (190°C). Remove baking beans and paper and bake for a further 5 minutes (190°C) until lightly golden. Remove from oven and allow to cool. Turn oven down to 160°C.
  4. Spread jam over base of pastry case. Spoon almond filling mixture over jam then sprinkle with flaked almonds. Return to oven and bake for 25-35 minutes (160°C) until golden and firm to the touch. Serve warm or cold, cut in slices.

Useful Items

cling film, rectangular tart tin (36 cm x 12 cm), rolling pin, baking paper, baking beans, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use homemade raspberry jam for an even better flavour.
  • Rice or dried lentils can be used in place of ceramic baking beans when blind baking the pastry case in step 5.
  • This British classic hails from the picturesque village of Bakewell in Derbyshire. The delicious, distinctive combination of almond and jam makes this a wonderful afternoon tea snack. Can also be warmed and served with custard or cream for pudding.
