Malt loaf

Ingredients

  • 150 g brewed tea (Earl grey or other black tea)
  • 170 g malt extract
  • 40 g pure maple syrup
  • 15 g chia seeds
  • 80 g pitted prunes, cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 120 g sultanas (see Tips)
  • 100 g raisins (see Tips)
  • 1 orange, zested, no white pith and juiced
  • extra virgin olive oil, for greasing
  • 100 g raw almonds
  • 150 g wholemeal spelt flour
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh tea, malt extract, syrup, chia seeds, dried fruit and orange juice into it. Mix with aid of spatula and set aside for a minimum of 2 hours (see Tips).
  2. Preheat oven to 150°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (25 x 10 x 11 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  3. Place orange zest and almonds into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add flour, salt and reserved soaked fruit mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add bicarbonate of soda and baking powder and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Working quickly, transfer into prepared loaf tin and cover with aluminium foil, ensuring foil doesn't touch the batter. Bake for 35 minutes (150°C).
  6. Remove aluminium foil and bake for a further 15-20 minutes or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out mostly clean (the texture of this loaf is sticky, so the skewer will still have some crumbs). Allow to cool in tin for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Serve plain or with butter, or transfer into a sealable container until ready to use (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking paper, loaf tin (25 x 10 x 11 cm), aluminium foil, wire rack, wooden skewer, sealable container, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can soak your fruit for up to 24 hours. The longer the fruit soaks the plumper it will be, resulting in a very moist loaf.
  • You can use any quantity of the dried fruits listed to make a total weight of 300 g.
  • You can store this malt loaf in a sealable container for up to 5 days.
