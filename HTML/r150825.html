Goat's Cheese Flans with Yoghurt Sauce and Leeks

Ingredients

Goat's Cheese Flans and Leeks

  • butter, for greasing
  • 40 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 130 g milk
  • 140 g soft goat's cheese
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 eggs, separated
  • 500 g water
  • 300 g leeks, white part only, sliced (5 mm)
  • 50 g red peppers, diced (1 cm)

Yoghurt Sauce

  • 60 g soft goat's cheese
  • 125 g plain yoghurt
  • 6 fresh chives, chopped
  • 2 - 3 pinches salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste

Preparation

Goat's Cheese Flans and Leeks

  1. Grease and flour 4 heatproof ramekins (approx. Ø 9 cm, height 5 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place milk, goat's cheese, flour and salt in mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add egg yolks and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl and lid thoroughly.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and whisk 2 min/speed 3 or until stiff.
  5. Add reserved egg yolk mixture and whisk 30 sec/speed 1.5. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to prepared ramekins, arrange in Varoma dish and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  6. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and add leeks and red peppers to it. Set Varoma containing ramekins in position and steam 18 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep flans warm. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Empty and clean mixing bowl.

Yoghurt Sauce

  1. Place goat’s cheese, plain yoghurt, chives, salt and black pepper in mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  2. Unmould flans by running a small thin knife around inside edge of each ramekin, then invert onto a plate, carefully helping flan out of ramekin with knife if necessary. Arrange steamed leeks and red peppers around flans, drizzle with sauce and serve hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • These delicate flans make a perfect starter or light lunch. The fresh herbed yoghurt sauce and the leeks steamed until meltingly soft offer a pleasant contrast to the hot cheese flans. You can also experiment with different types of soft cheeses to change the flavour of the dish.
  • The flans can also be served cold as a starter, with fresh green lettuce and diced tomatoes.
    - For a stronger flavour, replace goat’s cheese with blue cheese (e.g. Stilton,Gorgonzola) in the flan and 60 g blue cheese in the yoghurt sauce.
