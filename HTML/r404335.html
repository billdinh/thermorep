Fish pie

Ingredients

  • 100 g leek, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 1 ½ tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 250 g liquid vegetable stock (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 600 g red potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 600 g skinless, firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 sweet corn cob, kernels removed
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only, roughly chopped
  • 80 g milk of choice, plus 1 tablespoon extra
  • 1 tbsp arrowroot flour (see Tips)
  • 30 g butter

Preparation

  1. Place leek into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of the oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add liquid stock and lemon juice. Insert simmering basket, weigh potatoes into it and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 2 or until potatoes are cooked through.
  4. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C. Divide fish and corn between 4 ovenproof bowls (approx. 350mL) or 1 large casserole dish (approx. 1.5L)
  5. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Add parsley, 80 g of the milk and arrowroot to mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 8. Pour parsley sauce over reserved fish and turn to coat.
  6. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place butter, remaining 1 tablespoon milk and reserved potatoes into mixing bowl and blend 12 sec/speed 4 or until mashed.
  7. Spread mash over fish mixture, score with a fork and drizzle over the remaining 2 teaspoons oil. Bake for 25-35 minutes (200°C) or until topping is golden brown and fish is cooked through.

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: for a younger baby, blend together pie filling and topping, adding as much liquid (water or baby's milk) as needed to achieve desired consistency (you may not need any). For an older baby, keep the blended consistency more textured or simply serve as is, cut up as needed.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is, cut up as needed.
  • Like cornflour, arrowroot flour can be used to thicken custards and sauces. Arrowroot is a good option if you wish to avoid genetically modified corn (although you can buy GMO-free cornflour). Just be sure to buy a pure, preservative free product.
  • You can replace the liquid stock with stock paste of choice and water in this recipe. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe. In step 3 replace liquid stock with 2 teaspoons stock paste and 250 g water.
