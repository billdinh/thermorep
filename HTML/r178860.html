Sweet Shortcrust Pastry

Ingredients

  • 100 g sugar
  • 200 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 370 g plain flour
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla sugar, homemade

Preparation

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add butter, flour, egg, salt and vanilla sugar in mixing bowl then, with aid of spatula, mix 25-30 sec/speed 5 until clinging together. Tip out, bring together into a ball and shape into a flattened disc. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a tart tin (Ø 26 cm) or line a baking tray with baking paper.
  4. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of baking paper or cling film to 5 mm thick. Line prepared tin with dough or cut out shapes and place them on prepared tray. For a tart case, prick pastry with fork to avoid air bubbles. Bake tart case for 15-20 minutes (180°C), biscuits for 10-12 minutes (180°C), or until golden. Leave to cool completely on a wire rack before decorating, filling, or storing in an airtight container.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper or tart tin (Ø 26 cm), cling film, rolling pin, biscuit cutters, wire rack, airtight container, baking paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A crumbly pastry, enriched with egg, this is a super simple and reliable Thermomix® recipe. Use for sweet pie cases or to make biscuits.
  • Be careful not to knead the dough unnecessarily with your hands, as this will make the biscuits or tart case tough.
  • If the dough becomes warm and difficult to work with, place rolled out dough, still covered with sheets of baking paper or cling film, in fridge until dough is firm enough to handle.
  • Instead of rolling out the dough with a rolling pin, shape it into a log. For biscuits, roll the log so the cross section is round. For a tart case, make a flatter log. Wrap log in cling film and refrigerate until firm enough to cut into slices. For biscuits, cut round log in round slices (5 mm thick) and bake as directed by the recipe. For a tart case, line the tart tin with long dough slices (5 mm thick), overlapping them slightly, and press dough with fingers to fill in any cracks. Bake as directed by the recipe.
  • Decorated biscuits: decorate biscuit shapes with coloured icing, chocolate coating and sweets.
  • Jam-filled biscuits: spread cool biscuit with jam and place another on top. Dust with icing sugar.
