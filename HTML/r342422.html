No-bake coffee cake

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 200 g macadamia nuts (see Tips)
  • 50 g walnuts (see Tips)
  • 5 pitted dates
  • 1 tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking

Base

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g walnuts
  • 10 pitted dates
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste (see Tips)
  • 100 g shredded coconut

Coffee cake

  • 100 g coconut oil
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 50 g filtered water
  • 25 g pure maple syrup
  • 20 g cacao powder
  • 80 g brewed espresso coffee

Topping

  • 125 g raw cacao butter
  • 30 g cacao powder
  • 60 g raw cashews
  • 10 g chia seeds
  • 50 g brewed espresso coffee, cooled
  • 110 g pure maple syrup
  • toasted hazelnuts, chopped

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh macadamias and walnuts into it. Add dates, salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse nuts and dates. Set aside.

Base

  1. Grease and line a slice tin (16 x 26 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place all base ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer into prepared slice tin and press firmly into base of tin. Place into refrigerator while preparing cake. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coffee cake

  1. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/37°C/speed 2, until melted.
  2. Add remaining cake ingredients, plus drained nut mixture, into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10 or until smooth. Spread mixture evenly over base and smooth top with aid of spatula. Place into refrigerator while preparing topping. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Topping

  1. Place cacao butter into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7, then melt 10 min/37°C/speed 2 or until melted.
  2. Add cacao powder, cashews, chia seeds, coffee and syrup, then blend 40 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for a further 40 sec/speed 6. Spread topping over cake and decorate with chopped hazelnuts. Place into refrigerator until set (4- 6 hours), then cut into slices (approx. 18 slices) to serve.

Useful Items

slice tin (16 x 26 cm), baking paper, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your nuts and dates, soak in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • To make your own vanilla paste, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
