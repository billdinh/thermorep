Falafel in square flatbread (R'Gaif bread) with tahini sauce

Ingredients

Falafel

  • 270 g dried lima beans
  • 90 g dried chickpeas
  • water, for soaking
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 1 fresh red chilli, trimmed, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 - 2 tsp salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander

Tahini sauce

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 150 g tahini
  • 130 g water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp ground cumin

R'gaif bread

  • 180 g water
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 260 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp salt
  • olive oil, for coating

Assembly

  • sunflower oil, for frying (see Tips)

Preparation

Falafel

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh lima beans and chickpeas into it. Cover with water and set aside to soak overnight.
  2. Drain lima beans and chickpeas. Place drained beans and chickpeas, spring onions/shallots, garlic, parsley, mint, chilli, salt, pepper, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, ground cumin and ground coriander into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9, then 1 min 30 sec/speed 5 with aid of spatula. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator for 1 hour. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.

Tahini sauce

  1. Place garlic and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add tahini, water, lemon juice, salt and ground cumin and mix 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

R'gaif bread

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and salt and knead 1 min 30 sec/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, divide dough into 8 equal portions and shape each into a ball. Coat each ball with olive oil and set aside to rest for 10 minutes.

Assembly

  1. Place a deep saucepan over medium heat and add sunflower oil (see Tip). Working in batches, drop tablespoonfuls of falafel mixture into the oil and fry on all sides until crisp and golden brown (approx. 5 minutes). Using a slotted spoon, remove falafels and transfer onto a paper towel to drain. Set aside and cover to keep warm.
  2. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and place into oven. On a silicone bread mat or floured work surface, roll out 2 pieces of dough, stretching each into a thin square (approx. 15 cm).
  3. Arrange squares onto preheated baking tray and bake for 4-5 minutes (200°C) or until bread is just cooked but still soft enough to roll. Transfer bread squares onto a clean kitchen towel and wrap up to keep warm. Repeat rolling and baking with remaining dough balls.
  4. To serve, wrap warm falafels in R'gaif bread and top with reserved tahini sauce (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, baking tray, baking paper, kitchen towel, paper towel, silicone bread mat, deep saucepan, slotted spoon, rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • The amount of oil you need to fry falafels will depend on the size of your pan. You will need enough oil to cover the falafel while frying (approx. 4 cm).
  • Serve wraps with fresh greens and extra spring onions/shallots.
