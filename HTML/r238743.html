Salmon Soufflé

Ingredients

Soufflé

  • 6 sprigs fresh dill, plus extra to taste
  • 1 fresh chilli, deseeded, cut in pieces (optional)
  • 150 g hot smoked salmon, deboned, skinless, cut in pieces
  • 100 g single cream
  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 4 egg whites, from medium eggs
  • 1 pinch cream of tartar

Sauce

  • 60 g leeks, white part only, sliced
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 150 g white wine
  • ½ fish stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 150 g water
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 600 g fresh salmon fillets, skinless
  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • ½ lemon, juice only

Preparation

Soufflé

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  2. Place dill and chilli (if using) in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add smoked salmon, cream, egg yolks, 1 pinch of salt, pepper and nutmeg then blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl thoroughly.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites, cream of tartar and a pinch of salt in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whisk 1-2 min/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk, transfer whisked whites to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl and make sauce.

Sauce

  1. Place leeks and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add wine, stock and water then, without measuring cup, cook 10 min/100°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, fold whisked egg whites into reserved soufflé base using a metal spoon.
  3. Heat oil in an oven-proof frying pan (Ø 26 cm) until sizzling, then add fresh salmon. Pour soufflé mix over salmon and bake for 15-17 minutes (200°C) until the top is lightly browned. Meanwhile, finish sauce.
  4. Add butter and lemon juice to mixing bowl then cook 1 min/100°C/speed 3 then blend 20 sec/speed 3. Add salt and pepper to taste and extra dill then transfer to a serving jug.
  5. Serve soufflé immediately with sauce and buttered toast on the side.

Useful Items

large bowl, serving jug, oven-proof frying pan, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • After placing souffle in the oven, fill a sauce boat or jug with hot water. Discard water just before pouring in the finished sauce; this will help keep the sauce warm.
  • The sauce can be made in advance, blended 3 sec/speed 5, then reheated 3 min/80°C/speed  just before serving.
  • This dish can be made for 2 people using the same quantity of souffle but only 2 pieces of salmon.
  • The sauce may be served with any simple fish dish.
