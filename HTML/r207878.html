Bulgur and Salmon Rolls

Ingredients

  • 300 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 150 g bulgur wheat
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp wasabi paste
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar, plus 2 Tbsp extra for brushing
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 nori seaweed sheets
  • 200 g fresh salmon fillets, skinless, shashimi grade, sliced (1 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp Japanese pickled ginger, drained
  • 1 carrot, cut in matchsticks
  • ½ avocado, thinly sliced (2 mm)

149 kJ / 35.5 kcal

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add bulgur and cook 15 min/80°C//speed . Meanwhile, place soy sauce and wasabi paste in a small dish, mix together then set aside. Place vinegar, sugar and salt in another small bowl and stir until grains are dissolved.
  3. Transfer cooked bulgur to a large non-metal shallow dish and sprinkle vinegar mixture all over. Using a soft spatula, toss gently to distribute vinegar mixture then leave to cool.
  4. Once bulgur is cool, place a sheet of nori, shiny side down onto a bamboo sushi mat. Spoon 7 Tbsp prepared bulgur onto nori sheet and gently spread out with wet, soft spatula (see tip), leaving ⅓ clear at the far end. Lay a strip of salmon, ½ Tbsp pickled ginger, carrot and avocado in a line across the end closest to you, then, using bamboo sushi mat, begin rolling tightly to form a roll. Before reaching the other end brush edge of nori sheet with vinegar-water mixture (see tip) to help seal, then continue rolling tightly. Set finished roll aside then continue assembling remaining rolls.
  5. Slice each roll into 1.5 cm pieces using a wet, sharp knife then serve with reserved wasabi dipping sauce.

Useful Items

shallow bowl or dish, sharp knife, pastry brush, bamboo sushi mat, silicone spatula

Hints & Tricks

  • Many people think sushi means raw fish, but it is actually a reference to vinegared rice combined with various ingredients.
  • Place 200 g water and 2 Tbsp rice vinegar in a bowl to use for brushing and keeping hands and utensils wet so they don't stick to the rice.
  • Traditionally sushi rice should be body temperature rather than chilled, therefore for optimum quality, sushi is best served immediately or within a few hours of making. Wrap rolls in cling film and leave in a cool dry place then slice just before serving.
  • Sushi can be wrapped in cling film and refrigerated. Consume within 24 hours but be mindful of the freshness of the fish.
  • When using raw fish make sure it is sashimi/sushi grade. This ensures the fish has been frozen at extremely low temperatures to get rid of parasites making it safe to eat raw.
