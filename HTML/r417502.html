Turkey and Mashed Potato Croquettes

Ingredients

  • 4 oz yellow onions, in pieces
  • 2 oz celery, in pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • ½ tsp dried sage
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 oz cooked turkey meat
  • 10 oz mashed potatoes
  • 3 large eggs
  • 4 oz bread crumbs
  • neutral oil, to fry
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
247 kJ / 59 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
6 g

2 g

Preparation

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place onions, celery, garlic, parsley, sage, salt, pepper and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and sauté 5 min/250°F/speed 0.5.
  3. Add turkey and mashed potatoes, insert measuring cup and mix 10 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside until cool enough to handle (approx. 10 min.).
  4. Crack eggs into a bowl and whisk lightly with a fork. Set aside.
  5. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in bread crumbs and set aside.
  6. Once reserved turkey mixture is cool enough to handle, divide and shape into oval patties (approx. 1 oz ea.) (see Tip). Refrigerate 10 minutes.
  7. Dip chilled patties into egg mixture then coat in bread crumbs. Transfer to prepared baking sheet.
  8. Heat oil to fry in a small pot over medium heat (see Tip).
  9. Fry croquettes in small batches until golden brown (approx. 2 min. per batch). Transfer to paper towels to drain. Allow to cool slightly, garnish with chopped fresh parsley and serve.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, saucepot, paper towels, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If mixture is too soft to shape, add an additional 2 tbsp of bread crumbs.
  • Serve with cranberry sauce or gravy.
  • For crispy, golden results: use a thermometer to ensure oil temperature is maintained between 350°F-375°F.
