Kirsten Tibballs's Caramel chocolate mousse cake (TM6)

 4.5 (8 ratings)

Ingredients

Caramel powder

  • oil, to grease
  • 250 g white sugar
  • 20 g glucose
  • 1070 g water
  • 60 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 10 g vinegar

Caramel crème brûlée centre

  • 1 gold strength gelatine leaf (approx. 2 g)
  • cold water, to soak
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 250 g thickened cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Hazelnut dacquoise base

  • 25 g caster sugar
  • 40 g icing sugar, plus extra to dust
  • 20 g hazelnuts, skin on
  • 40 g hazelnuts, without skin
  • 4 egg whites
  • 1 pinch cream of tartar

Duo chocolate mousse

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 70 g white sugar
  • 50 g water
  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 140 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), broken into pieces
  • 140 g milk chocolate, broken into pieces

Assembly

  • oil, to grease

Glaze

  • 8 gold strength gelatine leaves (16 g)
  • cold water, to soak
  • 170 g white sugar
  • 160 g thickened cream
  • 50 g white chocolate
  • 160 g glucose

Chocolate decoration

  • 100 g white chocolate, broken into pieces

Preparation

Caramel powder

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) and set aside. Grease and line the sides of a round cake tin (15 cm) or a lipped dish with plastic wrap and place into freezer.
  2. To guarantee the success of recipes using Sugar Stages, please follow the exact quantity and description for all ingredients. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with the other ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  3. Place sugar, glucose and 70 g of the water into mixing bowl, then start Sugar Stages , without measuring cup.
  4. Quickly but carefully, add butter and mix with aid of spatula, then immediately pour onto prepared baking tray. Again, working quickly, but carefully, spread the caramel out into a thin even layer (5 mm thickness). Using the spatula, immediately remove any mixture from the mixing bowl rim, in order to close the lid correctly for pre-cleaning. Set caramel aside to cool and harden (approx. 30 minutes).
  5. Immediately place vinegar and remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl, then start Pre-clean /Fat or caramel. Rinse and thoroughly dry mixing bowl.
  6. Once caramel is completely hard, break into pieces, then place into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 10. Place caramel powder into a freezable container (see Tips) and set aside.

Caramel crème brûlée centre

  1. Place gelatine in a bowl and cover with cold water, then set aside to soak. Once the gelatine leaf is soft and pliable, remove from water and squeeze gently to remove excess water. Set aside at room temperature until required.
  2. Place egg yolks, cream, vanilla and 80 g of the Caramel powder into mixing bowl, then start Thicken /80°C.
  3. Add soaked gelatine and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl to cool to room temperature. Pour into prepared cake tin (from freezer), then return to the freezer until solid (approx. 6 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Hazelnut dacquoise base

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Draw 2 x 18 cm circles onto a large sheet of baking paper, then place the paper, pen-side down, onto a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh caster sugar into it, then set aside.
  3. Place icing sugar and 20 g hazelnuts (with skins) into mixing bowl, then mill 5 sec/speed 8. Using a sieve, sift into a large bowl and set bowl aside, reserving any nuts left in the sieve. Then, place the reserved nuts left in the sieve back into mixing bowl.
  4. Add hazelnuts (without skins) into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/1 time. Place in a separate bowl. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips).
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and cream of tartar into mixing bowl, then whip 1 min 30 sec/speed 3.5, without measuring cup or until soft peaks form.
  6. Mix 3 min/speed 3, slowly adding reserved caster sugar 1 teaspoon at a time, through hole in mixing bowl lid until combined. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into bowl with the sifted sugar and hazelnut meal and fold through with a flexible spatula. Divide the mixture between the two 18 cm circles and spread out evenly with a spatula. Sprinkle each disc with reserved chopped hazelnuts and dust with extra icing sugar. Bake for 15 minutes (170°C). Set aside on tray to cool at room temperature (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

Duo chocolate mousse

  1. Before proceeding with mousse check the crème brûlée has frozen. If not yet frozen, set aside other prepared elements and pause the recipe until it has.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks, sugar and water into mixing bowl, then whip 5 min/40°C/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Pour into a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  3. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until semi-whipped/speed 3 (has some body but still collapses). Remove butterfly whisk. Place in the bowl with egg mixture. Clean and thoroughly dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/50°C/speed 1 or until melted, then gently fold it through the cream and egg mixture until combined (see Tips). Place in a piping bag with a 1 cm nozzle. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Line a flat plate or small tray with baking paper. Remove the base of a round springform cake tin (20 cm) and place the ring onto the prepared plate or tray. Grease the sides with oil or line edges of the tin with acetate sheets. Place 1 of the prepared hazelnut dacquoise into the centre of the ring. Pipe half of the chocolate mousse onto dacquoise and halfway up the tin. Using the back of a spoon spread the mousse up the sides of the cake tin. Place the frozen caramel crème brûlée into the centre of the cake and pipe mousse over the top to cover (approx. 10 mm). Place the second dacquoise on top of the mousse and then cover with remaining mousse. Level the top to make a smooth flat surface and then return to the freezer for 4-6 hours or until frozen.
  2. Once frozen, carefully remove from the tin, then immediately return cake to the freezer until you are ready to glaze (see Tips).

Glaze

  1. Place gelatine in a bowl and cover with cold water, then set aside to soak. Once the gelatine leaves are soft and pliable, remove from water and squeeze gently to remove excess water. Set aside at room temperature until required.
  2. Place a small saucepan onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sugar into it. Place saucepan on the stove over a low heat until sugar is fully dissolved and you have achieved a light caramel colour. Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.
  3. Place cream into mixing bowl and start Kettle /100°C.
  4. Mix 1 min/60°C/speed 2, working quickly but carefully to pour caramelised sugar through the hole in the mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add chocolate, glucose and soaked gelatine, then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Set mixing bowl aside and remove the lid, allowing mixture to cool until temperature reaches 37°C (approx. 50 minutes).
  6. Place a 15 cm cake tin, upside down on a tray or plate and set aside a wooden skewer or toothpick. Remove cake from freezer and place mousse side up onto the upside down cake tin. Immediately pour glaze over the cake, starting from the inside and working outward in a circular motion to the edge of the cake. If any air bubbles are visible in the glaze, pierce them immediately with the wooden skewer or toothpick. Set aside for 10 minutes to set glaze, then place in the fridge to defrost (maximum 2 hours). Clean and thoroughly dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate decoration

  1. Remove the base of a round springform cake tin (20 cm), place the ring onto a piece of baking paper and trace a circle around it. Flip the paper and place, pen-side down, onto a baking tray and set aside.
  2. Place 75 g of the white chocolate into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/50°C/speed 2.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt for a further 2 min/50°C/speed 2.
  4. Add remaining 25 g of chocolate and temper 1 min/speed 2. Allow to cool in mixing bowl until temperature has dropped below 37°C (approx. 5 minutes), then transfer to a paper piping cone or a small piping bag. Snip the tip off the bag and pipe in a lattice pattern onto prepared marked paper, creating lines thick enough so it holds together and won't break when set and lifted off the paper. Allow to set at room temperature (approx. 30 minutes). Once the disc has set, use a dry warm knife and trim any areas you piped outside the lines (see Tips). Place set lattice on top of cake and store in the fridge until ready to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Do not leave caramel for long on the bench as it will become tacky. Once cooled, remaining caramel powder can be stored in a freezable container in the freezer for 3 months.
  • Do a vinegar wash to thoroughly clean your mixing bowl before whipping the egg whites: Insert butterfly whisk, then place 500 g water and 1 dash vinegar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk, empty mixing bowl, rinse with hot water and dry.
  • Dacquoise can be made in advance and stored in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 6 weeks. Dacquoise circles are soft and need to be removed carefully.
  • The chocolate mousse must be used immediately for this cake. Any leftover mousse can be placed into small serving glasses and served as a smaller dessert.
  • Formed chocolate mousse cake must be returned to the freezer after removing from the cake tin and before glazing.
  • Chocolate decoration can be stored in an airtight container for up to 8 weeks.
  • Once your cake is defrosted and lattice completed, it is best served immediately. Keep any remaining cake in the fridge at all times.
  • Recipe video available on YouTube, search 'ThermomixAustralia' on YouTube.
  • This recipe was contributed by pastry chef, Kirsten Tibballs. She recommends using Callebaut chocolate for this recipe.
