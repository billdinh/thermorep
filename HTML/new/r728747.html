Veal sweetbreads with creamy white wine sauce

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

  • 1000 g veal sweetbreads (see Tip)
  • 250 g white vinegar
  • 750 g cold water
  • 600 g water, room temperature
  • iced water
  • ice cubes
  • 300 g spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces, plus extra sliced to garnish
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 100 g white wine
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sweetbreads, vinegar and cold water into it, then cover and set aside for 2 hours. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse sweetbreads.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place room temperature water and drained sweetbreads into mixing bowl, then cook 30 min/100°C//speed . Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Using simmering basket, strain sweetbreads and place in a bowl with iced water and ice cubes (to stop cooking process). Leave for 5 minutes, then place sweetbreads on a chopping board and scrape surface gently with a fork to remove the membrane from sweetbreads. Discard membrane, then cut in half lengthways, then into pieces (approx. 2 cm). Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  4. Place spring onions/shallots, garlic and oil into mixing bowl, then chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then sauté 7 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Add sweetbreads, salt and pepper, then sauté 5 min/120°C//speed 0.5.
  6. Add wine and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 0.5, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  7. Add cream and cook for a further 15 min/100°C//speed 0.5. Season to taste, then serve garnished with extra spring onions/shallots.

Hints & Tips

  • Sweetbread is offal from calves and lambs, in particular the thymus glands and pancreas. You can find it at some butchers, but you may want to call ahead to see if they offer it (as it’s often a specialised item).
