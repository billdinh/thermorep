Christy Tania's Fraisier sponge (TM6)

 4.4 (11 ratings)

Ingredients

Sponge

  • butter, to grease
  • 6 eggs, separated, at room temperature
  • 115 g caster sugar
  • 70 g plain flour
  • 50 g vegetable oil

Vanilla diplomat mousse

  • 10 g titanium strength gelatine leaves
  • cold water, to soak
  • 330 g milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 25 g cornflour
  • 4 egg yolks (approx. 65 g)
  • 300 g thickened cream

Assembly

  • 10 - 12 fresh strawberries, whole, evenly sized, plus extra to decorate (see Tips)
  • 160 g fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced to layer
  • icing sugar, to dust
  • edible flowers, to decorate

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C. Grease and line the base of 2 springform cake tins (20 cm) and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks and 40 g of the sugar into mixing bowl, then whip 5 min/speed 4 or until mixture is pale and fluffy. Remove butterfly whisk. Place mixture in a large bowl. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk (see Tips).
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and remaining 75 g of sugar into mixing bowl, then whip for approximately 2 min 30 sec/speed 3.5, without measuring cup or until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Gently fold whipped egg whites into egg yolk mixture until just combined, taking care not to overmix.
  4. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour into it. Sieve flour over egg mixture and gently fold until just combined, taking care not to overmix.
  5. Place oil and 50 g of the batter into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 2. Add mixture to batter in bowl and gently fold until just combined, taking care not to overmix.
  6. Divide batter evenly between the 2 prepared tins and bake for 14 minutes (175°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean.
  7. Allow to cool in the tins for 5 minutes, then turn cakes out onto a wire rack to cool completely (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile conitnue with recipe.

Vanilla diplomat mousse

  1. Place gelatine leaves in a bowl, cover with cold water and set aside to soak until soft and pliable (approx. 10 minutes).
  2. Place milk, vanilla, sugar, cornflour and egg yolks into mixing bowl, then start Thicken /80°C.
  3. Remove gelatine from water and squeeze gently to remove excess water. Add gelatine to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3 or until gelatine is dissolved completely. Pour custard into a bowl, cover surface directly with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming, and leave for 1½ hours or until cool.
  4. Once custard is cooled. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3.5, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Gently fold into cooled custard. Cover the surface of the mousse directly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 2½–3 hours or until thick enough to pipe.

Assembly

  1. Cut an 8 x 63 cm strip of acetate or a double layer of baking paper and line the side of a 20 cm springform cake tin with the collar, then secure ends with tape. Trim the tops of each sponge level. Place one sponge, trimmed-side up, in the prepared tin.
  2. Trim the tops of the strawberries to form a flat, level base, then cut in half. Place strawberry halves side by side around the side of the tin, top-side down and with cut-side against the tin; press them to the acetate or baking paper collar so there are no gaps.
  3. Stir diplomat mousse to soften, then place in a piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm). Pipe a layer of mousse to cover the sponge base and reach halfway up the side of the strawberries. Using a palette knife or back of a spoon, spread mousse in a thin layer up and over the strawberries to fill the spaces in between and to cover their tops.
  4. Arrange half of the sliced strawberries over the mousse layer, then pipe ½ of the remaining mousse over the sliced strawberries and spread out evenly.
  5. Top the mousse layer with the remaining sliced strawberries, then pipe over the remaining mousse to cover the strawberries. Spread out evenly so the mousse finishes 1 cm below the rim of the acetate or baking paper collar. Place the remaining sponge layer, trimmed-side down, over of the layer of mousse and press down gently. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 3 hours or overnight to set.
  6. Place Fraisier cake on serving plate and gently remove from springform tin, then carefully remove acetate or baking paper collar. Dust the top of the cake generously with icing sugar, then top with extra strawberries and edible flowers before slicing to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Do a vinegar wash to thoroughly clean your mixing bowl before whipping the egg whites: Insert butterfly whisk, then place 500 g water and 1 tablespoon of vinegar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk, empty mixing bowl, rinse with hot water and dry.
  • Acetate is available from cake decorating supply stores, craft stores and office supply shops.
  • The Fraisier sponge can be made up to 2 days in advance and stored, covered, in the fridge. Remove and decorate 45 minutes before serving.
  • Choose similar sized strawberries, approx. 3–4 cm tall, for uniformity around the outside of the cake.
  • If you’d like to make Christy Tania's 20th Anniversary Show Stopper featuring this Fraisier cake, a croquembouche of the Sable choux with lime ganache and the Macarons with strawberry cream cheese ganache, see Cookidoo® for the complete recipe and how to assemble.
