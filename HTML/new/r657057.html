Pull-apart pizza sun

4.5 (62 ratings)

Ingredients

Pizza dough

  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to grease
  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 400 g baker's flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 tsp sea salt

Filling

  • 300 g mozzarella, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3cm)
  • 155 g sun-dried tomato strips, drained and oil reserved
  • 120 g sliced ham, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp dried oregano, to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • sea salt, to taste

Assembly

  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • fresh basil leaves, to garnish

Preparation

Pizza dough

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl with oil and set aside.
  2. Place water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl, then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour, oil and salt, then knead Dough /2 min. Shape dough into a ball and place in prepared bowl. Cover bowl with silicone bread mat or plastic wrap and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place mozzarella and Parmesan into mixing bowl, then grate 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining filling ingredients, including 50 g of the reserved sun-dried tomato oil and mix 10 sec/speed 7 with aid of spatula. Set aside.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a pizza tray (30 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place dough on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Cut into 2 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball, then roll out each ball into a circle (approx. 30 cm). Set one dough circle aside and place the other one on the lined pizza tray.
  3. To make the centre of your sun (see step photos to assist you), press ⅓ of the filling (approx. 200 g) firmly into a small bowl (10 cm), then turn it out onto the centre of the dough on the pizza tray to create a dome of filling.
  4. Then arrange the remaining filling in a circle (4-5 cm wide) around the centre dome, keeping a gap of 2-3 cm from the edge of the dough and also from the centre filling.
  5. Next, to enclose the filling, place the reserved circle of dough on top, to cover the bottom circle. Place the small bowl used to shape your dome, over the covered centre filling and press down gently to form an indentation, creating the centre of your sun.
  6. Next, seal the outside edges of the pizza with a fork. If necessary, dip the fork into a little flour to prevent it from sticking.
  7. To make the sun's rays, use a sharp long knife to first cut the pizza into quarters up to the central indentation, being careful not to cut through the centre dome. Then, cut each quarter in half, up to the indentation, to create 8 sections. Cut those sections in half again, up to the indentation, to create 16 in total. Now turn each section of dough 90 degrees onto its side so the filling is visible, creating the 'rays' of the sun.
  8. Brush pizza sun with beaten egg and bake for 15 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown and cooked through.
  9. The rays will easily pull-apart ready for serving. To serve the sun's centre, simply slice into wedges. Serve warm, garnished with basil leaves.
