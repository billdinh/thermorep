Hints & Tips

  • Congee is a type of rice porridge or gruel popular in many Asian countries, especially in China, where it is enjoyed for breakfast. Traditional congee is prepared by cooking 1 cup of rice with 8-10 cups of water over a low heat for about 3 hours until the rice disintegrates to produce a creamy porridge. Rice congee is served as it is or with savoury ingredients such as fish, meat, peanuts, chopped spring onions and soy sauce. In the West, sweet variations with fruit, nuts or even red beans or adzuki beans have become popular.
  • Rice congee can be enjoyed plain, sweet or savoury and in particular makes for a wholesome breakfast. Store cooked congee in the fridge for your whole week’s supply and simply reheat before serving.
  • Sweet congee: add dried dates, cranberries or raisins and season with cinnamon powder before cooking. Serve with fresh fruit and honey.
  • Savoury congee: before cooking, add 1-2 vegetable stock cubes, to taste, and vegetables of your choice, cut in pieces. Alternatively, reheat cooked plain congee with vegetables, cut in pieces, some vegetable stock paste or dried mushrooms.
  • Add other spices to taste before cooking, e.g. ground cinammon.
