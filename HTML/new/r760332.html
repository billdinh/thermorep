Apple Mousse Crumble with Rosemary Ice Cream (Matthew Kenney)

 1.3 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

Rosemary Ice Cream

  • 795 g oat milk
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 60 g white sugar
  • 200 g sugar syrup (see Tips)
  • ½ teaspoon xanthan gum

Apple Mousse

  • 425 g water
  • 1020 g Granny Smith apple (approx. 6 apples), peeled, cored, halved, each half cut into 4 wedges
  • 215 g white sugar
  • 1 tsp agar agar
  • oil spray
  • 315 g aquafaba
  • 2 tsp coconut cream
  • 1 pinch cream of tartar
  • ½ tsp xanthan gum

Toasted Oat Crumble

  • 1 tsp fresh rosemary, leaves only, chopped
  • 140 g brown sugar
  • 70 g rolled oats
  • 155 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 155 g vegan butter, in pieces (see Tips)

Preparation

Rosemary Ice Cream

  1. Place oat milk and rosemary into mixing bowl and simmer 15 min/90°C/speed 1. Set aside and allow to steep 30 minutes.
  2. Strain steeped milk through a fine-mesh sieve into a large bowl. Discard solids.
  3. Place strained milk, sugar and simple syrup into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 5.
  4. Start Kettle /85°C. Add xanthan gum and start Blend /30 sec. Transfer into ice cube trays and allow to cool. Once cool, freeze overnight or until frozen. Rinse mixing bowl and continue with the recipe.

Apple Mousse

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in apples. Transfer 12 apple wedges onto Varoma tray, insert Varoma tray over apple wedges in Varoma dish, secure Varoma lid and steam 16 min/Varoma/speed 2. Carefully, remove Varoma tray and reserve for garnish. Secure Varoma lid and continue cooking apples in Varoma dish a further 8 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and set aside. Empty mixing bowl.
  2. Place 570 g of the steamed apples and 115 g sugar into mixing bowl and start Blend /40 sec. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add agar and mix 10 sec/speed 5, then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool slightly. Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in 100 g sugar and set aside. Lightly grease 12 ring moulds with oil spray, place on a tray and set aside.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place aquafaba, coconut cream and cream of tartar into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/37°C/speed 3, without measuring cup.
  6. Add xanthan gum and whip 7-10 min/speed 3.5, without measuring cup, until stiff peaks form, while slowly adding reserved sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk. With spatula, scoop out one-third of the whipped mixture and gently fold into the reserved apple mixture until incorporated. Make sure to start folds from the bottom so all is mixed. Once incorporated, add in another third of the reserved whipped mixture. Gently fold in to incorporate and then fold in the final third whipped mixture until very light and airy and there are no visible white streaks.
  7. Spoon mixture into prepared ring molds and freeze until firm. Meanwhile, make the crumble.

Toasted Oat Crumble

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place rosemary, brown sugar, oats, flour, salt, and vegan butter into mixing bowl, then mix 10 sec/speed 5.5. Evenly spread onto prepared baking tray and freeze for at least 20 minutes.
  3. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C). Set aside to cool. Once cooled break into pieces and allow to cool completely. Then, store in an airtight container until ready to use. Meanwhile clean mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. When ready to serve, place frozen Rosemary ice cream cubes into mixing bowl and start Blend /1 min, with aid of spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk and whip 3 min/speed 3.5. Remove butterfly whisk. Remove a frozen Apple mousse from ring mold and place into a bowl. Cut reserved steamed apple wedges into pieces. Place a small scoop of Rosemary ice cream next to the mousse, add 1-2 pieces of reserved steamed apple, sprinkle with Toasted oat crumble and repeat with remaining servings. Serve immediately.

Hints & Tips

  • To make your own simple syrup place 395 g water and 395 g sugar into mixing bowl, then cook 20 min/100°C/speed 2, without measuring cup. Transfer into a jar and place in the fridge to cool completely (approx. 15 minutes). Any leftover syrup can be stored in a sealed jar in the fridge and used in other recipes.
  • To make your own vegan butter, see Cookidoo® for recipe.
