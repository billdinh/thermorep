Poppy seed strudel with crumble topping

 4.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

Poppy seed filling

  • 140 g poppy seeds
  • 300 g boiling water, to soak
  • 20 g raw almonds
  • 20 g walnuts
  • 50 g white sugar
  • 70 g honey
  • 40 g sultanas
  • 10 g unsalted butter
  • 50 g milk
  • 50 g mixed peel
  • ½ tsp natural almond extract
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 20 g orange juice
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 2 tbsp rum (optional)

Dough

  • 120 g milk, plus extra to brush
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 20 g white sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • cold water, to prove

Crumble topping

  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 170 g plain flour
  • 70 g white sugar
  • 70 g icing sugar
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Assembly

  • 1 egg white

Preparation

Poppy seed filling

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh poppy seeds and boiling water into it. Leave seeds to soak for 1 hour, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve and set aside.
  2. Place almonds and walnuts into mixing bowl, then chop 3 sec/speed 6. Place in a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place sugar and soaked, drained poppy seeds into mixing bowl, then mill 15 sec/speed 10.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mill for a further 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add honey, sultanas, butter and milk, then cook 10 min/90°C/speed 3.
  6. Add all remaining filling ingredients, including reserved chopped nuts, and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing 5 sec/speed 5. Spread poppy seed mixture out on a plate and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh milk into it. Stir in yeast to combine and set aside.
  2. Place butter, sugar, egg yolk and 15 g of the flour into mixing bowl, then mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add salt, remaining 235 g flour and reserved yeast mixture and mix 15 sec/speed 4, then knead Dough /2 min.
  4. Form dough into a ball, then wrap in a linen tea towel in a bon-bon shape, securing ends with elastic bands. Place in a large container and cover with cold water. Stand for 1 hour or until dough rises to the surface (see Tip). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Crumble topping

  1. Place all crumble topping ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Place in a bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a baking tray (40 x 35 cm) and a sheet of baking paper (40 x 30 cm).
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 1 egg white into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 3, without measuring cup, until stiff. Remove butterfly whisk. Place whipped egg white on the plate with the cooled poppy seed mixture and gently stir to combine.
  3. Place dough on a floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Using a rolling pin, roll dough out into a large rectangle (30 x 35 cm, approx. 5 mm thick).
  4. With a long edge facing you, spread poppy seed filling over the dough, leaving a 4 cm edge along the top. Insert baking paper halfway under the top edge of the dough. Starting at the bottom edge, roll dough up tightly to enclose the filling.
  5. Roll strudel onto the centre of the baking paper, seam-side down. Using the paper as an aid, transfer strudel on paper to baking tray.
  6. Brush the top of strudel with milk. Take small clumps of crumble mixture and press firmly together to form 2–3 cm pieces, then press lightly into the top of the strudel to cover.
  7. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown and cooked through. Stand strudel on tray for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool. Cut into slices to serve.
