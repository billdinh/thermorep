Crispy Hake with Sous-vide Cauliflower Purée and Pancetta Crisps

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

  • 40 g unsalted butter, diced, plus 1 tsp
  • 100 g double cream
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1400 g water
  • 3 lemons, freshly squeezed juice only (see tip)
  • 1 cauliflower (approx. 260 g), cut in small florets (1 cm)
  • 4 slices pancetta
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 2 hake fillets, boneless, skin on
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 150 g fresh baby spinach

Preparation

  1. Please ensure you have a blade cover before starting this recipe (visit our website for details).
  2. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in 40 g butter and double cream. Add 1 pinch salt then set aside.
  3. Insert blade cover. Place water and juice of 1 lemon in mixing bowl then preheat Kettle /85°C.
  4. In one large vacuum sealer bag (see tip), place cauliflower and reserved cream mixture. Seal using your vacuum sealer as per manufacturer's instructions.
  5. Place sealed bag with cauliflower in mixing bowl, ensuring it is completely covered with water (see tip), then start Sous-vide /55 min/85°C. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper. Lay pancetta out on prepared tray, lay another sheet of baking paper on top of the pancetta, then sandwich shut with another baking tray on top. Bake for 7-10 minutes (200°C) then leave to cool, uncovered on tray, until crisp.
  6. Carefully remove bag with cauliflower from mixing bowl with tongs. Remove blade cover. Empty mixing bowl.
  7. Place cauliflower along with all other contents in vacuum bag in mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 15 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually, or until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste, keep mixing bowl covered and set aside to keep warm.
  8. Heat 1 tsp oil and 1 tsp butter in a large frying pan until very hot and frothing. Season hake with salt and pepper then transfer skin-down to hot pan, frying for 30 seconds or until skin is crisp before turning over and cooking for a further 30-40 seconds until cooked but still slightly transluscent. Remove to a plate and keep warm.
  9. In the same frying pan, add 1 tsp oil then sauté spinach until lightly wilted (approx. 1 minute). Season to taste with juice of 1 lemon, salt and pepper.
  10. Dollop a spoonful of cauliflower purée onto two plates. Carefully transfer fried hake fillets to plates on top of cauliflower purée along with some sautéed baby spinach.
  11. Squeeze juice of 1 lemon over the hake and garnish with two pancetta crisps per portion. Serve hot.

Hints & Tips

  • To protect against discolouration with longer-term use, 30 g lemon juice or 1 level tsp vitamin C or ascorbic acid must be added to the water.
  • We recommend using high-quality ziplock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A). They are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • The amount of water required depends on the volume of the bags. Add more water if necessary to fully submerge the bags, not exceeding the maximum fill level.
