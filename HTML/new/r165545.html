Falafel in square flatbread (r’gaif bread) with tahini sauce

 3.0 (4 ratings)

This shows how many portions this recipe makes.

Ingredients

Tahini sauce

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 stalks fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 150 g tahini
  • 130 g water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ¾ - 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ - ¾ tsp ground cumin, to taste

R’gaif bread

  • 180 g water
  • 20 g fresh yeast
    or 1 heaped tsp dried instant yeast (10 g)
  • 260 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp salt
  • oil, for greasing

Falafel

  • 4 spring onions, cut in pieces
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 5 g fresh parsley
  • 4 stalks fresh mint, leaves only
  • ½ fresh red chilli, cut in halves and deseeded
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 720 g canned chickpeas, drained
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander

Finalising R’gaif bread and falafel

  • 400 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • oil, for frying

Preparation

Tahini sauce

  1. Place garlic cloves and parsley in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  2. Add tahini, water, lemon juice, salt and ground cumin and mix 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film and refrigerate until serving. Rinse mixing bowl.

R’gaif bread

  1. Place water and yeast in mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and salt and knead Dough /1 min 30 sec. Transfer to a lightly floured surface, divide dough with oiled hands into 8 equal portions and shape each into a ball. Coat each ball with oil and allow to rest for 10 minutes. Meanwhile continue with the recipe. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Falafel

  1. Place spring onions, garlic cloves, parsley, mint and chilli in mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  2. Add baking powder, chickpeas, salt, ground cumin and ground coriander and chop again 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  3. If the chickpeas are not completely chopped chop a further 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and shape the falafel mix into 18 small balls (approx. Ø 4 cm). Arrange on a tray and freeze for 15 minutes before cooking.

Finalising R’gaif bread and falafel

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and place in the oven.
  2. On a well floured surface flatten the bread balls with your hand as much as you can, and stretch and shape the dough using your fingers, lifting up the edges and pushing outwards gently. Avoid making holes in the dough. Form 12 cm-15 cm square shapes, as thin as possible. Repeat this step for remaining dough balls.
  3. Arrange dough squares on preheated baking tray and bake for 3-4 minutes (200°C) until the bread is just cooked, and still soft enough to roll. Roll up loosely and cool on a cooling rack.
  4. To cook the falafels, place flour in a bowl, and roll the falafel balls in flour. Put oil in a sauce pan (Ø 20 cm) to a depth of 4 cm and heat on the stove. When the surface shimmers, drop a small piece of dough in the hot oil if it sizzles immediately the oil is hot enough to fry the falafels. Carefully place 3 balls at a time into the hot oil, making sure they don't stick to the bottom. Cook, turning, for about 6 minutes, or until the balls are an even golden brown on all sides. Remove with skimmer and drain on paper towels. Serve falafel with square flatbreads and tahini sauce.

Hints & Tips

  • The falafel balls can be stored in the freezer at the end of step 6.
  • Add a cucumber salad to the falafel and flatbread.
