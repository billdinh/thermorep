Butter Mochi with Pineapple Sherbet (Matthew Kenney) Metric

Ingredients

Poached Pineapple

  • 1 pineapple, fresh (approx. 710 g)
  • water to fully submerge bags (not higher than the max fill level), plus extra for water bath
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • ice cube, for water bath
  • 60 g sugar, to store pineapple

Pineapple Sherbet

  • 395 g pineapple, fresh, skin and core removed, cut into pieces (2.5 cm)
  • 395 g coconut cream
  • 115 g sugar
  • 200 g simple syrup (see Tip)
  • ½ tsp citric acid
  • ½ tsp xanthan gum

Butter Mochi

  • 115 g vegan butter, room temperature, plus extra to grease
  • 395 g sugar
  • 340 g coconut cream
  • 395 g coconut milk
  • 200 g vegan egg substitute, e.g. JUST Egg
  • ¾ tsp natural vanilla extract, pure
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 450 g glutinous rice flour (mochi flour)

Coconut Powder

  • 85 g coconut milk powder
  • 30 g icing sugar

Preparation

Poached Pineapple

  1. With a sharp knife, cut off top and bottom of the pineapple and discard. Stand pineapple upright and slice off peel following the fruit's natural curve and discard. Cut pineapple in half along core then cut each half into 6 long spears and cut each spear in half. Remove hard core from pineapple spears and divide spears between two vacuum sealer bags, arranging so they are not overlapping. Turn top edge of bags inside out (approx. 5 cm) to keep bags open and top edges clean. Seal bags using a vacuum sealer machine as per manufacturer’s instructions (see Tip).
  2. Insert blade cover. Place sealed bags into mixing bowl and add enough water to fully submerge bags. Do not exceed the maximum fill level mark (2.2 l). Remove bags and set aside. Add 30 g lemon juice to water in mixing bowl and heat Sous-vide /15 min/80°C. Meanwhile, fill a large bowl with ice and water and set aside.
  3. Place sealed bags side by side into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bags are completely submerged in water and cook Sous-vide /1 h/65°C. Carefully remove bags using tongs and transfer to prepared ice water. Once cool, remove pineapple from bags, drain and discard juice and sprinkle with 60 g sugar. Store pineapple in an air-tight container and refrigerate until ready to serve. Remove blade cover and rinse mixing bowl with cold water.

Pineapple Sherbet

  1. Place 395 g fresh pineapple into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and start Blend /30 sec, until smooth. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place coconut cream, sugar and simple syrup into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add reserved pineapple purée, citric acid and xanthan gum and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Divide into freezable containers (e.g. aluminum, silicone), cover containers and freeze overnight.

Butter Mochi

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C/350°F. Grease a baking pan (approx. 30 cm x 25 cm x 5 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place vegan butter into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/45°C/speed 2.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add sugar, coconut cream, coconut milk, egg substitute, vanilla, baking powder, salt and mochi flour and mix 20 sec/speed 3.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 20 sec/speed 3.5.
  4. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer batter to prepared pan and bake 55-60 minutes (180°C/350°F) until centre is set. Allow to cool slightly, cover with cling film and refrigerate to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coconut Powder

  1. Place coconut milk powder and icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Slice chilled Butter Mochi into portions. Cut reserved chilled pineapple into 5 cm pieces. Place a serving of Butter Mochi into each bowl, add 2-3 pieces of prepared pineapple and top with a scoop of Pineapple Sherbet (see Tip). Sprinkle with Coconut Powder and serve.

Hints & Tips

  • To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bags add 30 g lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bags. Bags should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l).
  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking; resistant up to 100°C; resistant up to 8 hours of cooking; BPA-free; PVC-free.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: fill a large container or bowl with water. Place portions, side by side, into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 2.5 cm open. Slowly submerge bag into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bag closed just before it is completely submerged. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • To make your own simple syrup place 395 g water and 395 g sugar into mixing bowl and without measuring cup, cook 20 min/100°C/speed 2. Transfer into a jar and place in refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 15 min.). Any leftover syrup can be stored in a sealed jar in the refrigerator and used in other recipes.
  • When ready to serve, remove Pineapple Sherbet from freezer and allow to soften at room temperature (5-10 min.) before scooping.
