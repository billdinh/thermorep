Ingredients

  • 240 g cashew nuts
  • 240 g macadamia nuts
  • 370 g water, divided, plus extra to soak
  • 2 probiotic capsules, powder only, casings removed
  • 45 g lemon juice, divided
  • 1 ½ tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 15 g white miso (or shiro miso)
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 bunch fresh chives, chopped, to taste, divided

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in cashews and macadamia nuts, cover with water and soak for at least 4 hours to overnight. Drain nuts and discard soaking water.
  2. Place drained nuts and 115 g fresh water into mixing bowl and blend 3 min/speed 8 (see Tip). Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend a further 3 min/speed 8 or until smooth.
  3. Add contents of acidophilus capsules, stir with spatula to combine and mix 20 sec/speed 7. Pour mixture into a glass bowl that fits into Varoma, cover with cling film, place into Varoma dish and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Place 255 g water and 30 g lemon juice into mixing bowl. Set Varoma with mixture into position, secure Varoma lid and start Fermentation /12 h/40°C. Remove Varoma and discard water from mixing bowl.
  5. Once fermentation is complete, place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in nutritional yeast, 15 g lemon juice, miso, white wine vinegar, salt and half the chives, fold into cheese mixture. Divide into four portions and transfer each to a piece of parchment. Use parchment to form cheese mixture into logs, twisting ends tightly and refrigerate until firm (approx. 4 hr.).
  6. Once cheese is firm, remove from parchment, roll in remaining chives and slice to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Nutritional yeast is not to be confused with brewer's yeast or baking yeast. It is a natural flavour booster that lends a cheesy, nutty or savoury flavour to many dishes. Available in the natural food section of your supermarket or in health food stores.
  • Noise while blending at high speeds is normal and will subside as the blending continues and the air pockets diminish.
  • The firm Chevre log can be rolled in five pepper spice, mushroom powder, olive tapenade, fresh ground black pepper, or fresh herbs like basil, chives, and dill with a drizzle of olive oil.
  • Chevre can be frozen. When freezing the cheese, wrap it carefully in waxed or cling film and place into a freezer bag. Use within 1-2 months. When ready to use, allow to thaw gradually; leave in the fridge for one or two days to slowly thaw.
