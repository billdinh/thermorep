Cherry Crumble Cake

 4.5 (6 ratings)

Ingredients

Crumble Topping

  • 55 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 80 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • ⅛ tsp almond extract

Cake

  • 500 g black cherries, very ripe, pitted
    or frozen pitted cherries, defrosted
  • 100 g Kirsch (cherry brandy)
  • 315 g plain flour
  • ¾ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 160 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 300 g caster sugar
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 230 g buttermilk (see tip)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp almond extract

Preparation

Crumble Topping

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a springform tin (Ø 23 cm).
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 4 min/50°C/speed .
  3. Add flour, cinnamon, salt and almond extract then mix 3 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside in freezer.

Cake

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in cherries and Kirsch. Stir to combine thoroughly, then set aside to marinate and continue with recipe.
  2. Place flour, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, and salt in mixing bowl then mix 7 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then beat 2 min/speed 3.5 until pale and fluffy. Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add eggs and mix 20 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add buttermilk (see tip), vanilla extract and almond extract then mix 15 sec/speed 3.5.
  6. Add reserved flour mixture and mix 25 sec/speed 3.5. Transfer to prepared tin and tap tin on work surface to remove air bubbles from batter.
  7. Scatter reserved marinated cherries all over the surface of the cake and press gently into the batter using spatula. Sprinkle surface of cake with chilled crumble topping and bake for 1 hour 15 minutes (180°C) or until edges are golden, cake springs back to the touch, and a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean or with only a few crumbs attached. Leave to cool in tin for 15 minutes then transfer to a wire rack.
  8. Leave to cool completely before slicing and serving.
