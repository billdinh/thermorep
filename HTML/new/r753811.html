Sous-Vide Salmon with Lemon and Herbs

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp sea salt
  • 2 fresh salmon fillets, skin on, boneless (approx. 5 oz ea.)
  • 2 sprigs parsley, plus extra to garnish
  • 2 sprigs dill, fresh, plus extra to garnish
    or 2 sprigs thyme, fresh
  • 6 pieces lemon peel, divided
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 63 oz water, to fully submerge bags (not higher than the max. fill level)
  • 2 tsp lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Generously rub salt all over salmon fillets, transfer to a zip lock freezer bag, seal and set aside for 30 minutes (see Tip).
  2. Rinse off salt and pat fillets dry with paper towel. Place each fillet in a vacuum sealer bag (approx. 7 in. x 6.5 in.). Add 1 tbsp oil, 1 sprig parsley, 1 sprig dill and 1 piece lemon peel into each bag and seal bags using a food vacuum sealer machine as per manufacturer’s instructions (see Tip).
  3. Insert blade cover and press down to secure. Place sealed bags into mixing bowl and add water to fully submerge bags. Do not exceed the maximum fill level mark (2.2 l). Remove bags from mixing bowl and set aside. Add 2 tsp lemon juice to water in mixing bowl and heat Kettle /120°F.
  4. Place sealed bags, side by side into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bags are completely submerged in water and cook Sous-vide /45 min/120°F.
  5. Once cooked, remove bags using tongs. Carefully empty mixing bowl to prevent blade cover from falling out. Remove blade cover. Carefully remove salmon from bags, garnish each serving with fresh parsley, 2 lemon peels and dill and serve as desired.

Hints & Tips

  • Dry brining the salmon before cooking sous-vide helps to firm up the texture and prevent it turning watery and mushy.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: Fill a large container or bowl with water. Place a salmon fillet into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 1 inch open. Slowly submerge bag into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bag closed just before it is completely submerged, repeat with remaining fillet. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • Place blade cover & peeler onto mixing knife making sure the upward curved parts are on top of the upper positioned blades. Press down gently to secure it to the mixing knife.
  • To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bags add 1 oz lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • Before placing salmon in vacuum sealer bags, turn the top edge of bag inside out approximately 2 inches from the edge in order to keep the top clean.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bags. Bags should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l).
  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking; resistant up to 212°F; resistant up to 8 hours of cooking; BPA-free; PVC-free.
