Dave Barbaro's spiced pork sausages

 5.0 (2 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 30 g sea salt
  • 10 g paprika
  • 10 g dried chilli flakes
  • 80 g red wine
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1750 g boneless pork shoulder, skin removed but fat retained, cut into pieces (3 cm), partially frozen
  • 300 g pork fat (see Tips)
  • sausage casings (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh all ingredients into it, then stir well to combine.
  2. Working in batches, place 250-300 g of reserved spiced meat mixture into the mixing bowl and mince 20 sec/speed 8. Remove any gristle or sinew and discard, then repeat mincing 20 sec/speed 8 or until evenly minced (see Tips). Place in a large bowl, then repeat with remaining spiced meat mixture. Wearing gloves, massage or knead the meat for approx. 1-2 minutes to combine thoroughly.
  3. Fill sausage maker with mince, thread sausage casings (see Tips) onto nozzle and fill. Cook sausages or store them in the fridge or freezer until needed (see Tips).

Hints & Tips

  • This recipe was supplied from Dave Barbaro from "Barbaro Butchers on Preston - Como WA"
  • When making this recipe in the TM31, you may need to repeat mincing to achieve the desired consistency.
  • Ask your butcher for clean, plain pork fat to use in this recipe.
  • This recipe makes approx. 20 sausages (2 kg). To be cost effective, purchase exact quantities of meat from your local butcher.
  • You’ll find sausage casings at butcher shops. Ask for natural casing, thick or thin, enough to make 2 kg of sausages. Store the casings, submerged in salt in an airtight container, in the fridge for up to 1 month. Thoroughly rinse and clean casings before using.
  • Store homemade sausages in the fridge for 3 days or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
  • This recipe was supplied by Dave Barbaro from "Barbaro Butchers on Preston - Como".
  • Find the sausage maker we used at TheMix Shop.
