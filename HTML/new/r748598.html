Tomato Focaccia

 4.4 (5 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 500 g bread flour
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp dried yeast
    or 20 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 350 g water, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 30 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tsp sea salt, coarse

Preparation

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl and large piece of cling film then set aside.
  2. Place flour, sugar, yeast, oil, water and salt in mixing bowl, mix 20 sec/speed 6. Then knead Dough /2 min.
  3. Transfer sticky dough to greased bowl. Return mixing bowl to base and spin remaining dough off blades 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape out excess dough and add to bowl. Cover with prepared cling film and leave to prove until doubled in size (approx. 45-60 minutes). Towards the end of rising time, grease a large baking tray then set aside.
  4. Spread dough out on prepared baking tray with aid of greased spatula then leave to prove for a further 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  6. Using greased fingertips, make indentations all over the risen dough and press tomato halves, cut sides up, into the dough. Sprinkle evenly with chopped rosemary needles, coarse salt and extra water, then drizzle with olive oil. Bake for 20 minutes (220°C) or until golden brown.
  7. Allow to cool on baking tray for 5 minutes then transfer to a cooling rack. Serve warm or allow to cool completely.

Hints & Tips

  • Dip your fingertips in a little oil before making indentations in the dough in step 6.
  • Fry some red onions in a little olive oil and spread on top before baking. Use or omit cherry tomatoes as desired.
  • Add 40 g sundried tomatoes with the flour in step 2.
  • Sprinkle with 3 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese and some ground black pepper before baking, omitting the extra salt.
