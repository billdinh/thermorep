Brendan Pang's Prawn dumplings with dipping sauce

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

  • 100 g canola oil
  • 1 tbsp dried chilli flakes
  • 350 g peeled raw prawns
  • cold water, to cover
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 2 ½ tsp caster sugar
  • 20 g Chinese black vinegar
  • 30 g tamari
  • 30 g water
  • 50 g canned bamboo shoots, drained
  • 10 g fresh ginger
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt
  • 50 g pork fat, minced (optional)
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper

Dumpling wrappers

  • 125 g wheaten flour (see Tips)
  • 60 g tapioca starch, plus extra to dust
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 125 g boiling water
  • 20 g canola oil
  • 600 g water, to steam
  • fresh coriander, leaves only, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Add chilli flakes and mix 10 sec/speed 1. Pour into a bowl and set aside to infuse and cool for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh prawns into it. Cover with cold water and set aside for 30 minutes to firm up.
  4. Strain chilli oil through a fine-mesh sieve into a jug and discard solids.
  5. Place coriander and 2 teaspoons of the sugar into mixing bowl, then chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  6. Add cooled chilli oil, vinegar, tamari and water, then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Pour dipping sauce into a serving bowl and set aside until ready to serve.
  7. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place bamboo shoots and ginger into mixing bowl, then chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop for a further 5 sec/speed 5.
  9. Drain prawns using the simmering basket, then place on a plate and pat dry with paper towel. Add prawns to mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4 until coarsely chopped.
  10. Sprinkle cornflour and salt over the prawn mixture, avoiding blades, then combine 2-3 min//speed 0.5.
  11. Add pork fat (optional), oyster sauce, sesame oil, wine, remaining ½ teaspoon sugar and pepper, then combine 10 sec//speed 0.5.
  12. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then combine for a further 10 sec//speed 0.5. Place in a bowl, cover with a silicone bread mat or plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dumpling wrappers

  1. Place wheaten flour, tapioca starch and salt into mixing bowl.
  2. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and carefully weigh boiling water into it. Mix 20 sec/speed 2, adding water through hole in mixing bowl lid, until mixture is lumpy.
  3. Add oil and knead Dough /3 min or until smooth. Place dough on a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Divide dough into 4 equal portions, place in a large snap lock bag and set aside to rest for 5 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Shape 1 portion of the dough into a 12 cm log, then divide into 6 portions (approx. 15 g each). Using the palm of your hand, flatten each dough portion into a disc, then use a rolling pin to roll into a thin circle (10 cm diameter).
  5. Place approx. 3 heaped teaspoons of reserved filling in the centre of each dough round. To form the dumpling shape, pinch and pleat half the dumpling wrapper five times, then fold the other half over the filling to meet the pleated edge, forming a crescent shape; press edges together to seal. Repeat with remaining dough rounds and filling to make 24 dumplings.
  6. Line Varoma dish and tray with baking paper. Divide dumplings between Varoma dish and tray, allowing space between them as dumplings will expand while cooking. Place 600 g water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until dumplings and filling is cooked and firm. Repeat with any remaining dumplings, ensuring there is at least 500 g of water in mixing bowl for further steaming.
  7. Serve warm dumplings with dipping sauce and extra coriander.

Hints & Tips

  • Don’t confuse wheaten flour with plain flour. You will need to buy this low-protein flour from a good Asian grocer.
  • If peeled prawns are unavailable or you prefer to peel your own, you will need to buy 700 g raw prawns for this recipe. Peel and devein them to yield 350 g peeled prawns.
  • To freeze unsteamed dumplings, place them in a single layer on a tray in the freezer, then transfer to a freezable container and freeze for up to 3 months. There is no need to thaw the dumplings before steaming, as they steam well from frozen too. Simply adjust the steaming time accordingly.
  • Cooked dumplings will keep in the fridge for up to 2 days.
