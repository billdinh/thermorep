Spinach and smoked salmon roulade

4.7 (13 ratings)

Ingredients

Spinach roulade

  • 4 eggs, seperated
  • 200 g frozen spinach, defrosted, drained and excess moisture removed
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp freshly squeezed lemon juice

Smoked salmon filling

  • 5 sprigs fresh dill, fronds only
  • 350 g cream cheese, softened
  • 20 - 30 g horseradish cream, to taste
  • 200 - 300 g smoked salmon, thinly sliced

Preparation

Spinach roulade

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (25 x 38 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 45 sec/speed 4, without measuring cup or until firm peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Spoon egg whites into a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place spinach, egg yolks, salt, pepper, nutmeg and lemon juice into mixing bowl, then mix 15 sec/speed 3. Add to bowl with egg whites and gently mix to combine.
  4. Transfer mixture onto prepared baking tray and spread out evenly to cover. Bake for 12 minutes (180°C), until firm and lightly browned. Set aside to cool in tray for 5 minutes. Carefully flip over onto a sheet of baking paper slightly larger than the roulade. Gently peel away the paper from the roulade and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Smoked salmon filling

  1. Place dill into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cream cheese and horseradish, then mix 20 sec/speed 3 or until combined. Spread filling evenly over cooled roulade. Lay slices of smoked salmon over cream cheese, leaving no gaps. Using long edge of the roulade, carefully roll into a tight log shape using the baking paper to assist in rolling. Place wrapped roulade in the fridge for a minimum of 2 hours or overnight to firm up.
  3. Remove paper from roulade and slice to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • For a vegetarian option, replace smoked salmon slices with roasted capsicum strips.
  • Roulade will keep covered for up to 3 days in the fridge.
