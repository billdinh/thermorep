Kaiserschmarrn (sweet raisin pancake)

 4.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

  • 5 eggs, separated
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 150 g milk
  • 60 g white sugar
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 5 - 10 g vanilla sugar, to taste
  • 2 tsp grated lemon zest, no white pith
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp raisins
  • 2 tbsp icing sugar, to dust

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk into a thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips). Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl, then beat 6 min/speed 3.5, without measuring cup, until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk and place egg whites into a bowl. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place milk, white sugar, flour, vanilla sugar and lemon zest into mixing bowl, then mix 30 sec/speed 4, adding the egg yolks one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add beaten egg whites and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  5. Place an ovenproof frying pan (see Tips) over medium heat and add butter. Once melted, add all the batter, sprinkle with raisins and fry for approx. 3 minutes, until golden brown. Divide into quarters and turn each piece over, then cook for a further 1-2 minutes, until golden. Transfer to the oven and bake for 5 minutes (180°C). Using 2 forks, tear pancakes into small pieces and sprinkle with icing sugar to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Kaiserschmarrn is a classic and one of the best-known desserts in Austrian cuisine. The name goes back to Emperor Franz Joseph I, who appreciated this dessert.
  • Do a vinegar wash to thoroughly clean your mixing bowl before whipping the egg whites in step 2: Insert butterfly whisk, then place 500 g water and 1 tbsp vinegar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk, empty mixing bowl, rinse with hot water and dry.
  • Make sure to use an ovenproof frying pan. Many plastic handles cannot withstand temperatures above 85°C. If you are not sure whether your frying pan is ovenproof, transfer partially-cooked Kaiserschmarrn to a casserole dish before baking.
  • Serve with apple or blueberry sauce, stewed fruit, plum or apricot jam or a fruit compote (search Cookidoo® for the recipes)
  • Soak raisins in rum overnight and drain before adding.
