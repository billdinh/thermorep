Fruit "splice" bombs

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

Vanilla ice cream

  • ½ vanilla bean, split lengthways and seeds scraped
  • 300 g milk
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 90 g white sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp cornflour (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch sea salt (optional)

Pineapple coating

  • 1 lime, peeled, no white pith, pips removed (optional - see Tips)
  • 100 g coconut water
  • 350 g fresh pineapple, peeled and cut into pieces

Preparation

Vanilla ice cream

  1. Place vanilla bean, scraped vanilla seeds and remaining ice cream ingredients into mixing bowl, then cook 8 min/90°C/speed 4. Carefully remove the vanilla bean, then pour mixture into 3–4 ice-cube trays. Cover with plastic wrap. Cool to room temperature, then freeze cubes for a minimum of 4 hours.
  2. Place frozen ice cream cubes into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Divide ice cream between 8 silicone dariole moulds (100 ml), only filling moulds to two-thirds full. Insert a wooden popsicle stick in the middle of each mould. Freeze for a minimum of 4 hours or overnight, until completely frozen.

Pineapple coating

  1. Remove ice creams from moulds to a freezable container and return to the freezer. Wash and dry moulds.
  2. Place all pineapple coating ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10. Pour about 1 tablespoon into each mould and freeze for 20 minutes. Return ice creams to the moulds, then top each with remaining pineapple coating. Freeze for 4 hours or overnight until frozen. Remove the “splice” bombs from the freezer 5 minutes before removing from the moulds to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Check the cornflour packet to ensure it is gluten free.
  • During our taste testings, we found young children preferred the flavour without the addition of lime, while grown-ups loved the zing. You choose!
  • Substitute the dariole moulds with any other silicone moulds such as a muffin tray, ice cream moulds or plastic cups.
  • This recipe was developed as a twist on Louise Keats' Pineapple lime sorbet from "Sweet Nourish".
