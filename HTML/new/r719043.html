Baguette

Ingredients

  • 330 g water
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • oil, to grease
  • 500 g baker's flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 100 g hot water

Preparation

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl, then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, lightly grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Add baker's flour and salt, then knead Dough /2 min. Place dough in prepared bowl, cover with a silicone bread mat or tea towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1½ hours).
  3. On a floured surface, use a large knife to separate dough into two equal pieces. Gently flatten and roll each piece loosely into a log shape, then dust logs lightly with flour. Wrap loosely in silicone bread mat or cover with a tea towel and leave to prove for 20 minutes. Dust a baguette tray lightly with flour and set aside.
  4. Again, flatten each log very gently into a rectangle. Roll dough again into a log shape. Pinch ends of logs with your fingers to seal. Work logs into baguette shapes, gently rolling back and forth. Place baguettes seam-side down in the prepared baguette tray, cover loosely with silicone bread mat or a tea towel and leave to prove for a further 45 minutes.
  5. Twenty minutes before end of proving time, place a baking tray onto the lowest shelf of the oven. Preheat oven to 250°C.
  6. Once bread has proved, carefully prepare approx. 100 g very hot water in a cup. Score baguettes with 3 shallow cuts on each. Place baguette tray in the oven on the middle shelf. Quickly, but carefully, pour the hot water onto the lower baking tray and immediately close oven door to trap the steam (see Tips). Bake for 20 minutes (250°C), or until dark golden brown. If baguettes seem too brown before end of baking time, reduce oven temperature to 230°C. Place baguettes on a wire rack to cool before slicing to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • To test if baguettes are ready to bake, gently press dough with your fingertip. The indentation left by your finger should slowly disappear. If the dough springs up quickly, more proving time is needed. If the indentation remains, the dough has been left to prove too long. The baguette will not rise as much during baking but the end result will taste the same.
  • Creating the burst of steam at the beginning of baking gives the baguettes their crisp, golden crust. Be very careful not to splash yourself or any glass elements in your oven. You can omit the step of creating the steam if desired.
  • Baguettes are always best eaten freshly baked.
  • Baguettes taste even better if you chill the dough overnight in the refrigerator. After the dough has proven in step 2, gently de-gas the dough by folding it over itself, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and store in the refrigerator. The next day proceed with step 3 as per recipe. The baguettes may need 10-20 minutes longer to prove in step 4 prior to baking.
