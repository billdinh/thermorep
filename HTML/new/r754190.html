Dairy-free Stained-glass Orange Biscuits

Ingredients

Dairy-free Orange Biscuits

  • 120 g light brown sugar
  • 2 oranges, thin peelings of skin only
  • 225 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g margarine
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • edible cake decorations

Stained Glass

  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 45 g orange juice
  • 10 g liquid glucose
  • ¼ tsp food colouring (optional)
  • 1000 g water
  • 50 g vinegar

Preparation

Dairy-free Orange Biscuits

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place brown sugar and orange peel in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula then grind again 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour, margarine and egg yolk in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Tip dough out and bring together into a ball. Wrap in cling film or reusable wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. Halve chilled dough and roll out each piece on a lightly-floured surface until 3-4 mm thick. Cut out biscuits using a cookie cutter of desired shape (6-8 cm wide) then cut out and arrange on prepared baking trays. Remove centre of each biscuit using a cookie cutter (Ø 4 cm). Decorate as desired with edible decorations.
  5. Bake one tray at a time for 12-15 minutes (190°C) until lightly golden. Meanwhile, re-roll any trimmings and cut out more biscuits until all dough is used. Leave to cool on trays for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely and repeat until all biscuits have been baked.

Stained Glass

  1. To guarantee the success of recipes using Sugar Stages mode, the ingredients used must exactly match the quantities and descriptions specified. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing the ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Place cooled biscuits on baking paper on a level, heat-resistant surface or baking tray and set aside.
  3. Place granulated sugar, orange juice, glucose syrup and food colouring (if using) in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, start Sugar Stages .
  4. Immediately after cooking, carefully pour or spoon hot syrup into holes in biscuits (see tip). Immediately remove or dissolve with hot water any hardened syrup on mixing bowl rim so mixing bowl lid closes securely for Pre-clean mode.
  5. Place water and vinegar in mixing bowl then start Pre-clean .
  6. Leave syrup to cool completely and harden (10-15 minutes) then carefully remove from baking paper. Wrap individually in cellophane or cling film and store in an airtight container until needed (see tip).

Useful Items

airtight container, baking paper, rolling pin, cookie cutter (Ø 4 cm), cookie cutter, oven, wire rack, baking trays, cling film or reusable wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tips

  • The syrup hardens quickly so consider recruiting an extra pair of hands to help you pour.
  • If desired, drizzle any remaining syrup over biscuits to decorate or spoon discs onto baking paper and press in the ends of lollipop sticks.
  • Biscuits should be wrapped and stored in an airtight container in a cool, dark place to prevent the stained glass from absorbing moisture. They should keep a few days like this depending on the humidity.
  • To make hanging decorations, after transferring biscuits to baking tray in step 4, use a skewer or straw to make a hole towards the top of each biscuit. Thread string through hole at the top of each biscuit after baking and tie a knot.
