Sernik rosa slice

 5.0 (2 ratings)

Ingredients

Base

  • 65 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (1-2 cm), plus extra to grease
  • 50 g milk
  • 15 g white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 100 g self-raising flour

Cheesecake filling

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 250 g white sugar
  • 1000 g quark (see Tips)
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 80 g cornflour
  • 650 g milk
  • 40 g freshly squeezed lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 120 g grapeseed oil

Meringue topping

  • 100 g white sugar
  • 5 egg whites
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Preparation

Base

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line base and sides of a rectangular baking dish (32 x 22 x 7 cm), then set aside.
  2. Place all base ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 6 or until smooth. Spoon batter into prepared baking dish and spread evenly over the base, then set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cheesecake filling

  1. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl, then mill 20 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add all remaining filling ingredients and mix 3 min/speed 6.
  3. Carefully pour mixture over reserved base. Bake for 50 minutes (170°C) or until light golden brown and just set (10 minutes before the end of the baking time, proceed with meringue topping). Remove from oven and set aside in baking dish.

Meringue topping

  1. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips). Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a small bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl, then beat 3 min/speed 3, without measuring cup.
  3. Beat for a further 3 min/speed 3, slowly adding reserved icing sugar 1 teaspoon at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Spoon meringue over warm cheesecake straight from the oven and spread evenly. Return to the oven and bake for a further 10 minutes (170°C) or until set and lightly golden on top. Cool slice at room temperature for 30 minutes before refrigerating until firm. (On standing and refrigeration, the slice will form droplets of ʻdewʼ on top known in Polish as ʻrosaʼ.) Cut into slices to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • To make your own quark for use in this recipe, search Cookidoo®. You will need to make 2 batches of quark.
  • To thoroughly wash your mixing bowl, do a vinegar wash: Insert butterfly whisk, then place 500 g water and 1 dash vinegar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk and empty mixing bowl, rinse with hot water and dry.
  • Sernik rosa will keep in the fridge for up to 5 days.
  • TM31 users: Reduce quark quantities to 900 g and milk to 600 g. All other quantities and the method remain the same.
