Black-eyed Bean Stew with Vegetables

 4.3 (7 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 200 g black-eyed beans, dried (see tip)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 800 g water, plus extra for soaking beans
  • 150 g carrots
  • 150 g leeks
  • 300 g waxy potatoes (e.g. Charlotte), peeled
  • 1 - 2 tomatoes, to taste
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • 20 g cider vinegar
  • 40 g soy sauce
  • 20 g maple syrup
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp sweet paprika
  • ¾ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
    or 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in beans and cover generously with water. Leave to soak for 10 hours or overnight.
  2. The next day, place garlic, onions and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 4.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, drain and rinse soaked beans.
  3. Add drained beans and water then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, cut carrots, leeks and potatoes in pieces (2-3 cm) and quarter tomatoes (see tip).
  4. Add tomato purée, vinegar, soy sauce, maple syrup, salt, turmeric, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, vegetable stock, prepared carrots, leeks, potatoes and tomatoes then start Slow Cook /1 h/98°C. Leave to rest for a few minutes before serving (see tip).

Hints & Tips

  • When using other types of beans, adjust cooking time accordingly.
  • Cut the vegetables in large chunks to retain their shape and texture.
  • Serve with toasted bread or fresh crusty bread.
  • Substitute or add vegetables according to your preference. For example, try sweet potatoes or green beans. Avoid soft vegetables as they are easily overcooked and, if adding vegetables, make sure not to go over the maximum fill mark.
