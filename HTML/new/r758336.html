Gingerbread House

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

Ginger biscuits

  • 375 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 85 g unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 150 g brown sugar
  • 1 egg, room temperature
  • 120 g maple syrup

Royal icing

  • 300 g caster sugar
  • 1 egg white
  • ¼ tsp lemon juice

Assembly

  • 50 - 80 g breakfast cereals (oatmeal squares), for decorating (optional)
  • 10 - 15 multi-coloured button-shaped chocolates, for decorating (optional)
  • 3 pieces shortbread biscuits, for decorating (optional)
  • 6 pieces mini pretzels, for decorating (optional)
  • 1 - 3 sprigs dill, for decorating (optional)

Preparation

Ginger biscuits

  1. Place flour, baking soda, ground ginger, ground cinnamon and salt in mixing bowl, pulverize Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place butter and brown sugar in mixing bowl, whisk 2 min/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Whisk again 2 min/speed 3.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add egg and maple syrup, mix 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved flour mixture, mix 20 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Tip out and bring together into a disc, then wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line a layer of parchment paper on baking tray. Using rolling pin, roll out dough into thin sheet (approx. 2 mm thickness). Using gingerbread house mould, cut to the size of the mould and remove excess dough. Arrange dough sheets on prepared baking tray.
  7. Bake in middle rack (180°C) for 10-15 minutes until golden brown. Let cool completely.

Royal icing

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl, mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add egg white and lemon juice, mix 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a piping bag and use as required.

Assembly

  1. Cut a small hole on tip of piping bag. Use royal icing as glue to assemble gingerbread pieces into a house. Decorate as desired with cereals, candies, shortbread biscuits, mini pretzels and dill.
