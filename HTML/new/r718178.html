Christy Tania's 20th Anniversary Show Stopper

 3.8 (8 ratings)

Ingredients

  • Fraisier sponge
  • 10 - 12 macarons with strawberry cream cheese ganache (see Tips)

Lime ganache for sable choux

  • 10 g titanium strength gelatine leaves
  • cold water, to soak
  • 380 g thickened cream
  • 80 g freshly squeezed lime juice (approx. 4 limes)
  • 110 g white chocolate, cut into small pieces

Sable biscuit

  • 130 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 50 g brown sugar
  • 200 g plain flour

Choux pastry

  • 60 g milk
  • 50 g water
  • 50 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch salt
  • ¼ tsp caster sugar
  • 90 g plain flour
  • 3 eggs, lightly beaten

Assembly of mini croquembouche

  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 1070 g water
  • 20 g glucose syrup
  • boiling water
  • edible flowers, to decorate (optional)
  • 50 g vinegar

Assembly of show stopper cake

  • icing sugar, to dust
  • edible flowers, to decorate (optional)

Preparation

  1. We suggest starting this Show Stopper recipe at least 2 days before you plan to serve. For example day 1: make the Fraisier sponge, the Macarons with strawberry cream cheese ganache (see Cookidoo® for recipes) and the Lime ganache for the Sable choux. Then day 2: make the Sable biscuit, the choux and the remainder of the recipe, as well as assemble the Show Stopper. It is also recommended that you read through the full recipe and all the tips before starting.
  2. Fraisier Sponge - see Cookidoo® for recipe.
  3. Macarons with strawberry cream cheese ganache - see Cookidoo® for recipe (see Tips).

Lime ganache for sable choux

  1. Place gelatine leaves in a bowl, cover with cold water and set aside to soak until soft and pliable (approx. 10 minutes).
  2. Place 190 g of the cream and lime juice into mixing bowl, then heat 4 min/80°C/speed 2.
  3. Remove gelatine from water and squeeze gently to remove excess water. Add gelatine and white chocolate to mixing bowl, then mix 20 sec/speed 2.5 or until gelatine is dissolved and chocolate melts.
  4. Add remaining 190 g cream and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Pour into a bowl, cover surface directly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 3 hours or overnight, or until firm enough to pipe (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sable biscuit

  1. Place all sable biscuit ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4 or until ingredients start to clump together and form a dough; take care not to overmix. Transfer onto a large sheet of baking paper and gently form into a ball, then flatten into a disc. Top with another large sheet of baking paper and roll out to 5 mm thickness. Place biscuit dough in the freezer for 30 minutes, or until firm enough to cut. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.

Choux pastry

  1. Place milk, water, butter, salt and sugar into mixing bowl, then heat 2 min/85°C/speed 1 or until butter has melted.
  2. Add flour and mix 7 min/100°C/speed 2.5, without measuring cup. Set aside in mixing bowl to cool until mixture reaches 55°C (approx. 10 minutes). Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Mix speed 2.5, without measuring cup, adding beaten eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid. After adding the last of the egg, mix 30 sec/speed 3, without measuring cup. Allow to cool in mixing bowl. Continue with the recipe.

Assembly of Sable choux with lime ganache

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays and set aside.
  2. Remove sable biscuit dough from freezer. Using a round cookie cutter (3 cm), stamp out approx. 25 rounds to top each choux (see Tips).
  3. Transfer choux mixture into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (1 cm) and pipe small 2 cm wide choux puffs onto prepared baking trays, leaving space between each (see Tips). Top 25 of the choux with a sable biscuit disc and bake for 12 minutes (180°C), until risen and crisp (see Tips).
  4. Reduce oven temperature to 140°C and bake for a further 10 minutes. Remove pastries from oven then using the point of a small, sharp knife, make a small hole in the base of each choux. Set aside to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. When ready to assemble, stir lime ganache to soften, then transfer into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (1 cm). Place tip of the nozzle in the hole in the base of each choux and fill with lime ganache. Set filled choux aside.

Assembly of mini croquembouche

  1. This stage of the recipe (sugar stages and assembly) should be started approximately 2 hours before final result is needed.
  2. Set aside a ring mould (approx. 11.5 cm) on a silicone bread mat.
  3. Place caster sugar, 70 g of the water and glucose syrup into mixing bowl, then start Sugar Stages , without measuring cup to make a caramel.
  4. Place boiling water in a bowl. Place a smaller bowl in the boiling water so the water comes no more than half way up the sides of bowl. Carefully pour caramel into the warmed bowl.
  5. Using the spatula, immediately remove any mixture from the mixing bowl rim, to be able to close the lid correctly for pre-cleaning.
  6. Quickly but very carefully dip the sides of each choux into the caramel and place in the prepared ring mould, arranging side-by-side in a circle. Continue building the croquembouche by dipping and “gluing” each choux on top of the base circle. Follow with a second, third and fourth tier. Finally, place a single choux on top to achieve the cone shape. Gently remove the ring mould. Place edible flowers (optional) decoratively on croquembouche. Using a fork, dip prongs in the remaining caramel. Quickly pulling caramel to make thin strands, spin the caramel to form a web of sugar around the sable choux tower, ready for assembly.
  7. Place vinegar and remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl, then start Pre-clean /Fat or caramel.

Assembly of show stopper cake

  1. Place Fraisier sponge on serving plate and gently remove from springform tin, then remove acetate or baking paper collar. Dust the top of the sponge generously with icing sugar.
  2. Next, carefully place the croquembouche in the centre of the Fraisier cake and arrange 10-12 macarons around the base of the croquembouche. Decorate with extra edible flowers (optional) (see Tips).

Useful Items

oven, baking paper, bowl, bowls, plastic wrap, piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm), baking tray, silicone bread mat, silicone bread mat or baking paper, sharp knife, fork, rolling pin, freezer, round cookie cutter (3 cm), serving plate, ring mould (11.5 cm)

Hints & Tips

  • Lime ganache will keep in the fridge in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Left over Sable biscuit pastry can be frozen for up to 3 months for future use. Sable choux can be made 1 day ahead of serving. They soften on standing but can be crisped up in the oven for approx. 10 minutes (180°C). Cool before filling.
  • This 20th Anniversary Show Stopper only requires approx. 25 filled sable choux. Remaining choux can be filled and served on the side.
  • For this recipe you will need 10-12 completed macarons. Remaining unfilled macaron shells can be stored in an airtight container for up to 1 week. Remaining strawberry ganache will keep for up to 3 days in the fridge in an airtight container. Use leftover ganache as a filling for cakes, on scones or served with fruit.
  • This show stopper cake will keep for approx. 1 hour after assembly. To serve, remove macarons and croquembouche. Break croquembouche into individual pieces and serve with sliced Fraisier sponge cake.
