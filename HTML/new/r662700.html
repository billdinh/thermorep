Neroli Pichy's Pork belly with pickled apple and walnut salad

 4.3 (4 ratings)
Difficulty

Most of our recipes are easy. Those that require a little more time or cooking ability are rated medium or advanced.

This is the time it takes to prepare this meal from start to finish: marinating, baking, cooling etc.

This shows how many portions this recipe makes.

Ingredients

Pickled apple

  • 1 green apple, cored and cut into thin slices
  • 200 g water
  • 80 g white wine vinegar
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 3 juniper berries
  • ½ tsp whole black peppercorns

Pork belly

  • 1200 g pork belly meat, boned and rind scored
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 - 2 tsp sea salt flakes, to rub, plus extra to season
  • 1500 g water
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp juniper berries
  • ½ tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 8 new baby potatoes, cut into halves
  • 8 pickling onions, cut into halves
  • 80 g walnut halves
  • 3 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 egg white, lightly beaten
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, to season
  • 300 g red cabbage, finely shredded
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 20 g white wine vinegar
  • ½ tsp Dijon mustard
  • walnuts, to serve

Preparation

Pickled apple

  1. Place apple into a shallow bowl and set aside. Place all remaining pickled apple ingredients into mixing bowl, then heat 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Pour pickling liquid over apple to cover, then set aside until needed (see Tips).

Pork belly

  1. Place pork belly into Varoma dish, arrange sage and thyme sprigs under and around the pork, then rub skin generously with salt flakes.
  2. Place water, fennel seeds, juniper berries and peppercorns into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish with pork belly into position, secure Varoma lid, then steam 50 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Insert Varoma tray, add potatoes and season with salt and pepper. Secure Varoma lid, then steam for a further 20 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Remove Varoma and set aside. Insert simmering basket and add pickling onions. Place Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) with baking paper and set aside a large roasting tray.
  6. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh walnuts into it. Add 2 teaspoons of the caster sugar and egg white, then season with salt and pepper. Stir to coat nuts. Spread nuts, without excess egg white mixture, in a single layer on prepared baking tray. Bake for 10 minutes (200°C) or until golden. Remove from oven and set aside to cool and crisp. Increase oven to 240°C.
  7. Remove Varoma and set aside. Then remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside, reserving pork steaming liquid in mixing bowl. When pickling onions are cool enough to handle, separate layers into petals. Place pork, potatoes and onion petals on the large roasting tray. Roast for 20 minutes (240°C) or until pork rind crackles and vegetables are browned.
  8. Check that the reserved pork steaming liquid is 500 g, if not, top up with water. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh cabbage into it. Secure Varoma lid, then steam 2-3 min/Varoma/speed 2 until just wilted. Transfer to a serving bowl and cover to keep warm.
  9. To make dressing, place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil and vinegar into it. Add mustard and remaining 1 teaspoon sugar, season with salt and pepper, then stir well to combine. Toss cabbage with dressing.
  10. Serve sliced pork with potatoes, onion petals and cabbage topped with drained pickled apple and walnuts.

Hints & Tips

  • Pickled apple will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for 1 week. It can also be used in a sandwich or as an accompaniment on a cheeseboard.
  • If preferred, you can grill the pork, potatoes and pickled onions. After baking the walnuts, turn oven to grill function. Grill for 10 minutes or until pork rind crackles and vegetables brown.
