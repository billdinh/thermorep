Mushroom freekeh risotto with spring onion oil

 4.3 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

Spring onion oil

  • 1 bunch spring onions/shallots
  • 250 g olive oil

Mushroom freekeh

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 250 g cracked freekeh
  • 60 g dry white wine
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste or Chicken stock powder (see Tips)
  • 200 g fresh mushrooms, of choice, cut into slices (see Tips)
  • 700 g water
  • freshly squeezed lemon juice, to serve
  • ground black pepper, to serve
  • spring onion/shallot, sliced, to serve

Preparation

Spring onion oil

  1. Cut green tops from spring onions/shallots for Spring onion oil and cut into pieces. Reserve white part for Mushroom freekeh.
  2. Place green tops of the spring onions/shallots and oil into mixing bowl, then blend 1 min/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then heat 2 min/80°C/speed 1.
  4. Set mixing bowl aside until onion oil is cool enough to handle, then strain through a nut milk bag into a bowl, discarding solids. Pour oil into a serving bowl and set aside until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mushroom freekeh

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Place in a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place reserved white part of the spring onions/shallots and garlic into mixing bowl, then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and oil, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1 [TM31: 3 min/Varoma/speed 1].
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Add freekeh and sauté 1 min/120°C//speed 1.5, without measuring cup [TM31: 1 min/Varoma//speed 1.5, without measuring cup].
  5. Add wine and sauté 2 min/100°C//speed 1.5, without measuring cup.
  6. Add stock paste or powder, mushrooms and water. Then scrape base of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 25 min/100°C//speed 1.5, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Place freekeh in a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Add ¾ of the reserved Parmesan cheese and season with pepper, then stir to combine. Cover and set aside for a few minutes to thicken. Serve risotto drizzled with lemon juice and Spring onion oil, sprinkled with extra sliced spring onion and remaining parmesan.

Hints & Tips

  • Spring onion oil will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Remove from the fridge 1 hour before needed and stir before using.
  • To make your own Vegetable stock paste or Chicken stock powder in your Thermomix®, search Cookidoo® for the recipes.
  • We used Swiss brown and Shiitake mushrooms in our recipe. For a richer mushroom flavour, replace 100 g of the fresh mushrooms with 10 g soaked and drained porcini. Soak in 700 g boiling water for 10 minutes, then drain, reserving liquid to use in place of water listed in the recipe.
