Lebanese-style hommus

 4.0 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 250 g dried chickpeas
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1000 g water, plus extra to soak
  • 100 g freshly squeezed lemon juice, to taste
  • 100 g tahini
  • 1 garlic clove, to taste
  • 1 tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 80 g ice cubes
  • 1 - 2 tbsp olive oil, to drizzle
  • ½ tsp ground cumin, to sprinkle
  • ½ tsp paprika, to sprinkle

Preparation

  1. Start this recipe the night before to allow chickpeas to soak. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh dried chickpeas into it. Add bicarbonate of soda and cover with water. Set aside to soak overnight. Drain and rinse soaked chickpeas.
  2. Insert blade cover (optional - TM5 and TM6 only). Place drained chickpeas and 1000 g water into mixing bowl, then cook 40 min/95°C/speed 1 or until completely soft. Using a simmering basket, drain cooked chickpeas and discard cooking liquid. Carefully remove blade cover, if using.
  3. Place cooked chickpeas back into mixing bowl and blend 40 sec/speed 6, with aid of spatula.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then add lemon juice, tahini, garlic and salt. Set aside in mixing bowl for 20 minutes to cool, then blend 20 sec/speed 6, with aid of spatula.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 1 min/speed 6, with aid of spatula.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add ice cubes and blend 1 min 30 sec/speed 7, with aid of spatula. Place hommus in a serving bowl, drizzle with oil, then sprinkle with cumin and paprika before serving.

Hints & Tips

  • Leftover hommus will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 week.
  • To make bread crisps, cut pitta bread into triangles, then drizzle with oil and sprinkle with dukkah. Bake in a 180°C oven until crisp.
  • To make vegetable sticks for your hommus, peel, trim and halve a bunch of baby carrots and cut baby cucumbers into rounds or batons.
