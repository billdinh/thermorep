Ingredients

  • 255 g cashew nuts
  • water, to soak
  • 170 g butternut squash, peeled, deseeded and cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 450 g vegetable broth
    or 450 g water, plus 2 tsp vegetable stock paste
  • 60 g nutritional yeast
  • 170 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 45 g apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp chilli powder
  • 1 ½ tsp dried cayenne pepper flakes
  • ½ - 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • tortilla chips
  • guacamole, to garnish
  • pico de gallo, to garnish
  • fresh jalapeño chilli, sliced, to garnish
  • fresh cilantro, chopped, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in cashews, cover with water and soak for at least 4 hours to overnight. Drain and rinse nuts through a fine-mesh sieve, rinse and set aside. Discard soaking water.
  2. Place butternut squash, reserved drained cashews, vegetable stock, nutritional yeast, olive oil, apple cider vinegar, chilli powder and chilli flakes into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/85°C/speed 1 and blend 20 sec/speed 10.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Season with salt to taste and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Place chips on a plate and drizzle with warm Butternut Queso. Layer with guacamole, pico de gallo and sliced jalapeños. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve immediately.

Hints & Tips

  • Serve over vegetables and potatoes. Use to make nachos, smothered burritos, bean dips, or use to top enchiladas.
  • Nutritional yeast is not to be confused with brewer's yeast or baking yeast. It is a natural flavour booster that lends a cheesy, nutty or savoury flavour to many dishes. Available in the natural food section of your supermarket or in health food stores.
