Roast beef with Yorkshires and gravy

 5.0 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

Yorkshire puddings

  • 140 g plain flour
  • 100 g milk
  • 100 g cold water
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ tsp sea salt

Roast beef with wholegrain mustard crust

  • 20 g vegetable oil, plus extra to grease
  • 200 g onion, cut into wedges (4 cm)
  • 100 g celery, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 4 tbsp red wine
  • 2000 - 3000 g roast beef (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt, to season
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper, to season
  • 1 tbsp wholegrain mustard
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 650 g water
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 1 - 2 dashes Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Vegemite® (optional)
  • 2 bunches asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 bunches broccolini, trimmed

Preparation

Yorkshire puddings

  1. Place all Yorkshire pudding ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Pour into a jug and leave on the bench for a minimum of 3 hours (see Tips) or refrigerate overnight. If placed in the fridge, allow batter to return to room temperature before cooking.

Roast beef with wholegrain mustard crust

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Set aside roasting tray and wire rack.
  2. Place oil, onion, celery and carrot into mixing bowl and cook 25 min/120°C//speed 1 (TM31: 25 min/Varoma//speed 1). Place in roasting tray, then add the wine.
  3. Place wire rack over the vegetables in roasting tray and place beef on top. Season with salt and pepper, brush with mustard and sprinkle with thyme. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid, weigh 150 g of the water into it, then set aside.
  4. If using MEATER+® (see Tips), insert into the middle of the thickest part of the beef, then open the MEATER+® app and follow the prompts to cook to your liking.
  5. Place roasting tray into oven and reduce temperature to 180°C. Roast for 1¼-1½ hours (180°C - see Tips), carefully adding the reserved water to the roasting tray half-way through cooking, or sooner if vegetables start to brown. Remove from the oven once beef is cooked to your liking or when the MEATER+® app indicates beef is cooked. Place beef on a chopping board and cover with aluminium foil, then set aside to rest for 20 minutes or until MEATER+® indicates ready. Carefully remove MEATER+® (if using).
  6. Increase oven to 220°C. Place ½ teaspoon of oil into each mould of a 12-hole muffin tray and place in the oven for 5 minutes to heat. Carefully remove muffin tray from the oven and divide batter equally between moulds, then bake Yorkshires for 20 minutes (220°C) or until risen and crisp. Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.
  7. Transfer the roast vegetables and pan juices into mixing bowl. Add flour, Chicken stock paste (see Tips), tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, Vegemite® (optional) and remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and place asparagus and broccolini into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook 12-15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Carefully remove Varoma and place steamed vegetables into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  8. To complete gravy, pour any reserved meat resting juices into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 5-8, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 8. Pour gravy into a gravy boat. Serve Yorkshire puddings straight from the oven with roast beef, steamed vegetables and a generous pour of gravy.

Hints & Tips

  • Don’t be tempted to omit the resting time for the Yorkshire pudding batter. Resting the batter relaxes the gluten in the flour that develops during the mixing process and will give you a better rise.
  • To make your own Chicken stock paste in your Thermomix®, search Cookidoo® for the recipes.
  • Add roast potatoes to your spread and season with our rosemary salt from our Smashed roasted potatoes. Search Cookidoo® for the recipe.
  • Adjust cooking times according to the cut of beef used. The cut we used is Prime rib which is the ultimate cut of beef for a roast.
  • Cooked Yorkshire puddings can be stored in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 1 month. Reheat in a 200°C oven for 2 minutes.
  • The MEATER+® is a wireless meat thermometer which enables perfect results every time. See TheMix Shop for more details.
