Goats cheese and caramelised onion mille feuille

 5.0 (2 ratings)

Ingredients

Quick puff pastry

  • 200 g unsalted butter, cut into small pieces (1-2 cm) and frozen
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 90 g chilled water
  • ½ tsp salt

Caramelised onion

  • 20 g oil
  • 300 g eschalots, cut into wedges (3-4 cm)
  • 30 g dried cranberries
  • 20 g balsamic vinegar
  • 20 g sherry
  • 2 pinches sea salt

Cheese filling

  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 300 g soft goat's cheese
  • 200 g crème fraîche
  • 1 lemon, zested, no white pith
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Assembly

  • 3 tbsp pine nuts, toasted
  • rocket leaves, to serve
  • 1 green apple, thinly sliced

Preparation

Quick puff pastry

  1. Place butter, flour, water and salt into mixing bowl, then mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  2. Transfer pastry onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface, then shape into a ball and flatten into a square. Wrap pastry square and place in the fridge for 20 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place pastry onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Roll out into a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 60 x 20 cm). Fold into thirds and give a quarter turn to the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice, then wrap and return to the fridge for 20 minutes (see Tips).
  4. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside a second tray the same size.
  5. On a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface, roll pastry into a rectangle (5 mm thick), then cut into 12 rectangles (10 x 8 cm). Place on prepared baking tray, then place remaining tray directly on top of the pastry. Bake for 10 minutes (220°C). Reduce oven temperature to 190°C. Remove top oven tray and bake for a further 5 minutes (190°C) or until golden brown and crisp. Set aside to cool for 5 minutes then place on a wire rack to cool completely.

Caramelised onion

  1. Place oil and eschalot into mixing bowl, then start High Temp. .
  2. Add cranberries, vinegar, sherry and salt, stir to combine with spatula, then cook 3 min/100°C//speed . Place in a bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cheese filling

  1. Place parsley into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining cheese filling ingredients and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Place in a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (1 cm).

Assembly

  1. Pipe dots of cheese filling in rows (approx.1 cm) to cover each of the pastry rectangles. Sprinkle with pine nuts and top each with a heaped tablespoon of caramelised onion. Add rocket and apple slices, then place pastry rectangles on top, pushing down gently to secure. Place on a serving plate and serve immediately.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, refrigerator, rolling pin, oven, wire rack, baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm), serving plate

Hints & Tips

  • The success of puff pastry depends on keeping the dough chilled so the layers of butter do not melt. A cold work surface (marble or stone) and a cool room temperature are helpful. If at any time during the process the dough feels warm, place into the freezer for 5 minutes before rolling out again.
  • Raw puff pastry dough freezes very well. Partially roll out the dough before freezing to allow it to defrost faster and defrost in the refrigerator before using.
  • The pastry squares, cheese filling and caramelised onion can be made 1 day ahead of assembly.
