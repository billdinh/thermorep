Buckwheat Blini with Smoked Salmon (Bill Yosses)

 5.0 (8 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 2 oz all-purpose flour
  • 2 oz buckwheat groats (see Tip)
  • 1 oz sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 8 oz whole milk
  • 1 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, in pieces
  • 2 oz extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to fry
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 oz crème fraîche, to serve
    or 4 oz sour cream, to serve
  • 8 oz smoked salmon, sliced to serve
  • 2 oz caviar, e.g. American Paddlefish, to serve
    or 2 oz salmon roe, to serve
  • fresh dill fronds, to garnish
    or fresh chives, minced, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place flour, buckwheat, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place milk and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/100°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/160°F/speed 1.
  4. Add reserved flour mixture, reserved milk-yeast mixture and olive oil and mix 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a large bowl, cover with cling wrap and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 45 min.).
  5. In a small bowl, whisk eggs with a wire whisk. Once batter has risen, whisk to deflate; then whisk in eggs. Cover and refrigerate until ready to use or up to overnight (see Tip).
  6. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a blini pan or small skillet over medium heat. Once hot, spoon approximately 1 tbsp batter per blini into pan and cook on both sides until golden (approx. 1 min. per side). Transfer finished blini onto a piece of parchment without stacking too high to avoid crushing. Place one blini next to another with parchment between layers until ready to serve. Repeat until all batter is used.
  7. Arrange warm blini on a platter. Spoon 1 tsp crème fraîche on top of each blini. Top with smoked salmon and caviar, garnish with dill and serve.

Hints & Tips

  • If buckwheat groats are unavailable, skip first step and add 2 oz flour, 2 oz buckwheat flour, 1 oz sugar and ½ tsp salt in place of the flour-mixture when mixing with reserved milk-yeast mixture and olive oil.
  • After whisking eggs into batter, cover and refrigerate up to overnight but allow to come to room temperature before using (may increase in volume; rewhisk before using).
  • Blini can be made 1 day ahead. Allow fried blini to cool, then cover and refrigerate. When ready to serve, brush blini slightly with melted butter and warm on a baking sheet for 5 minutes at 350°F.
