George Calombaris' Liquorice ice cream with blackberries

 5.0 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 170 g soft liquorice
  • 380 g low fat milk
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 60 - 80 g caster sugar, to taste
  • 120 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • fresh blackberries or frozen, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place liquorice into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add milk and heat 12 min/80°C/speed 3 or until liquorice is melted. Pour into a jug and reserve. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks and sugar into mixing bowl, then whip 30 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add cream and ⅓ (approx. 180 g) of the reserved liquorice milk mixture, then mix 3 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add remaining liquorice milk mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a jug. Pour mixture into ice cube trays and freeze for a minimum 6 hours or overnight, until frozen.
  6. Place frozen liquorice cubes into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 9, with aid of spatula, until smooth. Place in a shallow freezable container and freeze until required. Serve scoops of ice cream with blackberries.

Hints & Tips

  • For a richer, purple coloured ice cream (as pictured), add a few drops of natural black gel food colouring to the ice cream mixture in step 5. Without the addition of the food colouring, the ice cream will be greyish in colour but will still be delicious.
  • This recipe was contributed by George Colombaris. George originally paired this ice cream with his galaktoboureko (you’ll find the full recipe on Recipe Community). We’ve kept it simple here and coupled it with in-season blackberries for an elegant and easy dessert.
