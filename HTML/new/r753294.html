Strawberry and meringue semifreddo

 4.9 (19 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 300 g strawberries, trimmed, plus extra to serve
  • 180 g caster sugar
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 600 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 60 g meringue, bite-sized pieces, plus extra to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Line a loaf tin (25 x 10 x 7 cm) with plastic wrap and set aside.
  2. Place 300 g of the strawberries and 100 g of the sugar into mixing bowl, then blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh remaining 80 g sugar into it. Set aside.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and beat 2 min/70°C/speed 3, without measuring cup, then cook 2 min 30 sec/70°C/speed 3, slowly adding reserved sugar 1 teaspoon at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add egg yolks and cook 1 min/70°C/speed 3.5, without measuring cup. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into bowl with reserved strawberry purée.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and vanilla into mixing bowl, then whip until soft peaks form/speed 3.5, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Weigh meringue pieces into mixing bowl. Spoon into bowl with reserved strawberry mixture, then fold gently until mixture is combined but still marbled. Transfer into prepared loaf tin and smooth the surface. Cover and place in the freezer overnight.
  7. Unmould ice cream on a serving plate. Decorate with extra meringues and fresh strawberries and serve immediately (see Tips).

Hints & Tips

  • To make your own meringue, see Cookidoo® for the recipe.
  • Semifreddo will keep in the freezer for up to 3 weeks.
  • You can also serve as an ice cream sandwich. Cut ice cream into thick slices and sandwich between wafers, trimming to fit as needed.
