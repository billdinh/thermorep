Pâté en croûte

 5.0 (2 ratings)

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 380 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 170 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 100 g chilled water

Pork filling

  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 900 g pork meat, sinew free, cut into cubes (2 cm), partially frozen
  • 2 tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 20 g brandy
  • 100 g button mushrooms, sliced
  • 20 g shelled pistachios (optional)

Assembly

  • butter, to grease
  • 300 g rindless bacon rashers (see Tips)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Jelly

  • 6 titanium strength gelatine leaves
  • 200 g Madeira wine (see Tips)
  • 130 g water
  • 30 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar

Preparation

  1. Start this recipe 1 day before serving.

Pastry

  1. Place all pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and knead Dough /3 min. Place pastry on a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface and form into a ball. Wrap and place in the fridge for approx. 30 minutes, until firm enough to roll. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pork filling

  1. Place parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Place in a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 300 g of the pork meat into mixing bowl and mince 20 sec/speed 8 or until evenly chopped. Remove any gristle or sinew. Place mince in bowl with parsley.
  3. Place another 300 g of the pork meat into mixing bowl and mince 20 sec/speed 8 or until evenly chopped. Remove any gristle or sinew. Add mince to bowl with mince and parsley.
  4. Place remaining 300 g pork meat into mixing bowl and mince 20 sec/speed 8 or until evenly chopped. Remove any gristle or sinew.
  5. Add salt, pepper, brandy, mushrooms, pistachios (optional) and reserved mince mixture, then mix 1 min//speed 3 or until evenly combined.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease base and sides of a terrine or loaf tin (approx. 25 x 10 x 11 cm) and line the base with baking paper.
  2. Transfer ¾ of the pastry (approx. 450 g) onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Roll out pastry to 5 mm thickness. Drape pastry over rolling pin and carefully transfer into loaf tin. Gently press pastry into base and up sides of tin. Press to repair any splits or tears.
  3. Line bottom and sides of the pastry in loaf tin with overlapping bacon rashers, ensuring long ends of bacon hang over edges of tin.
  4. Place pork mixture in tin and smooth down in an even layer. Fold over the overlapping bacon to encase completely and press down firmly. Trim overhanging pastry leaving a 1 cm edge, then fold this over and brush with egg wash.
  5. On a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface, roll out remaining pastry to 5 mm thickness. Using the base of tin as a template, cut pastry to fit top of tin. Place pastry lid over the top of bacon and press pastry edges decoratively together to seal (see Tips).
  6. Use a small, sharp knife or cookie cutter to cut out 2 x 1.5 cm holes from pastry lid. Make foil vents to allow steam to escape by placing two 10 cm squares of aluminium foil on a work surface. Fold each square into thirds, then starting from the short side, roll each around a round spoon handle to form a cylinder. Place foil vents into cut holes, then brush pastry with egg wash. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Bake for 1 hour 15 minutes (180°C) or until pastry is golden brown. Set aside to cool for 2 hours. Remove foil and discard. Place terrine in the fridge overnight.

Jelly

  1. Place gelatine leaves in a bowl, cover with cold water and set aside to soak until soft and pliable (approx. 10 minutes).
  2. Place wine, water, syrup and vinegar into mixing bowl, then heat 3 min/100°C/speed 2, without measuring cup.
  3. Squeeze gelatine gently to remove excess water. Add gelatine to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3 or until gelatine is dissolved completely. Set aside to cool but not set (approx. 30 minutes).
  4. Carefully pour jelly into the terrine through the holes until jelly reaches the top. Place in the fridge for approx. 4 hours, until jelly has set. Using a long sharp knife, gently loosen sides and turn out onto a chopping board. Slice pâté to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

Hints & Tips

  • Longer rashers of streaky bacon work best for this recipe.
  • Madeira can be replaced with a Port or sherry.
  • Use any pastry offcuts to decorate the terrine, if desired.
  • Serve terrine as a starter, as part of a charcuterie board, with pickles, mustard or cheese.
  • Terrine will keep in the fridge for up to 1 week.
