Lamb Meatballs, Minty Pea Houmous and Flatbreads

 5.0 (2 ratings)
Difficulty

Most of our recipes are easy. Those that require a little more time or cooking ability are rated medium or advanced.

This is how much time you need to prepare this meal.

This is the time it takes to prepare this meal from start to finish: marinating, baking, cooling etc.

Serving size

This shows how many portions this recipe makes.

Ingredients

Pickled Red Cabbage

  • 125 g red wine vinegar
  • 10 g caster sugar
  • 10 g fine sea salt
  • 250 g red cabbage, cored, cut in pieces (5 cm)

Pea Houmous

  • 500 g water
  • 125 g frozen peas
  • 5 g fresh mint, leaves only
  • 240 g tinned chickpeas, drained, rinsed (1 x 400 g tin)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 3 Tbsp tahini (see tip)
  • 30 g lemon juice

Tahini Yoghurt Sauce

  • 100 g plain yoghurt (see tip)
  • 60 g tahini
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 lemon, freshly squeezed juice only

Meatballs

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 800 g lamb mince
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika

Flatbreads

  • 175 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 250 g plain yoghurt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp caster sugar

Preparation

Pickled Red Cabbage

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in vinegar, sugar and salt. Stir contents with spatula until combined then set aside.
  2. Place cabbage in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Transfer to bowl with vinegar mixture and stir until well combined. Cover and set aside to marinate at room temperature. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat grill to 180°C.

Pea Houmous

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, weigh in peas and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1 until tender. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse peas under cold running water to stop cooking process then set aside. Empty and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place mint in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop again 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add reserved steamed peas, chickpeas, oil, salt, pepper, tahini and lemon juice then blend 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend again 8 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film or reusable wrap and set aside in fridge. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tahini Yoghurt Sauce

  1. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh in yoghurt and tahini. Remove bowl and add salt and lemon juice. Stir to combine, then cover and set aside.

Meatballs

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lamb mince, eggs, salt, cinnamon, cumin and paprika then mix Dough /30 sec. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Line a large baking tray with baking paper and set a raised wire rack over the tray (see tip). Form 20-25 meatballs from the lamb mixture and flatten them slightly using a fork. Place the meatballs on the wire rack and grill for 15-20 minutes until browned and cooked through. Meanwhile, make flatbreads.

Flatbreads

  1. Place flour, yoghurt, baking powder, salt and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead Dough /1 min 30 sec.
  2. Tip dough out onto a lightly-floured surface and bring together. Divide dough into 6 pieces then shape each into a ball. Roll dough balls out into circles using a rolling pin (approx. Ø 15 cm). Stretch bigger using hands if dough is bouncing back using rolling pin. Heat a skillet or griddle pan until very hot and cook flatbreads for 3-5 minutes on each side until golden brown. Set cooked flatbreads aside on a plate covered with a damp tea towel to keep them warm and soft.

Serving

  1. Spread 1 Tbsp pea houmous on each flatbread, top with 3-4 meatballs, a few tablespoons of pickled red cabbage and a drizzle of yoghurt sauce. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film or reusable wrap, refrigerator, baking paper, oven, griddle pan, rolling pin, hob, wire rack, tea towel, large baking tray, large bowl

Hints & Tips

  • Try to buy the liquid tahini variety for this recipe rather than the more solid variety.
  • Because lamb has a high fat content, it is best to cook the meatballs on a wire rack set over a lined tray so that the fat can drip through the rack onto the tray instead of pooling around the meatballs.
  • Ensure that thin plain yoghurt is used and not Greek yoghurt or else the sauce will be too thick.
