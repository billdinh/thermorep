Sous-Vide Scrambled Eggs

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

  • 1 oz unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 oz heavy whipping cream
    or 1 oz whole milk
  • 6 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 65 oz water, to fully submerge bags (not higher than the max. fill level)
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp fresh chives, finely chopped, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/140°F/speed 2.
  2. Add whipping cream, eggs and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 3, pour into a vacuum sealer food bag, remove air by hand then seal with a vacuum food sealer machine as per manufacturer's instructions. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Insert blade cover and press down to secure. Place sealed bag into mixing bowl and add water to fully submerge bag. Do not exceed the maximum fill level mark (2.2 l). Remove bag from mixing bowl and set aside. Add lemon juice to water and heat Kettle /175°F.
  4. Place sealed bag with eggs into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bag is completely submerged in water and cook Sous-vide /10 min/175°F.
  5. Carefully remove bag using tongs and place onto a work surface. Using spatula, gently but quickly break up egg mixture (ensure bag remains sealed and not punctured). Place bag back into mixing bowl and cook a further Sous-vide /9 min/175°F or until cooked to desired consistency.
  6. Open sous-vide bag and carefully transfer scrambled eggs to plates, sprinkle with black pepper and freshly chopped chives and serve hot.

Hints & Tips

  • This recipe uses room temperature eggs, using cold eggs straight from the refrigerator will affect the cooking time and the final result.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: Fill a large container or bowl with water. Place eggs into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 1 inch open. Slowly submerge bag into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bag closed just before it is completely submerged. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • Place blade cover & peeler onto mixing knife making sure the upward curved parts are on top of the upper positioned blades. Press down gently to secure it to the mixing knife.
  • To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bags add 1 oz lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • Before placing eggs in vacuum sealer bag, turn the top edge of bag inside out approximately 2 inches from the edge in order to keep the top clean.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bags. Bags should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l).
  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking; resistant up to 212°F; resistant up to 8 hours of cooking; BPA-free; PVC-free.
