Tarte tatin

 4.5 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra to grease
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 50 g cold water
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Caramel and filling

  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 70 g unsalted butter, cold, cut into pieces
  • 1030 g water
  • 1 tsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 8 Granny Smith apples, peeled and cut into sixths (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp vinegar

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Grease a round springform cake tin (24 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place all pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, flatten into a disc, wrap and place in the fridge until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Caramel and filling

  1. To guarantee the success of recipes using the Sugar mode, the ingredients used must match exactly the quantity and description given. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Place sugar, butter, 30 g of the water and lemon juice into mixing bowl, then start Sugar Stages . Immediately pour caramel into prepared tin, then scrape off any mixture left on the rim of mixing bowl using spatula so mixing bowl lid closes securely for Pre-clean mode. Arrange apple pieces as tightly as possible on top of the caramel, standing them on their side. Bake for 30 minutes (200°C). Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.
  3. Place vinegar and remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl, then start Pre-clean .
  4. Using a rolling pin, roll out dough into a disc about the size of the pan. Carefully cover the apples with the disc of dough and tuck it in around the edges. Prick a few holes in the pastry with a sharp knife, then bake for a further 30-45 minutes (200°C) or until pastry is golden brown and the juices are brown and thickened. Allow to cool for 30 minutes, then place a large serving plate over pastry and invert plate and tin together to turn out tarte tatin. Cool slightly before serving (see Tips).

Useful Items

oven, round cake tin (24 cm), refrigerator, rolling pin, silicone bread mat or plastic wrap, serving plate

Hints & Tips

  • Use as many apple pieces as you need to tightly pack them into the dish - they will shrink as they cook. If your apples are small, cut them into bigger pieces.
  • Allowing the steam to evaporate is important so the juices will thicken and the tart will be glossy and golden brown.
  • Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice-cream or a dollop of fresh cream.
