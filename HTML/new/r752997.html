Blueberry Bundt Coffee Cake

4.9 (8 ratings)

Ingredients

Topping

  • 1 oz unsalted butter, diced, cold, plus extra to grease
  • 1 oz brown sugar
  • 1 oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 oz slivered almonds
  • 2 tbsp old fashioned rolled oats

Batter

  • 8 oz blueberries, plus extra to garnish
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 8 oz unsalted butter, diced
  • 13 ½ oz sugar
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tsp almond extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 10 ½ oz buttermilk
  • 13 oz all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¾ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt

Frosting

  • 3 oz cream cheese
  • 2 oz confectioners sugar

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside. Grease a bundt pan (approx. 10 in.) and set aside.

Topping

  1. Place butter, brown sugar, flour, almonds and oats into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer to prepared baking sheet and bake 10-12 minutes (350°F), until golden brown. Set aside to cool. Once cooled, crumble the topping and set aside until ready to serve.

Batter

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in blueberries and 1 tbsp flour and toss together to coat. Set aside.
  2. Place butter and sugar into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 4.5. Meanwhile with a fine grater or zester, thinly grate zest from 1 lemon, avoiding the white pith and set aside. Cut lemon in half, squeeze the juice from both halves into a small bowl and set aside until needed.
  3. Add almond extract and without measuring cup, mix 45 sec/speed 4.5 while adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add buttermilk and reserved lemon zest, insert measuring cup and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 13 oz flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer ⅓ of the batter to prepared pan.
  6. Add 4 oz reserved dusted blueberries to the remaining batter in mixing bowl and fold in with spatula. Evenly spread over plain batter in pan.
  7. Top with remaining reserved dusted blueberries and bake 50-60 minutes (350°F) or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let cool 20 minutes in pan before inverting onto a cooling rack to cool completely. Clean mixing bowl.

Frosting

  1. Place cream cheese, confectioners sugar and 1 tbsp reserved lemon juice into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 10 sec/speed 3.5.
  2. Spread Frosting onto cooled cake. Garnish with blueberries, sprinkle with reserved crumbled Topping and serve.
