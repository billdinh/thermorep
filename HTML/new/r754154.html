Slow-cooked Hoisin Pulled Pork Buns with Pineapple Chutney

 4.7 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

Pineapple Chutney

  • ½ fresh red chilli, deseeded if desired
  • 100 g red pepper, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1000 g pineapple, peeled, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • ½ tsp chipotle chilli flakes
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 50 g apple cider vinegar
  • 200 g jam sugar

Hoisin Pulled Pork

  • 10 g vegetable oil
  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 15 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 800 g pork shoulder, boneless, in one piece
  • 40 g tomato purée
  • 1 chicken stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
    or 1 heaped tsp chicken stock paste, homemade
  • 200 g water
  • 220 g hoisin sauce, plus extra for serving
  • 40 g light brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 6 brioche burger buns
  • 2 - 3 little gem lettuce, for serving

Preparation

Pineapple Chutney

  1. Please ensure you have a blade cover before starting this recipe (visit our website for details).
  2. Place chilli in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add red pepper and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place pineapple in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add chilli flakes, turmeric and mustard seeds to mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, cook 2 min/110°C//speed 0.5.
  6. Add vinegar and sugar, stir to combine with spatula then cook 20 min/100°C//speed 0.5.
  7. Add reserved pepper mixture and, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 20 min/110°C//speed 0.5. Transfer to warm glass jars with airtight lids (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm gap at the top. Make sure that rims of jars are clean, close immediately and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Hoisin Pulled Pork

  1. Place oil, onions, garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl.
  2. Insert blade cover. Place onion mixture, pork, tomato purée, chicken stock, water, 100 g hoisin sauce, sugar, soy sauce and Chinese five spice powder in mixing bowl, cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1, then start Slow Cook /5 h/98°C.
  3. Remove pork from mixing bowl with tongs and drain on paper towels. Empty mixing bowl, reserving cooking liquid, and cut pork into 6 pieces.
  4. Remove blade cover. Place 3 pork pieces in mixing bowl and shred 6-8 sec//speed 4 until shredded. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place remaining 3 pork pieces in mixing bowl and shred 6-8 sec//speed 4 until shredded.
  6. Add reserved shredded pork, 120 g reserved cooking liquid (see tip) and 120 g hoisin sauce then mix 5 sec//speed 3.
  7. Toast brioche burger buns, fill each one with a few gem lettuce leaves, a little hoisin sauce, one sixth of pulled pork and a few teaspoons of pineapple chutney. Serve immediately.

Hints & Tips

  • To prepare jars, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jars and lids upside down in centre of Varoma and steam |20 min/Varoma/speed 1. |Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jars and lids in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heating in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • Leftover pineapple chutney pairs well with strong cheeses and BBQ meats.
  • The reserved cooking liquid in step 10 can be used as a base for soups or sauces.
