Red Wine-poached Pears with Crème Anglaise

(No ratings)

Ingredients

Crème Anglaise

  • 300 g whole milk
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only (see tip), reserving the pod for later use

Poached Pears

  • 720 g red wine, fruity and light
  • 100 g golden caster sugar
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 cinnamon sticks, halved
  • 3 cloves
  • 2 Conference pears, peeled

Preparation

  1. Please ensure you have a blade cover before starting this recipe (visit our website for details).

Crème Anglaise

  1. Place milk, sugar, egg yolks and vanilla seeds in mixing bowl then start Thicken /80°C. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film or reusable wrap, ensuring the film is in direct contact with surface of the custard. Set aside in the fridge to chill. Clean mixing bowl.

Poached Pears

  1. Insert blade cover. Place wine, sugar, honey, star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves and reserved vanilla pod in mixing bowl then heat 10 min/80°C//speed .
  2. Add pears and cook 20 min/80°C//speed 1. Gently turn the pears using tongs so any parts lacking in colour are face-down in the wine then cook again 20 min/80°C//speed 1.
  3. Carefully remove pears from mixing bowl and set aside to keep warm. Strain red wine liquor through simmering basket into a jug. Discard spices and vanilla pod. Remove blade cover.
  4. Place 200 g reserved red wine liquor (see tip) in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, reduce 15 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Serve pears warm in a puddle of chilled crème anglaise, drizzled with warm red wine syrup.

Useful Items

Hints & Tips

  • Vanilla seeds can easily be removed from the vanilla pod; hold the pod at one end, slit with a sharp knife lengthways, then scrape out the seeds with the tip of the knife.
  • Keep leftover spiced red wine to use for poaching other fruit like plums or apples.
