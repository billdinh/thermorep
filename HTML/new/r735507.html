Green Goddess Dressing (Bill Yosses)

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

  • 2 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 8 oz water
  • 1 tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C in powder form) (see Tip) (optional)
  • 6 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 15 sprigs fresh cilantro, leaves only
  • 8 sprigs parsley, leaves only
  • 8 leaves lacinato kale
    or 8 leaves Tuscan kale, (see Tip)
  • 1 ripe avocado, halved, peeled, pitted
  • 1 green apple, quartered, cored, skin on
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place a pitcher onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in olive oil and set aside.
  2. Place water and vitamin C powder into mixing bowl and boil Kettle /212°F.
  3. Add mint, cilantro and parsley and start Blend /30 sec. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add kale, avocado, apple, salt and pepper and start Blend /1 min.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 2 min/speed 4, while slowly pouring reserved olive oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades to create an emulsion. Transfer dressing to sterilized jar with airtight lid (see Tip), seal and store in refrigerator. Use as desired and consume within one week.

Hints & Tips

  • Use Vitamin C powder (ascorbic acid) or capsule (NOT a GEL capsule). Vitamin C is an antioxidant and will keep the dressing green.
  • Remove and discard the hard core center of the kale either stripping with your hand or cutting down either side.
  • If dressing is too thick add 2-4 oz water and blend 15 sec/speed 10.
  • To prepare jar: place 17.5 oz water into mixing bowl. Place jar and lid upside down in the center of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over with boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting pan and heating in the oven at 212˚F until required.
  • Replace Vitamin C powder (ascorbic acid) with a squeeze lemon juice, however this will add tartness to the dressing.
