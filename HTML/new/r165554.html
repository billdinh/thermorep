Almond briouats with honey and sesame seeds

4.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

Almond paste filling

  • 250 g blanched almonds
  • 80 g sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 30 g butter, unsalted, softened
  • 1 tbsp orange flower water

Folding and frying briouats

  • 250 g filo pastry, cut in strips (36 cm x 6 cm)
  • 50 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly whisked
  • vegetable oil, for frying
  • 300 g honey
  • ½ tbsp orange flower water
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds, toasted

Preparation

Almond paste filling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Spread 125 g blanched almonds in a single layer on the prepared baking tray and toast for 5-10 minutes (200°C) or until lightly browned. Allow to cool.
  3. Place reserved toasted almonds, remaining 125 g blanched almonds and sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add ground cinnamon, salt, butter and orange flower water and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and shape the almond paste into cherry-sized balls (approx. 40) and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Folding and frying briouats

  1. Brush the bottom ⅔ of a strip of filo pastry with melted butter. Place one almond paste ball near the bottom. Wrap up the bottom of the dough to enclose the filling, and shape the triangle by folding the filling, up to the right and then left, until reach the end of the dough. Trim the excess dough to make a neat flap, dab a little egg yolk on the flap to help seal and tuck it into the fold. Repeat for the remaining almond paste balls.
  2. Place a large frying pan (Ø 26 cm) over high heat and fill with 3-4 cm of vegetable oil. Fry briouats in batches (approx. 1-2 minutes), turning occasionally, until golden all over. Remove with slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  3. Place honey and orange flower water in mixing bowl and heat 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile place Varoma tray on Varoma lid and set aside. Transfer hot honey to a bowl. Using two forks dip each briouat in the hot honey and place them in the Varoma tray to drain. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and allow to cool before serving.

Hints & Tips

  • The briouats can be kept in a tightly sealed container at room temperature for two weeks, or in the freezer for 2 months. If storing in the freezer, place a sheet of cling film between layers to make it easy to remove only as many briouats as you need.
  • Filo pastry dries out very quickly, so handle it carefully. Unroll the sheets of filo pastry, and cover the pile with a piece of cling film. Remove one at a time, replacing the cling film after each one.
  • Instead to dipping the briouats in hot honey you can serve the briouats dusted with icing sugar.
