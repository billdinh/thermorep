Crab briks

 4.5 (2 ratings)

Ingredients

Tomato sauce

  • 2 cm fresh ginger root, peeled, cut in pieces
  • 1 red chilli, cut in halves and deseeded
  • 10 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tomatoes, cut in quarters (approx. 200 g)

Crab filling

  • 20 g fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 red chilli, cut in halves and deseeded
  • 1 pinch ground cumin
  • 300 g crab meat, fresh, without shell
  • 8 sheets filo pastry (approx. 48 cm x 25 cm)
  • oil, for frying

Preparation

Tomato sauce

  1. Place ginger, chili, lemon and salt in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add tomatoes and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a serving bowl and set aside.

Crab filling

  1. Place coriander, lemon zest, garlic clove, salt, red chilli, and ground cumin in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 3
  2. Add crab meat and stir 10 sec/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl.
  3. Take one filo pastry sheet, and spoon one eighth of the crab filling approximately 5 cm from the edge at one of the short ends. Tuck both sides in and roll up the brik in a neat rectangle shape. Set aside on a plate and fill the rest of the filo sheets in the same way.
  4. Heat oil to a depth of 2 cm in a suitable frying pan. When the surface shimmers, the oil is hot enough to cook the briks. Test the temperature by dropping a small piece of filo pastry into the oil, if it sizzles, the oil is ready.
  5. Fry the briks over medium heat in the oil for 8 minutes, turning once. When they are a golden brown colour on both sides, drain on paper towels. Serve the briks hot, with the tomato sauce.

Hints & Tips

  • Filo pastry dries out very quickly, so handle it carefully. Unroll the sheets of filo pastry, and keep the unused sheets covered with cling film. Remove one at a time, replacing the cling film after each one.
  • To make 16 smaller crab briks, use half a sheet of filo pastry per portion, and half the amount of crab filling.
