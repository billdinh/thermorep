Cranberry Chutney

 4.3 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 250 g sugar
  • 1 orange, thin peelings of skin and juice (approx. 80 g)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 3 cloves
  • 3 black peppercorns
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 250 g red onions, quartered
  • 500 g dried cranberries
  • 100 g red wine
  • 100 g red wine vinegar

Preparation

  1. Place sugar, orange peel, cinnamon, cloves, peppercorns, star anise and nutmeg in mixing bowl then grind 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onions and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add cranberries, wine, vinegar and orange juice then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Transfer to warm glass jars with airtight lids (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm gap at the top. Make sure that rims of jars are clean and close immediately. Leave to cool completely then store in a cool, dry place (see tip).

Hints & Tips

  • To prepare jars, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jars and lids upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jars and lids in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heat in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • Store chutney in fridge after opening for up to 1 month.
