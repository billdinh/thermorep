Mark Labrooy's Chilli crab with coconut rice and sambal (TM6)

Ingredients

Chilli sauce

  • 50 g olive oil
  • ¼ stalk lemongrass (approx. 3 cm), white part only, cut into thin slices
  • 4 fresh long red chillies, trimmed, deseeded if preferred and cut into halves
  • 50 g fresh ginger (approx. 5 cm), cut into pieces
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 eschalots, cut into halves
  • 5 - 6 sprigs fresh coriander, roots only (approx. 25 g); leaves reserved to serve
  • 60 g palm sugar, cut into pieces
  • 45 g fish sauce (see Tips)
  • 250 g cherry tomatoes

Coconut rice

  • 300 g long grain white rice
  • 300 g water
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 200 g coconut milk
  • sea salt, to taste

Sambal

  • 5 - 6 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • ¼ red onion (approx. 60 g)
  • 150 g cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 100 g desiccated coconut
  • 40 g freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • ½ tsp chilli powder
  • 2 pinches sea salt

Assembly

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 whole fresh crabs (approx. 300 g each), cleaned and cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed, cut into slices, to serve

Preparation

Chilli sauce

  1. Place oil, lemongrass, chillies, ginger, garlic, eschalots and coriander roots into mixing bowl, then sauté 10 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add palm sugar, fish sauce and tomatoes, then cook 5 min/120°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Insert measuring cup, then start Blend /10 sec. Transfer to a bowl.

Coconut rice

  1. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Rinse rice until water runs clear. Place water, oil, 100 g of the coconut milk, salt and rinsed rice into mixing bowl, stir with spatula, then start Rice Cooker , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  2. Place rice in a thermal serving bowl and drizzle with remaining 100 g coconut milk. Mix with a fork to incorporate the coconut milk and fluff the rice. Cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Sambal

  1. Place coriander leaves and onion into mixing bowl, then chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add remaining sambal ingredients and chop 10 sec//speed 4 or until combined. Place in a serving bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place a large heavy-based frying pan over high heat and add oil (see Tips). Add the crabs and reserved Chilli sauce and fry for 1-2 minutes, turning constantly, until crabs are well coated.
  2. Stir in coconut milk, reduce heat to medium, then cover frying pan with a lid. Cook for 5-8 minutes, stirring frequently to ensure even cooking, or until crab shell is bright orange and flesh is opaque; take care not to overcook.
  3. Sprinkle reserved coconut rice with sliced spring onion/shallot and the reserved coriander leaves. Serve crab straight from the pan with coconut rice and sambal.

Hints & Tips

  • To ensure this recipe is gluten free, be sure to read the label of your fish sauce.
  • We used blue swimmer crabs as they are sweet and easier to pop open, but mud crabs would be excellent too.
  • To clean crabs, turn over so the underside faces up. Remove the triangular flap and lift top shell away from body - most of the internal organs will come away too. Discard shell and innards. Break off the eyes and lift out and discard feathery gills (also known as dead man's fingers) and anything that isn't meat. Using the heel of a large knife, cut crabs in half.
  • If you do not have a large enough frying pan, cook your crabs in 2 batches, dividing the Chilli sauce and coconut milk between each batch.
  • To make spring onion curls, cut the spring onion into 6 cm lengths and slice thinly into strips. Place strips in a bowl of water with ice. Stand for 10 minutes or until curled, then drain.
  • This recipe was contributed by chef, Mark Labrooy.
