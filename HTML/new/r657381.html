Everyday gourmet butter

 5.0 (4 ratings)

Ingredients

Butter

  • 600 g pouring (whipping) cream (see Tips)
  • 1000 g chilled water

Flavouring

  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 5 sprigs fresh chives, cut into halves
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only or 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ tsp lemon zest
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 - 2 pinches cayenne pepper, to taste
  • 2 - 3 pinches smoked salt or salt of choice, to taste

Preparation

Butter

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and beat 1-3 min/speed 4 or until mixture separate into solids and liquids (see Tips). Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain over a jug, reserving liquid (buttermilk) for another use, if desired (see Tips).
  3. Place 500 g of the chilled water and butter solids into mixing bowl, then mix 5-10 sec/speed 4. Liquids and solids will separate again. Using simmering basket, strain mixture again so only butter remains. Discard liquid.
  4. Place remaining 500 g chilled water and butter solids in mixing bowl, then mix 5-10 sec/speed 4.
  5. Using simmering basket, strain so only butter solids remain, discarding liquid. Place butter in an airtight container and store in the fridge until needed.

Flavouring

  1. Place parsley, chives, thyme, garlic and lemon zest into mixing bowl, then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and remaining flavouring ingredients, then mix 15 sec/speed 3.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 25 sec/speed 3 or until well combined. Form butter into a log on a piece of baking paper, then wrap to enclose, twisting ends. Refrigerate for 20 minutes to firm, then use as desired (see Tips).

Hints & Tips

  • Cream closer to expiry date will separate faster.
  • This recipe will produce approx. 250 g buttermilk (liquid portion). Reserve the buttermilk after draining in step 2 to use in Spiced fruit loaf (search Cookidoo®), as well as in scones, breads and pancakes. Buttermilk can also be frozen for up to 1 month for later use.
  • Use this butter with baked potatoes, corn on the cob and grilled steaks.
  • Store butter in the fridge for up to 2 weeks or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
