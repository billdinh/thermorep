Prosciutto Herb Breadsticks

 4.6 (8 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 1 package frozen puff pastry dough, 2 count
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 12 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 16 fresh basil leaves
  • 5 ½ oz Gruyère cheese, cubed
  • 4 oz prosciutto, slices separated
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp water

Preparation

  1. Defrost puff pastry according to package instructions. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place thyme, parsley, basil and Gruyère cheese into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 5. Add prosciutto and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Lay 1 thawed puff pastry sheet on a flat surface and sprinkle with ½ of the reserved prosciutto-cheese and herb mixture. Using a rolling pin, roll over mixture and press into the dough. Cut into 12 strips (approx. ¾ in.), twist each strip and place onto prepared baking sheet. Refrigerate to chill. Repeat with remaining pastry sheet and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together egg and water. Brush breadsticks with egg wash and bake 15-20 minutes (375°F). Serve immediately or package for gifts to freeze and reheat (see Tip).

Hints & Tips

  • Prosciutto Herb Breadsticks make a perfect hostess gift, just prepare, package and freeze. Place the cooled baking sheet with baked breadsticks on it in the freezer for at least one hour. Once frozen, store breadsticks in an airtight container, between parchment and store in freezer for up to three months. Re-crisp Prosciutto Herb Breadsticks by placing in a preheated oven at 350°F and bake 12-13 minutes or until crisp.
