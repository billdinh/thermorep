Christy Tania's Macarons with strawberry cream cheese ganache

Ingredients

Strawberry cream cheese ganache

  • 10 g titanium strength gelatine leaves
  • cold water, to soak
  • 85 g cream cheese, cut into pieces
  • 80 g frozen strawberries
  • 10 - 20 g freshly squeezed lime juice, to taste
  • 170 g thickened cream
  • 80 g white chocolate, broken into small pieces

Macarons

  • 140 g almond meal (see Tips)
  • 210 g pure icing sugar
  • 100 g egg whites, room temperature
  • 25 g caster sugar
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • raw sugar, to sprinkle

Preparation

Strawberry cream cheese ganache

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh gelatine leaves into it. Cover with cold water and set aside to soak until soft and pliable (approx. 10 min).
  2. Place cream cheese, strawberries, lime juice and 85 g of the cream into mixing bowl, then heat 8 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Remove gelatine from water and squeeze gently to remove excess water. Add gelatine and chocolate to mixing bowl, then mix 10 sec/speed 3 or until gelatine dissolves and chocolate melts.
  4. Add remaining 85 g cream and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Pour into a bowl, cover ganache directly with plastic wrap. Cool, then refrigerate for 3 hours or overnight until firm enough to pipe. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips).

Macarons

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) or set aside a silicone macaron mat.
  2. Place almond meal and icing sugar into mixing bowl, then mix 10 sec/speed 6. Sieve into a large bowl and set aside. Discard contents of sieve. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips).
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites, caster sugar and cream of tartar into mixing bowl, then whip 5 min/speed 4, without measuring cup, until stiff and glossy. Transfer to a large bowl. Using a metal spoon, fold in one-third of the almond mixture at a time to form a smooth, shiny mixture.
  4. Place mixture in a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (1 cm) and pipe approx. 56 macarons (2 cm diameter) onto prepared baking tray or silicone macaron mat. Tap tray to remove any bubbles. Sprinkle with raw sugar and set aside for 30-60 minutes to dry and form a slight crust (see Tips).
  5. Preheat oven to 130°C. Bake macarons on the middle shelf of the oven for 20 minutes (130°C). Transfer macarons on the paper or mat to a wire rack to cool (approx. 1 hour - see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place ganache in mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer ganache to a piping bag fitted with a 1 cm plain nozzle. Pipe ganache onto the flat side of half the macaron shells, then sandwich together with the remaining macaron shells.

Hints & Tips

  • Do a vinegar wash to thoroughly clean your mixing bowl before whipping the egg whites: Insert butterfly whisk, then place 500 g water and 1 dash vinegar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk, empty mixing bowl, rinse with hot water and dry.
  • To make your own almond meal, mill blanched almonds for 10 sec/speed 7, until a powder consistency.
  • In humid climates, macarons could take up to 1 hour or more to form a crust. Check shells regularly; they will be dried and ready when you can gently run your finger over the top of macarons without batter sticking to your finger.
  • You can store unfilled macaron shells in a airtight container for up to 1 week.
  • Strawberry ganache will keep for up to 3 days in the fridge. Use leftover ganache as a filling for cakes, on scones or served with fruit.
