Sous-Vide Teriyaki Chicken Thighs (TM5)

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

  • 2 oz soy sauce
  • 1 ½ oz honey
  • ½ oz brown sugar
  • 1 oz rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • ¼ tsp chili powder
  • 2 ½ oz sake (rice wine)
    or 2 ½ oz water
  • 4 chicken thighs, skin on and bone in (approx. 6 oz ea.)
  • 48 oz water
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 ½ tsp corn starch
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 - 2 spring onion cross-sliced
  • 1 fresh chili, cored, cut diagonally

Preparation

  1. Place soy sauce, honey, brown sugar, rice vinegar, grated ginger, chili and sake into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Place chicken thighs in a bowl, pour marinade over chicken and mix well to coat. Cover with cling wrap and let marinate in refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Insert blade cover and press down to secure. Place water and lemon juice into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/160°F//speed 1. Meanwhile, drain chicken thighs, reserving marinade for later use. Place 2 drained chicken thighs side by side in each vacuum sealer bag. Seal bags using a vacuum sealer machine as per manufacturer’s instructions (see Tip). Set aside.
  3. Place sealed bags with chicken into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bags are well covered by water (do not exceed maximum fill level mark (2.2 l) and cook sous-vide 60 min/160°F//speed 1. Once cooked, remove bags from mixing bowl with kitchen tongs. Carefully empty mixing bowl to prevent blade cover from falling out. Carefully remove blade cover.
  4. Open bags, place meat juices from both bags, reserved marinade and corn starch into mixing bowl, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 8 min/212°F/speed 2.5. Meanwhile, dry cooked chicken thighs with paper towel and set aside.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan to medium heat. Carefully add cooked chicken thighs skin side down in the pan and sear 2-3 minutes, pressing gently with a spatula to maintain pan contact, until the skin is golden brown. Check thighs are cooked sufficiently, then add sauce from mixing bowl to pan and glaze the meat for 30-40 seconds. Transfer chicken thighs to a plate, garnish with toasted sesame seeds, spring onions and fresh chili and serve immediately.

Useful Items

Hints & Tips

  • If the bags are not sufficiently covered with water, add some more water to the mixing bowl, taking care not to exceed the maximum capacity of the mixing bowl.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: Fill a large container or bowl with water. Place 2 chicken thighs, side by side, into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 1 inch open. Slowly submerge bag into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bag closed just before it is completely submerged. Repeat with remaining 2 chicken thighs. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • Place blade cover & peeler onto mixing knife making sure the upward curved parts are on top of the upper positioned blades. Press down gently to secure it to the mixing knife.
  • To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bags add 1 oz lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • Before placing meat in vacuum sealer bag, turn the top edge of bag inside out approximately 2 inches from the edge in order to keep the top clean.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bags. Bags should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l).
  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking; resistant up to 212°F; resistant up to 8 hours of cooking; BPA-free; PVC-free.
