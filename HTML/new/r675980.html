20th anniversary ombré cake

 4.6 (5 ratings)
Difficulty

Ingredients

Syrup

  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 1 lime, zested and juiced
  • 120 g gin
  • 80 g tonic water
  • 10 juniper berries, crushed

Four cake layers

  • 450 g unsalted butter, softened and cut into pieces, plus extra to grease
  • 400 g caster sugar
  • 8 eggs, room temperature
  • 440 g self-raising flour
  • 4 tbsp milk
  • natural green food colouring

Butter icing

  • 500 g unsalted butter, softened and cut into pieces
  • 700 g icing sugar
  • 1 tbsp milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Assembly

  • natural green food colouring

Preparation

Syrup

  1. Place sugar and lime zest into mixing bowl, then chop 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add gin, tonic, lime juice and juniper berries, then cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Pour into a bowl and set aside.

Four cake layers

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line 4 round cake tins (20 cm - see Tips) and set aside.
  2. Place 225 g of the butter and 200 g of the sugar into mixing bowl, then mix 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Mix 2 min/speed 3, then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3, gradually adding 4 of the eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add 220 g of the flour and 2 tablespoons of the milk, then mix 40 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk and divide batter evenly between 2 bowls.
  5. Without cleaning mixing bowl or butterfly, place remaining 225 g butter and 200 g sugar into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 8.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Mix 2 min/speed 3, then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3, gradually adding remaining 4 eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add remaining 220 g flour and 2 tablespoons milk and mix 40 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk and divide batter between another 2 bowls.
  8. Add varying quantities of green food colouring to each bowl of batter to colour them in graduating shades of green, from light to dark. Mix well with a spatula to ensure they are evenly coloured. Pour each bowl of coloured cake batter into a prepared cake tin. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each of the cakes comes out clean. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  9. Allow to cool in the tins for 5 minutes, then turn cakes out and place onto a wire rack to cool completely. Using a wooden skewer pierce the cakes all over and brush the syrup over the cakes, allowing it to sink into each cake.

Butter icing

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add icing sugar and blend 1 min/speed 3, with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add milk and vanilla, then mix 3 min/speed 3 or until light and fluffy.

Assembly

  1. If necessary, trim the tops from each cake to ensure they are flat. Spread a little icing onto a cake board or serving plate to help secure the cake. Place the darkest layer of cake on the board, then spread with 4 heaped tablespoons (150 g) of butter icing. Repeat with the remaining cakes and icing, layering upwards from darkest to lightest.
  2. Using a palette knife or offset spatula, spread 300 g of butter icing in a thin layer over the top and sides of the cake, then refrigerate until icing is firm (approx. 15 minutes).
  3. Divide the remaining icing between four bowls, adding a little more to the first bowl. Leave the first bowl white, then colour the remaining bowls with varying quantities of green colouring, to colour them in graduating shades of green, from light to dark. Transfer each colour to a separate piping bag fitted with a 1 cm nozzle. Starting with the darkest colour, pipe a line of icing around the bottom cake layer. Pipe the next shade of green around the second cake layer and the lightest shade around the third layer. Finish with the white icing, piping it around the last cake layer and over the top of the cake. Using a palette knife or spatula, smooth the icing around the edge of the cake, blending the different tones together slightly to give your cake the desired ombré finish. Place in the fridge for 30 minutes to set before decorating and serving (see Tips).

Hints & Tips

  • If you don't have 4 cake tins, you can bake each portion of the batter separately. Once your first cake has cooled for 5 minutes in the tin, turn it out onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry your cake tin, then grease and line again, add the next portion of batter and bake. Repeat until you have 4 baked cake layers.
  • A palette knife has a blunt, rounded, flexible blade and is ideal for icing cakes. You can also use an offset palette knife or spatula, which has an angled blade to make icing cakes even easier. It is also handy for levelling batter in the cake tin.
  • For a more textured finish to the ombré effect, run a narrow offset spatula around the edge of the cake.
  • Before serving, decorate the cake with fresh white flowers.
