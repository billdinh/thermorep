Risotto with Broccolini

 4.3 (23 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, in pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 oz shallots, in pieces
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, divided
  • ½ tsp salt, divided, to taste
  • 4 oz sun-dried tomatoes, in oil, undrained, julienned
  • 8 oz fresh broccolini, in florets (2 in.)
  • 11 oz risotto rice (e.g. Carnaroli, Arborio) with 14-15 minutes cooking time (see Tip)
  • 2 oz dry white wine
  • 29 oz water
  • 1 heaping tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tip)
    or 1 heaping tsp homemade vegetable stock paste
  • 4 oz toasted pine nuts, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, shallots, 1 oz butter and ¼ tsp salt into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add sun-dried tomatoes (with oil, undrained) and broccolini and without measuring cup sauté 5-6 min/250°F/speed 1, until broccolini is just tender. Transfer to bowl and keep warm.
  3. Place 0.5 oz butter and rice into mixing bowl and without measuring cup cook 3 min/250°F//speed 1.5.
  4. Add wine and without measuring cup cook 1 min/212°F//speed 1.5.
  5. Add water, stock paste and ¼ tsp salt. Scrape bottom of mixing bowl thoroughly with spatula to loosen rice. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 12-13 min/212°F//speed 1, until rice is cooked. Season rice with salt, to taste.
  6. Allow risotto to rest in mixing bowl for 1 minute, then add remaining 0.5 oz butter, reserved grated cheese and stir with spatula. Transfer to bowls and top with reserved cooked broccolini. Sprinkle with toasted pine nuts and serve immediately.

Hints & Tips

  • Adjust cooking time according to time indicated on rice package.
  • Recipe for Vegetable Stock Paste can be found in The Basic Cookbook.
  • Depending on rice variety, different amounts of liquid are absorbed. If the risotto looks too dry before the rice is cooked, add more liquid.
  • Risotto tastes best when served immediately.
  • For a vegan version: omit Parmesan cheese from recipe and stock paste if using Vegetable Stock Paste in The Basic Cookbook.
  • Rule of thumb for creating your own risotto recipe: for every 3.5 oz risotto rice you will need 10 oz liquids (water and wine) combined with stock paste. The maximum quantity of rice that can be cooked at any one time for this basic recipe is 17.5 oz. Note that adding other ingredients (e.g. mushrooms or sausage) will increase overall volume of the dish, so adjust rice quantity accordingly.
  • Replace broccolini with white Swiss chard, cut in pieces.
