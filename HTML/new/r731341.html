Miniroscón de Reyes (Spanish King's cake)

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

Starter dough

  • 80 g milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 130 g baker's flour

Dough

  • 120 g sugar
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 60 g milk
  • 70 g unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 30 g orange blossom water (see Tips)
  • 450 g baker's flour
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Assembly and decoration

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 100 - 150 g mixed glacé fruit
  • 30 g flaked almonds
  • 50 g sugar (moistened with a few drops of water)

Preparation

Starter dough

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add yeast, sugar and flour into mixing bowl, then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Place dough on silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Place in a large bowl and cover dough with warm water. Dough will be ready when it floats and doubles in size (approx. 15 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Line 2 baking trays with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and citrus zests into mixing bowl, then mill 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add all remaining dough ingredients, including drained starter dough, then mix 30 sec/speed 6 and knead Dough /3 min. Leave to prove in mixing bowl until dough comes out of hole in mixing bowl lid (approx. 1 hour 30 minutes).
  4. Using spatula, push dough down towards bottom of mixing bowl and knead for a further Dough /1 min. Place dough, which will be soft, onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a smooth ball.

Assembly and decoration

  1. Divide into 12 portions (approx. 100 g each) and shape each portion into a ball. To form into a doughnut-like shape, use your finger to press into the centre of each ball to make a hole (approx. 4 cm). Repeat with remaining balls, then arrange on prepared trays. Using a pastry brush, brush with beaten egg. Decorate with glacé fruit, flaked almonds and moistened sugar, then leave to prove until doubled in size.
  2. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  3. Bake for 10 minutes (200°C) or until golden. Allow to cool before serving.
