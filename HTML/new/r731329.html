Pan-fried pork buns

 4.7 (7 ratings)
Difficulty

Ingredients

Pork filling

  • 400 g pork mince
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 15 g sugar
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 15 g soy sauce
  • 50 g water

Dough

  • 250 g water
  • 15 g sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g wheaten flour (see Tips), plus extra to dust
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil

Frying the buns

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • water, to cook
  • 1 tsp black sesame seeds
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, thinly sliced

Preparation

Pork filling

  1. Place all filling ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 3, then knead Dough /1 min. Place in a bowl and refrigerate until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Place water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl, then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, salt and oil, then knead Dough /3 min. Place dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside to prove for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into 20 portions (approx. 35 g each). On a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface, roll each into a disc (approx. 7 cm), making the centre slightly thicker than the edges. Place approx. 2 heaped teaspoons of the pork filling in the centre of each disc, then pull dough upwards over the filling, folding and pinching the edges tightly to close. Repeat with remaining dough portions and filling, then set aside for 15 minutes before frying.

Frying the buns

  1. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of oil. Once oil is hot, working in batches, add buns to pan, cover and fry until bottom of buns are golden brown. Then add 2 tablespoons of water, cover with lid and fry until water is absorbed (approx. 5 minutes). Add another 2 tablespoons of water, cover again and fry until water is absorbed (approx. 5 minutes). Remove lid and sprinkle with half of the black sesame seeds and spring onions/shallots. Fry for a further 1 minute. Repeat cooking process with remaining buns, then serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Don't confuse wheaten flour with plain flour. You will need to buy this low-protein flour from a good Asian grocer. It should contain around 10% protein.
  • The shaped, uncooked buns can be stored in the freezer for up to 1 month.
