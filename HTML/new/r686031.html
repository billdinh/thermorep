Perfect Roast Turkey and Gravy (Bill Yosses)

Ingredients

Turkey Brine - 2 Days Before Roasting

  • 1 whole turkey (15-18 lb), thawed if frozen
  • 40 oz water, plus extra to cover turkey (based on size of bird)
  • 12 oz kosher salt (no iodine)
  • 10 oz jaggery sugar
    or 10 oz light brown sugar
  • 4 oz honey
    or 4 oz agave syrup
  • 4 oz maple syrup
  • 3 oz allspice berries
  • 2 oz whole yellow mustard seeds
  • 2 oz whole black peppercorns (not ground)
  • 16 whole cloves
  • 8 whole star anise
  • 8 fresh bay leaves
    or 8 dried bay leaves
  • 15 green cardamom pods
  • 10 oz onions, quartered
  • 6 in. fresh ginger, peeled, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 3 cinnamon sticks (3-4 in. ea.)
  • 2 oranges, sliced in rounds

Roast Turkey

  • 12 oz unsalted butter, room temperature, divided
  • 28 oz white wine
  • 1 tsp salt, divided
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper, divided
  • 6 oz carrots, cut into pieces (2 in.)
  • 10 oz yellow onions, cut into eighths
  • 10 oz celery, cut into pieces (1 in.)

Gravy

  • 4 oz all-purpose flour
  • salt, to season, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to season, to taste
  • fresh sage leaves, to garnish
  • 6 - 8 sprigs fresh thyme, to garnish
  • pomegranates, halved, to garnish
  • persimmons, to garnish

Preparation

Turkey Brine - 2 Days Before Roasting

  1. Pat dry turkey and allow to sit at room temperature (30 min.). Meanwhile make the brine.
  2. Place 40 oz water, kosher salt, jaggery sugar, honey, maple syrup, allspice, mustard seeds, peppercorns, cloves, star anise, bay leaves and cardamom into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/212°F/speed 1. Transfer to a large bowl and allow to cool to room temperature. Meanwhile prep the turkey.
  3. Clean turkey cavities, remove neck and giblets, transfer neck to a bowl, cover and refrigerate, discard giblets. Place turkey into a brining bag or non-reactive container (see Tip).
  4. Place onions and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to bowl with turkey brine.
  5. Pour cooled brining mixture over turkey, add cinnamon sticks, orange slices and additional water to fully cover the turkey. Seal the bag or container, place in refrigerator and brine for 2 days.

Roast Turkey

  1. Approximately 30 minutes - 1 hour prior to cooking, remove turkey from refrigerator and discard brine. Pat bird dry inside and out with paper towels and allow to come to room temperature (approx. 30 min.-1 hr.).
  2. Place 8 oz butter and wine into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/212°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Set oven rack to lowest level and preheat oven to 450°F. Fold a large piece of cheesecloth into a few layers thick, large enough to drape over the turkey. Immerse folded cheesecloth in butter and wine mixture and allow to soak.
  4. Place turkey, breast side up, on a roasting rack in a heavy metal roasting pan. Fold wing tips under turkey. Sprinkle inside of turkey with ½ tsp salt and ½ tsp pepper. Place reserved neck into large cavity and fill with as many carrot, onion and celery pieces as it can hold loosely; do not pack tightly. Fold neck flap under and sprinkle with remaining ½ tsp salt and ½ tsp pepper. Place any remaining vegetables around the bottom of roasting pan. Tie legs together with butcher's twine (see Tip).
  5. Remove cheesecloth from liquid, squeeze slightly, leaving it very damp. Drape the cheesecloth evenly over breast and halfway down the sides of the turkey. Rub 4 oz softened butter all over turkey and cheesecloth. Place turkey into oven, legs first and roast 30 minutes (450°F). Using a pastry brush, baste cheesecloth and exposed parts of turkey with butter-wine mixture. Reduce oven temperature to 350°F and roast a further 2½ hours (350°F) basting every 30 minutes; if the pan gets too full, spoon out juices and reserve for gravy.
  6. Carefully remove and discard cheesecloth. Turn roasting pan so breast is facing back of oven and baste with pan juices or butter-wine mixture. Baste carefully to avoid tearing the fragile skin as it browns. Roast a further 60 minutes (350°F) or until a meat thermometer inserted in the thickest part of the thigh registers 155°F (see Tip), basting after 30 minutes.
  7. Once fully cooked, remove vegetables and neck from breast cavity and set aside. Transfer turkey to a serving platter and let rest (approx. 30 min.). Meanwhile, once cool enough to handle, remove meat from the reserved neck, shred and make the gravy.

Gravy

  1. Place all the pan juices, shredded neck meat, flour and reserved vegetables into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/212°F/speed 1. Blend 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Strain into a warmed gravy boat. Garnish Roast Turkey with sage leaves, thyme, pomegranates and persimmons and serve hot with gravy.

Useful Items

brining bag or large non-reactive container, paper towels, cheesecloth, heavy metal roasting pan with roasting rack, pastry brush, oven, meat thermometer, serving platter, refrigerator, gravy boat, fine-mesh sieve, butcher's twine, large bowl

Hints & Tips

  • Make sure your brining container is large enough to submerge the turkey in brine and still fit in the refrigerator. Use a non-reactive container, e.g. glass, stainless steel or extra large food safe sealable storage bag.
  • Trussing the turkey will guarantee even cooking and easy carving. To truss the turkey, take the center of a long piece of kitchen twine and wrap it around the neck bone in between the two breasts. Pull the two ends down either side of the breasts towards the legs, wrapping tightly underneath the breastplate and tie a knot to secure. Cross the ends of the drumsticks one over the other, wrap the twine around them and make another knot to secure and hold the legs together. Bring the two ends of the twine back up either side of the breasts towards the wings, tucking twine around the nubbins (small notch in the wings), flip the bird over and tie the ends of the twine together in a knot. Bring ends back up, towards the back end of the bird, flip and tie again over the legs. Trim off the ends of the twine.
  • If your roasting pan only fits sideways in the oven, turn the pan every hour so the turkey cooks and browns evenly.
  • Check the internal temperature in the innermost part of the thigh. If thighs are not yet fully cooked, baste turkey, return to oven, and cook an additional 20-30 minutes. Turkey will continue to cook while resting.
