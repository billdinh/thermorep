Walnut Brittle

 3.4 (9 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 7 oz walnut halves, toasted
  • 8 ½ oz brown sugar
  • 37 ½ oz water, divided
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, in 1 cube (1 in. x 1 in. x 1 in.)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice (see Tip)

Preparation

  1. To ensure the success of caramel recipes the ingredients must match exactly in quantity and description given. The recipe should be cooked immediately after placing sugar with other ingredients in the mixing bowl. The bowl must be filled for Pre-cleaning immediately after the finished caramel is transferred from mixing bowl and the mixing bowl edge cleaned with spatula before the Pre-cleaning is started.
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment.
  3. Place walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 1-2 sec/speed 5. Spread walnuts on prepared baking sheet. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Place brown sugar, 2.5 oz water, butter and lemon juice into mixing bowl, place splash guard onto mixing bowl lid and caramelize Sugar Stages . Pour caramel immediately over crushed walnuts on baking sheet, mix with spatula and smooth. Immediately clean all caramel residues from edge of mixing bowl with spatula, so mixing bowl lid closes properly for Pre-cleaning.
  5. Place 35 oz water and 1 drop dish soap into mixing bowl, insert measuring cup and start Pre-clean /Fats or Caramel.
  6. Allow brittle to cool to room temperature and break into pieces. Store Walnut Brittle in an airtight container (see Tip) and use as needed.

Hints & Tips

  • The lemon juice prevents caramel from crystallizing or burning without altering the taste.
  • The brittle will last up to a week in a cool place in an airtight container.
  • Toast walnuts in a fry pan over medium heat (approx. 5 min.) stirring frequently. Allow to cool before chopping.
