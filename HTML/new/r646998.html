Sriracha Sauce

 5.0 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

  • 8 - 10 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 500 g fresh red chillies, halved (see tips)
  • 10 - 15 g salt (see tip)
  • 60 g light brown sugar
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 60 g rice vinegar
  • 30 g fish sauce

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, chillies, salt, brown sugar and caster sugar in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a clean glass jar with airtight lid (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm gap at the top. Make sure that rim of jar is clean and close immediately. Leave to ferment at room temperature for 3-7 days, checking every day, letting out some gas before closing jar again. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. After this time, place vinegar, fish sauce and fermented chilli mixture in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/120°C/speed 1. Transfer to a clean glass jar with airtight lid (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm gap at the top. Make sure that rim of jar is clean and close immediately. Leave to cool then store in fridge for 2-3 months, using as desired.

Hints & Tips

  • Depending on the type of chilli use, this sriracha can be much spicier than shop-bought versions. If preferred, remove seeds before using.
  • Any red chilli will be delicious, but for a more authentic flavour, use red jalapeno chillies or Thai red chillies.
  • Salt is necessary for fermentation to encourage an environment for the bacteria to grow and for the chillies to ferment properly. In general, the weight of salt should be 2% of the weight of the vegetables being fermented. The quantity of salt needed here will depend on the weight of the garlic. Add this weight to the weight of the chillies then calculate 2% of this weight for required salt quantity.
  • To prepare jar, thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heating in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • To make this vegetarian, vegetarian and vegan fish sauces are available in some supermarkets. Alternatively, substitute with light soy sauce.
