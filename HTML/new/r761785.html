Sous Vide Orange Thyme Pork Chops with Cherry Port Sauce

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

Orange Compound Butter

  • 1 orange
  • 8 oz unsalted butter, diced, chilled
  • 6 cloves roasted garlic, homemade (see Tip) or 6 cloves roasted garlic, store bought
  • ½ oz honey
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Sous Vide Pork Chops

  • 2 pork chops, boneless (approx. 6-8 oz ea., 1 in. thick)
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 sprigs thyme, divided
  • 65 oz water, to fully submerge bag (not higher than the max. fill level)
  • 1 oz lemon juice

Cherry Port Sauce

  • 1 ½ oz shallots, cut into pieces
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • 7 oz port wine
  • 1 oz balsamic vinegar
  • 1 oz brown sugar
  • 4 oz frozen cherries, pitted, thawed, halved
  • vegetable oil, to sear

Preparation

Orange Compound Butter

  1. With a vegetable peeler, thinly peel zest from 1 orange, avoiding the white pith and set aside. Cut the orange in half, squeeze the juice from one half into a small bowl and set aside. Slice the remaining half of the orange and set aside.
  2. Place reserved orange peel into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add butter, roasted garlic, honey, salt, pepper and 2 tsp reserved orange juice and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a sheet of parchment and roll into a log shape (approx. 6 in.). Wrap parchment to seal and refrigerate until firm (approx. 1 hr.) or until ready to use. Clean mixing bowl.

Sous Vide Pork Chops

  1. Season pork chops with salt and pepper. Place side by side into a vacuum sealer bag so they are not overlapping. Top each pork chop with 1 tbsp reserved Orange Compound Butter, 1 slice reserved orange and 1 sprig thyme. Seal bag with a vacuum food sealer machine as per manufacturer’s instructions (see Tip) and set aside.
  2. Insert blade cover. Place sealed bag into mixing bowl and add enough water to fully submerge bag. Do not exceed the maximum fill mark (2.2 l). Remove bag from mixing bowl and set aside. Add lemon juice and heat Sous-vide /7 min/144°F.
  3. Place sealed bag into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bag is completely submerged and cook Sous-vide /1 h 30 min/144°F (see Tip). Once cooked, carefully remove bag from mixing bowl with kitchen tongs, transfer to an insulated serving bowl and keep warm until ready to sear. Discard water from mixing bowl. Remove blade cover.

Cherry Port Sauce

  1. Place 0.5 oz reserved Orange Compound Butter, shallots and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and without measuring cup cook 4 min/250°F/speed 1.
  2. Add port wine, balsamic vinegar, brown sugar and cherries and without measuring cup cook 30 min/Varoma//speed .
  3. Add 0.5 oz reserved Orange Compound Butter, insert measuring cup and mix 1 min//speed . Season to taste, set aside and keep warm.
  4. On a stovetop, heat oil in a large non-stick frying pan. Open bag, transfer pork to the hot pan and sear 2-3 minutes per side, adding 1 tbsp reserved Orange Compound Butter to the pan after the first flip and baste the pork. Garnish with thyme and serve hot with reserved Cherry Port Sauce.

Hints & Tips

  • Serve with steamed green beans and roasted potatoes, or a green salad.
  • To make your own Roasted Garlic, search Cookidoo® for Roasted Garlic in our Stock Your Staples collection.
  • For thicker pork chops sear until internal temperature reads 145°F or cooked to desired temperature.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: Fill a large container or bowl with water. Place pork chops into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 1 inch open. Slowly submerge bag into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bag closed just before it is completely submerged. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bag add 1 oz lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • Before placing meat in vacuum sealer bag, turn the top edge of bag inside out approximately 2 inches from the edge in order to keep the top clean.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bag. Bag should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l).
  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking, resistant up to 212°F, resistant up to 8 hours of cooking, BPA-free, PVC-free.
