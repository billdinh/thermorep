Caramel Éclairs

Ingredients

Choux Pastry

  • 5 oz water
  • 2 ½ oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • 4 oz all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs

Caramel Sauce

  • 9 oz sugar
  • 7 oz heavy whipping cream, divided
  • 36 oz water, divided
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, cold, in 1 cube (approx. 1 in. x 1 in.)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • ½ oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 tbsp vinegar

Pastry Cream

  • 1 vanilla bean pod
    or 1 ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 17 ½ oz whole milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3 ½ oz sugar
  • 1 ½ oz corn starch

Preparation

Choux Pastry

  1. Place water, butter, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/212°F/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl, open mixing bowl lid and allow mixture to cool in mixing bowl 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  4. Set mixing bowl back in position and without measuring cup and without setting a time mix speed 5, while adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. After adding the last egg, mix a further 30 sec/speed 5.
  5. Transfer dough into a pastry bag with a plain tip (approx. ¾ in.) and form 12 long sticks (4-5 in. long) by letting them fall from the pastry bag onto prepared baking sheet, spacing 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake 20-25 minutes (400°F) or until the éclair are golden. Turn oven off and leave oven door ajar, allowing éclairs to dry for 10 minutes. Carefully remove baking sheet from oven and allow to cool completely. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.

Caramel Sauce

  1. To ensure the success of recipes with Sugar Stages the ingredients must match exactly in quantity and description given. The recipe should be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with other ingredients in the mixing bowl. The bowl must be filled for Pre-cleaning immediately after the finished caramel is transferred from mixing bowl and the mixing bowl edge cleaned with spatula before Pre-cleaning is started.
  2. Place sugar, 2.5 oz whipping cream, 1 oz water, 1 oz butter and lemon juice into mixing bowl, place splash guard instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and start Sugar Stages .
  3. Add remaining 4.5 oz cream and 0.5 oz butter and without measuring cup mix 2 min/speed 2. Then, without measuring cup, cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 2. Pour into a heat-proof jar and set aside to cool to room temperature. Immediately clean edge of mixing bowl with spatula to remove any remaining caramel.
  4. Place 35 oz water and vinegar into mixing bowl, insert measuring cup and start Pre-clean /Fats or Caramel. Thoroughly wash and dry the mixing bowl and lid.

Pastry Cream

  1. Split a vanilla bean lengthwise, scrape out seeds and place into mixing bowl, reserve pod for another use (see Tip). Add milk, eggs, sugar and corn starch and start Thicken /212°F. Transfer pastry cream to a bowl, cover with cling wrap in direct contact with the Pastry Cream and refrigerate until cool (see Tip). Meanwhile clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place cooled pastry cream and 3 oz reserved caramel into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  3. Transfer Caramel Pastry Cream to a piping bag with a long, thin piping tip. With a sharp knife, make two ¼-inch holes on the underside of each éclair (one near each end) and fill with the Caramel Pastry Cream. Serve drizzled with reserved Caramel Sauce.

Useful Items

oven, baking sheet, parchment paper, pastry bag and plain nozzle, cling wrap, sharp knife, heat-proof jar, refrigerator, piping bag with long thin tip

Hints & Tips

  • For best results, the 1 oz butter in the caramel sauce should be added in one piece. Make sure the piece has the following dimensions (1 in. x 1 in. x 1 in.).
  • Search Cookidoo® for Vanilla Sugar recipe and use vanilla pods leftover from other recipes (rinse and dry pods after first use). When only using the pods, the vanilla flavor and aroma will not be strong.
  • The lemon juice prevents the sugar from crystallizing and burning, leaving the caramel flavor intact.
  • Store remaining caramel sauce in an airtight jar.
  • The pastry cream can take up to 90 minutes to cool. Reduce the cooling time by cooling in a wide, shallow bowl, or divide into 2-3 bowls.
  • Just before serving, sprinkle the drizzled caramel with flaky sea salt.
