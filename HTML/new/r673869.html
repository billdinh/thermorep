Zofia Wolski's Turkey empanada gallega (TM6)

 (No ratings)

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 100 g light olive oil
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 pinches sea salt
  • 200 g water

Filling

  • 400 g turkey, cooked, cooled and cut into bite sized pieces (see Tips)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 onion (approx. 150 g), cut into quarters
  • 100 g red capsicum, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 100 g green capsicum, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Assembly

  • plain flour, to dust
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Set aside.
  2. Place flour, baking powder and salt into mixing bowl, then knead Dough /1 min 30 sec, slowly pouring in reserved oil through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  3. Place the jug back onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it.
  4. Knead Dough /1 min, slowly pouring in reserved water through hole in mixing bowl lid, until combined and a smooth dough forms.
  5. Place dough on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Place in a large bowl, cover with a silicone bread mat or tea towel and set aside to rest for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cooked turkey into it. Set aside.
  2. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add oil, onion, red capsicum and green capsicum, then cook High Temp. .
  4. Add paprika, tomato paste, parsley, salt and pepper and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1, then chop 2 sec/speed 6.
  5. Add reserved turkey and mix with spatula to combine. Set aside to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a large baking tray (35 x 40 cm) and set aside.
  2. Divide dough into two equal portions, then cut off a 100 g piece from one portion and reserve to decorate. Using a rolling pin, roll out the larger portion of dough on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface into a 25 x 35 cm rectangle. Place on prepared tray and top with the cooled filling. Spread filling out evenly, leaving a 2 cm dough border.
  3. Repeat rolling the second portion of dough. Brush border with beaten egg, then cover filling with pastry, pressing edges firmly to seal. Roll out reserved dough and cut out decorative shapes or strips to place on pastry top. Brush pastry top with remaining egg. Using a small knife, make 6–8 small slits in the top for steam to escape. Bake for 20 minutes (190°C) or until golden brown. Serve warm (see Tips).

Hints & Tips

  • You can use any cooked meat of choice such as chicken, chorizo or cooked mince in this recipe. Add chopped olives, anchovies and other pantry items to personalise your empanada.
  • Serve empanada with a mixed green salad.
