Japanese pumpkin dumplings in dashi broth (Matthew Kenney)

Ingredients

Mushroom Dashi Broth

  • 450 g fresh Swiss brown mushrooms
  • 1420 g water
  • 30 g kombu

Toasted Coconut Cinnamon Broth

  • 450 g fresh shiitake mushrooms, trimmed
  • 60 g tamari
  • 2 ½ cinnamon quills, toasted (see Tips)
  • 85 g flaked coconut, toasted

Dumpling Filling

  • 1250 g water
  • 500 g japanese pumpkin, peeled, deseeded and cut into cubes (2.5 cm)
  • 30 g tamari
  • 15 g rice wine vinegar
  • 15 g coconut oil
  • 1 ½ tsp fresh ginger or ginger paste, to taste (optional)
  • 15 g agave syrup (optional)
  • 20 round wonton wrappers
  • micro herb or edible flowers

Preparation

Mushroom Dashi Broth

  1. Place mushrooms and water into mixing bowl, then chop 2 sec/speed 6.5, then cook 60 min/95°C/speed 1
  2. Add kombu and cook 10 min/95°C//speed . Strain broth through a large fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard kombu and reserve mushrooms for a later use (see Tips).

Toasted Coconut Cinnamon Broth

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in shiitakes mushrooms and set aside.
  2. Place 1360 g of the mushroom broth, tamari and cinnamon sticks into mixing bowl, then cook 8 min/95°C/speed 1. Add toasted coconut, remove mixing bowl, set aside and allow to steep 20 minutes.
  3. Strain steeped broth through a fine-mesh sieve over reserved fresh mushrooms in bowl. Discard solids. Place strained broth with mushrooms into a large saucepan and bring to a simmer. Once simmering, remove from heat, cover with lid and continue with the recipe.

Dumpling Filling

  1. Place 680 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, weigh in pumpkin, secure Varoma lid and steam 18-20 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until pumpkin is tender. Remove Varoma and set aside. Empty mixing bowl.
  2. Place steamed pumpkin, tamari, rice vinegar, coconut oil, ginger and agave into mixing bowl, then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool to room temperature.
  3. Place 1 tablespoon of reserved filling in the centre of each wonton wrapper, wet the edge of half the circle with a little water, then carefully fold dough over filling to create a half-moon shape enclosing the filling. Pinch edges with fingertips to seal. Repeat with remaining ingredients. Transfer dumplings into Varoma dish and tray, standing dumplings to fit if necessary.
  4. Place remaining 570 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma with dumplings into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Divide steamed dumplings between bowls, ladle in reserved broth, garnish with micro herbs and serve.

Hints & Tips

  • Use leftover mushrooms in stock or fry with garlic and olive oil and spread on toast.
  • Toast cinnamon sticks in a dry cast iron skillet.
  • Select a pumpkin that looks heavy for its size, has splashes of golden or grayish hues on the exterior. The lighter gray the exterior, the sweeter it will be. Adding the optional ginger and agave will brighten the flavour of a less flavourful pumpkin.
  • Not all flowers are edible, some are poisonous. Flowers should be purchased from a reliable source.
  • Those that suffer from pollen allergies, should not consume edible flowers.
