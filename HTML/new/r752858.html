Salted Caramel and Chocolate Mousse

 4.7 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

Caramel

  • 9 oz sugar
  • 3 oz maple syrup
  • 7 oz heavy whipping cream, divided
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, cold, in 1 cube (approx. 1 in. x 1 in.)
  • 36 oz water, divided
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp vinegar

Chocolate Mousse

  • 2 oz sugar
  • 7 oz dark chocolate (min. 50% cocoa), cut in pieces
  • 3 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 pinch salt

Whipped Cream

  • 16 oz heavy whipping cream
  • chocolate shavings, to garnish

Preparation

Caramel

  1. To ensure the success of recipes with Sugar Stages the ingredients must match exactly in quantity and description given. The recipe should be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with other ingredients in the mixing bowl. The bowl must be filled for Pre-cleaning immediately after the finished caramel is transferred from mixing bowl and the mixing bowl edge cleaned with spatula before Pre-cleaning is started.
  2. Place sugar, maple syrup, 2.5 oz whipping cream, butter, 1 oz water, salt and lemon juice into mixing bowl. Place splash guard instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and start Sugar Stages .
  3. Add 4.5 oz whipping cream and without measuring cup mix 2 min/speed 2. Then, without measuring cup, cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Evenly pour into 2 bowls and refrigerate until cool (approx. 1 hr.). Immediately clean edge of mixing bowl with spatula to remove any remaining caramel.
  4. Place 35 oz water and vinegar into mixing bowl, insert measuring cup and start Pre-clean /Fats or Caramel. Thoroughly wash and dry mixing bowl and lid.

Chocolate Mousse

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chocolate and whipping cream and melt 4 min/120°F/speed 2. Meanwhile, separate eggs and reserve yolks and whites in separate bowls.
  3. Add reserved egg yolks and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer chocolate mixture to a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl thoroughly.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place reserved egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and without measuring cup, whip 3 min/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer whisked egg whites into reserved chocolate mixture and gently fold in with spatula.
  5. Drizzle a layer of reserved Caramel (approx. 1 oz) into 6 small cups. Cover with a layer of Chocolate Mousse (approx. 2 oz). Transfer remaining mousse into a bowl and refrigerate cups and remaining mousse. Meanwhile clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk and continue with recipe.

Whipped Cream

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place whipping cream into mixing bowl and without measuring cup, whip without setting a time/speed 3 until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid over whipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer Whipped Cream into a piping bag with tip.
  2. Pipe a layer of Whipped Cream over Chocolate Mousse in cups. Drizzle with another layer of Caramel, divide remaining Chocolate Mousse between cups and refrigerate 3 hours. Refrigerate piping bag with remaining Whipped Cream.
  3. To serve, pipe remaining Whipped Cream over Chocolate Mousse, drizzle with Caramel and garnish with chocolate shavings.

Hints & Tips

  • Add up to 6 oz Salted Caramel with the cream to make Salted Caramel Whipped Cream.
  • Take care not to over whip the cream or it will turn into butter. Watch carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid and listen for a change in the mixing sound.
  • For a darker mousse use chocolate with 70% cocoa.
  • Add 2-3 tsp orange-flavored liqueur or rum when adding egg yolks.
  • Fold in finely grated orange zest or chocolate chips after folding in the egg whites.
  • For a cooked egg version: place egg yolks into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/150°F/speed 3. Transfer to bowl with chocolate mixture and stir to combine. Clean and dry mixing bowl thoroughly. Insert butterfly whisk, place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and whisk 1 min 30 sec/140°F/speed 3.5 or until egg whites are stiff and continue as per recipe.
