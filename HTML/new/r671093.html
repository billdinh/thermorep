Korean bao beef sliders

 4.5 (21 ratings)
Difficulty

Most of our recipes are easy. Those that require a little more time or cooking ability are rated medium or advanced.

This is how much time you need to prepare this meal.

Serving size

This shows how many portions this recipe makes.

Ingredients

Bao buns

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to grease
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 220 g milk
  • 70 g white sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 400 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Patties

  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 250 g beef mince
  • 250 g pork mince
  • 40 g breadcrumbs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 20 g soy sauce
  • 20 g chilli paste
  • 10 g sesame oil
  • 10 g brown sugar
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper

Spicy coleslaw

  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 capsicum, deseeded and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 150 g Chinese cabbage (wombok), shredded
  • 100 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp Sriracha® sauce (optional - see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp black sesame seeds
  • 1 ½ tsp white sesame seeds

Assembly

  • 500 g boiling water
  • vegetable oil, to shallow fry

Preparation

Bao buns

  1. Lightly grease Varoma dish and tray with oil, then set aside.
  2. Place spinach and milk into mixing bowl, then blend 30 sec/speed 7. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain spinach milk into a jug, then return to mixing bowl.
  3. Add sugar, yeast, baking powder and oil, then mix 30 sec/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Add flour and salt, then knead Dough /2 min. Place dough on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Using a rolling pin, roll into a rectangle (approx. 30 x 20 cm). Starting from a long edge, roll up dough into a log shape, then cut into 12 equal portions.
  5. Divide dough portions between greased Varoma dish and tray, placing them cut-side up. Cover with Varoma lid and leave in a warm place to prove until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, continue with the recipe.

Patties

  1. Place spring onions/shallots, carrot and oil into mixing bowl, then chop 2 sec/speed 7 and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Set aside in mixing bowl for 5 minutes to cool before continuing with the recipe.
  2. Add all remaining patties ingredients and mix 10-15 sec//speed 4, with aid of spatula, until just combined.
  3. Wearing food handling gloves or using clean, damp hands, divide mixture into 12 equal portions (approx. 80 g each, see Tips) and shape into patties. Place on a plate, cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up.

Spicy coleslaw

  1. Place carrot, capsicum and spring onion/shallot into mixing bowl, then chop 2-3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cabbage, mayonnaise and Sriracha® sauce (optional), then combine 2 sec//speed 4. Place in an airtight container, sprinkle with black and white sesame seeds and refrigerate until needed.

Assembly

  1. Carefully place boiling water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma with bao buns into position and secure Varoma lid, then steam 10-15 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until buns are cooked through.
  2. Meanwhile, heat a little oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat. Working in batches, fry patties for 3 minutes on each side or until browned and cooked through. Drain on paper towel.
  3. Cut warm bao buns in half, layer each base with a patty and coleslaw, then sandwich together with bun lids. Serve immediately.

Hints & Tips

  • It is easy to make your own mayonnaise and Sriracha® sauce in your Thermomix®. Simply search Cookidoo® for the recipes.
  • To ensure the patties are equal in size, place a plate on the mixing bowl lid and weigh out 80 g portions before shaping into patties.
  • Bao buns are best assembled and served warm, straight from the Varoma.
  • For plain buns, simply skip Step 2 and add the milk to mixing bowl at Step 3.
