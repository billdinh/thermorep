Karpatka

 4.1 (9 ratings)

Ingredients

Pastry cream

  • 150 g cornflour
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 650 g milk
  • 6 eggs
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract

Choux pastry layers

  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra to grease
  • 130 g plain flour, plus extra to dust
  • 250 g water
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 2 tsp caster sugar
  • 5 eggs

Assembly

  • 200 g unsalted butter, softened and cut into pieces
  • 20 g freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 100 g Advocaat (egg liqueur) (see Tips)
  • icing sugar, to dust

Preparation

Pastry cream

  1. Place cornflour and sugar into mixing bowl, then mix 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add milk, eggs and vanilla, then mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 14 min/90°C/speed 4.
  4. Transfer pastry cream into a bowl, then cover surface directly with baking paper to prevent a skin forming. Cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for 6 hours or overnight until chilled. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Choux pastry layers

  1. Grease 2 baking trays (22 x 32 cm) with butter and dust with flour. Set aside.
  2. Place water, butter, salt and sugar into mixing bowl, then cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour and mix 30 sec/speed 4, without measuring cup. Stand mixture in mixing bowl for 15 minutes, stirring frequently with spatula, until it reaches 40°C.
  4. Preheat oven to 230°C.
  5. Mix speed 5, adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. After adding the last egg, mix 30 sec/speed 5, without measuring cup.
  6. Evenly divide choux mixture between prepared baking trays and spread evenly over the base of the trays. Bake for 20 minutes (230°C) or until golden brown and crisp. Cool for 5 minutes before transferring to wire racks to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place butter into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 3 or until soft and smooth. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Mix 2 min/speed 3, adding reserved cooled pastry cream a spoonful at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  3. Add lemon juice and Advocaat, then mix 40 sec/speed 4 or until smooth and creamy. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Place a pastry layer, top-side up, on a serving plate, then spread with all the pastry cream filling and top with the remaining pastry layer, top-side up. Press down gently. Cover with foil and refrigerate for 2 hours to firm before slicing. Serve dusted with icing sugar.

Useful Items

oven, baking trays (22 x 32 cm), baking paper, bowl, plastic wrap, refrigerator, serving plate, aluminium foil, wire racks

Hints & Tips

  • Karpatka is best eaten on the day of baking when the choux is crisp. Choux layers can be made the day before and crisped up in the oven for a few minutes, then cooled to assemble. To crisp the surface, bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 8-10 minutes.
  • For a non-alcoholic version, replace Advocaat with pouring (whipping) cream.
