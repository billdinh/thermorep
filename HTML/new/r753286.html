White chocolate and strawberry trifle

 4.1 (12 ratings)

This shows how many portions this recipe makes.

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 3 eggs
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 100 g self-raising flour

Strawberry purée

  • 600 g fresh strawberries, trimmed
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar

Mousse

  • 160 g coconut milk
  • 1 ½ tsp gelatine powder
  • 250 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 80 g cream cheese
  • 450 g thickened cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Assembly

  • whipped cream, to decorate (optional)
  • fresh strawberries, to decorate (optional)

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line base of baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Set aside a trifle bowl (approx. 20 x 10 cm).
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, sugar and vanilla into mixing bowl, then whip 3 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 2.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 10 sec/speed 2. Pour into prepared baking tray and spread evenly. Bake 6-8 minutes (180°C), until golden. Set aside in tray to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Once sponge is cool, cut out 2 circles the size of the trifle bowl, reserving offcuts. Set aside.

Strawberry purée

  1. Place strawberries and sugar into mixing bowl, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2, then blend 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a jug and set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Refrigerate until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mousse

  1. Place coconut milk and gelatine powder into mixing bowl, then heat 2 min/50°C/speed 3 or until gelatine dissolves.
  2. Add chocolate and mix 1 min/speed 3.
  3. Add cream cheese and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside to cool, but not set (approx. 10 minutes).
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl, insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and vanilla into mixing bowl, then whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer cream into bowl with cream cheese mixture and gently fold through until combined.

Assembly

  1. Place 1 sponge circle into base of trifle bowl. Spread with a ⅓ of the mousse, then gently spoon over a ⅓ of the strawberry purée. Cut sponge offcuts and use to cover strawberry purée. Repeat layering process with mousse, purée and remaining circle of sponge, finishing with a layer of purée. Use a wooden skewer to marble top of trifle (optional).
  2. Cover trifle and place in the fridge to set (approx. 2 hours or overnight). Just before serving, decorate with cream (optional) and fresh strawberries (optional).

Useful Items

Hints & Tips

  • Sponge and strawberry purée can be made 1 day before assembly.
  • Trifle can be assembled and kept covered in the fridge for up to 2 days.
