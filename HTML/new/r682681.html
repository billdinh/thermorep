Hazelnut caramel bonbons (TM6)

 5.0 (3 ratings)

Ingredients

Tempered chocolate

  • 300 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa) (buttons or grated) (see Tips)

Caramel filling

  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 130 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1030 g water
  • 30 g butter
  • 50 g milk chocolate
  • 50 g vinegar
  • 15 hazelnuts, without skin, plus extra for any additional moulds
  • edible gold leaf

Preparation

Tempered chocolate

  1. Line a baking tray and place a 15-hole half sphere silicone mould (40 mm diameter, 20 mm deep - see Tips) onto it.
  2. Place 220 g of the chocolate into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/50°C/speed 3.
  3. Add remaining 80 g chocolate and temper 2 min/speed 3. Place in a piping bag, then fill each hole of the silicone moulds completely with tempered chocolate. Invert mould over prepared tray, draining excess chocolate onto it. Turn mould upright, then scrape the top of the mould with a metal spatula to clean the edges. Refrigerate mould and tray with drained chocolate for 20 minutes or until set.

Caramel filling

  1. To guarantee the success of recipes using Sugar Stages, please follow the exact quantity and description for all ingredients. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with the other ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Place sugar, 70 g of the cream, 30 g of the water, lemon juice and butter into mixing bowl, then start Sugar Stages .
  3. Add milk chocolate and remaining 60 g cream, then mix 2 min/speed 2, without measuring cup. Leave caramel to cool in mixing bowl until temperature has reached 60°C, then place in a piping bag and set aside until cool to touch. Continue with the recipe.
  4. Place remaining 1000 g water and vinegar into mixing bowl, then start Pre-clean /Fat or caramel. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Remove mould from the fridge and place a hazelnut into each chocolate shell. Pipe caramel filling over the hazelnut, leaving a 3 mm gap at the top. Set aside.
  6. Break up excess chocolate from the tray into small pieces and place into mixing bowl, then melt 3 min/45°C/speed 2.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then melt for a further 2 min/45°C/speed 2. Pour melted chocolate over the mould to cover the caramel filling. Use a offset spatula to gently scrape off any excess chocolate. Refrigerate for 1 hour to set.
  8. Turn bonbons out and decorate with gold leaf. Store in an airtight container in the pantry or fridge until ready to serve.

Hints & Tips

  • We developed these in our mini sphere moulds from TheMix Shop but feel free to make these in any chocolate mould of choice. Bear in mind, quantities may vary and it’s important you stick to the same ratios in order to temper your chocolate.
  • Remaining caramel can be stored in the piping bag in the fridge for up to 10 days. After this time, the caramel texture may become grainy. Remove from the fridge and allow caramel to reach room temperature before using.
  • Use leftover caramel in your recipes of choice. Some suggestions from Cookidoo® include Chocolate caramel bars, Salted caramel ice cream or Chocolate nougat bars.
  • For best results, we recommend using good quality chocolate for this recipe.
