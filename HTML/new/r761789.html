Sous-vide Steak with Creamy Lemon Sauce

 5.0 (1 rating)

Ingredients

Steamed Vegetables

  • 20 oz water
  • 10 oz baby red potatoes, quartered
  • 8 oz asparagus, trimmed

Sous-vide Steaks

  • 2 New York strip steaks (1 in. thick, approx. 8 oz ea.)
    or 2 filet mignons (1 in. thick, approx. 8 oz ea.)
  • 2 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 slices lemon, sliced in rounds, divided
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed, divided
  • 4 sprigs thyme, divided, plus extra to garnish
  • 2 sprigs rosemary, divided
  • 65 oz water, to fully submerge bags (not higher than the max. fill level)
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, divided

Creamy Lemon Sauce

  • 2 oz shallots, halved
  • 2 oz white wine
  • ½ oz lemon juice
  • 7 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Preparation

Steamed Vegetables

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in potatoes. Insert Varoma tray, weigh in asparagus and set aside. Secure Varoma lid over potatoes in Varoma and steam 18 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Carefully remove Varoma lid. Insert Varoma tray with asparagus over partially steamed potatoes, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 6 min/Varoma/speed 2, until potatoes and asparagus are tender. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove Varoma tray (with asparagus) and run under cold water. Set aside to drain. Discard steaming water.

Sous-vide Steaks

  1. Pat steaks dry with paper towel. Drizzle steaks with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Divide seasoned steaks between two vacuum sealer bags, top each steak with 2 lemon slices, 1 crushed garlic clove, 2 sprigs thyme and 1 sprig rosemary. Seal bags with a vacuum sealer machine as per manufacturer’s instructions (see Tip).
  2. Insert blade cover. Place sealed bags into mixing bowl and add water to fully submerge bags. Do not exceed the maximum fill mark (2.2 l). Remove bags from mixing bowl and set aside. Add lemon juice and heat Sous-vide /7 min/140°F.
  3. Place sealed bags into mixing bowl using kitchen tongs, ensure bags are well covered by water and cook Sous-vide /1 h/140°F. Once cooked, carefully remove bags from mixing bowl with kitchen tongs. Empty mixing bowl. Remove blade cover.
  4. In a cast iron frying pan (10 in.), heat 1 tbsp oil and 1 tbsp butter to medium-high heat. Open sous-vide bags, transfer steaks to the hot pan and sear until browned (2-3 min. per side). Set aside to rest.
  5. In the same frying pan, heat 1 tbsp oil and 1 tbsp butter to medium heat. Add reserved steamed potatoes and steamed asparagus and heat for approximately 5 minutes until warmed.

Creamy Lemon Sauce

  1. Place shallots into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add white wine and lemon juice and cook 5 min/195°F/speed 2.
  3. Add butter, egg yolks, salt and pepper and emulsify 4 min/160°F/speed 4. Transfer into a sauceboat.
  4. Transfer potatoes, asparagus and steaks to a serving platter, garnish with fresh thyme, and serve hot with reserved Creamy Lemon Sauce.

Useful Items

blade cover, paper towel, vacuum food sealer machine, vacuum sealer bags, cast iron frying pan (10 in.), kitchen tongs, sauceboat, serving platter

Hints & Tips

  • For sous-vide cooking use bags that are resistant to the times and temperatures specified in these recipes. Check at time of purchase to ensure bags meet the following criteria: specific for food and for sous-vide cooking, resistant up to 212°F, resistant up to 8 hours of cooking, BPA-free, PVC-free.
  • The amount of water is approximate and depends on the volume of bags. Bags should be completely covered with water and level must not exceed the maximum mark of the mixing bowl (2.2 l). To protect against metal oxidation due to prolonged heating of water, before adding filled bags add 1 oz lemon juice or 1 level tsp ascorbic acid (vitamin C) to the mixing bowl.
  • If you do not have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method: Fill a large container or bowl with water. Place each steak, into a zip lock bag and seal, leaving a gap of approximately 1 inch open. Slowly submerge bags into the water (pressure from the water will force the air out), sealing bags closed just before being completely submerged. We recommend using high quality zip lock bags. Look for bags made without BPA (Bisphenol A), which are widely available from major retailers. Polythene bags are not suitable for cooking food.
  • Before placing meat in vacuum sealer bags, turn the top edge of bags inside out approximately 2 inches from the edge in order to keep the tops clean.
