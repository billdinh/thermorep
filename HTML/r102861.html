Tangerine and Chocolate Log (French)

Ingredients

Chocolate Icing

  • 85 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 110 g caster sugar
  • 65 g double cream
  • 2 gelatine leaves
  • 40 g cocoa powder, unsweetened

Tangerine Sorbet

  • 450 g tangerines or clementines, peeled, quartered then frozen
  • 70 g icing sugar
  • 1 Tbsp Cointreau

Chocolate Ice Cream

  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 250 g mascarpone
  • 2 tsp cocoa powder

Preparation

Chocolate Icing

  1. Place water, sugar and cream in mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 11 min/Varoma/speed 2. Towards end of cooking time, soak gelatine leaves in cold water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add cocoa and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  3. Add soaked and squeezed out gelatine then dissolve 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool before placing in fridge. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tangerine Sorbet

  1. Place frozen tangerine quarters, icing sugar and Cointreau in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 5 to obtain a creamy sorbet. Transfer to a piping bag without a piping nozzle and freeze for about 1 hour 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make chocolate ice cream.

Chocolate Ice Cream

  1. Place egg yolks and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add mascarpone and cocoa then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and whisk 3 min/speed 3.5.
  4. Add mascarpone mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer half of cocoa mixture to a log mould or loaf tin. Using a spoon, draw a gutter in cocoa layer through length of mould (see tip) and place in freezer for about 1 hour 30 minutes. Transfer other half to a container and refrigerate. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. When tangerine sorbet is frozen, without being hard, pipe sorbet in a thick line into prepared gutter in cocoa layer (to fill it). Cover with remaining chocolate cream and return to freezer for about 5 hours or overnight.
  6. Near to the end of freezing time, place a wire rack on a sheet of baking paper. Remove log from freezer.
  7. Place reserved icing in mixing bowl and reheat 4 min/50°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, remove log from mould (see tip) and place on wire rack.  Allow to stand for 5 minutes before coating log evenly with icing. Return to freezer until ready to serve.

Useful Items

piping bag, straight sided loaf tin, wire rack, baking paper, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • The chocolate icing can be prepared the day before.
  • Prepare the ice log a day ahead.
  • When drawing the 'gutter', if the chocolate is too soft, freeze it for 30 minutes - 1 hour until firm enough to draw and hold the gutter shape.
  • To remove log from the mould, dip base in hot water for a few seconds to loosen before turning out.
  • To slice log, dip a long sharp knife in hot water then wipe dry before cutting slices.
