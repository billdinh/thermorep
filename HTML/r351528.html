Greek Easter Bread (Tsoureki)

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp mahlepi seeds (see tip)
  • 3 pieces mastic gum (see tip) (optional)
  • 105 g sugar
  • 100 g milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast (see tip)
  • 4 medium eggs, 1 beaten for glazing
  • 450 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 70 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • flaked almonds, for decorating (optional)

Preparation

  1. Line a baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place mahlepi, mastic gum (if using) and 100 g sugar in mixing bowl then grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place milk, remaining 5 g sugar, yeast and 3 eggs in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Add flour, butter, salt and reserved sugar mixture then knead 3 min/. Dough will be sticky. Transfer dough to a well-floured surface and shape into a ball. Place dough ball in a bowl, cover with a tea towel and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  5. Divide dough into 3 pieces and roll each piece into a long rope (approx. 40 cm). Join the 3 ropes at one end, plait them together, and join again at the other end. Place plait on prepared tray and brush with 1 beaten egg. Decorate with flaked almonds (if using) and leave to rise for 10 minutes.
  6. Place into cold oven, turn oven temperature to 180°C and bake for 35-45 minutes (180°C) until golden brown. Leave to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

tea towel, pastry brush, baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm), baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Mehlepi (also called mahlab) is an aromatic spice made from cherry seeds, which are ground to a powder. It's often used in traditional Greek Christmas and Easter dishes. It can be found in speciality food stores, spice stores or online. Alternatively, substitute with fennel seeds or cardamom.
  • Mastiha, or mastic gum, is a crystal, resinous spice and natural chewing gum. It is often used in bread-making in Greek bread recipes. It can be found in speciality food stores or online. If not available, add ½ tsp vanilla extract in step 4 instead.
