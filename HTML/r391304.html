Blackberry jam tarts

Ingredients

Blackberry chia jam

  • 500 g frozen blackberries
  • 70 g raw sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp agar agar (see Tips)
  • 3 tsp white chia seeds

Blackberry jam tarts

  • 1 sheet spelt puff pastry (see Tips)
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream, to serve
  • fresh blackberries, to serve

Preparation

Blackberry chia jam

  1. Place berries, sugar and agar agar into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  2. Add chia seeds and cook 30 sec/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into 2 sterilised sealable jars and seal immediately. Set aside at room temperature until mostly set (approx. 3 hours). Place jam into refrigerator to set completely (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Blackberry jam tarts

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place pastry sheet onto a clean work surface and using a rolling pin, roll pastry out to a 30 x 40 cm rectangle. Cut into 12 squares (approx. 10 x 10 cm). Transfer onto lined baking tray. Place 2-3 teaspoons of the reserved Blackberry chia jam into the centre of each square, then fold corners of pastry in to the centre over the jam. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) or until pastry is puffed and golden.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip without setting time/speed 3, until soft peaks form, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Serve tarts with fresh blackberries and whipped cream.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, sterilised sealable jars, rolling pin, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because these tarts include some added sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before serving.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small.
  • You can find spelt puff pastry at specialty or gourmet food stores. Otherwise, you can replace the spelt puff pastry with standard puff pastry in this recipe.
  • Agar agar is a gelatinous substance derived from seaweed and is a vegetarian alternative to gelatine. It dissolves best in hot liquids, then requires refrigeration to set completely. You can find agar agar at health food stores or online.
  • You can store the Blackberry chia jam in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
