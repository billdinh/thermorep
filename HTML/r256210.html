Moroccan prawns with preserved lemon couscous

Ingredients

  • 200 g couscous
  • 20 g preserved lemons, flesh discarded, rind rinsed and cut into small pieces (3 mm)
  • 200 g hot water
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 red onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp harissa paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 500 g raw prawns, peeled and deveined (see Tips)
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 50 - 80 g pitted green olives, to taste
  • fresh coriander leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh couscous, preserved lemon rind, hot water and 20 g of the oil into it. Add pepper and ½ teaspoon of the salt. Set aside.
  2. Place onion, garlic and remaining 20 g oil into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add harissa, ground coriander, cumin and remaining ½ teaspoon salt and cook 5 min/120°C/speed 2.
  4. Add tomatoes and sugar to mixing bowl. Fluff reserved couscous with a fork, then transfer onto Varoma tray. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Remove Varoma. Add prawns, chickpeas and olives to mixing bowl. Place Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 2-3 min/Varoma//speed 1, until prawns are cooked through.
  6. Serve prawns and couscous garnished with coriander leaves.

Hints & Tricks

  • Harissa is a spicy chilli paste originating from North Africa. You can find it in the international aisle of most major supermarkets. To make your own harissa, please refer to the recipe on Cookidoo (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • Please note that the time for this recipe is based on using pre-cleaned and deveined prawns (available at most supermarkets and fish mongers).
