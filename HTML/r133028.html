Orange Drizzle Vegetable Cake (gluten and dairy free)

Ingredients

  • 500 g swede, diced (1 cm)
  • 700 g water

Sponge

  • 200 g dairy free sunflower margarine, plus extra for greasing
  • 300 g blanched almonds
  • 2 oranges, thin peelings of skin only
  • 300 g caster sugar
  • 3 tsp gluten free baking powder
  • 5 medium eggs

Drizzle

  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 20 g orange juice, fresh

Preparation

  1. Insert simmering basket then weigh in swede. Pour water over then steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula then discard steaming water.
  2. Place cooked swede in mixing bowl and mash 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mash again 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool for 1 hour. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Sponge

  1. Once swede is cool, preheat oven to 160°C. Grease sides and line base of a spring form tin (Ø 23 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place orange peelings and 150 g sugar in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then grind again 5 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add remaining 150 g sugar and margarine then mix 20 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add baking powder, eggs, reserved mashed swede and reserved ground almonds then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to prepared tin. Bake for 1 hour 10 minutes - 1 hour 15 minutes (160°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tin.

Drizzle

  1. Once cake is cool, place sugar and orange juice in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/80°C/speed 2. Pierce holes in top of cake with a skewer then pour syrup over the top. Leave drizzle to soak in before removing from tin.
  2. Serve in slices at room temperature.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 23 cm), baking paper, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cook swede the day before so it is cool and ready to use when needed. The next day, begin recipe at step 3.

The mystery guest in this cake, swede, ensures a really moist sponge while the orange gives a lovely citrus flavour.
