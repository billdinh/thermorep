Sausage Stuffed Kabocha

Ingredients

  • 1 kabocha squash (3-5 lbs)
  • non-stick cooking spray
  • 10 oz day old bread, cubed (½ in)
  • 1 oz Parmesan cheese, in pieces
  • 1 oz bread crumbs
  • 8 oz purple onion, in pieces
  • 3 oz carrot, in pieces
  • 3 oz celery, in pieces
  • ½ oz olive oil
  • 3 fresh sage leaves, to taste
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 4 oz rainbow chard, halved lengthwise, in pieces (½ in.)
  • 8 oz Italian sausage, casings removed, broken in pieces
  • 2 oz dried cranberries
  • 2 oz pine nuts
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with foil. Prepare kabocha squash by removing the top with a sharp knife, cutting around at a 45-degree angle. Remove seeds and strings and discard or use for another purpose. Place squash onto baking sheet, spray with non-stick cooking spray and set aside.
  2. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in bread cubes and set aside.
  3. Place Parmesan and bread crumbs into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onion, carrot, celery, oil, sage leaves, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 8 min/250°F//speed 0.5.
  5. Add chard and cook 2 min/212°F//speed 0.5.
  6. Add Italian sausage, cranberries, pine nuts, Dijon and maple syrup and cook 5 min/195°F//speed 0.5. Transfer sausage mixture to bowl with bread cubes and stir through with spatula. Spoon stuffing mixture into prepared squash.
  7. Sprinkle top of the stuffing with reserved Parmesan mixture. Loosely tent foil over the stuffed squash and bake 90 minutes (350°F). Remove foil and bake a further 30 minutes (350°F) until browned. Let cool (approx. 10-15 min.) before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

baking sheet, large bowl, aluminum foil, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don’t have day old bread, simply trim the crust from fresh bread slices (e.g. brioche) and bake for 15-20 minutes (350°F) until golden brown. Let cool before using in recipe.
  • Avoid overstuffing the squash. Any leftover stuffing can be baked in a small baking dish, alongside the stuffed squash.
  • To roast seeds: scoop out seeds from squash, place seeds in a large bowl of water, seeds will float to the top and pulp will sink. Discard pulp and pat seeds dry. Toss seeds with 3 tbsp melted butter, season with salt, Worcestershire Sauce or your favorite hot sauce. Bake for 45 minutes (200°F), increase heat to 325°F and bake a further 5 minutes (325°F). Allow to cool before storing in an airtight container.
