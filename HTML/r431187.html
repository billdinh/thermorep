Cauliflower Mac and Cheese

Ingredients

  • 120 g Cheddar cheese, reduced fat, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 slice wholemeal bread, torn in pieces
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 5 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 900 g water
  • 1 ½ - 1 ¾ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 1 cauliflower (approx. 400 g), cut in small florets (2 cm)
  • 230 g macaroni pasta
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 600 g skimmed milk
  • 2 pinches ground cayenne pepper (optional)
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced (5 mm)

Preparation

  1. Place Cheddar in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place bread, Parmesan and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then bring to the boil 10 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, arrange cauliflower in Varoma dish, making sure that some holes remain uncovered for steam to circulate.
  4. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Remove covered Varoma. Add macaroni to mixing bowl, place covered Varoma back into position and cook 8-10 min/100°C//speed 1 until macaroni is al dente and cauliflower is just cooked.
  6. Remove Varoma and drain macaroni in simmering basket. Transfer macaroni to a large baking dish then add steamed cauliflower. Clean mixing bowl and preheat oven to 220°C.
  7. Place garlic, butter and flour in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 6.
  8. Add milk and cook 10 min/90°C/speed 2.5.
  9. Add reserved grated Cheddar, cayenne pepper (if using), ½-¾ tsp salt (to taste), pepper and mustard then stir 3 min/90°C/speed 3.
  10. Pour over macaroni and cauliflower in baking dish then stir with spatula to combine. Arrange tomatoes on top then sprinkle with reserved breadcrumbs.
  11. Bake for 20-25 minutes (220°C) until golden brown on top. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • We've lightened up the classic mac and cheese by replacing some of the pasta with steamed cauliflower in this indulgent pasta dish. At roughly half the calories of traditional mac and cheese, you could even enjoy a slice of garlic bread on the side!
