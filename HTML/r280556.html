Lamb Burgers with Garlic Herb Mayo

Ingredients

Garlic Herb Mayo

  • 200 g olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 15 g mixed fresh herbs (e.g. parsley, thyme, mint), cut in pieces
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 3 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • Â½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Lamb Burgers

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 fresh red chilli, halved, deseeded if preferred
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g fresh mint leaves
  • 10 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 500 g lamb mince, broken in pieces
  • 200 g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 5 g ground coriander
  • 1 medium egg
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Assembly

  • 6 brioche burger buns, halved horizontally
  • 80 g mixed lettuce leaves, for serving
  • 1 - 2 beef tomatoes, sliced, for serving
  • 100 g feta cheese, crumbled, for serving

Preparation

Garlic Herb Mayo

  1. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in oil. Set jug aside.
  2. Place garlic and herbs in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add lemon juice, egg yolks, salt and pepper then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4 while very slowly pouring reserved oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades to create an emulsion. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film and place in fridge until needed. Clean mixing bowl.

Lamb Burgers

  1. Place garlic, chilli, onions, mint and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add lamb, feta, coriander, egg, salt and pepper then mix Turbo/2 sec/4 times.
  3. Divide lamb mixture into 6 and shape into burgers, making a dimple in the middle of each (see tip). Place in fridge for at least 30 minutes until burgers are firm.

Assembly

  1. Heat a frying pan over a medium heat (see tip). Fry burgers for 3-5 minutes on each side, until cooked as desired. Meanwhile, lightly toast burger buns.
  2. Spread reserved mayonnaise on bottom half of each bun then top with lettuce and tomato. Place 1 burger and feta cheese on top then replace top of bun. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

jug, cling film, frying pan, toaster, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Creating a dimple in the middle of the burgers allows it to cook evenly in the middle while not drying out the sides. As the middle is thinner, the whole burger will cook at the same time.
  • When frying the burgers, the lamb mince will release fat so adding oil is usually not necessary. If using a mince with lower fat quantity or if burgers start sticking to the pan, add oil as necessary.
  • Mayonnaise and burgers can be prepared in advance and stored in fridge. Fry burgers and assemble just before serving.
