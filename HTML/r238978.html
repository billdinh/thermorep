Teriyaki salmon bao

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 300 g baker's flour
  • 170 g warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder

Salmon

  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 fresh red chilli, trimmed and deseeded if preferred
  • 100 g soy sauce
  • 120 g mirin (see Tips)
  • 50 g honey
  • 2 skinless salmon fillets (approx. 150 g each)
  • 700 g hot water
  • baker's flour, for dusting
  • ½ Lebanese cucumber, cut into matchsticks
  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, cut into long, thin slices

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil and set aside. Place yeast, sugar, flour, water, salt, oil and baking powder into mixing bowl and mix 6 sec/speed 6. Knead 2 min/. Transfer into oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside to double in size (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, continue with recipe while dough proves.

Salmon

  1. Place ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, mirin and honey and mix 5 sec/speed 7, then cook 13-18 min/100°C/speed 2, without measuring cup or until slightly thickened. Transfer half of the sauce into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Place remaining half of the sauce into a ceramic dish and set aside to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Add salmon to the ceramic dish with cooled sauce and turn to coat well. Cover and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes to marinate. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma tray into position, place marinated salmon onto Varoma tray, secure Varoma lid and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until salmon is cooked to your liking. Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl with reserved sauce, break or flake into pieces and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry Varoma and reserve hot water in mixing bowl.
  5. On a silicone bread mat or floured work surface, knock down dough. Shape into a log and cut into 18 equal pieces (approx. 30 g each). Flatten each piece into a disc (7 cm).
  6. Place a chopstick (or 2 long wooden skewers) lengthways into Varoma dish. Fold 3 dough discs over chopstick (hanging down). Insert Varoma tray and place a piece of wet, well-wrung baking paper into tray. Fold 3 dough discs over a chopstick (or 2 skewers) and lay on tray. Repeat with another chopstick and 3 more dough discs and lay on tray. Place Varoma dish and tray into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 6-8 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until dough is cooked. Transfer to a plate, cover to keep warm and set aside.
  7. Repeat with remaining dough discs. Place a chopstick (or 2 long wooden skewers) lengthways into Varoma dish. Fold 3 dough discs over chopstick (hanging down). Insert Varoma tray and place a piece of wet, well-wrung baking paper into tray. Fold 3 dough discs over a chopstick (or 2 skewers) and lay on tray. Repeat with another chopstick and remaining 3 dough discs and lay on tray. Place Varoma dish and tray into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 6-8 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until dough is cooked.
  8. Place 2 teaspoons of the salmon mixture into each bao and garnish with cucumber, coriander and spring onions/shallots to serve.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, baking paper, rolling pin, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, ceramic baking dish (12 cm), chopsticks, large bowl, plate, kitchen towel, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Mirin can be found in the international aisle of most major supermarkets or at Asian food stores.
