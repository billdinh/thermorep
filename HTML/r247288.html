Rabbit in a Citrus Marinade with Black Olives and Thyme - Coniglio marinato agli agrumi con olive e timo

Ingredients

  • ½ orange, zest and juice
  • ½ lemon, zest and juice
  • 55 g olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 sprigs thyme, leaves only
  • 500 g rabbit meat, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 2 shallots, halved
  • 100 g white wine, dry
  • 50 g water
  • 70 g pitted black olives
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1400 kJ / 336 kcal

28.3 g

Preparation

  1. Place orange zest and juice, lemon zest and juice, 40 g oil, garlic and thyme in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 7. Pour over rabbit pieces in a bowl, stir then cover and leave to marinate for 45 minutes.
  2. After this time, place shallots and remaining 15 g oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 7 min/100°C/speed . Meanwhile, brown rabbit pieces (reserving marinade) in a frying pan over a medium-high heat until browned.
  3. Add wine to mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, cook 3 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add browned rabbit pieces, reserved marinade and water to mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/98°C//speed .
  5. Add olives, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 5 min/98°C//speed .
  6. Serve with crusty bread and a side salad.

Useful Items

frying pan, cling film, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • The flavour combination works equally well with chicken thigh meat instead of rabbit.
  • Rabbit was introduced to Italy by the Romans in the third century BC. It is especially prevalent in dishes from the Northern regions. This is a country-style dish where the citrus flavours, married with thyme and contrasted with salty olives, are paired beautifully with delicate rabbit flesh. Butchers or farmers’ markets here in the UK and Ireland typically sell rabbit. Ask them to bone it for you.
