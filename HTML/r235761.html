Porchetta with Orange, Fennel, Chilli and Sage - Porchetta agrumata con finocchio, peperoncino e salvia

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp dried fennel seeds
  • ½ - 1 Tbsp chilli flakes, to taste
  • 1 orange, thin peelings of skin only
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 6 fresh sage leaves
  • 30 g coarse sea salt
  • 30 - 60 g white wine, to taste
  • 1250 g pork belly, boned, with rind
  • 750 g pork loin

Preparation

  1. Place fennel seeds and chilli flakes in mixing bowl then toast 7 min/Varoma/speed . Remove mixing bowl and allow to cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Return mixing bowl to position then add orange peel and grind 30 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic, sage and salt then chop 20 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add wine then mix 10-15 sec/speed 5 until a paste forms, adding more wine if too dry.
  5. Place pork belly skin-side down and score the flesh. Rub paste all over and into the flesh.
  6. Place pork loin in the middle, across the pork belly. Roll up (see tip) and tie tightly at 2-3 cm intervals with butcher's string. Place in a dish and rest in fridge, uncovered, for at least 8 hours (preferably overnight - see tip).
  7. The next day, preheat oven to 160°C.
  8. Remove joint from fridge and pat dry with paper towels. Place on a rack in a roasting tin and roast for 1½-2 hours (160°C) for 2 kg joint (see tip).
  9. Increase oven temperature to 220°C and roast for a final 30 minutes. Remove joint from oven, cover in foil and leave to rest for 30 minutes.
  10. Carve into slices and serve hot.

Useful Items

butcher's string, paper towels, roasting tin, aluminium foil, roasting rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It may be necessary to trim the loin and/or pork belly so that the belly can be rolled completely around the loin before tying.
  • Resting the joint uncovered in the fridge allows the skin to dry out and results in better crackling.
  • Adjust roasting times according to the total weight of the meat. A 3.5-4 kg joint will take 3-4 hours (160°C).
  • Also delicious served cold in sandwiches.
  • Traditionally porchetta was prepared using a whole deboned pig that was seasoned, rolled, and cooked on a pit over a wood-burning fire – a laborious process that meant it was a meal reserved for celebratory events where there would be large crowds to feed. Renowned as one of Italy’s best-loved dishes, porchetta is now so popular that it has been selected by the Italian Ministry of Agricultural, Food and Forestry Policy as one of a list of traditional Italian foods with cultural relevance.
