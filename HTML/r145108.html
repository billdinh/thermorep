Teriyaki salmon bowl

Ingredients

  • 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 100 g soy sauce
  • 120 g rice wine
  • 50 g rice malt syrup
  • 4 fresh salmon fillets (approx. 180 g each), skin removed
  • 1000 g water
  • 100 g wild rice (see Tip)
  • 200 g brown rice
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 150 g frozen edamame beans
  • 1 - 2 avocadoes, flesh only, cut into thin slices
  • 1 - 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices on the diagonal
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 nori sheet, cut into very thin strips (optional)
  • 2 tsp black sesame seeds, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, rice wine, and malt syrup and mix 5 sec/speed 7, then cook 10 min/100°C/speed 2,  without measuring cup or until slightly thickened. Transfer half of the sauce into a jug and set aside. Place remaining half of the sauce into a ceramic dish and set aside to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Add salmon fillets to cooled sauce in dish and turn to coat well. Place into refrigerator for 30 minutes to marinate. Meanwhile, proceed with recipe. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh wild rice and brown rice into it. Add salt and cook 35 min/100°C/speed 4 or until rice is cooked to your liking. Transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  5. Place marinated salmon onto Varoma tray. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh edamame beans into it. Place Varoma tray into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until salmon is cooked to your liking. Remove Varoma and set aside. Place salmon into a separate thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Shell edamame beans.
  6. To serve, divide rice between 4 serving bowls. Top with salmon fillets and edamame, then garnish with avocado slices, spring onions/shallots, coriander, nori (optional) and black sesame seeds (optional). Drizzle with remaining sauce before serving.

Useful Items

jug, ceramic or glass baking dish, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, 4 serving bowls, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find wild rice at most major supermarkets and health food stores.
