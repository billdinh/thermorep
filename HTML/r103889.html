Steamed pork pancakes (Bianca Mazur)

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 tsp Chinese five spice
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 30 g rice wine vinegar
  • 30 g soy sauce
  • 20 g honey
  • 30 g hoi sin sauce, plus extra to serve
  • 20 g sesame oil
  • 30 g kecap manis
  • 400 g pork loin steaks, cut into cubes (approx. 3 cm)

Steamed pancakes

  • 170 g water
  • 50 g white sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 300 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 30 g sesame oil

Quick pickled cucumber

  • 100 g rice wine vinegar
  • 25 g sugar
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • 2 Lebanese cucumbers, cut into halves and peeled into ribbons

Steaming and assembly

  • 1000 g water
  • 20 g Sriracha sauce (optional) (see Tips)
  • fresh coriander, leaves only, to serve
  • 2 fresh long red chillies, deseeded and cut into thin slices, to serve

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Place all marinade ingredients, except pork, into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add pork and mix 3 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a large ceramic or glass bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Steamed pancakes

  1. Place water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add all remaining pancake ingredients and mix 6 sec/speed 6, then knead 2 min 30 sec/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap in bread mat or place into a bowl and cover with kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1½ hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pork

  1. Place reserved pork and marinade into mixing bowl and cook 25 min/90°C//speed 1. Remove pork and set aside. Using simmering basket, strain cooking liquid into a jug.
  2. Place cooked pork and 120 g of the cooking liquid into mixing bowl and shred 4 sec//speed 4. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Quick pickled cucumber

  1. Place rice wine vinegar, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 3. Transfer pickling liquid into a bowl and set aside to cool slightly (approx. 5 minutes). Add cucumber to pickling liquid and set aside to pickle while steaming pancakes (see Tips).

Steaming and assembly

  1. On a silicone bread mat or floured work surface, knock down dough, then shape into a log and cut into approx. 35 equal pieces (approx. 15 g each). Flatten each piece into a 6 cm disc.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Place a piece of wet and well-wrung baking paper onto Varoma tray, then place 6 dough discs onto tray. Place Varoma into position, insert Varoma tray, secure Varoma lid and steam 6 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer pancakes into a separate thermal serving bowl or large plate and cover to keep warm. Repeat steaming with remaining dough discs, topping up water levels as needed.
  4. To serve, place a small amount of hoi sin sauce and Sriracha® (optional) onto steamed pancakes, top with 2 teaspoons shredded pork and garnish with pickled cucumber, coriander leaves and a slice of chilli.

Useful Items

large ceramic or glass bowl, plastic wrap, bowl, kitchen towel, silicone bread mat, jug, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, thermal serving bowl or plate, baking paper, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you are not using pickled cucumber immediately, place into the refrigerator to continue pickling for up to 4 hours.
  • Sriracha® is a type of spicy chilli sauce; you can find it in supermarkets and specialty food stores.
