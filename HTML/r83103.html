Spinach and Butternut Squash Roulade

Ingredients

  • 400 g butternut squash, peeled, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 250 g Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g fresh spinach, washed, dried and roughly chopped
  • 140 g unsalted butter, cut in pieces
  • 140 g plain flour
  • 250 g milk
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 5 large eggs, separated
  • 180 g cream cheese with chives

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C and line two Swiss roll tins (23 cm x 33 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place a baking tray on mixing bowl lid. Place butternut squash cubes on baking tray, toss with oil then roast 40 minutes (190°C) or until tender. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place cheddar in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 5 then tip out and set aside.
  4. Place spinach in mixing bowl and cook 4 min/90°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook again 1 min/90°C/speed 2. Drain cooked spinach through simmering basket to remove excess moisture.
  5. Place drained spinach, butter, flour, milk, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then cook 4 min/90°C/speed 4.
  6. Add egg yolks and 150 g of the reserved grated cheddar then mix 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  7. Wash and dry mixing bowl, ensuring it is grease-free. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and whisk 2 min/37°C/speed 4 until stiff. Transfer to bowl containing spinach mixture and gently fold in with a metal spoon, then pour into prepared Swiss roll tins.
  8. Bake 20-25 minutes (190°C) until golden. Remove from oven and gently turn each sponge upside down onto a piece of baking paper. Remove lining paper then cover sponges with a clean tea towel and leave to cool completely.
  9. Once cool, make a cut about 1 cm from the end along the shorter length of each sponge with a sharp knife, going halfway through the depth; this enables the roulade to be rolled more tightly. Spread with cream cheese, scatter with remaining 100 g grated cheddarand distribute cooled roasted butternut squash evenly. Roll up both roulades, using the paper to help keep the rolls tight. Place seam side down on a central platter and serve cut in 2 cm widths.

Useful Items

2 swiss roll tins (30 cm x 20 cm x 1 cm), baking paper, baking tray, tea towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with a dressed mixed leaf salad.
  • To serve the roulade hot, return the rolled up roulade to a baking tray and reheat in the oven for 15 minutes (190°C) or until piping hot in the centre.
  • This roulade freezes well. Allow to defrost fully before reheating as above.
  • Serves 6 as a main course or 10 as a starter.
