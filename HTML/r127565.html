Salmon fillets with buckwheat and asparagus

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp orange zest
  • 100 g orange juice (approx. 1 orange)
  • 30 g honey
  • ¼ tsp wasabi paste, ready-made, to taste (optional)
  • 10 - 12 sprigs mixed fresh herbs, leaves only (e. g. parsley, dill, coriander)
  • 400 g fresh salmon fillets, skinless, boneless (approx. 4 fillets)
  • 1 - 2 pinches fine salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches freshly ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 500 g green asparagus, trimmed (see tip)
  • 160 g leek, white part only, cut into quarters (approx. 160 g)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 20 g butter
  • 300 g buckwheat grains
  • 100 g dry white wine
  • 750 g water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • 40 g lemon juice (approx.1 lemon)
  • 30 g pumpkin seeds
  • 100 g cashew nuts, roasted, unsalted (optional)
  • 50 g goat's cheese, crumbled (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place orange zest, orange juice, honey and wasabi paste into mixing bowl and cook 6-7 min/Varoma/speed 1, without measuring cup, until a thick glaze consistency is achieved. Transfer orange glaze into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place mixed herbs into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place salmon into Varoma dish and tray, season with salt and pepper. Place asparagus into the Varoma dish and onto the Varoma tray arranging around the salmon and set aside.
  4. Place leek, oil and butter into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add buckwheat (or jasmine rice), dry white wine, water and stock paste into mixing bowl. Place Varoma with salmon and asparagus into position. Brush salmon fillets with half of the reserved orange glaze, then secure Varoma lid and steam 13 min/Varoma//speed 1.5.
  6. Check to see if salmon fillets and buckwheat are cooked to your liking. If not, prolong cooking time accordingly, then set Varoma aside.
  7. Transfer buckwheat to a bowl, add lemon juice and reserved chopped herbs, then season to taste with a little extra salt and pepper if necessary. Add cashews (optional) and pumpkin or sunflower seeds and mix using spatula.
  8. Place buckwheat and steamed asparagus onto individual plates and top with salmon fillets. Brush salmon fillets with remaining orange glaze, then garnish with crumbled goat's cheese and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • For this recipe use asparagus with thin stalks (approx. 1 cm). If the asparagus you are using have thicker stalks, cut them in half lengthwise.
