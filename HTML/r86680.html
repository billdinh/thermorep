Easter Cream Pastries

Ingredients

Choux Pastry

  • 250 g water
  • 130 g butter, diced
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 3 tsp sugar
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 5 medium eggs

Topping

  • 200 g double cream
  • 200 g sour cream
  • 20 g sugar
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 10 apricot halves, tinned

Preparation

Choux Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line two baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place water, butter, salt and sugar in mixing bowl then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl from machine and leave to cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Place mixing bowl in position and mix on speed 5 while adding one egg after the other through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  5. After adding the last egg, mix for a further 30 sec/speed 5.
  6. Transfer mixture to a piping bag fitted with a small piping nozzle (see Tip). Pipe pastry in 10 discs (Ø 10 cm) on prepared baking trays, leaving 5 cm between them. Pipe an extra layer on edge of each disc, forming a raised rim then flatten centre of each with the back of a wet spoon. Bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) until golden brown. Meanwhile clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Remove baking trays from oven. Push down risen centre of each disc with the back of a wet spoon to look like a nest. Transfer pastry nests, on baking paper, to a wire rack to cool.

Topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whisk speed 3, without setting a time, until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid over-whisking. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sour cream, sugar and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to bowl with whipped cream and fold in with spatula.
  3. Spread in the centre of each cooled choux pastry and place 1 apricot half in centre of each choux pastry disc, cut side down. Serve immediately at room temperature.

Useful Items

2 baking trays and baking paper, piping bag and nozzle, cooling rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you do not have a piping bag, you can use a freezer bag with the corner snipped off.
  • Serve with extra tinned apricot halves on the side if desired.
