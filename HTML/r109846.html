Two-Flavoured Picnic Rolls

Ingredients

Dough

  • 250 g milk
  • 50 g butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g strong white bread flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Filling

  • 60 g hard goat's cheese, cut in pieces
  • 40 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, drained
  • 50 g Brie, cut in pieces
  • 80 g bacon lardons
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp maple syrup

Decoration

  • 1 egg, beaten, for glazing
  • 1 Tbsp poppy seeds, for sprinkling
  • 1 Tbsp sesame seeds, for sprinkling

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place milk, butter, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Knead 3 min/. Divide dough into 24 pieces (approx. 35-40 g), roll into balls then set aside, covered with a tea towel, while preparing filling.

Filling

  1. Place cheese and sundried tomatoes in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place Brie in mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 4 then transfer to another bowl and set aside.
  3. Place bacon and oil in mixing bowl then sauté 4 min/100°C//speed . Transfer to bowl with Brie, mix in maple syrup then set bowl aside.
  4. Grease a baking tray (approx. 25 cm x 35 cm) with butter.
  5. Flatten by hand (or use a rolling pin) one of the pieces of dough to a circle (Ø 10-12 cm). Place in centre 1 tsp goat's cheese and tomato filling then close dough around filling pinching to form a firm seal. Repeat with rest of goat's cheese and tomato filling for another 11 rolls.
  6. Fill remaining 12 dough balls with Brie and bacon filling. Place rolls on prepared baking tray, seal side down. Cover with a tea towel and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 40 minutes).

Decoration

  1. Towards end of proving time, preheat oven to 180°C. Using a pastry brush, coat rolls with beaten egg then sprinkle poppy seeds on one flavour and sesame seeds on the other.
  2. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until golden then remove from oven and allow to cool. Once cool, pack into boxes or bags ready for your picnic.

Useful Items

tea towel, bowl, baking tray, rolling pin, pastry brush, airtight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

 

  • Fillings can be varied to taste. For example, try chorizo, sausage, different cheeses, turkey, chocolate or jam.
