Raspberry, apple and sweet potato purée

Ingredients

  • 500 g filtered water, plus extra if needed
  • 500 g filtered water
  • 200 g apples (red or pink), peeled, cored and cut into pieces
  • 200 g sweet potato, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 125 - 150 g fresh raspberries (see Tips)
  • 30 g butter
  • kefir milk, to serve (optional - see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh apples and sweet potato into it. Steam 20 min/100°C/speed 2 or until tender. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard water.
  2. Place raspberries, butter and cooked apple and sweet potato into mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 15 sec/speed 6 or until smooth. For an even smoother texture, strain purée through a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Set aside 1 serving, then freeze the remainder in individual portions for future use. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving. Serve with a drizzle of kefir milk (optional).

Useful Items

ice cube trays, fine-mesh sieve, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Using ripe fruit will ensure this recipe isn't too tart for your baby.
  • If fresh raspberries aren't in season, you can use frozen (thawed) berries instead.
  • Fruit purées like this one also make a lovely addition to your baby's porridge or rice cereal if you're serving one. Just be sure to give your baby plenty of savoury purées too. Fruit is nutritious and it's important that your baby develops a liking for various fruit flavours but offering less sweet options is crucial too.
  • Kefir, a fermented milk drink, is similar to a thin yoghurt with a mild fizz. Its nutritional profile varies considerably depending on production methods but milk kefir is typically a more potent probiotic with a broader range of beneficial bacteria than yoghurt. Kefir has also been found to be more digestible than yoghurt in individuals with lactose intolerance. Make it yourself or choose an unflavoured, unsweetened brand with active probiotic cultures.
