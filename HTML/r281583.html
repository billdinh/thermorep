Steak Fajita Sweet Potatoes

Ingredients

Sweet Potatoes

  • 6 sweet potatoes
  • olive oil, to taste
  • fine sea salt, to taste

Guacamole

  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, to taste
  • 150 g tomatoes, quartered
  • 25 g lime juice
  • 2 ripe avocados, cut in pieces (5 cm)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Steak Fajita Mixture

  • 2 - 3 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 150 g onions, sliced (1 cm)
  • 1 red pepper, sliced (1 cm)
  • 1 yellow pepper, sliced (1 cm)
  • 1 green pepper, sliced (1 cm)
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 450 g rump steak, sliced against the grain (1 cm x 5 cm) (see tip)

Assembly

  • fresh coriander leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Sweet Potatoes

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Wash potatoes, pierce skin all over with a fork then rub with a little oil and salt. Place on a baking tray and bake for 45-55 minutes (200°C) until cooked through. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.

Guacamole

  1. Place onions, garlic and coriander in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add tomatoes and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add lime juice, avocados and salt then mix 5 sec//speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Steak Fajita Mixture

  1. Place garlic and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 2 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add onions, red pepper, yellow pepper, green pepper, ground coriander, cumin, paprika, chilli powder, salt and pepper. Stir gently with spatula then cook 10 min/120°C//speed .
  3. Add steak, stir with spatula to combine then cook 2-3 min/120°C//speed  until steak is cooked as desired.

Assembly

  1. To serve, slice baked sweet potatoes in half and season to taste. Divide fajita mix evenly between sweet potato halves then top with 1-2 Tbsp reserved guacamole. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • A creative version of the traditional jacket potato, these baked sweet potatoes are loaded with flavourful fajita-spiced steak and vegetables. The steak filling and guacamole topping come together in the time it takes the sweet potatoes to bake, making a tasty and easy midweek meal option.
  • To slice steak, find the direction of the grain (the lines of muscle fibre) and slice across the grain rather than parallel to it.
  • The sweet potatoes add a lovely flavour to this dish but if you’d prefer you can use ordinary baking potatoes.
