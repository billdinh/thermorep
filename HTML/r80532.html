Pears in red wine

Ingredients

  • 500 g red wine (e.g. Beaujolais or Dolcetto)
  • 100 g sugar
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 2 cinnamon sticks (approx. 5 g)
  • 3 cloves
  • 800 g ripe pears, peeled, cut in quarters and cores removed

Preparation

  1. Place red wine, sugar, honey, cinnamon sticks and cloves in mixing bowl. Arrange pears in Varoma dish and tray, set Varoma in position and steam 20-25 min/Varoma/î€ƒ/speed 1 or until tender, adjusting the steaming time according to type, size and ripeness of pears. Remove Varoma and set aside.
  2. Strain spiced wine through simmering basket into a container (1 litre) and allow to cool. Place cooked pears in the container with spiced wine, cover and refrigerate for 2-3 hours, or 1-2 days for a deeper colour.
  3. When ready to serve, remove container from refrigerator, place spiced wine in mixing bowl and reduce without measuring cup 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until slightly thickened - it will thicken more as it cools. Serve pears with 1-2 tbsp hot or room-temperature wine reduction per portion.

Hints & Tricks

  • This easy dessert is fresh and aromatic and can be served in many ways. The pears develop a deep red colour after a day or two in the spiced wine, and if preserved as described in the tips they make a unique edible gift for the holidays.
  • Serve the pears with sabayon (zabaglione) or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
  • Store in the refrigerator and serve within 7 days.
  • Pears can be preserved: add 300 g water in step 1, then in step 2 place the cooked pears in sterilised jars and pour the hot spiced wine into the jars to cover the pears. Seal the jars immediately and turn upside down for 10 minutes. Preserved pears can be stored refrigerated for a few weeks, and must be eaten within 7 days of opening the jar.
  • For a sweet-savoury combination, serve the pears in red wine with Gorgonzola cheese as a starter or cheese course.
  • For a faster result, omit step 2 and reduce the spiced wine immediately, without marinating the pears. In this case the pears will remain white.
  • You can replace or add to the spices in the wine according to your preference (e.g. star anise, green anise, vanilla).
