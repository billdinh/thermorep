Slow Cooked Lamb Leg with Spring Vegetables

Ingredients

  • 42 - 48 oz leg of lamb, boneless (2.5-3 lbs.)
  • 7 garlic cloves, divided
  • 5 sprigs fresh oregano, leaves only
  • 3 tbsp dried oregano
  • 5 stalks fresh rosemary, leaves only, divided
  • 1 ½ tsp fresh ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to taste
  • 3 oz extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • 20 oz water
  • 2 tbsp beef stock paste
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 15 oz Yukon gold potatoes, quartered, (approx. 3 lg. potatoes)
  • 8 ½ oz carrots, sliced (2 in.), (approx. 4 md. carrots)
  • 8 oz zucchini, in pieces (2 in.), (approx. 2 zucchini)
  • 6 stalks broccolini
  • 1 heaping tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ oz red wine
  • ½ oz freshly squeezed lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)

Preparation

  1. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in lamb. Remove Varoma and place onto inverted Varoma lid to catch drips. Place 5 cloves garlic, fresh and dried oregano, 4 stalks rosemary leaves, pepper and salt into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add 2 oz olive oil and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Rub mixture over reserved lamb.
  2. Place water, stock paste, 2 cloves garlic, 1 stalk rosemary leaves and bay leaves into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 2.5 then steam 30 min/200°F/speed 2.
  3. Turn lamb over, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 30 min/200°F/speed 2.5. Remove Varoma dish and place onto inverted Varoma lid to catch drips.
  4. Insert simmering basket. weigh in potatoes and carrots. Place Varoma dish back into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Add zucchini into Varoma and arrange around lamb, add in broccolini by laying over top of lamb, secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Transfer vegetables and lamb from Varoma dish and simmering basket to a serving platter and pour remaining 1 oz olive oil over the top, cover with foil and keep warm. Remove simmering basket and bay leaves.
  6. Add flour and wine into mixing bowl, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 8 min/212°F/speed 4. Add lemon juice and adjust seasoning adding more salt and pepper if required.
  7. Slice lamb and serve with vegetables and au jus.

Useful Items

serving platter, aluminum foil

Hints & Tricks

  • If using a rolled lamb: unroll lamb and lay it flat in the bottom of the Varoma tray. Ensure some vents remain unobstructed to allow even distribution of steam. This method will produce a well done lamb.
  • USDA recommends cooking lamb to an internal temperature on 145°F followed by a 3 minute rest.
  • Prepare rub the night before and allow to marinate on lamb overnight.
  • To make your own stock: refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Chicken or Vegetable Stock Paste recipes. Use one heaping teaspoon stock paste per 16 oz water.
  • Replace vegetables with any of your favorites, e.g. cabbage or asparagus. Place quicker cooking vegetables on top of the lamb in the Varoma dish.
