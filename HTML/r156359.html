Apples with cider sabayon sauce

Ingredients

Steamed apples

  • 4 apples (approx. 150 g each) (e.g. Braeburn, Golden Delicious, Fuji, Royal Gala), unpeeled or peeled
  • 1 ½ tbsp lemon juice (25 g)
  • 500 g water

Cider sabayon sauce

  • 50 g sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 160 g dry alcoholic cider (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp cinnamon sugar, for sprinkling

Preparation

Steamed apples

  1. Remove apple cores, cut apples into eighths and place them into a bowl. Sprinkle with lemon juice and mix well to help prevent apples turning brown. Arrange apples into centre of Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma with tray into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 18-22 min/Varoma/speed 1, until apples are soft. Steaming time depends on variety and ripeness of the apples.
  3. Set Varoma aside, uncovered, and allow apples to cool. Discard cooking liquid. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cider sabayon sauce

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add egg, egg yolks and cider and heat 9 min/70°C/speed 3. Meanwhile, arrange apples on 4 plates.
  3. Pour sabayon over apples, sprinkle with cinnamon sugar and serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, plates

Hints & Tricks

  • Leaving the peel on the apples adds colour to the dessert.
    - Garnish with fresh mint leaves.
    - Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
    - When entertaining guests, you can make the recipe up to step 4 ahead of time. Continue making the sabayon from step 5 just before serving the dessert.
  • Replace cider with apple juice or sparkling wine or Champagne.
