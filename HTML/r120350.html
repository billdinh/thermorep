Seafood panzanella

Ingredients

  • 60 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 30 g white wine vinegar
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 400 g water
  • 1000 g mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 200 g ciabatta bread, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 5 spring onions/shallots, cut into thin slices (approx. 5 mm)
  • 1 sprig fresh basil, leaves only, torn into pieces
  • 200 g cherry tomatoes, cut into halves

Preparation

  1. Place oil, vinegar and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and weigh mussels into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 10-15 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until mussels have opened. Remove Varoma dish and set aside to cool. Strain cooking liquid through a fine-mesh sieve into a jug.
  3. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh bread into it. Remove mussel meat from shells and add to bowl with bread. Discard shells. Place bowl with bread mixture in it onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 50 g of the reserved cooking liquid into it. Gently stir to combine and set aside.
  4. Add spring onions/shallots, basil and tomatoes to bowl with bread mixture and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 20 minutes to allow flavours to develop. Drizzle with reserved dressing and serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, fine mesh sieve, jug, large bowl, plastic wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a good quality extra virgin olive oil for the dressing in this recipe.
  • You can replace mussels with clams or other bivalve seafood.
  • Boost your vegetable intake while keeping kilojoules low by adding cucumber, red capsicum or a finely sliced red chilli.

Panzanella is a typical rustic Italian summer dish made with soaked bread, vegetables and herbs. It's simple and delicious, while also being filling and low in kilojoules.
