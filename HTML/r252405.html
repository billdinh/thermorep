Cheat's sourdough

Ingredients

Sourdough starter

  • 100 g plain flour
  • 75 g warm water
  • 1 pinch salt
  • &frac18; tsp dried instant yeast

Bread

  • olive oil spray
  • 50 - 70 g potato, peeled
  • 750 g water
  • 500 g baker's flour
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • warm water, for sprinkling

Preparation

Sourdough starter

  1. Place a glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour and water into it. Add salt and yeast, then stir to combine. Cover with a kitchen towel and set aside for 3 days. Do not stir or agitate (see Tips).

Bread

  1. Spray a loaf tin (25 x 9 x 11 cm) with oil, then line with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place potato into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer potato into simmering basket.
  3. Place 500 g of the water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket with potato and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside to drain. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Place flour, remaining 250 g water, 60 g of the sourdough starter, oil, salt, yeast and reserved potato into mixing bowl and combine 10 sec/speed 5, then knead 3 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or into a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and allow to prove for 30-40 minutes in a warm place or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Knock down dough and shape into a loaf. Transfer into prepared loaf tin and prove again in a warm place for 30 minutes or until doubled in size.
  6. Cut a few slits on top of loaf, sprinkle with warm water and bake for 35-40 minutes (200°C), until golden and bread sounds hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

bowl, kitchen towel, loaf tin (25 x 9 x 11 cm), baking paper, silicone bread mat, wire rack, sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Unlike traditional sourdough starters, you do not need to stir or agitate this starter. The starter is ready when it's increased in size and looks aerated (you can see small bubbles). In warmer climates, it might take less than 3 days for starter to be ready (we suggest checking once each day). The starter has not fermented properly if it's hard or firm all the way through (it may have a hard crust on top, but that can be removed and starter underneath used).
  • Use leftover starter to make another loaf. You can store the starter in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
