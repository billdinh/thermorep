Hot and sour soup

Ingredients

  • 40 g black fungus (see Tips)
  • 1050 g water, plus extra warm water for soaking
  • 20 g oil
  • 2 - 3 fresh red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 150 g carrot, cut into matchsticks
  • 100 g canned bamboo shoots, drained and cut into thin strips
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp arrowroot flour
  • 80 g black vinegar (see Tips)
  • 70 g Chinese rice wine (Shaoxing)
  • 40 g soy sauce
  • ½ - 1 tsp ground white pepper, to taste
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 150 g silken tofu, cut into thin strips
  • sesame oil, for drizzling

Preparation

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh mushrooms into it. Cover mushrooms with water and set aside to soak.
  2. Place oil, chillies and spring onion/shallot into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add carrots and bamboo shoots and cook 3 min/100°C//speed . Drain mushrooms and cut into strips.
  5. Add stock paste, reserved mushrooms and 1000 g of the water and cook 15 min/100°C//speed .
  6. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh remaining 50 g water into it, then add arrowroot flour and stir until smooth. Add arrowroot flour mixture into mixing bowl.
  7. Add black vinegar, Chinese rice wine, soy sauce and pepper and cook 3 min/100°C//speed 2. While cooking, add eggs through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  8. Add tofu and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1. Divide between serving bowls and drizzle with sesame oil before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find black fungus at most Asian food stores. Alternatively, you can use fresh or dried wood ear mushrooms for this recipe (if using fresh, omit the soaking time).
  • Black vinegar is a dark Chinese vinegar with a woody, smoky flavour. Some versions are rice-based, while others are wheat-based. Chinese rice wine (also known as Shaoxing wine) is a common Chinese cooking wine. Both are available at Asian food stores.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
