Raspberry soufflés

Ingredients

Raspberry base

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 50 g caster sugar, plus 12 tsp for dusting
  • 220 g frozen raspberries
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 20 g cornflour
  • 40 g water

Soufflé mixture

  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 120 g hard nougat, broken into pieces (3 cm)
  • 8 egg whites (approx. 290 g)
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Raspberry base

  1. Grease 12 ramekins (7 cm) with butter. Add 1 teaspoon of sugar to each ramekin and swirl to coat base and sides evenly with sugar. Discard any excess sugar.
  2. Place raspberries, sugar and lemon juice into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 3.
  3. Place a small jug onto mixing bowl lid, weigh cornflour and water into it and stir to combine. Cook 15 min/100°C/speed 3, adding cornflour mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid. Transfer raspberry mixture into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 30 minutes), then transfer into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Soufflé mixture

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Set aside a large baking tray.
  2. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh sugar into it. Set aside.
  3. Place nougat into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whisk 5 sec/speed 4.
  5. Add cream of tartar and whisk 1 min/speed 4, without measuring cup.
  6. Mix 4-5 min/speed 4, gradually adding reserved sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid until sugar dissolves and egg whites are firm. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer egg whites into a large bowl.
  7. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place reserved raspberry mixture into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  8. Add one-quarter of the whipped egg whites and mix 10-20 sec//speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer mixture into bowl with remaining whipped egg whites.
  9. Add reserved nougat to bowl with egg whites and gently fold together until just combined. Fill prepared ramekin dishes, levelling top with a palette knife for a smooth, even surface. Wipe rims of ramekins with a clean damp cloth (see Tips). Place onto baking tray and bake for 12-15 minutes (200°C), until risen and tops are golden brown. Dust with icing sugar and serve immediately.

Useful Items

small jug, bowl, large baking tray, plastic wrap, large bowl, palette knife, ramekins (7 cm), small bowl, paper towel, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store raspberry base in the freezer for up to 1 month. Defrost before using.
  • Soufflés can be prepared (through to filling ramekins and wiping rims) up to 4 hours in advance. Store in the refrigerator until ready to bake and serve.
  • Soufflés can be frozen. Fill ramekins and wipe rims, then wrap loosely with plastic wrap and place into freezer for up to 1 month. When ready to bake, place into a preheated oven (200°C) and bake from frozen for 15 minutes, until puffed and golden.
