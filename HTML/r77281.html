Naan Bread with Onion and Cumin

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp cumin seeds
  • 450 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 30 g dried onions
  • 200 g Greek yoghurt
  • 100 g milk
  • 30 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp dried yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place cumin seeds in mixing bowl and toast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add bread flour, dried onions, yoghurt, milk, oil, yeast, sugar and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 3.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 3 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil then place dough into it. Cover with a tea towel and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (1-2 hours).
  4. Towards the end of rising time, preheat oven to 240°C and if you have a separate grill, preheat that as well. Line a baking tray with baking paper and place in oven to preheat. Meanwhile, on a lightly floured surface, knead dough briefly by hand and divide into 6 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a teardrop-shaped naan about 20 cm long and 12 cm wide.
  5. Remove hot baking tray from oven and carefully place naans on it, in batches if necessary. Immediately return tray to oven and bake for 4-6 minutes (240°C) until browned in spots - the naans should puff up.
  6. If they need more browning, either place under hot grill (about 10 cm below) for 30 seconds, or leave in oven for a further 1 minute. Wrap in a tea towel to keep warm until all the naans are baked then serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, tea towel, rolling pin, baking tray and paper, grill, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Omit or reduce dried onions.
  • Make two large naans instead of six small ones - perfect with a balti-style curry.
  • Making your own soft naan breads adds an excellent touch of authenticity to your Indian meal, and they are so easy!
