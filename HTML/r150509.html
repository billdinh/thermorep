Apple, rhubarb and goji berry crumble with vanilla cashew cream

Ingredients

Vanilla cashew cream

  • 150 g raw cashews (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 80 g water
  • 1 tbsp rice malt syrup

Crumble

  • 50 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 50 g rolled oats
  • 30 g coconut sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • 20 g coconut oil

Apple, rhubarb and goji berry compote

  • 500 g rhubarb, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 1 Pink Lady apple, cored and cut into quarters
  • 50 g freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 30 - 60 g rice malt syrup
  • 1 vanilla bean, split into halves lengthways, with seeds scraped
  • 2 tbsp dried goji berries

Preparation

Vanilla cashew cream

  1. Place all cashew cream ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 2 min/speed 9.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Blend 2 min/speed 9 or until smooth. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Crumble

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add remaining crumble ingredients and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer onto prepared tray and bake for 8-10 minutes (180°C) or until crisp. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Apple, rhubarb and goji berry compote

  1. Place rhubarb into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place apple into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add orange juice, rice malt syrup, vanilla bean and cook 5 min/100°C//speed .
  4. Add goji berries and reserved rhubarb and simmer 15 min/90°C//speed , without measuring cup, until fruit is tender. Remove and discard vanilla pod. Divide compote between bowls and top with crumble and cashew cream to serve.

Useful Items

serving bowls, sealable container, baking paper, bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For the Vanilla cashew cream, you can use soaked cashews. Store any leftover cream in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • For the crumble, you can use soaked, then dehydrated almonds.
  • To make this recipe gluten free, try replacing the oats with 50 g buckinis (activated buckwheat).
