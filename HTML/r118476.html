Prawn wontons

Ingredients

Dough

  • 100 g water
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 280 g plain flour
  • cornflour, for dusting

Peanut sauce

  • 100 g hot water, plus extra 50 g if needed
  • 20 g piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g peanut oil
  • 100 g raw peanuts
  • 30 g light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp chilli powder

Chilli oil

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g dried chilli flakes
  • 100 g vegetable oil
  • ½ tsp salt flakes

Wontons

  • 50 g eschalots (approx. 2)
  • 30 g piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into slices (1 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 350 g raw prawns, peeled, deveined and tails removed
  • 40 g tinned water chestnuts
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp Chinese five spice
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • vegetable oil, for greasing
  • 500 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 20 g raw peanuts, cut into pieces, for garnishing
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, cut into thin slices, for garnishing

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it.
  2. Place egg, salt and 50 g of the water into mixing bowl and mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and knead 2 min/, gradually adding remaining 50 g water through hole in mixing bowl lid until dough is smooth and sticky.
  4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Place into a large bowl, cover with kitchen towel and rest for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Lightly flour a work surface with cornflour. Cut dough into halves. Roll each portion into 2 equal-sized logs. Cut each log into 2 even sections. Flatten out each piece and, dusting generously with cornflour as you go, roll through a pasta machine starting at the widest setting, and reducing rollers each time to narrowest setting. You will form 4 thin sheets that you can just see your fingers through.
  6. Generously dust a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with cornflour. Cut each sheet into 6 squares, place onto baking tray and cover with a damp kitchen towel. Set aside while preparing peanut sauce.

Peanut sauce

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 100 g of the water into it.
  2. Place ginger and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add peanut oil and sauté 15 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add peanuts and chop 10 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend 10 sec/speed 5.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 1 min/speed 4, gradually adding 100 g water through hole in mixing bowl lid. Mix 10 sec/speed 7 to achieve a thick, pouring consistency. If mixture is paste-like, add 50 g water and mix for a further 5 sec/speed 5.
  7. Add soy, sugar, wine and chilli powder and blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chilli oil

  1. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chilli flakes, vegetable oil and salt and heat 5 min/60°C/speed 1. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Wontons

  1. Place eschalots, ginger and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop 3 sec/speed 5
  3. Add prawns and water chestnuts and mix 3 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add fish sauce, pepper, Chinese five spice and cornflour and combine 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Lightly grease Varoma dish and tray with vegetable oil and set aside.
  6. Place 2 teaspoons of the prawn mixture onto the centre of 1 wonton square and brush all edges with water. Gently pull up edges and use fingertips to pinch edges together to form a moneybag shape. Place wonton into prepared Varoma dish. Repeat with remaining wonton squares and prawn mixture (approx. 24 wontons total - if you have leftover dough, see Tips).
  7. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  8. Garnish wontons with peanuts and spring onions/shallots and serve with peanut sauce and chilli oil.

Useful Items

jug, kitchen towel, silicone bread mat, large bowl, pasta machine, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), bowl, sealable storage jar or container, pastry brush

Hints & Tricks

  • Look for dumpling flour or Hong Kong flour in Asian food stores for best results.
  • Dough can be rolled out very thinly using a rolling pin if you don't have a pasta machine. Wrap any leftover dough tightly in plastic wrap and store in the freezer. Use within 2 weeks.
  • Wontons can be served with soy sauce or Chinese black vinegar instead of peanut sauce and chilli oil.
