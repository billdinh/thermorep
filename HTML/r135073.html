Simple chocolate hazelnut cake

Ingredients

  • 150 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing (see Tips)
  • 200 g raw hazelnuts, skins on
  • 200 g dark chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 150 g brown sugar (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp maca powder, for dusting (optional) (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line a round cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 20 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and melt 2 min 30 sec/80°C/speed 1.
  5. Add sugar, baking powder, eggs, vanilla and reserved nuts and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  7. Pour mixture into prepared tin and bake for 45-50 minutes (160°C), or until just cooked (cake will appear slightly soft in the centre). Allow to cool in tin for 15 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Dust with maca powder (optional) before serving.

Useful Items

round cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, bowl, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you prefer, you can replace half of the butter with your preferred oil, such as hazelnut oil, macadamia oil or a lighter-flavoured extra virgin olive oil. Simply add the oil with the sugar and eggs.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this cake – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
  • Maca is a vegetable from the brassica family and is a source of protein, fibre, iron, calcium and potassium. You can buy maca powder from some supermarkets and health food stores. It makes a nourishing alternative to icing sugar for a pretty dusted look.
