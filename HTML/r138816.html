Mini strawberry mousse flower tarts

Ingredients

Simple butter biscuits

  • 2 pieces lemon zest (1 x 4 cm), no white pith
  • 130 g sugar
  • 220 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 350 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting

Strawberry mousse

  • 150 g strawberries, hulled, plus extra cut into halves for garnishing (optional)
  • 250 g cream cheese, room temperature, cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 100 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting (optional)

Preparation

Simple butter biscuits

  1. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 8 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula and beat 30 sec/speed 4.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add vanilla and egg and mix 1 min/speed 4.
  6. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface. Divide dough into 2 equal portions and shape into discs. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 2 hours or until firm enough to roll (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Preheat oven to 170°C. Set aside 2 x 24-hole mini muffin trays.
  9. Transfer 1 dough portion onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and roll out to a 5 mm thickness. Using a flower-shaped cookie cutter (6-7 cm), cut out approx. 24 flowers.
  10. Turn mini muffin trays upside down and shape each flower over a mini muffin mould, pressing down gently. With trays still upside down with dough, bake for 10-12 minutes (170°C) or until lightly golden. Allow to cool on trays for 10 minutes, then gently transfer onto wire racks to cool completely.
  11. Roll out remaining dough portion on silicone bread mat or floured work surface (5 mm thickness). Using a flower-shaped cookie cutter (6-7 cm), cut out approx. 24 flowers. Turn mini-muffin tray upside down and shape each flower over a mini muffin mould, pressing down gently. With trays still upside down with dough, bake for 10-12 minutes (170°C) or until lightly golden. Allow to cool on trays for 10 minutes, then gently transfer onto wire racks to cool completely.

Strawberry mousse

  1. Place strawberries into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cream cheese and mix 30 sec/speed 6 or until soft and creamy. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add vanilla and icing sugar and mix 40 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until needed (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Fill each flower with 1-2 teaspoons of the strawberry mousse. Transfer onto a baking tray and carefully cover with plastic wrap. Place into refrigerator to set (approx. 1 hour or overnight). Top with half a strawberry (optional) and dust with extra icing sugar (optional) just before serving.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, 2 x 24-hole mini muffin trays, rolling pin, flower-shaped cookie cutter (6-7 cm), wire rack, bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Gluten free strawberry mousse: For a gluten free biscuit, replace plain flour with 350 g gluten free plain flour.
  • To freeze biscuit dough: Place dough into freezer instead of refrigerator after shaping into discs. Remove from freezer 1-2 hours before using. Dough can be frozen up to 1 month.
  • You can store the strawberry mousse in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
