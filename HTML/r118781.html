Baked bacon and cranberry stuffing

Ingredients

  • 50 g dried cranberries
  • 50 g ruby port
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 1 sprig fresh sage, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 300 g rindless bacon rashers
  • 20 g salted butter
  • 500 g pork mince
  • 50 g lupin flakes
  • 1 egg
  • 30 g shelled and unsalted pistachios
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cranberries and port into it. Set aside to soak (approx. 1 hour).
  2. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  3. Place parsley, thyme and sage into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onion, garlic and 100 g of the bacon into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add butter and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2, or until onion is tender. Allow to cool slightly (approx. 5 minutes).
  6. Add mince, lupin flakes, egg, pistachios, salt, pepper, reserved herbs and soaked cranberry mixture and combine 20 sec//speed 2.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and combine 20 sec//speed 1, or until combined.
  8. Place remaining bacon rashers into base of a springform cake tin (20 cm) in a circular pattern (they do not need to overlap), ensuring each piece hangs over edge of tin by approx. 12 cm. Transfer stuffing mixture into tin and press down evenly. Twist each bacon rasher 2-3 times over stuffing to meet in the centre. Bake for 25-30 minutes (190°C) until browned and meat is cooked through. Remove from oven and allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer stuffing onto a serving plate and garnish top of stuffing with a bouquet of the extra herbs and rosemary. Cut into slices and serve warm.

Useful Items

bowl, springform cake tin (20 cm), serving plate, oven
