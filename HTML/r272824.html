Nordic Chicken and Asparagus Tartlets

Ingredients

Shortcrust Pastry

  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 80 g unsalted butter, cold, diced (2 cm)
  • 50 g water, cold
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Filling

  • 80 g onions, cut in pieces
  • 350 g chicken breasts, skinless, sliced (1 cm)
  • 220 g water
  • ½ chicken stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 1 dried bay leaf

Sauce

  • 25 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 25 g plain flour
  • 150 g milk
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper, or to taste
  • 80 g white asparagus, cooked, sliced (2 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp fresh parsley leaves, roughly chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

Shortcrust Pastry

  1. Place flour, butter, water and salt in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Tip dough out, shape into a flattened ball and wrap in cling film then set aside to rest in fridge for 30 minutes.
  2. After this time, roll out pastry on a lightly-floured surface to 5 mm thick. Place 1 tart tin (Ø 10 cm 3 cm deep) on the pastry and draw a rough circle, slightly larger, around it with a knife, then repeat until you have 8 circles. Line tart tins with pastry circles and prick bases with a fork then refrigerate for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C.
  3. Line rested bases with baking paper and fill with baking beans. Blind bake for 15 minutes (200°C). Meanwhile, make filling.

Filling

  1. Place onions, chicken, water, stock cube and bay leaf in mixing bowl then cook 7 min/100°C//speed . Pour through simmering basket, collecting cooking liquid in bowl below. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Remove baking beans and baking paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (200°C) until golden brown. Set aside on a wire rack.

Sauce

  1. Place butter, flour, milk, 100 g reserved cooking liquid, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then cook 6 min/100°C/speed 3. Meanwhile, remove and discard bay leaf and onions from simmering basket with chicken.
  2. Add reserved chicken and asparagus then stir together with spatula. Warm 1 min 30 sec/100°C//speed .
  3. Remove pastry shells from tartlet tins then divide filling between them. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and serve.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, tart tins (Ø 10 cm), baking paper and baking beans, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve one tartlet per person as a starter or two per person for a main course with vegetables.
