Mixed Berry Hand Pies

Ingredients

Dough

  • 14 oz all-purpose flour
  • 8 oz unsalted butter, chilled, cubed (½ in.)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 oz ice water

Filling

  • 4 oz strawberries, hulled
  • 4 oz blackberries
  • 2 oz sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Assembly

  • whole milk, to brush
  • turbinado sugar, to sprinkle

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, butter and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add water and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer dough to a clean surface. Without overworking the dough, quickly pat together and form into a flattened square. Divide in half, wrap each half in cling wrap and refrigerate 30 minutes. Meanwhile make berry filling.

Filling

  1. Place strawberries, blackberries, sugar and lemon juice into mixing bowl, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 28 min/212°F/speed 0.5. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate until needed.

Assembly

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Working with one dough packet at a time, roll into a rectangle (approx. 10 in. x 13 in. x ⅛ in.) directly on a silicone mat. Refrigerate first sheet while working with remaining dough. Cut ½ inch off each side to even out dough and cut each piece into 9 rectangles (approx. 3 in. x 4 in.), (see Tip).
  3. Lightly brush edges of the dough with milk. Place a heaping tablespoon of reserved berry filling in the center of each rectangle. Cover with remaining 9 dough pieces and seal edges with the tines of a fork. Place on prepared baking sheet and refrigerate 30 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  5. Prick the top of each hand pie multiple times with a fork to create steam vents. Brush tops with milk and sprinkle with turbinado sugar. Bake for 25-30 minutes (350°F) until pastry is golden brown. Let cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

cling wrap, parchment paper, pastry brush, baking sheet, rolling pin, silicone mat, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If dough becomes too soft, place in freezer for 10-15 minutes for easier handling.
  • Make on the weekend and freeze for lunches and treats.
  • Rolling dough directly on silicone mats will make it easier to chill soft dough and/or freeze hand pies for baking later. Alternately, roll dough between two sheets of cling wrap.
  • Any berry can be used for this recipe.
