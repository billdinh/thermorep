Southern-Style Fried Chicken & Sweetcorn

Ingredients

  • 300 g plain flour
  • 25 g cornflour
  • 20 g baking powder
  • 5 g fine sea salt, plus 1 pinch
  • 15 g paprika
  • 200 g buttermilk
  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 400 g fresh sweetcorn kernels
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 100 g water
  • ½ chicken stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 600 g chicken thighs, skinless, boneless
  • 500 g vegetable oil
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place flour, cornflour, baking powder, salt and paprika in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 8. Divide between two bowls and weigh buttermilk into a third bowl, set all three bowls aside.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add 200 g sweetcorn, butter, water and chicken stock cube.
  5. Place Varoma into position then place chicken in Varoma dish and tray, cover with Varoma lid, then cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Remove Varoma and allow chicken to cool slightly. Insert measuring cup and blend sweetcorn mixture 30 sec/speed 6-9, increasing speed gradually.
  7. Preheat oven to 160°C. Coat each piece of chicken in the first bowl of flour, then in buttermilk, then in the second bowl of flour. Place coated chicken on a baking tray.
  8. Heat 2-3 cm oil (see tips) in a wide, shallow pan then fry a few pieces of chicken on both sides until golden brown. Keep warm in preheated oven while cooking remaining pieces and finishing the sweetcorn.
  9. Insert butterfly whisk and add remaining 200 g sweetcorn then cook 5 min/60°C//speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk and add reserved grated Parmesan then mix 1 min/60°C//speed 2. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  10. Serve chicken with sweetcorn on the side.

Useful Items

baking tray, large frying pan, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Heat vegetable oil until shimmering but not smoking. To test temperature, carefully lower a small piece of bread into the oil, it should sizzle and turn golden. This is best done with a frying thermometer, the correct temperature should be between 180°C and 190°C.
  • For a gluten-free version, substitute plain flour for gluten-free cornflour.
  • For spicy fried chicken sprinkle with paprika or chilli powder, a squeeze of lemon and a pinch of extra salt.
  • The corn mixture can also be tossed through pasta with grated Parmesan and an added pinch of black pepper for a light summer lunch.
