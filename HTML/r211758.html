Japanese Ramen Noodle Soup

Ingredients

  • 400 g pork belly meat, boned, without rind
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 60 g Japanese soy sauce
  • 10 g sesame oil
  • 2000 g water
  • 375 g dried ramen noodles
  • 100 g fresh baby spinach
  • 750 g chicken broth
  • 20 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 1 tsp black vinegar
  • ½ tsp hot paprika
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 12 nori seaweed sheets (9 cm x 3.5 cm)
  • 4 Tbsp tinned sweetcorn
  • 2 spring onions, cut in thin slices, to garnish
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place pork belly in a casserole dish or roasting tin and set aside.
  2. Place garlic cloves, 30 g soy sauce and sesame oil in mixing bowl then chop 8 sec/speed 5. Pour over pork belly, rub mixture into pork belly then roast for 50-55 minutes (180°C). Increase oven temperature to 220°C and roast for a further 10-15 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place 1500 g water in mixing bowl and bring to a boil 15 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add ramen noodles and cook for time indicated on packet/100°C//speed . Drain noodles through Varoma dish and reserve cooking water in a bowl. Place noodles in a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place bowl with hot cooking water onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in baby spinach. Leave baby spinach in hot cooking water for 2 minutes, drain spinach leaves through simmering basket and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  6. Place chicken broth, 500 g water, 30 g Japanese soy sauce, ginger, 1 tsp black rice vinegar and paprika powder in mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, add 2 eggs, close Varoma lid and cook 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma, rinse eggs under running water or place in cold water to cool down, then peel eggs and cut in half.
  7. Divide reserved cooked ramen noodles between 4 soup bowls. Slice roasted pork belly and arrange on top of noodles, along with cooked baby spinach, half an egg, 3 nori seaweed sheets and 1 Tbsp sweetcorn kernels in each soup bowl. Pour over hot broth and serve hot garnished with spring onion slices and toasted sesame seeds.
