Lemon tart

Ingredients

Almond quinoa pastry

  • 100 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 60 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 40 g quinoa flakes
  • 80 g plain flour
  • 60 g wholemeal flour
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tsp cold water

Lemon filling

  • 3 - 4 lemons (to taste), zest only, no white pith
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • 60 g honey
  • 180 g lemon juice (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp cornflour
  • 150 g coconut milk
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 egg yolks

Preparation

Almond quinoa pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a round loose-bottom tart tin (22 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place almonds and quinoa into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add flours and butter and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add egg and water and knead 15 sec/, until mixture just comes together.
  5. Transfer pastry onto silicone bread mat or large lightly floured sheet of baking paper. Roll out pastry to 2-3 mm thickness. Drape pastry over rolling pin, gently removing baking paper, and ease pastry into tin. Gently press pastry into sides and press to repair any splits in pastry. Trim overhanging edges with a knife. Place dish into refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Line pastry case with baking paper and fill with rice, dried beans or pie weights. Place tin onto a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) and bake for 10-15 minutes (160°C). Remove baking paper and weights and bake for a further 10-15 minutes (160°C), or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool completely.

Lemon filling

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 9 or until finely chopped.
  2. Add sugar and mill 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add honey, lemon juice, cornflour, coconut milk, eggs and yolks and mix 10 sec/speed 6, until combined.
  4. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain mixture into cooled pastry case and bake for 20-25 minutes (160°C), or until just set. Allow tart to cool completely in tin before serving.

Useful Items

round fluted loose-bottom tart tin (22 cm), silicone bread mat, rolling pin, baking paper, pie weights, rice or dried beans, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), pie dish (22 cm), fine mesh sieve, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Almond quinoa pastry can be substituted with Gluten-free goodness pastry (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • If you prefer, you can use activated or roasted almonds instead of raw almonds in the pastry.
  • For a lovely flavour variation, you might like to substitute the lemon juice with half lemon juice and half lime juice.
