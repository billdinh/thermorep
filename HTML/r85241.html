Prawn and Asparagus Basil Ravioli

Ingredients

Basil Pasta Dough

  • 30 g fresh basil leaves
  • 3 large eggs
  • 330 g pasta flour (00 flour)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Ravioli Filling

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces
  • 10 - 15 g bread
  • 25 g butter, plus extra for sautéing
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 100 g asparagus, woody ends discarded
  • 30 g peeled, raw prawns
  • 1 egg yolk
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Cooking

  • water, for cooking pasta
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

Basil Pasta Dough

  1. Place basil leaves in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop again 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add eggs, flour, oil and salt then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and knead 4 min/. Tip out dough, bring together into a ball then wrap in cling film and leave to rest in fridge for 1 hour. Meanwhile, rinse and dry mixing bowl and make filling.

Ravioli Filling

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl. Remove 1 Tbsp grated Parmesan to another bowl then set both bowls aside.
  2. Place bread in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to bowl with larger amount of Parmesan and set aside.
  3. Place butter, onions and asparagus in mixing bowl then chop 8 sec/speed 5. Cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add prawns and mix 2 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add egg yolk, pepper, reserved Parmesan-breadcrumb mixture, leaving smaller amount of Parmesan for garnish then mix 20 sec//speed 2.5. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film and place in fridge.

Assembly

  1. Divide rested pasta dough into two pieces, then pass each piece through a pasta machine several times, gradually reducing the thickness until you reach the thinnest setting. Alternatively roll dough out on a floured surface using a rolling pin until as thin as possible (1 mm).
  2. Fold over in half lengthwise, then unfold. In centre of the bottom half, below crease line, dot heaped teaspoons of filling at approx. 8 cm intervals.
  3. Brush round each mound of filling with a wet pastry brush, then fold top half of dough over filling and press down lightly with your fingers in between each mound to squeeze out excess air. Using a fluted pastry wheel or cutter, cut out ravioli in between each dollop of stuffing to make squares (approx. 6 cm) then set aside until ready to cook.

Cooking

  1. Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil. Gently lower ravioli in and cook for 5-6 minutes then remove with a slotted spoon. Serve hot with reserved grated Parmesan.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin or pasta machine, pastry wheel or cutter, pastry brush, saucepan, slotted spoon, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Homemade ravioli is a real treat and this version, with an elegant filling of prawns and asparagus, creates an impressive starter or main course. Works well served with a dressed rocket salad.
