Sponge Cake

Ingredients

  • unsalted butter, to grease
  • 4 jumbo eggs
  • 4 ½ 4 - 4 ½ oz sugar, to taste
  • 1 tsp homemade vanilla sugar
  • 4 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder (optional)
  • 1 pinch salt (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
705 kJ / 168 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
26 g
Fat
5 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a springform pan (approx. 8½ in.) and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, sugar and vanilla sugar into mixing bowl and whisk 6 min/100°F/speed 4. Whisk a further 6 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour, baking powder and salt around butterfly whisk and mix 4 sec/speed 3.
  4. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully complete stirring with spatula.
  5. Transfer mixture into prepared springform pan and bake immediately for 20-25 minutes (350°F). Allow cake to cool for 10 minutes before removing from pan. Allow cake to cool completely on a wire rack before slicing in half horizontally for filling.

Useful Items

springform pan (approx. 8½ in.), wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This cake can be used as a base for a variety of different cakes with fillings such as fruit, flavored cream or whipped cream (e.g. San Marcos cake, truffle cake, fraisier). Or serve it on its own or with a dusting of confectioners sugar.
  • If springform pan is larger, or if you wish to cut the cake into three layers, increase recipe using 1½ times the ingredients and increase baking time by a few minutes.
  • Sugar has an impact on the cake's texture. For a very light and fluffy texture use 4.5 oz sugar.
  • The baking powder helps the sponge cake rise but it can be omitted for a traditional génoise recipe.
  • Cake texture is at its best when consumed within one day.
  • Chocolate sponge cake: use only 3 oz flour and add 8 tbsp cocoa powder.
  • Rolled cake: bake the sponge cake in a thin rectangular layer for use in jam rolls, Swiss rolls, etc. Transfer cake batter onto a baking pan lined with parchment or a silicone mat. Spread batter into a rectangle (16 in. x 12 in.) and bake immediately for 8 minutes (350°F). Roll sheet cake while still hot with the help of a clean kitchen towel. Allow to cool, carefully unroll, remove towel, spread filling and roll again.
