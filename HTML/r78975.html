Sultana, apple and custard scrolls

Ingredients

Dough

  • 300 g full cream milk
  • 60 g butter
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast or 20 g fresh yeast
  • 500 g baker's flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • grapeseed oil, for greasing

Custard

  • 30 g cornflour
  • 50 g raw sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 160 g full cream milk

Assembly

  • water, for brushing
  • 120 g sultanas
  • 180 g apple (approx. 2 medium apples), peeled and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • milk, for brushing

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place milk, butter and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add baker's flour, salt and egg and knead 8 min/, or until the dough is smooth and begins to come away from the sides of the bowl.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Place dough into a large greased bowl, then place bowl and dough into a large loose-fitting plastic bag (this will help the dough to prove). Leave in a warm place until dough has doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Custard

  1. Place cornflour and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 4 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add egg and milk and cook 6-8 min/90°C/speed 3, or until a thick custard is achieved. Custard has reached desired level of thickness when it coats the back of a spoon. Set custard aside in mixing bowl to cool for 5 minutes.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Gently transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and lightly press out into a small rectangle (approx. 8 x 15 cm), then roll out into a larger rectangle (approx. 38 x 34 cm). Brush the edge of dough closest to you with a little water, then spread reserved custard over the remaining dough. Evenly sprinkle sultanas, apple and cinnamon over the custard.
  3. Starting at the edge furtherest from you, carefully roll dough towards you, keeping it nice and tight to enclose the filling. Use the water brushed edge of the dough to seal closed.
  4. Using a sharp knife, cut rolls into 16 even pieces (approx. 2 cm thick). Turn pieces cut side up and place onto prepared baking tray. Cover baking tray with a kitchen towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  5. Brush each scroll with a little milk and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Set aside to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes) before serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, kitchen towel, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), silicone bread mat, large bowl, large plastic food bag, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Drizzle buns with icing for a sweeter variation.
