Mini Choux with Strawberries

Ingredients

Choux Pastry

  • 150 g water
  • 80 g butter, soft
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp caster sugar
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 3 medium eggs

Lime Infused Cream Filling

  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 1 lime, thin peelings of skin only
  • 300 g cream cheese (Philadelphia®)
  • 300 g double cream, cold
  • 500 g strawberries, thinly sliced, for serving

Preparation

Choux Pastry

  1. Place water, butter, salt and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl from base and leave with lid off to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  4. Return bowl to position and mix without setting a time/speed 5 while adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid then stir 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer dough to a piping bag fitted with a round nozzle.
  5. Pipe walnut-sized balls onto prepared trays, leaving 5 cm between each. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until puffed and golden brown. Turn off oven and open door for 10 minutes to allow the choux to dry. Remove trays from oven and allow choux to cool completely before filling. Clean and dry mixing bowl and make filling.

Lime Infused Cream Filling

  1. Place sugar and lime peel in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add cream cheese and cream, then whisk without setting a time/speed 3, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Transfer cream to a piping bag fitted with a star-shaped nozzle. Cut cooled choux buns in half then pipe cream into middle, and arrange layers of thinly sliced strawberries over the top before placing lid back on. Serve fresh.

Useful Items

2 baking trays and baking paper, piping bags and nozzles, long sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace strawberries with other fruits (e.g. raspberries, mango, pineapple, blackcurrants, redcurrants).
