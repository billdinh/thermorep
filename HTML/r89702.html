Gingerbread biscuits

Ingredients

Gingerbread biscuits

  • 380 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 150 g golden syrup
  • 150 g butter
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk

Royal icing

  • 240 g white sugar
  • 1 egg white
  • ½ tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp water, hot (optional)

Preparation

Gingerbread biscuits

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place all gingerbread ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 12 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 50 sec/.
  3. Transfer gingerbread dough onto a floured silicone bread mat or work surface and knead into a smooth ball. Place dough into a large bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Place dough into refrigerator to chill (approx. 1 hour).
  4. Roll out chilled dough between 2 pieces of baking paper to approximately 4 mm in thickness. Remove top layer of baking paper, then cut gingerbread into desired shapes using cookie cutters (see Tips).
  5. Transfer biscuit shapes onto prepared baking trays, then bake for 8-10 minutes (180°C), or until lightly browned at the edges. Cooking time will vary slightly depending on the size and shape of your biscuits.
  6. Allow to cool for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and allow to cool completely (30 minutes).

Royal icing

  1. Place white sugar into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add egg white and lemon juice and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Add hot water (optional) to thin out the icing if required.
  3. Transfer icing into a piping bag with a thin nozzle and decorate gingerbread biscuits as desired (see Tips). Serve biscuits once icing is set (approx. 20 minutes) or transfer into a sealable container to store until use.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, large bowl, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, cookie cutter, rolling pin, wire rack, piping bag (thin nozzle), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Use gingerbread biscuits as gift tags or to decorate your Christmas tree. To do this, punch a hole in an appropriate place on the biscuit with a drinking straw prior to baking so that a piece of string or ribbon can be threaded through later for hanging. Reform holes as required while biscuits are still hot.
  • If royal icing is too thick, add a few more drops of lemon juice or water.
  • For different icing colours, distribute icing in several different bowls, then add a few drops of different food colourings to each bowl and combine.
