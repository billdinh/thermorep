Piped Raspberry Mousse

Ingredients

  • 2 gelatine leaves
  • 250 g raspberry purée
  • 10 g lemon juice
  • 10 g Kirsch or cherry brandy
  • 100 g sugar
  • 60 g egg whites, from medium eggs
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 250 g whipping cream

Preparation

  1. Soak gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water. Meanwhile, place raspberry purée, lemon juice and Kirsch in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/90°C/speed 2.
  2. Squeeze out gelatine leaves, add to mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 1. Transfer to a large metal bowl then refrigerate. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in 100 g sugar. Remove bowl from lid.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and salt in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whisk 9 min/70°C/speed 3. With 5 minutes remaining, slowly add reserved sugar. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whip speed 3 until lightly whipped, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping.
  6. Place chilled raspberry mixture over a bowl of ice until it starts to thicken (see tip). Fold through meringue and whipped cream. Pipe into glasses and refrigerate until ready to decorate and serve.

Useful Items

metal bowl, piping bag and plain nozzle, dessert glasses, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own raspberry purée, blend 350 g fresh raspberries 30 sec/speed 9, then pass through a fine sieve to remove seeds.
  • Using a metal bowl allows you to chill the raspberry mixture faster.
  • A good sign of a mixture thickening is that it leaves a trail that holds it's shape momentarily when you drizzle it back into the bowl from a spoon.
