Red bean ice cream

Ingredients

Red bean paste

  • 120 g dried adzuki beans
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 50 g sugar

Red bean ice cream

  • 400 g milk
  • 100 g icing sugar
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 sprig fresh mint, leaves only, for ganishing

Preparation

Red bean paste

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh beans into it. Cover with water and set aside for 6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain beans using simmering basket. Place 1000 g water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with beans into mixing bowl and cook 40 min/100°C/speed 2 or until beans are soft. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Transfer cooking liquid into a jug and set aside.
  3. Place reserved beans, sugar and 150 g of the reserved liquid into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 4- 9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 8 min/90°C/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool for 30 minutes, then place into refrigerator until ready to use.

Red bean ice cream

  1. Place milk, icing sugar, egg yolks and cream into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add reserved bean paste and mix 15 sec/speed 6, until well combined. Transfer into a shallow, freezable container, allow to cool completely (approx. 1 hour), then transfer into freezer for a minimum of 8 hours or overnight.
  3. Remove ice cream from freezer, set aside for 20 minutes to soften, then cut into squares (4 x 4 cm). Place ice cream into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 7, with aid of spatula.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for a further 30 sec/speed 6, then transfer into a freezable container or bowl and place into freezer for 2-3 hours until firm. Garnish with mint leaves before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, jug, freezable storage container, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Adzuki beans can be found at most supermarkets or Asian food stores.
