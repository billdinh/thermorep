Cream Puffs (Profiteroles) with Vanilla Ice Cream and Chocolate Sauce

Ingredients

Cream Puff Pastry

  • 5 ½ oz water
  • 3 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 4 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • 3 jumbo eggs

Chocolate Sauce

  • 9 oz dark chocolate, 70% cocoa, cut into pieces
  • 1 ½ oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 7 oz whole milk
  • vanilla ice cream, homemade or ready-made

Preparation

Cream Puff Pastry

  1. Place water, butter, salt and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/212°F/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl and set aside to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.) with parchment and set aside.
  4. Set mixing bowl back into position and without setting a time mix speed 5, adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid onto rotating blades. After adding the last egg, mix a further 30 sec/speed 5.
  5. Place pastry mixture into a piping bag with nozzle (½ in.) and pipe balls (walnut-size) onto prepared baking sheet, leaving a 2 inch space between them. Bake for 20-25 minutes (400°F) or until golden brown. Turn off oven and open oven door slightly, leaving it ajar for 10 minutes to allow pastry to dry. Remove baking sheet from oven and allow pastries to cool completely. Clean mixing bowl.

Chocolate Sauce

  1. Place dark chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add butter and milk and heat 4 min/212°F/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Cut cold cream puffs in half. Place one scoop ice cream onto each bottom half and cover with top half. Serve 2-3 filled pastries per person with chocolate sauce drizzled on top.

Useful Items

baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), parchment paper, ice cream scoop, piping bag with nozzle (½ in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Another name for cream puff pastries is Profiteroles. Profiteroles are a traditional French dessert.
  • If you don't have a piping bag, cut the corner off a sturdy freezer bag and use as directed to pipe cream puffs onto baking sheet.
  • Use a wet finger to flatten any bumps on cream puff pastries before baking.
  • If using homemade ice cream, take it out of the freezer and mix it in a clean mixing bowl to soften before using.
  • Cream puff pastries can be prepared in advance: fill cream puffs with ice cream and store in the freezer. Prepare and pour the chocolate sauce just before serving.
