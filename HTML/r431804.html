Wagyu beef manti with peas, pickled onions and garlic yoghurt

Ingredients

Manti dough

  • 250 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 egg
  • 125 g water

Wagyu beef and sauce

  • 100 g beef wagyu, partially frozen, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 pinch Aleppo pepper, plus extra to serve (see Tips)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200 g Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • 1 pinch salt, plus an extra one teaspoon
  • water, for boiling
  • 20 g unsalted butter
  • 100 g Turkish sausage (sucuk), cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 100 g frozen peas
  • 100 g cocktail pickled onions
  • 50 g capsicum paste (see Tips)
  • 10 fresh mint leaves

Preparation

Manti dough

  1. Lightly flour a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place all dough ingredients into mixing bowl and knead 3 min/, until a smooth ball forms. Transfer mixture into a bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and set aside for 30 minutes to rest. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Wagyu beef and sauce

  1. Place beef, Aleppo pepper and 1 of the garlic cloves into mixing bowl and mince Turbo/0.5 sec/0 times.
  2. Transfer dough onto a silicone mat or lightly floured work surface. Roll dough out (2-3 mm thickness), then cut into small squares (1.5-2 cm). Place 1 pinch of the wagyu mince into the middle of each square, then bring up all 4 edges to make a diamond shape. Pinch the edges to seal shut and place onto prepared tray (see Tips). Repeat with remaining filling and squares of dough. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt into it. Set aside.
  4. Place a pinch of salt and remaining garlic clove into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into bowl with yoghurt and stir to combine. Cover and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Fill a deep, medium-sized saucepan three-quarters of the way with water. Add 1 teaspoon salt, place over high heat and bring to the boil. Working in batches, add manti and blanch for 2-4 minutes or until they start to float on the surface. Remove with a slotted spoon and transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Add 1 ladle of the cooking water into the bowl, then cover to keep warm.
  6. Place butter and Turkish sausage (sucuk) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 3 min/120°C/speed 2.
  8. Add peas, pickled onions and capsicum paste and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 2. Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl with manti and stir to combine. Divide between serving bowls, top with Garlic yoghurt and mint leaves and sprinkle with extra Aleppo pepper to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, slotted spoon, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, ladle, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), silicone bread mat, rolling pin, deep medium-sized saucepan, kitchen towel, plastic wrap, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Aleppo pepper (also known as pul biber) is a mild variety of dried, ground capsicum often used in Middle Eastern cooking. It's available in Middle Eastern delicatessens or specialty food stores. If you can't find it, try combining 1 tbsp mild paprika with 1 tsp cayenne powder instead. Or you can replace the Aleppo pepper with dried chilli flakes in this recipe.
  • Turkish sausage (sucuk) is usually made with beef and spiced with paprika and fenugreek. It can be found at some continental or Middle Eastern delicatessens. If you are unable to find it, replace the sucuk with salami.
  • You can find capsicum paste at Middle Eastern delicatessens or specialty food stores. You can replace the capsicum paste with a good-quality tomato paste.
  • Get the kids involved in making these, as small hands are good for this job. They're fiddly, so make a large batch and freeze the excess. If you want to prepare them ahead of time, you can store them in the freezer for up to 3 months. Just place them into the freezer on a floured baking tray and once frozen, transfer into freezer bags.
