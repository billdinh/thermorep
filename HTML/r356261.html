Illinois Pierogi

Ingredients

Cheese Filling

  • 17 ½ oz water
  • 16 oz potatoes, peeled, cubed (1 in.)
  • 4 oz yellow onion, quartered
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 ½ tsp semolina
  • 13 oz mascarpone cheese
  • ¾ tsp salt, to taste
  • ¾ tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Dough

  • 9 oz warm water, plus extra to seal and boil pierogi
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 oz grapeseed oil
  • 17 ½ oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 pinches salt, to taste, plus extra to boil water (approx. 3 tbsp per 4 qt)

Topping

  • 16 oz yellow onions, quartered
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, cubed, plus extra to fry
  • 2 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • fresh parsley leaves, to garnish (optional)

Preparation

Cheese Filling

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, weigh in potatoes. Secure Varoma lid and cook 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer potatoes to a bowl, place in refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 15 min.). Discard water from mixing bowl.
  2. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Add butter and sauté 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place reserved cooled potatoes and semolina into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add mascarpone, sea salt, pepper and reserved sautéed onion and with aid of spatula mix 20 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then with aid of spatula mix a further 20 sec/speed 3.5. Transfer filling into a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Place all dough ingredients into mixing bowl and knead 1 min/.
  2. Transfer dough to a lightly floured silicone mat or work surface, then roll out until very thin (approx. ⅛ in.). Using a cookie cutter (approx. 3 in.), cut out circles of dough (approx. 50).
  3. Place 1 tbsp of reserved filling into the center of one circle of dough. Wet the edge of half the circle with a little water, then carefully fold dough over filling to create a half-moon shape enclosing the filling. Pinch edges with fingertips to seal. Repeat process with remaining dough circles.
  4. On stove top, fill a saucepot (approx. 4-5 qt) three quarters of the way with water. Add salt, place over high heat and bring to a gentle boil. Cook pierogi in batches for 3-4 minutes, or until they rise to the surface. Remove with a slotted spoon and allow to drain before transferring into a large serving bowl. Cover to keep warm.

Topping

  1. Place onions into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Add butter and olive oil and without measuring cup cook 30 min/250°F/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and without measuring cup cook a further 20 min/250°F/speed 1.
  2. Sauté pierogi before serving by placing a frying pan over high heat and gently frying in oil or butter until golden on both sides. Serve pierogi hot, with onion topping, garnished with fresh parsley.

Useful Items

bread board or silicone mat, rolling pin, cookie cutter (approx. 3 in.), large saucepot (approx. 4-5 qt.), large serving bowl, frying pan

Hints & Tricks

  • Any uncooked pierogi can be frozen for later use. Divide pierogi onto baking sheets and place into freezer. Once frozen, transfer into sealable plastic freezer bags to store until required. Cook frozen pierogi in boiling water for 4-5 minutes.
  • To steam pierogi: spray Varoma dish and tray with nonstick cooking spray. Divide approximately 15-20 pierogi between Varoma dish and tray, spacing to allow steam to circlulate. Place 33 oz water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Repeat with remaining pierogi.
  • To fry onion topping on stovetop: Place 2 oz butter, 2 oz olive oil and 16 oz diced onions into a large non-stick frying pan and fry until onions are golden brown. Drizzle hot pierogi with sautéed onions and butter mixture before serving.
