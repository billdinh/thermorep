Chinese Vermicelli with Prawns and Garlic Sauce

Ingredients

  • 200 - 250 g Chinese vermicelli (made from mung beans or rice)
  • water, warm, for soaking
  • 70 g garlic cloves
  • 40 g vegetable oil
  • ½ tsp salt (optional)
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 35 g sugar
  • 40 g soy sauce
  • 20 g rice wine
  • 700 g water
  • 200 - 250 g medium raw prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 200 g flat green beans
  • 50 g spring onions, sliced (3 mm) or curled, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh vermicelli into it. Cover with warm water and set aside to soak for 30 minutes. Drain vermicelli through Varoma dish, transfer to a heatproof shallow bowl (Ø 12-14 cm bottom) and place bowl in Varoma dish, making sure that some holes remain unobstructed so that steam can circulate.
  2. Place garlic and oil in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed .
  3. Add salt (if using), white pepper, sugar, soy sauce, rice wine and 200 g water and cook 4 min/120°C/speed . Meanwhile, arrange prawns one by one on top of drained vermicelli.
  4. Pour garlic sauce from mixing bowl evenly over prawns. Place 500 g water in mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and add flat green beans. Set Varoma in position and steam 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until prawns are pink. Remove Varoma, then remove simmering basket with aid of spatula.
  5. Sprinkle prawns and vermicelli with spring onions (see tip) to garnish and serve hot with flat green beans. When serving, mix vermicelli until covered in sauce.

Useful Items

heatproof shallow bowl (Ø 12-14 cm bottom)

Hints & Tricks

  • This light dish is full of flavour and quick to prepare. Feel free to adapt flavourings to your taste. For instance, add chopped fresh chillies, or replace green beans with green peas. And don’t be alarmed by the amount of garlic: once cooked, it gives the sauce a mellow, subtle flavour.
  • If your shallow bowl is 14 cm or larger, ensure the holes of the Varoma dish are not blocked by using a steaming rack, 2 small chopsticks or a wooden chopstick broken in two to support the shallow bowl off the bottom of the Varoma dish.
    - Mung bean vermicelli are commonly used for this dish, however rice vermicelli also work well.
    - To curl spring onions, cut in 10 cm lengths and then lengthwise in thin strips (julienne), place in a bowl of cold water with a few ice cubes, and chill approx. 20-30 minutes until curled.
    - If raw prawns are small (6-7 cm) they can be steamed from frozen.
