Hazelnut torte

Ingredients

  • butter, for greasing
  • 250 g raw sugar
  • 250 g hazelnuts, plus extra for decorating
  • 8 eggs, separated, room temperature
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 40 g water
  • 60 g cornflour
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder

Filling

  • 1 egg
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 10 g instant granulated coffee
  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream, chilled
  • desiccated coconut, for decorating

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a springform cake tin (26 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place raw sugar into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a separate bowl to the sugar and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk . Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and whip  2-4 min/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer whipped egg whites into a large bowl and set aside.
  5. Place egg yolks, water and reserved sugar into mixing bowl and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 5.
  6. Add cornflour, plain flour, baking powder and reserved hazelnut meal and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  7. Spoon ⅓ of the reserved egg whites back into mixing bowl and whip 5-10 sec//speed 2. Transfer mix into large bowl with remaining egg whites and gently fold together using a metal spoon until all is combined, taking care not to overwork the mix. Pour into prepared springform tin and bake for 50 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow cake to cool (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place egg, caster sugar and granulated coffee into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add pouring (whipping) cream and whip without setting a time/speed 3 or until a smooth, silky consistency is achieved, watching carefully to avoid overwhipping. Transfer into a bowl and set aside in the refrigerator until ready to use.
  3. Slice cooled cake into 3 layers and spread chilled filling over each layer to create a sandwich. Sprinkle top with desiccated coconut and extra hazelnuts to decorate. Place into refrigerator for at least 1 hour to set prior to serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, springform cake tin (26 cm), large bowl, wooden skewer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The time it takes your filling mix to thicken in step 9 will depend on the cream used. If you would prefer larger amounts of filling between the layers of torte, make two separate batches as per recipe above.
  • Gluten free torte: replace 40 g plain flour with 40 g gluten free cornflour and proceed as per recipe.
