Quince Torta

Ingredients

Slow-baked Quinces

  • 1500 g water
  • 800 g sugar
  • 6 quinces, washed and halved
  • 250 g blanched almonds
  • 100 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed

Cake

  • 160 g sugar
  • 125 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 medium egg
  • 150 g self-raising flour
  • 3 Tbsp strong brewed coffee
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • icing sugar, for dusting
  • cream, for serving

Preparation

Slow-baked Quinces

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place water and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 12 min/120°C/speed 2.
  3. Place quince halves cut-side up in a baking dish that will hold them in a single layer. Pour in sugar syrup.
  4. Press a sheet of baking paper directly onto quinces and seal with foil.
  5. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in almonds then transfer to a baking tray.
  6. Bake quinces and almonds for 20 minutes (180°C) until almonds are golden. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  7. After this time, remove almonds from oven and set aside. With quinces still in oven, reduce oven temperature to 160°C and bake for a further 3 hours. Turn quinces hourly until they have turned red and syrup has reduced by half. Remove baking paper for the last hour.
  8. Remove quinces from oven and add lemon juice to syrup. Leave quinces to cool in syrup until cool enough to handle.
  9. Remove quince cores and seeds with a teaspoon then cut each quince half into four wedges.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line a springform cake tin (Ø 26 cm).
  2. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 8.
  4. Add butter and blend 5 sec/speed 4.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk and whip 1 min/speed 4.
  6. Add egg then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Add flour, ground almonds, coffee and baking powder then mix 20 sec//speed 4.
  8. Pour into prepared tin and smooth the top using a wet spatula. Arrange 16 quince wedges on top in a circular pattern. Place 2 wedges in the centre. Spoon over 4 Tbsp quince cooking syrup. Refrigerate or freeze remaining quince wedges in their syrup for future use.
  9. Bake for 40 minutes (170°C) or until a skewer in the centre comes out clean. Leave cake to cool in tin for 10 minutes.
  10. Brush quince wedges and cake with quince syrup and leave to cool completely.
  11. Remove from cake tin and dust with a little icing sugar. Serve with cream.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking dish, springform cake tin (Ø 26 cm), aluminium foil, skewer, pastry brush, baking tray, oven, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Quinces can be baked up to a week ahead and refrigerated in their syrup.
  • The cake mixture will be quite dense.
  • When arranging quince wedges, ensure they all face the same direction so each slice of cake will have a wedge.
  • For a deeper red colour in the quinces, cook for a further 1 hour.
  • Red plums can be used in place of quinces; halve and pit plums and arrange cut-side down; glaze with boiling redcurrant jelly or apricot jam.
  • Quantities for the slow-baked quince recipe can be halved if desired. If so, there will be enough quince wedges for one torta but few or no leftovers for use on a future torta. Baking time is the same.
  • Slow-baked quince keeps well in its syrup in the fridge or freezer. Leftover quince wedges can be served warm or cold with the syrup and thick cream.
