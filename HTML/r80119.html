Almond pastry

Ingredients

  • 30 g raw sugar or Rapadura sugar
  • 30 g blanched almonds
  • 210 g plain flour
  • 130 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • ½ tsp mixed spice
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 3 - 4 tsp water or orange juice

Preparation

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add almonds and mill 9 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour, butter, mixed spice and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 6, or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Add egg yolk and water or orange juice and knead 20 sec/.
  5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and work into a ball. Wrap pastry in plastic wrap and place in refrigerator for a minimum 20 minutes to chill.
  6. Once chilled, roll out between a silicone bread mat and a sheet of baking paper, or between two sheets of baking paper, to desired thickness.
  7. Blind bake (see Tips) or return pastry to refrigerator to chill until use. Use as needed.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking paper, rolling pin, silicone bread mat, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To blind bake pastry: Preheat oven to 200°C. Roll out pastry to line the base and sides of a greased tart tin and prick the base with a fork or pastry docker to prevent air bubbles and rising. Line pastry base with baking paper and fill with rice, dried beans or pie weights (this will stop the pastry base from rising during baking). Place tart tin onto a baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (200°C). Remove from oven, then remove baking paper with rice, dried beans or pie weights. Return pastry case to oven and bake for a further 5-10 minutes (200°C) or until light golden.
  • This recipe is great to use for sweet tarts and fruit mince pies.
  • This recipe fills a 20-22 cm tart tin.
