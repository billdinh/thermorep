Ancho-Cranberry Turkey Enchiladas

Ingredients

Ancho-Cranberry Sauce

  • 3 dried ancho chilies, de-stemmed and de-seeded
  • 1 dried 'chile de árbol' chili, de-stemmed and de-seeded (optional)
  • 4 oz red onions, in pieces
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • ½ oz olive oil
  • 14 oz chicken stock, low sodium
  • 10 oz cranberry sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp salt

Assembling Enchiladas

  • 3 oz Monterey Jack cheese, cubed
  • 3 oz cheddar cheese, cubed
  • 12 oz cooked turkey meat, chopped
  • 1 can black beans (approx. 15 oz), rinsed, drained
  • oil, to fry
  • 12 corn tortillas (6 in.)
  • chopped fresh cilantro, to garnish

Preparation

Ancho-Cranberry Sauce

  1. Place ancho and 'chile de árbol' chilies into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add onions, garlic and oil and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and sauté 5 min/212°F/speed 0.5.
  3. Add stock, cranberry sauce, tomato paste, oregano, cumin and salt, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 20 min/212°F/speed 2. Insert measuring cup and blend 1 min/speed 3-8, increasing speed gradually. Transfer Ancho-Cranberry Sauce into a bowl and set aside. Preheat oven to 350°F. Clean mixing bowl.

Assembling Enchiladas

  1. Place Monterey Jack cheese and cheddar cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place cooked turkey into mixing bowl and shred 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a large bowl, add half the reserved grated cheese and black beans, mix well with spatula to combine and set aside.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium heat and add oil as needed. Lightly fry corn tortillas (approx. 20-30 sec. per side). Drain on paper towels and dip into reserved Ancho-Cranberry Sauce to fully coat. Set aside.
  4. Divide shredded turkey mixture between reserved corn tortillas and roll up. Place seam-side down in a baking dish (9 in. x 13 in.). Top with Ancho-Cranberry Sauce, to taste and remaining grated cheese. Bake for 20 minutes (350°F).
  5. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro to serve.

Useful Items

skillet or frying pan, paper towels, baking dish (9 in. x 13 in.), large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace cooked turkey meat with store-bought rotisserie chicken.
