White Chocolate Cheesecake

Ingredients

Chocolate Biscuit

  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 40 g sugar
  • 30 g cocoa powder
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 90 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 medium egg

Cheesecake Base

  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing

Raspberry Coulis

  • 125 g raspberries
  • 50 g sugar
  • 1 tsp water (optional)

Cheesecake Filling

  • 380 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 90 g double cream
  • 500 g cream cheese
  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Preparation

Chocolate Biscuit

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line a large baking tray.
  2. Place flour, sugar, cocoa powder and salt in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add butter and egg then mix 15 sec/speed 6. Tip out onto a floured work surface and roll to 3 cm thick. Bake in one piece for 20 minutes (160°C). Leave to cool completely before using.

Cheesecake Base

  1. Grease and line a springform cake tin (Ø 26 cm).
  2. Place chocolate biscuit, broken in pieces, and butter in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 6. Tip mixture into prepared tin, smooth surface and freeze until required. Clean mixing bowl.

Raspberry Coulis

  1. Place raspberries, sugar and water (if using) in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, cook 6 min/90°C/speed 3.
  2. Strain through a fine sieve and set aside.

Cheesecake Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C.
  2. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 8.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3-5 min/50°C/speed 1.
  4. Add double cream, cream cheese, sugar, eggs, salt and vanilla then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk and whip 30 sec/speed 4. Pour mixture over prepared biscuit base. Dot with ⅓ raspberry coulis and create a marbled look by running a skewer through them. Bake for 1 hour (150°C).
  6. Turn oven off, prop oven door ajar and leave to cool inside for 1 hour, then chill in fridge for 12-24 hours.
  7. Serve with remaining raspberry coulis.

Useful Items

rolling pin, springform cake tin (Ø 26 cm), fine sieve, skewer, baking paper, large baking tray, oven, freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you want to use frozen raspberries, omit the 1 tsp water for the coulis.
