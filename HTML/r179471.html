Radish cakes

Ingredients

  • 120 g water
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 30 g oil, plus extra for frying
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into thin slices (separate white and green parts)
  • 10 g small peeled shrimp (see Tip)
  • 180 g pork fillet, cut into thin strips (5 mm)
  • 200 g daikon, peeled and cut into cubes (5 mm)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • soy sauce, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/60°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour, 15 g of the oil and ¼ tsp of the salt and knead 3 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and divide into 6 equal portions (approx. 60 g each). Cover with a damp kitchen towel to rest for 20 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, proceed with recipe.
  3. Place white part of spring onion/shallot, shrimp, pork  and remaining 15 g oil into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add daikon, sugar, pepper and remaining ½ teaspoon salt and cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  5. Add green part of spring onion/shallot and cook 1 min/100°C/speed . Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool completely.
  6. Roll out each portion of dough into a thin circle (approx. 10 cm) and place approx. ¼ cup of the daikon filling onto centre of the dough. Pull together all edges of dough and pinch together tightly to seal. Turn cake over and lightly press down to form a smooth circle (to approx. 1.5 cm thickness).
  7. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil (2 tablespoons). Fry radish cakes on both sides until golden brown (approx. 2-3 minutes per side). Transfer onto a plate lined with paper towel to drain, then serve warm with soy sauce.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, kitchen towel, bowl, rolling pin, large frying pan, 1 plate, paper towel, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Small peeled shrimp are dried and can be found in the refrigerated section in Asian food stores.
