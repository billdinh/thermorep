Raspberry and Vanilla Éclairs

Ingredients

Crème Pâtissière

  • 500 g whole milk
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 40 g cornflour
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Choux Pastry

  • 150 g water
  • 80 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 15 g caster sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 3 medium eggs

Icing

  • 250 g icing sugar
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp water, hot
  • pink food colouring paste, as desired
  • 2 Tbsp freeze-dried raspberries, for decoration

Preparation

Crème Pâtissière

  1. Place milk, sugar, cornflour, eggs and vanilla bean paste in mixing bowl then cook 7 min/90°C/speed 4. Blend 5 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl, cover surface with cling film and set aside until cool enough to refrigerate. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make choux pastry.

Choux Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place water, butter, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3. Remove mixing bowl and leave to stand for 10 minutes to cool.
  4. Place mixing bowl back in position and, without measuring cup, mix at speed 4 while slowly adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid until fully incorporated. Transfer to a pastry bag fitted with a plain 1.5 cm nozzle, or to a disposable piping bag with 1.5 cm cut off the end.
  5. Pipe 18 logs, 10 cm long, on prepared baking trays, leaving 3-4 cm between each. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until cooked and golden - it is important not to open the oven during baking to prevent pastries from collapsing. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  6. Make a small hole in ends of pastries, then return to the oven and bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C) until pastry is completely crisp. Transfer to a wire rack and allow to cool.

Assembly and Icing

  1. Once cooled, place reserved crème patissière in mixing bowl and blend 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a small nozzle then pipe into prepared holes in pastries. Set aside and prepare icing.
  2. Place a wide bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in icing sugar. Remove bowl then add pink food colouring paste as desired and enough water to form a very stiff icing. Dip tops of filled pastries into icing then transfer to a wire rack. Decorate with freeze-dried raspberries and leave to set.
  3. Best served immediately.

Useful Items

2 baking trays and baking paper, piping bags and nozzles, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use the seeds from 1 vanilla pod instead of the vanilla bean paste if preferred.

The word ‘éclair’ comes from the French ‘flash of lightning’ because they are eaten so quickly! There are many different flavour combinations you can try, but we think these will be gone in a flash too and make an elegant addition to an afternoon tea selection.
