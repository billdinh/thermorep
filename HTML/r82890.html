Bara Brith

Ingredients

  • 2 teabags (e.g. Earl Grey or English Breakfast)
  • 300 g water, boiling
  • 450 g dried mixed fruit
  • 250 g light brown sugar
  • 450 g self-raising flour
  • 2 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 large egg

per 1 slice

1399 kJ / 334 kcal

Preparation

  1. Place teabags in a large bowl then place bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh in boiling water and allow to steep for 5 minutes.
  2. Add dried fruit then remove bowl from lid and gently mix together. Cover with cling film and set aside to steep for 8 hours or overnight.
  3. The next day, preheat oven to 150°C and line a large loaf tin (900 g) with baking paper.
  4. Place sugar, flour, mixed spice and egg in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  5. Remove and discard teabags from soaked fruits. Add fruits and juices then mix 15 sec//speed 4 while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Finish mixing with spatula before pouring into prepared loaf tin. Bake for 1¼-1½ hours (150°C) or until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean.
  6. Leave in tin for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cool, cut in slices and serve.

Useful Items

cling film, baking paper, 900 g loaf tin (2 lb), skewer, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This traditional Welsh tea bread, which translates literally as ‘speckled bread’, has several variations. It can be made with yeast, but this is a quicker version without. The tea-soaked fruits give the loaf a lovely sticky texture and it keeps well, too. Best enjoyed cut in thick slices and spread with butter.
