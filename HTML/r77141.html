Zucchini, corn and ricotta pancakes

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1500 g full cream milk
  • 40 g white vinegar
  • ¼ tsp sea salt

Pancakes

  • 310 g zucchini (approx. 1), cut into pieces
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, white part only
  • 40 g butter, plus extra for frying
  • 170 g self-raising flour
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 200 g buttermilk
  • 2 eggs
  • 130 g fresh corn kernels

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 3 min/90°C/speed 1. Milk will separate into curds and whey. Leave to sit in mixing bowl for 1 minute.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large jug. Gently pour ricotta into lined simmering basket and leave to drain for 15 minutes.
  4. Stir salt through ricotta, then transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pancakes

  1. Place zucchini into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Transfer grated zucchini into simmering basket and drain off any excess liquid. Transfer into a separate bowl to the ricotta and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place spring onion/shallot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and cook 1 min 30 sec/60°C/speed 1.
  5. Add flour, salt, pepper, buttermilk and eggs and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add corn, 90 g of the reserved ricotta and reserved grated zucchini and combine 10 sec//speed 4.
  7. To cook pancakes, place a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add ½ tsp butter into pan.
  8. For each pancake, pour in a ladle of batter and cook 1-2 minutes or until bubbles start bursting on pancake surface, then turn pancake and cook for a further 1 minute.
  9. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or warm ceramic dish and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter to make 10 pancakes. Serve hot.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large jug, medium-sized non-stick frying pan, turner / fish slice, ladle, thermal serving bowl or warm ceramic dish, kitchen towel, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve pancakes with extra ricotta and your favourite toppings, such as chopped tomatoes or sweet chilli sauce.
