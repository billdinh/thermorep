Miso corn purée with almond ricotta

Ingredients

Almond ricotta

  • 200 g blanched almonds (see Tips)
  • 160 g filtered water, plus extra for soaking
  • 1 ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch garlic powder

Miso corn purée

  • 2 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 eschalot, cut into halves
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 - 4 sweet corn cobs, kernels removed
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 100 g filtered water
  • 1 ½ tbsp shiro (white) miso paste
  • 50 g roasted whole red pepper strips (optional)
  • fresh coriander, leaves only, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

Almond ricotta

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh almonds into it. Add salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, rinse and drain almonds.
  3. Place almonds into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10.
  4. Add all remaining almond ricotta ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 10, until smooth and creamy. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Miso corn purée

  1. Place oil, eshalot and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 2.
  2. Add corn kernels, stock paste, water and miso paste and cook 10 min/120°C/speed 4. Blend 1 min/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9. Serve corn purée dotted with almond ricotta, roasted pepper strips (optional) and garnish with fresh coriander (optional).

Useful Items

bowl, sealable container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your almonds, soak nuts in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • You can store the Almond ricotta in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • You can serve this dish as a side or as a dip with chips or crackers. We served it with our Vegan parmesan shards (refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
