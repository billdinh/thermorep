Mint and peanut chutney

Ingredients

  • 30 g peanut oil, plus extra for storing
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 70 g tamarind paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g water
  • 10 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 140 g unsalted peanuts
  • 60 g fresh coconut flesh (see Tips)
  • 60 g fresh tomatoes
  • 1 - 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ½ brown onion, cut into halves

Preparation

  1. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add garlic and mustard seeds and roast 2 min/100°C//speed 2. Transfer oil into a bowl and set aside, leaving garlic and mustard seeds in mixing bowl.
  3. Add tamarind paste, water, mint, peanuts, coconut, tomato, chilli and salt and blend 10 sec/speed 9.  Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onion and reserved oil and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Add reserved peanut mixture and simmer 10 min/80°C/speed 2. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for approx. 15 minutes, then transfer into a sealable storage jar. Top with extra oil. Store in refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, sealable storage jar or container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own tamarind paste, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can use fresh or frozen coconut flesh in this recipe. Fresh coconut flesh freezes well and can also be purchased pre-frozen in most Asian stores.
  • You can store this chutney in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  • Serve as a side to your favourite curry, snack, appetiser or bread. We served ours with Rava idli (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
