Basic berry muffins

Ingredients

  • 40 g sunflower seeds
  • 40 g pepitas
  • 80 g banana, broken into pieces
  • 1 egg
  • 240 g milk (see Tips)
  • 120 g extra virgin coconut oil
  • 120 g honey
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 280 g wholemeal self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 125 g fresh seasonal berries

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with paper cases and set aside.
  2. Place seeds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9 or until finely ground.
  3. Add banana, egg, milk, oil, honey and vanilla and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add flour and cinnamon and mix 10 sec/speed 3 or until combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 100 g of the berries and combine 10 sec//speed 3. Fold through further with spatula, if needed.
  6. Divide batter between paper cases. Top each muffin with some of the remaining berries, then bake for 25 minutes (160°C), or until golden and a skewer inserted into the centre of each muffin comes out clean. Allow to cool in tray for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool further before serving.

Useful Items

wooden skewer, 12 cupcake liners, 12-hole muffin tray, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace half of the milk with an equal quantity of plain yoghurt or buttermilk.
  • Use whichever fresh seasonal berries you have on hand, such as blueberries, raspberries or a mixture. Frozen berries also work in these muffins.
