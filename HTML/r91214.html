Margherita Pizza

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to grease
  • 8 oz water, room temperature
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ oz fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 14 oz bread flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 tsp salt

Margherita Topping

  • 7 oz mozzarella, drained, cut into pieces
  • 1 can diced tomatoes, (approx. 14 oz)
  • 2 - 3 pinches salt, to taste
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 10 - 15 fresh basil leaves, torn into pieces, divided

Preparation

Pizza Dough

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl and set aside. Place water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 2.
  2. Add bread flour, 1 oz olive oil and salt and knead 2 min/. Place dough into prepared bowl and form into a ball. Cover bowl with cling wrap or a damp kitchen towel and let dough rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hr.).

Margherita Topping

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F. Line a baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.) with parchment or grease with olive oil and set aside.
  2. Place mozzarella into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into simmering basket and leave to drain for 10 minutes, if necessary.
  3. Place dough on prepared baking sheet and shape into a rectangle to fit baking sheet by pressing and stretching it gently with fingertips. Form a small ridge around the edges.
  4. Spread diced tomatoes and mozzarella over dough and sprinkle with salt, 1 tbsp olive oil and 5-7 torn basil leaves. Bake pizza for 20 minutes (450°F). Scatter additional 5-8 torn basil leaves (to taste) over pizza and serve hot.

Useful Items

baking sheet (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), parchment paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Add your favorite toppings (e.g. vegetables, pepperoni, ham, cheese, fruit, herbs).
  • Alternatively, roll pizza dough out on a floured surface with a rolling pin, and transfer to prepared baking sheet.
  • Four individual round pizzas can be made from this dough. Italians bake them one at a time and share each pizza as it comes fresh out of the oven.
  • Lightly oil cling wrap to prevent it from sticking to the rising dough.
  • If chopped tomatoes have a lot of liquid, drain through simmering basket before using.
