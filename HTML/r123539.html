Baked Spinach and Feta Mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, rind removed and cut in pieces (3 cm) (optional)
  • 2 slices stale white bread, torn in pieces
  • 200 g fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin only (2 pieces, 1 cm x 5 cm)
  • 120 g onions, halved
  • 140 g smoked lean bacon, cut in pieces
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 180 g feta cheese, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts, toasted
  • &frac18; tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 6 - 8 fresh Portabello mushrooms, wiped clean and stalks removed

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese (if using) in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a large baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  3. Place bread in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place spinach in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5, while stirring with aid of spatula. Transfer to bowl with breadcrumbs and set aside.
  5. Place lemon zest, onions and bacon in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add oil and, without measuring cup, sauté 3 min/Varoma//speed .
  7. Add feta, pine nuts, nutmeg, salt, pepper and reserved spinach and breadcrumbs then combine 10 sec//speed 3.
  8. Place mushrooms on prepared baking tray. Place 2-3 tablespoons of spinach mixture into each mushroom and top with reserved grated Parmesan (if using). Bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C), until mushrooms are cooked through.
  9. Transfer mushrooms onto a plate lined with paper towel to rest for 1-2 minutes, then serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, baking tray and paper, baking paper, paper towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with toast and poached eggs for a larger meal.
  • You can substitute the Parmesan cheese with cheddar or mozzarella for a milder flavour or serve with a single piece of shaved Parmesan to garnish.
