Goat's Cheese Panna Cotta with Beetroot and Port Jelly, Beetroot Gel and Beetroot Pickles

Ingredients

Goats Cheese Panna Cotta

  • 600 g double cream
  • 400 g goat's milk
  • 6 gelatine leaves
  • 400 g soft goat's cheese, strong flavoured, rindless
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper

Beetroot and Port Jelly

  • 150 g beetroot juice
  • 150 g port
  • 50 g maple syrup
  • 2 gelatine leaves
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste

Beetroot Gel

  • 300 g beetroot juice
  • 80 g maple syrup
  • 4 Tbsp agar-agar (10 g)
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper
  • 125 g water, cold

Beetroot Pickles

  • ½ cinnamon stick
  • ½ tsp coriander seeds
  • 5 cardamom pods
  • 1 star anise
  • 250 g cider vinegar
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely sliced
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 300 g water
  • 300 g beetroots, peeled, diced (0.5 cm)

Beetroot Slaw and Garnish

  • 100 g beetroots, finely sliced (julienne)
  • 100 g fresh bean sprouts (mung bean)
  • 100 g red vein chard cress
  • 1 Tbsp rapeseed oil (cold-pressed)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 2 candy striped beetroots, thinly sliced

Preparation

Goats Cheese Panna Cotta

  1. Chill 12 small glass ramekins or glass bowls (Ø 8 cm x 4 cm deep) in fridge.
  2. Place cream and milk in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, soak gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water.
  3. Add soaked and squeezed gelatine leaves to mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 8.
  4. Add goats cheese, salt and pepper then blend 50 sec/speed 10. Pass through a fine sieve into a jug then pour into chilled glasses. Leave to set in fridge for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Beetroot and Port Jelly

  1. Place beetroot juice, port and maple syrup in mixing bowl. Place simmering basket in place of measuring cup and boil 12-15 min/120°C/speed 1 until reduced to approx. 200 g.
  2. Soak gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes then squeeze out excess water. Add leaves to mixing bowl together with 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch pepper. Stir 15 sec/speed 2 then pour into a storage container approx. 15 cm square. Place in fridge for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Beetroot Gel

  1. Place beetroot juice, maple syrup, agar-agar, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then heat 6 min/100°C/speed 2. Pour into a bowl and do not move until set (approx. 3 hours). Once set, cover and transfer to fridge for 12 hours.
  2. Place set gel in mixing bowl and blend 2 min/speed 10. If too firm, add up to 125 g cold water and blend again 2 min/speed 7 until a smooth fluid gel forms. Transfer to a squeezy bottle and chill until needed. Clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Beetroot Pickles

  1. Place cinnamon, coriander seeds, cardamom pods and star anise in mixing bowl then toast 4 min/Varoma/speed .
  2. Add vinegar, sugar, garlic, thyme, 1 pinch of salt and 1 pinch of pepper then simmer 10 min/95°C/speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool completely then strain, reserving the liquid.
  3. Place water and 1 pinch salt in mixing bowl then boil 6 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add diced raw beetroot and cook 7 min/98°C/speed  until just tender. Drain then add to ⅔ of strained pickling liquid, then leave to cool completely.
  5. Remove panna cotta from fridge 20 minutes before serving. Cut jelly into ½ cm cubes.
  6. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in beetroot julienne, bean sprouts, chard cress, oil, salt and pepper. Remove bowl, mix together then set aside.
  7. Add thinly sliced candy beetroot to remaining pickling liquid and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Place five 1 cm beetroot gel dollops and five cubes of jelly on each panna cotta. Place five pickled beetroot dice on each followed by one slice of candy striped beetroot on each gel dollop. Scatter a little salad over each panna cotta and serve immediately.

Useful Items

ramekins (Ø 8 cm, height 4 cm), fine sieve, jug, plastic container, cling film, squeezy bottle, medium mesh sieve, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The pickling liquid can be made a day in advance and chilled overnight.
  • A picture-pretty starter served in a glass bowl. Ideal for large parties as most components are best prepared in advance. The gel, jelly and panna cotta are best prepared and left to set in the fridge for a minimum of twelve hours.
