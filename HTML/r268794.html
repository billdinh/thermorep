Sri Lankan Chicken Curry (Koththu Roti)

Ingredients

Spice Mix

  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 3 tsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Curry

  • 500 g chicken breast fillets, skinless, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 ½ cm fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 2 cloves
  • 40 g oil
  • 8 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 50 g tomato purée
  • 120 g coconut cream
  • 120 g coconut milk
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 red chilli
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 - 2 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 3 roti (see tips)
  • 1 lime, cut in wedges, for garnish

Preparation

Spice Mix

  1. Mix chilli powder, ground turmeric, curry powder and pepper in a small bowl then set aside.

Curry

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in chicken. Add lime juice, 0.5 tsp salt and half reserved spice mix then stir to combine until chicken is evenly coated. Cover with cling film and allow to marinate in fridge for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Place garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, 20 g oil, 5 curry leaves and half onion slices in mixing bowl then fry 4 min/Varoma//speed .
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Add marinated chicken, remaining spice mix and half the ginger-garlic paste then cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add tomato purée, coconut cream, coconut milk and ½ tsp salt then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid, cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Add sugar and extra salt to taste. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a bowl and cover to keep warm.
  6. Heat a large frying pan over a medium heat and add remaining 20 g oil. Add remaining ginger-garlic paste, remaining onion and remaining 3 curry leaves then cook 5 minutes. Meanwhile, cut chilli and carrot in batons (approx. 2 cm x 5 mm) then season with salt and pepper.
  7. Add chilli and carrot batons then sauté for 2 minutes, until vegetables are partly cooked. Push vegetables to the edges of the frying pan, creating a space in centre of pan and crack eggs into it. Stir to scramble egg, then mix vegetables into scramble, until well combined. Add roti and cook for 3 minutes, stirring well to combine.
  8. Divide vegetable mixture between 6 plates, top with chicken curry and garnish with lime wedges before serving.
