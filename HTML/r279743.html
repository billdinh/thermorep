Spicy eggs with tomato sauce

Ingredients

Tomato sauce

  • 150 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 50 g tomato paste
  • 1 - 2 tsp white sugar, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)

Spicy eggs

  • oil, for greasing
  • 4 whole eggs
  • 8 pinches dried chilli flakes
  • 4 pinches salt
  • 4 pinches ground black pepper
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 500 g boiling water
  • labne, to serve (optional - see Tips)
  • flatbreads, to serve

Preparation

Tomato sauce

  1. Place onion, garlic and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar, cumin, coriander, oregano, paprika and stock paste and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.

Spicy eggs

  1. Line Varoma tray with a wet, well-wrung piece of baking paper. Grease 4 egg rings with oil (see Tips). Place rings onto lined Varoma tray and crack 1 egg into each ring. Sprinkle each egg with 2 pinches chilli flakes, 1 pinch salt, 1 pinch pepper and fresh coriander leaves.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and insert Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 4-6 min/Varoma/speed 4 or until eggs are cooked to your liking.
  3. Divide tomato sauce between serving bowls and top with eggs. Serve with spoonfuls of labne (optional) and flatbread.

Useful Items

baking paper, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, egg rings, serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • If you do not have egg rings, eggs can be broken directly onto lined Varoma tray. Cooking time may vary.
  • To make your own labne (a soft yoghurt cheese), please refer to the recipes on www.cookidoo.com.au. You can also replace the labne with Danish feta or soft goat's cheese.
