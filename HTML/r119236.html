Mighty mouse balls

Ingredients

  • 4 thin brown rice cakes, broken into halves
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces, plus extra cut into 20 thin discs for ears
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 500 g beef mince
  • 6 pitted black olives, cut into small pieces for eyes
  • 20 pumpkin seeds
  • coconut oil spray, for coating
  • 1 radish, cut into 20 thin discs for ears (optional)
  • 2 - 3 celery stalks, cut into thin matchsticks for tails

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place thin rice cakes into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer onto a plate and set aside.
  3. Place carrot into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and stock paste and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1,  without measuring cup.
  5. Add tomato paste and mince and mix 10 sec//speed 4, with aid of spatula.
  6. Roll 1 tablespoon of mixture into golf ball-sized balls and place onto plate with rice crumbs. Generously cover balls with crumbs, patting crumbs onto ball and shaping as needed before placing onto prepared baking tray. Repeat rolling and coating with remaining mixture (approx. 20 balls).
  7. Using a wooden skewer, make small holes for the eyes and tail. Place the tail hole high up on the ball for the best effect. Push olive pieces into holes to make eyes.
  8. Make a small cut behind the eyes where the ears (carrot or radish discs, optional) will go. Push a pumpkin seed, pointy end first into ball to create a nose.
  9. Liberally spray balls with coconut oil spray and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C), or until golden and firm to touch.
  10. Find the slits for the ears and hole for the tail and re-define with a wooden skewer. Insert carrot discs (or radish discs, optional) to create ears. Insert celery to create tail. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), 1 plate, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can replace beef mince with mince of choice.
  • Serve plain or with a favourite dipping sauce, such as homemade tomato sauce.
  • You can replace pitted black olives with diced red capsicum. You can replace celery tails with beetroot or carrot tails.
  • These balls can be made a day ahead up to adding pumpkin seed for the nose. Proceed with recipe from spraying with coconut oil to cook and assemble.
