Mediterranean vegetable bake

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 20 g mozzarella cheese, cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 2 slices bread, cut into pieces and slightly frozen
  • 1 onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 anchovy fillets
  • 1 tsp pickled capers, drained
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 30 g water
  • 190 g red capsicum, cut into strips (2 cm) then strips cut into halves
  • 130 g green capsicum, cut into strips (2 cm) then strips cut into halves
  • 310 g zucchini, cut into pieces
  • 4 yellow squash, cut into quarters
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 50 g pitted green Sicilian olives
  • 100 g feta cheese, cut into cubes
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan, mozzarella and bread pieces into mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onion, garlic, anchovy fillets, capers and oil into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  5. Add oregano, vegetable stock paste, tomatoes and water. Place Varoma into position and weigh red capsicum, green capsicum, zucchini and squash into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray. Steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Place drained chickpeas and steamed vegetables into an ovenproof dish (approx. 30 x 21 cm). Add olives, feta and pepper to ovenproof dish then combine well with spatula. Blend sauce in mixing bowl 10 sec/speed 7, then pour into ovenproof dish and sprinkle reserved cheese and breadcrumb mixture over the top.
  7. Bake for 15 minutes (200°C) or until cheese and breadcrumb mixture is golden. Serve hot.

Useful Items

ovenproof dish (30 x 21 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute Parmesan cheese with cheddar cheese.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • This recipe can be served as a main meal for two or as a side dish for four. Double the quantities for the cheese and breadcrumb mixture in step 1, and freeze to use as a quick coating for chicken or veal another day.
  • Transfer any leftovers into a freezable container and place into the freezer to enjoy for lunch or dinner later in the week.
  • Make this recipe to use up any leftover vegetables and avoid waste.
  • For a gluten free option, use 80 g almonds as a substitute for the bread. This is a great way to use any leftover almonds you may have from the Curried almonds.
