Easter Cheese Parcels (Flaounes)

Ingredients

Pastry

  • ½ Tbsp granulated sugar
  • 100 g whole milk
  • 60 g water
  • 100 g butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • &frac18; tsp ground cloves
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 380 g plain flour
  • 50 g olive oil

Filling

  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • &frac18; tsp ground cloves
  • 10 g fresh mint, leaves only
  • 175 g halloumi cheese, cut in pieces
  • 200 g kefalotyri cheese, cut in pieces
  • 50 g Cheddar cheese (mild), cut in pieces
  • 20 g polenta
  • 60 g sultanas (optional)
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp granulated sugar
  • 4 medium eggs (1 beaten for glaze)
  • 1 ½ Tbsp sesame seeds

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place sugar, milk and water in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Add baking powder, cloves, cinnamon, salt, flour, butter and oil then mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead 3 min/. Transfer to prepared bowl and leave to rise in a warm place for 15 minutes. Preheat oven to 180°C then prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place cinnamon, cloves, mint, halloumi, kefalotyri and cheddar in mixing bowl then grate 6 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add polenta, sultanas (if using), baking powder, sugar and 3 eggs then mix 3 sec//speed 5. Divide into 12 pieces and roll each into balls.
  3. Divide rested dough into 12 pieces. Roll each into a square (approx. 8 cm x 8 cm).
  4. Place a cheese ball in the centre of each square then fold the sides up to almost cover the cheese. Place parcels onto prepared baking tray.
  5. Brush a beaten egg over parcels then sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) then cover with foil and bake for a further 10-15 minutes (180°C). Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

large bowl, rolling pin, large baking tray, baking paper, pastry brush, aluminium foil, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Flaounes are a traditional Cypriot Easter bread. Both sweet and savoury, these cheese filled pastries are typically prepared by the whole family on Good Friday as part of the Easter celebrations.
  • Kefalotyri is a hard, salty white cheese made from sheep's milk and/or goat's milk in Greece and Cyprus.
