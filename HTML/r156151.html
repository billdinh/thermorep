Chicken corn chowder with broccoli terrines

Ingredients

Broccoli terrines

  • 500 g water
  • 400 g broccoli, broken into florets
  • butter, for greasing
  • 4 eggs
  • 150 g thickened cream
  • 1 - 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Chicken corn chowder

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 140 g onions, cut into halves
  • 60 g butter, cut in pieces
  • 280 - 320 g canned sweet corn kernels, drained
  • 350 - 400 g skinless chicken breast fillets, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 900 g water
  • 2 tsp Chicken stock paste (refer to The Basic Cookbook)
  • 1 fresh green chilli (e.g. jalapeño), deseeded if preferred and cut into pieces (optional)
  • 200 - 300 g thickened cream
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground sweet paprika
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, leaves only, cut into pieces

Preparation

Broccoli terrines

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, add broccoli florets to it, secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease 8 ceramic ramekins (7 cm diameter x 4.5 cm high) with butter. Set Varoma aside and empty mixing bowl.
  2. Place steamed broccoli, eggs, cream, salt, black pepper and nutmeg into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 5. Divide mixture evenly between prepared ramekins and arrange them in Varoma dish and tray. Set Varoma aside and clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chicken corn chowder

  1. Place garlic cloves and onions into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add corn and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed , then chop 6 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add chicken and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add water and chicken stock paste, place Varoma with broccoli terrines in position, secure Varoma lid and cook 27 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep terrines warm.
  6. Add green chilli, cream, salt, cumin and sweet paprika to mixing bowl, mix 10 sec/speed 4, then cook 5 min/90°C/speed 1.5.
  7. Serve chicken corn chowder sprinkled with chopped coriander leaves as a starter, followed by broccoli terrines as a main dish (see Tips).

Hints & Tricks

  • To keep the broccoli terrines warm while you enjoy the chowder, set the covered Varoma with the broccoli terrines on the empty but still warm mixing bowl.
    - Turn out terrines onto a plate for an attractive presentation.
    - Broccoli terrines can also be served as a starter or as a side dish with smoked salmon or roasted meat.
    - Instead of ceramic ramekins, use glass jars or metal moulds. If using metal moulds, reduce cooking time by 2 minutes in step 7.
