Garlic Lobster Ciabatta Pizza - Pizza ciabatta con aragosta all'aglio

Ingredients

Biga

  • 140 g plain flour
  • 120 g water, room temperature
  • &frac18; tsp dried instant yeast

Dough

  • 315 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 175 g water, room temperature
  • 60 g milk

Topping

  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 300 g San Marzano tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 lobster tails, meat only, cut in small pieces
  • 2 sprigs fresh basil leaves, shredded

Preparation

Biga

  1. Place flour, water and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and cover tightly with cling film. Leave to stand at room temperature overnight (minimum 8 hours).

Dough

  1. Place prepared biga, flour, yeast, salt, water and milk in mixing bowl then knead 1 min/. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead again 5 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  2. Transfer very sticky dough to greased bowl and cover tightly with cling film. Leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Grease a silicone spatula or dough scraper with oil. Fold partially risen dough over itself by gently lifting and folding edge of dough toward middle, using oiled spatula. Turn bowl a quarter turn and fold again. Repeat 6 more times (total of 8 turns). Re-cover with cling film and leave to rise for 30 minutes.
  4. Repeat folding process once more then leave until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 230°C and line 2 pizza trays (Ø 30 cm) with baking paper.
  5. Transfer dough to a well-floured surface, while not deflating it too much. Liberally flour top of dough and cut in half. Turn one piece of dough cut-side up and sprinkle with flour. With well-floured hands, press dough into a rough circle (Ø 30 cm) then transfer to one pizza tray. Repeat with second piece of dough. With floured fingertips, make dimples over both bases to prevent the crust from ballooning.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes (230°C) until bubbles on top and bottom are lightly browned. Transfer bases to a wire rack to cool while making topping.

Topping

  1. Place oil, tomatoes and salt in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place butter and garlic in mixing bowl, chop 3 sec/speed 5 then heat 10 min/95°C/speed . Brush edges of each pizza base with the garlic butter.
  3. Using a slotted spoon, spread tomato sauce over pizza bases. Scatter over lobster pieces then drizzle over remaining garlic butter. Scatter with basil leaves then bake for 10 minutes (230°C). Serve hot.

Useful Items

cling film, baking paper, pizza trays (Ø 30 cm), pastry brush, wire rack, large bowl, silicone spatula, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Crayfish tails can be used instead of lobster for a cheaper alternative.
  • The ciabatta dough can easily be made into loaves or rolls.
  • Ciabatta is an Italian bread that was created in 1982 by Francesco Favaron, a baker from Verona. He created the bread in response to the popularity of French baguettes and named it ciabatta (slipper) as the shape reminded him of his wife’s slippers! Ciabatta bread is made with a biga, a type of pre-fermentation often used in Italian baking that creates a light, open texture with holes. This ciabatta pizza is topped with flavourful San Marzano tomatoes and pieces of sweet lobster, before being drizzled with garlic butter for a truly luxurious finish.
  • This recipe will work with cooked or uncooked lobster meat.
