Sugar Cookie Pastry

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ oz sugar
  • 7 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 13 oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 ½ tsp homemade vanilla sugar,

Preparation

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add butter, flour, egg, salt and vanilla sugar and mix with aid of spatula 25-30 sec/speed 5. Remove pastry dough from mixing bowl, form into a flattened ball and wrap with cling wrap. Refrigerate for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a pie pan (9-11 in.) or line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  4. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of parchment or cling wrap to a thickness of ¼ inch. Line prepared pie pan with dough or cut out shapes and place them on prepared baking sheet. For a pie shell or crust, prick pastry with fork to avoid air bubbles. Bake pie shell or crust for 15-20 minutes (350°F), cookies for 10-12 minutes (350°F), or until golden. Allow to cool completely on a wire rack before decorating, filling, or storing in an airtight container.

Useful Items

cling wrap, rolling pin, cookie cutters, wire rack, pie pan (9 - 11 in.) or baking sheet and parchment paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Be careful not to knead the dough unnecessarily with your hands, as this will make the cookies, pie shell or crust tough.
  • If the dough becomes warm and difficult to work with, place rolled out dough, still covered with sheets of parchment or cling wrap, into refrigerator until dough is firm enough to handle.
  • Instead of rolling out the dough with a rolling pin, shape it into a log. For cookies made with cookie cutters, roll the log so the cross section is round. For a pie shell, make a flatter log. Wrap log in cling wrap and refrigerate until firm enough to cut into slices. For round cookies, cut round log into slices (¼ in. thick) and bake as directed by the recipe. For a pie shell or crust, line the pie pan with long dough slices (¼ in. thick), overlapping them slightly, and press dough with fingers to fill in any cracks. Bake as directed by the recipe.
  • Decorated Cookies: decorate cookie shapes with colored icing, chocolate coating and sweets.
  • Jam-Filled Cookies: spread cool cookie with jam and place another on top. Dust with confectioners sugar.
