Rhubarb and blackberry breakfast tart

Ingredients

Dough

  • 40 g cold water
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 1 tbsp raw sugar
  • 1 blood orange, zest only, no white pith (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 90 g unsalted butter
  • 1 egg

Filling

  • 150 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 120 g raw sugar
  • 350 g rhubarb, trimmed, woody stalks peeled and cut into slices (5 mm)
  • 100 g blackberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Blood orange yoghurt

  • 150 g Greek-style natural yoghurt
  • 1 blood orange, juice only (see Tips)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it, then set aside.
  2. Place flour, sugar and zest into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add salt and butter and mix 15 sec/speed 5, or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 1 min/, slowly pouring water and adding egg through hole in mixing bowl lid, until a soft dough forms.
  5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a thick, flat disc. Wrap dough in silicone bread mat or plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 1 hour. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add cinnamon and 70 g of the sugar, then mix 3 sec/speed 3. Set aside in mixing bowl.
  4. Roll out dough into a circle (approx. 30 cm) on a silicone bread mat or floured work surface. Place onto prepared baking tray and prick dough approx. 10 times with a fork.
  5. Sprinkle almond meal mixture evenly onto dough, leaving a 4 cm border on all sides.
  6. Arrange rhubarb in tight circular rows, each slice slightly overlapping the previous one. Scatter blackberries over rhubarb.
  7. Fold pastry borders over rhubarb filling and pinch to seal. Brush borders with egg wash and sprinkle tart with remaining 50 g sugar.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes (190°C), until pastry edges are a deep golden brown. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Blood orange yoghurt

  1. Place yoghurt and blood orange juice into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Serve tart warm or cool with yoghurt.

Useful Items

jug, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the blood orange with orange or mandarin.
  • You can use raw or blanched almonds in this recipe.
  • Use any seasonal fruit (such as apricots, peaches or apples) instead of the rhubarb.
