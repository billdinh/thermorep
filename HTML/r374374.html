Grilled eye fillet with Café de Paris butter

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp pickled capers
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 anchovy fillets
  • ½ tsp lemon, zest only, no white pith, plus 2 teaspoons juice
  • 130 g salted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp brandy
  • 1 tsp Madeira wine (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • ¼ tsp sweet paprika
  • ¼ tsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • olive oil, for brushing
  • salt, to season
  • 6 beef fillet steaks (approx. 200 g each - see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place capers, parsley, dill, garlic, anchovies and lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, tomato sauce, mustard, brandy, Madeira wine, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, curry powder, pepper and lemon juice into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 3.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 25 sec/speed 3 or until well combined. Form butter into a log (approx. 20 cm). Wrap in baking paper and transfer into freezer to firm (approx. 20 minutes) or until ready to use.
  4. Place a heavy-based frying pan over medium-high heat. Brush each portion of steak with oil and season with salt and pepper. Fry steaks on each side (approx. 4 minutes for medium - see Tips), turning once, or until steaks are cooked to your liking.
  5. Transfer steaks onto a plate. Place a slice of the reserved butter (approx. 1 cm thickness) on top of each steak. Cover to keep warm and set aside to rest for a few minutes. Serve with sides of choice (see Tips).

Useful Items

plate, heavy-based frying pan, baking paper, pastry brush, aluminium foil, stove top, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the Madeira with dry sherry in this recipe, if desired.
  • Cooking times for steaks may vary due to the size and thickness. Adjust the cooking time to suit your preferred level of doneness. You could also cook the steaks on the barbecue.
  • We served our steak with mashed potato and fresh steamed asparagus. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the Mashed potato recipe.
