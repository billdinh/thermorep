Empress Rice with Crème Anglaise

Ingredients

  • 350 g whole milk
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 50 g pudding rice
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest only
  • 3 jelly cubes, (quarter of one 135 g pack)
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only
  • 3 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 250 g milk
  • 4 gelatine leaves
  • 15 g candied cherries, diced (3 mm), soaked in Kirsch for 1 hour
  • 15 g candied angelica, diced (3 mm), soaked in Kirsch for 1 hour
  • 20 g Kirsch
  • 200 g whipping cream, chilled

Preparation

  1. Place milk and sugar in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, heat 5 min/100°C/speed 4.
  2. Add rice and lemon zest then cook 30 min/90°C//speed 1 until rice is cooked and creamy. Transfer to a bowl to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Dissolve jelly cubes according to packet instructions. Line base of 6 dariole moulds with liquid jelly then refrigerate until set. Meanwhile, make crème anglaise.
  4. Place vanilla seeds, egg yolks, caster sugar and milk in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/85°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, soak gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water.
  5. Add soaked and squeezed out gelatine and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with cooled rice pudding (skim off any skin that has formed) and mix well.
  6. Add soaked cherries and angelica and Kirsch then stir. Clean mixing bowl.
  7. Insert butterfly whisk. Place chilled cream and lightly whip on speed 3 to soft peak stage, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping.
  8. Place rice mixture over a bowl of iced water and stir until it slightly thickens due to the gelatine (see tip). Fold in whipped cream. Pour into chilled dariole moulds and refrigerate until fully set.
  9. To demould puddings, dip moulds in warm water for 7-10 seconds then invert onto the centre of a dessert plate. Serve, or decorate with fresh berries and raspberry sauce.

Useful Items

dariole moulds, serving plates, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Make this recipe in metal moulds so the puddings are easier to demould.
  • A good sign of a mixture thickening is that it leaves a trail that holds it's shape momentarily when you drizzle it back into the bowl from a spoon.
