Chickpea Gratin

Ingredients

  • breadcrumbs, for sprinkling
  • 120 g wholemeal bread, torn in pieces
  • 200 g milk
  • 3 medium eggs, separated
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 100 g walnut halves
  • 550 g cooked chickpeas (drained weight)
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 250 g onions, quartered
  • 550 g tomatoes, quartered
  • 60 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a baking dish (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm) then sprinkle with breadcrumbs.
  2. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in bread and milk. Remove and set aside to soak while continuing with recipe.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and a pinch of salt in mixing bowl then whisk 3 min/speed 3.5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Remove butterfly whisk. Place walnuts in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place chickpeas in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to bowl with nuts.
  6. Place garlic, onions, tomatoes and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Sauté 7 min/120°C/speed 1.
  7. Add squeezed-out, soaked bread, egg yolks, a pinch of salt, nutmeg, walnut and chickpea purée then stir with spatula. Mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  8. Remove mixing bowl and gently fold in reserved whisked egg whites. Transfer to prepared baking dish and bake for 35-40 minutes (180°C) until golden. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking dish (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Chickpeas are high in fibre and packed with nutrients, providing an excellent source of protein, vitamins and minerals. Including chickpeas in your diet may help to play a role in reducing the risk of heart disease, diabetes and cancer.
