Twice-Baked Watercress, Goat's Cheese and Hazelnut Soufflé

Ingredients

  • 175 g hard goat's cheese, diced (1 cm)
  • 50 g blanched hazelnuts
  • 25 g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 25 g plain flour
  • 300 g milk
  • 2 tsp English mustard
  • 4 medium eggs, separated
  • 50 g watercress, leaves and small stalks only, plus extra for garnish
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • water, boiling, for cooking

Preparation

  1. Place 25 g goat's cheese in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and toast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Chop 5 sec/speed 5 then transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter, flour and milk in mixing bowl then cook 6 min/90°C/speed 4. Remove lid and set bowl aside to cool for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C and generously butter 8 individual ramekins (Ø 8 cm).
  4. Add mustard and egg yolks then beat 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add remaining 150 g goat's cheese, watercress, reserved chopped hazelnuts and salt then stir 1 min/speed 2. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whisk 2 min 30 sec - 3 min/speed 3 until stiff peaks form. Fold a little of egg whites through cheese and watercress mixture to loosen it. Fold remainder of egg whites through in three stages.
  7. Divide soufflé mixture between prepared ramekins then place in a deep sided baking dish. Fill baking dish with boiling water so it reaches halfway up sides of ramekins. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C) until risen and cooked through. Carefully remove from dish and cool completely before removing from ramekins, or chill until required.
  8. When preparing to serve, preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper. To remove soufflés from ramekins, use a knife to loosen around the edge then tip out onto baking tray. Scatter over reserved grated goat's cheese.
  9. Return soufflés to oven for 10-15 minutes (200°C) until risen and golden on top. Serve warm with a garnish of watercress.

Useful Items

8 individual ramekins (Ø 8 cm), large bowl, deep sided baking dish, baking tray and paper, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Make up to the end of step 7 then wrap individually in cling film. Keep chilled for up to 24 hours or freeze for up to 1 month. Defrost in the fridge overnight. When ready to serve, continue with recipe.
  • Works well served with onion chutney.
  • Try a different cheese and nut combination, such as Stilton and walnut.
