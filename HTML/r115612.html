Vindaloo pork

Ingredients

Curry mix

  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 cm cinnamon quill
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp apple cider vinegar

Pork belly

  • 1000 g pork belly meat, boned, scored
  • 2 brown onions (approx. 240 g), cut into halves
  • 2 - 3 fresh bird's eye chillies, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 8 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp soft dark brown sugar
  • 1400 g water
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra for seasoning
  • 200 g basmati rice
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds (see Tips)
  • 50 g raw unsalted cashew nuts, toasted

Preparation

Curry mix

  1. Place corinader seeds, cumin seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, peppercorns and chilli flakes into mixing bowl and roast 5 min/100°C/speed 3, then mill 1 min/speed 9 to a fine powder.
  2. Add ginger, garlic, turmeric, salt, olive oil and vinegar and blend 25-30 sec/speed 7 until fine. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pork belly

  1. Place pork belly into large bowl and rub with curry mix. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for 3 hours or overnight.
  2. Place onions and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then add oil and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add curry leaves, sugar and 500 g of the water. Place marinated pork belly into Varoma dish ensuring steam can circulate freely. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam for 30 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Preheat oven to 250°C. Line a roasting tray with aluminium foil and set aside.
  6. Remove Varoma and set aside. Add 200 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Remove Varoma and transfer pork into prepared roasting tray. Remove any curry mix from rind of pork with spatula and add to sauce in mixing bowl. Season pork rind with salt. Roast for 20 minutes (250°C) until skin is crisp. Proceed with recipe while pork roasts.
  8. Transfer sauce into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  9. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear. Then re-insert simmering basket into mixing bowl.
  10. Add salt and mustard seeds to simmering basket. Add remaining 700 g water into mixing bowl and cook 12 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice into a separate thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  11. Reduce oven temperature to 160°C and roast pork for a further 20 minutes (160°C), or until tender and skin is crisp.
  12. Transfer pork onto a chopping board and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm) with a sharp knife and add to reserved sauce, then stir to combine. Sprinkle vindaloo with toasted cashews and serve with rice.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, roasting tray, aluminium foil, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, wooden chopping board, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can buy black mustard seeds from spice shops and online.
  • For a milder heat, replace bird's eye chillies with fresh red chillies (deseeded if preferred).
