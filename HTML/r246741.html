Brunch bread

Ingredients

  • 280 g warm water
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 500 g baker's flour
  • 30 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 100 g grated cheddar cheese
  • 100 g bocconcini
  • 30 g sun-dried tomatoes
  • 50 g bacon slices (cured) or ham
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • sesame seeds, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place water, yeast, oil and salt into mixing bowl and combine 5 sec/speed 3.
  3. Add flour and mix 6 sec/speed 8.
  4. Knead 2 min/. Transfer dough into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place to prove for 30 minutes, until doubled in size (see Tips).
  5. On a silicone bread mat, roll out dough into a large rectangle (20 x 45 cm). With the long edge facing you, spread grated cheese, spinach, bocconcini, sun-dried tomatoes and bacon in a long line along the bottom edge of dough, leaving a 1 cm border on each side. Roll up dough into a log, enclosing filling. Pull ends of dough around, creating a ring shape, and press ends together to seal. Set aside to prove for a further 10 minutes.
  6. Transfer bread onto prepared baking tray, brush top with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, plastic wrap, bowl, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any fillings of choice in this dish.
  • To help the dough prove faster, set the bowl with dough over a bowl of warm water until it has doubled in size.
  • To grate your own cheese, before step 1, place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 8-10 sec/speed 5.
  • Eggy brunch bread: After dough proves, roll out dough into a large rectangle (30 x 40 cm) and a large piece of baking paper. With the long edge facing you, down the centre of the dough, place two rows of bocconcini, spinach, sun-dried tomatoes and bacon or ham, leaving a 2 cm gap between rows. Crack 3 eggs into gap, using other fillings as a barrier. Fold shorter edges of dough over filling. Fold top half of dough over filling, then fold up bottom half of dough to fully enclose filling (making a rectangular-shaped parcel). Set aside to prove for 10 minutes. Brush with beaten egg, sprinkle with sesame seeds and bake for 30-40 minutes (200°C) until golden brown and eggs are cooked.
