Chhole (chickpea curry)

Ingredients

  • 20 g chickpea (besan) flour
  • 40 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (see Tips)
  • 300 g red onion, cut into quarters
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 10 g ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp green mango powder (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 800 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 500 g after draining)
  • 50 g raisins, to serve (optional)
  • 100 g natural yoghurt, to serve
  • 1 fresh long green chilli, trimmed and cut into thin slices, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place chickpea (besan) flour into mixing bowl and roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until colour darkens. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place ghee into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds and cook 40 sec/Varoma/speed 1, until crackling.
  4. Add asafoetida and stir with aid of spatula, then add onion, ginger paste and garlic paste and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add tomatoes, salt, chilli powder, turmeric, ground coriander, pepper, mango powder, garam masala and reserved chickpea (besan) flour and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 2.
  6. Add lemon juice and chickpeas and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Serve garnished with raisins (optional), yoghurt and sliced chillies.

Hints & Tricks

You can use dried chickpeas, instead of canned, in this recipe, but you will need to soak and cook them before using.

Weigh 200 g dried chickpeas into a bowl and cover with warm, filtered water. Allow to soak overnight, or up to 48 hours, at room temperture. Drain, rinse and re-cover with warm, filtered water at least 2-3 times in 24-48 hour period. Drain and rinse chickpeas and weigh 200 g of the soaked chickpeas into simmering basket. Place 900 g water into mixing bowl and cook 60 min/90°C/speed 3. If chickpeas are not cooked after 60 minutes, top up with water to 500 g and cook for a further 30 min/90°C/speed 3.

  • To make your own ghee, ginger paste, garlic paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Asafoetida comes from the root of a perennial Indian herb. It has a strong odour, but the aroma softens as it cooks, and it tastes similar to onion and garlic, enhancing the savoury flavour of the dish. It helps reduce flatulence and constipation, and reduce the growth of microflora in the gut. A little goes a long way, so you only need to use a small amount. You can find asafoetida at Indian grocers or online.
  • Green mango powder (also known as amchoor powder) can be purchased at most Indian or Asian food stores.
