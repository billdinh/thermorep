Spelt Margherita pizza

Ingredients

Pizza

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 280 g filtered water
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp coconut sugar
  • 250 g spelt flour (see Tips)
  • 270 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting

Vegetable sauce

  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 130 g carrot, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 150 g pumpkin, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 500 g ripe tomatoes, cut into quarters (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp tomato paste
  • 250 g zucchini, cut into pieces
  • 150 g button mushrooms, cut into halves
  • 3 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only (see Tips)

Assembly

  • extra virgin olive oil, for greasing
  • 450 g mozzarella, cut into slices
  • extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • fresh basil leaves, to serve

Preparation

Pizza

  1. Grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water, yeast, oil, salt and sugar into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Add flours and mix 6 sec/speed 3, then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Place into prepared bowl and lightly coat top of dough with oil. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place to prove until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Proceed with recipe while dough proves. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Vegetable sauce

  1. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil and sauté 2 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add carrot, pumpkin, tomatoes and tomato paste and chop 8 sec/speed 7.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 10 min/95°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add zucchini and mushrooms and cook 5 min/95°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add basil and blend 1 min/speed 1-7, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 7. Transfer into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Lightly grease 3 non-stick pizza trays (30 cm - see Tips).
  2. Punch down dough and divide into 3 equal portions. Using your hands, press each dough portion out over prepared trays (or if you do not have pizza trays, simply roll out to desired thickness - see Tips).
  3. Spread each base with 5-6 tablespoons (approx. 80-100 g) of the reserved Vegetable sauce and top with slices of mozzarella. Drizzle with oil and bake for 10-12 minutes (220°C) or until bases are crisp and cheese has melted. Top with basil leaves just before serving.

Useful Items

large bowl, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, pizza trays (30 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: wait until your baby is confidently managing finger foods (which may not happen until 8-9 months or older) before offering them some of this pizza. Cut it into pieces your baby can easily hold.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is, cut up if needed.
  • If you are new to pizza making, you might prefer to use more baker's flour and less spelt flour - use whichever amounts you like as long as you use a total of 520 g flour. The more baker's flour you use, the higher the gluten content and the easier it will be to stretch out and work with the pizza dough.
  • If you're time poor, you can add another ½ teaspoon yeast and the dough will prove in approx. 30-40 minutes.
  • You can use wholemeal or white spelt flour in this recipe. The wholemeal flour produces a denser result.
  • If you don't have pizza trays, after dividing the dough into three portions, you can simply roll out the dough using a rolling pin and place onto a lined baking tray (or pizza stone) instead. I like it rolled as thinly as possible - just thick enough to support the topping.
  • The riper your tomatoes, the sweeter the sauce will be.
  • You can replace the basil with oregano in the Vegetable sauce recipe.
  • If you only want to make 1-2 pizzas, you can roll the leftover pizza dough into balls for bread rolls. After shaping into balls, set aside for a further 30 minutes, then bake for 15 minutes (210°C) or until cooked through.
