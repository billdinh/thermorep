Blueberry Cobbler

Ingredients

Blueberry Filling

  • 30 - 32 oz fresh blueberries
  • 2 oz sugar
  • 1 oz corn starch
  • ½ oz lemon peel, no pith (approx. 1 lemon)

Biscuit Topping

  • 7 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • 7 ½ oz unsalted butter (approx. ¼ in.), plus extra to grease
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 ½ oz sugar
  • 4 ½ oz plain whole milk yogurt
  • turbinado sugar, to sprinkle

Preparation

Blueberry Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly grease a square baking pan (9 in. x 9 in.) and set aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid. Weigh in blueberries, rinse and allow to drain.
  3. Place sugar, corn starch and lemon peel into mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a large bowl, add drained blueberries and mix well with spatula. Transfer to prepared baking pan and bake 25 minutes (375°F) until the blueberries begin to bubble. Meanwhile make the topping.

Biscuit Topping

  1. Place flour, butter, baking powder, baking soda, salt and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add yogurt and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Divide mixture into 8 equal balls of dough (see Tip).
  4. After the blueberries have baked for 25 minutes, carefully remove dish from oven and drop dough on top of blueberries in staggered rows. Sprinkle with turbinado sugar.
  5. Bake a further 20-25 minutes (375°F) until top is golden. Allow to cool slightly and serve warm.

Useful Items

square baking pan (9 in. x 9 in.), bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace blueberries with any other type of berry. Stone fruit, such as peaches and plums, can be used in a cobbler as well. When using stone fruit, peel fruit, remove the pit and slice into wedges. Toss in sugar and allow to macerate for 30 minutes before draining the fruit to use. Whisk a few ounces of the liquid to create a slurry with the corn starch. Baking time for stone fruit may need reducing to 10-15 minutes.
  • Use wet hands when forming dough balls to prevent them from sticking to your hands.
  • Serve blueberry cobbler warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
