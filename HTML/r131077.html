Almond and Vanilla Dessert Cake

Ingredients

Almond Cakes

  • butter, for greasing
  • 50 g chopped almonds
  • 220 g almonds
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 30 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon

Vanilla Frosting

  • 3 egg whites
  • 140 g caster sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 100 g butter, unsalted, softened, cut in pieces

Preparation

Almond Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside. Line the bases of 2 sandwich cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper, grease with butter and set aside.
  2. Spread chopped almonds on prepared baking tray and bake for 5 minutes (180°C) or until golden.
  3. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer ground almonds to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, egg yolks and sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 10 min/37°C/speed 3.5.
  5. Add reserved ground almonds, self-raising flour, baking powder and ground cinnamon around butterfly whisk then mix 5 sec/speed 3.5. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully complete stirring with spatula, if necessary.
  6. Divide cake batter between the 2 prepared sandwich cake tins (approx. 370 g per tin, see tip). Place the 2 cake tins in the oven on the middle rack and bake immediately for 20-25 minutes (180°C). Allow cakes to cool approx. 5 minutes before removing from cake tins. Allow cakes to cool completely on a wire rack before filling (approx. 50 minutes). Clean mixing bowl thoroughly.

Vanilla Frosting

  1. Place egg whites, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then warm 4 min/70°C/speed 2.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk and whisk without measuring cup 5 min/speed 3.5.
  3. Add natural vanilla extract and butter then whisk 30 sec/speed 3.5. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate for 10 minutes.
  4. Place one reserved almond cake on serving dish and spread ½ of reserved vanilla frosting on top, place the second reserved almond cake over it, then spread the remaining vanilla frosting and sprinkle with the reserved toasted almonds. Serve, cut in slices.

Useful Items

2 sandwich cake tins (Ø 20 cm), baking tray, baking paper, cooling rack, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make the cake using one tin, choose a high cake tin (Ø 18 cm), and pour in the whole recipe amount of batter. Bake for 35-40 minutes in a preheated oven (180ºC), and allow to cool for 10 minutes before removing from cake tin. Cool completely on a wire rack, and simply slice in three horizontally before filling.
  • The sponge can be made in advance: keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Or keep for up to 1 month in the freezer.
  • Use the built-in Thermomix scales to measure out the exact quantity of cake batter for each cake tin. At the end of the recipe, turn scales on, lift out mixing bowl and pour some cake batter into the prepared tin. When you return the mixing bowl to the base, it will show a minus figure indicating the weight removed (- 320 g).
  • To chop almonds in the Thermomix place 50 g blanched almonds in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Sift the chopped almonds through a sieve to remove any dust.
  • For an almond topping instead of vanilla, replace the vanilla extract with almond extract.
