Tortelloni with Veal and Parmesan - Tortelloni di spinaci ripieni di vitello e formaggio

Ingredients

Dough

  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 70 g frozen spinach, defrosted, drained
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for serving

Filling

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 50 g onions, halved
  • 50 g pancetta
  • 30 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 300 g minced veal, broken in pieces
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra for serving
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, plus extra for serving
  • 1 large egg

Tortelloni

  • water, plus extra for cooking tortelloni
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, for cooking tortelloni

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour and spinach in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add eggs and oil then knead 2 min/. Tip out and bring together into a ball then wrap in cling film and allow to rest for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, onions, pancetta and butter in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, without measuring cup, sauté 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add veal, salt and pepper then, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Set mixing bowl aside to cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Place mixing bowl back into position. Add egg and reserved grated Parmesan then blend 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Tortelloni

  1. Divide dough into 4 equal-sized pieces. Take 1 piece, keeping remaining dough covered with cling film. Dust dough lightly with flour to prevent sticking then, using a rolling pin or pasta machine, roll out into a rectangle (2-3 mm thick).
  2. Using a Ø 6 cm pastry cutter, cut circles from dough, approx. 25 circles per quarter of dough. Remove excess dough and cover with cling film.
  3. Place ½ tsp of filling in the middle of each circle. Lift one side of the circle up and fold it over the filling to form a semi-circle. Working from one edge, carefully pressing out any air, create a seal around the filling. Dab a small amount of water on the corners of the semi-circle then bring both corners together, pressing gently to help them stick.
  4. Transfer tortelloni to a baking tray covered with baking paper and sprinkled with a little flour. Continue until all dough has been used.
  5. Bring a large saucepan of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil. Cook tortelloni for 2-3 minutes or until al dente then remove with a slotted spoon.
  6. Drizzle tortelloni with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray and baking paper, saucepan, round pastry cutter (Ø 6 cm), slotted spoon, rolling pin or pasta machine

Hints & Tricks

  • Tortelloni are small circles of rolled pasta dough folded around a filling. According to Italian folklore, the pasta was invented at a tavern in the town of Castelfranco Emilia where Venus, the goddess of love, stayed for a night. The exact details of the story vary but it is thought the innkeeper spied on Venus and then rushed to his kitchen to create pasta that resembled her belly button. We’ve added a twist to the classic veal-filled tortelloni by adding blanched spinach to the dough, resulting in a vibrant green pasta that looks spectacular dressed simply with olive oil.
