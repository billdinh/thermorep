Pancetta, pine nut and ricotta pappardelle

Ingredients

  • 1000 g water
  • 60 g olive oil
  • 200 g dried pappardelle pasta
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ long red chilli, deseeded if preferred
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 pieces lemon zest (5 x 2 cm), no white pith
  • 90 g pancetta, cut into pieces
  • 40 g pine nuts
  • fresh ricotta cheese (not smooth), to serve

Preparation

  1. Place water and 20 g of the oil into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add pappardelle pasta and cook 7-10 min/100°C//speed , until al dente, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Drain pasta using simmering basket, then transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place garlic, chilli, sage, salt, pepper and lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat chopping if necessary until a fine consistency is achieved.
  5. Add remaining 40 g oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Add pancetta and pine nuts and cook 3 min/100°C//speed . Transfer mixture into thermal serving bowl or other large bowl with pappardelle pasta and combine with spatula. Adjust seasoning to taste. Garnish with fresh ricotta cheese and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have a little time to spare, make your own ricotta and fresh pasta in your Thermomix. Visit the official Thermomix Recipe Community (recipecommunity.com.au) to learn how to make ricotta in the Thermomix.
  • Use any leftover ricotta in the Spinach and ricotta patties in tomato sauce or the Baked chocolate ricotta cakes with blueberries.
  • Pasta should be cooked until al dente, tender yet still firm to the bite.  Taste after 8 minutes then every minute after that.  If you make fresh pasta, cook as per the recipe.
  • Recipe for one: reduce all ingredients by half and proceed as per recipe.
