Italian ricotta doughnuts with citrus curd

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Citrus curd

  • 30 g orange juice, (approx. ½ orange)
  • 20 g lemon juice, (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 110 g caster sugar
  • 80 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 eggs

Ricotta doughnuts

  • 1 egg
  • 120 g caster sugar
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 50 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ lemon, zest only, grated, no white pith
  • 1500 g vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Citrus curd

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place all citrus curd ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/80°C/speed 3.
  2. Check setting point of curd by dipping a spoon into curd; mixture should coat the back of the spoon in a thick layer. If curd is not set, cook for a further 2-3 min/80°C/speed 3, or until curd has thickened. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Transfer into a sealable container and set aside to cool. Place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ricotta doughnuts

  1. Place egg, 20 g of the caster sugar, flour, milk, baking powder, zest and 150 g of the reserved ricotta into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large saucepan over medium-high heat, add oil and bring to 170°C. To check your oil is at 170°C, drop a small square of bread into the oil, it should turn golden brown in 20 seconds (see Tips).
  4. Working in batches, carefully drop a spoonful (approx. 2 teaspoons) of batter into hot oil and fry for approx. 1-2 minutes on each side or until golden brown and cooked through. The doughnuts will turn themselves over when one side is cooked through. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove doughnuts from oil and transfer onto lined plate to drain and cool slightly. Repeat with remaining batter to make approx. 24 doughnuts.
  5. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh remaining 100 g of the caster sugar into it. Remove bowl and stir through 1 teaspoon of cinnamon.
  6. Roll warm, drained doughnuts in cinnamon sugar, then transfer to a serving dish. Serve doughnuts with citrus curd.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, sealable container, large serving plate, paper towel, slotted spoon, serving dish, spoon, large saucepan, stove top, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have a kitchen thermometer that is capable of checking the temperature of your oil, then you can omit the bread test in step 8.
  • You can also fill the doughnuts with citrus curd using a kitchen syringe.
  • Use the extra ricotta for bruschetta, omelettes or in stuffed mini capsicums.
