Pear blueberry quinoa crumbles

Ingredients

  • 250 g blueberries (see Tips)
  • 50 g brown sugar (see Tips)
  • 4 ripe pears, peeled, cored and cut into dice (see Tips)
  • 50 g raw hazelnuts, with skins on
  • 70 g quinoa flakes (see Tips)
  • 30 g wholemeal plain flour
  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom, plus extra to serve
  • 70 g butter, cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • plain yoghurt, to serve

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh blueberries and 20 g of the sugar into it. Add pears to the bowl and stir to combine. Divide fruit mixture evenly between 6 ovenproof bowls or ramekins (250 ml capacity) and set aside.
  3. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 9 or until finely ground. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add quinoa flakes, flour, coconut, cardamom, butter and remaining 30 g sugar and mix 5 sec/speed 5, then knead 20 sec/.
  5. Sprinkle crumble mixture over fruit and bake for 25 minutes (160°C), or until crumble is golden and fruit is tender. Serve warm with yoghurt and an extra sprinkle of cardamom, if desired.

Useful Items

bowl, 6 ramekins (8 cm, 250 ml), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use‚ whichever berries are in season in these crumbles, or frozen berries if you prefer.
  • If your pears are firm you will need to bake the crumble for a bit longer. You may also need to cover your crumbles with aluminium foil to prevent the topping from getting too brown while the pear cooks.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in these crumbles – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
  • If you prefer, you can substitute the ground cardamom with ground cinnamon.
  • You can also substitute quinoa flakes with rolled oats or use a mixture of oats and quinoa flakes.
