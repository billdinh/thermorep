Mixed mushroom burgers

Ingredients

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus an extra 2 tablespoons for frying
  • 1 red onion (approx. 180 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g fresh mushrooms, cut into quarters (e.g. button or Swiss brown)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp sumac
  • 400 g canned pinto beans, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
  • 1 ½ tbsp psyllium husks
  • 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 6 bread rolls of choice, cut into halves horizontally (see Tips)
  • 6 slices vegan cheese (see Tips)
  • iceberg lettuce, to serve
  • ketchup, to serve (see Tips)
  • tomatoes, cut into slices, to serve
  • red onion, cut into slices, to serve
  • avocado, flesh only, cut into slices, to serve
  • vegan mayonnaise, to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place oil, onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 2 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add mushrooms, oregano, cumin and sumac and cook 5 min/120°C//speed 2, without measuring cup.
  4. Add beans, chia seeds, psyllium husks, salt and pepper and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 30 minutes).
  5. Divide mixture into 6 portions and shape into patties. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons oil. Fry patties on each side (approx. 3 minutes), until cooked through. Serve on a bread roll with sliced cheese, tomato, red onion, lettuce, avocado, ketchup and mayonnaise.

Useful Items

bowl, large frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • We used our Vegan mayonnaise, Beetroot ketchup and Lupini cheese on these burgers. Please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can use any bread roll of choice with this recipe. You could even replace the bread with avocado halves.
