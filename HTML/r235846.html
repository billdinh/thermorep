Goats Cheese and Caramelised Onion Quinoa Tart

Ingredients

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • 200 g quinoa
  • 1 butternut squash, (approx. 600 g), peeled and diced (2 cm)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 400 g red onions, thinly sliced (3 mm)
  • 15 g olive oil, plus 1 Tbsp
  • 30 g balsamic vinegar, plus 1 Tbsp
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 150 g soft goat's cheese
  • 150 g ricotta cheese
  • 150 2 - 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and vegetable stock in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in quinoa. Rinse 10 sec/speed 5 then cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer quinoa to a large bowl and set aside to cool. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C. Place butternut squash on a baking tray lined with baking paper. Sprinkle with ½ tsp salt and ½ tsp pepper then drizzle with 1 Tbsp oil. Roast for 30-35 minutes (200°C) until edges are brown and starting to caramelise. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  4. Place onions, 15 g oil and 15 g balamic vinegar in mixing bowl then cook 20 min/Varoma//speed . Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add remaining 15 g balsamic vinegar and, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/Varoma//speed . Transfer to a large bowl and set aside. Line a baking tray with baking paper and clean mixing bowl.
  6. Add eggs and reserved grated Parmesan to cooled quinoa then stir until combined. Transfer quinoa to prepared tray then, using your fingers, shape into a circle approx. (Ø 28 cm). Bake for 15 minutes (200°C) or until light golden then set aside to cool.
  7. Place 100 g goat's cheese and ricotta in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 5. Spread mixture over cooled quinoa base. Top with roasted butternut squash and reserved caramelised onions, then dot with remaining 50 g goat's cheese. Scatter over thyme leaves.
  8. Bake tart for a further 20-25 minutes (200°C) until warmed through.
  9. To finish, drizzle tart with 1 Tbsp vinegar and serve hot or cold in slices.

Useful Items
