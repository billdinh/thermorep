Sweet Potato Crust Pizza

Ingredients

Pizza Bases

  • 350 g dried chickpeas
  • 500 g water
  • 2 - 3 sweet potatoes (approx. 300-350 g), peeled, diced (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ - 1 tsp dried mixed herbs, to taste
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Tomato Sauce

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • ¼ tsp dried oregano
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper
  • 100 g Cheddar cheese, grated
  • 60 g pepperoni, or topping of choice

Preparation

Pizza Bases

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place chickpeas in mixing bowl and grind 2 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water and sweet potatoes in mixing bowl then cook 15-20 min/98°C/speed 0.5 until tender. Drain sweet potatoes in Varoma dish then return to mixing bowl.
  4. Add 300 g reserved chickpea flour, bicarbonate of soda, mixed herbs and salt then mash 10 sec/speed 3 (see tip). Leave bowl to cool below 60°C then knead 2 min/.
  5. Tip dough out onto a floured surface. Divide into 2 pieces and roll or press each out to a circle (approx. Ø 25 cm, 5 mm thick). Place on prepared trays then bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) until edges are slightly browned. Meanwhile, make tomato sauce.

Tomato Sauce

  1. Place garlic, onions and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add tomatoes, tomato purée, oregano, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, simmer 15 min/98°C/speed 1 (see tip). Meanwhile, when bases are cooked, remove from oven and preheat grill to 220°C.
  3. Spread tomato sauce evenly over pizza bases then top with grated Cheddar and pepperoni, or topping of choice. Grill for 3-4 minutes (220°C) until cheese has melted. Serve warm with a green salad.

Useful Items

rolling pin, baking trays, baking paper, oven, grill

Hints & Tricks

  • An alternative gluten-free pizza base that's easy with Thermomix®. In this nutritious option you make your own chickpea flour before mixing it with steamed sweet potatoes to create a simple yet tasty allergy-friendly pizza base. Just bake the bases, add your favourite toppings and grill for a few minutes – it’s that simple!
  • If the mixture looks too dry to form a rollable dough, add a little water after mashing in step 4.
  • Simmer the tomato sauce for longer for a thicker consistency.
  • For a smoother tomato sauce, after simmering in step 7, blend 1 min/speed 5-7, increasing speed gradually.
