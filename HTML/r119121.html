Pea and lettuce soup with scallops

Ingredients

Dill oil

  • 12 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only (approx. 60 g)
  • 200 g grapeseed oil

Soup

  • 2 eschalots
  • 1 leek, white part only, cut into pieces
  • 120 g cos lettuce, cut into pieces
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 50 g unsalted butter
  • salt, to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 500 g water
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 600 g frozen peas
  • 60 g olive oil

Scallops

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 24 scallops, roe removed
  • salt, to season
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season
  • 3 sprigs fresh dill, for garnishing, finely chopped

Preparation

Dill oil

  1. Place dill and oil into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/50°C/speed 6, then blend 1 min/speed 10.
  2. Line simmering basket with two layers of paper towel and place over a bowl. Transfer dill mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain. Discard solids and transfer oil into a sealable jar or container and place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Soup

  1. Place eschalots, leek, lettuce and lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, salt and pepper and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add water and stock paste and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add peas and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add olive oil and blend 2 min/speed 1-10, gradually increasing speed from speed 1 to speed 10.

Scallops

  1. Place a frying pan over high heat and add oil. Season scallops with salt and pepper and, working in batches, place into pan and sear for 1 minute on each side.
  2. Distribute scallops between shallow soup bowls and sprinkle with dill. Pour soup into bowls around scallops (do not submerge scallops) and garnish with dill oil before serving.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, paper towel, bowl, frying pan (19 cm), shallow soup bowl (heat-resistant), refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Dill oil can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
  • This pea and lettuce soup recipe is based on a Raymond Capaldi recipe from my first book, In the Mix: Great Thermomix Recipes. It's thickened with olive oil, rather than cream so it's very smooth, but also quite light.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
