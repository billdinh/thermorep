Pear and Goat's Cheese Flatbread

Ingredients

Dough

  • 15 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 110 g water
  • 10 g fresh yeast
  • 200 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 3 pears (e.g. Conference or Comice)
  • 1 lemon, juice only
  • 225 g goat's cheese, soft (e.g. Chavroux®)
  • 80 g double cream
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 1 - 2 Tbsp halved walnuts (optional)
  • 2 Tbsp runny honey

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil then set aside.
  2. Place water, yeast, flour, oil and salt in mixing bowl then knead 2 min/. Tip out onto a floured surface and bring together into a ball. Place dough in greased bowl, cover with a clean tea towel and leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours).

Topping

  1. Preheat oven to 240°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper (or use 2 smaller ones) then set aside.
  2. Peel pears and slice very thinly using a mandoline or sharp knife. Place in a bowl and mix with lemon juice then set aside.
  3. Place goat's cheese, cream and pepper in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 3.5.
  4. Using a rolling pin, roll rested dough out very thinly (approx. 40 cm x 30 cm) and place on prepared baking tray.
  5. Spread cheese mixture over dough then arrange pear slices on top, overlapping slightly.
  6. Turn oven down to 210°C then bake for 20-25 minutes (210°C) until cooked.
  7. Remove from oven, sprinkle with walnuts and drizzle with honey. Serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, tea towel, baking tray and paper, mandoline or sharp knife, bowl, rolling pin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace honey with cherry jam for a variation.
  • Will serve 6 large portions if served as a starter or lunch. If part of a sharing table or eaten at the same time as the cheese course, will serve 8.
