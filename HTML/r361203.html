New Jersey Tomato Pie

Ingredients

  • 7 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 4 oz warm water (approx. 100°F-110°F)
  • 3 tsp sugar, divided
  • 1 tsp rapid rise yeast
  • 1 ½ tsp salt, divided
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, to brush, plus extra to grease
  • 7 oz mozzarella cheese, cubed
  • 22 oz fresh tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Preparation

  1. Place flour, warm water, 2 tsp sugar, yeast and 1 tsp salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5, then knead 2 min/.
  2. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and roll into a ball. Transfer to a large piece of parchment and roll out dough into a circle (approx. 14 in.) (see Tip). Slide the parchment onto a large baking sheet. Cover with a damp paper towel and let rest at room temperature. Meanwhile, make the sauce.
  3. Preheat oven to 425°F. Lightly grease a pizza pan (14 in.) with olive oil and set aside.
  4. Place mozzarella cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place tomatoes, basil, oregano, 1 tsp sugar and ½ tsp salt into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 25 min/250°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  6. Roll dough onto a lightly dusted rolling pin, then unroll dough onto prepared pizza pan. To create a border: fold over ½ inch of the dough edge and pinch to hold in place. Evenly sprinkle 5 oz of reserved grated cheese over the rested dough and cover with reserved tomato sauce. Sprinkle remaining 2 oz grated cheese over sauce. Brush edges of crust with 1 tbsp olive oil. Bake for 20-25 minutes (425°F) or until Tomato Pie is cooked through and browned to your liking.
  7. Allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, paper towel, rolling pin, large baking sheet, pizza pan (14 in.), pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If dough shrinks after rolling out, let it rest 5-10 minutes covered with a damp paper towel. This will give the dough some time to relax before rolling a second time.
