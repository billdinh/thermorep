Pecan, apple and carrot pudding

Ingredients

Pudding

  • butter, for greasing
  • 80 g pecan nuts, plus extra for garnishing
  • 80 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 130 g red apple, core removed and cut into quarters
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon, plus extra to garnish
  • ½ tsp ground allspice
  • 2 eggs
  • 20 g cornflour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1200 g water

Syrup

  • 70 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 50 g raw sugar
  • ¼ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 40 g butter, cut into cubes
  • 2 star anise

Preparation

Pudding

  1. Grease a ceramic dish (10 cm) with butter and set aside.
  2. Place pecans into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place carrot and 100 g of the apple (approx. ¾) into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved pecans and all remaining pudding ingredients, except water, and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. Transfer pudding mixture into prepared ceramic dish. Thinly slice remaining apple and arrange on top of mixture. Grease a square of aluminium foil and cover ceramic dish. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place water into mixing bowl, place ceramic dish with pudding into Varoma dish and place Varoma into position. Steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. Set aside and keep warm. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

Syrup

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place all syrup ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/90°C/speed 2. Remove star anise and discard.
  2. Pierce top of warm pudding with wooden skewer and pour half of the syrup over. Allow syrup to soak into pudding. Sprinkle with extra cinnamon to taste and serve pudding warm with remaining syrup, garnished with extra pecan nuts if desired.

Useful Items

ceramic dish (10 cm), aluminium foil, wooden skewer

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: distribute mixture between 2 - 3 greased ramekins. Place a double layer of paper towel on top of each ramekin while steaming. Ensure Varoma lid closes properly. Proceed as per recipe.
  • You can substitute pecan nuts with almonds.
  • You can substitute raw sugar for Rapadura sugar.
  • Serve pudding warm with custard, yoghurt, whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.
  • Make your own gluten free baking powder to use in this recipe. Always read the label of your baking powder as some may contain traces or wheat or gluten.
