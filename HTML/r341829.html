Raw pineapple and turmeric cake

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 100 g almonds (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking
  • 200 g raw cashews (see Tips)
  • 5 pitted dates (see Tips)

Cake

  • extra virgin olive oil, for greasing
  • 120 g shredded coconut
  • 30 g flaxseeds (linseeds)
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 30 g hemp seeds (see Tips)
  • 200 g carrots, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 180 g fresh pineapple, peeled and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 cm piece fresh turmeric, peeled (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp psyllium husks
  • 150 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 100 g sultanas

Topping

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tbsp plant-based milk of choice
  • 60 g orange juice
  • 1 pinch salt

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh almonds into it. Add ½ teaspoon of the salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews and dates into it. Add remaining ½ teaspoon of salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  3. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse almonds, then place into a bowl and set aside. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse cashews and dates, then place into a separate bowl and set aside.

Cake

  1. Grease and line a slice tin (18 x 22 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place coconut, flaxseeds (linseeds), orange zest and hemp seeds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add carrot, pineapple, ginger, turmeric and drained almonds and blend 30 sec/speed 7, mixing with aid of spatula.
  4. Add psyllium husks, syrup, cinnamon, cardamom and salt, then stir to combine. Blend 20 sec/speed 6, mixing with aid of spatula.
  5. Add sultanas and stir to combine. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and press firmly into base. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Topping

  1. Place drained cashews and dates into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add remaining topping ingredients and blend 30 sec/speed 7, until smooth and creamy. Spread topping evenly over cake, then place into refrigerator until set (approx. 2 hours). Dust with orange powder just before serving, if desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking paper, bowl, slice tin (18 x 22 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your nuts and dates, soak them in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • Small and crunchy, hemp seeds are derived from the hemp plant. They contain all the amino acids (proteins) essential for the body and also are high in omega-3 and omega-6. These seeds can be great for reducing joint pain, improving gut health and reducing sugar cravings. You can find them at some major supermarkets, health food stores and online.
  • You can replace the fresh turmeric with ½ teaspoon of ground turmeric in this recipe.
  • Orange powder: Preheat oven to 60°C. Place peel of 6 oranges (cut into 4 cm long strips, no white pith) onto a lined baking tray and dehydrate for 3-4 hours (60°C), until peel is dry and crisp. Place peel into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10 or to fine powder. Use as desired or store in a sealable container in the pantry for up to 1 month.
