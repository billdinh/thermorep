Vegan Roulades with Dumplings and Red Cabbage

Ingredients

Vegan Roulade

  • water, for cooking
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade, vegan (see tip)
  • 6 soy steaks, large
  • 6 tsp mustard
  • 80 - 90 g firm tofu, smoked, cut in 6 thin, long slices
  • 2 pickled gherkins, thinly sliced
  • ½ onion, thinly sliced in rings
  • oil, for frying

Vegetable Sauce

  • 230 g red wine, dry, vegan
  • 260 g water
  • 50 g pickled gherkin liquid
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 20 g oil
  • 100 g red onions, quartered
  • 150 g chestnut mushrooms, halved
  • 120 g celery, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g carrots, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 3 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tsp mustard
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade, vegan (see tip)
  • 2 Tbsp agave syrup
  • 5 Tbsp dark soy sauce, vegan
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • 3 juniper berries
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Dumplings

  • 350 g bread rolls, vegan (approx. 4), stale, halved, sliced ​​(1 cm), (see tip)
  • ¾ bunch fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (approx. 15 g)
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g oil
  • 220 g soya milk, unsweetened
  • 60 g water
  • 1 Tbsp soya flour, full fat (see tip)
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 2 Tbsp nutritional yeast flakes (10 g)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg

Red Cabbage

  • 200 g dessert apples, cored, cut in pieces
  • 500 g red cabbage, cored, cut in pieces
  • 60 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g oil
  • 30 g sugar
  • 150 g water
  • 30 g red wine vinegar, vegan
  • 100 g red wine, vegan
  • 1 heaped tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade, vegan (see tip)
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 2 juniper berries
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground allspice

Preparation

Vegan Roulade

  1. Place plenty of water in a large saucepan on hob and bring to the boil. Add vegetable stock and soy steaks then simmer over a low heat for 7-10 minutes until soft. Drain steaks and leave to cool briefly then squeeze and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Brush each steak with 1 tsp mustard. On a long side, over one third of each, lay 1 slice tofu, 2 slices gherkin, and 2 onion rings. Roll up lengthways and secure with a metal skewer or tie with kitchen string.
  3. Heat oil in a large frying pan over a high heat. Place roulades in pan and fry on all sides. Transfer to a cast-iron casserole dish with a lid. Set frying pan with cooking juices aside.

Vegetable Sauce

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place a large jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in wine, water and gherkin liquid then set aside.
  3. Place garlic and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 2 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil, onions, mushrooms, celery and carrots then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 11 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Add flour and, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 2 min/120°C//speed 1.
  6. Add tomato purée and mustard then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 4 min/120°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, add red wine mixture to frying pan with cooking juices. Heat on the hob and simmer briefly.
  7. Add cooked red wine mixture, vegetable stock, syrup, soy sauce, paprika, juniper berries, bay leaf and salt then cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  8. Pour sauce over roulades in casserole dish, close lid and bake for 60 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, make dumplings.

Dumplings

  1. Place 175 g bread in mixing bowl and grate 9 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place remaining 175 g bread in mixing bowl and grate 9 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with breadcrumbs.
  3. Place parsley and onions in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add soya milk and heat 3 min/60°C/speed 1.
  6. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in water. Remove bowl from lid, add soya flour and stir until smooth.
  7. Add soya flour mixture, baking powder, nutritional yeast, salt, pepper, nutmeg and reserved breadcrumbs to mixing bowl then, with aid of spatula, mix 25 sec//speed 3.5 (see tip).
  8. Tip out, knead with hands and shape into 14 dumplings. Divide between Varoma dish and Varoma tray, ensuring some holes remain uncovered for steam to circulate. Insert Varoma tray. Rinse mixing bowl.

Red Cabbage

  1. Place apples in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place cabbage in mixing bowl and, with aid of spatula, chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions and oil in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 2.
  4. Add reserved chopped cabbage and cook 3 min/120°C//speed 2.
  5. Add reserved chopped apples, sugar, water, vinegar, wine, vegetable stock, bay leaf, juniper berries, salt and allspice. Place covered Varoma into position then cook 30 min/Varoma//speed 1. Remove Varoma and set aside.
  6. Transfer roulades to a plate and pass sauce through a sieve if desired. Carefully remove bay leaf and juniper berries from cabbage. Serve vegan roulades with vegetable sauce, dumplings and red cabbage.

Useful Items

large saucepan, paper towels, metal skewers, cast-iron casserole dish, large frying pan, kitchen string, large jug, pastry brush, sieve, hob, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a vegan stock paste, omit the Parmesan from the Vegetable Stock Paste in The Basic Cookbook.
  • If the dumpling dough is too dry in step 18, add 20 g soya milk to mixing bowl and, with aid of spatula, mix 5 sec//speed 3.5.
  • Soya flour is used as a substitute for egg and can be found in health food stores.
  • To serve, vegan rolls can be cut in half vertically and each half sliced (1 cm).
  • Substitute some stale bread rolls with stale vegan dark bread for a richer flavour.
