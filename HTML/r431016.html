Baked Kale Polenta Fries

Ingredients

Kale Pesto

  • 4 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed
  • 1 oz pine nuts
  • 1 lemon, thin peel only, no pith
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 4 oz kale, stems removed
  • 2 oz fresh basil leaves
  • 2 oz extra virgin olive oil

Polenta

  • 36 oz water
  • 10 oz polenta cornmeal
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • non-stick cooking spray
  • marinara sauce, to serve (see Tip)
Nutrition
per 1 piece

Preparation

Kale Pesto

  1. Place Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, lemon peel, garlic, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add kale, basil and olive oil and mix 15 sec/speed 7 with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 10 sec/speed 7 with aid of spatula. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Polenta

  1. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/212°F/speed 3.
  3. Add polenta, half the reserved kale pesto (refrigerate remaining for another use) and salt and without measuring cup cook 5 min/212°F/speed 2.5. Transfer to prepared baking sheet and spread evenly with spatula. Cover with cling wrap, refrigerate until set (approx. 30 min.).
  4. Preheat broiler. Line a baking sheet with foil and lightly spray with non-stick oil.
  5. Cut firm polenta into long fingers (approx. 3 in. x ½ in.). Lightly spray tops of polenta with non-stick oil. Place under broiler for 3-5 minutes each side until crispy. Serve with marinara sauce.

Useful Items

cling wrap, baking sheet, aluminum foil, rimmed baking sheet, parchment paper, refrigerator, oven broiler

Hints & Tricks

  • The polenta can be prepared, refrigerated and cut in advance.
  • To make your own marinara: refer to the Marinara Dipping Sauce recipe in the Pizza collection.
