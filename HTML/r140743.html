White chocolate mousse cups

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 3 eggs
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 100 g self-raising flour

Strawberry purée

  • 400 g frozen strawberries
  • 2 tsp caster sugar

Mousse

  • 160 g coconut milk
  • 1 ½ tsp gelatine powder
  • 250 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 80 g cream cheese
  • 450 g thickened cream

Assembly

  • 200 g fresh strawberries, cut into slices, for garnishing
  • fairy floss (optional), for decorating

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line base and sides of a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar into mixing bowl and whip 3 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 2.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 2. Pour into prepared baking tray and spread evenly (5 mm - 1 cm thickness) to create a smooth surface. Bake for 10-12 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Once cooled, cut out 20 circles using the glass/cup you will use for serving. Set sponge circles aside.

Strawberry purée

  1. Place strawberries and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2, then blend 1 min/speed 6. Transfer into a jug and set aside to cool (approx. 20 minutes). Transfer into refrigerator until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mousse

  1. Place coconut milk and gelatine powder into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/50°C/speed 3 or until gelatine dissolves.
  2. Add chocolate and mix 3 min/speed 3.
  3. Add cream cheese and mix 1 min/speed 3. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside to cool, but not set (approx. 10 minutes).
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl. I nsert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer cream into bowl with cream cheese mixture and gently fold through until combined.

Assembly

  1. Place 1 tablespoon of the mousse into base of a serving glass/cup. Top with 1 teaspoon of the strawberry purée, then a sponge disc. Place 1 teaspoon of the purée over sponge, then top with 1 tablespoon of the mousse and set aside. Repeat layering process with remaining sponge, purée and mousse in remaining serving glasses/cups, reserving some purée for serving.
  2. Transfer cups and leftover purée into refrigerator to set (approx. 2 hours or overnight). Just before serving, decorate with fresh strawberries, drizzle with remaining purée and decorate with fairy floss (optional).

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, 20 small cups or serving glasses, jug, large bowl, bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Sponge and strawberry purée can be made 1 day in advance.
  • Mousse cups can be assembled 1-2 days before serving. Store in refrigerator and garnish with fresh strawberries, drizzle with purée and decorate with fairy floss just before serving.
