Seville Orange Marmalade

Ingredients

  • 750 g water, plus extra for topping up mixture
  • 6 Seville oranges (approx. 900 g)
  • 1 lemon
  • 900 g jam sugar

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position. Place oranges and lemon in Varoma dish then steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Remove Varoma and leave fruit to cool for 20-30 minutes. Retain steaming water in mixing bowl.
  3. When cool enough to handle, cut fruit in half and scoop out the flesh, pips and pith into mixing bowl. Set skins aside to dry out. Replace measuring cup with simmering basket then cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Reduce temperature to 100°C if liquid bubbles up onto the lid.
  4. Strain liquid through simmering basket into a jug, pushing as much liquid and pulp through with spatula. Discard pips and residue left behind. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. When mixing bowl is cool, place orange skins in mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/2-3 times. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  6. Weigh reserved fruit pulp mixture into mixing bowl. Top up to 750 g with water.
  7. Add sugar and reserved chopped peel then place simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid. Cook 12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Cook again 18 min/100°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, towards the end of cooking time, place a saucer in freezer for setting point test.
  8. Test setting point of marmalade (see tip), then leave to cool for 10 minutes. Pour into warm, sterilised jars and seal immediately. Label before storing in a cool, dry place. Once opened, store in fridge.

Useful Items

sterilised jam jars, jam funnel, jug, long sharp knife, chilled saucer, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Setting point test: place a saucer in the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 tsp marmalade onto the saucer. When cool, push marmalade with your finger. If it wrinkles, it is ready. If not, boil for a further 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, then test again.
