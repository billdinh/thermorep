Banana Cream Pie

Ingredients

Pie Crust

  • 4 oz unsalted butter, chilled (cut into ¼ in. pieces), plus extra to grease
  • 2 oz vegetable shortening, chilled (cut into ½ in. pieces)
  • 10 oz all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp ultrafine sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 6 - 8 tbsp iced water

Banana Filling

  • 27 oz banana (approx. 6 bananas) cut in ¼ in. slices, divided
  • 1 oz orange juice (approx. 1 orange)
  • 4 oz unsalted butter, cut into ½ in. pieces, divided
  • 24 oz half and half
  • 5 oz ultrafine sugar
  • 6 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 4 tbsp corn starch
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt

Pie Topping

  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 16 oz heavy whipping cream
  • ¼ tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 ½ oz banana, cut in ¼ in. slices (approx. 1 banana)
  • ½ oz orange juice (approx. ½ orange), to coat bananas and prevent browning

Preparation

Pie Crust

  1. Place butter, shortening, flour, sugar, salt and iced water into mixing bowl and chop 25-30 sec/speed 5. Remove dough from mixing bowl, form into a ball, flatten and wrap in cling wrap. Refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Adjust oven rack to lower middle position. Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease a pie pan (approx. 9 in.) and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Roll out pie dough between two sheets of cling wrap into a circle (approx. 13 in.) with a ⅜ inch thickness. Line prepared pie pan with pie dough and prick dough with a fork to avoid air bubbles. Place in the refrigerator and chill for 15 minutes. Line chilled pie dough with parchment paper and baking weights (e.g. dried beans) and blind-bake for 30 minutes (375°F) until pie dough looks dry and lightly golden. Remove paper and weights and bake a further 10-12 minutes (375°F) or until golden brown. Transfer pie pan to a wire rack and cool completely (approx. 1 hr.). Meanwhile, make banana filling.

Banana Filling

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in 9 oz banana slices. Weigh in orange juice and gently toss with bananas and set aside.
  2. Place 18 oz banana slices and 2 oz butter into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/212°F//speed .
  3. Add half and half and cook 4 min/185°F//speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 15 minutes). Strain banana from half and half through a fine mesh sieve into a bowl (do not press on banana). Discard banana (see Tip) and set aside cooked half and half (approx. 21 oz).
  4. Place sugar, egg yolks, corn starch, remaining 2 oz of butter, vanilla, salt and reserved cooked half and half into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/185°F/speed 4. Transfer half banana filling to bottom of cooled pie crust and spread out evenly. Place reserved banana slices on banana filling and top with remaining banana filling. Allow to cool slightly. Cover with cling wrap pressing directly onto the surface of the filling to avoid a "skin" from forming. Refrigerate while making pie topping. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pie Topping

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add whipping cream and vanilla into mixing bowl and without setting a time, whip at speed 3 until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Place whipped cream on center of banana pie and spread out with spatula. Use the back of a spoon to make attractive swirls and peaks, then cover carefully with cling wrap and refrigerate 2-5 hours or until set.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh in banana slices. Weigh in orange juice and gently toss with bananas. Garnish pie with banana slices and serve immediately.

Useful Items

rolling pin, cling wrap, parchment paper, baking weights, bowl, fine mesh sieve, wire rack, pie pan (approx. 9 in.), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Blind baking is simply baking your unbaked pie crust before adding the filling so that the bottom doesn't puff and the sides don't slouch. You can buy special pie weights for this job, but you can also just use dry beans (approx. 24 oz).
  • Handle dough as little as possible to keep it chilled and easy to work with. Work quickly with cold ingredients.
  • If dough becomes too soft to work with chill until firm.
  • Discarded banana can be used in a milkshake or smoothie.
