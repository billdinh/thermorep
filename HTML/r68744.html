Herb and garlic pull-apart bread

Ingredients

Herb butter

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 5 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 6 fresh basil leaves
  • 600 g pure cream
  • 500 g water, chilled

Bread

  • 450 g water, lukewarm
  • 1 tbsp dried instant yeast
  • butter, for greasing
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 750 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g olive oil

Preparation

Herb butter

  1. Place garlic clove, fresh dill, flat-leaf parsley and basil leaves into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add pure cream into mixing bowl and whip 1-3 min/speed 4 until solids and liquids have separated. Remove butterfly whisk. Using the simmering basket, strain liquid from solids, reserving buttermilk for later use, if desired.
  3. Place water and butter solids back into mixing bowl and mix 5-10 sec/speed 4. Liquids and solids should again separate.
  4. Drain solids through simmering basket again so that only herb butter remains. Squeeze as much liquid out of the butter as possible then place into the refrigerator until ready to use.

Bread

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease and line the bottom of a springform cake tin (24 cm) and place on to a baking tray lined with baking paper. Set aside.
  2. Add salt, baker's flour and olive oil and mix 6 sec/speed 6, then knead  2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto ThermoMat or floured work surface and work into a ball. Wrap dough in ThermoMat or place into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap or kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes). Preheat oven to 180°C.
  4. Knock down dough then roll into a large rectangle (5 mm thick). Dust with a little flour to prevent butter from sliding. Spread herb butter over dough. Using a butter knife or spatula, cut dough into strips (3 - 4 cm wide).Fold each strip into a concertina pattern and place cut side up into prepared cake tin until all strips are pressed together. Leave to prove for further 10 minutes.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) until golden. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes then transfer to a wire rack. Serve warm or allow to cool completely before transferring to an airtight container until ready to use.

Useful Items

plastic wrap or kitchen towel, wire rack, ThermoMat, springform cake tin (24 cm), airtight container, baking tray, baking paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Herb and garlic pull-apart is an ideal bread to share at your next barbecue or picnic.
  • For a stronger garlic flavour, add an additional garlic clove to your herb butter.
  • You can bake your bread in a 24 cm cake tin. Grease and line the tin with baking paper to make your bread easy to remove after baking.
