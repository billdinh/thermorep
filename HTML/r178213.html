Peking-style duck pancakes

Ingredients

Peking-style duck

  • 2 duck breasts, skin on, trimmed of excess fat and sinew
  • 3 - 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 8 sprigs fresh coriander, stalks only
  • ¼ tsp Chinese five spice
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Plum sauce

  • 2 fresh plums, cut into quarters
  • 60 g honey
  • 2 star anise
  • ¼ tsp Chinese five spice
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 50 g water

Pancakes

  • 3 eggs
  • 200 g warm water
  • 90 g tapioca starch
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp coconut flour
  • coconut oil, for frying
  • 6 - 8 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into slices
  • 1 - 2 Lebanese cucumber, cut into batons

Preparation

Peking-style duck

  1. Place duck into a casserole dish and set aside.
  2. Place remaining duck ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Rub mixture over duck, cover with plastic wrap and marinate for 8 hours or overnight.

Plum sauce

  1. Place all plum sauce ingredients into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/100°C/speed 2 or until plums just start to break down. Transfer into a jug and set aside to cool completely.

Pancakes

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, water, tapioca starch, sesame oil and coconut flour into mixing bowl and whisk 5 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add a little coconut oil. Pour 2 tablespoons of the batter into frying pan, tilting the pan so the mixture spreads into a thin, crêpe-like pancake. Fry for 1 minute or until light, golden brown spots appear on the bottom. Flip pancake and fry for 30 seconds. Transfer onto a plate and cover with kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter (makes 12 pancakes total).
  3. Scrape excess marinade from duck breast and place skin side down into a cold frying pan. Cook over medium heat for 12-15 minutes, allowing as much fat as possible to render and the skin is golden brown and crisp. Turn duck and cook for 3-6 minutes, until cooked to your liking. Remove from heat and allow to rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  4. Serve duck and pancakes with spring onions/shallots, cucumber and reserved plum sauce.
