Red mullet couscous with peppers

Ingredients

  • 450 g red peppers, cut into large pieces
  • 280 g yellow peppers, cut into large pieces
  • 220 g green peppers, cut into large pieces
  • 250 g liquid fish stock
  • 30 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed (1 lemon)
  • 300 g couscous
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 pinch salt, adjust to taste
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 350 g water
  • 600 g red mullet fillets (approx. 18-20 fillets) (see tip)
  • 15 g fresh coriander, finely chopped

Preparation

  1. Insert simmering basket, weigh in 200 g red pepper and set aside. Place remaining red, yellow and green peppers into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and transfer to Varoma dish.
  2. Place fish stock and lemon juice into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, place Varoma into position and cook 12 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, place couscous, olive oil, salt and pepper into a bowl. Rub couscous in a circular motion between your hands to moisten grains evenly and remove any lumps that may form, cover with water and stir using spatula to moisten the couscous evenly. Cover and allow to soak for 5 minutes. Gently fluff couscous with a fork to separate the grains and transfer to Varoma tray.
  3. Remove Varoma and set aside. Add chopped peppers to couscous in Varoma tray and mix. Remove simmering basket with cooked red peppers and set aside.
  4. Place red mullet fillets into Varoma dish, making sure some holes remain unobstructed. Insert reserved Varoma tray, place Varoma dish and tray back into position and steam 7 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and set aside.
  5. Transfer fish stock to a jug. Place 150 g fish stock back into mixing bowl and add cooked red peppers of simmering basket. Blend 30 sec/speed 7. Transfer couscous and red mullet fillets to individual plates. Cover with red pepper sauce and sprinkle with fresh coriander before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe can be made with any type of thin white fish fillets, cut into pieces of approx. 6-8 cm.
