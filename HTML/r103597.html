Panna cotta with rhubarb topping (George Calombaris)

Ingredients

Panna cotta

  • oil, for greasing
  • 410 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 160 g full cream milk
  • 50 g sugar
  • ¼ tsp mastic gum (see Tips)
  • 350 g chilled water
  • 3 titanium strength gelatine leaves

Rhubarb topping

  • 6 stalks rhubarb (approx. 500 g), trimmed and cut into pieces (7 cm)
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 70 g honey
  • 70 g water

Preparation

Panna cotta

  1. Lightly grease 6 dariole moulds (6 cm) with oil and set aside.
  2. Place cream, milk, sugar and mastic into mixing bowl and cook 15 min/90°C/speed 2.
  3. Place a large ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it. Place gelatine leaves into water and leave to soak until swelled and softened (7-10 minutes).
  4. Squeeze water from gelatine leaves, then add gelatine leaves into mixing bowl and mix 40 sec//speed 3. Discard soaking water. Strain mixture through a nut milk bag or fine-mesh sieve into a large jug. Divide mixture between prepared dariole moulds, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to set overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Rhubarb topping

  1. Place all rhubarb topping ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 10-12 min/90°C//speed  or until soft and tender. Place simmering basket over a bowl, and carefully drain liquid from cooked rhubarb through simmering basket. Reserve cooking liquid. Transfer rhubarb into a bowl and leave to cool to room temperature.
  2. Place rhubarb cooking liquid back into mixing bowl and cook 15-20 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer syrup into a bowl and cool to room temperature.
  3. Carefully turn out panna cottas onto serving plates. Drizzle with cooled syrup and serve with reserved rhubarb.

Useful Items

6 dariole moulds, large ceramic or glass bowl, plastic wrap, nut bag or fine mesh sieve, jug, large bowl, bowl, serving plates, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

Since the introduction of the hugely popular MasterChef® Australia in 2009, George Calombaris has become a household name. He opened his first restaurant in Melbourne - The Press Club - in 2006, with three more to follow in 2008.

"I've used Thermomix® for years, both at home and in my restaurants. The precise temperature and speed settings allow me to be in complete control, even when I'm creating delicate mousses and emulsions. As a chef I'm always striving for perfection; the Thermomix® allows me to get very close."

This recipe is a fusion of the panna cotta recipe from George's Press Club book and rhubarb topping from Your place or Mine, his book with MasterChef® Australia co-host Gary Mehigan.

  • Originating in Greece, mastic is an edible plant resin that can be used as a stabiliser. You can find mastic in specialty food stores, health food stores or online.
