Raspberry-infused vinegar

Ingredients

  • 375 g fresh raspberries, washed and dried
  • 400 g red wine vinegar (see Tips)
  • 500 g balsamic vinegar
  • 180 g brown sugar

Preparation

  1. Place raspberries, red wine vinegar and balsamic vinegar into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a glass bowl or large jug, cover with plastic wrap, then place into refrigerator for 3 days.
  2. Using a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth, strain mixture into a glass bowl, allowing it to drip into bowl without pressing mixture.
  3. Place strained liquid and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 60 min/100Â°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid (see Tips).
  4. Carefully transfer into sterilised glass bottles, then set aside to cool. Seal well and store in a cool, dry place or wrap up to give as a gift (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, sterilised glass bottles, glass bowl or large jug, large glass bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Using good-quality vinegars is important to the outcome and quality of the finished product.
  • This recipe creates strong vapours. We recommend that you cook in a well-ventilated area by opening a window or using an extraction fan.
  • Use this vinegar to make a vinaigrette or dressing for salads, for dipping with olive oil and sourdough bread or drizzle over fresh fruit or ice cream.
  • You can store vinegar for up to 1 month in a cool, dark place or in the refrigerator.
