Salmon en Croute with Lemon Risotto

Ingredients

Puff Pastry with Salmon Cream

  • 275 g puff pastry, ready-rolled (see tip)
  • 30 g onion, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 10 g unsalted butter
  • 50 g cream, min. 30% fat (see tip)
  • 250 g cream cheese with herbs
  • 10 - 20 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed, to taste (optional)
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 200 g fresh salmon fillets, thin, cut in bite-sized pieces
  • 150 g smoked salmon, cut in bite-sized pieces
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg, lightly beaten, for glazing

Lemon Risotto

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g olive oil
  • 40 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 320 g risotto rice (e.g. carnaroli, Arborio), 10-15 minutes cooking time (see tip)
  • 40 g dry white wine
  • 740 g water
  • 100 g lemon juice
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 lemons, finely grated zest only
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Puff Pastry with Salmon Cream

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and remove puff pastry from fridge.
  2. Place onion and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add cream, cream cheese, lemon juice (if using), dill, salt and pepper then mix 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add fresh salmon and smoked salmon then stir 10 sec//speed 2.5. Unroll puff pastry with its baking paper on a baking tray. Transfer salmon filling to one long side of the pastry, leaving a 1 cm border, then fold pastry over filling. Press edges to seal. Use a knife to cut lines on top of the pastry 3 cm apart, then brush all over with beaten egg yolk (see tip). Bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) until golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make risotto.

Lemon Risotto

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and 20 g butter then sauté 4 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add rice and, without measuring cup, sauté 3 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Add wine and, without measuring cup, cook 1 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add water, lemon juice, vegetable stock and salt then scrape base of mixing bowl with spatula to loosen rice. With simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 12-13 min/100°C//speed 1 until rice is cooked.
  7. Add remaining 20 g butter, 40 g reserved grated Parmesan, lemon zest and pepper then stir with spatula. Leave risotto to rest in mixing bowl for 1 minute. Transfer to a serving bowl, sprinkle with remaining grated Parmesan. Serve immediately with salmon puff pastry.

Useful Items

baking tray, pastry brush, serving bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The recipe uses ready-made puff pastry for convenience, but you can easily make your own quick puff in your Thermomix®. Find the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
  • Double cream with 48% fat or whipping cream with 35% fat are both suitable for this recipe.
  • If time allows, return the salmon in puff pastry to the fridge for 30 minutes before baking. This will ensure an even bake.
  • The salmon in puff pastry can be made ahead of time and frozen uncooked. Use within 2 weeks.
  • Adjust cooking time in step 11 according to time indicated on rice packet.
  • This pastry log encases a classic, creamy salmon and dill filling. Served with a simple lemon-flavoured risotto, this is an impressive main course for a weekend dinner party.
  • For extra flavour, add ¼ tsp cayenne pepper to the salmon filling in step 4.
