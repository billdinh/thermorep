Alphabet cupcakes

Ingredients

Chocolate cupcakes

  • 250 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 250 g water
  • 50 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 150 g buttermilk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 300 g white sugar
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda

Chocolate buttercream icing

  • 500 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 40 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 240 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 1 tbsp boiling water
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Modelling paste

  • 40 g water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g glucose syrup
  • 15 g glycerine (see Tips)
  • 620 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting (see Tips)
  • natural food colouring of choice
  • 1 ½ tsp tylose (see Tips)

Assembly

  • sugar glue (see Tips)

Preparation

Chocolate cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Line 2 x 12-hole muffin trays with cupcake liners and set aside.
  2. Place butter, water and cocoa powder into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/80°C/speed 2 or until smooth.
  3. Add buttermilk, eggs and vanilla and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add flour, salt, sugar and bicarbonate of soda and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Pour mixture into prepared muffin trays to three-quarters full and bake for 18 minutes (170°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cupcake comes out clean. Allow to cool in the trays for 5 minutes, then transfer onto wire racks to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate buttercream icing

  1. Place sugar, cocoa powder and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Modelling paste

  1. Place water and gelatine into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  2. Add glucose and glycerine and cook 1 min/60°C/speed 3.
  3. Add 600 g of the icing sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 6, then knead 30 sec/. Dust a silicone bread mat or work surface well with icing sugar and transfer paste onto it (it will be heavy and sticky).
  4. To remove any remaining paste in mixing bowl, place remaining 20 g icing sugar into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Add to paste on work surface. Dust hands and silcone bread mat or work surface with enough icing sugar to knead into a ball that is smooth and less sticky. Divide paste into 6 equal portions.
  5. Add a small amount of natural food colouring to each portion of your paste. Knead in colour until there are no streaks. If the paste feels sticky, add more icing sugar as needed (see Tips).
  6. Divide tylose equally between paste portions. Knead each paste portion for 3-5 minutes to fully incorporate tylose and until paste is smooth and crease free. Wrap modelling paste in plastic wrap and store in a snaplock bag (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Roll out each modelling paste portion (3 mm thickness) and set aside a flower-shaped cookie cutter (6-7 cm), a small circle cookie cutter (3-3.5 cm), a small square cookie cutter (3 cm), a larger square cookie cutter (4-5 cm) and alphabet cookie cutters. For each cupcake, cut out a larger shape (flower or square) a smaller shape (circle or square) and a letter (the letters can spell out Happy birthday, your child’s name or a message of choice).
  2. Place a small amount of sugar glue to the back of a smaller cut-out and place onto one of the larger cut-outs (ex: a small circle glued to a flower). Then place a small amount of glue onto the back of a letter and attached to the smaller cut-out. Set aside on a flat surface until firm (approx. 7 hours or overnight).
  3. Transfer reserved icing into a piping bag fitted with a star nozzle. Pipe a ring of icing around the edge of each cupcake. Place a cut-out stack onto each cupcake and press down gently to secure. Serve or place into a sealable storage container until ready to serve (see Tips).

Hints & Tricks

  • Gluten free cupcake variation: For a gluten free cupcake, replace 300 g plain flour with 300 g gluten free plain flour.
  • Gluten free cupcakes can be made up to 4 days in advance.
  • You will need a soft, sifted icing sugar for the Buttercream icing and Modelling paste recipes.
  • Glycerine is an edible product and can be purchased from cake decorating shops or the health and beauty aisle in supermarkets.
  • Tylose is a vegetable gum, available from cake decorating supply stores. When added to fondant it makes a paste firm enough to hold its shape. If modelling paste is not holding it's shape, add a very small amount of additional tylose. Use sparingly, as too much will dry out your paste.
  • If you are not using Buttercream icing immediately, place into the refrigerator until ready to use. Before using, bring back to room temperature or stir until softened enough to pipe.
  • Liquid food colouring may affect the texture of your fondant and modelling paste; add sifted icing sugar as needed. Once your fondant or modelling paste is coloured, try to avoid excess use of icing sugar when rolling or modelling as this will leave white marks. We recommend natural food colouring gel when possible.
  • Always keep modelling paste wrapped in plastic wrap inside a snaplock bag since it dries out quickly. Work quickly with modelling paste and keep it covered while working with it.
  • If your modelling paste cracks and feels dry, very firm or crumbly, knead in a few drops of glycerine or a few drops of cooled boiled water into the paste to soften it and make it more elastic. This will help avoid cracks in the finished product. You can also rub some Copha® (white vegetable shortening) onto your palms and work surface and continue to knead. 
  • Sugar glue can be purchased from cake decorating shops or online.
  • Cupcakes can be made up to 5 days in advance. Place into a sealable container and store in a cool place or in the refrigerator.
