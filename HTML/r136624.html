Salmon en croute

Ingredients

  • 4 sprigs fresh chives
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill, fronds only
  • 30 g brown onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g salted butter
  • 50 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 250 g cream cheese, softened
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp capers, drained (optional)
  • 2 fresh boneless, skinless salmon fillet (approx. 150 g each), cut into pieces (approx. 5 cm)
  • 150 g smoked salmon, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 2 sheets puff pastry
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly beaten, for glazing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place chives, parsley and dill into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 5 minutes.
  5. Add cream, cream cheese, salt, pepper, lemon juice, capers (optional) and reserved herbs and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add fresh and smoked salmon and combine 10 sec//speed 2.5.
  7. Transfer pastry onto a sheet of baking paper (approx. 30 x 50 cm), overlapping pastry sheets by 1 cm and press together to form one sheet. Cut pastry into a 25 x 40 cm rectangle, discarding excess pastry. With long edge closest to you, place salmon filling onto bottom half of long edge, leaving a 1 cm border. Brush edges of pastry with a little of the beaten egg yolk, then fold pastry over to cover filling. Using a fork, press edges together to seal.
  8. Transfer baking paper with pastry onto baking tray. Using a sharp knife, score a few lines on top of pastry, then brush with remaining beaten egg yolk (see Tips). Bake for 25-30 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), bowl, baking paper, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If time allows, place pastry-wrapped salmon into the refrigerator for 30 minutes before baking to allow flavours to develop. You can also freeze the unbaked pastry-wrapped salmon. Use within 2 weeks. Defrost completely in refrigerator before baking (as per step 8).
