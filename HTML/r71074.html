Festive fruit cake

Ingredients

  • 100 g glacé cherries
  • 100 g mixed peel
  • 650 g mixed dried fruit (e.g. raisins, currants, sultanas)
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 220 g butter, cut into pieces and softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith
  • 50 g blanched almonds or nuts of choice (e.g. hazelnuts, pistachios), plus extra for decortaing (optional)
  • 220 g brown sugar
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 tsp instant granulated coffee
  • 1 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp molasses or black treacle
  • 220 g plain flour
  • water, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place Varoma dish into position then weigh glacé cherries, mixed peel and mixed dried fruit into it. Remove Varoma and rinse fruit well, then transfer fruit into a heat resistant bowl and pour enough boiling water over until fruit is well covered. Leave to soak for 3-4 hours.
  3. Once fruit has soaked, drain through Varoma dish and spread onto prepared baking tray. Set fruit aside to dry overnight.
  4. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line a cake tin (21 cm square or 23 cm round) and set aside.
  5. Place lemon and orange zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat grating 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add blanched almonds or nuts of choice and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides or mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Add 220 g butter and brown sugar and mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  9. Add eggs, mixed spice, coffee, cocoa and molasses or treacle and combine 5-10 sec//speed 3, until just combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  10. Add flour and combine 10 sec//speed 5, with aid of spatula.
  11. Transfer cake batter into a large bowl and add reserved fruit and peel mixture. Combine using the spatula, taking care not to overwork.
  12. Place mixture into prepared tin and moisten by lightly sprinkling with water. Decorate with extra blanched almonds or nuts of choice (optional).
  13. Bake for 1 hour (170°C), then reduce oven temperature to 150°C and bake for a further 1½ hours (150°C).
  14. Once cooled, place cake into a muslin cloth and wrap to store until ready to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, heat resistant bowl, square cake tin (21 cm) or round cake tin (23 cm), baking tray (30 x 40 cm), large bowl, muslin cloth, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve Festive fruit cake warm with pouring custard. Try adding a dash of brandy to your custard for festive flair.
  • Festive fruit cake is a great gift. Wrap the cake in decorative plastic wrap and seal with ribbon for friends and family.
  • Try creating a decorative pattern on your cake by cutting out a festive stencil, placing it over the centre of the cake and lightly dusting with icing sugar.
