Quick Lamb Dhansak

Ingredients

  • 1 fresh mint leaves (approx. 5 g)
  • 1 coriander leaves (approx. 5 g)
  • 500 g lamb, diced (2-3 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp garam masala
  • 30 g vegetable oil, plus more for frying
  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200 g butternut squash, diced (2-3 cm)
  • 150 g red split lentils, rinsed
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 500 g water, hot
  • 20 g rogan josh curry paste (or other indian curry paste)
  • 15 g tamarind paste
  • 25 g caster sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 30 g lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Place mint and coriander leaves in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh lamb into bowl, add garam masala and toss together to coat the meat. Heat some vegetable oil in a frying pan and brown the meat in batches. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. Place onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add 30 g oil and squash then cook 8 min/100°C//speed .
  5. Add lentils, stock, water, curry paste, tamarind paste, sugar and reserved lamb then, without measuring cup, cook 30 min/98°C//speed .
  6. Add salt, pepper, reserved fresh herbs and lemon juice then stir in. Adjust seasoning if necessary, then serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with pilau rice.
  • Dhansak is traditionally a slow-cooked dish that can take up to 2 hours to prepare - this is ready in 45 minutes!
