Feijoa chutney

Ingredients

  • 250 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 2 Granny Smith apples (approx. 250 g), cored and cut into quarters
  • 750 g feijoas (approx. 9-10 feijoas), washed, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 200 - 250 g brown sugar (see Tips)
  • 150 g apple cider vinegar (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 pinches salt

Preparation

  1. Place onion and apples into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add feijoas, sugar, vinegar, curry powder and salt and cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook for a further 30 min/95°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Carefully transfer into sterilised sealable jars, allow to cool, then seal tightly. Serve as desired or store in the refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).

sterilised sealable jars

Hints & Tricks

  • Adjust the brown sugar to taste depending on the ripeness of your fruit and degree of sweetness preferred.
  • You can replace the apple cider vinegar with malt vinegar in this recipe, if desired.
  • If using frozen feijoas, defrost completely and drain off any excess moisture before using.
  • This recipe makes a large quantity of chutney (1525 g), so you will need several large sealable jars.
  • Serve as part of a cheese board, in sandwiches, on crackers or as a side to curries and grilled meats.
  • You can store chutney in the refrigerator for up to 2 months.
