Potato Farls

Ingredients

  • 800 g water, for steaming
  • 500 g floury potatoes (e.g. Maris Piper, King Edward or Desiree) peeled, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 6 fresh chives, cut in lengths (2 cm)
  • 50 g butter, plus extra for frying
  • 50 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
Nutrition
per 1 piece

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in potatoes then steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until potatoes are cooked. Remove simmering basket then transfer potatoes to a large plate and allow to cool for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, discard steaming water and rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place chives in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add cooked potatoes, butter, flour, baking powder, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec/speed 4.5. Tip out onto a floured surface, and divide in two. Form each piece into a ball then flatten using a rolling pin until 5 mm thick (Ø 15 cm). Cut the circles into quarters.
  4. Heat a little butter in a non-stick frying pan over a medium heat. Place four pieces in pan and cook for 3 minutes or until golden brown. Flip over and cook for a further 3 minutes. Transfer to a warm plate then cook remaining four pieces. Serve potato farls while still hot.

Useful Items

large plate, rolling pin, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe is a great way to use up leftover mashed potato. Begin at step 3 with cold mashed potatoes.
  • Farl literally means ‘fourths’, as the dough is formed into a circle and then quartered. These Irish potato griddle cakes can be served with breakfast as an alternative to fried bread or hash browns, or they’re equally delicious spread with butter and served as an accompaniment to a bowl of hot soup.
