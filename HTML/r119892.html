Yoghurt soup cups

Ingredients

  • 80 g medium grain rice
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 120 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 800 g water
  • 300 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 egg
  • salt, to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 ½ tbsp unsalted butter
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 200 g after draining)
  • ½ - 1 tbsp dried chilli flakes
  • 3 stalks kale, stalks removed and leaves finely chopped

Preparation

  1. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Rinse rice until water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add onion and chop 1 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add stock paste and water. Insert simmering basket with rice and cook 15 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside.
  5. Add yoghurt then mix 30 sec/speed 3, adding egg through hole in mixing bowl lid, until combined.
  6. Cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 2, until slightly thickened.
  7. Add salt, pepper and cooked rice and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 3. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  8. Place a frying pan (19 cm) over medium-high heat and add butter. Once butter is foaming, add chickpeas, chilli flakes and kale and fry until chickpeas are just starting to crisp (3-5 minutes).
  9. Divide soup between 20 small cups and sprinkle with chickpea mixture to serve.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, frying pan (19 cm), 20 small cups or serving glasses, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can replace kale with silverbeet.
  • This yoghurt soup can also be a sauce for grilled meats or vegetables.
  • You can also serve this as an entrée at a dinner party.
