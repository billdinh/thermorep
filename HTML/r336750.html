Ravioli Stuffed with Artichokes and Pancetta

Ingredients

Artichoke and Pancetta Filling

  • 3 ½ oz carrots, in pieces (1 in.)
  • 3 ½ oz leeks, white part only, in pieces (1 in.)
  • 2 oz pancetta, diced (¼ in.)
  • 10 ½ oz artichoke hearts, packed in water, drained and cut into pieces
  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 oz goat cheese
  • 1 egg yolk, from a large egg

Ravioli

  • 3 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed
  • 1 homemade pasta dough, from Fresh Pasta Homemade
  • all-purpose flour, to dust
  • water, to boil pasta
  • salt, to boil with pasta
  • unsalted butter, to garnish

Preparation

Artichoke and Pancetta Filling

  1. Place carrots and leeks into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add pancetta, artichokes, olive oil and salt and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 15 min/250°F//speed 1. Add goat cheese and egg yolk and blend 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer filling to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Ravioli

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Transfer dough to a well-floured work surface and divide into four parts. Roll each piece of dough with a rolling pin into an elongated shape and feed through the pasta machine while guiding the dough. Run dough through pasta machine with rollers set on most open setting; fold dough into thirds and roll it back through the pasta maker. If dough sticks, coat with a little more flour. Fold and roll the dough three more times on this setting. Adjust rollers to the next level of thinness and guide dough back through the machine with the back of your hand, repeating the process until dough is approximately 1/16 inch thick. Keep remaining dough covered to prevent from drying out. Repeat with remaining dough.
  3. Fold pasta sheet in half lengthwise and press lightly. Unfold dough and drop 1 tsp of filling about 1½ inches apart along half the sheet of dough. Fold the unfilled half over the filling and press around filling to remove excess air and seal. Repeat with remaining dough.
  4. Shape ravioli using a round ravioli cutter or cut into squares with a pasta wheel and transfer to a baking sheet lined with parchment.
  5. Cook ravioli on stove top in plenty of boiling and salted water (approx. 1 tbsp salt per 16 cups water) for 2-4 minutes depending on thickness of dough. Drain and serve warm, garnished with reserved grated Parmesan cheese and butter.

Useful Items

baking sheet and parchment paper, round ravioli cutter, rolling pin, pasta machine, large saucepot (approx. 4-5 qt.)

Hints & Tricks

  • Cut ravioli using a square ravioli stamp or with a pasta wheel cutter.
