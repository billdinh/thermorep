Elderflower Cordial

Ingredients

  • 50 g citric acid
  • 1750 g granulated sugar
  • 1500 g water
  • 4 lemons, cut in thin slices
  • 25 elderflowers, large heads in full bloom
  • sparkling mineral water, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh in citric acid then set bowl aside.
  2. Place sugar and 1000 g water in mixing bowl. Heat 15 min/100°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, place lemon slices evenly over the base of a large glass bowl or deep stainless steel pan.
  3. Place elderflower heads on lemon slices with flowers down, stems up, then sprinkle reserved citric acid over them. Pour in half of the hot syrup.
  4. Add remaining 500 g water to syrup in mixing bowl, mix 10 sec/speed 2 then pour over the flower heads and lemons. Cover and leave at room temperature for at least 10 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain cordial through a fine sieve into sterilised glass bottles and cover with sterilised lids. When completely cool, store in the fridge. To serve, dilute 30-50 g cordial per person with 200 g sparkling water.

Useful Items

large glass bowl or deep stainless steel pan, sterilised glass bottles, fine sieve, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Elderberry plants are common all over the UK and Ireland. As these are wildflowers, shake the heads to remove dirt and bugs before using.
  • Citric acid is used to preserve the cordial, and can be purchased from pharmacies or shops stocking Asian ingredients.
  • Only use a glass bowl or stainless steel pan for the infusing.
  • Undiluted cordial will keep for several months in the fridge.
  • The cordial also works well poured over ice cream or drizzled over a plain sponge cake.
  • Refreshing and fragrant, homemade elderflower cordial is so much better and more pleasing than anything you can buy in the shops. The elderflower season is short, and falls in early summer, but makes a great excuse for long walks spent collecting flowers.
