Vegan butter

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 60 g raw cashews (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking
  • 3 tsp lecithin granules (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp boiling water

Butter

  • 100 g coconut oil (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp apple cider vinegar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 140 g filtered water
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pinch ground turmeric

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews into it. Add salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse cashews.
  3. Place lecithin granules and boiling water into a small bowl and set aside for 30 minutes.

Butter

  1. Place coconut oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add all remaining ingredients, including drained cashews and soaked lecithin granules, and blend 1 min/speed 10 or until completely smooth. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator to set (approx. 2 hours). Serve as desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, small bowl, sealable container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your cashews, soak nuts in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • We prefer to use refined coconut oil as it has a milder flavour. However, both refined and unrefined coconut oil can be used in this recipe.
  • Lecithin granules have a subtle, nutty flavour and are versatile enough to add a creamy texture (when soaked) or a crunchy texture (when sprinkled) to dishes. They may also lower bad cholesterol levels, improve heart health and improve digestion. You can find them at some major supermarkets, chemists and health food stores.
  • You can store this butter in the refrigerator for 1 week or the freezer for 1 month.
