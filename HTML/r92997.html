Irish Soda Bread

Ingredients

  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 250 g wholemeal flour
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 450 g buttermilk
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
521 kJ / 125 kcal
Protein
4.8 g
Carbohydrates
24.9 g

0.6 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Dust a baking tray with flour then set aside.
  2. Place plain flour, wholemeal flour, salt and bicarbonate of soda in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add buttermilk and mix 25 sec/speed 5.5. Working quickly, tip the sticky dough out onto a floured surface and bring together into a ball. Place on prepared tray.
  4. Dust loaf with flour then, with a floured sharp knife cut a cross in the top of dough about 3 cm deep. Bake for 25-35 minutes (200°C) until loaf sounds hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer to a wire rack to cool before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray, long sharp knife, wire rack, oven, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This tasty, slightly sweet, crumbly cake-like bread is really quick to make, thanks to the baking soda which is used as a leavening agent instead of yeast. There are several variations of soda bread to be found throughout different regions of Ireland - we’ve gone for a mixture of plain and wholemeal flours. A perfect accompaniment to a big bowl of steaming soup or simply enjoyed buttered with jam or lemon curd.
