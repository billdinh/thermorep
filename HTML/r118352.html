Spinach and lemon cream cake

Ingredients

Spinach cake

  • 500 g frozen chopped spinach
  • butter, for greasing
  • 130 g grapeseed oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 180 g caster sugar
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 240 g gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 50 g arrowroot flour
  • 2 ½ tsp gluten free baking powder (see Tips)

Lemon cream

  • 1 tbsp gelatine powder
  • 1 ½ lemons, zest cut into 5 strips (1 x 4 cm), no white pith and juice (approx. 60 g)
  • 400 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 80 g pure icing sugar
  • 1 pomegranate, arils only, for decorating

Preparation

Spinach cake

  1. Place simmering basket into a bowl and place onto mixing bowl lid. Weigh frozen spinach into simmering basket. Set aside to defrost and drain (approx. 2 hours). Squeeze out any excess moisture.
  2. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a springform cake tin (24 cm) and line base with baking paper. Set aside.
  3. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Set aside.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 2 min/speed 4.
  5. Mix 50 sec/speed 3, slowly pouring oil through hole in mixing bowl lid. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Add vanilla and drained spinach and combine 25 sec//speed 2.
  7. Add flour mix, arrowroot and baking powder and combine 30 sec//speed 2.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and combine 20 sec//speed 2.
  9. Transfer into prepared cake tin and bake for 40-45 minutes (170°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow cake to cool completely in tin, then turn out onto a wire rack. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  10. Using a sharp knife, gently remove the top layer of cake (5 mm). Place top layer of cake (approx. 350 g) into mixing bowl and mix 4 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lemon cream

  1. Place gelatine and 3 tablespoons of the lemon juice into a bowl. Leave gelatine to soak for 5 minutes, until a sponge consistency is achieved.
  2. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat grating 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place gelatine mixture and 50 g of the cream into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/50°C/speed 2, or until gelatine has melted.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Add sugar, reserved lemon zest and remaining 350 g cream into mixing bowl and whip 30 sec/speed 3.5, or until thickened, watching cream carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping.
  6. Spread cream over the top of cake, then sprinkle with reserved cake crumb mixture. Transfer into refrigerator to set (approx. 30 minutes). Garnish with pomegranate arils before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, springform cake tin (24 cm), baking paper, jug, wooden skewer, wire rack, serving platter, oven, refrigerator
