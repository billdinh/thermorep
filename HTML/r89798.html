Caribbean stew and Steamed rice cups

Ingredients

Caribbean stew

  • 800 g lamb shoulder meat, boneless (see variation), cut in pieces (3-4 cm),
  • 50 g orange juice, freshly squeezed (1 orange)
  • 20 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed (½ lemon)
  • 60 g Worcestershire sauce
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 150 g onions, cut in halves
  • 180 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 2 - 4 sprigs fresh coriander
  • 2 - 3 pinches dried oregano
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 - 1 ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 25 g oil
  • 20 g raw cane sugar
  • 50 g tomato purée (concentrated)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 450 g water

Steamed rice cups

  • 160 g basmati rice
  • 400 g water
  • 4 pinches salt
  • 4 tsp oil

Preparation

Caribbean stew

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh lamb, orange juice, lemon juice and Worcestershire sauce into it and set aside. Place garlic cloves, onions, celery, coriander, oregano, bay leaves and cayenne pepper in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to bowl with meat and stir to coat meat, then cover and allow to marinate 8-24 hours in refrigerator.
  2. After marinating lamb, place oil and raw cane sugar in mixing bowl and cook 7 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add tomato purée and cook 3 min/120°C/speed 1, then add reserved meat and marinade, salt and water. Set Varoma dish in position.

Steamed rice cups

  1. Arrange 4 heat-resistant tea cups or small mugs (Ø 8 cm, height 9 cm) in Varoma dish. Place in each cup 40 g rice, 100 g water, 1 pinch salt and 1 tsp oil. Close Varoma and cook 35 min/Varoma//speed . To serve, invert one rice cup on each plate, giving a firm shake downwards to unmould the rice, and serve hot with the Caribbean stew.

Useful Items

heat-resistant tea cups or small mugs (Ø 8 cm, height 9 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The rich flavours of this lamb stew are enhanced by the overnight marinade with citrus juices, spices and herbs. Steaming rice in cups above the stew allows you to save time and also to present the rice in an elegant moulded shape. The original Caribbean recipe, a classic from the Dominican Republic, is traditionally made with goat meat - try ordering goat at your butcher’s for a slightly more gamey flavour in the stew.
  • For a more traditional, richer flavour, use bone-in meat, increasing the amount of meat to 1000 g.
  • Caribbean goat meat stew: replace lamb meat with 1000 g bone-in goat shoulder, cut in 5-6 cm pieces. In step 4, cook the stew 90 min/95°C//speed  before setting the covered Varoma with rice cups on mixing bowl lid – then cook a further 30 min/Varoma//speed .
