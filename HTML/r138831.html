Six-layer cake

Ingredients

White chocolate ganache

  • 330 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 900 g white chocolate, broken into pieces

Cakes

  • 300 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 1020 g plain flour
  • 810 g sugar
  • 3 tbsp baking powder
  • 3 pinches salt
  • 750 g full cream milk
  • 3 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 6 eggs

Buttercream icing

  • 500 g icing sugar (see Tips)
  • 240 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 1 tbsp boiling water
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Preparation

White chocolate ganache

  1. Place cream into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Add chocolate and melt 4 min/50°C/speed 2 or until smooth. Transfer into a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until thick and spreadable (approx. 4 hours). Ganache is ready when it's thick enough to hold its shape, but still pliable. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Continue with cakes.

Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line 2 round cake tins (18-20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place 340 g of the flour, 270 g of the sugar, 1 tablespoon of the baking powder, 1 pinch of the salt, 100 g of the butter, 250 g of the milk and 1 teaspoon of the vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add 2 of the eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5.
  5. Divide evenly between prepared cake tins and bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Clean and dry cake tins. Grease and line cake tins again. Place 340 g of the flour, 270 g of the sugar, 1 tablespoon of the baking powder, 1 pinch of the salt, 100 g of the butter, 250 g of the milk and 1 teaspoon of the vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  7. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  8. Add 2 of the eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5.
  9. Divide evenly between prepared cake tins and bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  10. Clean and dry cake tins. Grease and line cake tins again. Place remaining 340 g flour, 270 g sugar, 1 tablespoon baking powder, 1 pinch salt, 100 g butter, 250 g milk and 1 teaspoon vanilla into mixing bowl and beat 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  11. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  12. Add remaining 2 eggs and beat 3 min/speed 3.5.
  13. Divide evenly between prepared cake tins and bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Buttercream icing

  1. Place icing sugar and butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add boiling water and vanilla and mix 2 min/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer into a bowl and set aside until ready to use (see Tips).

Assembly

  1. Trim top of each cooled cake to create a level surface. Place some Buttercream icing (approx. 2 tablespoons) onto the centre of a cake board and place 1 cake onto board and secure. Spread cake with a layer of reserved ganache (3-4 tablespoons). Top with another layer of cake (see Tips). Continue layering cakes with ganache (approx. 3-4 tablespoons). Use remaining ganache to lightly ice the top and sides of the cake. Insert 3 dowl sticks (approx. 25 cm each) into different sections of the cake to stabilise, then carefully smooth surface using a palette knife. Transfer cake into refrigerator to set until ganache is firm to touch (approx. 20 minutes).
  2. Using a palette knife, spread Buttercream icing over top and sides of cake, then decorate as desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

plastic wrap, bowl, 2 round cake tins (20 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, cake board or serving plate, dowl sticks, palette knife, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using a soft, sifted icing sugar for the Buttercream icing recipe.
  • If you are not using icing immediately, place into refrigerator until ready to use (can be stored for up to 1 week). Before using, bring the icing back to room temperature or stir until soft enough to spread.
  • You can use a small spirit level to ensure your cake is level. As you build your cake, after every level, check and adjust accordingly using ganache to get a straight and level cake.
  • We used half a batch of our Simple butter biscuit recipe to make the biscuits used for decoration on our cake. We also used a batch of our Royal icing to decorate biscuits. Please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Get creative with your cake! You can make the cakes any flavour you like and you can make the Buttercream icing into any colour (or flavour) you like. Decorate your cake with biscuits, lollies, fondant decorations, edible flowers, etc. Use your imagination to create cakes for any occasion!
