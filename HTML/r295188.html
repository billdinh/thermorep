Blueberry Buns

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 110 g sugar
  • 100 g milk
  • 370 g strong white bread flour
  • 50 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Filling and Assembly

  • 250 g fresh blueberries
  • 70 g sugar
  • 30 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 medium egg, lightly beaten, for brushing

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place eggs, yeast, sugar and milk in mixing bowl then warm 3 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour, butter, vanilla and salt then knead 3 min/. Transfer dough to a large bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2 hours). Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Towards end of rising time, line 2 baking trays (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm) with baking paper and continue with recipe.

Filling and Assembly

  1. Place blueberries, sugar and flour in mixing bowl then mix 2 sec//speed 2.
  2. Transfer risen dough to a floured work surface. Roll out to a rectangle (approx. 30 cm x 45 cm x 5 mm) and cut into 16 equally-sized rectangles.
  3. Spoon an equal amount of blueberry filling into the centre of each dough rectangle. Working with 1 rectangle at a time, pinch all 4 corners together into the middle to encase the filling. Seal well, then lightly roll between hands to shape into round buns. Place buns seam-side down on prepared baking trays leaving space for each bun to rise during baking. Leave buns to rise for 1 hour.
  4. Towards end of this time, preheat oven to 160°C.
  5. Brush egg evenly over buns. Bake for 25 minutes (160°C) or until lightly golden. Transfer to a wire rack and leave to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

baking trays (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm), baking paper, cling film, pastry brush, wire rack, rolling pin, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These buns are best eaten the day they are made.
  • Substitute blueberries with stoned peaches or peeled and deseeded apples, cut into pieces (1 cm).
