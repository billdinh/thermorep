Layered Chicken Dinner

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 1 chicken, whole (1.2-1.4 kg)
  • 1 lemon, whole, peeled (reserving zest in strips)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 Tbsp coarse sea salt

Butternut Squash Soup

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 120 g leeks, cut in pieces
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 700 g butternut squash, peeled, cut in pieces (4 cm)
  • 950 g water
  • 230 g potatoes, unpeeled, cut in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 170 g carrots, cut in pieces (2-3 cm)

Red Wine Gravy

  • 10 g olive oil
  • 300 g shallots, halved
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 1 - 2 chicken stock cubes (for 0.5 l each), crumbled, to taste
  • 120 g red wine
  • 250 g water
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 100 g broccoli, cut in large florets
  • 100 g green beans, trimmed

Preparation

Chicken

  1. Fill chicken cavity with lemon, garlic and rosemary then place whole chicken in Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Place reserved lemon zest and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add paprika and coarse salt then grind 10 sec/speed 9. Rub mixture evenly over chicken, cover Varoma then set aside.

Butternut Squash Soup

  1. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place garlic, leeks and oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add butternut squash and water. Place covered Varoma into position and cook 55 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Carefully remove Varoma lid then weigh potatoes and carrots into Varoma dish, arranging them around the chicken, leaving some holes unobstructed for steam to circulate. Cover Varoma and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Check chicken is cooked (see tip). If not, prolong cooking time as necessary, setting vegetables aside if cooked and covering to keep warm. Once chicken is cooked, remove Varoma and transfer chicken, potatoes and carrots to a serving platter then cover with foil to keep warm.
  4. Insert measuring cup and blend 30 sec/speed 5-9, increasing speed gradually. Transfer soup to a large bowl and cover with foil to keep warm. Clean mixing bowl.

Red Wine Gravy

  1. Place oil, shallots and butter in mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, sauté 7 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add rosemary, chicken stock, wine and water then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, reduce 8 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add cornflour and pepper to mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in broccoli and green beans. Cover Varoma and steam 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and transfer broccoli and green beans to serving platter with chicken. Cover to keep warm then strain gravy through simmering basket into a serving jug. Discard simmering basket contents.
  4. Serve butternut squash soup as a starter, followed by chicken, vegetables and gravy.

Useful Items

jug, serving platter, aluminium foil

Hints & Tricks

  • Depending on the size of the chicken, you may have to adjust cooking times slightly. To check your chicken is cooked, pierce the thickest part of the leg near the chest. If the juices run clear, it is cooked.
  • Change the vegetables to suit your taste and adjust cooking times as required for firmer or softer vegetables.
  • This impressive recipe produces a two-course chicken dinner in under two hours. First, a tasty butternut squash soup cooks as a whole chicken steams in the Varoma, guaranteeing incredibly tender and moist meat. Steamed fresh vegetables and a flavourful red wine gravy complete the main course, resulting in a delicious and simple dinner for six.
