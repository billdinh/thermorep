Haloumi

Ingredients

  • 2000 g full cream milk
  • 4 junket tablets (see Tips)
  • 250 g filtered water, plus an extra 1 tablespoon
  • 20 g non-iodised salt (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Place junket tablets and 1 tablespoon of the water into a small bowl and stir to dissolve.
  3. Add junket mixture into mixing bowl and combine 1 min/speed 1. Transfer milk into a warmed thermal serving bowl or warmed large ceramic bowl (see Tips), then cover and set aside for 1 hour.
  4. Leave curds in warmed bowl and cut curds into strips (2 cm) both ways at a 45° angle (into diamond shapes). Allow to sit for another 10 minutes to develop the curds and whey.
  5. Place curds and whey into mixing bowl and heat 30 min/37°C/speed .
  6. Place simmering basket over a jug and line with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth. Transfer curds and whey into lined simmering basket. Cover curds and whey with top of nut milk bag or excess cloth, then place a heavy weight on top of curds and whey. Set aside for 30 minutes, allowing whey to drain (do not discard whey).
  7. Set simmering basket with curds aside, transfer whey into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  8. Remove curds and cut into slices (1 cm thickness). Place curd pieces back into unlined simmering basket. Insert simmering basket with curds and cook 30 min/90°C/speed . Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside to cool. Transfer whey into a jug and set aside.
  9. Place water and salt into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  10. Add 250 g of the reserved whey and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Set aside to cool.
  11. Transfer cooled haloumi and cooled brine into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

jug, thermal serving bowl or large ceramic bowl, sealable container, small bowl, nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find junket tablets at most major supermarkets and online. You can find non-iodised salt at most major supermarkets.
  • To warm a thermal serving bowl or ceramic bowl, fill with hot water, let stand for a few minutes, then discard water and dry bowl.
  • You can store haloumi in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, however it will become saltier the longer it sits in the brine.
  • Serve haloumi in salads, burgers or simply pan-fried on its own. You can find more recipes using haloumi on Cookidoo®.
