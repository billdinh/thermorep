Cherry Pie with Pistachio Pastry

Ingredients

Pistachio Puff Pastry

  • 50 g pistachio nuts, unsalted, shelled
  • 200 g unsalted butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 175 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 6 Tbsp water, cold

Cherry Filling

  • 650 g tinned pitted black cherries, in syrup, drained
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 25 g tapioca starch
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • ¼ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten, for glazing

Preparation

Pistachio Puff Pastry

  1. Place pistachio nuts in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Remove approx. half to a bowl and set aside. Grind remaining nuts in mixing bowl 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, flour, salt and cold water then mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 6. Tip out and bring together into a flattened ball, then wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, make filling.

Cherry Filling

  1. Place cherries, sugar, tapioca starch, lemon juice and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C//speed . Remove mixing bowl and leave to cool.

Assembly

  1. Once pastry has rested, roll out on a well-floured surface into a long rectangle (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Sprinkle reserved chopped pistachios over middle third. Fold bottom third up then top third down and seal edges. Give dough a quarter turn. Repeat rolling and folding twice more then wrap in cling film and place in fridge for 20 minutes.
  2. After this time, divide pastry almost in half. Roll out larger piece to line a pie dish (Ø 23 cm). Set tin and remaining pastry aside in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 190°C.
  3. Pour cooled cherry filling into chilled pie base. Roll out remaining pastry to form a lid for the pie. Brush rim of pie base with beaten egg then place lid on top. Trim edges with a sharp knife then crimp edges with a fork to form a tight seal. Brush pastry lid with beaten egg then make a slit in centre to release steam. Bake for 30-40 minutes (190°C) until golden brown.
  4. Leave to rest for 10 minutes before serving - works well with custard, cream or ice cream.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, shallow metal pie dish or shallow tart tin (Ø 23 cm), pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cherries make a lovely rich pie filling, and the nutty puff pastry used here adds an extra dimension and means that the end result isn’t too dense. Serve warm with custard, cream or ice cream.
