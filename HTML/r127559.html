Jalapeño chicken lettuce wraps

Ingredients

Guacamole

  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander (see tip)
  • ¼ red onion (approx. 10-15 g)
  • 300 g avocados (2 avocados), flesh only
  • 120 g tomato (approx. 1 tomato), cut into quarters and deseeded
  • 1 tbsp jalapeño chillies, canned, drained and sliced (optional)
  • 1 - 2 tbsp lime juice (approx. 1 lime), to taste
  • 1 pinch fine salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch black pepper, freshly ground, to taste

Coriander and jalapeño chicken

  • ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper, to taste
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika powder
  • 1 tsp garlic, granulated
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 500 g chicken meat, skinless and boneless, cut into strips (2-3 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander (see tip)
  • 1 tbsp jalapeño chillies, canned, drained and sliced, plus extra to serve
  • 40 g lime juice (approx. 2 limes)
  • 50 g honey
  • ¼ tsp fine salt

Warm freekeh salad

  • 250 g freekeh (see tip)
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • 80 - 100 g red pepper, deseeded and cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 100 g macadamia nuts, cut into halves (optional)
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 sweetcorn cobs, kernels only
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, roughly chopped
  • 1 pinch fine salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 baby cos lettuce, leaves separated, to serve
  • 100 g sour cream, to serve (optional)

Preparation

Guacamole

  1. Place coriander and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add avocados, tomato, jalapeño chillies, lime juice, salt and black pepper and mix 2 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl, cover and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coriander and jalapeño chicken

  1. Place cayenne pepper, cumin seeds, dried oregano, sweet paprika, granulated garlic and ground cinnamon into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Place Varoma into position, insert Varoma tray and weigh chicken onto it. Remove Varoma, then rub spice mixture from mixing bowl over the chicken. Set Varoma with chicken aside.
  3. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place coriander and jalapeños into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add lime juice, honey and salt and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 3. Transfer jalapeño sauce into a jug and set aside.

Warm freekeh salad

  1. Without cleaning mixing bowl,insert simmering basket and weigh freekeh into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse freekeh until water runs clear.
  2. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl, then re-insert simmering basket with freekeh.
  3. Place Varoma with chicken into position, then drizzle half of reserved jalapeño sauce over chicken. Close Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and turn chicken pieces over on Varoma tray to ensure even cooking. Stir freekeh in simmering basket with aid of spatula.
  4. Place Varoma dish into position, then weigh capsicum and macadamias (optional) into it. Add red onion and corn kernels to Varoma dish, then re-insert Varoma tray with chicken, secure Varoma lid and cook 3-5 min/Varoma/speed 2, or until chicken is fully cooked.
  5. Remove Varoma and transfer chicken onto a serving dish. Transfer steamed vegetables into a serving bowl, then add coriander leaves and remaining jalapeño sauce.
  6. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and allow freekeh to drain. Transfer drained freekeh into serving bowl with the vegetables and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  7. Fill a lettuce leaf with the freekeh salad, top with a piece of spiced chicken, extra jalapeño chillies and a dollop of reserved guacamole and sour cream (optional).

Useful Items

large jug, serving dish, serving bowl, cling film, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For more flavour, use whole sprigs of coriander (leaves and stems).
  • Freekeh or farik is a cereal food made from green durum wheat that is roasted and rubbed to create its unique flavor. The seeds are cracked into smaller pieces so they resemble a green bulgur. Freekeh can be found in health stores and specialty shops.
