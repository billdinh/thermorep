Apple Parkin

Ingredients

  • 450 g Bramley cooking apples, peeled, cored, quartered
  • 50 g unsalted butter
  • 125 g golden syrup
  • 125 g treacle
  • 225 g self-raising flour
  • 125 g medium oatmeal
  • 75 g light brown sugar
  • 30 g milk
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 3 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1 large egg

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and line a large loaf tin (900 g) with baking paper then set aside.
  2. Place apples in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula  then cook 6 min/90°C/speed 3.
  3. Add butter, golden syrup, treacle, flour, oatmeal, sugar, milk, baking powder, ginger, mixed spice and egg then mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  4. Transfer to prepared loaf tin and bake for 1¼-1½ hours (170°C), covering with foil halfway through to prevent top from over-browning.
  5. Allow to cool in tin for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve sliced.

Useful Items

baking paper, 900 g loaf tin (2 lb), aluminium foil, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This loaf benefits from being made a few days ahead, giving time for the flavours to develop.

 

  • A traditional, slightly sticky cake with warming ginger and mixed spice flavours. Originating in northern England, parkin was typically served on 5th November for Guy Fawkes Night. Now enjoyed throughout the colder months we’ve teamed it here with apples, making this a deliciously warming tea time treat.
