Banh bao (steamed pork buns)

Ingredients

Bao (Buns)

  • 200 g full cream milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g sugar
  • 350 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 30 g vegetable oil

Pork filling

  • 10 g dried shiitake mushrooms
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 3000 g water
  • chilled water
  • 12 quail eggs (see Tips)
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, white part, for filling, green part, cut into thin slices on the diagonal, to serve
  • 20 sprigs fresh chives, cut into halves
  • 2 Chinese sausages (approx. 60 g), cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 500 g pork mince
  • 2 tsp rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Bao (Buns)

  1. Cut out 12 squares (approx. 8 x 8 cm) of baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place milk, yeast and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour, baking powder and oil and mix 6 sec/speed 6.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and 4 min/, until soft and smooth. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or clean work surface and shape into a ball. Cover with a kitchen towel and allow to prove for 1 hour or until doubled in size. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pork filling

  1. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh mushrooms into it. Cover with boiling water and set aside for 10 minutes. Using simmering basket, drain and discard soaking liquid.
  3. Place 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl and boil 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, fill a bowl with ice water and set aside.
  4. Insert simmering basket and place quail eggs into it, then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 3. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer eggs into ice water to cool (approx. 15 minutes). Peel eggs carefully and set aside. Discard water.
  5. Place spring onion/shallots (white parts only), chives, sausages and drained mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add mince, vinegar, oyster sauce, salt and pepper and combine 20 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. With damp hands, divide pork mixture into 12 portions (approx. 50-55 g) and shape into balls. Flatten each ball and place 1 quail egg into the centre. Carefully enclose egg in pork mixture by slowly rolling it back and forth in damp palms. Divide pork meatballs between Varoma dish and Varoma tray.
  8. Place 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Carefully transfer meatballs onto lined plate and set aside to drain and cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl and Varoma.

Assembly

  1. Knock down bun dough. Divide into 12 equal balls (approx. 45-50 g). Using a rolling pin, roll out a piece of dough into a circle (approx. 3 mm thickness). Place 1 pork meatball into the centre of the dough circle. Bring edges together, pleating edges to cover filling and seal bun. Place bun onto 1 piece of baking paper. Transfer onto a baking tray and cover with a kitchen towel. Repeat with remaining dough and filling. Set aside to prove for 20 minutes.
  2. Place remaining 1000 g water into mixing bowl and heat 6-8 min/Varoma/speed 1, until water is boiling. Place 3 buns into Varoma dish and 3 buns onto Varoma tray, leaving space for spreading, ensuring steam can circulate freely and Varoma lid does not touch buns when secured. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until warmed through. Transfer buns, with paper, onto a serving plate and cover to keep warm. Place 3 buns into Varoma dish and remaining 3 buns onto Varoma tray and steam 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until warmed through. Serve warm, garnished with sliced spring onion/shallot greens (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking paper, silicone bread mat, kitchen towel, large plate, paper towel, bowl, rolling pin, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), serving plate

Hints & Tricks

  • If you can't find quail eggs, you can replace them with 3 chicken eggs. Hardboil chicken eggs (refer to method in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook), and cut into quarters when cool enough to handle. Proceed as per recipe.
  • Chinese sausage, also known as Lup chong, can be found in the international aisle of most major supermarkets or at Asian grocers.
  • Serve warm with soy sauce, kecap manis or the Nuoc cham dipping sauce from this collection.
