Gluten-Free Pasta

Ingredients

  • 4 oz cassava flour, plus extra to dust
  • 4 oz cornstarch
  • 2 oz brown rice flour
  • 2 tsp guar gum
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 ½ oz water
  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • salt, to boil with pasta
  • water, to boil pasta

Preparation

  1. Place cassava flour, cornstarch, brown rice flour, guar gum, eggs, water and olive oil into mixing bowl and knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to a work surface and press into a ball. If the dough is too dry add a little water until you get a smooth and homogeneous ball.
  2. Transfer dough to a well-floured work surface and divide into eight parts. Roll each piece of dough with a rolling pin into an elongated shape and feed through the pasta machine while guiding the dough. Run dough through pasta machine with rollers set on most open setting; fold dough into thirds and roll it back through the pasta maker. If dough sticks, coat with a little more flour. Fold and roll the dough three more times on this setting. Adjust rollers to the next level of thinness and guide dough back through the machine with the back of your hand, repeating the process until dough is approximately 1/16 inch thick. Keep remaining dough covered to prevent from drying out. Repeat with remaining dough. Cut pasta into desired shapes (see Variation).
  3. Cook the pasta on stove top in plenty of boiling and salted water (approx. 1 tbsp salt per 16 cups water) for 2-4 minutes depending on shape and thickness of dough. Drain pasta and serve warm, seasoned with sauce to taste.

Useful Items

rolling pin, pasta machine, large saucepot (approx. 4-5 qt.), stove

Hints & Tricks

  • To prepare fresh pasta for short-term use: make pasta and then dry for approximately 30 minutes. Place pasta in plastic bags and refrigerate for up to 12 hours. To freeze the pasta, dry it completely before placing in bags. This can take several hours, but if the pasta remains moist, it’s possible for mold to form. Freeze the pasta for up to two months.
  • Cut dough into desired shapes according to the type of pasta you want to prepare. Lasagna or ravioli: cut dough into rectangles (8 in. x 4 in.). Tagliatelle: cut into strips (approx. 1/16 in. x 12 in.). Bow ties: cut small rectangles (1 in. x 2 in.) and pinch the center of each rectangle to form a bow tie.
