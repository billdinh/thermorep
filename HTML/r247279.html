Mini Filoni with Green Olives - Piccoli filoni alle olive verdi

Ingredients

  • 320 g water, cold
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 300 g manitoba flour or other strong, refined bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 10 g fine sea salt
  • 300 g green olives, pitted

Preparation

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil and set aside.
  2. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 120 g cold water and 40 g oil. Remove jug and set aside.
  3. Place 100 g cold water, yeast and sugar in mixing bowl then warm 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Add manitoba flour, plain flour, salt, remaining 100 g cold water then knead 2 min/. Remove measuring cup and knead again 2 min/ while adding pre-weighed water and oil a little at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add olives and knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  6. Dust a work surface with plenty of flour and gently tip dough out onto it (the mixture will be very soft, almost liquid). Sprinkle more flour over dough, then with floured hands, form dough into a rectangle (approx. 25 cm x 15 cm). Using a greased steel spatula (or knife), cut the rectangle into 10 strips parallel to the short side. As you cut each strip, roll it in flour then, using a long spatula or knife, transfer to prepared baking trays. Wipe the edge of the spatula or knife and grease between each cut.
  7. Leave to prove for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 220°C.
  8. Bake for 15-20 minutes (220°C) until golden. Leave to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, baking trays, baking paper, wire rack, large bowl, long sharp knife, jug, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is possible to freeze the rolls when cooked. In this case, wait for them to cool, place in suitable freezer bags and store in a freezer.
  • For a uniformly golden colour, brush with extra virgin olive oil before baking in step 8.
  • Filone is considered an ‘everyday’ bread in Italy - a simple yeast bread eaten at mealtimes. The name filoni comes from the Italian word for 'line' as the bread is shaped into a long stick. This recipe is for mini versions with the addition of green olives for an extra flavour dimension. You’ll need lots of flour when shaping this sticky dough into individual sticks before serving as an accompaniment to soup or salad.
  • If desired, replace green olives with black olives or sun-dried tomatoes in oil, then drained.
