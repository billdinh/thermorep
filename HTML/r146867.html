Sesame and Chia Seed Loaf

Ingredients

  • butter, for greasing
  • 110 g basmati rice
  • 80 g millet grain
  • 40 g quinoa grain
  • 40 g chickpea flour (see tip)
  • 90 g buckwheat flour (see tip)
  • 70 g arrowroot
  • 70 g coconut flour
  • 40 g chia seed, plus extra for garnishing
  • 2 tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 2 tbsp agave syrup
  • 400 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 30 g coconut oil
  • 1 tsp cider vinegar
  • 2 eggs
  • sesame seeds, for garnishing
  • sea salt flakes, for garnishing
Nutrition
per 1 slice

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a loaf tin (25 cm x 9 cm x 10 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place rice, millet and quinoa in mixing bowl then mill 1 min/speed 9. 
  3. Add chickpea flour, buckwheat flour, arrowroot, coconut flour, chia seeds, xanthan gum and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place agave, water and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  5. Add oil, vinegar, eggs and reserved flour mixture then mix 15 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix for a further 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer dough to prepared loaf tin.
  6. Leave dough to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 45 minutes). Brush top of loaf with extra water, then sprinkle with sesame seeds, sea salt flakes and extra chia seeds.
  7. Bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or until browned on top. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve warm.

Useful Items

900 g loaf tin (2 lb), pastry brush, loaf tin (25 x 9 x 10 cm), wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own chickpea flour, mill 40 g dried chickpeas with the rice, millet and quinoa in step 2.
  • To make your own buckwheat flour, mill 90 g raw buckwheat with the rice, millet and quinoa in step 2.
  • Serve gluten free bread fresh or toasted with your favourite jam, butter or spread.
  • If not served warm, allow bread to cool completely before placing into a sealable container to store until use.
  • Proving time may vary depending on the time of year and room temperature. Choose a warm, dark place to leave dough to prove and try covering the loaf tin with a kitchen towel to insulate if necessary. Leave for an additional 15-30 minutes if not doubled in size, a maximum of 60 minutes should be sufficient.
  • To ensure gluten free recipes are gluten free, always read the labels of your products to ensure no wheat or gluten has been used as part of the production process.
