Lamb Kebobs

Ingredients

  • 80 g raisins
  • 50 g blanched almonds
  • 1 tsp chilli powder (optional)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 500 g boneless lamb leg, cubed (3 cm)
  • 15 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp hot paprika
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch asafoetida
  • 50 g stale bread, torn in pieces
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 medium egg
  • 2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 3 Tbsp vegetable oil

Preparation

  1. Place raisins, almonds and chilli powder (if using) in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a freezer-proof container.
  3. Add lamb, butter, nigella seeds, coriander, paprika, pepper and asafoetida to container then toss together. Place in fridge to marinate for 1 hour, then in freezer for 80 minutes. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place bread in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place partially frozen meat in mixing bowl and mince 10-15 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, if desired (see tips), mince again 5 sec/speed 10.
  6. Add garam masala and salt then mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  7. Add egg and reserved breadcrumbs then mix 10 sec/speed 10.
  8. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  9. Place a sheet of baking paper on worktop and tip meat mixture out onto it. Using baking paper, form meat mixture into a long sausage shape and cut into 6 pieces.
  10. Gently flatten the pieces and top each with reserved raisin-almond-chilli mixture. Roll each back into a sausage shape, encasing the raisin-almond-chilli mixture. Skewer each piece to form a kabob and roll in flour to coat.
  11. Heat vegetable oil in a large oven-proof frying pan and fry floured kabobs until browned on all sides.
  12. Transfer pan to oven and cook for 10 minutes (180°C) or longer as desired. Remove and serve warm with rice or flatbreads.

Useful Items

baking paper, skewers, oven-proof frying pan, freezer-proof container, refrigerator, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Nigella seeds are often sold as black cumin.
  • For best mince texture, marinated meat pieces should be partially frozen and firm but not completely solid.
  • The bowl does not need to be cleaned after the breadcrumbs are made.
  • At the end of step 4 the preferred mince texture is coarse and crumb-like because it will be processed again in future steps.
  • If you do not have a large frying pan that can go in the oven, transfer the kabobs from the frying pan to a warmed baking tray to finish cooking in the oven.
  • Substitute fresh, seeded chilli, cut in pieces for the chilli powder.
  • Double the raisin-almond mixture if more stuffing is desired.
  • You can omit the skewer and fry or grill the kabobs as you would burgers.
  • Try wrapping the mince in flaky pastry and baking in a 180°C oven for 20-25 minutes to make spicy sausage rolls.
