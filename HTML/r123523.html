White Bean Purée and Anchovy on Crostini

Ingredients

Chilli Oil

  • 200 g olive oil
  • 1 tbsp dried chilli flakes
  • 2 whole dried red chillies

Bean Purée

  • 3 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 800 g tinned cannellini beans, rinsed and drained (weight is pre-draining)
  • ½ fresh red chilli, deseeded if preferred and cut it into halves (optional)
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • ½ tsp dried rosemary
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ¼ tsp ground smoked paprika, to taste
  • ¼ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Assembly

  • 40 - 50 g anchovy fillets, in oil, drained and separated
  • 1 baguette loaf, thinly sliced and toasted

Preparation

Chilli Oil

  1. Place all chilli oil ingredients in mixing bowl then heat 10 min/70°C//speed 1. Transfer into a jug and set aside to cool.
  2. Once cooled, remove whole chillies from oil and set aside. Carefully pour oil through a funnel into a sealable jar or bottle. Add reserved chillies to the jar or bottle and seal.  Store chilli oil in a cool, dark place for up to 3 months.

Bean Purée

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Wrap unpeeled garlic cloves in a piece of aluminium foil, place on a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) and roast for 20 minutes. Set garlic aside to cool for 10 minutes then remove outer skins.
  2. Place all remaining bean purée ingredients, including peeled garlic cloves, in mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for a further 20 sec/speed 7.

Assembly

  1. Spread bean purée over toasted baguette slices (crostini) and top with an anchovy fillet. Drizzle over a little of the reserved chilli oil and serve immediately.

Useful Items

sealable jar or bottle, jam funnel, tongs, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), aluminium foil, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The chilli flavour in the oil will intensify the longer it sits so if you want a real chilli kick we recommend making your oil in advance.
