Chocolate Lime Mousse with Sesame and Honey Streusel

Ingredients

Chocolate Lime Mousse

  • 70 g whole milk
  • 25 g caster sugar
  • 1 lime, finely grated zest and 30 g juice
  • 3 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 220 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 145 g whipping cream

Sesame Streusel

  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 180 g plain flour
  • 100 g soft brown sugar
  • 10 g sesame seeds, toasted
  • 5 g black sesame seeds, toasted
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
  • 50 g runny honey

Preparation

Chocolate Lime Mousse

  1. Line base of 4 metal chef rings (Ø 10 cm) with cling film.
  2. Place milk, caster sugar, lime zest and egg yolks in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/80°C/speed 2.
  3. Add chocolate and lime juice then blend 2 min/60°C/speed 3. Transfer to a metal bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whip speed 3 until lightly whipped (soft peaks stage, approx. 30-45 seconds), watching carefully to avoid overwhipping.
  5. When chocolate has cooled to 30°C, fold in whipped cream. Fill prepared rings then freeze until solid. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare streusel.

Sesame Streusel

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Place butter, flour and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 40 sec/speed 4. Tip out onto a silicone baking mat, leaving in small pieces, and bake for 20 minutes (160°C) until pale brown. Allow to cool completely.
  2. When streusel has cooled, place white sesame seeds, black sesame seeds, cinnamon and honey in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/50°C/speed 1.
  3. Add baked streusel pieces and mix 15 sec/speed 1. Return to prepared baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (160°C) until dry and golden brown. Leave to cool completely.
  4. When streusel has cooled, remove mousse rings from freezer, dust surface with cocoa powder, then remove metal rings (see tip). Place in the centre of serving plates and decorate the outsides with pieces of streusel before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, metal bowl, silicone baking mat, baking tray, serving plates, chef rings (10 cm), freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Heat the outside of the ring gently to remove the mousse.
  • Leftover streusel can be stored in an airtight container or frozen for up to 1 month.
  • It is normal for the streusel to melt and spread slightly during baking.
