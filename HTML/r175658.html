Poached Quince with Almond Flour Biscotti

Ingredients

Almond Biscotti

  • 3 large eggs
  • 80 g brown sugar
  • 50 g whole milk
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 250 g almond flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 100 g almonds

Poached Quince

  • 400 g water
  • 250 g sweet Marsala wine
  • 300 g caster sugar
  • 6 cardamom pods, gently crushed
  • ½ lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • ½ orange, thin peelings of skin only
  • 800 g quince (approx. 4), peeled, cut in eights and cores removed

Preparation

Almond Biscotti

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place eggs, brown sugar and milk in mixing bowl then beat 4 min/37°C/speed 5.
  3. Add plain flour, almond flour and baking powder then mix 1 min/speed 5.
  4. Add almonds and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer to prepared baking tray and, using damp hands, divide dough in two. Form each piece into a log (30 cm x 3 cm) leaving space between them. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C) or until brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Poached Quince

  1. Place water, Marsala wine, sugar, cardamom pods, lemon and orange zest in mixing bowl. Arrange quince in Varoma dish, set Varoma in position and steam 17-22 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until tender, adjusting steaming time according to size and ripeness of quince. Meanwhile, remove prebaked logs of biscotti from oven (they should feel soft to touch) and allow to cool for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 150°C. Set Varoma aside.
  2. Remove lemon peelings, orange peelings and cardamom pods from syrup. Place quince into a container (1 litre) then pour in cardamom syrup. Cover and refrigerate for 2-3 hours.
  3. Using a sharp knife, slice biscotti dough (approx. 1 cm thick). Place slices back onto prepared baking tray and bake for a further 25-35 minutes (150°C). Allow to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.
  4. When ready to serve, remove cardamom syrup container from fridge, pour syrup into mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, reduce 8-12 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until slightly thickened (it will thicken more as it cools). Serve poached quince with 1-2 Tbsp of cardamom syrup and a biscotti.

Useful Items

baking tray and baking paper, airtight container, plastic container (1 litre), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For a more intense flavour, store the poached quince for 1-2 days before serving.
  • Store quince in the fridge in a jar filled with syrup for up to one month.
