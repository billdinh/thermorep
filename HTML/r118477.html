Char siu bao pork buns

Ingredients

Char siu marinade

  • 30 g piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 60 g eschalots
  • 2 tsp Chinese five spice
  • 60 g hoi sin sauce
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 40 g water
  • 30 g molasses
  • 50 g Shaoxing wine
  • 40 g dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato sauce
  • 500 g pork belly, cut into 3-4 pieces

Buns

  • 1 tbsp dried yeast
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 50 g cornflour
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 180 g warm water
  • 10 g vegetable oil

Char siu bao

  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 600 g water, plus extra as needed
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, white and light green part only, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce

Preparation

Char siu marinade

  1. Place ginger, garlic and eschalots into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add Chinese five spice, hoi sin sauce, pepper, water, molasses, wine, soy and tomato sauce and mix 5 sec/speed 9.
  3. Place pork into a sealable container or a snaplock bag and rub marinade into it. Place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 4 hours or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place a wire rack onto a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  5. Place marinated pork onto wire rack, reserving marinade, and roast for 1 hour (180°C), basting pork with reserved marinade after 20 minutes, then basting every 10 minutes. Set aside to cool.

Buns

  1. Place yeast, plain flour, baking powder, cornflour and sugar into mixing bowl and Turbo/1 sec/2 times.
  2. Add warm water and oil and knead 2 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat and wrap in mat or place into an oiled bowl and cover with a kitchen towel. Allow to prove for 30 minutes or until dough increases in size by one-third. Divide dough into 12 equal-sized balls and place under a damp kitchen towel. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Char siu bao

  1. Cut baking paper into 12 squares (approx. 8 x 8 cm). Place cornflour and 100 g of the water into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 2
  2. Add spring onions/shallots, oyster sauce and soy sauce and cook 4 min/90°C/speed 3.
  3. Remove and discard skin and any bones from pork, then roughly cut meat into small pieces (1 cm). Add pork into mixing bowl and combine 30 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. On a lightly floured work surface, roll out each ball of dough into a circle (approx. 1 cm thickness).
  5. Place 1 heaped tablespoon (approx. 35 g) of the pork mixture into the centre of 1 dough round. Gently pull up all edges together into the middle and pinch edges together to stick. Repeat with remaining dough and pork mixture (makes 12 buns). Place 6 squares of baking paper into Varoma dish and place a bun onto each square with join facing down.
  6. Place remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma with buns into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until buns are fluffy and firm. Carefully transfer buns into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  7. Top up water if required (to approx. 500 g). Place remaining buns on baking paper squares into Varoma dish, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam  12 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until buns are fluffy and firm. Serve buns warm.

Useful Items

snaplock bag, sealable container, wire rack, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), pastry brush, bowl, kitchen towel, silicone bread mat, baking paper, rolling pin, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace vegetable oil with peanut oil.
  • You can replace pork belly with pork shoulder. 
  • Serve these buns with Chinese black vinegar or soy sauce.
