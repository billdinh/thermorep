Sweet and sour tempeh

Ingredients

  • extra virgin olive oil spray
  • 40 g buckwheat groats
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 300 g tempeh, cut into pieces (approx. 3-4 cm - see Tips)
  • 30 g plant-based milk of choice
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into slices
  • ½ brown onion
  • 1 carrot (approx. 100 g), cut into slices
  • ½ red capsicum (approx. 100 g), cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 350 g fresh baby corn (see Tips)
  • 350 g fresh pineapple, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 3 tomatoes, cut into eighths
  • 100 g snow peas
  • 2 - 3 tbsp coconut sugar
  • 2 - 3 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp gluten free cornflour
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper, lightly spray with oil then set aside.
  2. Place buckwheat into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add spring onions/shallots and 3 of the garlic cloves and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add tempeh, milk, 1½ tablespoons of the tamari and 1/4 teaspoon of the black pepper and combine 5 sec//speed 3.
  5. Roll 1 tablespoon of mixture into a ball and place onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining mixture (makes approx. 20 balls). Spray with oil and bake for 15 minutes (200°C), turning twice, until golden brown. Once cooked, transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Proceed with recipe while tempeh balls are cooking. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place oil, ginger, onion and remaining 2 garlic cloves into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/120°C/speed 2, without measuring cup.
  8. Add carrot, capsicum, corn and pineapple and cook 6 min/120°C//speed 1.
  9. Add tomatoes, snow peas, coconut sugar, vinegar, cornflour, salt and pepper to taste and remaining 1 tablespoon tamari and cook 6-8 min/90°C//speed 1 or until vegetables are cooked to your liking. Transfer vegetables into thermal serving bowl with tempeh balls and combine to coat. Serve warm (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using an unmarinated tempeh in this recipe.
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
  • You can replace the fresh baby corn with rinsed and drained canned baby corn.
  • We served ours with steamed jasmine rice.
