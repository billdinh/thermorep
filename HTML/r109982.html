Gluten Free Savory Crêpes with Seafood

Ingredients

Crêpe Batter

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 10 ½ oz whole milk
  • 5 ½ oz rice flour
  • 2 oz salted butter, room temperature

Filling

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 3 oz shallots, halved
  • 1 oz fresh parsley leaves (approx. 15 - 20 sprigs), plus extra to serve
  • 1 oz salted butter, room temperature
  • 18 oz seafood mix, frozen
  • 1 oz crème fraîche
  • 1 tbsp salted butter

Preparation

Crêpe Batter

  1. Place eggs, salt, milk, flour and butter into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer batter into a bowl and set aside to rest for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place garlic, shallots and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and mix 10 sec//speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add seafood mix and cook 6 min/250°F//speed . Strain through simmering basket collecting juices below. Cover cooked seafood with aluminum foil to keep warm.
  4. Pour juices back into mixing bowl. Reduce, without measuring cup, 15 min/212°F/speed .
  5. Discard half of reduced cooking juices (keep approx. 1 oz) add crème fraîche and stir 10 sec/speed 3. Leave in mixing bowl to keep warm and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a baking pan with parchment paper.
  7. Heat 1 tbsp of butter in a large crêpe pan or frying pan over medium heat. Pour a small ladle of batter into the center and spread out thinly. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side and transfer to a plate. Repeat, adding more butter when necessary, until all batter is used.
  8. Divide seafood mixture and sauce evenly between crêpes and fold in sides to a square. Place into the prepared baking pan. Bake for 10-15 minutes (350°F) until crisp and golden. Serve immediately with a sprinkling of freshly chopped parsley leaves.

Useful Items

aluminum foil, baking pan, parchment paper, crêpe or frying pan, small ladle, oven, stove

Hints & Tricks

  • Crème fraîche can be replaced with sour cream.
