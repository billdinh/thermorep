Cornish pasties

Ingredients

  • 500 g stewing beef (gravy beef or chuck steak), trimmed and cut into pieces (3 cm)

Pastry

  • 550 g plain flour
  • 135 g salted butter, chilled and cut into pieces
  • 135 g lard, chilled and cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 160 g chilled water

Filling

  • 140 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 160 g carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g salted butter, cut into pieces
  • 160 g swede or turnip, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 160 g potato, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp ground white pepper, to taste
  • 1 ½ tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp plain flour
  • 60 g Worcestershire sauce

Assembly

  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • tomato sauce, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place a plate onto mixing bowl lid and weigh meat onto it. Place into freezer until ready to use (maximum 45 minutes).

Pastry

  1. Place flour, butter, lard and salt into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add water and mix 10 sec/speed 4, then knead 30 sec/, until combined. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or clean work surface and shape into a disc. Wrap in a silicone bread mat or place onto a plate and cover with plastic wrap, then place into refrigerator to chill for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with filling.

Filling

  1. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place onion, carrot and 50 g of the butter into mixing bowl and chop 4-8 sec/speed 4, until evenly chopped. Transfer into a bowl.
  2. Place swede or turnip, potato, pepper, stock paste and remaining 50 g butter into mixing bowl and chop 4-8 sec/speed 4, until evenly chopped. Transfer into bowl with carrot mixture.
  3. Place flour, Worcestershire sauce and 250 g of the chilled beef into mixing bowl and chop 3-4 sec/speed 4, until roughly chopped. Transfer into bowl with vegetables.
  4. Place remaining beef into mixing bowl and chop 3-4 sec/speed 6, until roughly chopped. Transfer into bowl with vegetables and stir well to combine.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Remove pastry from refrigerator and divide into 10 equal portions (approx. 95 g each) and shape each portion into a ball. On a silicone bread mat or a lightly floured work surface, roll out each portion into circle (approx. 20 cm, 3 mm thickness). Brush edges of circle with beaten egg. Place 3 heaped tablespoons of the filling into the centre of dough portion. Bring the 2 sides of pastry together to meet at the top, then pleat pastry together to encase filling. Place onto prepared baking trays. Repeat with remaining pastry and filling. Brush pasties with beaten egg and bake for 25-30 minutes (200°C) until crisp and golden. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool slightly, then serve with tomato sauce on the side.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, rolling pin, pastry brush, plate, wire rack, plastic wrap, refrigerator, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Pastry can be made 2-3 days in advance and stored in the refrigerator.
  • Whole pasties can be made ahead of time and placed into refrigerator for up to 24 hours before baking. They can also be stored in the freezer for up to 1 month. Allow to thaw out in refrigerator overnight before baking.
  • To make your own tomato sauce, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook.
