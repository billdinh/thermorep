Cheeseboard Spaghetti

Ingredients

Pangrattato

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 100 g wholemeal bread, torn in pieces
  • 50 g walnuts
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh rosemary, needles only
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes (optional)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil

Cheese Sauce

  • 200 g cheese, mixed types, (e.g. Cheddar, brie, stilton), cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 40 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 500 g milk
  • 1 ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • water, for cooking pasta
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, for cooking pasta

Assembly

  • 300 g dried spaghetti
  • extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling

Preparation

Pangrattato

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Add bread, walnuts, thyme, rosemary, chilli flakes (if using) and salt then chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Heat 1 Tbsp olive oil in a non-stick frying pan over a medium heat, then add breadcrumb mixture and fry for 5 minutes until golden and crisp. Transfer pangrattato to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Cheese Sauce

  1. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Add flour and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add milk, reserved grated cheese, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, 1 tsp salt and pepper then cook 7 min/90°C/speed 3. Meanwhile, bring a large saucepan of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil.

Assembly

  1. Cook spaghetti according to packet instructions, or until al dente. Drain well using a colander and transfer to a large bowl.
  2. Pour cheese sauce over cooked spaghetti and mix well to combine. Divide spaghetti between 4 serving plates, drizzle over a little olive oil then sprinkle some pangrattato over each. Serve immediately with remaining pangrattato.

Useful Items

non-stick frying pan, colander, large bowl, large saucepan, serving plates, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Use cheese you may have leftover from a cheeseboard or dinner party. A combination of a creamy cheese such as brie, some sharp cheddar, and a strong blue works well.
  • Give leftover cheese a new lease of life with this simple yet flavourful pasta dish.
