Blueberry and Lemon Spumble

Ingredients

Blueberry Compote

  • 300 g fresh blueberries
  • 50 g honey
  • 30 g water

Oat and Seed Granola

  • 150 g hazelnuts
  • 100 g rolled oats
  • 75 g pumpkin seeds
  • 70 g runny honey
  • 1 Tbsp chia seeds (optional)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Lemon Sponge

  • 2 medium eggs
  • 175 g caster sugar
  • 150 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest only
  • 175 g self-raising flour
  • 125 g milk
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

Blueberry Compote

  1. Place blueberries, honey and water in mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10-15 min/100°C/speed 0.5 until slightly thickened. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 170°C and line a baking tray with baking paper. Transfer compote to a baking dish and spread over base then leave to cool. Clean mixing bowl.

Oat and Seed Granola

  1. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and roughly chop 2 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add oats, pumpkin seeds, honey, chia seeds (if using) and salt then mix 20 sec//speed 2. Tip out onto prepared tray. Bake for 7 minutes (170°C), stir with spatula then bake for a further 8 minutes (170°C). Set aside and leave to cool.

Lemon Sponge

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 1 min/speed 4.
  2. Add butter and lemon zest then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Add flour, milk and salt then mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 5. Pour mixture over blueberry compote in baking dish and spread evenly. Sprinkle over half the cooled granola then bake for 50-60 minutes (180°C) until a skewer inserted in centre of sponge comes out clean (see tip).
  4. Serve warm with cream or ice cream.

Useful Items

baking dish, baking tray, baking paper, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • What do you get when you cross a sponge with a crumble? A spumble! Two great puddings become one when a blueberry compote and moist lemon sponge are finished with a granola crumble topping. You’ll wonder why you’ve never done it before!
  • Cover with foil if browning too much in step 6.
  • Making more granola than you need for the recipe means you have enough to enjoy some for breakfast. Store in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
