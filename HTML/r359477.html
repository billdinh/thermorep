Mini Strawberry and Elderflower Mille Feuille

Ingredients

Puff Pastry

  • 200 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g water, cold
  • ½ tsp caster sugar

Assembly

  • 300 g double cream
  • 80 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 60 g elderflower cordial
  • 300 g fresh strawberries, hulled, thinly sliced (5 mm)

Preparation

Puff Pastry

  1. Place butter, flour, water and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Tip out dough, shape into a ball and flatten into a rough square. Wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 20 minutes.
  2. On a lightly-floured surface, roll out chilled dough to a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Fold bottom third up to cover middle third and top third down over this. Seal edges then give dough a quarter turn so that the fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice. Return to fridge and leave to rest for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, line 2 baking trays (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper then cut a 5 cm x 10 cm rectangular template out of baking paper and set aside.
  3. Divide chilled dough into 6 equal portions. On a lightly-floured surface, roll each dough piece into a 3 mm thick rectangle (approx. 35 cm x 10 cm). Use prepared template to cut 3 rectangles from each dough piece then transfer to prepared trays. Place baking trays in fridge for a further 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  4. Place a sheet of baking paper and a baking tray on top of each baking tray to act as a weight. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until lightly golden. Set aside to cool completely (approx. 1 hour).

Assembly

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream, sugar and elderflower cordial in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whip without setting a time/speed 3 until stiff peaks form, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer cream to a large piping bag fitted with a Ø 10 mm nozzle.
  2. Divide pastry rectangles into 6 portions (3 rectangles per portion). Pipe half the cream over 6 pastry layers, taking care to keep cream within edges of pastry. Top with half the sliced strawberries.
  3. Place second pastry layer on top. Pipe remaining cream over this layer and top with remaining strawberries. Place final piece of pastry on top and press down gently.
  4. Dust each generously with icing sugar then serve immediately (see tip).

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking paper, piping bag and Ø 10 mm nozzle, baking trays, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If preferred, make up to step 4 and assemble mille feuille just before serving.
