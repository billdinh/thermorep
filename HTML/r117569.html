Broad Bean and Chickpea Falafels

Ingredients

  • 280 g broad beans, fresh or frozen (see tip)
  • 500 g water
  • 1 Tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 Tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 5 g fresh mint, leaves only
  • 240 g tinned chick peas, drained and rinsed (1 x 400 g tin)
  • 1 Tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, or to taste
  • ½ - 1 tsp dried chilli flakes, to taste
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • olive oil, for frying
  • 1 lime, cut in wedges, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place broad beans and water in mixing bowl then cook 8-10 min/100°C//speed  (see tip). Strain through simmering basket, discarding cooking water then run under cold water. Set simmering basket aside and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place coriander and cumin seeds in mixing bowl then toast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, pod broad beans, discarding skins and set beans aside. Grind toasted seeds 1 min/speed 10.
  3. Add garlic, onions, parsley and mint then chop 8 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved broad beans (approx. 180 g), chick peas,  sesame seeds, baking powder, salt, pepper and chilli flakes then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.5.
  5. Using wet hands, shape mixture into 16 balls (Ø 5 cm). Set aside on a plate and dust with flour.
  6. Heat oil in a non stick frying pan over a medium-high heat. Fry falafels for 4-6 minutes on each side until browned on both sides. Serve with lime wedges on side - see tip for serving ideas.

Useful Items

frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Works well served with pitta bread and guacamole or plain yoghurt.
  • If using frozen broad beans, cook for 10 minutes in step 1.
  • Shaped, uncooked falafels can be frozen then cooked when needed. Will keep in freezer for up to 4 months.
  • The falafels can also be baked at 190°C. Bake for 8-10 minutes on one side then turn over and cook for a further 5 minutes until golden brown.
