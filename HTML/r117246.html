Cheese Biscuits

Ingredients

  • 16 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 4 oz salted butter, cold, cut into pieces
  • 5 oz cheddar cheese, sharp or aged, cut into pieces (approx. ¾ in.)
  • 10 sprigs fresh chives, cut in lengths (approx. ¾ in.)
  • 9 oz whole milk, plus extra to glaze

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place flour, baking powder and salt into mixing bowl and mix 4 sec/speed 8. Add butter, cheese and chives and chop 4 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add milk and knead 20 sec/. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead a further 3-10 sec/ until dough just starts to pull together.
  3. Turn mixing bowl upside down on a floured surface and use fingers to twist the black hub of the mixing knife, allowing dough to drop out. Gently bring dough together into a round shape and flatten (to approx. ¾ in. thick). Using a biscuit cutter (2½ in.), cut into rounds (press the cutter straight down without twisting). Gently reshape remaining dough and cut out more biscuits until all dough is used. Arrange biscuits on prepared baking sheet with a ½ in. gap between each biscuit and brush tops with milk.
  4. Bake 15 minutes (425°F) or until golden. Allow to cool for 5 minutes before serving warm.

Useful Items

baking pan, parchment paper, pastry brush, biscuit cutter (2½ in.), baking sheet, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipes uses strong or mature/aged cheddar. For children, use medium or mild cheddar.
  • When using a biscuit cutter, do not twist it or your biscuits will rise unevenly. Just press straight down and lift straight up, shaking cutter to loosen dough if necessary.
  • Best eaten the same day they're made, or freeze on day of baking once completely cooled.
  • Biscuit dough can become tough if handled too much or if overmixed so use a light hand and work swiftly.
