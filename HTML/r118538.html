Raspberry and vanilla marshmallows

Ingredients

  • 100 g raspberries
  • 350 g caster sugar
  • 30 g gelatine powder
  • 280 g water
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 40 g pure icing sugar, for dusting
  • 40 g gluten free cornflour, for dusting
Nutrition
per 1 pieces

307 kJ / 73 kcal

Preparation

  1. Line a square cake tin (25 cm) with plastic wrap and set aside.
  2. Place raspberries and 20 g of the caster sugar into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 2.
  3. Strain mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh gelatine and 140 g of the water into it. Set aside to soak (approx. 5 minutes).
  5. Place remaining 330 g sugar into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 9.
  6. Add remaining 140 g water and heat 15 min/100°C/speed 2.
  7. Add reserved gelatine mixture and mix 2 min/100°C/speed 2, or until gelatine is completely dissolved. Allow to cool in mixing bowl until the temperature falls below 50°C (approx. 45 minutes) (see Tips).
  8. Insert butterfly whisk. Whip 13-15 min/speed 3, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until gelatine mixture is thick.
  9. Add vanilla and mix 10 sec/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk.
  10. Divide marshmallow mixture evenly between 2 bowls. Add raspberry coulis to one bowl of marshmallow and stir to combine. Transfer vanilla marshmallow into prepared tin and smooth surface. Top immediately with raspberry marshmallow and smooth surface.
  11. Set aside at room temperature (approx. 1 hour) until set. Using a fine-mesh sieve, dust icing sugar and cornflour together onto a silicone bread mat or work surface. Tip marshmallow out onto icing sugar mixture. Remove plastic wrap. Dip a sharp knife into the icing sugar mixture then cut marshmallow into squares (approx. 5 cm). Dust marshmallows on all sides with icing sugar mixture to coat. Serve immediately or store in a sealable container until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

square cake tin (25 cm) , plastic wrap, bowl, fine mesh sieve, ceramic or glass bowl, 2 bowls, silicone bread mat, sealable container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To check that mixture has fallen below 50°C in step 5, remove mixing bowl from Thermomix base and set aside to cool. When you return the mixing bowl to Thermomix base the lights will indicate the current temperature of the mixture in mixing bowl.
  • Do not store marshmallows in the refrigerator.
  • Marshmallows can be stored for up to 3 days in a cool, dark place.
