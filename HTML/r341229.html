Tandoori portobellos with creamy coconut raita

Ingredients

Creamy coconut raita

  • 1 continental cucumber, cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 5 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 200 g coconut yoghurt (see Tips)
  • 50 g filtered water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt, to taste
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

Tandoori portobellos

  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 black cardamom pod (optional - see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 5 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into slices
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • 100 g coconut yoghurt (see Tips)
  • 8 fresh portobello mushrooms

Preparation

Creamy coconut raita

  1. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths and place into a bowl.
  2. Place cucumber into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into lined simmering basket and set aside to drain.
  3. Place garlic, spring onion/shallot and mint into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add yoghurt, water, lemon juice, sugar, salt and oil and mix 5 sec/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Transfer into a separate bowl.
  5. Using nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, squeeze excess liquid from cucumber. Add drained cucumber to the bowl with the yoghurt mixture and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Tandoori portobellos

  1. Preheat oven grill. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with aluminium foil and lightly grease with oil. Set aside.
  2. Place all ingredients, except mushrooms, into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl. Rub mushrooms all over with spice mixture, then place onto prepared baking tray.
  3. Grill mushrooms for 4 minutes. Turn over, then grill for a further 4 minutes, until tender. Serve immediately, with Creamy coconut raita.

Useful Items

bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), aluminium foil, plastic wrap, nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, oven grill, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own coconut yoghurt, refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can find black cardamom pods at most Indian food stores, spice stores or online.
  • You can store this raita in the refrigerator for 1 day.
