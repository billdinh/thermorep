Seekh kebabs

Ingredients

  • water, for soaking
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 3 tsp cumin seeds
  • 10 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves, stalks and roots cleaned (see Tips)
  • 5 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 2 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 2 fresh green chillies, deseeded, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 500 g lamb mince
  • 1 egg white
  • 50 g chickpea (besan) flour
  • 1 tbsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • olive oil, for brushing
  • natural yoghurt, to serve
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place 10 wooden skewers into a large bowl of water and set aside to soak for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place coriander seeds and cumin seeds into mixing bowl and roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 2 minutes, then mill 1 min/speed 10.  Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place fresh coriander, mint, garlic paste, ginger paste and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add lamb and mix 2 min/speed 4 or until a smooth paste and sticks together.
  5. Add egg white, chickpea (besan) flour, garam masala, salt and reserved spices and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large freezable bowl or container, cover with plastic wrap and place into freezer for 15 minutes (see Tips).
  6. Preheat grill to high. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with aluminium foil and brush with oil.
  7. Using wet hands, take a golf-ball size of lamb mixture and push onto top of a skewer. Pull lamb mixture down skewer, twisting as you go, to stretch meat over skewer (approx. 10 cm), keeping the width as even as possible. Place onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining lamb mixture and skewers (makes approx. 10 kebabs).
  8. Brush each kebab with a little oil and cover exposed wooden ends with aluminium foil. Place under a hot grill for 5-6 minutes, turning every 2 minutes, until kebabs are golden brown and cooked through. Serve with yoghurt and lime wedges.

Useful Items

large bowl, wooden skewers, bowl, plastic wrap, plastic container, freezable bowl or container, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), aluminium foil, pastry brush, freezer, oven grill

Hints & Tricks

  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Placing mixture into freezer for 15 minutes makes it easier to shape.
  • You can serve this with rice, bread, chutney or your favourite side. We served ours with Parathas, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
