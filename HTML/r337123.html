Mushroom stock paste

Ingredients

  • 30 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 400 g hot water
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 brown onions (approx. 280 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 carrots (approx. 260 g), cut into pieces
  • 250 g fresh mushrooms, cut into slices
  • 50 g balsamic vinegar
  • 40 g dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh dried porcini mushrooms and water into it. Set aside to soak for 20 minutes.
  2. Place oil, onion, carrot and fresh mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add vinegar, dried shiitake mushrooms, salt and reserved porcini mushrooms with soaking liquid (see Tips) and cook 60 min/95°C/speed 2.
  4. Add thyme and cook 5 min/95°C/speed 2, then blend 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 10 sec/speed 6. Use as needed or transfer into a sealable container and store in refrigerator or freezer (see Tips).

Useful Items

sealable container, bowl, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store this stock paste in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or in the freezer for up to 6 months. This recipe does not contain as much salt (acting as a preservative) as the Vegetable stock paste recipe in The Basic Cookbook, so it has a shorter shelf life.
  • Make sure you discard the last tablespoon of the porcini mushroom soaking liquid as this may contain any sediment from soaking.
