Salmon and Couscous Parcels with Sun-dried Tomatoes

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 70 g spring onions, cut in pieces
  • 10 g lemon juice, freshly squeezed
  • ½ lemon, finely grated zest only
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 9 sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • 220 g couscous
  • 4 fresh salmon fillets, skinless (approx. 125 g each)
  • 500 g water, warm
  • 10 g olive oil
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled

Preparation

  1. Place rosemary, thyme and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Chop again 3 sec/speed 8 then scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add spring onions, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, tomatoes, salt and pepper then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer tomato seasoning to a large bowl, reserve 4 Tbsp in a small bowl and set both aside.
  4. Place large bowl with remaining tomato seasoning on mixing bowl lid, weigh in couscous and set aside.
  5. Cut 4 squares of baking paper (25 cm). Place each salmon fillet on a baking paper square, season each with salt, pepper and 1 Tbsp reserved tomato seasoning. Fold baking paper to close parcels and place seam side up, 2 in Varoma dish (see tip) and 2 on Varoma tray, tucking the folded ends of paper underneath the parcels. Insert Varoma tray into Varoma dish and set aside.
  6. Place water, oil and vegetable stock in mixing bowl. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep salmon warm. Pour cooking water over reserved couscous and tomato seasoning, stir with a fork to combine and leave to rest for approx. 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork. Serve salmon parcels with couscous immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • Steaming in paper parcels keeps all of the flavour in and is a healthier way of cooking as it doesn’t require extra fats. The Varoma is the perfect accessory for all your steaming needs.
  • To make sure a few holes in the Varoma dish remain unobstructed for steam to circulate, place the butterfly whisk in the centre of the Varoma dish underneath the fish parcels to raise them off the bottom of the Varoma dish.
  • Instead of fresh herbs, you can use 2-3 tsp dried Herbes de Provence (to taste) in step 1.
