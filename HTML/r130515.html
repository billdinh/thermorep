Grissini with Parmesan and Herbs

Ingredients

  • 20 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp fresh rosemary leaves
  • ½ tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 240 g water
  • 15 g fresh yeast
  • olive oil, for greasing and sprinkling
  • 350 g strong white bread flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • semolina, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan, cayenne pepper, rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, oregano and bay leaf in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 1 min/37°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Add flour, salt and reserved cheese and herb mix then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Transfer to prepared bowl, cover and leave to rise in a warm place for approx. 40 minutes. Scrape down dough inside bowl with spatula then set aside in fridge for 4 hours.
  4. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  5. Divide rested dough into five portions and roll in semolina. Using a pasta machine, roll dough out thinly. Cut each piece into thin strips (approx. 5 mm) using a tagliatelle cutter or long sharp knife. Arrange strips on prepared baking tray, sprinkle with olive oil and sea salt. Bake for 8-10 minutes (200°C) until golden brown.
  6. Cool on a wire rack before serving or store in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, baking tray and paper, long sharp knife, rolling pin or pasta machine, wire rack, airtight jars or containers, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can also use a rolling pin to roll out dough.
