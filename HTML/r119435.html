Cheese puffs with honeyed goat's cheese

Ingredients

Goat's cheese filling

  • 50 g eschalots
  • 160 g goat's cheese
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Cheese puffs

  • 130 g Gruyère cheese, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 125 g water
  • 125 g full cream milk
  • 70 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg

Preparation

Goat's cheese filling

  1. Place eschalots into mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add goat's cheese, cream, vinegar and honey and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add salt and pepper to taste and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a shallow container and place into refrigerator for 2 hours or until firm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cheese puffs

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 4 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place water, milk, butter and salt into mixing bowl and warm 4 min/90°C/speed 3.
  4. Add flour and cook 5 min/80°C/speed 3, until mixture is glossy.
  5. Mix 1 min/speed 4, adding eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid, ensuring each is incorporated before adding the next. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add pepper, nutmeg and 100 g of the grated cheese, then mix 1 min/speed 3, until combined and smooth.
  7. Transfer dough into a pastry bag fitted with a round nozzle (approx. 1.5 cm). Pipe approx. 32 heaped tablespoon-size mounds onto prepared tray (3 cm apart). Sprinkle with remaining grated cheese and bake for 20 minutes (200°C), or until puffed and golden brown. Set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. To assemble, transfer goat's cheese filling into a piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm). Once puffs are completely cool, poke a hole in base and fill with goat's cheese filling. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

shallow freezable container, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, piping bag with a plain, round nozzle (1.5 cm), piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cheese puffs can also be served warm, without the Goat's cheese filling.
  • Cheese puffs can be frozen. If freezing, allow them to cool completely, then place into snaplock bags in the freezer. Reheat frozen or thawed puffs in a moderate oven (180°C).
  • If you have a dehydrator, make puffs extra crisp by placing cooled puffs in the dehydrator for 30 minutes at 50°C just before piping in filling and serving. This can also be done in a low oven (50°C), with the door slightly ajar.
