Berries and Cream Layer Cake

Ingredients

Sponge Cakes

  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 8 medium eggs
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 250 g self-raising flour
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt

Filling and Topping

  • 600 g cream, min. 30% fat, chilled (whipping or double)
  • 10 g caster sugar
  • 200 g fresh strawberries
  • 120 g fresh raspberries
  • 1 Tbsp icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Sponge Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease 2 springform tins (Ø 20 cm) and line bases with baking paper.
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 4 min/75°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 9 min/37°C/speed 4.
  4. Whisk again 3 min/speed 4 while very slowly pouring reserved melted butter onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream onto rotating blades.
  5. Around butterfly whisk, add flour and salt then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully finish stirring by hand with spatula. Transfer mixture to prepared tins (approx. 460 g per tin, see tip) and bake immediately for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk. Leave cakes to cool for 10 minutes before removing from tins and transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Filling and Topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whip speed 3, without setting a time, until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk, transfer whipped cream to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and 100 g strawberries in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. To assemble the cake, cut each sponge in half horizontally using a long knife. Place one bottom sponge half on a serving plate, spread ¼ reserved whipped cream on top, then add 2 Tbsp reserved strawberry purée over cream and combine a little with a fork. Dot 40 g raspberries around the edge. Repeat this step two more times, put the last layer of cake on top, spread remaining cream over, then decorate with remaining 100 g strawberries. Dust with icing sugar just before serving.

Useful Items

springform tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, wire rack, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use the Thermomix scales to measure out the 460 g cake batter for each cake tin. Turn scales on, then lift mixing bowl out and pour some of the cake batter out. When you return the mixing bowl to the base, it will show a minus figure indicating the weight removed (- 460 g).
  • Cream takes different times to whip according to how much fat is in it. 40% fat cream takes the shortest amount of time. Cream with less fat than this takes a longer time.
