Florentine potato cakes

Ingredients

  • 1 sweet potato (approx. 300 g), cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 potato (approx. 150 g), cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 400 g water
  • 5 whole eggs
  • 400 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • ½ tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, for frying
  • 2 tsp unsalted butter

Preparation

  1. Place both potatoes into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer mixture into a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and squeeze firmly to extract as much liquid as possible. Transfer mixture into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Place 4 of the eggs into it and cook 6 min/Varoma/speed 4 (see Tips).
  3. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh spinach into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 4. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Squeeze any excess moisture from spinach with aid of spatula and set aside.
  4. Add salt, pepper, flour, and baking powder to the reserved potato mixture, crack remaining egg into mixture and stir well to combine with aid of spatula.
  5. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add 1 tablespoon of the oil. Place 4 heaped tablespoonfuls of the potato mixture into pan, pressing each into a circle (approx. 8 cm in diameter). Fry for 3-4 minutes on each side, until cooked, golden brown and crispy. Transfer onto a plate and cover with a kitchen towel to keep warm. Place remaining 1 tablespoon oil into pan, add remaining 4 heaped tablespoonfuls of potato and fry for 3-4 minutes on each side (makes 8 cakes in total).
  6. Place frying pan over medium heat. Add butter and allow to melt. Add reserved spinach and season with salt and pepper. Fry for 1 minute or until heated through.
  7. When cool enough to handle, gently peel eggs and cut into halves. To serve, place 2 potato cakes onto a serving plate, top with one-quarter of the spinach and 2 egg halves (see Tips).

Useful Items

plate, bowl, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large frying pan, serving plates, frying pan, kitchen towel, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Using room temperature eggs or smaller eggs will reduce cooking time. Please see The Basic Cookbook for timings of both soft and hard boiled eggs. If you prefer soft boiled eggs, take care when peeling.
  • You can serve this dish with crispy bacon, smoked salmon or hot smoked trout. For a creamier version, sprinkle over crumbled feta or goat's cheese.
