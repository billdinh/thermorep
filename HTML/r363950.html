Feijoa celebration cake

Ingredients

Cakes

  • olive oil spray, for greasing
  • 100 g pecan nuts
  • 400 g feijoas (approx. 5 feijoas), peeled and cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 4 eggs
  • 350 g caster sugar
  • 350 g grapeseed oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp mixed spice
  • 450 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 440 g canned crushed pineapple, drained (approx. 230 g after draining)

Filling

  • 20 g stem ginger (see Tips)
  • 400 g feijoas (approx. 5 feijoas), peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith

Frosting

  • 20 - 30 g stem ginger, to taste (see Tips)
  • 200 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 500 g cream cheese, softened
  • 150 g icing sugar
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith

Preparation

Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line 2 springform cake tins (23 cm) with baking paper, then set aside.
  2. Place pecans into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place feijoas into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer into bowl with pecans and set aside.
  4. Place eggs, sugar, oil, cinnamon and mixed spice into mixing bowl and blend 5 min/speed 3.
  5. Add flour, bicarbonate of soda and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 5, with aid of spatula.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for a further 10 sec/speed 5.
  7. Weigh pineapple into mixing bowl, then transfer batter into bowl with feijoa mixture and gently fold to combine, taking care not to over mix. Divide batter evenly between prepared tins and bake for 45 minutes (180°C), until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cakes comes out clean. Leave in tins to cool for 5 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely. Continue with recipe while cakes cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add feijoas, sugar and orange zest and cook 15 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool to room temperature, then place into refrigerator to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Frosting

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, cream cheese, icing sugar and orange zest and mix 10 sec/speed 7, with aid of spatula.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 5 sec/speed 7 or until smooth.

Assembly

  1. Once cake has cooled, cut each reserved cake horizontally into 2 equal halves. Spread one-quarter of the frosting over base layer of cake. Top with one-third of the reserved feijoa filling and spread out evenly. Place another cake layer on top of filling and spread with another one-quarter of the frosting and one-third of the filling. Repeat with another layer of cake, frosting and filling. Top with final cake layer and spread with remaining frosting. Decorate as desired and serve immediately or place into the refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

springform cake tins (23 cm), baking paper, bowl, large bowl, wooden skewer, wire racks, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If using frozen feijoas, defrost completely and drain off any excess moisture before using.
  • You can find stem ginger at gourmet food stores or online. If stem ginger isn't available, you can replace it with minced crystallised ginger.
  • You can store assembled cake for 2-3 days in the refrigerator. Bring cake back to room temperature before serving.
