Millionaire's Chocolate Tart

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 35 g icing sugar
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg

Filling

  • 100 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 3 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 45 g golden caster sugar
  • 250 g caramel sauce (tinned)
  • 75 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, icing sugar, butter and egg yolk in mixing bowl then mix 25-35 sec/speed 4 until mixture begins to cling together. Tip dough out and bring together into a flattened ball. Wrap in cling film and place in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. On a lightly-floured surface, roll out chilled pastry (approx. 3 mm thick) then use to line a loose-based fluted square tart tin (22 cm), pressing well into corners. Trim edges with a knife, prick base with a fork and cover with cling film then place in fridge for a further 20 minutes. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 180°C.
  3. Once pastry shell has rested, remove cling film, line with baking paper and fill with baking beans. Bake blind for 15 minutes (180°C). Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C) until lightly golden. Remove from oven and leave to cool. Turn oven temperature down to 160°C. Meanwhile, make filling.

Filling

  1. Place dark chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, egg yolks and caster sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 10 min/37°C/speed 3.
  3. Add melted dark chocolate and mix 20 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk and finish mixing by hand with spatula if necessary.
  4. Spread caramel over base of tart case then pour over chocolate mixture. Clean mixing bowl.
  5. Place white chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3 min/50°C/speed 1. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a thin nozzle (2-3 mm), pipe lines of white chocolate across dark chocolate then drag a cocktail stick across the lines to create a pattern. Bake for 15-20 minutes (160°C) until set (may have a slight wobble).
  6. Leave to cool then serve warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking paper and baking beans, cocktail stick, piping bag and thin nozzle, loose-based fluted square tart tin (22 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This decadent tart is a dinner party version of the popular teatime treat - millionaire’s shortbread. Simple to make, yet so impressive to serve to your friends and family, enjoy warm drizzled with double cream for the ultimate indulgence.
  • Can also be made in a rectangular tart tin (36 cm x 12 cm) or fluted round tart tin (Ø 22 cm).
