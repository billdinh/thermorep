Sambhar

Ingredients

  • 50 g onions, quartered

  • 50 g tomatoes, halved

Masala

  • 25 g vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp chana dal (yellow dried split peas)
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp asafoetida
  • 2 - 4 dried Kashmiri chillies, to taste

Soup

  • 5 cm dried tamarind, soaked in hot water for 15 minutes
  • 1200 g water
  • 150 g chana dal (yellow dried split peas)
  • 100 g aubergines, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g butternut squash, peeled, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g fine green beans, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place onions and tomatoes in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Masala

  1. Place oil in mixing bowl and heat 2 min/85°C/speed 0.5.
  2. Add chana dal, fenugreek seeds, mustard seeds, asafoetida and chillies then fry 3 min/85°C/speed 0.5. Blend to a paste 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Soup

  1. Strain soaked tamarind through a fine sieve collecting tamarind water below. Discard pulp and set tamarind water aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl and bring to the boil 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add chana dal, aubergines, butternut squash, green beans and reserved chopped onions and tomatoes. Replace measuring cup with simmering basket, then simmer 20 min/95°C//speed 0.5.
  4. Add turmeric, sugar, salt and 2 Tbsp reserved tamarind water. Replace measuring cup with simmering basket and simmer 15 min/95°C//speed 0.5.
  5. Add reserved masala paste and stir 1 min/90°C//speed 1. Serve hot with Indian bread of choice.

Hints & Tricks

  • Sambhar can be served with a tarka, which is made by adding spices to very hot oil. The spice seeds pop and sizzle bringing out their intense flavour and aroma. For this recipe, a tarka made from fried onions, mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds added to hot oil and stirred in just before serving would work well.
  • This pulse-based vegetable soup originates from south India and is often served with rice or dosas, but it makes an excellent starter or accompaniment to an Indian spread of curries and rice. You can substitute the vegetables to your taste.
