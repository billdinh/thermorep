Chicken skewers and rice

Ingredients

  • 500 g water, plus extra for soaking skewers
  • 500 g skinless chicken thighs, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 70 g red onion
  • ½ stalk fresh lemongrass, white part only, cut into thin slices (optional)
  • 1 cm cube fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 tsp lime juice
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 pinches salt, to taste, plus extra to season
  • 2 pinches freshly ground black pepper, to taste, plus extra to season
  • 200 g basmati rice
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 tbsp light olive oil, for frying

Preparation

  1. Place 14 wooden skewers (see Tips) into a large bowl of water and set aside to soak (minimum of 10 minutes).
  2. Place chicken, onion, lemongrass (optional), ginger, lime juice, garlic, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 10 sec/speed 8, or until blended.
  4. Divide mixture into 14 portions. Using damp hands, mould mixture around the ends of skewers. Transfer onto a plate and cover with plastic wrap. Place into refrigerator until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear.
  6. Place water, coconut milk and stock paste into mixing bowl, re-insert simmering basket with rice and cook 15-18 min/100°C/speed 4 or until rice is cooked. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Season to taste and cover to keep warm.
  7. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Working in batches, add skewers to pan and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, or until cooked through and golden brown. Transfer 2-3 tablespoons of the rice into 14 small bowls and top each bowl with 1 chicken skewer. Serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, wooden skewers, 1 plate, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, large frying pan, serving bowls, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Some coconut milk products can be made using equipment that has come into contact with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern for you, be sure to check the label of your coconut milk or make your own (please refer to the Choc-mint milk recipe on this recipe chip).
  • For younger children, use smaller skewers or cut off the sharp ends of full-size skewers.
  • For adults, serve with your favourite chilli sauce.
