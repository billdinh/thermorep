Linzer torte

Ingredients

Berry chia jam

  • 250 g strawberries, hulled
  • 250 g raspberries
  • 70 g raw sugar
  • 1 ¼ tsp agar agar powder
  • 3 tsp white chia seeds

Linzer torte

  • 120 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 100 g hazelnuts
  • 100 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g wholemeal spelt flour
  • ¾ tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 - 3 tsp cold water

Preparation

Berry chia jam

  1. Place strawberries into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add raspberries, sugar and agar agar and cook 8 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Add chia seeds and cook 30 sec/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Transfer into a large sterilised sealable jar (approx. 600 ml) and seal immediately. Leave to set (approx. 3 hours). Store jam in refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Linzer torte

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a round fluted tart tin (23 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add flours, butter, baking powder, sugar, cinnamon, cloves and nutmeg and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add egg, vanilla and water and knead 15 sec/, or until just combined. Transfer pastry onto a lightly floured work surface and roll into a thick log. Separate out one-third of the pastry. Flatten both pastry portions into discs, wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for at least 10 minutes.
  5. Place larger portion of pastry onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured sheet of baking paper. Using a rolling pin, roll out to approx. 26 cm diameter (3 mm thickness), or until large enough to line base and sides of prepared tin. Drape pastry over rolling pin and ease pastry into tin. Gently press pastry into sides of tin. Trim overhanging edges with a knife, reserving pastry off-cuts for lattice strips. Place tart tin into refrigerator for 10 minutes.
  6. Combine remaining pastry disc with pastry off-cuts and roll out into a rectangle (approx. 23 x 20 cm). Cut pastry into strips (1.5 cm). If  pastry is too soft, place into refrigerator for 5-10 minutes to firm.
  7. Spread jam (approx. 580 g) over pastry case and transfer onto a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm). Weave or place pastry strips in a lattice pattern over tart, pressing edges to seal. Re-roll any pastry off-cuts, if needed. Trim pastry edges and bake for 45-50 minutes (160°C), or until pastry is golden brown and jam is bubbly. Set aside to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

sealable sterilised jar, round fluted tart tin (23 cm), plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, baking paper, rolling pin, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you prefer you can use any type of jam, but the Berry chia jam has much less sugar than conventional jams.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this recipe – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
