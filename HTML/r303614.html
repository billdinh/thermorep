Soup dumplings (xiao long bao)

Ingredients

Soup (jelly)

  • 200 g bacon, cut into pieces
  • 2 slices fresh ginger (1 cm), peeled
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 400 g water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 ½ tsp gelatine powder

Dough

  • 240 g baker's flour
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 350 g boiling water
  • 1 tbsp oil

Filling

  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 20 g fresh ginger, peeled
  • 200 g pork mince
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine
  • 100 g shrimp (small), peeled (see Tips)

Assembly and cooking

  • oil, for greasing
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 500 g boiling water

Preparation

Soup (jelly)

  1. Place bacon, ginger and spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add water, salt and pepper and cook 20 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Using the simmering basket, strain stock into a jug and discard contents.
  3. Place stock back into mixing bowl, add gelatine and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 2 or until gelatine dissolves. Transfer mixture into a square cake tin (20 cm) and set aside to cool to room temperature, then cover and place into refrigerator overnight to set (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Place flours and boiling water into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  2. Add oil, then knead 5 min/. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside for 1 hour to rest. Clean and dry mixing bowl (see Tips).

Filling

  1. Place spring onions/shallots and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add pork, salt, pepper, soy sauce, cooking wine and shrimp and combine 1 min/speed 3. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator while dough is resting (50-60 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly and cooking

  1. Remove filling and jelly from refrigerator. Cut jelly into small cubes (1 cm), add to filling and stir gently to combine.
  2. Line Varoma dish and tray with baking paper, poking a few small holes in paper to allow steam to circulate. Grease paper lightly with oil and set Varoma aside. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured work surface and divide dough into 4 equal portions, then divide each portion into 10 pieces (approx. 15 g each). Shape each piece into a ball, then roll out into a disc (approx. 10 cm), keeping dough thicker in the middle and thinner on the edges. Place 2 teaspoons of filling mixture into centre of each disc, then pull up edges (into a money bag shape) and pleat edges together, ensuring they are well sealed. Place half of the dumplings into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  4. Transfer remaining dumplings into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Serve while hot (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, rolling pin, square cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, jug, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We suggest making the filling while the dough rests.
  • You can replace the small shrimp with prawns, cut into pieces, if preferred.
  • Store the jelly in the refrigerator until ready to use; it will be easier to assemble when cold and firm.
  • Dumplings can be assembled and frozen, then cooked from frozen. Simply place into Varoma and steam for 15-17 minutes, as per recipe.
  • These dumplings are fragile once cooked as the filling is liquid, so take cake when serving.
