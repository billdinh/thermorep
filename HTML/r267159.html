Prawn Dumplings

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 100 g soy sauce
  • 25 g brown rice vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 5 - 10 dashes Tabasco, to taste
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

Dumpling Wrappers

  • 150 g water
  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 10 g water (optional)

Filling

  • 10 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 5 spring onions, sliced (3 mm)
  • 150 g medium raw prawns, peeled
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 Tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper

Assembly and Cooking

  • 1 carrot, sliced (2 mm)
  • 500 g water, for steaming

Preparation

Sauce

  1. Place soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, Tabasco and sesame seeds in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Dumpling Wrappers

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 3 min/80°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and knead 2 min/.
  3. If dough seems dry, add water and knead a further 1 min/. Tip dough out and shape into a ball. Cover with cling film and leave to rest for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place ginger in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add spring onions and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add prawns, sesame oil, rice wine, soy sauce and pepper then mix 15 sec/speed 5.

Assembly and Cooking

  1. Divide dough into 26 small balls (approx. 10 g) and cover with cling film or a damp tea towel to prevent them drying out. On a lightly-floured work surface, roll each ball into a thin disc (Ø approx. 10 cm).
  2. Place 1 tsp reserved filling in the centre of each and fold the wrapper in half over the filling to make a semi-circle shape. Pinch the seam in the centre and hold it with one hand while using the other hand to pleat the top edge of the wrapper from the middle outwards on both sides. Place each dumpling on a slice of carrot and arrange in Varoma dish and tray so they are not touching.
  3. Place water in mixing bowl. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and serve dumplings hot with reserved sauce.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, tea towel

Hints & Tricks

  • Dumplings are usually eaten in the north of China at Chinese New Year. Because the shape of dumplings is similar to the shape of ancient Chinese gold ingots, the currency of the day, they are believed to bring prosperity to the new year.
  • Shop-bought, ready-made dumpling pastry can be used if preferred. In this case, you only need to prepare the sauce and filling.
  • Dumplings can be prepared in advance and refrigerated. If steaming chilled dumplings, add a further 5 minutes to cooking time in step 10.
