Gooseberry Curd Layer Cake with Elderflower Icing

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 230 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 230 g caster sugar
  • 30 g elderflower cordial
  • 230 g self-raising flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda

Icing and Assembly

  • 100 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 - 1 ½ Tbsp elderflower cordial
  • 200 g gooseberry curd, homemade
  • fresh gooseberries, to decorate

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease sides and line bases of 3 cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add elderflower cordial, flour, eggs, baking powder and bicarbonate of soda then mix 30 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 40 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Divide evenly between prepared tins and bake for 25-30 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean.
  5. Allow sponges to cool in tins for 5 minutes then remove to wire racks to cool completely.

Icing and Assembly

  1. Once sponges are cool, place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in icing sugar and elderflower cordial. Remove bowl and mix together.
  2. Spread half the gooseberry curd (approx. 6 Tbsp) over the top of one sponge. Place another sponge on top then spread with remaining curd. Finish with third sponge layer. Drizzle elderflower icing on top, allowing it to drizzle down sides of cake.
  3. Decorate with fresh gooseberries and a dusting of icing sugar then serve.

Useful Items

3 round cake tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, skewer, wire racks, palette knife, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Can also be baked in two cake tins. Bake for 30-35 minutes. Use for a two-tiered cake, or slice each sponge in half for a four-tiered cake.

Gooseberries often lose out in baking to the more popular summer berries, but this beautifully delicate, floral cake is a real afternoon tea showstopper.
