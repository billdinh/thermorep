Plum and Almond Traybake

Ingredients

  • 60 g blanched almonds
  • 220 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 220 g caster sugar
  • 270 g self-raising flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 9 plums, halved, stone removed
  • 2 Tbsp flaked almonds
  • 1 Tbsp apricot jam
  • 1 Tbsp water, boiling
Nutrition
per 1 piece

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a brownie tin (32 cm x 18 cm) with baking paper, allowing edges to overhang (see tip).
  2. Place almonds in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 1 min/speed 4.
  4. Add flour, eggs, vanilla extract and reserved ground almonds then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 30 sec/speed 4.
  5. Transfer to prepared tin and spread out evenly. Press plum halves, cut side up, into mixture then scatter with flaked almonds. Bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) until golden and a skewer inserted in sponge comes out clean.
  6. Leave to cool in tin for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack.
  7. Dissolve apricot jam in boiling water then brush over surface of cake. Serve warm or cold, on its own or with crème fraîche.

Useful Items

brownie tin (32 cm x 18 cm), baking paper, skewer, pastry brush, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A great way to enjoy sweet English plums, particularly in the autumn months when the fruits are at their ripest. Combined with almonds, it's delicious served warm with crème fraîche as a pudding or cold as a teatime treat. Serving 18, it's also a good choice when there's a crowd to feed!
  • Use the overhanging baking paper as handles to help remove the traybake from the tin.
  • Substitute plums with fresh or tinned apricots.
  • Add 1 tsp almond extract in step 4 for an even more intense nutty flavour.
