Crunchy rice crackers

Ingredients

  • 410 g brown rice
  • 1280 g water
  • 1 tbsp flaxseeds (linseeds)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 50 g olive oil

Preparation

  1. Insert simmering basket and weigh 100 g of the rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear.
  2. Place 1200 g of the water into mixing bowl. Re-insert simmering basket with rice and cook 30 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside to cool for 10 minutes. Discard cooking liquid. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm).
  4. Place remaining 310 g rice into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10.
  5. Add flaxseeds (linseeds), salt, oil, remaining 80 g water and reserved cooked rice and blend 20 sec/speed 7 or until dough forms.
  6. Divide dough into 2 equal portions and shape into discs. Place each dough portion between 2 sheets of baking paper and roll out (approx. 5 mm thickness). Remove top layer of baking paper and place dough with bottom sheet of paper onto prepared baking trays. Score into squares (approx. 6 cm) and then score into triangles (see Tips). Bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C), until brown. Turn off oven. Remove trays from oven and set aside to cool for 10 minutes or until biscuits are cool enough to handle. Break into pieces and place back onto trays. Return to cooling oven for 1 hour until crisp. Place into a sealable storage container until ready to serve.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, sealable storage container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We cut these crackers into triangles to use as "quills" in the Cheese and bacon hedgehog (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip). However, you can cut them into any desired shape.
  • These crackers taste better the next day, so we recommend making them at least a day in advance. They can be stored in a sealable container for up to 2 weeks.
