Pizza Bianca with Artichoke and Truffle Oil - Pizza Bianca con carciofi e olio al tartufo

Ingredients

Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 400 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus 2 Tbsp and extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste

Topping

  • 100 g mozzarella cheese, drained, cut in pieces
  • 100 g Fontina cheese, cut in pieces
  • 240 g tinned artichoke hearts (drained weight), quartered
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • white truffle oil, to taste

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add remaining 170 g water, flour, 20 g oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Tip dough out onto a work surface, shape into a ball then transfer to prepared bowl. Cover with cling film and leave until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Topping

  1. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 220°C and line 2 pizza trays with baking paper (see tip).
  2. Place mozzarella and Fontina in mixing bowl then grate 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Divide rested dough into 2 equal pieces. On a lightly-floured surface, roll out each piece into a circle (Ø 30 cm) then place on prepared trays. With floured fingertips, make dimples over bases to prevent the crust from ballooning then bake for 5-10 minutes (220°C) until lightly browned.
  4. Remove pizzas from oven and drizzle 1 Tbsp olive oil over each. Scatter over artichokes then sprinkle over grated cheese. Season with salt and pepper. Return to oven for 5-10 minutes (220°C) until cheese is melted.
  5. Drizzle pizzas with truffle oil, to taste, then serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking paper, rolling pin, cling film, pizza trays (Ø 30 cm), large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • There is no need to line non-stick pizza trays.
  • In Rome, this refers to a plain pizza base that is cooked without toppings, drizzled with olive oil and salt and eaten as a street food. In other parts of Italy, pizza bianca (white pizza) is just that - a pizza without tomato sauce. Our version showcases two popular Italian cheeses, Fontina and mozzarella, as well as artichokes and white truffle oil. Truffles are a traditional product of Tuscany where they are used in everything from scrambled eggs to risotto. They are known as one of the most luxurious foods in the world and fetch a hefty price at market. Truffle oil is a more economical way of including this aromatic flavour in your dishes, just look out for a good quality oil with no artificial ingredients.
