Chicken and port pâté

Ingredients

Pâté

  • 1 eschalot, peeled and cut into halves
  • 1 bacon rasher (approx. 40 g), rind removed and cut into pieces
  • 50 g butter, cut into cubes
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp ground allspice
  • 40 g port
  • 100 g chicken livers, washed and patted dry
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 40 g double cream
  • 1 pinch ground ginger

White balsamic reduction

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 500 g white balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g dry sherry
  • 80 g raw sugar
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 8 white peppercorns (see Tips)
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into halves

Jelly topping

  • 1 gelatine leaf (titanium strength)
  • 80 g port
  • 40 g white balsamic reduction

Preparation

Pâté

  1. Place eschalot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add bacon rasher, butter, dried thyme, ground allspice and port and sauté 4 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Add chicken livers, sea salt and ground pepper and chop 5 sec/speed 6.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add double cream and ground ginger and blend 15 sec/speed 9. Pour into a large ramekin (8 cm) or 2 smaller ramekins and place into refrigerator to set for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

White balsamic reduction

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl then repeat grating 10 sec/speed 8 until a fine consistency is achieved.
  3. Add all remaining ingredients and cook 30 min/Varoma//speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a hot-rinsed sealable jar or bottle and set aside to cool until ready use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Jelly topping

  1. Once pâté has set, place gelatine leaf into a ceramic or glass bowl and cover with ice cold water for 5 minutes or until soft and swollen.
  2. Place port and cooled white balsamic reduction into mixing bowl and cook 2 min 30 sec/Varoma/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Squeeze water out of gelatine, then add gelatine to mixing bowl and combine 10 sec/speed 1 or until gelatine dissolves. Allow jelly mixture to cool .
  4. Gently pour cooled jelly over pâté and return to the refrigerator for 30 minutes, or until jelly has set, before serving.

Useful Items

1 large ramekin (8 cm) or 2 smaller ramekins, sealable jar or bottle, fine mesh sieve, ceramic or glass bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute 8 white peppercorns with 8 black peppercorns.
  • No cheese platter is ever complete without homemade pâté. The port jelly provides a pleasantly sweet contrast to the rich flavour of the pâté.
  • Use leftover port to make the port sauce in Lamb rack with port sauce and parnsip purée.
  • Freeze remaining chicken livers in 100 g portions for future use.
  • Store remaining White balsamic reduction in a cool dark place to use in other recipes.
