Cashew bread French toast

Ingredients

Cashew bread

  • coconut oil, for greasing
  • 130 g raw cashews
  • 4 eggs
  • 45 g pure maple syrup
  • 3 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 50 g almond milk
  • 30 g coconut flour
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom

French toast

  • 2 eggs
  • 80 g almond milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil, plus extra for frying
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp pure maple syrup, plus extra for drizzling (optional)
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom
  • 100 g pecan nut halves
  • 1 - 2 bananas, peeled and sliced for serving
  • 200 g coconut yoghurt, for serving

Preparation

Cashew bread

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (18 x 7 x 6 cm) with baking paper, leaving a 2 cm overhang and set aside.
  2. Place cashews into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add all remaining cashew bread ingredients and mix 7 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing 7 sec/speed 4.
  4. Transfer into prepared tin and bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the bread comes out clean.
  5. Leave bread to cool in tin for 10 minutes then using overhanging baking paper, remove bread from tin and transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving or place into refrigerator until ready to use.

French toast

  1. Place eggs, almond milk and vanilla into mixing bowl and combine 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place coconut oil, cinnamon, maple syrup, cardamom and pecans into mixing bowl and sauté 4 min/Varoma//speed 1. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Remove cashew bread from refrigerator and slice into 8 slices (2 cm thick).
  4. Place a heavy-based frying pan (26 cm) over medium heat and add 1 tsp coconut oil.
  5. Dip 4 slices of the cashew bread into reserved egg mixture, ensuring slices are completely submerged and both sides are coated. Using tongs, carefully pick up each slice and allow any excess egg mixture to drip off. Place slices into frying pan and fry each side (approx. 1-2 minutes) until golden brown. Transfer to a plate and cover to keep warm. Repeat with remaining 4 slices of cashew bread and egg mixture.
  6. Serve French toast warm, topped with reserved pecan mixture, banana, coconut yoghurt and extra maple syrup (optional).

Useful Items

loaf tin (18 x 7 x 6 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, large bowl, bowl, heavy-based frying pan (26 cm), tongs, oven, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Prepare bread in advance, wrap in plastic wrap or place into a sealable storage container and store in the refrigerator to make a quick and delicious breakfast. Bread will last in refrigerator for up to 1 week. Bread can also be frozen for future use.
  • You can also use this bread as an "everyday" bread for sandwiches or toast.
