Mongolian lamb hot pot

Ingredients

  • 2 cm cube fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp light brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 600 g lamb fillet , slightly frozen, cut into strips (6 x 1 cm)
  • 200 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 40 g peanut oil
  • 1 tbsp sambal oelek, taste
  • 1 tbsp hoi sin sauce
  • ½ tsp Chinese five spice
  • 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Preparation

  1. Place ginger, egg, soy sauce, brown sugar, cornflour, bicarbonate of soda and garlic cloves into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer marinade into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sliced lamb into a large bowl, pour marinade on top of lamb and cover bowl with plastic wrap. Place in the refrigerator to marinate for at least 1 hour (2-3 hours is preferable).
  3. Place brown onion and peanut oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 4 min 30 sec/100°C/speed 1
  4. Add marinated lamb and marinade, sambal oelek, hoi sin sauce or kecap manis, Chinese five spice, rice wine vinegar and sesame oil and cook 6 min/100°C//speed . Mix well using spatula and check seasoning. Cook for a further 1-3 min/100°C//speed , or until lamb is cooked to your preference. Serve hot.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, ThermoServer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with steamed Asian greens and a side of cooked rice or on a bed of warm noodles.
  • Add in extra seasonal vegetables to the dish as desired. Cooking times will need to be adjusted accordingly.
  • Sambal oelek is an Indonesian chilli paste and can be found at Asian markets or in the Asian food aisle at some major supermarkets.
