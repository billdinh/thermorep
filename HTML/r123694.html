Palak paneer

Ingredients

Ghee

  • 250 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces

Paneer

  • 1500 g full cream milk
  • 30 g white vinegar

Palak curry

  • 300 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1 tsp salt flakes
  • 500 g water
  • 1 red onion (approx. 100 g)
  • 15 g piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into slices
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 fresh green chillies, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (approx. 200 g), cut into pieces, plus 1 medium tomato (approx. 100 g) cut into dice, for garnishing
  • 1 fresh red chilli, cut into thin slices, for garnishing

Preparation

Ghee

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1 , without measuring cup. Remove mixing bowl and swirl to ensure melted butter is not catching on bottom of bowl.
  2. Insert measuring cup. Cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until white milk solids separate from the golden, clarified butter. If necessary, swirl again and cook for a further 2 min/Varoma/speed 1, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to ensure it does not burn.
  3. Strain butter through a nut milk bag or muslin cloth into a sealable jar or container. Discard milk solids. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Paneer

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 14 min/80°C/speed 2 (stop cooking when milk reaches 80°C;  this may occur before the cooking time has finished).
  2. Add vinegar and mix 1 min/90°C/speed 1, until milk separates into curds and whey. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 1 minute.
  3. Pour mixture into a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and firmly squeeze to extract whey into a large bowl or jug. Curds will remain in nut milk bag. Transfer whey into a sealable container and place into refrigerator (see Tips). Set curds (paneer) aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Palak curry

  1. Place Varoma dish onto mixing bowl lid and weigh baby spinach into it. Season with salt and set Varoma aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Place Varoma dish with spinach into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 4 min/Varoma/speed 2. Carefully toss spinach with aid of spatula and set Varoma aside to drain and cool. Discard steaming water. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Once spinach is cool enough to handle, firmly squeeze to remove excess water. Place spinach into mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 7.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 1 sec/speed 7, until desired an even consistency is achieved. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place onion, ginger, garlic, chillies and fenugreek seeds into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add cumin and 50 g of the reserved ghee and cook 6 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  8. Add tomatoes and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  9. Add reserved spinach and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  10. Add half of the paneer into mixing bowl and combine 5 sec//speed 2.
  11. Transfer into serving bowls. Garnish with remaining paneer (crumbled), diced tomato and sliced chillies before serving.

Useful Items

serving bowls, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, sealable container, sealable storage jar or container, large bowl, glass bowl or large jug, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store this ghee in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. Ghee has a high smoke point, making it excellent to use when frying.
  • Whey can be used in dressings, risotto, pickling mixtures, soups and smoothies.
