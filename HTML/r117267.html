Cake Pops

Ingredients

Cake

  • 1 tsp butter, for greasing
  • 2 medium eggs
  • ½ tsp vanilla sugar
  • 80 g sugar
  • 60 g self-raising flour
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Decoration

  • 200 g mascarpone
  • 250 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 2 Tbsp sprinkles, various
Nutrition

741 kJ / 177 kcal

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a spring form cake tin (Ø 22 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, vanilla sugar and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 6 min/37°C/speed 4. Whisk 6 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and salt then mix 4 sec/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk and scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix gently. Transfer to prepared tin.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes (180°C) until golden and a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Remove from oven and leave in tin to cool for 5 minutes then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Decoration

  1. When cake has cooled, cut in pieces (approx. 4 cm). Place cake pieces in mixing bowl and shred 2 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add mascarpone and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Using your hands, form 16 balls (approx. 3 cm). Place on a tray and set aside in fridge to firm up for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place 50 g white chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 2 min/50°C/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl.
  5. Dip blunt tip of a cake pop stick in melted chocolate then gently push halfway into ball. Repeat with remaining balls then return tray to fridge for 30 minutes.
  6. Once cake pops have set on the sticks, place remaining 200 g white chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 4 min/50°C/speed . Transfer to a bowl.
  7. Dip each ball in the melted chocolate, turning so that it is completely coated with a thick layer. Hold above bowl and keep turning until all excess chocolate has run back into bowl. Decorate with sprinkles or other edible decorations, as desired. Allow to stand upright while chocolate sets then place in fridge until ready to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, spring form tin (Ø 22 cm), wire rack, skewer, baking tray, cake pop sticks, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you do not have a cake pop stand, you can place in mugs filled with sugar. Alternatively, serve cake pops on a tray or plate with the sticks in the air. If you choose to do this focus on decorating the underside area around the stick.
  • For the best finish to your cake pops make sure the cake balls are smooth before coating with chocolate.
