Hibiscus Cheesecake

Ingredients

Crust

  • 3 ½ oz unsalted butter, sliced, plus extra to grease
  • 7 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 large egg

Hibiscus Water

  • ½ oz dried hibiscus flowers
  • 4 oz water
  • 3 tbsp sugar

Cheesecake Filling

  • 1 packet/envelope gelatin powder, unflavored
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 5 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 8 oz cream cheese, cut into pieces
  • 3 oz sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 oz fresh raspberries, plus extra to garnish

Preparation

Crust

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and flour a tart pan (8 in.) and set aside.
  2. Place flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, butter and egg into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  3. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and form into a flattened ball. Using a rolling pin, roll out dough (approx. 9½-10 in.). Line prepared tart pan with the dough, prick with a fork to avoid air bubbles.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes (350°F) or until golden brown. Remove from oven and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Hibiscus Water

  1. Place hibiscus flowers, water and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Strain with simmering basket and set aside the hibiscus water discarding the cooked flowers.

Cheesecake Filling

  1. Place gelatin powder and water into mixing bowl and mix 2 sec/speed 3. Stir with a spatula to ensure all gelatin has dissolved and allow to rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Add whipping cream and heat 5 min/195°F/speed 2.
  3. Add cream cheese, sugar and vanilla and mix 15 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add raspberries and mix 2 sec/speed 3.5.
  5. Pour the cheese filling into the baked crust and add one tablespoon at a time of the hibiscus water, mixing lightly every time you add one tablespoon to get a marbled effect.
  6. Refrigerate for 1 hour or until solid.
  7. Garnish with additional raspberries. Serve chilled.

Useful Items

rolling pin, tart pan (8 in.), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Look for dried, edible hibiscus flowers (often called “flor de jamaica”) in specialty shops and Mexican food markets.
  • Pregnant or nursing women should consult with their physicians before consuming hibiscus.
