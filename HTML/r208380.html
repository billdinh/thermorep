Argentinian Chicken Empanadas

Ingredients

Empanada Pastry

  • 100 g water
  • 175 g unsalted butter, diced
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 350 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 medium egg

Chicken Filling and Assembly

  • 60 g onions, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 240 g chicken breast fillets, skinless, cubed (3 cm)
  • 1 red pepper (approx. 150 g), cut in pieces
  • 8 green olives, pitted
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ - ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • milk, to glaze

Preparation

Empanada Pastry

  1. Place water, butter and salt in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/60°C/speed 2.
  2. Add plain flour, baking powder and egg then knead 2 min/. Transfer to a piece of cling film. Shape dough into a ball, cover with cling film, then place in fridge and allow to cool for 3 hours. Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Chicken Filling and Assembly

  1. Place onions and garlic clove in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chicken breast fillets, red pepper and green olives then chop 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add olive oil, tomato purée, smoked paprika, ground cumin, salt and black pepper then sauté 7 min/120°C//speed 1. Transfer to a bowl, set aside and allow to cool.
  4. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  5. On a floured surface, shape the cooled dough into a roll (30 cm) and divide into 14 pieces. Roll out each piece to a circle approx. Ø 12 cm, 3 mm thick. Divide filling between pastry circles (approx. 1.5-2 level Tbsp each) and moisten edges with water. Fold pastry over to enclose filling and crimp edges to seal. Arrange empanadas on prepared baking tray and brush with milk to glaze. Bake for 15 minutes (200°C), until risen and golden. Serve empanadas hot or cold.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray and paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If crimping with a fork, press down hard to ensure crimp detail can be seen in the pastry after cooking.
