Sweet Potato Stuffed Shells with Cauliflower Alfredo Sauce

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 30 conchiglie pasta (lg. shells)
  • 3 oz yellow onions, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 12 oz cauliflower florets (approx. ½ med. head)
  • 11 oz water
  • 1 ½ tsp vegetable stock paste
  • 5 oz coconut cream
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste
  • &frac18; tsp white pepper

Filling

  • 5 oz yellow onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 16 oz sweet potatoes, cut into 1 in. pieces
  • 4 oz water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable stock paste
  • 11 oz ricotta cheese
  • 4 oz baby spinach
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
393 kJ / 94 kcal

3 g

Preparation

Sauce

  1. Prepare pasta on the stove top according to package instructions. Meanwhile make sauce.
  2. Place onions, garlic and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 5 min/250°F/speed 1.
  3. Add cauliflower and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Add water and stock paste and cook 13 min/212°F/speed 2.
  4. Drain cooked pasta shells through Varoma dish. To avoid clumping after cooking, spread shells out onto a baking sheet and set aside.
  5. Add coconut cream, nutritional yeast, salt and pepper and increasing speed gradually purée 30 sec/speed 4-9. Pour into casserole dish and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 350°F.

Filling

  1. Place onions and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add sweet potatoes and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add water and stock paste and cook 10 min/212°F/speed 1.
  4. Add ricotta, spinach and thyme and with aid of spatula mix 15 sec//speed 4.
  5. With a spoon, generously fill cooked pasta shells with sweet potato mixture. Arrange shells on top of sauce in casserole dish. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes (350°F).
  6. Remove foil and bake a further 10 minutes (350°F). Serve hot.

Useful Items

aluminum foil, casserole dish (approx. 12 in. x 9½ in. x 2 in.), baking sheet, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Nutritional yeast is not to be confused with brewer's yeast or baking yeast. It is a natural flavor booster that lends a cheesy, nutty or savory flavor to many dishes. Available in the natural food section of your supermarket or in health food stores.
  • Serve cauliflower alfredo sauce with another pasta of choice, e.g. linguine and clams or penne with mushrooms.
