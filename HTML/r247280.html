Curried Panini Bread - Panini al curry e ricotta

Ingredients

  • 250 g milk
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 heaped tsp curry powder
  • 500 g pasta flour (type 00) , plus extra for dusting
  • 80 g ricotta cheese
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 10 g fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place milk and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add curry powder and flour then knead 1 min/.
  3. Add ricotta, butter and salt then knead 2 min/. Tip out onto a floured work surface.
  4. Line a baking tray with baking paper and dust with flour. Divide dough into 8 pieces, form each into a ball then arrange, spaced apart, on prepared tray. Using a smooth-blade knife, cut a cross on the surface of the balls, dust with more flour then cover with a clean tea towel. Set aside until doubled in size (approx. 1½ hours).
  5. Remove tea towel and leave to rise for a further 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until browned. Leave to cool before serving or store until needed (see tips).

Useful Items

tea towel, smooth-blade knife, baking paper, baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The word panino comes from ‘pane’, the Italian word for bread, and refers to a sandwich made from bread that isn’t sliced. The roll or bread is cut horizontally, filled with deli ingredients such as cheese or sliced meats and served cold, or pressed in a grill and served warm. In many English-speaking countries the name panini refers specifically to pressed or toasted sandwiches. The hint of curry seasoning added to our dough provides a little twist to a classic recipe.
  • If short of time, use 1½ tsp dried instant yeast or 10 g fresh yeast and leave to rise for 1 hour.
  • Place the rolls in a paper food bag and store in the fridge for 2-3 days or put them in a freezer bag and freeze for 30-40 days. Before eating, thaw the rolls then warm in a hot oven.
