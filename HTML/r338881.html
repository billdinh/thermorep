Chicken Pastilla

Ingredients

  • 150 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 150 g blanched almonds
  • 20 - 30 g sugar, to taste
  • 3 Tbsp fresh parsley leaves
  • 3 Tbsp fresh coriander leaves (optional)
  • 200 g onions, halved
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • 1 ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground white pepper
  • 600 g chicken breasts, skinless, cut in pieces (approx. 2 cm)
  • 50 g water
  • ½ vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 3 medium eggs
  • 8 sheets filo pastry

Preparation

  1. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 3 min/100°C/speed 0.5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place almonds and sugar in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place parsley and coriander (if using) in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add onions and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 50 g melted butter, saffron, cinnamon, ginger, coriander, salt and pepper then cook 3 min/120°C/speed .
  6. Add chicken, water and vegetable stock then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 15 min/Varoma//speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 160°C. Line the base of a springform tin (Ø 24 cm) with baking paper and brush sides of tin with melted butter.
  7. Add eggs and mix 1 min//speed 2.5.
  8. Line prepared tin with 5 overlapping filo pastry sheets, brushing each layer with a little melted butter and letting the edges overhang. Place chicken mixture inside and cover with 2 more filo pastry sheets. Add almond mixture and spread evenly, drizzle with some melted butter then seal by folding overhanging pastry edges to the middle. Cover with remaining pastry sheet, fold it to the middle and brush generously with melted butter. Bake for 20 minutes (160°C). Remove case of the springform tin, brush pastilla with melted butter again all around and bake for a further 20 minutes (160°C).
  9. Leave pastilla to cool briefly then serve warm.

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 24 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For an authentic Moroccan pastilla, sprinkle with some icing sugar and cinnamon before serving.
  • The pastilla tastes great the next day, briefly warmed up in the oven.
  • Replace 100 g chicken meat with 100 g frozen peas to give a nice colour. Just add peas with eggs in step 7.
  • Reserve and sprinkle some of the almond mixture over the pastilla before baking.
