Vegetable Stock Paste

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (1¼ in.) (optional)
  • 7 oz celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 9 oz carrots, cut into pieces
  • 3 ½ oz onions, halved
  • 3 ½ oz tomatoes, cut into pieces
  • 5 ½ oz zucchini, cut into pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 oz fresh mushrooms
  • 1 dried bay leaf (optional)
  • 6 sprigs mixed fresh herbs (e.g. basil, sage, rosemary), leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley
  • 4 ½ oz coarse salt
  • 1 oz dry white wine
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place celery, carrots, onions, tomatoes, zucchini, garlic, mushrooms, bay leaf, mixed fresh herbs and parsley into mixing bowl and chop with aid of spatula 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add salt, wine and olive oil. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid and cook 25 min/Varoma/speed 2. Mixture will become thick and fairly dry.
  4. Remove simmering basket, add reserved grated Parmesan, insert measuring cup and blend 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer into an airtight jar and allow to cool before using or storing in refrigerator.

Useful Items

airtight jars or containers

Hints & Tricks

  • If vegetables are very fresh (and therefore have a high water content), cook for 30 minutes.
  • 1 heaping tsp stock paste is roughly equivalent to 1 store-bought vegetable stock cube.
  • To make vegetable stock, use 1 heaping tsp stock paste per 16 oz water.
  • Stock paste keeps in the refrigerator for several months.
  • The salt in this recipe is used as the preserving agent. When mixed with water as described above, the stock does not taste too salty.
  • If you wish to decrease the salt in the recipe, you must store the stock paste in the freezer.
  • Adapt the stock paste recipe using the natural ingredients of your choice.
  • Add more mushrooms for a vegetable stock paste with a stronger mushroom flavor.
