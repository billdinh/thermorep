Manhattan Clam Chowder

Ingredients

  • 1 oz unsalted butter
  • 3 oz thick cut bacon, diced (approx. ¼ in.)
  • 9 oz yellow onions, quartered (approx. 1 large)
  • 3 oz celery, cut into 1 in. pieces (approx. 2 stalks)
  • 3 oz carrots, cut into 1 in. pieces (approx. 1 medium carrot)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper, to taste
  • ¼ tsp dried chili flakes, to taste
  • 19 ½ oz canned chopped clams (3 x 6.5 oz cans), reserve the clam juice
  • 9 oz Yukon gold potatoes
  • 28 oz canned crushed tomatoes
  • 12 - 15 oz clam juice, as needed
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only (approx. 20 leaves)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place butter and bacon into mixing bowl and without measuring cup sauté 10 min/250°F//speed 0.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté a further 2 min/250°F//speed 0.5. Leave approximately 2 tbsp of bacon fat in mixing bowl, drain bacon on paper towels and set aside.
  2. Add onions, celery, carrots, garlic, cayenne and chili flakes. Chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 5 min/250°F//speed 1.
  3. Drain clams through simmering basket into a bowl. Reserve clam juice (approx. 13 oz.) and set aside. Refrigerate clams until needed.
  4. Place potatoes, tomatoes, reserved clam juice (plus additional clam juice to total 24 oz), thyme and reserved bacon into mixing bowl. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and cook 25 min/250°F/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved clams, salt and pepper and stir 1 min//speed 1. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

paper towels, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Garnish with chopped fresh herbs, a drizzle of cream, and serve with sliced sourdough, garlic bread, or saltine crackers.
  • For a slightly clearer broth, replace crushed tomatoes with canned whole peeled tomatoes (including juice). Simply cut into pieces (approx. 1 in.) before adding into mixing bowl and continuing with the recipe.
