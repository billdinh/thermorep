Quick Puff Pastry

Ingredients

  • 200 g unsalted butter, diced (1-2 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g water, cold
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place butter, plain flour, water and salt in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Remove pastry dough from mixing bowl, form into a ball and flatten to a rough square. Wrap pastry square in cling film and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  2. Unwrap dough and place on a lightly floured work surface. Using a rolling pin, roll out dough to a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Fold dough into thirds and give folded dough a quarter turn so that the fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice more, then return to fridge for 20 minutes or place in freezer for 5 minutes before using.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  4. Roll dough into a rectangle (5 mm thick) and cut into required shape or shapes right before baking. Bake for 20 minutes (200°C) for one large piece of pastry, 12-15 minutes (200°C) for smaller pastries, or as directed by other recipes. Allow to cool on a wire rack before serving or storing in an airtight container.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking tray and baking paper, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This Thermomix® recipe simplifies a pastry that is traditionally more difficult to make. Chop frozen butter into flour in seconds, then create the layers by rolling and folding the pastry. It also freezes well – simply defrost in the fridge before baking.
  • This pastry serves as a base for cakes, pastries, cream puffs and fruit tarts.
  • The success of this recipe depends on keeping the pastry dough chilled so the layers of butter do not melt. A cold work surface (marble or stone) and a cool room temperature are helpful. If at any time during the process the dough feels warm, place in the freezer for 5 minutes before rolling out again. To prevent the dough from sticking to the work surface, whenever necessary use a light dusting of flour, or roll dough between two sheets of baking paper.
  • Puff pastry dough freezes very well before baking. Defrost in the fridge before using. Partially rolling out the dough before freezing allows for faster defrosting.
  • Once baked, pastry keeps well for up to 15 days stored in a sealed container in a cool dry place.
  • Replace water with very cold white wine.
