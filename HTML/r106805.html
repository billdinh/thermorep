Borscht with Polish dumplings

Ingredients

Porcini mushroom filling

  • 30 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 250 g water
  • 80 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 tsp oil
  • 30 g breadcrumbs
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp lemon juice

Dumplings

  • 140 - 160 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 70 g lukewarm water

Borscht

  • 600 - 700 g raw beetroot, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 5 dried porcini mushrooms, cut into slices
  • 1400 g water
  • 2 tsp sea salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 2 - 3 tbsp white vinegar to taste
  • 2 tbsp dried marjoram

Preparation

Porcini mushroom filling

  1. Place mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add water and cook 18 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add onion and oil and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add breadcrumbs, salt, pepper and lemon juice and mix 30 sec/speed 2.
  6. Transfer mushroom mixture into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dumplings

  1. Place flour, oil and lukewarm water into mixing bowl and knead 1 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface.
  2. Cut dough into halves. Roll out each dough portion into a rectangle (3 mm thickness). Cut dough portions into squares (6 x 6 cm).
  3. Place 1 teaspoon mushroom filling into centre of each dough square and fold edges together diagonally to form a triangle, sealing edges with your fingertips. Gently pull two corners together to form a dumpling, then pinch ends together to seal. Repeat with remaining filling and squares of dough.Cover with kitchen towel and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Borscht

  1. Place beetroot, garlic and dried mushrooms into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add water, salt, pepper, sugar, bay leaves and allspice and cook 25 min/90°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, cook dumplings. Place a large saucepan with water over high heat and bring to the boil. Working in 2-3 batches, add dumplings to saucepan and cook until dumplings float to the surface. Using a slotted spoon, remove dumplings and transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  3. Add vinegar and marjoram to soup in mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 2.
  4. Strain soup through simmering basket into a large bowl and season to taste. Discard contents of simmering basket. Divide soup between 8 serving bowls, then divide dumplings between serving bowls before serving.

Useful Items

kitchen towel, bowl, silicone bread mat, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, large saucepan, large bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • This traditional Polish recipe was contributed by Grace Mazur, Managing Director of Thermomix in Australia and New Zealand. The Polish dumplings (also called "uszka") are often referred to as "Polish Christmas ears" and are traditionally served in borscht at Christmas.
  • To make this recipe gluten free, simply omit the dumplings.
  • This soup is also traditionally served with the Polish croquettes. Please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
