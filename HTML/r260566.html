Strawberry and rose water macarons

Ingredients

Macarons

  • 90 g blanched almonds
  • 10 g freeze-dried strawberries
  • 20 g pistachio nuts, shelled and unsalted
  • 220 g icing sugar
  • 100 g egg whites, room temperature (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • 25 g caster sugar
  • ¼ tsp natural food colouring, red paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp rose water

Strawberry and rose water ganache

  • 10 g freeze-dried strawberries
  • 150 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 140 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 30 g icing sugar
  • 1 tsp rose water

Preparation

Macarons

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside (see Tips). Place almonds, strawberries and pistachios into mixing bowl and mill 5-10 sec/speed 7, to a powder consistancy. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add icing sugar and mill 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites, cream of tartar and caster sugar into mixing bowl and whisk 7 min/speed 4, without measuring cup, until stiff peaks form. Transfer into a large bowl, then add red food colouring gel, rose water and one-quarter of the reserved powdered strawberry mixture and gently fold through with a metal spoon (see Tips). Working in three batches, fold another one-quarter of the powdered strawberry mixture through macaron batter until you have a smooth, shiny mixture.
  4. Place mixture into a piping bag and pipe approx. 60 macarons (2 cm diameter) onto prepared baking tray. Tap tray to remove any bubbles and set aside for 30-60 minutes to dry and form a slight crust (see Tips). Preheat oven to 145°C (see Tips).
  5. Bake macarons on the middle shelf of the oven for 10 minutes (145°C), turn off oven and leave macarons in oven with door ajar for 5-7 minutes or until bottoms do not stick. Transfer macarons - whilst still on baking paper - onto a wire rack and allow to cool (approx. 1 hour). Using a palette knife, transfer cooled macarons into sealable containers (see Tips).

Strawberry and rose water ganache

  1. Place strawberries and chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add cream and cook 4 min/50°C/speed 1.
  3. Add icing sugar and rose water and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator to set (approx. 2 hours).

Assembly

  1. Transfer ganache into a piping bag and pipe a little ganache onto flat side of half of the macaron shells. Gently sandwich together by placing another shell on top and press together very lightly, then serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), piping bag, palette knife, baking paper, sealable containers, wire rack, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Separate eggs 1-2 days before starting recipe and store in refrigerator. Bring to room temperature before using. Using aged egg whites will produce drier, firmer meringues, yielding a better result for macaron shells.
  • You can find food colouring gel at some major supermarkets and at most baking supply stores.
  • In humid climates, macarons could take up to 1 hour or more to form a crust. To speed up this process, heat your oven to its maximum temperature. Once heated, turn off the oven and place macaron shells into oven, leaving door open. Check shells regularly; they will be dried and ready when you can gently run your finger over the top of macarons without batter sticking to your finger.
  • You can use a silicone baking mat instead of a baking tray and baking paper, if desired.
  • If you have a conventional oven, bake macaron shells at 160°C.
  • You can store macaron shells in sealable containers for up to 1 week.
  • When folding powdered strawberry mixture into macaron shell batter, using a metal spoon helps retain the air in the egg white mixture.
  • Instead of piping ganache onto shells, you can spoon mixture onto them. However, a piping bag will be faster and gives you more control.
  • You can store macarons in a sealable container for up to 3 days.
  • You can omit rose water from shells and ganache, if desired.
