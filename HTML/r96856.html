Polish doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 250 g jam
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 50 g fresh yeast
  • 200 g full cream milk
  • 80 g unsalted butter, cut into cubes (1 cm)
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 2 tbsp vodka
  • 1000 g sunflower oil, for frying
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

  1. Place jam into mixing bowl and heat 3-5 min/100°C/speed 1, or until jam has melted, then blend 20 sec/speed 7. Strain jam through a fine mesh sieve into a bowl and set aside. Discard sieve contents.
  2. Place sugar, yeast, milk, butter and egg yolks into mixing bowl and mix 1 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and lid with spatula.
  3. Add flour and salt and knead 2 min/.
  4. Add vodka and knead for a further 1 min/.
  5. Transfer dough into a large metal bowl, cover with kitchen towel and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  6. Transfer proved dough onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface, then form dough into a sausage shape (approx. 50 cm long). Cut sausage into 18 evenly-sized portions. Roll each portion of dough into a round ball, cover with a kitchen towel and leave to prove for a further 10 minutes.
  7. Place a large saucepan over heat, add oil and bring to 160°C. To check your oil is at 160°C, drop a small square of bread into the oil, it should turn golden brown in 30-35 seconds (see Tips). The oil is now ready for frying the doughnuts. Keep your oil at this temperature while frying.
  8. Place a ball of dough into the hot oil and fry for 3 minutes each side or until golden brown. Carefully remove doughnut using a large slotted spoon and transfer onto a plate lined with paper towel. Check the doughnut has cooked all the way through. If not, extend cooking time in the oil accordingly. Proceed with remaining balls of dough, working in batches.
  9. Transfer reserved jam into a piping bag with a small round nozzle (1 cm). Pipe approx. 1 tablespoon of the jam into the centre of each doughnut.
  10. Allow doughnuts to cool to room temperature (approx. 30 minutes), then dust with icing sugar before serving.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, kitchen towel, metal bowl, silicone bread mat, large saucepan, paper towel, slotted spoon, piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm)

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have a kitchen thermometer that is capable of checking the temperature of your oil, then you can omit the bread test in step 7.
  • You can use any jam flavour of choice in this recipe. Cherry and prune flavoured jams are popular choices in Poland. Another alternative is to fill your doughnuts with cooled vanilla custard for an extra decadent treat.
  • Save leftover egg whites in the freezer to use in other recipes such as meringues.
