Mocaccino Budino - Budino mocaccino

Ingredients

  • 100 g sugar
  • 70 g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 60 g cornflour
  • 680 g whole milk
  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 50 g espresso, brewed
  • 20 g Amaretto or other almond liqueur
  • whipped cream, to serve
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
933 kJ / 223 kcal
Protein
4.9 g
Carbohydrates

11.2 g

Preparation

  1. Place all ingredients except cream, in mixing bowl and cook 9 min/90°C/speed 4.
  2. Pour into 8 individual pudding basins (Ø 6 cm, see tip). Allow to cool then place in fridge to set for at least 6 hours.
  3. When ready to serve, dip moulds briefly in hot water then turn out puddings onto serving plates and serve with whipped cream.

Useful Items

individual pudding basins, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The word ‘budino’ is Italian for 'pudding'. It generally refers to a custard-like dessert typically flavoured with chocolate, caramel, butterscotch or lemon. There are many versions around, as different cooks have their own unique take on the traditional recipe. Ours combines espresso and Amaretto liqueur – be warned, it’s moreish!
  • Can also be made in a loaf tin (450 g/1 lb) lined with cling film, then cut in slices to serve.
  • The bitter cocoa powder can be replaced in whole or in part by sweetened cocoa. In this case, adjust the amount of sugar to taste.
  • To make a non-alcoholic, all-chocolate version, replace the quantity of alcohol and coffee with an equivalent quantity of additional milk.
