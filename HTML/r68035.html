Marble cake

Ingredients

  • 300 g butter, softened, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 370 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 280 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp homemade vanilla sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 70 g full cream milk
  • 30 g unsweetened cocoa powder
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
1841 kJ / 440 kcal
Protein
7 g
Carbohydrates
48 g
Fat
24 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and flour a bundt cake tin (approx. 22 - 24 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place butter, plain flour, 250 g of the sugar, vanilla sugar or vanilla extract, salt, eggs, baking powder and 40 g of the full cream milk into mixing bowl and mix 40 sec/speed 5 with aid of spatula. Transfer half of cake mixture into prepared bundt cake tin, leaving remainder in mixing bowl.
  3. Add cocoa powder, remaining 30 g sugar and remaining 30 g full cream milk into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Mix a further 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Layer cocoa cake mixture into bundt cake tin. To create a marble pattern, swirl through both layers with a fork. Bake for 50-60 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow cake to cool in tin for approx. 15 minutes before transferring to a serving plate and leaving to cool completely. Cut into slices and serve.

Useful Items

bundt cake tin (approx. 22 - 24 cm), wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important that the butter is very soft. If your butter is chilled or hard, place butter, sugar, salt and eggs first into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 5 before adding flour, baking powder and milk. Mix 15 sec/speed 6, then proceed as per recipe.
  • Make vanilla sugar in your Thermomix to use in this recipe.
  • Dust with icing sugar or cover with a chocolate glaze.
  • Pound cake: Omit cocoa marbling. Simply transfer all the cake mixture from step 2 into prepared bundt cake tin and bake for 50-60 min (180°C) as described in step 5.
  • Flavour the cocoa mixture with ½ tsp rum essence, or with 20 g rum. If using rum, reduce milk to 10 g in step 3.
