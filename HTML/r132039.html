Summer Pudding

Ingredients

Summer Pudding

  • 600 g fresh strawberries, hulled, halved
  • 300 g fresh mixed berries (blueberries, blackberries, etc.)
  • 250 g fresh raspberries
  • 380 g light brown sugar
  • oil, for greasing
  • 10 - 15 slices sliced white bread, crusts removed, cut in squares and triangles (400 g approx.)

Chantilly Cream

  • 400 g double cream, cold
  • 5 - 8 drops lemon juice, to taste
  • 1 Tbsp icing sugar

Preparation

Summer Pudding

  1. Place strawberries, mixed berries, raspberries and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 10 min/100°C//speed 0.5. Meanwhile, grease a pudding bowl or tin (approx. Ø 20 cm) and line with 2 pieces of cling film, overlapping in the middle. Strain fruit through Varoma dish collecting syrup in a bowl below. Set fruit and syrup aside.
  2. Dip slices of bread in fruit syrup (on both sides) for a few seconds, then use to line base and sides of prepared bowl or tin.
  3. Pour cooked fruit into bread lined bowl or tin. Enclose fruit with more dipped slices of bread, forming a lid. Fold over cling film then place a small plate on top and press down gently. Weigh plate down with tins or jam jars and refrigerate for 8 hours or overnight. Clean mixing bowl.

Chantilly Cream

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place double cream, 5-8 drops lemon juice and icing sugar in mixing bowl then whisk, without setting a time/speed 3, until soft peaks form - watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Transfer to a bowl and set aside in fridge. When ready to serve, turn out pudding onto a serving plate and enjoy with the chantilly cream.

Useful Items

heavy weight, small plate, bowl, cling film, deep bowl, round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Ensure there are no gaps in your bread 'shell' so the juices do not escape.
