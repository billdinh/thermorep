Houmous

Ingredients

  • 250 g dried chickpeas
  • 500 g water, plus extra cold for soaking
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 Tbsp tahini
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 40 g lemon juice (approx. 2 lemons)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander leaves, roughly chopped

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in chickpeas. Fill bowl with cold water and leave to soak for approx. 12 hours.
  2. After this time, drain soaked chickpeas through simmering basket. Place drained chickpeas, water, cumin and salt in mixing bowl then cook 40 min/100°C/speed . Leave to cool for 30 minutes in mixing bowl. Set aside a few whole chickpeas for garnishing.
  3. Add tahini, garlic, lemon juice and 20 g oil then blend 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a serving dish, cover with cling film and leave to cool for 2 hours.
  4. Before serving, use the back of a tablespoon to make circular grooves on surface of houmous then garnish with chopped coriander and reserved whole chickpeas. Drizzle with remaining 20 g oil then serve with bread, crisps or crudités.

Hints & Tricks

  • Chickpeas are a low cost protein, suitable for all ages and most diets.
  • Replace dried chickpeas with tinned chickpeas: 1) Place 2 tins chickpeas, with liquid (approx. 700 g), cumin and salt in mixing bowl, then heat 5 min/100°C/speed  . 2) Add garlic, lemon juice, oil and 2 Tbsp tahini then mix 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer to an airtight container and keep refrigerated until the mixture is cold. Transfer houmous to a serving dish and serve.
  • To speed up the soaking of chickpeas, add a pinch of bicarbonate of soda to soaking water. Chickpeas are sufficiently soaked when the skins can be peeled off easily.
