Chocolate brownie trifle

Ingredients

Chocolate brownie

  • 230 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 440 g Rapadura sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 70 g Dutch cocoa powder
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 200 g dark chocolate (min. 55% cocoa solids), broken into pieces
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract

Chocolate custard

  • 200 g milk chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 2 eggs
  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 40 g cornflour

Raspberry coulis

  • 250 g fresh raspberries
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 20 g lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)

Mascarpone layer

  • 6 tbsp Drambuie (50 g)
  • 500 g mascarpone cheese
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 250 g fresh raspberries, plus extra for garnishing
  • 50 g milk chocolate, shaved, for garnishing

Preparation

Chocolate brownie

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line 2 round cake tins (22 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 2 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add baking powder, cocoa and flour and mix 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  5. Place 230 g unsalted butter and salt into mixing bowl and melt 3-4 min/60°C/speed 1, until butter is completely melted.
  6. Add reserved cocoa mixture and eggs and mix 5 sec/speed 5, or until combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add grated chocolate and vanilla extract and combine 10 sec//speed 4, or until just combined.
  8. Distribute brownie batter evenly between 2 prepared baking tins, then use the spatula to smooth down surface until even.
  9. Bake for 50-55 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each brownie comes out clean. Allow to cool completely in tins (approx. 1 hour).
  10. Once cooled, remove 1 of the brownie cakes from cake tin and place upside down into the bottom of a trifle dish (22 cm). Remove other brownie cake from second cake tin and set aside until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chocolate custard

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add all remaining chocolate custard ingredients and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 2 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Raspberry coulis

  1. Place all coulis ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/90°C/speed 4, then blend 10 sec/speed 5, increasing speed gradually to speed 8. Transfer coulis into a bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mascarpone layer

  1. Place reserved cooled custard into mixing bowl blend 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat blending 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer custard into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Drizzle 3 tablespoons of the Drambuie over the brownie cake in trifle dish and set aside.
  3. Place mascarpone, caster sugar and egg yolks into mixing bowl and combine 40 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 10 sec/speed 3, or until smooth. Transfer mascarpone into a bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and whip 5 min/37°C/speed 3.5, without measuring cup, until stiff. Transfer whipped egg whites into the bowl with the mascarpone and gently fold in using the spatula.
  5. To assemble trifle, drizzle half of the coulis over the brownie cake in trifle dish, then top with half of the fresh or frozen raspberries. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over raspberries, then top with half of the chocolate custard and the second reserved brownie cake turned upside down. Drizzle remaining 3 tablespoons of the Drambuie over the brownie cake layer, then repeat layering process with the remaining raspberry coulis, remaining raspberries, remaining mascarpone mixture and remaining chocolate custard.
  6. Place trifle into refrigerator to chill for minimum 4 hours or overnight.
  7. Sprinkle grated chocolate over trifle, then top with additional raspberries and serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, 2 round cake tins (22 cm), wooden skewer, trifle dish, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important that the diameter of your cake tins matches the diameter of your trifle dish. If you are using a trifle dish that is smaller or larger, ensure you adjust your cake tins accordingly to ensure your brownie cakes fit into the trifle dish perfectly.
