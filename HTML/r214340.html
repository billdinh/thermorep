Beef, apricot and pine nut pastilla

Ingredients

  • 1 brown onion, cut into halves
  • 30 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing and brushing
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 500 g beef mince
  • 80 g water
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tip)
  • 20 g pine nuts, plus extra for garnishing
  • 150 g dried apricots, cut into quarters
  • 50 g sultanas (optional)
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • 8 sheet filo pastry
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

  1. Grease a round springform cake tin (24 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil, cumin, cinnamon, ground coriander, fennel seeds and saffron and cook 3 min/90°C/speed 1.
  4. Add mince and cook 4 min/Varoma//speed .
  5. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula, breaking up any lumps. Cook for a further 3 min/Varoma//speed .
  6. Add water, stock paste, 20 g of the pine nuts, dried apricots, sultanas (optional), lemon zest and juice and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer onto a plate and spread out to cool (approx. 10 minutes).
  7. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  8. Brush a sheet of filo pastry with oil. Line the springform tin with oiled pastry, allowing pastry to overlap the edge of the tin. Repeat with a second layer of filo pastry, positioning the corners so that a star shape is formed. Repeat with 3 more layers of filo pastry (using 5 sheets in total).
  9. Place cooled mince mixture into tin. Fold in overhanging edges of pastry to enclose the filling. Brush remaining 3 sheets of filo pastry with oil, then crumple and place over top of pastilla. Transfer pastilla into oven and reduce temperature to 190°C. Bake for 20-25 minutes (190°C) or until crisp on top. Cool in tin for 10 minutes, then remove from tin and transfer onto a serving plate. Garnish with extra pine nuts and dust with cinnamon and icing sugar.

Useful Items

springform cake tin (24 cm), pastry brush, plate, serving plate, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Pastilla, also called bastilla, is a sweet and savoury spiced meat and apricot pie topped with a sugared crust.
