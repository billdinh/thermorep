Curried chickpea and lentil sandwich filling

Ingredients

  • 100 g dried red lentils (see Tips)
  • 1000 g filtered water, plus extra for soaking
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 1 apple, peeled, cored and cut into pieces (approx. 2-3 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • ½ tsp lemon juice
  • 3 - 4 tbsp vegan mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground turmeric
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto a mixing bowl lid and weigh lentils into it. Cover with water and set aside to soak (approx. 15 minutes - see Tips). Transfer into simmering basket, then rinse and drain.
  2. Place water, oil and ¼ teaspoon of the salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with lentils and cook 14 min/95°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, rinse in cold water and set aside to drain. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place apple, garlic and spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5
  4. Add lemon juice, mayonnaise, nutritional yeast, turmeric, curry powder, chickpeas, pepper, remaining ½ teaspoon salt and reserved cooked lentils and pulse Turbo/2 sec/0 times. Serve as desired or transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, sealable container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • It is not essential to soak the lentils before cooking, however it will reduce the cooking time and make them easier to digest. If using unsoaked lentils, you will need to increase the cooking time accordingly.
  • You can replace the red lentils with tinned brown lentils. If using pre-cooked lentils, omit soaking and cooking of red lentils, then proceed as per recipe.
  • To make your own vegan mayonnaise, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • You can serve this filling in sandwiches, in wraps or pitas, as a topping for baked potatoes or as a dip with crudités.
  • You can store this filling in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
