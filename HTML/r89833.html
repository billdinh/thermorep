Turkey roll with cranberry and cherry sauce

Ingredients

Turkey breast roll

  • 1500 g water
  • 1 turkey breast roast roll (1-1.8 kg )

Cranberry and cherry sauce

  • 130 g fresh cherries, pitted
  • 50 g dried cranberries
  • 50 g cranberry sauce (jelly)
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 30 g brandy
  • ½ - 1 tsp hot English mustard

Preparation

Turkey breast roll

  1. Place water into mixing bowl, then place Varoma dish into position. Place turkey roast roll into Varoma dish, then secure Varoma lid and steam 50 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Check to see if the turkey it is cooked through. If not, top up water in mixing bowl if required and rotate turkey roll in Varoma dish. Secure Varoma lid and steam for a further 20-30 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until turkey is cooked through. The steaming time will vary depending on the size of your turkey roast roll.
  3. Transfer turkey roll into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Empty mixing bowl.

Cranberry and cherry sauce

  1. Place all sauce ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  2. Insert measuring cup and blend 3 sec/speed 7. Slice your turkey roast, then serve immediately with warm cranberry and cherry sauce on the side.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • If your turkey breast roll is not secured with string or a net, you will need to secure it with kitchen string to ensure it holds its shape during steaming. Most butchers or specialty poultry providers sell turkey roast rolls pre-wrapped.
  • Marinate your turkey roll in the sauce overnight to infuse the flavour into the meat. The turkey meat will have a purple hue after steaming though.
  • To add some colour to your turkey roll fry just before serving in a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over a high heat until golden.
  • This recipe is a great oven space saver at Christmas time. It removes the big turkey from the oven, allowing more room for other roast meats and vegetables. 
  • This is a great entertaining recipe all-year-round. If you can't find turkey roast rolls outside of the festive season, substitute with a chicken roast roll instead.
