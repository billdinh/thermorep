Naan Bread

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp olive oil, to grease
  • ¾ tsp dried yeast
  • 8 oz water
  • 6 oz whole milk
  • 21 oz bread flour, plus extra to dust
  • 2 - 3 tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 2 oz salted butter,
  • 1 tbsp black sesame seeds, divided

Preparation

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl with oil and cut 8 strips of parchment paper (approx. 12 in. x 10 in.) and set aside (see Tip). Place yeast, water and milk into mixing bowl and warm 4 min/110°F/speed 2.
  2. Add flour and salt and knead 3 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl. Shape into a ball, turning to coat all sides. Cover with cling wrap and let rise until doubled in size (approx. 60-90 minutes).
  3. Punch down risen dough and transfer to a separate piece of parchment paper or a lightly floured surface.
  4. Set oven rack to middle position. Place a large pizza stone in the center of the oven rack. Preheat oven to 500°F. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/110°F/speed 1. Divide dough in half leaving half in the greased bowl while working with first half. Cut dough into 4 equal portions and form each one into a ball. Brush with melted butter and allow to rest for 20 minutes, lightly covered in cling wrap. In the last 5 minutes of resting, prepare the remaining dough the same way.
  5. Place a dough ball onto a prepared parchment strip. Use fingertips to spread the dough out into a 6-7 inch circle, without turning the dough over. Set aside and repeat this step with each risen ball.
  6. Return to first piece of dough you worked on and gently stretch out to an oval shape (approx. 9 in. x 7 in.). Sprinkle with sesame or caraway seeds.
  7. Transfer two breads with parchment onto the preheated pizza stone and bake for 6-8 minutes (500°F) or until puffed and bubbled spots are golden (see Tip). Remove cooked naan bread from pizza stone with a long handled spatula. Repeat with remaining dough balls.
  8. Transfer hot naan breads onto a plate and cool for 5 minutes, brush with more melted butter and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft while you bake the remainder. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container.

Useful Items

long handled spatula, pizza stone, plate, airtight container, pastry brush, cling wrap, large bowl, parchment paper, clean kitchen towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Top unbaked naan with garlic butter, or try different dried herbs, spices and seeds.
  • Cut 8 strips of parchment paper (approx. 12 in. x 10 in.) for the 8 balls of dough. As you prepare the naan dough, work directly on a strip of parchment paper. When ready to bake, simply slide the entire piece of parchment paper onto the hot pizza stone. The paper will release the baked naan easily once baked.
  • Naan dough can be refrigerated in a bowl or plastic zip lock bag for up to 3 days. Bring dough to room temperature before using.
  • Black caraway seeds (Nigella seeds) can be found at any Asian grocery store.
  • Placing dough on preheated pizza stone helps it puff up. The preheated stone mimics the traditional cylindrical tandoor oven.
