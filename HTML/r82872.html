Beef in Stout

Ingredients

  • 20 g olive oil
  • 200 g onions, quartered
  • 800 g feather steak, diced (3 cm)
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¾ tsp ground black pepper
  • 20 g tomato purée
  • 415 g stout (e.g. Guinness)
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 250 g carrots, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 250 g parsnips, cut in pieces (2 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place oil and onions in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in beef and flour then add salt and pepper. Mix together with spatula until all the meat is coated in the seasoned flour then add to mixing bowl.
  3. Add tomato purée, stout and rosemary then cook 60 min/100°C//speed .
  4. Add carrots and parsnips, stir in with spatula, then cook again 30 min/100°C//speed . Serve piping hot; works perfectly with mashed potatoes.

Hints & Tricks

  • Quicker than a traditional stew as there is no step for browning the meat. We defy you to taste the difference! The meat will be so tender and the flavours are amazing.
  • This hearty and warming stew can be made ahead and then reheated when needed, making it perfect for a midweek meal or when you have guests.
  • Any root vegetables can be added depending on what you have available. Try squash, swede, or even potatoes.
  • Also works well served with crusty bread or rice.
  • You can use any stout in this one, but we chose the Irish favourite, Guinness. Particularly good when the weather turns colder, this beautifully wholesome and tender beef stew is the perfect winter warmer. Delicious served with mashed potatoes.
