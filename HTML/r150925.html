Steamed Cabbage and Pork Dumplings

Ingredients

Dumpling Wrappers

  • vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g water
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • water, plus extra as needed

Filling and Dumplings

  • 400 g white cabbage leaves, cut in pieces
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 350 g pork mince, broken in pieces
  • 530 g water
  • 20 g soy sauce
  • 10 g rice wine
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 Tbsp light sesame oil

Preparation

Dumpling Wrappers

  1. Oil Varoma dish, Varoma tray and a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water, flour, oil and salt in mixing bowl and knead 2 min/. If dough seems dry, add 10 g water (2 tsp) and knead a further 1 min/. Transfer dough to prepared small bowl and form into a ball. Cover bowl and allow to rest for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling and Dumplings

  1. Place white cabbage leaves and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 4 with aid of spatula. Transfer to simmering basket and set aside to drain.
  2. Place pork mince, 30 g water, soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, remaining ½ tsp salt, pepper and sesame oil in mixing bowl and mix 2 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Squeeze excess moisture out of cabbage by pressing it against sides of simmering basket, add cabbage to mixing bowl and mix 6 sec//speed 6. Transfer filling to a large plate and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Turn rested dough out onto a floured surface. Divide dough into 4 equal pieces and shape each piece into a rope (Ø 2 cm). Use spatula to cut each rope into 8-10 small pieces and form them into balls.
  5. With a rolling pin, roll each ball of dough into a thin round shape (Ø 9 cm). Place 1 Tbsp filling in the centre of each and fold the wrapper in half over the filling to make a semi-circle shape. Pinch the seam in the centre and hold it with one hand while using the other hand to pleat the top edge of the wrapper from the middle outwards on both sides. Arrange dumplings in prepared Varoma dish and tray.
  6. Place 500 g water in mixing bowl, set Varoma in position and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and serve dumplings hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • Thermomix® lends itself perfectly to making dim sum such as these; kneading the dough, chopping and mixing the filling, and steaming them can all be done with your Thermomix®. If you’ve never shaped dumplings before, take your time and practise. The next time you make these, you will be much faster – and you may find that you enjoy the process just as much as the fresh, homemade flavour of the end result!
  • These dumplings are usually served with a dip of Chinese black rice vinegar or soy sauce, or a mixture of both (e.g. 3 Tbsp soy sauce + 1 Tbsp black or white vinegar). Chopped garlic and chilli can also be added.
    - If you're in a hurry, use ready-made dumpling wrappers available in Asian supermarkets.
    - You can prepare dumplings ahead of time and freeze them. Steam as described in recipe without defrosting, adding 5 minutes to steaming time.
