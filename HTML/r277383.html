Mushroom quinoa tartlets with truffle oil

Ingredients

Pastry cases

  • 80 g unsalted butter cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 80 g brown rice
  • 60 g buckwheat groats
  • 50 g quinoa
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 ½ tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 egg
  • 40 g cold water

Mushroom filling

  • 20 g dried mushrooms (porcini, shiitake, etc)
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 60 g cream cheese
  • 10 sprigs fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 30 g walnuts
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp truffle oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 150 g fresh mushrooms of choice, cut into slices
  • fresh thyme leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Pastry cases

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a 24-hole mini-muffin tray.
  2. Place rice, buckwheat groats, quinoa, salt and xanthan gum into mixing bowl and blend 2 min/speed 10.
  3. Add butter, egg and water and mix 8 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes (see Tips).
  4. Roll 2 heaped teaspoons of the reserved dough into balls, then push into mini muffin moulds to cover base and sides, ensuring there are no holes in the pastry. Set aside while making filling.

Mushroom filling

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh dried mushrooms into it. Carefully cover with boiling water and set aside to soak for 10 minutes. Drain using simmering basket.
  2. Place drained mushrooms, garlic and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add cream cheese, chives, walnuts, salt and pepper and blend 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer 1 teaspoon of the mixture into each pastry case. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place truffle oil and fresh mushrooms into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/Varoma//speed 1. Top cream cheese mixture with mushrooms and bake for 15 minutes (180°C). Allow tartlets to cool slightly, then drizzle with extra truffle oil and garnish with fresh thyme leaves just before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, 24-hole mini muffin tray, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, make the mushroom filling while the pastry cools in the refrigerator.
  • You can use any kind of dried mushrooms in this recipe. You can find dried mushrooms at major supermarkets, gourmet food stores and Asian food stores.
