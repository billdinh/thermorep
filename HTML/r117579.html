Garibaldi Biscuits

Ingredients

  • 4 oz self-rising flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 oz baker's sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 oz whole milk
  • 2 oz currants
  • 1 egg white, from large egg
  • 1 tbsp confectioners sugar, for sprinkling
Nutrition
per 1 piece

239 kJ / 57 kcal

0.9 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment.
  2. Place flour, butter, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add milk and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Tip dough out and bring together into a ball. Dough will be sticky, roll out between two sheets of cling wrap into a rectangle, as thin as possible (approx. 8 in. wide x 12 in. long x 1/16 in. thick).
  4. Sprinkle currants over half the surface then fold the other half on top and roll out to a rectangle (8 in. wide x 12 in. long). Trim edges to neaten then transfer dough to prepared baking sheet. Score dough into 16 fingers (approx. 4 ¾ in. x 1 ½ in.) leaving them attached. Place egg white in a small bowl and mix with a wire whisk or fork.
  5. Brush cookies with a lightly beaten egg white and sprinkle with confectioners sugar. Bake for 12-15 minutes (350°F). Allow to rest on baking sheets for 2 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

airtight container, cling wrap, rolling pin, bowl, wire whisk, pastry brush, wire rack, baking sheet and parchment paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditional semi sweet, currant filled biscuit normally served with a cup of tea in the UK.
  • Make your own bakers sugar and confectioners sugar in your Thermomix®.
    - Bakers sugar - Place 7 oz sugar into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10, store in an airtight container.
    - Confectioners sugar - Place 7 oz bakers sugar into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10, store in an airtight container.
