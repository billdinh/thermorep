Avocado Cupcakes with Lime Drizzle

Ingredients

Cupcakes

  • 1 avocado, ripe, cut in pieces (approx. 140 g)
  • 150 g vegetable oil
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 160 g golden caster sugar
  • 225 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g milk

Lime Drizzle

  • 85 g golden caster sugar
  • 2 limes, finely grated zest and 40 g juice

1234 kJ / 295 kcal

Preparation

Cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a 12 hole muffin tin with paper muffin cases.
  2. Place avocado in mixing bowl then mash 1 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mash again 1 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in oil. Set aside.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 4 min 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 2 while slowly pouring reserved oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup onto rotating blades.
  5. Add reserved avocado, 125 g flour, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  6. Add milk and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with a spatula.
  7. Add remaining 100 g flour and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  8. Divide evenly between prepared muffin cases. Bake for 18-20 minutes (180°C) until cooked (see tip) and golden brown. Transfer cupcakes to a wire rack then make small holes with a cocktail stick in top of cakes. Make drizzle.

Lime Drizzle

  1. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sugar and lime juice. Remove bowl and mix well.
  2. Pour drizzle over top of cupcakes, allowing it to seep into prepared holes. Sprinkle with lime zest then allow to cool before serving or store in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

cocktail stick, muffin paper cases, 12 hole muffin tin, jug, wire rack, small bowl, airtight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Check cupcakes are cooked by lightly pressing the top of the cake. If it bounces back it is cooked, if not return to the oven and test again in 1-2 minutes.

The avocado in the sponge replaces some of the oil in the recipe with good fat as well as lending a natural moistness to the finished cake. The lime drizzle adds a sweet, tangy finish.
