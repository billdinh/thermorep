Fusilli with Pesto, Green Beans and Potato - Pasta alla genovese

Ingredients

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 30 g pecorino cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 30 g toasted pine nuts
  • ½ garlic clove
  • 80 g fresh basil leaves
  • 130 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1200 g water, plus extra for cooking pasta
  • 250 g potatoes, diced (2 cm)
  • 400 g dried fusilli pasta
  • 100 g green beans, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan and pecorino in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add pine nuts, garlic, basil, oil and 1 tsp salt then mix 20 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place 1200 g water, potatoes and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then cook 15 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, bring a large saucepan of water with remaining 1 tsp salt added to the boil. Cook fusilli according to packet instructions, or until al dente. Drain pasta through a colander then transfer to a large serving bowl and keep warm.
  4. Add green beans to mixing bowl with potatoes and cook 5-10 min/100°C//speed 1 until beans and potatoes are tender.
  5. Drain cooked potatoes and beans, transfer to serving bowl with cooked pasta, then stir in pesto. Serve hot or cold.

Useful Items

saucepan, colander, large serving bowl, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Originating in Genoa, the capital city of Liguria, pesto alla genovese is a sauce made with basil, pine nuts, Parmesan and olive oil. While pesto is a generic term given to any sauce made by pounding ingredients, ‘Genovese pesto’ remains the most popular pesto in Italy as well as being enjoyed around the world. In our take on the classic dish trenette al pesto, we’ve swapped traditional trenette pasta for fusilli, a spiral pasta shape that catches the sauce well.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan and pecorino for vegetarian hard cheeses.
