Lavender Shortbread

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp edible lavender flowers, dried
  • 55 g golden caster sugar
  • 40 g long grain rice
  • 115 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 130 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 Tbsp Demerara sugar, for sprinkling
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
871 kJ / 208 kcal
Protein
1.8 g
Carbohydrates
23.2 g

12.1 g

Preparation

  1. Place lavender and 25 g sugar in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place rice in mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer to bowl with lavender sugar and set aside.
  3. Place butter, remaining 30 g sugar and salt in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add flour then reserved lavender sugar and ground rice. Mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 10 sec/speed 4. Press evenly into a round cake tin (Ø 20 cm).
  5. Score into eight with a sharp knife then prick with a fork. Place in fridge for 15 minutes to firm up. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 150°C.
  6. Bake for 30 minutes (150°C) until cooked through and lightly golden. Sprinkle with Demerara sugar then set aside to cool completely. Remove from tin and serve or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), long sharp knife, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a plain shortbread, omit lavender.
  • Different flavoured sugars can also be used such as vanilla or rosemary.
  • For a citrus twist, add finely grated zest of 1 lemon or 1 orange.

  • These buttery, crumbly biscuits are strongly associated with Scotland where they originated. We’ve added a twist here with a subtle lavender flavour. Lovely with a cup of tea, or served on the side of a lemon posset.
