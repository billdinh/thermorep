Blueberry frozen yoghurt cheesecake

Ingredients

Frozen yoghurt

  • 40 g white sugar
  • 250 g cream cheese
  • 200 g Greek yoghurt
  • 250 g double cream
  • 1 pinch coarse salt
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Biscuit base

  • 85 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g gingernut biscuits
  • 200 g frozen blueberries

Blueberry coulis

  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 2 tsp icing sugar
  • 350 g frozen blueberries

Preparation

Frozen yoghurt

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 10.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add all remaining frozen yoghurt ingredients, except frozen bluberries, and mix 1 min/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for 20 sec/speed 9. Pour mixture into a shallow freezable container and cover in plastic wrap. Place into freezer for 10 hours, or until  frozen. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Biscuit base

  1. Grease and line the bottom of a springform cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place biscuits into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/90°C/speed 2.
  4. Add milled biscuits and mix 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and press down to form base. Place into refrigerator for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Remove frozen yoghurt from freezer and allow to stand for 15 minutes to soften. Cut into cubes (4 cm). Place half of the yoghurt cubes into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 9, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer into a bowl.
  2. Place remaining yoghurt cubes into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 9, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula.
  3. Add frozen blueberries and reserved blended frozen yoghurt and mix  40 sec//speed .
  4. Remove base from refrigerator and pour blueberry frozen yoghurt over base. Cover with plastic wrap and place into freezer for 2-3 hours, or until completely frozen. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Blueberry coulis

  1. Place lemon juice, sugar and 300 g of the frozen bluberries into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/90°C/speed 4, then blend 30 sec/speed 5. Strain coulis through a fine mesh sieve into a jug, then set aside until cool (approx. 30 minutes). Place into refrigerator until ready to use.
  2. Remove cheesecake from freezer 15-30 minutes before serving to soften. Drizzle coulis over cheesecake and decorate with remaining 50 g blueberries just before serving.

Useful Items

shallow freezable containers (e.g. plastic, silicone), plastic wrap, springform cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, bowl, jug, fine mesh sieve, freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace gingernut biscuits with gluten free biscuits to make this a gluten free dessert.
