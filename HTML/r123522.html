Meatballs with Romesco Sauce

Ingredients

Romesco Sauce

  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and cut into quarters
  • 1 fresh tomato, halved
  • ½ red onion, unpeeled
  • 2 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 50 g blanched almonds
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 1 slice day old bread, torn into pieces
  • 1 tbsp sherry
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • ¼ tsp ground smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste

Meatballs

  • 500 g topside beef, cut into pieces (3 cm) then placed in freezer for 20 minutes
  • 250 g chorizo, skin removed and cut in pieces
  • ½ red onion, (approx. 100 g), quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 slice day old bread, torn into pieces
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp ground smoked paprika
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, for frying

Preparation

Romesco Sauce

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Place red pepper, tomato, red onion and garlic onto it and roast for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until vegetables are softened and lightly blackened.
  2. Transfer roasted vegetables immediately into a ceramic or glass bowl and cover tightly with cling film. Leave to stand for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove roasted vegetables from bowl and transfer onto a plate covered with paper towel to drain. Gently peel skin from vegetables and discard skins. Transfer peeled vegetables back into bowl and set aside.
  4. Place blanched almonds in mixing bowl and roast 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove lid and leave to cool in mixing bowl (approx. 5 minutes).
  5. Add all remaining Romesco sauce ingredients, and reserved peeled vegetables, then blend 30 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend again 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer sauce into a bowl until ready to serve.

Meatballs

  1. Place beef in mixing bowl and mince 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place chorizo, red onion, garlic, parsley and bread in mixing bowl then chop Turbo/1 sec/6-7 times. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add nutmeg, paprika, salt, pepper, egg and reserved mince then blend 10 sec/speed 7. Roll mixture into walnut-sized meatballs, to make approx. 30, and set aside.
  4. Place a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add olive oil. Cook meatballs in batches for 10-12 minutes, or until golden and cooked through, turning occasionally.
  5. Transfer meatballs onto a plate lined with paper towel to drain excess oil, then serve with reserved Romesco sauce.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, glass or ceramic serving bowl, cling film, paper towel, serving bowl, tongs, non-stick frying pan, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Try making some Guacamole to serve with your meatballs as an alternative to the Romesco sauce (see The Basic Cookbook).
  • The steam created by tightly wrapping the bowl with cling film will loosen the vegetable skins making them easier to peel.
