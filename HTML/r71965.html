Fig and apricot tea bread

Ingredients

  • oil, for greasing
  • 150 g pecan nuts
  • 120 g dried apricots
  • 120 g dried figs
  • 100 g sultanas
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 280 g brewed earl grey tea (see Tips)
  • 50 g light brown sugar
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 240 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • 1 egg
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
1160 kJ / 276 kcal

5 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (25 x 9 x 10 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place pecan nuts into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/2 sec/1 time. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place apricots and figs into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add sultanas, vanilla bean paste, brewed tea, sugar and salt and cook 10 min/100°C//speed .
  5. Add all remaining ingredients, including reserved pecans, and combine 10 sec//speed 3.
  6. Pour into prepared tin and bake for 40 minutes (170°C). Cover tin with aluminium foil to prevent loaf from burning on top, then Continue to bake for 15-20 minutes (170°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the loaf comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 10 minutes before transferring onto a wire rack. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

baking paper, loaf tin (25 x 9 x 10 cm), wire rack, aluminium foil, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute oil for butter.
  • You can substitute Earl Grey tea for Ceylon tea.
  • This fruity yet dense tea bread is ideal in the morning or afternoon. The sugar can be omitted completely if you prefer, as the dried fruit offers plenty of sweetness.
  • To brew your tea in the Thermomix, weigh 280 g water into the mixing bowl and bring to the boil 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Add tea bag or tea leaves and allow to stand until golden brown in colour (approx. 2 - 3 minutes). Strain tea leaves or remove tea bag before using.
  • This bread can be sliced and stored in the freezer. Enjoy toasted at breakfast with butter or ricotta and maple syrup or in the evening on the side of a cheese platter.
