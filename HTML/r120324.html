Raspberry antioxidant juice

Ingredients

  • 1 grapefruit (approx. 200-250 g), flesh only, no white pith, deseeded and cut into halves
  • 1 Pink Lady apple (approx. 100 g), cored and cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 180 g raspberries
  • 1 sprig fresh mint, leaves only, plus extra to serve (optional)
  • 1 - 2 tbsp honey, to taste
  • 450 g water
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
300 kJ / 71 kcal

1.02 g
Carbohydrates

0.29 g

Preparation

  1. Place grapefruit, apple, raspberries, mint and honey into mixing bowl and blend 1 min 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer into a jug and place into refrigerator to chill for 20 minutes.
  3. Divide between 6 serving glasses and garnish with extra mint (optional) before serving.

Useful Items

jug, 6 serving bowls or glasses, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use either pink or red grapefruit in this recipe.
  • You can replace honey with pure maple syrup in this recipe.
  • Try replacing the raspberries with any other kind of berry (blueberries, blackberries, strawberries). You can use either frozen or fresh berries.
  • This juice is best consumed on the day it is made. The grapefruit may become bitter overnight.
  • Raspberries, apples and grapefruit are good sources of Vitamin C, which help support the immune system and contain antioxidant properties.
