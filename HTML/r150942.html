Rice Cake with Sweet Azuki Bean Paste

Ingredients

Sweet Azuki Bean Paste

  • 150 g dried azuki beans, soaked for 4-24 hours
  • 850 g water
  • 50 g brown sugar

Rice Cake

  • 100 g rice flour (see tip)
  • 125 g glutinous rice flour
  • 60 g icing sugar
  • 75 g milk
  • 15 g dried fruit (e.g. raisins, apricots, peaches), cut in small pieces
  • 1000 g water

Preparation

Sweet Azuki Bean Paste

  1. Drain soaked azuki beans through simmering basket and rinse under running water. Place drained azuki beans and 500 g water in mixing bowl and cook 45 min/100°C/speed . Allow azuki beans to rest in cooking water for 15 minutes. Drain partially-cooked azuki beans through simmering basket and discard cooking water.
  2. Place partially-cooked azuki beans back in mixing bowl. Add remaining 350 g water and, without measuring cup, cook a further 10 min/Varoma/speed 3.
  3. Add brown sugar and, without measuring cup, cook 8 min/Varoma/speed 3. Transfer bean paste to a bowl and allow to cool to room temperature. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Rice Cake

  1. Place white rice flour, glutinous rice flour, icing sugar and milk in mixing bowl and knead 1 min/, then blend 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer powdery mixture to another bowl and set aside.
  2. Line simmering basket with damp baking paper with enough to come up the sides all the way around. Spoon in half the rice flour mixture and use the back of a spoon to smooth the surface, cover with reserved bean paste, then cover with remaining rice flour mixture. Arrange mixed dried fruit on top and place simmering basket in Varoma dish.
  3. Place water in mixing bowl, set Varoma in position and steam 60 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside and allow rice cake to cool to room temperature. Use baking paper to lift cake out of simmering basket. Remove baking paper, cut cake in slices and serve as a dessert or snack.

Hints & Tricks

  • This rice cake is a sweet delicacy traditionally served for Chinese New Year, and making it in your Thermomix® saves a lot of time. Though made without oil or butter, the texture is very soft thanks to the rich layer of sweet azuki bean paste. Azuki beans are small red beans that are frequently used in sweets and desserts in Asia. Refer to the variations if you have difficulty sourcing ingredients for this recipe.
  • Replace azuki beans with dried red kidney beans. After soaking and draining them as for the azuki beans, in step 1 use 750 g water and cook for 95 minutes instead of 45 minutes. Continue with recipe.
    - Instead of step 4, grind your own rice flour mix: place 100 g white short-grain rice (pudding or risotto rice) and 125 g glutinous (sticky) rice or sushi rice in dry mixing bowl and grind 2 min/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and grind a further 1 min/speed 10. Add icing sugar and 35 g milk and knead 1 min/, then blend 30 sec/speed 10 and transfer powdery mixture to a bowl. Continue with recipe in step 5.
    - Use fruit jam or chocolate spread instead of azuki bean paste.
  • Azuki bean paste is cooked in three stages for the best possible flavour and texture. The first boiling eliminates bitter-tasting tannins and the second cooking ensures the beans soften fully before sugar is added in the final cooking stage.
    - Azuki beans (also called adzuki or aduki beans), white rice flour and glutinous rice flour are available in Asian or Japanese supermarkets.
    - You can make azuki bean paste in advance and chill for up to 1 week, or purchase it ready-made.
