Brie en Croute with Port Figs

Ingredients

  • ½ vanilla bean
  • 2 oz sugar
  • 1 orange, thin peel only, no pith, plus juice (1 oz)
  • 10 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • 6 oz port wine
  • 3 oz dried figs (Black Mission), stems removed, halved
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, ready-made, thawed
  • 8 oz wheel of Brie cheese
  • 1 large egg, whisked
  • crackers, to serve
  • 1 red apple, sliced to serve

Preparation

  1. Split vanilla bean lengthwise, scrape out seeds and discard pod. Place vanilla seeds, sugar, orange peel and thyme into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add port, 1 oz orange juice, dried figs and cloves and without measuring cup cook 10 min/212°F//speed . Transfer figs to a bowl. Strain port mixture through a fine mesh sieve into mixing bowl. Discard solids.
  3. Continue cooking port liquid without measuring cup 15 min/212°F/speed 3 until thick with a molasses-like consistency. Quickly transfer to a bowl, set aside and allow to cool slightly (approx. 10 min.).
  4. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  5. Lay puff pastry on a clean work surface and gently roll out one inch larger than original size (approx. 11 in. x 16 in.). With a small knife, score top of cheese rind in a crisscross star pattern (see Tip). Spread reserved thickened port syrup and figs in a circle over center of pastry and place Brie top-side down over figs. Fold pastry over cheese to cover. Trim excess pastry and press seams to seal. Place on prepared baking sheet seam-side down. Brush top and sides with whisked egg. Decorate with pastry scraps, brush decorations with remaining whisked egg and bake for 30-35 minutes (375°F), until puffed and golden brown. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves and serve hot with crackers and sliced apple.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, fine mesh sieve, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Score the top rind so the cheese can get better exposure to heat. Use a small knife to make crisscross cuts in a star pattern or remove the top rind to expose the cheese.
