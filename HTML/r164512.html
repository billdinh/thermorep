Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 oz light brown sugar
  • 4 oz unsalted butter, chilled, cut into ¼ in. pieces
  • 4 oz molasses
  • 1 large egg
  • 10 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • &frac18; tsp ground cloves
  • &frac18; tsp ground nutmeg
  • unsalted butter, to grease
Nutrition
per 1 piece

444 kJ / 106 kcal

Preparation

  1. Place brown sugar into mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add butter, molasses and egg and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add flour, baking powder, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves and nutmeg and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Knead 1 min/.
  4. Transfer dough to center of a sheet of cling wrap, wrap and flatten to a disk shape. Refrigerate for 1 hour (see Tip).
  5. Place dough on kitchen counter for 10 minutes to slightly soften. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease or line cookie sheets with parchment and set aside.
  6. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of cling wrap to ⅛ inch thick with a rolling pin. Remove top layer of cling wrap and cut dough with a round cookie cutter (2½ in.). Transfer to prepared cookie sheets and bake for 12 minutes (350°F). Allow to cool completely before icing, serving or storing in an airtight container.

Useful Items

parchment paper, cling wrap, rolling pin, round cookie cutter (2½ in.), cookie sheets (approx. 16 in. x 14 in.), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Dough can be stored in the refrigerator, wrapped in cling wrap, for up to 4 days. Return to room temperature before using.
  • Dough can be cut in half and wrapped as 2 small disks to allow for easier rolling. This also allows you to cook 12 cookies and make a second fresh batch another time.
  • Icing: Insert butterfly whisk. Place 1 egg white into mixing bowl and whisk 1 min/100°F/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Add 1 tsp lemon juice, 3 tbsp water and 16 oz confectioners sugar and whip 20 sec/speed 4. Divide icing into bowls, add desired food coloring gel and stir through thoroughly. Cover icing with a lid or cling wrap until ready to use. Place icing into an icing bag and decorate or garnish cookies as desired. Let icing set, then store cookies in an airtight container. Filled icing bags can be stored in a zip lock bag, in the refrigerator, for up to 2 days. Icing will harden if not sealed from air.
  • Transfer cut dough to cookie sheet by lifting bottom sheet of cling wrap and gently peeling cookie off and placing gently on cookie sheet.
  • Leaving dough to rest for 2 hours or overnight makes it easier to work with and allows flavor to develop.
  • If making gingerbread people you can add faces and buttons by using the thin end of a chopstick. Press eyes and buttons into dough before baking.
  • Any shape cookie cutter can be used with this dough, but may affect the resulting number of cookies, depending on size.
  • Replace 1 oz molasses with 1 oz maple syrup for a lighter cookie.
  • Replace cinnamon, nutmeg and cloves with 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice.
