Muhamara dip

Ingredients

  • 350 g red capsicum (approx. 2 large)
  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 40 g bread, torn in pieces
  • 2 - 4 garlic cloves
  • 80 g walnuts
  • 40 g pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tsp lemon juice, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • ¼ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes (optional - see Tips)
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • fresh parsley or mint leaves, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

  1. Roast capsicum (see Tips) and set aside to cool.
  2. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid, weigh oil into jug and set aside.
  3. Place bread into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9.
  4. Add reserved roasted capsicum, garlic, walnuts, pomegranate molasses, lemon juice, cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper, chilli flakes (optional) and salt and mix 15 sec/speed 6, for a chunky texture (for a smoother texture, see Tips). Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 4, while very slowly pouring reserved olive oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around measuring cup in a thin stream into mixing bowl. Transfer dip into a serving bowl, drizzle with a little oil and garnish with fresh parsley or mint leaves (optional) to serve.

Useful Items

jug, serving bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • To roast capsicum, preheat oven to 200°C. Place capsicum onto a baking tray and roast, turning occasionally, until all sides are charred and flesh is soft. Transfer to a heat-resistant snaplock bag, seal and leave to rest for 10 minutes, then peel off and discard skin.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture after intial blending, blend for an additional 20 sec/speed 9.
  • This sauce can be served as a dip with pita bread, spread onto flatbreads, mixed into stews or served with pasta or potatoes.
