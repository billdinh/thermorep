Limoncello Sorbet with Mint and Basil - Sorbetto al limoncello, menta e basilico

Ingredients

  • 425 g water
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 115 g limoncello, plus 6 tsp for drizzling
  • 170 g lemon juice (approx. 6 lemons)
  • 10 g fresh mint leaves, plus extra for garnishing
  • 20 g fresh basil leaves, plus extra for garnishing
  • lemon peel, cut in strips, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place water, sugar and limoncello in mixing bowl then heat 7 min/98°C/speed 2.
  2. Add lemon juice, mint and basil. Transfer to a large bowl and cover with cling film. Chill for 8 hours or overnight in fridge to allow flavours to infuse (see tip).
  3. Strain chilled mixture through a fine sieve then discard sieve contents. Pour strained liquid into ice cube trays and freeze for at least 10 hours or overnight.
  4. Remove from freezer, unmould frozen blocks into mixing bowl then crush 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scoop balls of sorbet into serving glasses, pour 1 tsp limoncello over each, and garnish with strips of lemon peel, mint and basil. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film, fine sieve, ice cube trays, large bowl, serving glasses, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Limoncello, a traditional Italian lemon liquor, was invented in southern Italy in the early 1900s. While the exact origins of the drink vary from region to region, there is no denying its popularity. With a unique flavour attained from using only the highest quality lemons grown in Campania, limoncello is enjoyed across Italy as a chilled digestif. We’ve used limoncello as a base for our sorbet, with fresh mint and basil resulting in a refreshing dessert that will impress anyone.
  • The mint and basil leaves can be left to infuse for up to 3 days before freezing.
  • Place serving bowls or glasses in the freezer 30 minutes ahead of serving time to keep the sorbet chilled.
