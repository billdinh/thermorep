Lemon and Roast Garlic Speltotto

Ingredients

  • 1 garlic bulb, large
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 40 g shallots, halved
  • 300 g pearled spelt, washed
  • 150 g white wine
  • 500 g chicken stock
  • 150 g single cream, plus extra to taste
  • 2 lemons, finely grated zest and 40 g juice
  • 2 Tbsp fresh chives, finely sliced
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • fine sea salt, to taste, plus extra for sprinkling
  • fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C.
  2. Slice the top off a whole garlic bulb to expose the tops of the cloves. Place on a piece of foil on a baking tray, drizzle with 1 Tbsp oil and sprinkle with a little salt. Wrap in the foil then bake for 1 hour (150°C). Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Unwrap roasted garlic and set aside to cool for 15 minutes, then squeeze pulp out. Place in mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place shallots and remaining 1 Tbsp oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed .
  6. Add spelt and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 
  7. Add wine and 250 g chicken stock then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, reduce 10 min/110°C//speed .
  8. Add remaining 250 g chicken stock then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/98°C//speed  or until spelt is cooked as desired.
  9. Add cream and simmer 1 min/100°C//speed 0.5.
  10. Add ½ heaped Tbsp reserved garlic purée and 20 g reserved grated Parmesan then stir 15 sec//speed 1.
  11. Add lemon zest, lemon juice and chives then mix well with spatula. Season to taste and adjust consistency with more cream if desired. Serve sprinkled with remaining grated Parmesan and garnished with chopped parsley.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Spelt is much more forgiving in this type of dish than risotto rice, as it won't overcook. Roasting the garlic to make a purée gives a nice depth of flavour to the end result.
