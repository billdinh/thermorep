Luxury Fish Pie with Parmesan and Garlic Sweet Potato Mash

Ingredients

  • 1000 g sweet potatoes
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • 30 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)

Filling

  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 6 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves, fresh or dried
  • 250 g cod fillet, skin removed, cut in pieces (5 cm)
  • 250 g smoked haddock, skin removed, cut in pieces (5 cm)
  • 250 g peeled, raw prawns
  • 600 g whole milk
  • 4 medium eggs, hard-boiled, quartered
  • 30 g salted butter
  • 20 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Sweet Potato Topping

  • 30 g butter
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Prick sweet potatoes with a fork then place in a roasting tin together with garlic bulb and roast for 40 minutes (200°C). Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Filling

  1. Place onions, cloves and bay leaves in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket.
  2. Place cod and haddock in basket then place prawns on top. Pour over milk and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.5.
  3. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Transfer partly cooked fish and prawns to an oven-proof dish (approx. 28 cm x 18 cm). Flake or cut fish and distribute with prawns evenly over base of dish. Arrange quartered eggs over fish. Strain milk through a sieve into a bowl then set aside.
  4. Place butter, flour, 300 g strained milk, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then cook 10 min/90°C/speed 4. Pour sauce over fish and eggs then set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Sweet Potato Topping

  1. When sweet potatoes are cooked and cool enough to handle, scoop out the flesh into mixing bowl. Squeeze out cooked garlic cloves and add to mixing bowl.
  2. Add butter, cayenne pepper, salt and pepper then mash 15 sec/speed 7.
  3. Spread mash over fish and eggs then smooth over. Sprinkle over reserved grated Parmesan then place dish on a baking tray to catch any drips and bake for 30 minutes (200°C) until golden brown and bubbling. Allow to stand for 5 minutes before serving hot.

Useful Items

roasting tin, oven-proof dish (approx. 28 cm x 18 cm), medium mesh sieve, baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To hard boil eggs in the Thermomix®, place eggs at room temperature in simmering basket then steam above 400 g water 12 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  • Salmon or other firm fish fillets can be used in place of cod.

  • This is fish pie like you’ve never known before – it will take your idea of comfort food to a whole new level. The sweet potato mash topping is just divine and brings a lovely, colourful variation to a classic British dish. Of course, if you prefer the more traditional version you can simply substitute the topping with normal mashed potato – the pie is still delicious.
