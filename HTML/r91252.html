Gazpacho

Ingredients

  • 35 oz plum tomatoes, ripe, halved
  • 2 oz green bell peppers
  • 1 ½ oz yellow onions
  • 2 ½ oz cucumber, partially peeled
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 oz vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 - 3 ½ oz extra virgin olive oil, to taste
  • 8 ice cubes
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
651 kJ / 155 kcal
Protein
2 g
Carbohydrates
5 g
Fat
13 g

Preparation

  1. Place tomatoes, green peppers, onions, cucumber, garlic, vinegar, salt, olive oil and ice cubes into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5. Blend 3 min/speed 10 and refrigerate for at least one hour. Serve cold.

Useful Items

refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Gazpacho is a summertime soup. Using fresh, ripe tomatoes gives the gazpacho its flavor. If good quality fresh tomatoes are not available, they can be replaced with canned tomatoes, though the soup’s flavor will not be as fresh.
  • Serve soup with small bowls of raw vegetables (e.g. 3.5 oz each of diced tomatoes, green peppers, onions and cucumbers) and bread, cut into small cubes, so guests can add garnishes as desired.
  • If you prefer gazpacho without emulsifying the oil, add it after blending the other ingredients and mix 2 sec/speed 7. Gazpacho will have a deeper red color instead of being orange.
  • Add 7 oz cold water if you prefer it thinner. For thicker gazpacho, add 5 oz stale white bread, cut into pieces.
