Lemon Caper Salmon with Ribbon Squash

Ingredients

  • non-stick cooking spray
  • 2 oz shallots, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 oz unsalted butter, divided
  • 7 oz vegetable stock
  • 1 oz white wine
  • 4 fresh salmon fillets (approx. 6 oz ea.)
  • salt, to season
  • ground black pepper, to season
  • 2 lemons, thinly sliced in rounds
  • 8 oz zucchini
  • 8 oz yellow squash
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • ½ oz lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp capers, drained
  • fresh dill, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Lightly grease Varoma tray with non-stick cooking spray and set aside.
  2. Place shallots, garlic and 0.5 oz butter into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 5 min/250°F/speed 1.
  3. Add vegetable stock and wine. Set Varoma into position, insert Varoma tray and weigh in salmon. Season with salt and pepper. Divide and lay lemon slices on salmon fillets. Secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile with a vegetable peeler, slice zucchini and yellow squash into long thin strips.
  4. Remove Varoma tray and place prepared zucchini and squash ribbons into Varoma dish. Re-insert Varoma tray with salmon, secure Varoma lid and steam for an additional 9 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until salmon is cooked to desired consistency. Set Varoma aside and keep warm.
  5. Add 0.5 oz butter and flour into mixing bowl, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and cook 6 min/Varoma/speed 3. Stir in lemon juice and capers with spatula. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  6. Place reserved steamed vegetable ribbons onto a serving platter and top with salmon fillets. Pour lemon caper sauce over salmon, garnish with dill and serve.

Useful Items

serving platter, vegetable peeler

Hints & Tricks

  • Sauce can be thinned with either lemon juice or vegetable stock.
