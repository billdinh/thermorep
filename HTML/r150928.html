Steamed Sticky Rice with Pork (Rice Tube Puddings)

Ingredients

Sweet Chilli Sauce

  • 2 Tbsp chilli sauce
  • 1 Tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 30 g sugar
  • 100 g water
  • ½ tsp cornflour

Steamed Sticky Rice with Pork

  • 450 g white glutinous rice, short-grain
  • water, plus extra for soaking
  • 50 g soy sauce
  • 30 g sugar
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper
  • 200 g pork meat, lean, sinew free, cut in small strips (5 cm x 1 cm x 0.5 cm)
  • 30 g vegetable oil
  • 12 - 15 dried shiitake mushrooms (approx. 20 g), soaked to soften, cut in strips
  • 50 g dried shrimp (optional)
  • 600 g water
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, roughly chopped, to garnish
  • 10 g roasted peanuts, unsalted, roughly chopped, to garnish

Preparation

Sweet Chilli Sauce

  1. Place chilli sauce, tomato ketchup, sugar, water and cornflour in mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed . Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.

Steamed Sticky Rice with Pork

  1. Place simmering basket on mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it, then rinse under cold running water. Transfer drained rice to a bowl and add water to cover. Set aside and soak for at least 1 hour or refrigerate overnight.
  2. Place a medium bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh soy sauce, sugar, white pepper and pork into it. Stir to coat pork with sauce and set aside to marinate for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place oil, shiitake mushrooms and dried shrimp (if using) in mixing bowl and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add marinated pork along with marinade and stir-fry 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add drained rice and stir-fry 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then transfer mixture to 10 metal moulds (Ø 5 cm, height 5 cm). Place filled moulds in Varoma dish.
  6. Place water in mixing bowl, set Varoma in position and steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and unmould rice puddings. Garnish with chopped coriander and chopped roasted peanuts and serve hot with sweet chilli sauce.

Useful Items

metal moulds (Ø 5 cm, height 5 cm), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Rice tube puddings are a popular snack at Taiwanese night markets. If you remember to soak the rice ahead of time, you can prepare this recipe quickly at home, thanks to your Thermomix®.
  • If using long-grain rice, soak longer: at least 3 hours or overnight in fridge.
    - Dried shrimp are available in Asian supermarkets. You can omit them or replace with shallots, for a different but pleasant result.
    - Use metal moulds such as dariole moulds. You can also use 7 heat-resistant drinking glasses (Ø 7 cm, 6 cm height). In this case, increase water to 900 g and steaming time to 50-55 minutes. If your moulds or glasses are a different size than specified, you may need to adjust the steaming time.
