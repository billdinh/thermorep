Chocolate Peach Trifle

Ingredients

Chocolate Sponge

  • 3 large eggs
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 90 g plain flour
  • 20 g cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp baking powder

Topping

  • 2 tins peach halves, tinned, drained (2 x 420 g tins)
  • 200 g double cream
  • 250 g mascarpone
  • 40 g vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 100 g flaked almonds

Preparation

Chocolate Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line base of a spring form tin (Ø 26 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour, cocoa powder and baking powder then mix 20 sec/speed 2.5. Remove butterfly whisk then finish mixing by hand with spatula. Transfer to prepared tin, smooth down to form an even layer then bake for 10 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  4. Allow sponge to cool for 5 minutes in tin before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
  5. Once cool, cut into 2 cm cubes then divide between 8 individual serving bowls or one large serving dish. Press sponge down slightly.

Topping

  1. Drain peach halves through simmering basket collecting juice in a bowl below. Cut peaches in 1 cm pieces.
  2. Drizzle 1 ½ tsp reserved peach juice over sponge in each serving bowl then arrange peach pieces evenly over sponge layer.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whip without setting a time/speed 3 watching carefully to avoid over-whipping.
  4. Add mascarpone and vanilla sugar then mix 10 sec/speed 4.  Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4. Spread evenly over peach pieces in serving bowls then set aside in fridge to chill for at least 30 minutes. Meanwhile, toast flaked almonds in a non-stick frying pan until browned, then set aside to cool.
  5. Sprinkle cooled, toasted almonds over chilled trifles before serving.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 26 cm), baking paper, wire rack, large serving bowl, 8 individual serving bowls or glasses, long sharp knife, non-stick frying pan, oven, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • For a grown up version, sprinkle sponge with peach juice and your favourite liqueur.
