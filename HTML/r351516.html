Steamed Sea Bass with Lime Sauce

Ingredients

  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 - 2 fresh red chillies, cut in pieces, to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp lime juice
  • 2 Tbsp fish sauce
  • 100 g liquid fish stock
  • 1 sea bass (approx. 500 g), gutted, scaled, belly cut open
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 30 g rice wine
  • 500 g water
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
510 kJ / 122 kcal
Protein
24 g
Carbohydrates
4 g
Fat
0.8 g

Preparation

  1. Place coriander, garlic, chillies, sugar, lime juice, fish sauce and fish stock in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Make three cuts on each side of sea bass. Season the fish inside and out with salt. Place fish in a ceramic baking dish (see tip) and pour over rice wine. Place a steaming rack in Varoma dish and place dish with fish on steaming rack.
  3. Place water in mixing bowl, place covered Varoma into position and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and discard cooking juices from the fish.
  4. Place dish with fish back in Varoma dish and pour over reserved sauce. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed 1. Carefully remove dish from Varoma and serve immediately.

Useful Items

steaming rack, ceramic baking dish

Hints & Tricks

  • Steamed fish is prepared in several different ways in oriental cooking. It is so popular it is often served at Chinese weddings as one of the courses.
  • The fish can be steamed in any heat-proof dish that will hold the fish and fit in the Varoma with the lid on. In Chinese cooking, fish is often steamed in the serving dish and served straight from the steamer.
  • Serve with steamed rice and vegetables for a complete meal.
  • Substitute sea bass with flounder, tilapia or any similar white fish that can be cooked whole.
