Slow cooked beef with wasabi mash (Louise Keats)

Ingredients

Slow cooked beef

  • 60 g soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp raw sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 4 star anise
  • 800 g beef rump cap (see Tips)

Wasabi mash

  • 800 g red skinned potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (approx. 2 cm)
  • 200 g full cream milk
  • 30 g butter
  • 2 tsp wasabi paste, to taste
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste

Steamed greens

  • 500 g water
  • 1 bunch bok choy (see Tips)
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, cut into thin slices, to serve (optional)

Preparation

Slow cooked beef

  1. Place soy sauce, oil, sugar, cinnamon and star anise into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place a large piece of baking paper (approx. 30 x 45 cm) onto a large piece of aluminium foil (approx. 30 x 45 cm). Place beef rump cap onto baking paper and pour over marinade, rubbing mixture into beef to ensure it is coated. Take care to ensure marinade is kept on the baking paper. Fold up sides of foil to create a package, and use another piece of foil to completely enclose coated beef. Place into refrigerator to marinate overnight.
  3. Preheat oven to 120°C.
  4. Place wrapped beef into a large roasting tray and slow roast for 5 hours (120°C), until tender.

Wasabi mash

  1. Place potatoes and milk into mixing bowl and cook 20-25 min/100°C/speed 1 or until potatoes are tender and falling apart.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add butter, wasabi paste and salt and mash 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer mash into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Steamed greens

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, then place bok choy into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 5-15 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until bok choy is cooked to your liking.
  2. Slice cooked beef and serve with Wasabi mash and steamed bok choy. Garnish with spring onion/shallot (optional), and drizzle with any beef cooking juices before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, aluminium foil, large roasting tray, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you can't find beef rump cap, you can also use brisket, beef short ribs or other types of beef cuts that benefit from slow cooking.
  • You may need to have your butcher order beef rump cap for you; a marbled piece will work best.
  • You can substitute bok choy with broccolini.
