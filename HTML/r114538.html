Pilaf with fragrant tarka

Ingredients

Pilaf

  • 300 g basmati rice
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 1 tsp rosewater
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 40 g ghee
  • 4 fresh curry leaves

Tarka

  • 60 g brown onion
  • 60 g ghee
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 4 cardamom pods, seeds only
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Assembly

  • 50 g roasted salted cashew nuts, cut into pieces
  • 1 lemon, juice only (approx. 40 g)
  • 1 tsp salt

Preparation

Pilaf

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh rice into it. Add enough water to cover rice, then add saffron and set aside to soak for 30 minutes.
  2. Drain rice using simmering basket. Place 1000 g water into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket with rice in it. Add rosewater, salt, ghee and curry leaves to simmering basket. Stir with aid of spatula and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl. Gently stir, then cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tarka

  1. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add ghee and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add cinnamon, cardamom seeds, bay leaf and nutmeg, then cook 7 min/Varoma/speed 2, without measuring cup.

Assembly

  1. Transfer tarka into thermal serving bowl with rice. Add cashews, lemon juice and salt and gently stir to combine, then serve.

Useful Items

bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Tarka is a flavoured oil that is used to season Indian dishes.

  • Lamb pilaf with tarka: Proceed as per recipe with steps 1-4. Add 300 g minced lamb to tarka, along with the spices in step 5. Layer cooked rice and lamb mixture in an ovenproof dish (remove cinnamon quill and bay leaf). Cover and bake for 30 minutes (150°C). Uncover and increase heat to 190°C and bake for 15 minutes (190°C), until top is crunchy.
