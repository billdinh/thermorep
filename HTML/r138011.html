Masala bread rolls

Ingredients

  • 300 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 430 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g wholemeal flour
  • 80 g tandoori paste (see Tips)
  • poppy seeds (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add oil, salt, flours and tandoori paste and mix 6 sec/speed 6, then knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap dough in silicone bread mat or place into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap or kitchen towel. Leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes).
  4. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Knock down dough and divide into 12 equal portions. Shape into balls and place onto prepared tray, keeping a 5 cm space between each roll. Brush rolls with a little water and sprinkle with poppy seeds (optional).
  5. Place into a cold oven and set temperature to 200°C. Bake for 20-30 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Rolls should sound hollow when tapped underneath. Allow to cool on tray for 5 minutes, then serve warm.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, bowl, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own tandoori paste, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • If rolls are not served warm, transfer to a wire rack to cool completely, then place into a sealable container for up to 3 days.
  • Replace the tandoori paste with 2-3 tablespoons of your favourite chutney or 1 teaspoon dried fruit and nuts.
