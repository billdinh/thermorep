Veal pizzaiola

Ingredients

  • 500 g lean veal steak or veal schnitzel, cut into thin pieces (5 mm - 1 cm thick)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 sprig fresh oregano, leaves only, finely chopped or 1/4 tsp dried oregano
  • 250 g potatoes, thinly sliced (approx. 1-2 mm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 150 g), cut into halves
  • 400 g canned diced tomatoes
  • 40 g pitted Kalamata olives, sliced
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 150 g broccoli, broken into florets

Preparation

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh veal and 20 g of the olive oil into it. Add sea salt and oregano to the veal and set aside for 10 minutes to marinate.
  2. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh potato into it. Insert Varoma tray and place marinated veal, with marinade, onto it. Set Varoma aside on top of a piece of baking paper to catch any drips.
  3. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add remaining 20 g olive oil and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add canned tomatoes, olives and pepper. Season to taste then cook 12 min/100°C//speed .
  6. Place Varoma with veal and potatoes into position. Secure Varoma lid and cook 5 min/Varoma//speed . Remove any cooked pieces of veal and transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Rotate remaining partially cooked pieces of veal with tongs, then lightly stir potato with spatula to ensure even cooking.
  7. Weigh broccoli into Varoma dish on top of potato, secure Varoma lid and cook for a further 3 min/Varoma//speed .
  8. Remove any cooked pieces of veal and set aside in thermal serving bowl or large bowl, then secure Varoma lid and cook for a further 1-2 min/Varoma//speed , or until vegetables and veal are cooked to your preference.
  9. Place veal and vegetables onto serving plates, top with sauce and season to taste. Serve warm.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, baking paper, tongs, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, serving plates

Hints & Tricks

  • Pour remaining sauce over rice or pasta, or transfer into a sealable container and place in the refrigerator to use in other recipes.
  • Try this recipe using thinly sliced chicken or turkey fillets.
