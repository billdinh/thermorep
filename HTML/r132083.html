Curried chicken rasam

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 200 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 200 g carrots, cut into quarters
  • 10 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves, stems and roots, cut into thirds (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 - 3 tsp Madras curry powder (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 12 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp ghee (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp black mustard seeds
  • 1 fresh tomato (approx. 120 g), cut into quarters
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 200 g basmati rice
  • 250 g skinless chicken breast fillets, cut into cubes (1 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place oil, onion, carrots, fresh coriander, ginger paste, garlic paste, Madras powder, chillies and 6 of the curry leaves into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat chopping 30 sec/speed 8 or until a paste. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place ghee into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until hot.
  4. Add mustard seeds and remaining 6 curry leaves and cook 30 sec/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add reserved onion mixture and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Add tomato and turmeric and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  7. Add water, stock paste, bay leaf, salt and pepper. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear. Re-insert simmering basket and cook 14 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  8. Add chicken to mixing bowl and cook 10 min/90°C//speed . Adjust seasoning as needed. Divide rice between 4 serving bowls and top with rasam before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, 4 serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • To make your own ginger paste, garlic paste, Madras curry powder and ghee, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
