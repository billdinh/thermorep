Soba noodle and tofu salad

Ingredients

Dressing

  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger
  • 6 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, roots and stalks (leaves reserved for garnishing)
  • 40 g rice wine vinegar
  • 40 g pure maple syrup
  • 40 g tamari
  • 1 tbsp tahini (see Tips)
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp miso paste
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 200 g extra firm tofu, cut into pieces (1 cm thick x 2-3 cm length)

Salad

  • 1600 g water
  • 200 g frozen edamame beans
  • 200 g dried soba noodles
  • 1 carrot (approx. 100 g), cut into matchsticks
  • 1 fresh Lebanese cucumber (approx. 100 g), cut into halves lengthways, deseeded and thinly sliced
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 80 g snow peas, cut into thin slices
  • 1 fresh chilli, cut into thin slices

Preparation

Dressing

  1. Place all ingredients, except tofu, into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 9.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Transfer dressing into a large bowl, add sliced tofu and stir to combine. Set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.

Salad

  1. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma dish into position and weigh edamame beans into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook  12 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and stir edamame.
  2. Add soba noodles into mixing bowl, place Varoma back into position and cook 3 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer edamame into a bowl and set aside to cool. Once cooled, remove beans from pods and discard pods. Set beans aside.
  3. Transfer noodles into Varoma dish and rinse under cold water to cease cooking. Set aside to drain.
  4. Transfer drained noodles into large bowl with dressing and tofu and stir to combine.
  5. Transfer noodles onto a serving platter, top with remaining ingredients and garnish with coriander leaves. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

large bowl, bowl, serving platter

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own tahini, refer to the Tahini recipe on this recipe chip.
  • This salad is best eaten on the day it is made.
  • You can find edamame beans in the frozen food section of most supermarkets.
