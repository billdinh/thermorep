Fish balls with yoghurt sauce

Ingredients

  • 250 g natural yoghurt
  • 40 g day old bread (approx. 1 slice), cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 540 g water
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • ½ lemon zested, no white pith, and juiced
  • 400 g firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces (2 cm) (see Tips)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 pinches chilli powder
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to season
  • extra virgin olive oil, for forming balls

Preparation

  1. Place a nut milk bag or fine-mesh sieve over a bowl. Place bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt into nut milk bag. Place bowl with yoghurt into refrigerator for 1 hour to drain. Discard any strained liquid.
  2. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh bread and 40 g of the water into it. Set aside to soak (approx. 10 minutes).
  3. Place parsley, spring onions/shallots and lemon zest into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Transfer half of the parsley mixture into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Add fish, egg, chilli powder, salt, pepper, lemon juice and reserved soaked bread into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  5. Lightly grease hands with oil. Form mixture into 16 balls (approx. 1 tablespoon each) and place into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray. Set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until balls are cooked through.
  7. Transfer strained yoghurt into a small bowl. Add reserved parsley mixture, season with salt and pepper and stir to combine. Serve fish balls immediately. 

Useful Items

small bowl , nut bag or fine mesh sieve, ceramic or glass bowl, bowl, serving platter, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any type of firm white fish fillet such as barramundi, snapper or ling in this recipe.
  • For a different flavour, try replacing the parsley with tarragon or coriander.
  • For a more complete meal, serve with a fresh green salad.
