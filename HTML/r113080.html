Queso fresco

Ingredients

  • 2000 g full cream milk
  • ½ rennet tablet
  • 4 tbsp filtered water
  • 1 tsp citric acid
  • ½ tsp non-iodised salt, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 30 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Place rennet and 2 tablespoons of the water into a bowl and set aside to dissolve. Place citric acid and remaining 2 tablespoons water into a separate bowl and set aside to dissolve.
  3. Mix 1 min/speed 1, adding rennet and citric acid mixtures through hole in mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a warmed thermal serving bowl or other warmed large ceramic bowl (see Tips) and set aside for 30 minutes.
  4. Gently stir curds with aid of spatula, then set aside to rest for 10 minutes. Gently stir again and rest for a further 10 minutes. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers muslin cloth, then place simmering basket over a bowl. Transfer curds into lined simmering basket. Set aside to drain for 30 minutes, discarding whey as needed.
  5. Add salt to curds and stir through with aid of spatula. Transfer simmering basket with cheese into refrigerator (see Tips) and place a heavy weight on top of the cheese. Chill for 1-2 hours or until cheese is firm, then serve as desired (see Tips).

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or large ceramic bowl, small bowl, large bowl, nut milk bag or 2 muslin cloths, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Queso fresco - "fresh cheese" - is a Mexican cheese, traditionally made from cow's milk or a combination of cow and goat milk. It is a creamy, soft, mild white cheese and is a great addition to dishes like enchiladas, huevos rancheros, soups, salads or grilled vegetables.
  • Rennet is a complex set of enzymes produced by ruminant mammals and is often used to separate milk into solid curds and liquid whey. You can purchase rennet at cheese making companies, in some major kitchenware stores and online.
  • To warm a thermal serving bowl or ceramic bowl, fill with hot water, let stand for a few minutes, then discard water and dry bowl.
  • You can store this cheese in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
