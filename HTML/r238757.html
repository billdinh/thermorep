Ingredients

  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • 170 g water
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 250 g Havarti cheese
  • 150 g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 50 g fresh baby spinach
  • 2 medium eggs
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 50 g unsalted butter, melted

Preparation

  1. Place yeast, sugar and water in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/45°C/speed 1. Leave to stand for 10 minutes.
  2. Add flour, oil and salt then knead 3 min/. Transfer to a lightly-greased bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 45 minutes).
  3. Preheat oven to 220°C and warm a baking tray for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place Havarti cheese in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl, add feta, then combine and set aside.
  5. Knock down dough and divide in 2. On a lightly-floured work surface, roll dough out into Ø 25 cm circles approx. 3 mm thick. Divide spinach between the 2 circles and sprinkle half the cheese mixture over dough, leaving a 1.5 cm border around the edge.
  6. Starting at one side of 1 circle, tightly roll the dough ⅓ of the way toward the centre. Repeat on the opposite end to leave a gap of 5-7 cm between the two rolls. Repeat with second circle. Pinch each end of the rolls together and twist to seal, making a boat shape. Finish by dividing remaining cheese mixture between the pastries.
  7. Transfer to warm baking tray and bake for 14-16 minutes (220°C) until golden brown.
  8. Crack 1 egg into centre of each boat and season with salt and pepper to taste. Return to oven for 4-5 minutes, until egg is just set. With a pastry brush, brush edges with melted butter and serve hot.

Useful Items

baking tray, pastry brush, rolling pin, cling film, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This cheese-filled bread is a traditional dish of Georgia.
  • This recipe can easily be doubled.
  • Semi-hard cow's milk such as Monterey Jack or Provolone may be used in place of Havarti.
