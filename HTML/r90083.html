Olive and fennel grissini

Ingredients

Olive and fennel

  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 150 g pitted Kalamata olives, drained

Grissini

  • 500 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 pinch caster sugar
  • 20 g fresh yeast
  • 2 tsp sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 70 g poppy seeds
  • 50 g sesame seeds
  • 80 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for brushing and greasing
  • 300 g water, warm

Preparation

Olive and fennel

  1. Place fennels seeds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat milling 10 sec/speed 9, or until a fine consistency is achieved.
  2. Add olives and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat chopping 5 sec/speed 6, until a coarse paste consistency is achieved. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Grissini

  1. Place all grissini ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 6 sec/speed 6, then knead 2 min/.
  2. Transfer dough onto a heavily floured silicone bread mat or work surface and work into a ball. Wrap dough in silicone bread mat or place into a lightly greased ceramic or glass bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Transfer proved dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, then gently flatten dough and roll into a rectangle (approx. 50 x 30 cm).
  5. Spread reserved olive and fennel paste over dough, then season to preference with extra salt.
  6. Fold dough in half along the vertical axis to form a 25 x 30 cm rectangle, enclosing the paste. Cut the folded dough horizontally into 3 evenly-sized pieces, then cut each piece horizontally into 8 evenly-sized strips. Stretch each strip to approx. 35 cm in length, then twist several times.
  7. Place twists onto prepared baking trays and brush with extra olive oil. Bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) or until golden, then transfer to a wire rack to cool. Depending on the size of your baking trays, you may need to bake grissini in batches.
  8. Turn off your oven, then when all grissini have finished baking, transfer back onto baking trays and place back into the oven to dry out and cool completely for a few hours or overnight. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable container until ready to use.

Useful Items

pastry brush, rolling pin, 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, ceramic or glass bowl, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, sealable container, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Grissini make a fantastic addition to a festive nibbles platter or cheese board. Try serving with the Baked artichoke dip.
