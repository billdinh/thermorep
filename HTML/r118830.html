Fortune cookies

Ingredients

  • 90 g egg whites, at room temperature
  • 90 g caster sugar
  • 60 g unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ tsp almond essence
  • ½ tsp vanilla essence
  • 60 g water
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch fine salt
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
123 kJ / 29 kcal
Protein
0.5 g
Carbohydrates
4.1 g
Fat
1.2 g

Preparation

  1. Cut 40 small rectangles of paper (1 x 6 cm). Write individual fortunes on each piece. Set aside until ready to fold cookies.
  2. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with silicone bread mats and set aside. Set aside an empty 12-hole muffin tray.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add melted butter, almond essence, vanilla essence and water and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add flour and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 2.  Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and lightly fold in any remaining flour until just combined.
  7. Spoon a dessert-spoonful of batter onto 1 of the prepared baking trays and use the back of the spoon to spread it into a circle (approx. 10 cm diameter). Repeat to make 2 more cookies, leaving a 2 cm gap between each circle (3 circles per tray).
  8. Bake for 6-8 minutes (180°C), until edge of each cookie just starts to turn golden. While the first tray is in the oven, spoon a dessert-spoonful of batter onto the remaining prepared baking tray and use the back of the spoon to spread it into a circle (approx. 10 cm diameter). Repeat to make 2 more cookies, leaving a 2 cm gap between each circle (3 circles per tray).
  9. Remove tray from oven and, working quickly, shape cookies before they set (see Tips). Using a palette knife or similar, gently remove a cookie from tray and turn it upside down onto a clean surface. Place a fortune in the middle of cookie and fold cookie in half. Pick up the cookie, place straight edge over the lip of a cup or mug and gently bend down both sides to form a crescent. Place fortune cookie into muffin tray to hold shape as it cools. Repeat with remaining cookies.
  10. Place second tray into oven and bake for 6-8 minutes (180°C), until edge of each cookie just starts to turn golden. Continue with remaining batter, preparing each tray while the other is in the oven, and quickly folding cookies (makes 40 cookies).
  11. If cooled cookies are not crisp and golden, transfer them onto a large baking tray. Turn off oven and leave oven door open for 1 minute to cool down a little, then place cookies into oven, close door and leave until oven is cold.
  12. Allow cookies to cool completely before serving or storing in a sealable container.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), 12-hole muffin tray, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), palette knife, sealable container, 2 silicone bread mats, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Folding the cookies before they harden can be tricky. It's a good idea to practice this recipe before making it for a special occasion.
  • If you're finding it hard to fold your fortune cookies before they harden, make just 2 at a time and bake them for 5-6 minutes (180°C).
  • Baking paper can be used instead of silicone bread mats, but is harder to work with.
