Green Bean Pizza

Ingredients

Dough

  • 230 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 100 g wholemeal bread flour
  • 300 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for flouring
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 1500 g water
  • 150 g green beans, cut in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 150 g mozzarella, drained, cut in pieces
  • 150 g cream cheese
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 50 g basil pesto (optional)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil (optional)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil.
  2. Add remaining 180 g water, wholemeal flour, strong white flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film then set aside to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours). Towards end of rising time, prepare topping.

Topping

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl, insert simmering basket, add green beans and cook 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Empty mixing bowl and rinse with cold water.
  3. Transfer risen dough to prepared baking tray. Use fingertips to spread dough out to the size of the tray, with a slightly thicker edge. Place in oven and parbake for 15 minutes (220°C). Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  4. Place mozzarella in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place cream cheese, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  6. Remove parbaked pizza base from oven then spread over cream cheese mixture, pesto, reserved chopped mozzarella and reserved steamed green beans. Bake for a further 10 minutes (220°C)
  7. Drizzle with oil and serve immediately.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, large baking tray, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  •      Alternatively, roll dough out on a floured surface with a rolling pin then transfer to prepared baking tray.
