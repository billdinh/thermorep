Raspberry Peach Crumb Cake

Ingredients

Fruit Filling

  • 170 g unsalted butter, cold, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g fresh raspberries
  • 300 g peaches, peeled, sliced (2 cm) (approx. 3-4 peaches)
  • 1 Tbsp cornflour

Crumble Cake

  • 320 g plain flour
  • 280 g granulated sugar, plus 1 Tbsp for sprinkling
  • 40 g flaked almonds
  • 100 g sour cream
  • 100 g milk
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

Fruit Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line base and sides of a spring form cake tin (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place raspberries, peaches and cornflour in mixing bowl then stir with spatula to coat fruit. Heat 3 min/90°C//speed . Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then heat again 2 min/90°C//speed .
  3. Set scales to zero and remove 180 g to a bowl (scales will show -180 g). Blend remaining fruit 20 sec/speed 8. Strain through a fine sieve into bowl with reserved fruit and stir to combine. Set aside, discard pulp and seeds from sieve then clean mixing bowl.

Crumble Cake

  1. Place flour, butter and sugar in mixing bowl then mix Turbo/2 sec/3 times. Set scales to zero and remove 200 g crumble mixture to a bowl (scales will show -200 g).
  2. Place bowl with crumble mixture on mixing bowl lid and weigh in almonds. Remove bowl and mix together well then set aside.
  3. Add to remaining crumble mixture in mixing bowl, sour cream, milk, egg, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, vanilla extract and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Transfer to prepared tin spreading out evenly. Spoon reserved fruit filling on top, gently spreading to cover, leaving a 1-2 cm border.
  5. Top with reserved almond crumble topping and sprinkle with 1 Tbsp sugar. Bake for 1 hour 10 - 1 hour 15 minutes (170°C), covering with foil halfway through to avoid browning too much, until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tin for 15 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. Serve in slices at room temperature.

Useful Items

baking paper, spring form tin (Ø 20 cm), fine sieve or nut milk bag, large bowl, 2 bowls, wire rack, aluminium foil, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

A cake for the summer if you prefer to use fruits when they’re in season. The crumb topping on this sponge provides a lovely sweet crunch against the soft fruits.
