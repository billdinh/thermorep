Lollipop Cake

Ingredients

Cake Layers

  • 400 caster sugar
  • 400 butter, unsalted, softened, cut in pieces
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp natural vanilla extract
  • 6 eggs
  • 400 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp pink food colouring
  • 1 tsp green food colouring

Filling and Topping

  • 400 g caster sugar
  • 300 g butter, unsalted, softened, cut in pieces
  • 100 g milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Cake Assembly

  • 50 g sprinkles
  • 50 lollipops

Preparation

Cake Layers

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Put an ovenproof-dish with water in the base of the oven, for example a loaf tin half filled with water. Grease 3 springform tins (Ø 20 cm) and line the base and sides with baking paper.
  2. Place sugar, butter, salt and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/speed 4.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk and whisk 2 min/speed 4.
  4. Add eggs and 40 g self-raising flour then whisk 3 min/speed 4.
  5. Add remaining 360 g self-raising flour and whisk 1 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk and transfer 480 g cake batter into the first prepared springform tin (see tip). Bake for 25 minutes (180°C), until a wooden skewer comes out clean when inserted. Meanwhile continue with recipe.
  6. Transfer half of the remaining cake batter from the mixing bowl to a bowl and set aside.
  7. Add pink food colouring to mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer to the second prepared springform tin and set aside.
  8. Add green food colouring to reserved cake batter in the bowl and mix well using spatula. Transfer to the third prepared springform tin. Place the two springform tins in the oven, on the middle rack, and bake for 25 minutes (180°C), until a skewer comes out clean when inserted. Allow cakes to cool in tin for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely (approx. 50 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling and Topping

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add butter, milk and natural vanilla extract then mix 2 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.

Cake Assembly

  1. Check that each cake layer is level. If it is not, use a bread knife to level the top. Place a small teaspoonful of reserved buttercream topping onto a plate and add the first reserved cake. Spoon ⅓ of reserved buttercream mixture onto the cake layer and carefully level off the buttercream. Repeat with the second reserved cake and add the third cake. Spoon the remaining buttercream topping onto the cake surface. Carefully press cake sprinkles, a spoonful at a time, into the edges of the buttercream filling, on both layers. Decorate the surface with cake sprinkles and mini lollipops.

Useful Items

springform tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, cooling rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This cake can be baked in two tins, if three are not available. Bake the vanilla cake first, and turn it out immediately onto a wire rack to cool. Wash, dry and prepare the tin, and use this for the third cake.
  • Use the built-in Thermomix scales to measure out the exact quantity of cake batter for each cake tin. At the end of the recipe, turn scales on, lift out mixing bowl and pour some cake batter into the prepared tin. When you return the mixing bowl to the base, it will show a minus figure indicating the weight removed (- 320 g).
  • Lollipops are a fun way to decorate this cake, however other confectionery can also be used. Choose confectionery with fun colours!
