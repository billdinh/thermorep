Peshwari naan

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 190 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 450 g baker's flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 90 g Greek-style natural yoghurt

Filling

  • 100 g blanched almonds
  • 3 tsp brown sugar
  • 3 tsp raisins
  • 1 ¼ tbsp desiccated coconut, plus extra for sprinkling (optional)
  • 20 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • ghee, melted, for brushing (see Tips)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl with vegetable oil and set aside.
  2. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add flour, salt, sugar, yoghurt and oil and mix 5 sec/speed 7 or until well combined, then knead 3 min/.
  4. Transfer dough into prepared bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and set aside to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 50 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place almonds, sugar, raisins, coconut and cream into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 7 or until a paste forms.
  2. Knock down dough, then transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface.
  3. Divide into 6 equal portions (approx. 120 g each) and shape into a ball. Make a shallow indent in the centre of each ball with your thumb. Place 1½ tablespoons of almond mixture into indent. Pull edges of dough together to enclose filling, pinch to seal and reshape into a ball. Using a rolling pin, roll each ball, into an oval (approx. 22 cm long).
  4. Set a plate aside. Place a large frying pan over high heat. Working 1 naan at a time, place into frying pan and cook until dark brown blisters form (approx. 1-1½ minutes each side). Remove naan from heat and place onto the plate. Then brush with ghee and sprinkle with extra coconut (optional). Transfer onto a serving dish and cover to keep warm. Repeat frying, brushing and sprinkling with remaining naan. Serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, kitchen towel, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, serving plates, pastry brush, large plate, large frying pan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ghee, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Baked naans can be frozen. Allow to cool and store in a freezable bag or container, layered between pieces of baking paper. Defrost thoroughly before reheating in a hot oven (220°C). Place the naan on a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) lined with baking paper and sprinkle with a little water and heat until warmed through (approx. 5 minutes).

Oven variation: Preheat oven to 220°C and place 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) into oven to warm. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and carefully weigh 250 g water into it. Working in batches, remove baking tray from top shelf of oven and place 3 naan onto it and return to top shelf in oven. Quickly, but carefully, pour half of the water onto the lower baking tray to create steam and immediately close oven door to trap steam. Bake for 4-6 minutes (220°C) or until naan are puffed with golden brown spots. Carefully remove naan and brush with reserved ghee and sprinkle with extra coconut (optional). Repeat with remaining naan. Serve warm.
