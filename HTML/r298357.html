Black Forest Trifle

Ingredients

Cherry Compote

  • 250 g frozen pitted dark cherries
  • 250 g tinned pitted black cherries, in syrup, drained, syrup reserved (1 x 425 g tin)
  • 50 g water
  • 1 Tbsp golden caster sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 Tbsp cornflour, mixed with 2 Tbsp water

Chocolate Swiss Roll

  • 4 large eggs
  • 100 g golden caster sugar, plus 1 Tbsp for sprinkling
  • 80 g self-raising flour
  • 20 g cocoa powder (preferably dark)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Chocolate Custard

  • 180 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 500 g milk
  • 100 g golden caster sugar
  • 20 g cornflour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 20 g unsalted butter (see tip) (optional)

Assembly

  • 40 g Kirsch (cherry brandy) (optional)
  • 300 g double cream

Preparation

Cherry Compote

  1. Place frozen cherries, 125 g tinned cherries, water, sugar and cinnamon (if using) in mixing bowl, heat 5 min/100°C/speed  then blend briefly 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add cornflour mixture and remaining 125 g tinned cherries then stir 1 min/95°C/speed 0.5. Transfer to a bowl and leave to cool completely. Clean mixing bowl then, once compote has cooled, make sponge.

Chocolate Swiss Roll

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a Swiss roll tin with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and 100 g sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 3 min/speed 4. Whisk again 3 min/37°C/speed 4 then remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Add flour, cocoa powder and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 3, finishing mixing by hand with spatula. Transfer to prepared tin and bake for 10-12 minutes (200°C) until sponge is cooked and begins to shrink from edges of tin. Meanwhile, place a large piece of baking paper on a work surface and sprinkle with 1 Tbsp sugar.
  4. Turn cooked sponge out onto sugared baking paper, carefully remove baking paper lining and leave to cool slightly.
  5. Spread with reserved cherry compote then, using the sugared paper to help, roll up firmly. Leave to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make custard.

Chocolate Custard

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Remove 3 Tbsp and set aside then grate again 7 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add milk, sugar, cornflour and eggs then cook 12 min/90°C/speed 3.
  3. Add butter (if using) and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and leave to cool slightly. Clean mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Cut cooled chocolate Swiss roll into 5 cm slices. Arrange enough slices in a single layer to cover base and slightly up the sides of a large glass trifle bowl. Drizzle Kirsch (if using) and half the reserved cherry syrup over cake slices (see tip). Pour over cooled chocolate custard, cover surface with cling film and leave to set in fridge (approx. 30 minutes).
  2. Once custard has set, spread remaining cherry compote over surface. Cover again and return to fridge.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whip without setting a time/speed 3 until softly whipped, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Smooth cream over surface of trifle.
  4. Cover and return to fridge until needed or serve immediately, sprinkled with reserved chopped chocolate.

Useful Items

baking paper, cling film, trifle bowl, oven, refrigerator, Swiss roll tin

Hints & Tricks

  • Smooth chocolate custard, fruity cherry compote and lashings of whipped cream come together to make our impressive trifle. Inspired by a retro classic, each layer is super simple to make thanks to your Thermomix®. The cherry compote and chocolate Swiss roll sponge can be made a day in advance then the remaining steps finished off on the day of serving. This one tastes as good as it looks!
  • Adding butter to the custard makes it extra rich and glossy.
  • If not using Kirsch, add extra cherry syrup.
