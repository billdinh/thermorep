Raspberry Cornettos - Cornetti di lamponi

Ingredients

Gelato

  • 225 g granulated sugar
  • 300 g double cream
  • 410 g whole milk
  • 4 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 vanilla pod and scraped out seeds
  • 450 g fresh raspberries

Raspberry Ripple Sauce

  • 10 g caster sugar
  • ½ Tbsp water

Assembly

  • 70 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 70 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 9 waffle cones
  • blanched hazelnuts, chopped, for garnish

Preparation

Gelato

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add cream, milk, egg yolks, vanilla pod and scraped out seeds then cook 6 min/80°C//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, remove vanilla pod and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place raspberries in mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 9. Pass through a fine sieve then return 190 g raspberry purée to mixing bowl. Set aside remaining sieved raspberry purée.
  4. Add reserved egg mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Pour into a shallow freezer proof container, set aside to cool, then freeze for at least 8 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make raspberry ripple sauce.

Raspberry Ripple Sauce

  1. Place reserved raspberry purée, sugar and water in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, cook 7 min/Varoma/speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool.

Assembly

  1. Place milk chocolate and dark chocolate in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 2 min/40°C/speed . Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt again 2 min/40°C/speed .
  2. Pour approx. 1 tsp melted chocolate into each waffle cone then, using a pastry brush, coat the inside and the outer rim of each cone with melted chocolate then set aside to dry. Once set, tightly wrap waffle cones in greaseproof paper leaving 2.5 cm paper above the top of each cone. Use tape to secure.
  3. Remove frozen mixture from freezer, briefly dip container in hot water then turn out frozen mixture onto a chopping board. Cut in 4 cm pieces then place half the frozen pieces in mixing bowl, crush 20 sec/speed 6, then churn 15 sec/speed 3. Divide churned gelato between all 9 waffle cones. Place remaining frozen pieces in mixing bowl, crush 15 sec/speed 3 then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl then pour in reserved raspberry sauce in a circular motion without stirring.
  4. Top each cone with rippled gelato then smooth the tops. Sprinkle with chopped nuts then freeze for at least 5 hours, or until fully set, before serving.

Useful Items

fine sieve, pastry brush, greaseproof paper, chopping board, long sharp knife, freezer-proof container, fridge, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Although it’s hard to imagine a world without ice cream cones, they haven’t always been around. The first ice cream cone was produced in 1896 by an Italian immigrant in New York, and the idea of eating frozen desserts out of a cone-shaped waffle soon caught on. In 1959, an ice cream manufacturer based in Naples came up with a way to sell frozen, ready-made ice cream cones by pouring a mixture of oil, sugar and chocolate into the inside of the waffle cone to protect it and prevent it turning soggy.
