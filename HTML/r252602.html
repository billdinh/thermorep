Sweet Potato Gnocchi - Gnocchi di patate dolci

Ingredients

Gnocchi

  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1000 g water
  • 850 g sweet potatoes, peeled, sliced (3 cm)
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Gorgonzola Sauce

  • 20 g plain flour
  • 20 g unsalted butter
  • 300 g milk
  • water, for cooking gnocchi
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g Gorgonzola piccante, cut in pieces
  • 2 Tbsp fresh chives, finely chopped

Preparation

Gnocchi

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl then place Varoma dish into position. Weigh sweet potatoes into Varoma dish and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until potatoes are cooked.
  3. Remove Varoma, transfer sweet potatoes to a plate and leave to cool to room temperature (approx. 15 minutes). Meanwhile, empty mixing bowl.
  4. Place cooled sweet potatoes in mixing bowl and mash 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mash again 5 sec/speed 6.
  5. Add reserved grated Parmesan, flour, garlic powder and salt then mix 5 sec/speed 1.
  6. Transfer dough to a well-floured surface and bring together into a ball. Add more flour if dough is too sticky to work with, although it should be soft and fairly tacky.
  7. Divide into pieces and roll each piece out to form ropes (Ø 2 cm). Cut ropes into 2.5 cm pieces. Gently press and roll each against a fork or gnocchi board to make small grooves. Transfer gnocchi to a baking tray dusted with flour, and continue until all dough has been used.

Gorgonzola Sauce

  1. Place flour, butter and milk in mixing bowl then cook 6 min/90°C/speed 4. Meanwhile, bring a large saucepan of water with 1 tsp salt added to the boil.
  2. Add Gorgonzola and remaining ½ tsp salt to mixing bowl then cook 2 min/90°C/speed 4.
  3. Cook gnocchi, in batches, in boiling water until they rise to the surface (2-3 minutes). Remove with a slotted spoon then transfer to serving plates and pour over Gorgonzola sauce.
  4. Sprinkle with chives and serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking tray, large saucepan, slotted spoon, serving plates, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Gnocchi are small dough dumplings that have been eaten in Italy since Roman times. Ancient gnocchi were made from a semolina porridge-like dough that was mixed with eggs, variations of which are still eaten today. Gnocchi made from potato is a fairly recent discovery, occurring after the introduction of potatoes to Europe in the 16th century. In this recipe we’ve used sweet potatoes to add an extra level of flavour and colour as well as complementing the decadent Gorgonzola sauce perfectly.
  • Shaped gnocchi can be frozen before being cooked, then boiled straight from frozen. Freeze in a single layer on a baking tray, then transfer to a freezer bag and keep frozen until needed.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese and Gorgonzola for a vegetarian blue cheese.
