Brown butter ice cream with peanut butter ganache swirl

Ingredients

Ice cream

  • 90 g butter
  • 500 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 250 g full cream milk
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 70 g raw sugar
  • 70 g dark brown sugar
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Ganache

  • 100 g roasted salted peanuts
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste (optional)
  • 100 g white chocolate, cut into pieces
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • waffle cones, to serve (optional)

Preparation

Ice cream

  1. Place a frying pan over low heat. Place butter into frying pan and cook until butter foams and starts to brown (approx. 5 minutes). The butter should have a nutty aroma, but not a burnt taste. Carefully strain butter through a nut milk bag or fine mesh sieve into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place remaining ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 9 min/80°C/speed 4.
  3. Mix speed 2.5, slowly pouring reserved brown butter onto mixing bowl lid until fully incorporated. Leave to cool completely in mixing bowl, then transfer into ice cube trays and place into freezer for a minimum 4 hours or overnight. Remove butterfly whisk. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ganache

  1. Place peanuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining ingredients and cook 5 min/50°C/speed 1.
  3. Blend 30 sec/speed 8, then transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place frozen ice cream cubes into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 7, with aid of spatula.
  2. Transfer half of the ice cream into a loaf tin (25 x 10 x 11 cm). Top with half of the ganache, and repeat layering with remaining half of the ice cream and ganache. Place into freezer until firm. Before serving, remove ice cream from freezer and leave for 5 minutes before scooping into waffle cones or serving bowls.

Useful Items

frying pan, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, fine mesh sieve, bowl, ice cube trays, loaf tin (25 x 10 x 11 cm), stove top, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • For a dinner party, you can serve this ice cream in slices by briefly placing loaf tin under hot tap water, then turning out onto a chopping board or serving platter and slicing as desired.
  • Instead of ice cube trays, you can freeze the ice cream mixture in a shallow freezable storage container.
