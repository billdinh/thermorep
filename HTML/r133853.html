Milk Chocolate and Banana Cake

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 90 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 3 large eggs, 2 separated, 1 whole
  • 200 g bananas, very ripe, cut in pieces
  • 50 g whole milk
  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 150 g self-raising flour
  • 100 g milk chocolate chips
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 4 Tbsp cocoa powder

Chocolate Icing

  • 100 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 100 g sour cream

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 140°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (450 g/1 lb) with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place 2 egg whites in very clean mixing bowl then whisk 3 min/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form, watching carefully to avoid over-whisking. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place bananas in mixing bowl and mash 6 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add 1 whole egg, 2 reserved egg yolks, oil and milk then mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  5. Add sugar, flour, chocolate chips, bicarbonate of soda and cocoa powder then mix 15 sec/speed 4.
  6. Add reserved whisked egg whites and gently fold in with spatula until completely incorporated. Transfer to prepared tin. Bake for 1 hour 15 minutes (140°C) or until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tin. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Chocolate Icing

  1. Towards the end of cooling time, place milk chocolate and sour cream in mixing bowl then melt 3 min/50°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and melt again 1 min/50°C/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and place in fridge for 30 minutes to thicken.
  2. Remove cake from tin and spoon icing over the top. Cut into slices to serve.

Useful Items

450 g loaf tin (1 lb), baking paper, skewer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

Every baking book needs a banana loaf. This one has a beautifully moist sponge that will appeal to both chocoholics and fans of banana cake.
