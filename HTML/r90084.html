Wasabi devilled eggs

Ingredients

Wasabi mayonnaise

  • 250 g grapeseed oil
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 25 g wasabi paste, to taste

Eggs

  • 500 g water
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 tsp wasabi paste (optional)
  • 3 - 4 fresh chives, finely chopped

Preparation

Wasabi mayonnaise

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh grapeseed oil into it. Set jug with oil aside.
  2. Place egg yolks, mustard, lemon juice and salt into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Mix 2 min/speed 4, slowly pouring reserved oil onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle into mixing bowl to emulsify.
  4. Add wasabi and mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  5. Transfer mayonnaise into a sealable jar or container and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Eggs

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and place eggs into it. Cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1, until hard boiled. Remove the simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer eggs into a bowl of cold water. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Once eggs have cooled, peel and cut each egg in half lengthways and gently remove the yolks. Place the 12 boiled egg white halves onto a serving platter and set aside.
  3. Place wasabi paste (optional), the 6 boiled egg yolks and 90 g of the reserved wasabi mayonnaise into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 10 sec/speed 3, or until smooth.
  4. Spoon or pipe the egg yolk-mayonnaise mixture into the reserved egg halves and sprinkle with chives. Cover eggs with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until chilled. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

large jug, sealable storage jar or container, serving platter, plastic wrap, piping bag with star nozzle, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This delicious little canapé can be made earlier in the day or the night before and stored in the refrigerator.
  • Taste the egg yolk-mayonnaise mixture before piping or spooning into the egg white halves in step 9 to ensure the right amount of wasabi flavour and adjust according to your preference. The additional teaspoon in step 8 gives the eggs a lovely little kick but can be omitted if you prefer a more subtle wasabi flavour.
