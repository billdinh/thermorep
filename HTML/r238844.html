Pizza Wheels

Ingredients

Tomato Sauce

  • 4 oz white cheddar cheese, cut into pieces
  • 3 oz yellow onion, cut into pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ oz olive oil
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 pinch salt

Dough

  • olive oil spray, to grease
  • 4 ½ oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 4 ½ oz whole wheat flour
  • 2 ¼ tsp rapid rise yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 5 oz water
  • 1 &frac25; oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 oz sliced deli ham
  • 2 oz baby spinach

Preparation

Tomato Sauce

  1. Place cheddar cheese into mixing bowl and grate 4 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions, garlic and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 5 min/250°F/speed 1.
  3. Add tomatoes, honey and salt and cook 10 min/250°F/speed 1. Blend 10 sec/speed 10. Reserve 4 oz sauce in a small bowl for dipping. Place remaining sauce in another bowl and allow to cool while making the dough. Clean mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly grease a large bowl and a muffin pan and set aside.
  2. Place all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, yeast, honey, salt, water and olive oil into mixing bowl and knead 2 min/. Place dough into prepared bowl and form into a ball. Cover bowl with cling wrap and let dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 min.).
  3. Place dough on a lightly floured surface and roll into a rectangle (12 in. x 10 in. x ½ in.) with a rolling pin.
  4. Spread tomato sauce over surface of dough leaving a ½ inch border around the edges. Layer ham, spinach and reserved grated cheese evenly over the sauce and starting with the longer side, roll tightly into a log.
  5. Cut into 12 slices, place in prepared muffin pan and bake for 15-20 minutes (400°F) or until golden brown. Serve warm or at room temperature with reserved tomato sauce in small bowl for dipping.

Useful Items

large bowl, 12 hole muffin pan, rolling pin, cling wrap, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Dough can be prepared a day ahead, wrapped in cling wrap and refrigerated. Remove from the refrigerator and allow dough to come up to room temperature while making sauce. Cool sauce completely before assembling Pizza Wheels.
