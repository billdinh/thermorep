Moroccan sweet pastry (Briouats)

Ingredients

  • 150 g blanched almonds
  • 60 g sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 15 g orange blossom water (see tip)
  • 2 sheets filo pastry
  • 50 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 40 g honey
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
2050 kJ / 490 kcal
Protein
12 g
Carbohydrates
37 g

33 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place almonds and sugar in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6.
  2. Add egg and orange blossom water, then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Line a small rectangular cake tin (5 cm x 20 cm) with baking paper. Cut sheets of filo pastry in half, so that they are the same size as the mould. Line the bottom of the tin with 2 half sheets filo pastry, brush with melted butter and cover with the almond and sugar mixture. Cover with the 2 remaining half sheets of filo pastry and brush with melted butter. Bake in oven for 20 minutes (180°C). Remove from oven, brush with the remaining honey and butter and allow to cool before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, small rectangular cake tin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Depending on the size of the mould used, you may need to use 1 or 2 additional sheets of filo pastry.
  • Orange blossom water is a by-product of the distillation of fresh bitter-orange blossoms for their essential oil. In Moroccan cuisine, it is mainly used as an ingredient for traditional sweets. If you are unable to find orange blossom water, use any flavouring to your liking (vanilla, cinnamon etc.). This however, will not result in the typical flavour of the briouats.
