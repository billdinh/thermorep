Lasagna

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ - 7 oz Parmesan cheese, cubed (1 in.), to taste

Bolognese Sauce

  • 7 oz mixed vegetables (celery stalk, carrots and onions), cut into pieces
  • 1 ½ oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 32 oz ground beef
  • 3 ½ oz dry white wine
  • 1 can puréed tomatoes (approx. 15 oz)
  • 1 - 1 ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ - 1 tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Béchamel

  • 35 oz whole milk
  • 3 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 3 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground nutmeg

Lasagna Assembly

  • unsalted butter, to grease, plus extra to dot (optional)
  • 9 - 12 oz no-boil lasagna pasta sheets, approx. 12 sheets (4 in. x 8 in.)

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.

Bolognese Sauce

  1. Place mixed vegetables and olive oil into mixing bowl. Chop 5 sec/speed 7, then sauté 5 min/250°F/speed 2.
  2. Add ground beef, stir with spatula to break it up if necessary and sauté 8 min/250°F//speed 1.
  3. Add wine and cook 5 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes, salt and pepper and cook 20 min/212°F//speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Béchamel

  1. Place milk, butter, flour, salt and nutmeg into mixing bowl and cook 12 min/212°F/speed 3.

Lasagna Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Lightly grease a casserole dish (approx. 12 in. x 9½ in. x 2 in.) and set aside.
  2. Cover base of casserole dish with a thin layer of bolognese sauce. Place a layer of lasagna sheets over the sauce, cover with another layer of bolognese sauce, sprinkle with some of the Parmesan cheese and cover with a layer of béchamel sauce. Repeat this step until all ingredients are used. Finish with a layer of béchamel sauce, sprinkle with remaining Parmesan cheese and dot with small pieces of butter.
  3. Bake for 30-40 minutes (350°F). Allow lasagna to rest for 15-20 minutes before cutting into squares and serving.

Useful Items

casserole dish (approx. 12 in. x 9½ in. x 2 in.), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a good dish for entertaining as it can be prepared ahead of time and baked when guests arrive.
  • Ground beef can be drained after sautéing if using a higher fat beef.
  • Usually lasagna pasta does not need to be boiled before baking (check package instructions). To cook the pasta in the traditional method, pre-cook lasagna sheets in a large saucepan of salted boiling water (approx. 1 tsp salt per 35 oz of water) for approximately 5 minutes. Drain in Varoma dish and blot excess moisture by placing lasagna sheets in a single layer on a kitchen towel. Prepare pasta while béchamel sauce is cooking.
  • Leftovers keep well in the freezer.
  • To use freshly ground meat, freeze pieces (1 in.) of sinew-free meat in a single layer for 30 minutes. Place partially-frozen meat into mixing bowl and grind 10-15 sec/speed 6.
  • Instead of dried lasagna sheets use homemade fresh egg pasta.
  • Replace ground beef with mixed ground meat (half pork and half beef).
  • Béchamel sauce can be substituted with 15 oz ricotta cheese, 8 oz mozzarella cheese, 14 oz of canned puréed tomatoes and 1 tbsp Italian seasoning. Add puréed tomatoes and Italian seasonings to the bolognese sauce. Omit béchamel steps and go straight to lasagna assembly, use ricotta and mozzarella in place of béchamel and continue with recipe.
