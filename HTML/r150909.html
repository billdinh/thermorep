Couscous with Chicken and Lamb

Ingredients

  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 4 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 200 g onions, quartered
  • 400 g lamb shoulder, preferably bone-in (see tip), cut in pieces (4 cm x 4 cm)
  • 3 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 400 g carrots, cut in pieces (approx. 1.5 cm)
  • 250 g turnips, cut in 6 or 8 pieces (depending on size)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 900 g water
  • 300 g chicken breasts, skinless, cut in fine strips (approx. 2 cm long)
  • 6 spicy sausages (e.g. merguez) (optional)
  • 400 g courgettes, sliced (approx. 1.5 cm)
  • 350 g couscous, medium grain
  • 70 g raisins
  • 350 g tinned chick peas, drained
  • 1 - 2 tsp harissa paste

Preparation

  1. Place coriander and parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add lamb, 2 tsp salt, pepper and 20 g olive oil and sauté 10 min/120°C//speed .
  4. Add carrots, turnips, cinnamon, ginger, 550 g water and reserved chopped herbs. Set Varoma dish in position and add chicken to it, making sure that some holes remain unobstructed. Arrange spicy sausages (if using) and courgettes on top of chicken. Close Varoma and steam 15 min/Varoma//speed . Meanwhile, place couscous, remaining 1 tsp salt and 20 g olive oil in a bowl. Cover with 350 g water and stir using spatula to moisten couscous evenly. Cover the bowl and set aside.
  5. Gently fluff couscous with a fork to separate the grains and transfer it to Varoma tray. Carefully remove Varoma lid, insert prepared Varoma tray, close Varoma and steam a further 10 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma.
  6. Add raisins and chickpeas to mixing bowl, set Varoma back in position and heat 5 min/Varoma//speed . Remove Varoma and transfer contents of mixing bowl to a large serving dish. Add chicken, sausages (if using) and courgettes and combine gently using spatula. Spoon out 100 g broth from the serving dish into a gravy boat, add harissa and mix with a fork. Transfer couscous to another serving dish. Serve couscous, vegetables, and meat hot, with harissa sauce on the side.

Hints & Tricks

  • This dish easily serves 6. If you serve a starter first, it can serve up to 8 people.
  • Couscous is a traditional dish from northwest Africa. Merguez are fresh, spicy sausages made from mutton and/or beef, and are typical of the region. You can replace them with any other spicy sausage, or omit them altogether. For a more traditional flavour, replace the cinnamon and ginger with 2 Tbsp ras-el-hanout, a North African spice blend that can contain up to 40 ingredients.
  • The bone in the lamb shoulder meat adds flavour. However if you are cutting the meat yourself, the dish will also be excellent with boneless meat.
    - If you have some couscous left over, use it to prepare a traditional Moroccan dessert. Place couscous onto a plate, sprinkle with 50 g sugar and 1 tsp ground cinnamon. Serve warm or cold.
