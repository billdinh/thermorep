Ingredients

Sponge cake

  • butter, for greasing
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ tsp cream of tartar
  • 20 g plain flour
  • 40 g cornflour
  • 2 eggs, room temperature and separated
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp water, hot

Orange custard

  • 380 g orange juice
  • 50 g cornflour
  • 80 g white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 600 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp gelatine powder
  • 60 g butter, softened
  • 40 g orange liqueur
  • 250 g plain sweet biscuits

Orange icing

  • 100 g icing sugar
  • 30 g orange juice

Preparation

Sponge cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a baking dish (30 x 20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place caster sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place bicarbonate of soda, cream of tartar, plain flour and cornflour into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a separate bowl to the sugar and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 3 min/speed 3.5, without measuring cup, until stiff.
  5. With butterfly whisk still in place, mix 3 min/speed 3, adding reserved milled sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid 1 spoon at a time.
  6. Add egg yolks and mix 15 sec/speed . Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Add vanilla extract, water and reserved flour mixture and combine 5 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat combining 5 sec/speed 3.
  8. Transfer mixture into prepared baking dish and bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C). Set sponge aside in baking dish to cool.

Orange custard

  1. Place orange juice, cornflour, sugar and egg into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer orange custard into a bowl and set aside to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  2. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place 250 g of the cream and 1 tsp of the gelatine into mixing bowl and whip 5-40 sec/speed 4, until soft peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer whipped cream into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  3. Place reserved cooled orange custard into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer back into bowl and set aside.
  4. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place butter into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  5. With butterfly whisk still in place, mix 3 min/speed 3, slowly adding reserved orange custard through hole in mixing bowl lid, 1 spoon at a time, until well combined.
  6. Add reserved whipped cream and mix 20 sec/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix for a further 10 sec/speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Drizzle 10 g of the orange liqueur over sponge in baking dish, then spread orange custard over the top. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  8. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place remaining 350 g of the cream and remaining 1 tsp gelatine into mixing bowl and whip 5-40 sec/speed 3.5, until soft peaks form.
  9. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer whipped cream over the top of the orange custard, then arrange biscuits in rows on top of the cream. Drizzle remaining orange liqueur over biscuits. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

Orange icing

  1. Re-insert butterfly whisk. Place icing sugar and orange juice into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Spread orange icing over biscuits, then place into refrigerator to set (approx 1 hour). Once set, cut into pieces and serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking dish (30 x 20 cm), oven, refrigerator
