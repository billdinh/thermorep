Chocolate love cake with Turkish delight jelly

Ingredients

Jelly

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 4 gold grade gelatine leaves
  • 220 g water, plus extra chilled water for soaking gelatine
  • 300 g caster sugar
  • ½ lemon, juice only
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 tsp rosewater
  • 4 drops natural red food colouring (optional)

Cake

  • 130 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (2 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 1 whole nutmeg
  • 80 g pistachio nuts
  • 100 g dark chocolate
  • 400 g raw almonds
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 180 g brown sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 250 g natural yoghurt, plus extra to serve
  • 20 g icing sugar
  • 20 g cornflour

Preparation

Jelly

  1. Grease and line a sealable container (15 x 15 cm) with plastic wrap and set aside.
  2. Place gelatine into a bowl, cover with chilled water and set aside to soften.
  3. Place sugar, 220 g water, lemon juice and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Remove gelatine from water, squeeze to remove excess water. Add gelatine to mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add rosewater and food colouring (optional) and mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  6. Transfer into prepared container and place into refrigerator for 3 hours or until set.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease and line a square cake tin (22 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place nutmeg into mixing bowl and mill 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place pistachios into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/2 times. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer into another separate bowl and set aside.
  5. Place almonds and rosemary into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  6. Add brown sugar, butter and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 6.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  8. Transfer half of the batter into prepared cake tin, smoothing out evenly. Sprinkle reserved grated chocolate over batter.
  9. Add eggs, yoghurt, half of the reserved nutmeg and half of the reserved pistachios into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Pour mixture on top of chocolate layer and smooth surface.  Reserve 1 tablespoon of pistachios, then sprinkle remaining pistachios over batter and bake for 45 minutes (170°C). Place cake tin onto a wire rack and allow to cool completely before removing cake from tin.
  10. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh icing sugar and cornflour into it and stir to combine. Cut jelly into diamond shapes. Using a fine-mesh sieve, dust jelly with icing sugar mixture. Serve cake with jelly, extra yoghurt and sprinkle with a little of the reserved nutmeg and pistachios.
