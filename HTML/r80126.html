Sour cream pastry

Ingredients

  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 200 g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into pieces
  • 120 g sour cream

Preparation

  1. Place flour and butter into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add sour cream and combine 10 sec/speed 8, then knead 30 sec/, or until pastry comes together and forms a ball.
  3. Transfer dough onto a floured silicone bread mat or work surface and work into a ball. Wrap pastry in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum 20 minutes to chill.
  4. Once chilled, roll out pastry between a silicone bread mat and a sheet of baking paper, or between two sheets of baking paper, to desired thickness.
  5. Blind bake (see Tips) or return pastry to refrigerator to chill until use. Use as needed.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking paper, rolling pin, silicone bread mat, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To blind bake pastry: Preheat oven to 200°C. Roll out pastry to line the base and sides of a greased tart tin and prick the base with a fork or pastry docker to prevent air bubbles and rising. Line pastry base with baking paper and fill with rice, dried beans or pie weights (this will stop the pastry base from rising during baking). Place tart tin onto a baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (200°C). Remove from oven, then remove baking paper with rice, dried beans or pie weights. Return pastry case to oven and bake for a further 5-10 minutes (200°C) or until light golden.
  • This is a versatile pastry, any leftover can be used to make sausage rolls or kept frozen for later use. Roll out your pastry between two sheets of baking paper or plastic wrap before freezing to make defrosting easier later on.
  • This recipe fills a 24 cm tart tin.
