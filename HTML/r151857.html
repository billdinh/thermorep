Basil Pesto

Ingredients

  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • 30 g pine nuts
  • 80 g fresh basil leaves
  • 150 g extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add garlic (if using), pine nuts, basil, oil and salt then chop 20 sec/speed 7. Serve as a sauce or transfer to a glass jar with airtight lid (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm. Make sure that the rim of the jar is clean and store in fridge (see tip).

Useful Items

glass jar with airtight lid, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Fresh, full of flavour, and ready in under 10 minutes, you’ll never need to buy shop-bought again. Serve with pasta, use to fill chicken breasts or add flavour to fish fillets, then store the remainder in the fridge for up to 1 week.
  • To prepare jar, place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place jar and lid upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jar and lid in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heating in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • To keep pesto fresh in the fridge for up to 7 days, place in a clean jar, cover with a layer of olive oil and seal the jar.
  • Serve with spaghetti or other types of pasta. If you prefer a thinner sauce, add 1-2 Tbsp cooking water from the pasta and mix before serving.
  • For a greater depth of flavour, substitute 30 g Parmesan cheese with 30 g pecorino cheese.
