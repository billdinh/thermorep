Banana tart tartin with chocolate sorbet

Ingredients

Chocolate sorbet

  • 60 g raw sugar
  • 30 - 50 g cocoa powder (70% cocoa), to taste
  • 580 g cold water
  • 30 g glucose syrup

Iced puff pastry

  • 40 g ice cubes
  • 90 g unsalted butter, frozen and cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • 100 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ¼ tsp sea salt

Filling

  • 60 g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g white sugar
  • 30 g crystalised ginger
  • 20 g water
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 ripe bananas, cut into halves lengthways

Preparation

Chocolate sorbet

  1. Place raw sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cocoa powder and 550g of water and cook 40 min/100°C/speed 3, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. The mixture should reduce by one quarter.
  3. Add glucose syrup, then  re-insert measuring cup and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Allow mixture to cool (approx. 15 minutes), then transfer into ice cube trays and place into freezer overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place 250g of the reserved chocolate sorbet cubes into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 10.
  5. Add remaining sorbet cubes and remaining 30 g of water and blend 15 sec/speed 8 with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Blend 15 sec/speed 8, until smooth and creamy. Transfer into a large bowl and place into freezer until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Iced puff pastry

  1. Place ice cubes and butter into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining pastry ingredients and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 2 min/. Work into a ball, then wrap pastry in a silicone bread mat or plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a round cake tin (22 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 2 sec/speed 9. Transfer sugar into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place crystallised ginger into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Chop 10 sec/speed 5.  Transfer ginger into a separate bowl and set aside.
  5. Place water and reserved sugar into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Add butter, cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla and reserved ginger, and cook 9 min/Varoma/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Pour filling mixture into prepared cake tin and arrange banana halves, cut side down, on top of filling.
  7. Roll out pastry between two floured sheets of baking paper into a circle (1 cm thick), roughly the same size as the cake tin (22 cm). Place pastry over bananas and gently push down around edges. Bake for 65-75 minutes (160°C) or until deep golden brown and sticky.
  8. Place a large serving plate over pastry, then quickly and carefully invert plate and tin together to turn out tart tartin onto plate. Scrape any remaining filling over the top, then serve hot with a scoop of chocolate sorbet.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, large bowl, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, round cake tin (22 cm), bowl, rolling pin, baking paper, large serving plate, freezer, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is best not to use a springform cake tin as the caramel may drip during baking.
