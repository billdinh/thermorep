Butternut Squash Soup, Fish with Rice and Vegetables, Apple and Pear Crumble

Ingredients

  • 100 g white long-grain rice
  • water, plus extra to soak rice
  • 2 white fish fillets, skinless, 150-180 g each (e.g. hake, sole, perch)
  • salt, plus extra to taste
  • ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 6 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 120 g apple (1 apple), cored, cut in pieces (1.5 cm)
  • 120 g pear (1 pear), cored, cut in pieces (1.5 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 130 g carrots, cut in sticks (1 cm x 5 cm)
  • 4 green asparagus, woody ends removed, halved lengthwise if thicker than 1 cm
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 20 g brown sugar
  • 20 g butter
  • 150 g onions, cut in pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 250 - 300 g butternut squash, peeled, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 80 g ripe tomatoes, cut in pieces
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 400 g water
  • fresh chives, chopped, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh long-grain rice into it. Add water to cover rice and set aside to soak.
  2. Sprinkle fish fillets with 1-2 pinches salt and pepper; if fish fillets are thin, roll them up and secure with a cocktail stick to avoid overcooking. Place fish fillets on a piece of baking paper (30 cm x 40 cm), add cherry tomatoes and sprinkle again with 1-2 pinches salt. On another piece of baking paper (30 cm x 40 cm), place apple and pear pieces and drizzle with honey and lemon juice. Close both parcels, folding up baking paper and twisting ends, then place them in Varoma dish. Insert Varoma tray and arrange carrots on it with green asparagus on top. Set Varoma aside.
  3. Place flour, brown sugar and butter in mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer crumble mixture to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place onions, garlic, butternut squash and olive oil in mixing bowl, chop 2 sec/speed 5, then sauté 8 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add tomatoes. Drain rice through simmering basket and insert simmering basket in mixing bowl. Add 1 tsp salt, 1 pinch ground black pepper and water. Set Varoma in position and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep warm and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Blend soup 1 min/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually.
  7. Divide steamed apple and pear pieces and their cooking juices between 2 ovenproof ceramic dishes (approx. Ø 12 cm), sprinkle with reserved crumble mixture and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until golden.
  8. Serve soup sprinkled with chopped chives as a starter (see tip), then fish with rice and vegetables as a main course (remove cocktail sticks from fish rolls) and finally, fruit crumble for dessert.

Useful Items

baking paper, ovenproof ceramic dishes (approx. Ø 12 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To keep the main course warm while soup is served, insert simmering basket with rice back into empty but still warm mixing bowl and set covered Varoma containing fish parcel and vegetables in position on mixing bowl lid.
  • A nutritious and complete, three-course menu for two, you can serve this easy meal on weekdays, weekends or even for a special occasion. The colourful soup and vegetables and the mouthwatering crumble are excellent companions for the deliciously healthy fish parcel.
  • Instead of asparagus, use flat runner beans, cut in pieces (2 cm).
    - Use chicken stock or vegetable stock instead of water in step 5 (in this case salt may be reduced in the same step to ¼ tsp).
