Tear and Share Snail Bread

Ingredients

  • 460 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 800 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only, to taste
  • 1 lemon, 1 strip of thin peel (4 cm long)
  • 2 pinches ground cayenne pepper
  • 175 g butter, soft, plus extra for greasing

Preparation

  1. Place water and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 3 min/37°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with butter.
  2. Add flour and 1 tsp salt then knead 3 min/. Transfer to prepared bowl then cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Towards the end of this time, place garlic, parsley, dill, rosemary and lemon peel in mixing bowl then grate 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then grate again 5 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add cayenne pepper, remaining ½ tsp salt and butter then mix 12 sec/speed 6.
  5. Grease 2 large baking trays. Tip dough out onto a lightly floured surface and cut in half. Roll out one piece of dough to a rectangle approx. 5 mm thick. Using a soft spatula, spread out half the prepared herb butter to edges of rolled out dough. Starting from a long edge, tightly roll up dough into a log.
  6. Slice long roll into 2 cm thick pieces then join them together on one of the prepared baking trays in the shape of a snail. Set aside and repeat with remaining dough and herb butter, creating another snail on second prepared tray.
  7. Cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 190°C.
  8. Bake for 12-15 minutes (190°C) until golden brown. Serve warm at the table.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, soft spatula, long sharp knife, large bowl, large baking trays, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use the end pieces of the cut rolls to create the eye stalks and tail as these pieces tend to be slightly smaller.
