Brutti ma buoni

Ingredients

  • 300 g blanched almonds
  • 360 g white sugar
  • 120 g egg whites
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
468 kJ / 111 kcal
Protein
2.5 g
Carbohydrates
12.5 g
Fat
5.6 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  2. Place almonds onto a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) and roast for 10-12 minutes (190°C) until golden brown (see Tips). Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Reduce oven to 120°C.
  3. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 40 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Add egg whites and mix 10 min/37°C/speed 2 ,  without measuring cup. Remove mixing bowl and allow egg white mixture to cool in mixing bowl for 5 minutes, then mix 5 min/37°C/speed 2 , without measuring cup . Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer egg white mixture into a large bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  5. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  6. Place reserved almonds into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 4, until roughly chopped. Transfer into bowl with egg white mixture and gently fold using spatula until well combined.
  7. Place large tablespoonfuls of mixture (approx. 30) onto prepared baking trays. Bake for 10 minutes (120°C), then reduce oven temperature to 100°C and bake for a further 50 minutes (100°C). Turn off oven and allow biscuits to cool completely in oven (approx. 3 hours). Serve or transfer into a sealable container until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), bowl, large bowl, sealable container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is best to toast the nuts to a deep golden colour to maximise the flavour in the biscuits.
  • You can replace 150 g blanched almonds with 150 g hazelnuts (skin removed) in step 2.
  • Biscuits will keep for up to 1 week in a sealable container.
  • These are a traditional Italian biscuit, translated it means "ugly but good". The recipe varies from region to region, with some regions preferring a mixture of nuts or a dusting of cocoa powder before serving.
