Bengali fish cakes

Ingredients

  • 1 slice bread, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 500 g water
  • 350 g potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 350 g fresh boneless, skinless salmon fillets (approx. 2 fillets)
  • 1 tsp olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 2 - 3 fresh long green chillies, trimmed, cut into halves and deseeded
  • 80 g brown onion
  • 12 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves and stalks, cut into thirds
  • 1 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • lemons, cut into wedges for serving

Preparation

  1. Place bread into mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh potatoes into it. Place Varoma into position and weigh salmon into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place oil, chillies, onion, fresh coriander leaves and stalks, garlic paste, ginger paste, ground turmeric, ground coriander, garam masala, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7, then cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Add reserved cooked potatoes and whip 15 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved breadcrumbs and cooked salmon and carefully break up mixture with aid of spatula, then mix 5 sec/speed 2 or until combined. Transfer into a large bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes.
  6. Using 2 tablespoons of mixture at a time, shape into balls (approx. 12 balls in total, 6 cm diameter), then gently flatten each ball with the palm of your hand.
  7. Place a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Working in batches, gently fry fish cakes for 3-4 minutes on each side, until crispy. Serve with lemon and chutney of choice.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, plastic wrap, large non-stick frying pan, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • We suggest using white or wheat sandwich bread in this recipe. To make this recipe gluten free, simply use gluten free bread.
  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • We served these with Cucumber and mint raita (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip), but you can pair it with any chutney you choose.
