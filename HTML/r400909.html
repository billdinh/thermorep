Apple Butter

Ingredients

  • 700 g Granny Smith apples
  • 310 g sugar
  • 225 g cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • 1 Tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Wash apples and cut in quarters, leaving peel on and cores (see tip). Remove any stems.
  2. Place 350 g prepared apples in mixing bowl and, with aid of spatula, chop 5 sec/speed 4.5.
  3. Add remaining apples and, with aid of spatula, chop 10 sec/speed 4.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add sugar, vinegar, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves and lemon juice then cook 25 min/100°C/speed 2. Purée 1 min/speed 5-8, increasing speed gradually. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Cook again 30 min/Varoma/speed 2. Check consistency. Apple butter should be thick and smooth (see tip). If necessary, cook again 30 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Transfer to warm sterilised jars and seal immediately (see tip). Turn jars upside down for 1 hour before turning right side up and leaving to cool. Serve or store in a cool dry place.

Hints & Tricks

  • The apple peel, seeds and cores provide flavour and pectin, which helps thicken the sauce.
  • Cooking time will vary depending on moisture content of your apples.
  • Apple purée will thicken and is ready when a sample is put on a cold plate and is the consistency of room temperature butter.
  • To ensure preservation, clean jars thoroughly in hot soapy water, then dry in a low oven or run them through the dishwasher. Pour apple butter into jars while jars are still warm and proceed as directed by the recipe.
  • Once opened, apple butter will keep in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Alternatively, store in freezer-proof containers or ice cube trays and freeze.
  • Spread on toast or scones or use as a filling for cakes and tarts. Also may be mixed with an equal amount of softly whipped cream and served with cookies for dessert.
