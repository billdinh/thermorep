Greek Yoghurt with Honey and Walnuts

Ingredients

Greek Yoghurt

  • 900 g whole milk
  • 200 g plain yoghurt (full fat with live cultures)
  • 100 g double cream

Walnuts and Honey

  • 75 g halved walnuts
  • 250 g honey
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon

Preparation

Greek Yoghurt

  1. Place milk in mixing bowl and heat 20 min/80°C/speed 3.
  2. Remove mixing bowl, remove lid and allow milk to cool to about 37°C (approx. 40 minutes).
  3. Place mixing bowl back into position. Add yoghurt and cream then heat 15 min/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Transfer warm mixture to a large glass bowl, cover with a lid or with cling film then wrap in tea towels and place in a warm place for 12 hours.
  5. Place a sieve lined with a muslin cloth or nut milk bag over a bowl and pour mixture through. Set aside in fridge to drain for approx. 5 hours.
  6. Place strained yoghurt in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a serving bowl and set aside in fridge. Clean mixing bowl.

Walnuts and Honey

  1. Place walnuts in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add honey and cinnamon then heat 3 min/37°C/speed 2. Drizzle warm walnut-honey mixture over yoghurt and serve.

Useful Items

tea towels, large bowl, cling film, fine sieve, muslin or nut milk bag, serving bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Milk and cream must be fresh and pasteurised.
  • The ideal temperature to ferment yoghurt is about 43°C. If you have an airing cupboard, this is the perfect place to leave the yoghurt to set.
  • The Greek yoghurt can be stored in the fridge for 7-10 days (at a temperature below 4°C).
  • Use the last 200 g of this homemade yoghurt as a new starter to make your next batch of homemade yoghurt.
