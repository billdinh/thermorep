Lemon Chicken with Couscous and Tzatziki

Ingredients

  • 60 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ lemon, finely grated zest only
  • 3 chicken breasts, skinless, butterflied (sliced through horizontally almost to the edge then opened out)
  • 200 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 courgette, cut in pieces, (approx. 150 g)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 625 g water
  • 250 g couscous
  • 50 g frozen peas
  • 50 g pitted black olives
  • 50 g red peppers, diced
  • 25 g spring onions, sliced (1 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place olive oil, oregano and lemon zest in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 5. Place butterflied chicken breasts between two large pieces of cling film and flatten with a rolling pin. Place chicken in a dish and pour over marinade. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in yoghurt. Remove bowl and set aside.
  3. Place courgette in mixing bowl and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/1-2 times. Tip out into a muslin cloth and squeeze out liquid. Stir squeezed courgette into yoghurt and adjust seasoning to taste. Set tzatziki aside in fridge.
  4. Place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Preheat grill to 230°C.
  5. Line Varoma dish with enough baking paper to come 2-3 cm up the sides, then place into position. Weigh in couscous, peas, olives, red peppers and spring onions. Sprinkle 75 g water over couscous and vegetables then stir with a fork to moisten. Cover Varoma  then steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Sprinkle another 50 g water over couscous and vegetables and stir well with a fork. Cover Varoma and steam again 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, grill marinated chicken for 5 minutes (230°C) on each side until browned.
  6. Slice grilled chicken into strips and serve with couscous and tzatziki on the side.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, muslin or nut milk bag, baking paper, refrigerator, grill

Hints & Tricks

  • The method in the recipe produces a lighter, steamed couscous. If you prefer, you can soak the couscous in 250 g water for 5 minutes then steam as directed in the recipe.
  • To butterfly a chicken breast, use a sharp knife to slice into one side of the breast, starting at the thicker end and ending at the thin point. Open out the breast so that it resembles a butterfly.
