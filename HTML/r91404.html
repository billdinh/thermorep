Apple Pie

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 10 ½ oz all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar, (optional)
  • 8 oz unsalted butter, chilled, cut into pieces (½ in.)
  • 4 oz cold water,

Apple Filling

  • 2 oz sugar, plus 1-2 tbsp to sprinkle
  • 1 1 - 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 6 - 8 tbsp all-purpose flour,
  • 6 - 8 dessert apples, large (e.g Golden Delicious, Braeburn) peeled, cored and quartered
  • 17 ½ 1 - 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 17 ½ oz water
  • unsalted butter, to grease

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, salt, sugar, butter and water into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Place two sheets of cling wrap side by side on your work surface. Empty the mixing bowl onto one of them, scraping down sides with spatula. Pastry will be crumbly and uneven. Quickly pat together without overworking dough, then cut in half with spatula. Transfer one half of the pastry to the other piece of cling wrap and shape both halves into flattened balls. Wrap up tightly and refrigerate for 1 hour.

Apple Filling

  1. In a large bowl, stir together 2 oz sugar, cinnamon and flour and set aside.
  2. Cut apples in thick slices, place in Varoma dish and sprinkle with lemon juice. Place water into mixing bowl, place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 4. Transfer apples to large bowl containing reserved cinnamon sugar mixture and gently toss with spatula to coat. Set aside to cool.
  3. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease a pie pan (approx. 9 in.) and set aside.
  4. Roll out half the pastry between 2 sheets of parchment into a circle (approx. 13 in.), working quickly and handling dough as little as possible to keep it cool and easy to work with. Peel off one sheet of parchment and line prepared pie pan with pastry, pastry side down. Carefully peel off top sheet of parchment. If paper sticks, place pie pan with pastry and parchment in refrigerator for a few minutes to chill pastry and allow easy removal of parchment.
  5. Add reserved cooled apples to pie pan lined with pastry. Roll out the other half of pastry between 2 sheets of parchment into a circle (approx. 13 in.). Peel off one sheet of parchment and lay pastry over top of apples. Carefully remove second sheet of parchment as described previously.
  6. Seal pie crust by pressing down on pastry, then trim excess pastry to ¼ inch from pie pan. Crimp pastry edges and sprinkle top of pie with 1-2 tbsp sugar. Cut a few slits in the top crust with a sharp knife. Bake for 15 minutes (425˚F) then reduce temperature to 350˚F and bake a further 30 minutes until pie is golden brown. Allow to cool on a wire rack for one hour before serving warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

cling wrap, pie pan (approx. 9 in.), parchment paper, rolling pin, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For flaky pastry, make sure butter is very cold and keep dough cool at all times.
  • If the dough becomes warm and difficult to work with, place rolled out dough, still covered with sheets of parchment or cling wrap, in refrigerator until dough is firm enough to handle.
  • If dough balls are left in refrigerator for longer than an hour, they may become hard to roll out. Leave at room temperature for a few minutes, and tap them with a rolling pin to make them more pliable.
  • Instead of rolling pastry between two sheets of parchment, roll it out on a lightly floured surface, and carry the pastry to the pie pan using the rolling pin.
