Curried lentils and steamed pumpkin

Ingredients

  • 3 heaped tsp cumin seeds (10 g)
  • 4 heaped tsp coriander seeds (10 g)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 30 g fresh coriander, plus extra to garnish
  • 50 g fresh coconut flesh, cut into pieces (2-3 cm) (optional)
  • 80 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 300 g brown onions, cut into pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ - ¾ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1000 - 1200 g butternut pumpkin, deseeded, cut into 8 pieces with skin on
  • 300 g dried lentils (green or brown)
  • 1000 g water
  • 250 - 300 g preserved roasted red capsicums, cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 130 g crème fraîche (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place cumin seeds, coriander seeds and salt into mixing bowl and toast 6 min/120°C/speed , then grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Place fresh coriander and coconut (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a second small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter, onions, garlic, cayenne pepper and reserved ground spice mix into mixing bowl, chop 7 sec/speed 5, then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed . Meanwhile, arrange pumpkin pieces skin side down into Varoma dish and set aside.
  4. Add lentils and water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 35-40 min/Varoma//speed 1, or until pumpkin is very soft and lentils are soft but not mushy (see Tips). Place covered Varoma aside onto a plate.
  5. Add reserved chopped coriander and coconut, capsicum and 50 g of the crème fraîche into mixing bowl, stir with aid of spatula, then adjust seasoning to taste. Serve curried lentils with steamed pumpkin pieces, garnishing with remaining crème fraîche and a few fresh coriander leaves. Scoop pumpkin off skin as you eat and discard skin.

Hints & Tricks

  • Lentils vary as to how much water they absorb and how long they take to cook. If lentils are not yet tender after cooking, set Varoma aside, add 100 g water and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1, then, if needed, add another 100 g water and repeat.
    - When cooking for children, use only ¼ tsp cayenne pepper, and serve with Greek yoghurt for a cooling effect.
    - To open a fresh coconut easily, heat it 5-7 minutes in a preheated oven (220-240°C) then remove from oven and carefully hit it with a hammer - the shell will crack easily because it will have expanded slightly from the coconut flesh. Cut coconut flesh into 3 cm pieces - no need to peel off the thin brown skin for this recipe. Extra coconut pieces can be frozen for up to 3 months.
  • If you prefer more of a dahl texture, blend the curried lentils 20 sec/speed 6 after cooking.
    - Use diced fresh red capsicums instead of roasted preserved. This adds a lovely fresh crunch to the curried lentils.
    - Replace butter with coconut oil and crème fraîche with coconut cream.
