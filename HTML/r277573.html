Fragrant curried mince with tomato salad

Ingredients

Curried mince

  • 100 g natural yoghurt
  • 20 g oil
  • 300 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1000 g beef mince
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 - 2 tsp salt, to taste
  • 100 g water

Tomato salad

  • 2 tomatoes, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 1 continental cucumber, deseeded and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp dried mint
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • fresh green chillies, trimmed and cut into thin slices (optional)
  • roti, warmed, to serve (see Tip)

Preparation

Curried mince

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt into it. Place into refrigerator until ready to use.
  2. Place oil, onion, garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 4 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add mince, tomato paste, chilli powder, garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric and salt and cook 5 min/120°C//speed 1.
  5. Scrape base and blades of mixing bowl with spatula to remove any lumps. Add water and cook 20 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.

Tomato salad

  1. Place tomatoes, cucumber, dried mint, salt and pepper into bowl with reserved yoghurt and stir to combine.
  2. Garnish curried mince with chillies (optional) and serve with tomato salad and roti on the side.

Useful Items

large bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own roti, please refer to the recipes on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au). Otherwise, you can replace the roti with cooked rice.
  • To save time, prepare your tomato salad while the mince is cooking.
  • To mince your own meat, please refer to the guide in the front of The Basic Cookbook. Please note that it may increase the total time of the recipe.
