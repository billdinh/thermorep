Eton Mess Parfait with a Fresh Berry Sauce

Ingredients

Sabayon

  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 100 g golden caster sugar
  • 50 g water, cold

Parfait

  • 160 g fresh raspberries
  • 250 g double cream
  • 60 g meringues, broken in pieces (2-3 cm)

Fresh Berry Sauce

  • 30 g sugar
  • 150 g fresh strawberries, hulled and halved, plus extra for garnish
  • 150 g fresh raspberries, plus extra for garnish
  • 15 g lemon juice

Preparation

Sabayon

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg yolks, caster sugar and water in mixing bowl then cook 15 min/70°C/speed 4.
  2. Without measuring cup, whisk 15 min/speed 3 to cool the sabayon. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside to cool further. Meanwhile, wash and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

Parfait

  1. Line a large loaf tin (2 lb) with cling film then set aside.
  2. Place raspberries in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Wash and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whisk speed 3, without setting a time, until softly whipped, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Add meringue pieces and reserved chopped raspberries to sabayon then fold in gently with spatula. Fold in whipped cream then transfer to prepared loaf tin. Cover with cling film then freeze for at least 8 hours or overnight. Meanwhile, wash and dry mixing bowl and make sauce.

Fresh Berry Sauce

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add strawberries, raspberries and lemon juice then blend 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Strain through a fine sieve then transfer to a serving jug and set aside to cool.
  3. Remove loaf tin from freezer and dip base in hot water for a few seconds. Turn out onto a plate and ease the parfait out using the cling film. Cut in slices using a hot sharp knife and serve with berry sauce and extra berries for garnish.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, 900 g loaf tin (2 lb), fine sieve, serving jug, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Make your own meringues instead of using ready made ones.
  • Add more sugar in the fresh berry sauce if fruits are not very ripe.
  • Another traditional English dessert which we’ve converted to a summer frozen option. Eton Mess, a mixture of strawberries, meringue and cream, dates back to at least the nineteenth century and is a traditional feature of Eton College's annual cricket game against Winchester College. Makes an impressive summer party dessert.
