Ricotta Cheesecake with Fig Jam

Ingredients

Almond Base

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 20 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 200 g roasted almonds (see tip)
  • 60 g brown sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Fig Jam

  • 100 g dried figs, stems removed
  • 100 g water
  • 80 g sugar
  • ½ lemon, juice only

Ricotta Cheesecake

  • 170 g sugar
  • ½ lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 170 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 250 g ricotta cheese
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 80 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 Tbsp ground cinnamon, for dusting

Preparation

Almond Base

  1. Line base of a springform tin (Ø 24 cm) with baking paper, then grease base and sides generously with butter.
  2. Place butter, roasted almonds, brown sugar and salt in mixing bowl then crush 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to prepared tin then use a spoon or your hands to press mixture down firmly to make a compact layer. Refrigerate for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make jam.

Fig Jam

  1. Place dried figs in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Add water, sugar and lemon juice then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool for 20 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl thoroughly.

Ricotta Cheesecake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C.
  2. Place sugar and thin peelings of lemon skin in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and 30 g of reserved sugar in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whisk 3 min/speed 3.5. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place butter and remaining reserved lemon sugar in mixing bowl then beat 2 min/speed 4.
  5. Add egg yolks, ricotta cheese and salt then beat 4 min/speed 4.
  6. Add self-raising flour and baking powder then mix 8 sec/speed 3. Transfer to reserved whisked egg whites and gently fold in with spatula.
  7. Pour half of cheesecake filling over almond base, dot spoonfuls of fig jam over filling then finish with remaining cheesecake filling. Place on a baking tray and bake on lowest oven shelf for 45 minutes (160°C). Turn off oven and allow cheesecake to cool inside for one hour, then cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours.
  8. Remove from tin and transfer to a serving plate, then dust with ground cinnamon just before serving.

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 24 cm), baking paper, baking tray, large bowl, cling film, serving plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For roasted almonds: preheat oven to 200°C, place almonds in a baking tray and bake for 5-10 minutes (200°C) until lightly golden.
