Piccalilli

Ingredients

  • 450 g mixed vegetables (see tip), cut in bite-sized pieces
  • 2 Tbsp fine sea salt
  • 30 g sunflower oil
  • 1 Tbsp yellow mustard seeds
  • 1 Tbsp ground coriander
  • 1 Tbsp ground turmeric
  • 1 Tbsp English mustard powder
  • 25 g cornflour
  • 350 g cider vinegar
  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in mixed vegetable pieces then sprinkle over 2 Tbsp salt. Mix well, cover with Varoma lid then place on a plate and leave in a cool place overnight. The following day, rinse vegetables in very cold water, drain thoroughly then dry with paper towels.
  2. Avoiding blades, place oil, mustard seeds, coriander and turmeric in mixing bowl then fry 3 min/100°C/speed . Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Place mustard powder in a small bowl. Place bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in cornflour and 20 g vinegar.  Remove from lid and mix to a smooth paste (adding a little more vinegar if necessary). Add paste to mixing bowl and stir 15 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add remaining 330 g vinegar, sugar and salt then boil 10 min/100°C/speed 2.
  5. Add drained vegetables and stir with spatula until coated in the sauce. Cook 5 min/80°C//speed  to soften vegetables slightly.
  6. Pour into warm, sterilised jars and seal immediately. Leave for a minimum of four weeks before using to allow flavours to develop.

Useful Items

paper towel, bowl, sterilised jam jars

Hints & Tricks

  • Add chillies and garlic to the vegetable mixture for a spicier version.
  • Suggested vegetables: cauliflower, green beans, shallots or pearl onions, cucumber (deseeded), courgettes (deseeded), peppers, carrots.
  • Use unopened jars within 1 year.
  • An English interpretation of Indian chopped, pickled vegetables with spices. This homemade version is so much better than that you buy ready-made in a jar, and the best thing about it is you can use whatever vegetables you have to hand. Lovely served with a ploughman’s, cheese or cold meats, or a meat pie (try it with our game pie).
