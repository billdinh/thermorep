Lamb with Artichoke and Spinach Purée

Ingredients

Lamb

  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 rack of lamb, Frenched, fat trimmed
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 oz fresh mixed herbs (parsley, mint, basil, thyme)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard

Artichoke and Spinach Purée

  • ½ oz Parmesan cheese
  • 1 lemon, thin peel only, no pith
  • 2 jars artichoke hearts, packed in water (12 oz ea.), drained
  • ½ oz water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice, to taste
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 3 oz fresh baby spinach

Preparation

Lamb

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with foil and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large cast iron pan (10 in.) over medium heat. Season lamb with salt and pepper and sear until browned (approx. 1-2 min. per side). Transfer to prepared baking sheet and set aside.
  3. Place garlic and mixed herbs into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to bowl and set aside.
  4. Brush seared lamb (just the meat, not the bone end) with mustard. Press the reserved chopped herbs into the meat to coat. Bake 20-25 minutes (400°F), until done to your liking (see Tip). When done, cover with foil and let rest 5 minutes. Meanwhile make the purée.

Artichoke and Spinach Purée

  1. Place Parmesan and lemon peel into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add artichokes, water, lemon juice and oil and cook 4 min/250°F/speed 1.
  3. Add spinach and mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  4. Slice the lamb into chops and serve over artichoke purée.

Useful Items

baking sheet, cast iron frying pan (10 in.), aluminum foil, basting brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cook lamb to a minimum internal temperature of 145°F and allow to rest for at least 3 minutes.
  • Wash lemons and all vegetables not to be peeled before use.
  • To steam lamb in the Varoma: after coating meat with herbs, place 20 oz water into mixing bowl, place rack of lamb into Varoma. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 22-25 min/Varoma/speed 1 until lamb is cooked to your liking.
