Ricotta pancakes with fruit compote and yoghurt

Ingredients

Dried fruit compote

  • 500 g water
  • 100 g raw sugar
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 lemon, zest only (4 x 1 cm pieces), no white pith
  • 50 g dried apricots
  • 50 g dried pears
  • 50 g pitted prunes
  • 40 g dried apples
  • 50 g dried peaches
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Ricotta pancakes

  • 440 g ricotta cheese (see Tips)
  • 190 g full cream milk
  • 4 eggs, separated
  • 180 g gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp gluten free baking powder (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 50 g butter, for frying
  • natural yoghurt, to serve

Preparation

Dried fruit compote

  1. Place water, sugar, cinnamon, cloves and lemon zest into mixing bowl and boil 10 min/100°C//speed .
  2. Add all remaining compote ingredients and cook 20 min/100°C//speed  or until fruit is plump and liquid is syrupy. Transfer into a sealable container, allow to cool then place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ricotta pancakes

  1. Place ricotta, milk and egg yolks into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add flour, baking powder and salt and mix 5 sec/speed 5 until just combined. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 4 or until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Fold egg whites through reserved batter in two batches.
  4. Place a large non-stick frying pan over a medium-low heat. Place a little of the butter into frying pan and allow to melt. Place 3 x ⅓-cupfuls of batter into pan (cooking 3 pancakes at a time) and cook for approx. 3 minutes, or until just starting to set at edges. Turn pancakes and cook for a further 3-4 minutes, or until golden. Transfer onto a plate and cover to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter (makes approx. 15 pancakes in total). Serve pancakes warm, topped with reserved dried fruit compote and yoghurt.

Useful Items

sealable container, large bowl, 1 plate, tea towel, large non-stick frying pan, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Pancake batter can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.
  • Compote can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
  • Omit steps 1 and 2, replacing compote with pure maple syrup, honey or fresh berries.
