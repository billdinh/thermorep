Yoyo slice

Ingredients

Filling

  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 50 g semolina
  • 150 g white sugar
  • 250 g butter, softened
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Prune jam

  • 300 g pitted prunes
  • 100 g water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Pastry

  • 250 g butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 500 g plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • 60 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 2 tbsp honey

Preparation

Filling

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place milk, semolina and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool completely (approx. 30 min).
  2. Without rinsing mixing bowl and with butterfly whisk still in place, place butter and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl wirth spatula, then repeat mixing 15 sec/speed 3.
  3. With butterfly whisk still in place, mix speed 3, slowly adding ½ of the reserved semolina mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid (approx 1-2 minutes).
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing speed 3, slowly adding remaining ½ of the semolina mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid (approx 1-2 minutes). Transfer semolina mixture into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Prune jam

  1. Place prunes into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water and mix 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add lemon juice and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Place 5 large pieces of baking paper onto a work surface. Using a ruler, trace a rectangle (30 x 22 cm) onto each piece of baking paper, then set aside.
  2. Place all pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 5, then knead 1 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and form into a log (approx. 24 cm long). Cut dough into 4 equal portions using the spatula.
  4. Place a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) upside-down onto work surface. Place 1 portion of the pastry between 2 of the stencilled pieces of baking paper and roll out to the size of the rectangle (30 x 22 cm). Continue rolling and shaping until pastry fits the size of the rectangle.
  5. Transfer 2 pieces of baking paper with rolled pastry onto upside-down baking tray. Remove top layer of stencilled baking paper from pastry and set aside. Bake for 6-7 minutes (180°C) or until pastry sheet is lightly brown.
  6. Carefully slide cooked pastry and baking paper off baking tray and onto work surface. Leave pastry sheet to cool. Repeat rolling and baking with remaining stencilled baking paper and 3 portions of dough so you have 4 baked pastry sheets the same size. Allow all pastry sheets to cool.
  7. To assemble slice, line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) or casserole dish with a fresh piece of baking paper, then carefully place a sheet of cooled pastry onto prepared baking tray.
  8. Carefully spread half of the prune jam over entire sheet of cooled pastry, taking care not to crack pastry, then cover with a second sheet of cooled pastry. Spread half of the semolina mixture over sheet of cooled pastry and cover with a third sheet of cooled pastry.
  9. Spread remaining prune jam over entire sheet of cooled pastry, spread remaining semolina mixture over jam and finish with the final sheet of cooled pastry.
  10. Place slice into refrigerator for a minimum 30 minutes, or overnight, before cutting into squares and dusting with icing sugar to serve.

Useful Items

baking paper, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The semolina filling and prune jam can both be made a day ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator. Remove from refrigerator and allow to reach room temperature prior to assembling the slice. You may need to re-whip the semolina filling with the butterfly whisk inserted 5-10 sec/speed 3 to make it light and fluffy again.
  • This slice is best made the day before as it will hold its shape better and will be easier to slice and serve. The flavours will also develop as it rests.
