Asian beef salad with peanut dressing

Ingredients

Miso marinade

  • 20 g peanut oil
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp miso paste
  • 20 g fish sauce
  • 2 sirloin steaks (approx. 400-500 g total - see Tips)

Peanut dressing

  • 1 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 fresh red chilli, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 50 g peanut butter
  • 20 g soy sauce
  • 50 g rice wine vinegar
  • 20 g caster sugar

Salad

  • 500 g boiling water
  • 200 g frozen edamame beans (see Tips)
  • 2 carrots, cut into matchsticks
  • 1 red capsicum, cut into thin strips (5 mm)
  • 1 cucumber, cut into ribbons
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 3 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

Miso marinade

  1. Place oil, chilli flakes, miso paste and fish sauce into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a glass or ceramic bowl. Add steaks, coat in marinade and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Peanut dressing

  1. Place ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add peanut butter, soy sauce, vinegar and sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a jug and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Salad

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh edamame beans into it. Cook 10 min/100Â°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside beans to cool. Continue with recipe while beans cool.
  2. Place carrot, capsicum, cucumber, spring onions/shallots, mint and coriander onto a plate and set aside.
  3. Place a frying pan over high heat. Add steaks and sear for 2-3 minutes, turning once, until browned and cooked to your liking. Transfer onto a separate plate and set aside to rest. Meanwhile, remove edamame beans from pods, discarding pods. Add beans onto plate with salad.
  4. Thinly slice rested steak, serve drizzled with reserved Peanut dressing, then serve with salad.

Useful Items

frying pan, plate, jug, ceramic or glass bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use any good-quality cut of steak in this recipe.
  • We used edamame beans in pods for this recipe.
