Milk jellies with poached stone fruit

Ingredients

  • 150 g freshly squeezed orange juice (approx. 2 oranges)
  • 40 g honey
  • 1 cinnamon quill, broken into pieces
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 3 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 100 g water
  • 5 plums or 6 apricots, stones removed
  • 500 g milk
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • 2 tsp gelatine powder
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract

Preparation

  1. Place orange juice, honey, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom pods and water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, place stone fruit into it and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 2, or until tender.
  2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer fruit into a large bowl or container. Pour syrup over fruit and set aside to cool slightly (approx. 10 minutes). Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place milk and sugar into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/80°C/speed 2.
  4. Add gelatine and mix 1 min/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl lid and set aside for 20 minutes until almost cool.
  5. Add cream and vanilla and mix 5 sec/speed 3, until combined.
  6. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain mixture into 6 glasses (120 ml capacity), then place glasses into refrigerator for a minimum of 4 hours or overnight until set. Serve with poached stone fruit.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, large bowl, fine mesh sieve, 6 glasses (120 ml), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you prefer, you can replace the cream with Greek yoghurt.
  • These jellies can also be turned out as delicious panna cottas. Simple strain the mixture into 6 greased dariole moulds or ramekins (120 ml capacity), then place into the refrigerator until completely set. Use a knife to loosen from the mould or simply invert onto a plate with a little shake. Lower, flatter shaped moulds will generally be easier to turn out.
