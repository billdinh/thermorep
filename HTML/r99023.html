Stuffed tomatoes

Ingredients

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 6 large fresh tomatoes (similar size - see tips)
  • 1 tsp sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 150 g pork loin steak, cut into cubes (2-3 cm)
  • 200 g beef mince
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp dried oregano

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Lightly oil a casserole dish (28 x 21 cm) and set aside.
  2. Cut the base off the tomatoes slightly so they sit flat, then cut the tops off the tomatoes and reserve the tops to use as lids.
  3. Remove the pulp and juice from inside the tomatoes and transfer into a bowl, then set aside. Sprinkle the insides of tomatoes with salt and turn each tomato upside down on a plate to allow excess juice to drain out.
  4. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add parsley, pork and beef and mix 8 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add pepper, oregano, 1 teaspoon of salt and 200 g of the reserved tomato pulp and juice and mix 8 sec/speed 5. Discard any remaining pulp and juice.
  7. Stuff reserved tomatoes with pork and beef mixture, then top with tomato lids and place into prepared casserole dish. Bake for 40 mintes (200°C), or until tomatoes are tender and the stuffing is cooked through. Serve hot.

Useful Items

casserole dish (28 x 21 cm), bowl, 1 plate, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Stuffed tomatoes are a classic French family cuisine.
  • Ensure you cook the tomatoes with their lids in place to prevent them from drying out.
  • For a more substantial dish, serve stuffed tomatoes with fresh pasta or rice.
  • 8 tomatoes will be needed to be purchased if large tomatoes are not available.
