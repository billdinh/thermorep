Profiteroles

Ingredients

Choux pastry

  • 150 g water
  • 80 g butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 3 eggs

Chocolate sauce and assembly

  • 250 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), cut into pieces
  • 40 g butter, cut into pieces
  • 200 g full cream milk
  • vanilla ice cream, homemade (see recipe) or shop-bought

Preparation

Choux pastry

  1. Place water, butter, salt and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 2 min 30 sec - 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl and set aside to cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  4. Place mixing bowl back into position and mix speed 5, adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid onto rotating blades.  After adding the last egg mix a further 30 sec/speed 5.
  5. Place pastry mixture into a piping bag and pipe 18 balls (walnut-size) onto prepared baking tray, leaving 5 cm space between each.Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Turn off oven then using a wooden skewer pierce each ball to release steam. Return tray to oven and open oven door slightly, leaving it ajar for 10 minutes to allow pastries to dry. Remove tray from oven and allow choux pastries to cool completely.

Chocolate sauce and assembly

  1. Place dark chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add butter and milk and heat 4 min/100°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Cut cold choux pastries into halves. Place one scoop of ice cream onto each bottom half and cover with top half. Serve 2-3 choux pastries per person with chocolate sauce drizzled on top.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, wooden skewer, piping bag (10 mm nozzle), ice cream scoop, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The cooking time required in step 1 will depend on the temperature of your butter.
  • If you don't have a piping bag, cut the corner off a sturdy freezer bag and use as directed to pipe choux pastries onto baking tray.
  • Use a wet finger to smooth any bumps on choux pastries before baking.
  • If you prepare your own ice cream, take it out of freezer and mix in a clean mixing bowl to soften before using in step 8.
  • For a thicker chocolate sauce, reduce the milk to 100 g and cook 4.5 min/50°C/speed 1, allow sauce to cool and thicken before serving.
  • Profiteroles can be prepared in advance: fill choux pastries with ice cream and store in the freezer. Prepare and pour the chocolate sauce just before serving.

Profiteroles are a traditional French dessert.
