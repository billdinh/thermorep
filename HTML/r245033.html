Mini Peppers Stuffed with Homemade Ricotta - Piccoli peperoni ripieni di ricotta

Ingredients

Homemade Ricotta

  • 1500 g whole milk
  • 30 g white vinegar

Peppers

  • 400 g mixed mini peppers, stalks and seeds removed
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil, for drizzling
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 10 - 12 fresh basil leaves, to taste
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Preparation

Homemade Ricotta

  1. Place milk in mixing bowl and heat 10 min/80째C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 1 min/90째C/speed 1. Leave in mixing bowl for 1 minute for the curds and whey to separate.
  3. Line simmering basket with muslin or a nut milk bag then set over a large bowl. Carefully pour ricotta mixture into lined simmering basket then leave to drain, ensuring ricotta is able to drain freely. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.

Peppers

  1. Preheat oven to 200째C. Place peppers in a roasting tin, drizzle with oil then roast for 15-18 minutes (200째C) until charred and soft. Meanwhile, make filling.
  2. Place Parmesan and basil in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add homemade ricotta, salt and pepper then mix 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a piping bag.
  4. Once peppers are roasted, allow to cool for 10 minutes. Snip end off piping bag and pipe filling into peppers. Serve or refrigerate until needed.

Useful Items

muslin or nut milk bag, roasting tin, piping bag, large bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Ricotta, synonymous with Italian cuisine, was originally produced from the whey left behind in the making of mozzarella and Provolone. In this colourful and tasty antipasto, the ricotta is homemade then used to stuff roasted and charred mini peppers for a dish to be proud of.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
