Davidson plum cheesecake slice

Ingredients

Base

  • 50 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 100 g pecans (activated - see Tips)
  • 100 g sunflower seeds (activated - see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut
  • 2 tsp ground roasted wattleseed (optional - see Tips)
  • 3 tsp honey (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon

Filling

  • 250 g quark (see Tips)
  • 400 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 120 g honey (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp hot water
  • 3 tsp gelatine powder
  • ½ tsp Davidson plum powder (optional - see Tips)

Jelly topping

  • 125 g fresh raspberries, plus extra to serve
  • 250 g fresh strawberries, hulled, plus extra to serve
  • 2 tsp Davidson plum powder (optional - see Tips)
  • 20 g honey (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp gelatine powder (see Tips)

Preparation

Base

  1. Grease and line a square cake or slice tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place all base ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 2 sec/speed 6.
  4. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and evenly spread out into base. Place into freezer until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place quark, cream, honey and vanilla into mixing bowl and heat 4 min/60°C/speed 3. Meanwhile, place hot water and gelatine into a small bowl and whisk together.
  2. Add gelatine mixture and plum powder (optional) into mixing bowl and heat 1 min 30 sec/60°C/speed 4.
  3. Using a coarse-mesh sieve, strain filling mixture over chilled base. Return to freezer until set but not frozen (approx. 90 minutes) or place into refrigerator overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Jelly topping

  1. Place all jelly topping ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 7 min/95°C/speed 3. Allow to cool in mixing bowl, with lid removed, for 10-15 minutes or until cool enough to touch but not set. Remove base from freezer and using a coarse-mesh sieve, strain topping over the filling. Spread evenly and place back into freezer or refrigerator until set firm (approx. 30 minutes). Cut into squares (approx. 24) and garnish with extra fresh berries (optional) to serve.

Useful Items

small bowl, baking paper, coarse-mesh sieve, square cake tin (20 cm), freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because this slice contains honey, you should wait until your child is over 12 months before offering to them.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small.
  • You can replace the pecans with activated walnuts in this recipe.
  • You can replace the sunflower seeds with activated almonds in this recipe.
  • Honey is not suitable for children under 1 year of age.
  • Wattleseed is an Australian native ingredient that's a source of protein, potassium, calcium, iron and zinc. You can find it in health food stores and online. It's not crucial to this recipe, so feel free to omit if you can't source it.
  • Quark is a soft farmer's cheese, available at specialty food stores or delis. If you can't find quark, you can make your own (you'll find the recipe on Cookidoo®) or you can replace it with soft cream cheese.
  • Gelatine is also of current research interest for its possible role in improving gut health - just make sure you buy a quality, grass-fed (preferably organic) gelatine if you can find one.
  • Davidson plums are a native Australian fruit and a nutritional powerhouse, loaded with antioxidants, potassium and lutein, amongst other goodness. You can buy the dried powder in health food stores or online. It is not crucial to the recipe, although it does lift the flavour with its lovely tartness. This slice is still delicious without it.
