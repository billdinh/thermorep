Wholegrain hamburger buns

Ingredients

  • 500 g water
  • 250 g waxy potatoes, diced (1 cm) (see tip)
  • 250 g wheat grains
  • 75 g milk
  • 1 cube fresh yeast (40 g)
  • 3 eggs
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp fine salt
  • 60 g butter, soft, in pieces

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Weigh potatoes in simmering basket and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer potatoes to a flat dish and allow to cool for approx. 30 minutes. Meanwhile, empty and dry mixing bowl and continue with recipe.
  2. Place 20 g wheat grains in mixing bowl, crush 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 230 g wheat grains in mixing bowl, grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Place cooled potatoes and milk in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add yeast, 2 eggs, flour, salt, butter and ground wheat and knead 2 min/ kneten. Transfer dough to a large bowl, cover with cling film or a damp kitchen towel and leave in a warm place for 30 minutes to rise until doubled in size.
  6. Line a baking tray with baking paper. With floured hands, shape 6 equally-sized balls from the dough and place on baking tray. Cover bread rolls with cling film or a damp kitchen towel and leave in a warm place for approx. 45 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C.
  7. Whisk 1 egg and glaze the rolls, sprinkle with shredded wheat and bake in oven for 18–20 minutes (200°C) until golden. Allow to cool for 10 minutes and serve as desired.

Useful Items

baking tray, baking paper, bowl, cling film or tea towel, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • In step 5, 250 g cooked potatoes from the previous day, cut into pieces, can alternatively be used. The cooking and cooling described in step 1 is then no longer necessary.
  • If the dough is very sticky after step 5, add 30 g flour and knead another 15 sec/.
  • These bread rolls make ideal burger buns but are also perfect for breakfast or as a savoury snack.
