Pumpkin, Carrot and Herb Bread

Ingredients

  • 40 g grapeseed oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g raw almonds
  • 1 tbsp flaxseeds (linseeds)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (approx. 10 cm long), leaves only
  • 40 g coconut flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp dried onions
  • 1 tsp dried mixed herbs
  • 30 g raw cashew nuts
  • 100 g carrots, cut into quarters
  • 100 g butternut squash, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tsp cider vinegar
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (18 x 7 x 6 cm) with baking paper leaving a 2 cm overhang and set aside.
  2. Place almonds, flaxseeds (linseeds), chia seeds and rosemary into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add coconut flour, baking powder, dried onions, dried mixed herbs and cashews and combine 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Place carrots and squash into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into bowl with nut mixture and set aside.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs and 40 g grapeseed oil into mixing bowl and whip 4 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Add all remaining ingredients, including reserved squash mixture, and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  7. Transfer into prepared tin and bake for 40-45 minutes (160°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the loaf comes out clean.
  8. Leave bread to cool in tin for 10 minutes then using overhanging baking paper, remove bread from tin and transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

loaf tin (18 x 7 x 6 cm), large bowl, baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Wrap bread in cling film or place into a sealable storage container and store in refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  • Serve this hearty bread with soup, as part of a ploughman’s platter or alongside a salad
