Sweet potato lasagne

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes (approx. 600 g), peeled and cut into thin slices lengthways (approx. 3-5 mm)

Plant-based béchamel sauce

  • 500 g plant-based milk of choice
  • 50 g gluten free cornflour
  • 30 g vegan butter (see Tips)
  • 40 g nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp salt

Lasagne

  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into pieces (approx. 2-3 cm)
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces (approx. 2-3 cm)
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 170 g tri-colour quinoa
  • 200 g filtered water
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • ¼ tsp salt,
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper,
  • 400 g canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 80 g vegan cheese, grated (see Tips)
  • fresh basil leaves

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper. Arrange sweet potato slices onto prepared baking trays and bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C), while still retaining some firmness. Meanwhile, proceed with recipe.

Plant-based béchamel sauce

  1. Place milk, cornflour, butter, nutritional yeast and salt into mixing bowl and cook 7 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lasagne

  1. Place onion, celery and carrot into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add oil and oregano and cook 3 min/120°C/speed 2.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh quinoa into it. Transfer into a fine-mesh sieve and rinse well.
  4. Add water, tomato paste, chopped tomatoes, stock paste, vinegar, salt, pepper and rinsed quinoa into mixing bowl and cook 15 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add chickpeas and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Grease a baking dish (23 x 31 cm) with oil. Spread a thin layer of quinoa mixture into base of baking dish, then arrange a single layer of sweet potato over quinoa. Spoon half of the remaining quinoa mixture over sweet potato and spread evenly. Spoon half of the béchamel sauce over quinoa and spread evenly with aid of spatula. Place another layer of sweet potato over béchamel. Spread remaining quinoa mixture over sweet potato, then top with remaining sweet potato and remaining béchamel. Sprinkle with grated cheese and bake for 25 minutes (200°C). Garnish with fresh basil leaves just before serving.

Useful Items

baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, fine-mesh sieve, ceramic baking dish (23 x 31 cm), thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own vegan butter and cheese, refer to recipes on this recipe chip. We used our Cashew cheddar cheese in this recipe.
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • You can use either Mushroom stock paste (refer to the recipe on this recipe chip) or Vegetable stock paste (please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook) in this recipe.
