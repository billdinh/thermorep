Stuffed Picnic Loaf

Ingredients

Bread Loaf

  • 300 g water, plus extra for baking
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • oil, for greasing
  • 500 g strong white bread flour, plus 1 Tbsp for sprinkling
  • 40 g butter, diced
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Filling

  • 100 g red onions, halved then thinly sliced (3 mm)
  • 70 g olive oil, plus extra for brushing
  • 2 tsp brown sugar
  • 80 g roasted red peppers, preserved, in pieces (5 cm)
  • 200 g cooked chicken meat, breast meat, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 small handful fresh basil leaves (approx. 5 g)
  • 30 g red wine vinegar
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 30 g pitted black olives, halved
  • 100 g cooked ham, cut in large pieces
  • 30 g rocket leaves
  • 50 g grated mozzarella

Preparation

Bread Loaf

  1. Place water and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 2 min/37°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Add flour, butter and salt then knead 5 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl then cover with oiled cling film and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  3. Once dough has doubled in size, knock back then shape into a round loaf and place on prepared baking tray, cover with oiled cling film and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size again (approx. 1 hour).
  4. Towards the end of proving time, preheat oven to 200°C. Place an empty roasting tin in bottom of oven.
  5. Reshape loaf making it approx. Ø 20 cm, 10 cm high. Dust loaf with 1 Tbsp flour, then use a large, sharp knife to make shallow cuts about 1 cm deep across the top of loaf to create a diamond pattern.
  6. Place baking tray with loaf into middle of oven then pour cold water into the empty roasting tray at the bottom of oven. Bake for 20-30 minutes (200°C) until loaf sounds hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer to a wire rack to cool.

Filling

  1. Place onions, 30 g oil and sugar in mixing bowl then caramelise 10 min/Varoma//speed . Meanwhile, slice a thin layer off top of bread and remove inside of loaf leaving a 3 cm shell, then brush shell and underneath lid with olive oil.
  2. Place roasted red peppers in bottom of loaf shell, then arrange caramelised red onion slices on top, and then chicken, packing down firmly between layers.
  3. Place basil, remaining 40 g oil, red wine vinegar, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then pour half the dressing onto chicken, spreading evenly.
  4. Place olives, then ham, then rocket into loaf packing down firmly. Pour remaining dressing over the top and finish with a sprinkling of grated mozzarella.
  5. Place lid back on loaf then wrap tightly in cling film. Place in fridge and lay a chopping board on top, weighted down with something heavy such as tinned food. Chill and compress for at least 2 hours or overnight before serving in slices at your picnic.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, large bowl, chopping block , pastry brush, wire rack, long sharp knife, roasting tin, cling film, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Adding water to the oven while the loaf is baking produces steam, which helps develop a crisp and shiny crust.
  • You can use the leftover bread to make breadcrumbs or croutons.
  • Can be heated in the oven and served warm.
