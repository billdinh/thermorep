Kimchi

Ingredients

  • 700 g Chinese cabbage, cut into halves lengthways, then cut into thirds
  • 2500 g filtered water
  • 150 g Himalayan salt
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 apple, cored and cut into quarters
  • 5 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 1 - 2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 - 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • 30 g coconut sugar
  • 1 nori sheet, torn into pieces
  • 20 g warm water

Preparation

  1. Place a large (4L) sealable container onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cabbage and 500 g of the water into it. Set aside.
  2. Place remaining 2000 g water and salt into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 3, until salt dissolves. Transfer into container with cabbage and water. Ensure cabbage is fully submerged in brine (see Tips), cover with lid and set aside for 24 hours. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Using Varoma, drain cabbage thoroughly and transfer into a large bowl.
  4. Place garlic, ginger, apple, spring onions/shallots, cayenne, paprika, tamari, sugar, nori sheet and warm water into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8.
  5. Add reserved cabbage and mix 1 min/î€ƒ/speed 2. Transfer 2-3 large spoonfuls of cabbage (with liquid) into a sealable sterilised jar (1L), pressing mixture down firmly with a wooden spoon or muddler to soften cabbage and release liquid. Repeat with remaining cabbage mixture until jar is full, allowing a 3 cm gap at the top of the jar. Mixture must be covered with liquid (see Tips). Seal jar, place into a bowl (see Tips) and store in a dark place.
  6. Kimchi will be ready after 5 days or you can continue to ferment for up to an additional 2 days, depending on your taste preference (a longer ferment generally yields a stronger flavour). Store kimchi in refrigerator and serve as desired.

Useful Items

large bowl, sealable container, sealable sterilised jar, wooden spoon or muddler, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important to brine the cabbage to tenderise it as well as inhibit the growth of bacteria. Weigh cabbage down with plates or other types of weights to ensure it is fully submerged.
  • When filling jar, ensure cabbage is fully covered by liquid and place jar into a bowl as liquid may overflow during fermentation.
  • Kimchi will continue to ferment, but at a much slower rate whilst in the refrigerator, once opened.
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
