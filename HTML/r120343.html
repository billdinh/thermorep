Almond milk jellies with fruit coulis

Ingredients

  • 1 titanium strength gelatine leaf
  • 300 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • ½ lemon zested, no white pith, and juiced
  • 90 g raw sugar
  • 100 g blanched almonds
  • 100 g fresh mixed red berries (e.g. strawberries, raspberries, red currants)

Preparation

  1. Place gelatine into glass or ceramic bowl and cover with water. Set aside to soak for 5 minutes.
  2. Place lemon zest and 50 g of the sugar into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add almonds and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add water and mix 10 sec/speed 5, then whisk 30 sec/speed 10.
  5. Squeeze excess water from gelatine sheet. Add gelatine to mixing bowl and cook 7 min/90°C/speed 3. Discard soaking water. Transfer gelatine mixture into serving jars (see Tips) and set aside to cool to room temperature (approx. 10 minutes). Once cooled, place into refrigerator for a minimum of 4 hours or overnight to set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Place berries, remaining 40 g sugar and 2 teaspoons of the lemon juice into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 5 -10, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 10.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Set aside to cool (approx. 20  minutes).
  8. Remove desserts from refrigerator, top with cooled coulis and serve immediately.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, 4 small serving glasses, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For a smoother texture to the almond milk, strain through a nut milk bag or fine-mesh sieve before adding gelatine.
