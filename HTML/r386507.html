Apple ginger cake with salted caramel sauce

Ingredients

Apple purée

  • 500 g red apples (approx. 4 apples), peeled, cored and cut into pieces (2 cm)

Cake

  • 180 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing (see Tips)
  • 400 g spelt flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 3 eggs
  • 150 g coconut sugar (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp ground cloves

Salted caramel sauce

  • 120 g salted butter
  • 150 g double cream (see Tips)
  • 100 g coconut sugar (see Tips)
  • ¼ - ½ tsp salt

Preparation

Apple purée

  1. Place apples into mixing bowl and purée 20 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and lightly flour a bundt tin (3L).
  2. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add eggs and sugar, then whisk 5 min/speed 4.
  4. Add oil, vanilla and reserved apple purée and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  5. Add flour, bicarbonate of soda, salt, pepper, cinnamon and ground cloves and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 5 sec/speed 3, mixing with aid of spatula, until combined. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and bake for 50 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean.
  7. Cool in tin for 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salted caramel sauce

  1. Place butter, cream, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and cook 5-6 min/120°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Serve cake drizzled with Salted caramel sauce (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, bundt cake tin, wooden skewer, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because this cake has some added sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before offering it to them.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small (with no or just a little caramel sauce).
  • You can replace the coconut sugar with Rapadura sugar in this recipe.
  • You can replace the double cream with pouring (whipping) cream in this recipe.
  • You can replace the olive oil with coconut oil (melted) in this recipe.
  • For a lower-sugar option, this cake is just as lovely without the caramel sauce.
