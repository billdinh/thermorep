Caramel almond cashew slice

Ingredients

  • 40 g virgin coconut oil, melted, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g almonds, activated or roasted
  • 150 g cashews, activated or roasted
  • 160 g pitted Medjool dates
  • 40 g dried cranberries
  • 65 g almond butter (see Tips)
  • 60 g honey (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ground roasted wattleseed (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp sea salt (see Tips)
  • 150 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)

Preparation

  1. Grease and line a square cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place almonds, cashews and dates into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6 or until roughly chopped.
  3. Add cranberries, oil, almond butter, honey, wattleseed, vanilla, cinnamon and salt, then stir 40 sec//speed 3, until combined. Transfer mixture into prepared tin and smooth evenly with spatula. Place into refrigerator for a minimum of 2 hours or until firm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then melt 2 min/40°C/speed 1 or until melted and smooth. Pour over base and smooth with spatula. Cut into squares before slice sets completely (approx. 24 pieces). Place back into refrigerator for 10-15 minutes or until set. Serve as desired.

Useful Items

square cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the almond butter with peanut butter in this recipe. Just be sure to use one made from 100 per cent nuts.
  • Honey is not suitable for children under 1 year of age.
  • You can omit the salt from this recipe if serving to children. You can also skip the chocolate topping if preferred.
  • Wattleseed is an Australian native ingredient that's a source of protein, potassium, calcium, iron and zinc. You can find it in health food stores and online. It's not crucial in this recipe, so feel free to omit if you can't source.
  • You can store this slice in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
