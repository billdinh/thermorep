Blackcurrant Cordial

Ingredients

  • 130 g blackcurrants, fresh
  • 300 g water
  • 320 - 360 g granulated sugar
  • 1 - 2 tsp lemon juice, to taste
  • water, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place blackcurrants and water in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Heat 5 min/90°C/speed 2.
  2. Pour juice through a fine sieve into a jug. Rinse mixing bowl then weigh strained juice back in.
  3. Add an equal weight of sugar and heat 6 min/90°C/speed 2. Set aside to cool for 10 minutes then add lemon juice to taste.
  4. Pour into a 1 litre sterilised glass bottle, seal and store in fridge for up to 4 weeks. To serve, dilute 30-50 g cordial to 3-6 parts still or sparkling water depending on desired taste.

Useful Items

fine sieve, jug, 1 litre sterilised glass bottle , refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Frozen blackberries can also be used. Follow same recipe, heating for 8 minutes in step 1.
  • Use 150 g redcurrants instead of blackcurrants to make redcurrant cordial.
  • Also wonderful diluted with a dry sparkling wine or champagne.
  • When blackcurrants are in season, freeze them so they are available for recipes all year round.
  • Strip blackcurrants from the stalks easily with a fork. Pick out and discard any leaves that come off with the berries.

  • Blackcurrants are one of the few fruits that will grow in Britain’s mild, rainy climate, and they’ve been doing so since the 17th century. During the Second World War, when other sources of vitamin C were difficult to get hold of, almost the entire crop was taken by the government and made into cordial for children throughout the country. This version tastes so much of the fresh fruit, you’ll only ever want to make your own from now on!
