Salt Cod Fritters

Ingredients

  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 300 g salt cod, presoaked and desalted, cut in pieces (see tip)
  • 200 g floury potatoes, peeled, cut in small pieces
  • 300 g water
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • frying oil (e.g. sunflower oil or vegetable oil)
Nutrition
per 1 piece

469 kJ / 112 kcal

3 g

Preparation

  1. Place onions and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add cod and mix 6 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place potatoes, water and salt in mixing bowl then cook 15 min/100°C//speed . Drain potatoes in simmering basket. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Place cooked potatoes in mixing bowl and mash 6 sec/speed 4.
  5. Add eggs, reserved cod mixture and pepper then mix speed 4 until smooth. Using 2 tablespoons, shape mixture into fritters (approx. 25).
  6. Heat plenty of oil in a saucepan then, once oil is hot, fry fritters, a few at a time, turning in the oil with a slotted spoon, until golden and crisp. Remove with slotted spoon and transfer to paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve hot or cold.

Useful Items

paper towels, saucepan, slotted spoon, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • For homemade salt cod: Place 200 g table salt in a baking dish and place cod on top, skin-side down. Cover cod with remaining 300 g table salt then cover dish tightly with cling film. Refrigerate for 24-48 hours. After this time, wash all the salt off. Cod can be used immediately but will taste quite salty. Alternatively, soak in water for 12 hours before using.
  • Fritters can be frozen before frying and cooked from frozen.
  • Serve as a starter, or as a main course with rice or black beans.
