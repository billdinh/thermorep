Taro bread

Ingredients

Dough

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 200 g baker's flour
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 120 g milk

Taro filling

  • 600 g water
  • 300 g taro, peeled, and cut into pieces (2 cm - see Tip)
  • 100 - 120 g sugar, to taste
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 20 g milk

Taro bread

  • 30 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 70 g baker's flour
  • 30 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp milk
  • 20 g caster sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 15 g flaked almonds, for sprinkling

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease a bowl and set aside. Place flour, yeast and milk into mixing bowl and knead 1 min/. Transfer dough into a greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place to prove until slightly risen (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Taro filling

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and weigh taro into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until soft. Remove Varoma and discard cooking liquid.
  2. Place steamed taro, sugar, butter and milk into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 5, until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Taro bread

  1. Grease a loaf tin (25 x 10 x 7 cm) and set aside. Grease a large bowl and set aside. Place flours, 1 of the eggs, milk, sugar, salt and the proved dough into the mixing bowl and knead 2 min/.
  2. Add butter and knead 2 min/. Transfer dough into large greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm place to prove until slightly risen (approx. 40 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 170°C. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface and divide dough into 3 portions (approx. 180 g each). Roll out each portion of dough into a rectangle (approx. 12 x 20 cm). Place one-third of the Taro filling (approx. 90 g) onto each portion of dough, spread out filling to cover dough, then roll into a log shape. Fold each log in half and place side by side, seam side down, into the base of prepared loaf tin. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside to prove for 40 minutes.
  4. Lightly beat the remaining egg and brush over top of loaf. Sprinkle with flaked almonds, then bake for 35 minutes (170°C), until golden brown and cooked. Cool in tin for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

small bowl , large bowl, bowl, plastic wrap, loaf tin (25 x 10 x 7 cm), silicone bread mat, rolling pin, pastry brush, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find taro at most Asian food stores and at some green grocers.
