Steamed white fish with tomato and Indian spices

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • ¾ tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 15 sprigs fresh coriander, roots, stalks and leaves (approx. 40 g) (see Tips)
  • 80 g red onion
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 ¼ tbsp Tandoori paste (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 300 g fresh tomatoes, cut into quarters
  • 40 g coconut cream
  • 4 hoki fillets, 3 cm thick (approx. 170 g each)
  • 250 g basmati rice
  • 1000 g water
  • lemon, cut into pieces, to serve

Preparation

  1. Line Varoma tray with baking paper, leaving a 1 cm gap around the edges to allow steam to circulate freely and set aside.
  2. Place oil, garlic paste, ginger paste, chillies, coriander, onion and 1 teaspoon of the salt into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5, then cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add Tandoori paste, lemon juice, tomatoes and coconut cream and chop 4 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Place fish onto prepared Varoma tray and pour marinade over fish fillets. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  5. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear. Place water and 1 teaspoon of the salt into mixing bowl, re-insert simmering basket and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 4.
  6. With aid of spatula, make a well in the centre of the rice to allow steam to circulate. Place Varoma into position, insert Varoma tray, secure Varoma lid and steam 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 1, until rice and fish are cooked to your liking (see Tips). Transfer onto a serving plate and serve with lemon.

Useful Items

baking paper, serving plate

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste and Tandoori paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • Steaming times for your fish may vary depending on the thickness of the fillets.
