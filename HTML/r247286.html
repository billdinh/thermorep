Pizza Capricciosa

Ingredients

Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 400 g strong white bread flour
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 200 g mozzarella, cut in pieces
  • 6 anchovy fillets, in oil, drained
  • 250 g tinned whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 4 artichoke hearts, in oil, drained, quartered
  • 50 g mushrooms, in oil, sliced (or 4 porcini mushrooms, sliced)
  • 50 g taggiasca olives
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 100 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add remaining 120 g water, flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl.
  3. Tip dough out onto a work surface, shape into a ball then transfer to prepared bowl. Cover with cling film (see tip) then leave until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Topping

  1. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 230°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place mozzarella in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to simmering basket and leave to drain for at least 10 minutes.
  3. Place anchovies, tomatoes and salt in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Tip dough out onto prepared baking tray. Spread out into a rectangle over the whole tray using the tips of your fingers, forming a thicker border around edges.
  5. Spread tomato mixture evenly over pizza base, then top with grated mozzarella, artichokes, mushrooms and olives.
  6. Season to taste then bake for 20 minutes (230°C). Serve pizza hot, cut in slices.

Useful Items

cling film, baking paper, baking tray, large bowl, large baking tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A traditional pizza that remains on the menus of many Italian pizzerias today, Capricciosa is popular amongst indecisive customers who like the idea of a pizza brimming with different ingredients. This pizza is similar to the quattro stagioni (four seasons) with artichokes, mushrooms and olives, but differs in that all ingredients are present in each bite. Combined with a rich tomato sauce brimming with the robust flavour of anchovies, this pizza is a real crowd-pleaser.
  • For a stronger flavour, in step 6, place anchovies and 20 g extra virgin olive oil in mixing bowl, chop 4 sec/speed 8 then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1, before adding tomatoes. Continue with recipe.
  • Lightly grease cling film to prevent it from sticking to the dough during the resting and leavening stages.
