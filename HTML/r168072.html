Gluten-Free Pretzels

Ingredients

  • 1 oz vegetable oil, plus extra to grease
  • 10 oz warm water
  • 1 packet/envelope dried instant yeast (2¼ tsp)
  • 10 oz rice flour, plus extra to dust
  • 6 oz brown rice flour
  • 4 oz tapioca starch
  • 3 tbsp coconut sugar
  • 2 tsp xanthan gum
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 130 oz water
  • 4 tbsp baking soda
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1 tsp coarse salt

Preparation

  1. Lightly grease a large bowl and line a baking sheet with parchment or silicone mat and set aside.
  2. Place warm water and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/100°F/speed 2. Add white and brown rice flours, tapioca starch, coconut sugar, xanthan gum, baking powder, eggs, oil and salt and mix 15 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 15 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a lightly floured surface and knead dough into a ball. Transfer dough into prepared bowl, cover with a damp kitchen towel or cling wrap and allow to rise in a warm place (approx. 75 minutes) until doubled in size.
  3. In a large pot, bring water to a rolling boil. Punch down dough to deflate. Cut dough like a pie, into 12 wedges. Cover dough with a towel while making pretzels. Knead each piece into a smooth ball and apply pressure from the center outwards to roll into a 14 inch rope. Form each rope into a U-shape and holding the ends of the rope, cross ends over each other then press the rope ends into the bottom of the U to form the pretzel. Place shaped pretzels onto prepared baking sheet. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  4. Add baking soda to boiling water. Wait until boiling subsides. Boil pretzels one at a time. If the pretzel floats, boil for 5 seconds. With slotted spoon return pretzel to prepared baking sheet. If the pretzel sinks, wait until the pretzel rises to the surface of the water and return pretzel to prepared baking sheet. Whisk egg whites in a small bowl. Brush pretzels with egg whites and sprinkle with coarse salt.
  5. Bake for 20-22 minutes (375°F) or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Useful Items

kitchen towel, baking sheet, parchment paper or silicone baking mat, pastry brush, large bowl, wire whisk, slotted spoon, 4 qt. pot, stove top, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Proofing is complete when the dough has doubled in size and bounces back slowly but steadily, it is ready to bake.
  • Make sure there is no flour on your work surface while rolling out the dough, or dough will not stick to itself when twisted into a pretzel shape.
  • The dough should be supple but slightly damp when kneading; when rolling pieces, the ropes may break apart, but press ends together until they reach 14 inches in length.
  • Serve with your favorite gluten-free dipping sauce, e.g. mustard.
