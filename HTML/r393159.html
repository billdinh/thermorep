Matcha and raspberry swirled custard tarts

Ingredients

Pastry case

  • 1 tbsp macadamia oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 10 g dried cranberries
  • 80 g pitted dates
  • 20 g pecans
  • 180 g raw almonds

Flavoured custards

  • 1 ½ tbsp freeze-dried raspberry powder (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp boiling water
  • 1 ½ tsp matcha powder (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 40 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla paste (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp gluten free cornflour
  • 2 tbsp filtered water

Preparation

Pastry case

  1. Grease and line bases of 6 tartlet tins (9 cm) with macadamia oil, then set aside.
  2. Place cranberries, dates, pecans and 100 g of the almonds into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add macadamia oil and remaining 80 g almonds and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Place 2 tablespoons of mixture into the base of each tartlet tin and press out evenly over base and sides. Place into refrigerator to chill while preparing custard. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Flavoured custards

  1. Place raspberry powder and boiling water into a small bowl and stir to combine. Strain mixture using a fine-mesh sieve to remove any seeds.
  2. Place matcha powder and 1 tablespoon of the lemon juice into a separate bowl and stir to combine.
  3. Place coconut milk, syrup and vanilla into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/98°C/speed 4.
  4. Place cornflour, water and remaining 1 tablespoon lemon juice into a small bowl and stir to combine.
  5. Add cornflour mixture into mixing bowl and heat 1 min/80°C/speed 4. Transfer half of the custard (approx. 215 g) into bowl with the matcha mixture and stir well to combine.
  6. Add reserved raspberry mixture to mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Pour both custards into each tartlet case and use a skewer to swirl colours. Transfer tarts into refrigerator to cool and set (approx. 30 minutes), then decorate as desired to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

small bowl, fine-mesh sieve, wooden skewer, baking paper, loose-based tartlet tins, bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find freeze-dried raspberry powder at some major supermarkets, health food stores and specialty food stores. You can find matcha powder at health food stores, specialty food stores, tea shops or online.
  • You can make various flavours of custard using different freeze-dried powders (such as strawberry, blueberry or lemon) or using cacao powder.
  • To make your own vanilla paste, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • We decorated our tarts with fresh fruit and edible dried flowers.
