Chawan mushi (Japanese savoury egg custard)

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs
  • 800 g water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp instant dashi powder (see variation)
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 pinch ground pepper
  • 100 g pork mince (optional)
  • 30 g fresh shiitake mushrooms, sliced, or any small mushrooms, sliced
  • 6 raw prawns, medium size, peeled
  • 30 frozen green peas (25 g)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
576 kJ / 138 kcal

12 g

Preparation

  1. Place eggs, 300 g water, salt, instant dashi powder, soy sauce, ground pepper and pork mince in mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 2.5. Transfer to 6 ceramic ramekins (approx. Ø 8 cm, height 4.5 cm), filling to 1 cm from the top of each. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place remaining 500 g water in mixing bowl. Place 3 filled ramekins in Varoma dish and cover loosely with a sheet of baking paper. Insert Varoma tray, place remaining ramekins in it and cover again with a sheet of baking paper. Set Varoma in position and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Carefully remove Varoma lid and tray, lift sheets of baking paper and carefully arrange shiitake mushrooms, 1 prawn and 5 green peas on top of each steamed egg dish. Replace baking paper, set Varoma tray and lid back in position and steam a further 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve hot.

Useful Items

ceramic ramekins (Ø 8 cm, height 4.5 cm), baking paper

Hints & Tricks

  • This delicate steamed egg dish is served both at home and in restaurants in Japan. Adapt the toppings as you feel inspired, choosing ingredients that will add both colour and flavour to the silky egg custard. Serve as a starter or as one of several small dishes in a main course. It is also pleasant served chilled on a hot day.
  • Steam in any heat-resistant cup or small bowl of similar size. If your dishes are a different size or they are not ceramic, you may have to adjust the cooking time. Once you know the perfect cooking time for your own dishes, make a note of it for next time.
  • Dashi, or Japanese cooking stock, adds flavour to the chawan mushi. You can replace the instant dashi powder with ½ tsp homemade chicken or vegetable stock paste: first dilute the stock paste in some of the water before adding it in step 1, and reduce salt to ¼ tsp. Or omit instant dashi powder and use 300 g stock instead of water in step 1. Finally you can omit the instant dashi powder altogether; the dish will have a more subtle egg flavour that is also very pleasant.
