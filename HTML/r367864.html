Sambal kacang (peanut sauce)

Ingredients

  • 4 - 7 dried red chillies, deseeded, to taste
  • warm water, for soaking
  • 300 g roasted unsalted peanuts
  • 50 g vegetable oil
  • 70 g eschalots
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 ½ cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 ½ cm piece fresh galangal, peeled
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, white part only
  • 2 candlenuts (see Tips)
  • 50 g tamarind paste
  • 500 g water
  • 50 g palm sugar
  • 1 tsp salt, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place chillies into a bowl, cover with warm water and set aside to soak for 5 minutes to soften.
  2. Place peanuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Using simmering basket, drain chillies. Place oil, eschalots, garlic, ginger, galangal, lemongrass, candlenuts and drained chillies into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved peanuts and sauté 8 min/120°C/speed 2.
  5. Add tamarind paste, water, palm sugar and salt and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 2. Serve as dipping sauce with meat or vegetable dishes, compressed rice cakes or satay.

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find candlenuts at most Asian food stores or speciality food stores.
  • You can store this sauce in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
