Mocha and hazelnut ice cream with toasted marshmallows

Ingredients

  • 100 g hazelnuts, without skins
  • 200 g dark chocolate melts
  • 600 g full cream milk
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 180 g white sugar
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 40 - 60 g strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1 pinch salt (optional)
  • 50 g marshmallows, assorted sizes (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add milk, cream, sugar, egg yolks, coffee and salt (optional) and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 2, then blend 5 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7.
  4. Add reserved hazelnuts and mix 4 sec//speed 4. Pour mixture, up to 4 cm high, into shallow freezable containers and cover with plastic wrap. Allow mixture to cool completely, then place into freezer (approx. 10 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place ice cream into refrigerator for 15 minutes, then cut into pieces (4 cm).
  6. Place half of the ice cream pieces into mixing bowl and crush 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer ice cream into a freezable container.
  7. Place remaining ice cream pieces into mixing bowl and crush 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer into freezable container with ice cream and place into freezer until set (approx. 6-8 hours).
  8. Place marshmallows (optional) onto a wooden or metal skewer (see Tips). Using a kitchen blow torch, carefully toast marshmallows all over until golden brown. Serve toasted marshmallows with ice cream.

Useful Items

bowl, shallow freezeable container, plastic wrap, freezable containers (e.g. plastic, silicone), wooden or metal skewer, kitchen blow torch, freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If using wooden skewers in step 8, be sure to soak them in a bowl of water for at least 15 minutes before using to prevent them from burning.
  • Use your leftover egg whites to make homemade marshmallows. Visit the Thermomix Recipe Community (recipecommunity.com.au) for recipes.
