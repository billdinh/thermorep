Steak and kidney pudding

Ingredients

Suet pastry

  • 200 g plain flour
  • 100 g suet mix (see Tips)
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 tsp mustard powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 120 g cold water

Steak and kidney filling

  • 20 g oil
  • 1 brown onion, cut into halves
  • 600 g gravy beef, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g lamb kidneys, cut into quarters and white cores removed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 100 g water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 200 g fresh mushrooms, cut into slices

Assembly

  • butter, for greasing
  • 2200 g water

Preparation

Suet pastry

  1. Place flour, suet mix, thyme, mustard powder, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add water and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and bring dough together, shape into a ball, wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 20 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Steak and kidney filling

  1. Place oil and onion into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add beef, kidneys and carrot and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Add flour, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, stock paste, water, salt and pepper and cook 60 min/90°C//speed .
  4. Add mushrooms and cook 15 min/90°C//speed .

Assembly

  1. Grease a 1L pudding basin (ensure basin fits comfortably into Varoma dish - see Tips) with butter. Roll out three-quarters of the pastry into a circle (30 cm, approx. 5 mm thickness). Line pudding basin with pastry, then trim off edges. Roll out remaining pastry into a circle large enough to cover top of basin. Cover lined basin and pastry lid with plastic wrap, then place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).
  2. Using a slotted spoon, transfer filling into lined basin, leaving a 1 cm gap at the top. Pour gravy over filling, to pastry edge, and reserve remaining gravy for serving. Brush pastry rim with water. Place pastry lid over gravy and firmly press edges together all the way around to seal.
  3. Place a large circle of baking paper loosely over pastry lid. Cover baking paper with a large circle of aluminium foil. Tie a piece of kitchen string loosely around basin to secure paper and foil, allowing space for pastry to expand during cooking. Place basin into Varoma dish.
  4. Place 1800 g of the water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 60 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add remaining 400 g water, secure Varoma lid and steam for a further 60 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and allow pudding to stand for 5 minutes. Carefully remove foil and paper from pudding basin, then invert pudding onto a plate. Cut into wedges and serve with reserved warmed gravy.

Useful Items

pastry brush, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, baking paper, aluminium foil, kitchen string, plastic wrap, slotted spoon, pudding basin (1L), plate, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Suet mix is a combination of beef suet and aerated flour. It's often used in puddings and dumplings, and can be found at most major supermarkets.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Ensure your pudding basin fits comfortably in the Varoma dish; the Varoma lid must close securely.
  • To save time, grease and line your pudding basin with pastry while the filling is cooking. Cover and store in refrigerator until needed.
  • Both pastry and filling can be made well in advanced and either frozen or stored in refrigerator for up to 3 days before assembling and cooking. Alternatively, pudding can be assembled in the basin, then frozen. Place pudding into refrigerator for 24 hours to defrost before cooking as per recipe.
