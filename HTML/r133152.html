Raspberry and Coconut Cake

Ingredients

Coconut Sponge

  • 100 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 100 g coconut oil
  • 180 g caster sugar
  • 300 g self-raising flour
  • 40 g desiccated coconut
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 4 large eggs

Coconut Buttercream Icing

  • 100 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 100 g coconut cream
  • 400 g icing sugar

Assembly and Garnish

  • 250 g raspberry jam
  • 150 g fresh raspberries, for garnish
  • 2 Tbsp flaked coconut, lightly toasted, for garnish

Preparation

Coconut Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease sides and line bases of 2 cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place coconut oil and butter in mixing bowl then melt 3 min/70°C/speed 2.
  3. Add sugar, flour, desiccated coconut, baking powder, salt and eggs then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Transfer to prepared tins. Bake for 30-35 minutes (170°C) until golden brown and a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tins for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make icing.

Coconut Buttercream Icing

  1. Place butter, coconut cream and icing sugar in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4.

Assembly and Garnish

  1. Once sponges are cool, use a bread knife to slice each in half horizontally to create 4 even layers. Place one layer on a serving plate.
  2. Spread half of the raspberry jam over surface of sponge then place another sponge on top. Spread half of the buttercream over surface then repeat with another sponge, a layer of jam, then last sponge, finishing with remaining coconut buttercream.
  3. Decorate with fresh raspberries then sprinkle with toasted flaked coconut. Serve in slices.

Useful Items

baking paper, 2 round cake tins (Ø 20 cm), skewer, wire rack, serving plate or cake stand, bread knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

The exotic flavour of coconut adds a lovely twist to this stunning sandwich cake. With alternating layers of jam and dangerously moreish buttercream, this pretty cake will definitely delight!
