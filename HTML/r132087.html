Onion bhaji

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp ajwain seeds (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves, stalks and roots (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 160 g chickpea (besan) flour
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • ½ tsp baking powder (see Tips)
  • 100 g water
  • 3 brown onions (approx. 500 g), cut into quarters
  • 1000 g oil, for frying

Preparation

  1. Place ajwain seeds and cumin seeds into mixing bowl and roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Place all remaining ingredients, except onions and oil, into mixing bowl and mix 30 sec/speed 8 or until mixture is thick. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into bowl with flour mixture and stir well with spatula until combined.
  4. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large saucepan over high heat and add oil. Heat oil to 170°C (see Tips) and ensure it stays at 170°C throughout cooking (you may need a kitchen thermometer).
  5. Working in batches, carefully drop heaped tablespoons of mixture into hot oil and fry for 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and crispy (approx. 20 bhaji in total). Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove cooked bhaji and transfer onto lined plate. Serve with your favourite condiment (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, large saucepan, large plate, paper towel, kitchen thermometer, slotted spoon, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside of the roots, before adding to the mixing bowl
  • Ajwain seeds are actually the fruit of the ajwain plant, and are also known as ajowan caraway. They have a strong and distinctive thyme-like fragrance; flavourwise, they have a bit of a kick followed by a milder, pleasant aftertaste.
  • To ensure your recipe is gluten free, always check the labels of your products to make sure they don't contain traces of wheat or gluten.
  • To check that your oil is at 170°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into the oil; bread will turn golden brown in 20 seconds at 170°C.
