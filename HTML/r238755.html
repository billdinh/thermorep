Indochine Chicken Salad

Ingredients

  • 80 g peanuts, roasted, unsalted
  • 4 limes, juice only
  • 3 tsp palm sugar
  • 1 fresh chilli, thinly sliced
  • 1 Tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 60 g onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1600 g water, plus extra for soaking
  • 700 g chicken breasts, boneless and skinless, halved on the grain
  • 60 g onions, thinly sliced
  • water, cold, for soaking
  • 200 g dried rice noodles
  • water, hot, for soaking
  • 15 g fresh mint leaves
  • ½ bunch fresh coriander leaves

Preparation

  1. Place peanuts in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl and make dressing.
  2. Stir lime juice, palm sugar, chilli, fish sauce and pepper together in a bowl then set aside and continue with recipe.
  3. Place ginger, 60 g onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add water and heat 10 min/80°C/speed .
  5. Insert simmering basket. Add chicken then cook 25-30 min/80°C/speed . Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Leave chicken to cool, then cover and refrigerate. Empty mixing bowl.
  6. Place 60 g onion slices in a bowl and cover with cold water. Leave to soak in fridge for 15 minutes, then drain.
  7. Soak noodles in hot water until soft and they come apart easily (approx. 10-15 minutes). Drain noodles through Varoma dish, rinse in cold water and drain again. Place drained noodles on a board and cut through a few times with a sharp knife.
  8. Once chicken has cooled, place chicken in mixing bowl and shred 5-7 sec//speed 3. The chicken should be in pieces approx. 6 cm x 1.5 cm. Transfer to a bowl, dress with half the reserved dressing, add reserved onion slices, mint and coriander. Toss lightly to combine and add more dressing to taste.
  9. Arrange noodles on a serving plateand pile chicken salad on top, working the chicken through the noodles with fingers. Scatter with reserved chopped peanuts to serve.

Useful Items

sharp knife, serving plate, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The chicken can be cooked a day in advance and placed in the fridge.
  • Chicken stock can be used to poach the chicken instead of water.
  • Any leftover chicken can be tossed with some dressing for a classic sandwich.
