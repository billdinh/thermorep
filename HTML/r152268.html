Tuna and Olive Empanaditas

Ingredients

  • 500 g water
  • 2 eggs

Tuna Filling

  • 2 pieces lemon zest, no white pith (5 x 1 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 100 grams red peppers, deseeded and cut into eighths
  • 80 g green peppers, deseeded and cut into eighths
  • 200 g tinned chopped tomatoes, drained
  • 80 g pitted Kalamata olives, sliced
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 185 g tinned tuna in oil, drained

Pastry

  • 90 g olive oil
  • 100 g water
  • 40 g dry white wine
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 350 g self-raising flour
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten for brushing

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and place eggs into it. Cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set eggs aside to cool. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

Tuna filling

  1. Place lemon zest and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining filling ingredients, except tuna, and chop 2 sec/speed 5, then cook 10 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  3. Add drained tuna and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer tuna mixture into a large bowl and set aside to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Peel boiled eggs, then place peeled egg into mixing bowl and mix 2 sec//speed 4. Transfer into large bowl with the tuna mixture and stir to combine. Cover mixture and place into refrigerator to cool completely (approx.1-2 hours).

Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place oil, water, wine and salt into mixing bowl and combine 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add flour and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix for a further 4 sec/speed 6.
  4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and gently knead into a ball, then divide dough into half.
  5. Working with one half of the dough at a time, roll out to a 1 mm thickness. Cut dough into 15 circles using a cookie cutter (Ø 10-12 cm), gathering and re-rolling the dough as required. Repeat with remaining half of dough, to make 30 pastry circles in total.
  6. Place 1 tsp of reserved tuna mixture into the middle of each pastry circle, then fold the pastry over to create a half-moon shape. Seal and crimp edges with a fork to enclose the filling.
  7. Brush empanaditas with the lightly beaten egg and bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Serve warm.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking paper, , 2 baking trays, silicone bread mat, , rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Empanaditas can be frozen at the end of step 11 (prior to baking). Remove from the freezer to defrost for a minimum 1 hour prior to baking, then proceed as per recipe.
  • The pastry circles can be pre-made and frozen. Layer the circles between squares of baking paper to separate, then freeze in a freezable container. The tuna filling can be made a day in advance. Simply cover and place into the refrigerator at the end of step 5 until required.
  • Make the most of any leftovers by combining any leftover tuna filling and tinned chopped tomatoes before tossing through fresh pasta.
