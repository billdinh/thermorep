Overnight Oats with Hemp Milk

Ingredients

Hemp Milk

  • 1000 g water
  • 80 g hemp seeds

Overnight Oats

  • 160 g oat flakes
  • 30 g chia seeds
  • 15 g agave syrup
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
  • mixed fresh fruit, for serving
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1000 kJ / 239 kcal
Protein
8 g
Carbohydrates
32 g
Fat
9 g

Preparation

Hemp Milk

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 500 g water and hemp seeds. Cover, then set aside to soak for approx. 8 hours.
  2. Place remaining 500 g water and reserved soaked drained hemp in mixing bowl then grind 1 min/speed 9. Strain mixture through a muslin cloth over a jug, then squeeze out to ensure all liquid is removed. Transfer liquid in jug to a sterilised glass bottle, then set aside in fridge.

Overnight Oats

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 400 g hemp milk, oats, chia seeds, agave syrup and cinnamon. Remove from lid then mix well with spatula. Divide between 4 bowls, cover with cling film and set aside in fridge overnight. Serve with fresh fruit as desired.

Useful Items

cling film, muslin or nut milk bag, jug, sterilised glass bottle, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Hemp is considered a superfood. It is rich in protein and can be eaten as a seed or ground into a powder then added to juices, milkshakes or soups.
  • The hemp milk can be stored in a sterilised glass bottle for 3-4 days in the fridge. Shake before use.
  • To sterilise the bottle, wash in hot water then place in a preheated oven at 100Â°C for at least 10 minutes before filling.
