Portobello Mushroom Rolls

Ingredients

  • 3 fresh portobello mushrooms
  • olive oil, for brushing
  • 1200 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 250 g short grain brown rice, rinsed well
  • 2 Tbsp brown rice vinegar, plus 2 Tbsp for brushing
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 1 spring onion, cut in pieces
  • 80 g tamari, plus 1.5 tsp
  • ½ tsp brown sugar
  • 2 fresh red chillies, halved, deseeded
  • 2 Tbsp black sesame seeds
  • 4 nori seaweed sheets
  • 4 tsp mayonnaise (kewpie)
  • 4 spring onions, sliced in half legnthways

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Place mushrooms on prepared baking tray, with underside facing tray. Brush with oil then brush ½ tsp tamari over each mushroom.
  2. Insert simmering basket. Place water in mixing bowl. Weigh rice into simmering basket then steam 35 min/Varoma/speed 4. Meanwhile, place vinegar, sugar and salt in a small bowl. Stir until grains are dissolved, then set aside. Once oven has reached temperature, roast mushrooms for 10 minutes (200°C).
  3. Remove mushrooms from oven and leave to rest for 10 minutes then slice each into 6 peices. Transfer steamed rice to a large non-metal shallow dish and sprinkle vinegar mixture all over. Using a soft spatula, toss gently to distribute vinegar mixture then leave to cool. Discard steaming water.
  4. Place garlic and ginger in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add spring onion, tamari, sugar and 1 red chili then chop 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a small dish and set aside.
  6. Once rice is cool, wrap a bamboo sushi mat in cling film. Place a sheet of nori, shiny side down onto bamboo sushi mat then gently spread ¼ prepared sushi rice over nori sheet with a wet, soft spatula (see tip), leaving ⅓ clear at the far end. Sprinkle ½ Tbsp black sesame seeds over rice then, very carefully, flip nori sheet over so that it is rice side down on mat and the clear end is nearest to you.
  7. Squeeze a line of mayonnaise (approx. 1 tsp) along edge of nori sheet closest to you. Add a line of mushrooms, spring onions and red chilli. Using bamboo sushi mat, begin rolling tightly to form a roll. Before reaching the other end brush edge of nori with vinegar-water (see tip) to help seal, then continue rolling tightly. Set finished roll aside then continue assembling remaining rolls.
  8. Slice each roll into 1.5 cm pieces using a wet, sharp knife then serve with reserved tamari dipping sauce.

Useful Items

baking tray and baking paper, pastry brush, cling film, sharp knife, shallow bowl or dish, bamboo sushi mat, silicone spatula, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Place 200 g water and 2 Tbsp rice vinegar in a bowl to use for brushing and keeping hands and utensils wet so they don't stick to the rice.
  • Traditionally sushi rice should be body temperature rather than chilled, therefore for optimum quality, sushi is best served immediately or within a few hours of making. Wrap rolls in cling film and leave in a cool dry place then slice just before serving.
  • Sushi can be wrapped in cling film and refrigerated. Consume within 24 hours but be mindful that the quality decreases quickly due to the rice losing moisture.
  • Many people think sushi means raw fish, but it is actually a reference to vinegared rice combined with various ingredients.
