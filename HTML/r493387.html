Thai Green Curry Paste

Ingredients

  • 8 tsp coriander seeds
  • 4 tsp cumin seeds
  • 70 g fresh green chillies, halved (approx. 4)
  • 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 60 g lime juice
  • 2 fresh kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 fresh lemon grass, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 60 g vegetable oil

Preparation

  1. Place coriander and cumin seeds in mixing bowl then toast 5 min/120°C/speed 1. Grind 2 min/speed 10 then scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chillies, ginger, garlic, lime juice, kaffir lime leaves and lemon grass then chop 1 min/speed 10, stopping to scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula as needed.
  3. Add oil then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a glass jar with airtight lid (see tip), leaving approx. 5 mm. Make sure that the rim of the jar is clean and seal. Refrigerate until needed.

Useful Items

glass jar with airtight lid, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To prepare jar, place 500 g water in the mixing bowl. Place jar and lid upside down in the centre of the Varoma and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Leave to drain - do not dry or touch the inside surfaces. Alternatively, thoroughly wash jars and lids in hot, soapy water. Rinse by pouring over boiling water. Prepare for filling, if needed, by placing on a clean tea towel in a roasting tin and heat in the oven at 100˚C until required.
  • Transfer finished paste to an ice cube tray, freeze until solid then pop out once frozen and store in a freezer bag. Remove and defrost cubes as and when needed.
  • For an even spicier paste, add extra chillies to taste in step 2.
