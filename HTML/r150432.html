Hazelnut dukkah with fennel

Ingredients

  • 40 g white sesame seeds
  • 15 g sunflower seeds
  • 20 g almonds
  • 20 g hazelnuts
  • ½ tsp coriander seeds
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 sheets nori sheets, torn into pieces
  • ½ tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place sesame seeds, sunflower seeds, almonds, hazelnuts, coriander seeds, fennel seeds and cumin seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove mixing bowl lid and allow to cool (approx. 30 minutes).
  2. Once cooled, add nori, garlic powder (optional), salt and pepper and chop 8-10 sec/speed 6 or to a chunky yet even consistency. Transfer into a sealable container and store in a cool, dark place until ready to use.

Useful Items

sealable container

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store dukkah in a sealable container in the pantry for up to 3 months.
  • Sprinkle over meat, fish or vegetables before cooking or just before serving. Or serve simply with fresh bread and extra virgin olive oil for dipping.
  • You can use soaked, then dehydrated seeds and nuts in this recipe.
  • Try adding ¼ teaspoon ground turmeric in step 2. Turmeric has potential anti-inflammatory properties and antioxidant properties.
