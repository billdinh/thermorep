Pide with Lamb, Cinnamon, Pine Nuts and Yoghurt

Ingredients

Dough

  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 15 g dried instant yeast
  • 320 g water, warm
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 30 g olive oil

Lamb Filling

  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for brushing and greasing
  • 500 g lamb mince, broken in pieces
  • 50 g toasted pine nuts
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Assembly

  • 100 g water
  • 4 tsp sesame seeds
  • 250 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 Tbsp sumac
  • ½ bunch fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, yeast, olive oil, water and salt in mixing bowl then knead 40 sec/.
  2. Transfer dough to a lightly-oiled bowl and leave to rise until doubled in size (at least 1 hour). Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Tip dough out onto a work surface and shape into a large ball using lightly-floured hands.
  4. Roll dough into a rectangle and divide into 8 equal pieces.
  5. Shape each piece into a smooth ball using lightly-floured hands. Cover with a tea towel and set aside to rest while making filling.

Lamb Filling

  1. Place onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add oil then cook 5 min/120°C/speed . Remove lid and leave to cool for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add lamb, pine nuts, cinnamon, salt and pepper then mix 20 sec//speed .

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. With lightly-floured hands and working with one ball at a time, roll out to form a long oval. Roll across the middle to make it slightly wider without losing the oval shape (approx. 23 cm x 13 cm). Repeat with remaining balls of dough.
  3. Place lamb filling down the centre of each oval of dough flattening it out a little, leaving 3-4 cm at each end.
  4. Fold long edges in, leaving 4 cm uncovered in the centre.
  5. Pinch each short end together and twist into a small ball to resemble a small boat.
  6. Transfer pides to prepared baking tray. Moisten dough edges with water and scatter with sesame seeds. Leave to rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Spoon 2 tsp yoghurt on top of each pide then bake 15-20 minutes (200°C) until puffed and golden.
  8. Remove from oven, brush with extra virgin olive oil and sprinkle with sumac and coriander leaves before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray and baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • When preparing the dough, at the end of step 5 the balls of dough can be covered and refrigerated or frozen for later use.
  • Depending on the size of your oven you may need two trays with 4 pides on each.
  • For a vegetarian option, replace the lamb mince with a similar weight of cooked spinach mixed with ricotta.
