Orange grove beghrir with orange blossom yoghurt

Ingredients

  • 500 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 - 2 tbsp orange blossom water
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 300 g water
  • 130 g fine semolina, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 200 g plain flour
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 200 g milk
  • 1 ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • butter, for frying
  • orange marmalade, to serve

Preparation

  1. Line a fine-mesh sieve with a nut milk bag and place over a bowl. Place bowl with sieve onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt into it. Add orange blossom water and place into refrigerator until mixture thickens (approx. 3 hours).
  2. Place yeast, sugar and water into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add semolina, flour, cream of tartar, salt and milk and mix 5 sec/speed 7. Set mixture aside to prove in mixing bowl for 1 hour.
  4. Add bicarbonate of soda and mix 30 sec/speed 4. Leave mixture to prove in mixing bowl for a further 5 minutes or until it starts to bubble.
  5. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add butter. Grease 4 egg rings with butter and place into frying pan for 1 minute to warm. Sprinkle some semolina into each ring. Fill each ring three-quarters full with batter. Keep heat at medium and cook for 5 minutes or until surface is dry to the touch. Carefully remove rings with tongs, then flip beghrir over and cook for a further 3 minutes (adjust heat as needed to ensure cooking is slow and even). Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a plate and cover to keep warm. Add more butter to pan, re-grease rings as needed and repeat process for remaining batter (makes approx. 10 beghrir). Serve with Orange blossom yoghurt and marmalade.

Useful Items

nut milk bag, bowl, egg rings, non-stick frying pan, fine-mesh sieve, tongs, thermal serving bowl or plate, refrigerator, stove top
