Millet cashew stir-fry

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ tbsp tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp gluten free cornflour
  • 1 lime (zest of half a lime, juice of whole lime)
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 150 g fresh bean sprouts
  • 200 g millet, rinsed and drained
  • 380 g filtered water
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 - 2 fresh long red chilli, trimmed and cut into halves, deseeded if preferred
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 ½ cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 120 g carrot, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 120 g cabbage, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g cashews
  • 1 red onion (approx. 120 g), cut into thin slices
  • 2 capsicums, cut into thin slices
  • 227 g canned water chestnuts, cut into thin slices

Preparation

  1. Place tamari, maple syrup, cornflour, lime zest and juice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and stir to combine. Place thermal serving bowl with sauce onto mixing bowl lid and weigh spinach and bean sprouts into it. Set aside.
  2. Place millet and water into mixing bowl and cook 12-16 min/98°C/speed 1, until water is absorbed. Transfer into the thermal serving bowl with spinach, then stir to combine and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place oil, chilli, garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add carrot and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add cabbage and chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 3 min/120°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  7. Add cashews, onion, capsicum and water chestnuts and stir with aid of spatula, then cook 3 min/120°C//speed 1. Transfer into thermal serving bowl with millet mixture and stir with aid of spatula, then serve.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
