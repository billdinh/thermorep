Cocoa Steamed Buns

Ingredients

Vanilla Dough

  • 110 g water
  • 25 g sugar
  • ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 230 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only
  • 5 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing

Cocoa Dough

  • 120 g water
  • 25 g sugar
  • ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 240 g strong white bread flour
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • 5 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing

For Steaming

  • 500 g water

Preparation

Vanilla Dough

  1. Place water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/37°C/speed 3.
  2. Add flour, salt, baking powder and vanilla seeds then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and knead 3 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place for 1 hour. Meanwhile, prepare cocoa dough.

Cocoa Dough

  1. Place water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/37°C/speed 3.
  2. Add flour, salt, baking powder and cocoa powder then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and knead 3 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place for 1 hour.
  4. Once doughs have risen, tip out onto a lightly floured work surface. Roll each dough into a rectangle (approx. 30 cm x 21 cm).
  5. Lay cocoa dough on top of vanilla dough and roll together tightly lengthways. Cut into 12 pieces with a sharp bread knife (approx. 2.5 cm thick). Place buns in Varoma dish and tray, cover with Varoma lid then set aside for 1 hour.
  6. Place water in mixing bowl and place Varoma into position then steam 27 min/Varoma/speed 2. Allow 2 minutes before removing Varoma lid. Transfer buns to a wire rack and allow to cool before serving.

Useful Items

2 large bowls, cling film, rolling pin, wire rack

Hints & Tricks

  • Chinese steamed buns are also called 'mantou'. Together with other wheat products such as noodles, they are one of the staple foods in China's northern regions. They are usually eaten for breakfast, served as a snack or side dish, or served with soy or rice milk.
  • Best eaten on day they are made.
