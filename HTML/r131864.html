Pizza Marinara

Ingredients

Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 400 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for flouring
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Topping

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 300 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil.
  2. Add remaining 170 g water, flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film then set aside to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours).

Topping

  1. Towards end of rising time, preheat oven to 230°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and mix 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place tomatoes, basil and salt in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 10.
  5. On a floured work surface, roll out risen dough to a circle (approx. Ø 30 cm). Transfer pizza base to prepared baking tray.
  6. Cut pizza into 8 slices with a long sharp knife or pizza wheel. Spread tomato sauce mixture over base and drizzle with reserved garlic mixture. Sprinkle with oregano.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes (230°C) until edges are crisp and golden. Separate pre-cut slices then serve hot.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, large baking tray, baking paper, rolling pin, long sharp knife, oven, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The Marinara Pizza, along with the Pizza Margherita, is one of the most popular Neapolitan pizzas. It owes its name to the fact that the ingredients are easily storable, and therefore could be brought by the sailors to prepare pizzas during their long journeys.
  • Alternatively, three thin crust pizzas can be made: in step 7, divide dough into 3 pieces and, on a floured surface, roll each into a circle (Ø 30 cm and 3 mm thick). Spread fillings on as normal and bake as per recipe.
