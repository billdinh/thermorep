Braised Lamb Shanks with Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Mash - Stinco d'agnello con purè di patate all'aglio e parmigiano

Ingredients

Braised Lamb Shanks

  • 200 g red wine, full-bodied Italian
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 60 g balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g tomato purée
  • 100 g water
  • 1 beef stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 110 g onions, quartered
  • 3 - 4 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 150 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 150 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for browning
  • 4 lamb shanks (approx. 400 g each)
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Mash

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1000 g floury potatoes, peeled, sliced (5 mm)
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • 350 g milk
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

Braised Lamb Shanks

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C.
  2. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh in wine, tomatoes, balsamic vinegar, tomato purée, water, stock cube, rosemary and oregano then set bowl aside.
  3. Place onions, garlic, celery, carrots and 40 g oil in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 0.5. Meanwhile, heat a little oil in a large casserole dish (with lid) over a medium-high heat then season lamb shanks and brown on all sides in the hot oil. Transfer lamb shanks to a plate and set aside.
  4. Transfer cooked vegetables to casserole dish then add reserved liquid ingredients from bowl. Bring to a simmer then add lamb shanks, pushing them down into the sauce, and cover with lid.
  5. Place casserole dish in oven and cook for 2-2½ hours (150°C) until lamb is tender and falling off the bones. With 45 minutes remaining, prepare mashed potatoes in a clean mixing bowl.

Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Mash

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Slice the top off a whole garlic bulb to expose the tops of the cloves. Place on a piece of foil on a baking tray, drizzle with oil and sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Wrap in the foil then roast for 35-40 minutes (150°C) until cloves come out easily when squeezed, then remove and set aside to cool. Meanwhile, cook potatoes.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place potatoes, milk and 1½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, cook 25-30 min/95°C/speed 1.
  4. Place squeezed out garlic flesh from cooled cloves in a bowl and mash with a fork then set aside.
  5. Add butter, reserved Parmesan and mashed garlic cloves to mixing bowl then mash 15 sec/speed 3.5. Season to taste and keep warm until lamb is cooked.

Assembly

  1. Serve lamb shanks and sauce with garlic and Parmesan mash on the side.

Useful Items

large bowl, large casserole dish, aluminium foil, baking tray, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • This meaty main course is a real show-stopper. Cooked in a traditional Italian sauce of red wine, rosemary and balsamic vinegar, lamb shanks simmer slowly in the oven until the meat is tender and falling off the bone. A batch of extra special Thermomix® mashed potatoes, containing salty Parmesan and a slow-roasted whole garlic bulb, makes an indulgent side dish and a perfect accompaniment to the lamb.
