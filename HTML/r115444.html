Sausage rolls

Ingredients

Puff pastry

  • 300 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces (2.5 cm) and frozen
  • 140 g potato starch
  • 140 g gluten free cornflour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g brown rice flour (see Tips)
  • 50 g millet flour (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp xanthan gum
  • ¼ tsp guar gum (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 180 g chilled water

Filling

  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 250 g pork mince
  • 500 g beef mince
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 50 g water
  • 30 g tomato sauce (gluten free), plus extra to serve (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (gluten free)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce (gluten free) (see Tips) (optional)
  • arrowroot flour, for dusting
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • sesame seeds, black or white, for sprinkling (optional)

Preparation

Puff pastry

  1. Place all puff pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and knead 20-30 sec/, or until the butter is visible in small chunks within the pastry dough.
  2. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or work surface dusted with extra cornflour and, with floured fingertips, gently shape into a rectangle.
  3. With the shorter end of the dough closest to you, roll dough into a long rectangle (approx. 60 x 20 cm), dusting pastry and rolling pin with more cornflour as needed, and lifting dough off work surface to ensure dough doesn't stick.
  4. Once rolled, start folding pastry over itself into a total of 6 folds (each fold approx. 10 cm wide). Wrap pastry in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to rest for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place onion and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 5 minutes.
  4. Add pork mince, beef mince, vinegar, water, tomato sauce, mustard, salt, pepper and Worcestershire sauce (optional) and combine 20 sec//speed 3, with aid of spatula.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then combine 20 sec//speed 3.
  6. Divide puff pastry into 3 equal portions and transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface.
  7. Roll out 1 portion of pastry at a time (22 x 30 cm, 5 mm thickness). Cut each pastry portion into halves horizontally. Brush with beaten egg.
  8. Place 1/6 of the meat mixture along long edge of 1 pastry portion and roll to enclose, placing the pastry seam underneath. Cut into 5 cm lengths and place onto prepared baking tray. Repeat with remaining mixture and pastry sheets (to make 36 rolls total). Place into refrigerator for 10 minutes. Brush pastry with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds (optional).
  9. Bake for 20-25 minutes (220°C) until golden and cooked through. Allow to cool slightly before serving warm with tomato sauce.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, rolling pin, plastic wrap, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own rice flours, place rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10. Use as needed.
  • You can buy millet flour and guar gum at health food stores.
  • To make your own tomato sauce, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook.
  • To make your own gluten free Worcestershire sauce, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
