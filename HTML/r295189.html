Bagels with Egg and Bacon Jam

Ingredients

Bagels

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 920 g water, plus 1 Tbsp extra for brushing
  • 250 g milk
  • 3 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 520 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 Tbsp sesame seeds, for sprinkling (optional)

Bacon Jam

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 200 g red onions, quartered
  • 500 g smoked bacon, cut in pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp orange zest, finely grated
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • 1 star anise
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 150 g coffee, brewed
  • 60 g cider vinegar
  • 60 g orange juice
  • 40 g bourbon whisky

Eggs

  • 4 medium eggs (depending on the number of portions)
  • 500 g water

Preparation

Bagels

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place 120 g water, milk, yeast and sugar in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/50°C/speed 2.
  3. Add flour and salt then knead 4 min/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with a tea towel and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2 hours).
  4. Transfer risen dough onto a floured work surface. With lightly-oiled hands, divide dough into 12 equal-sized portions, then shape each portion into a ball. Press your thumb through the middle of each ball to form a hole and create a bagel shape. Cover bagels with a tea towel and leave to rise again in a warm place for 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 220°C.
  6. Place remaining 800 g water in mixing bowl and heat 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, line Varoma tray with a piece of wet and well-wrung baking paper and arrange 4 bagels on it. Insert Varoma tray in Varoma dish.
  7. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma, transfer steamed bagels to prepared baking tray and arrange 4 further bagels on Varoma tray.
  8. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma, transfer steamed bagels to prepared baking tray and arrange remaining 4 bagels on Varoma tray.
  9. Place covered Varoma into position and steam 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove Varoma and transfer steamed bagels to prepared baking tray. Empty mixing bowl.
  10. Place egg yolk and 1 Tbsp water in a small bowl and lightly beat. Brush bagels with egg wash, then sprinkle with sesame seeds (if using) and bake for 8-10 minutes (220°C) or until golden. Meanwhile, make bacon jam.

Bacon Jam

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onions and bacon then, without measuring cup, sauté 10 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  3. Add Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, orange zest, thyme, ground ginger, star anise, pepper, coffee, vinegar and orange juice then, without measuring cup, cook 40 min/100°C//speed 1.
  4. Add whiskey and, without measuring cup, cook 80 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer to an airtight jar, leave to cool, then store in fridge until ready to use (jam will thicken as it cools).

Eggs

  1. Insert Varoma tray into Varoma dish. Line Varoma tray with a piece of wet and well-wrung baking paper, ensuring paper goes all the way to the edges.
  2. Grease 4 egg rings with oil, arrange on prepared Varoma tray and crack 1 egg into each ring.
  3. Place water in mixing bowl, place covered Varoma into position and steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until eggs are cooked. Set Varoma aside.
  4. To serve, slice bagels in half horizontally, spread with bacon jam and top with a cooked egg.

Useful Items

tea towel, baking paper, egg rings, baking tray, pastry brush, airtight jar, large bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe can serve up to 12 people at once. Simply repeat step 14 and cook as many eggs as you require. Serve any leftover bagels within 2 days.
  • Bacon jam and bagels can easily be prepared ahead of time for a quick and easy weekend breakfast.
  • Bacon jam is a perfect condiment on toasted cheese sandwiches, burgers, cheese platters or even pancakes.
