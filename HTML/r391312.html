Spiced chocolate doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 150 g butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tbsp raw sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 200 g almonds (activated or roasted)
  • 200 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), broken into pieces
  • 150 g Rapadura sugar (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp gluten free baking powder
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease 2 x 12-hole doughnut trays and set aside.
  2. Place raw sugar and 1 teaspoon of the cinnamon into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8 or until finely ground. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add butter and melt 2 min 30 sec/80°C/speed 1.
  6. Add Rapadura sugar, baking powder, eggs, vanilla, cardamom, remaining 1 teaspoon cinnamon and reserved milled almonds and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  8. Transfer mixture into a piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm). Pipe mixture into prepared doughnut trays, then bake for 15 minutes (160°C) or until just cooked. Allow to cool in tray for 15 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Dust doughnuts with reserved cinnamon sugar before serving (see Tips).

Useful Items

12-hole doughnut trays, bowl, wire rack, piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because these doughnuts include some added sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before serving.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small.
  • You can replace the Rapadura sugar with coconut sugar in this recipe.
  • You can serve the doughnuts without the cinnamon sugar, simply omit milling the sugar and reduce the cinnamon to 1 teaspoon.
