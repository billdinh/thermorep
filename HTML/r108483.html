Lemon and elderflower ombre cake

Ingredients

Light Sponges

  • 225 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • ½ lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tbsp elderflower cordial
  • 225 g self-raising flour
  • 3 pinches ground turmeric

Darker sponges

  • 225 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • ½ lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tbsp elderflower cordial
  • 225 g self-raising flour
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric

Lemon butter filling

  • 40 g lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 70 g caster sugar
  • 80 g unsalted butter
  • 2 eggs

Butter icing

  • 500 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 700 g icing sugar
  • 1 tbsp pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 tsp elderflower cordial
  • 1 ¼ tsp ground turmeric

Preparation

Light Sponges

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line 2 round cake tins (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and chop 8 sec/speed 8.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Beat 5 min/speed 3, then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3, adding eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of the elderflower cordial and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  6. Add flour and mix 40 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 30 sec/speed 1. Transfer half of the mixture (approx. 430 g) into 1 of the prepared tins and set aside.
  8. Add 3 pinches turmeric into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 1. Transfer mixture into remaining prepared tin. Bake both cakes for 25–30 minutes (160°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cakes comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool for 5 minutes in tins, then turn out onto a wire rack. Clean and dry mixing bowl, butterfly whisk and cake tins.
  9. Brush lightest coloured sponge with remaining 1 tablespoon elderflower cordial and set aside.

Darker sponges

  1. Grease and line 2 round tins (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place lemon zest and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and chop 8 sec/speed 8.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Beat 5 min/speed 3, then mix 1 min 30 sec/speed 3, adding eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of the elderflower cordial and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  6. Add flour and mix 40 sec/speed 1. Remove butterfy whisk.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 30 sec/speed 1. Transfer mixture into 2 bowls (approx. 430 g each). Add ½ teaspoon turmeric into the first bowl and stir to combine. Add remaining 1½ teaspoons turmeric into the second bowl and stir to combine.
  8. Transfer each bowl of batter into prepared tins and bake both cakes for 25–30 minutes (160°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cakes comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool for 5 minutes in tins, then turn out onto a wire rack. Clean and dry mixing bowl, butterfly whisk and cake tins.
  9. Brush lighter coloured sponge with remaining 1 tablespoon elderflower cordial and set aside.

Lemon butter filling

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place lemon juice, caster sugar, butter and eggs into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 3.
  2. Test to see if setting point has been reached by inserting a teaspoon; mixture should thickly coat the back of the spoon. If filling is not set, cook until mixture thickens. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Transfer into a sealable jar and allow to cool to room temperature, then place into refrigerator to cool completely and set. Clean and dry mixing bowl, 2 bowls and butterfly whisk.

Butter icing

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add icing sugar and blend 1 min/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add remaining ingredients, except turmeric, and mix 3 min/speed 3, or until light and fluffy. Divide icing between 2 bowls. Add turmeric to one of the bowls and stir to combine. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Spread a little of the coloured icing onto a cake board or serving plate. Place the darkest layer of cake on top of icing (this will help secure it to the cake board).
  2. Fill a piping bag fitted with a 10 mm nozzle with remaining coloured icing. Pipe a thin line of icing around edge of cake.
  3. Spread half of the lemon butter filling inside the circle of icing.
  4. Place the next darkest layer of cake on top of lemon butter filling. Pipe a small amount of coloured icing onto cake and spread into a thin layer.
  5. Place the darker of the two remaining cake layers over icing and pipe a thin line of coloured icing around the edge of cake. Spread remaining half of lemon butter filling inside the circle of icing.
  6. Place remaining cake layer (this should be the lightest coloured layer) on top of lemon butter filling. Place cake into refrigerator for 45 minutes or until frosting is set.
  7. Using a palette knife, spread the white frosting over top and sides of the top 2 layers of cake.
  8. Pipe coloured frosting around the bottom layer of cake. Using the palette knife, spread icing upwards to cover the bottom layer of cake.
  9. Using the palette knife, blend the white and coloured icing together over the middle layer of cake to create an ombre effect. Place into refrigerator for approx. 30 minutes to set icing, then slice and serve.

Useful Items

round cake tins (20 cm), baking paper, wooden skewer, wire rack, pastry brush, bowls, sealable jar or bottle, piping bag (10 mm nozzle), palette knife, cake board or serving plate, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • We used ground turmeric in this recipe as a natural food colouring. You can use other types of foods to add colour (such as raspberry or blackberry juice, etc.) or you can purchase natural food colouring in the baking aisle of your supermarket. Add food colouring to your preference to create the desired layers of colour.
  • A palette knife has a blunt, rounded, flexible blade and is ideal for frosting cakes. You can also use an off-set palette knife, which has an angled blade to make frosting cakes easier.
