Pasta Alfredo with mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1120 g filtered water
  • 250 g dried fettuccine (egg free - see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • ½ brown onion (approx. 90 g)
  • 40 g pepitas (see Tips)
  • 20 g nutritional yeast (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp tahini (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp dried basil
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp sorghum flour
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 350 g plant-based milk of choice
  • 200 g fresh brown mushrooms, cut into thin slices (5 mm)
  • 2 tbsp vegan Parmesan cheese (see Tips)
  • fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, chopped, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh spinach into it, then set aside.
  2. Place 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add pasta and 1 teaspoon of the salt and 1 teaspoon of the oil and cook 8-10 min/98°C//speed 1, until cooked to your liking. Using simmering basket, drain pasta, then transfer into thermal serving bowl with spinach and cover to keep warm.
  4. Place garlic, onion and remaining 1 teaspoon oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add pepitas and 60 g of the water and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2.
  6. Add nutritional yeast, tahini, thyme, basil, lemon juice, flour, pepper, milk, remaining ½ tsp salt and remaining 60 g water and blend 40 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add mushrooms and cook 4-6 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer sauce into thermal serving bowl with pasta and stir to combine. Garnish with cheese and parsley to serve.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the pepitas with 3-4 tablespoons of cashews, if desired.
  • With a cheesy, umami flavour, nutritional yeast contains all nine essential amino acids, is a great source of B vitamins and is often fortified with vitamin B12 (important for those following a vegan diet). You can find nutritional yeast at health food stores or online.
  • To make your own tahini, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • To make your own vegan Parmesan cheese, refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can replace the pasta with raw or blanched vegetable zoodles, if desired.
