Chocolate mint thins

Ingredients

Wafer

  • 85 g spelt grain
  • 110 g raw sugar
  • 250 g unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 85 g cocoa powder
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 180 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting

Mint layer

  • 260 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 25 g unsalted butter
  • 25 g evaporated milk
  • ¼ tsp peppermint oil (food grade)

Chocolate

  • 1000 g dark chocolate chips
  • 3 - 5 drops peppermint oil (food grade), to taste (optional)

Preparation

Wafer

  1. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place spelt or wheat grain and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 9.
  3. Add butter, vanilla, cocoa and salt and mix 6 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add flour and knead 1 min/, or until dough has come together. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface and knead into a smooth disc shape. Wrap dough in silicone mat or plastic wrap and place into the freezer to chill (approx. 1 hour).
  6. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  7. Once dough has chilled, roll out between silicone bread mat and a sheet of baking paper, or between two sheets of baking paper, to 0.5 cm thickness. Then, using a cooking cutter (5 cm), cut dough into approx. 45 rounds and place onto prepared baking trays.
  8. Bake for 8 minutes (180°C). Transfer biscuits onto a wire rack and allow to cool completely.

Mint layer

  1. Place all mint layer ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 30 sec/.
  2. Lightly dust silicone bread mat or work surface with extra icing sugar, then transfer dough onto it. Shape dough into a thin log (15 cm long, 2 cm wide). Wrap dough in baking paper and place into freezer for 1 hour. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Line 2 freezable containers or trays with baking paper, then spread cooled wafers out onto them.
  4. Remove mint thin dough from freezer and set aside for 2-3 minutes to soften slightly. Once softened, cut into slices (2 mm thick) and place 1 slice of the mint layer on top of each wafer. Transfer wafers back into freezable containers or onto trays, then place back into freezer for 30 minutes.

Chocolate

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/50°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add peppermint oil to taste, then melt for a further 3 min/50°C/speed 1, or until chocolate has fully melted. Transfer melted chocolate into a ceramic or glass bowl.
  3. Remove wafers from freezer, then working as quickly as possible so the chocolate doesn't harden, use 2 forks to gently dip each wafer into the chocolate.
  4. Place coated wafers back into containers or onto trays, then place into refrigerator for 2 hours to set.
  5. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable container and place in refrigerator to store until ready to serve.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, round cookie cutter (5 cm), 2 freezable containers or trays, ceramic or glass bowl, wire rack, freezer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • After dinner mints: omit the wafer component of the recipe and reduce dark chocolate chips in step 13 to 350-400 g. Proceed as per recipe.
