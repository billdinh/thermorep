Almond Pavlova with Berries and Chambord

Ingredients

Pavlova

  • 12 oz sugar, divided
  • 8 egg whites, room temperature
  • &frac18; tsp cream of tartar
  • 2 tbsp corn starch
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • ½ tsp almond extract
  • 1 oz sliced almonds

Sauce

  • 1 vanilla bean, cut into pieces
  • 1 oz sugar
  • 7 oz raspberry liqueur, type Chambord
  • 12 oz fresh mixed berries, prepared, halved or quartered
Nutrition
per 1 portion

920 kJ / 220 kcal

3 g

Preparation

Pavlova

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place 6 oz sugar into mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 6 oz sugar into mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to bowl with ground sugar and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and cream of tartar into mixing bowl and whisk 5 min/100°F/speed 3.5.
  5. Without measuring cup, whisk a further 5 min/100°F/speed 3.5 while slowly adding reserved ground sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add corn starch, vinegar and almond extract through hole in mixing bowl lid and mix 40 sec/speed 3.5.
  7. Remove butterfly whisk. With spatula, transfer meringue mixture onto prepared baking sheet and spread out evenly into a rectangular shape. Run a skewer through the meringue to create swirls. Sprinkle almonds around edge. Bake for 10 minutes (300°F). Reduce temperature to 250°F and bake a further 60 minutes (250°F). Turn off oven and without opening oven door allow Pavlova to cool (approx. 2 hrs.). Meanwhile clean mixing bowl.

Sauce

  1. Place vanilla bean and sugar into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add liqueur and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and let cool. Once cooled, strain over a bowl through a fine sieve. Discard solids.
  3. Just before serving, top cooled pavlova with mixed berries, drizzle with reserved sauce and serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, skewer, fine sieve, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Fat and moisture will prevent egg whites from whipping properly. Use a little vinegar to remove and clean the mixing bowl, blade and whisk. Dry thoroughly with a paper towel.
