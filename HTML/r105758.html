Bee sting cake

Ingredients

Filling

  • 130 g water
  • 1 tbsp gelatine powder
  • 500 g full cream milk
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 50 g cornflour
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract

Dough

  • 50 g canola oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tbsp breadcrumbs
  • 130 g smooth ricotta cheese, well drained
  • 50 g full cream milk
  • 1 egg
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 2 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 150 g white sugar
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 30 g apricot jam
  • 200 g flaked almonds
  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Sprinkle gelatine over water, then heat 1 min 30 sec/60°C/speed 1, until gelatine has dissolved. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place milk, sugar, cornflour and vanilla into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add dissolved gelatine and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer custard mixture into a bowl and place a piece of plastic wrap onto surface to prevent a skin from forming. Place into refrigerator for 1 hour, or until completely chilled and set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Dough

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a springform cake tin (26 cm) and sprinkle base with breadcrumbs.
  2. Place ricotta, milk, egg, flour, baking powder, salt, oil and 100g of the sugar into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Transfer dough into prepared tin and, with damp fingers, press down lightly to evenly press dough into base of tin. Set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place vanilla, butter, apricot jam and remaining 50 g of the sugar into mixing bowl and cook 3 min/60°C/speed 2, then purée 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with a spatula.
  5. Add almonds and combine 8 sec//speed 1. Spread almond mixture over top of dough in cake tin. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C). Carefully run a knife around edge of tin, then allow cake to cool completely in tin (approx. 40 minutes). Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Carefully remove cake from tin and cut lengthways into 2 equal halves. Place bottom half of cake back into tin. Leave top half of cake on a wire rack and set aside.
  7. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip 50 sec/speed 3 or until stiff peaks form, taking care not to overwhip cream.
  8. Add reserved custard filling and mix 1 min/speed 2, or until combined.
  9. Transfer filling onto bottom half of reserved cake in tin, then place reserved cake top onto custard filling (see Tips) and press down gently. Cover cake with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 4 hours or until set. Cut into 12 slices and serve.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, springform cake tin (26 cm), wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The top half of cake can be difficult to cut once set. You can pre-cut the top half of cake into 12 pieces before placing them onto the filling mixture. This will assist you when slicing the whole cake.
