Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces
  • 1 oz pecorino cheese (optional)
  • 1 shallot (approx. 1 oz)
  • 5 ½ oz bacon, cut into pieces (¼ in.)
  • 50 oz water
  • ¾ tsp salt, divided
  • 16 oz spaghetti
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk, from large egg
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan and pecorino cheeses into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place shallot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add bacon. Place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and sauté 20 min/250°F/speed 1. Transfer into a large serving bowl and keep warm.
  4. Place water and ½ tsp salt into mixing bowl and bring to a boil 10 min/212°F/speed 1.
  5. Add spaghetti through hole in mixing bowl lid and cook without measuring cup for time indicated on package/212°F//speed 1 or until al dente. Drain spaghetti through Varoma dish and transfer into serving bowl with bacon. Mix well and keep warm.
  6. Before pasta has a chance to cool, place eggs, egg yolk, reserved grated cheese, ¼ tsp salt, black pepper and olive oil into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Add egg mixture into serving bowl with pasta and bacon. Combine thoroughly with spatula and serve immediately.

ovenproof dish for serving

Hints & Tricks

  • If water bubbles into mixing bowl lid while pasta is cooking, add 1 tsp oil or butter to the cooking water to reduce frothing.
  • Pasta that is cooked al dente is tender but still firm to the bite.
  • The heat of the cooked pasta will partially cook and thicken the egg, so it is important the pasta is still hot when adding the egg mixture to the serving bowl.
  • To ensure eggs do not overcook and sauce is creamy, rinse the mixing bowl in cold water to cool it off, after draining the spaghetti, before adding eggs.
