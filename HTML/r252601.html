Artichoke Tart - Torta di carciofi

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 50 g milk
  • 1 egg white, lightly beaten

Filling

  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g frozen spinach, defrosted, drained
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 2 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 250 g double cream
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 480 g tinned artichoke hearts, large, drained, halved (drained weight)
  • 100 g large black olives, pitted

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place flour, butter and salt in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add milk and pulse Turbo/1 sec/2-4 times until just clinging together. Tip out, wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 30-40 minutes.
  4. Once pastry has rested, roll out on a lightly floured surface into a large circle (3 mm thick) then place in a loose-bottomed tart tin (Ø 25 cm). Chill in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 160°C.
  5. Line pastry case with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 15 minutes (160°C). Remove baking paper and beans then return to oven for 10 minutes (160°C).
  6. Brush beaten egg white all over part-baked pastry case, then bake for a further 5 minutes (160°C). Remove from oven, increase temperature to 200°C and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place onions, butter and garlic in mixing bowl, chop 3 sec/speed 5 then sauté 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add spinach, eggs, egg yolks, cream, 60 g reserved Parmesan, salt and pepper then mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  3. Pour mixture into pastry case then arrange artichoke halves in two concentric circles. Dot olives around artichokes then sprinkle with remaining grated Parmesan.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes (200°C) until pastry is golden, filling is set and artichokes are lightly browned. Serve warm or cold with a dressed salad.

Useful Items

cling film, loose-bottomed tart tin (Ø 25 cm), baking paper, baking beans, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Artichokes are a quintessentially Italian ingredient, particularly in Rome where they are in season in the spring. During this time artichokes take over restaurant menus, often prepared in one of two ways; carciofi alla giudìa, deep-fried, or carciofi alla romana, stuffed and braised. Here artichokes are combined with flavourful olives to make a beautiful rustic tart which would make a stunning centrepiece for lunch or dinner.
  • To make this recipe suitable for vegetarians, substitute the Parmesan cheese for a vegetarian hard cheese.
