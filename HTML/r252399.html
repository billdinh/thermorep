Cajun chicken schnitzel and slaw

Ingredients

Cajun spice

  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tsp onion salt
  • 2 tsp sweet paprika

Slaw

  • 1 carrot (approx. 80 g), cut into pieces
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 15 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves and stalks only, cut into halves
  • 250 g cabbage, cut into pieces
  • 80 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper

Chicken schnitzel

  • 100 g panko breadcrumbs
  • salt, to season
  • ground black pepper, to season
  • 80 g plain flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • 3 chicken breasts
  • oil, for frying

Preparation

Cajun spice

  1. Place all spice mix ingredients into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Slaw

  1. Place all slaw ingredients and ¼-½ teaspoon of the reserved Cajun spice into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 2 sec/speed 4 or to desired consistency. Transfer into a sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Chicken schnitzel

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place a shallow bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh panko crumbs into it, then add salt and pepper. Set bowl aside. Place another shallow bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour into it. Add 2-3 tablespoons of reserved Cajun spice, stir to combine and set aside. In a third shallow bowl, place eggs and milk and whisk to combine.
  3. Cut each chicken breast in half horizontally. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, flatten out each chicken portion (approx. 15 cm). Dip each piece into bowl with flour mixture and dust off excess flour. Then dip into egg wash, then into panko crumbs, gently patting each side to coat. Place onto a large plate.
  4. Place a large frying pan over high heat and add oil (1-2 cm high). Working in batches, place coated chicken pieces into pan and fry for 1-2 minutes each side, until golden. Transfer onto prepared baking tray. Place into oven and bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C) or until chicken is cooked through. Serve with reserved slaw.

Useful Items

sealable jar or container, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, large frying pan, whisk, large shallow bowl, large plate, meat mallet, sealable container, oven, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own mayonnaise, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook.
  • You can store the leftover Cajun spice in the pantry for up to 6 months. Use spice mix to season fish or pork, or sprinkle over scrambled eggs.
