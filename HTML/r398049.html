Pear Apricot Roses

Ingredients

  • non-stick cooking spray
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, cubed
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 8 oz dried apricots, pitted
  • 8 oz sugar
  • 1 lemon, thin peel only, no pith, plus 1 oz lemon juice
  • 2 oz water
  • 2 pears, quartered
  • 1 package puff pastry, thawed per pkg. instructions
  • all-purpose flour, to dust
  • confectioners sugar, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Spray a 12 hole muffin pan with non-stick cooking spray.
  2. Place butter, cinnamon and nutmeg into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/170°F/speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place apricots, sugar and lemon peel into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add lemon juice and water and cook 20 min/212°F/speed 1 then blend 40 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl, set aside and let cool to room temperature. Reserve half the cooled jam mixture for the recipe and transfer remaining jam to an airtight jar and refrigerate for another use.
  4. With a vegetable peeler, slice pears lengthwise into thin strips, transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Dust a large piece of parchment with flour. Transfer thawed puff pastry to prepared parchment and unroll. With a rolling pin, roll pastry to a ⅛ inch thickness. Cut into six strips (approx. 2 in. x 10 in.) Brush with reserved butter mixture, then with reserved apricot jam and top with a layer of sliced pears. Roll up the filled pastry and transfer each piece into a muffin hole with cut sides facing up. Repeat with remaining pastry creating 12 rosettes. Bake for 35-40 minutes (400°F) or until golden brown. Dust with confectioners sugar and serve.

Useful Items

vegetable peeler, 12 hole muffin pan, parchment paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Thaw and store puff pastry in refrigerator in its original sealed package: when you come home from the store, refrigerate puff pastry instead of freezing.
  • Thaw and use at room temperature: allow puff pastry to thaw in packaging at room temperature according to package directions. Do not open until all other ingredients are assembled and you are ready to work.
