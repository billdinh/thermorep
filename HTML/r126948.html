Stuffed veggie snails

Ingredients

  • 20 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 pinch fine salt
  • 2 carrots, peeled
  • 500 g water
  • 100 g fresh baby spinach, washed and dried
  • 4 slices cooked ham
  • 20 fresh rosemary leaves
  • 10 canned kidney beans
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
243 kJ / 58 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
2.5 g
Fat
3.2 g

Preparation

  1. Line Varoma tray with a sheet of baking paper that has been moistened and squeezed, making sure that some holes remain unobstructed.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl, grate 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer grated cheese into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Break egg into bowl with grated Parmesan, mix with a pinch of salt and beat with a fork
  4. Pour egg mixture into Varoma tray prepared earlier and set aside. Place carrots into Varoma dish.
  5. Place water into mixing bowl, place simmering basket into position and arrange spinach inside. Place Varoma into position and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. When cooking time has ended, remove lid of the Varoma, turn over and place Varoma tray on top of lid.
  6. Transfer omelette to a flat plate, top with ham and squeezed spinach, roll up and cut into slices (2-3 cm).
  7. Cut carrots into flat and square strips as wide as the omelette slices (2-3 cm).
  8. Place carrot strips on the plate and arrange omelette slices on top.
  9. Insert 2 rosemary leaves into each bean to create the antennae. Position the bean on the carrot to create the head of the snail.
  10. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking paper, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • To make the presentation on the plate more appealing and playful, you can create scenes out of vegetables: hedges made of salad leaves, trees created with broccoli florets, flowers made from carrot discs - let your imagination run wild!
