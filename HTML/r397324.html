Cauliflower chicken nuggets

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm), plus extra shavings to serve
  • 100 g quinoa flakes
  • 350 g cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (optional - see Tips)
  • 400 g skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 60 g gluten free plain flour (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 - 3 tbsp milk of choice
  • extra virgin olive oil, for shallow frying

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 20 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add quinoa flakes and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a shallow bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cauliflower and parsley (optional) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add chicken, tamari and tomato paste and blend 7 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Shape heaped tablespoons of mixture into nuggets (approx. 20 in total) and transfer onto a plate. Set aside (see Tips).
  6. Place a shallow bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flour into it. Combine egg and milk in a separate shallow bowl. Coat nuggets in flour, then egg mixture, then reserved quinoa crumb. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to fry.
  7. Place a frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Working in batches, fry nuggets for about 6-8 minutes turning occasionally, until golden brown on all sides and cooked through. Serve topped with Parmesan shavings (see Tips).

Useful Items

shallow bowl, plate, frying pan, plastic wrap, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • If your child protests at anything green, you can reduce or omit the parsley leaves and try reintroducing them at a later stage. For a child who’s happy with flecks of green, try using different herbs from time to time, such as coriander or chives.
  • If you don’t have time to do a three-layered crumb, you can just go straight to the quinoa crumb and skip the flour and egg mixture.
  • If gluten free is not needed, you can replace the gluten free plain flour with plain flour in this recipe.
  • Add a little sea salt to taste for adults and older children. However, there is salt in the tamari and parmesan, so go easy.
  • Your child might like these served with a homemade mayonnaise, avocado purée or tomato salsa. Feel free to use whichever is their preferred dip.
