Lebanese Spiced Flatbread

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast (8 g)
  • 200 g water
  • 85 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 1 ¼ tsp salt
  • 4 tsp zaatar (see tip)
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
437 kJ / 104 kcal
Protein
2 g
Carbohydrates
11 g

6 g

Preparation

  1. Place dried instant yeast, water and 55 g olive oil in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add plain flour and ½ tsp salt then knead 1 min 30 sec/. Meanwhile lightly oil a large bowl and spatula and set aside.
  3. With the aid of oiled spatula transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover bowl with cling film and refrigerate for a minimum of 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Line a baking tray with baking paper and grease with 2 tbsp olive oil. Transfer dough to prepared baking tray. With oiled hands, form dough into a round shape. Cover dough with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 minutes).
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  6. Without kneading the dough any further, spread it out with oiled fingertips into a rectangle (approx. 26 x 30 cm) and set aside.
  7. Place a small bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh 30 g olive oil in it. Remove bowl, add zaatar and remaining ¾ tsp salt to olive oil and mix together. Brush the flatbread with zaatar-olive oil mixture and allow to rise for further 15 minutes.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Serve warm, cut in squares.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray and paper, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Manaeesh or manakeesh is a popular Levantine food consisting of dough topped with thyme, cheese or minced lamb. Similar to a pizza it can be sliced or folded, and it can either be served for breakfast or lunch. The most popular form of manaeesh uses zaatar as a topping. The zaatar is mixed with olive oil and spread onto the bread before baking it in the oven. It is a popular breakfast in Levantine cuisine.
  • Zaatar is an aromatic Middle Eastern seasoning mixture that usually contains ground dried thyme, oregano, marjoram, mixed with toasted sesame seeds, and salt. It's a very versatile flavouring, and can be added to rice dishes, or rubbed into meat and fish before grilling, as well as vegetable dishes.
  • To make your own zaatar at home mix in a small bowl 2 tbsp dried thyme, 1 tbsp oregano, 2 tsp sumac, 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds and ½ tsp salt. Add a ¼ tsp of dried chilli if you like some heat.
  • This yoghurt sauce complements the flatbread very well: mix in a bowl 100 g Greek yoghurt, juice of ½ of a lemon, 8 chopped mint leaves, ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp ground pepper.
