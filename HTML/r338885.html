Fish with Mixed Pepper Couscous

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (approx. 700-800 g)
  • 1 tsp harissa sauce (see tip)
  • 2 tsp ras el hanout (see tip)
  • 2 tsp lemon-salt paste (see tip)
  • 1 ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 ½ Tbsp olive oil
  • 200 g red peppers, quartered
  • 250 g yellow peppers, quartered
  • 200 g green peppers, quartered
  • 250 g couscous
  • 290 g water
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 250 g liquid fish stock
  • 200 g red peppers, cut in large pieces
  • ¼ tsp brown sugar
  • 4 Tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Preparation

  1. Lay fish fillets in a large dish in a single layer. Mix harissa, 1 tsp ras el hanout, 1 tsp lemon-salt paste, ½ tsp salt and 1 Tbsp oil, then coat fish fillets, cover with cling film and refrigerate.
  2. Place 200 g red peppers, yellow peppers and green peppers in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer chopped peppers to Varoma dish placed on Varoma lid to catch any juices.
  3. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in couscous and water. Remove bowl, add 1½ Tbsp oil, 1 tsp ras al hanout, ½ tsp salt and pepper, stir then leave to soak. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  4. Place fish stock and 1 tsp lemon-salt paste in mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and weigh in 200 g red peppers. Place covered Varoma (discarding any juices collected in lid) into position then steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, crumple 2-4 sheets baking paper (depending on number of fish fillets), twist together at the ends and shape into boats. Remove Varoma, then add steamed peppers from Varoma to bowl with couscous and mix. Distribute couscous mixture in Varoma dish and leave a hole in the centre for steam to circulate.
  5. Dab some marinade off the fish fillets with paper towels. Place fillets in prepared paper boats, seal them and place on Varoma tray. Insert Varoma tray into Varoma dish, place covered Varoma into position and steam 7 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Transfer fish stock from mixing bowl to a jug.
  6. Place 150 g fish stock from jug, red peppers from simmering basket, ¼ tsp salt and sugar to mixing bowl then purée 30 sec/speed 7.
  7. Arrange couscous mixture and fish fillets on serving plates, pour over a little pepper sauce and serve sprinkled with chopped coriander.

Useful Items

pastry brush, paper towels, baking paper, jug, cling film, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Red mullet has thick bones, which can be easily removed with tweezers before cooking.
  • Ras el hanout is a spice blend of coriander seeds, cinnamon, cloves, ginger, cardamom and a few other spices. A recipe for this can be found in the collection Moroccan Adventure.
  • Harissa is a spicy paste made from chilli, cumin, coriander seeds, garlic, salt and oil. A recipe for this can also be found in the collection Moroccan Adventure.
  • Lemon-salt paste is a great way to preserve lemons and can be used anywhere lemon peel or juice and a pinch of salt is required.
