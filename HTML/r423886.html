Lentil, Mushroom and Nut Patties

Ingredients

  • 25 g wholemeal bread, torn in pieces
  • 50 g walnut halves
  • 30 g cashew nuts
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • 100 g red lentils, rinsed
  • 500 g water
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 150 g chestnut mushrooms, halved
  • 50 g sunflower seeds
  • 15 g Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 Tbsp linseeds, ground, mixed with 3 Tbsp water
  • ½ tsp dried parsley
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • mixed salad leaves, for serving

per 1 portion

Preparation

  1. Place bread in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place walnuts and cashews in mixing bowl then chop 3-5 sec/speed 4 until roughly chopped. Transfer to bowl with breadcrumbs and set aside.
  3. Place vegetable stock, lentils and water in mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, boil 10 min/Varoma//speed . With simmering basket in place of measuring cup, simmer 15-20 min/98°C//speed  until tender. Drain lentils in a fine mesh sieve and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Preheat oven to 190°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  5. Place garlic, onions and mushrooms in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add sunflower seeds, Worcestershire sauce, linseed mixture, reserved breadcrumbs and nuts, reserved lentils, parsley, salt, pepper and thyme then mix 20 sec//speed 3.
  7. Place a pastry cutter (Ø 9 cm) on prepared tray and fill with approx. ¼ of mixture, smoothing the surface with the back of a spoon. Remove pastry cutter and repeat with remaining mixture to make 4 equal-sized patties. Bake for 30-40 minutes (190°C) until crispy and brown. Leave to rest on tray for 5 minutes before serving with a salad.

Useful Items

baking tray, pastry cutter (Ø 9 cm), fine mesh sieve, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve the patties with a simple salad for a light lunch or for a more substantial meal, serve the patties in wholemeal burger buns with a dollop of tomato ketchup - the GI is a little higher but still low!
  • The nuts and lentils in these patties are naturally low in GI and using wholemeal bread instead of white bread also helps lower the GI.
