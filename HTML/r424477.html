Lamb sfiha

Ingredients

  • 200 g water
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 310 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ¼ tsp salt
  • 100 g pumpkin, cut into pieces (approx. 2 cm)
  • 80 g red onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 tsp Aleppo pepper (see Tips)
  • 1 vine-ripened tomato, cut into quarters
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 400 g lamb mince
  • fresh mint leaves
  • 1 lemon, cut into cheeks, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place water, oil and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add flour and 1 teaspoon of the salt and knead 3 min/. Transfer into a bowl, cover and set aside to prove in a warm place for 20-30 minutes, until doubled in size.
  3. Place pumpkin, onion, garlic, Aleppo pepper, tomato, ground black pepper and remaining ¼ teaspoon of the salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add lamb and mix 5 sec//speed 5, until combined. Transfer into a separate bowl, cover and place into refrigerator. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Sprinkle a pizza stone or baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with flour. Place pizza stone or prepared baking tray into a cold oven and preheat the oven to 200°C.
  6. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or a lightly floured work surface and divide into 28 pieces. Cover with a kitchen towel and keep covered while working with dough. Using a rolling pin, roll out each dough portion into a square shape (approx. 8 x 8 cm, 3 mm thickness). Place 1 tablespoon of the lamb mixture into the middle of the dough square and flatten slightly. Pinch together 1 corner at a time around the filling, to make an open pastry parcel with four pinched corners (see Tips). Repeat with remaining dough and mixture to make 28 sfiha. Place onto heated pizza stone or baking tray and bake for 12-14 minutes (220°C), until golden. Remove from oven, scatter with mint leaves and serve with lemon cheeks.

Useful Items

bowl, rolling pin, pizza stone or baking tray, silicone bread mat, tea towel, plastic wrap, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Aleppo pepper (also known as pul biber) is a mild variety of dried, ground capsicum often used in Middle Eastern cooking. It's available in Middle Eastern delicatessens or specialty spice stores. If you can't find it, try combining 1 tbsp mild paprika with 1 tsp cayenne powder instead. Or you can replace the Aleppo pepper with dried chilli flakes in this recipe.
  • These sfiha are shaped like square open pastry parcels with pinched corners to hold the filling in place.
