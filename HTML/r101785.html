Gluten Free Cheese Choux

Ingredients

  • 150 g Gruyère cheese, cut in pieces
  • 200 g milk
  • 100 g vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 250 g tapioca starch
  • 2 medium eggs
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
441 kJ / 105 kcal
Protein
2 g
Carbohydrates
9 g
Fat
7 g

Preparation

  1. Place Gruyère in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place milk, oil and salt in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/90°C/speed 1.
  3. Add tapioca starch and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and leave to cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Add eggs and reserved grated cheese then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Knead 2 min/. Leave to rest for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  5. After 20 minutes, use a piping bag with a large plain nozzle to pipe balls the size of a large walnut onto prepared baking tray, 3-5 cm apart. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C). Eat warm or leave to cool before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, piping bag and nozzle, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace oil with butter or margarine.
  • Tapioca flour is available in health food and Asian stores.
  • You can also scoop the choux dough using an ice-cream scoop and place on baking tray.
  • Raw choux can be frozen. There is no need to defrost before cooking, just extend cooking time by 10-15_minutes.
  • This recipe from the state of Minas Gerais is as famous as football or carnival. Crisp on the outside, soft on the inside, round and exquisite in texture, these Brazilian cheese choux make a perfect snack at any time of the day. Tapioca, one of the main ingredients of this recipe is a starch extracted from a plant native to Brazil (cassava), now cultivated worldwide.
