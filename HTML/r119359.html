Confit salmon, Parmesan gnocchi with cauliflower cream

Ingredients

Dried cauliflower florets

  • 1 cauliflower floret (medium to large)

Salmon cure

  • 8 fresh salmon fillets (approx. 100 g each), skin removed and reserved
  • water, for soaking salmon skin
  • 100 g rock salt, plus extra pinch for soaking salmon skin
  • 6 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper

Parmesan gnocchi

  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 300 g water
  • 30 g kudzu (see Tips)
  • water (iced)

Confit salmon

  • 600 g water
  • 500 - 550 g light olive oil

Cauliflower cream

  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 20 g olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 400 g cauliflower, broken into florets
  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 50 g full cream milk

Salmon skin, cauliflower and assembly

  • 1 ½ tbsp unsalted butter
  • 220 g cauliflower, broken into florets
  • 2 pinches salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch sea salt flakes, for garnishing
  • 1 lemon, zest only, finely grated, for garnishing

Preparation

Dried cauliflower florets

  1. Preheat oven to 60°C (alternatively, preheat a dehydrator to 60°C). Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Thinly slice cauliflower floret, using a mandoline if available. Place cauliflower slices onto prepared baking tray and bake for 6 hours (60°C), or until dry. Meanwhile, continue with Salmon cure.

Salmon cure

  1. Place salmon into a ceramic or glass dish and set aside. Place salmon skins into a bowl of water, add a pinch of the salt and set aside to soak.
  2. Place dill, lemon zest and juice, salt, sugar and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Transfer into dish with salmon and rub mixture into both sides of salmon fillets. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to cure for 1 hour, or until flesh is opaque. Meanwhile, continue with Parmesan gnocchi. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Parmesan gnocchi

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add water and cook 5 min/50°C/speed 6.
  3. Add kudzu and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 3, until thick and glossy. Transfer into a piping bag with a round nozzle (approx. 1 cm) and set mixture aside to cool to room temperature.
  4. Place iced water into a bowl and immerse tip of piping bag into water. Pipe gnocchi (approx. 2½ cm) into water, pinching or flicking nozzle tip to form each gnocchi. Place gnocchi, in water, into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Confit salmon

  1. Once cured, briefly rinse salmon fillets under cold water and pat dry with paper towel. Line a round cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and arrange salmon fillets in it (ensure tin fits comfortably into Varoma dish).
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position, insert cake tin with salmon into Varoma dish and weigh olive oil over salmon fillets (ensure fillets are completely submerged). Secure Varoma lid and cook 16-18 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until the thin part of salmon fillets start to flake. Carefully remove salmon from oil and transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cauliflower cream

  1. Place butter, olive oil, salt and cayenne pepper into mixing bowl and sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add cauliflower and chop 4 sec/speed 4, then cook 6 min/100°C/speed 1, until cauliflower is soft but not coloured.
  3. Add cream and milk and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 1 min/speed 5 -9 , increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 9, or until smooth. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salmon skin, cauliflower and assembly

  1. Place a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and place butter into pan. Break cauliflower into small florets, add to pan and fry until dark golden (approx. 5-7 minutes), stirring regularly. Sprinkle with salt and set aside.
  2. Place a heavy-based frying pan over low heat. Remove salmon skins from soaking water and pat dry with paper towel. Place between 2 sheets of baking paper, ensuring skins are very flat, then place into pan and place a weight (such as a smaller pan) on top. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until crisp. Set aside.
  3. Place cauliflower cream onto warm serving plates. Gently drain gnocchi using a slotted spoon and arrange (about 5-7 per plate) over cauliflower cream. Place salmon on top of cauliflower cream. Place salmon skin over fillet and arrange dried and pan-fried cauliflower around plate. Garnish with lemon zest, salt and reserved dill fronds before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, mandoline slicer, bowl, ceramic or glass baking dish, plastic wrap, piping bag with round nozzle (1 cm), round cake tin (20 cm), paper towel, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, non-stick frying pan (19 cm), heavy-based frying pan (26 cm), slotted spoon, serving plates, oven, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Kudzu is a natural vegetable-based thickening agent. You can buy it at specialty food stores or online.
  • Curing and cooking times will vary depending on the size and thickness of your salmon fillets.
