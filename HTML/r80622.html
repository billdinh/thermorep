Spelt and Honey Tear & Share Bread

Ingredients

  • 600 g spelt flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 330 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 50 g runny honey
  • oil, for greasing
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
775 kJ / 185 kcal
Protein
7.3 g
Carbohydrates
36.6 g
Fat
1.1 g

Preparation

  1. Place all ingredients in mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4, then knead 5 min/. The mixture should form a ball and the dough should begin to clean the sides of the mixing bowl.
  2. Tip dough out onto a floured surface and, using spatula, cut into 12 equal-sized pieces. Grease a large baking tray then shape dough pieces into balls and arrange in a flower shape (3 in the centre and 9 around the outside) on baking tray, leaving a gap in between each to allow room for dough to rise. Dust lightly with flour, cover with a clean tea towel and leave to rest in a very cool place until doubled in size (approx. 6 hours or overnight).
  3. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 220°C.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes (220°C). Transfer to a wire rack to cool before serving warm or cold.

Useful Items

wire rack, large baking tray, tea towel, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Breads made using spelt flour are richer in nutrients and have a nutty flavour. This attractive, pull-apart bread would make a great centrepiece to a meal. For a fun, creative and child-friendly version, try the Tear and Share Snail Bread recipe on Cookidoo®.
  • To make a loaf: Shape the dough into an oval in step 2, then place into a greased loaf tin and leaveto rise slowly in a cool place for 6 hours or overnight until doubled in size. Bake for 25-35 minutes at 220°C.
