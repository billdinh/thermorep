Fried Wontons

Ingredients

  • 3 dried shiitake mushrooms, rehydrated in hot water
  • 40 g fresh button mushrooms
  • 50 g spring onions, cut in pieces
  • 25 g celery stalk, cut in pieces
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • ¼ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 120 g peeled, raw prawns
  • 120 g pork meat, minced (see tip)
  • 3 filo sheets
  • water, for sealing
  • sunflower oil, for frying
Nutrition
per 1 piece

Preparation

  1. Place drained shiitake mushrooms, button mushrooms, spring onions and celery in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, chilli flakes, salt, prawns and pork mince then chop 3 sec/speed 4.
  3. Cut each of the filo sheets into 8 squares (approx. 11 cm), covering them with cling film to avoid pastry drying out.
  4. Place a teaspoon of mixture into centre of a filo square and moisten with water around 2 edges, fold into a triangle, and press to seal. Attach two points of triangle together, sealing with water. Repeat until all of mixture is used and approx. 24 wontons have been made.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan over a medium heat, then fry wontons in batches for approx. 2 minutes each side until golden brown and cooked thoroughly. Transfer to paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve immediately with dipping sauce as desired (see tip).

Useful Items

cling film, slotted spoon, soft spatula, non-stick frying pan, paper towel, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with soy sauce or teriyaki sauce.
  • Mince your own pork in the Thermomix, or use shop-bought pork mince.
  • Filo pastry is used in this recipe as wonton wrappers can be difficult to source. If available, use wonton wrappers for a truly authentic wonton!
