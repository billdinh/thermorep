Quinoa and Sweet Potato Rolls

Ingredients

  • 200 g sweet potatoes, cut in thin strips (5 mm)
  • 2 ½ Tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 Tbsp maple syrup
  • 180 g quinoa, rinsed
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 700 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 4 Tbsp rice vinegar, plus 2 Tbsp for brushing
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 ripe avocados, cut in pieces
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 8 sheets nori seaweed
  • 50 g red pepper, cut in matchsticks
  • soy sauce, for dipping

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Place sweet potatoes on a baking tray then drizzle with ½ Tbsp oil. Sprinkle with smoked paprika and maple syrup then toss. Bake for 17 minutes (170°C). Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place quinoa and 20 g oil in mixing bowl then fry 3 min/120°C//speed 1.
  3. Add water and stock cube then cook 22 min/90°C//speed . Meanwhile, place vinegar, sugar and ½ tsp salt in a small bowl. Stir until grains are dissolved, then set aside.
  4. Drain quinoa through a fine sieve, using a soft spatula to squeeze out liquid. Transfer to a large non-metal shallow dish and sprinkle vinegar mixture all over. Using soft spatula, toss gently to distribute vinegar mixture then leave to cool. Discard steaming water.
  5. Place garlic in mixing bowl then grate 3 sec/speed 7.
  6. Add avocados, vinegar, 2 Tbsp oil, salt and pepper then blend 4 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 6 sec/speed 10.
  7. Once quinoa is cool, place a sheet of nori, shiny side down onto a bamboo sushi mat then gently spread 6 Tbsp prepared quinoa over nori sheet with a wet, soft spatula (see tip), leaving ⅓ clear at the far end. Spread 1 heaped Tbsp avocado cream in a line across quinoa at the end closest to you, then layer 4 strips of baked sweet potato and 4 sticks of red pepper on avocado cream. Using bamboo sushi mat, begin rolling tightly to form a roll. Before reaching the other end brush edge with vinegar-water mixture (see tip) to help seal, then continue rolling tightly. Set finished roll aside then continue assembling remaining rolls.
  8. Slice each roll into 1.5 cm pieces using a wet, sharp knife, wiping clean between each slice. Serve with soy sauce for dipping.

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, sharp knife, baking tray, shallow bowl or dish, pastry brush, silicone spatula, bamboo sushi mat, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Many people think sushi means raw fish, but it is actually a reference to vinegared rice combined with various ingredients. Here. we've used quinoa instead of rice to create a plant based vegan sushi option packed with nutrients.
  • Place 200 g water and 2 Tbsp rice vinegar in a bowl to use for brushing and keeping hands and utensils wet so they don't stick to quinoa.
  • For optimum quality, sushi is best served immediately or within a few hours of making. Wrap rolls in cling film and leave in a cool dry place then slice just before serving.
  • Sushi can be wrapped in cling film and refrigerated. Consume within 24 hours and be mindful that the quality decreases quickly due to the quinoa losing moisture.
