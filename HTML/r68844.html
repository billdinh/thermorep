Citrus marmalade

Ingredients

  • 1300 g citrus fruit of choice (e.g. limes, oranges, cumquats, lemons, grapefruit)
  • 300 g water
  • 600 g sugar, adjust to taste

Preparation

  1. Using a vegetable peeler, remove citrus zest taking care not to include white pith. Place citrus zest into mixing bowl and grate 3-5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water and cook 4 min/100°C//speed 2. Drain zest through simmering basket and discard water. Place citrus zest back into mixing bowl.
  3. Remove white pith from citrus flesh, cut flesh into quarters and remove pips. Add citrus flesh and sugar into mixing bowl. Cook 15 min/Varoma//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Leave simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid and cook 60-80 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Test to see if your marmalade has set (see tips). If not, cook for a further 2-3 min/Varoma/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Transfer marmalade into warmed airtight jars to store. Allow to cool completely then, place in refrigerator until ready to use.

Useful Items

vegetable peeler, peeler, airtight jars, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Citrus marmalade is ideal for breakfast any day of the week.
  • This recipe makes approx. 1000 g of marmalade. Transferred into airtight storage jars, it will make ideal gifts for family and friends.
  • Reduce temperature to 90°C halfway through cooking time if marmalade begins to bubble onto mixing bowl lid.
  • Adjust sugar content according to the tartness of your fruit and your personal taste preference.
  • Setting point test: place a saucer into the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 tsp marmalade onto the chilled saucer. When marmalade on saucer is cool, push it with your finger. If the marmalade wrinkles, it is ready. If not, cook marmalade for a further 2 minutes then test again.
  • For a different texture to your marmalade, you may want to julienne (finely slice) your citrus zest instead of grating in step 1.
  • If a thicker consistency is desired, use jam setter or natural pectin obtainable from health food stores.
