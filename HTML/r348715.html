Fresh Prawn Spring Rolls

Ingredients

  • 40 g carrots, peeled, cut in sticks (5 cm x 5 mm)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1300 g water
  • 8 fresh green beans, ends removed, cut in long pieces
  • 1 bunch rice vermicelli (approx. 40 g), briefly rinsed with water to soften, cut in small sections (5 cm)
  • 8 large raw prawns, peeled, deveined
  • 8 pieces spring roll rice paper
  • 8 leaves iceberg lettuce, shredded
  • 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, plus 1 sprig roughly chopped leaves for garnishing (optional)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ fresh red chilli, deseeded
  • 2 Tbsp caster sugar
  • 2 Tbsp lime juice
  • 2 Tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 Tbsp rice vinegar

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in carrots. Remove bowl from lid and add salt. Mix well then leave to marinate for 20 minutes. Rinse salt from carrots with cold water and set aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and bringing to the boil 13 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add to simmering basket green beans and rice vermicelli then cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add prawns to simmering basket and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside. Discard cooking water.
  5. Lay a warm, damp tea towel over the spring roll rice paper until they become soft (see tip). Divide between rice paper rinsed carrots, steamed green beans, steamed prawns, steamed rice vermicelli, lettuce and 3 sprigs coriander. Roll into little parcels and set aside.
  6. Place garlic, chilli, sugar, lime juice, fish sauce and rice vinegar in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and garnish with 1 sprig coriander (if using). Serve dipping sauce with spring rolls.

Hints & Tricks

  • This kind of spring roll is traditional in Vietnam. They are cold and refreshing, great for summer dinners or lunches.
  • Do not leave the spring roll paper under the damp tea towel for too long, as they will absorb too much moisture and become unusable.
  • Adapt fillings according to personal preference. Try using mushrooms, cucumber or alfalfa sprouts.
