Rainbow Choux Buns

Ingredients

Choux Pastry

  • 80 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 10 g caster sugar
  • 150 g water
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 120 g strong white bread flour
  • 3 large eggs

Filling

  • 50 g icing sugar
  • 200 g fresh raspberries
  • 350 g double cream

Icing

  • 250 g icing sugar
  • water, for icing
  • 4 food colouring pastes

Preparation

Choux Pastry

  1. Place butter, sugar, water and salt in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Remove mixing bowl and leave to cool without lid for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  4. Place mixing bowl back into position then, without measuring cup, set speed 5 then add eggs, one at a time, through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  5. After adding the last egg, insert measuring cup then mix again 30 sec/speed 5.
  6. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle, then pipe walnut-sized buns onto prepared trays, leaving 5 cm between each (see tip). Bake for 20 minutes (200°C) or until puffed and golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl. Using a skewer, make holes in the bottom of choux buns then leave to cool completely (see tip). Meanwhile, make filling.

Filling

  1. Place sugar and raspberries in mixing bowl then heat 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Strain purée through a fine sieve into a bowl then set aside to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cooled raspberry purée and cream in mixing bowl then whip without setting a time/speed 4 until just stiff, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Remove butterfly whisk then transfer raspberry cream to a piping bag fitted with a thin, pointed nozzle.
  3. Once choux buns are cool, pipe raspberry cream into holes in buns then arrange in 4 rows in the shape of a rainbow.

Icing

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in sugar. Remove bowl then mix with water, 1 tsp at a time, to form a stiff icing. Divide between 4 small bowls then colour each bowl with a different food colouring paste.
  2. Dip tops of filled pastries into icing, using different colours for each row. Decorate with sprinkles or edible glitter if desired then serve or store in fridge for up to 24 hours.

Useful Items

baking trays, baking paper, piping bag and plain nozzle, skewer, sieve, piping bag and thin nozzle, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • When friends or family are coming over for dinner, get the kids to help make this gorgeous dessert. They’ll love piping the pastry and decorating the buns with different coloured icing. A fun and creative recipe that little chefs will be proud of!
  • Use a wet finger to smooth down any bumps on the top of the piped dough before baking.
  • Making the hole in the bottom of the choux buns whilst hot allows steam to escape and the interior to dry out.
  • The filling can be made without the raspberry purée if preferred. Instead whip cream with the icing sugar.
