Almond and Matcha Cake

Ingredients

Matcha Sponge

  • oil, for greasing
  • 4 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • 2 Tbsp granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 135 g icing sugar
  • 70 g ground almonds
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 3 tsp matcha green tea powder, plus extra for dusting

White Chocolate Buttercream

  • 50 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 170 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 350 g icing sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 Tbsp flaked almonds

Preparation

Matcha Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C and grease 5 layer cake tins (Ø 15 cm).
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and cream of tartar in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whisk 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add granulated sugar and whisk 10 sec/speed 4.  Remove butterfly whisk then transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place whole eggs, icing sugar and almonds in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/speed 4.
  5. Add flour, matcha tea powder and ⅓ whisked egg white mixture then mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  6. Add remaining egg white mixture and mix 15 sec/speed 2.5. Finish mixing by hand with spatula.
  7. Divide evenly between prepared tins. Bake for 6-8 minutes (220°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Loosen with a knife then remove to wire racks to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make buttercream.

White Chocolate Buttercream

  1. Place white chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 1 min 30 sec/60°C/speed 0.5.
  2. Add butter, icing sugar and vanilla extract then beat 30 sec/speed 3.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then beat again 30 sec/speed 3.5. Set aside.
  3. Toast almonds in a frying pan over a medium heat for a few minutes until lightly browned then set aside to cool.
  4. Place a sponge layer on a serving plate and spread with 2 Tbsp buttercream. Place another sponge on top then repeat with remaining buttercream and sponges. Ice final layer and sides of cake with buttercream.
  5. Sprinkle reserved toasted almonds on top then dust with matcha powder before serving.

Useful Items

5 layer cake tins (15 cm), wire racks, skewer, frying pan, serving plate or cake stand, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

This sponge has an incredibly subtle green tea flavour, with the matcha mainly lending its unique colour. The white chocolate makes this an extra-special buttercream and its sweetness complements the delicate sponge perfectly.
