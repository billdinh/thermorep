Cheesy biscuits

Ingredients

  • 30 g white chia seeds
  • 60 g water
  • 50 g cheddar cheese, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 180 g gluten free flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 130 g salted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1 - 2 tsp filtered water
  • 1 - 2 tbsp poppy seeds, for decorating (optional)
  • 1 - 2 tsp sweet paprika, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chia seeds and water into it. Stir well to combine, then set bowl aside.
  2. Place cheddar and Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add reserved chia mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add flour and butter and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  5. Mix until combined into a ball/speed 6, adding filtered water through hole in mixing bowl lid as needed to bring together into a ball.
  6. Transfer onto a lightly floured silicone bread mat or work surface. Knead dough together then divide into 3 equal portions. Roll each portion into a log shape (approx. 15-20 cm). Place poppy seeds (optional) onto a plate and roll logs in poppy seeds to coat the outside. Wrap each log in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 1 hour.
  7. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  8. Slice log into rounds (approx. 5 mm) using a non-serrated knife. Transfer biscuits onto prepared baking tray, sprinkle with paprika and bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C) or until golden. Allow to cool, then transfer biscuits into a sealable storage container until ready to serve.

Useful Items

small bowl , large plate, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This gluten free dough is really easy to work with. It doesn't dry and is very forgiving - if you break any of the biscuits, just press them right back together!
  • Once the log is formed and wrapped in plastic wrap, you can store dough in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or freeze for up to 1 month.
  • These biscuits freeze well and are great for lunchbox snacks.
  • You can replace poppy seeds with sesame seeds, if desired.
  • Recipe adapted from Louise Keats' cheesy biscuit recipe in her book, Something for Everyone.
