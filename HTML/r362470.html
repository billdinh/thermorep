Bow-Tie Antipasto Pasta Salad

Ingredients

Italian Vinaigrette

  • 6 oz olive oil
  • 1 lemon, thin peel only, no pith, plus 2 tbsp juice, divided
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 oz red wine vinegar
  • &frac18; tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard

Pasta Salad

  • 50 oz water
  • 16 oz dried bow-tie pasta
  • 4 oz red onions, quartered
  • 5 oz salami, sliced (¼ in.)
  • 4 oz roasted red peppers, julienned, drained
  • 4 oz pepperoncini, julienned, drained
  • 3 ½ oz sliced black olives, drained
  • 8 oz mozzarella bocconcini, drained
  • fresh basil leaves, thinly sliced, to garnish

Preparation

Italian Vinaigrette

  1. Place a pitcher onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in olive oil and set aside.
  2. Place lemon peel, garlic, basil, oregano, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add vinegar, 2 tbsp lemon juice, paprika, onion powder and Dijon and mix 2 min/speed 4-6 increasing speed gradually while pouring reserved olive oil in a slow steady stream onto mixing bowl lid, letting it drizzle around the measuring cup onto rotating blades to create an emulsion. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Return dressing to pitcher and set aside.

Pasta Salad

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and boil 12 min/212°F/speed 2.
  2. Add pasta, set Varoma dish into position and cook for time indicated on package/212°F//speed 1. Drain cooked pasta through Varoma dish and transfer to a large bowl. Discard water and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place onions into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add salami, roasted peppers and pepperoncini and stir through with spatula to combine.
  5. Transfer to bowl with cooked pasta and add black olives. Add reserved dressing, to taste and toss through to coat. Chill or serve at room temperature. Garnish with mozzarella and basil just before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • Refrigerate any leftover vinaigrette in a sealable jar.
