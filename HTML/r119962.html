Slow roasted lamb shoulder and fennel with agrodolce dressing

Ingredients

Lamb shoulder and vegetables

  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 lemon, zest peeled into strips (1 x 4 cm - no white pith) and juice
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 60 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp salt flakes
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1500 - 2000 g lamb, shoulder, bone in, scored around the bone
  • 250 - 500 g hot water
  • 2 fennel bulbs, trimmed and cut into thick wedges (fronds reserved for garnishing)
  • 16 small potatoes, cut into halves (see Tips)

Agrodolce

  • 100 g water
  • 100 g red wine vinegar
  • 50 g white sugar
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 red onions (approx. 300 g), cut into halves
  • 60 g currants
  • 2 tbsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 30 g balsamic vinegar
  • 10 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 6 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing

Preparation

Lamb shoulder and vegetables

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Set aside a roasting pan (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Place garlic, lemon zest and peppercorns into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil, salt, nutmeg and cinnamon and blend 4 sec/speed 4.
  4. Transfer paste onto lamb and rub over entire shoulder. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place lamb onto a wire rack into roasting pan. Place lemon juice and 250 g of the water into base of pan and cover tightly with aluminium foil or a secure lid. Place into oven, reduce temperature to 160°C and roast for 2 hours (160°C). Check liquid levels and top up with an additional 250 g water, if needed. Roast for a further 2 hours (160°C). Increase oven temperature to 180°C. Remove lamb from oven, uncover and add fennel and potatoes into pan. Baste lamb with pan juices. Roast for a further 30 minutes (180°C), or until fennel and potatoes are tender and lamb is falling off the bone. Cover lamb and rest for a minimum of 15 minutes before serving.

Agrodolce

  1. Place water, red wine vinegar and sugar into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add garlic and onions and chop 8 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add currants, fennel seeds, salt, pepper and balsamic vinegar and cook 30 min/90°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add parsley and mint and blend 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a serving bowl.
  5. Garnish lamb with fennel fronds and mint; serve with roasted vegetables and agrodolce.

Useful Items

large roasting tray, wire rack, pastry brush, aluminium foil, serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, ask your butcher to prepare the lamb for you.
  • Use the smallest potatoes you can find, or cut larger potatoes into cubes (2.5 cm).
  • Add 5 anchovies to lamb rub in step 2 for a rich, salty depth of flavour.
