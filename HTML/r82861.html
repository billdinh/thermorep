Leek and Lancashire Cheese Quiche

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
  • 80 g unsalted butter, chilled, diced
  • 50 g water, cold
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Filling

  • 150 g Lancashire cheese, cut in pieces
  • 350 g leeks, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 20 g unsalted butter
  • 280 g single cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, butter, cold water and salt in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 4. Tip out and shape into a disc approx. 1 cm thick. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out rested pastry on a lightly-floured surface. Line a loose-bottomed, fluted tart tin (Ø 26 cm) with pastry, pressing in well, then trim edges with a knife. Prick base with a fork then cover with cling film and chill in fridge for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  3. Line chilled pastry base with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 15 minutes (180°C) until golden. Remove baking paper and baking beans and bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C) then set aside to cool slightly. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place leeks and butter in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add cream, eggs, salt, pepper, nutmeg and reserved grated cheese then mix 5 sec/speed 4. Pour into pastry case and bake for 40 minutes (180°C) until golden brown and just set. Serve hot or cold.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking paper, baking beans, fluted tart tin (Ø 26 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Quiche has to be one of the most versatile savoury dishes we have. It can be served hot or cold, it can contain pretty much any filling you like, and can serve everything from a lunch centrepiece to picnic fare. We’ve created this one with Lancashire cheese, from the north of England and leeks which are the national emblem of Wales.
  • Serves 8 as a main course or 10 as a starter or light lunch.
  • Serve with a mixed, dressed salad.
  • Rice or dried lentils can be used in place of ceramic baking beans when blind baking the pastry case in step 3.
  • Cheddar cheese can be used instead of Lancashire cheese.
