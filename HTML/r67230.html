Fish and mixed vegetable parcels

Ingredients

  • 80 g extra virgin olive oil
  • ¾ tsp salt, plus extra for seasoning
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra for seasoning
  • 2 tbsp grated lemon zest
  • 4 turbot fillets (120-200 g each)
  • 200 g mixed vegetables (e.g. carrots, zucchini, celery stalks, turnip), cut into pieces as needed
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 50 g lemon juice (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 500 g water
  • 400 g potatoes, peeled, cut into slices (3 mm)

Preparation

  1. In a bowl mix extra virgin olive oil, ½ tsp of the salt, ¼ tsp of the pepper and grated lemon zest. Add turbot or white fish fillets to the bowl, coat well with marinade and cover with plastic wrap. Place bowl into refrigerator to marinate for 30 minutes, turning fish fillets over once during that time.
  2. Place mixed vegetables into mixing bowl with remaining ¼ tsp salt and ¼ tsp pepper and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and transfer fish fillets and marinade into it. Once marinade has drained into mixing bowl, set simmering basket and fish fillets aside. Leave marinade mixture in mixing bowl.
  4. Add parsley and lemon juice and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer lemon-parsley oil into a small bowl and set aside.
  5. Place fish fillets onto 4 separate squares of baking paper (30 x 30 cm) and distribute reserved vegetables evenly over each fillet. Fold baking paper to close packages and place parcels seam side up, 2 in Varoma dish and 2 on Varoma tray, tucking the folded ends of paper underneath the parcels.
  6. Clean and dry simmering basket. Place water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and add potatoes. Place Varoma into position and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve immediately with parcels partially opened, drizzled with reserved lemon-parsley oil. Season potatoes and serve on the side.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, baking paper

Hints & Tricks

  • As the fish marinade is served as a sauce, it is important to choose high quality, fresh fish.
