Mussels with Basil, Almonds and Butter

Ingredients

  • 100 g almonds
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 bunch fresh basil, leaves only (approx. 15 g)
  • 1 fresh chilli, halved, deseeded
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest and juice
  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 200 g water
  • 1000 g fresh mussels, shells well scrubbed
Nutrition
per 1 portion

574.2 kJ / 137.2 kcal

9 g

Preparation

  1. Place almonds in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, basil, chilli, lemon zest and juice in mixing bowl then blend 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend again 3 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add reserved chopped almonds and butter then blend 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend again 20 sec/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.
  4. Place water in mixing bowl and heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Place Varoma dish and tray into position then weigh mussels onto tray (and dish, if necessary, see tips). Cover Varoma and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until all the mussels have opened. Discard any mussels that remain closed at the end of cooking.
  6. Set Varoma aside and allow mussels to cool. Preheat oven to 200°C. Once mussels have cooled, remove from shells and transfer to a bowl, pulling away any “rope” while retaining the half shells.
  7. Arrange half shells on a baking tray and place two mussels on top of each half shell, then cover with reserved butter mixture.
  8. Bake for 8 minutes (200°C) or until butter is sizzling. Serve hot, in bowls.

Useful Items

baking tray, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • When steaming the mussels, you may need to divide them between the Varoma dish and tray.
  • Mussels re-heat well, without any loss of texture or flavour. Cook ahead then keep in the fridge until assembly.
  • Serve with crusty bread to soak up any herby butter remaining in the shells.
  • The quantity of mussels in the Varoma can be increased, so long as the lid of the Varoma closes properly. Adding more mussels may increase the cooking time. You can remove mussels that have opened, and continue cooking until all mussels have opened, discarding any that remain closed at the end of cooking.
  • The basil butter works well with scallops in the half shell too.
  • Instead of baking the mussels on the half shells, toss them and the basil butter through some freshly cooked pasta such as papardelle.
