Marathon Fettucine with Charred Tomatoes and Prawns

Ingredients

  • 12 plum tomatoes, halved
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 pinch chilli flakes, or to taste (optional)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, plus extra to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 300 g fettuccine
  • 300 g prawns, cooked (large)
  • 10 g fresh basil leaves, roughly chopped

Preparation

  1. Preheat grill to 230°C.
  2. Place tomato halves on a grill pan lined with foil. Brush with 1 Tbsp olive oil and sprinkle over salt and pepper. Grill until charred all over, for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Place 1 Tbsp oil and onions in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5, then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add garlic and chilli flakes (if using) then cook for 1 min/100°C/speed .
  5. Add charred tomatoes, salt and pepper then cook 20 min/98°C/speed . Meanwhile, cook pasta on hob according to packet instructions.
  6. Add prawns and basil then stir in with spatula. Season to taste.
  7. Divide cooked pasta between 4 serving bowls then pour over prawn and tomato sauce and serve.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, pastry brush, grill, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Dried or fresh pasta can be used - simply adjust the cooking time on the hob accordingly.
  • Bobby Flay (American celebrity chef and restaurateur) made this prerace meal with American marathon record holder Deena Kastor, who loves to cook. The two cooked together at Flay’s New York City restaurant a few months before they both ran the 2006 New York City Marathon, where Kastor finished 6th.
