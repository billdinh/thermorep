Beetroot and Halloumi Burgers

Ingredients

Beetroot Burgers

  • 100 g bread, torn in pieces
  • 240 g tinned chickpeas, rinsed, drained (1 x 400 g tin)
  • 250 g cooked beetroot, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 1 Tbsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 medium egg

Chilli Jam

  • 300 g red peppers, quartered
  • 3 fresh red chillies, halved, deseeded if desired
  • 150 g caster sugar
  • 75 g cider vinegar
  • 35 g water

Assembly

  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 250 g halloumi cheese, sliced (1 cm)
  • 6 brioche burger buns

Preparation

Beetroot Burgers

  1. Place bread in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place chickpeas, beetroot, garlic, parsley, cumin, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add egg and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to bowl with breadcrumbs and stir with spatula until combined.
  4. Shape mixture into 6 patties (approx. 110 g each, Ø 8 cm). Transfer to a plate, cover with cling film and place in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare chilli jam.

Chilli Jam

  1. Place red peppers and chillies in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add sugar, vinegar and water then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 0.5 until desired consistency is reached.

Assembly

  1. Heat oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat and fry chilled beetroot patties until browned and crisp on the edges (approx. 3-4 minutes on each side). Transfer to a plate and keep warm.
  2. In the same frying pan, fry halloumi until lightly golden (approx. 1-2 minutes on each side).
  3. Serve patties in brioche buns, with slices of halloumi and chilli jam to taste.

Useful Items

cling film, large frying pan, large bowl, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Sweet, earthy beetroot, smoky cumin and fresh parsley come together to make these fantastic veggie burgers. Shaped into colourful patties that are pan-fried until crispy, then served in a sweet brioche bun with homemade chilli jam, these burgers are real crowd-pleasers.
  • Serve with sweet potato fries and a side salad for a complete meal.
