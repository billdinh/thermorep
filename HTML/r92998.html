Steak and Kidney Pudding

Ingredients

Filling

  • 20 g olive oil
  • 200 g onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 450 g stewing steak, diced (2 cm)
  • 220 g lamb's kidneys, rinsed, halved and white cores removed, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 20 g tomato purée
  • 80 g red wine
  • 350 g beef stock

Pastry

  • 250 g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 125 g suet
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 165 g water, plus extra for sealing
  • butter, for greasing

Steaming

  • 2200 g water

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place oil, onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in beef, kidneys and flour. Remove bowl from lid, add salt and pepper then mix together until all the meat is coated.
  3. Add to mixing bowl seasoned beef and kidneys, thyme leaves, bay leaf, Worcestershire sauce, tomato purée, wine and stock then cook 90 min/100°C//speed . Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make pastry.

Pastry

  1. Place flour, suet, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add water and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Tip out, bring together into a ball, then wrap in cling film and allow to rest in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, grease a 1 litre pudding basin.
  3. Roll out ¾ of the pastry until large enough to line pudding basin (approx. 5 mm thick). Arrange in basin, leaving 2 cm hanging over the edge. Roll out remaining pastry into a round 2 cm larger than the diameter of the pudding basin then set aside.
  4. Remove bay leaf from cooled filling then, using a slotted spoon, transfer meat to the pastry-lined pudding basin. Top up with gravy juices until 2 cm from rim. Brush rim of pudding basin with water before topping with the pre-rolled pastry lid. Trim into place and firmly press edges together to seal.
  5. Cover basin with a large circle of baking paper, with a pleat in the middle to allow for pudding to expand. Cover baking paper with a circle of aluminium foil, again with a pleat in the middle. Secure both with string then place pudding into Varoma dish.

Steaming

  1. Place 1800 g water in mixing bowl. Set Varoma into position and steam 90 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Add 400 g water then continue steaming 60 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Once cooked, set Varoma aside and allow pudding to stand for 5 minutes. Remove foil and paper from pudding basin, then invert onto a plate and serve in wedges.

Useful Items

1 litre pudding basin, cling film, rolling pin, pastry brush, slotted spoon, butcher's string, aluminium foil, baking paper, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • A true British classic, homemade suet pastry is used to encase a rich filling of steak, kidneys and gravy. All parts, including the steaming can be done in the Thermomix® and the long, slow cook makes the meat really tender. A great example of a savoury steamed pudding cooked in the Varoma.
