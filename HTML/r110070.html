Chocolate Ganache Brownie

Ingredients

Brownie

  • 7 oz chocolate (64% cacao) in pieces or chips
  • 10 oz unsalted butter, room temperature, in pieces
  • 12 oz sugar
  • 4 - 5 large eggs, (approx. 7.5 oz)
  • 6 ½ oz all-purpose flour

Ganache

  • 12 oz organic heavy whipping cream
  • 4 ½ oz light corn syrup or liquid gluclose
  • 11 ½ oz chocolate (64% cacao) in pieces or chips

Preparation

Brownie

  1. Line a rectangular cake pan (approx. 12 in. x 8 in.) with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and melt 4 min/120°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place butter into mixing bowl and whip 1 min/100°F/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add sugar and mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  5. Mix 2 min/speed 4 while adding eggs and reserved chocolate through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 15 sec/speed 4.
  7. Add flour and mix 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer batter into prepared cake pan, spread out evenly with spatula and freeze for 2 hours. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  8. Preheat oven to 350°F. Remove brownie batter from freezer and bake 20 minutes (350°F). Remove from oven and flatten slightly with a spatula so all brownies are equal in height. Allow to cool while you make the ganache.

Ganache

  1. Place cream and light corn syrup into mixing bowl and mix 4 min/160°F/speed 2.
  2. Add chocolate and mix 30 sec/speed 3. Allow ganache to cool until firm enough to place into a piping bag. Transfer ganache to a piping bag with a flat piping tip. Refrigerate 20–25 minutes to firm. Cut brownies into 20 pieces and decorate by piping a ribbon of ganache on top. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

icing spatula, piping bag and tip, cake pan, rectangular (approx. 12 in. x 8 in.), freezer, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If using chocolate blocks or bars, cut into small pieces and place into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7.
  • Instead of piping ganache onto each piece of brownie, frost the whole brownie with ganache and cut into 20 pieces.
  • Ganache is rich and decadent which is great for parties, but for a quick treat, skip the ganache and pack up your brownies to go.
  • If you do not have a piping bag, use a gallon zip lock bag. Once the ganache is inside simply cut off a small piece of the corner and pipe your frosting.
