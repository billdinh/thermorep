Miso soup and sake seafood with rice

Ingredients

  • 1440 g water
  • 300 g short-grain white rice (e.g. sushi rice), rinsed and drained
  • 60 g Chinese cabbage, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g onions, cut into thin slices (2 mm)
  • 40 g carrot, cut into thin slices (2 mm)
  • 4 slices kombu (approx. 2 cm each) (optional)
  • 10 - 12 large raw prawns (approx. 170 g), peeled and deveined
  • 300 g clams, shells scrubbed
  • 250 g raw squid rings
  • 75 g Japanese soy sauce (see Tips)
  • 60 g sake
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp light sesame oil
  • 80 g miso paste
  • 5 pinches wakame flakes (see Tips)
  • 200 g silken tofu, cut into cubes (1.5 cm) (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place of the 1200 g water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and weigh rice into it.
  2. Select a heat-resistant bowl that fits into Varoma dish (approx. 20 cm top, 12 cm bottom, height 9 cm) and make sure to leave some holes unobstructed so steam can circulate. Place bowl into Varoma dish and place Varoma dish into position.
  3. Place Chinese cabbage, onions, carrot, kombu (optional), prawns, clams or mussels and squid rings into the heat-resistant bowl. Sprinkle with soy sauce, sake, salt, sesame oil and 40 g of the water. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 2. Meanwhile, mix miso paste with remaining 200 g water in a small bowl until dissolved and set aside.
  4. Set Varoma aside, remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer steamed rice to a bowl and cover to keep rice warm.
  5. Stir miso-water mixture again and add to mixing bowl. Add wakame flakes, place Varoma back into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 2. Set Varoma aside.
  6. Add tofu to mixing bowl and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1. Adjust seasoning to taste (see Tips). Serve soup as a starter, followed by steamed seafood and rice as the main course.

Useful Items

small bowl, bowl, tea towel, heat resistant bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • This combination recipe illustrates how your Thermomix® can be used to prepare a soup, main dish and rice all at once, resulting in clean, bright flavours and a healthy family meal.
  • Taste the soup before serving; if it isn’t salty enough, dissolve 10-20 g additional miso paste in the soup.
    - Soy sauce comes in different strengths and flavours. The most common Japanese soy sauce is usually lighter and slightly less salty than dark Chinese soy sauce. If only dark soy sauce is available, decrease quantity to 50 g.
    - Wakame and kombu (kelp) are two kinds of edible seaweed that are often used in Japanese, Korean and Chinese cuisines.
  • Replace soy sauce with flavoured Japanese soy sauce (e.g. kelp, dried bonito, clam), or add ¼ tsp instant dashi powder.
