Slow-Cooked Pulled Pork with Steamed Buns

Ingredients

Steamed Buns

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 300 g water
  • 3 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Pulled Pork and Apple

  • 1000 - 1200 g pork shoulder, boneless
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • 1 carrot (approx. 110 g), cut in pieces
  • 2 celery stalks (approx. 80 g), cut in pieces
  • ½ fennel bulb (approx. 80 g), trimmed and thinly sliced
  • 2 Granny Smith apples, core removed and cut in eighths
  • 40 g olive oil, plus extra to rub over skin
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp mustard powder
  • ½ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 50 g cider vinegar
  • 30 g white wine vinegar
  • 50 g treacle
  • 1 Tbsp chicken stock paste (see tips)
  • 1 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 - 2 Tbsp whole milk, for brushing
  • salad leaves, for serving

Preparation

Steamed Buns

  1. Grease a large bowl, Varoma dish and Varoma tray with oil. Place water and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and salt then knead 2 min 30 sec/. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Transfer proved dough to a lightly-floured work surface. Divide dough into 12 equal-sized portions, roll into balls and place in greased Varoma dish and tray. Set Varoma aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

Pulled Pork and Apple

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line a baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm) with baking paper, then line a deep-sided roasting tin with foil and place a wire rack on top.
  2. Carefully remove pork skin and fat layer from the shoulder. Cut shoulder meat into cubes (6-7 cm), then transfer to a bowl and set aside. Remove some excess fat from the underside of the pork skin, then using a sharp knife, score the top of the skin and rub with oil and salt.
  3. Place pork skin on the wire rack in the roasting tin and roast for 40-50 minutes (220°C) until fat has dissolved and crackling has become crispy. Remove crackling from oven and set aside. Reduce oven temperature to 200°C.
  4. Place garlic and onion in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add carrot and celery then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add fennel, apples and 40 g oil then sauté 10 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  7. Add reserved pork shoulder meat and sauté 2 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  8. Add paprika, mustard powder, chilli flakes,sugar, cider vinegar, white wine vinegar, treacle, stock paste and tomato purée, then place covered Varoma with buns into position and steam 30 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  9. Set Varoma aside then with simmering basket in place of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid and continue to cook 40 min/80°C//speed . Meanwhile, transfer steamed buns to prepared baking tray then brush with milk and bake for 5-10 minutes (200°C) until golden brown. Transfer buns to a wire rack and set aside.
  10. Remove simmering basket, check pork is thoroughly cooked with no pink meat visible, then insert measuring cup and shred 3 sec//speed 5. Transfer pork to a large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  11. Fill buns with shredded pork and serve warm with crackling and salad leaves on the side.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray (30 cm x 40 cm), baking paper, aluminium foil, wire rack, roasting tin, large bowl, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A great method for a typically tougher cut of meat, roasted pork is shredded in your Thermomix® to produce a juicy and tender filling with added spices. Served in fluffy steamed buns from the Varoma, this is a delicious and impressive lunch.
  • For the Chicken Stock Paste recipe, refer to Cookidoo®.
  • Substitute the salad leaves for coleslaw in your pulled pork buns. Look on Cookidoo® for a Coleslaw recipe.
  • Ask your butcher to separate the pork fat and skin from the roast for you to save time and effort.
  • Serve leftover pulled pork over rice, with corn tortillas or in sandwiches the next day.
  • Cooked pulled pork can easily be frozen and reheated later.
