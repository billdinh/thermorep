Banana sesame cake

Ingredients

  • 250 g cashews (raw or roasted)
  • boiling water, for soaking

Cake

  • olive oil spray, for greasing
  • 2 oranges (approx. 350 g), organic
  • 100 g sesame seeds, toasted
  • 80 g Rapadura sugar (see Tips)
  • 110 g macadamia oil
  • 280 g banana (approx. 3 bananas), cut into pieces
  • 100 g pitted Medjool dates
  • 3 eggs
  • 150 g self-raising flour
  • 50 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom
  • 2 tsp natural vanilla extract

Cashew cream icing

  • 30 g lemon juice
  • 80 g coconut cream
  • 20 g tahini (optional - see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 2 - 3 tbsp honey, to taste, plus extra for drizzling (see Tips)
  • nigella seeds, for sprinkling (optional)
  • 1 banana, cut into slices, for decorating

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews into it. Cover with boiling water and set aside to soak for 4-6 hours, until softened (see Tips). Proceed with recipe while nuts are soaking.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 140°C. Grease and line a round cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Using a vegetable peeler, remove zest from oranges (no white pith). Reserve orange flesh and set aside. Place 5-6 pieces zest, sesame seeds and sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9, until finely ground.
  3. Add oil, orange flesh, banana, dates and eggs and mix 6 sec/speed 6, until just combined.
  4. Add flours, bicarbonate of soda, cardamom and vanilla and mix 6 sec/speed 6 or until smooth and just combined. Transfer into prepared cake tin and smooth top. Bake for 80 minutes (140°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow cake to cool completely in tin. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cashew cream icing

  1. Using simmering basket, drain soaked cashews well. Place into mixing bowl and chop 1 min/speed 5, until finely chopped.
  2. Add lemon juice, coconut cream, tahini (optional), vanilla and honey and blend 3 min/speed 1.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend 2 min/speed 1, until smooth.
  4. Transfer cooled cake onto a serving plate and spread with icing. Drizzle with extra honey, sprinkle with nigella seeds (optional) and decorate with banana slices before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, baking paper, wooden skewer, round cake tin (20 cm), vegetable peeler, serving plate, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baby's serve: because this cake has a little sugar, you should ideally wait until your child is over 12 months before offering it to them.
  • Toddler's serve: serve as is as an occasional treat, cut up as needed, keeping serving sizes small.
  • The longer you allow your cashews to soak, the smoother the Cashew cream icing will be.
  • You can replace the Rapadura sugar with coconut sugar. Rapadura sugar or coconut sugar are less refined options; if you don't have them on hand, regular brown sugar is a more affordable and local option.
  • Tahini adds depth of flavour to the Cashew cream icing, but if you don't have it on hand don't worry, it's still lovely without it.
  • Honey is not suitable for children under 1 year of age.
  • You can replace the honey with coconut nectar in this recipe.
