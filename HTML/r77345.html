Kedgeree

Ingredients

  • 20 g fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 200 g long grain rice
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 350 g smoked haddock
  • 40 g butter, diced
  • 100 g cream
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 1 tsp curry powder, (e.g. Madras)

Preparation

  1. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and oil in mixing bowl, then insert simmering basket. Weigh in rice then add ½ tsp salt. Place Varoma dish into position and place eggs into it. Insert Varoma tray then weigh haddock onto it. Cover Varoma then steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 3.
  3. Set Varoma aside. Transfer eggs to a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes to cool, then transfer haddock to a plate and set aside. Meanwhile, line Varoma dish with wet baking paper, leaving some holes in the Varoma uncovered so steam can circulate.
  4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, transfer cooked rice to prepared Varoma dish and dot with butter. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in cream and 10 g of the reserved chopped parsley. Add ½ tsp salt, pepper and curry powder. Stir cream mixture together, pour over the rice and mix gently.
  5. Flake the haddock and scatter over the rice. Peel and quarter the eggs, then arrange on the rice. Place Varoma into position and steam 3 min/Varoma/speed 2 to warm through. Adjust seasoning to taste, scatter with remaining chopped parsley and serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking paper, large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Originally developed for the British in India, it is made there with a mixture of rice cooked with butter and dahl, together with spices and shredded onions. The British colonials brought the dish back to the UK during Victorian times, and it makes for a perfect weekend breakfast. Also works as a tasty lunch or supper too!
