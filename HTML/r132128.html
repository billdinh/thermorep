Bun kebab

Ingredients

Lamb patties

  • 70 g red lentils
  • 1700 g water
  • 1 tbsp ghee (see Tips)
  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 300 g lamb mince
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp dried mango powder (see Tips)
  • 70 g breadcrumbs
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 7 eggs
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 10 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into julienne
  • 20 g white vinegar

Assembly

  • 6 bread rolls, cut into halves horizontally
  • tamarind chutney, for garnishing (see Tips - optional)
  • 1 fresh tomato, cut into slices (optional)
  • ½ cucumber, cut into slices (optional)
  • 1 fresh long red chilli, trimmed and cut into thin slices (optional)

Preparation

Lamb patties

  1. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh lentils into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse until water runs clear. Place 500 g of the water into mixing bowl and re-insert simmering basket with lentils into mixing bowl. Cook 8 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer to a bowl to drain. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place ghee, onion and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7, then cook 6 min/100°C/speed 1,  without measuring cup. Set mixing bowl aside to cool (approx. 10 minutes).
  3. Add lamb, cumin, ginger paste, garlic paste, garam masala, mango powder, breadcrumbs, salt, 1 of the eggs and cooked lentils to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  4. Using damp hands, shape mixture into 6 patties (approx. 110 g each) and place onto a plate. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Add ginger and cook for 2 minutes or until crisp and golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside to drain on lined plate. Remove frying pan with oil from heat.
  6. Place vinegar and remaining 1200 g water into mixing bowl. Brush simmering basket with oil. Insert simmering basket and heat 11 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside. Crack 2 of the eggs into 2 separate bowls and set aside.
  8. Water should be boiling. Poach 3 min/Varoma/speed 2, adding the 2 cracked eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid into simmering basket. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer poached eggs onto paper towel to drain.
  9. Crack another 2 of the eggs into 2 separate bowls and set aside. Brush simmering basket with oil. Insert simmering basket. Top up water level as needed. Reheat 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Water should be boiling. Repeat poaching 3 min/Varoma/speed 2, adding the 2 cracked eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid into simmering basket. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer poached eggs onto paper towel to drain.
  10. Crack remaining 2 eggs into 2 separate bowls and set aside. Brush simmering basket with oil. Insert simmering basket. Top up water level as needed. Reheat 3 min/100°C/speed 1. Water should be boiling. Repeat poaching 3 min/Varoma/speed 2, adding the 2 cracked eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid into simmering basket. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and carefully transfer poached eggs onto paper towel to drain.
  11. Place large frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Add patties and cook for 3-5 minutes each side or until cooked to your liking. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a large plate and cover to keep warm.

Assembly

  1. Wipe frying pan clean with paper towel and place over medium heat. Add buns, cut side down, to pan and toast until golden brown. Transfer onto serving plates.
  2. Spread bun bases with Tamarind chutney (approx. 1½ teaspoons per bun) (optional) and top with patties. Garnish with tomato (optional), cucumber (optional), poached eggs, chilli (optional) and crispy ginger. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, 1 plate, large frying pan, paper towel, slotted spoon, pastry brush, 2 bowls, thermal serving bowl or plate, serving plates, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ghee, ginger paste, garlic paste, garam masala and Tamarind chutney, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Mango powder (also known as amchoor powder) can be purchased at most Indian or Asian food stores.
  • When entertaining or feeding a crowd, place garnish ingredients (chutney, cucumber, tomato, etc) into the centre of the table and allow everyone to build their own kebab.

Bun kebabs are a popular, quick, spicy snack on the streets of Karachi, Pakistan. There are a number of variations on this dish, with each vendor adding his or her own spin.
