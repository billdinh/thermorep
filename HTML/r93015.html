Walnut Scones

Ingredients

  • 225 g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g butter, cold, diced
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 130 g whole milk
  • 50 g walnut halves
  • 1 egg yolk, beaten
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
1169 kJ / 280 kcal
Protein
6 g
Carbohydrates

15.1 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 230°C. Lightly dust a baking tray with flour then set aside.
  2. Place flour, butter, baking powder and salt in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add milk and walnuts then mix 20 sec/. Tip out onto a floured surface and bring together into a disc (approx. 3 cm thick), being careful not to overwork the dough.
  4. Use a sharp knife to cut into six triangular segments. Arrange on prepared baking tray and brush with beaten egg yolk. Bake for 12-15 minutes (230°C) until lightly golden then transfer to a wire rack to cool. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

baking tray, wire rack, pastry brush, long sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Scones are best eaten on the day they are made.
  • Add raisins and 25 g caster sugar with the walnuts in step 3 for a sweeter, fruity version.

  • An English afternoon tea just wouldn’t be complete without scones. The sweet and savoury variations are endless, but the addition of walnuts here makes these scones equally delicious with cheese and chutney as with jam and clotted cream.
