Pumpkin and Apple Sponge Cake with Oats and Raisins

Ingredients

Sponge

  • 150 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g pecan nuts
  • 300 g pumpkin flesh, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g cooking apples, peeled, cored, quartered
  • 150 g rolled oats
  • 100 g raisins
  • 380 g light brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch ground cloves
  • 5 medium eggs
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Topping

  • 240 g sugar
  • 50 g butter, soft
  • 60 g cream cheese (see tip)
  • 10 g lemon juice

Preparation

Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and grease a spring form cake tin (Ø 28 cm).
  2. Place pecans in mixing bowl and toast 5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Roughly chop 2 sec/speed 4 then transfer to a bowl and set aside for garnish.
  3. Place pumpkin and apples in mixing bowl then grate 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then grate again 3 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add oats, raisins, brown sugar, oil, cinnamon, nutmeg, ground cloves and eggs then mix 10 sec//speed 4.
  5. Add flour and baking powder then mix 10 sec//speed 4. Transfer mixture to prepared tin and bake for 50-55 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  6. Allow sponge to cool in tin for 15 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

Topping

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, cream cheese and lemon juice then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Spread topping over cooled cake then sprinkle with reserved toasted and chopped nuts. Serve in slices, or store in fridge, removing 30 minutes before needed to bring back to room temperature.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 28 cm), skewer, wire rack, soft spatula, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Decorated with a layer of white cream cheese, this deliciously fluffy cake is a recipe that promises to impress your guests at tea time.
  • Full fat cream cheese gives the best texture for the topping.
  • Instead of pecan nuts you can use almonds or hazelnuts.
