Red Onion and Garlic Soda Bread

Ingredients

  • 75 g milk
  • 15 g barley malt extract
  • 30 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 15 g black treacle, melted
  • 50 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 400 g buttermilk, plus extra for dusting
  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 250 g wholemeal flour
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 10 g bicarbonate of soda, chopped
  • 40 g garlic cloves, chopped
  • 30 g olive oil, finely sliced
  • 1 red onion, finely sliced
  • 20 g sunflower seeds, for sprinkling
  • coarse sea salt, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Place milk, malt extract and yeast in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add treacle, butter and 200 g buttermilk then heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add remaining 200 g buttermilk, plain flour, wholemeal flour, salt, bicarbonate of soda and garlic then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, line a baking tray with baking paper and dust with flour. The dough will be very sticky. Tip out onto a well-floured surface and knead by hand for 1-2 minutes, adding more flour if needed. Form into a ball and place on prepared baking tray. Leave to prove in a warm place for 40 minutes. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 185°C.
  4. Cut a cross on top of the dough with an oiled knife or press the handle of a wooden spoon to make a deep impression. Bake for 20 minutes (185°C).
  5. Brush with olive oil. Sprinkle over sliced red onion, then sprinkle over sunflower seeds and coarse sea salt. Bake for 20 minutes (185°C) until deep golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped underneath.
  6. Place on a wire rack to cool. Serve with smoked mackerel pâté or soup of your choice.

Useful Items

long sharp knife, wooden spoon, pastry brush, wire rack, baking tray and baking paper, oven
