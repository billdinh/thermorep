Broccoli and pea soup with cauliflower cashew cream

Ingredients

  • 50 g raw cashews
  • water, for soaking

Chive oil (optional)

  • 250 g ice
  • filtered water, for ice bath and boiling
  • 60 g fresh chives
  • 150 g extra virgin olive oil

Soup and cashew cream

  • 1 - 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 3 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into green and white parts
  • 80 g ghee
  • 750 g water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 250 g broccoli, cut into small florets and stalks cut into thin slices
  • 300 g frozen baby peas
  • 200 g cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • 1 tsp sea salt, plus extra 1 tsp to taste
  • ground pepper, to taste (optional)
  • 2 egg yolks (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews into it. Cover with water and set aside while proceeding with recipe.

Chive oil (optional)

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh ice into it. Cover ice with water and set aside.
  2. Place a saucepan over medium heat, add water and bring to the boil. Once boiling, add chives and cook for 30 seconds. Using tongs, remove chives quickly and transfer into prepared ice bath. Stir gently until cool. Remove chives from ice bath and transfer onto paper towel and pat dry.
  3. Place chives and oil into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 8. Place a nut milk bag or 2 layers muslin cloth over a small bowl. Transfer chive mixture into nut milk bag and squeeze gently to extract oil. Discard pulp. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Soup and cashew cream

  1. Place garlic, onion, spring onions/shallots (all white parts and half of the green parts) into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 60 g of the ghee and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add 500 g of the water, lemon juice and 1 teaspoon of the stock paste into mixing bowl. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh broccoli and peas into it. Set bowl aside.
  4. Place Varoma into position and weigh cauliflower onto tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Carefully remove Varoma tray and place reserved broccoli and peas into Varoma dish. Re-insert Varoma tray with cauliflower, secure Varoma lid and steam 8-10 min/Varoma/speed 2, until vegetables are just tender (take care not to overcook as you will lose the bright green colour). Remove Varoma and set aside.
  6. Add broccoli and peas to mixing bowl. Add 1 teaspoon of the salt, pepper (optional) and egg yolks (optional), then blend 1 min/speed 1 -9, gradually increasing speed from speed 1 to speed 9. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Drain cashews and discard soaking water. Place drained cashews, steamed cauliflower, remaining 1 teaspoon salt, remaining 20 g ghee, remaining 250 g water and remaining 1 teaspoon stock paste and blend 1 min/speed 1 -9, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 9.

Assembly

  1. Divide broccoli soup between serving bowls, carefully spoon cauliflower cream over soup in a circular motion, then gently swirl through soup. Drizzle with a little Chive oil (optional) and garnish with remaining sliced green spring onions/shallots before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, saucepan, paper towel, small bowl , nut milk bag or muslin cloth, plastic wrap, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, 4 serving bowls, stove top, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • To make this recipe vegetarian, you can replace the Chicken stock paste with Vegetable stock paste (refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook).
  • You can replace the stock paste and water with 750 g liquid chicken stock, if you prefer.
  • You can store soup and cauliflower cream in the refrigerator for up to 2 days, or transfer into freezable containers and store in the freezer for up to 4 months.
  • Chive oil can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
