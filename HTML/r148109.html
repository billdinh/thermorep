Hunters' stew

Ingredients

  • 750 g white cabbage, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 200 g bacon, cut into cubes (1 cm)
  • 20 g oil
  • 350 g water
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 140 g tomato paste
  • 35 g pitted prunes, cut into halves
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • salt, to taste
  • 4 dried bay leaves
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 20 g dried porcini mushrooms, cut into slices
  • 280 g pork loin, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 280 g beef fillet steak (porterhouse), cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g sausage (kielbasa), cut into pieces (1-2 cm - see Tips)
  • 400 g sauerkraut (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place 350 g of the cabbage into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a large bowl.
  2. Place remaining cabbage into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into bowl with cabbage.
  3. Place garlic, onion, carrot, parsley and bacon into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and sauté 6 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add water, stock paste, tomato paste, prunes, pepper, salt, bay leaves, paprika and 450 g of the chopped cabbage and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Add mushrooms, then place Varoma dish into position. Weigh pork, steak, sausages, sauerkraut and remaining 300 g cabbage into Varoma dish. Secure Varoma lid and cook 20-30 min/Varoma//speed 1 or until meat is tender and cooked to your liking. Transfer contents of Varoma into a large bowl. Add cabbage mixture from mixing bowl and stir to combine, then serve.

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Polish sausage (kielbasa) can be found at European delis, some butchers and some specialty food stores.
  • Also known as "bigos", this traditional Polish dish is filled with various meats and plenty of sauerkraut and fresh cabbage. It's often served with rye bread or boiled potatoes, but it's a hearty and filling dish on it's own.
  • If you have extra time, make your own Sauerkraut (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
