Salmon and corn cakes

Ingredients

  • 160 g wholemeal sourdough bread, toasted and torn into pieces
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 300 g sweet potato, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 120 g red onion, cut into quarters
  • 1 sweet corn cob, kernels removed
  • 30 g butter
  • 415 g canned salmon (red salmon), well drained (see Tips)
  • olive oil spray
  • lemon, cut into wedges, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place bread and Parmesan into mixing bowl and chop 30-1 min/speed 8, to a fine crumb consistency. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place sweet potato and onion into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add corn kernels and butter and cook 6 min/90°C//speed 2.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook for a further 6 min/90°C//speed 2, then blend 30 sec/speed 1-6, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 6.
  5. Add salmon and 80 g of the reserved breadcrumb mixture, then mix 30 sec/speed 3 or until well combined. Transfer into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Place into refrigerator until chilled and firm (approx. 1-2 hours - see Tips).
  6. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper. Using your hands, shape 1 tablespoon of the mixture into a patty (approx. 4 cm diameter) and coat in remaining breadcrumb mixture. Place onto prepared trays and lightly spray with olive oil. Repeat with remaining mixture and breadcrumbs (makes approx. 25 patties in total).
  7. Bake for 13-15 minutes (200°C) or until golden (turning them over halfway through if necessary). Serve with lemon wedges.

Useful Items

baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, plastic wrap, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Be sure to include the soft bones from the tinned salmon - they're a great source of calcium.
  • For a dairy-free version, omit the Parmesan and replace the butter with extra virgin olive oil or virgin coconut oil.
  • It is not crucial to refrigerate the mixture but it does help with shaping into patties. If you prefer, you can shape the mixture into patties first, then put them into the refrigerator to firm.
