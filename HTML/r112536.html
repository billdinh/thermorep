Pancetta and asparagus galettes

Ingredients

Batter

  • 140 g buckwheat
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 140 g full cream milk
  • 140 g beer (lager)
  • 2 eggs

Filling

  • 120 g Gruyère cheese, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 20 g oil, plus extra for frying
  • 115 g pancetta, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 1 eschalot
  • 220 g asparagus, trimmed and cut into thin slices
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper

Preparation

Batter

  1. Place buckwheat into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10.
  2. Add salt and milk and mix 20 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add beer and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add eggs and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer mixture into a jug, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil, pancetta and eschalot into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5, then cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add asparagus and cook 4 min/100°C//speed , or until tender. Transfer mixture into bowl with cheese and set aside.
  4. Line a plate with baking paper and set aside. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add a little oil. Pour ⅓ of a cup of batter into pan to form a thin pancake and cook until bubbles appear on the top of pancake (approx. 30 seconds). Carefully flip pancake and cook for a further 10 seconds. Carefully transfer to lined plate and cover with a sheet of baking paper and a kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter to make a total of 6 pancakes.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper.
  6. Place 1 pancake onto baking tray. Place 2 tablespoons of the asparagus mixture into the centre of the pancake. Using the back of a spoon, make an indent in the middle of the filling. Carefully crack an egg into the indent. Working quickly, fold up each side of pancake to enclose asparagus mixture, leaving the yolk exposed, to form a square. If needed, push corners down and secure with toothpicks to help galette hold its shape. Repeat with remaining pancakes, filling and eggs to create a total of 6 galettes.
  7. Sprinkle with pepper and bake for 10-12 minutes (200 °C), or until eggs are baked to your liking. Serve immediately. 

Useful Items

jug, plastic wrap, bowl, kitchen towel, non-stick frying pan, 1 plate, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), toothpick, refrigerator, stove top, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, prepare your batter through step 4 the night before and store in the refrigerator until ready to use.
  • You can use any type of cheese in this recipe.
