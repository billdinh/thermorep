Matcha and mango frozen yoghurt

Ingredients

  • 50 g full cream milk
  • 450 g vanilla yoghurt
  • 1 - 2 tsp green tea powder (matcha)
  • 400 g frozen mango
  • 150 g fresh pineapple, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 fresh mango cheeks, cut into slices, for garnishing
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place milk, yoghurt  and green tea powder (matcha) into mixing bowl and mix 4 min/speed 6.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add frozen mango and fresh pineapple and mix 1 min/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 30 sec/speed 9.
  4. Pour mixture, up to 4 cm high, into shallow freezable containers and cover with plastic wrap. Place into freezer for 8-9 hours, or until firm but not completely frozen. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Cut into pieces (4 cm), then place half of the pieces into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 9, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer into a freezable storage container.
  6. Place remaining pieces into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 9, then mix 10 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer into freezable storage container and place into freezer for a minimum of 9 hours or overnight.
  7. Allow to thaw slightly, then garnish with mango slices and mint leaves to serve.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, shallow freezable containers (e.g. plastic, silicone), freezable storage container, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Matcha is a premium green tea powder with numerous health benefits, and is rich in nutrients and antioxidants. Matcha is available from speciality tea shops or online.
  • For a stronger matcha flavour, reduce the pineapple to 100 g.
