Beef Adobo

Ingredients

Rub

  • 2 oz salt
  • 2 oz garlic powder
  • 3 tbsp dried Mexican oregano leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 heaping tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme leaves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 6 dried ancho chilies
  • 160 oz beef ribs (10 lbs., approx. 2 racks)

Sauce

  • 5 oz yellow onions, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 &frac25; oz dried guajillo chilies, seeded and deveined
  • 3 oz ancho chilies, seeded and deveined
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano leaves
  • 1 tsp fresh marjoram leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme leaves
  • 5 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 15 oz water
  • 2 tsp salt

Preparation

Rub

  1. Place salt, garlic powder, oregano, peppercorns, onion powder, thyme, cinnamon and ancho chilies into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Rub into both sides of beef ribs, wrap in foil and refrigerate at least 3 hours.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F. Place ribs on baking sheet and cook for 2½-3 hours (350°F) or until meat is tender and falling off the bone.

Sauce

  1. Place onion, garlic, guajillos and ancho chilies, cinnamon, allspice, oregano, marjoram, thyme, vinegar, water and salt into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 5, then cook 15 min/212°F/speed 1.
  2. Blend 1 min/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually. Transfer to a bowl and serve with warm ribs.

Useful Items

foil, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Use pork ribs instead of beef ribs.
  • Preheat BBQ to medium high heat. Wrap ribs in foil and cook on the BBQ for 3 hours, remove foil and cook an additional 30 minutes.
