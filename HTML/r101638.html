Coconut & almond jelly with pineapple syrup

Ingredients

Almond milk

  • 220 g whole raw almonds
  • 660 g water
  • 4 pitted dates
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract

Coconut milk

  • 300 g desiccated coconut
  • 1000 g water

Jelly

  • 200 g water, room temperature
  • 2 tsp agar agar powder
  • 150 g pure maple syrup
  • &frac18; tsp natural almond extract, to taste

Pineapple Syrup

  • 150 g fresh pineapple, flesh only, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 1 tbsp orange liqueur (e.g. Grand Marnier®) (optional)
  • 140 g water
  • 2 tbsp soft dark brown sugar

Assembly

  • 100 g fresh pineapple, flesh only, cut into cubes (5 mm)
  • 30 g coconut flakes, toasted
  • 6 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, finely sliced

Preparation

Almond milk

  1. Place a large ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh almonds and water into it. Set aside to soak for 1 hour.
  2. Place all ingredients, including almonds and soaking water, into mixing bowl and blend 2 min/speed 9, until thick and creamy.
  3. Pour mixture into nut milk bag or muslin cloth and firmly squeeze to extract milk into a large jug or glass bowl. Set milk aside (keep at room temperature) and discard or reserve pulp (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coconut milk

  1. Place coconut into mixing bowl and mill 20 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add water and blend 1 min/speed 9. Pour mixture into a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and firmly squeeze to extract milk into a large jug or bowl. Set milk aside (keep at room temperature) and discard or reserve pulp (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Jelly

  1. Place water, agar agar powder, syrup, almond extract and 400 g of the reserved almond milk and 400 g of the reserved coconut milk into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 4. Place any remaining almond and coconut milk into refrigerator for future use.
  2. Pour jelly mixture into a loaf tin (25 x 10 x 7 cm) and allow to cool (approx. 10 minutes). Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to set overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pineapple Syrup

  1. Place all syrup ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 10-15 min/Varoma/speed 1, without measuring cup, until syrup is thick.
  3. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain syrup into a jug and allow to cool. Set aside until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh pineapple and coconut into it. Add mint leaves and stir to combine.
  2. Invert loaf tin onto a work surface and carefully unmould (gently tap sides of loaf tin if required). Cut jelly into 8 thick slices, and carefully transfer onto serving plates, then drizzle with syrup and garnish with pineapple mixture to serve.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, glass bowl or large jug, plastic wrap, loaf tin (25 x 10 x 7 cm), fine mesh sieve, jug, small bowl , serving plates, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Be sure to keep your almond and coconut milks at room temperature after making since the agar agar powder will not set as well if milks are cold.
  • You can store leftover almond and coconut milks in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • Use the leftover almond and coconut pulp in cakes, smoothies or as a base for protein balls.
