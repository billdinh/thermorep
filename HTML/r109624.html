Choux puffs with Swiss meringue

Ingredients

Choux puffs

  • 250 g water
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 120 g salted butter, cut into pieces
  • 70 g potato starch
  • 90 g white rice flour (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs

Swiss meringue

  • 100 g white sugar
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 pinch salt
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean, split into halves lengthways and seeds scraped
  • 1 - 2 drops natural pink food colouring (optional)
  • pure icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Choux puffs

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place water, salt and butter into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 2, or until just bubbling.
  3. Add potato starch and rice flour and mix 10 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 3, until a firm dough consistency is achieved. Allow to cool in mixing bowl for 10 minutes. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add vanilla and mix speed 3, adding eggs 1 at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid, until combined and smooth. Place pastry mixture into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (10 mm).
  6. Pipe approx. 30 balls (apricot-sized) onto prepared baking trays, leaving a 3 cm space between each ball. Bake for 20 minutes (200°C), then reduce oven to 180°C. Carefully remove choux puffs from oven and, using a wooden skewer, pierce each one to release steam. Return to oven and bake for a further 10 minutes (180°C), or until firm and crisp. Set aside to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Swiss meringue

  1. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites, lemon juice and salt into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/70°C/speed 3.
  3. Add vanilla seeds and cook 7 min/70°C/speed 3, slowly adding reserved sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid during the first 2 minutes of cooking time.
  4. Add food colouring (optional) and mix 20 sec/speed 3, or until colour is mixed through evenly. Transfer into a piping bag fitted with a star nozzle (10 mm).
  5. To assemble, cut cooled choux puffs into halves horizontally. Pipe Swiss meringue generously over bases, sandwich lightly with tops and dust with icing sugar before serving immediately.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, piping bag (10 mm nozzle), wooden skewer, bowl, piping bag with star nozzle, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own rice flour, place 250 g white rice into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10.
  • If you would like extra "lift" in your choux pastry, you can add a pinch of Gluten free baking powder (to make your own, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • The Swiss meringue recipe comes from New Zealand chef Andrew Brown, originally published in Dani Valent's cookbook, In the Mix.
  • Profiteroles: Omit steps 7-11. Cut choux puffs into halves horizontally. Place a scoop of  vanilla ice cream onto bases or pipe generously with thick custard (please refer to the ice cream and custard recipes in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook). Sandwich lightly with tops and drizzle with melted chocolate before serving.
