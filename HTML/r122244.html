Coconut vanilla sorbet with magic chocolate topping

Ingredients

  • 800 g unsweetened coconut water

Magic chocolate topping

  • 100 g dairy free dark chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 50 g coconut oil

Coconut vanilla sorbet

  • 80 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 150 - 250 g coconut cream
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
490 kJ / 116 kcal
Protein
0.6 g

Preparation

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh coconut water into it. Transfer into ice cube trays (approx. 4) and place into freezer overnight.

Magic chocolate topping

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add coconut oil and melt 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Scrape down sides and around blades with spatula and melt 1 min/37°C/speed 2. Transfer into a glass bottle or jug and set aside at room temperature. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Coconut vanilla sorbet

  1. Place half of the frozen coconut water cubes into mixing bowl and blend 5 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add remaining coconut cubes and blend 10 sec/speed 9 , with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add syrup, vanilla and 150 g of the coconut cream and blend 30 sec/speed 9, with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add more coconut cream (up to 100 g) if needed, until mixture is creamy.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Blend 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Transfer 1-2 scoops of sorbet into small serving cups and place into freezer for 10-15 minutes to firm up. Drizzle with a little Magic chocolate topping just before serving.

Useful Items

3 - 4 ice cube trays, jug, small serving cups, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Some coconut cream products can be made using equipment that has come into contact with dairy products. If dairy allergies are a concern for you, be sure to check the label of your coconut cream.
