Chocolate cupcakes with chocolate avocado icing

Ingredients

Hazelnut chocolate spread

  • 150 g raw hazelnuts, skins on
  • 40 g raw cacao powder
  • 100 g pure maple syrup
  • 100 g full cream milk

Cupcakes

  • 130 g raw hazelnuts, skins on
  • 60 g buckwheat flour (see Tips)
  • 40 g raw cacao powder (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp baking powder (see Tips)
  • &frac18; tsp stevia extract powder (optional)
  • 70 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 120 g pure maple syrup
  • 2 eggs
  • 80 g milk
  • 100 g hazelnut chocolate spread (see Tips)

Icing

  • 1 avocado, ripe, flesh only
  • 90 g pure maple syrup
  • 30 g raw cacao powder
  • 80 g hazelnut chocolate spread (see Tips)

Preparation

Hazelnut chocolate spread

  1. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 35 sec/speed 9 or until a fine consistency. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add remaining hazelnut chocolate spread ingredients and blend 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 1 min/speed 6 or until a smooth consistency is achieved. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Spread will thicken slightly in the refrigerator. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cupcakes

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a 12-hole muffin tray with paper cases and set aside.
  2. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 25 sec/speed 9 or until finely ground.
  3. Add buckwheat flour, cacao, baking powder and stevia extract (optional) and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil, syrup, eggs, milk and 100 g of the Hazelnut chocolate spread and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and if needed mix 10 sec/speed 5, until well combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Pour mixture into prepared paper cases and bake for 15-17 minutes (160°C), until mostly cooked through, but still slightly fudgy in the centre. Allow to cool in tin for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Icing

  1. Place avocado, syrup, cacao and 80 g of the Hazelnut chocolate spread into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 3.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 40 sec/speed 4 or until well combined and smooth.
  3. Spread icing onto cooled cupcakes before serving.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, 12-hole muffin tray, 12 cupcake liners, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This hazelnut chocolate spread can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 weeks, but no longer than the use by date of the milk you use.
  • If you don’t have any raw cacao on hand, you can use regular cocoa powder instead.
  • If you would prefer a lower fructose option, you can substitute the maple syrup with rice malt syrup. You can also use activated or roasted hazelnuts instead of raw, if preferred.
  • You can make your own buckwheat flour in your Thermomix from scratch. Place 250 g raw buckwheat groats into your mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10, until a fine, flour-like consistency is achieved.
  • If you’re making these cupcakes for someone with a wheat intolerance, then choose a gluten-free brand of baking powder. All the remaining ingredients are gluten free.
  • You can use homemade or store-bought hazelnut chocolate spread for this recipe. Homemade spread is the much healthier option. Store-bought spreads tend to be sweeter, so you’re unlikely to need the stevia extract powder in the cupcake mixture if using.
  • The avocado in the icing adds some green raw goodness to these cupcakes, with a boost of Vitamin C, folate, Vitamin K, Vitamin E, fibre and potassium. The flavour is quite subtle - it is a great way to get some extra green into your day.
