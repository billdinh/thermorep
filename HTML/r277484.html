Banoffee tartlets

Ingredients

Sweet pastry

  • 1 egg
  • 50 g white sugar
  • 120 g unsalted butter
  • 220 g plain flour

Toffee sauce

  • 120 g unsalted butter
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tsp gelatine powder
  • 2 tsp golden syrup
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste

Banana topping

  • 200 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 20 g icing sugar
  • 1 banana, cut into thin slices

Preparation

Sweet pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and set aside 12 small loose-bottom tartlet tins (6 cm base).
  2. Place egg and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add butter and flour and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for approx. 30 minutes.
  4. Roll out pastry to approx. 5 mm thick. Using a round cookie cutter (10 cm), cut out 12 pastry rounds. Press rounds into tartlet tins (see Tips), covering base and sides. Dock each pastry case with a fork, then line with baking paper and fill with pie weights, rice or dried beans. Bake for 12 minutes (180°C) or until cases are dry to the touch. Remove baking paper and weights, then set aside to cool. Remove tarts from tins once cooled. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Toffee sauce

  1. Place all Toffee sauce ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer 1 tablespoon of the Toffee sauce into the base of each pastry case, then transfer pastry cases into refrigerator to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Banana topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and sugar into mixing bowl and whisk without setting time/speed 3, until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Remove butterfly whisk. Spoon whipped cream over cooled toffee sauce, then top each tartlet with banana slices to serve.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, round cookie cutter (10 cm), rolling pin, small loose-bottom tartlet tins (6 cm), plastic wrap, baking paper, pie weights, rice or dried beans, fork, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Any remainder pastry can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or in the freezer for up to 2 weeks.
  • To save time, start making the toffee sauce while pastry cases are baking.
