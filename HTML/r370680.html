Spiced roasted cauliflower salad with minted yoghurt

Ingredients

Spiced cauliflower

  • 1 ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 ½ tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 ½ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ - 1 tsp chilli powder, to taste
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1500 g cauliflower, broken into bite-sized florets
  • 2 - 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, to taste
  • 60 g shelled pistachios, roughly chopped (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 3 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, shredded
  • 1 pomegranate, arils only (see Tips)

Minted yoghurt

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 200 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey

Preparation

Spiced cauliflower

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place cumin, coriander and fennel seeds into mixing bowl and roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 2, until aromatic. Then mill 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add paprika, ground cumin, chilli powder, salt and pepper and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  4. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cauliflower into it. Add oil and spice mixture and toss to coat. Transfer onto prepared baking tray and roast for 40-45 minutes (200°C), until browned and tender. Set aside to cool slightly.
  5. Place a serving bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pistachios into it. Add lemon juice, mint, pomegranate arils and cooled cauliflower, then toss to combine.

Minted yoghurt

  1. Place garlic and mint into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spaula.
  2. Add yoghurt, lemon juice and honey and mix 20 sec/speed 3 or until combined. Serve cauliflower with minted yoghurt dressing.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, serving bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the pistachios with macadamias in this recipe, if desired.
  • If pomegrante is not available, soak 1 tablespoon dried cranberries in 2 tablespooons hot water for 1 hour, until plump. Drain cranberries, then add to salad in place of pomegranate.
