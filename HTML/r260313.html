Meringue Christmas Trees

Ingredients

  • 16 oz confectioners sugar, plus extra to garnish
  • 3 egg whites, from large eggs, room temperature
  • ½ tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tbsp food coloring (green), to taste
  • decorative candy, to sprinkle
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
318 kJ / 76 kcal
Protein
0 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 250°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment and set aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in confectioners sugar and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 4 min 30 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add cream of tartar and without measuring cup whip 3 min/speed 3, while adding reserved confectioners sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid, 1 tbsp at a time. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add food coloring, insert measuring cup and whip 3 min/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Transfer into an icing bag with a large star tip. Pipe meringue directly onto prepared baking sheets. Start in a circle motion (approx. 1½ in. diameter) and spiral upward to a point for the top of the tree. Sprinkle with decorative candy.
  7. Bake for 35 minutes (250°F). Do not open oven door. Turn oven off and let meringues sit inside closed oven for 2 hours to dry out before removing.
  8. Garnish with confectioners sugar and serve or package for gifting.

Useful Items

baking sheets, parchment paper, icing bag and (star) piping tip, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For best results, make sure mixing bowl, lid and butterfly whisk are thoroughly clean and dry before starting meringues. Any residual grease or moisture will prevent eggs from properly aerating. Eggs at room temperature provide better results.
  • Do not refrigerate meringues as they absorb moisture from the refrigerator and will lose their crispness. Store Meringue Trees in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.
