Coconut Rice with Asian-inspired Pork and Eggs

Ingredients

Soy Reduction

  • 60 g soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp water
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • ½ tsp chilli sauce, hot, (e.g. Sriracha, see tip)

Pork Mince

  • 500 g pork fillet, cut in pieces (3 cm), partially frozen
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 cm fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 4 spring onions, white part only, quartered, reserving green fronds for garnishing
  • 20 g groundnut oil
  • 230 g water chestnuts, tinned, drained
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper
  • ½ tsp vegetable stock paste (see tip)
  • 1 Tbsp water

Coconut Rice and Eggs

  • 300 g rice
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 500 g water
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 20 g groundnut oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, finely chopped for garnishing

Preparation

Soy Reduction

  1. Place all reduction ingredients in mixing bowl and cook 6 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer to a jug and set aside.

Pork Mince

  1. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place 250 g pork fillet in mixing bowl and mince 5 sec/speed 7, or until desired consistency is achieved. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place remaining 250 g pork fillet in mixing bowl and mince 5 sec/speed 7, or until desired consistency is achieved. Transfer to bowl with reserved pork mince and set aside.
  3. Place garlic, ginger and spring onions in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add 20 g oil and sauté 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add water chestnuts and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add salt, pepper, vegetable stock, water and reserved pork mince then mix 10 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Coconut Rice and Eggs

  1. Place simmering basket on mixing bowl lid and weigh in rice. Remove simmering basket then rinse rice until water runs clear.
  2. Place coconut milk and water in mixing bowl then insert simmering basket with rice. Place Varoma into position and insert Varoma tray. Line Varoma tray with a sheet of wet and well-wrung baking paper. Grease 4 egg rings, place on prepared Varoma tray and crack 1 egg into each ring.
  3. Cover Varoma and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 4. Set covered Varoma aside then stir rice in simmering basket with spatula. Place Varoma back into position and cook 8-10 min/100°C/speed 4 until eggs and rice are cooked as desired. Meanwhile, heat 20 g oil in a medium-sized non-stick frying pan over a medium heat. Add pork mince mixture and cook for 10-15 minutes until golden, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and cover to keep warm.
  4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and fluff rice with a fork. Divide rice between 4 serving bowls, then top with pork mince mixture and an egg. Drizzle with reserved soy reduction and garnish with coriander and reserved spring onion fronds before serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, egg rings, jug, non-stick frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Sriracha is a type of hot sauce that can be found in most major supermarkets, or at Asian specialty grocers.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • This is a great way to cook perfect eggs in your Varoma.
  • Toss any leftover pork mince through some lettuce leaves or noodles and drizzle with the soy reduction to enjoy for lunch.
