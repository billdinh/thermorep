Gravlax

Ingredients

  • 1 oz dill seeds
  • 1 oz whole coriander seeds
  • 2 oz brown sugar
  • 2 oz salt
  • 1 fresh salmon fillet (sushi-grade), center cut, bones removed (approx. 3 lbs.)
  • 10 sprigs fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 5 oz cream cheese, to serve
  • 2 oz red onion, sliced, to serve
  • 2 oz capers, to serve
  • 6 hard-boiled eggs, sliced, to serve
  • 12 bagels, toasted, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place dill and coriander seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/212Â°F/speed 1. Add sugar and salt and stir 10 sec/speed 1.
  2. Lay a piece of cling wrap (approx. 8 in. longer than the salmon fillet) on a baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle cling wrap with half of the salt mixture. Transfer salmon onto cling wrap, sprinkle remaining salt mixture over fillet and rub in with your fingers. Cover seasoned salmon with another piece of cling wrap and tightly wrap salmon so it's securely covered with no air holes. Place a second baking sheet directly on top of the salmon. Weight the salmon with a heavy-based pan on the top baking sheet (see Tip). Refrigerate (approx. 48-72 hrs.).
  4. Unwrap salmon and rinse off as much of the salt mixture as you can, gently wipe off the rest with paper towels.
  5. Sprinkle salmon with dill and lightly press into salmon.
  6. With a sharp knife gently slice salmon into thin slices laying each piece on a flat serving plate.
  7. Serve with cream cheese, sliced red onion, capers, hard-boiled eggs and toasted bagels.

Useful Items

baking sheet, paper towels, serving plate, cling wrap, heavy-based frying pan, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The salmon must be absolutely fresh and kept properly chilled. It is best to seek a good-quality specialty fishmonger selling sushi-grade salmon and other fish.
  • Consuming raw or undercooked meats, poultry, seafood, shellfish or eggs may increase your risk of foodborne illness.
  • Weight the salmon to press moisture out of the fish while it cures. Use a smaller baking dish, or a flat plate roughly the size of the salmon and add approximately 2 lbs. weight, e.g. cans of beans, cast iron pan, large cans of tomatoes.
