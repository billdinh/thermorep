Apple strudel

Ingredients

Strudel pastry

  • 370 g plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • 70 g sunflower oil
  • 120 g water
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • ½ tsp salt

Apple filling

  • 100 g hazelnuts
  • 130 g butter, unsalted, cut in pieces
  • 80 g breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 100 g sugar
  • 1 Tbsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 20 g rum (optional)
  • 750 g apples, peeled
  • 80 g raisins

Strudel assembly

  • plain flour, for dusting
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Strudel pastry

  1. Place flour, egg, 40 g sunflower oil, water, white wine vinegar and salt in mixing bowl and knead 2 min/. Place dough on a lightly floured surface and form into a loaf shape. Brush with remaining sunflower oil, cover with cling film and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Apple filling

  1. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 40 g butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/70°C/speed 2.
  3. Add breadcrumbs and ½ tsp cinnamon and toast 6 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Add reserved chopped hazelnuts and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place sugar, remaining ½ tsp cinnamon, vanilla sugar, lemon juice and rum (if using) in mixing bowl and mix 15 sec//speed 4.
  6. Cut apples in quarters, remove cores, then slice quarters. Add apples and raisins to mixing bowl and mix 5-10 sec//speed 3 with aid of spatula, adjusting time according to desired size of apple pieces. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  7. Place remaining 90 g butter in mixing bowl, melt 3 min/70°C/speed 1 and set aside for brushing.

Strudel assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C and line a large baking tray (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place a large tea towel (approx. 80 cm x 45 cm) on a table and dust with flour. Place dough on tea towel, dust with flour and using a rolling pin, roll out to a thin rectangular shape. Cover with second tea towel and allow to rest for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove top tea towel and place on the table, its long edge overlapping slightly with the long edge of the other tea towel, to create a large surface on which to stretch the dough. Brush dough with some melted butter, then lift over backs of your hands and gently stretch it with your knuckles towards table edges. Move around the edge of the dough and keep stretching slowly and carefully toward and finally over the table edges, until dough is very large, thin and transparent. Use scissors to cut off thick edges of dough. If holes appear while stretching, pinch them together lightly (small holes will not be visible after rolling the strudel).
  4. Brush entire dough surface with more melted butter, sprinkle reserved breadcrumb and hazelnut mixture in a wide band along shortest edge of the pastry, leaving a few cm of dough uncovered along the edge, then top the crumb mixture with apple filling, forming a narrower band than the breadcrumb mixture. Lift up cloth at filling edge, using it to flip the dough. Roll strudel by pulling the first tea towel upwards and continuing with the next tea towel until the whole strudel is rolled. Pinch ends of roll shut, cutting off unnecessary excess dough and tucking dough ends underneath roll. Using remaining tea towel, carefully transfer strudel to prepared baking tray, forming strudel into a horseshoe shape if necessary to make it fit.
  5. Brush top with remaining melted butter. Bake for 25-30 minutes (190°C) or until crisp and golden. Allow to cool a little, dust with icing sugar and serve warm, cut in thick slices.

Useful Items

cling film, baking tray (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm), baking paper, tea towels, large tea towels (approx. 80 cm x 45 cm), rolling pin, pastry brush, scissors, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a faster version, use ready-made strudel dough.
  • You can form two apple strudels instead of one large one: when placing filling on top of dough, leave a channel of a few cm clear in the middle of the row of apples. Roll the strudel as described in the recipe, then cut through the channel to form two strudels, pinching the ends of each strudel shut.
  • Serve with vanilla sauce, vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
  • The breadcrumb and hazelnut mixture traps juices from the apples and keeps the strudel crust crisp. Instead of using both, you can use all breadcrumbs or all hazelnuts.
  • Before placing apples on breadcrumb mixture, check whether they have released a lot of juice. For a drier filling, discard part of the juice.
  • Dough can be prepared a day or two in advance and stored in the fridge.
  • Instead of two tea towels, you can use a large tablecloth to roll the strudel.
