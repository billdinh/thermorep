Veal Pilaf

Ingredients

  • 60 g Emmental cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • ½ bunch fresh parsley (approx. 10 g), leaves only, plus extra roughly chopped for garnishing
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 70 g red pepper, cut in pieces
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g clarified butter
  • 1 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 ½ Tbsp sweet paprika
  • ¼ - ½ tsp hot paprika, to taste
  • 600 g veal meat, sinew free, boneless, diced (3 cm)
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • 1 piece lemon peel (approx. 2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ - 1 tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 250 g water
  • 150 g long grain rice
  • 2 Tbsp sour cream, for serving

Preparation

  1. Place Emmental in mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 8. Transfer to bowl with cheese, mix together and set aside.
  3. Place garlic, red pepper and onions in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter, tomato purée, sweet paprika and hot paprika then sauté 3 min/120°C//speed 2.
  5. Add veal and cook 10 min/120°C//speed 1.
  6. Add vegetable stock, lemon peel, salt, pepper and water then cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 150°C.
  7. Add rice and mix 30 sec//speed 2. Transfer to a casserole dish (3-4 l) and cover with lid. Bake for 45-55 minutes (150°C) until rice is cooked (see tip). Remove lid and stir pilaf. Bake without lid for a further 5-10 minutes (150°C) until desired dryness is achieved (see tip).
  8. Remove lemon peel and transfer to serving bowls. Sprinkle with reserved grated cheese and garnish with extra chopped parsley then serve hot with sour cream.

Useful Items

casserole dish (3-4 l), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baking time will vary depending on the rice used.
  • The amount of sauce in the final dish can be adjusted according to personal preference. For a dryer pilaf, at the end of baking in step 7, bake for a further 10 minutes at 180°C.
  • A cast iron pot may also be used instead of a casserole dish, but will take longer to cook. Adjust baking time accordingly until rice is cooked.
