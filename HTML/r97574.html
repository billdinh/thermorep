Ricotta gnocchi

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Ricotta gnocchi

  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 150 g 00 pasta flour or plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • salt, to taste
  • ground nutmeg, to taste

Gnocchi sauce

  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • ¼ lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 30 g sunflower seeds
  • 30 g pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 30 g pickled capers
  • 150 - 200 g pancetta, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 250 g cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 30 g lemon juice, (approx. 1 lemon)
  • 3 leaves fresh silverbeet, (approx. 80 g), thinly sliced (1 cm)
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • water, for boiling

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 15 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 2 minutes. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers of muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for at least 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ricotta gnocchi

  1. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add flour, egg, salt, nutmeg and all of the reserved ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) and knead 30 sec/, or until just combined, prolong time if necessary.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and gently work into a ball, then divide into 4 portions. Roll each portion into a thin sausage shape, then cut into pieces (approx. 1 cm in length) using spatula. If desired, gently score gnocchi by rolling pieces over the tines of a fork. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Gnocchi sauce

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese and lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds and capers into mixing bowl and combine 5 sec//speed 3. Transfer onto prepared baking tray and bake for 15 minutes (180°C), or until lightly golden and toasted. Set aside to cool slightly.
  4. Place pancetta and oil into mixing bowl and sauté 10 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula, and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1. Transfer pancetta into a bowl. Discard oil, then place pancetta back into mixing bowl.
  6. Add tomatoes, lemon juice, silverbeet and pepper, and cook 3 min/100°C//speed . Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  7. Place a large saucepan with water over high heat and bring to the boil. Once water is boiling, carefully add gnocchi to saucepan. Gnocchi will rise to the surface when cooked (approx. 2 min). Remove cooked gnocchi with a slotted spoon and place into thermal serving bowl with sauce. Gently toss gnocchi through sauce.
  8. Divide between serving bowls and top with reserved Parmesan mixture and reserved toasted seeds and serve immediately.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or muslin cloth, large bowl, bowl, silicone bread mat, baking paper, small bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, slotted spoon, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), large saucepan, fork, serving bowls, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Gnocchi can be made in advance. After step 6, transfer gnocchi into a freezable container and place into freezer until ready to use. Allow to come to room temperature before cooking in step 12.
