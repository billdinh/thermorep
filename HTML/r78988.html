Chicken tikka masala with saffron rice

Ingredients

Tikka paste

  • 40 g coriander seeds
  • 40 g cumin seeds
  • 10 g dried red chillies
  • 50 g fresh coriander, washed thoroughly and left to dry on paper towel
  • 100 g garlic cloves
  • 100 g fresh ginger, peeled
  • 220 g olive oil, plus extra for covering
  • 100 g water
  • 100 g lemon juice (approx. 2-3 lemons)
  • 1 ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 30 g sea salt
  • 50 g dried onion flakes
  • 140 g tomato passata (purée)

Chicken tikka and saffron rice

  • 1000 - 1200 g skinless chicken thigh fillet, cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • 100 g natural yoghurt
  • 300 g jasmine rice
  • 900 g water
  • 3 pinches saffron threads
  • 2 tsp vegetable oil

Masala sauce

  • 1 brown onion (approx. 150 g), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 long green chilli, deseeded if preferred
  • 50 g butter or ghee
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • ¼ - ½ tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp raw sugar
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 150 g natural yoghurt
  • 150 g double cream
  • fresh coriander, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

Tikka paste

  1. Place coriander seeds, cumin seeds and dried chillies into mixing bowl and dry roast 5 min/120°C/speed 1, then mill 1 min/speed 8.
  2. Add fresh coriander, garlic, ginger and olive oil and blend 1 min/speed 5-10, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and mixing bowl lid with spatula. Repeat blending if necessary until a smooth paste consistency is achieved.
  3. Add all remaining tikka paste ingredients and cook 40-60 min/80°C/speed 2. Once the paste is cooked, a red sheen of oil should be visible on the surface. Transfer the paste into storage jars with sealable lids and cover with a little more olive oil if necessary to submerge the paste for storage. Allow paste to cool completely, then seal lids and store in the refrigerator until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Chicken tikka and saffron rice

  1. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chicken, natural yoghurt and 130 g of the reserved tikka paste into it. Combine well, then cover and leave to marinate in the refrigerator for a minimum 3 hours or overnight.
  2. Insert simmering basket then weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl. Re-insert simmering basket with rice and add saffron threads and cook 13-16 min/100°C/speed 4, pouring vegetable oil onto mixing bowl lid allowing it to trickle into mixing bowl.
  4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, then transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm while you prepare the chicken and masala sauce.

Masala sauce

  1. Place onion, garlic, ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter or ghee and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, salt, sugar, garam masala and 100 g of reserved tikki paste and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add yoghurt and cream and blend 10 sec/speed 4-8, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 8. Set sauce aside in mixing bowl to stay warm.
  5. Place a large non-stick, high-sided griddle or frying pan over a high heat and cook marinated chicken for 10 minutes, or until cooked through.
  6. Pour sauce into a serving bowl, then add chicken and stir to combine. Serve immediately on a bed of the saffron rice garnished with coriander leaves.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, plastic wrap, large non-stick high-sided griddle or frying pan, turner/fish slice, sealable storage jar, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, serving bowl, stove top, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve Chicken tikka masala with Naan bread and condiments of choice.
  • Top up jars with a little more oil after each use so that the paste stays submerged. This will assist with preserving the paste. If kept covered in a sealable jar in the refrigerator this paste will last up to 4 weeks.
  • Use the tikka paste to make a vegetable, meat, prawn, fish or paneer tikka, or tikka masala curry. It is also wonderful as a marinade for chicken to be barbecued.
  • To try making your own garam masala, please refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
