Mango, ginger and chilli chutney

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10 g fresh ginger, peeled and cut into thin slices
  • 1 - 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 2 tsp nigella seeds
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 700 g mango, flesh only, cut into pieces (2 cm) (see Tips)
  • 250 g raw sugar
  • 120 g white vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 100 g raisins

Preparation

  1. Place oil, garlic, ginger and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add nigella seeds, turmeric, coriander, cumin, cardamom and cinnamon and cook 30 sec/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add mango, sugar, vinegar, salt and raisins and cook 45 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Insert measuring cup. Mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into sterilised jars with lids and seal immediately. Turn jars upside down for 10 minutes. Turn upright and set aside to cool for 24 hours. Once cooled, store in the pantry until ready to use.

Useful Items

2 sterilised sealable jars

Hints & Tricks

  • After opening, store chutney in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.
  • You can replace the fresh mango with frozen (thawed) mango.
  • You can serve this as an accompaniment to any Indian snack or meal. We served ours with Samosas (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip). Garnish with coriander, if desired.
