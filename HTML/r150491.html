Steak tartare

Ingredients

  • 40 g spring onions/shallots, trimmed
  • 1 tsp pickled capers, drained
  • 1 pickled cucumber (approx. 60 g)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp soy sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 250 g beef tenderlion (high quality), fat trimmed, partially frozen and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 4 egg yolks, room temperature
  • bread, to serve
  • mixed salad greens, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place spring onions/shallots, capers and pickled cucumber into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add lemon juice, oil, soy sauce, salt and pepper and combine 3 sec/speed 3, until combined.
  3. Add meat into mixing bowl and chop 2-3 sec/speed 7, until finely minced, taking care not to over mince (meat should still retain some texture). Divide mixture into 4 equal portions and press each serving into a round cookie cutter (8 cm) or egg ring on serving plates. Pack meat down lightly with a fork. Make a slight indent in the centre of each serving and carefully place egg yolk into the indent. Gently remove cookie cutter, then serve immediately with bread and salad.

Useful Items

egg rings, serving plates

Hints & Tricks

  • Only select raw meat that is very fresh and high in quality (e.g whole muscle primal cuts) from a reputable supplier/butcher. We do not recommend buying pre-packaged meat (i.e. - supermarket cuts). Ask your butcher for recommendations on the best cut of meat to select.
  • If beef tenderloin is unavailable, you can use eye fillet.
  • Consider alternative recipes that do not require raw meat or raw eggs, especially when serving to young children, the elderly, pregnant women and those with poor immune systems.
  • Raw eggs and raw meat should only be prepared just before serving.
