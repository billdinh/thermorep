Apple Butter

Ingredients

  • 48 oz Granny Smith apples
  • 11 oz sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • 8 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Wash apples and cut in quarters, leaving on peel and cores. Remove any stems.
  2. Place 24 oz of prepared apples into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4.5 with aid of spatula. Add 24 oz of remaining apples into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 4.5 with aid of spatula. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, apple cider vinegar and lemon juice and cook 25 min/212°F/speed 2.
  4. Purée 1 min/speed 8 increasing speed gradually. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Cook a further 30 min/Varoma/speed 2. Check consistency. Apple Butter should be thick and smooth (see Tip). If necessary, cook in increments of 5 min up to a further 30 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until desired consistency.
  6. Pour into sterilized jars with twist-off lids and seal immediately (see Tip). Allow to cool. Serve or store refrigerated (see Tip).

Hints & Tricks

  • The apple peel, seeds and cores provide flavor and pectin, which helps thicken the sauce. Wash fruit before using.
  • Cooking time will vary depending on moisture content of your apples.
  • Apple purée will thicken and is ready when a sample is put on a cold plate and is the consistency of room temperature butter.
  • To ensure preservation, clean jars thoroughly in hot soapy water. Rinse by pouring boiling water over them. Prepare for filling by placing clean jars in a baking pan and heat in oven (212°F) until required. Pour Apple Butter into jars while jars are still warm and proceed as directed by the recipe.
  • Once opened, Apple Butter will keep in the refrigerator for up to two weeks. Alternatively, store in plastic containers or ice cube trays and freeze.
  • Spread on toast or scones or use as a filling for cakes and tarts. Also may be mixed with an equal amount of softly whipped cream and served with cookies for dessert.
