Little lasagne cups

Ingredients

Cheese sauce

  • 150 g mozzarella cheese, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 150 g tasty cheese, cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 20 g salted butter
  • 30 g plain flour
  • 350 g full cream milk
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Lasagne mince

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 150 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 150 g zucchini, cut into pieces
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 100 g tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 500 g beef mince (see Tips)

Lasagne cups

  • olive oil spray, for greasing
  • 60 gyoza wrappers (see Tips)

Preparation

Cheese sauce

  1. Place cheeses into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place butter and flour into mixing bowl and melt 2 min/100°C/speed 1 or until smooth.
  3. Add milk, salt, pepper and nutmeg and cook 6 min/90°C/speed 4.
  4. Add 200 g of the reserved grated cheeses and combine 1 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Set aside to cool for 10 minutes before transferring into refrigerator to cool until ready to use (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lasagne mince

  1. Place garlic, onion, carrot and zucchini into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add stock paste, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce and mince and stir with aid of spatula, then cook 6 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Stir with aid of spatula, then cook 6 min/100°C//speed 1 or until mince is cooked through, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 1 hour). Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until cold (see Tips).

Lasagne cups

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Spray 3 x 24-hole mini-muffin trays with oil and set aside (see Tips). Set aside 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm).
  2. Press 1 goyza wrapper into each mini-muffin mould using the handle of the Thermomix® spatula. Place 2 teaspoons of the reserved mince mixture into base of each wrapper, then top with 1 teaspoon of the reserved cheese sauce. Sprinkle evenly with reserved grated cheeses. Bake for 8-10 minutes (180°C) until browned. Set lasagne cups aside in trays for 5 minutes or until cool enough to handle. Remove carefully from muffin trays and place onto baking trays. Return to the oven and bake for a further 7-10 minutes (180°C) or until bases are crisp and brown. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm), 3 x 24-hole mini muffin trays, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Mincing your own meat: Cut 500 g beef topside into cubes and partially freeze. Place 250 g into mixing bowl and mince Turbo/2 sec/2 times, then transfer into a bowl. Repeat with remaining 250 g beef.
  • Goyza wrappers can be replaced with wonton wrappers in this recipe. Use a 9 cm round cookie cutter and cut wrappers into rounds.
  • This recipe is easy to assemble when the cheese sauce and mince mixture are completely cold. You can make these lasagne cups a day ahead and store in the refrigerator or freezer.
  • Lasagne cups can be frozen in the mini-muffin trays, then removed and stored in a freezable storage container for up to 1 month. To bake from frozen, place back into mini-muffin trays and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C).
  • If you only have one mini-muffin tray you will need to grease in between each batch.
