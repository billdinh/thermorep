Healthy biscuits

Ingredients

  • 3 pitted dates
  • 100 g mixed nuts of choice (e.g. almonds, cashews, walnuts), unsalted
  • 20 g pepitas
  • 30 g dried cranberries
  • 1 orange, zest only, no white pith (optional)
  • 4 dried apricots, cut into quarters
  • 170 g rolled oats
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 250 g banana
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 50 g dark chocolate chips (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds, for sprinkling

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 baking trays (approx. 30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place dates, nuts, pepitas, cranberries, orange zest (optional) and apricots into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add oats, chia seeds and salt and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place banana, egg and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  5. Add reserved fruit and nut mixture and chocolate (optional) and combine 20 sec//speed 3.
  6. Place tablespoonfuls of biscuit mixture onto prepared baking trays (makes approx. 22 biscuits). Using damp hands, lightly press mixture down, then sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for 16-18 minutes, until golden. Allow to cool on baking trays for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store these biscuits in a sealable container in the pantry for up to 3 days. These are a great "make ahead" option for lunchboxes or after school snacks.
  • Nut-free biscuits: replace nuts with extra dried fruit and seeds.
