Buckwheat Galettes with Scallops and Andouille Sausage Sauce

Ingredients

Batter

  • 200 g buckwheat flour
  • 300 g milk
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 250 g water
  • 40 g salted butter

Filling

  • 20 g butter
  • 3 dessert apple, peeled, cored, cut in 8 pieces
  • 12 scallops
  • 15 g brandy
  • 150 g double cream
  • 85 g smoked pork sausage, cut in pieces plus 6 thin slices (andouille or kabanos)

Preparation

Batter

  1. Place buckwheat flour, milk, egg and salt in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  2. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid and weigh in water. Remove and set aside.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Without measuring cup, whisk 1 min/speed 3, while pouring the water slowly through the hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Place a knob of butter in a large non-stick pancake or frying pan and melt over a medium heat. When butter is hot, pour a small ladle of batter into the pan and spread over the entire surface by rotating the pan in a circular motion. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side then transfer to a plate. Repeat until all the batter is used (adding more butter as necessary), making sure to make the thinnest possible pancakes. Clean mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 120°C.
  2. Heat 10 g butter in a frying pan over a medium high heat and brown apple pieces then transfer to a bowl and keep warm.
  3. Place remaining 10 g butter in frying pan and brown scallops for 1-2 minutes on each side.
  4. Add brandy and flambé (see tip). Remove from heat and cover to keep warm.
  5. Place cream and sausage pieces in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/90°C/speed .
  6. Take one pancake at a time and fill with a slice of sausage, a little reserved apple and 2 scallops. Reserve cooking liquid from scallops. Loosely fold pancake over, place in a serving dish and repeat with remaining pancakes.
  7. Add scallop cooking liquid to cream in mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 10. Adjust seasoning if necessary then arrange filled pancakes on hot plates, spoon over a little sauce then serve immediately.

Useful Items

jug, small ladle, pancake pan or frying pan, frying pan, serving dish, hob, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Scallops should be cooked quickly. They are cooked when the outside browns slightly, with the interior remaining white, almost translucent.
  • Be sure to choose a variety of apple that maintains its shape during cooking.
  • Flambéing is the process of setting fire to alcohol that has been poured over food. Once on fire, the alcohol burns off quickly. To flambé safely follow these guidelines:
  1. Never pour from an open bottle into a pan near a naked flame.
  2. After adding the alcohol, ignite it immediately without delay.
  3. Ignite using a long match or barbecue lighter, and always light the alcohol fumes near the edge not the actual liquid.
  4. Wait for the flames to die out.
  • The pancakes can also be made with regular plain flour.
  • If your pancakes are very thin, serve them aumonières (tied like money bags) on the plate, with the sauce poured over.
