Pineapple and orange glazed ham

Ingredients

Glaze

  • 150 g pineapple juice
  • 95 g orange marmalade
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp brandy
  • 50 g brown sugar
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground cinnamon
  • 1 star anise (optional)

Ham

  • 5000 g whole leg cured ham on the bone
  • whole cloves (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
2142 kJ / 510 kcal
Protein
59 g
Carbohydrates
12 g
Fat
25 g

Preparation

Glaze

  1. Place all glaze ingredients into mixing bowl and cook 20-25 min/100°C/speed 3, or until glaze is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer glaze into a ceramic or glass bowl and set aside to cool.

Ham

  1. Place an oven shelf into the lowest possible position and remove all other shelves. Preheat oven to 180°C and set aside a rectangular baking dish (35 x 30 cm).
  2. Using a sharp knife, cut a circle around the leg bone of the ham through the rind (approx. 10 cm from the end).
  3. Very carefully cut a horizontal line through the centre of the ham, from the top end of the ham to the leg bone, ensuring you only cut the rind and not into the fat. Run your fingers underneath the rind and lift one half of the rind from the layer of fat underneath, then discard rind. Repeat with remaining half of the rind.
  4. Score the fat of the ham approx. 5 mm deep in a diamond pattern, then transfer ham into rectangular baking dish.
  5. Brush ⅓ of the reserved glaze over the ham and stud the centre of each diamond with 1 whole clove (optional), pushing the clove into the fat.
  6. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C), then reduce oven temperature to 160°C and cook for a further 45 minutes-1 hour, re-brushing the ham with the glaze every 20 minutes, until the glaze has caramelised and the ham is cooked through.
  7. Transfer ham onto a serving plate, carve and serve.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, rectangular baking dish (35 x 30 cm), pastry brush, serving platter, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Allow ham to cool for 15-20 minutes, then transfer into the refrigerator to cool completely. Serve ham sliced on a cold meat platter.
