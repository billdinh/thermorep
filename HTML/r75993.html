Pear and Cucumber Smoothie

Ingredients

  • 350 - 400 g ripe pears, quartered, cores discarded
  • 30 - 60 g watercress, leaves and stems, roughly chopped
  • 300 - 350 g cucumber, cut in pieces
  • 200 g kefir
  • 100 g liquid minerals (optional)
  • 100 g noni juice
  • 25 g linseeds
  • 10 g lemon slice including skin
  • 100 g water, plus extra as needed
Nutrition
per 1 glass
Calories
505 kJ / 121 kcal
Protein
3.9 g
Carbohydrates
16.5 g
Fat
4.4 g

Preparation

  1. Place all ingredients in mixing bowl and blend 4 min/speed 10.
  2. Add extra water if desired to adjust consistency to taste and blend 1 min/speed 5. Pour into glasses and serve immediately.

Useful Items

4 glasses

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have enough ripe pears, use juicy oranges (peeled) to substitute for part or all of the pears.
  • The pear taste is stronger without additional water, so when adding extra water to adjust the consistency, you may wish to also add ½-1 tsp honey and blend again 30 sec/speed 10 - this helps to keep the pear flavour prominent.
  • If you have extra pears that are ripe, cut them up and freeze for an easy smoothie another day. Extra chunks of cucumber can also be frozen. If using frozen pears or cucumber, use water instead of ice cubes, otherwise the smoothie would be very thick.
  • This smoothie is full of natural goodness to start your day well and keep you going. It's also a refreshing and nourishing snack before or after a workout. And the linseeds are a good source of Omega-3 essential fatty acids and protein.
  • Increase the watercress to 60-70 g for a peppery green taste.
  • Increase the pears to 500 g and freeze in lollipop moulds to make a great tasting (and healthy) summer treat.
  • If you don't have liquid minerals, replace with apple juice. Liquid plant-based minerals are excellent for adding extra nutritional value to smoothies.
  • Best served immediately, over ice cubes.
  • Can be covered and kept in the fridge for up to 24 hours.
