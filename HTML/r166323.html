Chicken moghrabieh

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tsp ground caraway
  • 3 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 750 g chicken meat, skinless and boneless, cut into pieces (5 cm)
  • 1250 g water
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper, freshly ground
  • 70 g butter
  • 250 g dried chickpeas, soaked (for at least 12 hours) and drained
  • 300 g pearl onions
  • 1000 g frozen moghrabieh couscous (pearl couscous), defrosted

Preparation

  1. In a small bowl mix 1 tbsp olive oil with 1 tsp caraway and 1 tsp cinnamon. Distribute chicken in Varoma dish and tray and coat with prepared oil mixture and set aside.
  2. Place water, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp salt, pepper, butter, 1 tsp caraway and 1 tsp cinnamon into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/100Â°C/speed 1.
  3. Insert gently simmering basket, add drained chickpeas and pearl onions, place Varoma into position and steam 45 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer chicken into a bowl, cover and keep warm.
  4. Distribute moghrabieh into Varoma dish, add 1 tsp caraway, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp salt and mix with spatula. Place Varoma into position and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1.5. Remove Varoma and transfer cooked moghrabieh to a deep serving dish. Remove simmering basket, drain the contents and add them to serving dish. Cover with cooking liquid and layer with chicken on top. Serve hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • Lebanese pearl couscous, also known as moghrabieh, is a pearl pasta made from hard wheat flour. It is rather chewy in consistency and absorbs a lot of flavour from the things it is cooked with. It is mainly used for preparing the moghrabieh dish, a traditional dish in Lebanon.
