Cactus Cornbreads (Sopes) and Chicken

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 1000 g water
  • 700 g chicken breasts, skinless, cut in pieces
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Salsa

  • 2 garlic cloves, roasted (see tip)
  • 500 g tomatoes, roasted, cut in pieces
  • 200 g onions, roasted, halved
  • 10 g serrano chillies, roasted, halved, deseeded
  • 250 g poblano chillies, halved, deseeded
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt

Sopes

  • 300 g panela cheese (Mexican fresh cheese), cut in pieces (2 cm) (see tip)
  • 250 g onions, quartered
  • 500 g nopales cactus leaves, cut in pieces (see tip)
  • 60 g water
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 400 g masa harina (e.g. Maseca, see tip)
  • 400 g refried beans, tinned (see tip)
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, roughly chopped
  • 250 g sour cream

Preparation

Chicken

  1. Place water in mixing bowl, place Varoma dish into position and weigh in chicken. Season chicken with salt and pepper then cover Varoma and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and discard cooking water.
  2. Place steamed chicken in mixing bowl and shred 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Salsa

  1. Place garlic, tomatoes, onions, serrano chillies, poblano chillies and salt in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Sopes

  1. Place panela in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer to another bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cactus, water and salt in mixing bowl then blend 50 sec/speed 10.
  4. Add masa harina and knead 3 min/. Transfer to a bowl, cover with a damp tea towel and leave to rest for 15 minutes.
  5. Shape rested dough into 25 g balls, place one by one in a tortilla maker (see tip) and flatten into a disc (Ø 8 cm). Cook each sope on a hot griddle pan for 3 minutes each side, remove from griddle pan and, while still warm, pinch up the edges to make a border.
  6. Top sopes with refried beans, reserved shredded chicken, reserved grated cheese, chopped coriander, reserved chopped onions, sour cream and reserved salsa. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

tea towel, tortilla maker, griddle pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • To roast salsa ingredients, preheat oven to 160°C. Place ingredients on a baking tray, drizzle over olive oil and roast for 40-60 minutes until soft and browned.
  • Panela is a fresh and smooth Mexican cheese. It is very soft and crumbly in texture and has some similarities with paneer or Cheshire cheese. It is available from Mexican supermarkets and online. If not available, substitute with cheese of choice.
  • Nopales are the thick, flat stems of the prickly pear cactus, eaten as a vegetable. The flavour of fresh nopales is like a mix of asparagus and French beans and they are chewy in texture. They are available from Mexican supermarkets or online.
  • Masa harina is a Mexican, gluten-free maize flour, traditionally used to make tortillas. It is available from Mexican supermarkets and online.
  • If a tortilla maker is not available, use a rolling pin to roll dough into discs (approx. Ø 8 cm).
  • To make your own Refried Beans, follow the recipe in the Viva México collection.
