DIY chocolate truffles

Ingredients

Chocolate truffles

  • butter, for greasing
  • 600 g dark chocolate chips
  • 190 g pouring (whipping) cream

Toppings

  • 60 g shelled unsalted pistachios
  • 80 g candy canes
  • 2 tbsp dried chilli flakes
  • 50 g shredded coconut, toasted
  • 2 tbsp sea salt flakes
  • 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
554 kJ / 132 kcal
Protein
1.5 g
Carbohydrates

Preparation

Chocolate truffles

  1. Grease and line a square cake tin (20 cm).
  2. Place chocolate and cream into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat melting 3-4 min/50°C/speed 1, or until chocolate has melted.
  3. Transfer melted chocolate into prepared cake tin, then gently tap tin on work surface to distribute mixture evenly. Place tin into refrigerator for 2-3 hours, or until firm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Toppings

  1. Place pistachio nuts into mixing bowl and chop 2-6 sec/speed 6 or until an even chopped consistency is achieved. Transfer into a small serving bowl and set aside.
  2. Place candy canes into mixing bowl and crush 2-4 sec/speed 5 or until an even consistency is achieved. Transfer into a separate small serving bowl and set aside.
  3. Place dried chilli flakes, coconut and salt flakes into 3 separate small serving bowls and set each aside.
  4. Remove chocolate truffle mixture from refrigerator and bring to room temperature. Using a sharp knife with a thin blade, cut into 36 small squares (see Tips).
  5. Dust truffles with cocoa powder (optional), then place onto serving platter with toppings in 5 serving bowls and serve immediately.

Useful Items

baking paper, square cake tin (20 cm), 5 small serving bowls, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Wipe the blade of your knife clean with a wet kitchen towel, then dry blade with a dry kitchen towel between each cut to ensure the squares are neat.
  • This is an interactive dessert dish for entertaining. Your guests get to create their own chocolate truffle using as little or as many of the toppings as they like. Serve with toothpicks so the truffles are easy to pick up without getting sticky fingers.
  • The chocolate truffles can be made in advance and stored in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week (providing the cream is fresh when you make them). Separate layers of truffles with baking paper to store and whip up your toppings just before serving.
  • Be aware that the chilli flakes are hot. Don't add too much to each truffle if you don't like the heat.
