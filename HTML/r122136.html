Dukkah eggs with asparagus and feta

Ingredients

  • 50 g pistachio nuts
  • 20 g sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 2 tbsp cumin seeds
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 100 g Danish feta, cut into pieces
  • 30 g milk of choice
  • 500 g water
  • 4 eggs, chilled
  • 1 - 2 bunches green asparagus, ends trimmed
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling (optional)
  • 2 slices gluten free bread, toasted, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place pistachios into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sesame seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 1, until golden. Transfer into bowl with chopped pistachios and set aside.
  3. Place coriander seeds and cumin seeds into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until aromatic, then mill 30 sec/speed 9, or until finely crushed.
  4. Add salt, pepper and reserved pistachio mixture and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and set aside until ready to use (see Tips).
  5. Place feta and milk into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 6, or until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  7. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and place eggs into it. Place Varoma dish into position and place asparagus into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 9-10 min/Varoma/speed 1 until asparagus are tender and eggs are cooked to your liking (see Tips). Transfer asparagus into a thermal serving bowl or onto a plate and cover to keep warm. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and place eggs under cold running water to cease cooking. Set eggs aside to cool slightly.
  8. Place oil into a small bowl. Place 4 tablespoons of the reserved dukkah onto a plate. Once cooled, gently peel eggs and coat in oil, then roll in dukkah. Serve dukkah eggs with toast, steamed asparagus, creamed feta, a sprinkle of extra dukkah and a drizzle of extra oil (optional).

Useful Items

bowl, sealable storage jar or container, thermal serving bowl or plate, large plate, small bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • If using a firm feta, such as Greek or Australian, increase milk by up to 20 g for a creamy consistency.
  • You can store leftover dukkah in a sealable container for up to 1 month in a cool, dry place.
  • Using room temperature eggs or smaller eggs will reduce cooking time. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for timings for both soft and hard boiled eggs. If you prefer soft boiled eggs, take extra care when peeling.
