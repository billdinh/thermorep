Turkey Burgers with Caramelized Onions and Red Peppers

Ingredients

Turkey Burgers

  • 2 oz white bread (approx. 2 slices), roughly torn
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (approx. 4 in. long), leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh sage, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 3 ½ oz yellow onion, cut into quarters
  • 24 oz ground turkey
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Caramelized Onions and Red Peppers

  • 18 oz yellow onions (approx. 3-4), cut into eighths or sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and cut into thin strips (¼ in.)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces (½ in.-1 in.)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • olive oil, to fry burgers

Assembly

  • 6 Kaiser bread rolls
  • 5 ½ oz Swiss cheese, thinly sliced
  • 2 oz arugula leaves

Preparation

Turkey Burgers

  1. Place bread and herbs into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6.5.
  3. Add reserved bread-herb mixture, ground turkey, egg, salt and pepper and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Finish mixing together with spatula before removing from mixing bowl. With damp hands, shape mixture into 6 burgers and transfer onto a plate, cover with cling wrap and refrigerate until needed. Clean mixing bowl.

Caramelized Onions and Red Peppers

  1. Place all caramelized onion ingredients, except olive oil, into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed .
  2. Cook without measuring cup a further 10 min/Varoma//speed .
  3. Place a medium sized non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add a little olive oil. Cook burgers until golden brown (approx. 3-4 minutes each side) or until cooked to your preference.

Assembly

  1. Cut Kaiser rolls in half and toast, if desired. Top each roll with a cooked burger, slice of cheese, Caramelized Onions and Red Peppers and finish with arugula to serve.

Useful Items

cling wrap, bread knife, non-stick frying pan, stove, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make this an even quicker meal, fry your burgers on the stove top while the caramelized onion and red peppers finish cooking.
  • To grind your own meat: cut partially frozen meat into pieces (¾ in.-1 in.), place into mixing bowl and grind 8-15 sec/speed 6-7, or until desired consistency is achieved. Use ground meat immediately or place in refrigerator until ready to use. For a chunkier consistency, chop Turbo/1.0 sec/3-5 times. This process can be used with any kind of red meat, seafood or poultry. Chopping time will vary according to the type and weight of meat used.
  • Chicken burgers: replace the ground turkey with ground chicken.
