Tabbouleh-stuffed Peppers

Ingredients

  • 70 g bulgar wheat (see tip)
  • 120 g water, boiling
  • 4 peppers, mixed
  • 2 spring onions (approx. 35 g), cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 10 g fresh mint leaves
  • 120 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g ripe tomatoes, quartered, deseeded
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest and 30 g juice
  • 1 tsp baharat spice mix (see tip) (optional)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 100 g cucumber, diced (5 mm)
  • 80 g pomegranate seeds (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid then weigh in bulgar wheat and water. Cover with cling film and leave to soak for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Cut tops off peppers and discard. Remove pith and seeds from inside. Slice a little off the base so they sit flat but without making a hole. Place peppers on prepared tray, up side down, and bake for 15-20 minutes (200°C) until just softening. Meanwhile, make tabbouleh.
  4. Place spring onions, mint and 50 g parsley in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  5. Place remaining 70 g parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to bowl with herbs.
  6. Place tomatoes in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer to bowl with herbs.
  7. Place oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, baharat (if using), salt and pepper in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to bowl with herbs. When bulgar wheat has absorbed all the water, fluff with a fork to separate grains, add to bowl with herbs and stir well with spatula.
  8. Remove peppers from oven and carefully tip out any excess liquid. Divide tabbouleh mixture between peppers, filling each to the top. Bake for a further 10-15 minutes (200°C) until peppers are tender.
  9. Top peppers with cucumber and pomegranate seeds (if using) then serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

baking tray, baking paper, cling film, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If bulgar wheat is not available, substitute with couscous and adjust quantity of water and soaking time in step 2 according to packet instructions.
  • Baharat spice mix can be bought ready made or you can make your own. Place 1 Tbsp black peppercorns, 1 Tbsp coriander seeds, 1 Tbsp cumin seeds, ½ Tbsp allspice, ½ Tbsp ground nutmeg, 1 tsp ground sweet paprika, ½ tsp cardamom seeds, ¼ tsp whole cloves and 2 cinnamon sticks then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Store remaining baharat in an airtight container and add a pinch to soups and sauces or use as a rub on fish or meat.
  • Can be served as a starter or side dish.
