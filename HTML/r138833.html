Triple treat ice cream cake

Ingredients

Strawberry coulis

  • 350 g strawberries, hulled, fresh or frozen
  • 60 g water

Ice cream

  • 1 tbsp vanilla bean paste
  • 800 g full cream milk
  • 800 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 250 g white sugar
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 2 pinches salt (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder

Chocolate biscuits

  • 180 g plain flour
  • 50 g cocoa powder
  • 80 g icing sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 120 g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 egg

Chocolate sauce

  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 150 g dark chocolate, broken into pieces

Assembly

  • 150 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • lollies of choice, for decorating

Preparation

Strawberry coulis

  1. Place strawberries and water into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2. Strain coulis through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl; discard contents of sieve. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and transfer into refrigerator until needed. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Ice cream

  1. Place vanilla, milk, cream, sugar, egg yolks and salt (optional) into mixing bowl and cook 12 min/80°C/speed 3.5.
  2. Divide mixture between 3 large freezable bowls (approx. 700 g of the mixture into 2 bowls; approx. 550 g mixture into last bowl).
  3. Place contents of one of the 700 g bowls back into mixing bowl. Add cocoa powder and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer back into freezable bowl and set aside.
  4. Add reserved strawberry coulis to the bowl with 550 g reserved mixture and stir well to combine. Allow all 3 mixtures to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes), then place into freezer for a minimum of 10 hours or until frozen. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, make biscuits and chocolate sauce.

Chocolate biscuits

  1. Place flour, cocoa, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add butter and mix 10 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add egg and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a flat disc. Wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 1 hour or until firm enough to roll. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  5. Roll out dough between 2 sheets of baking paper into a large rectangle (26 x 40 cm). Remove top sheet of baking paper and cut into 4 portions (10 x 26 cm). Transfer biscuits with bottom sheet of baking paper onto baking tray and bake for 8-12 minutes (180°C), until firm and crisp. Allow to cool completely on baking tray, then set aside.

Chocolate sauce

  1. Place cream and chocolate into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/90°C/speed 1.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and heat 1 min/90°C/speed 1 or until melted and smooth. Transfer into a jug and set aside to cool (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place one of the chocolate biscuits into the bottom of a loaf tin (10 x 25 cm), trimming to fit if needed. Remove frozen strawberry ice cream from freezer and set aside for 15 minutes to soften, then cut into pieces (approx. 4 x 4 cm). Place ice cream pieces into mixing bowl and crush 2 min/speed 9, with aid of spatula. Transfer ice cream into loaf tin and spread evenly over biscuit. Top with another biscuit (trimmed to fit if needed) and place into freezer for 1 hour to set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Remove frozen vanilla ice cream from freezer and set aside for 15 minutes to soften, then cut into pieces (4 x 4 cm). Place ice cream pieces into mixing bowl and crush 2 min/speed 9, with aid of spatula. Transfer ice cream into loaf tin and spread evenly over biscuit. Top with another biscuit (trimmed to fit if needed) and return to freezer for 1 hour to set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Remove frozen chocolate ice cream from freezer and set aside for 15 minutes to soften, then cut into pieces (4 x 4 cm). Place ice cream pieces into mixing bowl and crush 2 min/speed 9, with aid of spatula. Transfer ice cream into loaf tin and spread evenly over biscuit. Top with another biscuit (trimmed to fit if needed) and return to freezer for 1 hour to set. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Remove ice cream cake from freezer. Quickly run base and sides of loaf tin under warm water to soften the sides, then turn out cake onto a serving platter. Return to freezer for approx. 30 minutes.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until stiff peaks form/speed 3, watching carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Remove cake from freezer and use a palette knife to scrape sides of ice cream cake to neaten edges. Spread cream decoratively on top of cake and return freezer for 15 minutes or until cream is just firm. Drizzle reserved chocolate sauce over cream and decorate with lollies of choice. Return cake to the freezer until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

fine mesh sieve, bowl, plastic wrap, freezable bowls or containers, silicone bread mat, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), rolling pin, baking paper, jug, deep-sided loaf tin (10 x 25 cm), serving platter, palette knife, refrigerator, freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If making chocolate sauce ahead of time, store in the refrigerator. Bring back to room temperature before using.
  • This recipe can be made up to 2 weeks in advance.
  • Remove cake from freezer 10 minutes to soften slightly before slicing and serving.
  • We decorated our cake using a variety of chocolate lollies. You can use any kind of lollies you like.
