Vienna sausages with split pea soup

Ingredients

  • 1 onion (approx. 150 g), cut into quarters
  • 50 g bacon, cut into pieces
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 100 g celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 150 g potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 150 g leek, trimmed and cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 200 g green split peas (see Tips)
  • 1000 g water
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp fresh marjoram, leaves only
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 Vienna sausages

Preparation

  1. Place onion, bacon, carrot, oil, celery and potatoes into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 5 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Add leek, peas, water, stock, marjoram and pepper, and cook 40 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Place Varoma dish into position then place sausages into it. Secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1. Remove Varoma and set aside.
  5. Transfer soup into serving bowls and serve with sausages on the side.

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • Split peas cook faster, however should you prefer to use whole dry peas they will need to be soaked overnight before using.
  • Vienna sausages can be substituted with kransky, block wurst or other similar sausages.
  • Celery can be substituted with celeriac.
  • Fresh marjoram can be substituted with 1 tsp dried marjoram.
