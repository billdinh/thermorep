Lemon, Almond and Olive Oil Cake

Ingredients

Cake

  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 125 g self-raising flour
  • 125 g ground almonds
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 3 lemons, grated zest and flesh only, minus white pith and pips
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 4 large eggs

Curd Topping

  • 125 g caster sugar
  • 65 g lemon juice
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 65 g butter, cold, diced
  • 40 g flaked almonds, lightly toasted

Preparation

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Grease and line base and sides of a Ø 20 cm spring form cake tin.
  2. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in flour, almonds and baking powder. Remove bowl and stir contents with spatula to combine.
  3. Grate lemon zest into bowl with flour then set bowl aside.
  4. Place olive oil, lemon flesh and salt in mixing bowl then blend 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place sugar and eggs in mixing bowl then whip 12 min/speed 4.
  6. Add half reserved lemon and oil mixture and half flour mixture then mix 20 sec/speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk.
  7. Add remaining lemon and oil mixture and remaining flour mixture then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to prepared cake tin and bake for 1 hour (150°C) or until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave in tin to cool for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make curd topping.

Curd Topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place sugar, lemon juice, egg, egg yolks and salt in mixing bowl then whip 12 min/80°C/speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Add butter and blend 40 sec/speed 4. Pass through a fine sieve into a bowl and chill in fridge until completely cold.
  3. Once cake and curd are completely cold, spread curd over surface of cake allowing it to drizzle down sides then garnish with flaked almonds and serve.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 20 cm), skewer, wire rack, fine sieve, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Store the cake in an airtight container (can be refrigerated) and eat within two days.
  • Swap lemons for oranges, grapefruit, or clementines in the cake and the curd.
  • For a dairy-free icing, mix 150 g icing sugar with 1-2 tsp fresh lemon juice. Pour over cooled cake and garnish with almonds.
  • This dairy-free sponge is simply delicious with a sharp, zesty taste from the lemons and fruitiness from the olive oil. The cake itself is dairy-free however I have garnished it with a delicious, smooth lemon curd that contains butter. If you prefer a dairy-free version I suggest a lemon icing instead of the curd (see variations). I used a good extra virgin olive oil that is quite bitter and fruity in taste however it’s your choice - you could use a lighter olive oil if you prefer, or even a cold pressed rapeseed oil would be perfect.
