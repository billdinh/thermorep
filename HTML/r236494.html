Irish stew

Ingredients

  • 200 g frozen green beans, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • 20 g salted butter
  • 100 g celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 400 g potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 60 g pearl barley
  • 300 g water
  • 150 g carrots, cut into rounds
  • 500 - 600 g diced lamb (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh beans into it. Set aside and allow beans to defrost.
  2. Place onion, garlic and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and sauté 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add celery, parsley and 200 g of the potatoes and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add barley, water, carrot, lamb, salt, pepper, bay leaves and stock paste to mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and weigh remaining 200 g of the potatoes into Varoma dish. Secure Varoma lid and cook 60 min/100°C//speed .
  6. Set Varoma aside and carefully add half of the cooked potatoes from Varoma into mixing bowl. Stir with aid of spatula and cook 5 min/100°C//speed .
  7. Add remaining potatoes and reserved green beans into mixing bowl and stir with aid of spatula. Cook 5-10 min/100°C//speed  or until lamb and potatoes are tender. Serve garnished with extra thyme.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • We recommend buying lamb that is already diced. If you choose to dice your own lamb, please adjust cooking time accordingly as the meat may become overcooked depending on the cut used.
