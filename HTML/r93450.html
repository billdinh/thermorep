Christmas cake

Ingredients

  • 1000 g mixed dried fruit
  • 50 g glacé cherries
  • 50 g dried apricots, chopped into pieces
  • 50 g glacé ginger, cut into pieces
  • 150 g sherry
  • 230 g brown sugar
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp natural almond extract
  • 14 tbsp Drambuie (approx. 280 g)
  • 150 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 110 g full cream milk
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 350 g plain flour

Preparation

  1. Place a large sealable container onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh mixed dried fruit, cherries, dried apricots, glacé ginger, sherry and brown sugar into it.
  2. Add spices, vanilla extract and almond extract to container and combine well with spatula. Set container aside overnight at room temperature.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of the Drambuie or ginger wine to the fruit mixture in the large container and stir contents well with spatula. Leave fruit mixture to soak again overnight. Repeat this process for the next 6 days with the remaining Drambuie or ginger wine, combining well each time with the spatula (see Tips).
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a cake tin (22 cm) and set aside.
  5. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 5 min/50°C/speed 1.
  6. Add all remaining ingredients and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer mixture into a large bowl with the soaked fruit and combine with spatula until just combined.
  7. Transfer mixture into prepared cake tin. Reduce oven temperature to 120°C and bake for 3-4 hours (120°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean.
  8. Allow cake to cool in tin for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack and allow to cool completely before serving or storing in a sealable container.

Useful Items

large sealable container, baking paper, cake tin (22 cm), large bowl, wooden skewer, wire rack, sealable storage container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can soak your dried fruit mixture for less than 7 days if preferred. Only add 2 tablespoons of Drambuie or ginger wine each day and reduce the total quantity required according to how many days you wish to soak your fruit. We recommend a minimum of 2 days to achieve the desired flavour.
  • A lovely Christmas tradition is to ensure that every member of your family is involved in making this cake. Ask each member to take turns stirring the fruit mix while it is soaking.
