Piri Piri Chicken with Pickled Curry Beans

Ingredients

  • 8 chicken legs, skin on and bone in
  • 250 g onions, quartered
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 200 g red peppers, cut in pieces
  • 12 fresh basil leaves
  • 1 - 1 ½ Tbsp chilli sauce, to taste
  • 2 Tbsp dried oregano
  • 130 g olive oil
  • 250 g fresh green beans, topped and tailed, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 90 g white wine vinegar
  • 80 g sugar
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp cornmeal, finely ground
  • 2 Tbsp water

Preparation

  1. Place chicken legs in a sealable bag and set aside.
  2. Place 100 g onions, garlic cloves, red peppers, basil leaves, red chilli sauce, oregano and 100 g olive oil in mixing bowl then chop 8 sec/speed 5. Transfer to sealable bag, mix with chicken then refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place 150 g onions and 30 g olive oil in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5, then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add green beans and cook 7 min/100°C//speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Place white wine vinegar, sugar, curry powder, ground turmeric and ½ tsp salt in mixing bowl then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, mix cornmeal with water in a small bowl until dissolved.
  6. Add dissolved maize meal mixture and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 2.5.
  7. Add reserved green beans and cook 2 min/100°C//speed . Transfer to a bowl, allow to cool for 30 minutes then refrigerate until serving. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  8. Preheat barbecue to medium heat.
  9. Remove chicken legs from bag, reserving piri piri marinade. Place chicken legs on preheated barbecue grill, season with 1 tsp salt and grill for 10-15 minutes on each side, turning chicken legs occasionally until cooked through. Meanwhile, prepare piri piri sauce.
  10. Place piri piri marinade in mixing bowl and cook 7 min/120°C/speed 1. Serve chicken legs with piri piri sauce and pickled curry beans.

Useful Items

sealable bag, refrigerator, barbecue

Hints & Tricks

  • In South Africa, pickled curry beans are called Kerrie-boontji. It is a very traditional dish, served with a variety of foods.
  • The pickled curry beans can be prepared up to 1 week ahead, and served cold as a salad side dish with your barbecue.
