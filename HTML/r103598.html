Porcini pepperleaf paté (Andrew Fielke)

Ingredients

  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 75 g brown onion
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 50 g unsalted butter
  • 10 g dried porcini mushrooms, crumbled
  • 150 g red wine
  • 250 g fresh Swiss brown mushrooms (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 200 g cream cheese
  • 2 tsp dried pepperleaf (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp Dijon mustard
  • fresh chives, cut into small pieces, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place rosemary and thyme into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat chopping 10 sec/speed 9, until fine. Repeat chopping if necessary to achieve a fine consistency. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add porcini mushrooms and red wine and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add mushrooms, salt and reserved chopped herbs and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1, then purée 20 sec/speed 5 -9, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 9, until smooth. Remove mixing bowl lid and allow to cool to room temperature in mixing bowl (approx. 30 minutes).
  7. Add cream cheese, pepperleaf and mustard and blend 20 sec/speed 5.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then blend 10 sec/speed 5, until smooth. Transfer into 2 large ramekins and place into refrigerator to set for a minimum of 2 hours. Garnish with chives before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, 2 ramekins, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

Andrew Fielke is a talented, award-winning Adelaide chef. His passion for Australia's native food resources led to helping pioneer a "Creative Native Australian Cuisine" known for creating wonderful dishes, utilising the unique textures and stunning flavours of nutritious nuts, seeds, fruits and herbs. Andrew founded the successful and famous Red Ochre Restaurants, but now works all over the world as a consulting/guest chef, as well as operating Creative Native Food Service and Tuckeroo retail businesses.

  • You can use either Swiss brown mushrooms or field mushrooms in this recipe.
  • Pepperleaf has the warm heat of black pepper, combined with a "bushy", eucalyptus flavour. You can find it online or in specialty spice stores. Otherwise, you can replace the pepperleaf with 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper.
  • Serve with crackers or slices of baguette toasted with extra virgin olive oil. Or try spreading over grilled steak or chicken.
