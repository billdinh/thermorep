Stewed apples with walnut crumble topping

Ingredients

Crumble

  • 50 g walnuts, plus extra for topping
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 100 g butter, chilled and cut into cubes
  • 50 g Rapadura sugar

Stewed apple

  • 2 apples, core removed and cut into quarters
  • 20 g butter
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • flaked coconut (optional)

Preparation

Crumble

  1. Place walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place flour and butter into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/1 sec/2-3 times, or until a breadcrumb consistency is achieved.
  3. Add Rapadura and reserved chopped walnuts and mix 3 sec/speed 3.  Transfer 60 - 80 g of the crumble mixture into a bowl and set aside for topping. Store remaining crumble mixture in a freezable container and place into freezer for future use.

Stewed apple

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place apples into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter and cinnamon and cook 5 min/100°C//speed .
  4. Transfer into 2 ramekins (8 cm) and top with reserved crumble mixture. Sprinkle with extra walnuts and coconut (optional).
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) or until topping is golden. Serve warm.

Useful Items

freezable containers (e.g. aluminium, silicone), 2 ramekins (8 cm), freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute Rapadura sugar for light brown sugar.
  • You can substitute flaked coconut for rolled oats.
  • This crumble is delicious served straight away with a side of Custard, yoghurt or cream.
  • Leftover apple filling can be stored in the refrigerator, chilled and served with Cloudy apple cider jelly and a dollop of mascarpone cheese as a quick summer time dessert.
  • Leftover walnut crumble can be kept in the freezer and enjoyed at a later date. Sprinkle over yoghurt or bircher muesli for a delicious breakfast.
  • Crumble and apple mix can be made ahead of time, stored in the refrigerator and assembled prior to baking on the day.
  • Experiment with different flavours by substituting one of the apples with 100 g pear or berries.
