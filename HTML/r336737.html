Eggless Pasta

Ingredients

  • 9 oz semolina flour
  • 5 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 1 pinch salt, plus extra to boil with water
  • 6 oz water, plus extra to boil pasta
  • 1 oz extra virgin olive oil

Preparation

  1. Place semolina flour, all-purpose flour, salt, water and olive oil into mixing bowl and knead 2 min/ÓÄÅ, until a crumbled dough forms. Transfer dough to a work surface and knead by hand until it becomes smooth and homogeneous. Wrap in cling wrap and let rest at room temperature for at least one hour.
  2. Transfer dough to a well-floured work surface and divide into four parts. Roll each piece of dough with a rolling pin into an elongated shape and feed through the pasta machine while guiding the dough. Run dough through pasta machine with rollers set on most open setting; fold dough into thirds and roll it back through the pasta maker. If dough sticks, coat with a little more flour. Fold and roll the dough three more times on this setting. Adjust rollers to the next level of thinness and guide dough back through the machine with the back of your hand, repeating the process until dough is approximately 1/16 inch thick. Keep remaining dough covered to prevent from drying out. Repeat with remaining dough. Cut pasta into desired shapes (see Tip).
  3. Cook the pasta on stove top in plenty of boiling and salted water (approx. 1 tbsp salt per 16 cups water) for 2-4 minutes depending on shape and thickness of dough. Drain pasta and serve warm, seasoned with sauce to taste.

Useful Items

cling wrap, pasta machine, rolling pin, large saucepot (approx. 4-5 qt.), stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace all-purpose flour with 00 extra fine unbleached flour for a more supple and malleable dough that is easy to work with.
  • Cut dough into desired shapes according to the type of pasta you want to prepare. Lasagna or ravioli: cut dough into rectangles (8 in. x 4 in.). Tagliatelle: cut into strips (approx. 1/16 in. x 12 in.). Bow ties: cut small rectangles (1 in. x 2 in.) and pinch the center of each rectangle to form a bow tie.
