Roosterkoek (Barbecue Bread) with Boerewors Burgers

Ingredients

Bread Dough

  • 180 g water
  • 30 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 10 g sugar
  • 10 g fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 300 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Boerewors Burgers

  • 1 Tbsp coriander seeds
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 ½ tsp black peppercorns
  • 20 g sunflower oil
  • 400 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • 400 g pork mince, broken in pieces
  • 30 g red wine

Tomato Sauce, Barbecue Bread and Burgers

  • 200 g onions, quartered
  • 5 g fresh parsley, leaves only
  • 30 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 10 g sugar

Preparation

Bread Dough

  1. Place water, sunflower oil, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add bread flour and salt then knead 1 min/. Meanwhile, line a baking tray with baking paper and grease a bowl and spatula with sunflower oil.
  3. Transfer dough to prepared bowl using oiled spatula, brush dough surface with sunflower oil, cover with cling film and allow to rest in a warm place for 1 hour. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, continue with boerewors burgers.

Boerewors Burgers

  1. Place coriander seeds, cloves, ground nutmeg, black peppercorns and sunflower oil in mixing bowl, toast 2 min/120°C/speed 1, then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add beef mince, pork mince and red wine then mix 15 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl. With damp hands, shape mixture into 6 burgers, then transfer to a plate, cover and set aside.

Tomato Sauce, Barbecue Bread and Burgers

  1. Transfer bread dough to a well-floured surface, divide dough into 6 equal pieces then shape into slightly flattened balls. Place on prepared baking tray, cover with a kitchen towel and leave to rise for a further 20 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Place onions, parsley and oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 8 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, black pepper, ½ tsp salt and sugar then cook 12 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat barbecue to a low heat.
  4. Grease metal grill with oil, place the slightly flattened balls of dough on the grill, cover with aluminium foil and grill for 3-5 minutes. Remove foil, turn roosterkoek over and grill for 3-5 minutes or until they are lightly browned, crispy on the outside and sound hollow when tapped. Season burgers with 1 tsp salt and grill for 3-4 minutes each side, or until cooked as desired. Serve barbecue breads warm with burgers and tomato sauce.

Useful Items

cling film, tea towel, aluminium foil, baking tray and paper, pastry brush, barbecue

Hints & Tricks

  • Boerewors is a type of sausage which originated in South Africa, and is very popular in South African cuisine. The name is derived from the Afrikaans words boer ("farmer") and wors ("sausage"). It can be made with lamb, beef or pork meat, and this version uses a beef-pork meat mixture.

    Roosterkoek, literally translated as "grill cake", represents a classic bread to supplement a braai or barbeque. They consist of grilled balls of bread dough and are usually served hot.
  • The roosterkoek can be cooked in the oven. Preheat oven to 180°C, place on a baking tray lined with baking paper and bake for 15-20 minutes.
