Baked White Chocolate Caramel Cheesecake

Ingredients

Biscuit Base

  • 90 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 280 g digestive biscuits

White Chocolate Filling

  • 230 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 110 g double cream
  • 700 g cream cheese
  • 220 g granulated sugar
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Caramel Sauce

  • 140 g soft brown sugar
  • 200 g double cream
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Preparation

Biscuit Base

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease and line base and sides of a spring form tin (Ø 26 cm).
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/90°C/speed 1.
  3. Add biscuits then chop 15 sec/speed 8. Transfer to prepared tin, pressing down with the back of a spoon forming an even layer over base, then push mixture evenly halfway up sides of tin. Set aside in fridge. Clean mixing bowl and make filling.

White Chocolate Filling

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add cream then melt 3 min 30 sec/50°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add cream cheese and sugar then beat 20 sec/speed 5.5.
  4. Add eggs, vanilla extract and salt then mix 15 sec/speed 6. Place tin on a large baking tray then pour mixture into tin over biscuit base. Bake for 1 hour (160°C) until browned and risen with a slight wobble.
  5. Leave in tin on a wire rack to cool completely - the cheesecake will sink considerably (see tip). Once cool, chill in fridge for at least 4 hours. Meanwhile, make caramel sauce.

Caramel Sauce

  1. Place soft brown sugar, cream, butter and lemon juice in mixing bowl then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool.
  2. Once cool and cheesecake has set, pour caramel sauce over centre of cheesecake. Leave at room temperature for 1 hour before removing from tin and serving.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 26 cm), large baking tray, wire rack, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Run a knife around the edge of the cheesecake as soon as it comes out of the oven - this will prevent cracks appearing in the surface.

This baked cheesecake is rich and creamy, with a sweetness added by the caramel and the perfectly crunchy base. A winning dessert option at any time of year.
