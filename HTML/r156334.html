Spinach and cheese-filled meatloaf

Ingredients

  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 50 g brown onions, cut into halves
  • 1 ½ - 2 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 40 g breadcrumbs
  • 400 g beef mince
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 50 g Gruyère cheese, cut into slices (2-3 mm)
  • 25 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 450 g yellow capsicum, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 4 - 5 fresh basil, leaves only
  • 400 g chopped tomatoes, canned or fresh cut into pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 200 g water
  • 1 pinch sugar (optional)

Preparation

  1. Grease Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese, onion, 1 of the garlic cloves and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add breadcrumbs, beef mince, eggs, pepper and 1 tsp of the salt and knead 1 min/. Transfer mixture onto a sheet of baking paper (40 cm x 40 cm) and spread out to create a rectangular shape (approx. 25 cm x 20 cm, 1 cm thick). Arrange Gruyère cheese and baby spinach evenly over the meat.
  4. Roll up meat with aid of baking paper to create a loaf shape. Remove baking paper and place meatloaf into greased Varoma dish.
  5. Place oil, ½-1 garlic cloves, capsicum and basil into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5, then sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, water, remaining 1 tsp salt and sugar (optional), place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Set Varoma aside and allow meatloaf to cool for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, insert measuring cup and blend sauce 40 sec/speed 8. Carefully remove meatloaf from Varoma, cut into slices and serve with sauce.

Hints & Tricks

  • Now this is not your ordinary meatloaf. Carefully rolled around a Gruyère-spinach filling, gently steamed to preserve a tender and moist interior and served with a fruity tomato and yellow capsicum sauce, it’s excellent both hot and cold. Great for a lunchbox or a picnic, this meatloaf can also be served at an elegant luncheon or a family supper.
  • Gruyère cheese can be replaced with other cheeses (e.g. scamorza, Gouda).
    - For an easy side dish, in step 6 steam up to 400 g vegetables (e.g. carrots, potatoes, green beans, courgettes, broccoli), cut in bite-size pieces, in simmering basket. Remove simmering basket when the vegetables are cooked to your liking.
