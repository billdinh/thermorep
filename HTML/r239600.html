Amaretti Stuffed Peaches with Saffron Zabaglione - Pesche ripiene con Amaretti e zabaione allo zafferano

Ingredients

Amaretti Biscuit

  • 1 egg white, from large egg
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 120 g ground almonds
  • ½ tsp almond extract

Peach Filling

  • 4 peaches, large, firm
  • 1 peach, peeled, pitted, quartered
  • 30 - 40 g flaked almonds, to taste
  • butter, for greasing

Zabaglione

  • 60 g dry Marsala wine
  • 60 g dry white wine
  • 120 g sugar
  • 5 saffron threads
  • 6 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

Amaretti Biscuit

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg white and sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 1 min 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add ground almonds and almond extract then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to prepared baking tray and spread out to a thickness of 1 cm. Bake for 15 minutes (180°C). Set aside to cool. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

Peach Filling

  1. Using a sharp knife, halve 4 peaches and remove stones. Scoop flesh out of each half, leaving a 1 cm thick shell.
  2. Place flesh from remaining peach, scooped-out peach flesh and broken up cooled amaretti biscuit in mixing bowl then chop 4 sec/speed 4.
  3. Lightly butter a baking dish and arrange peach halves in it, cut side up. Divide peach-amaretti filling between peach halves then top with flaked almonds. Bake for 18-20 minutes (180°C), until peaches are tender and filling is golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make zabaglione.

Zabaglione

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place Marsala wine, white wine, sugar, saffron, egg yolks and salt in mixing bowl then heat 9 min/70°C/speed 3.
  2. Serve peaches warm with hot zabaglione poured over.

Useful Items

large baking tray, baking paper, sharp knife, baking dish, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baked fruit is a popular way to end a meal, and the combination of sweet peaches and indulgent zabaglione make this dessert extra special. Best made in the summer months when peaches are in season, the fresh fruit is stuffed with crushed homemade amaretti biscuit then baked until the fruit is juicy and the topping is crispy. Accompanied by the rich Italian classic, zabaglione, a custard made with egg yolks, sugar and Marsala wine, this dessert is a triumph.
