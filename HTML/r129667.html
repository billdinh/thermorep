Raspberry and Orange Jaffa Cakes

Ingredients

Jelly

  • vegetable oil, for greasing
  • 150 g fresh raspberries
  • 250 g orange juice
  • 4 gelatine leaves
  • 1 - 2 Tbsp caster sugar

Cakes

  • 100 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 75 g caster sugar
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 125 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Topping

  • 200 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets

Preparation

Jelly

  1. Grease a shallow baking tray (20 cm x 30 cm) with oil and line with cling film.
  2. Place raspberries and orange juice in mixing bowl then boil 4 min/100°C/speed 0.5. Meanwhile, soak gelatine leaves in cold water for 5 minutes until soft. Press contents of mixing bowl through a fine sieve to extract as much liquid as possible. Rinse mixing bowl.
  3. Place strained liquid, 1-2 Tbsp sugar, to taste, and squeezed out gelatine leaves in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 1. Pour into prepared baking tray. Leave to cool at room temperature then chill in fridge for 4 hours or overnight, until set firm.

Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C and grease 2 x 12 hole bun or muffin tins.
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Mix at speed 3 while adding eggs one at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid, allowing first one to fully incorporate before adding the next.
  4. Add vanilla extract and mix 10 sec/speed 3.
  5. Add flour, baking powder and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 3. Divide between prepared tins (1 heaped tsp per hole) and level with the back of a wet teaspoon. Bake for 10-12 minutes (170°C) until pale golden around the edges. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  6. Run a palette knife around each cake (if necessary) and transfer to a wire rack to cool. When cakes are cool, use a knife to slice off any peaked tops.

Topping and Assembly

  1. Using the cling film, lift jelly layer from the baking tray onto a chopping board. Use a 4-5 cm cookie cutter to cut out discs of jelly. Use a palette knife to place each disc carefully on the cakes. Arrange cakes on a wire rack set over a baking tray then set aside in fridge.
  2. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 3 min/40°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix without heat 2 min/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 1 min/speed 1.
  3. Carefully spoon chocolate over the top of each cake, covering the jelly completely, tapping the wire rack to let any excess chocolate drip off (see tip). Leave to set for 20-40 seconds then gently press the tines of a fork into the chocolate to make a ridge pattern.
  4. Leave for at least 2 hours for the jelly to re-set before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray, cling film, fine sieve, 2 x 12 hole bun tins, palette knife, wire rack, cookie cutter, chopping block , refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If your bun tins are not non-stick, line the bases with a small disc of baking paper.
  • If the melted chocolate begins to set before you have finished covering the biscuits, re-melt it, 1 min/40°C/speed 1, then scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Repeat once more if necessary until chocolate is melted.
  • It is possible to use a packet of fruit jelly set in a thin layer, instead of making your own.
  • Frozen raspberries can be substituted for fresh.
