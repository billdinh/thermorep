Bhuna gosht

Ingredients

  • 1 - 2 brown onions (approx. 180 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves, stalks and roots (approx. 20 g), cut into thirds, plus extra to serve (see Tips)
  • 30 g vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 5 cardamom pods (green), crushed
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 800 g boneless lamb shoulder, cut into cubes (4 cm) (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 ½ tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 10 g garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 20 g ginger paste (see Tips)
  • natural yoghurt, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place onion and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add mustard seeds, cardamom pods, cinnamon and bay leaves and cook 40 sec/Varoma//speed 1.
  4. Add reserved onion mixture and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  5. Add lamb and cook 2 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add tomato paste, chilli powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, pepper, garlic paste and ginger paste and cook 8 min/100°C//speed , then cook 40-50 min/90°C//speed  or until lamb is tender. Serve with yoghurt, extra coriander and side of choice.

Hints & Tricks

  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander with a spoon to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • You can replace the boneless lamb shoulder with boneless lamb leg, if preferred.
  • To make your own garam masala, garlic paste and ginger paste, please refer to the recipes on this chip.
  • We served ours with Garlic and coriander naan (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip), but you can pair it with any bread or rice you choose.
