Coq au Vin

Ingredients

Marinade and Searing Chicken

  • 4 chicken thighs, skin on and bone in
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 2 oz shallots, in pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp dried rosemary
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 5 oz extra virgin olive oil, plus 2 tsp to sear
  • 10 oz strong dry red wine, e.g. red Burgundy wine

Coq au Vin

  • 3 pieces thick cut bacon, chilled
  • ½ oz dried porcini mushrooms
  • 3 oz carrots, peeled, in rounds (¼ in.)
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 oz pearl onions, peeled, thawed if frozen
  • 3 oz fresh cremini mushrooms, quartered
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • fresh baguette, to serve

Preparation

Marinade and Searing Chicken

  1. Place chicken and bay leaf into a large zip lock bag. Set aside.
  2. Place shallots, garlic, parsley, dried thyme, rosemary, salt and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add red wine and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer marinade into zip lock bag with chicken. Refrigerate 8 hours or overnight.
  4. Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat. Remove chicken thighs from marinade, gently pat chicken dry with a paper towel and reserve the marinade. Add 2 tsp oil to skillet, place chicken in hot skillet skin side down and sear until browned (approx. 3-5 min. per side). Set aside and keep warm. Meanwhile make the Coq au Vin.

Coq au Vin

  1. Place bacon and dried porcini mushrooms into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Without measuring cup sauté 20 min/250°F/speed .
  2. Add carrots and olive oil and without measuring cup cook 5 min/250°F//speed 1.
  3. Add reserved marinade, pearl onions, fresh cremini mushrooms, flour and thyme and without measuring cup cook 10 min/212°F//speed .
  4. Add reserved seared chicken thighs and juices and without measuring cup cook a further 10-13 min/195°F//speed , until chicken is cooked through. Remove bay leaf. Divide mixture between two bowls, garnish with additional thyme leaves and serve hot with fresh baguette.
