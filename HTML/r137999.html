Bun bo hue (noodle soup)

Ingredients

Stock

  • 400 g oxtail pieces (5-6 cm) (see Tips)
  • 450 - 550 g pork rashers, rind removed and meat cut into halves
  • 4 stalks fresh lemongrass, cut into halves, white part bruised, green part cut into thin slices
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp salt, plus extra to season
  • 900 - 1000 g water, plus extra as needed (approx. 300-400 g)

Soup

  • 100 g bean shoots, to serve
  • 100 - 150 g purple cabbage, cut into thin slices, to serve
  • 2 spring onions/shallots, cut into thin slices on the diagonal, to serve
  • 8 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, to serve
  • 6 sprigs fresh Thai basil, leaves only, to serve
  • 1 fresh long red chilli, cut into thin slices, to serve
  • 1 white onion (approx. 150 g), cut into thin slices
  • water, for soaking, plus extra as needed
  • 200 g flat rice stick noodles
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 30 g oil
  • 2 tsp annatto seeds (see Tips)
  • 70 g eschalot
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 3 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar (2 cm cube)

Preparation

Stock

  1. Place oxtail, pork, white parts of the lemongrass, onion, shrimp paste, salt and enough water to reach maximum 2L mark (approx. 900-1000 g) into mixing bowl and cook 60 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  2. Top up with extra water to reach maximum 2L mark again (approx. 300-400 g) and cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  3. Carefully remove bones and meat and place into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Line simmering basket with 2 layers of paper towel. Place simmering basket over a large jug and strain stock into it. Set jug aside. Discard contents of simmering basket. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Soup

  1. Place a large plate or bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh bean shoots and cabbage onto it.
  2. Add spring onion/shallots, coriander, Thai basil and chillies to plate. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.
  3. Place white onion slices into a bowl and cover with water. Set aside to soak for 10 minutes. Meanwhile continue with recipe. Using simmering basket drain and set aside.
  4. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh noodles into it. Cover with boiling water and leave to soak for 10-15 minutes or until soft. Drain and set aside.
  5. Place oil and annatto seeds into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1. Strain oil into a small bowl and set aside. Discard annatto seeds.
  6. Place eschalot, garlic, chilli flakes and reserved lemongrass greens into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add reserved annatto oil and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1 , without measuring cup. Transfer half of the mixture into a bowl and set aside.
  8. Add fish sauce and sugar into mixing bowl. Weigh reserved stock back into mixing bowl (note weight). Top up with extra water until it reaches 1200 g, then heat 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  9. Divide reserved rice noodles and onion slices evenly between 6 serving bowls.
  10. Remove meat from bones. Divide meat and pork between bowls. Pour over hot stock, then top with reserved lemongrass mixture and reserved fresh vegetables, herbs and chillies. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

glass bowl or large jug, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, paper towel, large plate, plastic wrap, bowl, fine mesh sieve, small bowl , serving bowls, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can buy oxtail from the meat section in most major supermarkets or at your butcher.
  • You can find shrimp paste in the international aisle of most major supermarkets.
  • You can find annatto seeds at specialty food stores, spice shops or online.
  • To save time, prepare the soup ingredients while the stock is cooking, or make the stock the day before.
