Cheesecakes in jars

Ingredients

Crumb base

  • 20 g butter
  • 40 g digestive biscuits
  • 20 g nuts (e.g. hazelnuts, walnuts, pistachios, almonds)
  • 20 g brown sugar

Filling

  • 300 g cream cheese
  • 50 g cream, min. 30% fat
  • 60 g sugar (caster sugar)
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 400 g water

Topping

  • 200 g mixed red berries, fresh or frozen
  • 40 g sugar

Preparation

Crumb base

  1. Place butter, digestive biscuits, nuts and brown sugar in mixing bowl and crush 10 sec/speed 7. Divide crumb mixture among 6 heat-resistant glass jars (Ø 6 cm, height 8 cm). Use a spoon or fingers to press crumb mixture down.

Filling

  1. Place cream cheese, cream, sugar, vanilla sugar, salt and eggs in mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 4. Pour cheesecake filling over crumb base in glass jars. Cover jars with heat-resistant cling film or lids and place in Varoma dish. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl, set Varoma in position and steam 22 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and set aside uncovered to cool a little. Discard water and proceed with the topping.

Topping

  1. Place red berries and sugar in mixing bowl and cook 11 min/100°C/speed 1. Top each cheesecake with 2 tbsp berry jam. Allow cheesecakes to cool completely, then refrigerate cheesecakes for at least 2 hours and serve chilled.

Useful Items

heat-resistant glass jars (Ø 6 cm, height 8 cm), heat-resistant cling film, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • These individual cheesecakes may seem ideal for portion control, but willpower is necessary to eat only one! They can be made ahead, brought to a picnic, offered as a gift, frozen for later, adapted to individual tastes… a great trick to have up your sleeve.
  • Add flavourings to the filling (e.g. zest from one lemon or lime, ½ tsp ground cinnamon).
  • For a nut-free version, omit nuts and use 60 g biscuits.
  • For a lighter version, omit the crumb base.
  • Instead of cooked berries, top with fresh seasonal fruit, caramel sauce or chocolate ganache.
  • Once thoroughly chilled, these cheesecakes can be frozen for serving later.
  • Instead of jars use small heat-resistant drinking glasses or coffee cups.
