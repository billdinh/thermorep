Moroccan Lamb Meatballs with Batbout Flatbreads

Ingredients

Batbout dough

  • 320 g water
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 20 g fresh yeast
  • 10 gram olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 80 g wholemeal flour
  • 240 g semolina, durum wheat
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt

Lamb meatballs

  • olive oil, for greasing and frying
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 5 - 6 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only, to taste
  • 600 g lamb meat for stew, boneless, cut in cubes (2 cm)
  • 2 ½ tsp hot paprika
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 6 - 7 sprigs fresh parsley, leaves only, to taste
  • 600 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 200 g water
  • 20 g sunflower oil

Preparation

Batbout dough

  1. Place 200 g water, sugar, yeast and olive oil in mixing bowl and warm 2 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add plain and wholemeal flour, semolina, remaining 120 g water and salt and knead 5 min/. Transfer dough to a lightly oiled surface.
  3. Gently flatten and roll dough loosely into a log shape. Divide into 8 equal size pieces, and use oiled hands to form each into a ball. Cover flat breads with a damp kitchen towel and allow to rest for approx. 1 hour. Continue with the recipe after the first 30 minutes resting time.

Lamb meatballs

  1. Grease Varoma dish and tray.
  2. Place 2 garlic cloves and mint in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  3. Add lamb meat and chop 10 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add 1 tsp paprika, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp ground cayenne and black pepper and mix 15 sec/speed 4.5. Transfer to a bowl, with oiled hands, shape small meatballs (Ø 3 cm) and place them in prepared Varoma dish and tray. Set aside.
  5. Place 2 garlic cloves and parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, water, sunflower oil, 1½ tsp paprika powder, 1 tsp ground cumin, ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper and ½ tsp salt. Set Varoma in position and cook 27 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat very lightly oiled griddle pan over medium heat, then oil the work surface and push rested dough balls into a flat circle (approx. Ø 10 cm, 1.5 cm thick). When pan is hot, cook batbout in batches, turning several times, until golden brown on both sides. Transfer to a wire rack. Repeat until all flatbreads are cooked.
  7. Remove Varoma. Serve moroccan lamb meatballs hot with sauce and batbout flatbread.

Useful Items

tea towel, griddle pan, wire rack, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • If you use minced lamb, add it in step 7.
  • These flatbreads are very versatile, and can be served as an accompaniment.
