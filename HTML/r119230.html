Choc-mint milk

Ingredients

  • 300 g shredded coconut (see Tips)
  • 8 fresh mint leaves (optional)
  • 2000 g filtered water, chilled
  • 20 - 30 g raw cacao powder, to taste
  • 100 g dark coconut nectar syrup (see Tips)
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
583 kJ / 139 kcal
Protein
1.7 g
Carbohydrates
7.2 g
Fat
11.2 g

Preparation

  1. Place coconut, mint (optional) and 1000 g of the water into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 9. Strain coconut milk through a nut milk bag or fine mesh sieve into a jug (squeeze nut milk bag firmly or press down on sieve firmly to extract as much coconut milk as possible). Reserve coconut pulp. Set coconut milk aside.
  2. Place remaining 1000 g water and reserved coconut pulp into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 9. Strain coconut milk through a nut milk bag or fine mesh sieve into jug with reserved coconut milk (squeeze nut milk bag firmly or press down on sieve firmly to extract as much coconut milk as possible). Discard coconut pulp. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place cacao powder, coconut nectar and approx. 1800-1900 g of the reserved coconut milk into mixing bowl (do not exceed the 2L maximum capacity mark) and mix 2 min/speed 3, until well combined. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable storage jar and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Useful Items

nut milk bag or fine mesh sieve, jug, storage jar, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace shredded coconut with flaked coconut in this recipe.
  • Adjust the taste/sweetness of this drink by increasing dark coconut nectar to taste.
  • You can make this drink with your milk of choice. If using rice milk, you will need to adjust the amount of cacao and dark coconut nectar to taste, as it has quite a different taste from other milks.
  • This drink can be made up to 3 days in advance. The mint flavour will develop over time.

Choc-Banana Milk

200 g dairy free milk chocolate

1000 g rice milk, chilled

1 banana, cut into pieces and frozen

4 pitted Medjool dates, to taste

100 g ice cubes

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 8 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add rice milk and heat 5 min/100°C/speed 2, or until chocolate melts. Transfer into a jug and place into refrigerator until cold (approx. 1-2 hours or overnight). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place banana, dates and ice into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add cold chocolate milk and blend 1 min/speed 8. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable jar and place into refrigerator to chill.
