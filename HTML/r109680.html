Peanut Butter Cheesecake

Ingredients

Base

  • 50 g butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g chocolate bourbons

Filling

  • 200 g cream cheese (full fat)
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tsp real vanilla extract
  • 120 g peanuts, dry-roasted, salted, plus extra for serving
  • 150 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets

Topping

  • 120 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 80 g whipping cream

Peanut Brittle Decoration

  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 50 g peanuts, dry-roasted, salted

Preparation

Base

  1. Grease sides and line base of a spring form tin (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min/70°C/speed 1.
  3. Add biscuits and crush 10 sec/speed 7. Transfer to prepared tin and press down with back of a spoon then set aside in fridge to chill. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C.
  2. Place cream cheese, sugar, eggs and vanilla in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place peanuts in mixing bowl and grind 8 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides and base of mixing bowl with spatula then grind again 8 sec/speed 8.
  4. Add 300 g reserved filling and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 1 min 30 sec/50°C/speed 3. Scrape down with spatula then melt again 1 min/50°C/speed 3.
  6. Add remaining 300 g filling and mix 20 sec/speed 3. Pour into tin over biscuit layer.
  7. Gently pour reserved peanut butter mixture over chocolate layer.
  8. Bake for 50 minutes (150°C) until almost set. Allow to cool in tin for 1 hour before carefully running a knife around edge of tin and removing. Place on a serving plate or cake stand. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make topping.

Topping

  1. Place milk chocolate and cream in mixing bowl then melt 3 min/60°C/speed 1. Set aside to thicken for 30 minutes then pour over top of cheesecake, allowing it to drizzle down sides. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours (or overnight) to set. Meanwhile, prepare peanut brittle.

Peanut Brittle Decoration

  1. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Heat sugar in a dry frying pan over a medium heat, without stirring, until sugar begins to clump together. Swirl and tilt pan until sugar is completely molten and turns caramel in colour - do not be tempted to stir as this will cause the caramel to crystalise.
  2. Stir in peanuts then immediately pour peanut mixture onto prepared baking tray. Quickly spread mixture to 5 mm thick using the back of a metal spoon. Set aside to cool completely (approx. 15 minutes).
  3. When ready to serve, break up peanut brittle and arrange over top of cheesecake before slicing.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, serving plate or cake stand, frying pan, baking tray and paper, refrigerator, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Shop bought peanut butter can also be used, but there is extra satisfaction gained from making everything yourself from scratch!
