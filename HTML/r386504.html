Polenta zucchini slice

Ingredients

  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing and brushing
  • 2 zucchinis, 1 deseeded and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 60 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ red capsicum (approx. 50 g), deseeded and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 700 g filtered water
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • 1 ½ tbsp stock paste (see Tips)
  • 150 g polenta
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts
  • vegan Parmesan cheese, for sprinkling (optional)

Preparation

  1. Grease a slice tin (25 x 16 cm) well with oil and set aside. Line a plate with paper towel.
  2. Using a vegetable peeler, peel 1 zucchini into ribbons and place onto lined plate to drain, discarding seeded core (see Tips).
  3. Place a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh spinach into it. Set aside.
  4. Place onion, garlic, capsicum, carrot and oil into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 3 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add chopped zucchini and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into bowl with spinach and cover to keep warm.
  7. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  8. Place water, oregano and stock paste into mixing bowl and heat 7 min/100°C/speed 1 or until water is boiling.
  9. Add polenta and pepper and cook 25 min/100°C/speed 3, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer polenta into bowl with vegetables and stir to combine.
  10. Transfer polenta mixture into prepared slice tin, smooth surface and decorate with reserved zucchini ribbons. Brush with oil, sprinkle with pine nuts and vegan Parmesan cheese (optional), then bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C), until golden brown and set. Set aside to cool for a minimum of 40 minutes. Serve warm or place into refrigerator to firm before serving cold (see Tips).

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, plate, paper towel, pastry brush, vegetable peeler, slice tin (25 x 16 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use the discarded zucchini core to make stock paste, dehydrated crackers, smoothies, soups or stews.
  • Please refer to Mushroom stock paste recipe on this recipe chip or The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • To make your own Plant-based parmesan cheese, refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can store this slice in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
