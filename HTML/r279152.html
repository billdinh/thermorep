Pumpkin fritters

Ingredients

  • 500 g butternut pumpkin flesh, peeled and cut into pieces (2 cm - see Tips)
  • 50 g water
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 60 g plain flour
  • 2 eggs
  • oil, for frying
  • cinnamon sugar, for sprinkling
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
190.7 kJ / 45.4 kcal

1.7 g

1.5 g

Preparation

  1. Place pumpkin, water and salt into mixing bowl and cook 8-10 min/120°C/speed 1, until pumpkin is completely soft and free of any lumps. Set aside in mixing bowl without lid to cool for 5 minutes.
  2. Add baking powder, flour and eggs and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 3 sec/speed 4 or until smooth.
  4. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Working in batches, place heaped tablespoonfuls of pumpkin mixture into pan and fry for 3-4 minutes on each side, until crispy and brown (makes approx. 18 fritters in total).
  5. Serve fritters warm, sprinkled with cinnamon sugar (see Tips).

Useful Items

paper towel, plate, large frying pan, serving plate, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use pumpkin of choice for this recipe. As the moisture content of pumpkin varies, you may need to add more flour to your mixture (it needs to hold its shape).
  • Pumpkin fritters sprinkled with cinnamon sugar are a traditional South African side dish and are served with stews, grilled meats or as a snack.
  • You can serve fritters with crumbled feta, blue cheese and/or chopped nuts as a savoury option or drizzled with syrup or honey for a sweet option.
  • Gluten free pumpkin fritters: replace the plain flour and baking powder with gluten free plain flour and gluten free baking powder. Proceed as per recipe.
