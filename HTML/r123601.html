Ricotta, Mint and Courgette Lasagne

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 eggs

Ricotta, Mint and Spinach Layer

  • 1500 g whole milk
  • 40 g white malt vinegar
  • ½ - 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith, finely grated
  • 8 fresh mint leaves, plus extra for garnishing
  • 20 g fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Courgette, Garlic and Pea Layer

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 500 g courgettes, cut into pieces
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 100 g frozen green peas
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 60 g dry white wine
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only

Parmesan Béchamel

  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 500 g whole milk
  • 50 g plain flour
  • 30 g butter, softened

Preparation

Pasta

  1. Grease a casserole dish or deep-sided baking tray (20 x 20 x 5 cm) with oil and set aside.
  2. Place flour, eggs and 1 tbsp olive oil in mixing bowl then knead 2 min/. Dough will appear crumbly.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and work into a ball. Wrap dough in cling film, then leave to rest for 15 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Using a rolling pin or pasta machine, roll out dough until 2 mm in thickness. Sprinkle extra flour onto pasta to prevent it from sticking. Cut pasta dough into 10 sheets (approx. 20 x 15 cm) and set aside.

Ricotta, Mint and Spinach Layer

  1. Place milk in mixing bowl and heat 10 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 2 min/90°C/speed 1. Milk will separate into curds and whey. Leave to rest for 1 minute.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Gently pour ricotta into simmering basket and leave to drain for 15 minutes. Stir through lemon zest and set ricotta aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place mint leaves in mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add spinach leaves and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add salt and reserved ricotta mixture then combine 10 sec//speed 3. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Courgette, Garlic and Pea Layer

  1. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add courgettes and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add peas, salt and pepper then mix 4 sec/speed 4.
  5. Add wine and thyme leaves then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Parmesan Béchamel

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place milk, flour and butter in mixing bowl then cook 7 min/90°C/speed 4.
  4. Add reserved grated Parmesan and mix 20 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. To assemble, cover base of prepared casserole dish or baking tray with 3 pasta sheets, overlapping each sheet slightly. Spread ½ ricotta mixture over the pasta, then place another layer of pasta sheets over the top. Spread ½ courgette mixture over pasta, then top with ¼ béchamel. Place another layer of pasta over top of béchamel and repeat layering process until all ingredients have been used, finishing with a layer of pasta and topped with a layer of béchamel.
  6. Bake for 30 minutes (180°C) or until cooked and lightly golden.
  7. Season with pepper, garnish with mint leaves and serve hot.

Useful Items

casserole dish or deep-sided baking tray (20 x 20 x 5 cm), silicone bread mat, cling film, large bowl, rolling pin or pasta machine, silicone pastry mat, casserole dish, muslin or nut milk bag, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve lasagne with a fresh green salad.
  • This light and summery recipe is a great money-saver because you make the pasta and ricotta from scratch.
  • Roll out small amounts of pasta dough at a time and cover remaining dough with a damp kitchen towel to prevent it from drying out.
  • For best results after rolling and cutting pasta, allow to dry 1-2 hours on a lightly floured kitchen towel.
  • After washing and drying the mixing bowl in step 3, you may wish to proceed with step 5 and the preparation of your fillings while the pasta dough rests.
  • If you have a second Thermomix bowl, it will come in very handy in step 7, as you can insert the simmering basket into it and allow the ricotta to drain.
  • To increase quantity, add 1 extra egg per additional 100 g flour. The maximum quantity at any one time is 500 g flour and 5 eggs. Increase kneading time to 3 minutes.
