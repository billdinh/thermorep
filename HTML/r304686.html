Beef and White Bean Purée on Flatbread

Ingredients

Marinated Beef

  • 75 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200 g beef fillet steak

Flatbreads

  • 350 g strong wholemeal bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 250 g plain yoghurt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp sugar

Tomato and Pepper Topping

  • 4 banana shallots, halved (120-130 g)
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 6 sun-dried tomatoes, sliced thinly (approx. 50 g)
  • 1 preserved red pepper, cut in thin strips (approx. 50 g)
  • 1 Tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp runny honey

Bean Purée

  • 240 g tinned cannellini beans, (1 x 400 g tin, drained)
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 Tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Marinated Beef

  1. Place oil and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Place beef in a bowl then pour over marinade. Stir to coat then cover and leave to marinate for at least 4 hours. Clean mixing bowl.

Flatbreads

  1. Place bread flour, yoghurt, baking powder, salt and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 15 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then knead 1 min/.
  2. Tip dough out onto a lightly floured surface and form into a log. Divide into 8 pieces then shape each into a ball. Roll each one out into a circle (approx. Ø 20 cm).
  3. Heat a frying pan over medium heat then cook each flatbread for 1 minute on each side or until lightly browned. Wrap in a clean tea towel and set aside to keep warm.

Tomato and Pepper Topping

  1. Place shallots and 20 g oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/100°C/speed .
  2. Add sun-dried tomatoes, red pepper, balsamic vinegar and honey then mix 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool.

Bean Purée

  1. Place beans, 30 g olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, thyme, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then purée 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 6.
  2. Preheat a griddle pan over a high then cook beef fillet for 4 minutes each side, or according to taste. Allow to rest for 10 minutes then slice thinly.
  3. Top 4 flatbreads with a layer of bean purée and a few slices of beef then finish with a spoonful of tomato and pepper topping.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, tea towel, griddle pan, frying pan, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Beef packs in protein, which is necessary to jumpstart muscle repair after a race, while the bread, beans, and vegetables provide carbs and fibre.
  • Serves 2 (2 flatbreads each) + 4 flatbreads left over
