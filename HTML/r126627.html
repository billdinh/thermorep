Beef burgers with cheese filling

Ingredients

  • 1 bread roll, dry
  • water for soaking
  • 15 g fresh parsley, stalks removed
  • 130 g onions, cut into halves
  • 20 g oil, plus extra for frying
  • 1 egg
  • 600 g beef mince
  • 20 g mustard
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp hot paprika powder
  • 150 g Cheddar cheese or any other medium-hard cheese, cut into 8 pieces (3 x 3 cm, 5 mm thick)

Preparation

  1. Soften the roll in water and squeeze out well.
  2. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add squeezed bread roll, egg, beef mince, mustard, salt, pepper, paprika powder and chopped parsley and mix 30 sec//speed 4 with aid of spatula.
  6. Press ⅛ mince mixture flat in your hand, place a piece of cheddar at the centre, closing mince around it. Press down firmly so that the cheese cannot leak out during frying and carefully shape into a flat burger patty (Ø approx. 10 cm). Repeat for 7 other burgers.
  7. Heat oil in a large non-stick frying pan. Fry beef burgers for approx. 3–4 minutes on either side until golden and serve hot.

Useful Items

bowl, large non-stick frying pan

Hints & Tricks

  • Serving suggestion: toast burger rolls in oven or toaster so they are nice and crisp. Spread e.g. ketchup or a dip of your choice on two roll halves, place beef burger on bottom half of roll, add lettuce, coleslaw or pickled gherkin to taste, place top half of roll on top and serve.
  • Replace the cheddar cheese with feta cheese.
