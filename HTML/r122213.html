Banana Mug Cakes

Ingredients

  • oil (e.g. olive, avocado, rapeseed), for greasing
  • 200 g bananas, peeled, sliced (2 cm), then frozen
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 Tbsp coconut flour
  • 1 tsp maple syrup, for serving
  • 1 banana, sliced thinly, for serving
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1216 kJ / 291 kcal

Preparation

  1. Grease two heatproof mugs with oil of choice (e.g. olive, avocado, rapeseed).
  2. Place bananas in mixing bowl then blend 7 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then blend again 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl then blend again 15 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add eggs and mix 5 sec/speed 4.5.
  4. Add coconut flour and mix 5 sec/speed 4.5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 5 sec/speed 4.5. Divide mixture evenly between mugs.
  5. Place mugs in microwave and cook on high for 4 minutes (see tip for microwave settings). Serve in mugs, drizzled with maple syrup, and topped with sliced banana.

Useful Items

heatproof tea cups or small mugs (Ø 8 cm, height 9 cm), microwave oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For easy blending, freeze bananas in slices spread out on a tray. Once frozen, use frozen fruit in recipe or transfer to a freezer bag for later use.
  • The cakes can also be turned out onto a plate if preferred.
  • Microwave cooking times vary for each machine. These are guidelines only:

For 2 mugs, 4 min 30 sec @ 600 W; 4 min 15 sec @ 800 W; 4 min @ 950 W

For 1 mug, 3 min 30 sec @ 600 W; 3 min 15 sec @ 800 W; 3 min @ 950 W.

  • Practicalities often dictate our lives. With little planning you can still produce a quick and easy post-exercise snack in next to no time with this simple mug cake. It can be created and made in less than 10 minutes. It’s a great recovery combination of protein and carbohydrates, too.
