Apple, feijoa and ginger sponge pudding

Ingredients

  • 120 g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 500 g water
  • 300 g apples, peeled, cored and cut into thin wedges (see Tips)
  • 30 g sultanas (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 200 g feijoas (approx. 2-3 feijoas), peeled and cut into wedges (see Tips)
  • 40 g stem ginger, cut into pieces (5 mm - see Tips)
  • 120 g caster sugar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 2 eggs
  • 20 g milk (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 pinch salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a ceramic or glass baking dish (23 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh apple and sultanas (optional) into it. Add cinnamon quill into simmering basket and cook 7 min/100°C/speed 4.
  3. Add feijoas and ginger into simmering basket and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 4. Gently stir with spatula to combine. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard cinnamon quill. Transfer mixture into prepared dish and place into the oven to cook (170°C), while preparing sponge topping. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place butter and sugar into mixing bowl and mix 1 min/speed 3.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 1 min/speed 3.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add eggs, milk and vanilla, then mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add flour, baking powder, nutmeg and salt, then mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 5 sec/speed 3.
  9. Remove hot fruit from oven and carefully spread sponge mixture evenly over fruit. Sprinkle with extra sugar and bake for 45-50 minutes (170°C), until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of sponge comes out clean. Serve warm (see Tips).

Useful Items

ceramic or glass baking dish (23 cm), wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use apples of choice and milk of choice in this recipe.
  • If using frozen feijoas, defrost completely and drain off any excess moisture before using.
  • You can find stem ginger at gourmet food stores or online. If stem ginger isn't available, you can replace it with minced crystallised ginger.
  • Serve with cream, custard or ice cream if desired.
  • Gluten free feijoa sponge pudding: Replace plain flour and baking powder with gluten free versions
