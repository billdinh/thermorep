Peanut butter & jam doughnuts

Ingredients

Dough

  • oil, for greasing
  • 140 g full cream milk
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 30 g salted butter, melted
  • 270 g baker's flour
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 120 g strawberry jam

Peanut butter glaze

  • 50 g roasted salted peanuts, plus extra for decorating
  • 100 g dark chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 80 g icing sugar
  • 20 g full cream milk
  • 150 g smooth peanut butter (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp natural vanilla extract
  • cinnamon sugar, for dipping

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease Varoma dish and tray and set aside. Line a large tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place milk, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 1 min/37°C/speed 3.
  3. Add flour, butter, cinnamon, salt and egg and mix 4 sec/speed 4, then knead 2 min/. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and shape into a ball. Cover and set aside to prove in a warm place for 30 minutes, until double in size.
  4. Divide dough into 12 equal portions (approx. 40 g each) and shape each portion into a flat disc (approx. 5 cm wide). Place 1 tsp jam into centre of each circle and pinch together edges to seal in jam. Transfer doughnuts, seam side down, into Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray, allowing space between each doughnut (approx. 3 cm). Cover with Varoma lid and set aside to prove for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 8 min/Varoma/speed 1. Place Varoma and tray into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 12 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until cooked through. Remove Varoma and transfer doughnuts onto prepared tray and set aside to cool (approx. 40 minutes).

Peanut butter glaze

  1. Place peanuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and melt 3 min/50°C/speed 2 or until chocolate is smooth. Transfer into a piping bag with a fine nozzle (5 mm). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place all remaining ingredients, excluding cinnamon sugar, into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4, or until combined. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Spread cinnamon sugar onto a plate and dip bottom half of each doughnut into cinnamon sugar. Spread peanut butter glaze over top half of each doughnut, then pipe melted chocolate over glaze and sprinkle with reserved peanuts.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, silicone bread mat, kitchen towel, bowl, piping bag with nozzle (5mm), plate

Hints & Tricks

  • The history of Donut Day goes back much farther than most national food holidays. This particular food holiday is about a little more than just a delicious fried ring of dough. National Donut Day was started by the Chicago branch of the Salvation Army in 1938 during the Great Depression as a way to raise funds and honor the “donut lassies” who served donuts at the front lines of World War I. Women of the Salvation Army who baked and served donuts for this effort were referred to as “donut lassies.” In an attempt to lift their spirits and give them a link to home, they served donuts and coffee to soldiers in the trenches of France during the war. With limited resources on the battlefield, they quickly got creative and would even fry donuts in soldiers’ helmets. They had a slogan of “Donuts will win the war!” National Donut Day now falls on the first Friday of every year to honor this service. The Salvation Army branch in Chicago still uses Donut Day as a way to raise funds for their cause.
  • The peanut butter and jelly sandwich, or PB&J, is a sandwich, popular in North America, that includes a layer of peanut butter and either jelly or jam.
