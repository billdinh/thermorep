Spiced roast lamb

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 250 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 120 g tomato paste
  • 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 100 g Greek-style natural yoghurt, plus extra for serving
  • 200 g coconut milk
  • 2500 g lamb shoulder, bone in
  • water, for roasting

Preparation

  1. Place oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Add mustard seeds and heat 30 sec/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add onion, ginger paste and garlic paste and chop 7 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add all remaining ingredients, except lamb and water, and mix 30 sec/speed 2.
  6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Set aside in mixing bowl to cool (approx. 30 minutes).
  7. Place lamb in a rectangular roasting tin (approx. 33 x 23 cm) and score several cuts into the lamb. Rub spice mixture over lamb and into cuts. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate overnight.
  8. Remove lamb, uncover and bring to room temperature (approx. 1 hour).
  9. Preheat oven to 150°C. Cover lamb with aluminium foil and roast for 2 hours (150°C), turning lamb over after 1 hour. Remove lamb from oven. Turn lamb over, then carefully pour water (2-3 cm high) into base of roasting tin, then place back into oven to roast for a further 90 minutes (150°C). Remove aluminium foil, turn lamb over and roast for a further 30 minutes (150°C) or until tender. Serve warm with extra yoghurt.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, rectangular dish (33 x 23 x 5 cm), aluminium foil, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ginger paste and garlic paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can serve this with rice and chutney of choice or as part of an Indian banquet. We served ours with Biryani (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
