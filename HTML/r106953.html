Lamb Meatballs with Couscous and Mint Yogurt

Ingredients

  • 3 oz dried apricots, pitted

Mint Yogurt

  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ oz fresh mint leaves (approx. 15 sprigs)
  • 7 oz Greek yogurt
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper

Lamb Meatballs and Couscous

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ oz fresh mint leaves, (from approx. 15 sprigs)
  • 24 oz ground lamb
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ tsp salt, divided
  • ½ tsp hot paprika
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp ground allspice
  • 10 oz couscous, medium
  • 21 oz water
  • olive oil, to fry
  • 1 oz unsalted butter

Preparation

  1. Place apricots into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Mint Yogurt

  1. Place garlic and mint leaves into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add Greek yogurt, salt, cumin and pepper and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Lamb Meatballs and Couscous

  1. Place garlic and mint leaves into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add ground lamb, egg, 1 tsp salt, paprika, cinnamon, cumin and allspice and mix 20 sec/. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in couscous and set aside.
  4. Place water and ½ tsp salt into mixing bowl and bring to a boil 6 min/212°F/speed 1. Meanwhile, form 12 meatballs from the lamb mixture.
  5. Pour boiling water from mixing bowl over couscous, cover and leave to absorb for 5 minutes. Meanwhile heat oil on medium heat in a large frying pan and pan fry meatballs cooking all sides until golden brown (approx. 8 min.).
  6. Add butter and reserved chopped apricots to couscous. Stir with spatula to combine. Serve meatballs hot with couscous and mint yogurt.

Useful Items

frying pan, large bowl, stove

Hints & Tricks

  • You can make your own ground lamb by chopping sinew-free, diced lamb in two batches on speed 6.5.
  • When forming meatballs, avoid overworking meat and use wet hands to prevent sticking.
  • Meatballs can be baked in the oven. Place meatballs in a baking pan (13 in. x 9 in.) lined with lightly greased foil or non-stick cooking spray and bake for 18-22 minutes (400°F). Turn meatballs half way through cooking.
  • Ground lamb can be replaced with ground beef.
