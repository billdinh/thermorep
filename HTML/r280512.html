Italian Sausage Pretzel Rolls

Ingredients

  • 78 oz water, divided
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 packet/envelope rapid rise yeast
  • 13 oz all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 4 oz cheddar cheese, diced (½ in.)
  • 4 oz Italian sausage, fully cooked, sliced (approx. 20 rounds)
  • 2 tbsp baking soda
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp water
  • coarse salt, to garnish
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
485 kJ / 116 kcal

5 g

Preparation

  1. Place 8 oz water, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/120°F/speed 2. Meanwhile, lightly grease a large bowl and line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Add flour and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 2 min/. Transfer to prepared bowl and cover with cling wrap. Let rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2 hr.).
  3. Once dough has doubled, punch down, divide into 20 pieces (approx. 1 oz ea.) and roll into balls.
  4. Gently press 1 cube of cheese and 1 slice of sausage into the center of each ball. Pinch shut and gently reshape into a football. Cover with a kitchen towel and set aside.
  5. Meanwhile, fill a large pot half-way with 70 oz water and bring to a boil over high heat.
  6. When water starts boiling, add baking soda a little at a time (water will bubble up rapidly). Working in batches, boil stuffed dough balls for 30 seconds. Remove with a slotted spoon and place onto prepared baking sheet. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  7. Lightly score the top of each "football" with a sharp knife, one vertical and three horizontal lines to resemble football laces.
  8. In a small bowl whisk together egg and water. Brush tops with egg wash, sprinkle with coarse salt and bake for 15-18 minutes (400°F) until golden brown. Transfer to a wire rack to cool for 5 minutes before serving.

Useful Items

baking sheet and parchment paper, clean kitchen towel, slotted spoon, sharp knife, pastry brush, cling wrap, large pot with lid (approx. 4-5 qt.), wire rack, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with your favorite mustard.
