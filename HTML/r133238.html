Struffoli (Italy)

Ingredients

Struffoli

  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 5 g butter
  • 90 g sugar
  • 50 g liqueur, Strega or Galliano
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 lemon, zest only, finely grated
  • 1 orange, zest only, finely grated
  • 1000 g groundnut oil for frying

Icing

  • 300 g honey
  • 25 g water
  • 50 g sugar
  • 1 orange, zest only, grated
  • 1 lemon, zest only, grated
  • 100 g candied orange peel

Preparation

Struffoli

  1. Place all dough ingredients except oil in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 5. Tip out onto a floured surface, and using your hands, roll pieces of dough into thin sticks (approx. 1 cm wide). Cut each stick into 0.5 cm pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a large saucepan over a high heat.
  3. Fry struffoli in hot oil, a few at a time, for about 5 minutes or until golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towel.

Icing

  1. Place honey, water, sugar, orange zest and lemon zest in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Pour liquid into a large saucepan and add struffoli and candied fruit then stir gently.
  3. When liquid has been absorbed by struffoli, pour onto a round serving dish and, with your hands slightly wet, form a doughnut shape and allow to set before serving.

Useful Items

long sharp knife, large saucepan, slotted spoon, paper towel, serving dish, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditionally struffoli are decorated with coloured sprinkles (in Naples called 'diavulilli') and a sprinkling of cinnamon, lemon and candied pumpkin for fragrance. The latter is only found in Naples and is called 'cucuzzata.'
  • Strega and Galliano are Italian liqueurs flavoured with herbs.
  • If you have enough time, rest the dough before frying.
