Fruit Tart with Camomile Custard

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 120 g unsalted butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 180 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 40 g icing sugar
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 3 tsp water

Camomile Custard

  • 500 g whole milk
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 3 camomile tea bags
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 50 g cornflour

Topping

  • 150 g double cream
  • 200 g fresh blueberries
  • 175 g fresh blackberries
  • edible flowers, for decoration (optional)

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place butter, flour, icing sugar, salt, egg yolk and water in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Tip out and bring together into a flattened ball, then wrap in cling film and chill in fridge for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make custard.

Camomile Custard

  1. Place milk, 100 g sugar and teabags in mixing bowl then heat 5 min/90°C//speed . Set aside for tea to infuse for 20 minutes.
  2. After this time, remove teabags then add eggs, egg yolks, cornflour and remaining 100 g sugar then heat 6 min/90°C/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, cover surface with a piece of cling film to prevent a skin forming then allow to cool to room temperature before placing in fridge. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare pastry shell.

Pastry Shell

  1. Roll out chilled pastry between two pieces of cling film, or on a lightly floured surface, and use to line a rectangular loose bottom fluted tart tin (36 cm x 12 cm). Prick base with a fork then set aside in fridge for 45 minutes.
  2. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 160°C. Line pastry shell with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 15 minutes (160°C). Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 6-8 minutes (160°C) until pastry is golden brown. Set aside on a wire rack and allow to cool.

Topping

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup, whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping.
  2. Add cooled custard and blend 20 sec/speed 3.
  3. Remove pastry case from tin, place on a serving plate then spoon custard into it. Decorate with fresh blueberries, blackberries and edible flowers (if using), then serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film, slotted spoon, rectangular loose bottom fluted tart tin (36 cm x 12 cm), rolling pin, wire rack, baking paper, baking beans, serving plate, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This tart is best eaten within a few hours of assembling as the pastry will begin to soften. Prepare up to the end of step 5 ahead of time, then finish topping and assemble the tart when needed.
  • Make mini tarts using any leftover pastry and custard. Can also be made in individual tartlet tins.

This tart has elegance on every level – from the camomile custard filling to the delicate fruit and flowers decoration. Perfect for a summer dinner party or an extra-special afternoon tea.
