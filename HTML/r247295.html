Tuna Meatballs with Tomato Sauce - Polpette di tonno con salsa di pomodoro

Ingredients

Meatballs

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 100 g white bread, torn in pieces
  • 10 g fresh parsley leaves, plus extra for garnish
  • 400 g fresh tuna, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 55 g toasted pine nuts
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 lemon, finely grated zest and juice

Sauce

  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 60 g onions, halved
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 800 g tinned peeled plum tomatoes (2 x 400 g tins)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • fine sea salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • red wine vinegar, to taste (optional)

Preparation

Meatballs

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place bread and parsley in mixing bowl then grate 3 sec/speed 9. Add to bowl with grated Parmesan.
  3. Place tuna, pine nuts and cinnamon in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add oregano, reserved parsley breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan, eggs, lemon zest and juice then mix 15 sec//speed 3. Shape mixture into 16-18 meatballs (approx. Ø 4 cm) then place on a plate and set aside in fridge for 1 hour. Clean mixing bowl and after 40 minutes prepare sauce.

Sauce

  1. Place oil, onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 10 min/98°C/speed .
  2. Add tomatoes, oregano, sugar (if using), salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, simmer 15 min/98°C/speed 0.5. Season to taste and add red wine vinegar (if using) then keep warm.
  3. Heat a little oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Add meatballs in batches and fry until golden brown all over. Place in a serving dish and pour over tomato sauce. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and serve.

Useful Items

frying pan, serving dish, hob, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Delicious served with spaghetti, linguini or tagliatelle.
  • Use swordfish instead of tuna.
  • Sicilian cuisine is considered one of the healthiest in Italy with its emphasis on fresh seafood, like tuna and swordfish, and fresh vegetables and fruits like tomatoes, olives, citrus and artichokes. We’ve brought the vibrant flavours of Sicily to these unusual meatballs, which feature fresh tuna instead of the usual pork or beef. Typically tuna steaks are lightly seared on a barbecue or grill, however, they work really well prepared in this way and served with a deliciously rich tomato sauce.
