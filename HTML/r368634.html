Burmese chickpea tofu with spicy dipping sauce

Ingredients

Chickpea tofu

  • 1 - 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g chickpea (besan) flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • 370 g filtered water

Spicy dipping sauce

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 65 g Sriracha® sauce
  • 30 g lime juice
  • 1 tbsp filtered water
  • 20 g pure maple syrup
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, cut into thin slices, to serve
  • sesame seeds, to serve

Preparation

Chickpea tofu

  1. Grease and line a small square or rectangular baking tin (12 cm) with plastic wrap. Set aside.
  2. Place flour, salt, garlic powder, turmeric, cumin and water into mixing bowl and blend 10 sec/speed 5, then cook 7-10 min/100°C/speed 4, until smooth. Quickly transfer mixture into prepared tin and smooth surface. Cover and set aside to cool, then transfer into refrigerator to set for a minimum of 1 hour (see Tip). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Cut tofu into slices (1.5 cm thickness), then into cubes (2 cm). Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Fry tofu for 3-4 minutes, turning frequently, until crispy and golden brown. Transfer onto a serving plate and set aside.

Spicy dipping sauce

  1. Place garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add Sriracha®, lime juice, water and syrup and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  3. Drizzle sauce over tofu. Sprinkle with spring onion/shallot and sesame seeds to serve.

Useful Items

square cake tin (12 cm), plastic wrap, large frying pan, serving plate, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store cooled and set tofu in a sealable container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
