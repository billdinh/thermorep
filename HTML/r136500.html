Salted Caramel Chocolate Tartlets

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 170 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 30 g cocoa powder
  • 100 g unsalted butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen for 30 minutes
  • 50 g icing sugar
  • 1 large egg

Salted Caramel

  • 80 g light brown sugar
  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 70 g double cream
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt

Chocolate Layer

  • 150 g dark chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 240 g double cream
  • 15 g unsalted butter

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place all pastry ingredients in mixing bowl then bring together Turbo/2 sec/6 times. Tip out onto a lightly floured surface and bring together into a flattened ball. Roll out to a thickness of 3 mm then cut out and line 10 individual tartlet tins (Ø 9 cm). Prick bases with a fork then set aside in fridge for 45 minutes. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. Towards the end of resting time, preheat oven to 180°C.
  3. Line pastry shells with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 10 minutes (180°C). Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (180°C). Set aside on a wire rack to cool. Meanwhile, prepare salted caramel.

Salted Caramel

  1. Place all salted caramel ingredients in mixing bowl and cook 20 min/120°C/speed 3.
  2. Add 1 tsp caramel to each pastry shell, spreading out to form an even layer over base. Clean mixing bowl and prepare chocolate layer.

Chocolate Layer

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add cream and butter then heat 2 min/80°C/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then heat again 3 min/80°C/speed 2. Pour over caramel layer in each pastry shell, allow to cool then set aside in fridge to set (approx. 2 hours).
  3. Remove tartlets from tins and serve chilled or at room temperature.

Useful Items

small tartlet tins (Ø 9 cm), rolling pin, wire rack, baking paper, baking beans, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

These delightfully rich, chocolatey tarts would make a perfect dinner party dessert – or they’d be just as at home as part of an afternoon tea spread.
