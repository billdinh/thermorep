Flourless Chocolate Coffin Cake

Ingredients

Chocolate Cake

  • 10 oz unsalted butter, room temperature, plus extra to grease
  • 8 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 6 oz sugar, divided
  • 10 oz dark chocolate (70% cocoa), cut into pieces
  • 5 oz hazelnuts
  • 10 egg yolks, from large eggs

Ganache

  • 7 oz dark chocolate (70% cocoa), cut into pieces
  • 6 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 1 ½ tsp gel food coloring, black

Preparation

Chocolate Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Lightly grease a baking dish, line with parchment and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and 3 oz sugar into mixing bowl and whip 5-6 min/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks are formed. Remove butterfly whisk, transfer whipped egg whites into a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place chocolate and hazelnuts into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and heat 2 min 30 sec/160°F/speed 2.
  5. Add egg yolks and 3 oz sugar and mix 1 min/speed 4. Gently fold chocolate mixture with spatula into reserved whipped egg whites until fully combined. Pour batter into prepared baking dish and bake for 35 minutes (350°F) or until an inserted toothpick comes out clean. Allow to cool completely before frosting (approx. 1 hr.).

Ganache

  1. Place chocolate, whipping cream and gel food coloring into mixing bowl and heat 2-3 min/110°F/speed 3 until melted.

Assembly

  1. Place a serving platter upside down over the cooled cake and carefully invert cake onto platter. Carefully remove baking dish and peel off parchment. Trim cake as needed with a sharp knife for straight edges (see Tip). Spread Ganache evenly on top of cake and down the sides with an icing spatula. Decorate to taste and allow frosting to set before serving.

Useful Items

toothpick, parchment paper, baking dish (9 in. x 13 in.), icing spatula, large bowl, serving platter, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Sprinkle chocolate wafer cookie crumbs on serving platter to look like dirt. Place coffin cake on top of wafer "dirt" crumbs and add a few gummy worms next to the coffin.
  • Cut cake to resemble a traditional coffin shape by cutting a 1 inch triangle from one corner of the cake. Cut opposite corner to match; this will be the top end of coffin. Cut long, narrow triangles (1 in. x 4 in.) off bottom corners to form bottom end. Decorate to your liking.
