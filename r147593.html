Slow Cooked Beans with Cider

Ingredients

  • 450 g cannellini beans, dried, soaked overnight then drained
  • water, to cover
  • 75 g tomato purée
  • 3 Tbsp sugar cane molasses
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 Tbsp cider vinegar
  • 375 g dry cider
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt, plus more to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, plus more to taste
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 170 g onions, quartered
  • 110 g carrots, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil

Preparation

  1. Place soaked beans in a saucepan and cover with water (depth at least 5 cm above beans). Bring to the boil then reduce heat and simmer gently for 90 minutes until beans are tender (see tip). Meanwhile, prepare cider sauce.
  2. Place tomato purée, molasses, brown sugar and mustard in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 3.
  3. Place a jug on mixing bowl lid. Weigh into jug cider vinegar and cider then remove jug.
  4. Without measuring cup, mix speed 2 then slowly pour cider mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid, stopping when fully mixed in.
  5. Add salt, pepper and thyme leaves then stir 10 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a jug and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  6. Place onions, carrots and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Sauté 15 min/100°C/speed 0.5.
  7. Add reserved cider sauce and stir 20 sec/speed 2.
  8. When beans are tender, drain and reserve cooking liquid.
  9. Add cooked beans and up to 375 g cooking liquid (no higher than maximum mark in mixing bowl). Replace measuring cup with simmering basket and simmer 20-30 min/98°C//speed  until beans have thickened the sauce. Adjust seasoning and serve with grilled meats.

Useful Items

saucepan, jug, medium mesh sieve, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • While cooking the beans on the hob, add extra water if necessary to keep them covered.
  • Cool leftovers to room temperature then place in an airtight container. Refrigerate for up to 1 week, or freeze for a month.
