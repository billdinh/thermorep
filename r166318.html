Marble sponge slice with peach crème

Ingredients

Sponge cake

  • 4 eggs
  • 200 g sugar, plus 2-3 tbsp extra
  • 1 tbsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 100 g plain flour
  • 100 g starch, e.g. corn starch or potato starch
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder

Crème filling

  • 8 gelatine leaves (approx. 15 g)
  • water, cold, for soaking gelatine
  • 200 g cream, 30-40% fat
  • 470 g peach halves, canned (weight after draining)
  • 200 g curd cheese (quark)
  • 40 g sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla sugar, homemade

Preparation

Sponge cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, sugar and vanilla sugar in mixing bowl and whisk until creamy 1 min/speed 4.
  3. Add flour, starch and baking powder and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Spread half of mixture onto prepared baking tray.
  4. Add cocoa powder to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Spread cocoa mixture on top of the light mixture and marble with a spoon handle. Bake 15-18 minutes (180°C). Meanwhile, sprinkle 2-3 tbsp sugar on a tea towel.
  5. Immediately after baking, turn sponge cake out onto the kitchen towel sprinkled with sugar. Coat the baking paper with cold water, remove and allow sponge cake to cool. Meanwhile, prepare the filling.

Crème filling

  1. Soak gelatine in some water according to packet instructions. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl, beat until stiff speed 3 and set aside. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Cut 2 peach halves into thin wedges and set aside. Place remaining peaches into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Then heat 3 min/70°C/speed 1.
  4. Squeeze out gelatine, add and dissolve 1 min/speed 2.
  5. Add quark, sugar and vanilla sugar and mix 10 sec/speed 2.5.
  6. Add whipped cream and stir 5 sec/speed 2.5. Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool in refrigerator until mixture is firmer and spreadable. This takes approx. 90 minutes.
  7. Set 5 tbsp crème filling aside. Divide sponge cake into 2 equally-sized squares. Spread remaining peach cream on one half, ensuring that edges are covered with enough cream. Lay other half on top, mark 10 slices and add a dab of cream in the centre of each. Decorate with reserved peach wedges and chill for approx. 2 hours. Cut 10 slices and serve.

Useful Items

baking tray, baking paper, tea towel, bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Other tinned fruit such as apricots or pears can be used instead of peaches.
