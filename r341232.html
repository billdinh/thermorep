Celeriac parmigiana

Ingredients

Coating

  • 20 g flaxmeal (see Tips)
  • 100 g filtered water
  • 4 slices day-old bread of choice (approx. 140 g), torn into pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Celeriac cutlets

  • 10 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for shallow frying
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 180 g), cut into quarters
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh sage, leaves only
  • 800 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 celeriacs, peeled and cut into 4 slices each (approx. 1.5 cm thickness)
  • 160 g vegan cheese (see Tips)

Preparation

Coating

  1. Place a wide shallow bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh flaxmeal and water into it. Stir to combine, then set aside.
  2. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  3. Place bread, garlic, thyme, sage, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 6.5 until forms a fine breadcrumb. Transfer onto a plate and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Celeriac cutlets

  1. Place oil, onion, garlic, thyme and sage into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add tomatoes into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and place 4 celeriac slices into Varoma dish and 4 slices onto Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 2, until celeriac is soft but still retains its shape.
  3. Remove Varoma and set aside. Continue to cook sauce 5 min/100°C/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Using paper towel, pat dry each slice of celeriac. Dip each slice in flaxmeal mixture, then into breadcrumb mixture, gently patting each side to coat. Place onto a large plate.
  5. Place a large frying pan over high heat and add oil (1-2 cm deep). Working in batches, place coated celeriac pieces into pan and fry for 1-2 minutes on each side, until golden. Transfer onto prepared baking tray and spread each piece with 2 heaped tablespoons of the tomato sauce. Top with cheese of choice, then bake for 5-8 minutes (180°C), until golden. Serve with sides of choice.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, plate, shallow bowl, large plate, paper towel, large frying pan, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can purchase flaxmeal at most major supermarkets, but to make your own, simply place 200 g flaxseeds into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable container and store in the fridge or freezer for up to 3 months.
  • If you have leftover flaxmeal, you can use it as an egg substitute in baking. Combine 1 tablespoon flaxmeal with 30 g boiling water and set aside until it becomes jelly-like (equates to approx. 1 egg).
  • You can use any vegan cheese you like in this recipe. We used our Stretchy melty cheese (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
