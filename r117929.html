Coconut fish curry

Ingredients

Garam masala

  • 2 cinnamon quills
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 10 whole cloves
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp black peppercorns
  • 2 dried bay leaves

Coconut rice

  • 300 g jasmine rice
  • 700 g water
  • 250 g coconut milk
  • 1 tsp salt

Curry sauce

  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 - 2 fresh green chillies, deseeded if preferred and cut into pieces
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 50 g coconut oil
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to season
  • 6 ripe Roma tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 400 g coconut milk
  • 3 fresh Kaffir lime leaves, cut into thin slices
  • 2 tsp fish sauce (gluten free)
  • 2 tsp palm sugar
  • 100 g snow peas, cut into thin slices
  • oil, for greasing
  • 750 g skinless snapper fillets, cut into portions (approx. 8 cm)
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season
  • 2 sprigs fresh Thai basil, leaves only, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

Garam masala

  1. Place all garam masala ingredients into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed , then mill 1 min/speed 9.
  2. Transfer into a sealable storage jar or container until ready to use (see Tips).

Coconut rice

  1. Insert simmering basket, then weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice under cold running water until water runs clear.
  2. Place water, coconut milk and salt into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with rice in it and cook 14 min/100°C/speed 4. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Curry sauce

  1. Place onion, garlic, turmeric, chillies and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add coconut oil and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add salt and tomatoes and cook 20 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add coconut milk, lime leaves, fish sauce, palm sugar and 3 teaspoons of the reserved Garam masala. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh snow peas into it. Grease Varoma tray. Place snapper fillets in a single layer onto tray and season with salt and pepper. Insert Varoma tray, then secure Varoma lid and cook 10-12 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until fish is cooked to your liking.
  5. Transfer snapper, snow peas and curry sauce into a seperate thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Allow to sit for 5 minutes.
  6. Spoon rice into serving bowls, top with fish and curry sauce. Garnish with Thai basil (optional) before serving.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, 4 serving bowls

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store leftover Garam masala in a cool, dark place for several months. Use to season Indian or Asian dishes.
