Lemon and herb salmon burgers with zucchini fries

Ingredients

Mayonnaise

  • 150 g sunflower oil
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp pickled capers, chopped
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt, to taste

Salmon burgers

  • 6 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • ½ lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 2 sprigs fresh tarragon, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill, fronds only
  • 3 fresh boneless, skinless salmon fillets (approx. 150 g each), cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 80 g frozen green peas
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Zucchini fries

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 60 g white bread
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 300 g zucchini, cut into batons (5 cm x 1 cm)

Assembly

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 160 g cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 8 green lettuce leaves, to serve

Preparation

Mayonnaise

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid, weigh sunflower oil into it and set aside.
  2. Place egg, pepper, lemon juice and mustard into mixing bowl and mix 3 min/speed 4, very slowly pouring reserved oil onto lid, letting it drizzle into mixing bowl to emulsify.
  3. Add capers and stir through with aid of spatula. Season to taste. Transfer mayonnaise into a bowl, cover and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Salmon burgers

  1. Place spring onions/shallots, lemon zest, tarragon and dill into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add salmon, peas, salt and pepper and chop 10 sec/speed 8. Divide mixture into 8 equal portions and shape into burger patties (approx. 2 cm thickness). Place onto a plate and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Zucchini fries

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese and bread into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer onto a large plate and set aside.
  3. Place egg, salt and pepper into a large bowl and lightly whisk. Dip zucchini batons into egg, then roll in breadcrumb mixture to coat and place onto prepared baking tray, leaving space between each baton. Bake for 25 minutes (180°C), until crisp and golden brown. Meanwhile, fry salmon burgers.

Assembly

  1. Place a frying pan over medium-high heat and add oil. Fry burgers on each side for 5-6 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through. Slice buns into halves and lightly toast buns in the hot frying pan (optional). Spread bottom half of bun with reserved mayonnaise, top with two salmon patties, cherry tomatoes and lettuce. Serve with zucchini fries.

Useful Items

jug, plastic wrap, bowl, 1 plate, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, large plate, whisk, large bowl, non-stick frying pan, refrigerator, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • If you have extra dill, add chopped dill fronds to the mayonnaise for extra flavour.
  • These burgers can be made with any firm-fleshed fish - e.g. haddock, ling, trout or tuna.
  • Try replacing the burger bun with ciabatta or foccacia.
