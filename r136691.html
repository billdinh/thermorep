Everyday Indian curry paste

Ingredients

  • 30 g cumin seeds
  • 2 - 3 dried bay leaves
  • 30 g coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp whole cloves
  • 12 cardamom pods (black)
  • ½ cinnamon quill
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 5 brown onions (approx. 700 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 40 g canola oil
  • 30 fresh curry leaves
  • 800 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt

Preparation

  1. Place cumin seeds, bay leaves, coriander seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon quill and peppercorns into mixing bowl and dry roast 3 min/Varoma/speed 1, then mill 1 min/speed 9, until a powder.
  2. Add chilli powder, ground ginger, turmeric, onion and green chillies (optional) and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add garlic paste, ginger paste, ghee, oil and curry leaves then sauté 6 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add tomatoes, sugar, stock paste and salt and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, then blend 20 sec/speed 4 -7, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 7.
  5. Set aside to cool then use as needed, or transfer into sealable containers or bags and store until ready to use (see Tips).

Useful Items

sealable container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own garlic paste, ginger paste and ghee, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can store this paste in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or in the freezer, in a freezable container, for up to 1 month.
  • Use as a base for curries or stir-fries. Or use it in place of the Tandoori paste in the Masala bread rolls (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
