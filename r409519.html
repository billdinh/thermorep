Orange Campari marmalade

Ingredients

  • 4 - 6 oranges (see Tips)
  • 300 g water
  • 300 g jam setting sugar
  • 100 g Campari

Preparation

  1. Using a vegetable peeler, peel oranges (see Tips). Weigh zest into mixing bowl (70 g total) and chop 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add water and cook 4 min/100°C//speed 2.
  3. Using simmering basket, drain zest and place back into mixing bowl.
  4. Add 650 g orange flesh and jam sugar and chop 15 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 45 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add Campari and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Test setting point of marmalade (see Tips).
  7. If needed, cook for a further 2-3 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  8. Allow marmalade to cool slightly, then transfer into sterilised glass jars and seal tightly. Allow to cool completely, then store in pantry until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

vegetable peeler, sterilised sealable jars, plate

Hints & Tricks

  • The number of oranges required will depend on the size and weight of your oranges.
  • When preparing your oranges, make sure you remove all white pith and pips.
  • Setting point test: place a small plate into the freezer (5-10 minutes). Place 1 teaspoon marmalade onto the chilled plate. When marmalade is cool, push it with your finger. If the marmalade wrinkles, it is ready. If not, cook for a further 2-3 minutes/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  • You can store jars of sealed marmalade in the pantry for up to 4 months. Once opened, marmalade should be stored in the refrigerator (for up to 2 months).
