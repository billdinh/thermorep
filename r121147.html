Vegan Burger

Ingredients

Bread

  • 15 g granulated sugar
  • 350 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 140 g water
  • 5 g fresh yeast
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp oil, for greasing

Burger

  • 1200 g water, plus 3 Tbsp
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 100 g quinoa
  • 1 Tbsp linseeds (flaxseeds)
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 30 g shallots
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 200 g black beans, tinned, drained and rinsed
  • 10 g breadcrumbs
  • 35 g halved walnuts
  • 20 g tomato purée
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika, sweet

To Serve

  • 1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 - 3 tsp poppy seeds
  • 4 slices red pepper, preserved (optional)
  • 4 Tbsp ketchup
  • 4 - 6 curly endive, wash and dried
  • 8 - 12 slices tomatoes
  • 1 avocado, peeled and sliced, or to taste (optional)
  • 1 red onion, sliced in rings, or to taste (optional)

Preparation

Bread

  1. Place 5 g sugar, 50  g flour, 50 g water and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 1 min/37°C/speed 2. Leave in mixing bowl, with lid on, to rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Add remaining 90 g water, remaining 300 g flour, 10 g remaining sugar and salt then knead 4 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl with oil. Transfer dough to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2-3 hours). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place water and 1 tsp salt in mixing bowl then insert simmering basket and weigh in quinoa. Cook 20 min/100°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, place linseeds and 3 Tbsp water in a small bowl and stir. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer quinoa to a bowl to cool. Empty mixing bowl.

Burger

  1. Place oil, shallots, garlic, salt and pepper in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  2. Add beans, breadcrumbs, nuts, tomato purée, cumin, paprika, reserved linseed and water, reserved quinoa and ½ tsp salt then mix 20 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling film then refrigerate.
  3. When dough has doubled in size, line 2 baking trays with baking paper. Tip dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead gently. Divide dough into 4 portions and shape into rolls. Arrange rolls on 1 prepared baking tray, cover with oiled cling film and allow to rise for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, using a pastry ring, form 4 burgers (Ø 10 cm) with reserved burger mixture then place in fridge. Towards end of rising time, preheat oven to 180°C.
  4. Brush tops of risen rolls with oil and sprinkle with poppy seeds. Bake for 15-20 minutes (180°C) until cooked and tops of rolls are golden brown.
  5. Remove rolls from oven and place on a wire rack to cool.
  6. Transfer burgers to second baking tray and bake for 10-15 minutes (180°C) until a 'crust' has formed on the burgers. Remove tray from oven and place peppers (if using) over burgers then cook for a further 2-3 minutes (180°C).

To Serve

  1. Slice buns in half, then place burgers inside. Top with tomato ketchup, lettuce, sliced tomatoes, sliced avocado and a few rings of red onion.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, pastry cutter, 2 baking trays and baking paper, pastry brush, wire rack, baking tray and paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The burgers can be seared in a hot frying pan if desired.
