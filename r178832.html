Sponge Cake

Ingredients

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 4 medium eggs (53-63 g each) (see tip)
  • 20 g caster sugar, or to taste
  • 1 tsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder (optional)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
705 kJ / 168 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
26 g
Fat
5 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a springform tin (Ø 22 cm).
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place eggs, caster sugar and vanilla sugar in mixing bowl. Whisk 6 min/37°C/speed 4 then whisk again without heat 6 min/speed 4.
  3. Around butterfly whisk, add flour, baking powder (if using) and salt (if using) then mix 4 sec/speed 3.
  4. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully finish stirring by hand with spatula.
  5. Transfer mixture to prepared tin and bake immediately for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool for 10 minutes then remove from tin and transfer to a wire rack. Once cool, slice in half horizontally for filling.

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 22 cm), wire rack, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If eggs are small (closer to 53 g than to 63 g), use 5 eggs instead of 4, lightly whisk them in a bowl and measure 240 g eggs into the mixing bowl in step 2.
  • If the springform tin is larger, or if you wish to cut the cake into three layers, increase recipe using 1.5 x the ingredients, and increase baking time by a few minutes.
  • This cake can be used as a base for a variety of different cakes with fillings such as fruit, flavoured cream or whipped cream. Or serve it on its own or with a dusting of icing sugar.
  • Cake texture is at its best when consumed within one day. To prevent sponge cake from drying out, store in a container in the fridge. Sponge cake will keep for 4-5 days in the fridge or up to 1 month in the freezer.
  • This is a basic and versatile recipe that can be layered and sandwiched with fillings. It is perfect as a simple, everyday cake, or you can make it into a celebration cake by icing and decorating in a more elaborate way. The batter for this low-fat sponge is whisked at 37°C, creating a light and airy sponge. Alternatively, bake this cake as a sheet to use for Swiss rolls or layered mousse cakes.
  • Sugar has an impact on the cake's texture. For a very light and fluffy texture, use 120 g sugar.
  • Chocolate Sponge Cake: use only 80 g flour and add 40 g cocoa powder.
  • Rolled Cake: bake the sponge cake in a Swiss roll tin for use in jam rolls, Swiss rolls, etc. Transfer cake batter to a baking tray lined with baking paper or a silicone mat. Spread batter into a rectangle (40 x 30 cm) and bake immediately for 8 minutes (180°C). Roll cake while it is still hot with the help of a clean tea towel. Allow to cool, carefully unroll, remove towel, spread filling and roll again.
