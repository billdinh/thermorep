Meat and Cheese Stuffed Cassava (Carimañolas de carne y de queso)

Ingredients

  • 500 g water
  • 700 g cassava, peeled, cubed (3 cm)
  • 100 g Cheddar cheese, cut in pieces
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 15 gram oil, plus extra for frying
  • 50 g onions, halved
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 50 g red pepper, deseeded
  • 30 g spring onions
  • 150 g beef mince
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 20 g tomato purée

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Set Varoma dish in position, add cassava to it, making sure that some holes remain unobstructed so steam can circulate. Close Varoma and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until cassava is very soft. Set Varoma aside. Empty mixing bowl, then rinse with cold water and dry.
  2. Place cheese in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add cooked cassava and 1 tsp salt then mash 40 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, cover and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place oil, onions, garlic cloves, red pepper and spring onions in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1
  6. Add minced beef, ground cumin, 1 tsp salt, black pepper and tomato puree, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid to help prevent splashing then cook 7 min/120°C//speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool for 1 hour.
  7. To make carimañolas, divide cassava mixture into 8 balls. Flatten each ball across the palm, place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the centre and gently fold cassava over to enclose filling. Shape into a diamond and finish off neatly.
  8. In a frying pan, add oil to a depth of 2 cm and heat to frying temperature, around 130°C. Cook carimañolas for approx. 3 minutes each, turning often. Drain on paper towels, and serve warm or cool.

Useful Items

paper towels, frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Cassava is also known as yucca or manioc. It is a very versatile starchy root vegetable, popular in South America and Africa.
  • Serve meat patties as a party platter, or serve 2 each as a main course.
  • The carimañolas can be made ahead and frozen. Allow them to cool completely to room temperature, and then spread out on a metal tray to freeze. When they have frozen, place in resealable freezer bags and return to freezer. This keeps them separate.
  • Serve with a tomato salsa: place 1 tomato, 1 red chilli, 80 g olive oil, 30 g lemon juice, 1 garlic clove and 10 g parsley in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  • Instead of the meat filling, use 150 g diced mozzarella cheese for a vegetarian option.
