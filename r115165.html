Kuruma iraichchi with coconut roti

Ingredients

Coconut Roti

  • 350 g plain flour
  • 200 g fresh grated coconut flesh (see Tips)
  • ½ - 1 tsp salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil, plus extra for frying
  • 100 g lukewarm water

Kuruma iraichchi

  • 750 g chuck steak, cut into cubes (3 cm)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 - 4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 3 - 5 fresh bird's eye chillies, trimmed and deseeded if preferred
  • 2 brown onions, cut into quarters
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 8 fresh curry leaves
  • 2 pandan leaves, tied into a knot
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 stalk fresh lemongrass, cut into halves
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 270 g thick coconut cream
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges, to serve

Preparation

Coconut Roti

  1. Place all coconut roti ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 6 sec/speed 6.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 1 min/. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface (see Tips). Divide dough into 12 pieces and use a rolling pin to flatten into rounds (approx. 12 cm diameter).
  3. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil into pan. Place 1 roti into frying pan and fry until browned (approx. 2-3 minutes on each side). Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or onto a large plate and cover with kitchen towel to keep warm. Repeat with remaining roti. Cover to keep warm. Set aside while you prepare kuruma iraichchi. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Kuruma iraichchi

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh steak into it. Add salt and pepper and combine until steak is evenly coated. Set bowl aside.
  2. Place coriander into mixing bowl and roast 2 min/Varoma/speed 2 ,  without measuring cup. Transfer into bowl with steak and stir to combine. Set bowl aside.
  3. Place cumin and fennel seeds into mixing bowl and roast 1 min/Varoma/speed 2 ,  without measuring cup. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  4. Place garlic, ginger and chillies into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add onion and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add turmeric, curry leaves, pandan leaves, vinegar, lemongrass, tomato paste, coconut cream and reserved beef and cook 8 min/Varoma//speed , then cook 50 min/90°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  7. Add ghee into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/90°C//speed .
  8. Add reserved toasted spice mixture and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until sauce has thickened. Serve warm with coconut roti and lime wedges.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • You can purchase fresh coconut at most supermarkets or fruit and vegetable stores.
  • For a milder curry, reduce the number of chillies and deseed before using.
  • To save time, prepare coconut roti up to step 2 and set aside, then continue with recipe. Fry roti in step 9 when Kuruma iraichchi is cooking. Cover to keep warm and set aside until Kuruma iraichchi is ready to serve.
  • Coconut roti can be prepared a day ahead and reheated in a warm oven before serving.
  • To save time, use store-bought roti and start recipe on step 4.
