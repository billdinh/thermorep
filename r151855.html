Brioche

Ingredients

  • 20 g milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 350 g bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 4 eggs
  • 60 - 100 g sugar, to taste
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 170 g butter, unsalted, cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly whisked, for glazing

Preparation

  1. Place milk, fresh yeast, bread flour, 4 eggs, sugar and salt in mixing bowl and knead 7 min/.
  2. Add butter and knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough to a large bowl. Dough will be stretchy and sticky. Cover with cling film or a damp tea towel and let rise in a warm place (1-2 hours). After this time, place covered bowl in fridge for 3-4 hours minimum or overnight.
  4. Grease a loaf tin (approx. 28 cm long) and set aside. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and gently flatten with floured hands to form a rough rectangle. Roll the rectangle of dough into a log shape the length of the loaf tin. Place dough in prepared loaf tin, seam side down, and cover with cling film or a damp tea towel. Let loaf rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 3 hours).
  5. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Brush surface with egg yolk and bake for 30 minutes (180°C) or until dark golden brown. Remove brioche from loaf tin and allow to cool on a wire rack before slicing.

Useful Items

wire rack, cling film or tea towel, pastry brush, loaf tin (approx. 28 cm), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a rich brioche dough that requires long kneading and long proving (rising) for the fluffiest texture.
  • Rising times depend on ambient temperature. Proving should take place in a constant temperature environment. Low temperature will lead to longer proving time, high temperature (above 40°C) may weaken or kill the yeast. To create a warm environment, you can place the shaped brioche in a turned-off cool oven with a bowl of hot water inside.
  • Brioche à tête (traditional French brioche shape): divide dough into 2 pieces, one large (⅘ of the final loaf) and one small (⅕). Form both pieces into smooth balls. Place the large ball in a greased fluted brioche tin, and using your fingers, create a deep indentation in the middle until your fingers are touching the bottom of the tin. Roll the small ball of dough into a tear-drop shape, with a longer, pointy part. Place the pointy part of the small piece of dough in the indentation of the large ball, forming a "head", and push down deeply all around the head to tuck it firmly into the large ball of dough. Let rise then bake as directed in the recipe.
