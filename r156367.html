Steamed lemon cake

Ingredients

  • 150 g unsalted butter, cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tbsp semolina, for dusting
  • 180 g raw sugar
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • 3 eggs
  • 20 g full cream milk
  • 180 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1400 g water
  • 100 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), cut into pieces
  • 1 tsp neutral oil (e.g. sunflower, grapeseed)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1209 kJ / 289 kcal

Preparation

  1. Grease a bundt cake tin (20 cm) (or loaf tin 21 cm x 10 cm x 6 cm) that fits into the Varoma without obstructing all holes dust with semolina and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and 5 g lemon zest (from approx. half the lemon) into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add butter, eggs, milk, 20 g lemon juice, flour, baking powder and salt and mix 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer to prepared tin and cover tin with wet, well-rung baking paper. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place water into mixing bowl and place Varoma dish into position. Place baking tin into Varoma dish, secure Varoma lid and steam 80 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Set Varoma aside, remove baking tin and set aside. After 5 minutes remove baking papper and allow cake to cool in tin. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Meanwhile, place chocolate and oil into mixing bowl and melt 4 min/50°C/speed 1.
  7. Transfer cake from tin onto wire rack. Pour glaze evenly over cake. Slice and serve warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

wire rack, baking paper, metal baking tin (e.g. Bundt cake tin 20 cm top and 12 cm bottom, or loaf tin 21 x 10 cm x 6 cm)

Hints & Tricks

  • If you use a silicone baking mould, increase the steaming time to 80 minutes and increase the amount of water for steaming into the mixing bowl to 1600 g.
    - If using a loaf tin, you can line it with baking paper instead of greasing it.
  • No oven? No problem! Steaming produces surprisingly tender cakes, and the gentle heat allows you to melt chocolate for glazing at the same time as the cake steams. Different types of tins or bowls can be used for steaming cakes, as the temperature is lower than in an oven. Have a look at the variations to see the many ways this recipe can be adapted to your preferences.
  • Instead of the chocolate glaze you can dust the cake with icing sugar, cover it with icing or leave it plain. Refer to The Basic Cookbook for an icing recipe.
    - Lemon icing: Grind 160 g raw sugar 15 sec/speed 10. Add 25 g lemon juice and stir 20 sec/speed 3.5.
    - Steamed chocolate cake: Omit the lemon peel and lemon juice and add 15 g (2 tablespoons) cocoa powder and 15 g more milk (total milk 45 g). Then proceed as described in the recipe.
    - Steamed chocolate cardamom cake: Prepare the chocolate cake as above and add 1-1½ tsp ground cardamom.
    - Steamed cupcakes: Prepare ⅔ of the lemon, chocolate or chocolate cardamom batter. Place 5-6 cups or glasses (height approx. 6-7 cm and 6-7 cm) into Varoma dish. Distribute batter between them then cover each cup with wet, well-rung baking paper, secure Varoma lid and steam 40 min/Varoma/speed 1.
