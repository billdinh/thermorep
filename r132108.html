Spiced tomato chutney

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 7 - 8 fresh curry leaves
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 - 10 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 5 fresh tomatoes (approx. 560 g), cut into quarters and deseeded
  • 1 tsp asafoetida (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp sugar

Preparation

  1. Place mustard seeds, curry leaves, coriander seeds and fenugreek seeds into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 1 tablespoon of the coconut oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  3. Add garlic and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add chillies and ginger paste and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add carrot, tomatoes, asafoetida, salt and sugar and cook 10 min/Varoma//speed 1 or until carrots are soft, then blend 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  6. Place remaining 1 tablespoon coconut oil into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  7. Add reserved spice mix and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer tempered spices into bowl with tomato chutney and stir to combine. Transfer into a sealable container until ready to serve.

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ginger paste, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Asafoetida comes from the root of a perennial Indian herb. It has a strong odour, but the aroma softens as it cooks, and it tastes similar to onion and garlic, enhancing the savoury flavour of the dish. It helps reduce flatulence and constipation, and reduce the growth of microflora in the gut. A little goes a long way, so you only need to use a small amount. You can find asafoetida at Indian grocers or online.
  • You can store this chutney in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
