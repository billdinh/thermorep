Quick Puff Pastry

Ingredients

  • 200 g unsalted butter, diced (1-2 cm) then frozen
  • 200 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 90 g water, cold
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place butter, plain flour, cold water and salt in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Tip out, form into a ball and flatten into a rough square. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out dough to a long rectangle 3 times as long as it is wide (approx. 60 cm x 20 cm). Fold bottom third up to cover middle third and top third down over this. Seal edges then give dough a quarter turn so that the fold is on the left. Repeat rolling and folding process twice.
  3. Wrap in cling film and return to fridge for 20 minutes or place in freezer for 5 minutes before using as directed in recipes with puff pastry.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, wire rack, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

It is often assumed that puff pastry is easier to buy...not anymore! This easy recipe allows you the satisfaction of knowing you’ve made it yourself, and makes a great base for pastries, cream puffs and fruit tarts.

  • The success of this recipe depends on keeping the pastry dough chilled so the layers of butter do not melt. A cold work surface (marble or stone) and a cool room temperature are helpful. If at any time during the process the dough feels warm, place in the freezer for 5 minutes before rolling out again. To prevent the dough from sticking to the work surface, whenever necessary use a light dusting of flour, or roll dough between two sheets of baking paper.
  • Puff pastry dough freezes very well before baking. Defrost in the fridge before using. Partially rolling out the dough before freezing allows for faster defrosting.
  • Once baked, pastry keeps well for up to 15 days when stored in an airtight container in a cool dry place.
  • To cook, roll dough into a rectangle (5 mm thick) and cut into required shape or shapes just before baking. Bake for 20 minutes (200°C) for one large piece of pastry, 12-15 minutes (200°C) for smaller pastries. Allow to cool on a wire rack before serving or storing in an airtight container until needed.
