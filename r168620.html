Portuguese Custard Tarts

Ingredients

  • unsalted butter, to grease
  • 9 oz whole milk
  • 9 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 6 oz sugar
  • 2 oz all-purpose flour, plus extra to dust
  • 5 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ½ oz lemon peel, thin peel only, no pith (approx. ½ lg. lemon)
  • 17 oz puff pastry (see Tip)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Grease a 12 hole muffin pan and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place milk, cream, sugar, flour and egg yolks into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add cinnamon and lemon peel and cook 18 min/195°F/speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk and set mixing bowl aside to cool.
  4. On a lightly floured surface roll puff pastry to a thickness of ⅛ inch using a rolling pin. Cut out 12 rounds using a cookie cutter. Gently press dough into base and up the sides of prepared muffin pan.
  5. Quickly strain custard through a fine sieve. Divide custard into prepared dough molds, filling cups about ¾ full. Bake for 20-25 minutes (400°F) until golden brown. Transfer to a wire rack. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

rolling pin, fine sieve, 12 hole muffin pan, round cookie cutter (approx. 4 in.), wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Just before serving, sprinkle the tarts with ground cinnamon, confectioners sugar or fresh lemon zest.
  • Refer to The Basic Cookbook for Quick Puff Pastry recipe.
  • Store-bought puff pastry needs to completely thawed before using. Remove package of puff pastry from freezer and let it thaw in the refrigerator overnight.
