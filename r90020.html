Pearl rice meatballs

Ingredients

  • 200 g white glutinous rice
  • 350 g pork belly meat, boneless, skinless, cut in pieces (5 cm) (see variation)
  • 3 - 4 fresh shiitake mushrooms (approx. 50 g),
  • 25 g carrot
  • 20 g fresh water chestnuts, skinned, cut in halves,
  • 5 - 10 g fresh ginger, thinly sliced
  • 20 g spring onions, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 pinches ground white pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornflour (starch)
  • 20 g soy sauce
  • 600 g water

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid, weigh glutinous rice into it, cover rice with water and set aside to soak. Set a plate on mixing bowl lid and weigh pork belly meat onto it, then place in freezer. Soak rice and freeze pork belly meat for 1 hour.
  2. Place semi-frozen pork belly meat in mixing bowl and chop 25 sec/speed 6.
  3. Add shiitake mushrooms, carrot, water chestnuts, ginger, spring onions, white pepper, salt, sugar, cornflour and soy sauce and chop 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  4. Knead 1 min/. Transfer mixture to a plate. Using spatula, divide into 4 equal parts, then from each part shape 8 small balls (approx. Ø 3 cm). Strain soaked rice through simmering basket then roll balls in the soaked rice, to cover each ball all over with rice. Arrange 14 balls in Varoma dish, insert Varoma tray and arrange 18 balls on it.
  5. Place water in mixing bowl. Set Varoma in position and steam 30 min/Varoma/speed 1. Serve hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • Delight your family and friends with these tasty and easy meatballs. The rice curls away from them as they steam, making the meatballs appear coated in pearls. Serve them as a light main dish for the family, or with drinks at a party.
  • If using dried mushrooms, cover them with hot water and soak for 30 minutes in step 1, then drain and set aside for step 3.
  • Pearl rice meatballs are usually served without a dip. However, Thai sweet and sour sauce can be served as a dip for this dish, if desired.
  • You can find glutinous rice in Asian supermarkets, or replace it with any other sticky rice, such as sushi rice.
  • You can replace pork belly with pork mince, provided the pork mince is fatty (30% fat), as pork fat is necessary to give the meatballs their smooth and soft texture. If using pork mince, omit freezing in step 1 and omit step 2.
  • Replace fresh water chestnuts with canned water chestnuts, drained before using.
