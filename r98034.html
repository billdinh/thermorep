Mini Hamburgers

Ingredients

Mini Brioche Buns

  • 20 g butter, plus 1 tsp for egg wash and extra for greasing
  • 85 g water, plus 1 tsp for egg wash
  • 25 g milk
  • 1 tsp dried yeast
  • 20 g caster sugar
  • 190 g bread flour
  • 25 g plain flour
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 15 g sesame seeds

Burgers

  • 60 g Stilton
  • 450 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 100 g port

Preparation

Mini Brioche Buns

  1. Line a large baking tray with baking paper and grease a large bowl.
  2. Place water, milk and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 1 min/37°C/speed 2.
  3. Add sugar, bread flour, plain flour, butter, 1 egg and salt then knead 5 min/. Scrape down sides of bowl with spatula then knead again 5 min/.
  4. Transfer dough to prepared large bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 2 hours).
  5. Once risen, divide sticky dough into 24 equal-sized pieces. Shape into balls (see tip) and place on prepared baking tray 2 cm apart. Cover loosely with greased cling film and leave to rise in a warm place for 1 hour. Meanwhile, make burgers.

Burgers

  1. Place Stilton in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in beef. Remove from lid.
  3. Season beef with salt and pepper then divide into 24 pieces. Take one piece and hide ¼ tsp chopped Stilton in centre then shape into a patty by hand or by pressing into a cookie cutter (approx. Ø 5 cm). Set aside on a baking tray. Repeat with remaining pieces then refrigerate until ready to cook.
  4. Place port in mixing bowl and reduce 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 190°C to bake buns.
  5. Place a shallow baking dish in the bottom of the oven. Beat 1 egg with 1 tsp water in a small bowl. Brush risen buns with egg wash then sprinkle sesame seeds over the top.
  6. Pour 2 cups of water into baking dish in oven (to create steam). Bake buns for 10-15 minutes (190°C) or until golden brown. Transfer to a wire rack to cool.
  7. Heat a griddle pan until hot. Cook patties over a moderately high heat for 2-3 minutes on each side. Transfer to a plate and drizzle reduced port over each patty.
  8. Serve burgers warm in brioche buns.

Useful Items

large bowl, baking tray and paper, cling film, baking tray, cookie cutter, pastry brush, baking dish (approx. 22 cm x 30 cm), wire rack, skillet or griddle pan, refrigerator, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • To shape dough into tight balls, gently flatten each piece. Pull up the sides pinching together in the centre. Turn over (seam side down) then place your palm over the top and gently roll into a smooth ball.
