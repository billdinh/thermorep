Broccoli and Cheddar Baked Potatoes

Ingredients

  • 40 oz water
  • 2 russet potatoes, large (approx. 28-32 oz total), washed
  • 6 oz broccoli florets
  • 7 oz sharp cheddar cheese, cubed
  • 6 oz plain Greek yogurt
  • 4 oz unsalted butter, softened, cubed
  • 2 oz whole milk
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • salt, to season (optional)
  • ground black pepper, to season (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • salt, to sprinkle potato skins
  • fresh chives, for garnish, minced

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position, weigh in potatoes, secure Varoma lid and steam 50 min/Varoma/speed 2. Carefully remove Varoma lid and add broccoli, secure Varoma lid and steam a further 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Remove Varoma. Quickly slice potatoes in half lengthwise and set aside to cool slightly. Rinse mixing bowl with cold water and dry thoroughly.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  4. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Scoop out and reserve the potato flesh leaving a ¼ inch border (see Tip), being careful not to pierce the skin. Transfer potato skins to prepared baking sheet and set aside.
  6. Place steamed potato flesh (approx. 14 oz), steamed broccoli, ¾ of the reserved grated cheese (approx. 5 oz), yogurt, butter, milk, salt, pepper and paprika into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Adjust seasoning and if necessary mix a further 15 sec/speed 4.
  7. Brush the outside of potato skins with oil and sprinkle with salt, for a crispier skin.
  8. Spoon potato mixture into potato skin shells. Bake potatoes for 20 minutes (375°F), then top with remaining grated cheese and bake for 5-10 minutes (375°F) or until cheese melts. Garnish with chopped chives and serve hot.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, basting brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • To scoop out potato flesh, use a paring knife to score the potato halves, leaving a border (approx. ¼ in. thick). Then use a spoon to carefuly scoop out the flesh.
  • If potato skin is compromised or too thin to hold the filling, create a foil boat by scrunching a strip of foil and shaping around the potato skin before adding filling.
  • Make the potato mixture ahead and stuff the shells just before baking.
  • Garnish with sour cream or plain yogurt.
