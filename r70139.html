Rosemary meat pies

Ingredients

Shortcrust pastry

  • 400 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 200 g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 100 g water, chilled, plus extra if needed

Iced puff pastry

  • 90 g ice cubes
  • 180 g unsalted butter, frozen and cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 1 ½ tsp salt

Pie filling

  • butter, melted, for greasing
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary (10 cm), leaves only
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 brown onion (approx.180 g), cut into halves
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 700 g beef rump steak, cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Vegemite®
  • 100 g button mushrooms, cut into pieces
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • full cream milk, for brushing

Preparation

Shortcrust pastry

  1. Place flour, butter and salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 6 or until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Add water and knead 20 sec/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface, then knead dough into a tight ball. Wrap shortcrust pastry in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Iced puff pastry

  1. Place ice cubes and butter into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add flour and salt and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 2 min/.
  4. Wrap iced puff pastry in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pie filling

  1. Grease 2 x 12-hole muffin trays with melted butter and set aside.
  2. Place rosemary into mixing bowl and chop 5-8 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add cornflour, beef, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, Vegemite®, mushrooms, pepper and reserved rosemary and cook 14 min/100°C//speed 1. Allow filling to cool completely before assembling pies.
  6. Preheat oven to 200°C. Using a rolling pin, roll out shortcrust pastry on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface to a 2 mm thickness.
  7. Line 1 prepared muffin tray with dough, cutting away any excess. Reform excess pastry into a ball, roll out again to a 2 mm thickness and repeat with remaining muffin tray. Place lined trays into freezer to chill for 15 minutes.
  8. Once chilled, fill each pie shell with cooled beef mixture.
  9. Using a rolling pin, roll out iced puff pastry on a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface to a 5 mm thickness. Cut into 24 discs large enough to cover the top of each pie. Place 1 disc on top of each pie and seal by pressing around the edges with your finger tips.
  10. Cut a slit in the top of each pie and brush the top of each pie with a little milk. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until pastry is golden and puffy. Allow to cool slightly before removing from trays. Serve pies warm or at room temperature.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, 12-hole muffin tray, rolling pin, pastry cutter, pastry brush, bowl, oven, refrigerator, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve meat pies with tomato ketchup (make your own using the recipe in The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook).
  • Wrap pies in plastic wrap and store in the freezer for future meals.
  • For lunchboxes, defrost pies in the refrigerator the night before, then place into lunchboxes in the morning.
  • For a mince meat pie: partially freeze your beef fillet, then cut into pieces and mince / Turbo/0.5 sec/2-4 times and proceed as per recipe.
  • Quick version: use 10-12 sheets shop-brought puff pastry instead of making shortcrust pastry and iced puff pastry.
