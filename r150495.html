Miso fish with Asian greens

Ingredients

  • 60 g mirin
  • 60 g sake (see Tips)
  • 150 g shiro (white) miso paste
  • 40 g white sugar
  • 600 - 650 g firm white fish fillets, skin on (see Tips)
  • peanut oil
  • 500 g water
  • 1 bunch Asian greens of choice
  • black sesame seeds, for garnishing
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1710 kJ / 407 kcal
Protein
41.1 g
Carbohydrates
26.1 g

15 g

Preparation

  1. Place mirin and sake into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  2. Add miso and sugar and cook 1 min/90°C/speed 3 or until miso has dissolved completely. Transfer marinade into a large baking dish and set aside to cool (approx. 10 minutes). Add fish fillets to cooled marinade and coat completely. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate overnight.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C. Set aside a baking tray (30 x 40 cm).
  4. Place a heavy-based frying pan over high heat and add oil. Remove fish fillets from marinade and set marinade aside. Place fish, skin side up, into frying pan and fry for 2 minutes on each sides, until browned. Transfer fish onto baking tray and bake for 5 minutes (200°C).
  5. Meanwhile, place water into mixing bowl and place Varoma dish into position. Place Asian greens into Varoma, secure Varoma lid and steam 5-8 min/Varoma/speed 1, until just wilted.
  6. Carefully remove baking tray from oven. Brush fish fillets lightly with some of the reserved marinade. Return fish to oven and bake for a further 5-8 minutes (200°C), until fish is opaque and flakes easily. Garnish fish with black sesame seeds and serve with Asian greens.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, large frying pan, pastry brush, ceramic or glass baking dish, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), refrigerator, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can find mirin in the international food aisle in most major supermarkets and in Asian food stores.
  • Sake is fermented rice wine, and can be found at most bottle shops and some Asian food stores.
  • We recommend using cod fillets in this recipe, but any firm white fish fillets can be used.
