Biltong, Mushroom and Feta Paptert

Ingredients

Filling and Topping

  • 120 g Gruyère cheese, cut in pieces
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 80 g red onions, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 250 g brown mushrooms, fresh, sliced
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 Tbsp fresh chives, chopped

Paptert Filling and Assembling

  • 800 g water
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 170 g maize flour, white
  • 20 g unsalted butter
  • 200 g tinned sweetcorn
  • 150 g biltong, cut in thin slices
  • 100 g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 200 g double cream
  • 10 fresh basil leaves, roughly chopped

Preparation

Filling and Topping

  1. Place Gruyère cheese in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place oil, onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add mushrooms and salt then sauté 8 min/120°C//speed .
  4. Add chives and mix with spatula. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Paptert Filling and Assembling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  2. Place water and salt in mixing bowl then heat 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add white maize flour and cook 12 min/90°C/speed 3.
  4. Add butter, corn kernels and biltong then stir in with spatula. Transfer half the filling to a casserole dish. Arrange mushroom filling on top, sprinkle with feta cheese and pour over 100 g cream. Spread over remaining filling, pour over remaining 100 g cream and sprinkle with reserved grated Gruyère cheese. Bake for 20 minutes (200°C) or until golden. Serve warm garnished with basil leaves.

Hints & Tricks

  • Make the paptert a day ahead and keep in the fridge until needed. Finish in the oven just before serving.
  • Leftovers will keep well in the fridge until the following day. Reheat before serving.
  • For a vegetarian version, try replacing the biltong with sautéed vegetables.
  • Experiment by using your favourite cheese for this recipe. Cheddar, Gouda, mozzarella and blue cheese will all work well.
  • Biltong is a form of dried, cured meat that originated in South Africa. Various types of meat are used to produce it, ranging from beef and game meats, to fillets of ostrich from commercial farms.
    Paptert (porridge pie) is a delicious modern addition to a South African barbeque (braai). It resembles a lasagne but is made from maize porridge (mieliepap) and includes a generous vegetable filling which ensures a rich, moist and decadent savoury tart.
