Banh mi

Ingredients

Pork

  • 300 g boneless pork shoulder, cut into slices (approx. 1 cm thick)
  • 60 g sugar
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 eschalots
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 stalk fresh lemongrass, crushed and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 500 g water
  • vegetable oil, for frying

Pickled carrot

  • 100 g white wine vinegar
  • 70 g sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 carrot, peeled into ribbons

Chicken liver pate

  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ brown onion (approx. 60 g)
  • 40 g salted butter
  • 250 g chicken livers, washed, trimmed and cut into pieces (approx. 4 cm)
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp brandy
  • 2 pinches freshly ground black pepper
  • 30 g pouring (whipping) cream

Assembly

  • 1 baguette (see Tips)
  • 50 g mayonnaise (see Tips)
  • 10 pieces pickled jalapeño chillies, cut into pieces
  • ¼ cucumber, cut into thin slices
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

Pork

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pork slices into it. Set bowl aside.
  2. Place sugar, fish sauce, pepper, eschalots, garlic, sesame oil and soy sauce into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into bowl with pork. Add lemongrass into bowl with pork and mix to coat completely. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 3 hours or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Remove excess marinade from pork, discard marinade and lemongrass.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl. Place pork into Varoma dish and tray. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove pork from Varoma. Use paper towel to remove excess moisture from pork slices.
  4. Place a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Drizzle pork with vegetable oil and fry until charred on both sides and cooked through (approx. 4 minutes on each side). Cut into thin slices (approx. 5 mm), cover and set aside to cool.

Pickled carrot

  1. Place vinegar, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/90°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl, add carrots into bowl and stir to combine. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes. Drain, discarding pickling liquid, and set aside.

Chicken liver pate

  1. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add all remaining pate ingredients and cook 5-7 min/100°C/speed 1, until livers are cooked but still pink, then blend 30 sec/speed 8.
  4. Place a fine-mesh sieve over a bowl and transfer pate mixture into it. Using spatula, press pate through sieve to get a smooth consistancy. Discard contents of sieve. Transfer pate into an sealable container and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Cut baguette into 4 portions, and then slice each portion in half lengthways. Spread each portion with reserved pate and mayonnaise, then layer with pork, chillies, cucumber, Pickled carrot and coriander.

Useful Items

large bowl, plastic wrap, fine mesh sieve, bowl, paper towel, large frying pan, plate, kitchen towel, sieve, spatula, serving plates, sealable container, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Bánh mì is a Vietnamese term for all kinds of bread. The word is derived from bánh and mì. Bread, or more specifically the baguette, was introduced by the French during its colonial period. The term is also used to describe a Vietnamese sandwich.
  • Ham can also be added to Bahn mi.
  • You can cover pate with extra melted butter to seal if desired. Pate will keep in refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Mayonnaise recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for baguette recipe.
