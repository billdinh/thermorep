Parsnip Blinis with Stilton, Walnuts and Honey

Ingredients

  • 400 g parsnips, peeled, sliced in rounds (1-2 cm)
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for frying
  • 50 g olive oil, plus extra for frying
  • 2 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 30 g crème fraîche
  • 30 g milk
  • 2 Tbsp rice flour
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 80 g walnut halves
  • 1 ½ Tbsp honey
  • 100 g Stilton, crumbled

Preparation

  1. Place parsnips, butter and oil in mixing bowl then sauté 20 min/100°C/speed . Purée 30 sec/speed 8 then transfer to a bowl. Cool to room temperature, then place in fridge to chill for 1 hour. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites in mixing bowl and whisk 30-1 min/speed 3.5 until soft peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer whisked egg whites to a bowl.
  3. Place egg yolks, crème fraîche, milk, reserved parsnip purée, rice flour, salt and pepper then whip 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a large bowl.
  4. Using a large spoon, gently fold reserved whisked egg whites into parsnip mixture.
  5. Heat a frying pan with a little oil and butter over medium heat. Drop 1 Tbsp mixture per blini into pan to make 4-5 cm discs, frying a few at a time. Cook until mixture bubbles on top, then flip over to cook other side. Continue cooking blinis in batches, adding more butter and oil as necessary.
  6. Add walnut halves and honey to warm frying pan then sauté for 1-2 minutes. Top blinis with crumbled Stilton then place a honeyed walnut half on top of each and serve immediately.

Useful Items

frying pan, large bowl, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • These blinis can be cooked ahead of time and frozen until needed. Simply defrost blinis, warm through on a frying pan over a medium heat, then follow recipe from step 6.
  • These delicious canapés are a take on traditional crème fraîche and smoked salmon blinis.
