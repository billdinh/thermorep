Peruvian Ceviche

Ingredients

  • 500 g water
  • 2 sweet potatoes, unpeeled (approx. 150 g each)
  • 150 g lime juice, freshly squeezed
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 Tbsp coriander leaves, plus extra to garnish
  • 80 g red onion, 40 g thinly sliced
  • ½ habanero chilli, seeded
  • 50 g clam juice (optional)
  • 4 ice cubes
  • 300 g fresh sea bass fillets, skinless, cubed (1 cm)
  • 300 g fresh salmon fillets, skinless, cubed (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and add sweet potatoes to it then steam 20-25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket using spatula and transfer sweet potatoes to a bowl. Allow to cool at room temperature for 40 minutes. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place lime juice, garlic cloves, coriander leaves, 40 g red onion, habanero chilli, clam juice and ice cubes in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 8. Strain tiger milk through a sieve into a medium bowl.
  3. Add to the liquid 40 g red onion, sea bass, salmon and salt and allow to marinate for 2-3 minutes. Meanwhile, halve sweet potatoes lengthwise and using a small melon baller, scoop out potato balls and add to ceviche.
  4. Using a slotted spoon, divide ceviche between 6 small bowls or onto 6 plates. Drizzle ceviche with tiger milk, garnish with coriander leaves and serve immediately.

Useful Items

slotted spoon, melon baller, fine mesh sieve

Hints & Tricks

  • Leche de tigre, or tiger's milk, is the Peruvian term for a citrus-based marinade that cures the seafood in a ceviche. Also known as leche de pantera, this leftover fish runoff usually contains lime juice, sliced onion, chillies, salt and pepper - along with some fish juice. 
    In Peru, this invigorating potion is often served alongside ceviche in a small glass and believed to be both a hangover cure as well as an aphrodisiac!
