Burfee

Ingredients

Sugar syrup

  • 200 g white sugar
  • 100 g water
  • 1 drop rose essence

Burfee

  • 360 g powdered milk, full fat
  • 20 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 80 g desiccated coconut
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 220 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 3 tbsp slivered almonds

Preparation

Sugar syrup

  1. Place sugar and water into mixing bowl and heat 18 min/Varoma/speed 2, without measuring cup (see Tips).
  2. Add rose essence and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer syrup into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Burfee

  1. Line a deep baking tin (18 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place milk powder, ghee, desiccated coconut, cardamom and cream into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  3. Mix 1 min/speed 2, slowly pouring reserved sugar syrup through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Insert measuring cup. Blend 1 min/speed 2 -6, increasing speed gradually from speed 2 to speed 6.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix 10 sec/speed 8, until mixture starts to come together. Transfer into prepared baking tin and sprinkle with almonds.
  6. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to set overnight. Remove from refrigerator and cut into pieces (approx. 2 x 3 cm). Serve immediately or store in a sealable container in refrigerator until ready to serve.

Useful Items

thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, square cake tin (18 cm), baking paper, square cake tin (20 cm), plastic wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ghee, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • The sugar syrup should be clear and have a sticky texture and will leave sticky residue on the spatula. To test the consistency, dip the tip of the spatula in hot syrup, allow to cool briefly, then touch syrup and lift your finger. If visible threads of syrup come away from spatula, then syrup consistency is correct. If needed, continue to cook until desired consistency is achieved.

Burfee (Barfi) is a fudge-like milk sweet, and often served at Deepavali/Diwali celebrations. Like fudge, the process of making burfee may seem simple however can be tricky. If the syrup doesn't have a sticky texture the Burfee will not set.
