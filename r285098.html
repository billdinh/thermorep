Lamb Roll with Freekeh Pilaf

Ingredients

Lamb Roll

  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 10 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 500 g lamb mince, low-fat (5-10%), broken in pieces (see tip)
  • 50 g tomato purée
  • 2 Tbsp Urfa chilli flakes (see tip)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 500 g puff pastry, ready-made (see tip)
  • 1 medium egg, beaten
  • 1 Tbsp nigella seeds

Freekeh Pilaf

  • 2 Tbsp cumin seeds
  • 15 g olive oil
  • 140 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • 400 g freekeh (uncooked)
  • 50 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 240 g tinned chickpeas, rinsed, drained (1 x 400 g tin)
  • 600 g water
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 150 g Greek yoghurt
  • 10 g fresh coriander leaves, roughly chopped

Preparation

Lamb Roll

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place garlic, onions and parsley in mixing bowl then chop 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add lamb mince, tomato purée, chilli flakes, salt and pepper then mix 20 sec//speed 3.
  4. Roll out pastry (approx. 40 cm x 30 cm) then transfer to prepared tray. Lay mince mixture across centre of pastry and shape into a log (approx. 30 cm x 7 cm). Fold one side of pastry over the log, brush with beaten egg and fold over other side of pastry. Roll log over so that the join is underneath then seal ends of pastry. Brush all over with beaten egg then sprinkle with nigella seeds.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes (180°C) until pastry is deep golden brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare freekeh.

Freekeh Pilaf

  1. Place cumin seeds in mixing bowl and toast 6 min/Varoma/speed .
  2. Add oil and onions then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add tomato purée, freekeh and butter then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1
  4. Add tomatoes and chickpeas then mix with spatula.
  5. Add water, vegetable stock, salt and pepper then cook 20-25 min/98°C/speed  until freekeh is cooked. Transfer to a serving dish.
  6. Spoon yoghurt over freekeh and sprinkle with coriander. Transfer roll to a serving platter then slice and serve warm with freekeh.

Useful Items

pastry brush, large baking tray, baking paper, rolling pin, serving platter, serving dish, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This exotic Middle Eastern dish is bursting with fresh, vibrant flavours and textures. Tender lamb seasoned with Turkish chilli flakes is rolled in buttery puff pastry, then baked until golden and crisp. Served with freekeh, an ancient, protein-rich grain with an earthy, nutty flavour, this impressive main would make a great choice next time you’re entertaining friends or family.
  • Use low-fat mince, otherwise too much fat may leak out during cooking causing the pastry to become soggy.
  • Try to use Urfa chilli flakes, if available. These flakes have a very particular taste - smoky, sweet, earthy but not too spicy.
  • To make your own puff pastry, follow the recipe in The Basic Cookbook.
  • This recipe also works with filo pastry - wrap in 6 large sheets. Brush with melted butter and reduce cooking time slightly.
