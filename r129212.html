Samke harra

Ingredients

Tahini sauce

  • olive oil, for frying
  • 40 g pine nuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 120 g tahini
  • 50 g lemon juice
  • 150 g warm water
  • ¼ tsp chilli powder
  • ½ - 1 tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground pepper

Steamed fish

  • 1 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 600 - 700 g skinless snapper fillets (appprox. 4 fillets)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • sea salt, to season
  • freshly ground pepper, to season
  • 500 g water
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, roughly chopped for garnishing

Preparation

Tahini sauce

  1. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add a little oil. Place pine nuts into pan and toast until browned (approx. 4 minutes), stirring frequently. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place 20 g of the toasted pine nuts into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Place garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add tahini, lemon juice, water, chilli powder, salt and pepper and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add reserved milled pine nuts and heat 8 min/90°C/speed 2. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Steamed fish

  1. Lightly grease Varoma tray. Place Varoma into position and weigh fish fillets onto Varoma tray, then drizzle with oil, sprinkle with lemon juice and season with salt and pepper. Set Varoma aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position with fish, secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until fish is cooked through and flakes easily.
  3. Transfer fish onto a serving platter. Pour reserved Tahini sauce over fillets, garnish with remaining pine nuts and sprinkle with parsley before serving.

Useful Items

non-stick frying pan, serving platter, bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Samke harra is a traditional Lebanese fish dish; try serving it with pita breads, fried cauliflower, Moghrabieh couscous, fattoush or tabbouleh.
  • For additional depth of flavour, add 1 teaspoon ground coriander to tahini sauce in step 4.
  • Add 40 g walnuts to the pine nuts when toasting.
  • Vegetarian samke harra: substitute fish with cauliflower florets or potato or a mixture of both.
