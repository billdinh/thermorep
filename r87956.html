Apricot Raspberry Cheesecake

Ingredients

Shortcrust Pastry

  • 100 g butter, soft, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 160 g plain flour
  • 25 g cocoa powder
  • 70 g sugar
  • 1 medium egg
  • ½ tsp baking powder

Filling

  • 500 g curd cheese (quark), or fromage frais
  • 150 g milk
  • 70 g sunflower oil
  • 170 g sugar
  • 45 g cornflour (starch)
  • 20 g lemon juice
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 400 g apricots, ripe, quartered
  • 150 g raspberries

Preparation

Shortcrust Pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a spring form cake tin (Ø 26 cm) then line base with baking paper.
  2. Place butter, flour, cocoa powder, sugar, egg and baking powder in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 6. Press pastry into prepared cake tin and form a rim of about 3 cm. Prick with a fork.

Filling

  1. Place quark or fromage frais, milk, oil, sugar, cornflour, lemon juice, eggs and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add apricots and raspberries then stir in with spatula. Transfer to tart shell then bake for 1 hour (170°C), covering with foil after 30 minutes if top gets too dark.
  3. Leave to rest in oven with door open for 10 minutes. Remove from oven and leave to cool completely before placing in fridge. Serve chilled, cut in slices.

Useful Items

baking paper, spring form (Ø 26 cm), aluminium foil, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a variation of a traditional European cheesecake, where it is very common to have a shortcrust pastry under a tasty filling made from quark or fromage frais.
  • Having the option to use frozen or tinned fruits means you can enjoy this dessert throughout the year.
