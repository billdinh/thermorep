Potted baby vegetables

Ingredients

Rye and chia soil

  • 150 g pitted black olives
  • 260 g lukewarm water
  • 5 g dried instant yeast (1 heaped teaspoon)
  • 30 g black chia seeds
  • 1 tsp squid ink (see Tips)
  • oil, for greasing
  • 230 g rye grains
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Tahini dip

  • 180 g tahini
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 300 g Greek yoghurt, drained for 1 hour
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes
  • salt, to taste

Raw vegetables

  • 16 baby carrots, peeled and trimmed (1 cm of tops intact)
  • 8 baby asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 24 fresh enoki mushrooms
  • 8 snow peas, split open lengthways to expose peas
  • 8 broccolini spears
  • 8 baby corn

Preparation

Rye and chia soil

  1. Preheat oven to 100°C. Place olives onto a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) and dry out for 3 hours (100°C), with door slightly ajar, until olives are dry but not hard. Set aside to cool. Turn oven off to cool completely.
  2. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water and yeast into it. Leave for 5 minutes, then place small bowl back onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chia seeds and add squid ink, then stir to combine. Leave for 5 minutes.
  3. Oil a large bowl and set aside. Place rye grains into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10.
  4. Add chia mixture and salt and knead 2 min/. Transfer into oiled bowl, cover and set aside in a warm place for 1 hour to rise.
  5. Line a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) with baking paper. Transfer dough onto baking tray and shape into a rectangle (approx. 25 x 15 cm).
  6. Place into cold oven and set oven temperature to 190°C. When oven reaches temperature (190°C), bake for 15 minutes (190°C) then set aside to cool.
  7. Cut loaf into thirds. Set aside two-thirds of loaf, and cut remaining portion into 4 pieces. Place bread pieces into mixing bowl and chop 8 sec/speed 7.
  8. Add dried olives and pepper and mix 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a sealable container and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tahini dip

  1. Place tahini, lemon juice and oil into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 4.
  2. Add yoghurt, cumin and chilli flakes and blend 10 sec/speed 6.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Season to taste, then transfer into a sealable containter and place into refrigerator until ready to serve.

Raw vegetables

  1. Fill 8 mini terracotta flower pots two-thirds full with Tahini dip, then place vegetables into tahini (to appear as if they are sticking up from the ground). Trim vegetables as needed. Sprinkle Rye and chia soil around vegetables. Serve with extra Rye and chia bread, or place into refrigerator for up to 2 hours until ready to serve.

Useful Items

baking tray (20 x 30 cm), small bowl , large bowl, plastic wrap or kitchen towel, baking paper, sealable container, 8 mini terracotta flower pots, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Olives can also be dried in a dehydrator.
  • Drying olives can be removed from oven while bread is baking, then returned to cooling oven to complete drying.
  • You can find rye grains at health food stores and bulk food stores.
  • Use seasonal baby vegetables as available.
  • Squid ink can be purchased in small sachets from speciality food stores.
