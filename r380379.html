Peach, apple and lamb purée

Ingredients

  • 2 yellow peaches (approx. 200 g)
  • boiling water
  • 500 g water, plus extra if needed
  • 100 g lamb, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 2 apples (approx. 200 g), peeled, cored and cut into pieces

Preparation

  1. Score a cross in the top and bottom of each peach. Place into a bowl and carefully cover with boiling water. Set aside for 5-10 minutes or until skin starts to peel and soften. Using simmering basket, drain and set aside to cool. Once cooled, remove and discard skin and stones.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh lamb into it. Add apples and peach flesh into simmering basket and steam 15 min/100°C/speed 2 or until tender (see Tips). Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard water.
  3. Place cooked peaches, apples and lamb into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 4-8, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 8 or until smooth.
  4. Set aside 1 serving, then freeze the remainder in individual portions for future use. If needed, stir through a little more water or your baby's milk to get the consistency just right before serving.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, bowl, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Lamb is a good iron-rich food for babies, but like many meats, it isn't always an ideal texture or taste for babies, who innately prefer sweeter flavours. Mixing it with fruit makes it more likely to be accepted by your baby.
  • If peaches are properly ripe, they will not need much cooking. You can add them just for the final few minutes of the cooking time, ensuring they're tender before blending.
