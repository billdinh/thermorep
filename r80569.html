Upside-down Gingerbread with Pears

Ingredients

  • 100 g butter, cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 110 g milk
  • 220 g brown sugar
  • 400 g tin of pear halves
  • 30 g walnut halves
  • 75 g black treacle
  • 1 large egg
  • 110 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch ground cloves

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Butter sides and base of a deep cake tin (Ø 20 cm) - must not be loose-bottomed!
  2. Set a bowl on mixing bowl lid and place lemon juice and milk in it then set aside to sour.
  3. Place 50 g butter and 110 g sugar in mixing bowl then heat 3 min/90°C/speed 3. Pour into prepared tin, spreading it out evenly. Drain pears through simmering basket then arrange pears on butter-sugar topping, cut side down if using halves and place walnuts or pecans between pears.
  4. Place 50 g butter in mixing bowl and heat 1 min/50°C/speed 3.
  5. Add 110 g brown sugar, reserved soured milk, black treacle and egg then mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  6. Add plain flour, ground cinnamon, ground ginger, bicarbonate of soda, salt, ground nutmeg and ground cloves then mix 10 sec/speed 6. Pour on top of pears - this is a very liquid cake mixture.
  7. Bake 40-50 minutes (180°C) or until a skewer inserted in middle comes out clean. Leave to cool 15 minutes in the tin then turn out onto a plate and serve warm.

Useful Items

round cake tin (Ø 20 cm), bowl, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with cream or vanilla ice cream.
  • Pecans make a nice alternative to walnuts.
