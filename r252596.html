Flourless Chocolate Coffin Cake

Ingredients

Chocolate Cake

  • 8 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 160 g sugar
  • 280 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 140 g hazelnuts
  • 280 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 10 egg yolks, from large eggs

Ganache

  • 200 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 180 g whipping cream
  • 1 ½ tsp black food colouring

Preparation

Chocolate Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Lightly grease a baking dish and line with baking paper.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and 80 g sugar in mixing bowl then whip 5-6 min/speed 3.5. Transfer whipped egg whites to a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place chocolate and hazelnuts in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add butter and heat 2 min 30 sec/70°C/speed 2.
  5. Add egg yolks and 80 g sugar then mix 1 min/speed 4. Gently fold chocolate mixture with spatula into reserved whipped egg whites until fully mixed. Pour cake batter into prepared baking dish and bake for 35 minutes (180°C) or until a cocktail stick inserted in centre comes out clean. Allow to cool completely before icing (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Ganache

  1. Place chocolate, cream and black food colouring in mixing bowl then melt 2-3 min/45°C/speed 3.

Assembly

  1. Place a serving platter upside down over the cooled cake and carefully invert cake onto platter. Carefully remove baking dish and peel off baking paper. Trim cake as needed with a sharp knife for straight edges.
  2. Evenly spread ganache on top of cake and down the sides with a palette knife. Decorate according to tastes (see tip) and allow ganache to set before serving.

Useful Items

cocktail stick, baking paper, sharp knife, palette knife, large bowl, serving platter, baking dish (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cut cake to resemble a traditional coffin shape by cutting a 2.5 cm triangle from one corner of the cake. Cut opposite corner to match; this will be the top end of coffin. Cut long, narrow triangles (2.5 cm x 10 cm) off bottom corners to form bottom end. Decorate to your liking.
  • Sprinkle chocolate wafer biscuit crumbs on serving platter to look like dirt. Place coffin cake on top of wafer "dirt" crumbs and add a few gummy worms next to the coffin.
