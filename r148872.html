Sultana and Cranberry Rolls

Ingredients

Dough

  • 100 g dried cranberries, roughly chopped
  • 250 g sultanas, roughly chopped
  • 2 oranges, juice and finely grated zest only
  • 40 g orange liqueur (optional)
  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of skin only
  • 40 g sugar
  • 250 g milk, plus extra for glazing
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 2 pinches ground nutmeg
  • 60 g butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 500 g strong white bread flour

Filling

  • 60 g sugar
  • 3 Tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 130 g butter, soft

Topping

  • 150 g icing sugar
  • 1 ½ Tbsp water

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh into bowl dried cranberries, sultanas, orange zest, orange juice and orange liqueur (if using). Remove from lid, stir then cover with cling film and leave to soak overnight.
  2. The next day, place lemon peel and sugar in mixing bowl then grind 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place milk and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 0.5. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and line a large, deep baking tray (approx. 26 cm x 34 cm) with baking paper.
  4. Add reserved lemon sugar mixture, egg, salt, cinnamon, mixed spice, nutmeg, butter and flour then mix 5 sec/speed 5. Knead 2 min/. Transfer to prepared bowl and leave to rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  5. Place sugar and cinnamon in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10. Add butter and blend 10 sec/speed 10.
  6. Divide dough into 3 pieces (approx. 280 g each) and roll each into a rectangle (25 cm x 30 cm). Leaving a 2 cm border along short edges, spread ⅓ cinnamon mixture from mixing bowl over rectangle then, stir marinated fruit, and distribute ⅓ over rectangle. Roll up tightly along longest edge and slice into 8 pieces, then tightly pack onto prepared baking tray (cut side up).
  7. Repeat with remaining 2 rectangles then cover with cling film and leave to rise in a warm place for 45 minutes. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 190°C.
  8. Brush rolls with milk then bake for 35-40 minutes (190°C), covering with foil after 25 minutes to prevent over browning. Transfer to a wire rack to cool.
  9. Once rolls are cool, mix icing sugar and water together in a bowl then drizzle over rolls. Serve or store in an airtight container.

Useful Items

cling film, large bowl, deep baking tray (24 cm x 24 cm x 4 cm), long sharp knife, rolling pin, pastry brush, aluminium foil, wire rack, airtight container, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These rolls are best eaten the same day they are made.
