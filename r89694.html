Speculaas

Ingredients

  • 10 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 1 vanilla bean, cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp whole cloves
  • 1 whole nutmeg
  • 6 whole white peppercorns
  • ½ tsp dried fennel seeds
  • 260 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
  • 120 g dark muscovado sugar
  • 200 g unsalted butter
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
273 kJ / 65 kcal
Protein
0.7 g
Carbohydrates
7 g
Fat
4 g

Preparation

  1. Place cardamom pods into mixing bowl and mix 6 sec/speed 6. Separate cardamom pod husks from the seeds, then transfer seeds into a bowl. Discard husks.
  2. Place cinnamon, vanilla bean, cloves, nutmeg, peppercorns, fennel seeds and reserved cardamom seeds into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 9.
  3. Add plain flour, dark muscovado sugar and butter and blend 10 sec/speed 6, then knead 30 sec/, or until mixture forms into a ball. Transfer into a bowl, cover and place into refrigerator for 30 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line 2 large baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  5. Place dough onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and dust with a little extra flour. Roll out into a rectangle (35 x 30 cm), then using a cookie cutter, cut into desired shapes to form biscuits.
  6. Re-form dough and repeat dusting, rolling and cutting process until all dough has been used. Place biscuits onto prepared baking trays, allowing room for each biscuit to expand during baking. Bake for 6-8 minutes (200°C) or until biscuits are crisp and brown.
  7. Transfer biscuits onto a wire rack to cool completely, then serve immediately or transfer into a sealable container to store until use.

Useful Items

2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, plastic wrap, rolling pin, silicone bread mat, cookie cutter, wire rack, sealable storage container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These Dutch biscuits traditionally feature elaborate designs transferred by biscuit moulds designed especially for that purpose. Place these biscuits into a lovely gift box and give to friends and family.
  • The warm and spicy flavoursof these biscuits go beautifully with a glass of egg nog or milk, making them the perfect treat to leave out for a special someone Christmas Eve.
  • Pierce your biscuits with a drinking straw just before and just after baking to create a hole. Then, when you are ready to serve, thread the biscuits onto a drinking straw sitting in a bottle of warm milk.
