Three Egg French Omelet

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Monterey Jack cheese, cut into pieces (approx. ¾ in.)
  • 1 oz yellow onion
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • 1 oz red bell pepper
  • 1 oz button mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 oz ham, diced (approx. ¼ in.)
  • 1 oz tomatoes, diced (approx. ¼ in.)
  • ½ oz olive oil
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp whole milk
  • &frac18; tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • &frac18; tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • 17 ½ oz water
  • fresh chives, chopped to garnish

Preparation

  1. Cut parchment paper to fit Varoma tray (see Tip). Scrunch parchment paper into a ball under running water, squeeze out excess water, lay onto Varoma tray and set aside.
  2. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add bell pepper and chop 6 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add mushrooms, ham, tomatoes and olive oil and without measuring cup sauté 4 min/Varoma//speed . Transfer vegetables to prepared Varoma tray and spread out evenly with spatula.
  6. Place eggs, milk, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer beaten eggs to Varoma tray with vegetables and mix gently with spatula.
  7. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma into position and cook 13 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  8. Sprinkle reserved cheese over omelet and cook a further 2 min/Varoma/speed 2. Remove omelet from parchment paper by sliding your spatula under one end of the omelet and rolling it in one direction until it is completely rolled up. Garnish with chives and serve immediately.

Useful Items

parchment paper, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Any condensation can be removed with a paper towel before serving.
  • To cut parchment to fit Varoma tray: tear off a piece of parchment paper wide enough to cover Varoma tray. Place Varoma tray on top of parchment and cut around the tray to form an oval shape.
  • To avoid eggshell in your omelet, crack eggs into measuring cup then add to mixing bowl.
  • Ham can be replace with 2 oz of diced bacon.
