Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (approx. 1 in.)
  • 1 oz dried porcini mushrooms
  • 1 oz yellow onions
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 oz olive oil
  • 1 oz salted butter
  • 12 oz Arborio rice
  • 2 oz white wine
  • 2 - 3 tsp vegetable stock paste
  • 7 oz fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 37 oz water
  • fine sea salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place dried porcini mushrooms into mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add onions and garlic and chop 3 sec/speed 6.
  4. Add oil and butter and without measuring cup sauté 3 min/250°F/speed 1.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Add rice and wine and sauté 3 min/250°F//speed 1.
  6. Add stock paste, fresh mushrooms and water, place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid to help prevent splashing and cook 14 min/212°F//speed 1.
  7. Allow risotto to rest in mixing bowl for 1 minute then transfer to a serving bowl and keep warm. Combine reserved Parmesan cheese with risotto, mix well with spatula, then add salt and pepper, to taste. Allow to rest for at least 1 minute before serving.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Add your choice of flavors to the cooked Risotto such as: sun dried tomatoes, frozen peas, baby spinach leaves, cubed feta, lemon juice, asparagus tips, baby Roma tomatoes, fresh basil leaves, olives, fresh sliced chilies, prawns or other seafood, cream or mascarpone. Replace Parmesan with different strong flavored cheeses, such as Feta cubes or Gruyere. Rice will absorb as many flavors as you incorporate, so experiment with ingredients you love.
  • The use of Italian Arborio is recommended in the Thermomix® as the higher starch content and fuller grain size of this rice both contribute to a perfect risotto.
