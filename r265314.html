Christmas Morning Sticky Buns

Ingredients

Dough

  • 180 g milk
  • 80 g unsalted butter, melted and cooled, plus extra for greasing
  • 4 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 Tbsp granulated sugar
  • 2 ¼ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 480 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Caramel Sauce

  • 160 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 160 g double cream
  • 320 g dark brown sugar
  • 1 ½ Tbsp vanilla bean paste

Filling

  • 200 g pecan nuts, toasted
  • 50 g dark brown sugar
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Assembly

  • 20 g unsalted butter, melted

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place milk, butter, yolks, egg, sugar and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 3.
  2. Add flour and salt then knead 2-3 min/. Transfer to a large greased bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place to rise until doubled in volume (approx. 1½-2 hours). Clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Caramel Sauce

  1. Place butter, cream, sugar and vanilla bean paste in mixing bowl then without measuring cup, cook 7-8 min/Varoma/speed 2, until caramel is golden brown and glossy. Transfer to a bowl and leave to cool. Clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Filling

  1. Place pecans in mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl.
  2. Place brown sugar, caster sugar, 70 g reserved chopped pecans, cinnamon and salt in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Spread reserved caramel sauce over the bottom and up the sides of a 32 cm x 20 cm x 5 cm baking dish, then sprinkle over remaining reserved chopped pecans. Place in fridge to chill. Meanwhile, assemble buns.

Assembly

  1. On a floured work surface, roll risen dough out into a rectangle (approx. 45 cm x 30 cm). Brush all over with melted butter then sprinkle over reserved sugar and pecan filling.
  2. Starting on one long side, roll dough into a tight log. Using a sharp serrated knife, gently cut into 12 pieces, each 4-5 cm wide. Arrange buns cut side up in baking dish over pecan-caramel base, with larger buns around the outside to ensure even cooking.
  3. Cover baking dish with cling film and place in fridge overnight (up to 16 hours) until buns have risen and are pressed tightly together in baking dish (see tip).
  4. The next day, remove buns from fridge 1 hour before baking and preheat oven to 180°C.
  5. Place baking dish on a large baking tray, then bake for 15 minutes (180°C). Cover with foil and bake for a further 25-30 minutes (180°C) until buns are puffed and golden and internal temperature reads 75°C.
  6. Let buns rest in baking dish for 5 minutes, then carefully and quickly invert onto a large serving plate, transferring any caramel left behind to the tops and sides of the buns. Serve warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, cling film, rolling pin, pastry brush, serrated knife, aluminium foil, large baking tray, temperature probe, serving plate, baking dish (32 cm x 20 cm x 5 cm), fridge, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you would prefer to bake the buns the same day, at step 8 cover with cling film and prove another 1-2 hours at room temperature until buns press tightly together, then continue recipe from step 10.
