Dessert dukkah

Ingredients

  • 80 g shelled pistachios
  • 50 g pine nuts
  • 90 g white sesame seeds (see Tips)
  • 70 g raw buckwheat groats
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 140°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pistachios, pine nuts, sesame seeds, buckwheat and cumin seeds into it. Spread mixture onto prepared baking tray. Bake for 30-40 minutes (140°C) or until toasted and aromatic, stirring once or twice if needed to ensure even cooking.
  3. Place cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, sugar and toasted nut mixture into mixing bowl and blend 6 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 2-6 sec/speed 7, until desired consistency is achieved.
  5. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and store in refrigerator for up to 3 months.

Useful Items

ceramic or glass bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, sealable container, sealable storage jar or container, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks


  • If you prefer, you can use black sesame seeds or a mix of black and white sesame seeds.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar for this dukkah - either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
  • Serve over yoghurt, fruit or ice cream. You can also use for breakfast sprinkled over porridge, cereal or smoothies.
