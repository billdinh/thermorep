Ricotta fruit tart

Ingredients

Almond quinoa pastry

  • 100 g butter, chilled and cut into pieces (3 cm), plus extra for greasing
  • 60 g raw almonds (see Tips)
  • 40 g quinoa flakes
  • 80 g plain flour
  • 60 g wholemeal flour
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tsp cold water

Ricotta filling

  • 600 g fresh ricotta cheese (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp vanilla bean extract
  • 60 g maple syrup
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 3 tamarillos (approx. 250 g), peeled and cut into slices
  • 3 figs (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 1 pomegranate, arils only

Preparation

Almond quinoa pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a round, fluted loose-bottom tart tin (22 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place almonds and quinoa into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add flours and butter and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add egg and water and knead 15 sec/, until mixture just comes together.
  5. Transfer pastry onto silicone bread mat or large, lightly floured sheet of baking paper. Using a rolling pin, roll out pastry (2-3 mm thickness). Drape pastry over rolling pin, gently removing baking paper and ease pastry into tin. Gently press pastry into sides and press to repair any splits in pastry. Trim overhanging edges with a knife. Place tin into refrigerator for 10 minutes or until firm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Line pastry with baking paper and fill with rice, dried beans or pie weights. Place tart tin onto a baking tray (20 x 30 cm) and bake for 10-15 minutes (160°C).
  7. Remove from oven, then remove baking paper with weights. Bake for a further 10-15 minutes (160°C), or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool completely.

Ricotta filling

  1. Place ricotta, vanilla, syrup and cinnamon (optional) into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and, if needed, mix for a further 10 sec/speed 5, until smooth and creamy.
  3. Transfer mixture into cooled pastry case and spread with aid of spatula. Arrange tamarillos, figs and pomegranate arils over filling before serving (see Tips).

Useful Items

round fluted loose-bottom tart tin (22 cm), silicone bread mat, baking paper, rolling pin, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), pie weights, rice or dried beans, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you prefer, you can use activated or roasted almonds instead of raw almonds in the pastry.
  • There is considerable variation in the moistness of different brands of ricotta. If using a drier ricotta, add 3-4 tbsp of milk (or more if desired) to the mixing bowl with the ricotta.
  • If you prefer, top this tart with whichever fresh seasonal fruit you have on hand.
