Lemon Meringue Cake with 10-Minute Frosting

Ingredients

Lemon Cakes

  • 80 g sunflower oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 1 lemon, preferably organic, zest and juice
  • 4 eggs
  • 240 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 130 g Greek yoghurt

Lemon Curd

  • 110 g caster sugar
  • 1 - 2 lemons, preferably organic, zest and juice
  • 80 g butter, unsalted, softened, cut into pieces
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 egg

Swiss Meringue

  • 3 egg whites
  • 140 g caster sugar
  • 1 pinch salt

Preparation

Lemon Cakes

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line the bases of 2 sandwich cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper, grease with sunflower oil and set aside.
  2. Place sugar and lemon zest in mixing bowl then grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add eggs and whisk 7 min/37°C/speed 4.
  4. Add self-raising flour, baking powder, 20 g lemon juice, Greek yoghurt and sunflower oil around butterfly whisk then mix 5-8 sec/speed 3.5. Remove butterfly whisk and carefully complete stirring with spatula, if necessary.
  5. Divide cake batter between the 2 prepared sandwich cake tins (approx. 410 g per tin, see tip). Place the 2 cake tins in the oven on the middle rack and bake immediately for 18-20 minutes (180°C). Allow cakes to cool approx. 5 minutes before removing from cake tins. Allow cakes to cool completely on a wire rack before filling (approx. 50 minutes). Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl, then prepare the lemon curd.

Lemon Curd

  1. Place sugar and zest of 1 lemon in mixing bowl then grind 8 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  2. Add 70 g lemon juice, butter, egg yolks and egg then cook 6 min/80°C/speed 4. Transfer immediately to a bowl to allow to cool for 10 minutes and then refrigerate for 1 hour. Clean mixing bowl thoroughly.

Swiss Meringue

  1. Place egg whites, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then warm up 4 min/70°C/speed 2.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk and whisk without measuring cup 5 min/speed 3.5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place one reserved lemon cake on serving dish and spread ⅔ of reserved lemon curd on top, then place the second reserved lemon cake, and spread ⅓ of reserved lemon curd, then finish by spreading the reserved Swiss meringue. Make some irregular waves using a spoon and burn with a blowtorch. Serve, cut in slices.

Useful Items

2 sandwich cake tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, cooling rack, cook's blowtorch, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • For a simple lemon cake, make double the recipe quantity of the lemon curd filling. Omit the meringue and use the lemon curd for filling and topping the cake.
  • Meringue frosting can be an everyday luxury. When you make it in the Thermomix, it only takes 10 minutes! Finish your cakes or desserts with this meringue frosting to make them extra special.
  • To make the cake using one tin, choose a high cake tin (Ø 18 cm) and pour in the whole amount of batter. Bake for 35-40 minutes in a preheated oven (180°C) and allow to cool for 10 minutes before removing from the cake tin. Cool completely on a wire rack, and simply slice in three horizontally before filling.
  • You can use the built-in Thermomix scales to measure out the exact quantity of cake batter for each cake tin. At the end of the recipe, turn the scales on, lift out mixing bowl and pour some cake batter into the prepared tin. When you return the mixing bowl to the base, it will show a minus figure indicating the weight removed (- 410 g).
  • The lemon cake can be made in advance: keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Or keep for up to 1 month in the freezer.
