Oat Pancakes with Peanut Milk

Ingredients

Peanut Milk

  • 130 g peanuts, roasted, unsalted
  • 1000 g water, plus extra for soaking

Pancakes

  • 20 g linseeds
  • 240 g oat flakes
  • 140 g bananas, cut in pieces
  • 30 g groundnut oil, plus extra for frying
  • 15 g honey, plus extra for serving
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • mixed fresh berries, for serving
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
570 kJ / 136 kcal
Protein
4 g
Carbohydrates
17 g
Fat
6 g

Preparation

Peanut Milk

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in peanuts. Remove bowl from lid, cover with water then set aside to soak for approx. 8 hours.
  2. Strain peanuts through a sieve, discarding water.
  3. Place reserved soaked peanuts and water in mixing bowl then heat 15 min/120°C/speed 1. Leave to stand for 5 minutes then chop 1 min/speed 7.
  4. Strain mixture through a muslin cloth set over a jug, squeezing to ensure all liquid is removed. Reserve peanut pulp in muslin (see tip). Transfer liquid in jug to a sterilised glass bottle and set aside in fridge. Clean mixing bowl.

Pancakes

  1. Place linseeds in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add oats and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add bananas, oil, honey, 360 g reserved peanut milk and baking powder then mix 10 sec/speed 7.
  4. Heat a little oil in a pancake or frying pan over a medium heat. Pour a small ladle of batter into the centre and fry for 2-3 minutes or until bubbles appear on the surface of pancake. Flip pancake and fry for 1 minute, then transfer to a plate and keep warm. Repeat until all batter is used.
  5. Serve with fresh berries and honey.

Useful Items

medium mesh sieve, muslin or nut milk bag, jug, pancake pan or frying pan, small ladle, sterilised glass bottle, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • These pancakes are a great choice for athletes. Banana is rich in potassium, carbohydrates, B vitamins and other nutrients that help in recovery and muscle building.
  • The peanut milk can be stored in a sterilised glass bottle for 3-4 days in the fridge. Shake before use.
  • To sterilise the bottle, wash in hot water then place in a preheated oven at 100°C for at least 10 minutes before filling.
  • Add the peanut pulp mixture left in the muslin cloth to bread, crackers, sauces or simply roast and eat with a yoghurt or smoothie.
