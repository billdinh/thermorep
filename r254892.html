Knusperhörnchen with Semolina and Apricot Filling

Ingredients

Dough

  • 370 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 40 g sunflower oil
  • 120 g water, lukewarm
  • 10 g vinegar
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Filling

  • 30 g dried apricots, pitted
  • 190 g milk
  • 30 g sugar
  • 10 g vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 30 g fine semolina
  • &frac18; tsp orange peel, finely grated (optional)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg

Assembly

  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 40 g sugar
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 20 g milk

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place flour, oil, water, vinegar, egg, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then knead 2 min/. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare filling.

Filling

  1. Place apricots in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place milk, sugar, vanilla sugar, semolina, orange zest (if using) and salt in mixing bowl then mix 3 sec/speed 4. Cook 6 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add egg yolk and reserved chopped apricots then stir 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and leave to cool (approx. 30 minutes). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place butter in mixing bowl and melt 2 min 30 sec/50°C/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Mix sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  3. Cut dough into 4 equal-sized pieces. Place 2 pieces on a floured work surface, and roll each out thinly. Pull over the back of your hand and use the knuckles to work the dough from below and draw it even thinner to form two 20 cm x 50 cm rectangles.
  4. Spread ¼ of melted butter over surfaces of dough then sprinkle them with 2 tsp reserved cinnamon sugar. Roll up the first rectangle from the narrow side, place it on the edge of the second rectangle and continue rolling up.
  5. Wrap dough roll tightly in cling film and freeze for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, repeat process with remaining 2 pieces of dough up to sprinkling them with cinnamon sugar (do not roll up).
  6. Remove chilled dough roll from freezer, unwrap and place on the edge of one of the rectangles then roll up. Place roll on the second rectangle and continue rolling. Wrap roll tightly in cling film and freeze for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C and line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  7. Cut chilled pastry roll into 10 slices. Press each disc slightly flat, then press lightly into the middle with both thumbs, forming a dip so that a heaped teaspoon filling fits inside it. Add a heaped teaspoon of cooled semolina-apricot mixture into the dips, squeeze the openings closed with your fingers then place on prepared baking trays.
  8. Mix egg yolks and milk in a small bowl then brush over surface of pastries. Bake one tray at a time for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until golden. Leave to cool before serving.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking trays, baking paper, pastry brush, refrigerator, freezer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Instead of apricots, you can also use other dried fruits such as pitted prunes or dates.
