Haggis and Pancetta Roulade with Whisky Sauce

Ingredients

Roulade

  • 80 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 250 g haggis, skinned, cut in pieces
  • 100 g Toulouse sausages, skinned, cut in pieces
  • 120 g pancetta, thin slices

Whisky Sauce

  • 150 g shallots, halved
  • 25 g butter
  • 100 g whisky
  • 250 g beef stock
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Roulade

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C and place a baking tray in oven to heat.
  2. Place carrots in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add haggis and sausage meat then mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  4. On a piece of cling film (approx. 25 cm long) lay 100 g pancetta slices out, overlapping slightly. Press the haggis mixture into a sausage shape (approx. 20 cm long) across the centre of the pancetta.
  5. Using the cling film, tightly roll the pancetta around the haggis mixture to create a roulade (approx. Ø 5 cm). Discard clingfilm then fold ends in to seal. Use remaining 20 g pancetta to seal the join along the length.
  6. Transfer roulade to preheated baking tray and bake for 20 minutes (220°C). Meanwhile,clean mixing bowl and make sauce.

Whisky Sauce

  1. Place shallots and butter in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/Varoma/speed .
  2. Add whisky and stock then cook, without measuring cup, 5 min/98°C/speed .
  3. Add cornflour, salt and pepper then cook, without measuring cup, 2 min/98°C/speed 0.5 then, with measuring cup in place, blend 30 sec/speed 9. Serve roulade cut in 2 - 3 cm slices, dressed with whisky sauce.

Useful Items

baking tray, cling film, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Neeps and tatties are the traditional Scottish accompaniment to haggis, although mashed potatoes and steamed vegetables would be delicious as well.
  • If preferred, serve the sauce unblended.
  • Scottish food is synonymous with haggis, so we just had to include this traditional savoury pudding. The roulade makes a wonderful centrepiece - perfect for a Burns Night supper, with neeps, tatties and a wee dram.
