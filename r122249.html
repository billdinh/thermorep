Steamed coconut custards

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp pure maple syrup, plus extra to serve
  • 50 g coconut milk powder (1 sachet)
  • 750 g water
  • 250 g milk
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon quill
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
629 kJ / 150 kcal
Protein
6.7 g
Carbohydrates
6.3 g
Fat
11.1 g

Preparation

  1. Place eggs, egg yolks and syrup into a large ceramic or glass jug and whisk to combine, then set aside.
  2. Place coconut milk powder and 250 g of the water into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5 or until completely dissolved and combined. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add milk, star anise and cinnamon and heat 5 min/80°C//speed 1.
  4. Using a fine-mesh sieve, slowly add coconut mixture to reserved egg mixture, whisking to combine. Discard spices. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Ensure 6 ramekins (125 ml each) fit comfortably into Varoma dish and tray. Divide mixture between ramekins.
  6. Place remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position. Place 3 ramekins into Varoma dish and cover with baking paper to prevent water from dripping into them. Insert Varoma tray. Place remaining 3 ramekins onto it and cover with baking paper. Secure Varoma lid, then steam 15-20 min/Varoma/speed 1, until just set (custards should have a slight wobble - see Tips).
  7. Top each custard with ¼-½ teaspoon maple syrup and swirl ramekin gently until surface is coated with syrup, then serve immediately.

Useful Items

large glass jug, fine mesh sieve, 6 ramekins (8 cm, 125 ml), baking paper

Hints & Tricks

  • Baked custards: Divide coconut mixture between 6 ramekins (125 ml). Place ramekins in a deep-sided baking dish and pour in enough hot water to come halfway up the sides of the ramekins. Bake for 20-25 minutes (160°C), or until a knife inserted into the centre of the custard comes out clean. The custard will still have a slight wobble when set. Drizzle each custard with ¼-½ tsp maple syrup and swirl ramekin gently until surface is coated with syrup, then serve immediately.
  • Depending on the size and shape of your ramekins, you may not be able to fit all of them into the Varoma at the same time. If this occurs, steam custards in batches. They will take less time to steam, so be sure to check them after 10 minutes of steaming, then check every few minutes until cooked to your liking.
  • The custards in the Varoma dish may cook more quickly and may need to be removed sooner than those on the Varoma tray.
