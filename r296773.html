Chocolate Lava Cookies

Ingredients

  • 220 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 280 g hazelnut chocolate spread (e.g. Nutella), or homemade equivalent
  • 120 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 200 g light brown sugar
  • 130 g caster sugar
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 360 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Preparation

  1. Heat butter in a saucepan over a medium-low heat until melted. Increase temperature to a medium heat and continue cooking, watching carefully, until butter is brown and has a nutty aroma. Once browned, remove from heat and transfer to a heat-proof bowl. Leave to cool at room temperature for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  2. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Drop 20 portions of hazelnut chocolate spread onto prepared tray (approx. 1½-2 tsp each) then place in freezer to harden. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  3. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 3-4 sec/speed 8 until roughly chopped. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place brown sugar, caster sugar, eggs, cooled browned butter and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 2 min/speed 3.
  5. Add flour, cornflour, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda and salt then mix 30 sec/speed 4.
  6. Add reserved chopped chocolate and mix 10 sec//speed 3. Transfer to a large bowl, cover with cling film and place in fridge for 15 minutes.
  7. Line 2 baking trays with baking paper and remove frozen chocolate spread drops from freezer.
  8. Taking 2 Tbsp chilled dough at a time, shape into a disc in your palm and press a frozen chocolate spread drop into the centre. Shape cookie dough around chocolate spread drop until completely concealed, adding more dough if needed. Roll cookie into a ball and place on a prepared tray, 5 cm apart. Repeat until all chocolate spread drops and cookie dough have been used.
  9. Freeze cookie dough balls for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 190°C.
  10. Bake chilled cookie dough balls for 11-12 minutes (190°C) until edges are just beginning to turn golden brown.
  11. Leave on trays to cool for 15 minutes then serve, or transfer to a wire rack to cool completely and store in an airtight container until needed.
