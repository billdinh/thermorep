Vanilla Sponge Layer Cake

Ingredients

Vanilla Sponge

  • 200 g unsalted butter, soft, plus extra for greasing
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 200 g self-raising flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract

Vanilla Buttercream

  • 200 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 400 g icing sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract

Assembly

  • 2 batches fondant icing (approx. 660 g)
  • food colouring pastes, as desired

Preparation

Vanilla Sponge

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease sides and line bases of 2 cake tins (Ø 20 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then cream 20 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Add flour, eggs, baking powder and vanilla extract then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 1 min/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Divide evenly between prepared tins. Bake for 30-35 minutes (170°C) until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Leave to cool in tins for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make icings.

Vanilla Buttercream

  1. Place butter, icing sugar and vanilla extract in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 20 sec/speed 4.
  2. Once sponges are cool, use a bread knife to slice each in half horizontally to create 4 even layers. Spread a layer of buttercream on each cake layer, stacking one on top of another, then spread buttercream around sides of cake. Set aside in fridge for 30 minutes for buttercream to harden.

Assembly

  1. Measure top and sides of cake with a piece of string then place cake on a cake stand and set string aside.
  2. Knead fondant icing together then roll out on a clean surface (see tip) dusted with icing sugar into a circle with a diameter slightly larger than the piece of string.
  3. Loosely roll icing onto a rolling pin, then gently drape over cake. Smooth over, starting on top then down sides, pushing out any air bubbles and creases. Use a knife to trim any excess icing, saving any offcuts.
  4. Colour offcuts as desired, then re-roll and cut out shapes. Use to decorate cake. Finish with bunting or candles before serving.

Useful Items

2 round cake tins (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, skewer, wire rack, bread knife, butcher's string, serving plate or cake stand, rolling pin, biscuit cutters, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

A failsafe vanilla sponge that is taken to new heights with the decoration. Perfect for any celebration, just decorate accordingly.

  • If the fondant is too sticky, add extra icing sugar. If it is dry, add more egg white (see recipe in the Basics and Icings section).
  • Make sure the surface you are rolling the fondant icing out on is completely clean and free of crumbs.
  • Using a cake smoother on the fondant icing helps create a really professional and smooth finish.
