Chicken and pepper souvlaki with rice

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 20 g fresh ginger, peeled, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100 g soy sauce
  • 50 g honey
  • 1 - 2 tsp toasted sesame oil (5-10 g), to taste
  • 25 g barbecue sauce
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper

Chicken and rice

  • 800 g chicken breast fillets, skinless, cut in cubes (2-3 cm)
  • 400 g peppers (approx. 2 peppers), cut in pieces (2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp vegetable stock paste, homemade
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 300 g white long-grain rice
  • 1000 g water
  • 2 tbsp cornflour (starch) (20 g)

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Place ginger, garlic cloves, soy sauce, honey, toasted sesame oil, barbecue sauce and black pepper in mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 6. Transfer marinade to a bowl.

Chicken and rice

  1. Add chicken to marinade, stir to coat and allow to marinate in refrigerator for at least 15 minutes (this time can be used for preparing the peppers).
  2. Thread the chicken cubes, alternating with peppers, onto skewers (approx. 18 cm long) and arrange skewers on Varoma dish and tray. Place remaining marinade, vegetable stock paste and salt in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, add long-grain rice to it, then add water over rice. Stir using spatula to wet all the rice. Set Varoma in position and steam 25 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside. Remove simmering basket using spatula, transfer rice to a serving bowl and cover to keep warm. Transfer cooking liquid to a bowl.
  3. Place 400 g reserved cooking liquid and cornflour in mixing bowl, set Varoma back in position and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2. Set Varoma aside and adjust seasoning to taste. Serve hot, presenting rice with chicken skewers on top and sauce on the side, or pour some sauce over the skewers and rice.

Useful Items

wooden skewers (approx. 18 cm long), refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Most travellers to Greece will have tasted souvlaki, the appetising barbecued meat skewers sold on many street corners. With this recipe you can easily prepare beautifully tender Thermomix®-style souvlaki at home, making them accessible even on a week night. And the best part is, as there’s no need to fire up the barbecue, this can be a year-round nourishing meal for the family.
  • For a stronger flavour, marinate the chicken overnight in the refrigerator in step 2.
