Beef Burger with Melting Cheese Centre

Ingredients

  • 2 slices sliced white bread (approx. 95 g)
  • water, for soaking
  • 5 g fresh parsley leaves
  • 130 g red onions, quartered
  • 20 g sunflower oil, plus 1 tsp for frying
  • 1 medium egg
  • ½ Tbsp English mustard, or to taste
  • 1 tsp hot paprika
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ½ tsp ground pepper
  • 500 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • 120 g Cheddar cheese, in 6 pieces (4 cm x 4 cm x 1 cm)

Preparation

  1. Soak bread in water then squeeze dry and set aside.
  2. Place parsley in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8.
  3. Add onions and oil then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  4. Add reserved soaked and drained bread, egg, mustard, paprika, salt, pepper and beef mince then mix 30 sec//speed 4.
  5. Divide mixture into 6 balls,  place a piece of Cheddar in centre and enclose burger mince around cheese so cheese is not visible. Flatten to form burgers (Ø 9 cm) and transfer to a plate, then refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  6. Heat oil in a large frying pan. Cook burgers over a medium heat for 4-5 minutes on each side until cooked through. Allow burgers  to stand in frying pan, off the heat, for 2-3 minutes to allow cheese to firm up a little. Serve in a bun of your choice.

Useful Items

frying pan, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Serving suggestion: grill burger buns in the oven or toaster, so they are crispy. Serve with ketchup or avocado mayonnaise, lettuce, coleslaw or pickles.
  • Feta also works well instead of Cheddar.
