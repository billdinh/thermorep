Kangaroo harissa polpette with lentils and kale

Ingredients

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 sprig fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 500 g kangaroo mince
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp harissa (see Tips)
  • 130 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 50 g celery stalk, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 140 g carrots, cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 400 g canned whole tomatoes
  • 100 g dried green lentils
  • 300 g water
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 60 g kale, ribs removed and leaves cut into strips (approx. 1 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan, parsley and rosemary into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add mince, salt, egg and 2 tablespoons of the harissa to mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Roll tablespoons of mixture into balls (approx. 20 in total) and place onto Varoma tray. Set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place onion, celery, carrot, garlic and oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 3 min/Varoma/speed 2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 2.
  6. Add tomatoes, lentils, water, stock paste and remaining 1 tablespoon harissa, then cook 15 min/90°C//speed 1, without measuring cup.
  7. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh kale into it. Insert Varoma tray with polpette, secure Varoma lid and cook 10-13 min/Varoma//speed 1, until kangaroo is cooked to your liking. Divide polpette between serving bowls, top with lentils and sauce and serve with kale.

Hints & Tricks

  • Harissa is a spicy, North African chilli paste. You can find it in the international aisle of most major supermarkets or in specialty food stores.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • To add more vegies to your meal, serve with the Parsnip and bean mash or Cauliflower mash (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip).
  • Kangaroo is best when it is just cooked (we suggest medium-rare).
  • If kangaroo isn't easily available, you can use any mince of choice but cooking times may vary.
