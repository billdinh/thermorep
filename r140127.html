Pink and White Macarons

Ingredients

Pink Macarons

  • 75 g icing sugar
  • 75 g ground almonds
  • 2 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 25 g water
  • 75 g caster sugar
  • pink food colouring paste, as desired

White Chocolate Buttercream Filling

  • 60 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 150 g icing sugar
  • 70 g unsalted butter, soft

Preparation

Pink Macarons

  1. Preheat oven to 120°C and line 2 baking trays with silicone macaron or baking mats.
  2. Place icing sugar and almonds in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then mix again 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl, add 1 egg white and mix together well, then set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place remaining egg white in mixing bowl. Place water and sugar in a saucepan then heat, without stirring, over a high heat until temperature reaches 100°C on a sugar thermometer. Once at 100°C, without measuring cup, whisk egg white 12 min/speed 3.5. When the temperature of the syrup reaches 118°C, pour it slowly through hole in mixing bowl lid onto rotating blades.
  4. Add food colouring and mix 5 min/speed 3.5.  Remove butterfly whisk. Add to bowl with almond mixture and stir together well. Transfer mixture to a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (approx. 5 mm) then pipe 3 cm rounds onto prepared trays. Tap trays 3-4 times on work surface to remove any air bubbles then bake for 15 minutes (120°C).
  5. Remove from oven and immediately transfer silicone baking mats to work surface to stop the cooking. Allow to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes) before gently peeling macarons off mats. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and nozzle then prepare filling.

White Chocolate Buttercream Filling

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt 2 min/40°C/speed 0.5.
  2. Add icing sugar and butter then mix 1 min/speed 4. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle (approx. 5 mm).
  3. Pipe buttercream on flat side of half the macarons, then sandwich with other halves.
  4. Decorate as desired (see tip) and serve, or store in an airtight container in fridge for up to 3 days.

Useful Items

2 silicone baking mats, 2 baking trays, saucepan, sugar thermometer, piping bags and nozzles, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Use shop bought egg whites if preferred; two large egg whites weigh approx. 70 g.
  • Decorate with a white chocolate drizzle: place 30 g white chocolate, in small pieces or callets, in mixing bowl then melt 1.5 min/50°C/speed . Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt again 30 sec/50°C/speed . Transfer to a small piping bag fitted with a 1-2 mm plain nozzle then pipe over tops of macarons.
  • Use other colours of food colouring for different coloured macarons.
  • Raspberry jam, white chocolate ganache or whipped cream would also make good fillings instead of buttercream.
