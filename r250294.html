Chicken Mole

Ingredients

  • 3 oz ancho chilies, stems and seeds removed, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 1 oz dried guajillo chilies, stems and seeds removed, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
  • 1 tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 3 tsp salt, to taste, divided
  • 5 ½ oz white onions, cut into pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 33 oz water
  • 1 oz apple cider vinegar
  • 5 ½ oz Mexican chocolate, cut into pieces (1 in.)
  • 6 chicken leg quarters, skin on, bone in
  • sesame seeds, to garnish

Preparation

  1. Place ancho and guajillo chilies, cinnamon, allspice, peppercorns, oregano, marjoram, thyme and 1 tsp salt into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9.
  2. Add onion and garlic and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté 3 min/212°F/speed 1.
  3. Add water, vinegar and chocolate. Place Varoma into position and distribute chicken in Varoma dish and tray as needed. Season with 2 tsp salt, secure Varoma lid and steam 35 min/Varoma/speed 2 until chicken is cooked through. Remove Varoma and keep chicken warm.
  4. Insert measuring cup and blend 1 min/speed 5-10 increasing speed gradually. Strain mole sauce through a fine sieve and reserve. Discard solids (see Tip).
  5. Remove chicken skin and serve immediately by pouring reserved mole sauce over chicken and garnishing with sesame seeds.

Useful Items

fine sieve

Hints & Tricks

  • If the strained sauce is too thick, add 1-2 tbsp water and blend 30 sec/speed 10.
  • Serve with rice, beans and warm corn tortillas.
  • Any unused mole sauce can be refrigerated and used within 3 days. Spoon over rotisserie chicken, eggs, rice, pork chops and roasted vegetables such as sweet potatoes.
