Yoghurt and white chocolate ganache (Mark Best)

Ingredients

Vanilla bean powder

  • 7 vanilla beans

Yogurt and white chocolate ganache

  • ½ orange, zest only, no white pith (see Tips)
  • 225 g white chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 120 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 180 g Greek yoghurt
  • 250 g fresh blueberries (see Tips)

Preparation

Vanilla bean powder

  1. Preheat oven to 100°C.
  2. Place vanilla beans onto baking tray and dry in oven for 3 hours (100°C), then turn off heat and leave to cool in oven until dry and brittle.
  3. Place dried vanilla beans into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 9 or until a fine powder consistency. Transfer into a sealable storage jar or container until ready to use (see Tips).

Yogurt and white chocolate ganache

  1. Place zest into mixing bowl and grate 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add chocolate and cream into mixing bowl and blend 4 min/50°C/speed 2 -4, increasing speed gradually from speed 2 to speed 4 as chocolate melts. Blend 1 min/speed 3.
  3. Add yoghurt and blend 1 min/speed 3, until well combined. Press ganache through a fine-mesh sieve or squeeze through a nut milk bag into a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 2 hours or until set.
  4. Dust chilled ganache with reserved Vanilla bean powder and serve with blueberries.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), sealable storage jar or container, plastic wrap, nut bag or fine mesh sieve, bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use either orange zest or lemon zest in this recipe.
  • Vanilla bean powder can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to 1 month.
  • You can use frozen blueberries in this recipe. Simply thaw, then drain on paper towel to absorb extra moisture before using.
