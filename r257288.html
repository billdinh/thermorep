Lamb tikka with spicy eggplant

Ingredients

Lamb tikka

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 200 g natural yoghurt
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 600 g lamb rump steak, trimmed

Spicy eggplant

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 fresh bird's eye chilli, trimmed
  • 150 g brown onion, cut into eighths, segments separated
  • 50 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 300 - 350 g eggplant, trimmed, quartered and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ Lebanese cucumber, peeled, deseeded and cut into slices
  • natural yoghurt, to serve
  • fresh mint leaves, for garnishing
  • 4 roti, warmed, to serve (optional)

Preparation

Lamb tikka

  1. Place garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add yoghurt, paprika, garam masala, turmeric and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl. Add lamb, stir to coat and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Spicy eggplant

  1. Place garlic and chilli into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add onion, oil, mustard seeds and ground coriander and cook 4 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Preheat oven grill. Line a large baking tray (40 x 30 cm) with aluminium foil and set aside.
  4. Add eggplant to mixing bowl and cook 6-8 min/120°C//speed 1, until softened.
  5. Add tomatoes and salt and cook 10 min/120°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Meanwhile, place lamb and marinade onto prepared baking tray and place on middle shelf of oven. Grill for 12-14 minutes, turning once, until browned and cooked to your liking.
  6. Serve lamb warm with eggplant, cucumber and yoghurt. Garnish with mint leaves and serve with roti on the side (optional).
