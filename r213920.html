Fatayer (spinach turnovers)

Ingredients

Dough

  • 150 g water
  • 90 g olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 350 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting

Filling

  • 400 g fresh spinach leaves (see Tips)
  • 350 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sumac
  • 100 g lemon juice

Assembly

  • plain flour, for dusting

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place water, oil, salt and flour into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4, then knead 1 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Shape into a ball and transfer into a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap, then set aside for a minimum of 30 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the filling. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Filling

  1. Place 200 g of the spinach into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 5, with aid of spatula. Transfer into Varoma dish and set aside.
  2. Place remaining 200 g spinach into mixing bowl and chop 15 sec/speed 5, with aid of spatula. Transfer into Varoma dish with reserved chopped spinach and strain well by pressing with the back of a spoon.
  3. Place onion, salt, sumac and lemon juice into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer mixture into Varoma dish with spinach and mix well to combine with spatula. Set aside to drain well.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line 2 large baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Divide reserved dough into 4 equal portion. On a silicone bread mat or lightly floured surface, shape each piece into a log. Cut each log into 8 equal portions (approx. 20 g each). Roll out each piece of dough into a circle (approx. 10 cm).
  3. Place 1 heaped teaspoon of the reserved spinach filling in the centre of each circle. Lift up three edges and pull together to form a pyramid shape. Pinch edges tightly to seal in the filling, then transfer onto prepared baking trays. Repeat with remaining dough circles and filling, then bake for 18-20 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool, then serve.

Useful Items

plastic wrap, rolling pin, baking paper, wire rack, silicone bread mat, bowl, large baking trays, spoon, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We recommend using English spinach in this recipe.
