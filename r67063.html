Plain yoghurt

Ingredients

  • 1000 g full cream milk, room temperature
  • 120 g natural yoghurt (min. 1.8% fat)
  • 50 g milk powder (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 jar
Calories
371 kJ / 89 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
7 g
Fat
5 g

Preparation

  1. Place full cream milk, natural yoghurt and milk powder (optional) into mixing bowl and warm 6 min/50°C/speed 3. Transfer into a ThermoServer and set aside or transfer into 8 airtight jars or containers and wrap immediately in a blanket to keep warm. Leave ThermoServer or jars undisturbed for 11 - 13 hours at room temperature, then chill in refrigerator for at least 3 hours before serving.

Useful Items

ThermoServer, 8 airtight jars or containers, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a thin set yoghurt ideal to enjoy for breakfast, mixed with fresh fruit, homemade jam, nuts or cereals.
  • Keeping the yoghurt warm and undisturbed during incubation is essential to achieving the right texture. To increase insulation, place into a completely cool, turned off oven.
  • The cooler the room temperature, the longer the incubation time required. If you live in a warm climate 8 - 10 hours may be sufficient.
  • The longer the incubation time, the more acidic the yoghurt.
  • For best results, thoroughly clean mixing bowl, mixing bowl lid, lid seal, mixing blades and measuring cup before making yoghurt.
  • Avoid using low fat milk or low fat yoghurt for this recipe.
  • Remember to reserve some of your yoghurt as a starting culture for the next batch.
  • Milk powder helps the yoghurt to set. To ensure this recipe is gluten free, read the label of your milk powder. Some commercial brands may contain traces of wheat or gluten.
  • Sweetened yoghurt: add 40 g sugar with other ingredients in step 1.
  • Creamy yoghurt: add 100 g cream with other ingredients in step 1.
  • Fruit yoghurt: first prepare a fruit purée. Place 200 g fruit (e.g. strawberries) and 80 g sugar into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1. Allow mixture to cool to 50°C. Leave fruit purée in mixing bowl and proceed as per recipe from step 1.
  • Thick set yoghurt: Place 1000 g full cream milk into mixing bowl and heat 25 min/90°C/speed 3. Allow to cool to 37°C (approx. 1 hour) and remove any skin that may have formed during this time. Place a small amount of the cooled milk into a bowl and combine with 120 g natural yoghurt (min. 10% fat) until no lumps remain. Add yoghurt and milk mix back into mixing bowl with remaining milk and heat 20 min/37°C/speed 2. Pour into a ThermoServer and leave undisturbed in a completely cool oven for 5-7 hours then refrigerate for at least 3 hours before serving.
