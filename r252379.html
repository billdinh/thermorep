Chorizo and bean winter warmer

Ingredients

  • 200 g dried black-eyed beans (see Tips)
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 1 brown onion, cut into halves
  • 20 g oil
  • 250 g chorizo, cut into slices (1 cm)
  • 5 potatoes (approx. 350 g), cut into cubes (3-4 cm)
  • 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 300 g water
  • 300 g cabbage, shredded
  • 1 lime, juice only
  • salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • crusty bread, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh beans into it. Carefully cover with boiling water and set aside to soak for a minimum of 1 hour. Drain and set aside.
  2. Place onion and oil into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add chorizo and sauté 5 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  4. Add potatoes, tomatoes, stock paste, water and drained beans. Place Varoma into position, weigh cabbage into it, then secure Varoma lid and cook 35 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Remove Varoma. Add lime juice, salt, pepper and cooked cabbage into mixing bowl and stir through using spatula. Divide between serving bowls and serve with crusty bread (optional).

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • You can find dried black eye beans at some major supermarkets or bulk food stores.
