Cardamom and Rose Shortbread

Ingredients

  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 2 tsp dried rose petals
  • 55 g golden caster sugar
  • 40 g long grain rice
  • 100 g ghee
  • 150 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp water
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
600 kJ / 144 kcal
Protein
1.3 g
Carbohydrates
15.5 g
Fat
8.5 g

Preparation

  1. Place ground cardamom, rose petals and sugar in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place rice in mixing bowl and grind 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to bowl with sugar and set aside.
  3. Place ghee in mixing bowl and whip 20 sec/speed 3.
  4. Add flour, salt and reserved ground sugar and rice then mix 30 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add water and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Tip out onto a work surface, wrap in cling film and refrigerate for 45 minutes to firm up. Meanwhile, line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 150°C. Roll out rested dough on a floured surface until 5 mm thick. Cut out biscuits using a biscuit cutter (approx. 9 cm x 4 cm), and place on prepared baking tray. Re-roll any trimmings until all dough is used.
  7. Bake for 25-35 minutes (150°C) until cooked through and lightly golden. Transfer to a wire rack to cool and harden. Enjoy straight away or store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Useful Items

biscuit cutter (approx. 9 cm x 4 cm), cling film, baking tray and paper, rolling pin, wire rack, airtight container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • We’ve taken a classic British biscuit here and delicately flavoured it with some quintessentially Indian flavours. Lovely served with masala chai or coffee at the end of a meal.
