Strawberry and Pistachio Semifreddo - Semifreddo alla fragola e pistacchio

Ingredients

  • 40 g pistachio nuts, unsalted
  • 225 g strawberries, hulled
  • 130 g caster sugar
  • 290 g double cream
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 egg yolks, from large eggs
Nutrition
per 1 slice
Calories
1348 kJ / 325 kcal

3.3 g

Preparation

  1. Line a loaf tin (450 g/1 lb) with cling film, leaving a 5 cm overhang on all sides.
  2. Place pistachios in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place strawberries and 50 g sugar in mixing bowl then purée 1 min/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream and vanilla in mixing bowl then, without measuring cup and without setting a time, whisk speed 4 until soft peaks form, watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. With butterfly whisk still in position, place egg yolks and remaining 80 g sugar in mixing bowl then whisk 3 min/80°C/speed 4. Remove lid then wait until temperature reaches 37°C to continue with recipe.
  6. With butterfly whisk still in position, add reserved whipped cream then mix 15 sec/speed 1.5. Transfer half to bowl with strawberry purée and remaining half to another bowl. With a soft spatula, gently fold together purée and cream until thoroughly combined, then pour into prepared loaf tin. Place in freezer for 5 hours until set. Meanwhile, continue with recipe.
  7. Fold reserved chopped pistachios into reserved vanilla cream then cover with cling film and place in fridge.
  8. Once strawberry layer has set, pour chilled pistachio layer evenly over strawberry layer and smooth surface. Fold cling film over the surface and freeze for at least 12 hours (and up to 3 days).
  9. Before serving, place semifreddo in fridge for 15 minutes then turn out onto a serving plate. Cut in 2 cm slices to serve.

Useful Items

loaf tin (450 g/1 lb), cling film, soft spatula, large bowl, serving plate, freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Semifreddo is an Italian partially frozen dessert that has the texture of a frozen mousse. As it requires no churning, it is even easier to make than gelato! It also contains whipped cream, which means it doesn’t freeze as solidly as ice cream so the end result is deliciously smooth and creamy. Typically frozen in loaf-shaped tins, the presentation once turned out allows for elegant layers of different colours and textures that can be seen once sliced. This stunning version has a creamy white, pistachio-studded layer on the bottom, topped with a delicate blush-hued layer of strawberry purée.
