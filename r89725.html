Nam Jim

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 - 2 long green chillies, deseeded if preferred
  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander (approx. 15 g), leaves, roots and stems
  • 2 spring onions/shallots (approx. 30 g), trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 50 g roasted unsalted cashew nuts
  • 30 g peanut oil
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 50 g lime juice (approx. 2 limes)

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, chillies, coriander and spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add palm sugar and cashews and chop 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then chop 5 sec/speed 9, until a paste consistency is achieved. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add peanut oil, fish sauce and lime juice and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a sealable jar or container, then store in the refrigerator or serve immediately.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve this tangy Asian sauce with cold meats (e.g. beef, pork, lamb, seafood).
  • Nam Jim can be prepared a day ahead. Transfer into a sealable storage jar or container or into a bowl and cover well with plastic wrap until required.
  • Clean the roots of your coriander before adding to the mixing bowl. Scrape down the root to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside.
