Blueberry Cheesecake, Yoghurt Sorbet

Ingredients

Blueberry Cream Cheese

  • ½ gelatine leaf
  • 100 g blueberries
  • 30 g caster sugar
  • ¼ lemon, juice only
  • 225 g cream cheese

Yoghurt Sorbet

  • 1 gelatine leaf
  • 120 g water
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 50 g liquid glucose
  • 600 g Greek yoghurt
  • 10 g lemon juice

Crumble

  • 125 g plain flour
  • 50 g ground almonds
  • 125 g unsalted butter, cut in pieces
  • 75 g Demerara sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Blueberry Compote

  • 750 g blueberries, plus extra to garnish
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 1 gelatine leaf
  • 1 lemon, juice only

Garnish

  • 40 leaves wood sorrel shoots or baby mint

Preparation

Blueberry Cream Cheese

  1. Place gelatine in a bowl of cold water and set aside to soak.
  2. Place blueberries, sugar and lemon juice in mixing bowl and heat quickly 2 min/120°C/speed  until the juices run.
  3. Add squeezed-out gelatine and purée 1 min/speed 7. Pass through a fine sieve into a bowl and leave to cool.
  4. When purée has cooled, pour back into mixing bowl. Add cream cheese and blend 10 sec/speed 5 until smooth. Transfer to a bowl, cover and place in fridge until needed. Clean mixing bowl.

Yoghurt Sorbet

  1. Place gelatine in a bowl of cold water and set aside to soak.
  2. Place water, sugar and glucose in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/80°C/speed 3.
  3. Add squeezed out gelatine then stir 20 sec/speed 1. Remove mixing bowl and leave to cool completely.
  4. When cool, return mixing bowl to position and add yoghurt. Mix 20 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add lemon juice and stir 10 sec/speed 5. Pass through a fine sieve into a bowl. Churn mixture in an ice cream machine (see tip for alternative method). Store in freezer until needed. Clean mixing bowl.

Crumble

  1. Place all ingredients in mixing bowl and pulse Turbo/1 sec/4-5 times. Tip out onto a lined tray and place in freezer for 2-4 hours to set. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 165°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Crumble frozen mixture onto prepared baking tray then bake for 15-20 minutes (165°C) until golden. Remove to a wire rack to cool.
  3. Break up mixture a little keeping lumps as large as possible. Store in an airtight container until needed.

Blueberry Compote

  1. Heat blueberries with sugar in a saucepan until juices just run (before they go mushy). Meanwhile, place gelatine in a bowl of cold water and set aside to soak.
  2. Drain berries through a sieve, being careful not to crush them.
  3. Place lemon juice and 100 g partially cooked berries in mixing bowl then blend 30 sec/speed 7 until smooth. Strain through a fine sieve pushing as much fruit through as possible.
  4. While still warm, place juice and squeezed out gelatine in mixing bowl then stir 20 sec/speed 1. Leave to cool then pour sauce over remaining partially cooked blueberries. Cover and set aside.

Garnish

  1. Pipe a big blob of blueberry cream cheese at one end of a serving plate and drag a little using a spoon. Sprinkle crumble over and to one side. Place some blueberry compote at different positions and dot with blueberry juices. Dress with wood sorrel leaves. Serve with a scoop of yoghurt sorbet.

Useful Items

fine sieve, cling film, baking paper, baking tray and baking paper, wire rack, airtight container, saucepan, medium mesh sieve, serving plate, ice cream machine, refrigerator, freezer, oven, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • To make ice cream without an ice cream machine - pour mixture into ice cube trays and freeze until solid. Place ice cream cubes in mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 6. Then, while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid, churn 10 sec/speed 4.
