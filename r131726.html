Strawberry and White Chocolate Mousse Dessert

Ingredients

Base

  • 200 g digestive biscuits
  • 70 g butter, diced

White Chocolate Mousse

  • 250 g white chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 350 g whipping cream, cold
  • 2 gelatine leaves

Strawberry Mousse

  • 60 g granulated sugar
  • 400 g fresh strawberries, hulled
  • 4 gelatine leaves
  • 200 g whipping cream, cold

Decoration

  • 400 g fresh strawberries, hulled, sliced

Preparation

Base

  1. Line a Ø 20 cm spring form cake tin with baking paper.
  2. Place biscuits and butter in mixing bowl then chop 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to prepared tin, flatten with the back of a spoon to form a firm layer then set aside in fridge. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

White Chocolate Mousse

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 150 g cream then melt 3 min/90°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, soak 2 gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes until softened then squeeze out the excess water. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then melt again 1 min/90°C/speed 1.
  3. Add squeezed out gelatine to melted chocolate and mix 20 sec/speed 3. Transfer to a jug and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place remaining 200 g whipping cream in mixing bowl then whisk, without setting a time/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form - watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Without measuring cup, mix 30 sec/speed 2 while pouring reserved melted chocolate mixture in through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 10 sec/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk then pour into tin on top of biscuit layer. Set aside in fridge to firm up slightly (approx. 1 hour). Clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk then prepare strawberry layer.

Strawberry Mousse

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add strawberries and blend 10 sec/speed 7 then scrape down sides sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula. Heat 2 min/80°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, soak 4 gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water for 5 minutes until softened then squeeze out excess water.
  3. Add squeezed out gelatine to mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a jug and set aside to cool (approx. 1 hour). Clean mixing bowl.
  4. When strawberry mixture is cool, insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl then whisk, without setting a time/speed 3.5 until stiff peaks form - watching carefully to avoid over-whipping. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Without measuring cup, mix 30 sec/speed 2 while pouring reserved blended strawberry mixture in through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix again 10 sec/speed 3.  Remove butterfly whisk then pour into tin on top of partly set white chocolate layer. Cover and place in fridge until set (approx. 3 hours or overnight).

Decoration

  1. Run a warm knife around edges of cake then remove from tin. Decorate with strawberry slices and serve chilled.

Useful Items

spring form tin (Ø 20 cm), baking paper, 2 jugs, refrigerator
