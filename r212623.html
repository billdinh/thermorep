Ham and Béchamel Croquettes

Ingredients

  • 5 ½ oz Black Forest ham, cubed (1 in.)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 oz extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 ½ oz unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 oz yellow onion
  • 6 oz all-purpose flour
  • 27 oz whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 3 large eggs, whisked
  • 7 oz bread crumbs
  • olive oil, to fry

Preparation

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside. Place ham into mixing bowl and chop Turbo/0.5 sec/0 times. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place hard-boiled eggs into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 4. Transfer to another bowl and set aside.
  3. Place olive oil, butter and onion into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5, then sauté 3 min/250°F/speed 1.
  4. Add flour and sauté 3 min/250°F/speed 2.
  5. Add milk, nutmeg, salt and pepper and mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  6. Add 2 tbsp reserved chopped ham and cook 8 min/250°F/speed 4.
  7. Let mixture rest for 5 minutes. Add remaining ham and chopped eggs and mix 45 sec/speed 4, stir through with spatula.
  8. Transfer béchamel mixture into a bowl, cover with foil or cling wrap pushed down on top of the béchamel to avoid forming a skin and allow to cool for 20 minutes before placing into a piping bag. Pipe béchamel with largest piping tip into 5 equal lengths (approx. 15 in. x 1 in.) onto prepared baking sheet and refrigerate for 8 hours minimum or overnight.
  9. Cut cold béchamel strips into croquettes (approx. 1½ in.), dip in whisked eggs and roll through bread crumbs. Fry each croquette in oil until brown on all sides, allow to drain on paper towels and serve warm.

Useful Items

deep frying pan, paper towels, baking sheet, parchment paper, piping bag with large tip (approx. 1 in.), aluminum foil or cling wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Scissors can be used to cut chilled bechamel into 1½ inch pieces. Use a fork to help run pieces through egg and bread crumbs before frying.
  • Use a deep fryer for best results.
  • If a piping bag is unavailable cut one of the corners off a zip lock bag, fill the baggie with bechamel and pipe onto baking sheet.
