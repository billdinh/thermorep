Chicken and Sweetcorn Pizza Muffins

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 220 g water
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 400 g strong white bread flour
  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Tomato Sauce

  • 1 - 2 garlic cloves, to taste
  • 3 fresh basil leaves
  • 50 g onions, quartered
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 pinch caster sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Assembly

  • 100 g cooked chicken meat, diced (1 cm)
  • 100 g tinned sweetcorn, drained
  • 100 g grated mozzarella

Preparation

Pizza Dough

  1. Place water, yeast and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 2.
  2. Add flour, oil and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl. Tip dough out into prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, make tomato sauce.

Tomato Sauce

  1. Place garlic, basil, onions and oil in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5 then sauté 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add tomatoes, oregano, sugar, salt and pepper then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1. Insert measuring cup then blend 20 sec/speed 5-7, increasing speed gradually.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C and grease a 12 hole muffin tin.
  2. Divide pizza dough into 12 pieces (approx. 60 g each). Roll each piece of dough out into a rough circle (Ø 10 cm) then place in a muffin hole, shaping to fit. Add 1-2 Tbsp reserved tomato sauce then divide chicken and sweetcorn equally between muffins and top with a sprinkle of mozzarella.
  3. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) until cheese is golden brown.
  4. Leave to rest in tin for 10 minutes then remove muffins and serve immediately.

Useful Items

cling film, 12 hole muffin tin, rolling pin, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • A twist on mini pizzas, the kids will love helping to make these pizza muffins. Stuffed with tomato sauce, chicken, sweetcorn and mozzarella, then baked in the oven until golden and bubbling, they’re delicious while still warm as an after-school snack or supper. They also make a good lunchbox option instead of a sandwich.
  • Substitute chicken and sweetcorn with chopped pepperoni or cooked ham and chopped peppers.
