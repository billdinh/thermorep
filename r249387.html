Rum Babas

Ingredients

Babas

  • 65 g milk
  • 10 g dried yeast
  • 240 g plain flour
  • 100 g salted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 30 g sugar
  • 4 medium eggs

Syrup and Cream

  • 300 g sugar
  • 300 g water
  • 1 orange, finely grated zest and juice
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 50 g rum
  • 200 g double cream
  • 3 oranges, segmented

Preparation

Babas

  1. Grease moulds and large bowl then set aside.
  2. Place milk and yeast in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add 30 g flour and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Allow to stand with lid on and measuring cup in place for 15-20 minutes until mixture is frothy.
  4. Add butter, sugar, eggs and remaining 210 g flour then knead 3 min/. Scrape down sides of bowl with spatula and knead again 3 min/. Transfer mixture to greased bowl, cover with cling film and leave to rise for 2 hours or until doubled in size. Clean mixing bowl.
  5. When dough has risen, mix with a wooden spoon until smooth and glossy. Preheat oven to 180°C and spoon mixture into prepared moulds ⅔ full. Bake 20–30 minutes (180°C) until well risen and deeply browned. Remove from moulds and leave to cool completely on a wire rack.

Syrup and Cream

  1. Place sugar, water, orange zest and juice and vanilla in mixing bowl then mix 5 sec/speed 3. Heat 15 min/100°C/speed 1. Remove lid then stir in rum with spatula.
  2. Transfer to a deep bowl, then submerge and saturate cold babas one at a time. Transfer babas back to wire rack to cool and reserve remaining syrup. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and, without measuring cup, whip speed 3, without setting a time, watching carefully to avoid overwhipping until desired stiffness is achieved. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. To serve, cut babas in half, place a few orange segments on top, drizzle with reserved syrup and top with whipped cream.

Useful Items

large bowl, deep bowl, cling film, wooden spoon, wire rack, dariole moulds, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The syrup will thicken as it cools.
  • The cooled cakes may float as they are submerged in the syrup; hold them under with a spoon if necessary.
  • Unsoaked babas can be frozen.
