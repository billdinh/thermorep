Almond Milk

Ingredients

  • 170 g almonds, raw, soaked overnight in filtered water with 1 tsp salt
  • 1000 g water, filtered
  • 1 Tbsp raw honey
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
Nutrition
per 1 total recipe
Calories
4561 kJ / 1090 kcal
Protein
35.9 g
Carbohydrates
21.2 g
Fat
94.9 g

Preparation

  1. Rinse and drain soaked almonds. Place almonds in mixing bowl.
  2. Add water, honey and salt then blend 1 min 30 sec/speed 9.
  3. Strain almond milk through a nut milk bag or into a sieve lined with muslin set over a large bowl. Squeeze and wring out bag or muslin to ensure all almond milk is extracted, reserving pulp (see tip).
  4. Pour strained milk into sterilised glass jars, seal and label then store in fridge. Use as desired within 3 days.

Useful Items

medium mesh sieve, muslin or nut milk bag, large bowl, sterilised jam jars, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe developed by Jenny Tschiesche BSc(Hons) Dip(ION) FdSc BANT. For more information see www.jennytschiesche.com.

  • Soaking nuts and seeds increases their nutrient value along with breaking down the problematic compounds that help enhance their digestibility. Activating nuts is actually a traditional practice that historically required the soaking of both nuts and seeds in salty water and letting them dry in the sun. Without the level of sunshine required to do this in many countries and with slightly more sanitary methods of drying we can activate nuts in our own kitchens. We can do this by a) using a dehydrator for around 12-24 hours or using an oven, at the lowest temperature possible, preferably no more than 65°C. Stir or turn them occasionally, for the required drying time.

Almonds : 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak for 12-14 hours

Cashews: 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 3-6 hours*

Macadamias: 1 tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 7-12 hours

Walnuts: ½ tsp salt per 1 cup nuts, soak 7-12 hours

* Cashews can and should be soaked for no more than 6 hours and should be dried rapidly (you can increase the heat to 120°C). That’s because they have already been treated (with heat) to get rid of a toxic oil called cardiol.
