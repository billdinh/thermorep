Garlic and coriander naan

Ingredients

Topping

  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 tbsp ghee (see Tips)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds

Naan

  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 240 g full cream milk (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling (optional)
  • 480 g baker's flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 120 g natural yoghurt

Preparation

Topping

  1. Place coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place ghee into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add garlic and chop 3 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add nigella seeds and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.

Naan

  1. Grease a large bowl with oil and set aside.
  2. Place milk and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  3. Add all remaining naan ingredients and mix 20 sec/speed 3, then knead 3 min/. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Transfer into prepared bowl and set aside in a warm place to prove until doubled in size (approx. 1-2 hours).
  4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface. Divide dough into 6 equal portions (approx. 140 g each). Gently work each piece into a ball and using the heel of your hands, gently stretch each dough portion into an oval shape (approx. 23 x 15 cm) then lightly dust with flour.
  5. Place a large frying pan over high heat. Working 1 naan at a time, place into frying pan and cook until dark brown blisters form (approx. 1-1½ minutes each side). Remove naan from heat and brush with reserved ghee and garlic mixture, then sprinkle with reserved coriander and salt (optional). Transfer onto a serving dish and cover to keep warm. Repeat frying with remaining naan. Serve warm.

Useful Items

2 bowls, large bowl, silicone bread mat, serving plate, large frying pan, pastry brush, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own ghee and garlic paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can replace the milk with the leftover whey from the Paneer recipe (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip).
  • Baked naans can be frozen. Allow to cool and store in a freezable bag or container, layered between pieces of baking paper. Defrost thoroughly before reheating in a hot oven (220°C). Place the naan on a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) lined with baking paper and sprinkle with a little water and heat until warmed through (approx. 5 minutes).

  • Dairy-free naan: Replace milk with water and proceed as per recipe.
  • Baked variation: Preheat oven to 250°C and place 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) into oven to warm. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and carefully weigh 250 g water into it. Working in batches, remove baking tray from top shelf of oven and place 3 naan onto it and return to top shelf in oven. Quickly, but carefully, pour half of the water onto the lower baking tray to create steam and immediately close oven door to trap steam. Bake for 4-6 minutes (250°C) or until naan are puffed with golden brown spots. Carefully remove naan and brush with reserved ghee and garlic mixture, then sprinkle with reserved coriander and salt (optional). Repeat with remaining water and naan. Serve warm.
