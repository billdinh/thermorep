Home-style soda bread with spring onion

Ingredients

  • 2 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into thirds
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 330 g buttermilk
  • 100 g crème fraîche, to serve
  • 200 g smoked salmon, to serve
  • fresh dill, leaves only, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour, bicarbonate of soda and salt and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Add buttermilk and knead 30 sec/, until just combined.
  5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a round (approx. 15 cm). Place onto prepared baking tray then, using a sharp knife, make a cross in the top of the loaf.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes (180°C), or until golden brown and bread sounds hollow when tapped on the underside.
  7. Slice and serve warm, topped with crème fraîche and smoked salmon, garnished with fresh dill.

Useful Items

baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), silicone bread mat, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditionally soda bread is made without the spring onion and served toasted at breakfast with butter and jam however, soda bread goes well with a wide variety of flavours so feel free to play around with some different combinations. Try serving with wilted spinach, grated cheese, avocado, feta, fresh tomato or even baked beans.
