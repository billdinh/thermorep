Saffron arancini

Ingredients

Arancini

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 180 g white wine
  • 3 pinches saffron
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 40 g unsalted butter
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 250 g arborio rice
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 700 g water
  • 1 ½ tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)

Assembly

  • 150 g quinoa flakes
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 100 g mozzarella cheese, cut into pieces (approx. 1 cm) (see Tips)
  • vegetable oil (approx. 750 g), for deep frying

Preparation

Arancini

  1. Line a rectangular baking tin (26  x 16 cm) with plastic wrap and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place wine and saffron into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid. Transfer into a seperate bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place onion and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add butter and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Insert butterfly whisk. Add thyme, rice and pepper and sauté 1 min/Varoma//speed 1,  without measuring cup.
  7. Add reserved wine mixture and sauté 2 min/Varoma//speed 1,  without measuring cup.Scrape bottom of mixing bowl with spatula to loosen rice.
  8. Add water and stock paste and cook 13-15 min/100°C//speed 1,  placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until rice is cooked to your liking.
  9. Add reserved Parmesan and combine 10 sec//speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk. Allow risotto to cool slightly in mixing bowl. Transfer risotto into prepared tin. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside to cool for 30 minutes, then place into refrigerator overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Assembly

  1. Place quinoa flakes, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and mill 5 sec/speed 9. Transfer onto a plate and set aside.
  2. Roll 1 tablespoon of the cooled risotto into a walnut-sized ball. Press a hole in the middle of each ball with your thumb, then place a cube of mozzarella inside and press risotto around it to enclose and form a ball. Repeat with remaining risotto and mozzarella to make approx. 36 balls.
  3. Roll each arancini ball in quinoa mixture, pressing to coat well all over. Transfer coated arancini balls onto a plate, then place into refrigerator for 20 minutes.
  4. Place a deep saucepan over medium-high heat and add enough oil to completely cover arancini. Heat oil to 170°C, or until a small cube of bread dropped in oil turns golden brown in 20 seconds. Working in batches of 5 balls, place arancini into oil and fry for 3-4 minutes, or until golden brown all over.
  5. Transfer onto paper towel to drain and allow to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can replace mozzarella with bocconcini in this recipe.
