Smoked Chocolate Mousse

Ingredients

  • 7 oz dark chocolate chips, 60% cocoa
  • 3 ½ oz heavy whipping cream
  • 2 oz sugar
  • 4 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 4 egg whites, from large eggs
  • ½ tsp salt
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
1849 kJ / 442 kcal

10 g

Preparation

  1. Place a stainless steel bowl on top of mixing bowl lid and weigh in chocolate and cream. Smoke chocolate and cream on grill (see Tip) until just beginning to melt (approx. 15-20 min.).
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add warm smoked chocolate and cream mixture and cook 4 min/120°F/speed 2.
  4. Add egg yolks and cook 3 min/160°F/speed 2. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and salt into mixing bowl and whisk 4 min/speed 3.5 until egg whites are stiff.
  6. Gently fold a heaping tablespoon beaten egg whites into the chocolate mixture with spatula, to loosen and lighten the mixture. Then gently fold remaining egg whites into the chocolate mixture until well incorporated.
  7. Gently pour chocolate mousse into dessert bowls and place in refrigerator until set (approx. 3 hr.). Serve chilled.

Useful Items

stainless-steel bowl, large bowl, bbq grill, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • To smoke chocolate and cream on a gas grill: preheat one side of grill on low and other side on high. Meanwhile soak woodchips in a bowl of water for 10 minutes. Place woodchips into a smoking box and see manufacturer's instructions for where to place. Once the chips begin to smoke, turn off low side of grill. To prevent scorching, place bowl of chocolate and cream on top of another empty stainless bowl or sauce pan. Place the bowl(s) of chocolate and cream on the unlit side of the grill. Close lid of BBQ and smoke until chocolate begins to melt (approx. 20 min.).
  • Garnish with chocolate chips and whipped cream.
  • Texas barbecue is smoked with mesquite, but try other wood chips like fruit woods or hickory.
