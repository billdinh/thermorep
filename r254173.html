Ricotta and spinach crespelle 'cake'

Ingredients

  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 5 sprigs fresh chives, cut into quarters, plus extra chopped for garnishing
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 2 tbsp polenta (fine)
  • 2 eggs
  • 350 g milk
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 4 spring onions/shallots, trimmed and cut into quarters
  • 20 g salted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra for frying
  • 300 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 350 g ricotta
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 50 g thickened cream

Preparation

  1. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place chives, flour, polenta, eggs, milk, oil and 40 g of the reserved Parmesan into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a jug and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 220°C. Set aside a shallow ovenproof baking dish (25 cm).
  4. Place spring onions/shallots into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add butter and spinach and sauté 5 min/100°C/speed 1, stirring with aid of spatula, until spinach wilts.
  6. Add ricotta, salt and pepper and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  7. Place a frying pan (20 cm) over medium-high heat and add 1 teaspoon of butter. Stir reserved batter and pour approx. ⅓ cup into frying pan, swirling pan as you add batter to cover base completely. Cook for 2 minutes, on 1 side only, until edges are golden, then transfer onto a plate, cooked side down. Stir batter and repeat frying, adding a teaspoon of butter after each crespelle (makes 8 crespelle - see Tips).
  8. Transfer 1 crespelle into the base of reserved baking dish. Spread approximately 3 tablespoons of the spinach mixture onto 1 crespelle, smoothing with spatula. Place another crespelle on top, then spread with a further 3 tablespoons of spinach mixture. Continue layering crespelle and spinach mixture, finishing with a crespelle (make 8 crespelle in total).
  9. Pour cream over crespelle "cake" and sprinkle with remaining 40 g grated Parmesan. Bake for 5 minutes (220°C). Reset oven setting to grill and grill for 1-2 minutes, until golden brown and bubbling. Scatter with chopped chives before serving (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, ovenproof baking dish, jug, frying pan (20 cm), plate, oven grill, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To save time, spread spinach mixture over each crespelle and layer while next crespelle is frying in the pan
  • Serve this dish as a main, with a green salad on the side, or as a side dish to grilled meat, chicken or fish.
