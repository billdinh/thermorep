Crumbed cutlets with cauliflower purée

Ingredients

Cauliflower purée

  • 500 g cauliflower, broken into florets
  • 50 g butter
  • 70 g quark (see Tips)
  • sea salt, to taste (see Tips)
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Mountain pepperberry crumbed cutlets

  • 130 g sourdough bread, torn into pieces
  • 2 tsp ground Mountain pepperberry (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp sea salt (see Tips)
  • 2 - 3 tbsp spelt flour, for coating
  • 1 egg
  • 8 lamb cutlets
  • 4 - 5 tbsp olive oil, for shallow frying
  • spinach pesto, to serve (see Tips)

Preparation

Cauliflower purée

  1. Place cauliflower into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and cook 12 min/100°C/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add quark, salt and pepper and blend 30 sec/speed 1-7, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 7. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Mountain pepperberry crumbed cutlets

  1. Place bread, pepperberry and salt into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 7 or until finely chopped and well combined. Transfer mixture onto a plate.
  2. Place flour onto a separate plate. Crack egg into a shallow bowl and beat lightly. Coat lamb cutlets in flour and shake off excess. Dip into egg, then press into breadcrumb mixture to coat on all sides. Set aside.
  3. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add oil. Fry cutlets, until golden brown on both sides and just cooked through or to your liking (approx. 3-5 minutes each side). Serve cutlets with reserved Cauliflower purée and spinach pesto (see Tips).

Useful Items

plate, large frying pan, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, shallow bowl, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Mountain pepperberry is native to Australia and has a sweeter taste than black pepper. You can find Mountain pepperberry at spice shops, specialty food stores and online.
  • To make your own quark, please refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au). You can also replace the quark with cream cheese in this recipe.
  • This meal can also be served to the whole family. Just omit or reduce the salt for children (and cut into appropriate sized pieces for younger children).
  • To make your own pesto, please refer to the Spinach and coriander pesto recipe on this recipe chip.
