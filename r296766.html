Butternut Squash, Sage and Hazelnut Tart

Ingredients

Butternut Squash

  • 500 g butternut squash, peeled, diced (2 cm)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 5 fresh sage leaves, torn in pieces
  • 1 pinch dried chilli flakes
  • fine sea salt, to taste
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Pastry

  • 100 g plain flour
  • 100 g spelt flour
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 75 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 45 g water, cold
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt

Filling

  • 80 g roasted hazelnuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 fresh sage leaves, plus extra roughly chopped for garnishing
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 150 g whole milk
  • 100 g feta cheese, roughly chopped
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Butternut Squash

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  2. Place butternut squash in a roasting tin and drizzle with oil. Scatter over sage leaves and season with chilli flakes, salt and pepper to taste. Roast for 30 minutes (200°C) until soft and browned around the edges. Meanwhile, make pastry.

Pastry

  1. Place plain flour, spelt flour and thyme in mixing bowl then grind 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add butter, water and salt then mix 15 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a springform tin (Ø 24 cm), pushing evenly into base and up sides using fingertips. Prick base with a fork then bake for 10 minutes (200°C). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make filling.

Filling

  1. Place hazelnuts in mixing bowl and chop 1 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, sage leaves, onions and oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/110°C/speed 1.
  3. Add milk, feta, eggs, reserved chopped hazelnuts, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec//speed 2.
  4. Distribute reserved butternut squash over pastry base then pour over filling. Scatter with extra sage leaves then bake for 25-30 minutes (200°C) until browned and set.
  5. Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 24 cm), roasting tin, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This eye-catching vegetarian tart not only looks delicious but is also easy to put together. Make the quick pastry while the squash is roasting, then add the squash to the pastry case before pouring in the egg filling. A flavourful tart that makes a great centrepiece for an informal weekend family lunch.
