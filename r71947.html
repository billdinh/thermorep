Bolognaise sauce for two

Ingredients

White balsamic reduction

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 500 g white balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g dry sherry
  • 80 g raw sugar
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 8 white peppercorns (see Tips)
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into pieces

Bolognaise sauce

  • ½ onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 carrot, cut into pieces
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into pieces
  • 1 rindless bacon rasher, cut into pieces
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 150 g beef mince
  • 100 g pork mince
  • 1 tbsp white balsamic reduction
  • 60 g tomato paste
  • 80 g water
  • 1 - 2 pinches sea salt, to taste
  • 1 - 2 pinches freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • fresh basil leaves, to garnish (optional)

Preparation

White balsamic reduction

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat grating until a fine consistency is achieved.
  3. Add all remaining reduction ingredients and cook 30 min/Varoma//speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a hot-rinsed sealable jar or bottle and seal. Allow to cool completely before placing into a cool, dark area to store until use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Bolognaise sauce

  1. Place onion, garlic, carrot, celery, bacon and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add beef and pork and cook 5 min/Varoma//speed .
  4. Add all remaining bolognaise ingredients and cook 8 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Serve immediately with fresh basil leaves to garnish (optional) or use as required in other recipes.

Useful Items

sealable jar or bottle, fine mesh sieve

Hints & Tricks

  • You can substitute white peppercorns for black peppercorns.
  • Perfect over cooked pasta or in a lasagne, this Bolognaise sauce is delicious fresh or frozen for another time. Try making your own pasta in the Thermomix to enjoy a fresh, delicious meal for two. Freeze leftover pasta.
  • If cooking for one, freeze any leftovers for dinner or lunch later in the week.
  • Use leftover beef mince as a substitute to the lamb in Lamb and feta meatballs.
  • Using both pork and beef mince in your sauce adds a more robust flavour but you can use 250 g of either.
