Rogale (Polish brioche crescents) (Grace Mazur)

Ingredients

Dough

  • 210 g full cream milk
  • 20 g fresh yeast (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
  • 60 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tbsp grapeseed oil (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting

Filling

  • 150 g prosciutto, cut into thin slices
  • 200 g Swiss cheese, cut into thin slices
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly beaten, for glazing
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place milk, yeast and sugar into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add all remaining dough ingredients and knead 2 min/.
  3. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat and wrap dough in bread mat, or transfer dough onto a lightly floured work surface, shape into a ball and place into a bowl and cover with a kitchen towel. Leave dough to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30-40 minutes).
  4. Knock down dough and leave to prove again (approx. 15-20 minutes).
  5. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a large baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  6. Divide dough into 3 equal portions (approx. 310 g each). On a lightly floured work surface, roll out 1 portion of dough into a circle (approx. 30 cm). Cut dough into 8 triangles (similar to slicing a pizza into wedges). Repeat with remaining 2 portions of dough.

Filling

  1. Place approx. 3 slices of the prosciutto and approx. 2 slices of the Swiss cheese at wide edge of each triangle and roll towards the pointed end, enclosing filling. Pinch and seal each corner to seal in filling, creating a croissant shape.
  2. Transfer Rogale onto prepared baking tray and prove again (approx. 30-40 minutes). Brush with egg yolk and sprinkle with sesame seeds (optional).
  3. Bake for 13-15 minutes (180°C) or until lightly golden brown. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool slightly, then serve.

Useful Items

pastry brush, pizza cutter, rolling pin, tea towel, large bowl, silicone bread mat, large baking tray, baking paper, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Traditional Rogale: Replace prosciutto and cheese with 150 g jam or 200 g quark mixture in step 7, then proceed as per recipe. To make the quark mixture, insert butterfly whisk. Place 1 egg yolk, 40 g caster sugar and ½ tsp vanilla sugar into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/60°C/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk. Add 250 g quark and ½ tsp lemon zest and mix 15 sec/speed 6, with aid of spatula. Adjust sugar to taste, scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then repeat mixing 15 sec/speed 6.
  • Fresh yeast can be replaced with 2 teaspoons dried yeast.
  • You can substitute grapeseed oil with light olive oil.
  • Traditionally, Rogale are filled with jam or a quark filling. This savoury version is our Australian take on this classic Polish recipe (see the "Traditional Rogale" variation below).
  • Rogale are best served on the day they are made or the following day. They're also perfect for lunchboxes!
