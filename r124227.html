Maple bread and butter pudding with walnut crumble

Ingredients

  • 300 g sliced wholemeal bread (see Tips)
  • 60 g butter, softened
  • 80 g pitted prunes, cut into pieces
  • 60 g walnuts
  • 3 tsp Rapadura sugar (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp self-raising flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup, plus extra to serve
  • 100 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 500 g milk
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • natural yoghurt or custard, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh bread into it. Set bowl aside. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 40 g butter into it. Set bowl aside. Place a separate bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh prunes into it. Set bowl aside.
  2. Lightly spread each bread slice with reserved butter, then cut each bread slice into halves. Place bread into a deep-sided ovenproof dish (approx. 25 x 16 cm). Sprinkle reserved chopped prunes over bread and set aside.
  3. Place walnuts into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 4, until coarsley chopped.
  4. Add sugar, remaining 20 g butter, cinnamon and flour and combine 8 sec//speed 4. Crumble should be quite lumpy. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place eggs, syrup, cream, milk and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4, until smooth.
  6. Pour mixture over bread, coating every slice. Set aside to absorb for 15 minutes.
  7. Bake for 30 minutes (160°C). Remove pudding from oven, scatter with reserved crumble topping and bake for a further 15-20 minutes (160°C), or until golden and pudding has set.
  8. Allow to cool for 5-10 minutes, then drizzle with extra maple syrup if desired, and serve with yoghurt or custard (optional).

Useful Items

bowl, rectangular casserole dish (25 x 16 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a lower-calorie option, you can leave out the cream and use 600 g milk.
  • If you prefer, you can replace the prunes with raisins or sultanas.
  • You can use whichever wholemeal or wholegrain bread you like for this pudding – multigrain and rye are also lovely. Choose a softer bread to allow the custard to properly absorb. If your loaf is unsliced, cut into 1 cm slices.
  • You can use your preferred brown sugar in this recipe – either regular brown sugar or a less refined option such as Rapadura sugar (Panela) or coconut sugar.
