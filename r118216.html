Fresh fruit and cream cheese tarts

Ingredients

Shortcrust pastry

  • 100 g glutinous rice
  • 1 tbsp white chia seeds
  • 40 g blanched almonds
  • 50 g arrowroot flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 20 g potato starch
  • ½ tsp fine salt
  • 80 g unsalted butter
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp chilled water

Cream cheese filling

  • 60 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 2 eggs
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 2 tsp gluten free flour mix (see Tips)
  • 250 g cream cheese
  • 60 g sour cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Fruit topping

  • 250 g fresh mixed berries (see Tips)
  • 1 kiwifruit, peeled and cut into thin slices
  • 150 g orange juice, room temperature
  • 60 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp agar agar powder

Preparation

Shortcrust pastry

  1. Set aside 8 individual loose based tart tins (8 cm).
  2. Place rice and chia seeds into mixing bowl and mill 1 min 30 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add almonds and mill 20 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add arrowroot, potato starch, salt and butter and mix 10 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add egg yolks and water and knead 40 sec/. Transfer onto a silicone bread mat or work surface. Shape pastry into a ball, then flatten into a disc (1 cm thickness), wrap in plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to chill (approx. 30 minutes). The pastry should be firm enough to roll. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Preheat oven to 170°C. Place pastry between 2 pieces of baking paper. Roll out thinly (approx. 4 mm thickness), dusting with arrowroot flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  7. Remove top layer of baking paper. Using a round cookie cutter (10 cm) cut pastry into discs and carefully press into tins. Re-roll pastry and cut out remaining discs to line all of the tart tins (8 in total). Prick bases with a fork and place into refrigerator for 10 minutes.
  8. Transfer tarts onto a baking tray (30 x 40 cm), then bake for 15 minutes (170°C). Set aside to cool.

Cream cheese filling

  1. Reduce oven temperature to 150°C.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cream into mixing bowl and whip until soft peaks form/speed 3, watching cream carefully through hole in mixing bowl lid to avoid overwhipping. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside. Remove butterfly whisk.
  3. Place eggs, sugar, flour mix, cream cheese, sour cream and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 6. Transfer into bowl with whipped cream and gently fold together.
  4. Pour cream mixture into each tart case, filling to the top. Bake for 20 minutes (150°C), or until set. Allow tarts to cool (approx. 30 minutes) then place into refrigerator for 1 hour. Clean and dry mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.

Fruit topping

  1. Remove tarts from the tins. Arrange berries and kiwifruit decoratively on top of the cooled tarts. Set aside.
  2. Place orange juice, sugar and agar agar into mixing bowl and cook 6 min/90°C/speed 3. Using a pastry brush, brush glaze over fruit immediately (the glaze will set very quickly), making sure to cover the entire tart.
  3. Serve tarts immediately or place into refrigerator until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

8 fluted tart tins (8 cm), silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, baking paper, rolling pin, round cookie cutter (10 cm), baking tray (30 x 40 cm), large bowl, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to the Gluten free flour mix recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Use any fresh seasonal berries of choice.
  • Agar agar is a jelly-like substance derived from seaweed. It thickens and sets very quickly, and is useful as a vegan substitute for gelatine. It is flavourless, odourless and tasteless. Agar agar works best with just warmed or room temperature liquids. You can buy it from health food stores and some supermarkets.
  • Tarts can be made up to 1 day in advance and stored in refrigerator.
