Birthday Cake

Ingredients

Cake Batter

  • 12 oz sugar,
  • 4 oz unsalted butter, room temperature, cut into pieces, plus extra to grease
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 12 oz whole milk
  • 13 oz cake flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt

Strawberry Buttercream Frosting

  • 13 oz confectioners sugar,
  • 6 oz strawberries, cleaned, hulled and halved
  • 8 oz unsalted butter, room temperature, cut into pieces
  • 1 oz whole milk
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 5 oz strawberry jam,
  • colored sprinkles, to decorate

Preparation

Cake Batter

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Lightly grease 2 round cake pans (8 in.), line bottoms with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. Place sugar into mixing bowl and grind 30 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add butter, eggs and vanilla and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Insert butterfly whisk and whip 3 min/speed 3.5. Remove butterfly whisk. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add milk, flour, baking powder and salt and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 10 sec/speed 4. Evenly divide batter between prepared pans.
  5. Bake 35 minutes (350°F) or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Clean mixing bowl and allow cakes to cool for 10 minutes before removing from pan. Meanwhile make frosting.

Strawberry Buttercream Frosting

  1. Place sugar and strawberries into mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter, milk, vanilla and salt and mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Insert butterfly whisk and whip 1-2 min/speed 3, frosting may appear separated but continue whipping as it will come together. Remove butterfly whisk. Once cakes are completely cooled, trim top of cake with a serrated knife to create a flat surface for icing.
  3. Spread a thin layer of frosting over one of the cooled cakes using icing spatula. Chill until buttercream is firm, about 10 minutes. Evenly spread strawberry jam over the firm frosting, stacking the other cake on top. Apply a crumb-coat by applying frosting on top and sides of cake and refrigerate until firm. Frost and decorate the cake to your liking, cover cake and refrigerate. Allow cake to sit at room temperature 5-10 minutes, decorate with sprinkles before serving.

Useful Items

parchment paper, toothpick or skewer, icing spatula, round cake pans (8 in.), oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Cut three to four pieces of parchment paper into strips (approx. 13 in. x 3 in.), place between cake edges and serving plate to keep the plate clean while frosting. Carefully remove strips of parchment paper when completely done.
  • Frosting can be kept in an airtight container and refrigerated for later use. Allow frosting to soften slightly at room temperature before use.
  • Fill cakes with sliced fresh strawberries. Alternatively, vanilla or chocolate pudding mixed with equal amounts of fresh whipped cream then beaten together for 1 min/speed 4 makes a delicious, creamy filling.
