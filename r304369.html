Chocolate Thumbprint Biscuits

Ingredients

Biscuits

  • 250 g unsalted butter, soft
  • 200 g granulated sugar
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 Tbsp vanilla extract
  • 350 g plain flour
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

Ganache

  • 180 g milk chocolate, small pieces or callets
  • 80 g double cream
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
607 kJ / 145 kcal
Protein
1.6 g

Preparation

Biscuits

  1. Line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place butter and sugar in mixing bowl then mix 2 min/speed 4.
  3. Add egg and vanilla extract then mix 20 sec/speed 4.
  4. Add flour and salt then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Finish mixing by hand with spatula if necessary.
  5. Roll heaped tablespoons of dough into balls then place on prepared trays, leaving 3-4 cm between each. Make an indent in dough with your thumb or the back of a teaspoon. Place trays in fridge for 1½ hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 180°C.
  6. Bake for 12-14 minutes (180°C), turning the trays halfway through, until lightly golden around the edges. If indents puff up, use the back of a teaspoon to push down again. Leave biscuits to rest on trays for 10 minutes, again using a teaspoon to recreate indents if necessary, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Ganache

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cream and heat 2 min 30 sec/50°C/speed 3.
  3. Spoon approx. 1 tsp ganache into the indent of each biscuit, then leave to set for 30 minutes before serving or storing in an airtight container until needed.

Useful Items

wire rack, baking trays, baking paper, airtight container, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • While away a weekend afternoon in the kitchen making these fun thumbprint biscuits. Typically made with jam, we’ve filled ours with a rich chocolate ganache which is quick and easy to make in the Thermomix®.
