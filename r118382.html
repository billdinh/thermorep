Tuna empanadas

Ingredients

Salsa brava

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g red onion
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ - 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 300 g roasted red capsicum, cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 200 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar

Pastry

  • 100 g light olive oil
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 200 g cold water

Tuna filling

  • 500 g water, plus extra for brushing
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 100 g red onion
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 200 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 40 g tomato paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, finely chopped
  • 6 anchovy fillets, cut into pieces
  • ½ lemon, juice and zest, finely grated
  • 185 g canned tuna, drained

Assembly

  • flour, for dusting
  • vegetable oil, for frying
  • salt, to season
  • freshly ground black pepper, to season

Preparation

Salsa brava

  1. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil and sauté 4 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and sauté for a further 4 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add remaining salsa ingredients and chop 4 sec/speed 5, then cook 7 min/90°C/speed 3. Transfer into a serving bowl and allow to cool to room temperature before serving, or cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator until ready to serve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pastry

  1. Place a jug onto mixing bowl lid and weigh oil into it. Set aside.
  2. Place flour, baking powder and salt into mixing bowl and knead 1 min 30 sec/, slowly pouring oil through hole in mixing bowl lid until dough resembles rough crumbs.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water into it.
  4. Knead 1 min/, slowly pouring water through hole in mixing bowl lid until dough is combined.
  5. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Place into a large bowl, cover with a kitchen towel and leave to rest for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tuna filling

  1. Place water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and place eggs into it and cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and place under cold running water to cease cooking. Set aside to cool eggs. When cool enough to handle, peel eggs and roughly chop. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Place garlic and onion into mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar and vinegar and cook 7 min/100°C/speed 2 , without measuring cup.
  6. Add parsley, anchovies, lemon zest, lemon juice, tuna and reserved eggs and mix 10 sec/speed 2, or until incorporated.
  7. Transfer mixture into a sealable container and place into the refrigerator to cool while rolling pastry.

Assembly

  1. On a lightly floured work surface, roll out pastry (approx. 2 mm thickness). Using a cookie cutter (9-10 cm), cut pastry into circles. Re-roll excess pastry to cut out more circles (makes approx. 34 circles in total). If pastry shrinks, roll out again to a 9-10 cm diameter just before filling.
  2. Place 1 heaped teaspoon of tuna mixture in the centre of the pastry circle, then lightly brush around all edges with water and fold over to form a semi-circle. Seal edges together, making sure to remove any air bubbles. Use a fork or your fingertips to crimp the edges. Continue until all pastry and filling is used.
  3. Line a large plate with a paper towel and set aside. Place a small saucepan over medium-high heat and add enough oil to cover empanadas. Heat oil to 180°C (see Tips). Working in batches, use a slotted spoon to lower empanadas into hot oil, ensuring they are not crowded (approx. 3-5 empanadas per batch) and fry for 2-3 minutes, turning once during frying, until golden brown. Carefully remove using slotted spoon and transfer onto prepared plate, then sprinkle with salt and pepper. Serve empanadas warm with Salsa brava.

Hints & Tricks

  • Roasted red capsicum can be purchased in jars at the supermarket or from delicatessens. To make your own, roast capsicum over a gas flame, turning with tongs until blackened all over. Place into a glass bowl and cover with plastic wrap for 10 minutes or until cooled. Peel and discard skin and deseed before using.
  • Salsa can be puréed if desired; blend for 15 sec/speed 10, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 10.
  • To check your oil is at 180°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into the oil; the bread will turn golden brown in 15 seconds.
  • Empanadas can also be made ahead of time. Generously dust uncooked empanadas with flour and store in refrigerator in a sealable container for up to 2 days. If freezing, freeze in a single layer. Defrost before frying or baking.
  • Baked empanadas: Preheat oven to 180°C and line a baking tray with baking paper. Brush empanadas with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C).
