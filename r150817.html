Thai-style Prawn Broth, Spiced Apples

Ingredients

Spiced Apples

  • 4 cooking apples (e.g. Bramley)
  • 2 tsp vanilla sugar, homemade
  • 4 star anise
  • 4 pinches ground ginger
  • 4 cloves
  • 4 pinches ground cinnamon

Thai Broth

  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 20 - 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 1 fresh chilli, deseeded
  • 15 g olive oil
  • 800 g raw prawns, unpeeled
  • 1400 g water
  • 40 g rice vinegar
  • 30 g fish sauce
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 star anise
  • 3 pinches salt, or to taste
  • 3 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste
  • 80 g dried Chinese egg noodles
  • 2 pinches wakame flakes (optional)
  • 100 g fresh bean sprouts
  • 15 fresh coriander, leaves only

Preparation

Spiced Apples

  1. Hollow out apples with an apple-corer, removing cores and leaving bases intact. Place each one on a sheet of heat-resistant cling film or baking paper (20 cm x 20 cm). Fill each apple cavity with ½ tsp vanilla sugar, 1 star anise, 1 pinch ginger, 1 clove and 1 pinch cinnamon. Close each parcel tightly, place in Varoma dish and set aside.

Thai Broth

  1. Place onions, ginger and chilli in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add olive oil, prawn heads and shells and, without measuring cup, sauté 5 min/120°C/speed . Meanwhile, set peeled raw prawns aside in fridge.
  3. Add water, rice vinegar, fish sauce, cloves, star anise, salt and black pepper. Set Varoma with apples in position and steam 35 min/Varoma/speed . Remove Varoma and set aside.
  4. Strain broth through a sieve over a large bowl, pressing solids using spatula to extract their juices. Discard solids, rinse mixing bowl well and pour strained broth back into it. Add egg noodles to mixing bowl, set Varoma back in position and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed . Remove Varoma.
  5. Add wakame, reserved peeled raw prawns and bean sprouts to mixing bowl. Set Varoma back in position and cook 3 min/Varoma//speed 0.5. Set covered Varoma aside on a plate to keep apples warm. Transfer soup to bowls, add coriander leaves, stir and serve immediately. Serve spiced apples for dessert.

Useful Items

apple corer, heat-resistant cling film or baking paper, medium mesh sieve, serving bowls, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • The slightly sweet and spicy flavours of this Thai broth complement the prawns and noodles beautifully. Take advantage of the steam produced by the broth to cook an easy yet delicious fruit dessert. Use a high-quality natural vanilla sugar for the best flavour.
  • Wakame is an edible seaweed often used in Japanese, Korean and Chinese cuisines.
    - If apples are large and prevent closing of Varoma lid, slice off the top portion of the apples.
  • Replace bean sprouts with finely sliced fresh mushrooms.
