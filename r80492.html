Penne with mussels

Ingredients

  • 4 - 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only,
  • 250 g water, plus extra as needed
  • 1000 g fresh mussels, shells scrubbed and de-bearded
  • 30 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for serving
  • 30 g shallots, cut in halves
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 fresh red chilli, small, deseeded (optional)
  • 50 g dry white wine (optional)
  • 120 g cherry tomatoes, cut in halves
  • 40 - 50 g tomato purée (concentrated)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 350 g penne or similar short dried pasta

Preparation

  1. Place flat-leaf parsley in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl. Scrub and de-beard mussels, if necessary, and place in Varoma dish, set Varoma in position and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma and check that mussels have all opened. If not, remove open mussels and transfer to a bowl, set Varoma back in position and steam a further 5-10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma, discard any mussels that remain closed, transfer open mussels to bowl and set aside. Strain the cooking liquid through a fine mesh strainer into another bowl and set aside.
  3. Place extra virgin olive oil, shallots, garlic cloves and red chilli in mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  4. Sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add white wine and cherry tomatoes and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add tomato purée and reserved cooking liquid, adding water to reach 700 g, then bring to a boil 7 min/100°C/speed 1.
  7. Add salt and penne and cook for time indicated on packet/100°C//speed  or until al dente. Meanwhile, remove mussels from opened shells, reserving some mussels in their shells for garnish, and set aside.
  8. Transfer pasta to a serving bowl, add reserved steamed mussels, combine using spatula and drizzle with extra virgin olive oil. Garnish with reserved chopped parsley and reserved mussels in their shells. Serve hot.

Hints & Tricks

  • In this dish the cooking method is borrowed from risotto: the pasta absorbs the liquid released by the mussels and at the same time adds starch to the sauce for a creamy and mouth-watering result. Add a little kick of chilli if you wish.
  • Fresh mussels can be replaced with frozen mussels. In this case, they must be defrosted first.
