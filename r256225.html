Pull-apart sunshine bread

Ingredients

  • 150 g cheddar cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 100 g rindless bacon rashers, cut into pieces (3 cm - see Tip)
  • 2 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 350 g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g salted butter, softened
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 200 g milk

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place cheese, bacon and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Transfer half of the mixture onto a plate and set aside.
  3. Add flour, butter, salt and pepper and mix 4 sec/speed 7.
  4. Add milk and mix 15 sec/speed 4. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and shape into a ball. Roll into a log (approx. 30 cm). Cut log into 15 pieces (approx. 2 cm). Press 1 cut surface of each piece of dough into reserved cheese mixture and place, cheese side up, side by side onto prepared baking tray in a circle. Sprinkle with any remaining cheese mixture.
  5. Bake for 20 minutes (190°C), until golden brown. Bread is best served warm on the day of baking.

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), plate, baking paper, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For a vegetarian option, omit the bacon. You can add chopped spring onions/shallots, sun-dried tomatoes or use herbs of choice in this recipe.
