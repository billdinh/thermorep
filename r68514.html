Gnocchi

Ingredients

  • 180 g plain flour
  • 600 g water
  • 500 g potatoes (e.g. Ruby Lou, Royal Blue, Coliban) peeled, cut into pieces (2 - 3 cm)
  • 2 tbsp semolina
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra for cooking water

per 1 portions

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl on top of mixing bowl lid and weigh plain flour or baker's flour into it. Set flour aside.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position then weigh potatoes into it. Steam 25-30 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until potatoes are cooked through and just soft.
  3. Set Varoma aside with lid in place and allow potatoes to cool completely (approx. 40 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Place cooled potatoes, 100 g of the flour and semolina into mixing bowl and mash 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  5. Add egg and salt and knead 30-40 sec/.
  6. Sprinkle remaining flour onto ThermoMat or work surface. Transfer dough onto ThermoMat and work in remaining reserved flour until dough comes together and is a soft, pliable texture. Roll dough into 2 cm wide strips then cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm long). Shape gnocchi with a fork or wooden gnocchi board.
  7. Working in batches, place gnocchi into a large pot of boiling, salted water and cook until they rise to the surface. Remove and drain with a slotted spoon then place into ThermoServer to keep warm. Repeat process in batches until all gnocchi is cooked. Serve immediately.

Useful Items

ThermoMat, wooden gnocchi board, large pot, ThermoServer, slotted spoon, stove

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve gnocchi with pan-fried butter and herbs (e.g. rosemary, sage) topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
  • Try serving with tomato pasta sauce or Arrabbiata sauce.
  • Form folded pillow-type gnocchi using two forks. Take a 1 - 3 cm piece of dough, place one fork on each diagonal corner and press the corners together to form 'folded' pillows. Repeat with all gnocchi.
  • To freeze, place uncooked gnocchi in a single layer on a floured board and place in the freezer for 3 - 4 hours. Once frozen, store in a sealed freezer bag and return to the freezer until required. Gnocchi can be cooked directly from frozen. Proceed as per recipe above from step 7.
