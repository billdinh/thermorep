Hot Cross Buns

Ingredients

Dough

  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced, plus extra for greasing
  • 300 g milk
  • 600 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 50 g sugar
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground mixed spice
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 70 g raisins
  • 70 g sultanas
  • 40 g chopped mixed peel

Paste

  • 40 g strong white bread flour
  • 20 g granulated sugar
  • 50 g water

Glaze

  • 25 g light brown sugar
  • 45 g milk
  • 1 Tbsp orange marmalade

Preparation

Dough

  1. Grease a large bowl and roasting tin.
  2. Place butter and milk in mixing bowl then heat 2 min/40°C/speed 1.5.
  3. Add flour, sugar, yeast, cinnamon, mixed spice, nutmeg, salt and egg. Mix 20 sec/speed 4 then knead 3 min/.
  4. Add raisins, sultanas and mixed peel then knead 1 min/. Tip out onto a lightly-floured surface and finish incorporating fruit and peel into dough by hand until evenly distributed. Transfer to prepared bowl, cover with cling film and leave in a warm place to rise until doubled in size (2-3 hours).
  5. Gently knock back dough, divide into 16 equal-sized pieces and shape into balls, then place in prepared roasting tin, 2 cm apart. Cover with greased cling film and leave to rise again until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, prepare paste.

Paste

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in flour, sugar and water. Mix by hand until smooth then transfer to a small piping bag (see tip).
  2. Preheat oven to 210°C. When buns are risen, snip corner off piping bag and pipe crosses on top of each bun. Bake for 20 minutes (210°C). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and prepare glaze.

Glaze

  1. Place sugar, milk and marmalade in mixing bowl then mix 1 min/80°C/speed 5. As soon as buns are removed from oven, brush tops with glaze. Leave to cool in tin then serve slightly warm or cool.

Useful Items

roasting tin, cling film, piping bag, pastry brush, large bowl, oven
