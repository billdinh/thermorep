Spinach and Ricotta Ravioli

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 200 g pasta flour (00 flour)
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 50 g fresh spinach, washed and dried
  • 1 egg, large
  • 1 egg yolk, from large egg

Ricotta and Spinach Filling

  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces
  • 50 g shallots, quartered
  • 15 g butter
  • 300 g fresh spinach, washed and dried
  • 120 g ricotta cheese
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg

Cooking

  • semolina, fine, for dusting
  • 1 egg white, for brushing
  • water, for cooking pasta
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • olive oil, for serving

Preparation

Pasta

  1. Place pasta flour, salt, spinach, egg and egg yolk in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 6. Knead 3 min/.
  2. Wrap dough in cling film and leave to rest in fridge for 20-30 minutes. Meanwhile prepare filling.

Ricotta and Spinach Filling

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place shallots and butter in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add spinach and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 2 whilst stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Add ricotta, grated Parmesan and nutmeg then mix 5 sec/speed 6.
  5. Divide chilled dough into two pieces, then use a pasta machine or rolling pin to roll out each piece very thinly (1-2 mm). Cut out rounds with a pastry cutter (Ø 9 cm). Brush each round with egg white and place approx. 1 tsp filling in centre of each round.
  6. Fold each pasta round over, enclosing filling and gently squeezing all the air out, then press firmly to seal edges together.
  7. Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil. Once at a rolling boil, gently lower a few ravioli at a time into it using a slotted spoon, then cook 3-4 minutes. Remove with slotted spoon, then serve immediately with a drizzle of oil and a sprinkling of grated Parmesan.

Useful Items

cling film, pastry brush, round pastry cutter, rolling pin or pasta machine, slotted spoon, saucepan, refrigerator, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • If pasta dough still looks like crumbs after mixing in step 1 add water, 1 tsp at a time, and mix a few seconds more at speed 6 until dough begins to cling together.
