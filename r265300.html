Seitan Roulade with Cranberry Stuffing and Vegan Gravy

Ingredients

Cranberry Stuffing

  • 3 - 4 fresh sage leaves
  • 70 g walnuts
  • 70 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 1 stalk celery, cut in 3 pieces (approx. 90 g)
  • 150 g chestnut mushrooms, sliced (1 cm)
  • 100 g wholemeal bread, cubed (2 cm)
  • 100 g dried cranberries
  • 70 g water
  • 1 ½ Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 ½ tsp chia seeds
  • 3 - 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper

Seitan

  • 300 g vital wheat gluten
  • 60 g nutritional yeast
  • 75 g quinoa flakes
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 350 g water, boiling
  • 60 g cannellini beans, tinned, drained
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp tahini

Assembly

  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp sesame oil

Vegan Mushroom Gravy

  • 50 g onion, quartered
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 120 g chestnut mushrooms, halved
  • 400 g water
  • 30 g almond milk
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (for 0.5 l)
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary, needles only
  • 2 Tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 2 tsp sherry
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste

Preparation

Cranberry Stuffing

  1. Place sage in mixing bowl, chop 1 sec/speed 5 then add walnuts and chop again 1 sec/speed 5. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place onions, oil and celery in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Add mushrooms and, without measuring cup, sauté 2 min/100°C//speed 0.5.
  3. Add bread, cranberries, water, soy sauce, chia seeds, reserved chopped walnuts and sage, thyme, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec//speed 0.5. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Seitan

  1. Place a large bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in vital wheat gluten, nutritional yeast and quinoa flakes. Add thyme, marjoram and chia seeds, stir with spatula to combine then set aside.
  2. Place garlic in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Add water, cannellini beans, stock cube, soy sauce and tahini then blend 10 sec/speed 7. Add reserved dry ingredients and knead 2 min/.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  2. Line work surface with cling film. Cover dough with more cling film then roll out to a rectangle approx. 25 cm x 35 cm. Remove all cling film and use your fingers to further shape dough, if needed.
  3. Distribute stuffing evenly over dough, leaving a 2.5 cm border on all sides.
  4. Starting at one of the short ends, roll seitan up, taking care to keep the roll tight. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce and sesame oil then brush mixture all over dough. Lay seitan roast on 2 large sheets of overlapping foil, seam side down, and roll up tightly, twisting the ends to seal well.
  5. Place on a baking tray and roast for 45-50 minutes (200°C), turning halfway through, until golden brown and firm to the touch. Meanwhile, make gravy.

Vegan Mushroom Gravy

  1. Place onion, oil and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Add mushrooms, chop 5 sec/speed 5 then sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add water, almond milk, stock cube, thyme, rosemary, nutritional yeast, flour, sherry and soy sauce then, with simmering basket instead of measuring cup, cook 15 min/100°C/speed 1. Season with salt and pepper to taste then transfer to a jug and keep warm.
  3. Remove foil from seitan roast, slice and serve hot, accompanied by mushroom gravy.

Useful Items

large bowl, aluminium foil, baking tray, cling film, rolling pin, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Walnuts can be substituted with another type of nut e.g. pine nuts or hazelnuts.
  • Substitute dried cranberries with dried cherries or raisins, if preferred.
  • With a festive cranberry stuffing and rich mushroom gravy, this vegan roulade will ensure your meat-free guests don't miss out this Christmas.
    Made from the protein portion of wheat, seitan is a meat alternative that mimics the texture of meat and takes on any flavours added to it, much like tofu. It is high in protein and versatile, with popular ways of cooking it including steaming, baking, boiling and stir-frying.
