Panzerotti with Ricotta and Sausage - Panzerotti con ricotta e salsiccia

Ingredients

Dough

  • 230 g water
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 150 g strong white bread flour
  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Filling

  • 150 g sausages, uncooked, skinned, cut in pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 2 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 180 g ricotta cheese, well drained
  • 100 g San Marzano tomatoes, seeds removed, diced (5 mm)
  • 1 sprig fresh basil, leaves only
  • ¼ - ½ tsp fine sea salt, to taste

Assembly and Cooking

  • frying oil (e.g. sunflower or vegetable)

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place 50 g water and yeast in mixing bowl then warm 2 min/37°C/speed 2.
  2. Add remaining 180 g water, bread flour, plain flour and salt then knead 3 min/. Transfer to a large bowl and bring together into a ball. Cover with cling film (see tip) and leave until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).

Filling

  1. Place skinned sausages in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add 1 Tbsp oil and cook 5 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add ricotta, tomatoes, basil, salt and 1 Tbsp oil then mix 10 sec//speed 3. Set mixing bowl aside to cool.

Assembly and Cooking

  1. Roll out dough on a lightly-floured work surface to 3-4 mm thick. With a pastry cutter (Ø 7 cm), cut out discs (see tip).
  2. Place ½-1 tsp of filling on half of each disc. Fold over the other half of the discs, forming semi-circles. Seal the edges, decorating as desired then place on a baking tray. Cover with cling film and leave to rest in a warm place for 30 minutes.
  3. After this time, heat frying oil in a deep saucepan or deep fat fryer then fry panzerotti, in batches, until golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve warm, or at room temperature.

Useful Items

cling film, rolling pin, baking tray, paper towels, large bowl, deep saucepan or deep fat fryer, slotted spoon, round pastry cutter (Ø 7 cm), hob

Hints & Tricks

  • Named after ‘pancia’, the Italian word for stomach, these delicious stuffed dumplings date back to the 18th century where they were a popular snack in Naples. Similar to a calzone but much smaller in shape, panzerotti were originally filled with a mixture of cheese and cooked meat, then deep-fried in lard and eaten hot. Today, this traditional preparation has changed very little. Ours are filled with ricotta cheese and sausage meat and make a moreish appetiser or snack with drinks.
  • Lightly grease cling film to prevent it from sticking to the dough during the resting and leavening stages.
  • Cover the remaining dough with cling film (or a damp cloth) during assembly to prevent the surface of the dough from drying out.
  • If preferred, cook the panzerotti in a preheated oven at 200°C for 20-30 minutes.
