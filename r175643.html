Persian Dill and Lima Bean Rice with Pomegranate

Ingredients

  • 10 g fresh dill, leaves only
  • 1000 g water
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 350 g basmati rice
  • 400 g frozen green baby lima beans
  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 30 g oil
  • 200 g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 50 g pomegranate seeds

Preparation

  1. Place dill in mixing bowl and chop 2 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and salt in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and add basmati rice to it. Set Varoma dish into position and weigh in green baby lima beans. Close Varoma with Varoma lid and steam 17 min/Varoma/speed 4. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C. Set Varoma aside, remove Varoma lid and allow beans to cool slightly (approx. 2 minutes).
  3. When cooked lima beans are cool enough to handle, pod and discard outer shells. Place podded lima beans back in Varoma dish. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer basmati rice to Varoma dish. Add reserved chopped dill to the rice and mix using spatula.
  4. Transfer 70 g hot water from mixing bowl to a small bowl. Add saffron threads and allow saffron to soak.
  5. Place ⅓ of the rice in a cake tin (Ø 25 cm x 10 cm), add half the saffron water and vegetable oil. Mix well with spatula, spread out the rice and bake for 10 minutes (200°C). Meanwhile, add feta cheese to remaining rice and mix with spatula.
  6. Scoop remaining rice out of Varoma dish and into cake tin, forming a pyramid shaped mound in middle of cake tin. Drizzle over remaining saffron water, cover with aluminium foil and bake for 10 minutes (200°C). Carefully transfer white rice to the middle of a serving dish. Arrange yellow rice around white rice. Garnish with pomegranate seeds and serve immediately.

Useful Items

aluminium foil, deep cake tin (Ø 25 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • In Iranian cooking, rice can be prepared simply with butter and saffron, known as chelo. But just as often, it’s cooked with other ingredients and then called polo. Polo can be made with herbs, vegetables, beans, nuts, dried fruit, meat and even noodles, and acts as the centrepiece of the meal. This polo is particularly good in the spring, when fava beans are young and tender and dill is in season. The dish is flecked with green dill and favas, and is often cooked with very tender chunks of lamb. Alternately, it may be served alongside lamb on the bone. The rice should have a mild saffron flavour, with the saffron mixed into the rice just before serving.
