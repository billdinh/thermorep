Deathly gravestone cake

Ingredients

Chocolate cake grave

  • unsalted butter, for greasing
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 70 g cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • 2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 300 g buttermilk
  • 100 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 100 g dark chocolate, broken into pieces (1 cm) (optional)

Headstone

  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 90 g marshmallows
  • 60 - 80 g Rice Bubbles®

Butter icing

  • 250 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 350 g icing sugar
  • 1 tbsp full-fat pouring (whipping) cream
  • natural food colouring (black - see Tips)

Assembly

  • lollies and decorations of choice

Preparation

Chocolate cake grave

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Grease a slice tin or lamington tin (20 x 30 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place flour, cocoa, cinnamon, cloves, bicarbonate of soda, baking powder, sugar and salt into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add eggs, buttermilk, oil and vanilla and mix 5 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 5 sec/speed 4
  5. Add chocolate (optional) and combine 5 sec//speed 3. Transfer batter into prepared cake tin and bake for 40-45 minutes (160°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 5 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack and cool completely (approx. 30 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Headstone

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 3 min/100°C/speed 2.
  2. Add marshmallows and melt 4 min/70°C/speed 2.
  3. Add Rice Bubbles® and combine 10 sec//speed 2.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and combine 10 sec//speed 2.
  5. Transfer mixture onto a silicone bread mat or work surface and allow mixture to cool slightly (approx. 5 minutes).
  6. Once cool enough to handle, place a piece of baking paper on top of mixture and, using a rolling pin, roll mixture into a rectangular shape (approx. 12 x 20 cm, 1-2 cm thickness).
  7. Remove top corners and use your hands to mould into a gravestone shape. Using a wooden skewer, gently push it through the centre of the grave stone lengthways (provides structural support), then cut off any visible parts of the skewer. Transfer gravestone into refrigerator to firm (minimum 10 minutes). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Butter icing

  1. Place butter into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add icing sugar and blend 1 min/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add cream and mix 2 min/speed 3, or until light and fluffy.  Transfer 3 tablespoons of the mixture into a small bowl. Add enough black food colouring to create black icing. Cover and set aside.
  4. Add a few drops of food colouring into mixing bowl and mix 10-20 sec/speed 3, to create grey icing. Add more food colouring as needed until desired colour is achieved. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 100°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Transfer cake onto a clean work surface, positioning short edge closest to you. Using a sharp knife, cut a slim rectangle from the top of the cake (1 cm wide, 2 cm from top and 2 cm in from each side) - this is where you will insert the gravestone.
  3. Using a sharp knife, cut a larger rectangular shape (14 x 22 cm, 1 cm deep) from the centre of the  cake, leaving a 3 cm edge on all sides.
  4. Using a spoon, gently scrape out the rectangle of cake and transfer onto prepared tray. Using your hands, crumble cake into pieces to make dirt.
  5. Place baking tray into oven and dehydrate for 30-35 minutes (100°C), stirring once or twice during baking, or until dry and crumbly. Set aside to cool completely.
  6. Frost headstone with a thin layer of grey icing. Transfer black icing into a piping bag with a thin nozzle. Pipe RIP across the top of the gravestone.
  7. Transfer cake onto a serving board or large platter. Insert gravestone into the thin gap at the top of the cake, and secure with toothpicks or skewers if needed. Frost edges of cake with grey icing. Fill cake with dirt and use lollies or decorations of choice to decorate cake.

Hints & Tricks

To make you own natural black food colouring. Place 70 g black liquorice into mixing bowl and chop 10 sec/speed 9. Add 100 g water and cook 8 min/80°C/speed 1. Strain mixture into a bowl and set aside to cool. Add several teaspoons of liquorice mixture to icing until desired colour is achieved.
