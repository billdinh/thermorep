Barley Nasi Goreng with Prawns

Ingredients

Pearl Barley and Eggs

  • 200 g pearl barley
  • 800 g water
  • 1 tsp groundnut oil
  • 4 medium eggs
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper

Nasi Goreng

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 stalk fresh lemon grass, white part only, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • 4 sprigs fresh coriander, cut in pieces
  • 120 g shallots, halved
  • 5 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 15 g groundnut oil
  • 200 g carrots, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 200 g red cabbage, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g cooked, peeled prawns
  • 20 g light soy sauce
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 spring onion, finely chopped, for garnishing
  • 2 Tbsp dried onion flakes, for garnishing
  • ½ cucumber, halved lengthways then thinly sliced, for serving
  • 2 tomatoes, cut in wedges, for serving
  • sriracha sauce, to taste (optional)

Preparation

Pearl Barley and Eggs

  1. Place barley and water in mixing bowl, boil 8 min/100°C//speed 1 then simmer 32 min/98°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, line Varoma tray with enough baking paper to go 2 cm up the sides and grease with oil. Insert Varoma tray then break eggs onto it. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Place covered Varoma into position (see tip) and steam 4 min/Varoma//speed 1. Set covered Varoma aside then drain barley in simmering basket. Rinse briefly under cold running water then cover with foil to keep warm and set aside. Rinse mixing bowl.

Nasi Goreng

  1. Place garlic, lemon grass, coriander, shallots, ginger and oil in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  2. Add carrots and cabbage then chop 2-4 sec/speed 4.5 until roughly chopped. Scrape down sides and lid of mixing bowl with spatula then sauté 3 min/100°C//speed 1.
  3. Add prawns, soy sauce, reserved cooked barley, salt and pepper, stir in with spatula then cook 5 min/100°C//speed  (see tip). Divide between serving plates (see tip) and top each with a reserved steamed egg. Garnish with spring onion and dried onion flakes before serving warm with cucumber, tomato and sriracha sauce (if using).

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • Whilst brown rice has more fibre than white rice, its GI is only slightly lower, both with a GI of around 50. This recipe substitutes the rice traditionally used in Nasi Goreng with pearl barley, which has much more fibre than white rice and has a GI approx. half that of rice.
  • In step 2, before placing Varoma into position, check the liquid in the barley. The starch can make the liquid become quite thick. If this happens, add 100-200 g boiling water before steaming the eggs.
  • In step 5, heating time will depend on the size of prawns used. 5 minutes will be enough for small prawns. If using larger prawns, prolong time as necessary until prawns are heated thoroughly.
  • For a nicer presentation, transfer nasi goreng to a bowl then invert onto a serving plate.
