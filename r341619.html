Flatbread (Manakish) with Za'atar

Ingredients

Za'atar

  • 20 g dried thyme
  • 15 g dried oregano
  • 15 g dried savory (see tip)
  • 10 g sumac (see tip)
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 10 g sesame seeds

Flatbread (Manakish)

  • 1 ½ tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 275 g water
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 50 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing

Preparation

Za'atar

  1. Place thyme, oregano, savory, sumac and salt in mixing bowl then grind 20 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add sesame seeds and mix 20 sec//speed 6. Transfer to an airtight jar and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Flatbread (Manakish)

  1. Place yeast, flour, water, sugar and salt in mixing bowl then knead 8 min/. Transfer dough to a floured surface and shape into a ball. Place in a bowl and leave to rise for 30 minutes at room temperature. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and grease 2 baking trays.
  2. Divide dough into 4 pieces then roll each piece into a disc (approx. Ø 20 cm). Arrange discs of dough on prepared baking trays and prick with a fork. Brush over 50 g oil and sprinkle with 1 Tbsp reserved za'atar (see tip).
  3. Leave flatbreads to rest for a further 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 200°C.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) until lightly browned.
  5. Leave to cool for a few minutes, cut in pieces and serve immediately.

Useful Items

rolling pin, baking trays, airtight jar, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Manakish are small Lebanese flatbreads flavoured with za'atar and enjoyed with a multitude of fillings.
  • Savory is a peppery herb, similar to thyme and sage. It can be bought from online retailers.
  • Sumac is a spice widely used in Turkey, the Lebanon, Syria, Armenia, Iran and also in Greece, where it is included in many dishes and is the essential ingredient for lamb, veal or beef kebabs. It is a fruity spice, with a delicate lemon flavour. It can be replaced with lemon or lemon balm in seafood or fish dishes. It can be used to flavour salads, poultry and meat balls, but also rice, vegetable and egg dishes, as well as bread. It makes an excellent sauce when mixed with yoghurt.
  • Sumac has a tendency to deteriorate when cooked. It is therefore preferable not to overcook it. It can also make food very salty. Therefore take care not to over-salt the mixture.
  • Za'atar is a thyme-based Middle Eastern spice mix. It is sometimes called Aleppo thyme. Like many spice mixes, the proportions vary from one grocer to another. However, za'atar is generally made up of thyme, oregano, savory, sesame seeds, sumac, marjoram and possibly green anise, coriander and cumin.
  • Any leftover za'atar can be stored in an airtight container and used to season chicken, beef or seafood before cooking or simply serve with bread and olive oil.
