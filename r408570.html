Cottage pie with purple mash

Ingredients

  • 80 g vintage cheddar cheese, cut into pieces
  • 500 g purple sweet potato, peeled and cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 300 g purple carrots, peeled and cut into pieces (see Tips)
  • 120 g liquid vegetable stock (or stock of choice - see Tips)
  • 40 g butter
  • 180 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 120 g celery stalks, cut into pieces
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 20 g tomato paste
  • 300 g ripe tomatoes, cut into quarters (see Tips)
  • 500 - 600 g beef mince
  • 150 g peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 5 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • fresh oregano, for garnishing (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place cheese into mixing bowl and grate 6 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place sweet potato and carrot into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add liquid stock and cook 15 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add butter and blend 1 min/speed 1-7, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 7, until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and place into refrigerator until ready to use. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Preheat oven to 200°C. Place onion, garlic and celery into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5.
  6. Add oil and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  7. Add tomato paste and tomatoes. Insert simmering basket, then weigh mince into it and cook 6 min/100°C//speed 2, without measuring cup.
  8. Using spatula, gently break up mince in simmering basket, then cook for a further 4 min/100°C//speed 2, without measuring cup. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer cooked mince into mixing bowl.
  9. Add peas and parsley into mixing bowl and stir through with spatula. Transfer beef mixture into an ovenproof dish (2.5 L capacity - see Tips). Top with reserved purple mash. Sprinkle over reserved grated cheese, then bake for 20 minutes (200°C) or until cheese melts and top is lightly golden. Serve sprinkled with fresh oregano (optional).

Useful Items

bowl, ovenproof baking dish, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you can’t source purple sweet potato or carrots, use white or orange sweet potatoes and/or regular orange carrots instead – your mash will simply be a different colour.
  • You can replace liquid stock with stock paste of choice and water in this recipe. Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the stock paste recipe. In step 3 replace liquid stock with 2 teaspoons stock paste and 120 g water.
  • If you like, make just the mash topping and serve it as a side dish with fish, steak or cutlets. It makes a delicious alternative to mashed potato.
  • Try to source properly ripe, naturally sweet tomatoes.
  • Instead of a large ovenproof baking dish, you can use 6 individual ovenproof dishes (approx. 450 mL capacity); you may need to reduce the baking time just a fraction.
