Mixed Seafood with Tomato and Herb Sauce

Ingredients

Tomato and Herb Sauce

  • ½ lemon, thinly peeled (peel only, no pith)
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 3 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only, plus extra to garnish
  • 15 fresh chives, cut into thirds, plus extra to garnish
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 ½ oz red onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 oz sun-dried tomatoes, drained of oil
  • 1 oz freshly squeezed lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 2 tsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • 3 ½ oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces (½ in.-1 in.)
  • 2 oz pitted Kalamata olives, sliced
  • 1 tbsp capers
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (approx. 14.5 oz)

Seafood

  • 32 oz fresh black mussels, scrubbed and debearded (see Tip)
  • 24 oz small littleneck clams, cleaned (see Tip)
  • 12 oz cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 1 oz toasted pine nuts, to garnish
  • crusty bread, to serve

Preparation

Tomato and Herb Sauce

  1. Place lemon peel into mixing bowl and grate 5 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all herbs, garlic, onion and sun-dried tomatoes and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add all remaining sauce ingredients and cook 5 min/212°F/speed 1. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.

Seafood

  1. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in mussels. Secure Varoma lid and steam 6 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  2. Weigh clams and cherry tomatoes into Varoma dish, secure Varoma lid and cook 6-8 min/Varoma/speed 1, or until mussels and clams open. Discard any unopened mussels and clams.
  3. Transfer seafood and cherry tomatoes into serving bowl and spoon sauce over seafood. Garnish with extra herbs and pine nuts and serve with crusty bread.

Hints & Tricks

  • To clean clams: examine all clams for cracks, chips or broken shells. Discard any clams with broken shells as the clam may be dead or unhealthy. Completely submerge clams in a bowl of cool, fresh water, then cover with a damp kitchen towel and refrigerate for approximately 20 minutes. As the clams are soaking they will naturally purge any salt, sand and particles. Drain clams, refresh water and submerge clams again. You can repeat this process 2-3 times if desired to ensure the clams are thoroughly purged. Once purged, scrub off any barnacles, sand or other debris which may be on clam shells.
    (Purchasing 32 oz of mussels leaves room for bad mussels to be removed, minimum amount required 26.5 oz. For clams, minimum amount required 16 oz.)
