Curried almonds

Ingredients

Curry powder

  • 1 tbsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 ½ tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 5 dried chillies
  • 1 tbsp rock salt
  • 2 tsp granulated garlic
  • 4 cardamom pods (see Tips)

Curried almonds

  • 1 egg white
  • 3 tsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 250 g raw almonds

Preparation

Curry powder

  1. Place all curry powder ingredients into mixing bowl and dry roast 8 min/Varoma/speed 2. Allow to cool then mill 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a sealable jar or container and set aside until ready to use. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.

Curried almonds

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg white into mixing bowl and whip 2 min/speed 4, or until soft peaks form. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add curry powder and sea salt and mix 6 sec/speed 2. Remove butterfly whisk.
  4. Add almonds and mix 4 sec//speed 2.
  5. Spread almonds onto prepared baking tray and bake for 12-15 minutes (180°C). Remove from oven and mix almonds on the baking tray to ensure they roast evenly. Return tray to the oven and bake for a further 5-6 minutes (180°C), checking the almonds regularly to ensure they do not burn.
  6. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely then store in a sealable container until ready to use.

Useful Items

sealable storage jar or container, baking tray (20 x 30 cm), baking paper, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These almonds are a great high protein snack or the perfect nibble with your beverage of choice.
  • Try roughly chopping some curried almonds to sprinkle over the top of the Spicy cauliflower soup.
  • You can substitute 4 cardamom pods with 1 tsp ground cardamom.
