Hawaiian Chicken Burger

Ingredients

Teriyaki Sauce

  • ½ oz ginger, peeled
  • 4 oz soy sauce
  • 2 oz water
  • 2 oz brown sugar
  • ½ oz honey
  • 2 tsp corn starch
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Pineapple

  • 8 pineapple slices, canned, drained

Patties

  • 2 oz red onions, cut into pieces
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1 in. pieces, partially frozen, divided
  • 2 oz bread crumbs
  • 4 hamburger buns, toasted
  • lettuce
  • red onions, sliced

Preparation

Teriyaki Sauce

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add soy sauce, water, brown sugar, honey, corn starch, sesame seeds and sesame oil and cook 6 min/212°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and allow to cool. Clean mixing bowl.

Pineapple

  1. Brush both sides of pineapple slices with reserved teriyaki sauce. Heat a cast iron pan over medium heat. Sear each side just until they begin to caramelize (approx. 4 min. each side). Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Patties

  1. Place onions and garlic into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 10 oz chicken and 1 oz bread crumbs and grind 10-12 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 10 oz chicken and 1 oz bread crumbs into mixing bowl and grind 10-12 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with reserved ground chicken mixture. Add 2 tbsp reserved teriyaki sauce to ground chicken and gently fold together with spatula.
  4. Divide and shape chicken mixture into four equal-sized patties and create a small dimple in the center by pressing down with your fingers to prevent them from rising while cooking. Cook patties over medium high heat on a grill or stovetop to taste (see Tip). Place on paper towels to drain.
  5. Brush chicken burgers and pineapple slices generously with teriyaki sauce and place between hamburger buns. Serve burgers hot, with lettuce and sliced red onions.

Useful Items

cast iron frying pan (approx. 10 in.), paper towels, large bowl, grill, thermometer

Hints & Tricks

  • Partially freeze chicken for up to 1 hour before grinding to ensure an even grind and to optimize texture.
  • Suggested internal temperature for ground chicken is 165°F.
  • For stovetop cooking: preheat cast iron skillet over medium high heat and grease with oil.
