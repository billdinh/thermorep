Soya Milk Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 300 g soya milk
  • 300 g whipping cream
  • 140 g sugar
  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • roasted soybean flour, to taste (see tip) (optional)
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
995 kJ / 237 kcal
Protein
4 g
Carbohydrates
28 g
Fat
13 g

Preparation

  1. Place soya milk, cream, sugar and egg yolks in mixing bowl then cook 8 min/90Â°C/speed 2. Pour into an ice cube tray and leave to cool slightly. Once cooled, cover with cling film and freeze for at least 10 hours.
  2. Place half the soya milk ice cubes in mixing bowl and crush 15 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, with aid of spatula, blend 30 sec/speed 6. Transfer to a serving bowl.
  3. Place remaining soya milk ice cubes in mixing bowl and crush 15 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, with aid of spatula, blend 30 sec/speed 6. Transfer to bowl with reserved ice cream. Serve with roasted soybean flour (if using) to taste.

Useful Items

ice cube tray, cling film, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Substitute soya milk with dairy-free milk of choice, such as almond milk or rice milk.
  • Roasted soybean flour, also know as kinako, is a finely-ground powder made from roasted soybeans. It has a warm, toasted and nutty aroma, slightly reminiscent of roasted peanuts. It can be found in oriental supermarkets or online.
  • Blackstrap molasses is the syrup produced after the third boiling of the juice from mature or green sugar cane plants. It is more bitter than "regular" molasses and has a rich, robust flavour.
  • Before serving, place ice cream in freezer for 15 minutes for a smoother texture.
