Cinnamon Panna Cotta with Apple, Apricot and Ginger Compote

Ingredients

Cinnamon Panna Cotta

  • 4 gelatine leaves (1.5 g each)
  • 110 g granulated sugar
  • 2 cm cinnamon stick
  • 480 g double cream, 48% butterfat
  • 250 g whole milk

Apple, Apricot and Ginger Compote

  • 15 g ginger, fresh root, sliced in 2 mm "coins"
  • 70 g apricots, soft dried
  • 600 g Cox apples, peeled, quartered, cores removed
  • 10 g sugar
  • 75 g water
  • 20 g butter, cubed (1 cm)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Preparation

Cinnamon Panna Cotta

  1. Place gelatine in a shallow bowl and cover with cold water to soften for 5 minutes. Continue with recipe while gelatine soaks.
  2. Place sugar and cinnamon stick in mixing bowl. Grind 40 sec/speed 10.
  3. Add double cream and milk, heat 4 min 30 sec/80°C/speed 3.
  4. Squeeze excess water from softened gelatine, add gelatine to mixing bowl and mix until fully dissolved 30 sec/speed 4. Pour into 6 individual ramekins and allow to cool 30 minutes before placing in refrigerator to set for 6 hours or overnight. Meanwhile clean mixing bowl and make the compote.

Apple, Apricot and Ginger Compote

  1. Place ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl then chop again 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add apricots and chop 4 sec/speed 6 then add Cox apples and sugar and chop 2 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add water and cook 12 min/90°C//speed 1 until apples are cooked but not puréed.
  4. Add butter and lemon juice, mix 20 sec//speed 1. Transfer compote to a small serving bowl and set aside to cool completely.
  5. When ready to serve, run a knife around the insides of the ramekins then immerse ramekin bottoms in a shallow bowl of very hot water for 1 or 2 seconds to help loosen the panna cottas. Turn out panna cottas and serve with the compote.

Useful Items

6 individual ramekins, small serving bowl, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This compote is a perfect complement to the creaminess of the panna cotta, and would also work well served with yoghurt or porridge for breakfast.

  • Serve the panna cotta with a fruit coulis or a different fruit compote of your choice.
  • For a vanilla panna cotta, remove cinnamon in step 1 and add seeds of 1 vanilla pod in step 2.
  • This deliciously spiced autumnal dessert is good for a dinner party as it can be made ahead.
