Pepper rasam

Ingredients

  • 1 cm cube fresh ginger, peeled
  • 6 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 35 g kithul Jaggery (optional - see Tips)
  • 60 g brown onion
  • 1 tsp ghee (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ - 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 750 g water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 tsp Tamarind paste (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place ginger, curry leaves, peppercorns, coriander, jaggery (optional) and onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place ghee, cumin and chilli into mixing bowl and cook 2 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until fragrant.
  4. Add water, salt, Tamarind paste and reserved ginger mixture and cook 7 min/100°C/speed 2. Divide between serving bowls or small drinking glasses and serve warm.

Hints & Tricks

  • Jaggery is a traditional raw cane sugar. It is a concentrated product of date, cane juice, or palm sap without separation of the molasses and crystals and is a healthier way to sweeten your foods. It may also act as a digestive aid.
  • To make your own ghee and Tamarind paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • Rasam is full of health benefits and is an integral part of south Indian meals and is more like a soup. It is often eaten in small amounts after meals to help aid digestion and to help with colds and flu. It can be eaten over plain rice, as a soup or added to main meals. If you prefer just the sour spiciness, omit the jaggery.
