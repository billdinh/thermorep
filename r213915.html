Falafel

Ingredients

  • 270 g dried fava beans (broad beans) (see Tips)
  • 90 g dried chickpeas
  • water, for soaking
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 300 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 10 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 - 2 tsp salt, to taste
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp plain flour
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • sunflower oil, for frying (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh dried fava beans (broad beans) and dried chickpeas into it. Cover with water and set aside to soak overnight. Drain beans and chickpeas and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, onion, fresh coriander, parsley, salt, pepper, flour, cumin, ground coriander, bicarbonate of soda, baking powder and drained beans and chickpeas into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 4-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 4 to speed 9, then blend for a further 30 sec/speed 4, with aid of spatula. Transfer into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for 1 hour.
  3. Place a deep saucepan over medium high heat and add oil (see Tips). Working in batches, fry heaped tablespoonfuls of falafel mixture for 3-4 minutes, until golden brown and crisp (makes approx. 35 pieces). Using a slotted spoon, carefully transfer to paper towel to drain. Serve warm (see Tips).

Useful Items

paper towel, deep saucepan, bowl, plastic wrap, slotted spoon, stove top, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use dried lima beans instad of fava beans (broad beans) in this recipe.
  • The amount of oil used for frying will depend on the size of the frying pan. You need enough oil to cover the falafel.
  • Serve with Tarator (tahini dip) or hummus. Please refer to the recipes in this collection.
