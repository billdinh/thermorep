Vegan Christmas cake

Ingredients

  • 700 g mixed dried fruit
  • 300 g brandy, plus extra for feeding (see Tips)
  • 200 g vegan butter, plus extra for greasing (see Tips)
  • 40 g boiling water
  • 1 tbsp flaxmeal (see Tips)
  • 200 g coconut sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp apple sauce
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 200 g wholemeal plain flour
  • 100 g slivered almonds
  • 150 g glacé cherries, cut into quarters
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp mixed spice
  • ¼ tsp salt

Preparation

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh mixed dried fruit and brandy into it, then set aside to soak overnight.
  2. Preheat oven to 140°C. Grease and line base and sides of a deep, round cake tin (20 cm) or a deep, square cake tin (18 cm). Line a baking tray with 2 layers of baking paper. Wrap 3 layers of baking paper around outside of tin (paper should be approx. 5 cm higher than the tin - see Tips). Wrap a piece of kitchen string around tin twice, then tie ends together securely. Place wrapped tin onto prepared tray.
  3. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and carefully weigh water into it. Add flaxmeal and set aside for 5 minutes.
  4. Place butter, sugar and vanilla into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 8.
  5. Add apple sauce, vinegar and reserved flaxmeal mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 3
  6. Add flour, almonds, glacé cherries, baking powder, cinnamon, mixed spice, salt and reserved mixed fruit, then combine 20 sec//speed 4, with aid of spatula.
  7. Transfer into prepared tin and bake for 2 hours 20 minutes (140°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool in tin for 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack and leave to cool.
  8. Once cool, use a wooden skewer to poke several holes in cake. Pour 2-3 tablespoons of the brandy over cake and allow to soak for 10 minutes. Wrap cake in aluminium foil (holes side up) and place into a sealable container. For a rich, moist cake, pour 2-3 tablespoons brandy over cake ("feeding" your cake) every week until ready to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

round cake tin (20 cm), square cake tin (18 cm), baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, wooden skewer, wire rack, aluminium foil, sealable container, kitchen string, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • For an alcohol-free Christmas cake, replace the brandy with black tea or orange juice. Proceed as per recipe. Cake without alcohol should be stored in the refrigerator and consumed within 1 month.
  • To make your own vanilla paste, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • To make your own vegan butter, refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • To mill your own flaxmeal, place 200 g flaxseeds (linseeds) into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Use as needed. Transfer any leftovers into a sealable container and store in the fridge or freezer for up to 3 months.
  • The baking paper lining is essential to prevent tin getting too hot and burning the cake. The more layers of baking paper used (on the baking tray and around the cake tin), the more evenly the cake will bake without drying out (and it's less likely the fruit will burn).
  • Cake will keep for up to 6 months in an airtight container or longer if stored in refrigerator.
  • For best result, "feed" your cake for 4 weeks before serving.
