Tomato Kasoundi

Ingredients

  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 3 fresh red chillies, deseeded
  • 25 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 100 g malt vinegar
  • 50 g grapeseed oil
  • 20 g black mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 720 g passata
  • 10 g fine sea salt
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 1 lime, finely grated zest only

Preparation

  1. Place garlic, chillies, ginger and vinegar in mixing bowl then chop 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.
  2. Place oil in mixing bowl and heat 2 min/120°C/speed .
  3. Add mustard seeds, turmeric, cumin and chilli powder then, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add reserved garlic mixture then, without measuring cup, cook 5 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add passata, salt, sugar and lime zest then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup on mixing bowl lid, cook 90 min/90°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 160°C. Place clean jars and lids in oven for 10 minutes (160°C).
  6. Pour hot mixture into hot jars and seal. Serve as a condiment to your main meal.

Useful Items

jars with screw-top lids, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Kasoundi can be served as a condiment with meats including chicken, lamb, sausages or hamburgers; strongly flavoured fish, fried eggs or baked potatoes; as a spicy relish on sandwiches; as a flavouring in fish or vegetable soups.
  • Sunflower oil or similar neutrally flavoured oil can be used instead of grapeseed oil.
  • Fresh, peeled and roughly chopped tomatoes (approx. 12 ripe Roma tomatoes) can be substituted for homemade or shop-bought passata. The fresh tomatoes do not need to be puréed as they will break down during cooking.
  • The cooking time can be further extended to make the kasoundi thicker.
