Duck Breasts with Orange Ginger Sauce, Mashed Potatoes and Green Beans

Ingredients

  • 40 g shallots, halved
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 20 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 60 g Seville orange marmalade
  • 1 pinch fresh thyme leaves
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 20 g balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g port
  • 550 g water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper, adjust to taste
  • 2 duck breasts, skinless (approx. 150-200 g each) (see tip)
  • 350 g floury potatoes, peeled, cut in pieces (1 cm)
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • 30 g butter
  • 120 g milk
  • 120 - 150 g fresh green beans, cut in pieces (2 cm) if necessary

Preparation

  1. Place shallots, garlic, ginger, marmalade, thyme, olive oil, balsamic vinegar, port, 50 g water, ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp black pepper in mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 10. Meanwhile, line Varoma tray with baking paper with enough to go 2 cm up the sides.
  2. Place duck breasts on lined Varoma tray and pour marinade over them. Insert Varoma tray into Varoma dish, cover and set aside to marinate. Clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place 500 g water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, add potatoes to it and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 50°C and warm a serving dish for the mashed potatoes.
  4. Set Varoma in position and steam 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma. Keeping baking paper lining intact, gently scrape marinade off duck breasts onto baking paper lining. Transfer duck breasts to a plate and set Varoma with marinade and duck juices aside for the sauce. Sprinkle duck breasts with sweet paprika, cover and keep warm in oven.
  5. Check that potatoes are completely cooked and tender (see tip). Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and discard steaming water. Insert butterfly whisk. Place cooked potatoes, butter, milk, remaining ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp black pepper in mixing bowl and mash 10-15 sec/speed 3.5 until smooth. Transfer to warmed serving dish and keep hot in oven. Clean mixing bowl.
  6. Pour reserved marinade and duck juices from Varoma tray into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket, add green beans to it and cook 14 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer sauce to a serving jug. Slice duck breasts diagonally and serve hot with mashed potatoes, orange ginger sauce and beans.

Useful Items

baking paper, serving jug, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The sauce accompanying the duck in this special dinner for two is exceptional – Seville orange marmalade gives it a slightly bitter edge which balances the sweetness and gingery heat. Feel free to use a normal orange jam or marmalade with the extra lemon juice if you can’t find a Seville variety. Marinating and steaming the duck makes it beautifully tender. Can serve four if required – see variations.
  • Replace green beans with vegetables of your choice in step 6, for instance thinly sliced carrots, halved Brussels sprouts, shredded savoy cabbage, sliced red peppers or pak choi. Place the vegetables that require the longest steaming time in the simmering basket first.
    - To serve 4, use 4 duck breasts, increase ingredients for marinade by 1½ times, and double the ingredients for mashed potatoes and beans. Water for steaming and the recipe instructions remain unchanged.
  • Potatoes must be cooked through and tender before mashing to create a creamy mash that is lump-free and not gluey. In step 4, cook potatoes 5-10 minutes longer if needed - cooking time depends on potato variety.
    - If you buy duck breasts with skin, they should be approx. 225-300 g each. Peel off skin and fat with the help of a small sharp knife before marinating duck.
    - Duck breasts can be marinated for up to 24 hours before steaming. In step 2, place duck and marinade in a sealed bag or covered container and refrigerate. When ready to cook, transfer duck and marinade to lined Varoma tray and continue with recipe.
