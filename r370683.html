Bourbon pork ribs

Ingredients

Spice rub

  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp mustard powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ - 1 tsp chilli powder, to taste
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 4 - 5 pork rib racks (approx. 2-3 kg total)

Bourbon BBQ glaze

  • 1 brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 - 4 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 375 g tomato sauce
  • 60 g bourbon
  • 120 g apple cider vinegar
  • 50 g brown sugar
  • 60 g soy sauce
  • 140 g honey
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Spice rub

  1. Place sugar, salt, mustard, paprika, smoked paprika, ginger, chilli powder, allspice and pepper into a bowl and stir to combine. Rub spice mixture over both sides of each rib rack. Cover ribs and transfer into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 2½ hours or overnight.
  2. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a large baking tray with baking paper. Place a wire rack onto lined tray.
  3. Place ribs, meat side up, onto wire rack. Set aside to come to room temperature (approx. 30 minutes). Cover with aluminium foil and cook for 1¼-1½ hours (180°C), until tender. Meanwhile continue with Bourbon BBQ glaze.

Bourbon BBQ glaze

  1. Place onion, garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add oil and cook 10 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add tomato sauce, bourbon, vinegar, sugar, soy sauce, honey, salt and pepper and cook 30 min/100°C/speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Remove simmering basket and insert measuring cup. Blend 20 sec/speed 4 gradually increasing from speed 4 to speed 7.
  5. Carefully remove ribs from oven. Brush a thick coating of glaze over ribs, cover with aluminium foil and continue to cook for a further 15-20 minutes (180°C), until glaze has caramelised and ribs are tender (see Tips). Serve as whole racks or cut into individual ribs.

Useful Items

large baking tray, baking paper, aluminium foil, wire rack, bowl, pastry brush, plate, plastic wrap, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can freeze any leftover barbecue sauce in a freezable container for up to 1 month. This sauce doesn't freeze solid so you can scoop out only the amount you need.
  • You can cook these ribs on the barbecue, if desired. After baking ribs in oven, remove and grill over low heat, while basting occasionally with sauce, until caramelised and slightly charred. If using a barbecue hot plate, lay baking paper down first so glaze does not burn too much.
