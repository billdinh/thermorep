Tapioca fruit cereal

Ingredients

  • 135 g small grain tapioca (see Tips)
  • 300 g water
  • 225 g coconut milk
  • 135 g almonds
  • 90 g pitted dates
  • 3 apples, cut into quarters and cores removed
  • 3 bananas, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 1 ½ - 2 ½ tbsp maple syrup, plus extra to serve
  • natural yoghurt, to serve

Preparation

  1. Insert simmering basket and weigh tapioca into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse tapioca well. Place a ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh water and coconut milk into it. Add rinsed tapioca to ceramic or glass bowl and leave to soak overnight.
  2. Place almonds and dates into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place apple into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add banana and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into the bowl with the almonds and dates and set aside.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place soaked tapioca (with coconut milk and water) into mixing bowl and cook 5 min/90°C//speed . Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Add maple syrup and reserved almond and fruit mixture and mix 10 sec/speed 4, or until fully combined.
  7. Serve with a spoonful of yoghurt per portion and extra maple syrup, to taste.

Hints & Tricks

  • Although often taken as the same thing, sago and tapioca come from completely different plants. Sago, often called 'Tapioca seed', is always a very small grain; whilst Tapioca can come as both a small grain or larger Tapioca 'pearls'. This recipe uses small grain Tapioca. Cooking times will vary for Tapioca pearls or sago.
  • Serve tapioca fruit cereal with additional sliced banana and mango on top if desired.
  • The key to cooking tapioca is to soak the grain for at least 6 hours or overnight prior to cooking. Once soaked through, the grain will have a porridge-like consistency.
