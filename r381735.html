Green pea cakes

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing if needed
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 140 g frozen peas, defrosted
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp almond meal
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only (or herb of choice)
  • 1 tbsp wholemeal self-raising flour
Nutrition
per 1 portion
Calories
144 kJ / 34.3 kcal
Protein
2.6 g
Carbohydrates
0.6 g

2.3 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line a 24-hole mini-muffin tray with silicone mini-muffin cases or grease well with oil. Set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add peas, 1 of the eggs, almond meal, oil and parsley and blend 10 sec/speed 8.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend for a further 10 sec/speed 8 or until mixture is smooth and well combined.
  5. Add flour and remaining 3 eggs and mix 10 sec/speed 5, until just combined. Transfer into prepared mini-muffin tray and bake for 10-12 minutes (160°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of each cake comes out clean. Set aside to cool in tray for 5 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

wooden skewer, wire rack, silicone mini muffin cases, 24-hole mini muffin tray, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Finger foods can be offered from six months onwards, but only if your baby can sit up unsupported and hold their head up straight (and be sure to avoid any choking hazards). You might like to start with softer options, such as pieces of avocado or steamed sweet potato, before progressing to slightly firmer foods like these pea cakes. By ten months, your baby should be given a wide variety of finger food options with plenty of different tastes and textures.
