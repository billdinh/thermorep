Indian-style fish with yoghurt sauce, potatoes and vegetables

Ingredients

  • 600 g water
  • 2 garlic cloves (approx. 5 g total)
  • ¼ - ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 50 g fresh coriander, plus extra to garnish
  • 100 g fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 600 g boneless, skinless, white fish fillets (e.g. halibut, haddock, cod; 1 cm thick)
  • 400 g potatoes, cut into small pieces (1 cm)
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 4 tsp chickpea (besan) flour (approx. 15 g, see Tips)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp freshly ground pepper, plus extra to taste
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 200 g carrots, cut into slices (1 cm thick)
  • 200 g broccoli, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 250 g plain Greek yoghurt

Preparation

  1. Place a small bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh 100 g of the water into it and set aside.
  2. Place garlic, cayenne pepper and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Remove 2 heaped tsp, add to small bowl with water and set aside to soak.
  3. Add spinach, olive oil and ½ tsp of the salt into mixing bowl and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix for a further 10 sec/speed 4 or until a coarse paste consistency is achieved.
  5. Pour reserved soaking mixture through simmering basket into another small bowl and set the spicy liquid aside. Return contents of simmering basket to mixing bowl. Spread half the spinach paste onto the middle of a large piece of baking paper (30 cm x 45 cm), place fish on top and cover with remaining spinach paste. Fold up paper and twist ends to make a closed parcel. Place butterfly whisk flat into Varoma dish and place fish parcel on top of whisk, to ensure some holes remain unobstructed.
  6. Place remaining 500 g water into mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and weigh potatoes into it. Place Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and steam 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Meanwhile, add turmeric, chickpea flour, sugar, remaining ½ tsp salt, pepper and lemon juice to reserved spicy liquid in small bowl, and arrange carrots and broccoli onto Varoma tray.
  7. Carefully remove Varoma lid and insert Varoma tray. Secure Varoma lid and steam 8 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  8. Carefully remove Varoma lid and tray and set aside to check that fish and vegetables are cooked - fish should flake easily. If needed, steam for a further 2-5 min/Varoma/speed 1. Transfer potatoes, vegetables and fish parcel onto a warm serving dish and keep warm while making the sauce. Discard steaming water.
  9. Place Greek yoghurt and reserved spicy liquid into mixing bowl and cook 8 min/90°C/speed 3. Adjust seasoning as desired and serve fish, potatoes and vegetables with sauce, garnishing with a few fresh coriander leaves.

Useful Items

baking paper, small bowl, serving dish

Hints & Tricks

  • To make chickpea flour, grind 100 g dried chickpeas 3 min/speed 10 and store extra in a sealable jar to use in future recipes.
    - To serve two people, halve all ingredients except steaming water. Insert Varoma tray and cook for 6 minutes instead of 8 minutes.
    - If fresh fish is not available, use frozen fish which has been thawed.
  • Replace carrots and broccoli with vegetables of your choice with similar steaming times.
    - Omit coriander and replace with extra spinach.
