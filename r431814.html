Preserved lemon and desert vegetable medfouna

Ingredients

  • 6 pieces preserved lemon, rind only, no white pith, 2 cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp coarse semolina
  • 2 tbsp poppy seeds
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 250 g water
  • 80 g milk
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 160 g wholemeal plain flour
  • 300 g plain flour, plus extra if needed
  • 1 tsp salt, plus an extra 1-2 pinches
  • 300 g zucchini, cut into pieces
  • 400 g brown onion, cut into eighths
  • 100 g extra virgin olive oil, plus an extra one tablespoon
  • 1 tomato, cut into halves
  • 460 g ricotta
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 6 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 100°C. Line 2 baking trays (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set one aside. Place 4 pieces of the preserved lemon onto a baking tray and bake for 3 hours (100°C) or until completely dried out. Remove from oven and set aside to cool completely.
  2. Place semolina, poppy seeds and coriander seeds into mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 10. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water, milk, sugar and yeast into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/37°C/speed 2.
  4. Add flours and 1 teaspoon of the salt and knead 3 min/. Leave dough in mixing bowl for 20 minutes to rest.
  5. Knead dough 3 min/, adding extra flour as needed if the mixture is sticky. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or a lightly floured work surface. Divide dough into 4 portions, then cover with a damp kitchen towel and set aside to rise until almost doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  6. Preheat oven to 200°C
  7. Place zucchini into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  8. Place onion and 100 g of the oil into mixing bowl and cook 20 min/120°C//speed 2 or until caramelised.
  9. Add tomato and remaining 2 pieces preserved lemon peel and cook 5 min/120°C//speed 2, without measuring cup.
  10. Add ricotta, paprika, coriander, parsley, pepper, 1-2 pinches salt and reserved zucchini, then mix 5 sec//speed 4.
  11. Dust silicone bread mat or work surface with reserved semolina mixture. Divide each dough portion into halves. Roll out 4 halves into circles (18 cm), then roll the remaining 4 halves into slightly larger circles (20 cm). Place one-quarter of the vegetable filling onto each of the larger circles, leaving some room around the edge. Place the smaller circle on top, fold edges over and press together to seal.
  12. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon oil. Working in batches, fry medfouna for 3 minutes each side. Transfer onto baking trays and bake for 10 minutes (200°C), until golden. Sprinkle each medfouna with 1 tablespoon of the reserved lemon powder just before serving.

Useful Items

baking trays (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, bowl, silicone bread mat, large frying pan, rolling pin, kitchen towel, oven, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • Medfouna is a traditional Moroccan dish typically made with lamb and beef, but this version uses a vegetable filling.
