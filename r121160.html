Nectarine and Raspberry Ice Cream Sandwiches

Ingredients

Ice Cream

  • 500 g whole milk
  • 170 g caster sugar
  • 8 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only
  • 100 g double cream
  • 350 g nectarines, ripe, stoned, diced (2 cm) then frozen for at least 6 hours
  • 150 g raspberries, roughly chopped

Biscuits

  • 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 125 g butter, chilled, diced
  • 75 g cocoa powder
  • 125 g caster sugar
  • 2 Tbsp golden syrup
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 3 - 5 Tbsp milk

Preparation

Ice Cream

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place milk, sugar, egg yolks and vanilla seeds in mixing bowl then cook 15 min/80°C/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk .
  2. Add cream and blend 30 sec/speed 5. Pass through a fine sieve into a jug. Pour into ice cube trays and freeze for at least 6 hours until solid.
  3. Line a deep baking tray (24 cm x 24 cm x 4 cm) with a triple layer of cling film. Place in freezer to chill.
  4. Once solid, place frozen ice cream cubes and frozen nectarines in mixing bowl and crush 1 min/speed 6 while stirring with spatula through hole in mixing bowl lid. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then churn 1 min/speed 6 until smooth.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Add chopped raspberries and whip 30 sec/speed 4. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer to chilled tray, spread out evenly then freeze until solid. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe.

Biscuits

  1. Place flour, butter, cocoa, sugar, golden syrup and bicarbonate of soda in mixing bowl then mix 30 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula. Mix 40 sec/speed 2 while adding milk through hole in mixing bowl lid until mixture comes together. Tip out onto a large piece of cling film, form into a ball then chill in fridge for 1 hour. Meanwhile, line 2 baking trays with baking paper.
  2. On a lightly floured work surface, roll dough out to 0.5 cm thickness. Cut out biscuits (3 cm x 8 cm) and place, 4 cm apart, on prepared trays. Use a metal skewer or fork to prick holes on each biscuit. Place trays in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  3. Once chilled, bake biscuits for 20-25 minutes (180°C) then place on a wire rack to cool completely.
  4. When ready to serve, remove ice cream from tray, using clingfilm. Place biscuits on frozen ice cream and cut out rectangular shapes of ice cream. Place another biscuit on other side to form a sandwich then serve immediately.

Useful Items

ice cube trays, fine sieve, jug, deep baking tray (24 cm x 24 cm x 4 cm), cling film, 2 baking trays and baking paper, skewer, biscuit cutters, rolling pin, wire rack, freezer, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These are delicious and fun. They sum up the best of summer flavours. You can make ice cream sandwiches all year round if you wish - omit the nectarines and raspberries for flavours in season, and of your choice, for year-round enjoyment.
