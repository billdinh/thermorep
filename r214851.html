Banana Cream Pie

Ingredients

Pie Crust

  • 120 g unsalted butter, chilled, diced
  • 60 g vegetable shortening, chilled, diced
  • 280 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 6 - 8 Tbsp water, cold
  • unsalted butter, for greasing

Banana Filling

  • 760 g bananas, sliced
  • 30 g orange juice (approx. 1 orange)
  • 60 g unsalted butter, diced
  • 680 g single cream
  • 140 g caster sugar
  • 6 egg yolks, from large eggs
  • 4 Tbsp cornflour
  • 60 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt

Pie Topping

  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 450 g whipping cream
  • ¼ tsp vanilla extract
  • 130 g bananas, sliced (1 cm) (approx. 1 banana)
  • 15 g orange juice (approx. ½ orange)

Preparation

Pie Crust

  1. Place butter, shortening, flour, sugar, salt and cold water in mixing bowl then chop 25-30 sec/speed 5. Tip out, form into a ball, flatten and wrap in cling film. Refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Adjust oven rack to lower middle position then preheat oven to 190°C. Grease a tart tin (Ø 22 cm) and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Roll out rested pie dough between 2 sheets of cling film into a circle (approx. Ø 33 cm, 0.5 cm thick). Line prepared tart tin with pie dough and prick with a fork to avoid air bubbles. Chill in fridge for 15 minutes. Line chilled pie dough with baking paper and baking beans then blind bake for 30 minutes (190°C) until pie dough looks dry and lightly golden. Remove paper and beans then bake for a further 10-12 minutes (190°C) until golden brown. Transfer tart tin to a wire rack and cool completely (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, make banana filling.

Banana Filling

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in bananas. Weigh in orange juice, gently toss with bananas then set aside.
  2. Place bananas and butter in mixing bowl then cook 2 min/100°C//speed .
  3. Add cream and cook 4 min/85°C//speed . Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool (approx. 15 minutes). Strain banana from cream through a fine mesh sieve, collecting cream in a bowl below (do not press on banana). Set banana aside (see tip).
  4. Place sugar, egg yolks, cornflour, butter, vanilla, salt and reserved cooked cream in mixing bowl then cook 6 min/85°C/speed 4. Transfer half banana filling to bottom of cooled pie crust and spread out evenly. Place reserved banana slices on banana filling and top with remaining banana filling. Allow to cool slightly. Cover with cling film, pressing directly onto surface of filling to avoid a "skin" from forming. Refrigerate while making pie topping. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pie Topping

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 10.
  2. Insert butterfly whisk. Add whipping cream and vanilla then, without setting a time, whip at speed 3 until desired stiffness is achieved, watching carefully to avoid overwhipping. Place whipped cream in middle of banana pie and spread out with spatula. Use the back of a spoon to make attractive swirls and peaks, then cover carefully with cling film and refrigerate for 2-5 hours or until set.
  3. When ready to serve, place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in bananas. Weigh in orange juice and gently toss with bananas. Garnish pie with banana slices and serve immediately.

Useful Items

rolling pin, cling film, baking paper, baking beans, tart tin (Ø 22 cm), bowl, fine mesh sieve, wire rack, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Blind baking is simply baking your unbaked pie crust before adding the filling so that the bottom doesn't puff and the sides don't slouch. You can buy special pie weights for this job, but you can also just use dry beans (approx. 680g).
  • Handle dough as little as possible to keep it chilled and easy to work with. Work quickly with cold ingredients.
  • If dough becomes too soft to work with chill until firm.
  • Reserved banana can be used in a milkshake or smoothie.
