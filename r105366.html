Ricotta and polenta chips

Ingredients

Ricotta

  • 1800 g full cream milk
  • 80 g white vinegar

Polenta

  • 20 g Parmesan cheese, crust removed and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 480 g water
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 150 g polenta (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp olive oil, plus extra for baking
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Preparation

Ricotta

  1. Place milk into mixing bowl and heat 10 min/80°C/speed 2.
  2. Add vinegar and heat 1 min/90°C/speed 1. Allow to sit in mixing bowl for 1 minute. Milk will separate into curds and whey.
  3. Line simmering basket with a nut milk bag or 2 layers muslin cloth and set over a large bowl. Pour mixture into simmering basket and allow to drain for 30 minutes. Do not squeeze liquid from ricotta. Set drained ricotta (approx. 300-340 g) aside in a bowl. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Polenta

  1. Line a square cake tin (20 cm) with plastic wrap and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan cheese into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer into a seperate bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl and bring to the boil 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add polenta and cook 15 min/100°C/speed 2.
  5. Add oil, thyme, paprika, pepper and 250 g of the reserved ricotta and mix 10 sec/speed 5.
  6. Spoon polenta mixture into prepared tin, smooth surface and place into refrigerator to set (approx. 30 minutes).
  7. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  8. Remove set polenta from tin and slice into thick chips (2 x 9 cm). Place onto prepared baking tray. Sprinkle with reserved Parmesan cheese, drizzle with extra olive oil and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) until cheese has melted and chips are golden brown. Serve warm.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, nut milk bag or muslin cloth, plastic wrap, square cake tin (20 cm), baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • This recipe uses coarser cornmeal polenta, not instant polenta.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
