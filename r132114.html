Avial (vegetables in yoghurt)

Ingredients

Vegetables

  • 100 g fresh coconut flesh (see Tips)
  • 3 fresh long green chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • ¾ tsp cumin seeds
  • 300 g water
  • 140 g natural yoghurt
  • 2 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 2 tsp Tamarind paste (see Tips)
  • 1 - 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 100 g potato, cut into batons (1 x 5 cm)
  • 100 g sweet potato, cut into batons (1 x 5 cm)
  • 200 g carrot, cut into batons (1 x 5 cm)
  • 200 g fresh green beans, trimmed and cut into halves

Tempering/Seasoning

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 5 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds

Preparation

Vegetables

  1. Place coconut, green chillies, cumin seeds, 50 g of the water and 80 g of the yoghurt into mixing bowl and blend 20 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 20 sec/speed 8 or until smooth. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place garam masala, turmeric, Tamarind paste, salt and remaining 250 g water into mixing bowl and heat 5 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  4. Add vegetables and cook 5-7 min/100°C//speed  or until vegetables are cooked to your liking, but still crisp.
  5. Add all of the reserved coconut paste and remaining 60 g yoghurt and cook 3 min/90°C//speed . Transfer vegetables into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tempering/Seasoning

  1. Place coconut oil into mixing bowl and heat 3 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Add curry leaves and mustard seeds and cook 1 min/Varoma/speed 1 or until they begin to pop. Pour over vegetables and stir well to combine, then serve immediately.

Useful Items

bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl

Hints & Tricks

Avial is a popular and much loved dish in Kerala. It's often served at weddings or as part of a festive menu.

  • Fresh coconut can be replaced with frozen grated coconut - both can be found at Asian and Indian food stores.
  • To make your own garam masala and Tamarind paste, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
