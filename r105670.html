Black forest cake

Ingredients

Filling

  • 300 g frozen cherries, pitted and cut into halves
  • 80 g Kirsch (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 50 g water
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Cake

  • butter, for greasing
  • 150 g plain flour
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 30 g cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • 100 g water
  • 90 g vegetable oil
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 3 egg whites

Assembly

  • 60 g dark chocolate, plus extra, shaved, for garnishing
  • 800 g pouring (whipping) cream
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 80 g Kirsch

Preparation

Filling

  1. Place a large ceramic or glass bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cherries into it. Remove 12 cherry halves and set aside (to use for decorating cake). Weigh Kirsch into bowl on mixing bowl lid with cherries. Set aside to macerate at room temperature for 2 hours.
  2. Insert simmering basket, then place macerated cherries with liquid into it, allowing liquid to drain into mixing bowl. Remove simmering basket, with cherries, with aid of spatula and set aside.
  3. Add sugar, water and vanilla into mixing bowl and cook 23-25 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup, until a thick syrup consistency is achieved. Add reserved drained cherries.
  4. Transfer cherry syrup mixture into a bowl and set aside to cool while preparing cake. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a springform cake tin (20 cm), line bottom with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place flour, sugar, cocoa powder, salt and 2 teaspoons of the baking powder into mixing bowl and stir 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place water into mixing bowl and heat 2 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add oil, eggs yolks and vanilla and beat 15 sec/speed 5.
  5. Add reserved cocoa mixture and combine 4 sec/speed 4, until just mixed.
  6. Place egg whites and remaining 1 teaspoon of the baking powder into a small bowl and whisk with a fork until foamy and baking powder has dissolved.
  7. Mix 20 sec/speed 3, slowly adding egg white mixture through hole in mixing bowl lid until combined, taking care not to over whip.
  8. Transfer into prepared cake tin and bake for 35 minutes (170°C), or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Transfer cake onto a wire rack and allow to cool (approx. 30 min). Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  9. Slice cooled cake into three equal layers and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Place chocolate into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Insert buttlerfly whisk. Place cream, sugar and vanilla into mixing bowl and whip 1 min/speed 4, or until stiff peaks form. Remove butterfly whisk. Transfer cream mixture into a bowl and set aside.
  3. Transfer bottom layer of the cake onto a serving platter and sprinkle with 30 g of the Kirsch. Spread half of the reserved cherries with syrup onto cake and top with a quarter of the reserved cream mixture, spreading to edges of cake. Top cream mixture with half of the reserved grated chocolate. Place second layer of cake onto filling and sprinkle with 30 g of the Kirsch, remaining reserved cherries with syrup and another quarter of the reserved cream mixture. Top cream with remaining grated chocolate. Place final layer of cake on top and sprinkle with remaining 20 g of the Kirsch, then evenly spread remaining cream mixture over top and sides of cake. Decorate edges of cake with reserved cherry halves. Garnish top of cake with extra shaved chocolate and place into refrigerator for a minimum of 1 hour before serving.

Useful Items

large ceramic or glass bowl, bowl, springform cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, small bowl, springform cake tin (20 cm), wooden skewer, wire rack, serving platter, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • You can use either Kirsch or cherry brandy in this recipe.
  • Jarred sour cherries or Morello cherries can be used instead of frozen cherries.
  • Kirsch is a clear, colourless fruit brandy made by distilling cherries. Unlike cherry brandy, Kirsch is not sweet. Omit or reduce the amount of Kirsch used in this recipe to suit your taste.
