Supreme Plant Poke Bowl

Ingredients

Marinated Edamame Beans

  • 100 g edamame beans, shelled
  • 1 ½ Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ¼ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 lime, juice only
  • 1 tsp honey

Rice and Vegetables

  • 1000 g water
  • 100 g quinoa
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 450 g butternut squash, diced (1 cm)
  • 100 g pak choi, cut in lengths (2 cm wide)
  • oil, for frying
  • 10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced

Preparation

  1. Cook edamame beans according to packet instructions. Allow to cool then prepare marinade.
  2. Place sesame oil, soy sauce and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add chilli flakes, lime juice and honey then mix 20 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a large bowl and add cooled edamame beans, stirring to coat beans in marinade. Set aside and prepare vegetables.
  4. Place water in mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh in quinoa and salt.
  5. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh in butternut squash. Rinse 20 sec/speed 4 then steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  6. Carefully remove Varoma lid, insert Varoma tray and weigh in pak choi then steam 7 min/Varoma/speed 2. Transfer pak choi to large bowl and stir to coat with marinade. Set Varoma dish aside.
  7. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer cooked quinoa to 4 bowls. Set aside to cool for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Meanwhile, heat oil in a frying pan over a high heat. Fry reserved steamed butternut squash until slightly crispy on the outside, turning regularly to avoid burning (approx. 10 minutes).
  8. Divide cooked butternut squash, pak choi, avocado, cherry tomatoes and chopped spring onions between bowls. Top with marinated edamame beans and marinade then serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, bowl, frying pan, serving bowls, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • This vegetarian Poke bowl breaks tradition with edamame beans being used to provide protein instead of sushi grade tuna.
  • Mix and match with your favourite vegetables.
