Tandoori chicken

Ingredients

Tandoori paste

  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 ½ tbsp cumin seeds
  • 6 cardamom pods (black)
  • 10 whole cloves
  • 2 tbsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp ground turmeric
  • 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 60 g brown sugar
  • 120 g tomato paste
  • 120 g garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 80 g ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g olive oil, plus extra for preserving

Tandoori chicken

  • water, for soaking
  • oil, for greasing
  • 800 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • ½ lemon, juice only
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 200 g natural yoghurt
  • 30 g ghee (see Tips)
  • fresh mint leaves, for garnishing
  • lemon wedges, to serve

Preparation

Tandoori paste

  1. Place all Tandoori paste ingredients into mixing bowl and purée 25 sec/speed 9 or until smooth. Transfer into a sealable sterilised jar or container and cover with extra oil to preserve. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Tandoori chicken

  1. Place 6 wooden skewers into a large bowl of water to soak for 30 minutes. If using metal skewers, coat skewers with oil. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with aluminium foil and set aside.
  2. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chicken into it. Add lemon juice and toss to combine. Add salt and pepper to bowl and rub evenly over chicken. Weigh yoghurt and 120 g of the reserved Tandoori paste into bowl, then coat chicken in marinade. Thread chicken onto skewers and place onto prepared baking tray. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum of 20 minutes or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat grill to high.
  4. Place ghee into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/Varoma/speed 1. Drizzle over chicken skewers, then place skewers under grill and cook for 8 minutes, turning every 3-4 minutes until chicken is cooked through and edges are slightly blackened. Garnish with mint and serve with lemon wedges and your favourite chutney (see Tips).

Useful Items

sealable sterilised jar, large bowl, 6 wooden skewers, metal skewers, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), aluminium foil, plastic wrap, bowl, refrigerator, oven grill

Hints & Tricks

Tandoori roast chicken: Place a whole chicken (approx 2.5 kg) into a sealable container. Make thin cuts over chicken (thighs, breasts or drumsticks). Rub juice from ½ lemon and 1 teaspoon salt over chicken. Combine 160 g of the tandoori paste with approx. 200 g yoghurt. Using your hands, massage marinade into chicken, ensuring it gets into cuts. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator for a minimum 4 hours or overnight.

Preheat oven to 200°C. Place chicken into a roasting pan and reduce oven temperature to 180°C. Roast chicken for approx. 1 hour 50 minutes (180°C) or until cooked (approx. 20 minutes per 500 g of chicken), plus an additional 15 minutes.

Traditionally cooking in a tandoor/traditional clay oven (where the temperature is higher than any regular oven) helps chicken cook quickly, while still keeping it tender and succulent with a smoky flavour. Our take on this delicious dish cooks the marinated chicken under the grill, which helps keep it juicy while cooking quickly.

  • To make you own garlic paste, ginger paste and ghee, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • You can store Tandoori paste in the refrigerator for up to 4 weeks or place into ice cube trays and store in the freezer for up to 3 months.
  • The tandoori marinade (yoghurt and tandoori paste) also works well with fish and lamb.
  • We served our Tandoori chicken with Mint and peanut chutney. Please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
