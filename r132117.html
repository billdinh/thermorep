Beef madras

Ingredients

  • 100 g boiling water
  • 100 g fresh coconut flesh, grated (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 30 g tamarind paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g canned tomatoes
  • 200 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed,1 cut into thin slices, for garnishing
  • 1 tbsp ghee (see Tips)
  • 10 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp Madras powder (see Tips)
  • 750 - 800 g chuck beef, cut into cubes (2 cm)
  • 1 tsp salt

Preparation

  1. Place water and coconut flesh into mixing bowl and set aside to soak for 20 minutes.
  2. Add ginger paste, tamarind paste and tomatoes into mixing bowl and blend 1 min/speed 7. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  3. Place onion, 1 of the chillies, ghee and 5 of the curry leaves into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula. Add garlic paste and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  6. Add turmeric, chilli powder and Madras powder and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 1 , without measuring cup. Scrape down sides and bottom of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add beef, salt, remaining 5 curry leaves and reserved coconut milk mixture and cook 40-50 min/90°C//speed 1, until beef is tender. Transfer into serving bowls and garnish with sliced red chillies. Serve warm. 

Useful Items

Hints & Tricks

  • You can buy fresh or frozen grated coconut flesh from most Asian and Indian food stores.
  • To make your own ginger paste, tamarind paste, ghee, garlic paste and Madras powder please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can serve this with rice, bread, chutney or your favourite side. We served ours with Garlic and coriander naan (please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip) and rice.
