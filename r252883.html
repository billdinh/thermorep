Frangipane Cake

Ingredients

Sweet Shortcrust Pastry

  • 50 g sugar
  • 100 g butter, chilled, diced
  • 230 g plain flour
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Frangipane Cake

  • butter, for greasing
  • plain flour, for dusting
  • 150 g roasted unsalted almonds
  • 50 g caster sugar
  • 150 g butter, diced
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 40 g blanched almonds
  • 50 g plain flour
  • 100 g sugar
  • 250 g apricot jam, smooth
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Preparation

Sweet Shortcrust Pastry

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add all remaining pastry ingredients and mix 20-25 sec/speed 5. Tip out onto work surface and bring together into a flattened ball. Wrap in cling film, then transfer to fridge to rest for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, clean and dry mixing bowl.

Frangipane Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a spring form cake tin (26 cm) with butter, then dust lightly with plain flour.
  2. Once pastry has rested, roll out between 2 sheets of baking paper to 5 mm thickness, then transfer into prepared cake tin. Prick the bottom of the pastry with a fork to prevent air bubbles and rising. Cut around the edge of the pastry leaving a 1.5 cm border of pastry around the bottom of the tin. Place cake tin with pastry into freezer for another 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from freezer and line pastry with a piece of baking paper, fill pastry with baking beans. Bake for 10-13 minutes (180°C) or until slightly golden. Remove baking paper and baking beans then bake for a further 3-5 minutes or until golden brown. Remove from oven and set aside to cool, reduce oven temperature to 160°C.
  4. Place roasted almonds and 50 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind 8 sec/speed 7. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Without rinsing mixing bowl, place butter in mixing bowl and warm 1 min/37°C/speed 1.
  6. Add eggs, vanilla, yeast, blanched almonds, plain flour, remaining 100 g sugar and the reserved almond mixture then mix 10 sec/speed 6.
  7. Place spring form tin with pastry onto mixing bowl lid, then weigh apricot jam into it. Remove from lid then spread jam evenly over pastry base in tin. Pour over cake batter in mixing bowl.
  8. Bake for 30 minutes (160°C), or until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean. Remove cake from oven and leave to cool in tin.
  9. Once cooled, remove from tin, dust with icing sugar and serve.

Useful Items

cling film, spring form tin (Ø 26 cm), baking paper, rolling pin, baking beans, skewer, refrigerator, oven, freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Decorate the top of your cake with fresh seasonal fruits before dusting with the icing sugar and serving.
