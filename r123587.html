Lentil and Pumpkin Pot Pies

Ingredients

Rosemary Ice Puff Pastry

  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (approx. 10 cm long), leaves only
  • 90 g ice cubes
  • 180 g unsalted butter, frozen and cut into pieces
  • 200 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt

Pumpkin and Lentil Filling

  • butter, for greasing
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 150 g), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 1 carrot (approx. 250 g), thinly sliced into rounds
  • ¼ tsp dried rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • ¼ tsp dried fennel seeds
  • 150 g red lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 460 g water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g pumpkin, cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • whole milk, for brushing

Preparation

Rosemary Ice Puff Pastry

  1. Place rosemary in mixing bowl and chop 20 sec/speed 8. Rosemary should be a very fine consistency. Repeat chopping if necessary.
  2. Add ice and butter then chop 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add flour and salt then combine 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then knead 2 min/. Wrap in cling film and place in refrigerator for 30 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Pumpkin and Lentil Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Grease 6 ramekins (8 cm) with butter and set aside.
  2. Place onion and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add 20 g  oil and sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add carrot and remaining 20 g oil then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add rosemary, thyme, fennel seeds, lentils, water and vegetable stock paste. Insert simmering basket then weigh pumpkin into it. Cook 15 min/Varoma//speed 1. Transfer lentil and pumpkin mixture into a large bowl and stir to combine.
  6. Fill ramekins with lentil and pumpkin mixture, ensuring the mixture comes ¾ of the way up the sides of ramekins.
  7. Place pastry onto a silicone bread mat or floured surface, then place a sheet of baking paper over pastry and roll out to a 5 mm thickness.
  8. Using a pastry cutter, cut out 6 pastry rounds (9 cm), ensuring the circle has a 1 cm border overlapping the edge of the ramekin. Place a pastry circle over ramekin and seal edges by pressing down against the side of the ramekin. Repeat with remaining pies.
  9. Brush milk on the top of each pie and bake for 30 minutes (200°C), or until golden brown. Serve hot with a green salad.

Useful Items

cling film, 6 ramekins (8 cm, 140 ml), large bowl, silicone bread mat, rolling pin, baking paper, pastry cutter, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven
