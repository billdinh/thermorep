Prawn And Chicken Laksa

Ingredients

  • 1 stalk fresh lemon grass, white part only, cut in pieces
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 8 macadamia nuts
  • 1 - 2 bird's eye chillies, fresh, to taste, plus extra for garnish
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 5 cm fresh root ginger, peeled
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, plus extra for garnish
  • 1 tsp shrimp paste
  • 450 g coconut milk
  • 20 g butter
  • 150 g water
  • 2 tsp chicken stock paste (see tips)
  • 8 fresh kaffir lime leaves, bruised
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • 2 tsp lime juice
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt, to taste
  • 200 g dried rice noodles (approx. 3 mm wide)
  • boiling water
  • 200 g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, cubed (2 cm)
  • 300 g prawns, raw, peeled, deveined
  • 180 g pak choi, cut in pieces
  • 100 g fresh bean sprouts, for garnishing

Preparation

  1. Place lemongrass, onion, garlic, macadamia nuts, chilli, curry powder, ginger, turmeric, cumin, coriander, shrimp paste and 50 g coconut milk in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and repeat blending 10 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add butter and cook 8 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Add remaining 400 g coconut milk, water, stock paste, lime leaves, fish sauce, lime juice and salt then cook 15 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid, then weigh in noodles. Cover noodles with boiling water and set aside to cook.
  5. Add chicken to mixing bowl and cook 7 min/100°C//speed 1.
  6. Add prawns and pak choi then cook 4-5 min/100°C//speed 1, or until prawns are cooked through. Carefully remove kaffir lime leaves using a slotted spoon.
  7. Drain rice noodles through simmering basket and divide noodles evenly between 4 serving bowls. Pour laksa over the top.
  8. Garnish with bean sprouts, extra sliced chilli and extra coriander leaves before serving.

Useful Items

serving bowls, slotted spoon, bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • To prepare coriander roots for use in the curry paste, remove all the fibrous small brown off-shoots from the main root by scraping with a teaspoon.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook for the chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Shrimp paste, also known as belachan, can be found in the International aisle of most supermarkets or specialty Asian grocers.
