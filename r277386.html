Smoked salmon and preserved lemon choux buns

Ingredients

Salmon and ricotta filling

  • 100 g smoked salmon
  • 200 g ricotta
  • 1 ½ pieces preserved lemon, rind only, rinsed and chopped (approx. 1 tsp chopped)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill, leaves only
  • ground black pepper, to taste

Choux pastry

  • 50 g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into pieces (2 cm)
  • 125 g water
  • 75 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 eggs, plus extra 1 egg lightly beaten, for brushing
  • nigella seeds, for sprinkling

Assembly

  • 100 g smoked salmon, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • fresh dill, for garnishing

Preparation

Salmon and ricotta filling

  1. Place salmon, ricotta, preserved lemon, lemon juice, dill and pepper into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 6, until soft. Transfer into a piping bag with a star nozzle (1 cm) and place into refrigerator to firm (approx. 1 hour).

Choux pastry

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place butter and water into mixing bowl and heat 2-3 min/100°C/speed 1 or until butter melts.
  3. Add flour and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 4, until well incorporated. Set mixing bowl aside, remove lid and allow paste to cool (approx. 10-15 minutes).
  4. Replace mixing bowl and mix speed 5, adding 1 egg at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid, until incorporated. Then mix 20 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix 10 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a separate piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm). Pipe small, walnut-sized balls onto prepared baking tray (approx. 12), leaving a 5 cm space between each ball. Brush each ball with beaten egg and sprinkle with nigella seeds. Bake for 12 minutes (190°C), then lower oven temperature to 160°C and bake for a further 10 minutes (160°C).
  6. Turn off oven, remove buns from oven and pierce the bottom of each bun with a wooden skewer. Place buns back into oven to dry for 5 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Assembly

  1. Once cooled, cut buns into halves and pipe reserved salmon filling onto bases. Top with a slice of smoked salmon and a little dill and secure top to serve.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, piping bag with plain nozzle (1 cm), toothpick or wooden skewer, pastry brush, wire rack, piping bag with star nozzle (1 cm), refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Choux buns can be made up to 2 days in advance and stored in an airtight sealable container. Fill just before serving.
