Hot and sour pineapple pork with turmeric rice

Ingredients

Turmeric rice

  • 1150 g water
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g white rice

Hot and sour pineapple pork

  • 350 g pork belly meat, boned, rind removed, meat cut into pieces (2 cm) and partially frozen
  • 2 fresh red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 30 g garlic cloves (approx. 10 cloves)
  • 100 g carrot, cut into pieces
  • 20 g oil
  • 40 g lime juice
  • 10 g fish sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 10 g sugar
  • 50 g plum tomatoes, cut into halves
  • 150 g fresh pineapple, flesh cut into pieces (1 cm)
  • 1 lemon, cut into thin slices, for garnishing
  • fresh mint, leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Turmeric rice

  1. Place water, turmeric, curry powder and stock paste into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh rice into it. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and rinse rice until water runs clear. Re-insert simmering basket with rice and cook 15-18 min/100°C/speed 4, until rice is cooked to your liking. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, then transfer rice into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Discard cooking liquid (see Tips). Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Hot and sour pineapple pork

  1. Place pork belly into mixing bowl and mince 10-12 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place chilli, garlic and carrot into mixing bowl and chop 6 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and cook 5 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add reserved pork mince, lime juice, fish sauce, salt, soy sauce and sugar and cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Scrape down sides, base and blades of mixing bowl with aid of spatula and cook for a further 5 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add tomato and pineapple and cook 2 min/Varoma//speed 1,  without measuring cup or until pork is cooked. Serve warm with reserved Turmeric rice and garnish with lemon slices and mint leaves.

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to the The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Reserve the turmeric liquid after cooking rice and use as a base for soups or stews.
