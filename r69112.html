Orange and almond cake

Ingredients

Cake

  • 1000 g water
  • 2 whole oranges (approx. 450 g total)
  • butter, for greasing
  • 250 g almonds
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Syrup

  • 60 - 70 g orange juice (approx. 1 orange)
  • 80 g caster sugar
  • 80 g orange marmalade

Preparation

Cake

  1. Place water into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket. Place oranges into simmering basket. Ensure oranges fit comfortably in simmering basket without obstructing hole in mixing bowl lid then cook 45 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  2. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set oranges aside to cool. Empty mixing bowl.
  3. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a springform cake tin (20 cm) and set aside.
  4. Once cooled, remove top and bottom of oranges, cut into segments and discard pips. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Place almonds into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  6. Add caster sugar, eggs, baking powder and reserved orange segments and chop 20 sec/speed 7.
  7. Pour mixture into prepared tin and bake for 40-45 minutes (180°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. Allow cake to cool in tin for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Syrup

  1. Place orange juice, caster sugar and orange marmalade into mixing bowl and cook  6 min/100°C/speed 1.5, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  2. Place cooled cake onto serving plate then pierce cake multiple times using a wooden skewer. Pour syrup over cake and allow to absorb prior to serving.

Useful Items

baking paper, springform cake tin (20 cm), wire rack, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Orange and almond cake is ideal for morning or afternoon tea, served with whipped cream.
  • Make individual cakes by baking mixture in a lined 12-hole muffin tray and reduce baking time accordingly.
  • To ensure this recipe is gluten free, read the label of your baking powder or make your own in the Thermomix. Some commercial brands may have traces of wheat or gluten.
