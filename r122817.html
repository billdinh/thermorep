Apricot macadamia biscuits

Ingredients

  • 30 g desiccated coconut, plus extra for sprinkling (optional)
  • 100 g dried apricots
  • 200 g pitted fresh (Medjool) dates (see Tips)
  • 150 g macadamia nuts, plus extra cut into halves, for garnishing
  • 30 g pepitas
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
Nutrition
per 1 portions
Calories
336 kJ / 80 kcal
Protein
1.1 g
Carbohydrates
6.4 g

5.4 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Line a large baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place all ingredients into mixing bowl and blend 2 min/speed 9 or until finely chopped and mixture is smooth and well combined.
  3. Roll 2 teaspoons of mixture into a ball, place onto prepared baking tray and flatten slightly with your hands or a fork. Top with half a macadamia nut or sprinkle with extra coconut (optional). Repeat with remaining mixture (makes approx. 30 biscuits).
  4. Bake for 10-15 minutes (150°C), or until lightly coloured (they will still be soft). Allow to cool on tray for 5-10 minutes to firm slightly. Transfer onto wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Useful Items

baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • It is important to use fresh dates in this recipe. Dried dates will make the mixture too crumbly.
  • Try to source preservative-free desiccated coconut and dried apricots.
  • If you prefer, you can use mixed spice instead of cinnamon.
  • Bliss balls: You can make an unbaked bliss ball with this mixture. After combining the ingredients, roll the uncooked mixture into balls and coat in extra desiccated coconut. Place into a sealable container and store in the refrigerator until ready to serve.
