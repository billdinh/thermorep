Sweet potato frittata with coriander chilli sauce

Ingredients

Coriander chilli sauce

  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 fresh green chillies, deseeded if preferred
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves and stems
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 20 g lemon juice (approx. ½ lemon)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground sea salt

Frittata

  • 100 g red onion, cut into halves
  • 2 cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 100 g zucchini, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 250 g sweet potato, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 tsp ground sea salt
  • 50 g feta cheese
  • 20 g pepita seeds, toasted (optional)

Preparation

Coriander chilli sauce

  1. Place garlic, chilli and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add all remaining sauce ingredients and mix 10 sec/speed 6. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Frittata

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Line a square cake tin (20 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  2. Place onion and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add oil and cook 3 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add zucchini and sweet potato and chop 3 sec/speed 5.
  5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then cook 2 min/100°C//speed . Transfer mixture into prepared tin and spread evenly.
  6. Without cleaning mixing bowl, place eggs and salt into mixing bowl and beat 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer into prepared tin and gently mix through sweet potato mixture with spatula until evenly combined. Drizzle with 1-2 tbsp reserved Coriander chilli sauce, then crumble over feta and sprinkle with toasted pepitas (optional).
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes (180°C) or until golden on top. Allow frittata to cool in tin for 15 minutes, then place into refrigerator to cool completely (approx. 30 minutes). Cut frittata into 6 pieces and serve with extra Coriander chilli sauce on the side.

Useful Items

square cake tin (20 cm), baking paper, bowl, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • This is a quick and easy dish to make the night before. Serve the next day with a garden salad, dressed with some of the Coriander chilli sauce.
  • For a more child-friendly meal, serve the Coriander chilli sauce on the side or substitute with a mild sweet chilli sauce.
