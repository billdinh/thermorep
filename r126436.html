Hazelnut quince friands

Ingredients

  • 850 g water
  • 1 quince, peeled, cored and cut into 6-8 wedges (see Tips)
  • 80 g olive oil, plus extra for greasing (see Tips)
  • 70 g raw sugar
  • 70 g hazelnuts
  • 4 egg whites
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 60 g brown rice flour
  • 60 g soy milk (see Tips)
  • 1 ½ tbsp honey, warmed (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place water into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and add quince into it, then cook 40 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until quince is tender. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and transfer quince into a bowl to cool completely. Thinly slice quince once cool. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease an 8-hole friand tin with oil and line each base with a small round of baking paper.
  3. Place sugar into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Place hazelnuts into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a separate bowl and set aside. Thoroughly clean and dry mixing bowl.
  5. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites into mixing bowl and whip 3 min/speed 4, adding reserved sugar 1 tablespoon at a time through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add vanilla and whip 10 sec/speed 3.
  7. Add half (30 g) of the flour, half of the reserved hazelnuts, half (40 g) of the oil and half (30 g) of the milk and mix 5 sec/speed 3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  8. Add remaining hazelnuts, 30 g flour, 40 g oil and 30 g milk and mix 5 sec/speed 3 or until just combined. Remove butterfly whisk.
  9. Divide mixture evenly between prepared moulds. Place sliced quince on top of each friand (using as much as desired). Drizzle with a little honey (optional) and bake for 10-15 minutes (160°C), or until lightly golden and springs back when touched. Allow to cool in tin for 10 minutes, then transfer onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving. 

Useful Items

bowl, 8-hole friand tin , baking paper, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If you prefer, you can replace the quince with fresh raspberries. Simply top each (uncooked) friand with a raspberry before baking.
  • Soy milk can be replaced with almond milk.
  • If you don’t have a friand tin, you can also use a muffin tray and line 8 of the holes with paper cases. Depending on the size of your tin, your portions may vary.
  • You can make your own brown ride flour in your Thermomix® from scratch. Place 250 g brown rice flour into mixing bowl and mill 2 min/speed 10 to a fine consistancy. Store the leftover flour in a sealable container in your freezer or refrigerator to preserve its nutrient content and keep it fresh for longer.
  • I suggest using a lightly-flavoured extra virgin olive oil as it's much more mild.
