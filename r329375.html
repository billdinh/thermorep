Rendang curry

Ingredients

  • 1 fresh long red chilli, trimmed and cut into thirds, deseeded if preferred
  • ½ red onion, cut into halves
  • 2 ½ cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • 300 g coconut milk
  • 100 g filtered water
  • 500 g potatoes (approx. 3 large potatoes), peeled and cut into chunks (3 cm)
  • 6 fresh Kaffir lime leaves
  • 100 g green beans, trimmed and cut into halves or thirds
  • 1 carrot (approx. 100 g), cut into julienne
  • 1 red capsicum (approx. 100 g), cut into julienne
  • 225 g fresh baby corn (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp tamari, gluten free (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 lime, juice only

Preparation

  1. Place chilli, onion, ginger, garlic and turmeric into mixing bowl and blend 3 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 100 g of the coconut milk and blend 3 sec/speed 7 or until a paste consistency.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula, then heat 2 min/100°C/speed 1.
  4. Add water, potatoes, Kaffir lime leaves and remaining 200 g coconut milk, then cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Add beans, carrot, capsicum, corn, tamari, tamarind paste and salt, then cook 6 min/100°C//speed , placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  6. Add lime juice and stir with aid of spatula, then cook 2-3 min/100°C//speed , until vegetables are tender. Divide curry between serving bowls and serve warm.

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the fresh baby corn with rinsed and drained tinned baby corn.
  • Be sure to check the label of your tamari, as not all brands are gluten free.
  • We served our curry with steamed jasmine rice.
