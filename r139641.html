Apple Rose Tart

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 170 g unsalted butter, cold, diced
  • 220 g plain flour
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 egg yolk, from medium egg
  • 45 g water, cold

Filling

  • 1 lemon, thin peelings of half and juice for glaze (approx. 25 g)
  • 80 g granulated sugar
  • 600 g cooking apples (e.g. Bramley), peeled, cored, quartered

Apple Rose

  • 4 red apples, cored, halved, thinly sliced (1 mm) (approx. 520 g)
  • 15 g lemon juice (approx. half a lemon)
  • 20 g granulated sugar
  • 300 g water

Glaze

  • 60 g apricot jam

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place butter, flour and salt in mixing bowl then pulse Turbo/1 sec/2-4 times until coarse crumbs form.
  2. Add egg yolk and 30 g cold water then bring together Turbo/1 sec/2-4 times. If too dry, add up to 15 g cold water and pulse Turbo/1 sec/1-3 times.
  3. Transfer to a loose bottom fluted tart tin (Ø 25 cm) and press evenly into base and sides, discarding any excess pastry. Prick base with a fork then chill in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, make filling.

Filling

  1. Place lemon peel and sugar in mixing bowl then grind 15 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add cooking apples and chop 10 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 7 min/90°C/speed 1. Insert measuring cup then blend 10 sec/speed 8. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Once pastry has rested for 30 minutes, preheat oven to 220°C. Line chilled pastry case with baking paper and fill with baking beans then blind bake for 10 minutes (220 °C). Remove baking beans and paper then bake for a further 5 minutes (220°C).
  4. Place tin on a wire rack then add reserved apple filling, leaving any excess juice in bowl. Lower oven temperature to 180°C then prepare apple rose.

Apple Rose

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in red apple slices, lemon juice and sugar. Remove bowl then mix together well, separating apple pieces.
  2. Place water in mixing bowl then insert simmering basket. Add apple slices to simmering basket and cook 6-8 min/Varoma/speed 2 until soft and flexible.
  3. Working in concentric circles, lay overlapping slices of apple, skin facing outwards, at a slight angle in filling. Repeat, working from the outside into the middle, until all apple slices are used. Bake for 5-10 minutes (180°C) until slightly golden. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.

Glaze

  1. Place apricot jam and lemon juice in mixing bowl then heat 1 min 20 sec/80°C/speed 2. Strain through a fine sieve if necessary to remove any lumps, gently brush over apple rose on cooked tart then leave to cool completely in tin.
  2. Remove and serve in slices with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, cream or crème fraîche.

Useful Items

fluted tart tin (Ø 25 cm), baking beans, baking paper, wire rack, fine sieve, pastry brush, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

It can be great fun getting creative with your bakes, as this pretty apple rose demonstrates! This tart makes a really elegant centrepiece.
