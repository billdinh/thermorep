Meatballs, Tomato Sauce and Parmesan Polenta

Ingredients

Meatballs and Sauce

  • 40 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 100 g onions, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 500 g beef mince, broken in pieces
  • 500 g pork mince, broken in pieces
  • 60 g single cream
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper
  • 800 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 70 g tomato purée

Parmesan Polenta

  • 100 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 20 g olive oil, plus extra for garnish
  • 1000 g whole milk
  • 100 g quick polenta
  • 100 g salted butter, diced (2 cm)
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt
  • parsley leaves, for garnishing

Preparation

Meatballs and Sauce

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease an oven-proof baking dish with a little oil.
  2. Place onions and garlic in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop again 5 sec/speed 4.
  3. Add beef mince, pork mince, cream, 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch pepper then mix 1 min 30 sec/. Transfer to a bowl and form into approx. 30 walnut-sized balls then place in prepared baking dish.
  4. Place meatballs in oven and bake for 10 minutes (180°C) to brown. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make sauce.
  5. Place tomatoes, tomato purée, oil, 1 pinch salt and 1 pinch pepper in mixing bowl, blend 5 sec/speed 5 then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 4.
  6. Remove meatballs from oven, drain any excess oil from the meatballs then pour sauce over meatballs.
  7. Return to oven and bake uncovered for 40–45 minutes (180°C) until sauce is simmering and reduced and meatballs are cooked through. Clean mixing bowl and continue with recipe 15 minutes before meatballs will be ready.

Parmesan Polenta

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl and grate 15 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place garlic and oil in mixing bowl then chop 3 sec/speed 8. Sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1.
  3. Add milk and polenta then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 10 min/100°C/speed 4, until polenta is thick enough to coat spatula (see tips).
  4. Add reserved grated Parmesan, butter and salt then mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  5. To serve, spoon hot polenta onto a serving platter or wooden board, spreading with the back of a wooden spoon. Place meatballs and sauce on top. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and scatter with parsley leaves.

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own mince, part freeze beef and pork in 3 cm pieces then mince 10-12 sec/speed 6 or until the mince is fine.
  • You may want to check the meatball seasoning at the end of step 4 by cooking a small amount of the mixture in a frying pan and tasting the cooked version. Add more salt and pepper if desired.
  • To check if the polenta is ready, insert spatula into the polenta. Once cooked the polenta will leave a thick layer on each side of the spatula.
