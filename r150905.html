Pork Fillet with Sauerkraut and Potato Purée

Ingredients

  • 350 g onions, quartered
  • 100 g bacon lardons
  • 500 - 600 g pork fillet
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 - 1 ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • 6 - 8 slices smoked lean bacon (60-80 g)
  • 500 g sauerkraut, preserved
  • 1 dried bay leaf (optional)
  • 3 juniper berries (optional)
  • 500 g water
  • 750 g potatoes, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 3 Tbsp frying oil (e.g. vegetable, groundnut)
  • 140 g milk
  • 40 g butter
  • 2 - 3 pinches ground nutmeg

Preparation

  1. Place 100 g onions in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add bacon and sauté 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, season pork with ½ tsp salt, pepper and sweet paprika, wrap in bacon slices and set aside.
  3. Add sauerkraut, bay leaf (if using) and juniper berries (if using) to mixing bowl and cook 3 min/120°C/speed 1. Drain contents of mixing bowl through Varoma dish over a bowl. Set Varoma dish aside and reserve collected cooking liquid.
  4. Place water and 1¼ tsp salt in mixing bowl, insert simmering basket and add potatoes to it. Season potatoes by sprinkling with reserved cooking liquid. Set Varoma dish containing sauerkraut in position, place wrapped pork on sauerkraut, close Varoma and steam 35 min/Varoma/speed 1. In the last 10 minutes, heat oil in a frying pan, slice 250 g onions and fry until golden brown. Remove Varoma and remove simmering basket with aid of spatula, set both aside and empty mixing bowl.
  5. Place milk, butter, remaining ¼ tsp salt, nutmeg and cooked potatoes in mixing bowl and purée 25 sec/speed 3.5 with aid of spatula . Slice the pork and arrange on a dish with sauerkraut and potato purée. Top the meat and purée with fried onions and serve hot.

Useful Items

frying pan, hob

Hints & Tricks

  • This German classic, while usually made with a cured and slightly smoked pork loin (‘Kassler’), is just as good with pork fillet, served ever-so-slightly pink in the centre.
  • Replace the pork fillet with smoked pork loin, thick slices of bacon (1.5 cm) or smoked pork sausages.
    - For a quicker and lighter recipe, omit fried onions and bacon slices.
  • Sauerkraut varies in sourness and you can adjust it to your preference: rinse it briefly through the simmering basket with cold water if you prefer it less sour or season with vinegar or white balsamic vinegar to increase its acidity.
