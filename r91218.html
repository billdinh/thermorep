Brioche

Ingredients

  • 1 oz milk
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast,
  • 13 oz bread flour, plus extra to dust
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 - 3 ½ oz sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 6 oz unsalted butter, cut into pieces, plus extra to grease
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly whisked, to glaze

Preparation

  1. Place milk and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 1 min/100°F/speed 2.
  2. Add bread flour, 4 eggs, sugar and salt and knead 10 min/ while adding butter little by little through hole in mixing bowl lid onto rotating blades.
  3. Transfer dough into a large bowl, cover with cling wrap or a damp kitchen towel (tea towel) and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
  4. Grease a loaf pan (approx. 9 in. x 5 in.) and set aside. Transfer dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently flatten with floured hands to form a rough rectangle. Roll the rectangle of dough into a log shape the length of the loaf pan. Place dough into prepared loaf pan, seam side down and cover with cling wrap or a damp kitchen towel (tea towel). Let loaf rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350°F.
  5. Brush surface with egg yolk and bake for 30 minutes (350°F) or until dark golden brown. Remove brioche from loaf pan and allow to cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Useful Items

cling wrap or damp kitchen towel, pastry brush, loaf pan (approx. 9 in. x 5 in.), large bowl, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Refrigerate the dough for easier shaping. After first rise gently fold dough over itself to deflate it, cover bowl with cling wrap and place in refrigerator. Continue with the recipe at your convenience up to 24 hours later, allowing more time for the loaf to rise the second time.
  • This is a rich brioche dough that requires long kneading and long proving (rising) for the fluffiest texture.
  • Rising times depend on ambient temperature. Proving should take place in a constant temperature environment. Low temperature will lead to longer proving time, high temperature (above 104°F) may weaken or kill the yeast. To create a warm environment you can place the shaped brioche in a turned-off cool oven with a bowl of hot water near it.
  • Brioche à tête (traditional French brioche shape): divide dough into 2 pieces, one large (⅘ of the final loaf) and one small (⅕). Form both pieces into smooth balls. Place the large ball in a greased fluted brioche tin, and using your fingers, create a deep indentation in the middle until your fingers are touching the bottom of the tin. Roll the small ball of dough into a tear-drop shape, with a longer, pointy part. Place the pointy part of the small piece of dough in the indentation of the large ball, forming a "head", and push down deeply all around the head to tuck it firmly into the large ball of dough. Let rise, then bake as directed by the recipe.
