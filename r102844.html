Prawn Toast

Ingredients

  • 280 g peeled, raw prawns
  • 15 g fish sauce
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 1 egg white, from large egg
  • 2 tsp cornflour
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper
  • 4 spring roll pastry sheets (21 cm x 21 cm)
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 30 g sesame seeds
  • sweet plum sauce, or dipping sauce of your choice
Nutrition
per 1 piece
Calories
295 kJ / 70 kcal
Protein
5 g
Carbohydrates
4 g
Fat
3 g

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Place prawns, fish sauce, coriander, egg white, cornflour and white pepper in mixing bowl then blend 15 sec/speed 5.
  3. Lay 1 spring roll pastry sheet on work surface and spread half of filling over surface. Cover with second spring roll pastry sheet. Cut square into quarters then cut out 2 triangles from each quarter and place on prepared baking tray. Repeat this step with remaining two sheets of pastry and filling.
  4. Brush surface of triangles with olive oil and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for 20 minutes (180°C). Serve immediately with dipping sauce.

Useful Items

baking tray and paper, soft spatula, long sharp knife, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve with different sauces such as sweet and sour sauce or peanut sauce.
  • It is often difficult to find authentic spring roll wrappers suitable for frying and baking but it is possible to make your own. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid and weigh in 110 g plain flour, 70 g cornflour, 2 pinches salt and 350 g water then stir until combined. Place 1 tsp oil and 2 tsp water in a small bowl. Heat a pancake pan or frying pan over medium-low heat and, using a paper towel, lightly grease with oil-water mixture. Using a silicone pastry brush, brush a thin layer of batter around pan until completely covered. Once sides are beginning to come away from pan, transfer to a plate then repeat until all the batter is used.
