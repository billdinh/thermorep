Chicken and thyme pâté

Ingredients

  • 150 g brown onion, cut into quarters
  • 250 g apples, peeled, cored and cut into pieces
  • 80 g salted butter, cut into pieces (1-2 cm)
  • 400 g chicken livers (organic), sinew trimmed
  • 1 - 2 sprigs fresh thyme leaves only, plus extra for garnishing
  • ghee or butter, melted, for topping
  • bread or toast, to serve (optional)
  • apple or pear, cut into slices, to serve (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place onion and apple into mixing bowl and chop 4 sec/speed 6. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add 20 g of the butter and cook 6 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  3. Add remaining 60 g butter, chicken livers and thyme and cook 4 min/100°C/speed 1, without measuring cup.
  4. Insert measuring cup and blend 1 min/speed 1-10, increasing speed gradually from speed 1 to speed 10 or until smooth.
  5. Transfer mixture into a large lidded glass dish or smaller individual dishes. Gently shake to even out mixture. Leave to cool for 10 minutes, then evenly pour enough melted ghee or butter to coat surface of pâté. Sprinkle with extra thyme leaves. Place into refrigerator overnight. Serve pâté with thinly sliced bread or toast (optional) or as a dip with slices of apple or pear (optional).

Hints & Tricks

  • The ghee/butter helps to preserve the pâté and prevent a skin forming on top.
  • You can store this pâté in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
  • Pâté is a great food for ensuring your baby or toddler is getting enough iron. Because of their rapid growth, this is one mineral they're often deficient in.
  • Liver is one of the best sources of iron and a rich source of vitamin A. However because it is possible to have toxic amounts of vitamin A, you need to watch that your child doesn't regularly eat large quantities of liver. A serve of this pâté a couple of times a week is well within safe levels.
  • You can use less liver - try 350 g - if your child is new to the flavour. Once they're accepting the taste, you can move to 400 g or even up to 500 g for a stronger flavour again. The natural sweetness of the apple and onion and the creaminess of the butter will help them to accept the taste.
  • This pâté is delicious for adults, too. It's a much better option than store-bought, which virtually always has preservatives. This recipe has no added salt, which is why I like to use salted butter, so it has just a fraction.
