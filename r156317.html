Cucumber soup with prawn skewers

Ingredients

  • 40 g olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 24 raw prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 80 g brown onions, cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 500 g cucumbers, cut into pieces
  • 1 lemon, cut into slices, each slice quartered
  • 500 g water
  • 1 heaped tsp Vegetable stock paste (refer to the recipe in The Basic Cookbook)
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 200 g full cream milk
  • 40 g plain flour
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, leaves only (optional)

Preparation

  1. Place olive oil, 1 tbsp of the lemon juice and salt into mixing bowl and mix 3 sec/speed 3.
  2. Place prawns into a bowl and coat with 2 tablespoons of the marinade. Allow to marinate for 10 minutes while preparing the soup.
  3. Add onions and garlic cloves to remaining marinade in mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  4. Sauté 2 min/120°C/speed 1.
  5. Add cucumber and chop 4 sec/speed 5, then sauté 6 min/120°C/speed 1. Meanwhile, thread prawns and quartered lemon slices onto 6 wooden skewers and arrange them in Varoma dish (see Tips).
  6. Add water, stock paste, pepper and remaining marinade from prawns to mixing bowl, Varoma into position, secure Varoma lid and cook 10 min/Varoma/speed 1. Set Varoma aside.
  7. Add milk, flour and remaining 1 tbsp lemon juice to mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 5-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 9. Remove measuring cup, set Varoma back into position and cook 2 min/100°C/speed 3. Set Varoma aside.
  8. Add dill to mixing bowl, season to taste and blend 30 sec/speed 5-9, increasing speed gradually from speed 5 to speed 9, until frothy. Divide soup between 6 soup bowls, place a prawn skewer on top of each bowl and serve immediately.

Hints & Tricks

  • If using frozen prawns, thaw before marinating.
    - Unpeeled cucumbers add more colour and flavour to the soup.
    - Long skewers can be cut to fit the Varoma dish.
    - In hot weather serve this soup chilled. For a fresh summer flavour, replace dill with fresh mint or coriander.
  • Instead of flour use 150 g cooked potatoes, cut into pieces, to thicken the soup.
    - For a thicker soup, replace milk with pouring (whipping) cream.
    - For a vegetarian option, replace prawns with small mushrooms (3 cm), feta cheese cubes, or tofu cubes marinated in soy sauce; and replace lemon slices with cherry tomatoes.
    - Try adding a splash of dry white wine to the soup in the final step.
