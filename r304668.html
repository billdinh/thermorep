Seeded Breakfast Bread with Avocado Ricotta Spread and Smoked Salmon

Ingredients

Seeded Breakfast Bread

  • 250 g wholemeal flour
  • 230 g spelt flour
  • 70 g sunflower seeds, plus 2 tsp for sprinkling
  • 35 g black sesame seeds, plus 1 tsp for sprinkling
  • 1 Tbsp caraway seeds, plus 1 tsp for sprinkling (optional)
  • 1 Tbsp linseeds, plus 1 tsp for sprinkling
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 170 g whole milk
  • 180 g buttermilk
  • 30 g runny honey
  • 20 g extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for greasing

Avocado Ricotta Spread

  • 125 g ricotta cheese
  • 3 ripe avocados, quartered
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 pinches fine sea salt, or to taste
  • 2 pinches ground black pepper, or to taste, plus extra for sprinkling

Assembly

  • 100 g smoked salmon, sliced
  • 1 lemon, cut in wedges

Preparation

Seeded Breakfast Bread

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line a loaf tin (900 g/2 lb) with baking paper.
  2. Place wholemeal flour, spelt flour, 70 g sunflower seeds, 35 g black sesame seeds, 1 Tbsp caraway seeds (if using), 1 Tbsp linseeds, salt, baking powder and bicarbonate of soda in mixing bowl then mix 20 sec/speed 3.
  3. Add milk, buttermilk, honey and oil then mix 30 sec/speed 3. Transfer to prepared tin then sprinkle with remaining 2 tsp sunflower seeds, 1 tsp black sesame seeds, 1 tsp caraway seeds (if using) and 1 tsp linseeds, pressing gently to adhere. Bake for 1 hour (180°C), rotating tin halfway through, or until a skewer inserted in centre comes out clean.
  4. Leave to cool in tin for 20 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

Avocado Ricotta Spread

  1. Insert butterfly whisk. Place ricotta in mixing bowl and whip 10 sec/speed 3. Remove butterfly whisk.
  2. Add avocados, lemon juice, salt and pepper then mix 10 sec/speed 3.

Assembly

  1. Cut 6 slices of cooled seeded bread and toast on both sides in a toaster or griddle pan. Spread each slice with plenty of avocado ricotta spread. Top with smoked salmon and a sprinkle of pepper then serve with lemon wedges.

Useful Items

loaf tin (900 g/2 lb), wire rack, toaster, baking paper, skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Cooled bread can be tightly wrapped and stored for 3 days, or frozen for up to 1 month.
  • Our version of the ever-popular avocado on toast has a slight twist with the addition of ricotta. Served on a seedy and nutritious loaf that requires no proving time, it makes a great option when you want fresh bread in the morning. Topping the toast with omega 3-rich salmonalso boosts the protein, keeping you fuller for longer.
  • Could also be topped with a poached egg. See Smashed Plum Tomato Bruschetta with Poached Eggs for method.
