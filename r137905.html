Chicken San Fernando

Ingredients

  • 2 - 3 dried chipotle chillies, stalks removed
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 150 g roasted unsalted peanuts
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 8 red peppercorns, plus extra for garnishing
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 400 g tomatoes, cut into halves (see Tips)
  • 1 brown onion (approx. 120 g), cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 3 tsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1000 g skinless chicken thigh fillets, cut into pieces (3-4 cm)

Preparation

  1. Place chillies into a bowl, cover with boiling water and set aside to soak (approx. 15 minutes). Drain chillies and discard soaking liquid.
  2. Place peanuts, cumin seeds, red peppercorns and cloves into mixing bowl and toast 8 min/Varoma/speed .
  3. Add tomatoes, onions, garlic and soaked chillies and blend 20 sec/speed 8.
  4. Add oil, stock paste and chicken. Stir using spatula and cook 30 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  5. Transfer chicken and sauce into a large bowl (see Tips) and garnish with extra peppercorns before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl

Hints & Tricks

  • Chipotle chillies are smoked and dried jalapeño chillies. This medium spicy pepper is primarily used in Mexican cuisine, and can be found in spice shops or online.
  • You can use either tinned or fresh tomatoes in this recipe.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Chicken can be shredded if desired. Place the cooked chicken breasts, without sauce, back into mixing bowl and shred 4 sec//speed 4. Add shredded chicken back to sauce and stir well to combine.
  • Serve the chicken and sauce with rice for a simple meal, or use as a filling in tortillas with guacamole and sour cream.
