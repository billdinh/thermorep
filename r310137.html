Little lemon cheesecakes

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 200 g raw cashews (see Tips)
  • 100 g pitted Medjool dates (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking

Base

  • 100 g tigernuts, skinned (see Tips)
  • 1 pitted Medjool date
  • 2 tsp pure maple syrup
  • 2 tsp coconut oil
  • 2 tsp raw cacao powder
  • 1 tbsp filtered water

Lemon filling

  • 150 g raw cacao butter
  • 150 g lemon juice
  • 150 g plant-based milk of choice
  • 1 tbsp vanilla bean paste (see Tips)
  • 1 pinch ground turmeric
  • fresh raspberries, for decorating
  • fresh blueberries, for decorating
  • lemon slices, for decorating

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews and dates into it. Add salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse cashews and dates. Set aside.

Base

  1. Place all base ingredients into mixing bowl and chop 30 sec/speed 10.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop Turbo/2 sec/2-3 times. Place 12 pastry rings (7 cm) onto a large plate. Press 1½ tablespoons of mixture evenly into base of each pastry ring. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Lemon filling

  1. Place cacao butter into mixing bowl and melt 12 min/37°C/speed 2 or until melted.
  2. Add all remaining lemon filling ingredients, including drained cashews and dates, into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 7.
  3. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend 20 sec/speed 6 or until smooth. Divide mixture between pastry rings and smooth tops. Transfer into refrigerator until firm (approx. 3 hours). Gently remove pastry rings, then decorate with berries and lemon slices to serve.

Useful Items

bowl, pastry rings (7 cm), large plate, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your cashews and dates, soak in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • Skinned tigernuts can be purchased from some speciality food stores, health food stores or online. If tigernuts are unavailable, you can replace them with pecans.
  • To make your own vanilla paste, refer to the recipe on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
