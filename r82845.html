Sausage Rolls

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 220 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
  • 170 g butter, diced (1 cm) then frozen
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 80 g water, cold

Sausagemeat Filling

  • 150 g onions, quartered
  • 450 g good quality pork sausagemeat
  • 1 tsp mixed dried herbs
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 1 egg, beaten, for glazing

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place flour, frozen butter and salt in mixing bowl then coarsely mix Turbo/1 sec/2 times.
  2. Add water and mix 15 sec/speed 6 until just clinging together. Tip out and wrap in cling film then chill in fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, make filling.

Sausagemeat Filling

  1. Place onions in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then chop again 2 sec/speed 5.
  2. Add sausagemeat, dried herbs and pepper then mix together 30 sec/. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl and mix again 15 sec/.
  3. Preheat oven to 220°C and line two large baking trays with baking paper.
  4. Remove pastry from fridge and roll out on a lightly floured surface, to a large rectangle (45 cm x  50 cm). Trim the edges then cut pastry lengthways into three equal pieces. Divide sausagemeat also into three (approx. 200 g each), laying one part on each section of pastry.
  5. Make a long log shape with the sausagemeat down the centre of each pastry strip. Brush one edge with beaten egg, then fold pastry over and seal firmly. Roll log over so sealed edge is underneath then brush with beaten egg. Using a sharp knife, cut log at 5 cm intervals forming individual rolls.
  6. Using scissors, snip two holes along the top of each roll to allow steam to escape during baking. Transfer to baking trays and bake for 20-25 minutes (220°C). Serve warm or cold.

Useful Items

cling film, 2 baking trays and baking paper, rolling pin, pastry brush, scissors, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • These sausage rolls can also be made ahead and frozen before being cooked. Defrost and bake when needed.

  • One of Britain’s most loved savoury snacks, these can be made in all sizes which make them perfect for lunches, snacks, picnics and party food alike. Lovely served warm with a dollop of tomato ketchup or onion jam.
  • Serve with mixed leaf salad and chutney.
