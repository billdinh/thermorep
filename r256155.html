Anise-cured salmon

Ingredients

  • 500 - 800 g fresh salmon fillet, skin removed if preferred (see Tips)
  • 3 star anise
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 125 g white sugar
  • 125 g salt
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • cold water, for rinsing

Preparation

  1. Place salmon into a ceramic or glass dish (23 cm).
  2. Place all spices into mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9.
  3. Add sugar, salt and thyme and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Evenly coat both sides of salmon with spice mixture. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator overnight (or a minimum of 8 hours) to cure.
  4. Uncover, turn salmon over, re-cover with plastic wrap and return to refrigerator for 6 hours or until salmon is opaque and firm to the touch (but not tough or dry - see Tips). Rinse fillet under cold water and pat dry with paper towel. Cut into very thin slices to serve (see Tips).

Useful Items

ceramic or glass baking dish (23 cm), plastic wrap, paper towel, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Curing time will vary, depending on the size and thickness of your fillet
  • Serve thin slices of cured salmon, with a dollop of crème fraîche, on pikelets, potato röstis or your favourite toasted bread.
  • The salmon can be left whole or cut into fillets for this dish.
