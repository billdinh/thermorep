Tuna mornay

Ingredients

  • 40 g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 300 g penne pasta
  • boiling water, for soaking
  • 200 g tasty cheese, cut into pieces
  • 6 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 60 g panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 brown onion, cut into halves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 350 g milk
  • 425 g canned tuna, drained
  • 410 g canned sweet corn kernels, drained (approx. 250 g after draining)
  • 410 g canned creamed corn
  • ¼ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste, plus extra to season
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper, to taste, plus extra to season
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a casserole dish (approx. 30 x 25 x 6 cm) and set aside.
  2. Place a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh pasta into it. Place bowl with pasta onto kitchen bench and cover with boiling water. Stir pasta and water, then cover and set aside. Stir pasta once or twice while sauce is cooking.
  3. Place cheese and parsley into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 9. Transfer 100 g of the mixture into a large bowl and set aside.
  4. Add panko breadcrumbs into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer mixture into a separate bowl and set aside. Do not clean mixing bowl.
  5. Place onion, garlic and butter into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7, then sauté 3 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  6. Add flour and milk and mix 4 sec/speed 5, then cook 6 min/80°C/speed 4.
  7. Add tuna, corn kernels, creamed corn, paprika, salt, pepper and lemon juice (optional) and mix 5 sec//speed 4. Transfer into bowl with cheese and parsley mixture.
  8. Drain pasta, add to tuna mixture and stir to combine. Season to taste, then transfer into prepared casserole dish. Top with reserved breadcrumb mixture and bake for 20-25 minutes (180°C) or until golden brown on top. Serve while warm.

Useful Items

large bowl, bowl, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, casserole dish (30 x 25 x 6 cm), oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Replace penne with pasta of choice (e.g. spelt or wholemeal pasta) and cook as per packet instructions.
  • You can store tuna mornay in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 3 weeks. Simply reheat and serve.
  • Gluten free tuna mornay: substitute breadcrumbs, plain flour and pasta with gluten free options.
