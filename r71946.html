Lamb rack with port sauce and parsnip purée

Ingredients

White balsamic reduction (see Tips)

  • 1 lemon, zest only, no white pith
  • 500 g white balsamic vinegar
  • 50 g dry sherry
  • 80 g raw sugar
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 8 white peppercorns
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • 1 spring onion/shallot, trimmed and cut into halves

Lamb rack

  • 600 g water
  • 2 tsp vegetable stock paste (see Tips)
  • 400 g parsnips, peeled and cut into pieces
  • 1 rack of lamb, well trimmed and cut into two small racks
  • 1 tbsp white balsamic reduction
  • 1 pinch sea salt, to taste
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 100 g snow peas, ends trimmed
  • 20 g olive oil
  • 60 g port

Preparation

White balsamic reduction (see Tips)

  1. Place lemon zest into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then repeat chopping as required until a fine consistency is achieved.
  3. Add all remaining ingredients and cook 30 min/Varoma//speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  4. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a hot-rinsed sealable jar or bottle and seal. Allow to cool completely before placing into a cool, dark area to store until use. Use as needed.

Lamb rack

  1. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket and weigh parsnip into it.
  2. Rub lamb with ½ tablespoon balsamic reduction and season to taste. Place Varoma into position. Place lamb racks, bone side down, into Varoma dish with rosemary and garlic cloves and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Insert Varoma tray. Weigh snow peas onto Varoma tray and steam 3 min/Varoma/speed 1. Check lamb is cooked to your preference. Remove Varoma tray with snow peas and continue to cook lamb, if required.
  4. Transfer lamb, garlic and snow peas into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula.  Transfer steaming stock into a jug and set aside.
  5. Place olive oil and cooked parsnip into mixing bowl, season to taste and blend 20 sec/speed 5. Add a little of the steaming stock if consistency is too thick and repeat blending 20 sec/speed 5. Transfer purée into a bowl, cover and set aside. Do not rinse mixing bowl.
  6. Place port, reserved garlic, 1 tablespoon parsnip purée, 100 g of the reserved steaming stock and ½ tablespoon white balsamic reduction into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/Varoma/speed 2, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid.
  7. Insert measuring cup and blend 20 sec/speed 6. Adjust seasoning to taste and pour sauce into a serving jug.
  8. Serve carved lamb on top of parsnip purée with snow peas or green vegetables and port sauce on the side.

Useful Items

sealable jar or bottle, fine mesh sieve, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, plastic wrap

Hints & Tricks

  • Recipe for one: reduce the parsnip and snow peas by half. Proceed as per recipe.
  • You can substitue White balsamic reduction for balsamic vinegar.
  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or the Everyday Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can substitute white peppercorns with black peppercorns.
  • You can substitute snow peas with green vegetables of your choice.
  • You can substitute parsnip with any root vegetable (e.g. carrot, celeriac, sweet potato).  Try peeling and roasting any leftover parsnip for handcut chips to serve with Lemon and caper aioli.
  • Making the best of your Varoma is an ideal way to save time and eat healthy. This recipe is a great example of what you can achieve in around 30 minutes.
  • The remaining steaming stock can be kept in the refrigerator and used as stock in other dishes.
