Ancient Grain Bread

Ingredients

  • olive oil, to grease
  • 15 oz water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 packet/envelope rapid rise yeast
  • 3 oz amaranth flour
  • 3 oz kamut flour
  • 3 oz whole wheat flour
  • 3 oz spelt flour
  • 10 ½ oz bread flour
  • 2 oz millet grain
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ oz coconut sugar (optional)
  • salted butter, to serve

Preparation

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and lightly grease a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water, lemon juice and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 2 min/100°F/speed 2.
  3. Add amaranth flour, kamut flour, whole wheat flour, spelt flour, bread flour, millet, salt, coconut sugar and knead 3 min/. Transfer to a lightly floured surface and shape dough into a ball. Place dough into prepared bowl, cover with a damp kitchen towel and allow to rise in a warm place (approx. 1 hr.) or until doubled in size (see Tip).
  4. Place risen dough into mixing bowl and knead 1 min/.
  5. Turn dough out onto a floured surface. Using floured hands, shape dough into a round loaf, transfer to prepared baking sheet and allow to rise again in a warm place (approx. 20 min.). Preheat oven to 425°F.
  6. Slash top of loaf in a criss-cross pattern with a sharp knife and bake for 15 minutes (425°F).
  7. Reduce temperature to 375°F and bake for 20-25 minutes (375°F) or until loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the underside. Allow to cool on wire rack before slicing. Serve with butter.

Useful Items

baking sheet, parchment paper, kitchen towel, wire rack, large bowl, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use your oven's proof mode to rise bread, if available. Alternatively, dough can be proofed in the mixing bowl for first rise (approx. 1 hr. or until dough doubles in size).
  • To remove dough from mixing bowl, turn mixing bowl upside down on a floured surface and use fingers to twist the black hub of the mixing knife, allowing dough to drop out. Remaining dough can be pulsed 2 sec/speed 10 to flick the dough to sides of mixing bowl for easy removal.
  • If using active dry yeast allow bread to rise approximately 30 minutes on second rise before baking.
  • Amaranth flour can be replaced with amaranth grain, kamut flour can be replaced with kamut grain and spelt flour can be replaced by spelt grain. Grind 1 min/speed 10. Add to mixing bowl with other dry ingredients.
