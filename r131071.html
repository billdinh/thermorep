Raspberry and Chocolate Swirl Pavlova

Ingredients

Pavlova

  • 300 g granulated sugar
  • 6 egg whites, from large eggs
  • 1 pinch cream of tartar
  • 2 Tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp cocoa powder, sieved

Topping

  • 400 g double cream
  • 225 - 250 g fresh raspberries
  • chocolate curls, for decoration

Preparation

Pavlova

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Trace 2 circles (Ø 20 cm) on 2 pieces of baking paper, then turn over and use to line 2 baking trays.
  2. Place 150 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 9. Transfer to a bowl.
  3. Place remaining 150 g sugar in mixing bowl and grind 15 sec/speed 9. Add to bowl of icing sugar and set aside.
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Place egg whites and cream of tartar in mixing bowl then whisk 5 min/37°C/speed 3.5. Without measuring cup, mix again 5 min/37°C/speed 3.5 while slowly adding reserved icing sugar through hole in mixing bowl lid, 1 Tbsp at a time, until combined.
  5. Mix 40 sec/speed 3 while adding cornflour and vinegar through hole in mixing bowl lid until combined. Remove butterfly whisk.
  6. Spoon mixture inside traced circles on baking paper to form discs approx. 4-6 cm deep. Sprinkle cocoa powder over then lightly swirl through using a metal skewer. Bake for 10 minutes (150°C) then reduce oven temperature to 120°C and bake for 1 hour (120°C). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and butterfly whisk.
  7. Turn off oven and, with oven door closed, leave pavlova to cool completely (at least 3 hours, preferably overnight).

Topping

  1. When ready to serve,  insert butterfly whisk. Place cream in mixing bowl and whisk without setting a time/speed 3 until soft peaks form - watching carefully to avoid over-whipping.

Assembly

  1. Place one pavlova disc on a serving plate then pile with the whipped cream and half the raspberries. Place second pavlova disc on top then scatter remaining raspberries over. Decorate with chocolate curls and serve immediately.

Useful Items

2 baking trays and baking paper, metal skewers, medium mesh sieve, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Use the base of a Ø 20 cm cake tin to mark out circles on baking paper in step 1.
  • As with any whisked egg white recipe, make sure your mixing bowl and butterfly whisk are completely clean and dry before starting this recipe.
