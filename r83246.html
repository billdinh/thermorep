Slow-cooked pulled pork with steamed buns

Ingredients

Steamed buns

  • olive oil, for greasing
  • 300 g water
  • 3 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 500 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp sea salt

Pulled pork and apple

  • 1000 - 1200 g rolled pork shoulder
  • 40 g olive oil, plus extra to rub over crackle
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 red onion (approx.120 g), cut into halves
  • 1 carrot (approx. 110 g), cut into quarters
  • 2 celery stalks (approx. 80 g), cut into quarters
  • ½ fennel bulb (approx. 80 g), trimmed and thinly sliced
  • 2 Granny Smith apples, core removed and cut into eighths
  • 2 tsp ground smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp mustard powder
  • ½ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 50 g apple cider vinegar
  • 30 g white wine vinegar
  • 50 g treacle
  • 1 tbsp Chicken stock paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste

  • 1 - 2 tbsp full cream milk, for brushing
  • salad greens, to serve

Preparation

Steamed buns

  1. Grease a large bowl, Varoma dish and Varoma tray with olive oil and set aside. Place water and yeast into mixing bowl and warm 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and salt and knead 2 min 30 sec/. Transfer dough into large oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap and leave to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  3. Transfer proved dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface. Divide dough into 12 equal-sized portions, roll into balls and place into greased Varoma dish and onto Varoma tray. Set Varoma aside. Rinse and dry mixing bowl.

Pulled pork and apple

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper, then line a deep-sided baking tray with aluminium foil and place a cooling rack on top. Set baking trays aside.
  2. Carefully remove the pork skin and fat layer from the shoulder. Cut the shoulder meat into cubes (6-7cm), then transfer into a bowl and set aside. Remove some of the excess fat from the underside of the pork skin, then using a sharp knife, score the top of the skin and rub with olive oil and salt.
  3. Place pork skin onto the wire rack on the deep-sided baking tray and roast for 40-50 minutes (220°C), or until the fat has dissolved and crackling has become crispy. Remove crackling from oven and set aside. Reduce oven temperature to 200°C.
  4. Place garlic and red onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add carrot and celery and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add fennel, apple and 40 g olive oil sauté 10 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  7. Add reserved pork shoulder meat and sauté 2 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  8. Add paprika, mustard powder, chilli flakes, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, white wine vinegar, treacle, stock paste and tomato paste, then place Varoma with buns into position and steam 30 min/Varoma//speed 1.
  9. Set Varoma with buns aside, then place simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid and continue to cook 40 min/80°C//speed . Meanwhile, transfer steamed buns onto prepared baking tray (30 x 40 cm), then brush with milk and bake for 5-10 minutes (200°C) or until golden brown. Transfer buns onto a wire rack and set aside.
  10. Remove simmering basket, insert measuring cup and shred 3 sec//speed 5. Transfer pork into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  11. Fill buns with shredded pork and serve warm with crackling and salad greens on the side.

Useful Items

wire rack, thermal serving bowl or other large bowl, baking paper, large bowl, plastic wrap, silicone bread mat, aluminium foil, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), deep-sided baking tray, pastry brush, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Please refer to The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for the Chicken stock paste recipe.
  • Substitute the salad greens for Coleslaw in your pulled pork buns. See The Basic Cookbook or Everyday Cookbook for a Coleslaw recipe.
  • Ask your butcher to separate the pork fat from the roast for you to save time and effort.
  • Serve leftover pulled pork over rice, with corn tortillas or in sandwiches the next day.
  • Cooked pulled pork can easily be frozen and reheated later.
