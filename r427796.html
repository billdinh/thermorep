Salted Caramel Ice Cream

Ingredients

Salted Caramel Sauce

  • 250 g sugar
  • 200 g cream, min. 30% fat (whipping or double cream)
  • 30 g unsalted butter, cold, in one piece (60 mm x 40 mm x 10 mm)
  • 1030 g water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 50 g vinegar

Ice Cream

  • ½ vanilla pod
  • 600 g whole milk
  • 200 g cream, min. 30% fat (whipping or double cream)
  • 30 g sugar
  • 6 egg yolks, from medium eggs

Preparation

Salted Caramel Sauce

  1. To guarantee the success of recipes using Sugar Stage modes, the ingredients used must match exactly the quantity and description given. The recipe should also be cooked immediately after placing the sugar with the other ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Place sugar, butter, 70 g cream, 30 g water and lemon juice in mixing bowl then start Sugar Stages .
  3. Add remaining 130 g cream and salt then, without measuring cup, mix 2 min/speed  then cook a further 3 min/Varoma/speed 2. Pour into an airtight jar then set aside.
  4. Place 1000 g water and vinegar in mixing bowl then start Clean . Discard water and dry mixing bowl, then make ice cream.

Ice Cream

  1. Split vanilla pod lengthwise and scrape out seeds. Place vanilla seeds, milk, cream, sugar, egg yolks and 100 g reserved caramel sauce in mixing bowl then cook 12 min/90°C/speed 2.5. Store remaining caramel sauce in fridge.
  2. Pour mixture up to 4 cm deep into shallow freezer-proof containers (e.g. aluminium, silicone) and cover with cling film. Allow mixture to cool completely before freezing for at least 10 hours until solid.
  3. Drizzle 2 Tbsp salted caramel sauce over ice cream and set aside.
  4. Place frozen mixture in fridge for 15 minutes then, using a sharp knife, cut in pieces (4 cm). Place half the pieces in mixing bowl, crush 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a loaf tin (900 g/2 lb).
  5. Place remaining frozen ice cream pieces in mixing bowl, crush 20 sec/speed 6, then mix 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to loaf tin.
  6. Drizzle 2 more Tbsp salted caramel sauce over ice cream and use a knife to swirl the sauce into the ice cream. Cover the surface of the ice cream with cling film then freeze for 2 hours before serving. Serve with remaining salted caramel sauce on the side.

Useful Items

airtight jar, shallow freezer-proof container (e.g. aluminium, silicone), cling film, long sharp knife, loaf tin (900 g/2 lb), freezer, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Perfect for summer treats, salted caramel ice cream can be served in many ways. Enjoy scoops in a cone, softly swirled into a sundae or sandwiched between cookies for an ice cream sandwich.
  • Adding lemon juice prevents the sugar from burning or crystallising, without affecting the flavour of the caramel.
