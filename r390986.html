Apricot Bars

Ingredients

Dough

  • 3 ½ oz dark brown sugar
  • 1 orange, thin peel only, no pith
  • 4 oz unsalted butter, cubed, room temperature
  • 8 oz all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 egg yolks, from large eggs

Filling

  • 9 oz dried apricots, pitted
  • 4 oz dried dates, pitted
  • 3 oz orange marmalade
  • ½ oz honey, to taste
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • &frac18; tsp salt

Preparation

Dough

  1. Place sugar and orange peels into mixing bowl and grind 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add butter and mix 30 sec/speed 3.5.
  3. Add flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon and egg yolks and mix 10 sec/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a futher 10 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a large piece of cling wrap, press dough together and shape into a rectangle. Refrigerate 1 hour.

Filling

  1. Place apricots and dates into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 10.
  2. Add marmalade, honey, cinnamon, ginger and salt and mix 5 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and mix a further 5 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a large round piping tip (½ in.). Refrigerate until needed.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.
  2. Divide chilled dough in half. Working with one half at a time, roll out dough to a long strip (approx. 3 ¼ in. x 15 in.). Pipe two strips of apricot filling down middle of dough. Fold one third of the dough over the filling, then close with the opposite third and gently pinch to seal. Repeat with remaining dough and place both logs on prepared baking sheet, seam-side down. Gently flatten logs to a ½ inch thickness and refrigerate 20 minutes to chill. Bake for 15-18 minutes (350°F).
  3. Remove from oven and immediately cut logs into 1¾ inch bars. Place into an airtight container, with paper towels between each layer and let sit covered for at least 6 hours (see Tip). Serve at room temperature.

Useful Items

cling wrap, piping bag and ½ in. piping tip, airtight container, paper towels, baking sheet, parchment paper, rolling pin, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Placing the apricot bars into an airtight container helps to steam the bars, giving them a soft and cakey texture.
  • Store for up to one week at room temperature or up to one month refrigerated.
