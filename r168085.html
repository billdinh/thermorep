Baklava with Walnuts

Ingredients

  • 16 oz shelled walnuts
  • 4 oz sugar, divided
  • 2 tbsp ground cinnamon, divided
  • 10 oz unsalted butter
  • 16 oz phyllo dough, store-bought
  • 8 whole cloves
  • ½ lemon, thin peel only, no pith
  • 8 oz water
  • 8 oz honey

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Place 8 oz walnuts, 2 oz sugar and 1 tbsp cinnamon into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place remaining 8 oz walnuts, 2 oz sugar and 1 tbsp cinnamon into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 4. Transfer to bowl with chopped nut mixture and set aside. Reserve 4 tablespoons of nut mixture in a separate bowl and set aside to garnish.
  4. Place butter into mixing bowl and melt 8 min/160°F/speed 1. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  5. Use reserved melted butter to grease the bottom and sides of baking pan with a pastry brush. Lay phyllo dough flat and cut dough in half using a sharp knife, so sheets will fit into the bottom of your pan (approx. 9 in. x 13 in.).
  6. Place one sheet of dough into the bottom of prepared pan and lightly brush with melted butter. Repeat this process until you have 8 sheets of dough layered with butter.
  7. Evenly sprinkle with a ¼ of the reserved chopped nuts. Continue layering with 6 layers of dough, brushing each with melted butter. Evenly sprinkle ¼ of the reserved nuts every 6 sheets. The top 6 layers of your Baklava should only have dough and butter, no nuts. Brush the top layer with the remaining butter.
  8. Cut ¾ way through the layers of dough using a sharp knife to make diamond shaped pieces (see Tip).
  9. Bake for 1 hour (350°F) or until golden and crispy. Meanwhile make the syrup.
  10. Place cloves, lemon peel and water into mixing bowl and boil 10 min/212°F/speed 1. Allow to cool to 160°F. Remove and discard cloves and lemon peel. Add honey and stir 10 sec/speed 3.
  11. Remove Baklava from oven and evenly pour honey syrup over the top.
  12. Cut diamond pieces all the way through. Sprinkle with 4 tablespoons reserved nut mixture.
  13. Allow to sit overnight before serving.

Useful Items

baking pan (approx. 9 in. x 13 in. x 2 in.), pastry brush, sharp knife, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • Baklava needs to sit overnight for the stickiness to really set in and for flavors to develop.
  • Phyllo dough can dry out quickly. Place dough onto a damp kitchen towel, cover phyllo with a damp cloth while assembling the baklava to keep it from drying out.
  • To make diamond shapes: make a diagonal cut from one corner of the pan to the opposite corner with a sharp knife. Continue making diagonal cuts parallel to the first line. Then make lengthwise cuts parallel to the long edge of the pan at regular intervals to form a diamond pattern.
  • Number of portions can be doubled: cut 24 pieces for more and smaller servings.
  • Use a light flavored honey.
