Jam-filled sweet potato doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 300 g jam (see Tips)
  • 500 g filtered water
  • 200 g sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 200 g plant-based milk of choice
  • 50 g coconut oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 tsp vanilla sugar (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp dried instant yeast
  • 480 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tbsp vodka
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • extra virgin olive oil, for deep frying
  • sugar of choice (milled), for dusting (see Tips)

Preparation

  1. Place jam into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain jam into a bowl and set aside. Discard any seeds or solids. Rinse mixing bowl.
  2. Place water into mixing bowl. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh sweet potato into it. Secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove Varoma dish and set aside. Discard water.
  3. Place steamed sweet potato and milk into mixing bowl and mix 15 sec/speed 7. Scrape down base and sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil, vanilla sugar and yeast, then heat 3 min/37°C/speed 1.
  5. Add flour, vodka and salt and knead 3 min/. Lightly oil a bowl and transfer dough into it. Cover with a kitchen towel and set aside to prove in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour).
  6. Transfer dough onto a silicone bread mat or lightly floured work surface and roll out (1-1.5 cm thickness). Using a cookie cutter (7 cm), cut portions of the dough and place onto floured work surface (leaving approx. 5 cm between portions). Re-roll remaining dough and cut out remaining portions (approx. 14 doughnuts in total). Set aside to prove for a further 30 minutes. Line a plate with paper towel and set aside.
  7. Place a deep saucepan over medium-high heat, add oil and bring to 170°C (see Tips). Be sure to keep your oil at this temperature while frying.
  8. Working in batches, carefully place doughnuts into hot oil and fry on both sides for 3-4 minutes or until golden brown. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove doughnuts and transfer onto lined plate to drain for 5 minutes.
  9. Using the handle end of a teaspoon, make a hole in the bottom or side of each doughnut. Transfer reserved jam into a piping bag with a long nozzle (1 cm). Pipe approx. 1 tablespoon of the jam into hole in each doughnut.
  10. Allow doughnuts to cool to room temperature (approx. 30 minutes), then dust with milled sugar to serve.

Useful Items

large bowl, paper towel, silicone bread mat, round cookie cutter (7 cm), slotted spoon, bowl, fine-mesh sieve, deep saucepan, plate, piping bag with a plain nozzle (1 cm), rolling pin, kitchen towel, kitchen thermometer, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • To make your own jam and vanilla sugar, refer to the recipes on Cookidoo® (www.cookidoo.com.au).
  • To check your oil is at 170°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into oil; it should turn golden brown in approx. 20 seconds.
  • You can mill Rapadura or coconut sugar to dust over doughnuts.
  • If you are unable to find a piping bag with a long nozzle, you could use a food syringe with a plastic tip.
