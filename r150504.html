Prosciutto and sun-dried tomato muffins

Ingredients

  • olive oil spray, for greasing
  • 80 g Parmesan cheese, cut into pieces (3 cm)
  • 60 g buckwheat groats
  • 200 g raw almonds
  • 3 sprigs fresh basil, leaves only
  • 80 g sun-dried tomatoes, cut into pieces
  • 300 g cottage cheese
  • 1 ½ tsp gluten free baking powder
  • 100 g water
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 12 slices prosciutto (approx. 200 g)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Grease a 12-hole muffin tray and set aside.
  2. Place Parmesan into mixing bowl and grate 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer 2 tablespoons into a small bowl and set aside. Leave remaining Parmesan in mixing bowl.
  3. Add buckwheat groats to mixing bowl and mill 1 min/speed 9.
  4. Add almonds and mill 10 sec/speed 7.
  5. Add basil, tomatoes, cottage cheese, baking powder, water, eggs and salt and mix 20 sec/speed 4. Line each muffin mould with 1 slice of prosciutto and divide mixture evenly between muffin moulds. Sprinkle with reserved Parmesan, then bake for 20-25 minutes (200°C) or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre of the muffins comes out clean. Transfer onto a wire rack to cool slightly before serving.

Useful Items

12-hole muffin tray, wire rack, small bowl, wooden skewer, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • You can store these muffins in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • These high protein muffins are a wonderful "on-the-go" breakfast or an energy-boosting afternoon snack.
