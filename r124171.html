Balti coconut fish curry

Ingredients

Balti paste

  • 2 fresh long red chillies, trimmed and cut into halves
  • 40 g fresh ginger, peeled
  • 20 g garlic cloves
  • 320 g fresh coriander (approx. 5 bunches), leaves, stalks and roots, cut into thirds (see Tips)
  • 160 g brown onion, cut into halves
  • 60 g olive oil (see Tips)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 20 g ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 120 g fresh tomatoes, cut into quarters
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp raw sugar
  • 2 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 120 g water

Balti coconut fish curry

  • 1500 g firm white fish fillets, cut into pieces (4 cm)
  • 1 pinch salt, to season
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper, to season
  • 250 g string beans, trimmed
  • 800 g coconut milk

Preparation

Balti paste

  1. Place chillies, ginger, garlic and 160 g of the coriander into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and if necessary, repeat chopping 5 sec/speed 8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  3. Add onion and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add oil, turmeric, ground coriander and cumin and sauté 7 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  5. Add tomatoes, salt, sugar and garam masala and blend 20 sec/speed 7. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  6. Add water and cook 6 min/100°C/speed . Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  7. Add remaining 160 g coriander and chop 5 sec/speed 5.
  8. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a bowl and set aside. Clean and dry mixing bowl.

Balti coconut fish curry

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh fish into it. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl and weigh 750 g of the fish into it. Place Varoma dish into position and weigh remaining 750 g fish into it. Spread 100 g of the reserved Balti paste onto fish in Varoma dish only.
  3. Insert Varoma tray and weigh string beans onto it. Remove Varoma and set aside. Remove simmering basket with aid of spatula and set aside.
  4. Place coconut milk and 400 g reserved Balti paste into mixing bowl. Insert simmering basket with fish and place Varoma into position. Secure Varoma lid and cook 20 min/Varoma/speed 2.
  5. Stir fish and beans, secure Varoma lid and cook 5 min/Varoma/speed 2 or until fish and beans are cooked to your liking. Transfer fish and beans into a serving bowl and top with curry sauce before serving.

Hints & Tricks

  • Serve your curry with rice, yoghurt and garnished with coriander, if preferred.
  • Clean and scrape down the roots of your coriander to remove the tougher, more fibrous bits on the outside before adding to the mixing bowl.
  • You can replace olive oil with ghee, if preferred. To make your own ghee, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • To make your own garam masala, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.
  • Leftover paste can be frozen.
