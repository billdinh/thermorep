Sichuan pepper and chilli salt squid

Ingredients

Pepper and chilli salt

  • 4 tbsp sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp sea salt flakes
  • 1 tbsp dried chilli flakes

Squid

  • 100 g potato starch
  • 500 g medium whole squid (approx. 2), cleaned
  • vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • 2 - 3 sprigs fresh chives, cut into thin slices
  • 2 lemons, cut into wedges

Preparation

Pepper and chilli salt

  1. Place peppercorns, salt and chilli into mixing bowl and mill 30 sec/speed 10. Transfer two-thirds of the mixture into a bowl and set aside.

Squid

  1. Add potato starch into mixing bowl and Turbo/1 sec/2 times. Transfer into a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Slice each squid tube into 2 flat sheets and pat dry. Finely score the outer side with a sharp knife, then cut into pieces (4 x 5 cm). Cut larger tentacles into halves.
  3. Place a small saucepan over medium-high heat, then add vegetable oil (5 cm high) and bring to 170°C (to check your oil is at 170°C, use a kitchen thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into oil; it should turn golden brown in 20 seconds). Keep your oil at this temperature while frying. Working in batches, coat squid in reserved flour mixture and gently shake off any excess flour. Carefully drop squid into oil and fry for approx. 2 minutes or until very light golden. Carefully transfer squid onto paper towel to drain and immediately sprinkle with salt mixture and garnish with chives. Repeat until all squid is cooked. Serve hot with lemon wedges.

Useful Items

bowl, large bowl, paper towel, saucepan, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • If you can't buy whole squid, thawed frozen squid tubes work well.
  • You can replace the squid with prawns, fish or chicken pieces (adjust frying time accordingly).
