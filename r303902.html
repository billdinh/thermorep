Fermented beetroot dip

Ingredients

Soaking

  • 200 g raw cashews (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • filtered water, for soaking

Beetroot dip

  • 500 g beetroot (approx. 3 beetroots), peeled and cut into pieces (approx. 3 cm)
  • salt, to season
  • ground black pepper, to season
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for covering dip
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 dairy free probiotic capsules, contents only (see Tips)

Preparation

Soaking

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh cashews into it. Add salt, cover with water and set aside to soak for 3-6 hours.
  2. Using simmering basket, drain and rinse soaked cashews.

Beetroot dip

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper.
  2. Place prepared tray onto mixing bowl lid and weigh beetroot onto it. Season with salt, pepper and drizzle with oil. Roast for 20-25 minutes (200°C), until tender.
  3. Place garlic, contents of probiotic capsules, drained cashews and roasted beetroot into mixing bowl and blend 30 sec/speed 10. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Carefully transfer mixture into sterilised sealable jars, only filling three-quarters of the jar. Gently tap bottom of jar on kitchen bench. Pour enough oil over dip so it is completely covered (see Tips). Seal and store in a dark place away from direct sunlight for 24-48 hours or until small air pockets start to appear (see Tips). The dip will taste tangy and tart. Serve as desired or place into refrigerator until ready to use (see Tips).

Useful Items

bowl, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), baking paper, sterilised sealable jars, oven, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • If you don't have time to soak/activate your cashews, soak nuts in boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain and use as directed.
  • You can store this dip in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Be careful to keep the jar topped up with a layer of oil whenever you use some of the dip.
  • To increase shelf life, dehydrate drained cashews in oven (50°C) or dehydrator for 12-24 hours, until nuts have completely dried out. By using dehydrated, activated cashews, it will increase the shelf life of your dip to 12 days when dip is covered with oil.
  • Probiotics are live micro-organisms that can support digestive health and immune function, including improving resilience to infections and improving digestion of lactose. You can find them in the refrigerated section of most chemists and health food stores. We recommend purchasing chilled probiotics, to ensure the organisms are most effective.
  • To avoid the lid popping off during fermentation, do not fill jars more than three-quarters full. When filling the jars, take care not to get any dip on the sides of the jar as if left exposed (not covered by oil), it may get mouldy and spoil the dip.
  • In colder climates, dip may take up to 4 days to ferment.
