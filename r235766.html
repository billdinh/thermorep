Mixed Meat Stew with Polenta - Spezzatino misto con polenta

Ingredients

Stew

  • 1 - 2 Tbsp olive oil, for frying
  • 1000 - 1200 g meat pieces, sinew free, mixed types - pork, lamb or wild boar
  • 50 g olive oil, plus 1-2 Tbsp, for frying
  • 150 g celery stalks, cut in pieces
  • 80 g onions, quartered
  • 125 g carrots, cut in pieces
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 100 g red wine, full-bodied Italian
  • ½ Tbsp juniper berries, lightly crushed
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 3 fresh sage leaves
  • 1 tsp fresh rosemary, leaves only
  • 700 g passata
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 Tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper

Polenta

  • 40 g Parmesan cheese, cut in pieces (2 cm)
  • 1500 g water
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 300 g polenta (quick cook)
  • 60 g Fontina cheese, cut in pieces

Preparation

Stew

  1. Heat 1-2 Tbsp oil in a large frying pan over a high heat, then brown meat in batches.
  2. Place 50 g oil, celery, onions, carrots and garlic in mixing bowl, chop 5 sec/speed 5 then sauté 7 min/100°C/speed .
  3. Add wine and simmer 3 min/100°C/speed .
  4. Add browned meat, juniper berries, peppercorns, sage, rosemary and passata then cook 5 min/100°C/speed 0.5.
  5. Add nutmeg and tomato purée then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 60 min/98°C//speed 0.5 until meat is tender. Towards the end of this time, preheat oven to 120°C.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, stir with spatula, then transfer to a large casserole dish and place in oven to keep warm. Clean mixing bowl and make polenta.

Polenta

  1. Place Parmesan in mixing bowl then grate 10 sec/speed 10. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  2. Place water and salt in mixing bowl then bring to the boil 7 min/100°C/speed 1.
  3. Without setting a time, set speed 2, set scales on, then slowly weigh in polenta through hole in mixing bowl lid.
  4. Replace measuring cup and cook time indicated on packet/100°C/speed 2.
  5. Add Fontina cheese and reserved Parmesan then mix 2 min/speed 2. Serve with the hot stew.

Hints & Tricks

  • Polenta alla spianatora literally means ‘polenta on the table’ and is an ancient Italian way of serving a meal. Wet polenta is spread over a special wooden board in the middle of the table and topped with a rich, meaty stew. Families sit around the board and simply tuck into the stew with their forks, making it a very sociable way of dining. Our stew is based on a Tuscan ‘sottiglia’ and can be made with whatever meats you have on hand. Serve on plates, or why not try the traditional way of pouring it out onto a board or table!
