Bunny Chow with Durban Curry

Ingredients

Bread Dough

  • 1 Tbsp dried instant yeast
  • 350 g water
  • 750 g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 ½ tsp fine sea salt

Durban Curry

  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 120 g onions, quartered
  • 30 g fresh root ginger, peeled, cut in round slices (2 mm)
  • 30 g vegetable oil, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 star anise
  • 2 Tbsp garam masala
  • 1 ½ tsp dried fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp chilli powder
  • 50 g tomato purée
  • 750 g stewing steak, cut in pieces (3 cm)
  • 1 beef stock cube (for 0.5 l), crumbled
  • 400 g water

Sambal and Assembly

  • 200 g tomatoes, diced (1 cm)
  • 100 g red onions, diced (5 mm)
  • ½ - 1 fresh green chilli, deseeded, diced (5 mm), to taste
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only, roughly chopped
  • 1 Tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 3 Tbsp olive oil

Preparation

Bread Dough

  1. Place yeast and water in mixing bowl then heat 1 min 30 sec/37°C/speed 1.
  2. Add flour and salt then knead 2 min/. Meanwhile, grease a large bowl and a 6-hole jumbo muffin tray.
  3. Tip dough out onto a work surface, shape into a ball then transfer to prepared bowl. Cover with cling film then leave in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hour). Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl. Towards the end of rising time, make curry.

Durban Curry

  1. Place garlic, onions, ginger and vegetable oil in mixing bowl then chop 5 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add star anise, garam masala, fennel seeds, cinnamon and chilli powder then cook 2 min/100°C/speed 2.
  3. Add tomato purée and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 2.
  4. Add beef then cook 5 min/100°C//speed 1. Meanwhile, knock back risen dough. On a lightly-floured surface, divide dough into 6 equal-sized pieces, roll each into a ball and place one in each hole of prepared muffin tray. Cover with cling film then leave in a warm place for a further 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 180°C.
  5. Add beef stock and water then, with simmering basket in place of measuring cup, cook 60 min/110°C//speed . Meanwhile, once dough has risen, bake for 20-30 minutes (180°C) until bread sounds hollow when tapped underneath. Transfer to a wire rack to cool slightly.

Sambal and Assembly

  1. Place a bowl on mixing bowl lid. Weigh in tomatoes and red onions then remove bowl. Add chilli, coriander, vinegar and olive oil to bowl with tomatoes and mix well with spatula.
  2. Slice tops off bread rolls and reserve, then remove the soft middle with a tablespoon (see tip). Fill hollowed out bread with curry, replace reserved bread top and serve hot with sambal.

Useful Items

cling film, 6-hole jumbo muffin tray, large bowl, wire rack, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • The bread taken out of the middle of the roll is usually used to mop up the curry on the plate at the end. Alternatively, it can used to make breadcrumbs.
