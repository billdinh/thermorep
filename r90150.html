Pfeffernüsse (German spice biscuits)

Ingredients

Pfeffernüsse

  • 150 g molasses
  • 70 g honey
  • 90 g butter
  • 550 g plain flour
  • 150 g white sugar
  • 80 g brown sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground cloves
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 2 eggs

Glaze

  • 280 g icing sugar
  • 1 tbsp rum (optional)
  • 30 g hot water

Preparation

Pfeffernüsse

  1. Place molasses, honey and butter into mixing bowl and heat 7 min/80°C/speed 2. Transfer into a bowl and allow to cool. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  2. Place all remaining pfeffernüsse ingredients, except eggs, into mixing bowl and mix 5 sec/speed 5.
  3. Add eggs and reserved honey and molasses mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 5. Wrap dough in plastic wrap or a silicone bread mat, then place in refrigerator for a minimum 2 hours or overnight. Clean and dry mixing bowl.
  4. Preheat oven to 160°C. Line 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside.
  5. Take 2 teaspoons of the dough at a time and roll into balls (approx. 47). Arrange balls on prepared baking trays 4 cm apart and press down on each ball to flatten slightly.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes (160°C), then transfer onto a wire rack and allow to cool.

Glaze

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and mill 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  2. Add rum (optional) and water and mix 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Place pfeffernüsse onto a sheet of baking paper then, using a pastry brush, spread glaze onto the top of each biscuit. Serve immediately or transfer into a sealable storage container to store until use.

Useful Items

wire rack, silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, 2 baking trays (40 x 35 cm), baking paper, pastry brush, sealable container, refrigerator, oven

Hints & Tricks

  • If your icing is too thick add 1 tsp water and mix 3 sec/speed 3.
  • Pfeffernüsse or Peppernuts are a traditional spice biscuit enjoyed in Germany, Denmark and The Netherlands. These delicious little biscuits have been closely related to Christmas since the 1850's and make a delightful gift when packaged up for friends and family.
