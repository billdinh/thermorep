Rogan josh

Ingredients

Marinade

  • ½ tsp asafoetida (see Tips)
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 300 g natural yoghurt
  • 1000 g boneless lamb leg, trimmed and cut into pieces (3 cm)

Curry

  • 3 fresh tomatoes, 2 deseeded and cut into quarters, 1 cut into eighths, for garnishing
  • 300 g red onion, cut into quarters
  • 30 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 3 cm piece cinnamon quill
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp cardamom pods (green)
  • 2 cardamom pods (black)
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 20 g garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 40 g ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 60 g tomato paste
  • 2 tsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves only, torn into pieces, for garnishing

Preparation

Marinade

  1. Place asafoetida and water into a large bowl and stir to dissolve. Place the large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh yoghurt and lamb into it. Stir to combine, then set bowl aside.

Curry

  1. Place 2 of the tomatoes and onion into mixing bowl and blend 8 sec/speed 8. Transfer into a seperate bowl and set aside.
  2. Place ghee into mixing bowl and melt 1 min/Varoma/speed 1.
  3. Add bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, green cardamom, black cardamom and fennel seeds and cook 30 sec/Varoma/speed 1.
  4. Add garlic paste and ginger paste and cook 1 min/100°C/speed 1.
  5. Add ground coriander, chilli powder (optional), cumin, turmeric, tomato paste and reserved onion mixture and cook 5 min/100°C/speed 2.
  6. Add reserved lamb with marinade and cook 50 min/90°C//speed , without measuring cup.
  7. Add garam masala, salt and pepper and cook 20 min/90°C//speed  or until lamb is tender. Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and garnish with tomato wedges. Cover and set aside for 5 minutes, then sprinkle with coriander leaves to serve.

Hints & Tricks

  • Asafoetida comes from the root of a perennial Indian herb. It has a strong odour, but the aroma softens as it cooks, and tastes similar to onion and garlic, enhancing the flavour of savoury dishes. It helps reduce flatulence and constipation, and reduces the growth of microflora in the gut. A little goes a long way, so only a small amount is needed. Asafoetida can be found in Indian grocers or online.
  • To make your own ghee, garlic paste, ginger paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • You can serve this with rice, bread, chutney or your favourite side. We served ours with Parathas, please refer to the recipe on this recipe chip.

Rogan josh orginated from the Kashmir region of India and is traditionally served with lamb or mutton, which is stewed for a couple of hours with spices. Cockscomb or ratton jot was originally used to give the curry its trademark red colour, however we use tomato paste and Kashmiri chilli to achieve the red tone.
