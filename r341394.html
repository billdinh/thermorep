Freekeh and broad bean salad

Ingredients

Tofu feta

  • 300 g firm tofu, pressed and cut into pieces (approx. 2 cm - see Tips)
  • 60 g lemon juice
  • 120 g filtered water
  • 120 g apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • Â½ tsp salt

Freekeh and broad bean salad

  • 2 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 1 sprig fresh flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves only
  • 100 g freekeh
  • 1000 g filtered water
  • 1 tsp stock paste (see Tips)
  • 200 g frozen broad beans
  • 2 baby cos lettuce, cut into slices (1.5 cm)
  • 2 tsp preserved lemon rind, finely diced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 - 2 pinches salt
  • 1 - 2 pinches ground black pepper

Preparation

Tofu feta

  1. Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh tofu, lemon juice, water and vinegar into it. Add oregano and salt and cover with plastic wrap. Place into refrigerator for a minimum of 2 hours (up to 3 days).

Freekeh and broad bean salad

  1. Place mint, parsley and coriander into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 7. Transfer into a large serving bowl and set aside.
  2. Place simmering basket onto mixing bowl lid and weigh freekeh into it. Rinse until water runs clear.
  3. Place water and stock paste into mixing bowl and insert simmering basket with freekeh. Place Varoma into position and weigh broad beans into it, then secure Varoma lid and steam 20 min/Varoma/speed 3. Remove Varoma, then remove simmering basket with aid of spatula. Rinse freekeh under cold water and transfer into large bowl with herbs. Carefully remove beans from pods and add to bowl with freekeh and herbs. Discard pods.
  4. Place lettuce, preserved lemon, lemon juice, salt and pepper into bowl with freekeh and beans. Drain tofu feta, add to salad and gently stir to combine before serving.

Useful Items

bowl, large serving bowl, plastic wrap, refrigerator

Hints & Tricks

  • Tofu is often packaged in water and it is best to press it before using to achieve a better result. To press tofu, use a tofu press or line a plate with paper towels. Place tofu block on the paper towels and cover with another layer of paper towel. Place a plate or a cutting board on top of the paper towels, then place heavy weights (books or cans) on top. Set aside for several hours until liquid has been pressed out.
  • We recommend using organic tofu whenever possible.
  • Please refer to the Mushroom stock paste recipe on this recipe chip or The Basic Cookbook for the Vegetable stock paste recipe.
  • You can serve this dish as a main meal or a side dish.
