Samosas

Ingredients

Pastry

  • 260 g water
  • 20 g ghee (see Tips)
  • 500 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp salt

Filling

  • 100 g red onion, cut into quarters
  • 2 - 3 fresh long green chillies, trimmed
  • 5 sprigs fresh coriander, leaves and stalks
  • 4 sprigs fresh mint, leaves only
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 300 g lamb mince
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 ¼ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 3 tsp garlic paste (see Tips)
  • 3 tsp ginger paste (see Tips)
  • 1 tbsp garam masala (see Tips)
  • 1250 g vegetable oil, for frying

Preparation

Pastry

  1. Place all pastry ingredients into mixing bowl and mix 20 sec/speed 2, then knead 2 min/.
  2. Transfer pastry onto a silicone bread mat or floured work surface and shape into a ball. Wrap dough in silicone bread mat or plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to rest for 20 minutes. Clean and dry mixing bowl. Meanwhile, continue with filling.

Filling

  1. Place onion, chillies, fresh coriander, mint, lemon juice and salt into mixing bowl and chop 7 sec/speed 5. Place simmering basket over a bowl and transfer mixture into it. Set aside to drain.
  2. Place lamb, pepper, cumin seeds, ground coriander, turmeric, garlic paste and ginger paste into mixing bowl and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1, without measuring cup.
  3. Add garam masala and reserved onion mixture and combine 30 sec//speed 1. Transfer into a bowl and set aside to cool slightly.

Assembly

  1. Line a baking tray (30 x 40 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Transfer chilled pastry onto silicone bread mat or floured work surface. Divide pastry into 3 equal portions. Using a rolling pin, roll out each portion into a thin rectangle (64 x 18 cm, approx. 1 mm thickness). Cut each rectangle into 8 strips (8 x 18 cm), creating 24 pastry strips in total.
  2. Place 1 tablespoon samosa filling at the top of each pastry strip. Fold top left corner of pastry strip diagonally to the right, enclosing filling and forming a triangle. Fold again (folding down) along bottom crease of triangle. Keep folding, creating triangles, to the bottom of the strip. Using your fingertips, dampen remaining pastry edge with water, then fold over to close and seal samosa. Set aside on prepared tray and cover with kitchen towel. Repeat with remaining pastry strips and samosa filling (makes approx. 24 samosas in total).
  3. Line a large plate with paper towel and set aside. Place a large saucepan over high heat and add oil. Heat oil to 170°C (see Tips). Working in batches, carefully place 4-5 samosas into hot oil using a slotted spoon and fry for 7-10 minutes, turning once during frying, until golden brown and crispy. Using a slotted spoon, carefully transfer samosas onto lined plate. Serve with chutney of choice (see Tips).

Useful Items

silicone bread mat, plastic wrap, bowl, baking paper, baking tray (30 x 40 cm), rolling pin, kitchen towel, kitchen thermometer, large plate, paper towel, large saucepan, slotted spoon, refrigerator, stove top

Hints & Tricks

  • You can replace the lamb with minced chicken or beef, or make vegetable samosas by using the Bombay aloo recipe on this recipe chip as a vegetarian filling option.
  • To make your own ghee, garlic paste, ginger paste and garam masala, please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip.
  • To check your oil is at 170°C, use a thermometer or drop a small cube of bread into the oil; bread will turn golden brown in 20 seconds at 170°C.
  • We served these samosas with the Tomato chutney and Tamarind chutney (please refer to the recipes on this recipe chip), but you can pair with any chutney of your choice.
