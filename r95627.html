Ice Cream Layer Cake

Ingredients

Berry Layer

  • 160 g sugar
  • 500 g double cream
  • 2 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt
  • 50 g fresh raspberries
  • 150 g blackberries

Vanilla Layer

  • 470 g double cream
  • 240 g milk
  • 140 g sugar
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Chocolate Layer

  • 180 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa solids), small pieces or callets
  • 200 g milk
  • 400 g double cream
  • 160 g sugar
  • 4 Tbsp cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1 tsp natural vanilla extract
  • 4 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt

Serving

  • 150 g fresh mixed berries

Preparation

Berry Layer

  1. Place sugar in mixing bowl and grind 10 sec/speed 9. Transfer 2 Tbsp to a small bowl and set aside for garnishing.
  2. Add cream, egg yolks, vanilla extract and salt then cook 6 min/80°C/speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. Place raspberries and blackberries in mixing bowl then blend 10 sec/speed 9. Scrape down lid and sides of mixing bowl with spatula.
  4. Add reserved egg mixture and mix 5 sec/speed 4. Pour into a shallow freezer-proof container, set aside to cool, then freeze for at least 8 hours or until solid. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make vanilla layer.

Vanilla Layer

  1. Place all vanilla layer ingredients in mixing bowl and cook 7 min/80°C/speed 4.
  2. Transfer to shallow freezer-proof containers, set aside to cool, then freeze for at least 8 hours or until solid. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and make chocolate layer.

Chocolate Layer

  1. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 8.
  2. Add milk, cream, sugar, cocoa powder, vanilla extract, egg yolks and salt then cook 8 min/90°C/speed 2.
  3. Transfer to shallow freezer-proof containers, set aside to cool then freeze for at least 6 hours or until solid Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl and line sides and base of a springform tin (Ø 24 cm) with baking paper.

Churning and Assembling

  1. Remove berry ice cream from freezer, tip out and cut into squares (3 cm). Place squares in mixing bowl and churn 1 min/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and churn again 20 sec/speed 4. Pour into prepared tin, level the surface, then freeze for at least 2 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  2. After 2 hours, remove vanilla ice cream from freezer, tip out and cut into squares (3 cm). Place half the frozen squares in mixing bowl and churn 1 min/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then churn again 20 sec/speed 4. Pour into tin, on top of frozen berry layer.
  3. Place remaining frozen vanilla ice cream squares in mixing bowl and churn 40 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and churn again 20 sec/speed 4. Pour into tin, level the surface, then freeze for at least 2 hours. Meanwhile, clean mixing bowl.
  4. After 2 hours, remove chocolate ice cream from freezer, tip out and cut into squares (3 cm). Place half the frozen squares in mixing bowl and churn 40 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then churn again 30 sec/speed 6. Pour into tin, on top of frozen vanilla layer.
  5. Place remaining frozen chocolate ice cream squares in mixing bowl and churn 40 sec/speed 9. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula then churn again 30 sec/speed 6. Pour into tin, level the surface, then freeze for at least 8 hours or overnight.

Serving

  1. When ready to serve, remove from tin and invert onto a serving plate, revealing berry ice cream as the top layer. Garnish with berries and a dusting of reserved icing sugar then serve immediately.

Useful Items

springform tin (Ø 24 cm), baking paper, long sharp knife, serving plate, shallow freezer-proof containers (e.g. aluminium, silicone), freezer

Hints & Tricks

  • Place serving plate in freezer for 30 minutes before serving.
