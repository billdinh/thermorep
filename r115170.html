Koththu roti

Ingredients

Spice mix

  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 3 tsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper

Curry

  • 500 g skinless chicken breast fillet, cut into strips (2.5 cm x 5 mm)
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp salt, plus extra to taste
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 ½ cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 cinnamon quill
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 40 g oil
  • 8 fresh curry leaves
  • 2 brown onions, cut into slices
  • 50 g tomato paste
  • 120 g coconut cream
  • 120 g coconut milk
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 red capsicum, cut into julienne
  • 1 carrot, cut into julienne
  • 1 - 2 pinches freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 rotis, cut into julienne (see Tips)
  • 1 lime, cut into 8 wedges, for garnishing

Preparation

Spice mix

  1. Place all spice mix ingredients into a small bowl and stir to combine. Set bowl aside.

Curry

  1. Place a large bowl onto mixing bowl lid and weigh chicken into it. Add lime juice, ½ tsp of the salt and half of the reserved spice mix and stir to combine until chicken is evenly coated. Cover with plastic wrap and place into refrigerator to marinate for a minimum 30 minutes.
  2. Place garlic and ginger into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5. Transfer into a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Place cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, 20 g of the oil, 5 of the curry leaves and half of the sliced onions into mixing bowl and cook 4 min/Varoma//speed .
  4. Insert butterfly whisk. Add marinated chicken, remaining spice mix and half of the ginger-garlic paste and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1.
  5. Add tomato paste, coconut cream, coconut milk and remaining ½ tsp salt and cook 10 min/100°C//speed 1, placing simmering basket instead of measuring cup onto mixing bowl lid, until curry is thick. Add sugar and extra salt to taste.  Remove butterfly whisk.Transfer into a thermal serving bowl or other large bowl and cover to keep warm.
  6. Place a large frying pan over medium heat and add remaining 20 g oil. Add remaining ginger-garlic paste, remaining half of the onions and remaining 3 curry leaves and cook for 5 minutes to soften.
  7. Add capsicum and carrot and season with salt and pepper, then sauté for 2 minutes, until vegetables are partly cooked. Push vegetables to the edges of the frying pan, creating a hole in centre of pan and crack eggs into it. Stir to scramble egg, then mix vegetables into scramble, until well combined. Add roti pieces and cook for 3 minutes, stirring well to combine.
  8. Divide vegetable mixture between 6 plates, top with chicken curry and garnish with lime wedges before serving.
